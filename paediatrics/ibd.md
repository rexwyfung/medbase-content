# Inflammatory Bowel Disease (Crohn’s Disease & Ulcerative Colitis) (炎症性腸病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- **Definition**: Chronic, relapsing idiopathic inflammation of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract.
- **Classification**: Comprises **Crohn's Disease (CD) (克隆氏症)** and **Ulcerative Colitis (UC) (潰瘍性結腸炎)**.

### Local Epidemiology
- **HK/Asia Incidence**: Rapidly ↑ due to westernization of diets & environment.
- **Onset**: Peaks in adolescence and early adulthood.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathogenesis
- Inappropriate, aggressive mucosal immune response to normal intestinal flora in a genetically susceptible host.

### Genetics
- **Mutations**: **NOD2** gene mutations strongly linked to CD.
- **Inheritance**: Polygenic, strong family history risk factor.

### Crohn's Disease Pathophysiology
- **Inflammation**: Transmural (full-thickness).
- **Location**: Any part of GI tract from mouth to anus.
- **Pattern**: "Skip lesions" (patchy, discontinuous).

### Ulcerative Colitis Pathophysiology
- **Inflammation**: Mucosal only.
- **Location**: Colon only.
- **Pattern**: Starts at rectum → spreads continuously proximally.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Common GI Symptoms
- Abdominal pain (腹痛).
- Bloody diarrhea (血便).
- Weight loss, lethargy, fever.

### Crohn's Disease Specific Signs
- **Oral**: Aphthous ulcers.
- **Abdominal**: Right lower quadrant (RLQ) pain (terminal ileitis mimicking appendicitis), palpable mass.
- **Perianal Disease (肛周疾病)**: Deep fissures, complex fistulas, massive skin tags.
- **Development**: Unexplained profound growth failure or delayed puberty may be the sole initial sign.

### Extraintestinal Manifestations
- **Dermatological**: Erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum.
- **Ocular**: Anterior uveitis.
- **Musculoskeletal**: Arthritis, clubbing.
- **Hepatic**: Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC) (原發性硬化性膽管炎) - tightly linked with UC.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating IBD from Infectious Colitis
- **Time Course**: Infectious is acute (days to weeks) vs. IBD is chronic (months).
- **Pattern**: IBD is relapsing/remitting.

### Differentiating CD from UC
| Feature | Crohn's Disease (CD) | Ulcerative Colitis (UC) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bowel Segment** | Mouth to anus | Colon only |
| **Inflammation** | Transmural | Mucosa only |
| **Distribution** | Skip lesions (patchy) | Continuous from rectum |
| **Symptoms** | +/- Blood, ↑ pain | Bloody diarrhea & mucus |
| **Perianal Disease** | Common | Rare |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Infectious & Autoimmune Differentials
- **Infectious Colitis**: *Salmonella*, *Shigella*, *Campylobacter*, *C. difficile*.
- **Autoimmune**: Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉).

### Surgical & Granulomatous Differentials
- **Surgical**: Appendicitis (盲腸炎) (can mimic terminal ileal CD).
- **Infectious Granulomatous**: GI Tuberculosis (腸結核) (endemic in HK, indistinguishable from CD).

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
- **Stool**: **Fecal Calprotectin (糞便鈣衛蛋白)** (highly sensitive/specific marker for active gut inflammation, used to track disease activity).
- **Stool Culture**: Exclude superimposed infection/ *C. diff*.

### Blood Tests
- **Inflammatory Markers**: ↑ CRP, ↑ ESR.
- **Hematology**: Microcytic anemia (bleeding), ↑ platelets.
- **Biochemistry**: ↓ Albumin (protein-losing enteropathy), ↓ Iron, ↓ Vitamin B12.

### Definitive Investigations (Gold Standard)
- **Procedure**: Upper and Lower Endoscopy with multiple biopsies.
- **CD Findings**: Cobblestone mucosa, deep linear ulcers, non-caseating granulomas on biopsy.
- **UC Findings**: Continuous mucosal erythema, friability, loss of vascular pattern, crypt abscesses on biopsy.

### Imaging
- **MRI Enterography (MRE)**: Evaluate small bowel involvement in CD (strictures, fistulas, mural thickening).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Crohn's Disease Acute Induction
- **1st Line**: **Exclusive Enteral Nutrition (EEN)** for 6-8 weeks (induces remission, promotes mucosal healing without steroids, ↑ growth).
- **2nd Line**: Systemic steroids (Prednisolone) if EEN fails or is intolerable.

### Ulcerative Colitis Acute Induction
- **Mild/Moderate**: **5-ASA (Mesalazine)** oral & topical (enemas/suppositories).
- **Severe Flare**: IV Hydrocortisone. Monitor closely for toxic megacolon.

### Chronic Maintenance Therapy (Both)
- **Immunomodulators**: Azathioprine, Methotrexate.
- **Biologics**: **Anti-TNF antibodies** (Infliximab, Adalimumab). Used early in severe disease ("top-down" therapy).

### Surgical Management
- **Ulcerative Colitis**: Curative (Panproctocolectomy with ileoanal pouch).
- **Crohn's Disease**: NOT curative. Bowel conservation is key. Reserved for complications (strictureplasty, abscess drainage).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Input
- **Core Team**: Paediatric gastroenterologist, specialized IBD nurses, dietician (crucial for EEN).
- **Psychosocial Care**: Clinical psychologist and Medical Social Worker (MSW) required due to massive psychosocial impact (chronic illness burden, steroid side effects like moon face, delayed puberty).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect
- **Refusal of Treatment**: Parents refusing essential immunosuppressants or biologics due to "natural therapy" beliefs, directly leading to permanent bowel destruction and growth stunting.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Life-Threatening Complications
- **Toxic Megacolon**: Massive colonic dilation + systemic toxicity (especially in UC) → high risk of perforation.
- **Bowel Obstruction**: Secondary to fibrotic strictures (especially in CD).

### Long-Term Complications
- **Malignancy**: Massive ↑ risk of **colorectal cancer** 10-15 years post-diagnosis → necessitates regular surveillance colonoscopies.
- **Systemic**: Permanent short stature, osteopenia/osteoporosis.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Forgetting to look at the anus during physical examination. Pearl: In Crohn's, the perianal inspection can immediately clinch the diagnosis (deep fissures, complex fistulas, massive skin tags).
* Viva Question: Why is surgery curative for Ulcerative Colitis but not Crohn's Disease? Answer: UC is strictly limited to the colonic mucosa; removing the entire colon eradicates the disease. CD can affect the entire GI tract from mouth to anus, so removing one segment does not prevent recurrence elsewhere.
* Pitfall: Assuming isolated delayed puberty is always constitutional. Unexplained profound growth failure or delayed puberty may be the sole initial presentation of Crohn's Disease.
