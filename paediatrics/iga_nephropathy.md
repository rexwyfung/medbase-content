# IgA Nephropathy (IgA 腎病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Disease Definition
* Most common primary **glomerulonephritis (原發性腎小球腎炎)** worldwide.
* Characterised by predominant deposition of **IgA immune complexes** in the glomerular mesangium.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology
* Highly prevalent in **East Asia**, including **Hong Kong (香港)**.
* Major cause of **chronic kidney disease (CKD) (慢性腎病)** & **end-stage renal failure (ESRF) (末期腎衰竭)** in young HK adults.
* Often detected incidentally during **Student Health Service (學生健康服務)** routine screening.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### The Multi-Hit Hypothesis
* **Hit 1**: Overproduction of poorly O-glycosylated IgA1 (**Galactose-deficient IgA1**).
* **Hit 2**: Formation of **autoantibodies (IgG)** against the abnormal IgA1.
* **Hit 3**: Formation of **circulating immune complexes**.
* **Hit 4**: Deposition in **renal mesangium** → local inflammation → mesangial proliferation → eventual glomerulosclerosis.

### Exacerbating Triggers
* Frequently triggered by **mucosal infections (黏膜感染)** (URTI or GI tract), as mucosal surfaces are the primary sites of IgA production.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Classic Synpharyngitic Presentation
* **Synpharyngitic Macroscopic Haematuria (肉眼可見血尿)**:
  * Painless, visible (cola-coloured/red) haematuria.
  * Occurs **concurrently (within 1-2 days)** with a URTI or GI infection.

### Incidental Presentation
* **Asymptomatic microscopic haematuria (無症狀微血尿)**.
* ± mild proteinuria discovered on routine school/clinic screening.

### Rare Severe Presentations
* **Nephrotic syndrome (腎病綜合症)** (heavy proteinuria, severe oedema).
* **Rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis (RPGN) (快速進展性腎小球腎炎)** (acute kidney injury, crescentic GN).

### Physical Examination Findings
* Often entirely normal in early stages.
* Assess for **Hypertension (高血壓)** (poor prognostic sign).
* Check for fluid overload/oedema if presenting with heavy proteinuria.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing IgA Nephropathy vs APSGN

| Feature | IgA Nephropathy (IgA 腎病) | Post-Streptococcal GN (APSGN) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Timing of Haematuria** | **Synpharyngitic** (concurrent with URTI, 1-2 days) | **Post-infectious** (1-2 weeks AFTER infection) |
| **Complement C3 Level** | **Normal** | **Low** |
| **Systemic Symptoms** | Rare | Common (fluid overload, severe hypertension) |
| **Recurrence** | Frequent with subsequent URTIs | Extremely rare |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Primary Differentials

| Condition | Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Post-Streptococcal GN (鏈球菌感染後腎小球腎炎)** | History of strep throat/impetigo, ↓ C3 complement, +ve ASOT/Anti-DNase B. |
| **Alport Syndrome (阿爾波特綜合症)** | X-linked, progressive nephritis, +ve **sensorineural hearing loss (感音神經性聽力喪失)**, anterior lenticonus. |
| **Thin Basement Membrane Disease (薄基底膜病)** | Benign familial haematuria, normal renal function, usually NO proteinuria. |
| **Lupus Nephritis (狼瘡性腎炎)** | Multisystem involvement (malar rash, arthritis), +ve **ANA/Anti-dsDNA**, ↓ C3/C4. |
| **IgA Vasculitis / Henoch-Schönlein Purpura (IgA 血管炎)** | Systemic variant of IgA Nephropathy. Palpable purpura on buttocks/legs, arthritis, colicky abdominal pain. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**:
  * **Dysmorphic RBCs** & **RBC casts** (confirms glomerular origin).
  * Variable proteinuria.
* **Blood Pressure (血壓) Monitoring**: Detect secondary hypertension.

### Blood Investigations
* **U&E, Creatinine (eGFR)**: Baseline renal function.
* **Serum IgA**: ↑ in ~50% (supportive but NOT diagnostic).
* **Complement Levels (C3 & C4)**: **Normal** (crucial to exclude APSGN & Lupus).
* **Autoimmune Screen**: ANA, Anti-dsDNA, ANCA (-ve in primary IgAN).

### Gold Standard Investigation
* **Renal Biopsy (腎活檢)**: Definitive diagnosis.
  * **Light Microscopy**: Mesangial proliferation.
  * **Immunofluorescence**: Granular deposition of **IgA & C3** in the mesangium.

### Oxford Classification (MEST-C Score)
* Histological grading used by pathologists to predict ESRF progression risk:
  * **M**: Mesangial hypercellularity.
  * **E**: Endocapillary proliferation.
  * **S**: Segmental glomerulosclerosis.
  * **T**: Tubular atrophy/interstitial fibrosis.
  * **C**: Crescents.

### Disease Monitoring Investigations
* **Early Morning Urine Protein-to-Creatinine Ratio (UPCR)**: Essential to quantify & track proteinuria.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
* Usually conservative & supportive for isolated macroscopic haematuria.
* If presenting with **RPGN / Crescentic IgAN**:
  * Pulse **IV Methylprednisolone (甲基柏尼松龍)**.
  * Followed by oral taper + **Cyclophosphamide (環磷酰胺)** or **MMF**.

### First-Line Chronic Management (Renoprotection)
* **ACE Inhibitors (Enalapril)** or **ARBs (Losartan)**:
  * Indicated for ANY patient with proteinuria >0.5g/day or hypertension.
  * Action: ↓ intraglomerular pressure → ↓ proteinuria → slows CKD progression.
* Strict BP control (Target <75th percentile for age/sex/height in children).

### Advanced Immunosuppression Protocols (Hong Kong HA)
* Indicated for persistent significant proteinuria despite max ACEi/ARB (if eGFR >50 ml/min).
* **Corticosteroids (Prednisolone)**: Guided by TESTING trial protocols; carefully balances renoprotection vs. infection risk.
* **Mycophenolate Mofetil (MMF)**: Effective steroid-sparing agent, highly prevalent in Asian/Chinese treatment protocols.

### Emerging HA Therapies
* **SGLT2 Inhibitors (Dapagliflozin/Empagliflozin)**: Increasingly standard adjunct for proteinuric CKD to ↓ progression.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Approach
* **Paediatric Nephrologist (兒科腎科醫生)**: Lifelong follow-up to monitor UPCR, eGFR, and BP.
* **Dietitian (營養師)**: Low-sodium diet (<2g/day) to optimize ACEi/ARB efficacy and BP control.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW, 醫務社工)**: Facilitate financial support for off-formulary/self-financed drugs (e.g., SGLT2i) under HA safety net.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Parental Counselling & Reassurance
* **Chronicity**: Educate that this is a chronic disease requiring lifelong compliance.
* **Recurrence**: Warn parents that frightening macroscopic haematuria will likely recur with future URTIs but is usually self-limiting and does not equal immediate renal failure.
* **Red Flags**: Haematuria is organic here, but always maintain a low threshold to suspect urogenital trauma or fabricated illness if presentation is bizarre or accompanied by unexplained bruising.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### The Rule of Thirds Prognosis
* **1/3**: Benign non-progressive disease.
* **1/3**: Slow, indolent progression.
* **1/3**: Progress to **End-Stage Renal Failure (ESRF) (末期腎衰竭)** within 10-20 years.

### Poor Prognostic Factors
* **Clinical**: Hypertension, severe persistent proteinuria, impaired eGFR at presentation, male sex.
* **Histological**: Extensive fibrosis or crescents on biopsy (High MEST-C score).
* **Post-Transplant**: High recurrence rate in renal allografts (~30%), though subsequent graft loss is usually slow.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the classic timing of macroscopic haematuria in IgA nephropathy compared to post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis? Answer: IgA nephropathy is 'synpharyngitic' (occurs concurrently with URTI, within 1-2 days). APSGN is 'post-infectious' (occurs 1-2 weeks AFTER the URTI).
* Trap: Assuming low C3 in a patient with IgA nephropathy. Pearl: Complement levels (C3, C4) are typically NORMAL in IgA nephropathy. Low C3 suggests APSGN, Lupus Nephritis, or C3 Glomerulopathy.
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents that a single episode of haematuria is entirely benign without arranging follow-up. Always establish long-term monitoring of BP and UPCR, as 1/3 of patients will silently progress to ESRF over decades.
