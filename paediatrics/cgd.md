# Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD) (慢性肉芽腫病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Chronic Granulomatous Disease
* **Primary Immunodeficiency** (原發性免疫缺陷病) affecting phagocytic cells (neutrophils and macrophages).
* Cells have normal phagocytosis but a profound inability to generate a **respiratory burst** (呼吸爆發).
* Phagocytes are rendered entirely incapable of killing specific intracellular **catalase-positive** (+ve) bacteria and fungi.

### Local Epidemiology of Chronic Granulomatous Disease
* Rare disorder globally (~1 in 200,000 live births).
* Consistently seen in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) paediatric immunology clinics.
* Predominantly affects males due to its primary X-linked inheritance pattern.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Inheritance of CGD
* **X-linked recessive** (X連鎖隱性): ~70% of cases (affects males, mutation in *CYBB* gene).
* **Autosomal recessive** (體染色體隱性): ~30% of cases (affects both sexes).

### Pathophysiology of the Respiratory Burst Defect
* Mutation involves the **NADPH oxidase** (NADPH氧化酶) enzyme complex.
* **Normal Physiology:** * Neutrophil engulfs microbe into a phagosome.
  * NADPH oxidase pumps massive amounts of superoxide radicals ($O_2^-$, $H_2O_2$ / hydrogen peroxide) into the phagosome.
  * Causes a lethal "respiratory burst" neutralizing the pathogen.
  * **CGD Pathology:** * Phagocytosis is intact.
  * "Bleach pump" (NADPH oxidase) is completely broken.
  * Microbes survive and replicate happily inside the macrophage.

### Pathophysiology of Catalase-Positive Vulnerability
* **Catalase-Negative (-ve) Bacteria:** * Generate their own small amounts of $H_2O_2$ metabolic waste.
  * CGD neutrophil steals this microbial $H_2O_2$ to kill the organism.
* **Catalase-Positive (+ve) Bacteria/Fungi:** * Produce **Catalase** (過氧化氫酶) enzyme which destroys their own $H_2O_2$ waste.
  * Leaves the CGD neutrophil with absolutely zero oxidative weapons.

### Pathophysiology of Granuloma Formation
* Immune system detects unkillable intracellular microbes.
* Attempts to wall off infection by recruiting massive numbers of macrophages.
* Results in widespread **Granulomas** (肉芽腫) formation.
* Causes severe physical obstruction of adjacent tracts/organs.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Classic Clinical Presentation of CGD
* Typically presents in infancy or early childhood (toddler years).
* A 3-year-old boy presenting with recurrent deep visceral abscesses.
* History of recurrent pneumonia, lymphadenitis, or chronic osteomyelitis.
* **Faltering growth** (生長遲緩) / failure to thrive.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Infectious Symptoms & Signs
* **Deep Visceral Abscesses** (深部內臟膿腫):
  * Liver abscesses (肝膿腫) (Classic hallmark)
  * Splenic abscesses
  * Lung abscesses / severe pneumonia
* **Lymphatic:** Suppurative lymphadenitis (化膿性淋巴結炎).
* **Skeletal:** Chronic osteomyelitis (慢性骨髓炎).
* **Skin:** Recurrent severe subcutaneous abscesses, poor wound healing.

### Specific Catalase-Positive Organism Susceptibility (SPACE)
* **S**taphylococcus aureus (金黃色葡萄球菌)
* **P**seudomonas aeruginosa (綠膿桿菌)
* **A**spergillus species (曲霉菌) (Fungus → leading cause of mortality)
* **C**andida / Corynebacterium / Nocardia
* **E**nterobacteriaceae (e.g., *Serratia marcescens*, *Burkholderia cepacia*, *Klebsiella*)

### Granulomatous Symptoms & Signs
* **GI Tract:** * Gastric outlet obstruction (vomiting).
  * Granulomatous colitis resembling Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) (chronic diarrhoea, bloody stools).
* **Genitourinary Tract:** * Ureteral obstruction (hydronephrosis, dysuria).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing CGD from Typical Childhood Abscesses
* **Healthy Children:** * Develop superficial, minor skin abscesses (usually *Staphylococcus*).
* **CGD Children:** * Develop **deep visceral abscesses** (liver, spleen).
  * Infections caused by opportunistic/weird organisms (e.g., *Serratia*, *Burkholderia*).
  * Presence of a single deep liver abscess strongly mandates functional testing for CGD.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Recurrent Paediatric Abscesses

| Condition | Key Differentiating Features | Common Organisms |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)** | Deep visceral abscesses, obstructive granulomas, normal WBC counts. | *S. aureus*, *Aspergillus*, *Serratia*, *Burkholderia*. |
| **Hyper-IgE Syndrome (Job Syndrome)** | "Cold" skin abscesses lacking prominent inflammation/pus, eczema, ↑↑ IgE, coarse facies. | *S. aureus*, *Candida*. |
| **Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD)** | Delayed umbilical cord separation, profound neutrophilia (↑↑ WBC), **absent pus** at infection sites. | *S. aureus*, Gram-negative rods. |
| **Cystic Fibrosis (CF)** | High sweat chloride, pancreatic exocrine insufficiency, malabsorption, bronchiectasis. | *Pseudomonas*, *Aspergillus*. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Basic Blood Investigations
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC):** * Normal WBC count.
  * Normal absolute neutrophil and lymphocyte counts.
  * No quantitative immune defect; this is a purely *functional* defect.
* **Immunoglobulins (IgG/IgA/IgM):** * Normal or ↑ (Hypergammaglobulinemia) due to chronic immune stimulation.
* **Inflammatory Markers:** * ↑ CRP and ↑ ESR during acute flares.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigations
* **Dihydrorhodamine 123 (DHR) Flow Cytometry** (DHR流式細胞儀分析):
  * **Current Gold Standard** in HK HA.
  * *Normal neutrophils:* Fluoresce brightly (DHR oxidized to rhodamine).
  * *CGD neutrophils:* Zero or severely ↓ fluorescence.
* **Nitroblue Tetrazolium (NBT) Test** (NBT試驗):
  * *Historical/Classic test.*
  * *Normal neutrophils:* Reduce yellow dye to dark blue formazan.
  * *CGD neutrophils:* Dye remains yellow (fails to reduce).

### Specialized & Genetic Investigations
* **Microbiological Cultures:** * Aggressive sampling of abscesses for tissue culture (Fungal and Bacterial).
* **Genetic Testing:** * Mutational analysis of *CYBB* gene (X-linked) or other NADPH oxidase complex genes.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Lifelong Prophylactic Management
* **Antibacterial Prophylaxis:** * Daily oral **Co-trimoxazole** (Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole) (復方新諾明).
* **Antifungal Prophylaxis:** * Daily oral **Itraconazole** (伊曲康唑).

### Acute Infection Management
* **Empirical Antimicrobials:** * Prompt initiation of broad-spectrum IV antibiotics/antifungals for any fever.
* **Targeted Antimicrobials:** * Prolonged courses (weeks to months) tailored to specific culture sensitivities.
* **Surgical Intervention:** * Early and aggressive surgical drainage of deep abscesses (liver/lung/lymph nodes).

### Immunomodulation & Curative Management
* **Interferon-gamma (IFN-$\gamma$) Injections:** * Subcutaneous injections.
  * Super-activates macrophages to partially circumvent the enzymatic defect.
* **Haematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT)** (造血幹細胞移植):
  * The **ONLY** definitive curative therapy.
  * Considered early for severe phenotypes before irreversible organ damage occurs.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Environmental Risk Avoidance Strategies
* **Absolute Restriction:** * Gardening.
  * Mulching or playing in damp hay/leaves.
  * Exploring caves or dusty basements.
* **Reasoning:** * These activities release massive clouds of **Aspergillus spores**.
  * Rapidly fatal necrotizing pneumonitis if inhaled by a CGD patient.

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工):** * Support for chronic illness burden, hospitalization costs, and schooling disruption.
* **Clinical Psychologist:** * Coping with chronic illness, isolation from restricted activities, and transition to adult care.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Considerations in CGD
* **Scarring Misinterpretation:** * Multiple surgical scars from previous abscess drainages must be properly documented.
  * Do not falsely attribute them to Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) or cigarette burns.
* **Medical Neglect:** * Failure to administer lifelong daily prophylaxis can lead to fatal infections; requires strict adherence monitoring.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Major Infectious Complications
* **Invasive Pulmonary Aspergillosis** (侵襲性肺曲霉菌病): 
  * Leading cause of mortality in CGD.
* **Septicaemia:** * Overwhelming *Burkholderia cepacia* complex or *S. aureus* sepsis.
* **Refractory Osteomyelitis:** * Difficult to eradicate, causing permanent skeletal deformity.

### Granulomatous / Inflammatory Complications
* **Gastrointestinal:** * Granulomatous colitis (mimicking Crohn's disease).
  * Gastric outlet obstruction.
* **Genitourinary:** * Urinary tract obstruction → post-renal kidney injury.
* **Prognosis:** * Highly variable. Without HSCT, lifespan is significantly reduced, with survival into adulthood requiring meticulous adherence to prophylaxis and early treatment of flares.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Forgetting that CGD patients have completely normal levels of White Blood Cells, B-cells, T-cells, and immunoglobulins. All standard baseline immune workups look perfect. Pearl: You must maintain a high index of suspicion based on the clinical history and request the specific functional test (DHR flow cytometry).
* Viva Question: "Why do patients with CGD get sick from Staphylococcus but easily fight off Streptococcus?" Answer: Streptococcus is catalase-negative. It produces small amounts of its own hydrogen peroxide waste. Since the CGD neutrophil can't make its own peroxide, it just uses the Strep's own peroxide waste to kill it. Staphylococcus is catalase-positive, meaning it destroys its own peroxide waste, leaving the CGD neutrophil completely defenceless.
* Pitfall: Attributing a deep liver abscess in a 2-year-old to a random community-acquired infection. Always suspect an underlying primary immunodeficiency, specifically CGD, when deep visceral abscesses present in early childhood.
* Viva Question: "What are the essential components of lifelong prophylaxis for a child with CGD?" Answer: Daily oral Co-trimoxazole (antibacterial) and daily oral Itraconazole (antifungal), along with strict avoidance of environments rich in fungal spores like compost or mulch.
