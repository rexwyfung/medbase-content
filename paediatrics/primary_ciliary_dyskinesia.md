# Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia (PCD) / Kartagener Syndrome (原發性纖毛運動障礙)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Rare, genetically heterogeneous, autosomal recessive disorder
* Characterized by structural/functional defects of motile cilia → impaired mucociliary clearance
### Definition of Kartagener Syndrome
* Specific subgroup (≈50%) of PCD comprising the clinical triad:
  * **Situs inversus totalis** (內臟全反位)
  * **Chronic sinusitis** (慢性鼻竇炎)
  * **Bronchiectasis** (支氣管擴張)
### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Rare, exact local prevalence unknown (estimated 1:10,000 to 1:20,000 globally)
* High rate of underdiagnosis/delayed diagnosis in HK Hospital Authority (HA) → often presents late to paediatric respiratory clinics with irreversible lung damage

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Mutations in Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Autosomal recessive inheritance
* Mutations in >40 genes coding for ciliary motor proteins
* Most common: **DNAH5**, **DNAI1** (coding for outer dynein arms)
### Pathophysiology of Ciliary Dysfunction
* Defective cilia → lack of normal ciliary beating → abolished mucociliary clearance
* Stagnant mucus → recurrent bacterial infections (e.g., *H. influenzae*, *S. aureus*, *P. aeruginosa*) → chronic inflammation → destruction of airway architecture (**Bronchiectasis**)
### Embryological Pathophysiology of Situs Inversus
* Embryonic node cilia normally beat to generate leftward fluid flow → dictates normal organ laterality
* Defective nodal cilia → random internal organ placement → 50% chance of **Situs inversus**

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Neonatal Presentation of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Unexplained neonatal respiratory distress syndrome (NRDS) in term baby
* Requires prolonged oxygen therapy (>24-48 hrs)
* Continuous, unexplained **rhinitis** (鼻炎) since birth
### Childhood Presentation of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Chronic, daily, wet/productive cough starting early infancy
* Recurrent severe **otitis media with effusion** (積液性中耳炎) → **conductive hearing loss** (傳導性聽力受損)
* Chronic nasal congestion/discharge

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Respiratory Symptoms of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Continuous nasal discharge (**chronic rhinitis**) since birth
* Chronic wet cough
* Recurrent lower respiratory tract infections (LRTIs)
### ENT Symptoms & Signs of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Chronic serous otitis media / "glue ear"
* Nasal polyps (鼻息肉)
* Conductive hearing loss
### Clinical Signs on Physical Examination
* Auscultation: Widespread coarse crackles/wheeze
* Inspection: Clubbing (杵狀指) (late sign indicating chronic hypoxia/suppuration)
* Palpation/Auscultation: **Dextrocardia** (右位心) (apex beat on right → present in 50%)

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia from Cystic Fibrosis

| Feature | Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia (PCD) | Cystic Fibrosis (CF) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Growth & Nutrition** | Normal growth | Faltering growth (pancreatic insufficiency) |
| **GI Symptoms** | Normal bowel habits | Steatorrhoea, Meconium ileus |
| **Situs Inversus** | Present in 50% | Absent |
| **Bronchiectasis Distribution** | Mid/lower lobe predominant | Upper lobe predominant |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnoses for Chronic Wet Cough in Children
* **Cystic Fibrosis** (囊性纖維化)
* **Immunodeficiency** (免疫缺陷) (e.g., Common variable immunodeficiency)
* **Severe Asthma** (嚴重哮喘) (though asthma is typically dry, PCD is definitively wet)
* **Post-infectious Bronchiectasis** (感染後支氣管擴張) (e.g., post-adenovirus/pertussis/TB)
* **Retained Foreign Body** (異物吸入) (usually unilateral, localized signs)

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Screening Investigations for Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* **Nasal Nitric Oxide (nNO) Test** (鼻一氧化氮測試):
  * Extremely ↓ in PCD (<77 nL/min)
  * (Note: Exhaled NO is ↑ in asthma, normal/low in CF)
### Imaging Studies for Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* **Chest X-ray (CXR)**:
  * May show dextrocardia / situs inversus
  * Bronchial wall thickening, ring shadows (bronchiectasis)
* **High-Resolution CT (HRCT) Thorax**:
  * Confirms mid-lobe / lower-lobe predominant bronchiectasis
### Definitive and Special Investigations for Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* **High-speed Video Microscopy Analysis (HSVA)**:
  * Sample from nasal brushing
  * Assesses ciliary beat frequency and pattern (immotile or dyskinetic)
* **Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)**:
  * Cross-section of cilia → shows structural defects (e.g., missing outer/inner dynein arms)
* **Genetic Panel Testing**:
  * Identifies known PCD gene mutations

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Chronic Airway Clearance Management
* **Daily Chest Physiotherapy** (胸部物理治療) (cornerstone of HA management)
  * Must physically clear mucus (e.g., active cycle of breathing techniques, positive expiratory pressure (PEP) mask)
* Mucolytics: Hypertonic saline nebulization to hydrate airway surface liquid
### Acute Exacerbation Management
* Aggressive targeted **Antibiotic Therapy** (抗生素治療) based on sputum cultures
* HA protocol dictates lower threshold for IV antibiotics for *P. aeruginosa* eradication to prevent rapid lung decline
* *H. influenzae* & *S. pneumoniae* common early; *P. aeruginosa* later
### ENT Management
* Regular audiology surveillance
* Hearing aids preferred for conductive hearing loss
* Grommet (tympanostomy tube) insertion is generally AVOIDED (often leads to prolonged purulent otorrhoea)
### Preventive Management
* Annual Influenza vaccine (流感疫苗)
* Pneumococcal vaccine (肺炎鏈球菌疫苗)
* Absolute avoidance of environmental tobacco smoke

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Roles
* **Paediatric Pulmonologist**: Disease surveillance, spirometry tracking
* **Physiotherapist**: Daily airway clearance techniques
* **ENT Specialist**: Audiology, sinus management
### Psychosocial Care & Transition
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Financial assistance for home nebulizers/PEP devices
* Genetic counseling for family planning
* Infertility counseling during transition to adult HA services

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect in Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Neglect in maintaining daily chest physiotherapy / poor clinic attendance → rapid acceleration of irreversible bronchiectasis
* Failure to collect / administer prescribed antibiotics during exacerbations

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Respiratory Complications of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Progressive **Bronchiectasis** (支氣管擴張)
* Gradual decline in lung function (FEV1)
* Chronic *Pseudomonas aeruginosa* colonization
* Cor pulmonale (right heart failure) in end-stage disease
### ENT and Fertility Complications of Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia
* Permanent conductive hearing loss (from ossicular chain damage)
* **Infertility** (不育):
  * Males: Almost 100% infertile (sperm tails are modified cilia → immotile)
  * Females: Subfertility / ↑ risk of ectopic pregnancies (fallopian tube cilia dysfunction)

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Assuming situs inversus is a benign incidental finding. Pearl: While harmless structurally, its presence in any child MUST instantly prompt the question: "Does this child have chronic cough or runny nose?" to rule out Kartagener syndrome.
* Viva Question: Why do 50% of PCD patients have situs inversus, and the other 50% have normal situs? Answer: Because without the embryonic nodal cilia to direct leftward lateralization, internal organ placement defaults to pure random chance (a 50/50 coin flip).
* Pitfall: Inserting grommets for chronic glue ear in PCD. This often results in chronic, continuous purulent ear discharge; hearing aids are the preferred management for conductive hearing loss.
* Viva Question: What is the best bedside screening test for PCD and what is the expected result? Answer: Nasal nitric oxide (nNO) testing, which will show extremely low levels.
* Trap: Confusing the typical lobar distribution of bronchiectasis in CF versus PCD. Pearl: Cystic Fibrosis typically affects the upper lobes predominantly, whereas PCD typically affects the middle and lower lobes.
