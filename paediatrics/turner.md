# Turner Syndrome (特納氏綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Turner Syndrome
* **Chromosomal disorder** affecting females
* Complete/partial absence of one **X chromosome (X染色體)**
* Cardinal cause of **short stature (身材矮小)** & **primary amenorrhoea (原發性閉經)**

### Local Epidemiology of Turner Syndrome
* Incidence: ~1 in 2,500 live female births in HK
* Prenatal mortality: >95% miscarry in early pregnancy
* Common presentation in HK Hospital Authority (HA) pediatric endocrine clinics

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Karyotypes in Turner Syndrome
| Karyotype | Prevalence | Pathophysiology & Phenotype |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Classic 45,X0** | ~50% | Complete absence of 2nd sex chromosome. Usually non-disjunction during paternal meiosis. Severe phenotype. |
| **Mosaicism (45,X0 / 46,XX)** | ~30% | Mixed cell lines. Milder phenotype, possible spontaneous puberty/menses. |
| **Structural Abnormalities** | ~20% | e.g., Isochromosome X or ring X chromosome. Deletion of short arm of X chromosome. |

### Pathophysiological Mechanisms in Turner Syndrome
* **SHOX gene** absence → **Short stature (身材矮小)** & skeletal anomalies (e.g., **cubitus valgus (肘外翻)**)
* **Gonadal dysgenesis (性腺發育不全)** → Accelerated loss of ovarian follicles → **Streak ovaries (條索狀卵巢)**
* Resultant **Oestrogen deficiency (雌激素缺乏)** → Lack of secondary sexual characteristics & **infertility (不孕)**
* Presence of Y chromosome material (rare) → ↑ risk of **gonadoblastoma (性腺母細胞瘤)**

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Life-Stage Presentations of Turner Syndrome
* **Antenatal**: **Cystic hygroma (囊狀水瘤)**, **hydrops fetalis (胎兒水腫)**, nuchal edema on USG
* **Neonatal**: Puffy hands/feet (**lymphoedema (淋巴水腫)**), webbed neck, heart murmur (**coarctation of aorta (主動脈縮窄)**)
* **Childhood**: Unexplained **short stature (身材矮小)** (falling off growth centiles)
* **Adolescence**: **Delayed puberty (青春期延遲)**, **primary amenorrhoea (原發性閉經)**

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Physical Phenotype & Dysmorphic Features of Turner Syndrome
* **Growth**: **Short stature (身材矮小)** (>95%)
* **Head & Neck**: **Webbed neck (頸蹼/翼狀頸)**, low posterior hairline, high-arched palate, micrognathia, prominent/low-set ears
* **Chest**: Broad **shield chest (盾狀胸)** with widely spaced nipples
* **Limbs**: ↑ carrying angle of elbows (**cubitus valgus (肘外翻)**), short 4th metacarpals, spoon-shaped nails
* **Skin**: Multiple pigmented moles
* **Reproductive**: **Streak ovaries (條索狀卵巢)**, lack of breast development, **infertility (不孕)**

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Turner Syndrome from Noonan Syndrome
| Feature | Turner Syndrome (特納氏綜合症) | Noonan Syndrome (努南綜合症) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sex Affected** | Females ONLY | Males & Females |
| **Genetics** | 45,X0 | 46,XX or 46,XY (Autosomal Dominant, PTPN11) |
| **Cardiac Defect** | **Left-sided** (**Coarctation of Aorta**, **Bicuspid Aortic Valve**) | **Right-sided** (Pulmonary Stenosis) |
| **Intellect** | Usually normal | Mild learning difficulties common |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis for Short Stature and Delayed Puberty
* **Noonan Syndrome (努南綜合症)**
* Familial short stature
* Constitutional delay of growth and puberty
* **Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能低下)**
* Growth Hormone Deficiency

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Blood Investigations for Turner Syndrome
* **Karyotype (Chromosomal Analysis)**: **Definitive test (黃金標準)** → Confirms 45,X0 or mosaicism
* **Endocrine Bloods**:
  * ↑ **FSH (促卵泡激素)** & ↑ **LH (促黃體生成素)** → **Hypergonadotropic hypogonadism (高促性腺激素性性腺功能低下)**
  * **TFTs (甲狀腺功能測試)** → Screen for hypothyroidism
  * **Fasting Glucose / HbA1c** → ↑ risk of Type 2 DM
  * **Coeliac screen** → tTG-IgA (↑ risk)

### Imaging & Special Investigations for Turner Syndrome
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: Mandatory baseline. Screen for **Bicuspid Aortic Valve (二葉式主動脈瓣)** & **Coarctation of the Aorta (主動脈縮窄)**
* **Renal USG (腎臟超聲波)**: Mandatory baseline. Screen for structural anomalies (e.g., **Horseshoe kidney (馬蹄腎)** in ~30%)
* **Pelvic USG**: Assess for infantile uterus and **streak ovaries (條索狀卵巢)**
* **DEXA Scan**: Screen for osteoporosis

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Endocrine & Growth Management of Turner Syndrome
* **Growth Hormone (GH) Therapy (生長激素治療)**: Start early (4-6 yrs) to maximize final adult height
* **Oestrogen Replacement (雌激素補充)**: Start ~11-12 yrs to induce puberty (breast development) & protect bone health
* **Progesterone Addition**: Added 1-2 yrs after starting oestrogen (or post-menarche) to induce withdrawal bleeds & prevent endometrial hyperplasia

### Reproductive Management of Turner Syndrome
* **Fertility**: Infertility expected. Pregnancy possible via IVF + donor eggs
* **Cardiology Warning**: Pregnancy carries extreme risk of **fatal aortic dissection (主動脈夾層)**; necessitates strict cardiology clearance before and during pregnancy

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Involvement in Turner Syndrome
* **Paediatric Endocrinology (兒童內分泌科)**: Lead specialty
* **Cardiology (心臟科)**: Lifelong surveillance for aortic root dilation & dissection
* **ENT / Audiology**: Monitor for recurrent otitis media & conductive hearing loss
* **Psychology/MCHC**: Support for short stature, infertility, self-esteem
* **Education**: Normal intelligence; monitor for specific learning difficulties (e.g., spatial awareness, mathematics)

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations in Turner Syndrome
* N/A for primary disease process
* Ensure psychological safeguarding against bullying secondary to short stature or dysmorphic features

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Systemic Complications of Turner Syndrome
* **Cardiovascular**: **Aortic root dilation (主動脈根部擴張)**, **Aortic dissection (主動脈夾層)**, Hypertension
* **Endocrine/Metabolic**: **Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能低下)**, Type 2 DM, Osteoporosis
* **ENT**: Recurrent otitis media → Conductive hearing loss
* **Renal**: Structural anomalies (horseshoe kidney) → ↑ risk of UTIs

### Prognosis of Turner Syndrome
* Slightly ↓ life expectancy (primarily due to cardiovascular complications)
* Normal, productive life with adequate hormone replacement & MDT care

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What are the two most common congenital heart defects associated with Turner Syndrome? Answer: Bicuspid Aortic Valve and Coarctation of the Aorta.
* Viva Question: A 14-year-old girl is investigated for primary amenorrhoea. She is below the 3rd centile for height. Her FSH and LH levels are markedly elevated. What is the most likely diagnosis, and what is the definitive test? Answer: Turner Syndrome. The definitive test is a Karyotype.
* Trap: Assuming girls with Turner Syndrome invariably have severe intellectual disability. Pearl: Intelligence is usually normal, though specific learning difficulties (e.g., spatial awareness, math) may occur.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to arrange a baseline Echocardiogram and Renal Ultrasound in a newly diagnosed patient with Turner Syndrome. Explanation: Structural anomalies in these organs are extremely common and potentially life-threatening.
* Pitfall: Discharging an adult Turner Syndrome patient from cardiology follow-up. Explanation: Aortic root dilation and fatal aortic dissection remain lifelong risks, especially if the patient attempts pregnancy via IVF.
* Pitfall: Confusing Turner Syndrome with Noonan Syndrome. Explanation: Noonan Syndrome can affect males, has a normal karyotype, and features right-sided heart defects (pulmonary stenosis), whereas Turner Syndrome features left-sided defects (coarctation of the aorta).
