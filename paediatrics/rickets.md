# Vitamin D Deficiency (Rickets) (維生素D缺乏症 / 佝僂病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
- Disease of growing bone unique to children/adolescents.
- Failure of **osteoid** to calcify at epiphyseal plates due to severe **Vitamin D (維生素D)** and/or **Calcium (鈣)** deficiency.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
- Resurging in HK due to:
  - Prolonged indoor lifestyles (high-density urban living).
  - Heavy sunscreen use (cultural preference for pale skin).
  - Exclusive breastfeeding (**母乳餵哺**) without supplementation (common MCHC observation).
- Peak age of presentation: 6-24 months.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Vitamin D Metabolism
- **Sunlight**: UV-B converts 7-dehydrocholesterol → **Cholecalciferol (Vitamin D3)** in skin.
- **Liver**: Hydroxylation → **25-OH Vitamin D (25-OH Vit D)** (main storage form).
- **Kidney**: 1-alpha-hydroxylation → **1,25-OH2 Vitamin D (1,25-OH2 Vit D)** (active form).

### Pathological Cascade
- ↓ **Active Vit D** → ↓ intestinal **Calcium** & **Phosphate (磷酸鹽)** absorption.
- ↓ **Calcium** → triggers secondary **Hyperparathyroidism (甲狀旁腺功能亢進)**.
- ↑ **Parathyroid Hormone (PTH)** → mobilizes **Calcium** from bone, wastes **Phosphate** in kidneys.
- Result: Demineralized, soft **osteoid** → bone deformities (**Rickets (佝僂病)**).

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Infantile Presentation (6-24 months)
- Delayed motor milestones (late walking).
- Muscle weakness (**Myopathy (肌病)**).
- Bone deformities noticed by parents.
- Tiredness/pain in legs, worse on exertion.

### Acute Presentation
- **Hypocalcaemic seizures (低鈣性抽搐)**.
- **Tetany (手足搐搦)** (neuromuscular irritability/spasms).
- Apnoea, stridor, or cardiomyopathy (rare but life-threatening).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Cranial Signs
- **Craniotabes (顱骨軟化)**: "Ping-pong ball" soft skull.
- Delayed fontanelle closure.
- **Frontal bossing (前額突出)**.

### Thoracic Signs
- **Rachitic rosary (佝僂病串珠)**: Enlarged costochondral junctions feeling like beads.
- **Harrison's sulcus (哈里森溝)**: Groove at lower margin of thorax caused by diaphragm pulling on soft ribs.

### Extremity Signs
- Widened wrists and ankles (expanded epiphyseal plates).
- **Genu varum (O型腿/膝內翻)**: Bowing of legs once weight-bearing begins.
- **Genu valgum (X型腿/膝外翻)**: Knock-knees in older children.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Rickets vs. Achondroplasia
| Feature | Nutritional Rickets (營養性佝僂病) | Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pathology** | Mineralization defect | FGFR3 mutation (chondrocyte defect) |
| **Stature** | Normal or stunted | Disproportionate short stature (rhizomelic) |
| **Thorax** | Deformed (Rosary, Harrison's sulcus) | Normal |
| **Biochemistry** | ↓ Ca, ↓ PO4, ↑ ALP, ↑ PTH | Normal |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Alternative Causes of Rickets
- **Renal Osteodystrophy (腎性骨營養不良)**: 2° to Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) → ↓ 1-alpha-hydroxylase.
- **Hypophosphatemic Rickets (低磷酸鹽性佝僂病)**: X-linked dominant → renal phosphate wasting.
- **Liver Disease**: Biliary atresia → ↓ fat-soluble vitamin absorption.

### Genetic/Structural Bone Diseases
- **Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全)**.
- **Osteogenesis Imperfecta (成骨不全症)**.

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Clinical Evaluations
- Diet & sunlight exposure history.
- Growth chart plotting (Weight, Height, HC).

### Blood Investigations (Biochemistry)
- **25-OH Vitamin D**: ↓ (Definitive diagnostic marker for body stores).
- **Calcium**: Normal or ↓ (maintained initially by ↑ PTH).
- **Phosphate**: ↓ (wasted by ↑ PTH).
- **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)**: ↑↑↑ (Markedly high → active bone turnover).
- **Parathyroid Hormone (PTH)**: ↑ (Secondary hyperparathyroidism).
- **Urea & Electrolytes (U&E)**: Rule out renal failure.
- **Liver Function Tests (LFT)**: Rule out hepatic cause.

### Radiological Investigations
- **X-ray Wrist/Knee**:
  - Cupping (凹陷) of metaphyses.
  - Fraying (邊緣模糊) of metaphyses.
  - Widening of growth plates.
  - Osteopenia.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management (Hypocalcaemia)
- **IV Calcium Gluconate (葡萄糖酸鈣)**: For acute tetany/seizures (given slowly with ECG monitoring).

### Chronic Management (Vitamin D Replacement)
- **Stoss Therapy (衝擊療法)**: Single massive PO/IM dose of **Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol (膽鈣化醇))** (e.g., 100,000 - 600,000 IU) if compliance is an issue.
- **Alternative Maintenance**: High-dose daily oral **Vitamin D** (e.g., 2000-6000 IU/day) for 2-3 months, then step down to standard maintenance.
- **Calcium Supplementation**: Essential during initial phase to prevent **Hungry Bone Syndrome (飢餓骨綜合徵)** (healing bone rapidly absorbs blood Ca → severe hypocalcaemia).

### HA & Local Preventive Protocols
- **MCHC Guidelines**: 400 IU/day oral **Vitamin D** drops for ALL exclusively breastfed infants starting within days of birth.
- Maternal supplementation during pregnancy/lactation.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Approach
- **Dietitian**: Review nutritional intake, ensure adequate dietary Calcium & Vit D.
- **MCHC Nurse**: Monitor growth, milestone achievement, and medication compliance.
- **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Assess living conditions (sunlight access) and financial ability to buy supplements.

### Psychosocial Support
- Education on safe sun exposure (15-30 mins daily, arms/legs exposed, avoiding peak UV index).
- Culturally sensitive counseling regarding sun avoidance practices.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Recognizing Neglect
- Severe, prolonged nutritional rickets may indicate severe dietary/medical neglect.
- Failure to attend MCHC follow-ups or administer prescribed supplements.

### Differentiating NAI (Non-Accidental Injury)
- **Fractures**: Severe osteopenia in rickets can cause pathological fractures.
- Requires expert **Paediatric Radiologist** to differentiate rachitic fractures from typical NAI patterns (e.g., classic metaphyseal lesions/corner fractures).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications
- **Hypocalcaemic Seizures**.
- **Dilated Cardiomyopathy (擴張型心肌病)**: Reversible with early treatment.
- Tetany & Laryngospasm.

### Long-term Complications
- Permanent skeletal deformities if untreated before growth plate fusion.
- Pelvic distortion → obstetric complications (obstructed labour) in adulthood.
- Restrictive lung disease 2° to severe thoracic deformity.

### Prognosis
- Excellent if treated early.
- Biochemical resolution in days/weeks.
- Radiological healing (mineralization line) in weeks/months.
- Bone deformities correct significantly over years of growth.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Ordering 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D to check vitamin D status.
* Pearl: 1,25-OH Vit D has a short half-life and is artificially elevated by secondary hyperparathyroidism. Always order 25-OH Vitamin D to check body stores.
* Viva Question: Why do we test 25-OH Vitamin D instead of the active 1,25-OH form? Answer: Because 1,25-OH Vit D has a very short half-life and is artificially elevated by secondary hyperparathyroidism, making it a terrible marker of body stores. 25-OH Vit D accurately reflects total body reserves.
* Pitfall: Prescribing high-dose Vitamin D without Calcium replacement. This triggers Hungry Bone Syndrome, leading to profound hypocalcaemia and seizures as healing bone rapidly absorbs serum calcium.
* Viva Question: What is the earliest radiological sign of healing in rickets? Answer: The appearance of a provisional zone of calcification (a dense white line) at the metaphysis.
* Trap: Assuming a child with bow legs has rickets.
* Pearl: Physiological genu varum is normal in toddlers up to age 2. Pathological bowing in rickets is usually associated with other signs (short stature, widened wrists, rosary) and progressive deformity.
