# Vitamin D Deficiency (Rickets) (維生素D缺乏症 / 佝僂病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A disease of growing bone unique to children and adolescents, caused by a failure of osteoid to calcify at the epiphyseal plates due to severe Vitamin D and/or calcium deficiency.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Resurging due to prolonged indoor lifestyles, heavy sunscreen use, and exclusive breast feeding without supplementation.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Vitamin D is primarily produced in the skin via UV light exposure, then hydroxylated in the liver (25-OH Vit D) and kidney (active 1,25-OH2 Vit D).
- Active Vit D is essential for intestinal absorption of calcium and phosphorus. 
- Without it, severe hypocalcemia triggers secondary hyperparathyroidism, which leeches calcium from bones, leaving behind soft, unmineralized osteoid (Rickets).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Presenting age often 6-24 months. 
- Presents with delayed motor milestones (late walking), muscle weakness (myopathy), or bone deformities noticed by parents.
- Sometimes presents acutely with hypocalcaemic seizures.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Skull**: Craniotabes (ping-pong ball skull/soft skull bones), delayed fontanelle closure, frontal bossing.
- **Chest**: Rachitic rosary (prominent costochondral junctions feeling like beads), Harrison's sulcus (groove at lower margin of thorax caused by diaphragm pulling on soft ribs).
- **Limbs**: Widened wrists and ankles (expanded epiphyseal plates), bowing of the legs (genu varum) once weight-bearing begins, or knock-knees (genu valgum) in older kids.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Achondroplasia: Achondroplasia causes disproportionate short stature with a normal chest and normal calcium/phosphate levels. Rickets causes a deformed chest and deranged biochemistry.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Achondroplasia 
- Osteogenesis Imperfecta
- Hypophosphatemic Rickets (X-linked dominant, kidneys waste phosphate)
- Renal Osteodystrophy (Chronic kidney disease)

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bloods**: 
  - Low 25-OH Vitamin D (definitive test for store levels).
  - Calcium (Normal or Low), Phosphate (Low).
  - Alkaline Phosphatase (MARKEDLY HIGH - indicates high bone turnover).
  - Parathyroid Hormone (High - secondary hyperparathyroidism).
- **Imaging**: Wrist/Knee X-ray showing cupping, fraying, and widening of the metaphyses.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Medical**: High-dose oral Vitamin D replacement (e.g., Stoss therapy with massive single doses, or daily high dosing for 2-3 months), followed by standard daily maintenance.
- **Calcium**: Crucial to supplement calcium alongside Vit D initially to prevent "hungry bone syndrome" (where active healing bone rapidly sucks all calcium from the blood, causing hypocalcaemic tetany/seizures).
- **Prevention**: Endocrine society mandates 400 IU/day Vit D for ALL exclusively breastfed infants starting within days of birth.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- MCHC dietary review. Addressing cultural practices regarding sun avoidance where necessary.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Severe nutritional neglect. Differentiating the fractures of severe rickets from deliberate NAI requires expert pediatric radiological interpretation.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Hypocalcaemic tetany / dilated cardiomyopathy (from severe hypocalcaemia).
- Permanent skeletal deformities (pelvic distortion affecting childbirth later in life) if not treated before growth plates fuse.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Ordering 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D to check vitamin D status. 
- **Viva Q**: "Why do we test 25-OH Vitamin D instead of the active 1,25-OH form?" Answer: Because 1,25-OH Vit D has a very short half-life and is artificially elevated by secondary hyperparathyroidism, making it a terrible marker of body stores. 25-OH Vit D accurately reflects total body reserves.
