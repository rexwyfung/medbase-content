# Nocturnal Enuresis (Bedwetting) (夜間遺尿)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Nocturnal Enuresis
- **Def**: Involuntary urination during sleep in child **≥5 yrs**
- Requires absence of congenital/acquired CNS or urinary tract defects
- **Primary (原發性)**: Child has never been consistently dry at night for 6 months
- **Secondary (繼發性)**: Bedwetting restarts after ≥6 months of complete night-time dryness (Red flag for medical/psych issue)

### Local Epidemiology of Nocturnal Enuresis
- Highly prevalent in HK
- Affects ~**15%** of 5-yr-olds, ~**5%** of 10-yr-olds, ~**1-2%** of 15-yr-olds
- **Gender**: ♂ > ♀ (ratio ~2:1)
- Spontaneous resolution rate: ~**15%/yr** after age 5

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Main Pathophysiological Mechanisms
Multifactorial, typically involving 3 mechanisms:
- **Nocturnal Polyuria (夜間多尿)**: Lack of physiological overnight surge in **ADH/Vasopressin (抗利尿激素)** → ↑ urine production
- **Detrusor Overactivity / ↓ Bladder Capacity (逼尿肌過度活躍)**: Premature bladder contraction or ↓ functional capacity
- **High Arousal Threshold (高覺醒閾值)**: Deep sleep preventing CNS awakening in response to full bladder

### Genetic Predisposition to Enuresis
- Highly heritable condition
- One affected parent → **40%** risk to child
- Both affected parents → **70%** risk to child
- Often exacerbated by emotional stress blocking continence learning

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Key Historical Elements to Elicit
- Frequency of wet nights (>2 nights/week indicates need for tx)
- Fluid intake habits (excessive fluid/caffeine before bed)
- **Daytime symptoms (日間症狀)**: Urgency, frequency, daytime wetting, poor stream (indicates lower urinary tract dysfunction, NOT simple primary enuresis)
- **Bowel habits**: Constipation is a major, often missed cause (full rectum compresses bladder)
- Snoring/Sleep Apnoea (OSA) (睡眠窒息症) (can cause secondary enuresis)

### Important Clinical Examination Findings
- Examination is usually **normal** in primary enuresis
- **Abdomen**: Palpate for bladder/faecal mass (constipation)
- **Lower back/spine**: Check for sacral dimple, hairy tuft, sinus (indicates spina bifida occulta)
- **Neurological**: Brief lower limb neuro exam to rule out neurogenic bladder

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing Primary vs Secondary Enuresis
- **Primary**: Never dry for >6 mos. Psychologically/physically normal. 
- **Secondary**: Dry for >6 mos, then relapses. Requires investigation to exclude organic pathology (UTI, DM, constipation) or emotional stress (bullying, abuse, parental divorce).

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnoses for Nocturnal Enuresis
| Condition | Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Nocturnal Enuresis (原發性夜間遺尿)** | Most common, never dry >6 mos, normal exam |
| **Constipation (便秘)** | Palpable faecal mass, infrequent hard stools |
| **UTI (尿道炎)** | Dysuria, fever, secondary enuresis, daytime symptoms |
| **Type 1 DM / Diabetes Insipidus (糖尿病/尿崩症)** | Polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, secondary enuresis |
| **Lower Urinary Tract Dysfunction (下尿道功能障礙)** | Daytime urgency/frequency, poor stream, daytime wetting |
| **Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (睡眠窒息症)** | Snoring, poor sleep quality, large tonsils/adenoids |
| **Spinal Cord Pathology / Tethered Cord (脊髓病變)** | Lower limb neuro signs, sacral dimple, abnormal gait |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### First-Line Bedside Investigations
- **Urinalysis (尿液測試)**: **Mandatory** for ALL presentations
  - Dipstick for **glucose** (rule out DM), **specific gravity** (rule out DI), **leukocytes/nitrites** (rule out UTI)
- **Bladder Diary (排尿日記)**: Record fluid intake, daytime voids, and wet/dry nights for 1-2 weeks

### Imaging Investigations
- **Renal/Bladder Ultrasound (KUB USG) (腎臟及膀胱超聲波)**: 
  - NOT routinely needed for simple primary enuresis
  - Indicated IF: daytime symptoms, recurrent UTIs, secondary enuresis, or structural anomaly suspected

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Step 1: Education and Behavioral Modification
- **First-line management** for children >5 yrs
- **Reassurance**: Remove blame/punishment, explain condition is common & involuntary
- **Fluid Management**: Ensure adequate daytime fluids; restrict fluids (esp. caffeine/sugar) **2 hours before bedtime**
- **Toileting**: Empty bladder immediately before sleep
- **Treat Constipation**: Aggressive management of constipation if present

### Step 2: Enuresis Alarm Therapy (遺尿報警器)
- **Most effective long-term treatment**
- Sensor in underwear sounds loud alarm upon wetting
- **Mechanism**: Classical conditioning trains brain to wake to full bladder sensation
- Requires high motivation; takes **2-3 months** to work
- Highest long-term success, lowest relapse rate

### Step 3: Pharmacological Therapy (Desmopressin / DDAVP) (去氨加壓素)
- **Synthetic ADH analogue** → ↓ overnight urine production
- Given as oral melt (sublingual) at bedtime
- **Pros**: Immediate effect. Ideal for short-term use (school camps, sleepovers)
- **Cons**: High relapse rate upon cessation
- **CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING**: **Strict fluid restriction** mandatory from 1 hour before to 8 hours after dose to prevent life-threatening **water intoxication** and **hyponatraemic seizures (低鈉血症抽搐)**

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Psychosocial Impact and Multidisciplinary Support
- **Psychological Impact**: Causes shame, low self-esteem, social isolation
- **MDT approach**: Involve school nurses (學童保健), clinical psychologists if associated with severe stress/anxiety
- Emphasize positive reinforcement (e.g., Star Charts for agreed behaviors like helping change sheets, NOT just for dry nights)

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Red Flags in Enuresis
- Secondary enuresis in previously dry child can indicate **emotional abuse** or **sexual abuse (性侵犯)**
- Excessive punishment/physical abuse by parents frustrated with bedwetting
- Always explore psychosocial history and family dynamics carefully

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Prognosis of Primary Nocturnal Enuresis
- **Excellent prognosis**
- Vast majority resolve spontaneously by adolescence (~15% resolution rate per year)
- Untreated, can lead to severe psychological morbidity, poor self-esteem, and family conflicts

## 11. Examiner's Pearls
* Viva Question: A 7-year-old boy who has been dry at night since age 4 suddenly starts wetting the bed every night for the past month. What is the most important initial investigation?
* Answer: Urinalysis to rule out UTI or Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (secondary enuresis requires organic workup).
* Viva Question: What is the most effective long-term treatment for primary nocturnal enuresis, and how does it work?
* Answer: Enuresis alarm, utilizing classical conditioning to train the child's brain to awaken to the sensation of a full bladder.
* Trap: Recommending fluid restriction all day for enuresis.
* Pearl: Fluid restriction should ONLY occur 2 hours prior to bedtime; daytime fluid intake must be adequate to maintain hydration.
* Pitfall: Prescribing Desmopressin without explicitly warning parents to restrict fluid intake overnight. This risks severe hyponatraemia and seizures.
