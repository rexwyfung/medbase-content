# Osgood-Schlatter Disease (脛骨結節骨軟骨炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* **Traction apophysitis** (牽引性骨骺炎) of **tibial tuberosity** (脛骨粗隆)
* Occurs at insertion of **patellar tendon** (髕腱)
* Self-limiting inflammatory condition

### Local Epidemiology of Osgood-Schlatter Disease in Hong Kong
* Peak age for males: 10–14 years
* Peak age for females: 8–12 years
* ↑ Incidence in active Hong Kong adolescents
* Triggered by high-impact sports
* Common aggravating sport: Basketball
* Common aggravating sport: Football
* Common aggravating sport: Hiking (爬山)
* Male predominance (+ve)
* Bilateral presentation in 20-30% of cases

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* Repeated microtrauma → **avulsion** (撕裂) of cartilage at tibial tuberosity
* Rapid growth phase → bone grows faster than muscle/tendon 
* Growth mismatch → ↑ tension on apophysis
* Subsequent bone healing → **hypertrophy** (肥大) of tubercle
* Permanent prominent tibial tubercle formation

### Genetics & Risk Factors of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* Specific genetic mutation: -ve
* Risk factor: ↑ Quadriceps tightness
* Risk factor: ↑ Hamstring tightness
* Risk factor: Rapid adolescent growth spurts
* Risk factor: High-impact jumping sports

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* **Anterior knee pain** (膝前痛)
* Gradual onset
* Pain ↑ with jumping
* Pain ↑ with running
* Pain ↑ with kneeling
* Pain ↑ with squatting
* Pain ↓ with rest

### Clinical Signs of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* Localized point tenderness over **tibial tuberosity** (脛骨粗隆)
* Visible swelling/prominence of tibial tuberosity
* Pain elicited by resisted knee extension
* Ely's test: +ve (indicating tight rectus femoris)
* Knee joint effusion: -ve
* Range of motion (ROM): Normal

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features: Osgood-Schlatter vs Sinding-Larsen-Johansson
* Osgood-Schlatter Disease → Tenderness at tibial tuberosity
* **Sinding-Larsen-Johansson** (辛丁-拉森-約翰遜綜合徵) → Tenderness at inferior pole of patella (髕骨下極)

### Discriminatory Features: Osgood-Schlatter vs Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome
* Osgood-Schlatter Disease → Point tenderness at tubercle
* **Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome** (髕股關節疼痛綜合徵) → Diffuse peripatellar pain
* **Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome** (髕股關節疼痛綜合徵) → Pain ↑ with descending stairs
* **Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome** (髕股關節疼痛綜合徵) → Pain ↑ with prolonged sitting (movie sign)

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Investigations for Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* Exclusively clinical diagnosis
* Routine laboratory investigations: -ve
* Routine imaging: -ve

### Imaging Indications for Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* **Plain Radiograph** (X光) (Lateral knee view) indicated ONLY for atypical features
* Atypical feature: Night pain
* Atypical feature: Pain at rest
* Atypical feature: Acute trauma (rule out avulsion fracture)
* Atypical feature: Unilateral swelling failing conservative management
* X-ray finding: Soft tissue swelling anterior to tubercle
* X-ray finding: Fragmentation of tibial tubercle apophysis (normal variant)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management & HA Protocols for Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* **Activity Modification** (活動調整): ↓ intensity/frequency of aggravating sports
* **Cryotherapy** (冰敷): 15-20 mins post-activity
* Short course **Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs** (非類固醇消炎止痛藥) (e.g., Ibuprofen)
* Simple analgesia (e.g., Paracetamol)
* Patellar tendon strap application during activity

### Chronic Management & Physiotherapy for Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* **Physiotherapy** (物理治療) referral via Hospital Authority GOPC/SOPC
* Prescribe quadriceps stretching
* Prescribe hamstring stretching
* Prescribe core strengthening
* Prescribe gluteal strengthening
* Strict contraindication: Immobilization/Casting (→ rapid muscle atrophy)

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement in Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* General Practitioner: Initial diagnosis and reassurance
* Physiotherapist: Structured rehabilitation protocols
* Physical Education Teachers: Manage training loads
* Sports Coaches: Integrate necessary rest days

### Psychosocial Care in Adolescent Athletes
* Validate frustration from sports restriction
* Reassure regarding benign nature of condition
* Encourage alternative low-impact activities (swimming)
* Encourage alternative low-impact activities (cycling)
* Set realistic expectations for recovery timeline (months to years)

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Red Flags in Knee Presentations
* Inconsistent history of trauma
* Delay in seeking medical attention for severe pain/limping
* Unexplained bruising at varying stages of healing
* Concurrent injuries incompatible with typical sports
* Inappropriate parental indifference to child's pain

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognosis of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* Overall prognosis: Excellent
* Natural history: Self-resolving
* Resolution aligns with closure of tibial growth plate (fusion)
* Typical symptom duration: 12–24 months

### Long-Term Complications of Osgood-Schlatter Disease
* Prominent tibial tubercle (cosmetic, painless lump)
* Pain with kneeling in adulthood (ossicle formation)
* Rare complication: Tibial tubercle avulsion fracture
* Rare complication: Genu recurvatum (premature anterior growth plate closure)

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the specific anatomical site of pathology in Osgood-Schlatter Disease? Answer: The insertion of the patellar tendon at the apophysis of the tibial tuberosity.
* Trap: Ordering bilateral knee X-rays for a classic Osgood-Schlatter presentation. Pearl: Diagnosis is entirely clinical; imaging is strictly reserved for ruling out fractures or tumors in atypical presentations.
* Pitfall: Advising the adolescent to completely stop all sports activities. Prescribe relative rest and activity modification instead, allowing pain-free participation to prevent psychosocial distress and muscle deconditioning.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate Osgood-Schlatter from Sinding-Larsen-Johansson syndrome? Answer: Osgood-Schlatter causes point tenderness at the tibial tuberosity, whereas Sinding-Larsen-Johansson causes tenderness at the inferior pole of the patella.
* Trap: Interpreting physiological apophyseal fragmentation on an adolescent X-ray as an acute avulsion fracture. Pearl: Fragmentation of the tibial tubercle is a normal developmental variant and does not independently confirm pathology without localizing clinical signs.
