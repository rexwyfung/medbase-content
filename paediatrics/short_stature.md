# Short Stature (身材矮小)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Short Stature
* Height (Ht) < 3rd percentile
* OR Ht < -2 Standard Deviations (SD) for age & sex
* Plotted on appropriate local charts (e.g., Hong Kong 1993 Growth Charts)

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Highly common referral to HA Paediatric Endocrine clinics
* 80% → Normal physiological variants
* Normal variants → Familial Short Stature (FSS) & Constitutional Delay of Growth and Puberty (CDGP)
* 20% → Pathological causes

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Normal Variants
* **Familial Short Stature (FSS):**
  * Genetic Ht potential limited by parental Ht
  * Normal Growth Velocity (GV)
* **Constitutional Delay of Growth and Puberty (CDGP) (體質性青春期延遲):**
  * "Late bloomers"
  * Delayed hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis activation
  * Delayed Bone Age (BA)

### Pathophysiology of Endocrine Causes
* **Growth Hormone Deficiency (GHD) (生長激素缺乏症):**
  * ↓ GH secretion from anterior pituitary → ↓ IGF-1 production at epiphyses
* **Laron Syndrome:**
  * GH receptor defect → GH resistance
  * ↑ GH but ↓ IGF-1
* **Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能低下):**
  * ↓ T4 → Impaired cartilage cell division & bone formation
* **Cushing's Syndrome (庫欣綜合症):**
  * ↑ Cortisol → Suppresses osteoblast function & GH secretion

### Genetics of Syndromic Causes
* **Turner Syndrome (特納綜合症):**
  * 45,XO karyotype
  * Loss of SHOX gene → Severe short stature & skeletal abnormalities
* **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症):**
  * Trisomy 21 → Intrinsic growth retardation
* **Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全):**
  * Autosomal dominant FGFR3 gene mutation
  * Inhibits chondrocyte proliferation → Disproportionate short limbs
* **Prader-Willi Syndrome (普拉德-威利綜合症):**
  * Loss of paternal chromosome 15q11-q13 → Hypothalamic dysfunction & ↓ GH

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Anthropometric Presentation
* **Normal Variants:**
  * Grow parallel to lower centiles
* **Pathological Causes:**
  * Cross centiles downwards
  * Poor GV (< 4-5 cm/yr during childhood phase)

### Nutritional vs Endocrine Presentation
* **Nutritional/Chronic Illness:**
  * Wt affected before Ht
  * Thin & short (Wt centile < Ht centile)
* **Endocrine Pathology:**
  * Ht affected more than Wt
  * Chubby & short (Ht centile < Wt centile)

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Dysmorphic Signs
* **Turner Syndrome:**
  * Webbed neck
  * Wide-spaced nipples
  * Cubitus valgus
  * Short 4th metacarpals
* **Achondroplasia:**
  * Frontal bossing
  * Midface hypoplasia
  * Rhizomelic (proximal) limb shortening

### Systemic Signs
* **Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉):**
  * Abdominal distension
  * Wasted buttocks
* **Hypothyroidism:**
  * Coarse facies
  * Dry skin
  * Bradycardia
* **Brain Tumour (e.g., Craniopharyngioma):**
  * Visual field defects (bitemporal hemianopia)
  * Papilloedema

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Table: Discriminating Common Causes of Short Stature

| Feature | FSS | CDGP | GHD / Endocrine | Nutritional / Chronic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Growth Velocity (GV)** | Normal | Normal | ↓ (Crosses centiles) | ↓ (Crosses centiles) |
| **Bone Age (BA)** | = Chronological Age | Delayed | Delayed | Delayed |
| **Wt vs Ht Centile** | Wt ≈ Ht | Wt ≈ Ht | Wt > Ht (Chubby) | Wt < Ht (Thin) |
| **Puberty Onset** | Normal | Delayed | Delayed | Delayed |
| **Final Adult Height**| Short | Normal | Short (if untreated) | Compromised |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Table: Differential Diagnosis of Short Stature

| Category | Specific Conditions |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Most Likely (Normal Variants)** | FSS, CDGP |
| **Must-Not-Miss (Endocrine/Genetics)** | Turner Syndrome (females), GHD, Brain Tumour (Craniopharyngioma) |
| **Systemic / Chronic Illness** | Coeliac Disease, Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), Congenital Heart Disease (CHD) |
| **Iatrogenic** | Chronic high-dose corticosteroid use (e.g., severe asthma) |
| **Psychosocial** | Psychosocial dwarfism (neglect/abuse) |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Anthropometric Investigations
* Accurate Ht measurement (Stadiometer)
* Accurate Wt measurement
* Head Circumference (HC) measurement
* Plot on HA HK 1993 Growth Charts
* Calculate Mid-Parental Height (MPH)
* Calculate Growth Velocity (GV)

### Imaging Investigations
* Left hand & wrist X-ray
* Evaluated via Greulich and Pyle method
* Assesses Bone Age (BA)

### Tier 1 Blood Investigations (Baseline Screen)
* Complete Blood Count (CBC)
* Urea & Electrolytes (U&E)
* Liver Function Tests (LFT)
* Bone profile (Ca, PO4, ALP)
* Thyroid Function Tests (Free T4, TSH)
* Anti-tissue transglutaminase (Anti-tTG) IgA (Coeliac screen)

### Tier 2 Blood Investigations (Endocrine & Genetic)
* IGF-1
* IGFBP-3
* Karyotype (MANDATORY in ALL short girls to rule out Turner Syndrome)

### Special Investigations
* Dynamic GH Stimulation Tests (e.g., Glucagon, Clonidine, Insulin Tolerance Test)
* MRI Brain/Pituitary (Rule out structural lesions if GHD confirmed)

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Management of Normal Variants
* Reassurance
* Regular monitoring of GV (every 6 months)
* No medical treatment required

### Chronic Medical Management
* **Hypothyroidism:**
  * Oral Thyroxine replacement
* **Coeliac Disease:**
  * Strict gluten-free diet for life
* **Brain Tumour:**
  * Neurosurgical referral

### HA Protocols for rhGH Therapy
* Daily subcutaneous Recombinant Human Growth Hormone (rhGH) injections
* **HA Funding Indications:**
  * Severe GHD
  * Turner Syndrome
  * Prader-Willi Syndrome
  * Chronic Renal Failure
  * Small for Gestational Age (SGA) failing to catch up by age 4

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* Paediatric Endocrinologist
* Clinical Psychologist
* Medical Social Worker (MSW)
* School Social Worker

### Psychological Support Focus
* Address low self-esteem
* Manage school bullying related to height
* Support compliance with daily rhGH injections

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Psychosocial Dwarfism Red Flags
* Emotional abuse or severe neglect
* High-stress home environment suppresses hypothalamic GH secretion
* Rapid GV catch-up upon removal from abusive environment
* Requires immediate MSW & Social Welfare Department (SWD) involvement

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognosis of Normal Variants
* Excellent prognosis
* CDGP patients reach normal final adult height

### Complications of Untreated Pathological Short Stature
* Severely compromised final adult height
* Osteoporosis (in Turner Syndrome & GHD)
* Cardiovascular morbidity (in GHD)
* Severe psychological distress

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Diagnosing a short, chubby girl with "familial short stature" without further tests. Pearl: ALWAYS check a karyotype in a short girl to rule out Turner syndrome, even if she doesn't have classic dysmorphic features.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate endocrine causes of short stature from nutritional causes clinically? Answer: Endocrine causes make children short and relatively overweight (height centile < weight centile). Nutritional causes make them thin and short (weight centile < height centile).
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents of a boy crossing centiles downwards. Normal variants grow parallel to centiles; crossing centiles downwards always warrants pathological investigation.
* Viva Question: Which non-invasive bedside parameter is the most important for differentiating pathological from physiological short stature? Answer: Growth velocity. A normal growth velocity rules out most active pathological causes.
* Trap: Assuming a normal TSH rules out central hypothyroidism in a child with a brain tumour. Pearl: Central hypothyroidism presents with a low free T4 and a low or inappropriately normal TSH. Always check Free T4.
