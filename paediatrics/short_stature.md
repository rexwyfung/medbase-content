
# Short Stature (身材矮小)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Height below the 3rd percentile (or < -2 Standard Deviations) for age and sex on the appropriate local growth chart (e.g., Hong Kong 1993 Growth Charts).
- **Local Epidemiology**: A very common reason for referral to paediatric endocrine clinics in HA hospitals. The vast majority are normal variants (Familial or CDGP).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Normal Variants (80%)**:
  1. **Familial Short Stature (FSS)**: Parents are short. Child's height is appropriate for mid-parental target height. Normal growth velocity.
  2. **Constitutional Delay of Growth and Puberty (CDGP)**: "Late bloomers." Delayed bone age, delayed puberty, but eventually reach a normal adult height. Often a family history of late puberty.
- **Pathological Causes (20%)**:
  - *Endocrine*: Growth Hormone Deficiency (GHD), Hypothyroidism, Cushing's syndrome (weight gain + poor linear growth).
  - *Genetic/Syndromic*: Turner Syndrome (45,XO - ALWAYS consider in short girls), Down Syndrome, Achondroplasia, Prader-Willi.
  - *Chronic Disease*: Coeliac disease, Chronic Kidney Disease, Congenital Heart Disease, severe asthma (or chronic high-dose steroid use).
  - *Nutritional/Psychosocial*: Severe malnutrition, psychosocial dwarfism (abuse/neglect).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Growth Velocity**: The most important clinical parameter. Normal variants grow parallel to the centiles (normal velocity). Pathological causes cross centiles downwards (poor velocity, < 4-5 cm/year in childhood).
- **Weight vs. Height**:
  - *Weight affected more than height*: Nutritional, GI (Coeliac), chronic systemic disease.
  - *Height affected more than weight (chubby and short)*: Endocrine (Hypothyroidism, GHD, Cushing's).
- **Dysmorphic features**: Webbed neck, wide-spaced nipples, cubitus valgus (Turner Syndrome). Disproportionate short limbs (Achondroplasia).
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **FSS vs. CDGP**:
  - *FSS*: Bone age = Chronological age. Puberty is on time. Final height is short.
  - *CDGP*: Bone age is delayed (matches height age). Puberty is delayed. Final height is normal.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Familial Short Stature, Constitutional Delay of Growth and Puberty.
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Turner Syndrome in girls (can present with isolated short stature).
  - Brain tumour (Craniopharyngioma) causing acquired GHD (look for visual field defects, headaches).
  - Coeliac disease (can be clinically silent except for short stature).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **Accurate Measurement**: Height (stadiometer), Weight, Head Circumference. Plot on HK growth charts. Calculate Mid-Parental Height.
  - **Bone Age X-ray**: X-ray of the left hand and wrist (Greulich and Pyle method).
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Bloods (Tier 1)**: CBC, U&E, LFT, Bone profile, TFTs (Free T4, TSH), Coeliac screen (Anti-tTG IgA), IGF-1 and IGFBP-3 (screening for GHD).
  - **Karyotype**: **MANDATORY** in any girl with unexplained short stature to rule out Turner Syndrome.
  - **Dynamic Endocrine Testing**: GH stimulation tests (e.g., Glucagon, Clonidine, Insulin Tolerance Test) if IGF-1 is low and GHD is suspected.
  - **MRI Brain/Pituitary**: If GHD is confirmed, to rule out structural lesions (e.g., craniopharyngioma).
- **Monitoring**:
  - Serial height measurements every 6 months to calculate growth velocity.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Not applicable.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - **Normal Variants (FSS/CDGP)**: Reassurance. No medical treatment required.
  - **Pathological**: Treat the underlying cause (e.g., Thyroxine for hypothyroidism, gluten-free diet for Coeliac).
  - **Recombinant Human Growth Hormone (rhGH)**: Daily subcutaneous injections.
  - *HA Indications*: Severe GHD, Turner Syndrome, Prader-Willi Syndrome, Chronic Renal Failure, SGA failing to catch up by age 4. (Requires strict criteria for HA funding).
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Psychological support if the child is experiencing bullying or low self-esteem due to height.
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Rule out psychosocial dwarfism (a form of severe emotional abuse/neglect that suppresses GH secretion).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Prognosis depends entirely on the cause.
- Untreated GHD or Turner syndrome results in significantly compromised final adult height.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Diagnosing a short, chubby girl with "familial short stature" without further tests. *Pearl*: ALWAYS check a karyotype in a short girl to rule out Turner syndrome, even if she doesn't have classic dysmorphic features.
- **Viva Question**: "How do you differentiate endocrine causes of short stature from nutritional causes clinically?" *Answer*: Endocrine causes make children short and relatively overweight (height centile < weight centile). Nutritional causes make them thin and short (weight centile < height centile).
