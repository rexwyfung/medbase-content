# Alopecia Areata (斑禿 / 鬼剃頭)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Autoimmune Definition
* **Chronic, organ-specific autoimmune disease** → results in non-scarring hair loss (非疤痕性脫髮).
* Characterized by the sudden appearance of discreet, completely bald patches on the scalp or body.

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology
* Very common in the Hong Kong pediatric population.
* ~20% of all cases initially present during childhood.
* Frequently co-exists with other autoimmune disorders prevalent in the local population (e.g., Hashimoto's thyroiditis, Vitiligo).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Collapse of Follicular Immune Privilege
* Normally, the anagen (growing) hair follicle bulb maintains "immune privilege" (免疫豁免) → the immune system ignores it.
* In Alopecia Areata, this privilege collapses → an aggressive swarm of **CD8+ cytotoxic T-cells** specifically targets and attacks the hair follicle bulb.

### Follicular Cycle Arrest
* The targeted follicle is forced prematurely into the resting/shedding phase (**telogen**).
* The hair shaft weakens and falls out.
* Crucially, the stem cells within the follicle are **NOT permanently destroyed** → the condition remains non-scarring and fully reversible.

### Genetic & Environmental Triggers
* Polygenic inheritance pattern; strong HLA associations.
* ↑ risk with +ve family history of Alopecia Areata or other autoimmune diseases.
* Often triggered by acute physical or psychological stress.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Acute Clinical Presentation
* An 8-year-old child typically presents with a sudden, perfectly smooth, circular patch of complete hair loss (often coin-sized) noticed by a parent or barber.
* Completely painless and non-pruritic (zero itching).

### Scalp Examination Findings
* The exposed scalp is completely **smooth, soft, and flesh-colored**.
* Absolutely **NO** scaling, crusting, erythema, tenderness, or surface inflammation visible.

### Pathognomonic Hair Signs
* **Exclamation Mark Hairs (感嘆號髮)**:
  * Pathognomonic finding.
  * Short (1-3mm), broken hairs situated exactly at the margin of the bald patch.
  * Under magnification, the base (at the scalp) is narrowed and unpigmented, while the top is thicker and darker → resembling an exclamation mark (!).
* **Positive "Pull Test"**:
  * Gentle traction on the hair at the periphery pulls out hairs easily → indicates actively expanding/active disease.

### Extracutaneous Signs
* **Nail Pitting (指甲凹陷)**: Fine, pinpoint dimples in the nail plate found in 10-20% of patients.
* Trachyonychia (rough, sandpaper-like nails) or longitudinal ridging may also occur.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Alopecia Areata from Common Mimics
| Discriminatory Feature | Alopecia Areata (斑禿) | Tinea Capitis (頭癬) | Trichotillomania (拔毛癖) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scalp Appearance** | Completely smooth, flesh-colored | Inflamed, intense scaling/dandruff | Normal scalp, irregular geometric patches |
| **Hair Shaft Morphology** | Exclamation mark hairs | Broken "black-dot" hairs | Broken hairs at wildly varying lengths |
| **Pruritus / Symptoms** | Asymptomatic (-ve) | Severely pruritic (+ve) | Asymptomatic (driven by psychiatric urge) |
| **Wood's Lamp** | -ve | May be +ve (fluorescent) | -ve |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Pediatric Differentials
* **Tinea Capitis (頭癬)**: Fungal dermatophyte infection of the scalp.
* **Trichotillomania (拔毛癖)**: Obsessive-compulsive psychiatric hair pulling.
* **Traction Alopecia (牽引性脫髮)**: Mechanical loss from dangerously tight ponytails/braids.
* **Telogen Effluvium (休止期脫髮)**: Diffuse, global shedding 3 months post-severe stressor or illness (not a discreet patch).

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Clinical Investigations
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Largely relying on visual inspection and pull test.
* **Trichoscopy/Dermoscopy (皮膚鏡檢查)**: Confirms exclamation mark hairs, "yellow dots" (sebum/keratin in empty follicles), and absent inflammation.

### Laboratory Autoimmunity Screening
* **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs) & Anti-TPO**: High yield to check for concurrent autoimmune thyroiditis.
* **Celiac Screen**: Anti-tTG IgA (if concurrent GI symptoms or faltering growth).
* **Fasting Glucose/Autoimmune Screen**: If clinically indicated by family history (Vitiligo/T1DM clustering).

### Special Dermatological Tests
* **Fungal Culture / Skin Scraping**: ONLY indicated if scaling/inflammation is present to formally rule out Tinea.
* **Scalp Biopsy**: Rarely needed; histopathology shows classic peribulbar lymphocytic infiltrate ("swarm of bees" 蜂群狀).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### 1st Line Active Observation
* **Active Observation (觀察)**:
  * For limited, localized disease (<3 patches).
  * Spontaneous regrowth occurs within 6 to 12 months in up to 80% of children.
  * Reassurance and education are strongly indicated over aggressive therapy.

### 1st Line Topical Therapy
* **Topical Corticosteroids (局部皮質類固醇)**:
  * Potent or super-potent topical steroids (e.g., Clobetasol 0.05% ointment) applied OD/BD to bald patches to suppress the local T-cell attack.
* **Topical Minoxidil (米諾地爾)**:
  * 5% solution often used as an adjunct to stimulate follicular entry into anagen phase.

### 2nd Line Intralesional & Topical Immunotherapy
* **Intralesional Steroid Injections**:
  * Triamcinolone acetonide (曲安奈德) microinjections.
  * Painful; usually reserved strictly for older adolescents or highly refractory patches.
* **Topical Immunotherapy**:
  * DPCP (Diphenylcyclopropenone) application.
  * Induces a mild allergic contact dermatitis to distract/modulate the local immune response; used in extensive disease.

### Systemic & Novel HA Therapies
* **Systemic Corticosteroids**: Short-course oral prednisolone only for rapidly progressing, extensive flares.
* **Oral JAK Inhibitors (JAK 抑制劑)**:
  * e.g., Baricitinib, Tofacitinib.
  * Emerging, highly effective novel therapies strictly reserved for incredibly severe, refractory cases (e.g., Alopecia Universalis) under HA Dermatology specialist care.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Psychosocial Support
* The psychosocial impact of sudden baldness in children can be catastrophic, leading to intense bullying, stigma, and school refusal.
* **Clinical Psychology (臨床心理學家)**: Vital for CBT, self-esteem building, and coping strategies.
* **Medical Social Worker (醫務社工)**: Essential for financial assistance/sponsorship for high-quality medical wigs (假髮) to enable normal school attendance.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Abuse & Neglect Considerations
* Alopecia Areata itself is NOT a sign of NAI.
* However, when differentiating from **Trichotillomania**, the clinician must be vigilant: severe psychiatric hair pulling may serve as a coping mechanism indicating underlying severe psychosocial stress, domestic abuse, or neglect.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Severe Disease Progression
* **Alopecia Totalis (全禿)**: Progression to the complete loss of ALL scalp hair.
* **Alopecia Universalis (普禿)**: Progression to the total loss of EVERY single hair on the entire body (eyebrows, eyelashes, body/pubic hair).

### Prognostic Indicators
* Patchy AA generally carries a high spontaneous recovery rate.
* **Poor Prognostic Factors**:
  * Progression to Totalis/Universalis (regrowth prognosis is extremely poor).
  * Pre-pubertal / early childhood onset.
  * Ophiasis pattern (band-like hair loss wrapping around the temporal and occipital scalp margins).
  * Concurrent severe atopy or significant nail involvement.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Ordering oral anti-fungals for a perfectly smooth, non-scaly bald patch just in case it is Tinea. Pearl: Examine the scalp closely: if there are no scales and no inflammation, it is not fungal.
* Viva Question: At the border of an enlarging patch of Alopecia Areata, what specific morphological hair finding confirms the diagnosis, and why does it look that way? Answer: The 'exclamation mark' hair. It occurs because the autoimmune attack selectively starves and narrows the hair shaft near the bulb at the scalp level, leaving the older, thicker hair shaft resting above it before it breaks.
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents that all hair loss will definitely grow back quickly. Pearl: You must appropriately counsel families that while patchy AA has a high spontaneous recovery rate, cases with early onset, nail changes, or ophiasis pattern carry a high risk of progression to Alopecia Totalis/Universalis and a poor long-term prognosis.
