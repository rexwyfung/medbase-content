# Alopecia Areata (斑禿 / 鬼剃頭)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A chronic, organ-specific autoimmune disease resulting in non-scarring hair loss. 
- **Local Epidemiology**: Common. Approximately 20% of all cases begin in childhood.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Normally, the anagen (growing) hair follicle bulb enjoys "immune privilege," meaning the immune system ignores it.
- In AA, this privilege collapses, and an aggressive swarm of CD8+ cytotoxic T-cells specifically attacks the hair follicle bulb. 
- The follicle forces the hair into an early resting/shedding phase (telogen), causing the hair to fall out. However, the stem cells within the follicle are NOT permanently destroyed (hence it is non-scarring and reversible).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- An 8-year-old boy's mother suddenly notices a perfectly smooth, completely bald circular patch the size of a coin on the back of his head while cutting his hair. The child reports zero pain or itching.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Circumscribed, well-demarcated patches of complete hair loss.
- The exposed scalp is **completely smooth, soft, and flesh-colored**. There is absolutely no scaling, crusting, erythema, tenderness, or inflammation visible on the surface.
- **Exclamation Mark Hairs**: Pathognomonic finding. Short (1-3mm), broken hairs situated right at the margin of the bald patch. Under magnification, the base of the hair (at the scalp) is narrowed and unpigmented, while the top is thicker and darker, exactly resembling an exclamation mark (!).
- A positive "pull test" at the periphery (hair pulls out easily) indicates actively expanding disease.
- Nail pitting (fine dimples in the nails) is found in 10-20% of patients.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Tinea Capitis (Ringworm): Tinea causes hair loss *with* severe inflammation, intense scaling/dandruff, and broken "black-dot" hairs. AA is totally smooth and uninflamed.
- Distinct from Trichotillomania: Trichotillomania (psychiatric compulsive hair pulling) results in an irregular patch with hairs broken at wildly varying lengths, without exclamation mark hairs.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Tinea Capitis
- Trichotillomania
- Traction alopecia (caused by dangerously tight ponytails/braids).
- Telogen Effluvium (Diffusely shedding hair globally 3 months after a severe stressor or illness; not a discreet patch).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- A clinical diagnosis largely relying on finding exclamation mark hairs on trichoscopy/dermoscopy.
- **Comorbid Autoimmunity Screening**: Check Thyroid function tests (TFTs/anti-TPO) and consider checking for Celiac disease/Vitiligo, as these autoimmune conditions frequently cluster together.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Active Observation**: For limited disease (<3 patches), spontaneous regrowth occurs within 6 to 12 months in up to 80% of children. Reassurance is strongly indicated.
- **Topical Therapy**: Potent or super-potent topical Corticosteroids (e.g., Clobetasol) applied to the bald patches to suppress the local T-cell attack.
- Intralesional steroid injections (painful, usually reserved for older adolescents).
- New therapies: Oral JAK Inhibitors (e.g., Baricitinib) are emerging for incredibly severe, refractory cases.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- The psychosocial impact can be catastrophic. Children with visible hair loss face intense bullying and stigma. Clinical psychology support for coping strategies (and occasionally the use of medical wigs) is vital.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A (unless differentiating from Trichotillomania, which may indicate severe underlying psychosocial stress/abuse).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **Alopecia Totalis**: Progression to total loss of all scalp hair.
- **Alopecia Universalis**: Total loss of every hair on the entire body (eyebrows, eyelashes, body hair).
- While patchy AA has a high spontaneous recovery rate, the prognosis for regrowth in Totalis/Universalis or cases beginning before puberty is extremely poor.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Ordering oral anti-fungals for a perfectly smooth, non-scaly bald patch just in case it is Tinea. Examine the scalp closely: if there are no scales and no inflammation, it is not fungal.
- **Viva Q**: "At the border of an enlarging patch of Alopecia Areata, what specific morphological hair finding confirms the diagnosis, and why does it look that way?" Answer: The 'exclamation mark' hair. It occurs because the autoimmune attack selectively starves and narrows the hair shaft near the bulb at the scalp level, leaving the older, thicker hair shaft resting above it before it breaks.
