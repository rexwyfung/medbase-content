# Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (二型糖尿病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* Chronic metabolic disorder characterized by **hyperglycemia (高血糖)**
* Driven by **insulin resistance (胰島素抵抗)** relative to insulin secretion
* Progressive decline in **pancreatic β-cell (胰島β細胞)** function

### Local Epidemiology of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus in Hong Kong
* Incidence ↑ in HK children/adolescents alongside ↑ **childhood obesity (兒童肥胖)**
* Peak age of onset: **Mid-puberty (青春期中期)** (mean age 13–14 years)
* Prevalence higher in **females (女性)**
* Local Department of Health student health service data: ↑ prevalence of overweight/obesity directly correlates with newly diagnosed T2DM in HK secondary school students

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* ↑ **Adiposity (肥胖)** (esp. visceral fat) → ↑ free fatty acids & inflammatory adipokines → peripheral **insulin resistance (胰島素抵抗)**
* Liver: ↑ Hepatic glucose production (gluconeogenesis)
* Muscle: ↓ Insulin-stimulated glucose uptake
* Pancreas: Initial compensatory hyperinsulinemia → progressive **β-cell exhaustion (β細胞衰竭)** → ↓ insulin secretion → overt **hyperglycemia (高血糖)**

### Genetics of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Polygenic inheritance (多基因遺傳)**
* Strong familial clustering: >75% of pediatric patients have a 1st or 2nd-degree relative with T2DM
* Higher risk in Asian/Chinese populations vs Caucasian

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* Often **asymptomatic (無症狀)** (detected via HA school screening)
* **Polyuria (多尿)**
* **Polydipsia (多飲)**
* **Nocturia/Enuresis (夜尿/尿床)**
* **Weight loss (體重減輕)** (less severe than T1DM)
* Lethargy/Fatigue
* Blurred vision

### Key Clinical Signs of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* ↑ BMI (>90th centile) / **Central obesity (中央肥胖)**
* **Acanthosis nigricans (黑棘皮症)** (thickened, hyperpigmented skin on neck, axillae → clinical marker of insulin resistance)
* Hypertension (高血壓)
* Hepatomegaly (肝腫大) (indicative of NAFLD)
* Fungal infections (e.g., recurrent candidiasis)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features between Type 2 and Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus
| Feature | Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) | Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Insidious, gradual | Acute, rapid |
| **Body Habitus** | **Obese (肥胖)** / Overweight | Normal / Thin, significant weight loss |
| **Acanthosis Nigricans** | **Present (+ve)** | Absent (-ve) |
| **Autoantibodies** | **Absent (-ve)** | **Present (+ve)** (GAD, IA-2, ZnT8) |
| **C-peptide / Insulin** | **Normal or High (↑)** | Low (↓) or undetectable |
| **Ketosis at onset** | Rare (but ~10-20% can present in DKA) | Common (>30% present in DKA) |
| **Family History** | Strong (multi-generational) | Weak (~10%) |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis for Pediatric Hyperglycemia
| Diagnosis | Differentiating Clinical & Laboratory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Type 1 Diabetes (T1DM)** | Autoantibodies +ve, C-peptide ↓, young age, abrupt onset, ketosis-prone |
| **MODY (單基因糖尿病)** | Autosomal dominant (+ve family hx across 3 generations), non-obese, autoantibody -ve, genetic testing +ve (e.g., HNF1A, GCK) |
| **Steroid-induced DM** | History of systemic corticosteroid use, cushingoid features |
| **Neonatal Diabetes** | Onset < 6 months of age, genetic mutations (e.g., KCNJ11) |
| **Secondary Diabetes** | Underlying cystic fibrosis, iron overload (thalassemia major - common in HK), Cushing syndrome |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Capillary Blood Glucose (CBG)**: Fasting or random
* **Urine dipstick**: +ve glucose, +/- ketones (to rule out severe insulin deficiency)
* **Blood pressure**: Essential for comorbidity screening
* **Growth parameters**: Plot weight, height, BMI on HK growth charts

### Laboratory Investigations for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Diagnostic Gold Standards**:
  * Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) ≥ 7.0 mmol/L
  * Random Plasma Glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (with symptoms)
  * **HbA1c (糖化血紅素)** ≥ 6.5% (48 mmol/mol)
  * 2-hour Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) ≥ 11.1 mmol/L
* **Discriminatory Workup**:
  * **Pancreatic Autoantibodies**: GAD-65, IA-2, ZnT8, IAA (must be -ve)
  * **Fasting C-peptide & Insulin**: Concurrently measured with glucose; shows hyperinsulinemia/normal levels
* **Comorbidity Baseline Screening (HA Protocol)**:
  * Fasting lipid profile (Cholesterol, LDL, HDL, Triglycerides)
  * Liver function tests (ALT, AST for NAFLD)
  * Renal function & Early morning urine albumin:creatinine ratio (ACR)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS - 高滲透壓高血糖狀態)**:
  * Fluid resuscitation is paramount (0.9% NaCl)
  * Gradual reduction of serum osmolality
  * Insulin (IV infusion) only *after* fluid expansion to prevent cardiovascular collapse
* **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA - 糖尿病酮症酸中毒)**:
  * IV fluids, IV insulin (0.05-0.1 U/kg/hr), monitor K+ and neuro status (cerebral edema risk)

### Chronic HA Protocol Management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Tier 1: Lifestyle Modification (生活模式改變)**:
  * Dietitian referral: ↓ simple carbohydrates, portion control, structured meals
  * Exercise: ≥60 mins moderate-vigorous physical activity daily
* **Tier 2: Pharmacological Management**:
  * Initiate if HbA1c target not met with lifestyle alone, or symptomatic at presentation

### Pharmacological Agents for Pediatric Type 2 Diabetes
| Medication | Indication / Mechanism | HA Clinical Notes & Side Effects |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Metformin (二甲雙胍)** | **1st line**. ↓ Hepatic glucose output, ↑ peripheral sensitivity | GI upset (diarrhea). Risk of lactic acidosis. Maximum 2g/day. Start low, titrate slow. |
| **Insulin (胰島素)** | 1st line if metabolically unstable, HbA1c >8.5%, or DKA/ketosis | Basal insulin (e.g., Glargine) often used. Titrate to fasting glucose goals. Weight gain, hypoglycemia. |
| **GLP-1 Agonists (e.g., Liraglutide)** | 2nd line adjunct for >10 yrs. ↑ glucose-dependent insulin release, slows gastric emptying | Subcutaneous injection. Promotes weight loss. GI side effects (nausea). |

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Pediatric Endocrinologist**: Medical management & screening
* **Diabetes Specialist Nurse (DM Nurse)**: Education, glucometer training, adherence monitoring
* **Dietitian (營養師)**: Culturally tailored dietary advice (e.g., modifying standard HK rice/noodle portions)
* **Clinical Psychologist**: Address eating disorders, body image, depression

### Psychosocial Care for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* High risk for **depression (抑鬱)** and anxiety
* Social stigma associated with obesity and chronic illness
* Motivation assessment: Crucial for adolescent adherence to lifestyle modifications and oral medications

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Red Flags in Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **Medical Neglect**: Consistent failure to attend HA clinic appointments, failure to provide medications (Metformin/Insulin), persistent extreme hyperglycemia despite education
* **Fabricated/Induced Illness**: Intentional insulin overdose (rare in T2DM but possible if on insulin)
* Severe, unaddressed psychological distress or deliberate self-harm (non-compliance as a form of self-harm)

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* **HHS (Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State)**: High mortality, profound dehydration, severe hyperglycemia, lack of significant ketosis
* **DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis)**: Can occur in T2DM under extreme stress or progressive β-cell failure
* **Hypoglycemia (低血糖)**: Usually iatrogenic (insulin/sulfonylureas)

### Chronic Complications & Prognosis of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
* Microvascular (develop faster in pediatric T2DM vs T1DM):
  * **Nephropathy (腎病變)**: Microalbuminuria → ESRF
  * **Retinopathy (視網膜病變)**: Macular edema, blindness
  * **Neuropathy (神經病變)**: Peripheral sensory loss
* Macrovascular:
  * Early onset **Cardiovascular Disease (心血管疾病)**, stroke
* Associated Comorbidities:
  * **NAFLD (非酒精性脂肪肝)**
  * **PCOS (多囊卵巢綜合症)** in females
  * Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most definitive clinical sign of insulin resistance to look for on physical examination? Answer: Acanthosis nigricans, typically found on the posterior neck and axillae.
* Viva Question: Which autoantibodies must be tested to rule out Type 1 Diabetes in an obese adolescent presenting with hyperglycemia? Answer: GAD-65, IA-2, ZnT8, and IAA.
* Trap: Assuming a child presenting in DKA must have Type 1 Diabetes. Pearl: 10-20% of adolescents with Type 2 Diabetes can present with ketonuria or frank DKA at the time of diagnosis.
* Trap: Starting IV insulin immediately upon diagnosing Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS). Pearl: Fluid resuscitation must be initiated first to slowly lower osmolality and prevent circulatory collapse before initiating an insulin infusion.
* Pitfall: Relying solely on Metformin for an adolescent presenting with an HbA1c of 10% and symptomatic hyperglycemia. In patients presenting with metabolic instability, severe symptoms, or HbA1c >8.5%, insulin therapy should be initiated alongside Metformin to rapidly reverse glucose toxicity.
