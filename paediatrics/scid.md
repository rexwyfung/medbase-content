# Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID) (嚴重聯合免疫缺陷症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of SCID
* Profound, life-threatening **primary immunodeficiency (原發性免疫缺陷病)**.
* Characterized by absent or non-functional **T-cells (T細胞)**, often accompanied by absent/non-functional B-cells and NK-cells.
* Pediatric emergency → universally fatal <1 yr without definitive treatment.

### Local Epidemiology of SCID in Hong Kong
* Extremely rare (approx. 1 in 50,000 to 100,000 live births globally).
* X-linked form is most common.
* Consanguinity ↑ risk for autosomal recessive variants in local minority populations.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Variants and Immunophenotypes of SCID
| Genetic Defect | Inheritance | Mechanism | Phenotype |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common $\gamma$ chain ($\gamma$c) mutation** | X-linked (Most common) | Defective cytokine receptors (IL-2, 4, 7, 9, 15, 21) | **T- B+ NK-** |
| **ADA (Adenosine Deaminase) Deficiency** | Autosomal Recessive | Toxic dATP accumulation → apoptosis of all lymphocytes | **T- B- NK-** |
| **RAG1/RAG2 Deficiency** | Autosomal Recessive | Defective V(D)J recombination | **T- B- NK+** |

### Immunological Consequences of SCID
* Absent T-cells → no **cellular immunity (細胞免疫)** → vulnerable to viruses, fungi, and opportunistic pathogens.
* Absent T-helper cells → secondary B-cell failure → no **humoral immunity (體液免疫)** → vulnerable to bacteria.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Infantile Presentation of SCID
* Age of onset: 3–6 months (when maternal IgG wanes).
* Severe **failure to thrive (FTT) (生長遲緩)**.
* Relentless, intractable chronic diarrhea.
* Widespread, unresolving **oral thrush/Candidiasis (鵝口瘡)**.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Pathognomonic Physical Signs of SCID
* Absent palpable lymph nodes.
* Absent tonsils (lack of lymphoid tissue).

### Severe Opportunistic Infections in SCID
* *Pneumocystis jirovecii* pneumonia (PCP).
* Severe viral pneumonitis (RSV, Adenovirus, Parainfluenza).
* Disseminated CMV or EBV.
* **BCGosis (卡介苗感染)** (if given live BCG vaccine at birth).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing SCID from Vertical HIV/AIDS
* **Onset**: SCID presents earlier (<6 months) with rapidly fatal opportunistic infections; HIV usually progresses slower.
* **Maternal Serology**: HIV test -ve in SCID.
* **Lymphocyte Profile**: SCID shows profound absolute lack of naive T-cells (low TRECs); HIV shows declining CD4+ count but initially present T-cells.

### Distinguishing SCID from DiGeorge Syndrome
* **DiGeorge Syndrome (迪喬治症候群)**: Associated with dysmorphic facies, conotruncal congenital heart defects, and hypocalcemia (parathyroid hypoplasia). SCID lacks these structural/metabolic defects.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis of Profound Infantile Immunodeficiency
| Condition | Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Vertical HIV infection (Neonatal AIDS)** | Maternal HIV +ve, normal newborn TREC screen, ↓ CD4+ but normal total lymphocytes initially. |
| **DiGeorge Syndrome (22q11.2 deletion)** | Cardiac defects, hypocalcemia, cleft palate, variable T-cell deficiency. |
| **Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome (X-linked)** | Microthrombocytopenia, severe eczema, recurrent infections, WASp mutation. |
| **X-linked Agammaglobulinemia (Bruton's)** | T-cells normal, B-cells absent. Presents slightly later (>6 mo) with recurrent bacterial (not viral/fungal) infections. |
| **Severe Malnutrition** | History of inadequate intake, social deprivation; lymphocytes may be low but recover with refeeding. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
### Screening & Bedside Investigations for SCID
* **Newborn Dried Blood Spot**: **TREC (T-cell Receptor Excision Circles) assay**.
* Low or zero TREC count → absolute failure of naive T-cell maturation in the thymus.

### Blood Investigations for SCID
* **CBC with Differential**: Profound **absolute lymphopenia (淋巴細胞減少症)** (Caution: Engrafted maternal T-cells can falsely normalize lymphocyte count).
* **Immunoglobulins (IgG, IgA, IgM)**: Profoundly ↓.

### Special & Definitive Investigations for SCID
* **Flow Cytometry**: Definitive diagnosis. Shows ABSENT/severely low T-cells (CD3 <300/$\mu$L or <20% of lymphocytes).
* **Genetic Sequencing**: To identify specific mutations (e.g., IL2RG, ADA, RAG1/2) for targeted therapy and family counseling.

### Imaging Findings in SCID
* **Chest X-ray (CXR)**: Classic **absent thymic shadow (胸腺陰影消失)** in a neonate/infant.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Resuscitation & Environmental Control
* **ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION**: **NEVER GIVE LIVE VACCINES** (BCG, Rotavirus, MMR, Varicella). → Causes fatal disseminated infection.
* Immediate strict protective isolation (Positive pressure, HEPA-filtered room).
* Use ONLY **irradiated, CMV-safe, leukodepleted blood products** (prevents fatal Transfusion-Associated Graft-Versus-Host Disease).
* Cease breastfeeding if mother is CMV +ve (prevents CMV transmission).

### Pharmacological Prophylaxis in SCID
* **PCP Prophylaxis**: Co-trimoxazole.
* **Antifungal Prophylaxis**: Fluconazole.
* **Antiviral Prophylaxis**: Acyclovir.

### Hospital Authority (HA) Replacement & Curative Therapies
* **Replacement Therapy**: Lifelong IVIG or SCIG (intravenous/subcutaneous immunoglobulin) to replace missing antibodies.
* **Curative Therapy**: **Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT) (造血幹細胞移植)**. Optimal if performed <3.5 months of age before organ damage from infections.
* **ADA Deficiency Specifics**: Enzyme replacement therapy (PEG-ADA) or hematopoietic gene therapy.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
### Multidisciplinary Team Coordination for SCID
* Immediate transfer to specialized HA tertiary pediatric immunology unit (e.g., Hong Kong Children's Hospital).
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Crucial for intense psychological support for parents dealing with infant mortality risk and extreme isolation protocols.
* **Clinical Geneticist**: For parental screening, counseling, and future pregnancy planning (prenatal diagnosis).

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Issues in Severe Immunodeficiency
* Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) is rare, but **medical neglect** (e.g., parents defaulting on prophylactic medications, refusing isolation, or declining HSCT) is acutely life-threatening.
* Requires immediate intervention by pediatricians and **Social Welfare Department (SWD)** if compliance is compromised.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Iatrogenic Complications of SCID
* **Transfusion-associated Graft-versus-Host Disease (TA-GVHD)**: Un-irradiated donor T-cells attack the defenseless baby → fatal rash, diarrhea, liver failure.
* **Disseminated BCGosis**: Systemic, potentially fatal mycobacterial infection if given routine neonatal BCG before diagnosis.

### Prognosis of SCID
* Untreated: 100% mortality <1 year of age.
* Treated (HSCT <3.5 months): >90% survival rate with reconstituted cellular immunity.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the classic radiological finding in a neonate with SCID? Answer: Absent thymic shadow on Chest X-ray.
* Viva Question: A baby with SCID is incidentally given a blood transfusion for anemia before the diagnosis is known. A week later, the baby develops a horrific rash, diarrhea, and liver failure. Why? Answer: Transfusion-Associated Graft-Versus-Host Disease (TA-GVHD). The baby has no immune system to reject the donor T-cells, which engraft and mount a massive, fatal attack against the baby's tissues.
* Trap: Ordering a generic "CBC" to check for SCID and being reassured by a "normal" white count. Pearl: The white count can be falsely normal due to high neutrophils or engrafted maternal T-cells. You MUST look specifically at the lymphocyte absolute count and demand Flow Cytometry.
* Pitfall: Giving the standard neonatal BCG and Rotavirus vaccines to a baby with an undiagnosed family history of infant deaths. Always rigorously check family history of immunodeficiency before giving live vaccines to prevent fatal vaccine-strain infections.
