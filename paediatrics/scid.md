# Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID) (嚴重聯合免疫缺陷症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A profound, life-threatening primary immunodeficiency characterized by the absence of functional T-cells, often accompanied by absent or non-functional B and NK cells. It is a pediatric emergency.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Extremely rare, but universally fatal in infancy if completely missed.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Multiple genetic defects cause SCID. 
  - **X-linked SCID** (most common): Mutation in the common gamma chain ($\gamma$c) of cytokine receptors (IL-2, IL-4, IL-7, IL-9, IL-15, IL-21). Result: T- B+ NK- phenotype.
  - **ADA Deficiency**: Autosomal recessive lack of Adenosine Deaminase, causing toxic accumulation of dATP which kills all lymphocytes. Result: T- B- NK- phenotype.
- Without T-cells, cellular immunity is absent (vulnerable to viruses/fungi). Without T-helper cells, B-cells cannot produce antibodies (vulnerable to bacteria).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A 3-month-old infant presenting with failure to thrive, relentless chronic diarrhea, and widespread, unresolving oral thrush (Candidiasis).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Continuous, severe opportunistic infections: *Pneumocystis jirovecii* pneumonia (PCP), severe RSV, disseminated CMV.
- Chronic persistent diarrhea.
- **Classic Sign**: Absence of palpable lymph nodes and tonsils (no lymphoid tissue).
- **Classic X-ray Finding**: Absent thymic shadow on a chest X-ray in a neonate.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from HIV/AIDS: SCID presents much earlier (first few months of life) with a broader and more severe absolute lack of T-cells, and the mother is HIV negative.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Vertical HIV infection (Neonatal AIDS)
- DiGeorge Syndrome (Thymic aplasia - also causes low T cells but has heart/calcium issues)
- Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
- Severe malnutrition

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Screening**: **TREC** (T-cell Receptor Excision Circles) assay on Newborn Dried Blood Spot. A low or zero TREC count indicates failure of T-cell maturation in the thymus.
- **Bloods**: Profound lymphopenia on CBC (though maternal T-cells engrafting in the baby can falsely elevate the count).
- **Definitive Diagnosis**: Flow Cytometry showing ABSENT T-cells (CD3 <300/$\mu$L or <20%).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION**: **NEVER GIVE LIVE VACCINES** (Rotavirus, BCG, MMR, Varicella). A live vaccine will cause fatal disseminated infection.
- **Immediate**: Strict protective isolation (Positive pressure room). Prophylactic Co-trimoxazole (for PCP), Fluconazole (for fungi), and Acyclovir.
- **Replacement**: Lifelong IVIG (to replace the missing antibodies).
- **Curative**: **Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT)**. Ideally performed before 3 months of age before severe infections damage the organs.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Immediate transfer to a specialized tertiary pediatric immunology unit. Parents require intense psychological support due to the fragility of the child.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Death before age 1 invariably occurs without HSCT.
- Transfusion-associated Graft-versus-Host Disease (TA-GVHD): If un-irradiated blood is transfused, donor T-cells will attack the defenseless baby. All blood products MUST be irradiated, CMV-safe, and leukodepleted.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Ordering a generic "CBC" to check for SCID and being reassured by a "normal" white count. The white count can be normal due to high neutrophils or engrafted maternal T-cells. You MUST look specifically at the lymphocyte absolute count and demand Flow Cytometry.
- **Viva Q**: "A baby with SCID is incidentally given a blood transfusion for anemia before the diagnosis is known. A week later, the baby develops a horrific rash, diarrhea, and liver failure. Why?" Answer: Transfusion-Associated Graft-Versus-Host Disease. The baby has no immune system to reject the donor T-cells, so the donor T-cells engraft and mount a massive, fatal attack against the baby's own tissues.
