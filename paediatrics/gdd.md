# Global Developmental Delay (GDD) (整體發展遲緩)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Global Developmental Delay
* Significant delay (performance ≥2 SD below age-appropriate mean) in ≥2 of 4 domains:
  * Gross Motor (大肌肉)
  * Vision & Fine Motor (視覺及小肌肉)
  * Hearing, Speech & Language (聽覺及言語)
  * Social, Emotional & Behavioural (社交及情緒)
* Terminology restricted to children <5 yrs; >5 yrs → **Intellectual Disability (ID)** (智力障礙).

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Screening
* Common presentation at **Child Assessment Centres (CAC)** (兒童體能智力測驗中心) under Dept of Health (DH).
* Affects ~1-3% of children.
* Universal screening via **Maternal and Child Health Centres (MCHC)** (母嬰健康院) developmental surveillance programme.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Prenatal Causes (Most Common ~80%)
* **Genetic/Chromosomal**: **Down syndrome** (唐氏綜合症) (Trisomy 21), **Fragile X syndrome** (脆性X綜合症), Microdeletions (e.g., 22q11, Williams syndrome).
* **Congenital Infections**: TORCH (Toxoplasmosis, Rubella, CMV, HSV, Syphilis).
* **Teratogens**: Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD), maternal anticonvulsants (e.g., Valproate).
* **Brain Malformations**: Cortical dysplasia, holoprosencephaly.

### Perinatal Causes
* **Hypoxic Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE)** (缺氧缺血性腦病).
* Extreme prematurity (極度早產) & intraventricular haemorrhage.
* Severe neonatal jaundice → **Kernicterus** (核黃疸).

### Postnatal Causes
* CNS infections: Meningitis/Encephalitis.
* Traumatic brain injury / Non-accidental injury (NAI).
* Severe psychosocial deprivation/neglect.
* Metabolic/Endocrine: **Congenital Hypothyroidism** (先天性甲狀腺功能低下), Inborn Errors of Metabolism (IEM).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Red Flag Developmental Milestones
* Not sitting unsupported by 9 months.
* Not walking independently by 18 months.
* No meaningful single words by 18 months.

### Neurological & Physical Examination Clues
* **Neurological**: Hypotonia (↓ tone) → genetic syndromes; Spasticity/Asymmetry (↑ tone) → **Cerebral Palsy** (腦性麻痺).
* **Dysmorphic Features**: Suggests chromosomal anomaly.
* **Neurocutaneous Stigmata**: Café-au-lait macules (Neurofibromatosis type 1), Ash-leaf spots (Tuberous Sclerosis).
* **Growth Parameters**: Microcephaly (congenital infection, fetal alcohol); Macrocephaly (hydrocephalus, specific syndromes).
* **Organomegaly**: Hepatosplenomegaly → lysosomal storage disorders (e.g., Mucopolysaccharidosis).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing GDD vs Specific Language Impairment vs Autism vs Cerebral Palsy
| Feature | Global Developmental Delay (GDD) | Specific Language Impairment | Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) | Cerebral Palsy (CP) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Domains Affected** | ≥2 domains delayed | Isolated speech/language | Social communication, rigid behaviours | Predominantly motor (tone/posture) |
| **Social Engagement** | +ve (attempts pointing/eye contact) | +ve (uses non-verbal cues) | -ve (poor eye contact, no joint attention) | +ve (unless severe cognitive deficit) |
| **Motor Skills** | Delayed | Normal | Usually normal | Spasticity, dystonia, ataxia |
| **Repetitive Behaviours**| Absent | Absent | +ve (flapping, lining up toys) | Absent |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Broad Differentials for Delayed Milestones
* **Isolated delay**: e.g., isolated speech delay due to hearing loss, isolated motor delay.
* **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)** (自閉症譜系障礙).
* **Cerebral Palsy (CP)**.
* **Progressive Neurodegenerative Disorder**: e.g., Leukodystrophies, Mitochondrial disease.

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Mandatory Investigations
* **Audiology**: Formal hearing test (e.g., OAE, ABR/VRA) → Rule out deafness before diagnosing central speech delay.
* **Vision Assessment**: Formal ophthalmology/orthoptist review.

### Genetic Investigations
* **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA)**: 1st line for unexplained GDD/ID & dysmorphism.
* **Fragile X DNA testing**: Mandatory in boys with GDD.

### Metabolic & Blood Investigations
* **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs)**: Rule out treatable hypothyroidism.
* **Creatine Kinase (CK)**: Rule out Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy in boys with isolated gross motor delay.
* Baseline metabolic screen: U&E, LFTs, bone profile, lead levels, blood gas/lactate, urine amino/organic acids.

### Neuroimaging
* **MRI Brain**: Indicated if +ve focal neurological signs, micro/macrocephaly, or seizures.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute & Disease-Specific Management
* Treat underlying specific cause if found: e.g., Thyroxine for Hypothyroidism, dietary restriction for Phenylketonuria (PKU).
* Treat comorbidities: Anticonvulsants for epilepsy, baclofen/botulinum toxin for spasticity.

### Chronic Management & HA Early Intervention Protocols
* **Child Assessment Centre (CAC)**: Referral for formal multidisciplinary diagnostic evaluation.
* **Special Child Care Centre (SCCC)** / **Early Education and Training Centre (EETC)**: Placements arranged via SWD for intensive pre-school training.
* **Targeted Therapies**:
  * **Physiotherapy (PT)**: Gross motor skills, seating, orthotics.
  * **Occupational Therapy (OT)**: Fine motor, ADLs, sensory integration.
  * **Speech Therapy (ST)**: Language stimulation, swallowing/feeding issues.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Social & Family Support Systems
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Navigate **Social Welfare Department (SWD)** (社會福利署) system, assist with **Disability Allowance** (傷殘津貼) application.
* **Psychological Support**: Clinical psychologist for parental grief counseling ("loss of perfect child") and behavioral management strategies.
* **Parent Support Groups**: Connect families for peer support.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Environmental & Abuse Considerations
* Profound psychosocial deprivation or neglect → environmental GDD.
* Assess home environment, caregiver-child interaction.
* Unexplained injuries, burns, or missed appointments → refer to MSW & suspected child abuse/NAI team.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognosis and Neuroplasticity
* Dependent on underlying aetiology & severity of brain injury.
* Early intensive EETC/SCCC intervention maximizes neuroplasticity and long-term potential.

### Long-Term Complications
* Transition to Intellectual Disability (ID) diagnosis at >5 yrs.
* Behavioural/psychiatric issues (ADHD, anxiety).
* Epilepsy, feeding difficulties, recurrent aspiration (if associated CP).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: A 2-year-old boy presents with no speech. What is the absolute most important first investigation? Answer: Formal hearing test / Audiology assessment. Never assume ASD or GDD without ruling out deafness.
* Viva Question: What is the difference between developmental delay and developmental regression? Answer: Delay = slow to acquire milestones. Regression = losing previously acquired milestones.
* Trap: Assuming speech delay is just "late talking" without testing hearing. Pearl: Always refer for formal audiology in any speech delay; never wait for "catch-up".
* Pitfall: Missing Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy in a boy with "clumsiness" or delayed walking. Always check a CK level in boys with unexplained gross motor delay.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing GDD in a child over 5 years old. The term GDD is strictly for <5 yrs; >5 yrs use Intellectual Disability (ID).
* Trap: Ignoring the possibility of a progressive neurodegenerative disorder. Pearl: Developmental regression is a massive red flag indicating a progressive neurodegenerative or metabolic disorder, requiring urgent investigation (MRI, metabolic screen).
