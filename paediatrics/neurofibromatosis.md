# Neurofibromatosis Type 1 (第一型神經纖維瘤病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Neurofibromatosis Type 1
* Multisystem neurocutaneous disorder (phakomatosis) (斑痣性母斑病)
* Characterised by abnormal skin pigmentation
* Characterised by growth of tumours along nerve sheaths

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Incidence: ~1 in 3,000
* Clinical follow-up: Specialised neurogenetics (神經遺傳學) clinics in HA hospitals (e.g., Duchess of Kent Children's Hospital)
* General paediatric clinics manage routine surveillance

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Mutation & Inheritance
* **Genetics**: Autosomal Dominant (體染色體顯性遺傳)
* **Gene locus**: Mutation in **NF1** gene on Chromosome 17 (17q11.2)
* **De novo rate**: ~50% de novo mutations (associated with ↑ paternal age)
* **Penetrance**: 100% penetrance by adulthood
* **Expressivity**: Highly variable clinical severity

### Pathophysiology of Tumour Formation
* **NF1** gene encodes **neurofibromin** (神經纖維瘤蛋白)
* **Neurofibromin** = tumour suppressor protein
* Normal physiological role: Negatively regulates RAS-MAPK pathway
* Loss of function mutation → ↑ RAS hyperactivation → uncontrolled cell proliferation → tumour formation

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Timeline of Clinical Presentation
* **Infancy**: **Café-au-lait macules** (咖啡牛奶斑) emerge
* **Toddler/Preschool**: Axillary/inguinal freckling appears
* **Early Childhood**: **Optic pathway gliomas** (視神經膠質瘤) become symptomatic
* **Adolescence/Adulthood**: Cutaneous **neurofibromas** (神經纖維瘤) multiply rapidly

### Systemic Presentation
* Presenting complaint often cosmetic (skin lesions)
* May present with progressive scoliosis
* May present with specific learning delays

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Diagnostic Criteria (NIH) for Neurofibromatosis Type 1
Requires **≥2** of the following 7 criteria:
* **Café-au-lait macules** (咖啡牛奶斑): 
  * ≥6 spots
  * >5mm in prepubertal
  * >15mm in postpubertal
* **Neurofibromas** (神經纖維瘤): 
  * ≥2 of any type
  * OR 1 **plexiform neurofibroma** (叢狀神經纖維瘤) (palpates like a "bag of worms")
* **Freckling** (雀斑): 
  * Axillary region (Crowe sign)
  * Inguinal region
* **Optic pathway glioma** (OPG) (視神經膠質瘤)
* **Lisch nodules** (虹膜錯構瘤): 
  * ≥2 nodules
  * Iris hamartomas confirmed via slit-lamp
* **Distinctive osseous lesions**: 
  * Sphenoid dysplasia (蝶骨發育不良)
  * Tibial pseudoarthrosis (脛骨假關節) (anterolateral bowing)
* **Family History**: 
  * First-degree relative with diagnosed NF1

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Table of Discriminatory Features
| Condition | Differentiating Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Legius Syndrome** (雷吉斯症候群) | +ve Café-au-lait spots<br>+ve Freckling<br>**NO** neurofibromas<br>**NO** Lisch nodules<br>**NO** CNS tumours<br>SPRED1 gene mutation |
| **McCune-Albright Syndrome** (麥庫恩-奧爾布賴特症候群) | Large irregular Café-au-lait spots ("coast of Maine")<br>Polyostotic fibrous dysplasia<br>Precocious puberty |
| **Tuberous Sclerosis** (結節性硬化症) | Ash-leaf macules<br>Shagreen patches<br>Facial angiofibromas<br>Epilepsy |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Most Likely Diagnoses
* **Neurofibromatosis Type 1** (if NIH criteria strictly met)
* **Legius Syndrome** (if only pigmentary changes present)

### Must-Not-Miss Diagnoses
* **Optic pathway glioma** (視神經膠質瘤) → painless progressive visual loss
* **Malignant Peripheral Nerve Sheath Tumour** (MPNST) (惡性周邊神經鞘瘤) → rapid growth in pre-existing plexiform neurofibroma
* **Phaeochromocytoma** (嗜鉻細胞瘤) → severe malignant hypertension

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Clinical Investigations
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Primary diagnosis relies on NIH criteria
* **Ophthalmology Assessment**: 
  * Slit-lamp examination (for Lisch nodules)
  * Visual field testing (for OPG)
  * Visual acuity testing
* **Blood Pressure Measurement**: 
  * Annual screening mandatory
  * Detects renal artery stenosis
  * Detects phaeochromocytoma
* **Growth Charting**: 
  * Plot height
  * Plot weight
  * Plot head circumference (macrocephaly common)

### Blood Investigations
* **Routine Bloods**: Not required for primary diagnosis
* **Plasma metanephrines** (血漿變腎上腺素): Indicated ONLY if hypertension present

### Imaging Investigations
* **MRI Brain/Orbits**: 
  * Not for routine screening
  * Indicated for visual symptoms
  * Indicated for precocious puberty
  * Indicated for new neurological signs
  * Identifies OPG or Unidentified Bright Objects (UBOs)
* **X-ray / MRI Spine**: 
  * Indicated for suspected scoliosis

### Gold Standard Special Investigations
* **Genetic Testing**: 
  * NF1 gene sequencing
  * Confirms diagnosis in atypical presentations
  * Facilitates genetic counselling

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
* **Hypertensive Crisis**: 
  * Immediate α-blockade
  * Subsequent β-blockade if phaeochromocytoma confirmed
* **Raised Intracranial Pressure**: 
  * Urgent neurosurgical referral
  * Ventriculoperitoneal shunting if CNS tumour obstructs CSF

### Chronic & Definitive Management
* **Active Surveillance**: 
  * Annual HA multidisciplinary review
  * Monitor visual acuity
  * Monitor spinal curvature
  * Monitor blood pressure
* **Surgical Interventions**:
  * Cosmetic excision of disfiguring neurofibromas
  * Spinal fusion for severe scoliosis
  * Amputation/grafting for severe tibial pseudoarthrosis

### Hong Kong HA Specific Protocols
* **MEK Inhibitors** (e.g., **Selumetinib**): 
  * Indicated for inoperable symptomatic plexiform neurofibromas
  * High-cost drug
  * Requires application via Samaritan Fund or Community Care Fund (CCF)

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Interventions
* **Clinical Geneticist**: 
  * Confirms familial inheritance
  * Counsels regarding recurrence risks
* **Educational Psychologist**: 
  * Assesses specific learning disabilities (present in ~50%)
  * Assesses ADHD (present in ~40%)
  * Formulates Individualised Education Programmes (IEPs)
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: 
  * Assists with Disability Allowance applications
  * Facilitates CCF funding for targeted therapies
* **Clinical Psychologist**: 
  * Manages body image distress
  * Provides coping strategies for chronic disease burden

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Genetic Safeguarding & Counselling
* **Recurrence Risk**: 50% transmission risk to offspring
* **De Novo Verification**: Must clinically examine both parents before confirming de novo mutation
* **Reproductive Options**: Prenatal diagnosis or Preimplantation Genetic Diagnosis (PGD) available via HA Clinical Genetic Service

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Red Flags
* Tibial pseudoarthrosis causes spontaneous fractures after trivial trauma
* High susceptibility to bruising
* Strict exclusion of NAI required if trauma history remains inconsistent with NF1 skeletal pathology

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Neurological Complications
* **Epilepsy** (癲癇)
* **Specific Learning Disabilities** (特殊學習障礙)
* **Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder** (ADHD)

### Oncological Complications
* **Malignant Peripheral Nerve Sheath Tumour** (MPNST) ( lifetime risk ~8-13%)
* **Optic Pathway Glioma** (OPG)
* ↑ risk of Juvenile Myelomonocytic Leukaemia (JMML)
* ↑ risk of Breast Cancer (in adults)

### Orthopaedic Complications
* **Dystrophic Scoliosis** (脊柱側彎) (rapidly progressive)
* **Tibial pseudoarthrosis** (脛骨假關節)
* Sphenoid wing dysplasia

### Endocrine & Cardiovascular Complications
* **Precocious puberty** (性早熟) (2° to optic chiasm involvement)
* **Renal artery stenosis** (腎動脈狹窄) → Hypertension
* **Phaeochromocytoma** (嗜鉻細胞瘤) → Hypertension

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Assuming all café-au-lait spots indicate an immediate NF1 diagnosis.
* Pearl: Small numbers of spots are common in healthy individuals; formal diagnosis strictly requires ≥6 spots of validated sizes alongside secondary criteria.
* Viva Question: What specific bedside clinical assessments must you perform on a child with suspected NF1?
* Answer: Measure blood pressure to exclude renal artery stenosis, examine the spine for scoliosis, and request a slit-lamp examination for Lisch nodules.
* Pitfall: Ordering a routine screening MRI Brain for all asymptomatic newly diagnosed NF1 patients. This generates unnecessary anxiety regarding benign Unidentified Bright Objects (UBOs) and should only be ordered if focal neurological or visual symptoms arise.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate Legius Syndrome from Neurofibromatosis Type 1?
* Answer: Legius Syndrome presents with pigmentary changes but completely lacks neurofibromas, Lisch nodules, and central nervous system tumours.
* Trap: Recommending immediate surgical biopsy for a suspected Optic Pathway Glioma in an NF1 patient.
* Pearl: Optic Pathway Gliomas in NF1 are typically diagnosed via MRI alone; biopsy is contraindicated due to severe risks of irreversible visual damage.
* Viva Question: An adolescent with NF1 reports rapid growth and severe rest pain in a pre-existing plexiform neurofibroma. What is the most feared oncological complication?
* Answer: Malignant Peripheral Nerve Sheath Tumour.
