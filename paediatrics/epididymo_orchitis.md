# Epididymo-orchitis (附睾睾丸炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Epididymo-orchitis
* **Definition**: Acute inflammation of the **epididymis (附睾)** ± spreading to involve the **testis (睾丸)** (orchitis).
* **Pathology**: Swelling, hyperemia, and leukocytic infiltration of scrotal structures.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Bimodal Age Distribution**:
    * **Infants/Prepubertal**: Often 2° to **congenital urinary tract anomalies (先天性泌尿道異常)**.
    * **Postpubertal/Adolescents**: Strongly correlated with ↑ sexually transmitted infections (**STIs**) in local youth.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology in Infants & Prepubertal Boys
* **Mechanism**: Ascending bacterial infection from the urinary tract.
* **Organism**: Predominantly enteric Gram-negative bacilli → **E. coli (大腸桿菌)**.
* **Anatomical Link**: High association with structural anomalies e.g., **ectopic ureter (異位輸尿管)** inserting into vas deferens, **Posterior Urethral Valves (PUV; 後尿道瓣)**, or **neurogenic bladder (神經性膀胱)**.

### Pathophysiology in Postpubertal Adolescents
* **Mechanism**: Retrograde spread of pathogens from the urethra via the vas deferens.
* **Organisms**: Exclusively driven by STIs → **Chlamydia trachomatis (沙眼衣原體)** & **Neisseria gonorrhoeae (淋球菌)**.

### Pathophysiology of Viral Orchitis
* **Organism**: **Mumps virus (腮腺炎病毒)** (Paramyxovirus).
* **Epidemiology**: Now rare in HK due to routine **MMR vaccination (麻疹、流行性腮腺炎及德國麻疹混合疫苗)**.
* **Mechanism**: Hematogenous spread during systemic viremia.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Clinical Presentation of Epididymo-orchitis
* **Patient Profile**: Typically a sexually active adolescent male (e.g., 16yo) or a prepubertal boy with recurrent UTIs.
* **Onset**: Gradual onset (over days) of worsening unilateral **scrotal pain (陰囊痛)**.
* **Associated Symptoms**: Accompanied by **dysuria (排尿困難)**, urethral discharge, and low-grade **fever (發燒)**.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Key Scrotal Symptoms & Signs
* **Inspection**: Unilateral scrotal swelling, marked **erythema (紅斑)**, and overlying skin edema.
* **Palpation**: Exquisitely tender epididymis (posterior to testis) initially → progresses to diffuse tender hemiscrotum.
* **Prehn's Sign (普倫氏徵)**: **+ve** → Pain is partially relieved by manually elevating the affected testicle (relieves traction).
* **Cremasteric Reflex (提睾反射)**: **Intact/+ve** (crucial for differentiating from torsion).

### Key Systemic & Urological Symptoms
* **Infective signs**: **Fever (發燒)**, rigors, malaise.
* **Urological signs**: **Dysuria (排尿困難)**, urinary frequency, purulent/mucoid urethral discharge.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Discriminatory Features vs. Testicular Torsion
* **Onset**: Gradual (days) vs. Sudden/Acute (minutes/hours in torsion).
* **Infective Symptoms**: Present (fever, dysuria, discharge) vs. Absent (often presents with N/V in torsion).
* **Prehn's Sign**: **+ve** (pain relieved on elevation) vs. **-ve** (no relief or worsens in torsion).
* **Cremasteric Reflex**: **Intact** vs. **Absent** in torsion.
* **Doppler Ultrasound**: **↑ Hyperemia (extra blood flow)** vs. **Ischemia (no blood flow)** in torsion.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis of Acute Scrotum
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features | Doppler USG Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Testicular Torsion (睾丸扭轉)** | Sudden onset, -ve Prehn's, absent cremasteric reflex, severe N/V. Surgical emergency. | ↓ or absent blood flow |
| **Epididymo-orchitis (附睾睾丸炎)** | Gradual onset, +ve Prehn's, intact cremasteric reflex, fever/dysuria. | ↑ blood flow (hyperemia) |
| **Torsion of Appendix Testis (睾丸附件扭轉)** | Gradual onset, **"Blue dot" sign (藍點徵)** visible through skin, superior pole tenderness. | Normal testicular flow, ↑ flow at appendage |
| **Incarcerated Inguinal Hernia (嵌頓性腹股溝疝)** | Bowel sounds in scrotum, irreducible groin mass, signs of bowel obstruction. | Bowel loops in scrotum |
| **Scrotal Abscess (陰囊膿腫)** | Fluctuant mass, severe localized erythema, usually sequela of untreated epididymitis. | Loculated fluid collection |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations for Epididymo-orchitis
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: Dipstick +ve for **leukocyte esterase (白細胞酯酶)** & **nitrites (亞硝酸鹽)**.
* **First-void Urine**: For STI **Nucleic Acid Amplification Test (NAAT/PCR)** (Chlamydia & Gonorrhoea).

### Laboratory Blood Tests for Epididymo-orchitis
* **CBC**: ↑ WBC (neutrophilia).
* **Inflammatory markers**: ↑ **CRP (C-反應蛋白)** & ↑ **ESR (紅血球沉降率)**.
* **Blood Cultures**: If systemically toxic/septic.

### Imaging Modalities for Epididymo-orchitis
* **Gold Standard**: **Doppler Ultrasound of Scrotum (多普勒超聲)**.
* **Findings**: Enlarged epididymis/testis with striking **hypervascularity (血流增加)**. Conclusive rule-out of torsion.

### Special Post-Infection Imaging (Prepubertal Protocol)
* **Mandatory Step**: Prepubertal boys MUST receive further urinary tract imaging post-infection to rule out congenital anomalies.
* **Modalities**: **USG KUB (泌尿系統超聲波)** ± **MCUG (排尿期膀胱尿道造影)** to check for VUR/PUV.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Pharmacological Management (HA Protocols)
| Patient Group | Suspected Etiology | First-Line Empirical Therapy (HA Guidelines) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Prepubertal Boys** | Enteric flora (E. coli) | PO/IV **Co-amoxiclav (Augmentin)** or **Cefuroxime (頭孢呋辛)**. |
| **Postpubertal / STI** | N. gonorrhoeae & C. trachomatis | IM **Ceftriaxone (頭孢曲松)** (single dose) + PO **Doxycycline (多西環素)** x 14 days. |

### Supportive & Symptomatic Management
* **Bed rest & Scrotal elevation**: Use tight briefs/jockstrap to ↓ venous congestion.
* **Analgesia**: **NSAIDs (非類固醇消炎止痛藥)** (e.g., Ibuprofen) & Paracetamol.
* **Cold Compresses**: Ice packs to ↓ localized swelling.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team & STI Counselling
* **Adolescent Health**: Refer to **Social Hygiene Clinic (社會衛生科診所)** for comprehensive STI screening (HIV, Syphilis, HBV).
* **Contact Tracing**: Mandatory confidential **partner notification (伴侶通知)** & treatment to prevent reinfection.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Child Protection Red Flags in Epididymo-orchitis
* **Absolute Red Flag**: Detecting an STI (Chlamydia/Gonorrhea) in any **prepubertal child**.
* **Action**: Immediate suspicion of **Child Sexual Abuse (兒童性虐待)**.
* **Protocol**: Urgent admission, strict multi-agency involvement → **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**, **Social Welfare Department (SWD; 社會福利署)**, and Police **Child Protection Policy Unit (CPPU; 保護兒童課)**.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Complications of Epididymo-orchitis
* **Scrotal Abscess (陰囊膿腫)**: Requires urgent surgical drainage.
* **Testicular Infarction**: Secondary to severe epididymal swelling compressing testicular blood supply.

### Long-term Prognosis and Sequelae
* **Chronic Scarring**: Can lead to epididymal duct obstruction.
* **Infertility (不育)**: Bilateral involvement or untreated chronic scarring ↑ risk of oligozoospermia.
* **Mumps Orchitis Sequela**: Extremely high rate of **bilateral testicular atrophy (睾丸萎縮)** & subsequent sterility if acquired post-puberty.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Treating an 18-year-old for epididymo-orchitis with Ciprofloxacin without running an STI screen.
* Pearl: Gonorrhea is highly resistant to fluoroquinolones globally; you MUST cover using IM Ceftriaxone for STIs.
* Viva Question: "A 6-year-old boy develops true E. coli epididymitis. What follow-up imaging is absolutely mandatory once the infection clears?"
* Answer: A renal and bladder ultrasound (USG KUB) and potentially an MCUG, because prepubertal epididymitis is very frequently caused by a major underlying anatomical abnormality (e.g., ectopic ureter, PUV).
* Pitfall: Relying solely on clinical signs to rule out testicular torsion in a distressed child. Always order an urgent scrotal Doppler ultrasound if there is any diagnostic ambiguity, as missing torsion leads to irreversible testicular loss within 6-8 hours.
* Viva Question: "Which physical sign distinguishes Epididymo-orchitis from Testicular Torsion when elevating the scrotum?"
* Answer: Prehn's Sign. It is positive (pain relief) in Epididymo-orchitis and negative (no relief or exacerbation) in Testicular Torsion.
