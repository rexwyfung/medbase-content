# Gillick Competence & Fraser Guidelines (吉利克勝任能力與弗雷澤準則)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Gillick Competence (吉利克勝任能力)**: Common law principle dictating a minor (<18 yrs) can independently consent to medical treatment IF they possess sufficient maturity, intelligence, and understanding of the proposed intervention.
* **Fraser Guidelines (弗雷澤準則)**: Specific legal criteria enabling doctors to provide contraception and sexual health advice to minors WITHOUT parental consent or knowledge.

### Local Epidemiology & HK Context
* **Age of Majority (法定成年歲數)**: 18 yrs in HK (Age of Majority Ordinance).
* **Age of Sexual Consent (合法性行為年齡)**: 16 yrs in HK (Crimes Ordinance).
* ↑ Rates of adolescent sexual activity necessitate strict adherence to these principles by **Hospital Authority (HA)** and **Family Planning Association HK (FPAHK)** to balance **Patient Confidentiality (病人保密原則)** vs **Child Protection (保護兒童)**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Legal Pathophysiology (Origins of the Law)
* Derived from UK House of Lords ruling (*Gillick v West Norfolk & Wisbech AHA [1986]*).
* HK adopts UK common law precedents → forms foundation of **Medical Council of Hong Kong (MCHK) (香港醫務委員會)** ethical guidelines on treating minors.
* Principle: Parental rights exist primarily to protect the child and yield to the child's right to self-determination when capacity is acquired.

### Neurodevelopmental Maturation
* ↑ Maturation of prefrontal cortex during adolescence.
* → ↑ Abstract reasoning.
* → ↑ Risk assessment capability.
* → ↑ Executive function.
* Competence is **decision-specific (特定決定能力)** → fluid across developmental stages; a 14-yr-old may comprehend antibiotics but not complex transplant surgery.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Clinical Signs of Competence
* **+ve** Clear understanding of the medical condition.
* **+ve** Comprehension of proposed treatment nature.
* **+ve** Accurate appraisal of risks & side effects.
* **+ve** Recognition of alternative treatments.
* **+ve** Appreciation of consequences of refusal/inaction.

### Clinical Signs of Incompetence
* **-ve** Grasp of long-term consequences.
* **+ve** Evidence of coercion by peers, partners, or adults (Undue Influence).
* **+ve** Fluctuating, irrational decisions driven by acute emotional lability.
* **+ve** Presence of severe psychiatric illness clouding judgment (e.g., Anorexia Nervosa).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Gillick vs Fraser
| Feature | **Gillick Competence (吉利克勝任能力)** | **Fraser Guidelines (弗雷澤準則)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scope** | General medical & surgical treatments | Strictly contraception & sexual health advice |
| **Criteria** | General intelligence & understanding | 5 strict, specific criteria |
| **Application** | Any age <18 | Usually adolescents seeking reproductive care |

### Differentiating Consent vs Refusal
| Action | Legal Status for Competent Minor |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Consent (同意)** | Legally valid and binding |
| **Refusal (拒絕)** | Can be **OVERRIDDEN (推翻)** by parents or courts in child's best interests (e.g., life-saving blood transfusion for Jehovah's Witness) |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Legal Statuses
| Legal Status | Clinical Definition | Consent Authority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Non-competent Minor** | Lacks capacity/maturity | Parents / Legal Guardians |
| **Gillick Competent Minor** | Has capacity for specific decision | Minor (for consent); Parents/Court (can override refusal) |
| **Emancipated Minor** | Legally independent (rare in HK medical context) | Minor |
| **Ward of Court (法庭受監護人)** | Court assumes ultimate guardianship | High Court of HK |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Tier 1 Assessment for General Competence
* Step 1: Assess cognitive ability to understand diagnosis.
* Step 2: Assess understanding of proposed intervention (nature, risks, benefits).
* Step 3: Assess understanding of consequences of inaction.
* Step 4: Verify absolute absence of coercion.

### Tier 2 Assessment for Fraser Criteria
* Doctor MUST satisfy **ALL 5 CRITERIA** to prescribe contraception without parental knowledge:
* 1: Minor fully understands doctor's advice.
* 2: Doctor cannot persuade minor to inform parents (or allow doctor to inform them).
* 3: Minor is very likely to begin/continue sexual intercourse regardless of treatment.
* 4: Minor's physical/mental health is likely to suffer without treatment.
* 5: Intervention is strictly in the minor's best interests.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Emergency Management
* Life-saving emergency (e.g., major trauma, acute abdomen) → Treat immediately under **Doctrine of Necessity (必要性原則)**.
* Do NOT delay life-saving treatment to locate parents or assess competence.

### Chronic Elective Management
* Non-urgent care → Meticulous documentation of competence assessment in **Clinical Management System (CMS) (臨床醫療管理系統)**.
* Actively encourage parental involvement.
* Document all attempts made to persuade minor to disclose to parents.

### HA & Legal Protocols
* **Confidentiality (保密原則)**: Competent minors have adult-level confidentiality rights.
* **Breach of Confidentiality**: Justified ONLY if significant risk of serious harm exists (e.g., suicide risk, severe abuse, NAI).
* **Under 16 Contraception**: Must assess Fraser + Mandatory assessment for statutory rape/abuse.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Roles
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Crucial for complex family dynamics, child protection assessments, and welfare support.
* **Clinical Psychologist/Psychiatrist (臨床心理學家/精神科醫生)**: For assessing complex capacity or underlying mental health disorders clouding competence.

### Psychosocial Support
* Provide a confidential, non-judgmental safe space for adolescent.
* Facilitate de-escalation of parent-child conflicts.
* Offer counseling for peer pressure and relationship dynamics.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding & Statutory Rape
* Legal age of consent = 16 yrs.
* Sexual intercourse <16 is a **Criminal Offence (刑事罪行)** in HK.
* Presentation for contraception/STI <16 yrs → MANDATORY assessment for exploitation/abuse.
* Suspicion of abuse/exploitation → **Breach confidentiality** → Mandatory referral to MSW and **HK Police Force Child Abuse Investigation Unit (香港警務處虐兒案件調查組)**.

### NAI Red Flags
* Large age gap between minor and sexual partner.
* Evidence of transactional sex / **Compensated Dating (援交)**.
* Fearful, withdrawn behavior.
* Overly controlling partner or accompanying adult.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Legal & Clinical Complications
* **Battery/Assault (毆打/襲擊)**: Treating non-competent minor without parental consent.
* **Negligence (疏忽)**: Withholding indicated treatment from a competent minor.
* **MCHK Disciplinary Action (醫委會紀律處分)**: Unjustified breach of confidentiality.
* **Missed Safeguarding**: Continued sexual exploitation or physical abuse.

### Prognosis & Future Capacity
* Competence naturally ↑ with chronological age and cognitive maturation.
* Capacity is dynamic → Must strictly re-evaluate competence at EACH clinical encounter (pain, sepsis, hypoxia can acutely ↓ capacity).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: A 15-year-old girl requests the oral contraceptive pill. She explicitly asks you not to tell her parents. Under what guidelines can you prescribe this, and what are the criteria? Answer: The Fraser Guidelines. You must assess if she understands the advice, cannot be persuaded to tell her parents, is likely to have sex anyway, her physical/mental health will suffer without it, and it is in her best interests.
* Viva Question: A 14-year-old boy is deemed Gillick competent. He has acute appendicitis but refuses surgery because he is scared of scars. Can you operate? Answer: Yes. While he is competent to consent, a competent minor's refusal of life-saving or necessary treatment can be overridden by the parents or the courts in the child's best interests.
* Trap: Assuming that Fraser Guidelines apply to all medical treatments for minors. Pearl: Fraser Guidelines strictly apply ONLY to contraception and sexual health advice. Gillick Competence applies to general medical and surgical treatments.
* Pitfall: Assuming absolute confidentiality for Gillick competent minors <16 engaging in sexual activity. Pearl: In HK, the legal age of consent is 16; any suspicion of statutory rape, exploitation, or abuse MANDATES breaching confidentiality to involve the Medical Social Worker (MSW) and Police.
