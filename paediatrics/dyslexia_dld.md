# Dyslexia & Developmental Language Disorder (DLD) (讀寫障礙及發展性語言障礙)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Dyslexia (讀寫障礙)
* **Specific Learning Difficulty in Reading/Spelling**: Unexpected, persistent ↓ in acquiring fluent reading, writing & spelling skills.
* Occurs despite **normal intelligence**, adequate schooling & normal vision/hearing.

### Clinical Definition of Developmental Language Disorder (DLD) (發展性語言障礙)
* Persistent ↓ in understanding (receptive) or using (expressive) spoken language.
* **Exclusionary criteria**: Not caused by known neurological disorders, hearing impairment, or **Global Developmental Delay (整體發展遲緩)**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (香港流行病學)
* **Dyslexia**: Highly prevalent (affects **~10%** of HK children).
* **Unique HK Context**: Reading traditional Chinese characters demands **dual processing** (visual-orthographic + phonological). Deficits in either → significant literacy struggle.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Dyslexia
* **Phonological Processing Deficit**: Brain fails to match graphemes (letters/strokes) to phonemes (sounds) rapidly.
* **Neuroimaging**: Functional MRI reveals **under-activation** in **left parieto-temporal** reading pathways during decoding tasks.

### Pathophysiology of Developmental Language Disorder (DLD)
* **Neurodevelopmental Delay**: Immaturity in brain's language centers (**Broca's/Wernicke's networks**).
* Affects linguistic domains: **Syntax** (grammar), **Semantics** (vocabulary), & **Morphology** (word structures).

### Genetic Factors in Learning Disorders
* Strong familial clustering in both conditions.
* **Dyslexia**: Polygenic inheritance (associated loci on chromosomes 6 & 15). +ve family history is a major risk factor.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms and Signs of Dyslexia
* **Poor Phonemic Awareness**: ↓ ability to rhyme or manipulate syllables.
* **Reading (Decoding) Deficits**: Slow, halting reading; skips words; letter reversals (e.g., 'b' for 'd').
* **Writing (Encoding) Deficits**: Highly phonetic but incorrect spelling; poor character spatial arrangement in Chinese.
* **Cognition**: **Normal** verbal intelligence & non-verbal cognitive ability.

### Key Symptoms and Signs of Expressive DLD
* ↓ Vocabulary size for age.
* Excessive grammatical errors beyond typical age norms.
* Struggle to formulate sentences or narrate events sequentially.
* Frequent omission of verbs or connecting words.

### Key Symptoms and Signs of Receptive DLD
* Misunderstanding questions or complex instructions.
* Difficulty grasping complex grammar (e.g., Passive voice: "The boy was chased by the dog" → child incorrectly assumes boy chased dog).
* Normal social intent but responses seem inappropriate due to misunderstanding.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features: Dyslexia & DLD vs. Global Developmental Delay
* **Non-Verbal IQ (NVIQ)**: NVIQ (pattern recognition, visual-spatial puzzles) is **normal/high** in Dyslexia/DLD, but **<70** in Global Developmental Delay (GDD).
* **Domain Specificity**: Dyslexia/DLD deficits are strictly confined to language/reading; GDD involves pervasive delays (motor, cognitive, ADL).

### Discriminatory Features: DLD vs. Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
* **Social Intent**: DLD patients desire social interaction and use non-verbal cues (pointing, eye contact) to compensate.
* **Pragmatics**: ASD (自閉症譜系障礙) features profound deficits in social pragmatics (turn-taking, maintaining context), whereas DLD primarily involves grammar/vocabulary deficits.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Dyslexia and DLD

| Differential Diagnosis | Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Untreated Hearing Loss (聽力受損)** | Strongly mimics DLD (especially chronic Otitis Media with Effusion / Glue Ear). Requires audiometry. |
| **Visual Impairment (視力障礙)** | Refractive errors or strabismus mimic Dyslexia reading errors. Requires visual screening. |
| **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)** | Poor social pragmatics, repetitive behaviors, ↓ eye contact. |
| **Global Developmental Delay (GDD)** | ↓ IQ across all domains (cognitive, motor, language). |
| **ADHD (專注力不足/過度活躍症)** | Inattention/impulsivity primary. Note: **Highly co-morbid** with Dyslexia. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Mandatory Bedside & Clinical Investigations
* **Formal Audiometry**: Mandatory 1st step to rule out sensorineural/conductive hearing loss in all language delays.
* **Vision Screening**: Snellen chart / autorefraction to rule out visual acuity deficits.

### Specialist Allied Health Assessments in Hong Kong
* **Educational Psychologist (EP) Assessment**: 
    * Standardized IQ tests (e.g., **HK-WISC**) to confirm normal NVIQ.
    * Specific literacy/phonological testing (e.g., **HKT-SpLD** for Hong Kong primary school children).
* **Speech & Language Therapy (ST) Assessment**:
    * Standardized tests (e.g., **Reynell Developmental Language Scales**) to measure Receptive & Expressive Language Quotients against age norms.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Chronic Management of Dyslexia (Education Bureau / EDB Protocols)
* **Remediation**: Specific Structured Phonics Interventions (Multisensory reading programs).
* **School Accommodations (Legally mandated via EDB)**:
    * **+25% extra time** in examinations.
    * Enlarged font size & tailored exam formatting.
    * Use of text-to-speech software.
    * Waiver of spelling penalties.

### Chronic Management of DLD
* Intensive, play-based early **Speech & Language Therapy (ST)**.
* **Classroom Strategies**: Optimal seating (front of class), chunking instructions into single steps, using visual aids.

### Non-Evidence-Based Treatments to Avoid
* Colored overlays / tinted lenses (No proven benefit for core decoding).
* Omega-3 / Fish oil supplements (Lacks robust clinical evidence).
* Ocular motor tracking exercises.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Referrals in Hong Kong
* **Maternal & Child Health Centre (MCHC)**: First-line developmental surveillance.
* **Child Assessment Centre (CAC)**: Diagnostic multidisciplinary formulation (Developmental Pediatrician, CP, EP, ST).
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) / SEN Coordinator (SENCO)**: Bridging HA clinics with school-based support.

### Psychosocial Impact and Support
* **Immense Psychosocial Burden**: Undiagnosed children often labeled "lazy/stupid" → severe school refusal, anxiety, ↓ self-esteem.
* **Secondary Behavioral Issues**: Frustration from inability to communicate (DLD) or academic failure (Dyslexia) manifests as acting out.
* **Intervention**: Self-esteem counseling, parental psychoeducation to reframe expectations.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Child Protection Considerations in Learning Disorders
* **Neglect**: Parental refusal to provide hearing aids, glasses, or attend mandatory ST/EP sessions despite repeated counseling.
* **Physical/Emotional Abuse**: Unrealistic academic expectations in HK culture leading to physical punishment for "poor grades" which are actually manifestations of an undiagnosed learning disorder.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-Term Prognosis of Dyslexia
* **Persistent Trait**: Continues into adulthood.
* **Compensation**: Excellent functional outcomes if accommodated early (assistive tech, career matching).

### Long-Term Complications of DLD
* **Academic Failure**: Poor foundation for reading comprehension & written tasks.
* **Social Isolation**: Difficulty maintaining peer relationships due to communication barriers.
* **Psychiatric Co-morbidity**: ↑ risk of anxiety, depression, and behavioral disorders if expressive frustration is unmanaged.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Telling a parent "he is a late bloomer, boys just talk late" for an isolated language delay at age 3.
* Pearl: While some boys talk later, DLD is a distinct pathology. If a child has no single words by 18 months, or no 2-word phrases by 24 months, urgent audiology and ST referral is mandatory.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing dyslexia purely based on poor academic performance. Ensure normal NVIQ and adequate educational exposure before confirming specific learning difficulty; always rule out visual/hearing impairment first.
* Viva Question: A child reads poorly but has normal intelligence. A teacher suggests using colored overlays over text to cure his visual dyslexia. What is the evidence-based consensus?
* Answer: Dyslexia is primarily a phonological (auditory-linguistic) processing defect, not a visual one. Multi-sensory structured language teaching (phonics) is the gold standard, while colored overlays have not been proven to treat the core decoding deficit of classical dyslexia.
