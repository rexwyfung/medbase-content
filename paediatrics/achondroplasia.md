# Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Achondroplasia and Thanatophoric Dysplasia
* **Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全)**: Most common form of short-limbed (rhizomelic) dwarfism.
* **Thanatophoric Dysplasia (致死性骨發育不全)**: Related, severe, universally lethal skeletal dysplasia.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Incidence**: ~1 in 20,000–30,000 live births globally & locally. 
* **Clinical Burden**: Accounts for majority of disproportionate short stature referrals to HA Paediatric Endocrine/Clinical Genetics clinics.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Basis of Achondroplasia
* **Inheritance**: Autosomal Dominant (AD).
* **De Novo Rate**: ~80% *de novo* mutations (universally correlated with advanced paternal age).
* **Gene Mutation**: *FGFR3* (Fibroblast Growth Factor Receptor 3) gene on chromosome 4.

### Pathophysiological Mechanism of Achondroplasia
* **Cellular Effect**: Gain-of-function mutation → constitutively active FGFR3 → severely inhibits chondrocyte proliferation/hypertrophy at the growth plate.
* **Bone Development**: ↓ Endochondral ossification (軟骨內骨化) → short, thick long bones.
* **Skull Development**: Normal membranous ossification → skull vault unaffected → relative Macrocephaly (大頭).

### Pathophysiology of Thanatophoric Dysplasia
* **Genetic Variant**: Distinct, more severe *FGFR3* missense mutations. Usually sporadic.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Antenatal Presentation
* **Imaging Findings (MCHC / HA O&G)**: 3rd-trimester US → shortened femur length (FL), frontal bossing, polyhydramnios.
* **Thanatophoric Variants**: Extreme micromelia, narrow chest, "cloverleaf" skull.

### Postnatal Presentation
* **Growth Manifestation**: Extreme disproportionate short stature at birth.
* **Monitoring Protocol**: Growth must be tracked strictly on condition-specific Achondroplasia Growth Charts (not standard HK Dept of Health charts).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Physical Signs of Achondroplasia
* **Extremities**: Rhizomelic (proximal) limb shortening.
* **Facial Features**: Frontal bossing (前額突出), depressed nasal bridge & Midface hypoplasia (中面部發育不全).
* **Hands**: "Trident" hands (三叉手) (short, stubby fingers, ↑ gap between 3rd/4th digits).
* **Spine**: Thoracolumbar kyphosis (胸腰椎後凸) in infancy → Hyperlordosis (過度前凸) in childhood.
* **Lower Limbs**: Genu varum (O型腿/弓形腿).
* **Cognition**: Normal intelligence.

### Physical Signs of Thanatophoric Dysplasia
* **Extremities**: Extreme micromelia.
* **Thorax**: Narrow, bell-shaped thorax (鐘形胸) → severe Pulmonary hypoplasia (肺發育不全).
* **Skull**: Cloverleaf skull (三葉草形頭骨) (*Kleeblattschädel*) in Type II.
* **Clinical Outcome**: Death shortly after birth (respiratory failure).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differential Features Table: Short-Limbed Dysplasias
| Feature | Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全) | Thanatophoric Dysplasia (致死性骨發育不全) | Hypochondroplasia (軟骨發育低下) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Limb Shortening** | Rhizomelic (Proximal) | Extreme Micromelia | Mild, often generalized |
| **Thorax** | Normal | Narrow / Bell-shaped | Normal |
| **Skull** | Frontal bossing | Cloverleaf (Type II) | Normal or mild bossing |
| **Onset of Dx** | Antenatal / Birth | Antenatal | Late childhood |
| **Prognosis** | Normal lifespan | Lethal | Normal lifespan |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Key Differentials for Disproportionate Short Stature
* **Hypochondroplasia (軟骨發育低下)**: Milder *FGFR3* mutation.
* **Osteogenesis Imperfecta (成骨不全症)**: Severe types (II/III) present with short stature & bowing secondary to multiple in-utero fractures.
* **Pseudoachondroplasia (假性軟骨發育不全)**: Normal facies/head size, delayed onset (toddler years).
* **Diastrophic Dysplasia (骨畸形性發育不良)**: Short stature + clubfeet, hitchhiker thumbs, cauliflower ears.

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Clinical Investigations
* **Growth Surveillance**: Serial Occipitofrontal Circumference (OFC) tracking → screen for Hydrocephalus (腦積水).
* **Respiratory Screening**: Pulse oximetry / overnight sleep monitoring → screen for central/obstructive apnoea.

### Radiological Investigations
* **Skeletal Survey (骨骼普查)**: ↓ interpedicular distance in lumbar spine (L1-L5), squared iliac wings, "champagne glass" pelvic inlet, short/thick tubular bones.
* **Neuroimaging (Cranial US / MRI Brain & C-Spine)**: Mandatory to screen for Foramen magnum stenosis (枕骨大孔狹窄) and Hydrocephalus (腦積水).

### Laboratory & Genetic Investigations
* **Molecular Diagnosis**: Targeted *FGFR3* genetic testing (via HK Clinical Genetic Service, HKCH).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Neurological Management
* **Surgical Decompression**: Urgent neurosurgical decompression if +ve signs of foramen magnum compression (central sleep apnoea, hyperreflexia, sudden hypotonia).
* **CSF Diversion**: Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt (腦室腹腔分流術) if +ve progressive hydrocephalus.

### Orthopaedic Management
* **Spinal Monitoring**: Monitor progressive thoracolumbar kyphosis (HA orthopaedic protocols, e.g., DKCH).
* **Corrective Surgery**: Osteotomies (截骨術) for severe genu varum (usually delayed until adolescence).
* **Limb Lengthening (肢體延長術)**: Controversial, high complication rate, rarely routinely recommended in HK public sector.

### ENT & Respiratory Management
* **Sleep Assessment**: Regular polysomnography (PSG) → rule out Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA) (阻塞性睡眠窒息症).
* **Airway Interventions**: Adenotonsillectomy (腺樣體扁桃體切除術) or CPAP (持續正壓呼吸機) for OSA.
* **Otological Interventions**: Tympanostomy tubes (耳咽管) for recurrent Acute Otitis Media (AOM) to prevent conductive hearing loss.

### Novel Medical Therapies
* **Vosoritide (伏索利肽)**: Daily SC CNP analog. Bypasses FGFR3 block → ↑ height velocity. Emerging therapy (Requires HA compassionate use / self-finance via Samaritan Fund evaluations).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Community & Allied Health Support
* **MCHC (母嬰健康院) Role**: Strict developmental and OFC surveillance.
* **Clinical Psychology / MSW (醫務社工)**: Coping strategies for physical differences, integration into mainstream schools vs. physical bullying. Assess eligibility for Disability Allowance (傷殘津貼) via SWD.
* **Occupational Therapy (職業治療)**: Environmental mods (step stools, modified school desks/toilets, reachers) to foster independence.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Critical Infantile Handling Precautions
* **Cervical Spine Vulnerability**: Foramen magnum stenosis is a silent killer in infancy.
* **Equipment Contraindications**: Infants MUST NEVER be placed in unsupportive "umbrella strollers" (傘車) or soft carriers. 
* **Fatal Mechanism**: Lack of head support → sudden backward head jerk → fatal Cervical cord crush (頸髓壓迫).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Long-Term Complications of Achondroplasia
* **Neurological**: Spinal Stenosis (椎管狹窄) in adulthood → neurogenic claudication / paraparesis.
* **Respiratory**: Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA) (阻塞性睡眠窒息症).
* **Metabolic**: Obesity (肥胖) → caloric requirement matches height (low) but appetite matches age (high). Aggressive dietetic input needed.
* **Audiological**: Recurrent otitis media → Conductive hearing loss (傳導性聽力受損) → speech delay.

### Prognosis Summary
* **Achondroplasia**: Normal lifespan & intellect (if early cervicomedullary compression avoided).
* **Thanatophoric Dysplasia**: Universally fatal.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Misdiagnosing benign macrocephaly as hydrocephalus. Pearl: Plagiocephaly & macrocephaly are expected in Achondroplasia; TRUE Hydrocephalus (腦積水) requires imaging confirmation (dilated ventricles on US/MRI) before shunting.
* Viva Question: Why is paternal age relevant in Achondroplasia? Answer: ~80% of cases are de novo mutations. These mutations arise almost exclusively in the paternal allele during spermatogenesis and have a strong, proven correlation with advanced paternal age.
* Pitfall: Missing subtle signs of cord compression in infants. ALWAYS ask about central apnoeas, poor feeding, or check for unusually brisk reflexes/clonus in an Achondroplastic infant to avoid missing fatal cervicomedullary compression.
