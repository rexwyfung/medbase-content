## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
* **Definition**: 
    * **Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全)**: Most common form of short-limbed (rhizomelic) dwarfism.
    * **Thanatophoric Dysplasia (致死性骨發育不全)**: Related, severe, universally lethal skeletal dysplasia.
* **Local Epidemiology (HK)**: 
    * Incidence: ~1 in 20,000–30,000 live births globally & locally. 
    * Accounts for majority of disproportionate short stature referrals to HA Paediatric Endocrine/Genetics clinics.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
* **Genetics**: Autosomal Dominant (AD). ~80% de novo mutations (universally correlated with **advanced paternal age**).
* **Gene**: **FGFR3 (Fibroblast Growth Factor Receptor 3)** on chromosome 4.
* **Pathophysiology**: 
    * Gain-of-function mutation → constitutively active FGFR3 → severely inhibits chondrocyte proliferation/hypertrophy.
    * ↓ **Endochondral ossification (軟骨內骨化)** → short, thick long bones.
    * Normal membranous ossification → skull vault unaffected → relative **Macrocephaly (大頭)**.
* **Thanatophoric**: Distinct, more severe *FGFR3* missense mutations.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
* **Antenatal (MCHC / HA O&G)**: 
    * 3rd-trimester US → shortened femur length (FL), frontal bossing, polyhydramnios.
    * Thanatophoric → extreme micromelia, narrow chest, "cloverleaf" skull.
* **Postnatal**: 
    * Extreme disproportionate short stature.
    * Growth must be tracked strictly on condition-specific **Achondroplasia Growth Charts** (not standard HK Dept of Health charts).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
* **Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全)**:
    * Rhizomelic (proximal) limb shortening
    * **Frontal bossing (前額突出)** & Midface hypoplasia (中面部發育不全)
    * **"Trident" hands (三叉手)** (short, stubby fingers, gap between 3rd/4th digits)
    * Thoracolumbar kyphosis (胸腰椎後凸) in infancy → Hyperlordosis (過度前凸) in childhood
    * Genu varum (O型腿/弓形腿)
    * Normal intelligence
    ![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/2d7eb731534b25c8129c10d046c1abd0.png)
* **Thanatophoric Dysplasia (致死性骨發育不全)**:
    * Extreme micromelia
    * Narrow, bell-shaped thorax (鐘形胸) → severe **Pulmonary hypoplasia (肺發育不全)**
    * **Cloverleaf skull (三葉草形頭骨)** (Kleeblattschädel) in Type II
    * Death shortly after birth (respiratory failure)

## 5. Discriminatory Features
| Feature             | Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全) | Thanatophoric Dysplasia (致死性骨發育不全) | Hypochondroplasia (軟骨發育低下) |
| :------------------ | :---------------------- | :--------------------------------- | :------------------------- |
| **Limb Shortening** | Rhizomelic (Proximal)   | Extreme Micromelia                 | Mild, often generalized    |
| **Thorax**          | Normal                  | Narrow / Bell-shaped               | Normal                     |
| **Skull**           | Frontal bossing         | Cloverleaf (Type II)               | Normal or mild bossing     |
| **Onset of Dx**     | Antenatal / Birth       | Antenatal                          | Late childhood             |
| **Prognosis**       | Normal lifespan         | Lethal                             | Normal lifespan            |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
* **Hypochondroplasia (軟骨發育低下)**
* **Osteogenesis Imperfecta (成骨不全症)**
* **Pseudoachondroplasia (假性軟骨發育不全)**
* **Diastrophic Dysplasia (骨畸形性發育不良)**

## 7. Tiered Investigations
* **Bedside**:
    * Serial Occipitofrontal Circumference (OFC) tracking → screen for hydrocephalus
    * Pulse oximetry / Sleep monitoring → screen for apnoea
* **Imaging**:
    * **Skeletal Survey (骨骼普查)**: ↓ interpedicular distance in lumbar spine (L1-L5), squared iliac wings, "champagne glass" pelvic inlet, short/thick tubular bones.
    * **Cranial US (頭部超聲波) / MRI Brain & Cervical Spine**: Mandatory to screen for **Foramen magnum stenosis (枕骨大孔狹窄)** and **Hydrocephalus (腦積水)**.
* **Special/Bloods**:
    * Targeted *FGFR3* genetic testing (HK Clinical Genetic Service, HKCH).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
* **Neurological**:
    * Urgent neurosurgical decompression if +ve signs of foramen magnum compression (central sleep apnoea, hyperreflexia, sudden hypotonia).
    * Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt (腦室腹腔分流術) if +ve progressive hydrocephalus.
* **Orthopaedic (e.g., DKCH / HA protocols)**:
    * Monitor progressive thoracolumbar kyphosis.
    * Osteotomies (截骨術) for severe genu varum (usually delayed until adolescence).
    * Limb lengthening (肢體延長術) → controversial, high complication rate, rarely routinely recommended in HK public sector.
* **ENT / Respiratory**:
    * Regular sleep studies (PSG) → rule out **Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA) (阻塞性睡眠窒息症)**.
    * Adenotonsillectomy (腺樣體扁桃體切除術) or CPAP (持續正壓呼吸機) for OSA.
    * Tympanostomy tubes (耳咽管) for recurrent Acute Otitis Media (AOM) to prevent conductive hearing loss.
* **Medical Therapies**:
    * **Vosoritide (伏索利肽)**: Daily SC CNP analog. Bypasses FGFR3 block → ↑ height velocity. Emerging therapy (Requires HA compassionate use / self-finance via Samaritan Fund evaluations).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
* **MCHC (母嬰健康院)**: Strict developmental and OFC surveillance.
* **Clinical Psychology / MSW (醫務社工)**: Coping strategies for physical differences, integration into mainstream schools vs. physical bullying. Assess eligibility for Disability Allowance (傷殘津貼) via SWD.
* **Occupational Therapy (職業治療)**: Environmental mods (step stools, modified school desks/toilets, reachers) to foster independence.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
* **Red Flag**: Foramen magnum stenosis is a silent killer in infancy.
* Infants MUST NEVER be placed in unsupportive "umbrella strollers" (傘車) or soft carriers. 
* Lack of head support → sudden backward head jerk → fatal **Cervical cord crush (頸髓壓迫)**.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
* **Complications**:
    * **Spinal Stenosis (椎管狹窄)** (adults) → neurogenic claudication / paraparesis.
    * **Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA)**.
    * **Obesity (肥胖)** → caloric requirement matches height (low) but appetite matches age (high). Aggressive dietetic input needed.
    * Recurrent otitis media → **Conductive hearing loss (傳導性聽力受損)** → speech delay.
* **Prognosis**:
    * Achondroplasia: Normal lifespan & intellect (if early cervicomedullary compression avoided).
    * Thanatophoric Dysplasia: Universally fatal.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls
* **Trap**: Misdiagnosing benign macrocephaly as hydrocephalus. Plagiocephaly & macrocephaly are expected; TRUE **Hydrocephalus (腦積水)** requires imaging confirmation (dilated ventricles on US/MRI) before shunting.
* **Viva Q**: "Why is paternal age relevant in Achondroplasia?"
    * **Answer**: ~80% of cases are de novo mutations. These mutations arise almost exclusively in the *paternal* allele during spermatogenesis and have a strong, proven correlation with **advanced paternal age**.
* **Trap**: Missing subtle signs of cord compression in infants. ALWAYS ask about central apnoeas, poor feeding, or check for unusually brisk reflexes/clonus in an Achondroplastic infant.
