# Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF) (法洛四聯症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
* **Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF)**: Most common cyanotic **Congenital Heart Disease (CHD) (先天性心臟病)** presenting beyond the neonatal period.
* Characterized by 4 classic anatomical defects stemming from a single embryological error.

### Local Epidemiology
* Accounts for ~7-10% of all **CHD**.
* Incidence: ~3-5 per 10,000 live births globally; presentation managed locally under Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) paediatric cardiology networks.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Embryological Error
* Anterior & cephalad deviation of the **infundibular septum (漏斗部中隔)**.

### The Four Classic Features
* **Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) (心室中膈缺損)**: Large & non-restrictive.
* **Overriding Aorta (主動脈跨位)**: Aorta shifted right, sits directly over VSD, receiving blood from both ventricles.
* **Right Ventricular Outflow Tract Obstruction (RVOTO) (右心室流出道阻塞)**: Pulmonary stenosis (infundibular muscle spasm, valvular, or supravalvular).
* **Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (RVH) (右心室肥大)**: Secondary adaptation to pumping against ↑ resistance of RVOTO.

### Haemodynamics & Shunting
* Degree of **Cyanosis (發紺)** depends entirely on RVOTO severity.
* Blood takes path of least resistance.
* Severe pulmonary stenosis → deoxygenated blood from RV shunts Right-to-Left across VSD into overriding aorta → systemic cyanosis.

### Genetic Associations
* Strongly associated with **DiGeorge Syndrome (22q11.2 microdeletion)** (~15% cases).
* ↑ risk in Trisomy 21 (Down Syndrome) & Alagille Syndrome.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation by Severity
* **Severe RVOTO**: Profound cyanosis at birth. Pulmonary circulation is duct-dependent (relies on **Patent Ductus Arteriosus, PDA (開放性動脈導管)**).
* **Mild RVOTO ("Pink Tet")**: Infant acyanotic initially, presents only with murmur. As child grows, infundibular muscle hypertrophies → worsening obstruction → progressive cyanosis.

### Hypercyanotic Spells ("Tet Spells") (缺氧發作)
* Life-threatening medical emergency.
* Features: Sudden profound cyanosis, hyperpnoea (deep/rapid breathing), irritability, syncope/seizures.
* **Triggers**: Crying, feeding, defecation, waking.
* **Mechanism**: Triggers ↓ Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR) or ↑ infundibular spasm → worsens Right-to-Left shunt.

### Physical Signs
* **Cyanosis (發紺)**: Central (lips, tongue).
* **Clubbing (杵狀指)**: In older, uncorrected children (rare today due to early HA surgical repair).
* **Murmur**: Harsh, ejection systolic murmur at upper left sternal border.
* **Murmur Source**: Blood forced through pulmonary stenosis (VSD is large & silent).
* **Heart Sounds**: Single S2 (pulmonary component P2 soft/absent due to abnormal valve).
* **During Tet Spell**: Murmur paradoxically softens/disappears → infundibular spasm shuts RV outflow tract → no flow through stenosis = no murmur → all blood shunts silently through VSD.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### TOF vs Transposition of the Great Arteries (TGA)
| Clinical Feature | Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF) | Transposition of the Great Arteries (TGA) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset of Cyanosis** | Weeks to months (unless severe RVOTO) | Day 1 of life (Most common cyanotic CHD in 1st week) |
| **Murmur** | Loud ejection systolic murmur | Often absent (unless VSD present) |
| **CXR Appearance** | "Boot-shaped heart", oligemic lungs | "Egg-on-string", plethoric lungs |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Cyanotic Congenital Heart Diseases
* **Transposition of the Great Arteries (TGA) (大動脈轉位)**
* **Tricuspid Atresia (三尖瓣閉鎖)**
* **Truncus Arteriosus (動脈幹)**
* **Total Anomalous Pulmonary Venous Return (TAPVR) (完全性肺靜脈異常回流)**

### Acyanotic/Other Conditions
* Severe isolated **Pulmonary Stenosis (肺動脈狹窄)**.
* **Heart Failure (心臟衰竭)** secondary to severe left-to-right shunting lesions.

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
* **Pulse Oximetry (脈搏血氧定量計)**: ↓ SpO2 (degree dependent on shunt severity).
* **ECG (心電圖)**: **Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (RVH)** (tall R waves in V1) & Right Axis Deviation.

### Blood Tests
* **CBP**: ↑ Hb/Hct (**Polycythaemia (紅血球增多症)**) as physiological response to chronic hypoxia.
* **ABG/CBG**: Metabolic acidosis + ↑ lactate during Tet spells.

### Imaging (Diagnostic)
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: Gold standard. Visualizes overriding aorta, VSD size/location, RVOTO anatomy, coronary artery mapping.
* **Chest X-ray (CXR)**: 
  * Classic **"Boot-shaped heart" (Coeur en sabot)**.
  * Upturned apex (due to RVH).
  * Concave main pulmonary artery segment.
  * **Oligemic lung fields**: Abnormally dark lungs due to ↓ pulmonary blood flow.

### Special Investigations
* **Genetics**: Chromosomal microarray for 22q11.2 deletion.
* **Cardiac Catheterization**: Rarely for diagnosis; used pre-operatively to map complex coronary anatomy or collateral vessels.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management: Hypercyanotic "Tet Spell"
* **Positioning**: **Knee-to-Chest Position (膝胸臥位)** (or squatting in older child) → kinks femoral arteries → ↑ SVR → forces blood back into pulmonary circulation.
* **Oxygen**: High-flow 100% O2 (limited effect if Right-to-Left shunt is massive).
* **Calming**: Parental cuddle, minimize agitation.
* **Analgesia**: IV/IM/SC **Morphine (嗎啡)** → calms child, stops crying, ↓ hyperpnoea, ↓ sympathetic drive.
* **Fluid Resuscitation**: IV crystalloid bolus (10-20 mL/kg) → ↑ RV preload.
* **Beta-Blockers**: IV **Propranolol (普萘洛爾)** or **Esmolol** → relaxes infundibular muscle spasm → opens RV outflow tract.
* **Vasopressors**: IV **Phenylephrine** (if refractory) → directly ↑ SVR to reverse shunt.

### Neonatal Management (Severe RVOTO)
* IV **Prostaglandin E1 (Alprostadil) (前列腺素E1)** → maintains PDA to ensure life-saving pulmonary blood flow prior to surgery.

### Definitive Surgical Management
* **Palliative Shunt**: **Blalock-Taussig-Thomas (BTT) Shunt** (subclavian artery to pulmonary artery) → performed if infant too small, premature, or unfavorable anatomy. Artificially ↑ pulmonary blood flow.
* **Complete Repair**: Elective at ~6 months of age (HA protocol). Involves patch closure of VSD + surgical relief of RVOTO (often requires transannular patch across pulmonary valve).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT)
* **Paediatric Cardiology & Cardiothoracic Surgery**: Long-term surveillance post-repair.
* **Genetic Counseling (遺傳諮詢)**: Vital if 22q11.2 deletion identified (guides surveillance for immune dysfunction, hypocalcaemia, & future pregnancy risk).
* **Paediatric Dentistry**: Aggressive caries prevention to ↓ risk of endocarditis.

### Psychosocial Care
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Support for chronic disease burden & frequent HA admissions.
* **Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC)**: Optimize routine immunizations (esp. RSV, Influenza).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect Red Flags
* NAI rarely directly causes TOF presentations, but **Medical Neglect** must be suspected if:
  * Persistent defaulting of cardiology follow-ups.
  * Ignored profound dental decay in a child with uncorrected/palliated CHD (massive risk of fatal endocarditis).
  * Failure to seek emergency care for obvious cyanotic Tet spells.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Pre-Operative Complications
* **Polycythaemia**: Body's response to chronic hypoxia → hyperviscosity → ↑ risk of **Stroke (中風)** / cerebral vein thrombosis.
* **Brain Abscess (腦膿腫)**: Right-to-Left shunt bypasses pulmonary capillary filtration of systemic bacteria.
* **Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎)**.

### Post-Operative Complications (Long-Term)
* **Pulmonary Regurgitation (肺動脈瓣逆流)**: Inevitable consequence of transannular patch. 
* **RV Failure & Arrhythmias**: Decades of pulmonary regurgitation cause severe RV volume overload & dilation.
* **Re-operation**: Pulmonary valve replacement (surgical or transcatheter) is very frequently required in late adolescence or early adulthood.

### Prognosis
* Excellent short-to-medium term survival (>95% post-repair).
* Requires lifelong Adult Congenital Heart Disease (ACHD) surveillance.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls

* Viva Question: Why does a toddler with Tetralogy of Fallot instinctively squat when they feel breathless? Answer: Squatting kinks the femoral arteries, which significantly increases Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR). This higher systemic pressure decreases the Right-to-Left shunt across the VSD and forces more blood through the narrowed pulmonary artery, improving oxygenation.
* Viva Question: What happens to the intensity of the murmur during a severe hypercyanotic Tet spell, and why? Answer: The murmur gets softer or disappears entirely. The infundibular muscle spasms shut, stopping flow across the pulmonary outflow tract, which is the source of the murmur. All blood shunts silently through the VSD.
* Trap: Assuming the murmur in TOF is caused by blood flowing through the Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD). Pearl: The VSD in TOF is large and non-restrictive, making it completely silent. The harsh ejection systolic murmur originates entirely from blood forced through the Right Ventricular Outflow Tract Obstruction (RVOTO).
* Pitfall: Administering inotropes like adrenaline during a Tet spell. These agents increase heart rate and contractility, which acutely worsens the infundibular muscle spasm and fatally exacerbates the right-to-left shunt.
