# Constipation & Encopresis (便秘與遺糞症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Functional Constipation
* Diagnosed via **Rome IV Criteria** (must include ≥2 for 1 month in infants/toddlers or 2 months in older children):
* ≤2 defecations/week
* History of excessive stool retention
* History of painful/hard bowel movements (BMs)
* History of large-diameter stools (may obstruct toilet)
* Presence of large fecal mass in rectum
* In toilet-trained children: ≥1 episode/week of incontinence

### Clinical Definition of Encopresis
* Repeated voluntary/involuntary passage of feces in inappropriate places
* Occurs in children >4 years old (chronological/developmental)
* Usually secondary to severe chronic **Constipation (便秘)** with overflow incontinence (**Retentive Encopresis**)

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Highly prevalent in HK pediatric population (up to 30% of school-aged children)
* Peaks during toilet training, starting preschool/kindergarten, and early primary school
* Exacerbated by typical HK diet (↓ fiber, ↓ water, ↑ processed foods)
* Strong association with academic stress, lack of physical activity, and unhygienic school toilets → withholding behavior

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Functional Constipation
* Painful BM (e.g., due to **Anal Fissure (肛裂)**) → fear of defecation → withholding behavior
* Withholding → stool remains in colon → prolonged fluid absorption → harder, larger stool
* Rectal vault dilates (**Megarectum**) → ↓ rectal sensory feedback → loss of urge to defecate
* Vicious cycle: Pain → Withholding → Harder Stool → More Pain

### Pathophysiology of Encopresis (Overflow Incontinence)
* Chronic retention → severe fecal impaction in rectum
* Liquid stool from proximal colon bypasses impacted solid mass
* Leaks out involuntarily through relaxed internal anal sphincter → soiling underwear

### Pathophysiology & Genetics of Hirschsprung Disease (巨結腸症)
* Arrest of craniocaudal neural crest cell migration
* Absence of ganglion cells in Meissner (submucosal) & Auerbach (myenteric) plexuses
* Affected segment (usually rectosigmoid) fails to relax → functional obstruction
* Associated with **RET proto-oncogene** mutations and **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症)**

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Pediatric Constipation
* Infrequent BMs (<3/week)
* Passing hard, pebble-like, or unusually large stools
* Pain/crying during defecation
* Abdominal pain, bloating, ↓ appetite (resolves post-defecation)
* Soiling of underwear (often mistaken by parents as diarrhea)

### Key Signs on Physical Examination
* Palpable fecal mass in lower abdomen (especially Left Iliac Fossa)
* Visual inspection of perineum: **Anal Fissure (肛裂)**, skin tags, erythema
* Abnormal position of anus (anteriorly displaced)
* Sacral dimple or hair tuft (suggests spinal dysraphism)
* PR examination (only if strictly necessary/organic cause suspected):
* Functional: dilated ampulla loaded with stool
* Hirschsprung: empty ampulla, tight sphincter, explosive release of stool/gas upon withdrawal

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features of Functional vs Organic Constipation
* **Age of onset:** Functional usually >1 year (toddler/preschool); Organic often <1 month (neonatal)
* **Meconium passage:** Functional <48hrs; Hirschsprung >48hrs
* **Growth:** Functional → normal; Organic → faltering growth / Failure to Thrive (FTT)
* **Withholding behavior (e.g., crossing legs, squatting, hiding):** Hallmark of Functional; absent in Organic
* **Neurological signs (e.g., ↓ lower limb tone/reflexes):** Suggests spinal cord lesion (Spina Bifida)

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Pediatric Constipation
| Category | Condition (Chinese) | Key Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Functional** | Functional Constipation (功能性便秘) | Most common (95%). Withholding behavior, large stool, normal growth. |
| **Aganglionic** | Hirschsprung Disease (巨結腸症) | Delayed meconium (>48h), empty rectum, explosive stool post-PR, FTT. |
| **Endocrine** | Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能減退) | Lethargy, cold intolerance, poor growth, bradycardia, prolonged neonatal jaundice. |
| **Metabolic** | Hypercalcemia / Hypokalemia | Polyuria, polydipsia, hypotonia, muscle weakness. |
| **Anatomical** | Imperforate Anus / Anterior Anus | Visible on perineal inspection at birth. |
| **Neurological** | Spina Bifida / Spinal Dysraphism | Lower limb weakness, absent anal wink, sacral dimple/tuft. |
| **Dietary** | Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (牛奶蛋白過敏) | Refractory constipation, eczema, family history of atopy. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Investigations for Functional Constipation
* Strictly clinical diagnosis (History & Examination)
* **Abdominal X-Ray (AXR):** NOT routinely recommended. Consider ONLY if severe impaction suspected and examination impossible/refused.
* **Urine Culture:** If recurrent UTIs present (constipation → bladder compression → urinary stasis).

### Specialist Investigations for Suspected Organic Constipation
* **Rectal Suction Biopsy:** Gold standard for Hirschsprung. Shows absence of ganglion cells, ↑ acetylcholinesterase staining.
* **Contrast Enema (Barium/Water-soluble):** Shows transition zone in Hirschsprung (dilated proximal, narrow aganglionic distal segment).
* **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs) / Calcium profiles:** If FTT or systemic signs present.
* **MRI Spine:** If sacral dimple or abnormal lower limb neurology identified.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management: Disimpaction Protocol
| Medication | Dosage / Regimen | Notes (HA Context) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Macrogol 3350 (PEG) + Electrolytes** | High dose (1-1.5 g/kg/day) orally | 1st line in HA. Mix with water/juice. Titrate until clear liquid stool. |
| **Glycerin Suppository** | 1-2g PR | Used if oral PEG ineffective or rapid relief needed for severe pain. |
| **Phosphate Enema** | Weight-based (avoid in <2 yrs) | Used only in inpatient setting for refractory impaction. Risk of hyperphosphatemia. |

### Chronic Management: Maintenance Therapy
* Start maintenance immediately post-disimpaction. Duration: weeks to months. Do NOT stop abruptly.
* **1st Line:** **Macrogol 3350 (聚乙二醇)** (0.4-0.8 g/kg/day). Titrate to 1 soft BM/day.
* **2nd Line:** **Lactulose (乳果糖)** (1-2 mL/kg/day). Osmotic laxative.
* **Adjuncts:** Senna / Bisacodyl (stimulant laxatives) added temporarily if osmotic laxatives insufficient.

### Non-Pharmacological & Behavioral Interventions
* **Toilet Training:** Sit on toilet for 3-5 mins exactly 15-30 mins after meals (utilizes gastrocolic reflex).
* **Posture:** Use footstool (hips flexed >90°, knees higher than hips) to straighten anorectal angle.
* **Diet:** ↑ Dietary fiber (fruits, veg, whole grains), ↑ hydration.
* **Positive Reinforcement:** Star charts for sitting on toilet (not just for passing stool). No punishment for soiling.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Pediatrician:** Medical management, exclusion of organic causes.
* **Dietitian:** Tailoring high-fiber diet to local HK culinary habits (e.g., substituting white rice with brown rice, adding choi sum/spinach).
* **School Nurse / Teacher:** Arranging unrestricted toilet access during school hours.

### Psychosocial Support for Encopresis
* **Clinical Psychologist:** Encopresis causes severe embarrassment, bullying, and low self-esteem. Address underlying anxiety, school phobia, or family stress.
* **Destigmatization:** Explain pathophysiology to parents and child. Emphasize that soiling is INVOLUNTARY (overflow).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Indicators in Constipation/Encopresis
* **Sexual Abuse:** Suspect if acute onset of severe encopresis/constipation, presence of anal trauma (lacerations, bruising, patulous anus), or inappropriate sexualized behavior.
* **Severe Neglect:** Profound FTT, severe diaper dermatitis, consistent failure to provide prescribed medications.
* **Emotional Abuse:** Parents exhibiting excessive anger, physically punishing the child for involuntary soiling.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Common Complications of Chronic Constipation
* **Anal Fissures (肛裂):** Leads to bright red blood per rectum (BRBPR) and severe pain.
* **Recurrent UTIs / Enuresis (尿床):** Megarectum compresses bladder neck → voiding dysfunction.
* **Rectal Prolapse:** Due to chronic straining.
* **Psychological Distress:** Social isolation, depression secondary to encopresis.

### Long-Term Prognosis
* Often a chronic condition requiring prolonged (months-years) laxative therapy.
* High relapse rate (up to 50%) if laxatives weaned too early.
* Prognosis excellent if behavioral modifications are strictly adhered to and medication tapered slowly.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of encopresis in children? Answer: Chronic functional constipation leading to overflow incontinence.
* Viva Question: When should you suspect Hirschsprung disease in a neonate? Answer: Failure to pass meconium within the first 48 hours of life.
* Trap: Assuming encopresis is a deliberate behavioral problem. Pearl: Soiling in encopresis is involuntary overflow of liquid stool around a hard fecal impaction; it requires medical disimpaction, not punishment.
* Pitfall: Stopping laxatives immediately after the child has a normal bowel movement. Maintenance laxative therapy must be continued for months to allow the distended rectum to regain normal tone and sensation.
* Trap: Routinely ordering an abdominal X-ray for every child presenting with constipation. Pearl: Diagnosis of functional constipation is clinical; AXR is only indicated if severe impaction is suspected but physical examination is unreliable or impossible.
* Pitfall: Performing a digital rectal examination (DRE) routinely in children with obvious functional constipation. A DRE is highly traumatic for children and should only be performed if organic pathology is strongly suspected.
