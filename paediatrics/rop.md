# Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) (早產兒視網膜病變)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of ROP
* Vasoproliferative disorder of developing retina (視網膜) in premature infants.
* Characterized by abnormal neovascularization (新生血管) at the vascular-avascular junction.

### Hong Kong HA Epidemiology
* Most common in extremely preterm (<28 wks) & ELBW (<1000g).
* Affects ~35% of VLBW infants; severe disease in ~5%.
* HA/DH protocols emphasize strict SpO2 targeting (91-95%) to minimize incidence.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Phase 1: Vaso-obliteration (Hyperoxic Phase)
* Premature birth interrupts normal retinal angiogenesis.
* Hyperoxia (高氧) → ↓VEGF & ↓IGF-1.
* → Cessation of normal vessel growth & obliteration of existing vessels.

### Phase 2: Vasoproliferation (Hypoxic Phase)
* Maturing retina → ↑metabolic demand.
* Unperfused retina → Hypoxia (缺氧) → ↑↑VEGF.
* → Abnormal neovascularization into the vitreous.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Asymptomatic Presentation
* Initially silent disease.
* Solely detected via structured NICU screening & fundoscopy (眼底檢查).

### Fundoscopic Signs of Progression
* Abnormal vessel growth at boundary of vascularised/avascular retina.
* Progression: Demarcation line → Ridge → Extraretinal fibrovascular proliferation.

### Plus Disease (附加病變)
* ↑Venous dilation & ↑arteriolar tortuosity in posterior pole.
* Indicates severe activity/florid disease.

### Late/Missed Disease Signs
* Leukocoria (白瞳症)
* Strabismus (斜視)
* ↓Vision / Blindness (失明)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating ROP from Retinoblastoma (視網膜母細胞瘤)
* **ROP**: +ve Hx of extreme prematurity/NICU care, bilateral involvement common, structural vascular ridge.
* **Retinoblastoma**: Usually normal GA, calcified mass on ultrasound, rarely bilateral.

### Differentiating ROP from Congenital Cataract (先天性白內障)
* **ROP**: Retinal/vascular pathology.
* **Cataract**: Anterior lens opacity, absent red reflex (紅光反射).

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Table of ROP Differentials
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Retinoblastoma (視網膜母細胞瘤)** | Normal GA, calcified intraocular mass, leukocoria early. |
| **Familial Exudative Vitreoretinopathy (FEVR)** | Full-term infants, +ve autosomal dominant family Hx. |
| **Persistent Fetal Vasculature (PFV)** | Usually unilateral, microphthalmia, stalk from optic nerve to lens. |
| **Congenital Cataract (先天性白內障)** | Lens opacity, absent red reflex, associated with TORCH infections. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Fundoscopic Screening
* Pupil dilation (Cyclopentolate/Phenylephrine).
* Indirect ophthalmoscopy (間接眼底鏡檢查) by Paediatric Ophthalmologist.

### HA NICU Specialist Imaging
* **RetCam**: Digital wide-field retinal imaging.
* Utilized for documentation, precise staging, and tracking progression.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Preventive HA NICU SpO2 Protocols
* Strict targeting of SpO2 (91-95%) in NICU.
* Prevents Phase 1 hyperoxia & limits extreme hypoxia.

### HA ROP Screening Criteria
* Inclusion: GA ≤32 wks OR BW ≤1500g.
* Timing: 1st screen at 31-32 wks PCA (post-conceptional age) OR 4 wks postnatal (whichever is later).

### Gold Standard Surgical Treatment
* **Laser photocoagulation (激光光凝治療)**: Ablation of avascular retina.
* ↓Ischemic drive → ↓VEGF production.

### Pharmacological Therapy
* **Intravitreal Anti-VEGF (抗血管內皮生長因子)** (e.g., Bevacizumab/Ranibizumab).
* Reserved for aggressive posterior ROP or cases unfit for laser.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Structure
* Neonatologist
* Paediatric Ophthalmologist (小兒眼科醫生)
* Specialized NICU nurses

### MCHC & MSW Support
* Intense parental counseling required due to blindness risk.
* **MSW (醫務社工)**: Financial & psychosocial support.
* **MCHC (母嬰健康院)**: Continued developmental follow-up & visual screening.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect Indicators
* Not directly associated with NAI.
* **Red Flag**: Defaulting urgent ophthalmology screening post-discharge.
* → Medical neglect (醫療疏忽); requires immediate MSW intervention to safeguard child's vision.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Ocular Complications
* Retinal detachment (視網膜脫落)
* Severe Myopia (近視)
* Strabismus (斜視)
* Amblyopia (弱視)
* Glaucoma (青光眼)

### Long-term Prognosis
* Prompt tx of threshold disease prevents blindness in most.
* Severe bilateral visual impairment occurs in ~1% of VLBW infants (mostly <28 wks).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Forgetting the timing of the first screening. Pearl: HA criteria mandates screening for GA ≤32 wks or BW ≤1500g at 31-32 wks PCA or 4 wks of age (choose the later one).
* Viva Question: What is Plus disease and why does it matter? Answer: It indicates florid disease (+ve venous dilation & arteriolar tortuosity) and mandates urgent treatment within 48-72 hours.
* Pitfall: Discharging extreme preemies without a documented ROP screen or strict follow-up plan. Ensure an ironclad appointment with paediatric ophthalmology prior to NICU discharge to avoid medical neglect.
