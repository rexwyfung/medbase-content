# Acute Leukaemia (ALL & AML) (急性白血病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Malignant clonal proliferation of haematopoietic stem cells in bone marrow → marrow failure & organ infiltration.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Most common childhood cancer in HK (~30% of all paediatric malignancies).
  - **Acute Lymphoblastic Leukaemia (ALL)**: ~80% of cases. Peak age 2-5 yrs.
  - **Acute Myeloid Leukaemia (AML)**: ~20% of cases.
- Managed centrally at **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH) Paediatric Oncology Centre**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Mechanism**: Genetic mutations in single lymphoid/myeloid progenitor cell → arrest in development & uncontrolled proliferation (**blasts**) → crowd out normal haematopoiesis → pancytopenia.
- **Genetics/Risk Stratification (ALL)**:
  - *Favourable*: Hyperdiploidy (>50 chromosomes), t(12;21) [ETV6-RUNX1].
  - *Unfavourable*: Hypodiploidy (<44 chromosomes), t(9;22) [Philadelphia chromosome/BCR-ABL1], MLL gene rearrangements (infants).
- **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症)**: 10-20x ↑ risk of ALL & AML (esp. Acute Megakaryoblastic Leukaemia, AML M7).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Insidious onset over weeks or rapid/acute progression.
- Consequence of:
  - **Bone marrow infiltration**: Systemic ill-health, cytopenias.
  - **Tissue infiltration**: Localised mass effects, organomegaly.
  - **Oncological Emergencies**: e.g., Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS), SVC obstruction.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Bone Marrow Failure**:
  - *Anaemia (貧血)*: Pallor, lethargy, tachycardia.
  - *Thrombocytopenia (血小板減少症)*: Bruising, petechiae, epistaxis.
  - *Neutropenia (中性粒細胞減少)*: Recurrent/severe infections, unexplained fever.
- **Tissue Infiltration**:
  - **Bone pain (骨痛)**: Severe, waking child at night, limp/refusal to walk.
  - **Hepatosplenomegaly (肝脾腫大)**: Abdominal distension/mass.
  - **Lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)**: Generalised, firm, non-tender.
  - **CNS involvement**: Headaches, vomiting, cranial nerve palsies.
  - **Testicular enlargement**: Painless, unilateral (sanctuary site, more common at relapse).
  - **Mediastinal Mass**: T-cell ALL → SVC obstruction, airway compromise, orthopnoea.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
| Feature | Acute Leukaemia | ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenia) | JIA (Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Systemic Symptoms** | ↑ (Fever, lethargy, weight loss) | Absent (Well child) | Variable (Fever in systemic JIA) |
| **Organomegaly** | Common (+ve) | Absent (-ve) | Variable (Hepatosplenomegaly in systemic JIA) |
| **Bone/Joint Pain** | Deep bone pain, non-articular | None | Articular, morning stiffness |
| **CBC** | Pancytopenia/blasts | Isolated ↓ Plt | Normal or ↑ WBC, ↑ ESR/CRP |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Acute Leukaemia (ALL > AML).
- **Must-Not-Miss (Oncological Emergencies)**:
  - **Tumour Lysis Syndrome (TLS) (腫瘤溶解綜合症)**: ↑ K+, ↑ Urate, ↑ PO4, ↓ Ca2+ → AKI & arrhythmias.
  - **Neutropenic Sepsis (中性粒細胞減少性敗血症)**: Life-threatening.
  - **Hyperleukocytosis**: WBC > 100 x 10^9/L → Leukostasis → stroke, pulmonary oedema.
  - **SVC Obstruction**: Associated with T-cell ALL mediastinal mass.
  - **DIC**: Common in Acute Promyelocytic Leukaemia (APML).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside/Bloods**:
  - **CBC & Differential**: Pancytopenia (or bi-cytopenia), WBC variable (↓, normal, or ↑↑).
  - **Peripheral Blood Smear**: **Crucial**. Shows circulating blasts (large, high N:C ratio, prominent nucleoli).
  - **Coagulation Profile**: R/o DIC (esp. APML), check PT/APTT/Fibrinogen/D-dimer.
  - **Renal & Liver Function, Urate, Ca2+, PO4**: Screen for TLS.
- **Imaging**:
  - **CXR**: Check for mediastinal mass (T-cell ALL).
- **Special/Gold Standard**:
  - **Bone Marrow Aspirate & Trephine (BMAT)**: Definitive Dx (>20% blasts).
  - **Flow Cytometry (Immunophenotyping)**: Subclassifies ALL (B-cell vs T-cell) and AML.
  - **Cytogenetics/Molecular**: Karyotyping, FISH, PCR for translocations (risk stratification).
  - **Lumbar Puncture (LP)**: Assess CSF for CNS involvement.
- **Monitoring**:
  - **Minimal Residual Disease (MRD)**: PCR/Flow Cytometry post-induction to guide therapy intensity.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute (Emergency HA Protocols)**:
  - **Neutropenic Sepsis**: Admit immediately → cultures → IV broad-spectrum Abx (e.g., Tazocin) within 1 hr.
  - **Tumour Lysis Syndrome**: Hyperhydration (IV fluids w/o K+), Allopurinol (or Rasburicase for high WBC/urate).
  - **Hyperleukocytosis/Leukostasis**: Avoid packed red cell transfusion initially (worsens viscosity) → vigorous hydration, cytoreduction.
  - **Anaemia/Bleeding**: Transfuse packed RBCs & Platelets carefully.
- **Chronic/Definitive (Chemotherapy via HKCH POC)**:
  - **Phases**:
    1. *Induction*: Eradicate blasts → achieve remission (target <5% blasts in marrow). E.g., steroids + vincristine + asparaginase.
    2. *Consolidation*: Intensive block to solidify remission.
    3. *Maintenance*: Prolonged low-dose chemo (up to 2-3 yrs for ALL).
  - **CNS Directed Therapy**: Intrathecal Methotrexate/Cytarabine to prevent/treat CNS relapse (systemic chemo penetrates CNS poorly).
  - **Pneumocystis Prophylaxis**: PO Cotrimoxazole daily/thrice weekly.
  - **Targeted Therapy**: e.g., Imatinib (TKI) for Philadelphia chromosome +ve ALL.
  - **HSCT (Stem Cell Transplant)**: Reserved for high-risk ALL, relapsed ALL, AML.
  - **CAR-T Cell Therapy**: Approved/available at HKCH for relapsed/refractory B-cell ALL.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **HKCH Support Team**:
  - **Clinical Psychologists / Play Therapists**: Cope with procedural anxiety & diagnosis.
  - **Medical Social Workers (MSW)**: Financial aid, transport, housing.
  - **Hospital School (Red Cross)**: Prevent educational delay during prolonged admissions.
  - **Children's Cancer Foundation (CCF)**: Local NGO support, sibling support, respite.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- **Safety Netting**: Parents MUST be educated on **neutropenic fever protocols** → bring child to A&E immediately for Temp > 38.0°C.
- Beware assuming bruising is NAI without checking CBC first.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **Prognosis**:
  - ALL: 5-year survival >90%.
  - AML: 5-year survival ~60-70%.
  - *Poor prognostic indicators (ALL)*: Age <1 or >10 yrs, WBC >50 x 10^9/L, hypodiploidy, MLL rearrangement, slow response to induction (persistent MRD).
- **Late Effects of Therapy**:
  - *Anthracyclines*: Cardiotoxicity (dilated cardiomyopathy).
  - *Cranial Irradiation*: Neurocognitive deficits, endocrine dysfunction (GH deficiency).
  - *Alkylating agents*: Infertility.
  - *General*: Secondary malignancies.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Pearl 1 (The Limping Child)**: "Never dismiss a limping child as just 'growing pains' or transient synovitis without a CBC." Bone pain from marrow expansion is a classic early presentation of leukaemia.
- **Pearl 2 (TLS Metabolics)**: TLS metabolic triad = **↑ K+, ↑ Urate, ↑ PO4, ↓ Ca2+**. Why ↓ Ca2+? Because it precipitates with the massive efflux of intracellular PO4.
- **Pearl 3 (Steroid Trap)**: NEVER prescribe empirical steroids to a child with unexplained lymphadenopathy or bone pain without a definitive diagnosis. Steroids are cytotoxic to lymphoblasts → can partially treat leukaemia, mask the diagnosis, trigger TLS, and ruin BMAT staging.
