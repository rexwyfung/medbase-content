# Acute Leukaemia (ALL & AML) (急性白血病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Leukaemia
- Malignant clonal proliferation of haematopoietic stem cells in bone marrow → marrow failure & organ infiltration.
### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology
- Most common childhood cancer in HK (~30% of all paediatric malignancies).
- Managed centrally at **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH)** Paediatric Oncology Centre.
### Acute Lymphoblastic Leukaemia (ALL) (急性淋巴細胞白血病) Epidemiology
- ~80% of childhood leukaemia cases.
- Peak age: 2-5 yrs.
### Acute Myeloid Leukaemia (AML) (急性髓系白血病) Epidemiology
- ~20% of cases.
- More common in neonates, infants & older teenagers.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Leukaemia Pathophysiological Mechanism
- Genetic mutations in single lymphoid/myeloid progenitor cell → developmental arrest & uncontrolled proliferation (**blasts**).
- Blasts crowd out normal marrow → **pancytopenia**.
- Blasts enter blood → tissue/organ infiltration (e.g., CNS, testes, liver, spleen).
### ALL Genetics and Risk Stratification
- **Favourable (Standard Risk):** Hyperdiploidy (>50 chromosomes), t(12;21) [ETV6-RUNX1].
- **Unfavourable (High Risk):** Hypodiploidy (<44 chromosomes), t(9;22) [Philadelphia chromosome/BCR-ABL1], MLL gene rearrangements (esp. infants).
### Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症) Association
- 10-20x ↑ risk of childhood leukaemia.
- Specifically linked to **Acute Megakaryoblastic Leukaemia (AML M7)** in <4 yrs.
- Transient Abnormal Myelopoiesis (TAM) in neonates → 20% progress to AML.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Leukaemia Onset Timeline
- Presentation spans days to weeks (insidious) or rapid acute decline.
### Bone Marrow Failure Symptoms
- **Anaemia (貧血):** Pallor, lethargy, tachycardia, breathlessness.
- **Thrombocytopenia (血小板減少症):** Bruising, petechiae, purpura, epistaxis, bleeding gums.
- **Neutropenia (中性粒細胞減少):** Recurrent/severe infections, unexplained fever.
### Tissue Infiltration Signs
- **Bone Pain (骨痛):** Severe, deep, waking child at night, limp, refusal to walk.
- **Organomegaly:** **Hepatosplenomegaly (肝脾腫大)** → abdominal distension.
- **Lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大):** Generalised, firm, non-tender cervical/axillary/inguinal nodes.
- **CNS Involvement:** Headaches, early morning vomiting, cranial nerve palsies (esp. CN VI), papilloedema.
- **Testicular Involvement:** Painless, unilateral enlargement (sanctuary site).
- **Mediastinal Mass:** Anterior mediastinal mass (classic in T-cell ALL) → SVC obstruction, orthopnoea, stridor.
- **Skin/Gums (AML specific):** Leukaemia cutis (blueberry muffin spots), gingival hypertrophy (AML M4/M5).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differential Diagnosis Comparison Table
| Feature | Acute Leukaemia | ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenia) | JIA (Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Systemic Symptoms** | ↑ (Fever, lethargy, weight loss) | Absent (Well child) | Variable (Fever in systemic JIA) |
| **Organomegaly** | Common (+ve) | Absent (-ve) | Variable (Hepatosplenomegaly in systemic) |
| **Bone/Joint Pain** | Deep bone pain, non-articular, wakes at night | None | Articular, morning stiffness |
| **CBC Profile** | Pancytopenia / blasts | Isolated ↓ Plt | Normal or ↑ WBC, ↑ ESR/CRP |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Most Likely Primary Diagnosis
- Acute Leukaemia (ALL > AML).
### Must-Not-Miss Oncological Emergencies
- **Tumour Lysis Syndrome (TLS) (腫瘤溶解綜合症):** ↑ K+, ↑ Urate, ↑ PO4, ↓ Ca2+ → AKI & fatal arrhythmias.
- **Neutropenic Sepsis (中性粒細胞減少性敗血症):** Life-threatening overwhelming bacterial/fungal infection.
- **Hyperleukocytosis & Leukostasis:** WBC > 100 x 10^9/L → microvascular sludging → stroke, pulmonary oedema.
- **SVC Obstruction:** Associated w/ T-cell ALL mediastinal mass → airway compromise.
- **DIC:** Common in **Acute Promyelocytic Leukaemia (APML / AML M3)** → catastrophic haemorrhage.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside and Blood Tests
- **CBC & Differential:** Pancytopenia (or bi-cytopenia). WBC variable (↓, normal, or ↑↑).
- **Peripheral Blood Smear:** Crucial → circulating blasts (large size, high N:C ratio, prominent nucleoli).
- **Coagulation Profile:** PT/APTT/Fibrinogen/D-dimer → R/o DIC (esp. APML).
- **TLS Screen:** Renal function (Cr/Urea), Urate, Ca2+, PO4, K+.
### Imaging Studies
- **CXR:** Mandatory to screen for mediastinal mass (crucial before GA/sedation for bone marrow).
- **Ultrasound Abdomen:** Quantify organomegaly, assess renal size (urate nephropathy).
### Special and Gold Standard Tests
- **Bone Marrow Aspirate & Trephine (BMAT):** Definitive Dx (>20% blasts in marrow).
- **Flow Cytometry (Immunophenotyping):** Differentiates B-cell ALL, T-cell ALL, and AML.
- **Cytogenetics/Molecular (FISH/PCR):** Detects translocations (e.g., t(9;22), t(12;21)) for risk stratification.
- **Lumbar Puncture (LP):** Assess CSF for CNS blast infiltration (given intrathecal chemo simultaneously).
### Hospital Authority (HA) Monitoring Protocol
- **Minimal Residual Disease (MRD):** PCR/Flow Cytometry at specific post-induction timepoints → detects 1 leukaemic cell in 10^4-10^5 normal cells → guides therapy intensity.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute HA Oncology Emergency Protocols
- **Neutropenic Sepsis:** Immediate A&E admission → blood cultures → IV broad-spectrum Abx (e.g., **Tazocin/Piperacillin-Tazobactam**) within 1 hr.
- **Tumour Lysis Syndrome (TLS):** Hyperhydration (IV fluids w/o K+ @ 125-200% maintenance). Meds: PO **Allopurinol** or IV **Rasburicase** (for high WBC/urate/bulky disease). Monitor ECG.
- **Hyperleukocytosis (>100):** Aggressive hydration, cytoreduction, avoid packed RBC transfusion initially (prevents ↑ blood viscosity).
- **Symptomatic Anaemia/Bleeding:** Careful packed RBCs & Platelet transfusions.
### Definitive Chemotherapy (HKCH POC Protocol)
- **Phase 1: Induction (4-6 wks):** Eradicate blasts → achieve morphological remission (<5% blasts). Regimen: Steroids (**Dexamethasone/Prednisolone**) + **Vincristine** + **L-Asparaginase** + Anthracycline (**Daunorubicin**).
- **Phase 2: Consolidation:** Intensive block to prevent regrowth & target sanctuary sites.
- **Phase 3: Maintenance (2-3 yrs):** Prolonged low-dose chemo (Daily PO **Mercaptopurine** + Weekly PO **Methotrexate**) to prevent relapse.
### CNS-Directed Therapy
- Systemic chemo poorly crosses blood-brain barrier.
- Require repeated **Intrathecal (IT) Methotrexate/Cytarabine** via LP to prevent CNS relapse.
- Cranial irradiation reserved for extreme CNS disease.
### Supportive and Targeted Therapies
- **Pneumocystis Prophylaxis:** PO **Cotrimoxazole** daily or 3x/week throughout chemo.
- **Targeted TKI:** **Imatinib** added for Philadelphia chromosome +ve ALL.
- **Haematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT):** For high-risk ALL, relapsed ALL, and most AML.
- **CAR-T Cell Therapy:** Available at HKCH for relapsed/refractory B-cell ALL (e.g., Tisagenlecleucel).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### HKCH Multidisciplinary Support Team
- **Clinical Psychologists / Play Therapists:** Manage procedural anxiety, diagnosis adjustment, and medical trauma.
- **Medical Social Workers (MSW):** Arrange HA fee waivers, transport subsidies, housing adjustments.
- **Hospital School (Red Cross):** Maintain education during prolonged inpatient stays.
- **Children's Cancer Foundation (CCF):** Local NGO providing financial aid, sibling support, respite, and palliative care.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safety Netting for Parents
- MUST be educated on **neutropenic fever protocols** → attend A&E immediately for Temp ≥ 38.0°C (Do NOT give PO Paracetamol at home and wait).
### NAI Diagnostic Pitfall
- Child protection red flag: NEVER assume extensive unexplained bruising is Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) without first checking a CBC to rule out leukaemia/thrombocytopenia.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Prognosis (HK Statistics)
- **ALL:** 5-year survival >90%.
- **AML:** 5-year survival ~60-70%.
- **Poor ALL Prognostic Indicators:** Age <1 or >10 yrs, WBC >50 x 10^9/L, hypodiploidy, MLL rearrangement, slow response to induction (persistent MRD +ve).
### Late Effects of Therapy (Survivorship)
- **Anthracyclines (Daunorubicin):** Dose-dependent cardiotoxicity → dilated cardiomyopathy (Requires lifelong Echo).
- **Cranial Irradiation:** Neurocognitive deficits, endocrine dysfunction (GH deficiency, hypothyroidism).
- **Alkylating Agents:** Gonadal toxicity → infertility (Sperm/oocyte preservation required pre-treatment).
- **Steroids:** Avascular necrosis of bone, obesity, cataracts.
- **General:** Secondary malignancies (e.g., brain tumours, secondary AML).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the metabolic triad of Tumour Lysis Syndrome (TLS)? Answer: ↑ K+, ↑ Urate, ↑ PO4, ↓ Ca2+. (↓ Ca2+ occurs because calcium precipitates with massive efflux of intracellular PO4).
* Trap: Dismissing a limping child with nocturnal pain as "growing pains" or "transient synovitis" without a blood test. Pearl: Deep bone pain from marrow expansion is a classic early presentation of leukaemia; always check a CBC.
* Pitfall: Prescribing empirical steroids for unexplained lymphadenopathy or bone pain. This can partially treat leukaemia, mask the diagnosis, trigger fatal TLS, and ruin BMAT staging. ALWAYS obtain a definitive tissue diagnosis first.
* Viva Question: Why do we avoid transfusing packed RBCs in a newly diagnosed leukaemic patient with hyperleukocytosis (WBC >100)? Answer: It ↑ blood viscosity, worsening microvascular leukostasis and precipitating stroke or pulmonary oedema.
* Trap: Waiting for a fever to develop in a severely neutropenic patient to diagnose sepsis. Pearl: A neutropenic child might not mount a typical inflammatory response; clinical deterioration, lethargy, or hypothermia can be the only signs of life-threatening sepsis.
