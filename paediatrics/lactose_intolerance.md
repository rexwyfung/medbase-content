# Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Lactose Intolerance
**Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)**: Clinical syndrome characterized by **abdominal pain (腹痛)**, **bloating (腹脹)**, **flatulence (胃腸氣)**, & **diarrhea (腹瀉)** induced by lactose malabsorption due to **lactase (乳糖酶)** enzyme deficiency.

### Local Epidemiology of Lactose Intolerance in Hong Kong
* Extremely ↑ prevalence in Hong Kong.
* **80-90%** of adults of **East Asian descent** exhibit primary lactase non-persistence.
* Genetically programmed ↓ in lactase post-weaning.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Normal Lactose Digestion Mechanism
* **Lactase (乳糖酶)** is an enzyme located on the **brush border (刷狀緣)** of the small intestine.
* Splits lactose (disaccharide) → **glucose (葡萄糖)** + **galactose (半乳糖)** for absorption.

### Primary Lactase Non-Persistence Pathophysiology
* Normal genetic downregulation of the **LCT gene**.
* Gradual ↓ in lactase production post-weaning.
* Most common variant globally & locally in HK.

### Secondary Lactose Intolerance Pathophysiology
* Transient loss of brush border lactase directly following severe mucosal damage.
* Common triggers: 
    * **Severe viral gastroenteritis (腸胃炎)** (e.g., **Rotavirus (輪狀病毒)**).
    * Untreated **Coeliac disease (乳糜瀉)**.
    * Severe mucosal malnutrition/enteropathy.

### Mechanism of Osmotic Diarrhea in Lactose Intolerance
* Undigested lactose reaches the colon.
* Colonic bacteria ferment lactose → massive gas production (**H2, CO2, methane**).
* Unabsorbed sugars exert osmotic load → draws massive amounts of H2O into colonic lumen → **explosive osmotic diarrhea (滲透性腹瀉)**.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Typical Age of Onset for Lactose Intolerance
* **Primary**: Usually presents around age **4-5 years** (or older) as lactase levels naturally decline.
* **Secondary**: Immediately follows a severe bout of acute gastroenteritis in infants/toddlers.

### Timing of Symptoms Post-Ingestion
* Explosive onset of symptoms usually **30 mins to 2 hours** post-consumption of large amounts of dairy.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Gastrointestinal Symptoms of Lactose Intolerance
* **Explosive, watery, acidic diarrhea (水漾酸性腹瀉)**.
* Severe **bloating (腹脹)** & cramping.
* **Borborygmi (腸鳴)** (loud hyperactive bowel sounds).
* Massive **flatulence (胃腸氣)**.

### Dermatological Signs of Lactose Intolerance
* Extreme **perianal excoriation (肛周皮膚破損)**.
* Acidic stool (pH < 5.5) physically burns the infant's perianal skin.

### Absence of Systemic Symptoms in Pure Lactose Intolerance
* **NO** systemic immune symptoms:
    * **-ve** Fever.
    * **-ve** Weight loss (in older children).
    * **-ve** Blood in stool (no hematochezia).
    * **-ve** Urticaria or anaphylaxis.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Lactose Intolerance from Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA)
* **CMPA (牛奶蛋白過敏)**: Immune-mediated (IgE or non-IgE). Occurs in **small infants**. Often presents with **bloody stools (血便)**, **eczema (濕疹)**, or anaphylaxis.
* **Lactose Intolerance**: Purely a digestive enzyme deficiency. Occurs in **older kids** (primary) or post-infection (secondary). **NEVER** causes anaphylaxis or bloody stools.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Major Differential Diagnoses for Childhood Diarrhea
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)** | Watery/acidic stool, extreme perianal excoriation, flatulence, NO blood, older child or post-gastroenteritis. |
| **Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA)** | Small infants, immune-mediated, **bloody streaks in stool**, eczema, urticaria, wheezing. |
| **Toddler's Diarrhoea (幼兒腹瀉)** | "Peas and carrots" in stool (undigested food), age 1-3, thriving well, no malabsorption, related to ↑ fruit juice/sugar intake. |
| **Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉)** | Autoimmune response to gluten. Faltering growth, steatorrhea, anemia, positive anti-tTG IgA. |
| **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)** | Chronic abdo pain relieved by defecation, alternating bowel habits, normal growth, diagnosis of exclusion. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Clinical Trial Investigations for Lactose Intolerance
* **Best First Step**: Clinical trial of strict lactose elimination for **2 weeks**.
* **+ve Result**: Complete resolution of symptoms, followed by relapse upon reintroduction of dairy.

### Stool Investigations for Lactose Malabsorption
* **Stool pH**: **Reduced (Acidic, pH < 5.5)** due to bacterial fermentation.
* **Stool Reducing Substances (糞便還原糖測試)**: **+ve** (detects unabsorbed sugars like lactose).

### Gold Standard Special Investigation for Lactose Intolerance
* **Hydrogen Breath Test (氫氣呼氣測試)** (Definitive test).
* **Mechanism**: Baseline breath H2 measured → oral lactose load given → breath H2 measured serially.
* **+ve Result**: ↑ of **>20 ppm** hydrogen over baseline (indicates colonic fermentation of unabsorbed lactose).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Secondary Lactose Intolerance (HA Protocols)
* Temporary switch to **Lactose-free formula (無乳糖配方奶粉)**.
* Duration: **4-8 weeks** post-severe gastroenteritis to allow complete regeneration of the intestinal brush border.
* Gradually reintroduce standard formula/dairy after brush border healing.

### Chronic Management of Primary Lactose Intolerance
* **Dietary Restriction**: Limit heavily lactose-laden foods (milk, ice cream).
* **Tolerated Dairy**: Hard cheeses & yogurts are often tolerated (bacteria pre-digest the lactose).
* **Exogenous Enzyme Replacement**: **Lactase enzyme drops/tablets (乳糖酶補充劑)** taken simultaneously with dairy consumption.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Dietetic Team Involvement for Lactose Intolerance
* Referral to **Hospital Authority (HA) Dietitian (營養師)**.
* **Goal**: Ensure adequate **Calcium (鈣)** & **Vitamin D (維生素D)** intake despite milk restriction.
* **Alternatives**: Fortified soy/almond milks, dark leafy greens, tofu, calcium supplements.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Red Flags for Neglect in Chronic Diarrhea
* Chronic unchecked secondary lactose intolerance post-infection in an infant → massive dehydration.
* Can lead to catastrophic **Failure to Thrive (生長遲緩)**.
* Must assess for medical neglect if parents refuse to adapt diet despite severe weight loss and clear medical advice.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-Term Complications of Dairy Exclusion
* **Osteopenia / Osteoporosis (骨質疏鬆症)** later in life.
* Requires vigilant Calcium/Vitamin D substitution via diet or supplements.

### Prognosis of Primary Lactase Non-Persistence
* Excellent prognosis.
* Not a "disease" per se, but a normal human physiological/genetic variant affecting the majority of the global adult population (especially in East Asia).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Confusing CMPA with Lactose Intolerance. Pearl: True congenital lactase deficiency is extraordinarily rare. Lactose intolerance does not cause bleeding, eczema, or immune reactions.
* Viva Question: A 2-month-old formula-fed baby presents with bloody streaks in stool and eczema. Is this lactose intolerance? Answer: Absolutely NOT. This is the classic presentation of Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA). Lactose intolerance does not cause bleeding or immune reactions.
* Pitfall: Recommending permanent lactose-free formula for post-gastroenteritis diarrhea. Secondary lactose intolerance is transient; normal dairy should be reintroduced after 4-8 weeks once the brush border heals to prevent unnecessary dietary restriction.
