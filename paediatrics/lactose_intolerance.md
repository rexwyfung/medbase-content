# Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: The clinical syndrome of abdominal pain, bloating, flatulence, and diarrhea induced by lactose malabsorption.
- **Local Epidemiology**: **Extremely high prevalence**. Upwards of 80-90% of adults of East Asian descent have primary lactase non-persistence (genetically programmed decline in lactase).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Lactase** is an enzyme on the brush border of the small intestine that splits lactose into glucose and galactose.
- **Primary**: Normal genetic downregulation of the *LCT* gene after weaning.
- **Secondary**: Loss of brush border enzymes directly following severe mucosal damage (e.g., severe viral gastroenteritis like Rotavirus, untreated Coeliac disease).
- Undigested lactose reaches the colon, where bacteria ferment it, producing gas (H2, CO2, methane) and drawing in massive amounts of water via osmosis.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Usually presents around age 4-5 (if primary) or immediately after a nasty bout of gastroenteritis (if secondary).
- Onset of symptoms is explosive, usually 30 mins to 2 hours after consuming large amounts of dairy.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Explosive, watery, acidic diarrhea.
- Extreme perianal excoriation (the acidic stool literally burns the perianal skin).
- Severe bloating, cramping, borborygmi (loud bowel sounds), and massive flatulence.
- NO systemic symptoms (no fever, no weight loss if older, no blood in stool).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA). CMPA involves the immune system (IgE or non-IgE), occurs in small infants, often causes bloody stools, eczema, or anaphylaxis. Lactose intolerance is purely a digestive enzyme deficiency, occurs in older kids, and never causes anaphylaxis or blood.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Cow's milk protein allergy (CMPA)
- Toddler's Diarrhoea
- Coeliac Disease
- Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Clinical trial (Best first step)**: Strict elimination of lactose for 2 weeks with resolution of symptoms, followed by relapse upon reintroduction.
- **Stool tests**: Reduced stool pH (acidic, pH < 5.5), positive test for reducing substances.
- **Special (Definitive)**: **Hydrogen Breath Test**. A baseline measurement followed by a lactose load; an increase of >20 ppm hydrogen implies colonic fermentation of unabsorbed lactose.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Primary**: Dietary restriction of heavily lactose-laden foods (milk, ice cream). Hard cheeses and yogurts (where bacteria have pre-digested lactose) are often tolerated. Use of exogenous lactase enzyme drops/tablets taken with dairy.
- **Secondary**: Temporary switch to a lactose-free formula for a few weeks after severe gastroenteritis until the brush border fully regenerates.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Dietetic team to ensure adequate Calcium and Vitamin D intake via alternative nutritional sources (fortified soy/almond milks, dark leafy greens) since milk is restricted.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Chronic unchecked secondary lactose intolerance post-infection in an infant can lead to massive dehydration and catastrophic failure to thrive.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Osteopenia/Osteoporosis later in life if calcium/vitamin D are not adequately substituted.
- Prognosis for primary non-persistence is standard; it is a normal human physiological quirk, not a "disease" per se.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Confusing CMPA with Lactose Intolerance. 
- **Viva Q**: "A 2-month-old formula-fed baby presents with bloody streaks in stool and eczema. Is this lactose intolerance?" Answer: Absolutely NOT. True congenital lactase deficiency is extraordinarily rare. This is the classic presentation of Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA). Lactose intolerance does not cause bleeding or immune reactions.
