# Childhood Asthma (兒童哮喘)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* Chronic inflammatory airway disease.
* Characterized by reversible airway obstruction (**可逆性氣道阻塞**).
* Characterized by airway inflammation (**氣道發炎**).
* Characterized by bronchial hyperresponsiveness (**氣管過敏**).

### Local HK Epidemiology
* Prevalence: ~10% in HK children.
* Triggers: Highly correlated with local Air Quality Health Index (AQHI ↑), high humidity, & household dust mites (**塵蟎**).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Immune Pathophysiology
* Allergen/Trigger → IgE cross-linking on mast cells.
* Histamine/Leukotriene release (Type I Hypersensitivity / **I型過敏反應**).
* Eosinophilic/lymphocytic infiltration → Mucosal oedema (**黏膜水腫**).
* Smooth muscle bronchoconstriction (**支氣管收縮**).
* Mucus plugging (**黏液栓塞**).

### Genetic Predisposition
* Polygenic inheritance.
* Strong association with **Atopy (**特應性**)** triad: Asthma, Eczema (**濕疹**), Allergic Rhinitis (**過敏性鼻炎**).
* Filaggrin gene mutations → ↑ risk.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Clinical Phenotypes in Preschoolers (<5yrs)
* **Viral Episodic Wheeze (**病毒性發作性喘鳴**)**: 
  * Wheeze ONLY during viral URTI. 
  * No interval symptoms. 
  * Usually resolves by age 5.
* **Multiple Trigger Wheeze (**多誘因性喘鳴**)**: 
  * Triggered by viruses, allergens, exercise, cold air. 
  * Interval symptoms present. 
  * Progresses to true asthma.

### Chronic Interval Symptoms
* **Wheeze (**喘鳴**)**: High-pitched expiratory polyphonic sound.
* **Cough (**咳嗽**)**: Dry, non-productive. Worse at night/early morning.
* **Shortness of Breath (**氣促**)** / Dyspnoea.
* **Chest Tightness (**胸悶**)**.

### Chronic Interval Signs
* Usually normal between attacks.
* Hyperinflated chest (**胸部過度充氣**).
* **Harrison sulci (**哈里森溝**)** in severe chronic cases.

### Acute Exacerbation Signs
* Tachypnoea (**呼吸急促**).
* Tachycardia.
* Widespread polyphonic expiratory wheeze (**多音性呼氣喘鳴**).
* Prolonged expiratory phase (**呼氣期延長**).
* Subcostal/intercostal recession (**肋間凹陷**).
* Tracheal tug.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Reversibility and Diurnal Variation
* **Reversibility**: Dramatic clinical / spirometric improvement post-**SABA (**短效β2受體激動劑**)**.
* **Diurnal Variation**: ↑ symptoms at night / waking from sleep.

### Interval Symptoms and Atopy
* **Interval Symptoms (**發作間歇期症狀**)**: Persistent underlying inflammation between acute attacks (e.g., exercise-induced).
* **Atopic Background**: Concomitant rhinitis/eczema strongly favors asthma vs anatomical anomalies.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentials by Clinical Presentation
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bronchiolitis (**細支氣管炎**)** | Age < 1 yr (RSV). Preceding coryza, widespread fine crackles + wheeze. |
| **Foreign Body (**異物吸入**)** | Sudden onset, unilateral wheeze/↓ breath sounds, focal hyperinflation. |
| **Pneumonia (**肺炎**)** | High fever, focal crackles/dullness, bronchial breathing. |
| **Croup (**哮吼**)** | Inspiratory stridor (**吸氣性喘鳴**), barking cough, hoarse voice. |
| **Anaphylaxis (**過敏性休克**)** | Acute onset wheeze + urticaria, angioedema, hypotension. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Clinical Diagnosis
* Standard for children < 5 yrs (based on history, exam, and trial of treatment).

### First-Line Investigations (> 5 yrs)
* **Spirometry (**肺活量測定**)**: Obstructive pattern → FEV1 ↓, FVC normal, **FEV1/FVC < 80%**.
* **Bronchodilator Reversibility**: ↑ FEV1 ≥12% post-SABA inhalation.

### Second-Line Investigations
* **PEFR (**最高呼氣流量**)**: Diurnal variability > 20% over 2-4 weeks.
* **FeNO (**呼出氣一氧化氮**)**: > 35 ppb → active eosinophilic airway inflammation.

### Adjunct Investigations
* **Allergy testing**: Skin prick test (**皮膚點刺測試**) or specific IgE (RAST) for triggers.
* **CXR**: Unnecessary routinely. Shows hyperinflation (flat diaphragm, horizontal ribs). Excludes pneumothorax.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Exacerbation Severity Assessment (HA Protocol)
* **Mild/Moderate**: SpO2 ≥ 92%, no clinical features of severe asthma.
* **Severe**: SpO2 < 92%, too breathless to talk/feed, HR > 125 (1-5 yrs) or > 110 (>5 yrs), RR > 40 (1-5 yrs) or > 30 (>5 yrs), use of accessory muscles.
* **Life-Threatening**: Silent chest, cyanosis, poor respiratory effort, exhaustion, confusion.

### Acute Exacerbation Management (HA Protocol)
* **Mild/Moderate**:
  * O2 via nasal cannula/mask → target SpO2 94-98%.
  * **SABA**: Salbutamol (**沙丁胺醇**) via pMDI + spacer (up to 10 puffs) OR Nebulized.
  * **Systemic Steroids**: Oral Prednisolone (1-2 mg/kg) x 3-5 days.
* **Severe / Life-Threatening**:
  * High-flow O2.
  * Nebulized Salbutamol + **Ipratropium bromide (**異丙托溴銨**)**.
  * IV Hydrocortisone (if oral intolerable).
  * **IV Magnesium Sulphate (**硫酸鎂**)**.
  * Escalation: IV Aminophylline / IV Salbutamol → PICU for CPAP/Intubation.

### Chronic Stepwise Management (GINA Age-Dependent Guidelines)
| Step | < 5 Years | 6-11 Years | ≥ 12 Years (MART Track Preferred) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | PRN **SABA (**短效β2受體激動劑**)** | PRN **ICS-Formoterol** OR PRN SABA + ICS | PRN low-dose **ICS-Formoterol** |
| **2** | Daily low-dose **ICS (**吸入性皮質類固醇**)** | Daily low-dose ICS OR PRN ICS-Formoterol | PRN low-dose ICS-Formoterol |
| **3** | Double low-dose ICS | Low-dose **ICS-LABA (**長效β2受體激動劑**)** OR Medium-dose ICS | Low-dose ICS-Formoterol (**MART (**維持及緩解治療**)**) |
| **4** | Continue Controller + Refer Specialist | Medium-dose ICS-LABA | Medium-dose ICS-Formoterol (MART) |
| **5** | Specialist escalation (Add **LTRA (**白三烯受體拮抗劑**)**) | Refer for phenotypic assessment ± **Biologics (**生物製劑**)** | Add **LAMA (**長效毒蕈鹼拮抗劑**)** / Refer for Biologics (e.g., Omalizumab) |

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Patient Education and Action Plan
* **Asthma Action Plan (**哮喘行動計劃**)**: 
  * Written traffic-light system (Green/Yellow/Red) for parents/schools.
  * Details when to step up therapy/seek emergency care.
* **Inhaler Technique**: 
  * Check inhaler technique EVERY visit. 
  * pMDI + spacer ALWAYS for kids (mask <4 yrs, mouthpiece >4 yrs).

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* Respiratory nurse specialist (education/monitoring).
* School nurse (medication coordination).
* Medical social worker (home environment/housing, addressing poverty/overcrowding triggers).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Indicators of Medical Neglect
* Repeated ED visits for severe attacks due to parental failure to administer preventers.
* Chronic defaulted HA outpatient follow-ups.
* Exposing child to heavy indoor smoking despite medical warnings.
* Unjustified/excessive school absences.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute and Chronic Complications
* **Acute Complications**: 
  * Status asthmaticus.
  * Pneumothorax/pneumomediastinum.
  * Respiratory failure.
  * Cardiac arrest.
* **Chronic Complications**: 
  * **Airway remodeling (**氣道重塑**)** → fixed, irreversible obstruction.
* **Iatrogenic Complications**: 
  * **Oral candidiasis (**口腔念珠菌病**)** from ICS (rinse mouth post-use).
  * **Growth faltering (**生長遲緩**)** from high-dose ICS (monitor height).

### Long-term Prognosis
* Excellent overall. 
* Most childhood asthma improves or resolves by adolescence.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Pitfall: Prescribing a pMDI without a **spacer (**儲霧器**)**. NEVER do this in paediatrics; coordination impossible for young children. 
* Viva Question: Why is wheeze absent in a silent chest? Answer: Extreme bronchoconstriction prevents airflow generation; indicates impending respiratory arrest → immediate escalation.
* Viva Question: Distinguish viral episodic wheeze from asthma. Answer: Viral wheeze resolves entirely between URTI episodes; asthma has interval symptoms (exercise/cold triggers).
* Pitfall: Finger clubbing is NOT a sign of asthma. If present → investigate for Cystic Fibrosis, Bronchiectasis, or Congenital Heart Disease immediately.
* Viva Question: What is the rationale behind GINA MART strategy in ≥12 yrs? Answer: Using ICS-Formoterol as both preventer and reliever ensures patient receives an anti-inflammatory dose every time they experience symptoms, reducing severe exacerbation risk.
