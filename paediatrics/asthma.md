# Childhood Asthma (兒童哮喘)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Childhood Asthma (兒童哮喘)**: Chronic inflammatory airway disease → reversible airway obstruction (**可逆性氣道阻塞**), mucosal airway inflammation (**氣道發炎**), & bronchial hyperresponsiveness (**氣管過敏**).

### HK Epidemiology
* **Prevalence**: ~10% in HK children. Most common chronic childhood illness.
* **Triggers**: ↑ correlation with local Air Quality Health Index (AQHI), high humidity (spring/summer), & household dust mites (**塵蟎**).
* **Morbidity**: Major cause of school absence & paediatric emergency admissions in HK.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology
* Allergen/Trigger → IgE cross-linking on mast cells → Histamine/Leukotriene release (Type I Hypersensitivity / **I型過敏反應**).
* Eosinophilic/lymphocytic mucosal infiltration → Mucosal oedema (**黏膜水腫**).
* Smooth muscle bronchoconstriction (**支氣管收縮**).
* Mucus hypersecretion & plugging (**黏液栓塞**).
* Chronic phase: Airway remodeling (**氣道重塑**) → fixed, irreversible obstruction.

### Genetics
* Polygenic inheritance.
* Strong association with **Atopy (**特應性**)** triad: Asthma, Eczema (**濕疹**), Allergic Rhinitis (**過敏性鼻炎**).
* Filaggrin gene mutations → ↑ epithelial permeability → ↑ risk of sensitization.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Phenotypes (<5 yrs)
* **Viral Episodic Wheeze (**病毒性發作性喘鳴**)**: 
  * Wheeze ONLY during viral URTI (e.g., RSV, Rhinovirus). 
  * No interval symptoms. 
  * Usually resolves by age 5.
* **Multiple Trigger Wheeze (**多誘因性喘鳴**)**: 
  * Triggered by viruses, allergens, exercise, cold air. 
  * Interval symptoms present. 
  * High risk of progression to true asthma.

### Acute Exacerbation (**急性發作**)
* Rapid onset respiratory distress.
* Tachypnoea (**呼吸急促**).
* Accessory muscle use / recession (**肋骨下凹陷**).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Symptoms
* **Wheeze (**喘鳴**)**: High-pitched, expiratory polyphonic sound.
* **Cough (**咳嗽**)**: Dry, non-productive. Worse at night / early morning.
* **Shortness of Breath (**氣促**)** / Dyspnoea.
* **Chest Tightness (**胸悶**)**.

### Signs (Interval/Chronic)
* Often normal.
* Hyperinflated chest (**胸部過度充氣**).
* Harrison sulci (**哈里森溝**) in severe, chronic, poorly-controlled cases.
* Co-existing atopic signs: Eczema, allergic shiners, transverse nasal crease (allergic salute).

### Signs (Acute Exacerbation)
* Tachypnoea (**呼吸急促**).
* Tachycardia (**心跳過速**).
* Widespread polyphonic expiratory wheeze (**多音性呼氣喘鳴**).
* Prolonged expiratory phase (**呼氣期延長**).
* Subcostal/intercostal/sternal recession (**肋間凹陷**).
* Tracheal tug.
* Silent chest (**靜寂胸**) in life-threatening attacks (no wheeze due to inadequate air entry).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Key Differentiators
* **Reversibility**: Dramatic clinical / spirometric improvement post-**SABA (**短效β2受體激動劑**)**.
* **Diurnal Variation**: ↑ symptoms at night / waking from sleep / early morning.
* **Interval Symptoms (**發作間歇期症狀**)**: Persistent underlying inflammation between acute attacks (e.g., exercise-induced cough/wheeze).
* **Atopic Background**: Concomitant rhinitis/eczema or +ve family history strongly favors asthma vs anatomical anomalies.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentials Table
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bronchiolitis (**細支氣管炎**)** | Age < 1 yr (RSV). Preceding coryza, widespread fine crackles + wheeze. Poor response to SABA. |
| **Foreign Body Aspiration (**異物吸入**)** | Sudden onset, unilateral wheeze / ↓ breath sounds, focal hyperinflation on CXR. History of choking. |
| **Pneumonia (**肺炎**)** | High fever, focal crackles/dullness, bronchial breathing. Asymmetrical signs. |
| **Croup (**哮吼**)** | Inspiratory stridor (**吸氣性喘鳴**), barking cough, hoarse voice, parainfluenza virus. |
| **Cystic Fibrosis (**囊性纖維化**)** | Faltering growth, chronic wet/productive cough, finger clubbing (**杵狀指**), steatorrhoea. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Primary Diagnosis
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Standard for children < 5 yrs (history of recurrent wheeze, +ve response to trial of bronchodilators/ICS).

### 1st Line (> 5 yrs)
* **Spirometry (**肺活量測定**)**: Obstructive pattern → FEV1 ↓, FVC normal, **FEV1/FVC < 80%** (or < lower limit of normal).
* **Bronchodilator Reversibility**: ↑ FEV1 ≥12% post-SABA inhalation.

### 2nd Line
* **PEFR (**最高呼氣流量**)**: Diurnal variability > 20% over 2-4 weeks monitoring.
* **FeNO (**呼出氣一氧化氮**)**: > 35 ppb in children → active eosinophilic airway inflammation.

### Adjuncts
* **Allergy testing**: Skin prick test (**皮膚點刺測試**) or specific IgE (RAST) for aeroallergens/food triggers.
* **CXR**: Unnecessary routinely. Indicated only if focal signs, atypical history, or suspecting complications (pneumothorax). Shows hyperinflation (flat diaphragm, horizontal ribs).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Exacerbation (HA Protocol)
* **Severity Assessment**: SpO2, RR, HR, accessory muscle use, conscious level, speech (sentences vs words).
* **Mild/Moderate**:
  * O2 via nasal cannula/mask → target SpO2 94-98%.
  * **SABA**: Salbutamol (**沙丁胺醇**) via pMDI + spacer (up to 10 puffs) OR Nebulized.
  * **Systemic Steroids**: Oral Prednisolone (1-2 mg/kg, max 40mg) x 3-5 days.
* **Severe**:
  * High-flow O2.
  * Nebulized Salbutamol + **Ipratropium bromide (**異丙托溴銨**)**.
  * IV Hydrocortisone (if oral intolerable).
* **Life-Threatening**:
  * **IV Magnesium Sulphate (**硫酸鎂**)**.
  * Escalation: IV Aminophylline / IV Salbutamol → PICU for CPAP/Intubation.

### Chronic Stepwise Management (GINA / HA Paediatric Guidelines)
| Step | < 5 Years | 6-11 Years | ≥ 12 Years (MART Track Preferred) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | PRN **SABA (**短效β2受體激動劑**)** | PRN **ICS-Formoterol** OR PRN SABA + ICS | PRN low-dose **ICS-Formoterol** |
| **2** | Daily low-dose **ICS (**吸入性皮質類固醇**)** | Daily low-dose ICS OR PRN ICS-Formoterol | PRN low-dose ICS-Formoterol |
| **3** | Double low-dose ICS | Low-dose **ICS-LABA (**長效β2受體激動劑**)** OR Medium-dose ICS | Low-dose ICS-Formoterol (**MART (**維持及緩解治療**)**) |
| **4** | Continue Controller + Refer Specialist | Medium-dose ICS-LABA | Medium-dose ICS-Formoterol (MART) |
| **5** | Specialist escalation (Add **LTRA (**白三烯受體拮抗劑**)**) | Refer for phenotypic assessment ± **Biologics (**生物製劑**)** | Add **LAMA (**長效毒蕈鹼拮抗劑**)** / Refer for Biologics (e.g., Omalizumab, Mepolizumab) |

*Note: Montelukast (LTRA) is often trialled early in HK due to high overlap with allergic rhinitis.*

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Roles
* **Asthma Action Plan (**哮喘行動計劃**)**: 
  * Written traffic-light system (Green/Yellow/Red) for parents/schools.
  * Details when to step up therapy / seek emergency care.
* **Education (Respiratory Nurse Specialist)**: 
  * Check inhaler technique EVERY visit. 
  * pMDI + spacer ALWAYS for kids (mask <4 yrs, mouthpiece >4 yrs).
* **School Nurse**: Coordination of PRN inhalers before PE / during school hours.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Assess home environment (dust mites, mold, passive smoking), provide housing/financial support.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect (**醫療疏忽**)
* Repeated ED visits for severe attacks due to parental failure to administer preventers.
* Chronic defaulted HA outpatient follow-ups.
* Exposing child to heavy indoor smoking despite medical warnings.
* Unjustified/excessive school absences.
* *Action*: Multidisciplinary case conference + Medical Social Worker (MSW) / Child Protection Team involvement.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Complications
* **Acute**: 
  * Status asthmaticus.
  * Pneumothorax / pneumomediastinum (**氣胸/縱隔氣腫**).
  * Respiratory failure → PICU admission.
  * Cardiac arrest / Death.
* **Chronic**: 
  * **Airway remodeling (**氣道重塑**)** → fixed, irreversible airway obstruction.
  * Psychological impact (anxiety, exclusion from sports).
* **Iatrogenic**: 
  * **Oral candidiasis (**口腔念珠菌病**)** from ICS (prevent: rinse mouth/brush teeth post-use).
  * **Growth faltering (**生長遲緩**)** from high-dose ICS (monitor height/weight centiles).
  * Tremor / Tachycardia from high-dose SABA.

### Prognosis
* Excellent for most.
* Viral episodic wheeze usually resolves by mid-childhood.
* True atopic asthma may improve during adolescence but can persist or relapse in adulthood.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why is wheeze absent in a silent chest? Answer: Extreme bronchoconstriction prevents sufficient airflow generation to produce a wheeze; indicates impending respiratory arrest requiring immediate escalation.
* Trap: Using finger clubbing as a sign of severe asthma. Pearl: Finger clubbing is NEVER a sign of asthma; if present, investigate immediately for Cystic Fibrosis, Bronchiectasis, or congenital heart disease.
* Pitfall: Prescribing a pMDI without a spacer for a child. A spacer must ALWAYS be prescribed with a pMDI in paediatrics, as coordinating inhalation with actuation is impossible for young children.
* Viva Question: How do you distinguish viral episodic wheeze from multiple trigger asthma? Answer: Viral wheeze resolves entirely between URTI episodes; multiple trigger asthma has persistent interval symptoms triggered by exercise, cold air, or allergens.
* Viva Question: What is the rationale behind the GINA MART strategy in adolescents? Answer: Using ICS-Formoterol as both a daily preventer and a PRN reliever ensures the patient receives an anti-inflammatory dose every time they experience symptoms, reducing the risk of severe exacerbations.
