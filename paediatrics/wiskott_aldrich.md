# Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome (威斯科特-奧爾德里奇綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* **Primary Immunodeficiency (原發性免疫缺陷)**
* X-linked recessive (X連鎖隱性遺傳) inheritance
* Characterized by classic triad: **Microthrombocytopenia (微血小板減少症)**, **Eczema (濕疹)**, and **Severe Immune Dysfunction (免疫功能障礙)**

### Local Epidemiology of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome in Hong Kong
* Extremely rare in HK; managed exclusively at **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH)**
* Exclusively affects males (females are asymptomatic carriers)
* Incidence: ~1-10 per 1 million live male births

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Basis of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* Mutation in **WAS gene** on **X chromosome (Xp11.22-p11.23)**
* Encodes **Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome Protein (WASP)**

### Immunological Pathophysiology of WASP Deficiency
* **WASP** critical for **actin cytoskeleton reorganization (肌動蛋白細胞骨架重組)** in hematopoietic cells
* ↓ WASP → Defective T-cell/B-cell immunological synapse formation
* ↓ T-cell function → Cellular immunodeficiency
* Defective B-cell response to polysaccharide antigens (e.g., encapsulated bacteria)

### Hematological Pathophysiology of WASP Deficiency
* Megakaryocytes lack normal actin structure → Produce highly defective, **micro-platelets (微小血小板)**
* Micro-platelets undergo rapid destruction in spleen → **Profound thrombocytopenia (血小板減少症)**

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Classic Clinical Presentation of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* Male infant (usually <6 months old)
* **Prolonged bleeding (延長出血)** (e.g., post-circumcision, bloody diarrhea from birth)
* Recurrent severe respiratory or ear infections
* Intractable skin rashes

### Classic Triad of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome (WATER Mnemonic)
* **W**iskott-**A**ldrich
* **T**hrombocytopenia (血小板減少症): Petechiae (瘀點), purpura, spontaneous GI bleeding/bloody stools, epistaxis
* **E**czema (濕疹): Severe, weeping, highly prone to secondary *Staphylococcus aureus* infection
* **R**ecurrent Infections (反覆感染): Otitis media, pneumonia, sepsis (primarily encapsulated bacteria: *S. pneumoniae*, *H. influenzae*; later opportunistic viruses/fungi)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Discriminatory Features vs Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP)
* **ITP:** Normal or large platelets (↑ MPV), normal immune function, no chronic severe eczema
* **WAS:** Distinctly tiny platelets (**↓ Mean Platelet Volume - MPV**), severe eczema, recurrent infections

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis Table for Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features | Platelet Count / MPV | Immune Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP)** | Isolated bruising/bleeding post-viral illness; NO severe eczema | ↓ Count / **Normal or ↑ MPV** | Normal |
| **Hyper-IgE Syndrome (Job Syndrome)** | Coarse facial features, retained primary teeth, cold staph abscesses | Normal / Normal | ↑↑ IgE, normal platelets |
| **X-linked Agammaglobulinemia (XLA)** | Recurrent infections starting >6 months (loss of maternal IgG) | Normal / Normal | ↓↓ All Ig classes, no eczema |
| **Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID)** | Failure to thrive, severe opportunistic infections from birth | Normal / Normal | Profound ↓ T & B cells |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations for Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* **Skin swabs:** Culture weeping eczema lesions for *Staph aureus* or Herpes Simplex (Eczema herpeticum)

### Blood Investigations for Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC):** ↓ Platelets (<50,000/μL), **↓↓ Mean Platelet Volume (MPV)** (Pathognomonic)
* **Immunoglobulin Profile:** * **↓ IgM** (Defective polysaccharide response)
  * **Normal/↓ IgG**
  * **↑ IgA**
  * **↑ IgE**
* **Lymphocyte Subsets:** Progressive decline in T-cell (CD3, CD4, CD8) counts
* **Vaccine Response:** Absent or poor antibody response to Pneumococcal vaccine (polysaccharide antigens)

### Definitive Diagnostic Investigations for Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* **Flow Cytometry:** Shows absent or significantly ↓ **WASP protein** inside lymphocytes
* **Genetic Sequencing:** Confirms **WAS gene mutation** (Gold Standard)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management of Bleeding & Infection
* **Catastrophic Bleeding (GI/Intracranial):** Acute **Platelet Transfusion (血小板輸血)** (Avoid routine use → prevents alloimmunization)
* **Sepsis:** Prompt broad-spectrum IV antibiotics (e.g., Ceftriaxone) according to local HA guidelines

### Chronic & Prophylactic Management Protocols
| Management Category | Specific Interventions (HK HA Protocol) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Infection Prophylaxis** | • **IVIG (靜脈注射免疫球蛋白) replacement** every 3-4 weeks<br>• Prophylactic Co-trimoxazole (PCP prophylaxis)<br>• Avoid live vaccines (MMR, BCG, Varicella) |
| **Eczema Control** | • Aggressive topical emollients & high-potency topical corticosteroids<br>• Prompt treatment of bacterial superinfections |
| **Bleeding Precautions** | • Helmet for toddlers<br>• Avoid contact sports, NSAIDs, IM injections |
| **Splenectomy** | • **Avoid if possible** due to ↑↑ functional asplenia sepsis risk<br>• Only for life-threatening, refractory thrombocytopenia |

### Curative Management for Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* **Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT) (造血幹細胞移植):** Only definitive cure; usually performed at HKCH; ideally matched sibling donor
* **Gene Therapy:** Advanced clinical trials for patients without matched HSCT donors

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* **Pediatric Immunology/Hematology:** Lead coordinators for IVIG, HSCT planning
* **Pediatric Dermatology:** Refractory eczema management
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW):** Financial assistance for lifelong treatments (e.g., Samaritan Fund for specific biologics/transplant costs)
* **Clinical Psychology:** Support for family coping with high mortality risk and chronic illness burden

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) vs Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* Extensive, unexplained bruising in a non-mobile infant mimics physical abuse
* **Immediate Discriminator:** CBC showing profound microthrombocytopenia immediately points to a medical/hematological etiology rather than NAI
* Always verify CBC/coagulation profile before triggering MSW/Police interventions for bruising in sick infants

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Major Complications of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* **Massive Hemorrhage:** Intracranial hemorrhage (ICH) or severe gastrointestinal bleeding
* **Autoimmune Disorders (~40%):** Autoimmune Hemolytic Anemia (AIHA), Vasculitis, Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)
* **Malignancies (~10-20%):** Extremely high risk of **EBV-driven B-cell Lymphomas** and leukemias in adolescence/adulthood

### Long-Term Prognosis of Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
* Without HSCT: Poor prognosis; mortality usually in first 1-2 decades due to infection, bleeding, or malignancy
* With successful early HSCT: Excellent long-term survival and resolution of hematological/immunological defects

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Assuming a male infant with petechiae and low platelets just has viral ITP. Pearl: Always check the eczema history and the MPV; ITP has large/normal platelets, WAS has tiny platelets.
* Viva Question: What is the classic immunoglobulin profile seen in Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome? Answer: Low IgM, normal IgG, High IgA, and High IgE. (Mnemonic: WASP makes IgM go down to the ground, but IgA and IgE fly high).
* Pitfall: Routinely transfusing platelets for minor bleeding in WAS. Avoid this as it causes alloimmunization, making future curative HSCT much more difficult and increasing risk of graft rejection.
* Viva Question: What is the primary cause of death in adolescent patients with Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome who did not receive a transplant? Answer: EBV-associated malignancies (B-cell lymphoma) and autoimmune complications.
* Trap: Giving routine childhood vaccinations. Pearl: Live attenuated vaccines (BCG, rotavirus, MMR) are absolutely contraindicated due to severe T-cell deficiency.
