# Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome (威斯科特-奧爾德里奇綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A rare X-linked recessive primary immunodeficiency characterized by microthrombocytopenia, eczema, and severe immune dysfunction.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Mutation in the **WAS** gene on the X chromosome, which encodes the Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome Protein (WASP).
- WASP is critical for actin cytoskeleton reorganization in hematopoietic cells.
- Without WASP, T-cells cannot form immunological synapses to communicate with B-cells, and megakaryocytes produce highly defective, tiny platelets that are rapidly destroyed in the spleen.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A 6-month-old male infant who has had bloody diarrhea since birth, develops severe weeping eczema, and keeps getting admitted for otitis media and pneumonia.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- The classic triad (WATER):
  - **W**iskott-**A**ldrich
  - **T**hrombocytopenia: Causes petechiae, easy bruising, and spontaneous bloody stools.
  - **E**czema: Usually very severe, difficult to treat, and highly prone to secondary Staph aureus infections.
  - **R**ecurrent Infections: Encapsulated bacteria (Pneumococcus, H. influenzae) and opportunistic viruses/fungi.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from ITP (Idiopathic Thrombocytopenic Purpura): ITP has *large* or normal platelets. Wiskott-Aldrich has distinctly **tiny (micro) platelets** (Low Mean Platelet Volume - MPV).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenia)
- Severe Atopic Dermatitis with secondary infection
- Hyper-IgE Syndrome (Job Syndrome - has eczema and infections, but normal platelets and coarse facial features)
- XLA (has infections, but no eczema or bleeding)

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **CBC**: Low platelets (<50,000) with incredibly low MPV (Mean Platelet Volume).
- **Immunoglobulins**: Classic pattern is **Low IgM**, normal IgG, and **High IgA and IgE**.
- **Definitive**: Flow cytometry showing absent WASP protein inside lymphocytes, followed by genetic sequencing of the WAS gene.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Supportive**: Aggressive eczema care. Prophylactic antibiotics. IVIG replacement.
- **Bleeding**: Platelet transfusions for acute catastrophic bleeding, but avoid routine use to prevent alloimmunization. Splenectomy significantly improves the platelet count but massively increases the risk of functional asplenia sepsis.
- **Curative**: Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT). Gene therapy is currently in advanced clinical trials.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- High risk of early mortality requires intense multidisciplinary coordination between Immunology, Hematology, and Dermatology.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Extensive bruising might mimic physical abuse, but the profound thrombocytopenia on CBC immediately reveals the medical cause.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Massive hemorrhage (intracranial or gastrointestinal).
- Autoimmune disorders (Autoimmune Hemolytic Anemia, Vasculitis).
- Extremely high risk of malignancies (especially EBV-driven B-cell Lymphomas and leukemias) in adolescence/adulthood.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Assuming a male infant with petechiae and low platelets just has viral ITP. Always check the eczema history and the MPV.
- **Viva Q**: "What is the classic immunoglobulin profile seen in Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome?" Answer: Low IgM, normal IgG, High IgA, and High IgE. (Mnemonic: WASP makes IgM go down to the ground, but IgA and IgE fly high).
