# Paediatric Inflammatory Multisystem Syndrome (PIMS-TS / MIS-C) (小兒多系統炎症綜合炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A post-infectious, hyperinflammatory syndrome temporally associated with recent SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) infection in children.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Peaked significantly 2-6 weeks after major COVID-19 waves in Hong Kong.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Not an acute viral infection, but an aberrant, delayed auto-immune or immune-complex mediated reaction following viral clearance.
- Causes intense systemic vasculitis and cytokine storm (high IL-6), leading to profound endothelial dysfunction, myocardial stunning (shock), and coagulopathy.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A school-aged child (median age 9, older than typical Kawasaki disease) presenting with unrelenting high fever containing a mix of Kawasaki-like features, profound GI symptoms, and rapid progression to "cold shock".

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Fever** > 3 days (mandatory).
- **Gastrointestinal**: Severe abdominal pain, vomiting, diarrhea (often so severe it perfectly mimics acute appendicitis).
- **Kawasaki-like**: Bilateral non-purulent conjunctivitis, red cracked lips, strawberry tongue, polymorphous rash.
- **Cardiovascular**: Tachycardia completely out of proportion to the fever, profound hypotension, cold extremities.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from typical Kawasaki Disease (KD): PIMS-TS usually occurs in older children (average 9 years, whereas KD is usually <5 years), has prominent GI pain, high ferritin/D-Dimer, and very frequently presents in profound shock (which is rare in classic KD).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Kawasaki Disease Shock Syndrome
- Toxic Shock Syndrome (Staph/Strep)
- Acute Appendicitis or Gastroenteritis
- Sepsis

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bloods**: 
  - Massive inflammatory markers (CRP, Ferritin, Procalcitonin).
  - Deranged coagulopathy (very high D-Dimers).
  - Cardiac markers mapping myocardial injury (Trop I, greatly elevated BNP/NT-proBNP).
  - Lymphopenia (unlike the leukocytosis in bacterial sepsis).
- **Serology**: Positive SARS-CoV-2 IgG antibodies (nasal PCR is often negative as the virus has already cleared).
- **Imaging**: Echocardiogram (mandatory) to check for severely reduced Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (myocarditis) and coronary artery aneurysms.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Resuscitation**: Careful fluid boluses (10ml/kg aliquots) for shock, but MUST monitor closely since the heart is often failing (myocardial stunning) and fluid overload can cause pulmonary edema. Inotropes (Epinephrine/Milrinone) are often required quickly.
- **Immunomodulation**: 
  - IVIG (Intravenous Immunoglobulin).
  - High-dose systemic Corticosteroids (Methylprednisolone IV).
  - Biologics (e.g., Anakinra/IL-1 receptor antagonist) if refractory.
- **Anticoagulation**: Prophylactic Low Molecular Weight Heparin (Enoxaparin) or Aspirin due to massive thrombosis risk in the coronary arteries.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Continuous long-term Cardiology follow-up to monitor coronary arteries and myocardial recovery.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Coronary artery aneurysms (risk of sudden adult myocardial infarction).
- Cardiogenic shock leading to multi-organ failure.
- Fortunately, with prompt IVIG/steroids, myocardial function usually recovers completely within weeks.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Sending a child with PIMS-TS straight to the operating room for suspected appendicitis. Always check for shock, rash, red eyes, and a COVID history if the "appendicitis" looks overly systemic.
- **Viva Q**: "Why do we give fluid boluses cautiously in PIMS-TS compared to pure septic shock?" Answer: Because PIMS-TS heavily features myocardial stunning. In pure sepsis, the heart works well but the pipes are leaky. In PIMS-TS, the pump itself is failing, so dumping large volumes of fluid can instantly tip them into pulmonary edema.
