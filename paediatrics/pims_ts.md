# Paediatric Inflammatory Multisystem Syndrome (PIMS-TS / MIS-C) (小兒多系統炎症綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- **PIMS-TS/MIS-C**: Post-infectious, hyperinflammatory syndrome temporally associated with recent **SARS-CoV-2** (COVID-19) infection in children.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
- **Peak Incidence**: Typically 2-6 weeks post-major COVID-19 waves in Hong Kong.
- **Local HA Data**: Incidence much lower than Western cohorts, but severe cases require immediate PICU admission.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiological Mechanism
- **Not an acute viral infection**: Aberrant, delayed auto-immune/immune-complex mediated reaction following viral clearance.
- **Cytokine Storm**: Massive release of pro-inflammatory cytokines (especially **IL-6**, **IL-1**).
- **Systemic Vasculitis (血管炎)** → Profound endothelial dysfunction → **Capillary leak**.
- **Myocardial Stunning**: Direct cytokine-mediated myocardial depression → **Cardiogenic Shock (心源性休克)**.
- **Coagulopathy (凝血障礙)**: Prothrombotic state → ↑ risk of microvascular thrombosis.

### Genetic Predisposition
- Exact genetic basis unknown; suspected variable penetrance of immune-regulating genes affecting viral response.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Presentation Profile
- **Demographics**: School-aged child (median age 9 yrs; older than typical **Kawasaki Disease (川崎病)**).
- **Core Presentation**: Unrelenting high **Fever (發燒)** + profound **GI symptoms (胃腸道症狀)** + rapid progression to **"Cold Shock" (冷休克)**.
- **Spectrum**: Can range from Kawasaki-like features to toxic shock-like presentation.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Essential Diagnostic Symptoms
- **Fever (發燒)**: > 38.0°C lasting > 3 days (Mandatory).

### Gastrointestinal Signs (Very Prominent)
- Severe abdominal pain (can perfectly mimic acute appendicitis), vomiting, diarrhea.

### Kawasaki-like Mucocutaneous Signs
- **Eyes**: Bilateral non-purulent **Conjunctivitis (結膜炎)**.
- **Mouth**: Red cracked lips, **Strawberry tongue (草莓舌)**.
- **Skin**: Polymorphous rash (erythematous, generalized).
- **Extremities**: Erythema/edema of hands/feet (peeling occurs later).

### Cardiovascular & Shock Signs
- **Tachycardia (心跳過速)**: Out of proportion to fever.
- **Hypotension (低血壓)** & poor perfusion (prolonged capillary refill >2s, cold extremities).
- **Gallop rhythm/Muffled heart sounds**: Indicating myocardial dysfunction.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### PIMS-TS vs. Classic Kawasaki Disease (KD)
| Feature | PIMS-TS (MIS-C) | Classic Kawasaki Disease (KD) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Age** | Older children (Average 9 yrs) | Infants/Toddlers (< 5 yrs) |
| **GI Symptoms** | Very prominent & severe | Mild or absent |
| **Cardiovascular** | **Myocardial stunning / Shock** very common | Shock is rare (KDSS) |
| **Inflammatory Markers** | Massively ↑ (CRP, Ferritin, D-Dimer) | Moderately ↑ |
| **Lymphocytes** | **Lymphopenia (淋巴細胞減少)** | Normal or high |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Main Differential Diagnoses
| Condition | Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Kawasaki Disease Shock Syndrome (KDSS)** | Usually <5 yrs, typical KD progression, less prominent GI symptoms. |
| **Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)** | Staph/Strep origin, massive erythroderma, desquamation, negative COVID history. |
| **Acute Appendicitis (急性盲腸炎)** | Localized RIF pain, rebound tenderness, normal echocardiogram. |
| **Bacterial Sepsis (敗血症)** | Positive blood cultures, usually neutrophilic leukocytosis (not lymphopenia). |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
- **ECG (心電圖)**: Check for arrhythmias, prolonged PR interval, ST/T wave changes (myocarditis).
- **VBG/ABG**: Severe metabolic acidosis, ↑ Lactate.

### Laboratory Bloods (Hyperinflammation & End Organ Damage)
- **Inflammatory Markers**: Massively ↑ CRP, Procalcitonin, ESR, Ferritin.
- **Cardiac Markers (Mandatory)**: ↑↑ **Trop I/T (肌鈣蛋白)**, ↑↑ **BNP/NT-proBNP** (indicates myocardial stretch/failure).
- **Coagulation Profile**: ↑ D-Dimer, ↑ PT/APTT, ↓ Fibrinogen (prothrombotic state).
- **FBC**: **Lymphopenia**, neutrophilia, mild anaemia, thrombocytopenia (or thrombocytosis later).
- **Renal/Liver Function**: ↑ Urea/Creatinine, ↑ ALT/AST, hypoalbuminaemia (capillary leak).

### Microbiology & Virology
- **COVID-19 Status**: +ve SARS-CoV-2 IgG/IgM serology (Nasal PCR often -ve as virus cleared).
- **Septic Screen**: Blood, urine, stool cultures to rule out bacterial sepsis.

### Imaging (Gold Standard for Complications)
- **Echocardiogram (超聲心動圖) (Gold Standard)**: Mandatory to assess Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF) (myocardial stunning) & screen for **Coronary Artery Aneurysms (冠狀動脈瘤)**.
- **CXR**: Pleural effusions, cardiomegaly, pulmonary edema.
- **Abdominal US**: Check for terminal ileitis, mesenteric adenitis (rule out appendicitis).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Resuscitation & PICU Management
- **Fluid Resuscitation (Caution!)**: 10ml/kg aliquots (not standard 20ml/kg). **Monitor closely**: Heart pump is failing (myocardial stunning) → overzealous fluids → sudden **Pulmonary Edema (肺水腫)**.
- **Inotropic Support**: Early initiation of Epinephrine (Adrenaline) or Milrinone to support failing myocardium.

### Immunomodulation (First-Line HA Protocol)
- **IVIG (Intravenous Immunoglobulin)**: 2g/kg single dose to halt immune cascade.
- **Corticosteroids**: High-dose IV Methylprednisolone (10-30mg/kg/day for 3 days) → transition to oral taper.
- **Biologics**: Anakinra (IL-1 receptor antagonist) or Tocilizumab (IL-6 inhibitor) if refractory to IVIG/Steroids.

### Anticoagulation & Antiplatelet Therapy
- **Aspirin (阿士匹靈)**: Low-dose (3-5 mg/kg/day) for antiplatelet effect, especially if coronary arteries involved.
- **LMWH (Enoxaparin)**: Prophylactic dose for massive thrombosis risk (D-Dimer ↑↑).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
- **Cardiology (心臟科)**: Crucial for serial echocardiograms (at baseline, 2 weeks, 6 weeks) to monitor coronary arteries and LVEF recovery.
- **PICU/Infectious Disease**: Acute phase stabilization and biological therapy coordination.
- **Physiotherapy/Occupational Therapy**: Post-PICU deconditioning rehabilitation.
- **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Support family through sudden, life-threatening critical illness of a previously healthy child.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Considerations
- N/A for disease etiology, but extreme parental anxiety during sudden PICU admission requires careful, empathetic communication. 

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Complications
- **Cardiogenic Shock**: Leading to multi-organ failure.
- **Arrhythmias**: Due to myocarditis.
- **Thrombosis**: DVT, PE, or stroke due to hypercoagulable state.

### Long-Term Prognosis
- **Coronary Artery Aneurysms (CAA)**: Risk of sudden adult myocardial infarction if aneurysms persist.
- **Myocardial Recovery**: With prompt IVIG/steroids, LVEF usually completely recovers within weeks. Overall mortality is low if aggressively managed.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Sending a child with PIMS-TS straight to the operating room for suspected appendicitis. Pearl: Always check for shock, rash, red eyes, and a COVID history if the "appendicitis" looks overly systemic.
* Viva Question: Why do we give fluid boluses cautiously in PIMS-TS compared to pure septic shock? Answer: Because PIMS-TS heavily features myocardial stunning. In pure sepsis, the heart works well but the pipes are leaky. In PIMS-TS, the pump itself is failing, so dumping large volumes of fluid can instantly tip them into pulmonary edema.
* Pitfall: Waiting for a positive COVID-19 PCR to diagnose PIMS-TS. PIMS-TS is a post-infectious phenomenon occurring 2-6 weeks later; the nasal swab PCR is often negative, but IgG serology will be positive.
* Viva Question: What is the most critical imaging investigation in a suspected PIMS-TS patient? Answer: An urgent echocardiogram to assess left ventricular function and identify coronary artery aneurysms.
* Trap: Treating profound hypotension in PIMS-TS exclusively with massive volume expansion. Pearl: Recognize early myocardial depression and initiate inotropic support (e.g., Epinephrine/Adrenaline) promptly to prevent fluid overload and pulmonary edema.
