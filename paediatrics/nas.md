# Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome (NAS) (新生兒戒斷綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A withdrawal syndrome of neonates caused by the cessation of exposure to addictive drugs (most commonly opioids e.g. methadone, heroin) in utero.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Increasingly driven by "polydrug" abuse (opioids + benzodiazepines + methamphetamine). Strong link to SWD cases.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Drugs cross the placenta $ightarrow$ fetal dependence $ightarrow$ abrupt separation from placenta at birth $ightarrow$ massive CNS hyperirritability, autonomic dysfunction, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Onset varies based on drug half-life: Heroin (24-48 hours), Methadone (often delayed 48-72 hours or even up to a week). Required mandatory prolonged observation in HA.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **CNS**: High-pitched cry, excessive jitteriness/tremors, irritability, increased tone, hyperactive Moro reflex, poor sleep, seizures (rare).
- **GI**: Frantic but uncoordinated sucking, poor feeding, vomiting, loose watery stools.
- **Autonomic**: Sweating, frequent yawning, sneezing, tachypnoea, fever.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Must aggressively rule out infectious or metabolic mimics. You cannot assume jitteriness is 'just NAS' without checking blood glucose.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Hypocalcaemia / Hypoglycaemia
- Meningitis / Neonatal Sepsis
- Hypoxic Ischemic Encephalopathy (HIE)
- Thyrotoxicosis

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**: CBG for glucose/electrolytes.
- **Toxicology**: Maternal urine toxicology screen (with consent if strict legally). Neonatal meconium toxicology or urine screen (meconium reflects 2nd/3rd trimester exposure).
- **Screening**: MUST screen the baby for Hepatitis B, C, HIV, and syphilis given maternal high-risk behaviors.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Scoring System**: Finnegan Neonatal Abstinence Scoring System (performed every 3-4 hours).
- **First Line (Non-Pharmacological)**: Swaddling, dark/quiet room, minimizing handling, pacifier use, high-calorie feeds.
- **Pharmacological (if Finnegan score persistently high, e.g. >8 on three occasions)**:
  - Oral Morphine or Methadone taper (treatment of choice for opioid withdrawal).
  - Phenobarbitone (added for non-opiate or polydrug withdrawal, or refractory symptoms).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **CRITICAL ROLE of MSW/SWD**: The mother's capacity to parent must be formally assessed. A Care and Protection Order (CPO) via the Social Welfare Department (SWD) is frequently required.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Parental erratic behavior on the ward, requesting early discharge against medical advice, failure to attend antenatal checks. These mandate urgent MSW review. Breastfeeding is contraindicated if the mother is actively using illicit drugs.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Faltering growth, severe dehydration from diarrhea.
- Long-term behavioral, attentive, and cognitive difficulties, though challenging to separate from environmental/social upbringing factors.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Viva Q**: "Why is NAS often exceptionally difficult to manage in modern contexts?" Answer: Due to polydrug use. Opiate replacement (morphine) only covers opiate withdrawal. Symptoms driven by simultaneous meth/benzo use will persist.
- **Trap**: Automatically recommending breastfeeding. While methadone maintenance is compatible with breastfeeding (encouraged), active illicit drug use or HIV positivity are strict contraindications.
