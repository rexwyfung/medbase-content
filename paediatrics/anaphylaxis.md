# Anaphylaxis (過敏性休克)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Anaphylaxis
* **Definition**: Severe, life-threatening, generalized/systemic **Type I Hypersensitivity (第一型過敏反應)**.
* **Core feature**: Rapidly developing Airway (A), Breathing (B), or Circulation (C) compromise, usually +ve for skin/mucosal changes.

### Local Epidemiology of Anaphylaxis in Hong Kong
* **Incidence**: ↑ in HK pediatric population.
* **Common HK Triggers**:
    * **Food (食物)**: Peanut (花生), tree nuts, shellfish (海鮮), egg (雞蛋), cow's milk (牛奶).
    * **Insects**: Bees, wasps.
    * **Medications**: Penicillin (青黴素), NSAIDs.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### IgE-Mediated Type I Hypersensitivity Mechanism
* **Sensitization Phase**: Initial allergen exposure → specific **IgE** production → binds high-affinity FcεRI receptors on **mast cells (肥大細胞)** & **basophils (嗜鹼性粒細胞)**.
* **Re-exposure Phase**: Allergen cross-links bound IgE → massive sudden degranulation.

### Mast Cell Degranulation & Mediators in Anaphylaxis
* **Mediators Released**: **Histamine (組織胺)**, **tryptase (類胰蛋白酶)**, leukotrienes, prostaglandins.
* **Pathophysiological Effects**:
    * **Vasodilation & ↑ Capillary Permeability**: → Hypotension, distributive shock, urticaria, angioedema.
    * **Smooth Muscle Spasm**: → Bronchospasm, abdominal cramps.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Onset & Prodromal Symptoms of Anaphylaxis
* **Onset**: Rapid (minutes → hours) post-exposure.
* **Prodrome**: "Impending doom" sensation, perioral tingling, sudden severe pruritus (瘙癢).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Airway (A) Signs of Anaphylaxis
* Pharyngeal/laryngeal oedema → throat tightness, hoarse voice.
* **Stridor (喘鳴)**.
* Complete airway obstruction.
* **Angioedema (血管性水腫)**: Swelling of tongue/lips.

### Breathing (B) Signs of Anaphylaxis
* **Bronchospasm (支氣管痙攣)** → dyspnoea, tachypnoea.
* **Wheeze (哮鳴音)**.
* Cyanosis, respiratory arrest.

### Circulation (C) Signs of Anaphylaxis
* Vasodilation/fluid shift → **Tachycardia (心搏過速)** initially.
* Profound **Hypotension (低血壓)**.
* Pale/clammy skin, dizziness, collapse.
* Cardiac arrest.

### Skin, Mucosal & Gastrointestinal Signs of Anaphylaxis
* **Skin/Mucosa**: **Urticaria (蕁麻疹)**, erythema, pruritus. (Note: Absent in ~20% of cases, esp. in rapid profound shock).
* **GI**: Severe colicky abdominal pain, vomiting, diarrhoea.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing Anaphylaxis vs. Vasovagal Syncope
| Feature | Anaphylaxis (過敏性休克) | Vasovagal Syncope (血管迷走神經性昏厥) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Heart Rate** | **Tachycardia** (↑ HR) | **Bradycardia** (↓ HR) |
| **Skin** | Urticaria, angioedema, erythema | Pallor, clammy, no rash |
| **Respiratory** | Wheeze, stridor common | Normal breathing |
| **Response to lying flat** | Temporary stabilization | Rapid complete recovery |

### Distinguishing Anaphylaxis vs. Acute Severe Asthma
| Feature | Anaphylaxis (過敏性休克) | Acute Severe Asthma (急性嚴重哮喘) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cutaneous Signs** | +ve Urticaria/Angioedema | -ve |
| **Cardiovascular Signs** | Profound hypotension, shock | Tachycardia (compensatory), BP usually maintained |
| **GI Symptoms** | +ve Vomiting/Cramps | -ve |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnoses for Suspected Anaphylaxis
* **Vasovagal syncope**.
* **Acute severe asthma**.
* Panic attack / hyperventilation.
* Choking / foreign body aspiration.
* **Hereditary angioedema (遺傳性血管性水腫)** (presents w/ swelling & abdominal pain, but NO urticaria & DOES NOT respond to adrenaline).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)

### Clinical Diagnosis of Acute Anaphylaxis
* Diagnosis is **100% CLINICAL**.
* **CRITICAL**: Treatment MUST NEVER be delayed for investigations.

### Acute Blood Investigations for Anaphylaxis
* **Mast Cell Tryptase (肥大細胞類胰蛋白酶)**:
    * Timing: Draw ideal sample **1-2 hrs** post-reaction onset.
    * Follow-up: Baseline sample drawn **>24 hrs** later.
    * Utility: ↑ levels confirm mast cell degranulation retrospectively.

### Outpatient Special Investigations for Anaphylaxis
* **Allergy Testing**:
    * Performed weeks post-event (avoid false -ve from mast cell depletion).
    * **Skin prick testing (皮膚點刺測試)**.
    * Specific IgE blood tests (RAST).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Resuscitation & Positioning for Anaphylaxis
* **Remove Trigger**: Stop IV infusion, remove stinger, etc.
* **Call for Help**: ABCDE approach.
* **Positioning (CRITICAL)**: Lay flat, elevate legs. **DO NOT** let patient stand/sit up (prevents sudden fatal cardiac arrest due to empty ventricles).

### First-Line Pharmacotherapy for Anaphylaxis (HK HA Protocol)
* **ADRENALINE (Epinephrine) (腎上腺素)**: Single most important life-saving therapy. Reverses laryngeal oedema & shock.
* **Route**: Intramuscular (IM) into anterolateral aspect of middle third of thigh.
* **Repeat**: Every 5 mins if no improvement.

| Age Group | Adrenaline 1:1000 (1mg/mL) IM Dose |
| :--- | :--- |
| **< 6 years** | **0.15 mL** (150 mcg) |
| **6 - 12 years** | **0.3 mL** (300 mcg) |
| **> 12 years** | **0.5 mL** (500 mcg) |

### Second-Line Adjuncts for Anaphylaxis (Given AFTER Adrenaline)
* **Oxygen**: High-flow 100% O2 via non-rebreather mask.
* **IV Fluids**: Rapid bolus **20 ml/kg 0.9% Saline** for hypotension.
* **Meds (Do NOT delay Adrenaline)**:
    * IV/IM **Chlorphenamine** (Antihistamine).
    * IV **Hydrocortisone** (Steroid).
    * Nebulized **Salbutamol** (for persistent wheeze).

### Post-Acute Observation for Anaphylaxis
* Observe for **6-12 hours**.
* Rationale: Risk of **Biphasic reaction (雙相反應)** (second wave of anaphylaxis hours later w/o re-exposure).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)

### Long-Term MDT Care for Anaphylaxis
* **Paediatric Immunology/Allergy Referral**: Formal testing & management plan.
* **Education (Critical)**:
    * Provide written **Anaphylaxis Action Plan**.
    * Train child, parents, school staff on **Adrenaline Auto-Injector (AAI) (腎上腺素自動注射器)** (e.g., EpiPen, Jext) technique.
    * Dietitian referral for strict allergen avoidance advice.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding in Anaphylaxis
* Generally N/A.
* Assess for severe parental medical neglect if known critical allergen is repeatedly fed to child despite clear medical warnings.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications & Prognosis of Anaphylaxis
* **Complications**: Hypoxic brain injury, cardiac arrest, death.
* **Prognosis**: Excellent if IM adrenaline given promptly. Fatalities strongly correlate w/ delayed adrenaline administration.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the dose, route, and location for administering adrenaline in a 7-year-old child with anaphylaxis? Answer: 0.3 ml of 1:1000 adrenaline, given intramuscularly into the anterolateral aspect of the middle third of the thigh.
* Viva Question: Why must a patient with anaphylaxis be laid flat and not allowed to stand up? Answer: Anaphylaxis causes massive vasodilation; standing causes venous pooling in the legs, leading to an empty heart, loss of cardiac output, and sudden fatal cardiac arrest.
* Trap: Assuming skin signs (urticaria/rash) must be present to diagnose anaphylaxis. Pearl: Up to 20% of children with anaphylaxis do not have skin signs, particularly those presenting with rapid-onset profound shock or respiratory arrest.
* Pitfall: Giving IV antihistamines or steroids first and delaying IM adrenaline. Adrenaline is the ONLY drug that rapidly reverses the life-threatening features of anaphylaxis (laryngeal oedema and distributive shock) and must always be the first drug administered.
