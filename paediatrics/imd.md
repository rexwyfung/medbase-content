# Inherited Metabolic Disorders (遺傳性代謝疾病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Inherited Metabolic Disorders
* Genetic disorders (mostly single-gene defects) → absent/defective enzyme, receptor, or transport protein
* Result: ↑ accumulation of toxic substrates OR ↓ production of essential energy/metabolites
* Present as acute life-threatening metabolic crisis or chronic progressive encephalopathy/organ damage

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: ~1 in 4,000 live births overall
* **Newborn Screening (NBS) (新生兒篩查)**: HK Department of Health and HA expanded NBS in 2015 to screen for >20 conditions via tandem mass spectrometry (MS/MS), including:
  * Amino acid disorders [e.g., Phenylketonuria (苯丙酮尿症)]
  * Fatty acid oxidation defects [e.g., MCADD (中鏈酰基輔酶A去氫酶缺乏症)]
  * Organic acidaemias [e.g., Glutaric acidaemia type I (戊二酸血症第一型)]

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Inheritance Patterns
* Vast majority are **Autosomal Recessive (AR) (體染色體隱性)**
* Exceptions (X-linked recessive):
  * Ornithine Transcarbamylase (OTC) deficiency
  * Fabry disease
  * Hunter syndrome (MPS II)
  * Lesch-Nyhan syndrome

### Pathophysiological Classification
* **Intoxication Type (中毒型)**: Symptom-free interval → acute toxicity from metabolite buildup (e.g., Ammonia, organic acids). Ex: Urea Cycle Defects (UCD), Aminoacidopathies.
* **Energy Deficiency Type (能量缺乏型)**: Symptoms in liver, myocardium, muscle, brain (high energy demand). Ex: Glycogen Storage Diseases (GSD), Mitochondrial disorders, Fatty Acid Oxidation Defects (FAOD).
* **Complex Molecule Type (大分子代謝異常)**: Progressive accumulation → dysmorphism, organomegaly, neurodegeneration. Ex: Lysosomal Storage Diseases [e.g., Gaucher (高雪氏症)], Peroxisomal disorders.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Acute Neonatal Presentation (Metabolic Crisis)
* Normal at birth → rapid deterioration after initiating milk feeds (protein load)
* Encephalopathy (腦病): Lethargy, poor feeding, abnormal tone (floppy/spastic), seizures, coma
* Respiratory: Tachypnoea (compensating for metabolic acidosis), apnoea
* GI: Vomiting, feed intolerance

### Chronic or Late-Onset Presentation
* **Neurological**: Global developmental delay, regression (loss of skills), ataxia, seizures
* **GI/Hepatic**: Hepatomegaly (肝腫大), jaundice, liver failure, persistent vomiting
* **Musculoskeletal**: Myopathy, cardiomyopathy, coarse facial features (MPS)
* **Ophthalmic**: Cherry-red spot [Tay-Sachs (泰薩氏症)], cataracts (Galactosaemia)

### Pathognomonic Odours in Metabolic Disorders
* Musty/mousy odour: Phenylketonuria (PKU)
* Sweet/burnt sugar odour: Maple Syrup Urine Disease (MSUD)
* Sweaty feet odour: Isovaleric acidaemia

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features vs. Neonatal Sepsis
* **IEM**: Sudden profound encephalopathy/coma, severe metabolic acidosis, extreme hyperammonaemia, negative inflammatory markers (CRP/Procalcitonin often normal initially), unresponsiveness to broad-spectrum antibiotics
* **Sepsis**: ↑ Inflammatory markers, +ve blood/CSF cultures, temperature instability (fever/hypothermia), responds to antimicrobials/resuscitation

### Discriminatory Features vs. Congenital Heart Disease (CHD)
* **IEM**: Tachypnoea due to metabolic acidosis (Kussmaul breathing) → clear lungs, normal heart structure on Echo, profound hypoglycemia/hyperammonaemia
* **CHD**: Tachypnoea due to hypoxia/heart failure, cyanosis unresponsive to 100% O2 (hyperoxia test -ve), structural defects on Echocardiogram

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Key Differential Diagnoses for Suspected Metabolic Crisis

| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Neonatal Sepsis (新生兒敗血症)** | +ve cultures, ↑ CRP, prominent cardiovascular collapse / DIC |
| **Duct-Dependent CHD (先天性心臟病)** | Cyanosis/shock closing on day 2-3, abnormal ECG/Echo, absent femoral pulses |
| **CNS Infection (腦膜炎/腦炎)** | Bulging fontanelle, ↑ WCC/protein in CSF, fever |
| **Hypoxic-Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE)** | Clear history of severe perinatal asphyxia, low Apgar scores, sentinel event (e.g., cord prolapse) |
| **Non-Accidental Injury (非意外損傷)** | Retinal haemorrhages, unexplained fractures, inconsistent history |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### 1st Line Emergency Investigations (Bedside & STAT)
* **Capillary Blood Glucose (CBG)**: Rule out profound hypoglycemia
* **Arterial/Venous Blood Gas (ABG/VBG)**: Assess metabolic acidosis, calculate Anion Gap [Na - (Cl + HCO3)]
* **Plasma Ammonia (氨) (CRITICAL)**: Normal < 50 µmol/L; > 200 µmol/L highly suggestive of UCD or organic acidaemia
* **Plasma Lactate**: ↑ in hypoxia, sepsis, mitochondrial disorders
* **Urine Ketones**: Inappropriately absent in hypoglycemia suggests FAOD

### 2nd Line Diagnostic Investigations
* **Plasma Amino Acids (PAA)**: Detects PKU, MSUD, UCDs
* **Urine Organic Acids (UOA)**: Detects organic acidaemias (e.g., MMA, PA)
* **Blood Acylcarnitine Profile (MS/MS)**: Detects FAODs (e.g., MCADD)
* **Liver Function Tests & Coagulation**: Assess hepatic involvement

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Metabolic Crisis (Emergency Protocol)
* **Step 1: Stop Toxic Intake**: Immediately cease all enteral protein/feeds
* **Step 2: Reverse Catabolism**: IV 10% Dextrose (高濃度葡萄糖) at high maintenance rate (often 1.5x) ± IV Intralipid (if FAOD excluded) to drive insulin release and stop muscle breakdown
* **Step 3: Scavenge Toxins**: Hyperammonaemia requires IV Sodium Benzoate / Sodium Phenylbutyrate, IV Arginine
* **Step 4: Correct Acidosis**: IV Sodium Bicarbonate (碳酸氫鈉) only if pH < 7.1 and ventilation is adequate
* **Step 5: Dialysis**: Continuous Veno-Venous Haemofiltration (CVVH) if ammonia > 500 µmol/L or rapidly rising despite scavengers

### Chronic Management Strategies
* **Dietary Modification**: Strict lifelong restriction of offending substrate (e.g., low Phe diet for PKU) + metabolic formula supplements
* **Cofactor Therapy**: High-dose vitamins (e.g., B12 for Methylmalonic acidaemia, Biotin for Biotinidase deficiency)
* **Enzyme Replacement Therapy (ERT) (酵素替代療法)**: IV infusions for lysosomal storage diseases (e.g., Pompe, Gaucher)
* **Organ Transplantation**: Liver transplant for UCDs, Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT) for certain leukodystrophies

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Roles
* **Metabolic Paediatrician / Clinical Geneticist**: Leads diagnostic workup and acute/chronic management pathways
* **Specialist Metabolic Dietitian (營養師)**: Crucial for calculating daily protein allowances, specialized formulas, and emergency sick-day regimens
* **Clinical Psychologist (臨床心理學家)**: Neurocognitive assessment, support for chronic illness burden
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Facilitate financial aid (e.g., Samaritan Fund for expensive ERT drugs)

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Metabolic Disorders Mimicking Non-Accidental Injury (NAI)
* **Glutaric Acidaemia Type 1 (GA-1)**: Causes enlarged subarachnoid spaces and bridging vein tearing → presents with **Subdural Haematomas (SDH) (硬腦膜下血腫)** mimicking Shaken Baby Syndrome
* **Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI)**: Mimics inflicted multiple fractures (look for blue sclerae, family history)
* **Menkes Disease**: Copper transport defect → subdural haematomas, metaphyseal changes mimicking fractures (look for kinky hair)

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute & Chronic Complications
* **Neurological**: Irreversible intellectual disability, cerebral oedema, coma, intractable epilepsy
* **Hepatic**: Cirrhosis, hepatocellular carcinoma (Tyrosinaemia type 1)
* **Sudden Unexpected Death**: High risk during fasting or minor intercurrent illnesses (especially FAODs like MCADD)

### Prognosis and Screening Impact
* Highly dependent on the specific defect and speed of intervention
* Universal NBS has revolutionized prognosis (e.g., PKU and MCADD patients now expect normal intelligence and lifespan if managed strictly from birth)

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of investigations ordered immediately for any neonate presenting with unexplained encephalopathy? Answer: Blood glucose, blood gas (acidosis), and plasma ammonia.
* Viva Question: An infant presents with massive hepatomegaly, hypoglycemia, and lactic acidosis, but normal ammonia. What is the most likely category of IEM? Answer: Glycogen Storage Disease (GSD) Type 1 (Von Gierke disease).
* Trap: Assuming a mildly elevated ammonia (e.g., 80 µmol/L) in a struggling neonate means a Urea Cycle Defect. Pearl: Mild hyperammonaemia can be secondary to liver immaturity, asphyxia, or sepsis. UCDs typically present with profound hyperammonaemia (>200-500 µmol/L).
* Pitfall: Fasting a child with an undiagnosed metabolic disorder prior to an elective procedure. This can precipitate a fatal metabolic crisis (especially in fatty acid oxidation defects). Always provide IV dextrose maintenance if fasting is required.
