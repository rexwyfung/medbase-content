# X-linked Agammaglobulinaemia (Bruton) (X連鎖無丙種球蛋白血症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- **X-linked Agammaglobulinaemia (XLA) (X連鎖無丙種球蛋白血症)**: A primary humoral (B-cell) immunodeficiency.
- Characterized by a near-total absence of mature B-cells & severe **hypogammaglobulinemia (低丙種球蛋白血症)**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
- Rare genetic disorder; strictly affects males (X-linked recessive).
- Incidence: ~1 in 200,000 live births globally; similar local prevalence.
- Patients are centralized for specialized care at **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH) (香港兒童醫院)** immunology referral center.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Mutation Mechanism
- X-linked recessive mutation in the **BTK (Bruton Tyrosine Kinase) gene (布魯頓酪氨酸激酶基因)** (located at Xq22.1).

### Pathophysiological Cascade
- BTK is absolutely essential for B-cell development.
- Mutation → Maturation completely arrests at the pre-B cell stage in the bone marrow.
- Result: ↓ mature B-cells in peripheral blood (<1%) → ↓ Plasma cells → ↓↓↓ All immunoglobulin isotypes (IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE are near zero).

### Intact T-Cell Immunity
- T-cells (CD3+) remain perfectly normal.
- Cellular immunity against most viruses & fungi is intact.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Clinical Vignette
- A 9-month-old boy, completely healthy for the first 6 months of life.
- Admitted to PICU (兒童深切治療部) multiple times for severe *S. pneumoniae* pneumonia & *H. influenzae* sepsis.

### Characteristic Age of Onset
- Typically presents beyond infancy (6-12 months).
- Protected before 6 months by transplacental maternal IgG; symptoms start exactly as maternal antibodies deplete.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Characteristic Infection Pattern
- **Severe, recurrent pyogenic infections (反覆性化膿性感染)**.
- Highly susceptible to encapsulated bacteria (*S. pneumoniae*, *H. influenzae*, *S. aureus*).
- Frequent sites: Otitis media (中耳炎), sinusitis (鼻竇炎), pneumonia (肺炎), skin infections, chronic diarrhea.

### Physical Exam Hallmarks
- **Hypoplastic lymphoid tissue (淋巴組織發育不全)**.
- Absent or remarkably tiny **tonsils (扁桃體)** & **lymph nodes (淋巴結)** (Since B-cells normally make up the bulk of germinal centers, their absence makes the nodes disappear).
- **Faltering growth (生長遲緩)** secondary to recurrent illnesses & diarrhea.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating XLA from SCID
- **SCID (Severe Combined Immunodeficiency)**: Defective T-cells. Presents <6 months. High risk of opportunistic infections (PCP, Thrush, CMV).
- **XLA**: Intact T-cells. Patients easily fight off Thrush, PCP, and CMV. Susceptibility is purely bacterial & enteroviral.

### Differentiating XLA from CVID
- **CVID (Common Variable Immunodeficiency)**: Usually later onset (2nd-3rd decade). Has normal/near-normal B-cell numbers.
- **XLA**: Onset <1 year. B-cells <1%.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Primary Immunodeficiency Differentials
| Condition | Typical Onset | B-cell Count | Ig Levels | Key Distinguishing Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **XLA (Bruton)** | 6-12 months | <1% | ↓↓↓ All | Absent tonsils, strictly males |
| **CVID** | Teens / Adults | Normal | ↓ IgG, ↓ IgA/IgM | Autoimmunity common |
| **THI (Transient Hypogammaglobulinemia)** | 6-24 months | Normal | ↓ IgG (recovers) | Self-limiting by age 2-3 |
| **Selective IgA Deficiency** | Any age | Normal | ↓ IgA only | Mostly asymptomatic |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Baseline Bloods
- **Complete Blood Count (全血細胞計數)**: Normal total lymphocytes (flow cytometry needed to differentiate subsets).
- **Serum Immunoglobulins (血清免疫球蛋白)**: Pan-hypogammaglobulinemia (IgG <2 g/L, IgA & IgM near zero).

### Functional Assays
- **Vaccine Response**: Total failure to mount specific antibody titers to Tetanus, Diphtheria, or Pneumococcal vaccines.

### Definitive Specialist Investigations (Gold Standard)
- **Lymphocyte Flow Cytometry (流式細胞術)**:
  - T-cells (CD3+): Normal or ↑.
  - B-cells (CD19+ / CD20+): <1% (near 0%).
- **Genetic Testing**: BTK gene mutation analysis (confirms diagnosis & allows carrier testing for family).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management (HA Protocols)
- **Aggressive Sepsis Protocol**: Early, aggressive use of broad-spectrum IV antibiotics for any febrile illness (e.g., Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin).
- Prolonged antibiotic courses are mandatory due to lack of endogenous antibody assistance.

### Chronic Maintenance Therapy
- **Lifelong Replacement Therapy**:
  - **IVIG (Intravenous Immunoglobulin) (靜脈注射免疫球蛋白)** every 3-4 weeks at HA day center, OR
  - **SCIG (Subcutaneous Immunoglobulin)** weekly (home administration).
- Target IgG trough level: >5-8 g/L to prevent chronic lung disease.
- **Prophylactic Antibiotics**: Often co-prescribed (e.g., Co-trimoxazole or Azithromycin).

### Contraindications (CRITICAL)
- **Live Vaccines (活疫苗)**: Strictly contraindicated (e.g., OPV, MMR, BCG, Rotavirus, Varicella).
- OPV can → Vaccine-associated paralytic poliomyelitis (VAPP). (Note: HK uses IPV locally, but counsel for travel).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Involvement
- **Immunologist**: Regular monitoring of IgG troughs & complication screening.
- **Respiratory Physician**: Monitor lung function / HRCT for bronchiectasis.
- **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Financial assessment for lifelong IVIG/SCIG (Samaritan Fund / CCF in HK).
- **Clinical Psychologist**: Coping with chronic illness burden, needle phobia, and transition to adult HA care.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect Risks
- NAI red flags generally N/A directly for XLA.
- **Medical Neglect (醫療疏忽)**: Non-compliance with lifelong IVIG infusions poses a severe risk of fatal sepsis or permanent lung damage. Requires close monitoring via MCHC (母嬰健康院) / school social worker if non-adherence is suspected.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Long-term Complications
- **Chronic Lung Disease**: Recurrent pneumonias → **Bronchiectasis (支氣管擴張)** & eventually cor pulmonale.
- **Enteroviral Meningoencephalitis**: Defective clearance of gut enteroviruses (Echovirus, Coxsackievirus) → chronic, persistent, often fatal CNS infection.
- **Giardiasis**: Chronic diarrhea and malabsorption.

### Prognosis
- Excellent if diagnosed early and IVIG is consistently maintained. Normal lifespan achievable with strict adherence to HA protocols.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Diagnosing X-linked Agammaglobulinaemia (XLA) in a female patient. Pearl: XLA is strictly X-linked recessive; a female infant with zero B-cells and no IgG has Autosomal Recessive Agammaglobulinemia (μ-heavy chain mutation), NOT Bruton's.
* Viva Question: Why are the tonsils completely invisible in a boy with XLA? Answer: Tonsils are secondary lymphoid organs where B-cells normally proliferate in germinal centers. Since XLA patients have a genetic arrest in B-cell development, no B-cells populate the tonsils, leaving them completely hypoplastic.
* Pitfall: Administering live oral polio vaccine (OPV) to a patient with XLA. Avoid all live vaccines as XLA patients cannot clear gut enteroviruses, risking vaccine-associated paralytic poliomyelitis (VAPP).
