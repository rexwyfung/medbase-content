# Paediatric Brain Tumours (小兒腦腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Paediatric Brain Tumours
* Neoplasms of the central nervous system (CNS)
* Can be primary (arising from brain tissue) or secondary (rare in children)

### Local Epidemiology and HK Triage
* **Most common solid tumour** in childhood
* **2nd most common paediatric cancer** overall (after leukaemia)
* Centralised HA management: All cases triaged to tertiary paediatric oncology/neurosurgery centres (e.g., **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH) (香港兒童醫院)**)

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology by Anatomical Location
* **Children (<10yo):** ~60% are **Infratentorial (幕下)** (posterior fossa, cerebellum, brainstem)
* **Adults:** Predominantly supratentorial
* Growth in enclosed cranial vault → **↑ Intracranial Pressure (ICP) (顱內壓增高)** early in disease course

### Common Paediatric Brain Tumour Types
| Tumour Type (English) | Tumour Type (Chinese) | Location | Characteristics & Prognosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pilocytic Astrocytoma** | 毛狀星形細胞瘤 | Cerebellum | WHO Grade I. Most common overall. Excellent prognosis with complete resection. |
| **Medulloblastoma** | 髓母細胞瘤 | Cerebellar vermis | WHO Grade IV. Most common malignant tumour. Highly cellular, seeds via CSF (**"Drop metastases"**). Requires radiotherapy. |
| **Ependymoma** | 室管膜瘤 | 4th Ventricle floor | Arises from ependymal lining → obstructive hydrocephalus. |
| **Craniopharyngioma** | 顱咽管瘤 | Suprasellar / Rathke's Pouch | Benign but locally invasive. Causes visual field defects & endocrine deficits. |
| **DIPG (Diffuse Intrinsic Pontine Glioma)** | 瀰漫性內因性腦橋膠質瘤 | Brainstem (Pons) | Highly aggressive. Cranial nerve palsies. Universally fatal (inoperable). |

### Associated Neurocutaneous Syndromes
* **Neurofibromatosis Type 1 (NF1):** → **Optic Pathway Glioma (視神經膠質瘤)**
* **Tuberous Sclerosis Complex (TSC):** → **SEGA (Subependymal Giant Cell Astrocytoma)**
* **Li-Fraumeni Syndrome:** (TP53 mutation) → Medulloblastoma, high-grade gliomas

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP)
* **Headache (頭痛):** Classic pattern → progressively worse in morning, wakes child from sleep, worsened by coughing/straining (Valsalva)
* **Vomiting (嘔吐):** Typically early morning, often sudden and without nausea
* **Lethargy:** Progressive drowsiness, declining academic performance

### Signs in Infants vs Older Children
* **Infants (Open Sutures):**
  * **Macrocephaly (大頭症)** (rapid ↑ in head circumference)
  * Tense, bulging anterior fontanelle
  * Splayed cranial sutures
  * **"Sun-setting" eyes (落日眼):** Upward gaze paresis due to dorsal midbrain compression
* **Older Children (Closed Sutures):**
  * **Papilloedema (視乳頭水腫):** Swelling of optic disc on fundoscopy
  * Bradycardia + Hypertension (Cushing's reflex → late sign)

### Focal Neurological Signs by Location
* **Cerebellar (Infratentorial):** **Ataxia (共濟失調)**, broad-based gait, dysmetria, intentional tremor, nystagmus
* **Brainstem:** **Cranial Nerve (CN) Palsies (腦神經麻痺)** (e.g., CN VI palsy → diplopia), bulbar palsy, hemiparesis
* **Supratentorial (Cerebral Hemispheres):** Seizures (focal or generalised), hemiplegia
* **Pituitary/Hypothalamic (Suprasellar):** **Bitemporal Hemianopia (雙顳側偏盲)**, growth failure, diabetes insipidus, delayed/precocious puberty

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Brain Tumour from Migraine
* **Migraine:** Episodic headaches, family history +ve, complete resolution between attacks, normal neurological exam
* **Tumour:** Progressively worsening headaches (specifically morning), focal neurological signs persist between headache episodes

### Differentiating Brain Tumour from Gastroenteritis
* **Gastroenteritis:** Vomiting accompanied by diarrhoea, fever, and diffuse abdominal pain
* **Tumour:** Morning vomiting (often projectile) WITHOUT diarrhoea/fever, associated with headache or lethargy

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Most Likely Differential Diagnoses
* **Brain Tumour (腦腫瘤):** Especially with classic morning headache/vomiting and progressive focal signs
* **Hydrocephalus (腦積水):** Congenital (e.g., aqueductal stenosis) or acquired (post-meningitis/haemorrhage)
* **Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH):** ↑ ICP without a mass lesion, often in obese adolescent females

### Must-Not-Miss Differentials (Life-Threatening)
* **Brain Herniation (腦疝):** Sudden ↓ GCS, asymmetric/blown pupil (CN III compression), Cushing's triad (hypertension, bradycardia, irregular breathing). Requires immediate neurosurgery.
* **Brain Abscess/CNS Infection:** Fever, meningism, acute deterioration

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
* **Detailed Neurological Exam:** Check CNs, cerebellar signs, motor/sensory
* **Fundoscopy:** Mandatory to check for **Papilloedema (視乳頭水腫)**
* **Growth Charting:** Plot Head Circumference (HC) in infants, Height/Weight in older children (endocrine failure)

### First-Line Acute Imaging
* **CT Brain (Non-contrast):**
  * Modality of choice in A&E for acute deterioration
  * Rapidly identifies hydrocephalus, haemorrhage, midline shift, or large masses

### Gold Standard Investigations
* **MRI Brain and Whole Spine with Gadolinium (磁力共振):**
  * **Gold Standard** for definitive characterisation
  * Essential to scan the **ENTIRE spine** to exclude **"Drop Metastases"** (crucial for Medulloblastoma and Ependymoma staging)

### Contraindicated Interventions
* **Lumbar Puncture (LP):**
  * **ABSOLUTELY CONTRAINDICATED** before imaging if ↑ ICP or focal signs present
  * Risk of fatal **Coning (腦疝)** (tonsillar herniation)
  * Safe ONLY post-imaging and used for CSF cytology to check for microscopic tumour spread

### Endocrine & Special Investigations
* **Hormonal Profile:** TFTs, early morning Cortisol, GH (IGF-1), LH/FSH, Prolactin (if suprasellar mass suspected)
* **Visual Field Testing:** Perimetry (for craniopharyngioma)
* **Biopsy/Resection:** Essential for histological grading and molecular typing (e.g., BRAF, WNT, SHH mutations)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Raised ICP
* **Airway/Breathing:** Intubate if GCS ≤ 8 or poor airway protection
* **Positioning:** Elevate head of bed to 30° (↑ venous drainage)
* **Medical Decompression:**
  * **IV Dexamethasone (地塞米松):** Dramatically ↓ tumour-associated vasogenic oedema
  * **IV Mannitol** or **Hypertonic Saline (3% NaCl):** Osmotic therapy if signs of active herniation
* **Surgical Decompression:** Urgent neurosurgery consult for External Ventricular Drain (EVD) or Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt if severe obstructive hydrocephalus

### Definitive Surgical Management
* **Maximal Safe Resection:** Primary goal for most accessible tumours (e.g., Pilocytic astrocytoma) to reduce mass effect and provide tissue diagnosis without causing neurological deficits

### Adjuvant Oncological Therapies
* **Radiotherapy (Craniospinal Irradiation - CSI):**
  * Highly effective for radiosensitive tumours (e.g., Medulloblastoma, Ependymoma)
  * **CRITICAL HA PROTOCOL:** **AVOID in children < 3 years old** whenever possible. The developing brain is extremely sensitive → severe, irreversible cognitive impairment and endocrinopathy.
* **Chemotherapy:**
  * Used to delay radiotherapy in infants (<3yo)
  * Used as an adjunct for high-grade/malignant tumours
  * Molecular targeted therapies (e.g., BRAF inhibitors) increasingly used

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Setup
* Centralised care at **HKCH**
* Core team: Paediatric Neurosurgeons, Paediatric Oncologists, Radiation Oncologists, Paediatric Endocrinologists
* Allied Health: Physiotherapy, Occupational Therapy, Speech Therapy (especially post-resection of posterior fossa tumours)
* Clinical Psychologists and Medical Social Workers (MSW)

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Psychosocial Safeguarding
* Devastating, life-altering diagnosis for families
* Immense MSW support required for financial assistance, school reintegration, and psychological buffering
* Referrals to local NGOs: **Children's Cancer Foundation (兒童癌病基金)** or Make-A-Wish HK

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognosis by Tumour Type
* **Pilocytic Astrocytoma (Cerebellar):** >90% cure rate with complete surgical excision
* **Medulloblastoma:** ~70-80% 5-year survival depending on molecular subgroup and extent of drop mets
* **DIPG (Brainstem Glioma):** Dismal prognosis, virtually 0% survival (median survival < 1 year)

### Late Complications of Treatment
* **Posterior Fossa Syndrome (顱後窩綜合症):** Transient mutism, ataxia, and severe emotional lability 1-2 days post-resection of cerebellar tumours
* **Endocrine Deficits:** **Panhypopituitarism (全垂體功能減退)** requiring lifelong hormone replacement (esp. post-CSI or craniopharyngioma resection)
* **Neurocognitive Decline:** Directly proportional to the dose of radiation and inversely proportional to age at treatment
* **Ototoxicity:** Hearing loss secondary to Platinum-based chemotherapy (Cisplatin)
* **Secondary Malignancies:** Radiation-induced meningiomas or sarcomas later in life

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Pitfall: Reassuring a parent of a child with repeated morning vomiting without nausea. Always suspect a CNS space-occupying lesion; do a thorough neurological and fundoscopic exam.
* Trap: Performing an LP to look for meningitis in a child with a 2-week history of morning headaches, vomiting, and ataxia.
* Pearl: ALWAYS do a CT/MRI brain before an LP if there are signs of raised ICP or focal neurological deficits to avoid fatal coning.
* Viva Question: Why do we try to avoid cranial radiotherapy in a 2-year-old with a medulloblastoma?
* Answer: The rapidly developing infant brain is highly sensitive to radiation, which causes severe, irreversible cognitive impairment and neuroendocrine deficits. We use multi-agent chemotherapy to delay radiation until the child is >3 years old.
* Viva Question: What is the classic visual field defect caused by a craniopharyngioma and why?
* Answer: Bitemporal hemianopia. It compresses the optic chiasm (specifically the decussating nasal retinal fibres) from above.
