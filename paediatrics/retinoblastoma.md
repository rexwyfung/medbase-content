# Retinoblastoma (視網膜母細胞瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Retinoblastoma (視網膜母細胞瘤)**: The most common primary malignant intraocular tumor of childhood.
* Arises from the immature retina (視網膜).

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
* Incidence: ~1 in 15,000 to 20,000 live births globally; Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) sees ~5-10 new cases annually.
* Age of onset: Vast majority diagnosed <3 years old.
* Laterality: ~60% unilateral (單側), ~40% bilateral (雙側).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Two-Hit Hypothesis (Knudson's Hypothesis)
* Driven by loss-of-function mutation in the **RB1 tumor suppressor gene (RB1 腫瘤抑制基因)** on **Chromosome 13q14**.
* Requires 2 mutations ("hits") to inactivate both alleles → malignant transformation.

### Sporadic (Non-Hereditary) Form (60%)
* 2 acquired somatic mutations in a single retinal cell.
* Presentation: **Unilateral (單側)**, unifocal, later onset (usually >1 year).
* Family Hx: -ve.
* 2nd primary cancer risk: Not elevated.

### Hereditary (Germline) Form (40%)
* 1st hit: Inherited germline mutation (present in all cells of the body).
* 2nd hit: Acquired somatic mutation in a retinal cell.
* Presentation: Often **Bilateral (雙側)** or multifocal, earlier onset (often <1 year).
* Family Hx: Often +ve (Autosomal Dominant inheritance with ~90% penetrance).
* 2nd primary cancer risk: Massive lifelong ↑ risk.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Primary Ocular Signs
* **Leukocoria (White Pupil / 白瞳症)**: Classic & most common presentation (>60%). Tumor replaces normal red retina → replacing normal red reflex (紅光反射) with a white/yellow reflection. Best seen in dim light or flash photography.
* **Strabismus (Squint / 斜視)**: 2nd most common sign (20%). Occurs because central macular vision is destroyed by tumor → eye wanders.

### Advanced Disease Signs
* **Vision Loss (視力喪失)**: Often goes unnoticed in pre-verbal infants.
* **Secondary Glaucoma (繼發性青光眼)**: ↑ Intraocular pressure → pain, irritable child, buphthalmos (牛眼 / enlarged globe).
* Extraocular spread: Orbital inflammation, proptosis (突眼), pseudohypopyon.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Retinoblastoma vs. Congenital Cataract
| Feature | Retinoblastoma (視網膜母細胞瘤) | Congenital Cataract (先天性白內障) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Usually months to years | Present at birth |
| **Opacity Location** | Behind the lens (Retinal mass) | The lens itself is physically opaque |
| **Ultrasound** | +ve Calcifications | -ve Calcifications |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentials for Leukocoria
* **Congenital Cataract (先天性白內障)**: Opaque lens at birth.
* **Coats Disease (外層滲出性視網膜病變)**: Severe retinal vascular disease → massive exudates mimicking leukocoria. Usually unilateral, males > females.
* **Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP / 早產兒視網膜病變)**: Hx of extreme prematurity/NICU stay → retinal scarring/detachment.
* **Toxocariasis (弓蛔蟲病)**: Parasitic infection causing white retinal granuloma. Hx of puppy/kitten exposure.
* **Persistent Hyperplastic Primary Vitreous (PHPV)**: Unilateral developmental anomaly of the eye.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Clinical Screening
* **Red Reflex Test (紅光反射檢查)**: Using an ophthalmoscope in a darkened room. Mandatory at all routine DH MCHC (母嬰健康院) well-child checks.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic
* **Examination Under Anesthesia (EUA / 麻醉下檢查)**: By pediatric ophthalmologist for detailed fundoscopy (眼底檢查), tumor mapping, and staging.

### Imaging
* **B-scan Ultrasound (眼科超聲波)**: Highly specific → shows intraocular mass with **calcifications (鈣化)**.
* **MRI Orbit & Brain (磁力共振)**: Essential to assess optic nerve (視神經) invasion, extraocular extension, and check for **Trilateral Retinoblastoma (三側性視網膜母細胞瘤)** (concurrent primary tumor in the pineal gland). *Note: CT is strictly avoided due to radiation risk.*

### Special Tests
* **Genetic Testing (基因測試)**: CRITICAL. Blood drawn to check for germline RB1 mutations to determine if child and relatives have the hereditary form.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Overall Treatment Goals
1. Save the life.
2. Save the eye.
3. Save the vision.

### Focal/Local Therapies (Small/Early Tumors)
* **Laser Photocoagulation (激光光凝術)**: Destroys tumor blood supply.
* **Cryotherapy (冷凍治療)**: Freezes and destroys small peripheral tumors.
* **Plaque Brachytherapy (近距離放射治療)**: Radioactive plaque sutured directly to sclera over tumor.

### Chemotherapy (Moderate/Large Tumors)
* **Systemic Chemotherapy**: Carboplatin, Etoposide, Vincristine (CEV protocol) for chemoreduction prior to focal therapy.
* **Intra-arterial Chemotherapy (IAC / 動脈內化療)**: Threading a micro-catheter via femoral artery directly into ophthalmic artery. Delivers concentrated chemo directly to the eye, avoiding systemic toxicity.
* **Intravitreal Chemotherapy (玻璃體內化療)**: Direct injection for vitreous seeding.

### Surgical Management (Massive/Advanced Tumors)
* **Enucleation (眼球摘除術)**: Surgical removal of the entire eye and the optic nerve. Mandatory if:
  * No hope of useful vision.
  * Massive tumor replacing globe.
  * Tumor spreads into anterior chamber or optic nerve.
  * Secondary glaucoma.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* Pediatric Oncologist & Pediatric Ophthalmologist.
* **Clinical Geneticist (臨床遺傳學家)**: Mandatory genetic counseling to predict risk for parents, siblings, and future pregnancies.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW / 醫務社工)**: Support for enucleation, adjusting to monocular vision, and practical care.
* **Ocularist**: Fitting and maintaining a cosmetic artificial eye (義眼) to promote normal orbital bone growth.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### NAI Considerations
* N/A for pathogenesis.
* However, extreme delayed presentation despite obvious leukocoria/proptosis may indicate medical neglect; warrants MSW/SWD evaluation.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Prognosis
* Excellent overall survival (>95%) if contained entirely within the eye.
* Rapidly fatal if it tracks down the optic nerve into the brain or causes hematogenous metastasis.

### Long-term Complications
* **Secondary Cancers (繼發性癌症)**: Children with the Hereditary form (germline RB1 mutation) have a massive lifetime risk of developing **Osteosarcoma (骨肉瘤)**, soft tissue sarcomas, and melanomas (黑色素瘤) in their teens/20s.
* Vision loss / Blindness in affected eye(s).
* Orbital hypoplasia post-enucleation.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the genetic basis of retinoblastoma? Answer: A loss-of-function mutation in the RB1 tumor suppressor gene on Chromosome 13q14, adhering to Knudson's two-hit hypothesis.
* Viva Question: A 16-year-old who lost his right eye to cancer as a baby now presents with a destructive bone tumor in his distal femur. What is the genetic link? Answer: Germline mutation in the RB1 tumor suppressor gene, which causes hereditary retinoblastoma in infancy and classically dramatically increases the risk of osteosarcoma in adolescence.
* Trap: Diagnosing a strabismus (squint) in a toddler, referring them for eye patching, and failing to perform a direct fundoscopy/red-reflex test. Pearl: Always perform a red-reflex test in new-onset childhood strabismus; strabismus is the 2nd most common presentation of retinoblastoma.
* Pitfall: Ordering a CT scan to investigate leukocoria or suspected retinoblastoma. Avoid CT scans in these children because ionizing radiation dramatically triggers and accelerates the risk of secondary malignancies in patients with a germline RB1 mutation.
