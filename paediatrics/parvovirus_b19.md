# Parvovirus B19 (Erythema Infectiosum / Slapped Cheek Syndrome) (微小病毒 B19 / 傳染性紅斑)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Erythema Infectiosum
* Common childhood viral exanthem (病毒性皮疹)
* Classically known as **"Fifth Disease" (第五病)**.
* Caused by **Parvovirus B19 (微小病毒 B19)**, a single-stranded DNA virus.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Peak Season:** Outbreaks common in late winter to spring in HK kindergartens/primary schools.
* **Transmission:** Respiratory droplets (飛沫傳播), vertical transmission (mother-to-fetal), and blood products.
* **Incubation Period:** 4–14 days.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Viral Tropism & Cellular Target
* Virus binds strictly to **Blood Group P antigen (P抗原)**.
* Specifically targets and destroys rapidly dividing **human erythroid progenitor cells (紅血球前體細胞)** in bone marrow.

### Mechanism of Transient Aplastic Crisis
* Cytotoxicity → Transient halt in **erythropoiesis (紅血球生成)** (lasts ~5-7 days).
* **Normal children:** RBC lifespan = 120 days → asymptomatic halt.
* **Chronic Hemolytic Anemia children** (e.g., Thalassemia (地中海貧血), Sickle Cell Disease): RBC lifespan = 10-20 days → halt causes sudden, severe drop in Hb → **Aplastic Crisis (再生不良性危機)**.

### Pathogenesis of Classic Rash
* Rash & arthropathy are NOT caused by direct viral damage.
* Mediated by **Immune Complex Deposition (免疫複合物沉積)** (Type III Hypersensitivity) occurring days *after* viremia clears.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Biphasic Illness Pattern
* **Phase 1 (Viremic Phase):** Occurs 7-10 days before rash. Highly infectious. Nonspecific viral symptoms.
* **Phase 2 (Immune Phase):** Rash appears. Child is systemically well and **NO LONGER infectious (不再具傳染性)**.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Prodromal Symptoms (Phase 1)
* Mild fever (輕微發燒)
* Coryza (傷風)
* Headache & myalgia (頭痛及肌肉痛)

### Dermatological Signs (Phase 2)
* **"Slapped Cheeks" (掌摑臉):** Intense, confluent, bilaterally symmetrical erythema on cheeks.
* **Circumoral Pallor (口周蒼白):** Spared area around the mouth.
* **Reticular Rash (網狀皮疹):** Lace-like (蕾絲狀), pink, macular rash on trunk & extensor surfaces of limbs. Appears 1-4 days post-facial rash. Fades over weeks, exacerbates with heat/sunlight/stress.

### Atypical Manifestations by Age
* **Adolescents/Adults:** Often lack classic rash. Present with **symmetrical polyarthropathy (對稱性多發性關節病)** (mimics Rheumatoid Arthritis) affecting hands, wrists, knees.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating from Group A Streptococcus
* **Scarlet Fever (猩紅熱):** Features **Strawberry tongue (草莓舌)**, rough **sandpaper rash (沙紙狀皮疹)**, cervical lymphadenopathy, and toxic appearance. 
* **Parvovirus:** Child is generally completely well/playful once the "slapped cheek" rash erupts.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Exanthems Comparison
| Diagnosis | Pathogen | Key Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Erythema Infectiosum** | Parvovirus B19 | Slapped cheeks, lacy limb rash, asymptomatic upon rash onset. |
| **Scarlet Fever (猩紅熱)** | Group A Strep | Sandpaper rash, strawberry tongue, exudative tonsillitis, penicillin responsive. |
| **Rubella (德國麻疹)** | Rubella virus | Prominent postauricular/suboccipital lymphadenopathy, pinpoint pink maculopapular rash. |
| **Measles (麻疹)** | Rubeola virus | 3Cs (Cough, Coryza, Conjunctivitis), Koplik spots, high fever, toxic appearance. |
| **Roseola Infantum (玫瑰疹)** | HHV-6 | 3-5 days of high fever → fever drops suddenly → maculopapular rash appears. |
| **SLE (系統性紅斑狼瘡)** | Autoimmune | Butterfly rash (spares nasolabial folds), systemic involvement (renal, joints, ANA +ve). |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **Diagnosis:** Predominantly a **clinical diagnosis (臨床診斷)**.
* **Urinalysis:** Rule out hematuria/proteinuria if SLE or post-streptococcal complications suspected.

### Blood Investigations (Only if clinically indicated)
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC) (全血細胞計數):** Mandatory if pale, tachycardic, or known hemolytic anemia.
* **Reticulocyte Count (網狀紅血球計數):** **Gold Standard** to confirm Aplastic Crisis. ↓↓↓ (near 0%) in B19 infection.
* **Parvovirus Serology:** * **IgM +ve:** Indicates acute/recent infection.
    * **IgG +ve:** Indicates past immunity.
* **Viral PCR:** Serum B19 DNA (Useful in immunocompromised patients who cannot mount an IgM response).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management for Healthy Children
* **Supportive Care:** Antipyretics (Paracetamol/Ibuprofen) for prodrome.
* **Infection Control (HK HA/CHP Guideline):** Child with slapped cheek rash is **NO LONGER infectious**. No school exclusion (無需停學) required once rash appears.

### Management of Transient Aplastic Crisis
* **Resuscitation:** O2, strict input/output.
* **Transfusion:** Urgent **Packed RBC transfusion (紅血球輸血)** if hemodynamically unstable or severe symptomatic anemia.
* **Isolation:** *Unlike* healthy kids, those in aplastic crisis are **highly infectious** (high viral load). Require strict droplet/contact isolation.

### Management for Immunocompromised Patients
* Unable to clear virus → Chronic anemia.
* **Treatment:** **Intravenous Immunoglobulin (IVIG) (靜脈注射免疫球蛋白)** to provide passive neutralizing antibodies.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Maternal Health & Obstetric Referral
* **Exposed Pregnant Women:** Daycare workers/teachers exposed to B19 prodrome must be referred to **Obstetrics (產科)** immediately.
* Required monitoring: Serial fetal ultrasounds to detect hydrops fetalis.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Distinguishing Slapped Cheek from Physical Abuse
* **Child Abuse (Non-Accidental Injury / NAI):** Slap marks are usually unilateral, show distinct finger/hand outlines, and lack the lacy truncal rash.
* **Parvovirus:** Rash is perfectly symmetrical, lacks bruising/petechiae, and is accompanied by systemic prodrome history.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Hematological Complications
* **Transient Aplastic Crisis (短暫性再生不良危機):** Life-threatening drop in Hb in patients with underlying chronic hemolysis (Thalassemia, Hereditary Spherocytosis, Sickle Cell).

### Obstetric Complications
* **Fetal Hydrops (胎兒水腫):** Infection in a non-immune pregnant woman (especially <20 weeks gestation) → virus destroys fetal RBCs → severe fetal anemia → high-output cardiac failure → generalized edema/anasarca. Intrauterine death risk.

### Rheumatological Complications
* **Arthropathy (關節病):** Small joint symmetrical polyarthritis. Self-limiting (weeks to months) but highly painful.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: A child with known Sickle Cell Disease reports a history of slapped cheeks and is now lethargic with a Hb of 3 g/dL. What test will confirm the cause of the drop? Answer: A reticulocyte count. It will be near zero, confirming Parvovirus-induced aplastic crisis.
* Trap: Sending a child with slapped cheek rash home from school to "isolate". Pearl: By the time the rash appears (immune phase), the child is no longer shedding the virus and does not need to be excluded from school.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing an immunocompromised patient with B19 via serology (IgM/IgG). These patients cannot mount an adequate antibody response; you must order a Parvovirus B19 DNA PCR instead.
* Trap: Suspecting child abuse immediately in a child with bright red cheeks. Pearl: Always check the trunk and extremities for the pathognomonic lace-like reticular rash of Parvovirus B19 before raising an NAI alert, and note if the redness is perfectly symmetrical.
* Viva Question: What specific cellular receptor does Parvovirus B19 bind to in the human body? Answer: The blood group P antigen located on erythroid progenitor cells.
