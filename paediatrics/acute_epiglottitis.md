## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
* **Definition**: Rapidly progressive, life-threatening bacterial infection & inflammation of epiglottis & supraglottic structures (急性會厭炎).
* **Local Epidemiology (HK)**: 
  * Extremely rare in HK children today due to low incidence rate of **Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib)**.
  * Rare cases mostly in unimmunised immigrants or adults.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
* **Pathogen**: 
  * Historically: **Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib)** (>90%).
  * Currently (in vaccinated pop.): *Streptococcus pneumoniae*, *Group A Streptococcus* (GAS), *Staphylococcus aureus* (including MRSA).
* **Mechanism**: 
  * Bacterial invasion of supraglottic tissues → rapid & intense inflammatory oedema.
  * Epiglottis becomes **"cherry red" (櫻桃紅)** & swollen like a thumb.
  * Mechanical supraglottic airway obstruction just above vocal cords → ↑ risk of sudden asphyxia & death.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
* **Onset**: Abrupt & rapid progression over hours (not days).
* **Preceding coryza (鼻水)**: Absent.
* **Appearance**: Toxic, very ill, panicked.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
* **The "4 Ds"**:
  * **Drooling (流口水)**: Unable to swallow saliva due to severe pain.
  * **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**: Severe pain on swallowing.
  * **Dysphonia (發音困難)**: Muffled, "hot potato" voice.
  * **Distress (呼吸窘迫)**: Severe respiratory distress, high fever (>38.5°C).
* **Posture**: 
  * **Tripod position (三腳架姿勢)**: Sitting upright, leaning forward, neck hyperextended, chin thrust forward (to maximize airway patency).
* **Stridor (喘鳴)**: Soft, whispering, inspiratory stridor (late, ominous sign).
* **Cough**: Absent or slight (NO barking cough).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
| Feature | Acute Epiglottitis (急性會厭炎) | Croup (哮吼) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Hours (Rapid) | Days (Gradual) |
| **Appearance** | Toxic, extremely ill | Unwell but not toxic |
| **Fever** | High (>38.5°C) | Low grade (<38.5°C) |
| **Drooling/Swallowing** | Severe drooling, unable to drink | Able to drink, no drooling |
| **Cough** | Absent or slight | Severe, barking (狗吠聲咳嗽) |
| **Stridor** | Soft, whispering | Harsh, rasping |
| **Voice** | Muffled ("hot potato") | Hoarse (沙啞) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Diagnosis | Differentiating Factors |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Acute Epiglottitis** | Classic "4 Ds", Tripod posture, Toxic. **Must-not-miss**! |
| **Bacterial Tracheitis** | Toxic child, copious thick airway secretions, soft stridor, no drooling. |
| **Viral Croup** | Barking cough, gradual onset, preceding coryza, non-toxic. |
| **Foreign Body Aspiration** | Sudden onset choking/stridor, afebrile, no prodrome. |
| **Retropharyngeal Abscess** | Neck stiffness, gradual onset, bulging posterior pharyngeal wall. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
* **Bedside (CRITICAL WARNINGS)**: 
  * **DO NOT EXAMINE THROAT**: Using tongue depressor/precipitating crying → sudden, irreversible airway obstruction.
  * **DO NOT** perform painful procedures (bloods, IV cannula) until airway secure.
  * Keep child calm, seated with parents.
* **Gold Standard (Diagnostic & Therapeutic)**: 
  * **Direct Laryngoscopy (直接喉鏡檢查)**: Done **ONLY** in OT by Senior Anaesthetist/ENT. Shows "cherry red" swollen epiglottis.
* **Imaging (Not Routine)**:
  * **Lateral Neck X-ray**: DO NOT DELAY AIRWAY MANAGEMENT for X-ray. If done, shows classic **"Thumbprint Sign" (拇指印徵)**.
* **Bloods (Post-Intubation)**: 
  * Blood cultures, Epiglottic swab (for culture & sensitivities), CBC, CRP.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
* **Acute (HA Emergency Protocol)**:
  * **AIRWAY IS THE ONLY PRIORITY**.
  * Call for senior help **IMMEDIATELY**: Senior Paediatrician, Senior Anaesthetist, ENT Surgeon (MDT response).
  * Do not agitate child. Give O2 gently (e.g., wafting) if tolerated. Nebulized adrenaline offers temporary benefit while awaiting definitive care.
  * **Transfer to OT**: Accompanied by senior team.
  * **Inhalational Induction**: Gas anaesthesia (e.g., Sevoflurane) in sitting position → secure IV access once asleep → **Intubation**.
  * **Surgical Airway**: Emergency Tracheostomy/Cricothyroidotomy equipment prepared on standby if intubation fails.
* **Chronic / Definitive**:
  * **IV Antibiotics**: Broad-spectrum (e.g., **Ceftriaxone** or **Cefotaxime**) post-intubation to cover Hib, GAS, S. pneumo.
  * **Extubation**: Usually safe after 2–3 days when swelling subsides (confirmed by direct visualization).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
* **PICU (兒童深切治療部)**: Mandatory admission post-intubation.
* **MDT Involvement**: Anaesthesia, ENT, Paediatrics, PICU nurses.
* **Parental Support**: Extreme acute stress for parents; Medical Social Worker (MSW) support if needed for coping.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
* **Immunisation Status**: Check Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) record. If unimmunised due to parental refusal/neglect, explore reasons. Arrange catch-up vaccination.
* **Prophylaxis**: **Rifampicin** prophylaxis for close household contacts if Hib +ve & vulnerable contacts present (e.g., unimmunised infants <4 yrs).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
* **Prognosis**: Excellent if airway is secured promptly before hypoxic arrest.
* **Complications**:
  * **Hypoxic brain injury / Death** (from complete airway obstruction & asphyxia).
  * Pulmonary oedema, pneumothorax.
  * Secondary spread: Meningitis, septicaemia (especially if Hib).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls
* **Killer OSCE Trap**: "I will look in the throat to confirm..." → **Immediate Fail**. You must state: "I will **NOT** examine the throat, keep child calm, & summon senior Anaesthetic/ENT help to OT."
* **Spot Diagnosis**: Toxic child + Drooling + Tripod posture = Epiglottitis until proven otherwise.
* **Viva Question**: "Why is Epiglottitis virtually unseen in HK wards today?" → "Success of the routine HK Childhood Immunisation Programme containing the **Hib conjugate vaccine**."
