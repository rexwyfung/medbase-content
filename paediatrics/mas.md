# Meconium Aspiration Syndrome (胎糞吸入綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Meconium Aspiration Syndrome
* Respiratory distress in a neonate born through **meconium-stained amniotic fluid (MSAF) (胎糞羊水)**
* Accompanied by characteristic radiological changes + compatible clinical symptoms
* Symptoms cannot be otherwise explained (e.g., must exclude congenital anomalies)

### Local Epidemiology & Risk Factors in Hong Kong
* Incidence: MSAF occurs in ~10-15% of all deliveries; MAS develops in ~5% of MSAF neonates
* ↑ risk in **post-term deliveries (過期妊娠)** (>41 weeks)
* ↓ incidence locally due to aggressive **Hospital Authority (HA)** protocols for induction of labor (IOL) at 41 weeks
* High-risk maternal factors: Pre-eclampsia, maternal diabetes, intrapartum fetal distress (胎兒窘迫), heavy smoking

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Meconium Release
* Fetal hypoxia (缺氧) / stress → ↑ Vagal tone
* → Relaxation of fetal anal sphincter
* → Passage of **meconium (胎糞)** into amniotic fluid
* Deep fetal gasping (due to hypoxia) → Aspiration into tracheobronchial tree

### Mechanism of Lung Injury in MAS
* **Mechanical Obstruction:** Viscous meconium blocks airways. Partial block → **Ball-valve effect (球閥效應)** → Air trapping → Hyperinflation → **Air leak / Pneumothorax (氣胸)**. Total block → Atelectasis (肺不張).
* **Chemical Pneumonitis (化學性肺炎):** Bile salts & pancreatic enzymes induce severe local inflammatory response within 24-48 hours.
* **Surfactant Inactivation (肺表面活性物質失活):** Free fatty acids in meconium strip surfactant from alveoli → ↓ Lung compliance.
* **Vascular Remodeling:** Chronic in-utero hypoxia + acute acidosis → Pulmonary vasoconstriction → **Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension of the Newborn (PPHN) (新生兒持續性肺動脈高壓)**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Early Clinical Presentation of MAS
* Onset: Immediate distress at or shortly after birth
* **Tachypnea (呼吸急促)**
* Expiratory **grunting (呻吟)**
* Severe intercostal & subcostal **retractions (肋間凹陷)**
* Central **cyanosis (發紺)** refractory to standard O2 therapy

### Physical Signs on Examination
* **Meconium staining (胎糞染色)** of vocal cords, umbilical cord, fingernails, vernix
* **Barrel chest (桶狀胸)** due to severe air trapping / hyperinflation
* Auscultation: Widespread coarse **crackles (濕囉音)** & expiratory **wheezes (哮鳴音)**
* Shifted apex beat / muffled heart sounds → Suggests secondary pneumothorax (氣胸)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing MAS from Other Neonatal Respiratory Distress
* **Amniotic Fluid:** MSAF is a strict prerequisite for MAS (clear fluid completely excludes MAS).
* **Gestational Age:** MAS typically affects term/post-term infants; RDS almost exclusively affects preterm infants.
* **Chest Morphology:** MAS presents with hyperinflated "barrel chest"; RDS presents with under-inflated, low-volume chest.
* **Time Course:** Transient Tachypnea of Newborn (TTN) resolves rapidly (24-72h); MAS worsens over 24-48h due to chemical pneumonitis.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis of Neonatal Respiratory Distress
| Diagnosis | Key Differentiating Features | Typical CXR Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **MAS (胎糞吸入綜合症)** | Term/post-term, MSAF, severe distress | Hyperinflation, patchy infiltrates, air leaks |
| **RDS (呼吸窘迫綜合症)** | Preterm, clear fluid, surfactant deficiency | Low volume, ground-glass opacity, air bronchograms |
| **TTN (新生兒短暫性呼吸急促)** | Term/late-preterm, elective LSCS | Fluid in horizontal fissure, perihilar streaking, normal volume |
| **Congenital Pneumonia (先天性肺炎)** | Maternal GBS +ve, PROM, fever | Indistinguishable from MAS; requires blood culture/CRP |
| **PPHN (原發性肺動脈高壓)** | Severe hypoxemia out of proportion to CXR | Often clear/oligemic lungs, +ve Echo findings |
| **Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia** | Scaphoid abdomen, shifted apex beat | Bowel loops in thorax, mediastinal shift |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Point-of-Care Investigations
* **Pulse Oximetry (血氧飽和度監測):** Pre-ductal (Right hand) vs Post-ductal (Lower limb) gradient. Difference >5-10% indicates right-to-left shunting (suggests PPHN).
* **ABG / CBG (動脈/微血管血氣分析):** Show hypoxemia (↓ PaO2), hypercapnia (↑ PaCO2), respiratory & metabolic acidosis (↓ pH, ↑ Lactate).

### Radiological Investigations (Gold Standard)
* **Chest X-Ray (CXR) (胸部X光):**
  * Coarse, irregular, patchy infiltrates (斑片狀浸潤)
  * Lung hyperinflation (flattened hemidiaphragms > 8th rib posteriorly)
  * Rule out pneumothorax / pneumomediastinum
  * Rule out structural anomalies (e.g., diaphragmatic hernia)

### Advanced & Laboratory Investigations
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波):** Essential to confirm/quantify PPHN (evaluate TR jet, ductal shunting) and rule out congenital heart disease.
* **Septic Screen (敗血症篩查):** 
  * Blood culture (血培養)
  * Complete Blood Count (CBC)
  * C-Reactive Protein (CRP) → Chemical pneumonitis makes superimposed bacterial infection hard to exclude.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Delivery Room Management (HA Resuscitation Protocol)
* **If infant is VIGOROUS** (good cry, tone, HR >100): Routine care. Do NOT perform routine intrapartum suctioning.
* **If infant is NON-VIGOROUS** (apnea, floppy, HR <100): 
  * Initiate standard **Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP)** steps immediately.
  * *Update*: Routine intubation purely for tracheal suction is NO LONGER recommended by AHA/HA guidelines. Focus on clearing airway via bulb syringe/large-bore suction catheter + positive pressure ventilation (PPV).
  * Intubate ONLY if airway is obstructed and PPV is ineffective.

### Respiratory Support & NICU Management
* **Oxygen Therapy:** Target pre-ductal SpO2 95-99% to prevent hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction.
* **NCPAP (經鼻持續正壓通氣):** Splints airways open, prevents atelectasis.
* **Mechanical Ventilation:** 
  * Conventional (SIMV/SIPPV): Use longer expiratory times to prevent air trapping.
  * **High-Frequency Oscillatory Ventilation (HFOV) (高頻振盪通氣):** Used if conventional fails, severe PPHN, or severe air leak.
* **Surfactant Therapy (肺表面活性物質):** Bolus administration or bronchoalveolar lavage via ETT to overcome surfactant inactivation.

### PPHN Specific Management
* **Inhaled Nitric Oxide (iNO) (吸入性一氧化氮):** Potent selective pulmonary vasodilator. 1st-line for confirmed PPHN.
* **Inotropes:** Dopamine/Dobutamine to maintain systemic BP higher than pulmonary BP (reverses right-to-left shunt).
* **ECMO (體外膜氧合):** Ultimate rescue therapy if oxygenation index (OI) > 40 despite iNO and HFOV. Transfer to Queen Mary Hospital (瑪麗醫院) or Hong Kong Children's Hospital (香港兒童醫院) required.

### Pharmacological & Supportive Care
* **Antibiotics (抗生素):** Intravenous **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** (HA standard 1st line) pending 48h culture results.
* **Sedation:** Morphine/Fentanyl infusions to prevent agitation-induced pulmonary hypertensive crises.
* Minimal handling protocol.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* **Neonatologist (新生兒科醫生):** Lead critical care and ventilation strategies.
* **Pediatric Cardiologist:** Serial echoes for PPHN tracking.
* **Pediatric Surgeon:** Standby for ECMO cannulation or chest drain insertion.
* **Physiotherapist (物理治療師):** Chest physio delayed until acute air leak risk passes and hemodynamics stabilize.

### Psychosocial Care & Communication
* Extreme parental anxiety due to sudden deterioration of a perceived "healthy term baby".
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工):** Refer for counseling, emotional support, and logistical help during prolonged NICU stay.
* Transparent daily communication regarding ECMO risks, HIE prognosis, and potential neurodevelopmental outcomes.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Identifying Safeguarding Concerns
* MAS itself is an intrapartum event and NOT indicative of Non-Accidental Injury (NAI).
* Evaluate maternal history for causes of profound fetal distress:
  * Maternal illicit drug abuse (e.g., cocaine, methamphetamines causing placental abruption)
  * Unbooked pregnancies / avoidance of antenatal care
  * Domestic violence leading to maternal trauma/hypoxia
* If present → Mandatory referral to **Medical Social Worker** and **Child Protective Services (保護兒童服務)** prior to discharge.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Complications
* **Air Leaks:** Pneumothorax (10-20% incidence), pneumomediastinum.
* **PPHN:** Major driver of acute mortality.
* **Hypoxic-Ischemic Encephalopathy (HIE) (缺氧缺血性腦病):** Due to severe pre-/intra-partum asphyxia. May require therapeutic hypothermia.

### Long-Term Prognosis
* **Respiratory:** High risk of reactive airway disease (asthma) in childhood. Severe cases may develop Chronic Lung Disease of Prematurity / BPD equivalent.
* **Neurological:** Risk of cerebral palsy (腦性麻痺), developmental delay, or sensorineural hearing loss (often secondary to ECMO, prolonged hypoxia, or ototoxic drugs).
* Overall survival >95% in modern HA NICUs, but morbidity heavily depends on the severity of accompanying HIE.

## 11. Prevention & Follow-up
### Obstetric Prevention Strategies
* Strict adherence to HA guidelines for induction of labor at 41 weeks to prevent post-maturity.
* Continuous electronic fetal monitoring (CTG) during labor for high-risk pregnancies to detect early fetal distress.
* Amnioinfusion (羊膜腔灌注) is NO LONGER routinely recommended for diluting thick meconium.

### Neonatal Outpatient Follow-up
* **Neonatal Follow-up Clinic:** Monitor growth and respiratory status.
* **Child Assessment Center (CAC) (兒童體能智力測驗中心):** Standardized neurodevelopmental screening at 6, 12, and 24 months to detect subtle HIE sequelae.
* Routine audiology screening (OAE/AABR) post-discharge (mandatory for iNO/Gentamicin/HIE patients).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls
* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of pathophysiological mechanisms in MAS? Answer: Airway obstruction (ball-valve effect), chemical pneumonitis, and surfactant inactivation.
* Trap: Routine intubation and tracheal suctioning of non-vigorous infants with MSAF is standard practice. Pearl: This is an outdated guideline. NRP/HA protocols now dictate initiating PPV immediately for non-vigorous infants, reserving intubation only for cases where the airway is completely obstructed preventing effective PPV.
* Pitfall: Starting aggressive chest physiotherapy immediately to clear thick meconium. Wait until acute pulmonary hypertension and air leak risks resolve, as early aggressive handling exacerbates PPHN and risks pneumothorax.
* Trap: Assuming a pneumothorax will present with hyperresonance on percussion in a neonate. Pearl: Percussion is notoriously unreliable in tiny neonatal chests; look for transillumination, shifted apex beat, or asymmetric chest rise, and confirm with acute CXR or bedside ultrasound.
* Viva Question: What is the primary medical treatment to reverse Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension of the Newborn (PPHN) in MAS? Answer: Inhaled Nitric Oxide (iNO) acts as a selective pulmonary vasodilator.
