
# Delayed Puberty (青春期延遲)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**:
  - **Girls**: Absence of breast development (Thelarche) by age 13, or primary amenorrhoea by age 15.
  - **Boys**: Absence of testicular enlargement (volume < 4 mL) by age 14.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Common presentation in adolescent clinics. More common in boys (usually CDGP).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **1. Constitutional Delay of Growth and Puberty (CDGP)**: The most common cause, especially in boys. A normal variant. The HPG (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal) axis is intact but matures late.
- **2. Hypergonadotropic Hypogonadism (Primary Gonadal Failure)**: The gonads fail to respond to pituitary stimulation. High FSH/LH, low sex steroids.
  - *Causes*: Turner Syndrome (45,XO) in girls, Klinefelter Syndrome (47,XXY) in boys, prior chemotherapy/radiotherapy, gonadal dysgenesis.
- **3. Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism (Central Failure)**: The hypothalamus/pituitary fails to stimulate the gonads. Low FSH/LH, low sex steroids.
  - *Causes*: Kallmann Syndrome (associated with anosmia), Pituitary tumours (Craniopharyngioma), severe chronic illness (Anorexia Nervosa, severe IBD, excessive exercise).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Boys**: Lack of testicular growth, lack of pubic hair, short stature compared to peers.
- **Girls**: Lack of breast buds, primary amenorrhoea.
- **Clues to Aetiology**:
  - *Anosmia/Hyposmia*: Kallmann syndrome.
  - *Webbed neck, short stature*: Turner syndrome.
  - *Tall stature, gynaecomastia, small firm testes*: Klinefelter syndrome.
  - *Headaches, visual field defects*: Pituitary tumour.
  - *Extreme weight loss*: Anorexia nervosa.
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **CDGP vs. Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism**: Can be very difficult to distinguish clinically initially. Both have low LH/FSH and delayed bone age. A family history of late puberty strongly suggests CDGP.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Boys: CDGP.
  - Girls: CDGP or Turner Syndrome.
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Intracranial tumour (Craniopharyngioma).
  - Turner Syndrome (requires screening for cardiac and renal anomalies).
  - Anorexia Nervosa.

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **Tanner Staging**: Accurate assessment of breast/pubic hair (girls) and testicular volume using an orchidometer (boys).
  - **Bone Age X-ray**: Usually delayed in CDGP and central causes.
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Bloods**:
    - **FSH and LH**: Crucial to differentiate primary (Hypergonadotropic - high FSH/LH) from secondary (Hypogonadotropic - low/normal FSH/LH) hypogonadism.
    - Oestradiol (girls) / Testosterone (boys).
    - TFTs, Prolactin (to rule out prolactinoma).
    - **Karyotype**: Mandatory if Hypergonadotropic (high FSH/LH) to diagnose Turner or Klinefelter.
    - **MRI Brain/Pituitary**: Mandatory if Hypogonadotropic (low FSH/LH) with no obvious chronic illness, to rule out structural lesions.
    - **Pelvic Ultrasound (Girls)**: To assess presence/size of uterus and ovaries.
- **Monitoring**:
  - Regular follow-up every 6 months to monitor for spontaneous onset of puberty.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Not applicable.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - **CDGP**: Reassurance. If significant psychological distress, a short course of low-dose sex steroids (e.g., oral testosterone in boys for 3-6 months) can be given to "kick-start" puberty and growth.
  - **Primary/Secondary Hypogonadism**: Long-term sex steroid replacement therapy to induce and maintain secondary sexual characteristics and bone health.
  - *Girls*: Start with low-dose oestrogen, gradually increase, then add progesterone to cycle the endometrium.
  - *Boys*: Intramuscular testosterone injections (gradually increasing dose).
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Psychological support is vital, as delayed puberty can cause severe anxiety, low self-esteem, and bullying in adolescents.
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Address fertility concerns sensitively. Patients with Turner/Klinefelter will likely be infertile, while those with CDGP will have normal fertility.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **CDGP**: Excellent prognosis, normal final height and fertility.
- **Hypogonadism**: Risk of osteoporosis if sex steroids are not replaced. Infertility is common depending on the cause.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Ordering an MRI brain for a boy with high FSH/LH. *Pearl*: High FSH/LH means the pituitary is working fine, but the testes are failing (primary hypogonadism). You need a karyotype (for Klinefelter), not an MRI.
- **Viva Question**: "What is the most important initial blood test to differentiate the causes of delayed puberty?" *Answer*: Basal FSH and LH levels to distinguish between hypergonadotropic and hypogonadotropic hypogonadism.
