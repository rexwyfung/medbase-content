# Delayed Puberty (青春期延遲)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Delayed Puberty in Girls
- Absence of **breast development (Thelarche) (乳房發育)** by age 13
- OR primary **amenorrhoea (原發性閉經)** by age 15
- OR no progression of pubertal stages for > 2 years after initial onset

### Clinical Definition of Delayed Puberty in Boys
- Absence of **testicular enlargement (睪丸增大)** (volume < 4 mL) by age 14
- OR no progression of pubertal stages for > 2 years after initial onset

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
- Common presentation in HA adolescent endocrine clinics
- M > F presentation (boys present earlier due to peer comparison for short stature)
- **Constitutional Delay of Growth and Puberty (CDGP) (體質性生長和青春期延遲)** accounts for majority (~80% boys, ~30% girls)

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of CDGP
- Benign, normal variant
- **HPG (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal) axis (下丘腦-垂體-性腺軸)** functionally intact but matures late
- Strong familial trait (autosomal dominant pattern with incomplete penetrance)

### Pathophysiology of Hypergonadotropic Hypogonadism
- **Primary Gonadal Failure (原發性性腺功能衰竭)**
- Gonads fail to respond to pituitary stimulation
- ↓ sex steroids → absent -ve feedback → **↑ FSH/LH**

### Causes of Hypergonadotropic Hypogonadism
- **Girls**: **Turner Syndrome (特納氏綜合症)** (45,XO)
- **Boys**: **Klinefelter Syndrome (克氏綜合症)** (47,XXY)
- **Acquired**: 
  - Prior chemotherapy (alkylating agents)
  - Pelvic/testicular radiotherapy
  - Gonadal dysgenesis
  - Autoimmune oophoritis/orchitis

### Pathophysiology of Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism
- **Central Failure (中樞性性腺功能衰竭)**
- Hypothalamus/pituitary fails to stimulate gonads
- ↓ GnRH/gonadotropins → **↓ FSH/LH** & ↓ sex steroids

### Causes of Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism
- **Functional/Transient**: 
  - **Anorexia Nervosa (神經性厭食症)**
  - Severe chronic illness (e.g., severe IBD, SLE)
  - Excessive exercise (female athlete triad)
- **Permanent (Congenital)**: **Kallmann Syndrome (卡爾曼綜合症)** (disordered GnRH neuron migration)
- **Permanent (Acquired)**: Pituitary tumours (e.g., **Craniopharyngioma (顱咽管瘤)**), cranial irradiation

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms & Signs in Boys
- Lack of testicular growth (< 4mL on orchidometer)
- Absence of pubic/axillary hair
- Lack of penile enlargement
- Short stature compared to peers (delayed pubertal growth spurt)

### Key Symptoms & Signs in Girls
- Lack of breast buds (Tanner stage 1)
- Primary amenorrhoea
- Absence of pubic/axillary hair

### Clues to Specific Aetiologies
- **Kallmann Syndrome**: **Anosmia/hyposmia (嗅覺喪失/減退)**, midline defects (cleft lip/palate)
- **Turner Syndrome**: 
  - Webbed neck
  - Short stature
  - Shield chest
  - Cubitus valgus
- **Klinefelter Syndrome**: 
  - Tall stature (eunuchoid proportions)
  - Gynaecomastia
  - Small firm testes
- **Pituitary tumour**: 
  - Headaches
  - Visual field defects (**bitemporal hemianopia (雙顳側偏盲)**)
  - Polyuria/polydipsia (Diabetes Insipidus)
- **Anorexia Nervosa**: 
  - Extreme weight loss
  - BMI < 3rd centile

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating CDGP from Permanent Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism
| Feature | CDGP | Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Family History** | +ve for late puberty | Usually -ve (unless Kallmann) |
| **Growth Velocity** | Normal prepubertal rate | May be impaired (if GH deficient) |
| **Bone Age** | Delayed (matches height age) | Delayed or normal |
| **Olfaction** | Normal | Impaired in Kallmann |
| **GnRH Test** | Eventual LH response | Blunted LH response |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Most Likely Differential Diagnoses
- **Boys**: **CDGP** (most common)
- **Girls**: **CDGP** OR **Turner Syndrome**

### Must-Not-Miss Differential Diagnoses
- **Intracranial tumour**: e.g., **Craniopharyngioma**
- **Turner Syndrome**: Requires lifelong cardiac (coarctation of aorta) & renal screening
- **Anorexia Nervosa**: High morbidity/mortality, requires urgent psychiatric intervention
- **Chronic occult systemic disease**: e.g., IBD, Coeliac disease

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
- **Tanner Staging (坦納分期)**: Assess breast/pubic hair
- **Orchidometer (睪丸計)**: Measure testicular volume
- **Growth Charting**: Plot height/weight, assess growth velocity
- Calculate **mid-parental height (父母中間身高)**

### Gold Standard Initial Blood Tests
- **Basal FSH and LH**: Differentiates 1° (↑) from 2° (↓/normal) hypogonadism
- **Early morning Sex Steroids**: **Oestradiol (雌二醇)** (girls) / **Testosterone (睾酮)** (boys)

### Baseline Screening Blood Tests
- **TFTs** (Thyroid function)
- **Prolactin (催乳素)**
- **IGF-1** (screen for GH deficiency)
- **LFTs, RFTs, CRP, ESR**
- **Coeliac screen** (Anti-tTG IgA + total IgA)

### Imaging Investigations
- **Bone Age X-ray (骨齡X光)** (Left hand/wrist): Delayed in CDGP/central causes; predicts final adult height
- **Pelvic Ultrasound (盆腔超聲波)** (Girls): Assess presence/size of uterus and ovaries (streak ovaries in Turner)

### Special / Advanced Investigations
- **Karyotype (染色體核型分析)**: Mandatory if ↑ FSH/LH to diagnose Turner (45,XO) or Klinefelter (47,XXY)
- **MRI Brain/Pituitary (腦部磁力共振)**: Mandatory if ↓ FSH/LH with no functional cause/chronic illness
- **GnRH Stimulation Test (促性腺激素釋放激素刺激試驗)**: Selectively used to differentiate CDGP from central failure

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management
- N/A unless presenting with acute crisis of underlying disease (e.g., severe anorexia, intracranial hypertension)

### Chronic Management of CDGP
- **1st Line**: Reassurance, watchful waiting, regular growth monitoring
- **Pharmacological (if significant psychological distress/bullying)**: Short course low-dose sex steroids
  - **Boys**: IM **Testosterone Enanthate (庚酸睾酮)** (50-100mg monthly for 3-6 months) OR oral Testosterone Undecanoate
  - **Girls**: Oral **Ethinylestradiol (炔雌醇)** (low dose for 3-6 months)

### Chronic Management of Primary/Secondary Hypogonadism
- **Goal**: Long-term sex steroid replacement for secondary sexual characteristics, bone health, & psychosocial well-being
- **Girls**: 
  - Start low-dose **17β-oestradiol**
  - Titrate up gradually over 2-3 years
  - Add cyclical **Progesterone (黃體酮)** to cycle endometrium
- **Boys**: 
  - IM **Testosterone** injections
  - Gradually increasing dose every 6 months

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
- **Clinical Psychologist (臨床心理學家) / MSW (醫務社工)**: Support for anxiety, low self-esteem, poor body image, and bullying
- **Dietitian (營養師)**: Optimize nutrition in anorexia or female athlete triad
- **Endocrinologist (內分泌科醫生) / Gynaecologist / Urologist**: Transition to adult clinics for long-term HRT and fertility management

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Fertility & Safeguarding Considerations
- Handle fertility concerns sensitively
- **Turner/Klinefelter**: High risk of **infertility (不育)**; discuss future options early (e.g., donor options, adoption)
- **CDGP**: Assure patients of normal future fertility
- **Eating Disorders**: Monitor for medical neglect if parents obstruct psychiatric/dietetic treatment for severe anorexia

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications of Untreated Hypogonadism
- **Osteoporosis/Osteopenia (骨質疏鬆症)** due to lack of sex steroids
- Altered body composition
- Infertility
- Social isolation and severe psychological distress

### Prognosis
- **CDGP**: Excellent (normal final adult height & normal reproductive function)
- **Pathological Hypogonadism**: Dependent on underlying cause; requires lifelong **Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) (荷爾蒙替代療法)**

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Ordering an MRI brain for a boy with high FSH/LH. Pearl: High FSH/LH means the pituitary is working fine, but the testes are failing (primary hypogonadism). You need a karyotype (for Klinefelter), not an MRI.
* Viva Question: What is the most important initial blood test to differentiate the causes of delayed puberty? Answer: Basal FSH and LH levels to distinguish between hypergonadotropic (primary) and hypogonadotropic (secondary) hypogonadism.
* Pitfall: Prescribing high-dose sex steroids initially to accelerate growth. High doses cause rapid epiphyseal fusion and compromised final adult height, so always start low and titrate slowly.
* Viva Question: What is the classic presentation of Kallmann syndrome? Answer: Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, anosmia/hyposmia, and a normal karyotype.
* Pitfall: Failing to check the blood pressure in a girl with Turner syndrome. Coarctation of the aorta is common; always check upper and lower limb blood pressures.
