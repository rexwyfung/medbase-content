# Acute Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (APSGN) (急性鏈球菌感染後腎小球腎炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of APSGN
- Most common cause of acute nephritic syndrome (急性腎炎綜合症) in childhood.
- Immune complex-mediated glomerulonephritis (免疫複合物介導的腎小球腎炎).
- Occurs post-infection with nephritogenic strains of Group A β-Haemolytic Streptococcus (GAS) (A族β溶血性鏈球菌).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
- **Peak Age**: 5-12 years (school-aged).
- **Gender**: Male > Female (2:1).
- **Seasonality (HK HA Context)**:
  - Winter/Spring: Post-pharyngitis (咽炎).
  - Summer: Post-impetigo (膿疱疹).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiological Mechanism
- Nephritogenic GAS infection occurs.
- **Latent period**: 1-3 wks (pharyngitis) or 3-6 wks (impetigo).
- Ag-Ab immune complexes (免疫複合物) form & deposit in glomerular basement membrane (GBM) (腎小球基底膜).
- Activation of alternate complement pathway → **↓ C3**.
- Glomerular inflammation → ↓ GFR (腎小球濾過率).
- ↓ GFR → Na+/water retention → volume overload & hypertension.

### Genetic Predisposition
- Specific HLA types may confer susceptibility.
- Primarily an acquired, infection-driven pathology.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Classic Patient History
- School-aged child.
- Hx of sore throat 2 wks prior OR skin infection 4 wks prior.
- Sudden onset of:
  - Facial puffiness.
  - Dark urine.
  - Headache.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms
- **Macroscopic Haematuria** (肉眼血尿): "Cola-coloured" or "tea-coloured" urine.
- **Oedema** (水腫): Mild-moderate, classic morning periorbital puffiness (眼周水腫).
- **Headache** (頭痛): Secondary to severe hypertension.
- **Oliguria** (少尿): ↓ urine output secondary to ↓ GFR.

### Key Clinical Signs
- **Hypertension** (高血壓): Can be severe → hypertensive encephalopathy (高血壓腦病).
- **Signs of Fluid Overload** (體液超負荷):
  - ↑ JVP (頸靜脈壓升高)
  - Basal lung crepitations (pulmonary oedema)
  - Hepatomegaly (肝腫大)
  - Peripheral oedema

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### APSGN vs IgA Nephropathy
| Discriminator | APSGN | IgA Nephropathy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Latent Period** | 1-3 wks post-pharyngitis | Concurrent (1-2 days) |
| **Complement C3** | **↓ Low** | Normal |
| **Prognosis** | Excellent, rarely recurs | Chronic, recurrent macroscopic haematuria |

### APSGN vs Nephrotic Syndrome
| Discriminator | APSGN (Nephritic) | Nephrotic Syndrome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Proteinuria** | Mild-Moderate (+ to ++) | Massive (+++ to ++++) |
| **Oedema** | Mild (periorbital) | Severe/Generalized (ascites) |
| **Blood Pressure**| **↑ High** | Normal/Low |
| **Haematuria** | Macroscopic | Microscopic (usually absent) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differentials for Acute Nephritic Syndrome
- **IgA Nephropathy** (IgA 腎病).
- **Lupus Nephritis** (狼瘡性腎炎) (SLE - suspect in HK adolescent females).
- **Henoch-Schönlein Purpura (HSP) Nephritis** (過敏性紫癜腎炎).
- **Membranoproliferative Glomerulonephritis (MPGN)** (膜增生性腎小球腎炎).
- **Alport Syndrome** (奧爾波特綜合症) (Hereditary nephritis + sensorineural deafness).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)

### Bedside Investigations
- **Urinalysis** (尿液分析): Blood (+++), Protein (+ to ++).
- **BP Monitoring**: Essential continuous monitoring to detect hypertensive crisis.

### Blood Tests for APSGN
- **Complement Levels**: **C3 ↓ LOW**, C4 normal. (CRITICAL: C3 must normalize by 6-8 wks).
- **Evidence of Strep**: ↑ ASOT (Anti-Streptolysin O Titre) or ↑ Anti-DNase B.
- **Renal Function (U&E/Cr)**: May show AKI (↑ Urea, ↑ Cr, ↑ K+).
- **CBP**: Mild normochromic normocytic anaemia (dilutional effect).

### Special Investigations
- **Urine Microscopy** (尿液顯微鏡檢查): **RBC casts** (紅血球管型) (pathognomonic for glomerular bleeding) & dysmorphic RBCs.
- **Microbiology Swabs**: Throat/Skin swab for GAS culture (often -ve at presentation).
- **Renal Biopsy**: NOT routinely indicated. Reserve for atypical cases.

### Indications for Renal Biopsy in APSGN
- Normal C3 at presentation.
- C3 remains ↓ > 8 weeks.
- Prolonged oliguria/AKI > 2 weeks.
- Massive nephrotic-range proteinuria.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Supportive Management
- **Fluid Restriction** (限制水分): Strict restriction = 400ml/m²/day (insensible loss) + previous day's urine output.
- **Dietary Modification**: Low Na+, low K+ diet.
- **Daily Monitoring**: Strict I/O charting, daily weights, strict BP monitoring.

### Acute Pharmacological Therapy
- **Diuretics** (利尿劑): 1st-line = IV/PO **Furosemide** (呋塞米) to treat fluid overload & HTN.
- **Antihypertensives** (降血壓藥): Calcium channel blockers (e.g., **Amlodipine**) if BP ↑ despite aggressive diuresis.
- **Antibiotics** (抗生素): 10-day course PO **Penicillin V** (青黴素 V) or Amoxicillin. Eradicates carriage & prevents spread, but DOES NOT alter current nephritis course.

### Indications for Acute Dialysis (透析指徵)
- Severe refractory hyperkalaemia.
- Refractory pulmonary oedema.
- Severe symptomatic uraemia.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### MDT Involvement
- **Paediatric Nephrology**: Involve if severe AKI, dialysis required, or atypical biopsy-warranting features.
- **Dietitian**: Urgent input for low Na+/K+ diet planning during oliguric phase.

### Psychosocial Care
- **Parental Reassurance**: Educate that macroscopic haematuria resolves in weeks.
- **Discharge Education**: Microscopic haematuria can safely persist for up to 1-2 years without indicating active disease.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications
- **Hypertensive Encephalopathy** (高血壓腦病) → Seizures, visual disturbances.
- **Acute Pulmonary Oedema** (急性肺水腫) → Hypoxia, respiratory failure.
- **Acute Kidney Injury** (急性腎損傷) → Severe uraemia/hyperkalaemia requiring haemodialysis.

### Prognosis
- Excellent in paediatric populations.
- >95% recover completely with no long-term renal impairment.
- Recurrence is extremely rare.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most important blood test to confirm the diagnosis of APSGN, and what is its expected trajectory? Answer: Complement C3. It should be low at presentation and must return to normal within 6-8 weeks. If it does not, suspect chronic glomerulonephritis (e.g., MPGN or Lupus Nephritis).
* Viva Question: A child with APSGN presents with a blood pressure of 150/100. What is the first-line pharmacological treatment? Answer: Furosemide. The hypertension is driven by fluid and sodium retention, so aggressive diuresis is the most effective treatment.
* Trap: Assuming a 10-day course of oral Penicillin will cure the glomerulonephritis. Pearl: Antibiotics eradicate the remaining nephritogenic streptococci to prevent transmission, but they do NOT alter the clinical course or duration of the established nephritis.
* Pitfall: Prescribing steroids for APSGN. Steroids have no role in APSGN and may actively worsen fluid retention and hypertension.
* Pitfall: Requesting a renal biopsy for a classic presentation of APSGN. Biopsy is only indicated if the clinical course is atypical (e.g., C3 remains low >8 weeks, normal C3 at presentation, or prolonged severe AKI).
