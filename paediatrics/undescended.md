# Undescended Testis (Cryptorchidism) (隱睪症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Definition**: Failure of one or both testes to descend completely into the scrotum along the normal pathway.
* **Anatomical Locations**: May arrest in the abdomen, inguinal canal, or just external to the superficial inguinal ring.

### Local Epidemiology & Demographics
* **Prevalence**: Very common in Hong Kong. Affects **~5% of full-term** male infants and up to **30% of premature** male infants.
* **Spontaneous Descent**: Majority descend spontaneously within the first 3-6 months of life. By 3 months of age, only **~1%** remain undescended.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Embryology & Descent Mechanism
* **Origin**: Testes develop intra-abdominally on the posterior abdominal wall (near kidneys).
* **Descent Phase**: Guided by the **gubernaculum** (引帶) and driven by fetal **androgens** during the 3rd trimester, descending through the inguinal canal into the scrotum.

### Pathophysiology of Cryptorchidism
* **Temperature Effect**: The higher intra-abdominal/inguinal temperature impairs normal **spermatogenesis** (精子生成), leading to histological degradation by 6-12 months of age → ↑ risk of **infertility** (不育).
* **Dysplasia**: Undescended testes are intrinsically dysplastic, dramatically ↑ risk of **malignant transformation** (seminoma).

### Associated Anomalies & Syndromes
* **Patent Processus Vaginalis**: Almost 100% of true undescended testes have an associated patent processus vaginalis (indirect inguinal hernia).
* **Syndromic Associations**: Can be associated with **Prune-Belly Syndrome** (absent abdominal musculature, megacystis, cryptorchidism), **Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21)**, or **Disorders of Sex Development (DSD)**.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Mode of Presentation
* **Screening Finding**: Vast majority are asymptomatic, detected incidentally during the routine **Newborn Infant Physical Examination (NIPE)** or at **Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) (母嬰健康院)** checks at 6 weeks or 6 months.

### Scrotal Appearance
* **Visual Inspection**: The affected hemiscrotum appears **empty, hypoplastic, underdeveloped**, or asymmetrical.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Palpation Findings
* **Palpable Undescended Testis**: Testis can be felt in the groin/inguinal canal but cannot be manipulated into the scrotum.
* **Impalpable Testis**: Testis cannot be felt anywhere (may be intra-abdominal, absent/agenesis, or severely atrophic).

### The Retractile Testis (伸縮性睪丸)
* **Definition**: A normally descended testis pulled high into the groin by a hyperactive **cremasteric reflex** (提睪肌反射).
* **Key Clinical Sign**: Can be manually coaxed down into the base of the scrotum and will **stay there temporarily without tension** when released. 
* **Environment**: Often descends spontaneously when the child is warm and relaxed (e.g., in a warm bath).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Testicular Maldescent
| Condition | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **True Undescended Testis** | Cannot be manipulated into the scrotum, or immediately springs back up under tension when released. |
| **Retractile Testis** | Can be gently brought into the scrotum and stays there without tension. Normal variant; no surgery needed. |
| **Ectopic Testis** | Descended through the external ring but deviated from the normal path (e.g., superficial inguinal pouch, perineum, femoral triangle). |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Differentials for an Empty Scrotum
* **Retractile testis** (most common mimic).
* **Ectopic testis**.
* **Absent testis (Anorchia)**: Often due to in utero testicular torsion (intrauterine vascular accident).
* **Disorders of Sex Development (DSD) (性發育障礙)**: Highly suspect if **bilateral impalpable testes**, especially if associated with hypospadias. (Must exclude virilized female with **Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH)**).

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Clinical Assessment
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Diagnosis of a unilateral palpable undescended testis is entirely clinical.

### Imaging (HA Guidelines)
* **Ultrasound (US) Scrotum/Groin**: Strictly **NOT recommended** for unilateral palpable testes. It is often inaccurate, does not change the need for surgery, and causes unnecessary delays in referral.

### Bloods & Special Tests (For Bilateral Impalpable Testes)
* **Urgent DSD Workup**: Mandatory for bilateral impalpable testes to exclude life-threatening CAH (salt-wasting crisis).
* **Karyotype**: To determine chromosomal sex (46,XX vs 46,XY).
* **Hormonal Profile**: **17-OH Progesterone** (↑ in CAH), LH, FSH, Testosterone.
* **U&Es**: Check for hyponatremia and hyperkalemia (CAH).

### Gold Standard Localization
* **Diagnostic Laparoscopy (腹腔鏡檢查)**: The definitive Gold Standard investigation and therapeutic tool to locate intra-abdominal testes or confirm anorchia (blind-ending vas and vessels).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Conservative Management (Observation)
* **Active Surveillance**: Observe for the **first 6 months** of life, as spontaneous descent can still occur (more likely in premature infants).

### Surgical Management: Orchidopexy (睪丸固定術)
* **Optimal Timing**: Recommended between **6 and 12 months** of age.
* **Dual Rationale for Early Surgery**: 
  1. Halt histological degradation and maximize future **fertility potential**.
  2. Relocate testis to a palpable position to allow for **early detection of testicular cancer** via self-examination in adulthood.
* **Standard Procedure**: Mobilize the testis + ligate the associated patent processus vaginalis (hernia sac) + fix the testis without tension in a sub-dartos pouch in the scrotum.

### Management of Intra-abdominal Testes
* **Laparoscopic Orchidopexy**: Often requires a 2-stage **Fowler-Stephens procedure** (Stage 1: clip testicular vessels to promote collateral supply; Stage 2: mobilize and bring down into scrotum months later).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Routine Screening & Referral
* **MCHC Role**: Vigilant screening at newborn, 6-week, and 6-month checks. Timely referral to **Paediatric Surgery** (兒科外科) if undescended by 6 months.
* **Psychosocial Care**: Achieving a symmetrical, normal-appearing scrotum provides significant psychological and cosmetic benefits for the boy's self-esteem during childhood and adolescence.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect
* **Delayed Presentation**: While rare, an uncorrected undescended testis presenting in late childhood or adolescence may indicate significant failure of health-seeking behavior by parents or lack of engagement with universal MCHC screening programs.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Long-Term Complications
* **Malignancy (Testicular Cancer)**: **10-fold ↑ risk** of germ cell tumors (predominantly **Seminoma**). 
* **Infertility (不育)**: Risk is significantly ↑, especially with bilateral undescended testes, even after early surgery.
* **Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉)**: Anatomically predisposed to torsion if left undescended.
* **Inguinal Hernia (腹股溝疝)**: Clinical hernia may develop due to the obligate patent processus vaginalis.
* **Testicular Atrophy**: The intra-abdominal testis may undergo complete atrophy if neglected.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate a retractile testis from a true undescended testis?
  * Answer: A retractile testis can be gently manipulated into the base of the scrotum and will remain there temporarily without tension when released. A true undescended testis cannot be brought down, or immediately springs back up.
* Viva Question: At what age should an orchidopexy be performed for an undescended testis, and what are the two main reasons for doing the surgery?
  * Answer: Between 6 and 12 months of age. The reasons are to optimize future fertility and to relocate the testis into the scrotum to allow for easy palpation and early detection of testicular cancer.
* Viva Question: What is the mandatory immediate step if a newborn presents with bilateral impalpable testes?
  * Answer: Urgent referral and investigation for a Disorder of Sex Development (DSD), specifically ordering Karyotype, 17-OH Progesterone, and electrolytes to rule out life-threatening Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH).
* Trap: Assuming that performing an orchidopexy eliminates the risk of testicular cancer.
  * Pearl: Orchidopexy DOES NOT eliminate the increased risk of malignancy; it merely brings the testis into the scrotum so that any malignant lump can be easily palpated and detected early.
* Pitfall: Ordering an ultrasound for a unilateral palpable undescended testis.
  * Avoidance: Do not order an ultrasound; it is unnecessary, often inaccurate, does not alter the surgical indication, and delays definitive Paediatric Surgery referral.
