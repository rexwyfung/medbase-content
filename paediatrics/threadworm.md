# Enterobiasis / Threadworm (蟯蟲病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Disease Definition
* **Enterobiasis (蟯蟲病)** = common intestinal parasitic infection caused by nematode **Enterobius vermicularis (蟯蟲)**.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong)
* Highly contagious → endemic in HK kindergartens and primary schools.
* Transmission = fecal-oral route.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology Mechanism
* Adult worms live in cecum/appendix.
* Gravid ♀ migrates to perianal region at night → lays eggs.
* Eggs secrete irritating mucus → intense **pruritus ani (肛門瘙癢)**.
* Scratching → eggs lodge under fingernails → **autoinfection (自體感染)** or transmission via fomites (bedding/toys).
* Eggs survive up to 3 weeks in indoor environments.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Classic Symptoms
* Intense nocturnal **pruritus ani (肛門瘙癢)**.
* Restless sleep / insomnia / irritability.
* **Vulvovaginitis (外陰陰道炎)** in ♀ (from ectopic migration).

### Clinical Signs
* Perianal erythema/excoriations.
* Visible 1cm white, thread-like worms near anus/stool (best seen at night/early AM).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Key Discriminators for Threadworm
* Strictly **nocturnal** exacerbation of pruritus.
* Visual confirmation of motile worms by parents at night.
* Concurrent household/school contacts with similar symptoms.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Table of Differentials for Perianal Pruritus in Children
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Enterobiasis (蟯蟲病)** | Nocturnal pruritus, +ve tape test, visible worms. |
| **Perianal Streptococcal Dermatitis (肛周鏈球菌皮炎)** | Bright red, sharply demarcated perianal erythema, painful defecation, blood-streaked stools. |
| **Candidiasis (念珠菌感染)** | Satellite lesions, beefy red rash, common in diapered infants/recent antibiotic use. |
| **Poor Hygiene** | Pruritus throughout day, responds to basic washing/barrier creams. |
| **Sexual Abuse** | Complex psychosocial presentation, severe trauma/tears, concurrent STIs. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Gold Standard Investigation
* **Adhesive Tape Test (膠紙測試)**: Apply clear tape to perianal skin immediately upon waking (before washing/defecation) → view under microscope for oval, asymmetric eggs. 
* Repeat x3 consecutive mornings to ↑ sensitivity to >90%.

### Secondary Investigations
* **Stool O&P (Ova & Parasite)**: Usually -ve (females lay eggs on skin, not in stool). Only useful if suspecting co-infections with other helminths.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### 1st Line Pharmacological Management
* **Mebendazole (甲苯咪唑)**: 100mg PO stat. MUST repeat dose in 2 weeks (kills newly hatched worms, ineffective against eggs).
* Treat ALL household members simultaneously regardless of symptoms.

### Alternative HA Protocol Options
* **Pyrantel Pamoate (雙羥萘酸嘧啶)**: 11mg/kg (max 1g) PO stat. Repeat in 2 weeks.

### Environmental Control & Hygiene Steps
* Wash bed linens/towels/underwear in hot water.
* Keep fingernails short & clean.
* Strict morning showering to wash away overnight eggs.
* Disinfect shared toys/surfaces.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Public Health & School Management
* School nurse notification if outbreak in kindergarten.
* Psychoeducation for parents → reassure it's extremely common, easily treated, and not strictly a sign of a "dirty" home to ↓ stigma.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Sexual Abuse Differentiation
* Severe/recurrent **vulvovaginitis (外陰陰道炎)** or perianal trauma must be fully evaluated.
* Threadworm is a common innocent cause of vulvar itching/erythema in young girls → always exclude before considering NAI, but maintain index of suspicion if history is highly inconsistent.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Common Complications
* **Secondary Bacterial Infection**: From chronic excoriation (Staph/Strep).
* Ectopic migration → **Salpingitis**, **Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)**, or **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)**.
* **Appendicitis (盲腸炎)**: Adult worms obstructing appendiceal lumen (rare but documented).

### Prognosis
* Excellent. Near 100% cure rate if compliance with 2-dose regimen and simultaneous household treatment is achieved.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why must the Mebendazole dose be repeated in 2 weeks? Answer: Anthelmintics kill adult worms but not eggs; the 2nd dose eradicates newly hatched worms before they mature and lay new eggs.
* Trap: Ordering a stool O&P to diagnose a threadworm infection. Pearl: Stool tests are typically negative for Enterobius; the gold standard is the early morning cellophane tape test.
* Pitfall: Treating only the symptomatic child. Treatment failure and reinfection will almost certainly occur if the entire household is not treated simultaneously.
