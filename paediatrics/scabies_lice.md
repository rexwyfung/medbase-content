# Scabies & Pediculosis Capitis (疥瘡及頭蝨)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition & Epidemiology of Scabies (疥瘡)
* **Definition**: Intensely **pruritic (痕癢)** ectoparasitic skin infestation caused by *Sarcoptes scabiei* var. *hominis* mite.
* **HK Epidemiology**: Highly contagious. Endemic in dense populations; frequent outbreaks in **residential care homes (院舍)**, kindergartens, and crowded living environments (e.g., sub-divided flats).

### Clinical Definition & Epidemiology of Pediculosis Capitis (頭蝨)
* **Definition**: Scalp infestation by blood-sucking louse *Pediculus humanus capitis*.
* **HK Epidemiology**: Very common in local primary schools. Spread via direct head-to-head contact; rarely via fomites (hats/combs).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Scabies (疥瘡)
* **Mechanism**: Gravid female mite burrows into **stratum corneum (角質層)** → lays 2-3 eggs/day + scybala (feces).
* **Immunology**: Intense itch is a **Delayed Type IV Hypersensitivity** reaction to mite antigens (feces/eggs).
* **Timeline**: 1st infection → itch takes **2-6 weeks** to develop. Re-infection → itch develops in **24-48 hours** (pre-sensitized).

### Pathophysiology of Pediculosis Capitis (頭蝨)
* **Mechanism**: Adult lice live directly on scalp → feed on human blood multiple times/day.
* **Lifecycle**: Female violently glues eggs (**nits (蝨卵)**) to base of hair shaft near warm scalp. Hatch in 7-10 days.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Classic Presentation of Scabies in Older Children & Adults
* **Pruritus**: Exquisite, unbearable itching → strictly **worse at night (夜間痕癢)**.
* **Burrows (隧道)**: Pathognomonic fine, wavy, 2-15mm greyish/silvery threads.
* **Classic Distribution**: Finger webs, flexor wrists, axillae, periumbilical, groin/genitals, areolae.
* **Sparing**: Strictly **spares** the face, scalp, palms, and soles in older patients.

### Atypical Presentation of Scabies in Infants (<2 years old)
* **Atypical Distribution**: **Involves** the head, face, neck, **palms (手掌)**, and **soles (腳掌)**.
* **Morphology**: Often presents as generalized papules, vesicles, or pustules rather than classic burrows.

### Classic Presentation of Pediculosis Capitis
* **Pruritus**: Intense scalp itching → predominantly behind ears (retroauricular) & nape of neck (occipital).
* **Nits (蝨卵)**: Tiny, firmly attached, white/grey tear-drop shaped eggs stuck fast to hair shaft (<1cm from scalp).
* **Excoriations**: Scratch marks + secondary cervical lymphadenopathy.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Scabies from Atopic Eczema (濕疹)
* **Location**: Eczema classically affects flexural creases (antecubital/popliteal fossae); Scabies targets finger webs/genitals.
* **Morphology**: Eczema lacks linear burrows.
* **History**: Eczema is chronic/relapsing; Scabies presents with acute onset of severe night itch affecting multiple family members simultaneously.

### Differentiating Lice Nits from Dandruff
* **Adherence**: **Dandruff** flakes off easily; **Nits** are tightly cemented to the hair shaft and cannot be flicked off.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Intensely Pruritic Rashes
| Condition | Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Scabies (疥瘡)** | Night itch, web-space burrows, multiple family members affected. |
| **Bedbug Bites (臭蟲叮咬)** | Linear track of 3-4 erythematous bites ("breakfast, lunch, dinner") on exposed skin. |
| **Atopic Eczema (濕疹)** | Flexural involvement, personal/family history of atopy, dry scaly skin. |
| **Papular Urticaria (丘疹性蕁麻疹)** | Hypersensitivity to insect bites (fleas/mosquitoes); recurrent grouped crops. |
| **Contact Dermatitis (接觸性皮炎)** | Rash strictly limited to areas of allergen/irritant contact (e.g., nickel belt buckle). |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Diagnosis of Scabies & Lice
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: History (night itch + contacts) + classic examination findings usually sufficient.
* **Wet Combing (Lice)**: Fine-toothed nit comb physically extracts live lice/nits on white paper (diagnostic).

### Special Dermatological Investigations for Scabies
* **Dermatoscopy (皮鏡檢查)**: Magnifies burrow → reveals **"delta wing" / "jet plane" sign** (mite at the blind end).
* **Skin Scraping (皮膚刮片)**: Scrape burrow with scalpel + mineral oil → microscopy definitively confirms live mites, eggs, or **scybala (feces)**.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### 1st-Line Pharmacological Management of Scabies
* **Medication**: **Topical 5% Permethrin Cream (Lyclear) (氯菊酯)**.
* **Application Rule (>2 yrs & Adults)**: Apply strictly to the **ENTIRE body** from jawline down to soles of feet (include under nails/genitals).
* **Application Rule (Infants <2 yrs & Immunocompromised)**: Must include the **face and scalp**, but carefully avoid eyes/mouth.
* **Duration**: Leave on for **8-12 hours** overnight → wash off thoroughly in morning.
* **Repetition**: MUST repeat exact process **1 week later** (kills newly hatched nymphs).

### Contact Tracing & Environmental Decontamination for Scabies
* **Treat All Contacts**: EVERY household member/close contact must be treated **simultaneously** on the exact same night.
* **Rationale**: Asymptomatic contacts may have incubatory mites (takes 4 wks for itch) → prevents "ping-pong" transmission.
* **Decontamination**: Wash all recently used bedding/clothes/towels at **>60°C**. Unwashable items must be sealed in plastic bags for 72 hours (mites die off-host).

### Acute Management of Pediculosis Capitis
* **Physical Removal**: Wet combing with fine-toothed nit comb + hair conditioner every 3-4 days for 2 weeks (Bug Busting).
* **Topical Pediculicides**: **0.5% Malathion liquid** OR **Dimeticone lotion** (suffocates lice physically).
* **Application**: Apply to dry hair → leave for specified time (often overnight) → wash. Repeat in 7 days.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Public Health & School Management in Hong Kong
* **School Exclusion**: Children with Scabies must stay home until 24 hours after completion of 1st medical treatment.
* **Notification**: Scabies outbreaks in HK institutions (e.g., kindergartens) must be reported to the **Centre for Health Protection (CHP) (衞生防護中心)**.
* **Education**: Reassure parents regarding stigma; poor hygiene is NOT the primary cause of transmission.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Neglect Indicators in Ectoparasitic Infections
* **Red Flags**: Chronic, untreated, severe, or heavily encrusted scabies (Norwegian scabies) in a child.
* **Action**: Assess for broader signs of **Child Neglect (疏忽照顧)**. Involve Medical Social Worker (MSW) and consider referral to Social Welfare Department (SWD).

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Infectious Complications of Scabies & Lice
* **Secondary Bacterial Infection**: Excoriation → breach of skin barrier → **Impetigo (膿疱瘡)** or **Cellulitis (蜂窩組織炎)**.
* **Pathogens**: *Staphylococcus aureus* or Group A *Streptococcus* (GAS).
* **Post-Streptococcal Sequelae**: Untreated GAS superinfection of scabies can → **Post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (PSGN)**.

### Immunological Complications of Scabies
* **Post-Scabietic Itch (疥瘡後痕癢)**: Persistent severe pruritus lasting **4-6 weeks** AFTER successful eradication.
* **Mechanism**: Prolonged hypersensitivity to dead mites/feces retained in the epidermis.
* **Management**: Emollients, moderate topical corticosteroids, oral antihistamines (DO NOT re-treat with Permethrin).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Prescribing Permethrin only for the child who is currently itching. Pearl: The asymptomatic parents/siblings likely have incubatory mites. Failure to treat the whole house simultaneously on the exact same day leads to instant reinfection (ping-pong transmission).
* Viva Question: A mother treats her child strictly with Permethrin for scabies. Two weeks later, she returns furious because the child is still itching, though no new burrows are seen. Why? Answer: Post-scabietic itch. The itching is a massive allergic Type IV reaction to the dead mites and their feces under the skin. It takes 4-6 weeks for the epidermis to shed and the allergy to subside, long after all mites are dead. Treat with topical steroids, not more Permethrin.
* Pitfall: Missing scabies in a 6-month-old infant by only checking the finger webs. Infants classically have atypical involvement of the scalp, face, palms, and soles. You must examine the entire body and modify the Permethrin application to include the head.
* Viva Question: What is the classic dermatoscopic sign of a scabies mite in a burrow? Answer: The "delta wing" or "jet plane" sign.
* Trap: Keeping a child out of school for weeks until all nits are gone. Pearl: Children with head lice can return to school immediately after the first appropriate treatment has been applied. Nits (empty egg casings) can remain glued to the hair for months and do not indicate active infection.
