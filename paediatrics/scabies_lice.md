# Scabies & Pediculosis Capitis (Head Lice) (疥瘡及頭蝨)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Scabies**: An intensely pruritic skin infestation caused by the *Sarcoptes scabiei* mite. Very common in crowded living conditions, schools, and residential care homes in Hong Kong.
- **Pediculosis Capitis (Head Lice)**: An infestation of the scalp hair by the blood-sucking louse *Pediculus humanus capitis*. Common in primary school children.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Scabies**: The female mite physically burrows into the stratum corneum of the epidermis to lay eggs. The hallmark intense itching is actually a delayed Type IV hypersensitivity allergic reaction to the mite's feces and eggs (which takes 2-6 weeks to develop after initial infection).
- **Head Lice**: The lice live exclusively directly on the scalp, feeding on human blood multiple times a day. The female violently glues her eggs (nits) to the base of the hair shaft near the warm scalp.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **Scabies**: A 6-year-old child who cannot sleep because he is scratching himself raw every night. His father and brother have also recently started intensely itching.
- **Lice**: A 7-year-old girl constantly scratching her head at school. The teacher notices tiny white specks glued to her hair.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **SCABIES**:
  - Pruritus: Exquisite, unbearable itching that is fiercely **worse at night**.
  - **Burrows**: Tiny, fine, wavy, greyish threads on the skin. Specifically found in the **finger webs, flexor surfaces of the wrists, axillae, groin, and areolae**.
  - In infants <2 years old, scabies often atypically involves the head, face, palms, and soles (which is strictly spared in older children/adults).
- **LICE**:
  - Intense scalp itching, particularly behind the ears and at the nape of the neck.
  - **Nits**: Tiny, firmly attached, white/grey tear-drop shaped eggs stuck fast to the hair shaft within 1cm of the scalp. (Dandruff flakes off easily; nits do not).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Atopic Eczema: Eczema classically affects the flexural creases (knees/elbows) and lacks the distinct linear finger-web burrows. Eczema also rarely suddenly affects the whole family simultaneously in a 2-week window.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Bedbug bites (often present as a linear track of 3 red bites: "breakfast, lunch, and dinner").
- Tinea Corporis.
- Contact Dermatitis.

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- Mostly a clinical diagnosis based on history (night itch + family members).
- **Dermatoscopy**: Examining a burrow under magnification can reveal the "delta wing" or "jet plane" sign (the tiny mite at the end of the burrow).
- **Skin Scraping**: Scraping the burrow and viewing under a microscope to definitively see the mite, eggs, or scybala (mite feces).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **SCABIES**:
  - **Topical 5% Permethrin Cream** (Lyclear). 
  - **CRITICAL APPLICATION RULE**: Must be applied to the ENTIRE body from the jawline down to the soles of the feet (including under nails). In infants <2yrs, it must include the face/scalp but avoid the eyes/mouth.
  - Leave on for 8-12 hours overnight, then wash off. Repeat the exact process 1 week later to kill newly hatched eggs.
  - **Treat All Contacts**: EVERY member of the household must be treated simultaneously on the exact same night, even if they have zero symptoms. (Because the allergic itch takes 4 weeks to develop, asymptomatic people are actively spreading mites).
  - Decontaminate: Wash all bedding/clothes at 60°C.
- **HEAD LICE**:
  - Wet combing with a fine-toothed nit comb and hair conditioner every 3-4 days to physically drag out bugs.
  - Topical pediculicides (e.g., 0.5% Malathion liquid or Dimeticone lotion).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Secondary bacterial infection (Staphylococcus aureus or Group A Strep) leading to impetigo or Cellulitis from relentless scratching.
- "Post-scabietic itch": The allergic itch can persist for up to 4-6 weeks *after* all the mites are dead. (Requires immense parental reassurance and topical steroids/antihistamines, not more Permethrin).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Prescribing Permethrin only for the child who is itching. The asymptomatic parents/siblings likely have incubatory mites and will instantly reinfect the child next week (ping-pong transmission). Treat the whole house on the same day.
- **Viva Q**: "A mother treats her child strictly with Permethrin for scabies. Two weeks later, she returns furious because the child is still itching, though no new burrows are seen. Why?" Answer: Post-scabietic itch. The itching is a massive allergic reaction to the dead mites and their feces under the skin. It takes 4-6 weeks for the epidermis to shed and the allergy to subside, long after the mites are dead.
