# Atrial Septal Defect (心房中隔缺損)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Definition**: Congenital defect in the **interatrial septum (心房中隔)** → allows left-to-right shunting of blood.
* **Haemodynamic Effect**: ↑ right atrial (RA) & right ventricular (RV) volume overload → ↑ pulmonary blood flow.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong)
* Accounts for **~10%** of **congenital heart disease (先天性心臟病)** in HK.
* **Ostium secundum (第二孔缺損)** = most common type (80%).
* **Detection**: Often incidental via **Student Health Service (學生健康服務)** school screenings in asymptomatic children.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Shunt
* **Mechanism**: L-to-R shunt occurs due to ↑ left atrial pressure & ↑ left ventricular compliance compared to right side.
* **Result**: Volume overload in right heart → **Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (RVH)** & **Pulmonary artery dilatation**.
* **Flow Murmur**: Murmur is NOT across the ASD itself → it's an **ejection systolic murmur (收縮期噴射性雜音)** across the normal pulmonary valve due to ↑↑ blood volume.

### Anatomical Classification
| Type | Location | Associations/Genetics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Secundum (第二孔)** | Central septum (foramen ovale) | Most common. |
| **Primum (第一孔)** | Lower septum (AV septum) | **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症)**, cleft mitral valve (mitral regurgitation). |
| **Sinus Venosus (靜脈竇)** | Upper septum (near SVC) | **Partial Anomalous Pulmonary Venous Return (PAPVR)**. |

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### General Presentation
* **Infancy/Early Childhood**: Usually **asymptomatic (無症狀)**.
* **Late Childhood/Adolescence**:
  * Recurrent **chest infections (胸腔感染)**.
  * Mild **exercise intolerance (運動耐力下降)** / wheeze.
  * Arrhythmias (e.g., Atrial Fibrillation - rarer in childhood, peaks in 4th decade).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Auscultation Findings (Gold Standard Signs)
* **S2**: **Fixed, widely split S2 (固定且寬的分裂第二心音)** → Hallmark sign. RV stroke volume remains equal during inspiration & expiration due to constant ↑ volume.
* **Murmur 1**: **Ejection systolic murmur** at Left Upper Sternal Border (LUSB) → ↑ flow across pulmonary valve.
* **Murmur 2**: **Mid-diastolic rumble** at Left Lower Sternal Border (LLSB) → seen in large shunts due to ↑ flow across tricuspid valve.

### Additional Cardiac Signs
* **Palpation**: **RV heave (右心室抬舉)** at LLSB (due to RVH).
* **Pulses**: Normal volume and character.
* **Cyanosis**: Absent (unless Eisenmenger syndrome develops late).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating ASD from Other Murmurs
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ASD** | **Fixed, widely split S2**, ejection systolic murmur LUSB, RV heave. |
| **Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)** | **Pansystolic murmur** at LLSB, palpable thrill, S2 splitting varies with respiration. |
| **Pulmonary Stenosis (PS)** | Ejection systolic murmur LUSB, but **S2 splitting varies** with respiration, +ve ejection click. |
| **Innocent Flow Murmur** | **Normal physiological S2 splitting** (varies with respiration), murmur changes with posture. |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Primary Differentials
* **Innocent pulmonary flow murmur (無害性心雜音)**.
* **Pulmonary stenosis (肺動脈狹窄)**.
* **VSD (心室中隔缺損)** (small/muscular).
* **PAPVR** (often coexists with sinus venosus ASD).

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Bloods
* **ECG (心電圖)**: 
  * **Secundum ASD**: **Partial RBBB (rsR' in V1)**, **Right Axis Deviation (RAD)**.
  * **Primum ASD**: **Left Axis Deviation (LAD)** (superior QRS axis negative in aVF), 1st-degree heart block.

### Imaging
* **CXR (胸部X光)**: Cardiomegaly (RA/RV enlargement), prominent pulmonary artery knob, **↑ pulmonary vascular markings (plethora)**.
* **Gold Standard**: **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**:
  * Confirms defect size, anatomical location, shunt direction (L→R).
  * Quantifies RV volume overload & estimates pulmonary artery pressures.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
* ASD rarely presents as an acute emergency in childhood. Treat intercurrent respiratory infections with standard HA antibiotic protocols.

### Chronic / Definitive Management
* **Observation**: Small defects (<5mm) → high chance of spontaneous closure by 18 months. Follow up in pediatric cardiology clinic.
* **Indications for Closure**: Significant L→R shunt causing **RV volume overload**, usually planned at **3–5 years of age** (before school entry).

### HA Protocols & Surgical Options
| Intervention | Indication | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Transcatheter Device Closure (導管微創手術)** | 1st-line for suitable **secundum ASDs** with adequate tissue rims. | **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH)**. |
| **Open Surgical Closure (外科修補術)** | Primum ASDs, sinus venosus ASDs, or secundum ASDs with inadequate rims. | **HKCH** (Cardiothoracic Surgery). |

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Approach
* **Dental Care**: Good dental hygiene education (MCHC/School Dental Care Service). Note: **Infective Endocarditis (IE) prophylaxis** is strictly **NOT** required for isolated, uncomplicated secundum ASDs prior to closure.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Assess financial eligibility for Samaritan Fund/CCF if non-eligible person fees apply or specialized devices needed.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### NAI Considerations
* Not directly applicable to ASD etiology.
* Ensure parents attend cardiology follow-ups; repeated defaulting of HKCH appointments despite reminders requires MSW/school social worker exploration for medical neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Untreated Complications
* **Adulthood (30s-40s)**: Atrial arrhythmias (Atrial Fibrillation/Flutter), right-sided heart failure.
* **Paradoxical Embolism (矛盾栓塞)**: DVT crossing ASD → **Stroke (中風)**.
* **Eisenmenger Syndrome**: Rare & late; irreversible pulmonary hypertension reversing shunt (R→L) causing cyanosis.

### Prognosis
* Excellent post-closure. Normal life expectancy and exercise tolerance.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Thinking the ASD murmur is caused by blood flowing through the septal defect. Pearl: The pressure gradient across the atria is too low to produce a murmur. The ejection systolic murmur is generated by increased blood volume flowing across the normal pulmonary valve.
* Viva Question: Why is the second heart sound (S2) fixed and widely split in ASD? Answer: The massive left-to-right shunt equalizes the volume in the right atrium during both inspiration and expiration, causing a constant delay in pulmonary valve closure regardless of the respiratory cycle.
* Pitfall: Recommending endocarditis prophylaxis for an isolated secundum ASD. Current guidelines do not recommend antibiotic prophylaxis for uncomplicated secundum ASDs before dental procedures.
* Viva Question: What ECG finding strongly differentiates a primum ASD from a secundum ASD? Answer: A primum ASD typically shows Left Axis Deviation (LAD) / superior axis, whereas a secundum ASD shows Right Axis Deviation (RAD) with partial RBBB.
