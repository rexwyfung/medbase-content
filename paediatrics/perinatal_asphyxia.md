# Perinatal Asphyxia & Hypoxic Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE) (圍產期窒息與缺氧缺血性腦病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Perinatal Asphyxia (圍產期窒息)**: Impaired gas exchange intrapartum → fetal hypoxia, hypercapnia, and metabolic acidosis
* **Hypoxic-Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE) (缺氧缺血性腦病)**: Neurological syndrome resulting from asphyxial brain injury

### Local Epidemiology & HA Care
* Incidence: ~1-2/1000 live births (moderate-severe HIE)
* HK Hospital Authority (HA) Management: Admitted to regional NICUs (e.g., QMH, PWH, KWH, UCH) equipped for **Therapeutic Hypothermia (亞低溫治療)**

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Primary Energy Failure
* Hypoxia → ↓ ATP → cell depolarization
* ↑ Intracellular Ca2+ influx → acute cell death

### Latent Phase
* 1-6 hrs post-insult
* Partial metabolic recovery
* **CRITICAL** therapeutic window for cooling intervention

### Secondary Energy Failure
* 6-48 hrs post-insult
* Reperfusion injury → oxidative stress
* Glutamate excitotoxicity → apoptosis and irreversible brain damage

### Multi-organ Dysfunction
* Kidneys → **Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN) (急性腎小管壞死)**
* Heart → Myocardial dysfunction / hypotension
* Gut → **Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC) (壞死性小腸結腸炎)**
* Liver → Transaminitis / Coagulopathy / DIC

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Antenatal & Intrapartum History
* Sentinel event (e.g., placental abruption, cord prolapse, uterine rupture)
* Pathological cardiotocography (CTG) (e.g., prolonged decelerations, persistent fetal bradycardia)
* Thick meconium-stained liquor

### Immediate Neonatal Presentation
* Poor Apgar scores (e.g., < 5 at 5 & 10 mins)

* Profound cord blood acidemia

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Sarnat Staging of HIE
| Stage | Level | Consciousness | Tone / Reflexes | Autonomic | Seizures |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 1** | Mild | Hyperalert / Irritable | Normal / Uninhibited | Sympathetic (Tachycardia, Mydriasis) | None |
| **Stage 2** | Moderate | Lethargic | Hypotonia / Weak suck | Parasympathetic (Bradycardia, Miosis) | Common |
| **Stage 3** | Severe | Comatose | Flaccid / Absent | Depressed / Fixed pupils | Intractable |

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### HIE vs. Inborn Errors of Metabolism (IEM)
* **HIE (缺氧缺血性腦病)**: Abnormal birth history, low Apgars, onset immediately at birth
* **IEM (先天性代謝異常)**: Normal birth history, normal Apgars, encephalopathy/seizures develop *after* feeding initiation (protein load)

### HIE vs. Congenital Infection
* **HIE**: History of acute intrapartum hypoxia/sentinel event
* **TORCH Infection**: Intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR), microcephaly, hepatosplenomegaly, petechial rash present at birth

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis Table
| Category | Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Most Likely** | **HIE (缺氧缺血性腦病)** | Sentinel event, severe fetal distress, cord pH < 7.0 |
| **Must-Not-Miss** | **Intracranial Haemorrhage (顱內出血)** | Bulging fontanelle, ↓ Hb, subgaleal/intraventricular bleed |
| **Infectious** | **Neonatal Sepsis / Meningitis (新生兒敗血症/腦膜炎)** | Maternal GBS +ve, prolonged rupture of membranes, fever/hypothermia |
| **Metabolic** | **Urea Cycle Defects (尿素循環障礙)** | Normal Apgars, sudden deterioration day 2-3, ↑↑ Ammonia |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **Cord Blood Gas (臍帶血氣分析)**: Objective asphyxia marker (pH < 7.0, Base Deficit > 16 mmol/L)
* **Amplitude-Integrated EEG (aEEG) (振幅整合腦電圖)**: 
  * Continuous background brain monitoring
  * Detects subclinical seizures
  * Assesses cooling eligibility (e.g., burst suppression, flat trace)

### Laboratory Investigations
* **Multi-organ Screen**:
  * U&E (AKI screening)
  * LFTs (Hepatic injury)
  * Troponin / CK-MB (Myocardial ischaemia)
  * Coagulation Profile (DIC screening)

### Gold Standard Imaging
* **MRI Brain (腦部核磁共振)**:
  * Performed at Day 5-7 (post-rewarming)
  * **Acute profound insult**: Deep grey matter (basal ganglia/thalamus) injury
  * **Prolonged partial insult**: Watershed (parasagittal) cortical injury

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Delivery Room Management
* **Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP) (新生兒復甦)**:
  * Prioritize effective ventilation
  * Start with 21% O2 (room air) for term infants (avoid hyperoxia → ↑ free radicals)

### HA Protocol: Definitive Management
* **Therapeutic Hypothermia (Cooling) (亞低溫治療)**:
  * Indication: ≥ 36 weeks gestation with Moderate/Severe HIE
  * Target: Rectal temp 33.5°C for 72 hrs
  * Mechanism: ↓ metabolic rate, ↓ excitotoxins, ↓ apoptosis during secondary energy failure
  * **CRITICAL**: MUST initiate within 6 hrs of birth

### Supportive NICU Management
* **Seizure Control**: **Phenobarbitone (苯巴比妥)** is 1st-line anti-epileptic
* **Fluid Restriction**: Manage transient renal impairment / SIADH
* **Systemic Targets**: Maintain normoglycaemia, normotension; avoid hyperoxia/hypocapnia (alters cerebral blood flow)

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team
* **Physiotherapy / Occupational Therapy**: Motor tone and developmental management
* **Speech Therapy**: Swallowing / feeding assessments

### HA Psychosocial Follow-up
* **Child Assessment Service (CAS) (兒童體能智力測驗服務)**: Mandatory long-term neurodevelopmental monitoring
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Family psychosocial and financial support

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations
* Clear documentation of all intrapartum events and resuscitation timelines
* Honest, empathic prognostication by NICU Consultant (avoiding definitive long-term predictions in first 72h)

### NAI Red Flags
* Unexplained profound encephalopathy without sentinel event/asphyxia
* Consider Shaken Baby Syndrome (inflicted traumatic brain injury) or undisclosed maternal drug abuse if history is inconsistent

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Prognosis
* **Mild HIE**: Usually normal outcome
* **Moderate/Severe HIE**: High risk of mortality or severe neurodevelopmental impairment (dictated by Sarnat stage and MRI findings)

### Long-term Complications
* **Cerebral Palsy (CP) (腦性麻痺)**:
  * Dyskinetic/Athetoid CP (if basal ganglia injured)
  * Spastic Quadriplegia (if severe global injury)
* **Epilepsy (癲癇)**
* Intellectual disability
* Cortical visual impairment
* Sensorineural hearing loss

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Giving 100% oxygen during term neonatal resuscitation. Pearl: Resuscitation MUST start with 21% (room air) as hyperoxia generates free radicals which worsen reperfusion injury in the asphyxiated brain.
* Viva Question: What is the therapeutic window for starting cooling in HIE, and why? Answer: Within 6 hours of birth to target the latent phase before the onset of secondary energy failure and irreversible apoptosis.
* Pitfall: Assuming all neonatal encephalopathy is HIE. Always check maternal history, cord gas, and exclude metabolic disorders or congenital infections if the birth history and Apgar scores were entirely uncomplicated.
