
# Learning Disability / Intellectual Disability (智力障礙 / 學習障礙)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**:
  - **Intellectual Disability (ID)**: Significant limitations in both intellectual functioning (IQ < 70) AND adaptive behaviour (conceptual, social, and practical skills), originating before age 18.
  - **Specific Learning Difficulties (SpLD)**: e.g., Dyslexia. Normal intelligence, but specific difficulties in reading, writing, or mathematics.
- **Local Epidemiology**: ID affects ~1-3% of the population. Dyslexia is common (~10%). Diagnosed and managed via the Child Assessment Service (CAS) and the Education Bureau (EDB) in HK.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Prenatal**: Chromosomal abnormalities (Down syndrome, Fragile X), structural brain malformations, congenital infections (TORCH), fetal alcohol syndrome.
- **Perinatal**: Hypoxic Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE), extreme prematurity, intraventricular haemorrhage.
- **Postnatal**: Meningitis/encephalitis, severe head trauma, severe psychosocial deprivation.
- *In many mild cases, no specific cause is found.*

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Infants/Toddlers**: Global Developmental Delay (GDD) - delay in 2 or more domains (gross/fine motor, speech/language, cognition, social/personal).
- **School-age**: Academic failure, behavioural problems, difficulty with peer relationships, lack of self-care skills appropriate for age.
- **Dysmorphic features**: Suggest a syndromic cause (e.g., Down syndrome facies, large ears in Fragile X).
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **Specific Learning Difficulty (Dyslexia)**: The child has normal or above-average intelligence but struggles specifically with reading/writing. ID involves global cognitive impairment.
- **Hearing/Vision Impairment**: Can mimic ID due to poor academic performance.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Idiopathic ID, Down Syndrome, Fragile X Syndrome.
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Treatable metabolic disorders (e.g., Phenylketonuria - screened at birth in HK, Hypothyroidism).
  - Progressive neurodegenerative disorders (loss of previously acquired skills).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **Hearing and Vision Screening**: Mandatory to rule out sensory deficits.
  - **Thorough Physical Exam**: Look for dysmorphic features, neurocutaneous stigmata (e.g., ash-leaf spots for TSC), and neurological deficits.
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Formal Psychometric Assessment**: Conducted by a Clinical Psychologist at CAS using standardised tools (e.g., WISC-V for older children, Griffiths Mental Development Scales for younger children).
  - **Genetic Testing**: Chromosomal Microarray (CMA) is the first-line genetic test for unexplained GDD/ID. Fragile X DNA testing.
  - **MRI Brain**: If there are focal neurological signs, micro/macrocephaly, or seizures.
- **Monitoring**:
  - Thyroid function tests, metabolic screening if clinically indicated.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Not applicable.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - **No medical cure for ID**. Treat co-morbidities (e.g., anti-epileptics for seizures, methylphenidate for ADHD).
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **Educational Support (EDB)**: Crucial. Depending on severity, placement in mainstream schools with intensive support, or Special Schools (Mild, Moderate, or Severe Grade ID schools).
- **Pre-school**: SWD services (EETC, SCCC, OPRS).
- **Therapies**: Occupational therapy (life skills), Speech therapy.
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- **Vulnerability**: Children with ID are at a significantly higher risk of abuse and neglect. High index of suspicion required.
- MSW referral for family support, respite care, and disability allowances.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Lifelong condition. Prognosis depends on the severity of the ID and the presence of co-morbidities (e.g., severe epilepsy).
- Transition to adult services (psychiatry, adult SWD services like sheltered workshops) is a critical period.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Confusing GDD and ID. *Pearl*: GDD is the term used for children < 5 years old. ID is the formal diagnosis made > 5 years old when IQ testing is reliable.
- **Viva Question**: "What is the first-line genetic investigation for a child with unexplained global developmental delay?" *Answer*: Chromosomal Microarray (CMA), not a standard karyotype.
