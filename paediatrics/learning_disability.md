# Learning Disability / Intellectual Disability (智力障礙 / 學習障礙)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definitions
- **Intellectual Disability (ID) (智力障礙)**: Significant limitations in BOTH intellectual functioning (IQ < 70) AND adaptive behaviour (conceptual, social, and practical skills). Onset must be <18 years of age. Severity (Mild/Moderate/Severe) is based on functional assessment, not just IQ.
- **Specific Learning Difficulties (SpLD) (特殊學習困難)**: Normal or above-average intelligence with specific functional deficits. Examples include **Dyslexia (讀寫障礙)** (reading), **Dyscalculia (運算障礙)** (mathematics), and **Dysgraphia (書寫障礙)** (writing).

### Hong Kong Epidemiology
- ID affects ~1-3% of the general population.
- SpLD (especially Dyslexia) is common, affecting ~10%.
- Formal diagnosis and triaging in HK are managed via the **Child Assessment Service (CAS) (兒童體能智力測驗服務)** and supported by the **Education Bureau (EDB) (教育局)**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Prenatal Causes
- **Chromosomal**: **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症)** (Trisomy 21 - most common genetic cause), **Fragile X Syndrome (脆性X綜合症)** (most common inherited cause).
- **Microdeletions**: 22q11.2 deletion syndrome, Williams syndrome (7q11).
- **Structural**: Cerebral dysgenesis, neural tube defects.
- **Teratogenic/Infective**: Congenital infections (TORCH, Zika virus), Fetal Alcohol Syndrome.

### Perinatal Causes
- **Hypoxic Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE) (缺氧缺血性腦病)**, extreme prematurity, intraventricular haemorrhage, periventricular leukomalacia.

### Postnatal Causes
- Central nervous system infections (Meningitis/Encephalitis), severe head trauma, symptomatic hypoglycaemia, severe psychosocial deprivation/neglect.
- *Note*: In ~50% of mild ID cases, no specific aetiology is ever identified.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Infant/Toddler Presentation
- **Global Developmental Delay (GDD) (整體發展遲緩)**: Delay in ≥2 domains (gross motor, vision/fine motor, hearing/speech/language, social/emotional/behavioural).

### School-Age Presentation
- Academic failure, behavioural challenges, difficulty maintaining peer relationships, lack of age-appropriate self-care skills.

### Dysmorphic Features & Physical Signs
- Suggests syndromic aetiology.
- e.g., Macrocephaly/large ears (Fragile X), flat occiput/up-slanting palpebral fissures/single palpebral crease (Down Syndrome), neurocutaneous stigmata like ash-leaf spots (Tuberous Sclerosis).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### ID vs Specific Learning Difficulty (SpLD)
- **ID**: Involves global cognitive impairment and poor adaptive functioning across multiple domains.
- **SpLD**: Child has normal/high IQ but struggles specifically in one academic domain (e.g., reading in Dyslexia).

### ID vs Sensory Impairment
- **Sensory Deficits**: Unrecognized hearing or vision impairment strongly mimics ID/GDD due to poor academic/social performance. Must explicitly test for and exclude.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Key Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) (自閉症譜系障礙)** | Impaired social communication + restrictive/repetitive behaviours. Often co-exists with ID. |
| **ADHD (專注力不足/過度活躍症)** | Inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity. Normal IQ but poor focus mimics learning failure. |
| **Treatable Metabolic Disorders (代謝病)** | e.g., Phenylketonuria, Hypothyroidism. Universal newborn screening in HK detects most. |
| **Neurodegenerative Disorders** | Loss of previously acquired skills (e.g., Mucopolysaccharidoses, Tay-Sachs disease). |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
- **Hearing/Vision Screening (聽力及視力普查)**: Mandatory 1st step to rule out sensory deficits.
- **Growth Parameters**: Plot HC, weight, length/height to detect microcephaly/macrocephaly or faltering growth.

### Bloods & Genetic Investigations
- **Gold Standard 1st Line Genetic Test**: **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA) (染色體微陣列分析)** for unexplained GDD/ID.
- **Targeted Genetics**: Fragile X DNA testing (FMR1 gene trinucleotide repeat).
- **Metabolic Screen**: TFTs, amino acids, acylcarnitine profile if clinically/historically indicated.

### Imaging & Special Assessments
- **MRI Brain**: Indicated if focal neurological signs, micro/macrocephaly, or seizures.
- **Formal Psychometric Assessment**: Gold standard functional test. Conducted by Clinical Psychologist at **CAS** using standardized tools (e.g., WISC-V for >5y, Griffiths Mental Development Scales for <5y).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management
- Manage concurrent acute emergencies only (e.g., status epilepticus, acute intercurrent infections).

### Chronic & Definitive Management
- No medical cure for ID. Treat specific comorbidities aggressively.
- **Epilepsy**: Standard anti-epileptic drug (AED) protocols.
- **ADHD**: **Methylphenidate (喀他林)** for symptom control.
- **Spasticity/Cerebral Palsy**: Baclofen, botulinum toxin, orthoses.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Educational Support (EDB)
- Crucial intervention. Depending on IQ/severity:
  - Mainstream schools + Intensive Support Grant.
  - **Special Schools (特殊學校)**: Segregated into Mild, Moderate, or Severe Grade ID schools.

### Social Welfare Department (SWD) Services
- Preschool rehabilitation services:
  - **EETC (早期教育及訓練中心)**: Early Education and Training Centre.
  - **SCCC (特殊幼兒中心)**: Special Child Care Centre.
  - **OPRS (到校學前康復服務)**: On-site Pre-school Rehabilitation Services.

### Allied Health Therapy
- **Occupational Therapy (OT)**: ADLs, self-care, fine motor, sensory integration.
- **Speech Therapy (ST)**: Language development, alternative communication (Makaton), swallowing.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Vulnerability Profile
- Children with ID/GDD have significantly ↑ risk of abuse (physical, emotional, sexual) and neglect. High clinical index of suspicion required.

### Management of Safeguarding Risks
- Mandatory **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)** referral for holistic family support.
- Arrange respite care to prevent carer burnout.
- Assist family in applying for **Disability Allowance (傷殘津貼)**.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognostic Factors
- Lifelong condition. Prognosis heavily depends on ID severity (mild vs severe) and the burden of physical comorbidities (e.g., congenital heart disease, severe therapy-resistant epilepsy).

### Transition of Care
- Transitioning from paediatric to adult services (Adult Psychiatry, Adult SWD services like Sheltered Workshops) is a critical high-risk period requiring dedicated MDT handover.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Confusing GDD and ID.
  Pearl: GDD is the term used for children < 5 years old. ID is the formal diagnosis made > 5 years old when IQ testing is reliable.
* Viva Question: What is the first-line genetic investigation for a child with unexplained global developmental delay?
  Answer: Chromosomal Microarray (CMA), not a standard karyotype.
* Pitfall: Assuming poor academic performance is purely an intellectual issue. Exclude vision/hearing impairment and specific learning difficulties (SpLD) before diagnosing ID.
* Trap: Failing to recognize treatable metabolic causes of GDD.
  Pearl: Always check newborn screening results and consider TFTs/metabolic workup (e.g., Hypothyroidism) as early intervention prevents irreversible ID.
