# Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21) (唐氏綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- Most common chromosomal abnormality causing intellectual disability.
- Trisomy of chromosome 21 (entire or partial).
### Local Epidemiology
- HK Incidence: ~1 in 700-1,000 live births.
- Global incidence without screening: ~1 in 650.
- Risk exponentially ↑ with advancing maternal age (e.g. 1/1530 at age 20 → 1/110 at age 40).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Nondisjunction (94-95%)
- Failure of chr 21 separation during meiosis (mostly maternal meiosis I).
- Karyotype: 47, XX,+21 or 47, XY,+21.
- Recurrence risk: ~1% (or age-related risk if >35y).
### Translocation (4-5%)
- Unbalanced Robertsonian translocation, usually involving chr 14 & 21 [e.g., t(14;21)], occasionally t(15;21) or t(22;21).
- Karyotype: 46 chromosomes but 3 copies of chr 21 long arm.
- **CRITICAL**: If parent is balanced carrier, recurrence risk is 10-15% (maternal carrier), ~2.5% (paternal carrier), 100% (21;21 carrier).
### Mosaicism (1%)
- Post-zygotic mitotic error.
- Mix of 46 & 47 chr cell lines.
- Phenotype tends to be milder.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Antenatal Presentation
- Screened via **Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing (NIPT)** (cell-free DNA, ~90% positive predictive value).
- Ultrasound findings: ↑ nuchal translucency, AVSD, duodenal atresia.
- Confirmed via amniocentesis/CVS.
### Postnatal Presentation
- Recognized immediately in delivery room due to profound generalized **hypotonia** ("floppy baby") & pathognomonic dysmorphic facial features.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Craniofacial Signs
- Flat facial profile, flat occiput, flat nasal bridge.
- Upslanting palpebral fissures, prominent epicanthic folds.
- **Brushfield spots** (white/grey spots on iris periphery).
- Small low-set ears.
- Small mouth with protruding tongue (**Macroglossia**).
- Short neck, 3rd fontanelle.
### Musculoskeletal Signs
- Severe generalized **hypotonia**.
- Single transverse palmar crease (Simian crease).
- Clinodactyly (incurved, short 5th finger).
- Wide "sandal gap" between 1st & 2nd toes.
- Joint hypermobility.
### Neurological Signs
- Global developmental delay.
- Mild-moderate intellectual disability (IQ 35-70).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differential Table of Autosomal Trisomies
| Feature | Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21) (唐氏綜合症) | Edwards Syndrome (Trisomy 18) (愛德華氏綜合症) | Patau Syndrome (Trisomy 13) (巴陶氏綜合症) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Craniofacial** | Flat profile, upslanting fissures, Brushfield spots | Prominent occiput, micrognathia | Cleft lip/palate, microphthalmia |
| **Musculoskeletal** | Single palmar crease, sandal gap, hypotonia | Clenched hands, overlapping fingers, rocker-bottom feet | Polydactyly |
| **Other** | AVSD, Duodenal atresia | Short sternum, cardiac/renal malformations | Holoprosencephaly, scalp defects |
| **Prognosis** | 50-60+ years | Usually fatal <1 year | Usually fatal <1 year |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Congenital Hypothyroidism (先天性甲狀腺機能低下)
- Presents with hypotonia, macroglossia, developmental delay.
- Often co-exists with Down syndrome (must evaluate TFTs).
### Zellweger Syndrome
- Severe hypotonia, craniofacial dysmorphism, hepatomegaly.
### Chromosomal Microdeletions/Duplications
- Phenotypic overlap requires microarray to rule out if karyotype is atypical.

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Diagnostic Investigations
- **Karyotype or Chromosomal Microarray (CMA)**: Mandatory to confirm dx & differentiate nondisjunction from translocation for genetic counseling.
- **Rapid FISH / Real-time PCR**: Used for rapid bedside confirmation (1-2 days).
### Screening Investigations (HA Guidelines)
- **Echocardiogram**: Mandatory for ALL newborns (40-50% have CHD).
- **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs)**: At birth, 6mo, 12mo, then annually (15% risk of hypothyroidism).
- **Complete Blood Count (CBP)**: At birth (Transient Abnormal Myelopoiesis) & annually (leukaemia screen).
- **Audiology (OAE/ABR)**: Annual screen (75% risk secretory otitis media/sensorineural loss).
- **Ophthalmology**: Annual screen (cataracts 15%, myopia/strabismus 50%).
- **C-Spine X-ray**: Screen for atlantoaxial instability (~age 3-5, before contact sports/Special Olympics).
- **Coeliac Screen**: IgA tissue transglutaminase (tTG).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
- Aggressive tx of intercurrent respiratory infections (↑ susceptibility).
- Surgical correction of life-threatening anomalies: GI (Duodenal Atresia) & Cardiac (AVSD repair).
### Chronic & Anticipatory Management
- No medical cure; management is multidisciplinary.
- Plot growth on Down Syndrome-specific growth charts (risk of short stature & obesity).
- Monitor for Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA).
### Early Intervention Programmes (EIP)
- Referral to **Child Assessment Centre (CAC)** → Special Child Care Centre (SCCC).
- **Physiotherapy**: Improve motor milestones delayed by hypotonia.
- **Speech Therapy**: Address anatomical challenges (macroglossia) & hearing deficits. Sign language (Makaton) used early.
- **Occupational Therapy**: Fine motor skills & ADLs.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Medical Social Worker (MSW) Support
- Assist with Disability Allowance applications.
- Navigate HK special education system (Mild vs Moderate Grade Special Schools).
### Parental Support
- Connect to Hong Kong Down Syndrome Association (HKDSA).
- Psychological support for grief & adjustment post-diagnosis.
- Genetic counseling for future pregnancies.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Abuse Vulnerability
- Statistically significant ↑ risk of physical, emotional, sexual abuse, and neglect.
- Maintain high index of suspicion; involve MSW/Police early if signs are inconsistent with history.
### Carer Burnout
- Monitor for signs of carer fatigue, which ↑ risk of unintentional neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Cardiac & Gastrointestinal Complications
- **Cardiac (40-50%)**: **Atrioventricular Septal Defect (AVSD)** most classic. Also VSD, ASD, Tetralogy of Fallot. Major cause of early mortality.
- **GI (5%)**: Duodenal atresia ("double bubble" on AXR), Hirschsprung disease (<1%), imperforate anus, Coeliac disease.
### Haematological, Neurological & Respiratory Complications
- **Haem/Onc**: 10-20x ↑ risk of leukaemia. Acute Megakaryoblastic Leukaemia (AMKL) <3yrs, Acute Lymphoblastic Leukaemia (ALL) in older children.
- **Neurology**: Early-onset Alzheimer's disease (~40s, APP gene on chr 21), Epilepsy.
- **Respiratory/ENT**: OSA (50-75%), recurrent otitis media.
- **Orthopaedic**: Atlantoaxial instability, acquired hip dislocation.
### Prognosis
- Life expectancy dramatically improved (50-60+ years) due to proactive medical/surgical care.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common congenital heart defect in Down syndrome? Answer: Atrioventricular Septal Defect (AVSD).
* Viva Question: Why is it absolutely necessary to perform a karyotype if the clinical diagnosis of Down syndrome is obvious? Answer: To determine if it is caused by nondisjunction or a translocation. If it is a translocation, the parents must be tested, as the recurrence risk for future pregnancies can be extremely high (up to 100% for 21;21).
* Trap: Attributing a Down syndrome child's increasing lethargy and weight gain simply to "having Down syndrome". Pearl: Always check Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs) as there is a 15% risk of acquired autoimmune hypothyroidism which mimics these symptoms.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to screen for Coeliac disease. Children with Down syndrome have a much higher risk of Coeliac disease, which can present as faltering growth or behavioural changes.
