# Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS) (呼吸窘迫綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of RDS
* **Definition:** Respiratory disorder of premature neonates caused by quantitative/qualitative deficiency of **pulmonary surfactant (肺表面張力素)**.
* **Synonym:** Hyaline Membrane Disease (透明膜病).
* **Result:** → Alveolar collapse at end-expiration → impaired gas exchange.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Incidence:** Most common cause of neonatal respiratory distress in HK HA NICUs.
* **Gestational Age (GA) Inverse Relationship:**
    * Affects ~60% of neonates born <28 weeks GA.
    * Affects ~30% of neonates born 28-34 weeks GA.
    * Rare in term infants.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Surfactant Production and Function
* **Source:** Secreted by **Type II pneumocytes**.
* **Composition:** Phospholipids (predominantly **DPPC** - Dipalmitoylphosphatidylcholine) + Surfactant proteins (SP-A, B, C, D).
* **Normal Function:** ↓ alveolar surface tension → prevents end-expiratory alveolar collapse (atelectasis) → maintains Functional Residual Capacity (FRC).

### Pathogenesis of Alveolar Collapse
* **Surfactant Deficiency** → diffuse alveolar atelectasis.
* **Physiological Cascade:**
    * ↓ Lung compliance ("stiff lungs") → ↑ work of breathing.
    * Ventilation-perfusion (**V/Q**) mismatch & intrapulmonary shunting.
    * → **Hypoxaemia (低氧血症)**.
    * → **Hypercapnia (高碳酸血症)** & respiratory acidosis.

### Formation of Hyaline Membranes
* **Endothelial Damage:** Hypoxia + acidosis → damage to alveolar epithelium and capillary endothelium.
* **Exudation:** Proteinaceous exudate leaks into alveolar spaces.
* **Membrane Formation:** Exudate forms eosinophilic "hyaline membranes" → further impairs gas diffusion.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Onset of Clinical Presentation
* **Timing:** Presents immediately at birth or within first few hours of life.
* **Course:** Progressively worsening respiratory distress over first 48-72 hours.

### Key Respiratory Signs (呼吸系統體徵)
* **Tachypnoea (氣促):** Respiratory rate >60 breaths/min.
* **Expiratory Grunting (呼氣呻吟):** Exhalation against partially closed glottis → generates intrinsic **PEEP** to keep alveoli open.
* **Retractions/Recessions (胸部凹陷):** Subcostal, intercostal, & sternal retractions due to ↑ negative intrathoracic pressure needed to expand stiff lungs against highly compliant neonatal chest wall.
* **Nasal Flaring (鼻翼煽動):** Compensatory mechanism to ↓ airway resistance.

### Severe Systemic & Auscultatory Signs
* **Cyanosis (發紺):** Central cyanosis if hypoxaemia is severe & untreated.
* **Auscultation:**
    * ↓ Air entry bilaterally.
    * Fine inspiratory crackles.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing RDS from Transient Tachypnoea of the Newborn (TTN)
* **GA & History:** TTN typically occurs in term/near-term infants, classically post-elective Lower Segment Caesarean Section (LSCS) without labor.
* **Pathology:** TTN caused by delayed clearance of fetal lung fluid, NOT surfactant deficiency.
* **Clinical Course:** TTN features mild retractions & rapid improvement within 24-48 hours.
* **CXR:** TTN shows fluid in horizontal fissures, prominent perihilar streaking, hyperinflation (unlike RDS low volumes).

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Common Neonatal Respiratory Differentials
| Differential Diagnosis (鑑別診斷) | Key Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Transient Tachypnoea of Newborn (TTN)** | Term/near-term, post-LSCS, rapid improvement, CXR: fluid in fissures, hyperinflation. |
| **Neonatal Pneumonia/Sepsis** | e.g., Group B Streptococcus. Clinically indistinguishable from RDS initially. ↑ inflammatory markers (CRP), +ve blood culture. |
| **Meconium Aspiration Syndrome (MAS)** | Term/post-term, meconium-stained liquor, CXR: patchy infiltrates, hyperinflation. |
| **Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia (CDH)** | Scaphoid abdomen, bowel sounds in hemithorax, CXR: bowel loops in thorax. |
| **Pneumothorax (氣胸)** | Sudden clinical deterioration, asymmetrical chest movement, +ve transillumination. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Gold Standard Imaging
* **Chest X-Ray (CXR):**
    * Bilateral, diffuse **"ground-glass"** appearance (reticulogranular pattern) → due to microatelectasis.
    * Prominent **air bronchograms** → air-filled bronchi standing out against opaque, collapsed alveoli.
    * ↓ Lung volumes (bell-shaped thorax).

### Bedside & Blood Investigations
* **Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) / CBG:**
    * ↓ PaO2 (Hypoxaemia).
    * ↑ PaCO2 (Hypercapnia).
    * ↓ pH (Respiratory or mixed acidosis ± ↑ lactate).
* **Sepsis Screen (Mandatory):**
    * **Blood culture:** Rule out early-onset sepsis.
    * **Complete Blood Picture (CBP):** Assess for neutropenia/leukocytosis.
    * **C-Reactive Protein (CRP):** Baseline & serial monitoring.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Antenatal Prevention Protocol
* **Maternal Corticosteroids:**
    * IM **Dexamethasone** or **Betamethasone** to mothers at risk of preterm delivery (<34 weeks GA).
    * **Mechanism:** Accelerates fetal lung maturity & ↑ endogenous surfactant production.

### Delivery Room Stabilization (HA Protocol)
* **Delayed Cord Clamping:** 30-60 seconds if neonate is vigorous.
* **Immediate Respiratory Support:** Early stabilization with **CPAP** (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) via NeoPuff/T-piece to prevent initial alveolar collapse.

### Non-Invasive & Invasive Respiratory Support
* **1st Line (Non-Invasive):**
    * **CPAP** (typically 5-8 cmH2O) or High-Flow Nasal Cannula (**HFNC**).
* **2nd Line (Invasive):**
    * Intubation & mechanical ventilation indicated if CPAP fails (e.g., FiO2 requirement >0.30-0.40 to maintain SpO2, severe intractable acidosis [pH <7.20], or frequent severe apnoeas).

### Exogenous Surfactant Replacement Therapy (表面張力素替代療法)
* **Indications:** Intubated infants or those failing CPAP.
* **Administration:** Intratracheal instillation of porcine/bovine extracts (e.g., **Curosurf**, **Survanta**).
* **Delivery Protocols:**
    * **INSURE method:** **IN**tubate → **SUR**factant → **E**xtubate immediately to CPAP (minimizes barotrauma).
    * **LISA/MIST method:** **L**ess **I**nvasive **S**urfactant **A**dministration / **M**inimally **I**nvasive **S**urfactant **T**herapy → administering via thin catheter while baby remains spontaneously breathing on CPAP.

### Pharmacological & Supportive Therapy
* **Empirical IV Antibiotics:**
    * **Ampicillin/Penicillin + Gentamicin** (Standard HA NICU protocol for early-onset sepsis) until 48h blood cultures are -ve.
* **Fluid & Nutrition:**
    * IV fluids (typically 10% Dextrose initially).
    * Strict fluid restriction to prevent PDA opening & pulmonary oedema.
* **Thermoregulation:** Nurse in pre-warmed incubator/radiant warmer to prevent cold stress (which ↑ surfactant depletion).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary NICU Team
* **Members:** Neonatologists, specialized neonatal nurses, respiratory therapists, physiotherapists, dietitians.

### Parental Support and Involvement
* **Psychosocial Impact:** NICU admission is highly traumatic for parents.
* **Interventions:**
    * Medical Social Worker (MSW) referral for financial/emotional support.
    * Regular structured medical updates.
    * Promote early **Kangaroo Care** (skin-to-skin) & breast milk pumping to facilitate bonding.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Considerations in Prematurity
- Extreme prematurity & prolonged NICU stay ↑ risk of impaired parental bonding.
* Monitor for parental disengagement, failure to visit NICU, or lack of preparation for discharge → involve MSW/SWD early to ensure safe home environment.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Neonatal Complications
| System | Acute Complications |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Respiratory** | Pneumothorax, Pulmonary Haemorrhage, Air leaks (PIE) |
| **Neurological** | Intraventricular Haemorrhage (**IVH**) due to BP fluctuations/hypoxia |
| **Cardiovascular** | Patent Ductus Arteriosus (**PDA**) - hypoxia/acidosis keeps ductus open |
| **Infectious** | Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP), Central Line-Associated Bloodstream Infection (CLABSI) |

### Chronic Complications & Long-Term Prognosis
* **Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia (BPD) / Chronic Lung Disease (CLD):** Oxygen dependency beyond 36 weeks corrected GA. Due to barotrauma, volutrauma, & oxygen toxicity.
* **Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP):** Abnormal retinal vascularization driven by extreme prematurity and hyperoxaemia.
* **Prognosis:** Survival ↑ dramatically with antenatal steroids & exogenous surfactant. Long-term respiratory (asthma-like symptoms) & neurodevelopmental outcomes depend heavily on GA & presence of IVH/BPD.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the physiological purpose of expiratory grunting in a neonate with RDS?
  Answer: The infant exhales against a partially closed glottis to create intrinsic Positive End-Expiratory Pressure (PEEP), which helps prevent alveolar collapse at the end of expiration.
* Trap: Assuming RDS and early-onset neonatal pneumonia can be easily distinguished clinically or radiologically.
  Pearl: They are often clinically indistinguishable; you MUST always start empirical IV antibiotics (Ampicillin/Penicillin + Gentamicin) pending blood culture results.
* Pitfall: Giving 100% FiO2 indiscriminately to a cyanotic premature neonate.
  Target SpO2 is 91-95% in preterm neonates; hyperoxaemia significantly increases the risk of Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) and oxygen free-radical lung damage.
* Viva Question: Describe the classic chest X-ray findings in RDS.
  Answer: Low lung volumes, a diffuse bilateral ground-glass or reticulogranular appearance, and prominent air bronchograms.
* Viva Question: What is the benefit of the LISA/MIST technique over traditional surfactant administration?
  Answer: It allows surfactant delivery via a thin catheter while the infant remains on non-invasive CPAP, completely avoiding the barotrauma and volutrauma associated with positive pressure mechanical ventilation via an endotracheal tube.
