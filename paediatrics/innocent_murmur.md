# Innocent Murmur (無害性心雜音)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- Heart murmur without cardiac structural/physiological abnormality
- AKA functional/physiological murmur

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
- Extremely common in HK paediatric practice
- >50% of normal children have innocent murmur at some point
- Commonly detected during **Department of Health (DH) Student Health Service (SHS) (學生健康服務)** screenings or incidental GP visits for **URTIs (上呼吸道感染)**

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Innocent Murmurs
- Generated by normal turbulent blood flow through structurally normal heart/vessels
- No underlying genetic/structural defect
- Exacerbated by ↑ cardiac output states (e.g., fever, anaemia, thyrotoxicosis, anxiety, exercise)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### General Clinical Presentation
- Usually asymptomatic; discovered incidentally
- Normal growth & development
- Normal exercise tolerance

### The "7 S's" of Innocent Murmurs
- **S**oft (Grade 1-2/6, never thrill)
- **S**hort (not holosystolic/pansystolic)
- **S**ystolic (except venous hum)
- **S**weet (not harsh, often vibratory/musical)
- **S**mall (localised, no radiation)
- **S**ingle (no clicks, normal split S2)
- **S**ensitive (changes with posture/respiration)

### Common Innocent Murmur Types
| Murmur Type | Characteristics | Location | Postural Changes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Still's Murmur (史蒂爾氏雜音)** | Vibratory/musical, low-pitched | LLSB (Lower left sternal border) | Loudest supine; ↓ when upright |
| **Pulmonary Flow Murmur (肺動脈血流雜音)** | Blowing, high-pitched | LUSB (Upper left sternal border) | ↑ in supine/fever/anaemia |
| **Venous Hum (靜脈嗡鳴聲)** | Continuous, low-pitched | Infraclavicular (right > left) | Disappears when supine/turning neck/jugular compression |

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Pathological Red Flags
- Diastolic murmurs
- Pansystolic/holosystolic murmurs
- Loudness Grade ≥3/6
- Presence of a thrill (震顫)
- Radiation (e.g., to back or neck)
- Abnormal S2 (e.g., fixed split in ASD)
- Extra heart sounds (clicks, gallop rhythm)
- Cyanosis (發紺) or abnormal O2 saturation (≤94%)
- Absent/weak femoral pulses
- Failure to thrive (生長遲緩)

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Differential Diagnoses
| Differential | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Atrial Septal Defect (ASD) (心房中隔缺損)** | Mimics pulmonary flow murmur; **Fixed widely split S2**; RV heave |
| **Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) (心室中隔缺損)** | Small VSDs can be soft but are **pansystolic**; thrill if loud |
| **Pulmonary Stenosis (PS) (肺動脈瓣狹窄)** | **Ejection click** present; radiation to back |
| **Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA) (動脈導管未閉)** | Mimics venous hum but **continuous machinery murmur** at LUSB; bounding pulses; unaffected by neck turning/supine position |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
- **Clinical Examination**: Usually sufficient for diagnosis
- **Four-Limb Blood Pressure**: Rule out coarctation
- **Pulse Oximetry (血氧定量法)**: Rule out cyanotic congenital heart disease (target ≥95%)

### Imaging & Special Investigations
- **Echocardiogram (Echo) (超聲心動圖)**: Gold standard but ONLY indicated if "red flag" features present or unresolvable parental anxiety. Routine Echo discouraged in **Hospital Authority (HA) (醫院管理局)** to avoid over-medicalisation.
- **ECG/CXR**: Not routinely indicated unless underlying pathology suspected (e.g., abnormal axis, cardiomegaly).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
- None required

### Chronic Management & HA Protocols
- **Reassurance (保證)**: Explain benign nature to parents
- **Activity**: Unrestricted; normal sports participation
- **Endocarditis Prophylaxis**: NOT required
- **HA Protocol**: Discharge from paediatric outpatient clinic (SOPD) if clinically confident; re-examine after resolution of intercurrent illness (e.g., fever, anaemia) if murmur detected during acute illness.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MCHC & Psychosocial Support
- **Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) (母嬰健康院)**: Routine developmental checks with ongoing reassurance.
- Address parental anxiety ("cardiac neurosis"), common in HK when informed of a "heart problem".

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations
- Not directly applicable, but ensure child receives regular well-child care and vaccinations.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Long-Term Prognosis
- Excellent prognosis.
- Most resolve spontaneously by adolescence.
- Zero long-term cardiovascular complications.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Ordering an Echo for a classic Still's murmur in an OSCE. Pearl: Confidently diagnose it clinically and explain to the "parent" why an Echo is unnecessary.
* Viva Question: What makes a venous hum disappear? Answer: Lying supine, turning the head, or gentle pressure over the jugular vein.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing an innocent murmur during an acute febrile illness. Re-examine the child after the fever has resolved to ensure the murmur was truly functional and related to increased cardiac output.
