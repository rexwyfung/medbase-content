# Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF) (急性風濕熱)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- Delayed, non-suppurative, autoimmune, multisystem inflammatory disease.
- Follows pharyngeal infection with **Group A Beta-Haemolytic Streptococcus (GAS)**.

### Local Epidemiology
- Rare in HK (<2/100,000) due to ↑ living standards & prompt Abx for strep throat.
- Critical "must-not-miss" diagnosis → potential to cause permanent, severe heart damage.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Molecular Mimicry Mechanism
- Fundamental pathological mechanism.
- Susceptible child develops GAS throat infection (**Strep throat**).
- Immune system mounts defense → produces antibodies against **M protein** on GAS surface.

### Autoimmune Cross-Reactivity
- Antibodies cross-react with structurally similar host proteins.
- Primary Targets:
  - Heart (myosin, valves)
  - Joints (synovium)
  - Skin
  - Brain (basal ganglia)
- Triggers severe, sterile inflammation typically **2-4 weeks** post-infection.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Presentation
- School-aged child (5-15 years).
- Preceding sore throat (a few weeks prior).
- Now presents with:
  - High fever
  - Severe, migrating joint pain

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Diagnostic Criteria (Revised Jones Criteria)
- Requires: Evidence of preceding GAS infection PLUS (2 Major OR 1 Major + 2 Minor criteria).

### Major Criteria (J♥NES)
- **J (Joints/Migratory Polyarthritis)**: 50% cases. Large joints involved:
  - Knees
  - Ankles
  - Wrists
  - Elbows
  - Exquisitely painful, swollen, red, hot. 
  - Migrates over days. 
  - Highly responsive to NSAIDs.
- **♥ (Carditis)**: 60% cases. Pancarditis. Presents with:
  - New murmur (classically **mitral regurgitation**)
  - Tachycardia out of proportion to fever
  - Congestive Heart Failure (CHF) signs
- **N (Nodules)**: <10% cases. 
  - Subcutaneous
  - Firm, painless, pea-sized
  - Located over extensor surfaces/spine
- **E (Erythema Marginatum)**: <5% cases.
  - Non-pruritic
  - Pink maculopapular rash with pale centres
  - Serpiginous advancing margins
  - Located on trunk/proximal limbs
- **S (Sydenham Chorea) (西登納姆舞蹈症)**: 20% cases. Late sign (2-6 months post-GAS).
  - Involuntary movements
  - Purposeless & rapid
  - Jerking (face/limbs)
  - Emotional lability

### Minor Criteria
- Fever.
- Arthralgia (if polyarthritis not used as Major criterion).
- ↑ ESR or CRP.
- Prolonged PR interval on ECG.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Septic Arthritis vs ARF
- **Septic Arthritis**: 
  - Monoarticular
  - Highly toxic child
  - Purulent joint aspirate
- **ARF**: 
  - Polyarticular
  - Migratory
  - Sterile joint fluid

### Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA) vs ARF
- **JIA**: 
  - Arthritis >6 weeks
  - Non-migratory
  - No preceding strep/carditis/chorea
- **ARF**: 
  - Migratory
  - Rapid onset
  - Follows strep infection

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Differentials
- Septic arthritis (化膿性關節炎)
- Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA) (幼年特發性關節炎)
- Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) (系統性紅斑狼瘡)
- Kawasaki Disease (川崎病)
- Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎)
- Post-Streptococcal Reactive Arthritis (PSRA) (鏈球菌感染後反應性關節炎)

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Evidence of Preceding GAS Infection
- Mandatory for diagnosis.
- **ASOT** (Anti-Streptolysin O Titre) or **Anti-DNase B**: ↑ or rising titres.
- Throat swab culture: +ve for GAS (often -ve by ARF presentation).
- Rapid antigen test: +ve for GAS.

### Blood Investigations
- Markers of Inflammation: Markedly ↑ ESR & ↑ CRP.
- FBC: Leukocytosis.

### Bedside & Imaging Investigations
- **Echocardiogram**: Mandatory (Gold Standard) for all suspected cases. Detects subclinical carditis/valvulitis even if no murmur is heard.
- **ECG**: Checks for prolonged PR interval (Minor criterion) or pericarditis signs.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### 1st Line Acute Management (Eradicate GAS)
- Single dose IM **Benzathine Penicillin G** OR 10-day oral **Penicillin V**.
- Goal: Eliminates residual GAS in pharynx.

### Anti-Inflammatory Therapy
- **Arthritis only**: 
  - High-dose NSAIDs (e.g., **Naproxen**) or **Aspirin**. 
  - Dramatic response within 48h.
- **Severe Carditis/CHF**: 
  - High-dose Corticosteroids (**Prednisolone**).
  - Goal: Suppress severe cardiac inflammation.

### Secondary Prophylaxis
- Crucial long-term intervention to prevent recurrent attacks & cumulative, irreversible valve damage.
- Medication: IM **Benzathine Penicillin G** every 3-4 weeks (or daily oral Penicillin V).

### Duration of Secondary Prophylaxis
| Cardiac Involvement | Prophylaxis Duration |
| :--- | :--- |
| **No carditis** | Until age 21 OR for 5 years post-attack (whichever longer) |
| **Mild carditis (healed)** | Until age 21 OR for 10 years post-attack |
| **Severe carditis/permanent damage** | Until age 40 OR lifelong |

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Support
- **Paediatric Cardiology**: Essential for long-term monitoring/management of Rheumatic Heart Disease.
- **Nursing Support**: Ensuring compliance with painful, monthly IM penicillin injections for years.
- **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Support for treatment burden & ensuring family compliance.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations
- N/A directly for pathogenesis.
- Defaulting on secondary prophylaxis warrants active follow-up & MSW involvement to prevent severe cardiac morbidity.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD) (風濕性心臟病)
- Permanent, long-term consequence of ARF carditis.
- Valve pathology:
  - Chronic scarring
  - Fibrosis
  - Calcification
- **Mitral Stenosis (二尖瓣狹窄)**: Classic late lesion.
- Other lesions: Mitral regurgitation, aortic valve disease.
- Long-term sequelae:
  - Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)
  - Atrial fibrillation
  - Need for valve replacement surgery in adulthood

### Prognosis
- Depends entirely on initial carditis severity & strict adherence to secondary penicillin prophylaxis.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the underlying pathophysiological mechanism of Acute Rheumatic Fever? Answer: Molecular mimicry. Antibodies produced against the Group A Streptococcus cross-react with the host's own tissues, particularly the heart and joints.
* Viva Question: A 12-year-old is diagnosed with ARF and has evidence of mitral regurgitation on echo. How long must they receive secondary penicillin prophylaxis? Answer: Until they are at least 21 years old, or for 10 years after the attack, whichever is longer. If the valve damage is severe and permanent, it may be lifelong.
* Trap: Assuming joint pain in ARF is monoarticular. Pearl: ARF classically causes a migratory polyarthritis; monoarticular arthritis strongly suggests septic arthritis and requires urgent joint aspiration.
* Pitfall: Failing to order an Echocardiogram in a child with suspected ARF who has no audible murmur. Subclinical carditis is common and must be identified to determine the duration of secondary prophylaxis.
