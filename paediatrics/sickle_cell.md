# Sickle Cell Disease (鐮狀細胞病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Sickle Cell Disease
* Inherited hemoglobinopathy (血紅蛋白病) → structurally abnormal **Hemoglobin S (HbS)**.
* Most severe/common form: **HbSS** (homozygous).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Extremely rare in local ethnic Chinese (華人).
* Predominantly seen in expatriate/immigrant populations (African, Mediterranean, Middle Eastern descent).
* Classic "exam-favorite" hematology topic in HA/MBBS finals despite low local prevalence.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Mutation in Sickle Cell Disease
* **Autosomal Recessive** (體染色體隱性遺傳).
* Point mutation on **$\beta$-globin gene** (Chromosome 11).
* **Glutamic Acid** (hydrophilic) → replaced by **Valine** (hydrophobic) at 6th position.

### Pathophysiology of Sickling
* Deoxygenation/stress → **HbS polymerization** into stiff, insoluble rods.
* RBCs distort into rigid **sickle/crescent shape** (鐮狀).

### Pathophysiology of Vaso-occlusion
* Sickled RBCs = sticky & inflexible.
* Jam up microvasculature → severe tissue ischemia, infarction → **Vaso-occlusive Crises (VOC)**.

### Pathophysiology of Hemolysis
* Rigid cells mechanically fragile → premature destruction in spleen/vessels.
* ↓ Lifespan: **~10-20 days** (normal = 120 days).
* → Chronic baseline anemia, jaundice, pigment gallstones.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Age of Initial Presentation
* **Asymptomatic < 6 months**: Protected by high levels of **Fetal Hemoglobin (HbF)** (胎兒血紅蛋白) which prevents HbS polymerization.
* **Initial Presentation (6-9 months)**: Typically presents as **Dactylitis** (手足炎) when HbF drops and HbS predominates.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Vaso-occlusive Crisis (VOC) Presentation
* Severe, throbbing bone/joint/muscle pain.
* Triggers: Hypoxia, cold, dehydration, infection, stress.

### Acute Chest Syndrome (ACS) Presentation
* Life-threatening lung crisis mimicking severe pneumonia.
* Fever, chest pain, tachypnea, hypoxia.
* **+ve** New pulmonary infiltrates on CXR.
* Leading cause of mortality in SCD.

### Splenic Sequestration Crisis Presentation
* Sudden, massive pooling of blood into spleen.
* Rapid ↓ Hb, massive **splenomegaly** (脾臟腫大), hypovolemic shock.
* Highest risk in young children (before autosplenectomy).

### Aplastic Crisis Presentation
* Sudden halt of RBC production in bone marrow.
* Classically triggered by **Parvovirus B19** (微小病毒B19) infection.
* Severe sudden anemia with **↓ reticulocytes**.

### Neurological Signs
* **Cerebrovascular Accident (Stroke)** (中風): High risk in childhood due to cerebral vessel occlusion.
* Requires annual **Transcranial Doppler (TCD)** screening.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Sickle Cell Disease vs Thalassemia
| Feature | Sickle Cell Disease (鐮狀細胞病) | Thalassemia (地中海貧血) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Defect Type** | **Qualitative** (Broken/abnormal globin chains) | **Quantitative** (Not enough globin chains) |
| **Pathology** | RBCs sickle → vaso-occlusion & hemolysis | Defective Hb production → profound microcytosis & hemolysis |
| **Smear** | Sickle cells, Howell-Jolly bodies | Severe microcytosis, target cells, tear drop cells |
| **Clinical Hallmarks**| Pain crises, acute chest syndrome | Frontal bossing, hepatosplenomegaly, iron overload |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis for Bone Pain in SCD
* **Osteomyelitis** (骨髓炎): Clinically identical to VOC. Classically caused by **Salmonella** or **S. aureus**.
* **Leukemia** (白血病): Bone pain with cytopenias.

### Differential Diagnosis for Acute Abdomen in SCD
* **Appendicitis** (闌尾炎): Mesenteric VOC can mimic acute abdomen.
* **Cholecystitis** (膽囊炎): Very common due to chronic pigment gallstones.

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations for Sickle Cell Disease
* **Vitals**: SpO2 (detect hypoxia/ACS trigger), Temp (detect infection).
* **Pain Score**: Crucial for titrating analgesia.

### Blood Investigations for Sickle Cell Disease
* **CBC**: Baseline Hb **6-9 g/dL**.
* **Reticulocyte Count**: **↑** (except in Aplastic Crisis where it is **↓**).
* **Peripheral Blood Smear**: Sickle cells, target cells, **Howell-Jolly bodies** (豪威爾-喬利體) (DNA remnants due to functional asplenia).
* **LFTs**: ↑ Unconjugated bilirubin (chronic hemolysis).

### Definitive Diagnostic Investigations for Sickle Cell Disease
* **Hemoglobin Electrophoresis** (血紅蛋白電泳) or **HPLC**:
    * **Absent HbA**.
    * **Predominant HbS** (>80%).
    * Variable HbF.

### Imaging Investigations for Sickle Cell Complications
* **CXR**: Mandatory if febrile/respiratory signs (rule out Acute Chest Syndrome).
* **Transcranial Doppler (TCD)**: Annual screening for stroke risk (cerebral blood flow velocity).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management of Vaso-occlusive Crisis (VOC)
* **Hydration**: Aggressive IV fluids (corrects dehydration trigger).
* **Analgesia**: Rapid escalation. Paracetamol/NSAIDs → Oral Opiates → **IV Morphine** (嗎啡) or PCA.
* **Oxygen**: Only if SpO2 < 95% (does not relieve pain if normoxic).
* **Infection control**: Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics if febrile.

### Acute Management of Splenic Sequestration & Aplastic Crises
* **Resuscitation**: Urgent IV fluids & careful packed RBC transfusion.
* Avoid over-transfusion in sequestration (trapped RBCs can release back into circulation → hyperviscosity).

### Acute Management of Acute Chest Syndrome (ACS) & Stroke
* **Emergency Exchange Transfusion** (換血治療): Rapidly replaces HbS with normal HbA to stop sickling cascade.
* Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics + Bronchodilators for ACS.

### Chronic Prophylaxis (HA Protocol for Asplenia)
* **Antibiotic Prophylaxis**: Mandatory daily oral **Penicillin V** (青黴素) until at least age 5.
* **Vaccinations**: Standard HK Child Immunisation Programme + extra **Pneumococcal, Meningococcal, Haemophilus influenzae type B** vaccines.

### Disease Modifying Therapy & Curative Treatment
* **Hydroxyurea** (羥基脲): Chemotherapy agent → ↑ Fetal Hemoglobin (HbF) production → ↓ HbS polymerization → dramatically ↓ VOC frequency and mortality.
* **Curative**: **Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT)** (造血幹細胞移植).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team for SCD
* **Hematologist & Pain Specialist**: Complex chronic pain management.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)** (醫務社工): Financial assistance for meds, school absence support.
* **Clinical Psychologist**: High psychosocial burden, fear of early death, depression from chronic pain.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Differentiating VOC from NAI
* Dactylitis or acute bone pain in an infant with undiagnosed SCD can easily be misidentified as fractures from Non-Accidental Injury (NAI).
* Always consider underlying hemoglobinopathy in ethnic minority children presenting with unexplained limb swelling/pain before jumping solely to safeguarding protocols.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Hematological & Immune Complications
* **Autosplenectomy** (脾臟自動切除): Repetitive splenic infarctions → completely dead spleen by age 5.
* **Functional Asplenia**: Profound susceptibility to encapsulated bacteria (*S. pneumoniae*, *N. meningitidis*).

### Orthopedic & Renal Complications
* **Avascular Necrosis (AVN)** (缺血性壞死): Especially femoral/humeral heads.
* **Sickle Nephropathy**: Micro-infarctions in renal medulla → inability to concentrate urine, proteinuria, CKD.

### Hepatobiliary & Ophthalmic Complications
* **Pigment Gallstones** (膽結石): From chronic hemolysis.
* **Proliferative Sickle Retinopathy**: Retinal vessel occlusion → blindness.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Under-dosing pain medication in a VOC because the patient "doesn't look like they are in pain." Pearl: Chronic pain patients often mask their symptoms. Rely on their self-report and dose generously.
* Viva Question: Why do you see Howell-Jolly bodies on the blood film of a 6-year-old with Sickle Cell Disease? Answer: Because of repetitive splenic infarctions causing functional asplenia (autosplenectomy). The spleen's normal job involves pitting/removing these nuclear remnants from RBCs. Without a spleen, the Howell-Jolly bodies remain in the circulation.
* Pitfall: Giving routine blood transfusions for baseline anemia in SCD. Only transfuse for symptomatic severe exacerbations (e.g., Aplastic crisis) or specific acute emergencies (exchange transfusion for ACS/Stroke) to avoid alloimmunization and iron overload.
* Trap: Assuming oxygen therapy will cure the pain of a VOC. Pearl: Unless the patient is hypoxic (SpO2 < 95%), supplemental O2 does not reverse sickling that has already occurred in the microvasculature; aggressive analgesia and hydration are the mainstays of pain management.
* Viva Question: How does Hydroxyurea work in Sickle Cell Disease? Answer: It induces the bone marrow to produce more Fetal Hemoglobin (HbF). HbF cannot sickle and it dilutes the concentration of HbS, preventing polymerization.
