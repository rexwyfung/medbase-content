# Childhood Obesity (兒童肥胖症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Childhood Obesity
* **Definition:** Excess body fat negatively impacting health.
* **Objective Measurement:** Body Mass Index (**BMI**) plotted on age- & sex-specific growth charts.
* **Overweight:** BMI > 85th centile.
* **Obese:** BMI > 95th centile.
* **Severe Obesity:** BMI > 99th centile.

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology
* **Prevalence:** Major HA/DH public health crisis; ~1 in 5 primary school children in HK are overweight/obese.
* **Drivers:** * Highly sedentary lifestyle (heavy academic burden, ↑ screen time).
  * Easy access to calorie-dense, nutrient-poor fast food.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Exogenous (Primary) Obesity Pathophysiology
* **Prevalence:** >95% of cases.
* **Mechanism:** Chronic energy imbalance (Caloric Intake > Energy Expenditure).

### Endogenous (Secondary) Obesity Pathophysiology
* **Prevalence:** <5% of cases.
* **Mechanism:** Driven by underlying endocrine pathology, CNS lesions, or genetic syndromic disorders.

### Genetic Factors in Obesity
* **Inheritance:** Polygenic inheritance governs susceptibility to obesogenic environments.
* **Risk:** 80% risk of obesity if both parents are obese.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Typical Modes of Presentation
* Detected during routine **Student Health Service (學生健康服務)** screening.
* Parental concern regarding weight gain or delayed puberty.
* Presentation of complications (e.g., snoring, joint pain, daytime somnolence).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### General Physical Examination Findings
* **Height:** Exogenous obesity usually presents with **TALL** stature (accelerated bone maturation). *Note: Obese + SHORT = Investigate for endogenous cause.*
* **Striae (妊娠紋):** Pink/purple stretch marks on abdomen/thighs.
* **Adipomastia (脂肪乳房):** Excess pectoral fat (differentiate from true gynaecomastia in boys).
* **Adiposity Distribution:** Central vs. peripheral fat distribution.

### Endocrine & Metabolic Signs
* **Acanthosis Nigricans (黑棘皮症):** Dark, velvety hyperpigmentation in skin folds (neck, axillae) → pathognomonic for **Insulin Resistance**.
* **Blood Pressure:** Hypertension common → MUST measure with appropriate cuff size (bladder covering 80-100% of arm circumference).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Exogenous vs. Endogenous Obesity Comparison
| Feature | Exogenous (Primary) Obesity | Endogenous (Secondary) Obesity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Prevalence** | > 95% | < 5% |
| **Stature** | **TALL** for age (↑ bone age) | **SHORT** for age (↓ or arrested growth) |
| **Intelligence** | Normal | Often associated with developmental delay / ID |
| **Puberty** | Normal or Early | Often Delayed (Hypogonadism) |
| **Dysmorphism** | Absent | Often Present |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Endocrine Causes of Obesity
* **Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能減退症):** ↓ Growth velocity, cold intolerance, constipation (rarely causes *massive* obesity).
* **Cushing's Syndrome (庫欣綜合徵):** Central obesity, moon face, buffalo hump, growth arrest, purple striae.
* **Growth Hormone Deficiency:** ↑ truncal fat, ↓ growth velocity.

### Genetic Syndromes Causing Obesity
* **Prader-Willi Syndrome (普瑞德威利症候群):** * Severe neonatal hypotonia.
  * Early childhood hyperphagia (insatiable appetite) → massive obesity.
  * Short stature, hypogonadism, intellectual disability.
* **Bardet-Biedl Syndrome:** Obesity, retinitis pigmentosa, polydactyly, renal dysfunction.

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Baseline Anthropometric Investigations
* **Diagnosis:** Clinical (BMI calculation).
* **Bloods:** None required for primary diagnosis.

### Investigations for Metabolic Comorbidities
* **Fasting Lipid Profile:** Screen for dyslipidaemia (↑ LDL, ↓ HDL, ↑ Triglycerides).
* **Fasting Glucose & HbA1c:** Screen for **Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (2型糖尿病)** / impaired fasting glucose.
* **Liver Function Tests (LFTs):** ↑ ALT suggests **Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD) (非酒精性脂肪肝)**.
* **Sleep Study (Polysomnography):** If OSA suspected.

### Investigations for Endogenous Causes (If Indicated)
* **Indications:** Short stature, developmental delay, dysmorphism.
* **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs):** Rule out hypothyroidism.
* **Overnight Dexamethasone Suppression Test:** Rule out Cushing's.
* **Microarray / Genetic Testing:** Rule out Prader-Willi / syndromic causes.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### First-Line Lifestyle Management (Family-Based)
* **Primary Goal:** Weight *maintenance* during linear growth (↓ BMI centile as child grows taller), rather than absolute weight loss.
* **Dietary Modification:** * Eliminate sugary drinks (soda, juice).
  * Reduce processed fast food.
  * ↑ Water and vegetable intake.
  * Strict portion control.
* **Physical Activity:** ≥ 60 minutes moderate-to-vigorous activity daily.
* **Screen Time Control:** < 2 hours recreational screen time per day.
* **Family Engagement:** Crucial → whole household MUST adopt changes.

### Pharmacological Management Protocols
* **Indications:** Rarely used in paediatrics; reserved for older adolescents with severe comorbidities failing lifestyle modification.
* **Agents:** * **Orlistat** (Lipase inhibitor).
  * **GLP-1 Agonists** (e.g., Liraglutide) (↑ satiety, ↓ gastric emptying).

### Surgical Management (Bariatric)
* **Indications:** Extreme, life-threatening obesity with severe complications.
* **Criteria:** Post-pubertal adolescents who have failed extensive multidisciplinary interventions.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Roles
* **Paediatric Dietitian:** Culturally appropriate, practical dietary restructuring.
* **Clinical Psychologist:** Management of low self-esteem, bullying, eating disorders, and behavioral modification techniques.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW):** Assess home environment and financial barriers to healthy eating.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect in Severe Obesity
* **Red Flags:** Extreme, life-threatening obesity with severe comorbidities (e.g., severe OSA, T2DM) where parents consistently refuse to engage with medical/dietetic advice.
* **Action:** May necessitate Child Protective Services / Social Welfare Department (SWD) involvement for medical neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Metabolic & Endocrine Complications
* **Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (2型糖尿病):** Increasingly prevalent in HK youth.
* **Dyslipidaemia & Hypertension:** ↑ Premature cardiovascular risk.
* **NAFLD:** Can progress to steatohepatitis/cirrhosis.
* **Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS):** Irregular menses, hirsutism.

### Mechanical & Orthopaedic Complications
* **Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA) (阻塞性睡眠窒息症):** Snoring, daytime somnolence, morning headaches, right heart strain.
* **Slipped Capital Femoral Epiphysis (SCFE) (股骨頭骨骺滑脫):** Hip/knee pain, limp.
* **Blount Disease:** Pathological bowing of the tibia (tibia vara).

### Psychosocial Complications & Prognosis
* **Psychosocial:** Severe bullying, clinical depression, social isolation.
* **Prognosis:** High rate of tracking into adulthood → significant ↑ risk of premature cardiovascular disease and all-cause mortality.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: You are examining an obese 8-year-old boy and notice dark, velvety thickening of the skin on the back of his neck. What is this sign called, and what underlying metabolic abnormality does it indicate?
* Answer: Acanthosis Nigricans. It is a clinical marker of severe insulin resistance.
* Viva Question: What is the most important physical measurement to help differentiate between simple exogenous obesity and a pathological endocrine cause like Cushing's syndrome?
* Answer: Height. Exogenous obesity usually presents with tall stature for age, whereas endocrine causes characteristically present with short stature or arrested growth.
* Pitfall: Focusing solely on the child's diet and ignoring the family environment. Interventions that do not involve parents changing the household food environment and their own habits are doomed to fail.
* Trap: Assuming gynaecomastia in an obese prepubertal boy is true glandular tissue.
* Pearl: It is usually adipomastia (lipomastia). Palpation reveals soft fat without the firm, tender glandular disc of true gynaecomastia.
* Trap: Recommending rapid, extreme weight loss for a growing 7-year-old.
* Pearl: The goal in growing prepubertal children is weight maintenance, allowing linear growth to naturally lower the BMI centile over time.
