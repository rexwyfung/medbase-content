# Infectious Mononucleosis (傳染性單核白血球增多症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Syndrome** typically caused by primary **Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) (愛潑斯坦-巴爾病毒)** infection
* Characterized by classic triad: **fever, lymphadenopathy, pharyngitis**

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
* Very common in HK Hospital Authority (HA) settings
* Asia: Primary infection often occurs in early childhood → frequently asymptomatic or mild
* Adolescents/Young Adults: Classic **"glandular fever" (腺熱)** presentation ↑ common

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathogen & Tropism
* **Pathogen**: Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) / Human Herpesvirus-4 (HHV-4)
* **Tropism**: Has particular tropism for **B lymphocytes (B淋巴細胞)** & **epithelial cells of oropharynx**

### Transmission Mechanism
* Via **saliva** (唾液) → "kissing disease"
* Sharing utensils/cups

### Disease Pathogenesis
* EBV infects B cells in oropharynx → systemic dissemination
* Profound immune response → massive proliferation of **cytotoxic T cells (細胞毒性T細胞)**
* T cell proliferation → profound **lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)** & **hepatosplenomegaly (肝脾腫大)**
* **Atypical lymphocytes** seen on blood film = reactive T cells (NOT the infected B cells)
* Latency: EBV establishes lifelong latency in memory B cells
* **Oncogenic associations**: ↑ risk for **Burkitt lymphoma (伯基特淋巴瘤)**, nasopharyngeal carcinoma (鼻咽癌), & lymphoproliferative disease (in immunocompromised)

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### General Clinical Course
* Incubation: 4-8 weeks
* Prodrome: 
  * Malaise
  * Fatigue
  * Low-grade fever
* Acute phase: 1-3 weeks (severe symptoms)
* Resolution: Fatigue can persist 1-3 months (Post-viral fatigue)

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Classic Triad
* **Fever (發燒)**: Prolonged (1-2 weeks)
* **Pharyngitis/Tonsillitis (咽炎/扁桃體炎)**: Severe, thick white/grey exudate → Can cause airway compromise
* **Lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)**: Symmetrical, striking in **posterior cervical nodes**

### Other Common Signs
* **Splenomegaly (脾腫大)**: ~50% cases. Peaks in 2nd week → ↑ risk of splenic rupture
* **Hepatomegaly (肝腫大)**: ~10% cases
* **Jaundice (黃疸)**: Mild
* **Petechiae on soft palate** (軟腭瘀點)
* Severe **fatigue/malaise** (疲勞) → lasts 1-3 months

### Maculopapular Rash (斑丘疹)
* Classic generalized rash occurs in >90% if erroneously given **Amoxicillin/Ampicillin** (阿莫西林/氨苄西林)
* Occurs ~5% spontaneously

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing from Streptococcal Pharyngitis
* **Strep Pharyngitis (鏈球菌性咽炎)**:
  * Lacks severe splenomegaly
  * Lacks prominent posterior cervical lymphadenopathy
  * Rapid resolution with antibiotics
  * *Note: 5% of EBV patients have concurrent Group A Strep (GAS)*

### Distinguishing from CMV Infection
* **CMV (巨細胞病毒)**:
  * "Mononucleosis-like syndrome"
  * Pharyngitis usually less severe
  * **Heterophile antibodies (Monospot) = -ve**

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Differentials Summary Table
| Condition | Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CMV Infection** | Monospot -ve, milder pharyngitis |
| **Group A Strep Pharyngitis** | Rapid Ag test +ve, prominent anterior (not posterior) cervical nodes, quick response to Penicillin |
| **Acute HIV Infection** | High-risk sexual history, Monospot -ve, HIV RNA +ve |
| **Toxoplasmosis** | Cat/raw meat exposure, milder symptoms |

### Must-Not-Miss Diagnoses
* **Airway Obstruction (呼吸道阻塞)**: 2° to massive tonsillar hypertrophy
* **Splenic Rupture (脾臟破裂)**: Rare but fatal; presents with acute LUQ pain/shock
* **Leukaemia/Lymphoma (白血病/淋巴瘤)**: Presents with fever, lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly, pancytopenia

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Basic Bloods
* **CBC (全血細胞計數)**:
  * Lymphocytosis
  * **>10% atypical lymphocytes** (異型淋巴細胞) (large, irregular nuclei, abundant cytoplasm)
* **LFTs (肝功能測試)**: 
  * Mild/moderate transaminitis (↑ ALT/AST) very common

### Gold Standard / Specific Serology
* **Monospot Test (Heterophile Antibodies)**:
  * Rapid latex agglutination
  * Detects heterophile Abs (cross-react with horse/sheep RBCs)
  * Often -ve in children <4 yrs & in 1st week of illness
* **EBV-Specific Serology (EBV特異性血清學)**:
  * **Acute Infection**: VCA IgM (+ve), VCA IgG (+ve), EBNA IgG (-ve)
  * **Past Infection**: VCA IgM (-ve), VCA IgG (+ve), EBNA IgG (+ve)

### Imaging
* **Ultrasound Abdomen (腹部超聲波)**: 
  * To quantify splenomegaly (especially before return to contact sports)

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute & Airway Management
* **Supportive Care (支持性治療)**: Mainstay.
  * Rest
  * Hydration
  * Antipyretics/analgesia (Paracetamol/Ibuprofen)
* **Airway Compromise**: 
  * If massive tonsillar hypertrophy threatens airway (stridor/dyspnoea) → short course **Corticosteroids** (e.g., IV Dexamethasone/Oral Prednisolone)

### Antimicrobial Therapy Protocol
* **Antivirals (e.g., Aciclovir)**: NOT routinely recommended (does not alter clinical course)
* **Concurrent Group A Strep (5% cases)**: 
  * Treat with **Penicillin (青黴素) or Macrolide**
  * STRICTLY AVOID Amoxicillin/Ampicillin → causes florid rash

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Post-Viral Fatigue Management
* Reassure adolescents/parents: profound fatigue may persist 1-3 months
* Phased return to school/study
* Provide psychological support (MSW/School Social Worker) to mitigate exam stress and academic anxiety

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Crucial Safety Netting
* **Splenic Rupture Risk**: Strictly advise **AGAINST all contact sports** (e.g., rugby, martial arts) & heavy lifting for **≥4 weeks** (or until ultrasound confirms resolution of splenomegaly)

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### General Prognosis
* Self-limiting, excellent prognosis for complete recovery

### Severe Complications
* **Splenic Rupture (脾臟破裂)**: Highest risk weeks 2-3
* **Airway Obstruction**
* **Neurological**: 
  * Guillain-Barré syndrome (吉蘭-巴雷綜合症)
  * Meningoencephalitis
  * Facial nerve palsy
* **Haematological**: 
  * Autoimmune haemolytic anaemia (AIHA)
  * Thrombocytopenia
* **Oncological**: 
  * Long-term risk of EBV-associated malignancies (Burkitt lymphoma, NPC)

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Prescribing Amoxicillin for a teenager with a sore throat and exudate without considering EBV. Pearl: If you give Amoxicillin to a patient with EBV, they will almost certainly develop a florid, pruritic maculopapular rash.
* Viva Question: What is the most important advice to give a 16-year-old rugby player diagnosed with infectious mononucleosis? Answer: He must avoid all contact sports for at least 4 weeks due to the risk of a fatal splenic rupture.
* Pitfall: Ruling out EBV because the Monospot test is negative in a 3-year-old. The Monospot test has high false-negative rates in children <4 years old and during the first week of illness; order EBV-specific serology (VCA IgM/IgG, EBNA) if clinical suspicion is high.
* Viva Question: Which specific antibody marker indicates past (resolved) EBV infection? Answer: Positive EBNA IgG (Epstein-Barr Nuclear Antigen IgG), as it only appears weeks to months after the acute infection.
