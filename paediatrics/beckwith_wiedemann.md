# Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome (貝克威斯-威德曼綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
* Most common genetic overgrowth & cancer predisposition disorder in childhood.
* Characterized by triad: **Exomphalos (臍膨出)**, **Macroglossia (巨舌症)**, **Gigantism/Macrosomia (巨嬰症)**.

### Local Epidemiology of Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
* Global & HK Incidence: ~1:10,000 to 1:13,700 live births.
* Equal male-to-female ratio.
* ↑ incidence associated with **In Vitro Fertilization (IVF / 體外受精)**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Basis of Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
* Complex epigenetic dysregulation at **Chromosome 11p15.5**.
* Involves dysregulation of parentally imprinted genes.
* ~80% cases sporadic; ~20% inherited (autosomal dominant transmission with preferential maternal inheritance).

### Molecular Pathophysiology of Overgrowth
* **Insulin-like growth factor 2 (IGF-2)**: Paternal allele overexpression → massive somatic growth drive & ↑ tumor formation risk.
* **CDKN1C**: Maternal allele underexpression (a cell cycle inhibitor) → loss of growth inhibition.
* ↑ IGF-2 also drives severe neonatal **Hyperinsulinaemic Hypoglycaemia (高胰島素性低血糖)**.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Antenatal Presentation of Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
* **Polyhydramnios (羊水過多)**
* Fetal **Macrosomia (巨嬰症)**
* Fetal abdominal wall defects (omphalocele) seen on USG
* Enlarged placenta & visceromegaly

### Postnatal Presentation of Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
* Large, hypotonic/floppy infants.
* Profound, refractory neonatal **Hypoglycaemia (低血糖)**.
* Airway obstruction or feeding difficulties secondary to **Macroglossia (巨舌症)**.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Diagnostic Triad of Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
* **Exomphalos / Omphalocele (臍膨出)** or umbilical hernia.
* **Macroglossia (巨舌症)**: Massive, protruding tongue.
* **Macrosomia (巨嬰症)**: Weight/length >97th centile.

### Craniofacial & Otological Signs
* Pathognomonic ear signs: **Ear creases (耳垂摺痕)** & **Ear pits (耳前廔管)**.
* Facial naevus flammeus (stork bite).
* Prominent occiput.

### Somatic & Visceral Signs
* **Hemihypertrophy (半身肥大)**: Asymmetric growth of one side of body → strongly correlates with ↑ embryonal cancer risk.
* **Visceromegaly (內臟腫大)**: Enlarged liver, kidneys, spleen, pancreas.
* Renal anomalies: Medullary dysplasia, nephromegaly.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating BWS from Simpson-Golabi-Behmel Syndrome
* **BWS**: Normal facial features (aside from tongue), 11p15.5 defect.
* **Simpson-Golabi-Behmel**: Coarse facies, skeletal anomalies (polydactyly, supernumerary nipples), X-linked recessive (GPC3 gene).

### Differentiating BWS from Infant of Diabetic Mother (IDM)
* **BWS**: Presence of classic triad (macroglossia, exomphalos), ear pits, hemihypertrophy.
* **IDM (糖尿病母親嬰兒)**: Macrosomia & hypoglycaemia present, but lacks true macroglossia, structural abdominal wall defects, or distinct ear creases.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Common Differential Diagnoses for BWS
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome** | **Macroglossia**, **Exomphalos**, Ear pits/creases, 11p15.5 |
| **Infant of Diabetic Mother (IDM)** | Maternal DM history, ↑ insulin, structurally normal organs |
| **Congenital Hypothyroidism (先天性甲狀腺機能低下)** | ↑ TSH, ↓ T4, prolonged jaundice, hypotonia, coarse facies |
| **Sotos Syndrome** | Prominent forehead, pointed chin, advanced bone age, NSD1 mutation |
| **Simpson-Golabi-Behmel** | Coarse facies, skeletal defects, X-linked |
| **Mucopolysaccharidoses (MPS)** | Progressive coarsening of features, hepatosplenomegaly, skeletal dysplasia (dysostosis multiplex) |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Blood Investigations for BWS
* **Urgent Capillary Blood Gas (CBG) / Blood Glucose**: Exclude/monitor recurrent profound hypoglycaemia.
* **Serum Insulin / C-peptide**: Assess for hyperinsulinism during hypoglycaemic episodes.
* **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs)**: Exclude congenital hypothyroidism.
* **Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP / 甲胎蛋白)**: Baseline & surveillance for hepatoblastoma.

### Genetic & Definitive Investigations for BWS
* **Methylation Studies (甲基化分析)**: Target Chromosome 11p15.5.
* **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA)**: Detect chromosomal duplications/deletions or paternal uniparental disomy (upd(11)pat).
* Refer to HK Department of Health **Clinical Genetic Service (CGS / 醫學遺傳服務)** for formal diagnosis.

### Imaging Investigations for BWS
* **Abdominal Ultrasound (腹部超聲波)**: Baseline organ assessment (visceromegaly, renal structural anomalies) & tumor screening.
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: Exclude concurrent congenital structural heart defects.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Neonatal Management of BWS
* **Aggressive Hypoglycaemia Reversal**:
  * 1st Line: **IV Dextrose (10% 葡萄糖)** bolus + high continuous infusion rate.
  * Medical Therapy: **Diazoxide (二氮嗪)** or Octreotide for refractory hyperinsulinism.
  * Surgical: **Partial Pancreatectomy (部分胰腺切除術)** if unresponsive to meds to prevent irreversible brain damage.
* **Airway Management**: Prone positioning, nasopharyngeal airway, or rarely tracheostomy if severe macroglossia causes obstruction.

### Chronic Surgical Management of BWS
* **Exomphalos Repair (臍膨出修補術)**: Timed surgical closure by Paediatric Surgery.
* **Tongue Reduction Surgery (舌縮小術)**: Indicated if macroglossia obstructs airway, prevents feeding, or impairs speech/orthodontic development.
* **Orthopaedic Intervention**: Leg-length discrepancy correction for severe hemihypertrophy.

### HA Protocol for BWS Tumor Surveillance
* **Risk**: 7.5% risk of embryonal tumors (Wilms tumor, Hepatoblastoma, Neuroblastoma).
* **Abdominal Ultrasound (USG)**: Q3M until age 8 (Targeting **Wilms tumor (腎母細胞瘤)** & **Hepatoblastoma (肝母細胞瘤)**).
* **Serum AFP (甲胎蛋白)**: Q3M until age 4 (Targeting Hepatoblastoma).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) for BWS
* **Paediatric Endocrinology**: Hyperinsulinism & growth tracking.
* **Paediatric Oncology**: HA tumor surveillance protocol management.
* **Paediatric Surgery / Craniofacial Team**: Exomphalos & macroglossia management.
* **Speech & Language Therapist (ST)**: Swallowing & articulation post-tongue reduction.
* **Clinical Genetics**: Family counseling & recurrence risk calculation.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect in BWS Surveillance
* **Defaulted Follow-up**: Absolute adherence to HA tumor screening protocol is critical.
* **Action**: Any parental non-compliance to USG/AFP screening requires early **Medical Social Worker (MSW / 醫務社工)** input.
* Persistent refusal to engage in life-saving tumor surveillance warrants escalation to **Social Welfare Department (SWD / 社會福利署)** for medical neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Malignant Complications in BWS
* **Wilms Tumor (腎母細胞瘤)** (Most common)
* **Hepatoblastoma (肝母細胞瘤)**
* **Neuroblastoma (神經母細胞瘤)**
* *Note: Risk ↑↑ in patients with hemihypertrophy or upd(11)pat.*

### Neurological & Growth Prognosis in BWS
* **Neurological**: Intellect is typically NORMAL unless irreversible hypoxic brain damage occurs secondary to uncontrolled neonatal hypoglycaemia.
* **Growth**: Explosive somatic growth normalizes by age 8. Final adult height is usually normal to high-normal.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why do we stop routine tumor ultrasound screening at age 8? Answer: The risk of these specific embryonal tumors drops to the general population rate after age 8 as explosive embryonic-type cell division ceases.
* Trap: Attributing macroglossia and hypotonia entirely to congenital hypothyroidism without fully examining the child. Pearl: Always fully expose a large baby and check for exomphalos, hemihypertrophy, and classic ear creases/pits to rule out BWS.
* Pitfall: Discharging a macrosomic newborn with mild hypoglycaemia after a single normal CBG reading. Hyperinsulinaemic hypoglycaemia in BWS can be profound, refractory, and highly recurrent; rigorous serial CBG monitoring and continuous glucose delivery are mandatory to prevent irreversible neurological damage.
