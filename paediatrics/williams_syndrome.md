# Williams Syndrome (威廉氏綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
* Sporadic multisystem neurodevelopmental disorder (神經發育障礙).
* Driven by classic microdeletion on chromosome **7q11.23**.
* Characterized by dysmorphic facies, cardiovascular anomalies, and a distinct cognitive-behavioural profile.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong)
* Rare condition; prevalence ~1:7,500 to 1:10,000 live births.
* >99% occur as **de novo** mutations (sporadic); familial transmission is exceedingly rare.
* Diagnosed and managed largely via Hospital Authority (HA) Clinical Genetic Service (CGS).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Microdeletion Mechanism
* Deletion of ~1.5 to 1.8 megabases at the **7q11.23** locus.
* Contiguous gene deletion syndrome encompassing ~26–28 genes.

### ELN Gene Haploinsufficiency
* Deletion of the ***ELN* gene** → haploinsufficiency of **elastin** (彈性蛋白).
* ↓ Elastin → widespread connective tissue abnormalities → arteriopathy (vessel narrowing) & classic facial dysmorphism.

### Additional Contiguous Genes
* ***GTF2I* & *GTF2IRD1* genes**: Deletion correlates with characteristic visuospatial cognitive deficits and hyper-sociable behavioural profile.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Infantile Presentation
* Often delayed diagnosis.
* **Hypercalcaemia (高血鈣)** → presents as extreme irritability, intractable vomiting, feeding refusal, severe constipation, and failure to thrive (FTT).
* Marked hypotonia and delayed early motor milestones.

### Toddler & Early Childhood Presentation
* Global developmental delay (DD) & expressive speech delay.
* Highly sociable personality ("cocktail party" demeanor) emerges as expressive language develops.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Characteristic Elfin Facies (精靈樣面容)
* Broad forehead (寬額頭) with bitemporal narrowing.
* Short upturned nose with broad tip.
* Full cheeks & prominent periorbital fullness.
* Wide mouth with full, prominent lips.
* **Stellate/lacy iris pattern (星狀虹膜)** (best seen in light-colored eyes).

### Cardiovascular Signs
* **Supravalvular Aortic Stenosis (SVAS, 主動脈瓣上狹窄)**: Most common (~75%).
* Ejection systolic murmur (ESM) best heard at right upper sternal border, radiating to the neck.
* Peripheral Pulmonary Stenosis (PPS): Common in infancy; often resolves spontaneously.

### Cognitive & Behavioural Profile
* **Extreme Sociability**: Over-friendly, lacks stranger anxiety, intense eye contact.
* Cognitive split: Strong expressive verbal skills but profound visuospatial deficits.
* **Hyperacusis (聽覺過敏)**: Phobia of loud noises (e.g., vacuum cleaners, sirens).
* High rates of generalized anxiety and ADHD.

### Endocrine & Growth
* Transient infantile hypercalcaemia.
* Postnatal growth restriction → adult **Short Stature (矮小症)**.

## 5. Discriminatory Features

### Distinguishing Williams Syndrome from Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
* **Williams Syndrome**: Obsessive drive for social interaction, intense empathy, inappropriate lack of stranger boundary, highly expressive (though superficial) language.
* **ASD**: Impaired social reciprocity, active avoidance of eye contact, delayed/absent language, restricted/repetitive behaviours.

### Distinguishing Williams Syndrome from Noonan Syndrome
* **Williams Syndrome**: SVAS, 7q11.23 microdeletion, infantile hypercalcaemia, over-friendly behaviour.
* **Noonan Syndrome**: Pulmonary valve stenosis, *PTPN11*/RASopathy mutations, normal calcium, normal or ASD-like social behaviour.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis Table
| Condition | Distinguishing Features | Genetics / Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Noonan Syndrome (努南氏綜合症)** | Webbed neck, Pulm Stenosis, bleeding diathesis, short stature | *PTPN11*, *SOS1* (Autosomal Dominant) |
| **Fetal Alcohol Syndrome (胎兒酒精綜合症)** | Smooth philtrum, thin upper lip, microcephaly, maternal alcohol hx | Environmental Teratogen |
| **Autism Spectrum Disorder (自閉症譜系障礙)** | Social withdrawal, poor eye contact, repetitive stereotypic movements | Polygenic / Multifactorial |
| **Idiopathic Infantile Hypercalcaemia** | Normal facies, normal cardiac echo, isolated calcium abnormality | *CYP24A1* pathogenic variants |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Clinical Investigations
* **4-Limb Blood Pressure**: Essential to screen for Renal Artery Stenosis (RAS) or aortic coarctation.
* **ECG**: Check for Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH) secondary to SVAS.
* **Growth Charting**: Serial monitoring for FTT and short stature on specialized charts.

### Blood Investigations
* **Calcium Profile**: Total Ca, PO4, ALP + Ionized Calcium → essential in infants with FTT/irritability to detect hypercalcaemia.
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: Baseline U&E to monitor secondary renal damage.
* **Thyroid Function Test (TFT)**: ↑ Risk of subclinical hypothyroidism.

### Imaging Investigations
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: Gold standard to define extent of SVAS, PPS, and coronary artery involvement.
* **Renal USG**: Rule out nephrocalcinosis (secondary to hypercalcaemia) and check renal vascular flow (RAS).

### Definitive Genetic Investigations
* **Microarray (CMA) or targeted FISH**: Specifically probes for the 7q11.23 *ELN* deletion. Gold standard for definitive diagnosis.

## 8. Management 

### Acute Infantile Hypercalcaemia Management
* IV Hydration (0.9% NaCl) to promote calciuresis.
* **Dietary Restriction**: Prescribe low-calcium / low-vitamin D formula (e.g., Locasol).
* Close electrolyte monitoring; condition typically self-resolves by age 2.

### Cardiovascular Management (HA Protocols)
* Lifelong Paediatric Cardiology surveillance (e.g., at Hong Kong Children's Hospital).
* **Surgical Patch Repair**: Indicated for severe SVAS (gradient >50mmHg) to prevent sudden cardiac death.
* Strict pharmacological BP control if RAS is present.

### Neurodevelopmental & Long-term Management
* Early Education & Training Centre (EETC) / Special Child Care Centre (SCCC) placement.
* Speech Therapy (ST): Focus on language pragmatics.
* Occupational Therapy (OT): Focus on visuospatial integration and fine motor skills.
* Physiotherapy (PT): Manage infantile hypotonia and later joint contractures.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Roles
* **Maternal & Child Health Centre (MCHC, 母嬰健康院)**: Routine developmental surveillance & vaccination.
* **Clinical Geneticist**: Diagnostic counselling and recurrence risk assessment (recurrence <1% for unaffected parents).
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Assist with Disability Allowance (DA) and school placement via Social Welfare Department (SWD).
* **Clinical Psychologist (CP)**: Psychoeducational assessment, managing noise phobias, and targeted anxiety therapy.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Vulnerability & Grooming Risks
* Extreme sociability + zero stranger anxiety + lack of social boundary awareness → ↑↑ risk of Child Sexual Abuse (CSA) and stranger abduction.
* Requires intensive parental education on safety grooming and strict "stranger danger" counselling.

### Parental Burnout & NAI Risk
* Infantile phase characterized by intractable crying, colicky hypercalcaemia, and severe sleep disruption.
* ↑ Risk of parental burnout leading to physical Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) or neglect. Proactive MSW and respite support are crucial.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Life-Threatening Cardiovascular Complications
* **Sudden Cardiac Death (SCD)**: Highest risk complication. Caused by severe SVAS compromising coronary artery perfusion (coronary ostia thickening).
* Exceptionally high risk of cardiovascular collapse during **general anaesthesia** → requires expert paediatric cardiac anaesthetist.

### Chronic Medical Complications
* **Renal**: Nephrocalcinosis (from historical hypercalcaemia), recurrent UTI, Secondary Hypertension (from RAS).
* **Endocrine**: Premature adrenarche/puberty, adult-onset diabetes mellitus, hypothyroidism.
* **Musculoskeletal**: Progressive joint contractures and radioulnar synostosis in adulthood.

### Prognosis
* Normal life expectancy if cardiac complications are mild or surgically corrected.
* Vast majority require lifelong supported living and sheltered employment due to permanent intellectual disability and visuospatial deficits.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls

* Trap: Assuming their highly expressive, fluent vocabulary reflects a normal IQ. Pearl: Do not be fooled by the "cocktail party" chatter; their visuospatial skills and executive functioning are profoundly impaired, requiring extensive educational support.
* Viva Question: Why do infants with Williams Syndrome develop hypercalcaemia? Answer: The exact mechanism is unknown, but it is suspected to involve abnormal sensitivity to Vitamin D or the deletion of contiguous genes near the elastin locus affecting calcium metabolism.
* Pitfall: Proceeding with routine elective surgery without a cardiology review. An unrecognized SVAS significantly increases the risk of Sudden Cardiac Death during anaesthesia induction due to compromised coronary perfusion.
* Viva Question: What is the classic cardiac lesion in Williams Syndrome and where is the murmur best heard? Answer: Supravalvular Aortic Stenosis (SVAS), producing an ejection systolic murmur best heard at the right upper sternal border with radiation to the neck.
