# Marfan Syndrome (馬凡氏綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Marfan Syndrome
* Autosomal dominant (AD) systemic connective tissue disorder
* Multisystem involvement: primarily skeletal, cardiovascular (CVS), and ocular systems

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Incidence: ~1 in 5,000 globally and locally
* High risk of underdiagnosis in HA general outpatient/pediatric clinics until catastrophic CVS events (e.g., **Aortic Dissection (主動脈夾層)**) occur
* ~25-30% cases are *de novo* mutations (no family history)

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Mutation in Marfan Syndrome
* Mutation in **FBN1** gene (chromosome 15q21)
* Encodes **Fibrillin-1 (原纖維蛋白-1)**: key glycoprotein for extracellular microfibril assembly in elastic connective tissues

### Pathophysiological Mechanism of Marfan Syndrome
* **Structural Failure:** Mutated *FBN1* → structurally deficient microfibrils → weak connective tissue matrix → extreme joint laxity & progressive aortic root dilation
* **Signaling Dysregulation:** Defective fibrillin fails to bind/sequester Transforming Growth Factor Beta (TGF-β) → ↑ free **TGF-β signaling** → pathological tissue overgrowth (tall stature) & myxomatous degeneration of heart valves (e.g., **Mitral Valve Prolapse (二尖瓣脫垂)**)

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Typical Pediatric Presentation of Marfan Syndrome
* Often picked up by **Maternal and Child Health Centres (MCHC)** or School Dental/Student Health Services due to extreme tall stature
* Unexplained progressive **Scoliosis (脊柱側彎)**
* Routine optometry detecting **Ectopia Lentis (晶狀體異位)**
* Incidental heart murmur detection (MVP or aortic regurgitation)

### Red Flag Presentation in Marfan Syndrome
* Sudden family history of early unexpected death (<50 years) → highly suggestive of fatal aortic rupture
* Acute, severe tearing chest/back pain → acute aortic dissection

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Skeletal Symptoms & Signs in Marfan Syndrome
* Unusually tall stature & long limbs (arm span > height ratio > 1.05)
* **Arachnodactyly (蜘蛛狀指)**: abnormally long, slender fingers
* Chest wall deformities: **Pectus Excavatum (漏斗胸)** or Pectus Carinatum
* Severe progressive **Scoliosis (脊柱側彎)**
* Highly arched palate with dental crowding
* Severe joint hypermobility & pes planus (flat feet)

### Ocular Symptoms & Signs in Marfan Syndrome
* **Ectopia Lentis (晶狀體異位)**: strictly UPWARD/SUPERIOR & OUTWARD dislocation
* Severe progressive myopia (nearsightedness)
* ↑ Risk of early cataracts, glaucoma, & retinal detachment

### Cardiovascular Symptoms & Signs in Marfan Syndrome
* Progressive aortic root dilation → Aortic aneurysm
* **Mitral Valve Prolapse (二尖瓣脫垂)** → late systolic murmur + mid-systolic click
* Aortic Regurgitation → early diastolic murmur at Left Lower Sternal Border (LLSB)

### Pulmonary Symptoms & Signs in Marfan Syndrome
* Apical lung blebs → ↑ risk of spontaneous pneumothorax

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Marfan Syndrome vs. Homocystinuria
* **Homocystinuria (同型半胱氨酸尿症)**: AR inheritance, intellectual disability, pro-thrombotic state (DVT/stroke risk), and DOWNWARD/INWARD lens subluxation. Marfan features normal intellect, upward lens, and bleeding/dissection risk.

### Marfan Syndrome vs. Loeys-Dietz Syndrome
* **Loeys-Dietz Syndrome**: AD inheritance. Features generalized arterial tortuosity, widespread aneurysms (not just aortic root), bifid uvula, cleft palate, and hypertelorism. Lacks ectopia lentis.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Marfanoid Habitus
| Condition | Inheritance | Key Distinguishing Features | Ocular Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marfan Syndrome** | AD | Aortic root dilation, arm span > height, arachnodactyly | Upward lens subluxation |
| **Homocystinuria** | AR | Intellectual disability, thrombosis, Marfanoid habitus | Downward lens subluxation |
| **Loeys-Dietz Syndrome** | AD | Bifid uvula, extreme arterial tortuosity, hypertelorism | Normal lens |
| **Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome** | AD / AR | Skin hyperelasticity, easy bruising, lacks extreme tall stature | Normal lens |
| **Klinefelter Syndrome (XXY)**| Sporadic | Hypogonadism, gynecomastia, learning delays, tall | Normal lens |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Clinical Scoring for Marfan Syndrome
* **Modified Ghent Nosology**: Diagnostic criteria heavily weighting Aortic Root Z-score, Ectopia Lentis, and *FBN1* mutation.

### Imaging Investigations for Marfan Syndrome
* **Echocardiogram (超聲心動圖)**: *Gold Standard/Mandatory*. Serial measurements of aortic root diameter (requires Z-score >2 for diagnosis). Assesses MVP.
* **X-ray Spine/Chest**: Baseline assessment of scoliosis (Cobb angle) and pectus deformities.
* **MRI/CT Angiography**: If widespread aortic/arterial aneurysms are suspected or mapping for surgery.

### Bloods & Special Investigations for Marfan Syndrome
* **FBN1 Genetic Testing**: Definitive molecular diagnosis (often arranged via HA Clinical Genetic Service at HKCH).
* **Ophthalmology Slit-Lamp Exam**: To detect subtle ectopia lentis.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Aortic Dissection in Marfan Syndrome
* Medical Emergency (A&E → ICU)
* Strict BP control: IV **Labetalol** or **Esmolol** (↓ HR and ↓ dp/dt arterial wall shear stress)
* Urgent cardiothoracic surgery consultation for aortic repair

### Chronic Medical Management of Marfan Syndrome
* 1st Line CVS prophylaxis: **Beta-blockers** (e.g., Atenolol/Propranolol) to ↓ heart rate, ↓ inotropy, and slow aortic root dilation.
* Alternative/Adjunct: **ARBs** (e.g., **Losartan (氯沙坦)**) → antagonizes excessive TGF-β signaling and lowers BP.

### Surgical Management of Marfan Syndrome
* Prophylactic **Aortic Root Replacement**: Indicated when aortic root diameter >5.0 cm, rapid expansion (>0.5 cm/year), or significant family history of early dissection.
* Orthopaedic Surgery: Spinal fusion for progressive severe scoliosis.
* Ophthalmology: Lens extraction/refractive surgery for ectopia lentis.

### Lifestyle & HA Outpatient Protocols for Marfan Syndrome
* **ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION**: Contact sports (rugby, martial arts) & isometric exercises (heavy weightlifting) to prevent acute aortic dissection.
* Regular low-to-moderate aerobic exercise is encouraged.
* HA Protocol: Annual Echo, annual slit-lamp exam, 6-monthly pediatric follow-up. Endocarditis prophylaxis *only* if prosthetic valves or prior endocarditis.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) for Marfan Syndrome
* Lead Coordinator: General Paediatrician / Paediatric Cardiologist
* Subspecialties: Orthopaedics, Ophthalmology, Cardiothoracic Surgery.
* **Clinical Genetic Service (CGS)**: Genetic counseling for family planning; screening 1st-degree relatives.

### Psychosocial Support for Marfan Syndrome
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)** / Clinical Psychologist: Address body image issues (tall stature, severe chest deformities, thick glasses) and chronic illness anxiety.
* Career counseling: Avoid heavily physical/manual labor jobs.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect and Misdiagnosis Risks in Marfan Syndrome
* Disproportionate bruising from fragile connective tissue or severe joint laxity may mimic Non-Accidental Injury (NAI). Careful clinical exam is required.
* Main safeguarding issue: **Medical Neglect**. Failure to attend regular cardiology follow-ups is life-threatening due to silent progressive aortic dilation.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Life-Threatening Complications of Marfan Syndrome
* **Aortic Root Dissection / Rupture**: Primary cause of sudden cardiac death.
* Severe Mitral Regurgitation → Congestive Heart Failure.
* Spontaneous Pneumothorax.

### Prognosis of Marfan Syndrome
* Historically: High mortality in 30s-40s due to untreated aortic disease.
* Modern Era: With strict HA pediatric follow-up, β-blocker/Losartan therapy, and timely prophylactic surgery, life expectancy approaches that of the general population.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls

* Viva Question: What are the specific eponymous signs for arachnodactyly in Marfan Syndrome? Answer: Steinberg (wrist) sign (+ve if thumb and little finger overlap completely when grasping the opposite wrist) & Walker-Murdoch (thumb) sign (+ve if the entire distal phalanx of the thumb extends beyond the ulnar border of a clenched fist).
* Trap: Confusing the direction of lens dislocation between connective tissue disorders. Pearl: Marfan Syndrome features strictly UPWARD (superior) lens subluxation. DOWNWARD subluxation is pathognomonic for Homocystinuria.
* Pitfall: Discharging a tall, hypermobile pediatric patient with a newly identified heart murmur without proper imaging. Always perform an Echocardiogram to rule out silent, asymptomatic aortic root dilation, which is lethal if missed.
* Viva Question: Why is Losartan theoretically superior to standard antihypertensives in Marfan Syndrome? Answer: Beyond lowering blood pressure, Losartan (an ARB) directly antagonizes the excessive TGF-β signaling pathway caused by the FBN1 mutation, potentially halting myxomatous tissue degradation.
