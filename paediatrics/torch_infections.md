# TORCH Infections (TORCH綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Congenital TORCH
* Acronym for vertically transmitted in utero infections
* **T**oxoplasmosis (弓形蟲感染)
* **O**ther: Syphilis (梅毒), Parvovirus B19 (微小病毒B19), Varicella-Zoster (水痘帶狀皰疹病毒), Zika (寨卡病毒), HIV (愛滋病毒)
* **R**ubella (風疹)
* **C**ytomegalovirus [CMV] (巨細胞病毒)
* **H**erpes Simplex Virus [HSV] (單純皰疹病毒)
* Shared clinical phenotype → growth restriction, CNS abnormalities, reticuloendothelial system involvement

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Department of Health Context
* **CMV**: Most common congenital infection in HK (0.5-1% live births)
* **Rubella**: Extremely rare locally due to successful HK Childhood Immunisation Programme (HKCIP 香港兒童免疫接種計劃) (universal MMR)
* **Syphilis & HIV**: Universal antenatal screening via Maternal and Child Health Centres (MCHC 母嬰健康院) → minimal vertical transmission if mothers attend standard antenatal care
* **HSV**: Rare intrapartum transmission locally, predominantly HSV-2

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Mechanism of Vertical Transmission
* **Transplacental (in utero)**: CMV, Toxoplasmosis, Rubella, Syphilis, Parvovirus B19
* **Intrapartum (during delivery via infected secretions)**: HSV, HIV, CMV, Hepatitis B/C
* **Postnatal (breast milk)**: CMV, HIV

### Pathogenesis of Fetal Damage
* Early gestation infection (1st Trimester) → ↓ Transmission rate, ↑ Fetal damage (teratogenesis, miscarriage)
* Late gestation infection (3rd Trimester) → ↑ Transmission rate, ↓ Fetal damage (often asymptomatic at birth)
* Pathological mechanisms:
* Direct tissue destruction (viral cytopathic effect)
* Vascular endothelial damage → tissue ischaemia/infarction
* Disruption of fetal neuroblast migration → structural CNS anomalies

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### General Neonatal Presentation of TORCH
* Intrauterine Growth Restriction [IUGR] (宮內生長遲滯)
* Hepatosplenomegaly (肝脾腫大)
* Prolonged neonatal Jaundice (黃疸) (conjugated hyperbilirubinaemia)
* Thrombocytopenia (血小板減少症)
* Microcephaly (小頭畸形)
* Non-immune hydrops fetalis (非免疫性胎兒水腫)

### Pathognomonic Signs by Specific Organism
| Pathogen | Key Pathognomonic Clinical Signs (Classic Triads/Tetrads) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Toxoplasmosis** | Diffuse intracranial calcifications (廣泛性顱內鈣化), Hydrocephalus (腦積水), Chorioretinitis (脈絡膜視網膜炎). |
| **Syphilis** | Snuffles (梅毒性鼻炎), Maculopapular rash on palms/soles (斑丘疹), Osteochondritis (骨軟骨炎), Hutchinson teeth (late). |
| **Rubella** | Cataracts (白內障), Sensorineural hearing loss [SNHL] (感音神經性聽力喪失), Patent Ductus Arteriosus [PDA] (動脈導管未閉). |
| **CMV** | Periventricular calcifications (腦室周圍鈣化), Petechiae/Blueberry muffin rash (瘀斑/藍莓鬆餅嬰兒), SNHL. |
| **HSV** | Vesicular skin rash (水皰疹), Keratoconjunctivitis (角膜結膜炎), Meningoencephalitis (腦膜腦炎). |
| **Parvovirus B19** | Severe fetal anaemia (胎兒貧血), Hydrops fetalis (胎兒水腫). |

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating TORCH from Chromosomal Anomalies
* **TORCH**: +ve inflammatory markers (often ↑ liver enzymes, ↓ platelets), characteristic eye lesions (chorioretinitis/cataracts), specific brain calcification patterns
* **Chromosomal (e.g., Trisomy 13/18/21)**: Distinct dysmorphic facial features, major structural cardiac/renal defects without widespread visceral inflammation/petechiae

### Differentiating Specific TORCH Pathogens via CNS Calcifications
* **CMV**: **C**alcifications are **C**ircumventricular (periventricular)
* **Toxoplasmosis**: Calcifications are disseminated/scattered throughout the cortex and basal ganglia

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Neonatal Petechiae/Purpura Differentials
* Congenital TORCH infections
* Neonatal Alloimmune Thrombocytopenia [NAIT] (新生兒同種免疫性血小板減少症)
* Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation [DIC] (彌散性血管內凝血) due to severe bacterial sepsis (e.g., GBS)
* Congenital leukaemia (rare)

### Neonatal Conjugated Hyperbilirubinaemia Differentials
* Biliary Atresia (膽道閉鎖)
* Neonatal Hepatitis (新生兒肝炎) (TORCH-induced)
* Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (α1-抗胰蛋白酶缺乏症)
* Galactosaemia (半乳糖血症)

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Maternal Antenatal Investigations
* Serology (IgG, IgM) for Syphilis (VDRL/RPR), Rubella, HIV, HBV (Standard HA Antenatal Screen)
* Targeted TORCH serology if abnormal fetal ultrasound
* Fetal ultrasound → checking for IUGR, hydrops, cerebral ventriculomegaly

### Neonatal 1st Line Investigations
* Full Blood Count → ↓ Platelets, abnormal WBCs
* Liver Function Tests → ↑ AST/ALT, ↑ Conjugated Bilirubin
* Cranial Ultrasound (USG) → Identify calcifications, ventricular dilation
* Ophthalmology Consult → Dilated fundoscopy (眼底檢查) for chorioretinitis/cataracts
* Audiology Consult → Otoacoustic Emissions [OAE] / Automated Auditory Brainstem Response [AABR] for SNHL

### Neonatal Confirmatory Pathogen-Specific Investigations
* **CMV**: Urine or Saliva CMV PCR (must be collected <21 days of life to distinguish congenital from acquired)
* **Toxoplasmosis**: Toxoplasma specific IgM/IgA, CSF PCR
* **Syphilis**: Infant VDRL/RPR (compare titre to mother's), FTA-ABS, Long bone X-rays
* **HSV**: Swab PCR of vesicles/mucosa, CSF HSV PCR, Blood HSV PCR
* **Rubella**: Rubella specific IgM, Pharyngeal/Urine PCR

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Pharmacological Management by Organism
| Pathogen | Targeted Antimicrobial Therapy (HA Standard) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CMV (Symptomatic)** | IV Ganciclovir (更昔洛韋) or PO Valganciclovir (纈更昔洛韋) for 6 months (to prevent SNHL progression). |
| **Toxoplasmosis** | PO Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Folinic Acid (to prevent marrow suppression) for 12 months. |
| **Syphilis** | IV Aqueous Crystalline Penicillin G for 10-14 days. |
| **HSV** | IV Acyclovir (阿昔洛韋) (High dose: 60mg/kg/day divided q8h) for 14 days (SEM) or 21 days (CNS/Disseminated). |

### HA Infection Control Protocols
* **Rubella/Varicella**: Strict airborne & contact isolation in NICU/SCBU
* **CMV**: Standard precautions (avoid pregnant HCWs handling bodily fluids)

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Paediatric Infectious Disease**: Guide complex antimicrobial duration
* **Ophthalmology**: Serial eye exams, cataract surgery
* **Audiology/ENT**: Hearing aids, cochlear implants for CMV/Rubella-induced SNHL
* **Physiotherapy/Occupational Therapy**: Early intervention for neurodevelopmental motor delays
* **Neurology**: Management of symptomatic seizures

### Family Psychosocial Support
* Medical Social Worker (MSW) referral for chronic disability support/allowance (e.g., Disability Allowance 傷殘津貼 in HK)
* Parental psychological support due to guilt related to maternal transmission
* Genetic counselling/Obstetric review for future pregnancies

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding in Congenital Infections
* **Syphilis/HIV/HSV-2**: Strongly associated with maternal Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)
* Assess maternal social context, partner risk factors, illicit drug use
* Check for maternal engagement with antenatal care; unbooked pregnancies in HA hospitals raise major social safeguarding concerns
* Involve MSW for vulnerable mothers (e.g., commercial sex workers, substance abusers) to ensure safe discharge environment for neonate

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-term Neurological Complications
* Severe global developmental delay (廣泛性發育遲緩)
* Cerebral Palsy (腦性麻痺)
* Intractable Epilepsy (癲癇)
* Progressive SNHL (especially CMV) leading to speech/language delay
* Visual impairment/blindness

### Prognostic Indicators
* **HSV**: Disseminated and CNS disease have high mortality/morbidity despite Acyclovir; SEM (Skin, Eyes, Mouth) disease has better prognosis if treated early
* **CMV**: Presence of intracranial calcifications strictly correlates with poor severe neurocognitive outcomes

## 11. Prevention & Public Health

### Maternal Prevention Strategies
* **Rubella**: Ensure immunity prior to pregnancy via HKCIP MMR
* **Toxoplasmosis**: Avoid raw/undercooked meat, unpasteurised milk, and handling cat litter during pregnancy
* **CMV**: Strict hand hygiene, especially after changing diapers of toddlers (major vector in daycares)
* **Syphilis/HIV**: Safe sex practices, universal antenatal screening

### Public Health Notification (Hong Kong)
* Rubella and Syphilis are statutory notifiable infectious diseases under Cap. 599 (Prevention and Control of Disease Ordinance) to the Centre for Health Protection (CHP 衞生防護中心)

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of non-genetic sensorineural hearing loss in children? Answer: Congenital Cytomegalovirus (CMV).
* Viva Question: How do you radiologically differentiate the brain calcifications of CMV from Toxoplasmosis? Answer: CMV calcifications are periventricular; Toxoplasmosis calcifications are diffuse/scattered throughout the brain parenchyma.
* Trap: Assuming a positive CMV urine test at 2 months of age confirms congenital CMV. Pearl: Urine/saliva CMV PCR must be collected within the first 21 days of life to definitively differentiate congenital CMV from postnatally acquired CMV (e.g., via breastmilk).
* Trap: Treating a neonate with HSV using a standard 5-7 day course of antivirals. Pearl: Neonatal HSV requires high-dose IV Acyclovir (60mg/kg/day) for an extended 14 days (for Skin-Eye-Mouth) or 21 days (for CNS or disseminated disease) to prevent devastating neurological sequelae.
* Pitfall: Missing congenital syphilis in a neonate born to a mother adequately treated for syphilis 5 years ago. Maternal non-treponemal titres (VDRL/RPR) can remain low-level positive for life (serofast). You must compare the infant's quantitative VDRL/RPR titre to the mother's at the time of delivery; an infant titre $\ge$ 4-fold higher than the mother's indicates active congenital infection.
