# Angelman Syndrome (安格曼綜合症 / 快樂木偶症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Disease Definition
* Complex genetic neurodevelopmental disorder.
* Characterised by severe **Intellectual Disability (智力障礙)**, **Microcephaly (小頭畸形)**, characteristic facies, & a happy/excitable demeanour with frequent laughter.

### Local Epidemiology
* Rare: incidence ~1:15,000.
* Locally managed jointly by **Clinical Genetic Services, Department of Health (DH) (衞生署醫學遺傳服務)** & **HA Paediatric Neurology (醫管局兒童腦神經科)**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Locus
* Loss of function of **UBE3A** gene.
* Located on maternally inherited **Chromosome 15 (15q11-q13)**.

### Pathogenic Mechanisms
| Genetic Mechanism | Prevalence | Phenotypic Severity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maternal Deletion (母系缺失)** | ~70% | Most severe (strongly +ve for hypopigmentation) |
| **Paternal Uniparental Disomy (UPD) (單親二倍體)** | ~11% | Milder phenotype |
| **Imprinting Centre Defect (印記中心缺陷)** | ~3% | Variable |
| **UBE3A Mutation (基因突變)** | ~11% | Variable |

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Typical Age of Presentation
* Normal prenatal/birth history; initial presentation often delayed to age 1-2 years.
* First parental concerns: severe delayed motor milestones, absent babbling/speech, or onset of **Seizures (癲癇)**.

### Behavioral Presentation
* Incessant unprovoked laughing, smiling, & profound excitability.
* Constant water-seeking behaviour & hypermotoric activity.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Consistent Features (100% of Cases)
* Severe **Developmental Delay (發展遲緩)** / **Intellectual Disability (智力障礙)**.
* Severe speech impairment (minimal/no spoken words; non-verbal communication > verbal).
* Movement/balance disorder (wide-based **Ataxia (共濟失調)**, tremulous limb movements).
* Happy demeanour, excitable, +ve hand-flapping stereotypies.

### Frequent Features (>80% of Cases)
* **Microcephaly (小頭畸形)** (absolute/relative ↓ head circumference by age 2).
* Early-onset **Seizures (癲癇)** (typically <3 years).
* Abnormal **EEG (腦電圖)** (out of proportion to clinical seizure activity).

### Physical Dysmorphology
* Prominent mandible, wide mouth, widely spaced teeth.
* Hypopigmentation (pale skin/eyes/hair) specifically if deletion mechanism +ve.
* Strabismus.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Versus Prader-Willi Syndrome (普瑞德威利症候群)
* PWS: Involves SAME chromosomal region (15q11-q13) but PATERNAL loss/deletion.
* PWS features: Profound neonatal hypotonia → subsequent hyperphagia → severe **Obesity (肥胖)**. (No happy/ataxic phenotype).

### Versus Rett Syndrome (雷特氏症)
* Rett: Affects females exclusively (MECP2 mutation), also causes severe ID & microcephaly.
* Rett features: Developmental *regression* (loss of purposeful hand use) + midline hand-wringing stereotypies. Lacks the hallmark happy/laughing demeanour of Angelman.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Most Likely Diagnosis
* **Angelman Syndrome (安格曼綜合症)** (in child with severe speech delay, ataxia, & excessive laughter).

### Must-Not-Miss Diagnoses
* Treatable early-onset epileptic encephalopathies.
* **GLUT1 Deficiency Syndrome (葡萄糖轉運蛋白1缺乏症)** (Treatable with strict ketogenic diet).
* Cerebral Palsy (ataxic/dyskinetic subtypes).

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **EEG (腦電圖)**: Highly characteristic (notched delta waves / large amplitude slow-spike waves) often present before clinical seizures emerge.

### Gold Standard Genetic Testing
| Tier | Test | Diagnostic Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Line** | **DNA Methylation Analysis (DNA甲基化分析)** | Detects abnormal maternal imprinting. Identifies ~80% of all cases. |
| **2nd Line** | **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA) (染色體微陣列分析)** | Determines specific size of 15q11-q13 deletion. |
| **3rd Line** | **UBE3A Sequencing (基因測序)** | Indicated if methylation is normal but clinical index of suspicion remains ↑. |

### Routine Monitoring
* Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM) for AED levels.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
* Standard HA Paediatric A&E Resuscitation for **Status Epilepticus (癲癇重積狀態)**.
* IV **Lorazepam (勞拉西泮)** / Diazepam → IV **Phenytoin (苯妥英)** / **Levetiracetam (左乙拉西坦)**.

### Chronic Medical Management
| Target | First-Line Pharmacotherapy / Intervention |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Seizures (癲癇)** | **Sodium Valproate (丙戊酸鈉)**, **Clonazepam (氯硝西泮)**, **Levetiracetam (左乙拉西坦)** |
| **Sleep Disturbance** | Strict sleep hygiene + **Melatonin (褪黑激素)** |
| **GERD / Constipation** | PPIs, regular laxatives (Macrogol) |

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Communication & Physical Therapy
* **Speech Therapy (言語治療)**: Intensive focus on **Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) (擴大及替代通訊)** (e.g., picture boards, speech-generating iPads) due to absent verbal speech.
* **Physiotherapy (物理治療)**: Gait training for **Ataxia (共濟失調)**, orthotics to prevent joint contractures/scoliosis.

### Social & Educational Support
* **Special Education**: Placement via Education Bureau (EDB) into **Severe Grade Intellectual Disability Special School (嚴重智障兒童學校)**.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Assess eligibility for Disability Allowance (DA) & Carer Allowance (Social Welfare Department).

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Genetic Recurrence Risk Counselling
* **Clinical Geneticist** referral is mandatory to guide family planning:
    * <1% recurrence risk for Deletion / UPD.
    * 50% recurrence risk for UBE3A mutation.

### Carer Stress & Neglect Risks
* Severe chronic sleep disturbances + severe hyperactivity + lifelong dependency → extreme risk of carer burnout.
* Requires proactive respite care allocation via MSW to prevent non-accidental injury (NAI) or neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Disease Trajectory & Prognosis
* Normal lifespan expected.
* Requires lifelong 24/7 care due to profound **Intellectual Disability (智力障礙)** & lack of independent living skills.

### Long-term Complications
* **Seizures (癲癇)**: Often peak in early childhood & may spontaneously improve or resolve by adulthood.
* **Ataxia (共濟失調)** & severe sleep issues: Persist chronically throughout adult life.
* High risk of developing severe scoliosis in adolescence.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Confusing the genetics of Angelman and Prader-Willi. Pearl: Maternal deletion = AngelMan. Paternal deletion = Prader-Willi.
* Viva Question: A 3-year-old girl presents with severe speech delay, ataxia, and frequent inappropriate laughter. What is the most likely diagnosis and the first-line genetic test? Answer: Angelman Syndrome. First-line test is DNA methylation analysis of chromosome 15.
* Pitfall: Attributing severe developmental delay and seizures purely to perinatal causes (e.g., HIE) without assessing the behavioral phenotype. Taking note of a uniquely happy/excitable demeanour and microcephaly prevents missing the underlying genetic diagnosis.
