
# Angelman Syndrome (安格曼綜合症 / 快樂木偶症)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A complex genetic disorder that primarily affects the nervous system. Characterised by severe intellectual disability, microcephaly, characteristic facial appearance, and a happy, excitable demeanour with frequent smiling and laughter.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Rare, estimated incidence ~1 in 15,000. Managed by Clinical Genetic Services (DH) and paediatric neurology.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Genetics**: Loss of function of the *UBE3A* gene on the **maternally** inherited Chromosome 15 (15q11-q13).
- **Mechanisms**:
  1. Maternal deletion (most common, ~70%, most severe phenotype).
  2. Paternal uniparental disomy (UPD) (inheriting two copies of chromosome 15 from the father).
  3. Imprinting centre defect.
  4. *UBE3A* gene mutation.
- *Note: Deletion of the PATERNALLY inherited 15q11-q13 causes Prader-Willi Syndrome.*

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Consistent Features (100%)**:
  - Severe developmental delay / Intellectual disability.
  - Severe speech impairment (minimal or no use of words; non-verbal communication is better).
  - Movement or balance disorder (ataxia of gait, tremulous movement of limbs).
  - **Unique Behaviour**: Happy demeanour, frequent laughing/smiling, easily excitable, hand-flapping.
- **Frequent Features (>80%)**:
  - Microcephaly (developing by age 2).
  - Seizures (onset usually < 3 years).
  - Abnormal EEG (characteristic large amplitude slow-spike waves).
- **Physical Features**: Prominent mandible, wide mouth, widely spaced teeth, hypopigmentation (if deletion mechanism).
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **Prader-Willi Syndrome**: Same chromosomal region, but presents with profound neonatal hypotonia, followed by hyperphagia and severe obesity.
- **Rett Syndrome**: Also causes severe ID and microcephaly in girls, but features *regression* (loss of purposeful hand use) and characteristic hand-wringing stereotypies, rather than the happy demeanour of Angelman.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Angelman Syndrome (in a child with severe speech delay, ataxia, and a happy disposition).
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Treatable causes of seizures (e.g., GLUT1 deficiency).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **EEG**: Highly characteristic even before seizures start (notched delta waves).
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Genetic Testing**:
1. **DNA Methylation Analysis**: Detects ~80% of cases (identifies abnormal maternal imprinting).
2. **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA)**: To detect the specific 15q11-q13 deletion.
3. **UBE3A Sequencing**: If methylation is normal but clinical suspicion is high.
- **Monitoring**:
  - Regular monitoring of anti-epileptic drug levels if on treatment.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Management of status epilepticus if presenting with prolonged seizures.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - **No cure**. Management is symptomatic.
  - **Seizure Control**: Often difficult to control. Valproate, Clonazepam, Levetiracetam are commonly used.
  - **Sleep Management**: Severe sleep disturbances are common. Melatonin, strict sleep hygiene.
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **Communication**: Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) devices are crucial as verbal speech is minimal. Intensive speech therapy.
- **Physiotherapy**: For ataxia and to prevent contractures.
- **Special Education**: Placement in a Severe Grade Intellectual Disability special school.
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Genetic counselling is essential to determine recurrence risk (varies from <1% for deletion/UPD to 50% for UBE3A mutation).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Normal lifespan.
- Requires lifelong care due to severe intellectual disability and lack of speech.
- Seizures may improve in adulthood, but ataxia and sleep issues persist.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Confusing the genetics of Angelman and Prader-Willi. *Pearl*: **M**aternal deletion = Angel**M**an. **P**aternal deletion = **P**rader-Willi.
- **Viva Question**: "A 3-year-old girl presents with severe speech delay, ataxia, and frequent inappropriate laughter. What is the most likely diagnosis and the first-line genetic test?" *Answer*: Angelman Syndrome. First-line test is DNA methylation analysis of chromosome 15.
