# Oesophageal Atresia & Tracheo-Oesophageal Fistula (OA & TOF) (食道閉鎖及氣管食道瘻管)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Congenital interruption of the oesophagus (OA), usually with a fistulous connection to the trachea (TOF).
- **Local Epidemiology**: Occurs in approx 1 in 3000-4000 live births globally.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Defective separation of the foregut into the respiratory and gastrointestinal tracts around the 4th-5th week of gestation. 
- Over 85% is Type C: Proximal blind oesophageal pouch and a distal TOF connecting the lower oesophagus to the trachea.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **Antenatal**: Maternal polyhydramnios (fetus cannot swallow amniotic fluid), absent stomach bubble on scan.
- **Postnatal**: Salivation ("frothing" at the mouth), coughing/choking, and cyanosis triggered by the first feed.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Inability to swallow saliva or milk.
- Abdominal distension (air crosses from trachea via distal fistula into the stomach).
- Respiratory distress (chemical pneumonitis from reflux of gastric acid into lungs via distal TOF).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from duodenal atresia (bile-stained vomit is late; OA/TOF is frothing saliva and immediate choking on feeds).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Respiratory distress syndrome (RDS)
- Meconium Aspiration Syndrome (MAS)
- High bowel obstruction

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**: Attempt to pass a stiff, wide-bore (10Fr) nasogastric tube. It will arrest at ~9-11 cm from the lips.
- **Imaging**: Erect CXR (shows the NG tube curled in the proximal blind pouch; gas in the bowel confirms the presence of a distal TOF).
- **Special**: Echocardiogram (mandatory to exclude cardiac defects and establish the side of the aortic arch for surgical planning) and Renal Ultrasound (for VACTERL).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute Resuscitation**: Nil By Mouth (NBM), strict upright positioning (minimizes gastric acid reflux), continuous suction of the proximal pouch (Replogle tube).
- **Surgical**: Primary extrapleural surgical repair (ligation of fistula and primary oesophageal anastomosis). If the gap is too long, staged repair with initial gastrostomy.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Extensive multidisciplinary care required. **MCHC** monitors for failure to thrive post-operatively.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Parents must be hyper-vigilant for feeding complications. Ensure secure social support network.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **Early**: Anastomotic leak, stricture.
- **Long-term**: Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD) is almost universal. Tracheomalacia ("TOF cough" - a harsh, brassy bark).
- Prognosis depends heavily on associated VACTERL anomalies.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Viva Q**: "What does VACTERL stand for?" Answer: Vertebral, Anorectal, Cardiac (VSD/TOF), Tracheal, Esophageal, Renal, Limb (radial hypoplasia).
- **Trap**: Using contrast studies for diagnosis. It is deeply unsafe and contraindicated due to extreme risk of aspiration. Passing a firm NG tube and obtaining an X-ray is sufficient.
