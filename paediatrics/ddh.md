# Developmental Dysplasia of the Hip (DDH) (發育性髖關節發育不良)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of DDH
* **Definition**: Spectrum of disorders affecting proximal femur & acetabulum → hip subluxation & dislocation.
* **Range**: Mild acetabular dysplasia → complete hip joint dislocation.

### Local & Global Epidemiology of DDH
* **HK Incidence**: ~1-2/1000 live births. True global birth prevalence ~1.3/1000.
* **Screening Incidence**: NIPE detects abnormalities in 6-10/1000, but most resolve spontaneously.
* **Sex Ratio**: ♀:♂ = 4:1.
* **Screening**: Routine at birth & 8 weeks via Maternal and Child Health Centres (MCHC) (母嬰健康院) & paediatricians.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Acetabular Dysplasia
* **Mechanism**: Shallow acetabulum (socket) → femoral head (ball) slips out partially (subluxation) or completely (dislocation).
* **Consequence**: Unreduced hip → failure of normal joint development → permanent deformity & early osteoarthritis.

### Genetic & Mechanical Risk Factors (The 6 F's)
* **Female sex**: ↑ sensitivity to maternal relaxin hormone.
* **Family history**: Genetic predisposition.
* **Firstborn**: Tighter uterine environment.
* **Frank breech presentation**: Most significant risk factor (esp. ≥34 weeks gestation).
* **Fluid**: Oligohydramnios (restricts fetal movement).
* **Foot abnormalities**: e.g., clubfoot/talipes, packaging disorders.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Neonatal and Infant Presentation
* **Usually asymptomatic**: Uncommon to have pain or physical limitations early on.
* **Detection**: Picked up during routine NIPE (Newborn and Infant Physical Examination) at birth or 8-week review.

### Toddler and Older Child Presentation
* **Missed cases**: Present with painless limp, abnormal/waddling gait (if bilateral), or delayed walking.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Neonatal Examination Signs (0-3 Months)
* **Barlow Test (巴洛氏測試)**: Attempts to dislocate unstable hip. 
  * Action: Adduct hip & apply posterior pressure. 
  * +ve Sign: Palpable "clunk" (hip slips out of acetabulum).
* **Ortolani Test (奧托拉尼測試)**: Attempts to reduce dislocated hip.
  * Action: Abduct hip & apply anterior pressure.
  * +ve Sign: Palpable "clunk" (sensation of relocation back into acetabulum).
* **Note**: Clicks are common & benign (ligamentous snapping); a "clunk" is always pathological.

### Infant and Toddler Signs (>3 Months)
* Barlow/Ortolani tests become -ve as soft tissues tighten.
* **Limited Hip Abduction**: Most reliable sign in older infants. Affected hip cannot fully abduct when flexed to 90°.
* **Galeazzi Sign (蓋氏徵)**: Supine, knees flexed → knee on dislocated side is lower (femoral head lies posterior to acetabulum).
* **Asymmetrical Skin Folds**: Asymmetry of gluteal/thigh creases (soft sign).
* **Trendelenburg Test (特倫德倫伯格氏試驗)**: +ve in walking children (standing on affected leg → pelvis drops on opposite side due to hip abductor weakness).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating DDH from Inflammatory/Infectious Hip
| Feature | DDH | Septic Arthritis | Transient Synovitis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Congenital/Insidious | Acute | Acute/Subacute |
| **Pain** | Painless in infancy | Severe pain | Mild-moderate pain |
| **Fever** | Afebrile | High fever (>38.5°C) | Low-grade/None |
| **Mobility** | Limited abduction, painless | Refusal to move (pseudoparalysis) | Limp, antalgic gait |
| **Age** | 0-18 months | Any (often <2 yrs) | 3-8 years (post-viral) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Key Differential Diagnoses for Abnormal Gait/Hip Signs
* **Septic Arthritis (化膿性關節炎)**.
* **Transient Synovitis (暫時性滑膜炎)**.
* **Perthes Disease (Legg-Calvé-Perthes) (佩斯氏病)**.
* **Slipped Capital Femoral Epiphysis (SCFE) (股骨頭骨骺滑脫)**.
* **Neuromuscular Disorders**: e.g., Cerebral Palsy → spastic hip subluxation.
* **Positional Talipes/Packaging defects**.

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Imaging Modalities for DDH
| Modality | Indication | Key Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ultrasound Hips** | Modality of choice for age < 4-6 months | Assesses acetabular depth (alpha angle) & femoral head stability. |
| **X-ray Pelvis (AP)** | Modality of choice for age > 4-6 months | Shallow acetabular index, lateral/superior displacement of femoral head, delayed ossification center. |

### Screening Protocols in Hong Kong (MCHC)
* **Clinical Screening**: Routine at birth and 8 weeks for all.
* **Targeted Ultrasound**: Routine US NOT recommended for all (high false +ve rate). US indicated at 6 weeks if:
  * Clinical suspicion of hip instability.
  * Inconclusive hip exam.
  * +ve Risk factors: Breech presentation ≥34 weeks, +ve Family History.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Treatment Goals for DDH
* **Primary Goal**: Maintain concentric reduction of femoral head within acetabulum to allow/stimulate normal joint development.

### Age-Stratified Evidence-Based Management
| Age Group | 1st Line Management | Details & Prognosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **< 6 months** | **Pavlik Harness (帕夫利克吊帶)** | Dynamic splint holding hips in flexion & abduction. Worn 24/7 for 6-12 weeks. Success rate >90%. |
| **6-18 months** (or failed Pavlik) | **Closed Reduction & Spica Cast** | Under GA + arthrogram to confirm reduction. Immobilized in plaster spica cast for months. |
| **> 18 months** | **Open Reduction (切開復位術)** | Surgical clearance of obstacles (e.g., inverted limbus, tight psoas) ± pelvic/femoral osteotomy. Followed by spica casting. |

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Paediatric Orthopaedics**: Specialist management, surgery, & long-term follow-up.
* **MCHC/Paediatricians**: Universal clinical screening & early detection.
* **Nursing/Physiotherapy**: Educating parents on caring for baby in Pavlik harness/spica cast (nappy changing, positioning, car seats, skin care).
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Support for families coping with prolonged immobilization & transport logistics.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Considerations
* **DDH-Specific**: N/A. Condition is developmental/congenital. 
* **Red Flags in associated musculoskeletal presentations**: Unexplained fractures, delayed presentation of limp, bruising in non-mobile infants, injuries inconsistent with developmental age.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Iatrogenic and Long-Term Complications
* **Avascular Necrosis (AVN) of Femoral Head (股骨頭缺血性壞死)**: Most devastating iatrogenic complication. Caused by excessive forced abduction in harness/cast compressing fragile blood supply. Splinting MUST be done expertly.
* **Early Osteoarthritis (早發性骨關節炎)**: If hip remains dysplastic/subluxated → premature wear & tear → severe OA in young adulthood (often requires Total Hip Replacement).

### Overall Prognosis
* **Early Diagnosis (<6 months)**: Excellent prognosis with conservative management.
* **Late Diagnosis (> walking age)**: Poor prognosis, high surgical complication rate, permanent dysplasia risk ↑.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why do we use Ultrasound instead of X-ray to screen for DDH in a 6-week-old infant? Answer: The femoral head and acetabulum are entirely cartilaginous at this age and radiolucent on X-ray. The ossification centre appears at 4-6 months.
* Viva Question: What is the most reliable clinical sign of DDH in a 9-month-old infant? Answer: Limited abduction of the flexed hip. Barlow and Ortolani tests are no longer useful.
* Viva Question: What is the mechanism of the Trendelenburg test in DDH? Answer: Unreduced DDH leads to functional shortening and weakness of the hip abductors (gluteus medius/minimus). Standing on the affected leg causes the contralateral pelvis to drop.
* Trap: Thinking a hip "click" is diagnostic of DDH. Pearl: Clicks are common and usually benign ligamentous snapping. A palpable "clunk" (Ortolani/Barlow) is the true pathological sign of instability.
* Pitfall: Discharging a baby born via breech presentation just because the newborn clinical hip exam is normal. Always arrange a targeted screening Ultrasound of the hips at 6 weeks for any baby with significant risk factors (breech ≥34 wks or +ve FHx), as clinical exam can miss mild dysplasia.
* Pitfall: Applying the Pavlik harness with excessive forced abduction. This compresses the femoral head blood supply, leading to the devastating iatrogenic complication of Avascular Necrosis (AVN).
