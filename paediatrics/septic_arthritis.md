# Septic Arthritis (化膿性關節炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Septic Arthritis
* **Definition**: Acute, purulent infection within joint space.
* **Significance**: True orthopaedic emergency. Bacterial enzymes & host inflammatory response → rapid/irreversible articular cartilage destruction (軟骨破壞) within days.

### Local Epidemiology of Septic Arthritis in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: Relatively common paediatric emergency in HK HA hospitals.
* **Age**: Most frequently <5 yrs.
* **Common Sites**: Hip (髖關節) & Knee (膝關節) most commonly affected.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathogenesis of Septic Arthritis
* **Haematogenous Spread** (Most common): Transient bacteraemia (e.g., from URTI/skin infection) → lodge in highly vascular synovial membrane.
* **Contiguous Spread**: From adjacent osteomyelitis (骨髓炎). Common in neonates (blood vessels cross growth plate into epiphysis) or intra-capsular metaphysis joints (hip/shoulder).
* **Direct Inoculation**: Penetrating wound, intra-articular injection, or surgery.

### Mechanism of Cartilage Destruction in Septic Arthritis
* Bacteria + massive host neutrophil response → release proteolytic enzymes & cytokines → rapid degradation of articular cartilage.
* ↑ pus volume → ↑ intra-articular pressure → compromises blood supply → **Avascular Necrosis (AVN) (缺血性壞死)** (esp. femoral head).

### Causative Organisms of Septic Arthritis by Age Group
| Age Group | Primary Causative Organisms | Clinical Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **All Ages** | *Staphylococcus aureus* (金黃色葡萄球菌) | Most common overall. |
| **Neonates (<1 mo)** | Group B *Streptococcus*, *E. coli*, *S. aureus* | High risk of contiguous osteomyelitis. |
| **Children <5 yrs** | *S. aureus*, Group A Strep, *Kingella kingae* | *K. kingae* increasingly recognised. Hib now rare in HK due to vaccination. |
| **Adolescents** | *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* (淋球菌), *S. aureus* | Consider in sexually active teens (presents with polyarthralgia → monoarthritis). |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Systemic Presentation of Septic Arthritis
* **Onset**: Very short history (hours → days).
* **Systemic Signs**: High fever (發燒), rigors, tachycardia, toxic appearance.

### Local Joint Signs of Septic Arthritis
* **Pain**: Exquisite, severe, localized to single joint.
* **Pseudoparalysis (假性癱瘓)**: Absolute refusal to bear weight or move the limb.
* **Inspection**: Swelling, erythema, warmth (obvious in superficial joints like knee/ankle; often absent in deep joints like hip).
* **Range of Motion (ROM)**: Severely restricted. ANY active/passive movement causes excruciating pain.

### Typical Position of Comfort in Septic Arthritis
* Joint involuntarily held to maximize capsular volume (↓ pressure & pain).
* **Hip**: Flexed, abducted, externally rotated (**FABER** position).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Septic Arthritis from Transient Synovitis (短暫性滑膜炎)
* **Transient Synovitis**: Child generally well, afebrile/low-grade fever, mild-to-mod pain. Can usually bear *some* weight/limp.
* **Septic Arthritis**: Toxic, highly febrile, absolutely non-weight-bearing, excruciating pain on micromovement.

### Differentiating Septic Arthritis from Osteomyelitis (骨髓炎)
* **Osteomyelitis**: Infection in bone (metaphysis). Exquisite point tenderness over bone. Gentle movement of adjacent joint *may* be tolerated.
* **Septic Arthritis**: Joint itself is the source. ANY joint movement is completely intolerable.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Key Differential Diagnoses for Acute Hot Joint in Children
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Septic Arthritis** | Toxic, ↑↑ fever, purulent aspirate, severe pain on passive ROM. |
| **Transient Synovitis (Irritable Hip)** | Well-looking, post-URTI, mild pain, able to bear some weight. |
| **Osteomyelitis** | Bone tenderness, pain worse on bearing weight but passive joint ROM relatively preserved. |
| **Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)** | Insidious onset, morning stiffness >6 weeks, less toxic, often polyarticular. |
| **Reactive Arthritis** | Post-GI or GU infection, often asymmetric oligoarthritis. |
| **Haemarthrosis** | Hx of trauma or bleeding diathesis (e.g., Haemophilia (血友病)). |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Kocher Criteria for Differentiating Septic Arthritis from Transient Synovitis of the Hip
* Tool to assess probability of septic arthritis.
* **The 4 Criteria**:
    1.  Non-weight bearing.
    2.  Fever > 38.5°C.
    3.  ESR > 40 mm/hr.
    4.  WBC count > 12 x 10^9/L.
* **Interpretation**: 3 or 4 criteria = >93% to 99% probability of septic arthritis.

### Bedside and Blood Investigations for Septic Arthritis
* **Bloods**: FBC (↑ WBC with left shift), ESR (↑↑), CRP (markedly ↑).
* **Blood Cultures**: **MANDATORY** before starting antibiotics (often +ve in haematogenous spread).

### Imaging Investigations for Septic Arthritis
* **Ultrasound (US) (超聲波)**: Best initial test. Highly sensitive for joint effusion (積液) (esp. hip). Used to guide needle aspiration.
* **X-ray**: Baseline essential to R/O fractures/tumours. Early stage: usually normal or shows widened joint space (effusion). Late stage: cartilage destruction/osteopenia.
* **MRI**: Excellent for concurrent osteomyelitis/soft tissue abscess, but requires sedation/GA in kids and takes time.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Test for Septic Arthritis
* **Urgent Joint Aspiration (Arthrocentesis) (關節穿刺術)**.
* Must be done urgently (US-guided or in theatre).
* **Synovial Fluid Analysis**:
    * Appearance: Purulent (pus).
    * Cell Count: WBC > 50,000 cells/mm³ (>90% neutrophils).
    * Microbiology: Gram stain +ve, send for culture & sensitivity.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Surgical Management of Septic Arthritis
* **Surgical Emergency**.
* **Urgent Joint Washout (Arthrotomy and Lavage) (關節切開灌洗術)**: Surgically open joint, copious saline washout, and leave drain.
* **Rationale**: Removes pus, ↓ intra-articular pressure, stops enzymatic destruction of cartilage.

### Acute Pharmacological Management of Septic Arthritis (HA Protocol)
* **Timing**: Start empiric IV Abx **immediately AFTER** joint aspiration & blood cultures.
* **Empiric 1st Line (Children >3 months)**: High-dose IV **Cloxacillin** (氯唑西林) or **Cefazolin** (頭孢唑林) to cover MSSA.
* **Empiric 1st Line (Neonates/<3 months or unimmunized)**: IV **Cloxacillin** + 3rd Gen Cephalosporin (e.g., IV **Cefotaxime** (頭孢噻肟)) to cover GBS, *E. coli*, and Hib.
* **Duration**: Total 3-4 weeks (IV initially until clinical improvement and falling CRP, then switch to high-dose PO).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement for Septic Arthritis
* **Paediatric Orthopaedics**: Primary managing team for arthrotomy/washout.
* **Paediatricians / ID**: Guide targeted antibiotic therapy based on cultures.
* **Physiotherapy (物理治療)**: Crucial post-operatively to regain ROM, strengthen muscles, and prevent joint contractures.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Considerations in Septic Arthritis
* Septic arthritis itself is rarely an NAI presentation.
* However, carefully examine for unexplainable skin abrasions, punctures, or occult trauma masking as infection.
* Take thorough history regarding prior trauma or "injections".

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Immediate and Long-Term Complications of Septic Arthritis
* **Irreversible Joint Destruction**: Occurs if treatment delayed by 24-48 hours → early severe osteoarthritis & permanent disability.
* **Avascular Necrosis (AVN) (缺血性壞死)**: Esp. femoral head (pus pressure occludes retinacular vessels).
* **Growth Arrest / Limb Length Discrepancy (肢體長度差異)**: If infection spreads to adjacent physis (growth plate).
* **Systemic**: Sepsis, osteomyelitis, systemic dissemination.

### Prognosis of Septic Arthritis
* Excellent **IF** diagnosed and surgically washed out within the first 24 hours. Delayed treatment correlates linearly with permanent morbidity.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: A 3-year-old presents with a high fever and refuses to walk. He holds his right hip flexed and externally rotated. Ultrasound shows a hip effusion. What is the definitive diagnostic and therapeutic next step? Answer: Urgent joint aspiration and surgical washout (arthrotomy) to confirm the diagnosis, remove the destructive pus, and relieve pressure.
* Viva Question: What are the four Kocher criteria used to assess the risk of septic arthritis in a child with a painful hip? Answer: Fever >38.5C, Non-weight bearing, ESR >40, WBC >12.
* Trap: Assuming a child with a limp and low-grade fever has septic arthritis without checking Kocher criteria. Pearl: Transient synovitis is far more common; use Kocher criteria and US to risk-stratify, but if in doubt, treat as septic arthritis until proven otherwise.
* Pitfall: Starting oral antibiotics for a "suspected infection" in a child with acute severe joint pain without performing a joint aspiration. This masks the symptoms, delays surgical washout, and guarantees permanent joint destruction.
