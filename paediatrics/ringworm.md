# Ringworm (Tinea) & Kerion (金錢癬及膿癬)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Tinea
* Superficial fungal infection of skin, hair, or nails
* Caused by **Dermatophytes (皮癬菌)**
* Primarily involves **Trichophyton (毛癬菌)** or **Microsporum (小孢癬菌)** species

### Hong Kong Epidemiology
* Extremely common in HK due to hot, humid climate
* Frequently acquired from infected pets (especially stray kittens, puppies, and guinea pigs) or contaminated soil
* Highly contagious → spreads rapidly via fomites in kindergartens & primary schools

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Mechanism of Fungal Invasion
* Dermatophytes possess **keratinase (角蛋白酶)**
* Fungi actively invade & consume **keratin (角蛋白)** in the stratum corneum, hair shafts, and nails
* Pathogens cannot survive in living tissue → strictly restricted to dead keratinized layers

### Host Immune Response & Lesion Morphology
* Centrifugal spread → fungi migrate outward to seek fresh keratin
* Center of lesion exhausts keratin supply → fungi die off → characteristic **central clearing (中央消退)**
* Localized delayed-type hypersensitivity reaction (Type IV) to fungal antigens → intense inflammation at the active, raised leading edge

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Typical Case Scenario
* 6-year-old boy presents following recent stray kitten adoption
* 3 highly itchy, red ring-shaped rashes on bilateral forearms
* Large bald patch on scalp that oozes pus
* Associated tender occipital lymphadenopathy

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Tinea Corporis (體癬) Signs
* Classic "Ringworm" appearance
* **Annular (ring-shaped) erythematous plaque (環狀紅斑斑塊)**
* Raised, active, red, scaly leading margin
* Visible central clearing
* Intensely **pruritic (痕癢)**

### Tinea Capitis (頭癬) Signs
* Patchy **alopecia (脫髮)** with visible scaling on scalp
* **"Black dot" appearance** → brittle hair shafts break off flush with scalp surface
* Prominent occipital or cervical **lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)**

### Kerion (膿癬) Signs
* Most severe inflammatory manifestation of Tinea Capitis
* Massive cell-mediated hypersensitivity reaction to fungal antigens
* Large, boggy, exquisitely tender, inflamed mass
* Studded with pustules → actively oozes purulent discharge
* Mimics a bacterial abscess but usually lacks severe systemic toxicity

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Tinea vs. Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹)
* PR: Single **Herald Patch (母斑)** → followed by massive diffuse truncal breakout along skin tension lines (Christmas tree pattern) → -ve KOH → self-resolving, does not respond to antifungals
* Tinea: Localized, asymmetric, distinct expanding rings → +ve KOH → responds completely to antifungals

### Kerion vs. Bacterial Scalp Abscess (細菌性膿腫)
* Abscess: Acute onset, extreme focal fluctuance, usually minimal hair loss initially, +ve for Staph aureus
* Kerion: Subacute onset, profound localized alopecia, numerous broken hairs, +ve for fungal hyphae

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Diagnostic Differentials
| Condition | Differentiating Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nummular Eczema (錢幣狀濕疹)** | Intensely itchy coin-shaped lesions, solid red plaques, **ZERO central clearing** |
| **Granuloma Annulare (環狀肉芽腫)** | Painless ring, completely lacks surface scale, deeper dermal pathology |
| **Alopecia Areata (斑禿)** | Autoimmune bald spots, beautifully smooth/flesh-colored scalp, **ZERO scaling or inflammation** |
| **Seborrheic Dermatitis (脂溢性皮炎)** | Diffuse greasy yellow scales, minimal hair loss, non-scarring |
| **Psoriasis (銀屑病)** | Thick silvery plaques, extensor surfaces, nail pitting |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **Wood's Lamp (伍德氏燈)**: UV light in dark room → some species (e.g., Microsporum canis) fluoresce bright apple-green. Note: Trichophyton species do NOT fluoresce.

### Gold Standard Special Investigations
* **Skin Scrapings / Plucked Hairs**: Must sample firmly from the active leading edge of the lesion
* **KOH Preparation Microscopy**: +ve for branching septate fungal hyphae
* **Fungal Culture (真菌培養)**: Sabouraud dextrose agar → takes 2-4 weeks for growth to identify exact species

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Tinea Corporis (Skin) Management
* **1st Line**: **Topical Antifungals (局部抗真菌藥)** (e.g., Clotrimazole 1%, Terbinafine 1%, Ketoconazole creams)
* Apply BD until 1-2 weeks AFTER rash completely disappears to eradicate spores and prevent relapse

### Tinea Capitis & Kerion Management (HA Protocol)
* **CRITICAL**: Topical creams DO NOT WORK for scalp disease (cannot penetrate deep hair follicles)
* **MUST GIVE Systemic Oral Antifungals (口服抗真菌藥)**
* **1st Line**: **Oral Griseofulvin (灰黃黴素)** for 6-8 weeks (HA preferred for pediatric scalp tinea). *Counseling: Take with a fatty meal (e.g., whole milk/ice cream) to ↑ GI absorption.*
* **2nd Line**: Oral Terbinafine (特比萘芬) for 4 weeks (Requires baseline LFTs)
* **Adjunctive Therapy**: Antifungal shampoo (e.g., Ketoconazole 2% or Selenium Sulfide 2.5%) twice weekly to ↓ spore shedding & ↓ transmission

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Family & Environmental Advice
* **Veterinary Check**: Advise parents to universally screen all household pets via vet. Asymptomatic pets = recurring vectors.
* **Infection Control**: Do not share combs, hats, towels, or pillows. Hot wash all bed linens.
* **School Exclusion**: Child can return to school once systemic/topical treatment is initiated.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Hygiene Neglect Red Flags
* Severe, widespread, untreated kerion with secondary myiasis (maggots) or overwhelming bacterial superinfection may indicate profound medical or hygienic neglect → refer MSW/SWD for family assessment.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Kerion Complications
* Unrecognized/Untreated Kerion → intense inflammation destroys hair follicles permanently
* Leads to irreversible **cicatricial (scarring) alopecia (疤痕性脫髮)** in a young child
* Secondary bacterial superinfection (Staph/Strep)
* Psychosocial distress/bullying due to permanent visible bald patches

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Giving a child with a boggy scalp mass (Kerion) a course of oral Flucloxacillin thinking it is a bacterial abscess, or giving them just topical Clotrimazole cream. Pearl: You must recognize it as deep fungal (Kerion) and prescribe ORAL antifungals to save their hair; never incise and drain.
* Trap: Prescribing combination "Steroid + Antifungal" creams (like Daktacort/Travocort) for undiagnosed red rings. Pearl: The steroid dampens the local immune response, allowing the fungus to grow wildly while suppressing the redness (Tinea Incognito/隱蔽癬).
* Viva Question: A child presents with a highly inflamed, boggy, pus-oozing mass on the scalp with severe hair loss over the area. What is the diagnosis and what is the MUST-GIVE route of medication? Answer: Diagnosis is a Kerion (severe Tinea Capitis). It MUST be treated with ORAL systemic antifungals to penetrate the hair follicles.
* Pitfall: Stopping topical antifungal treatment as soon as the Tinea Corporis rash fades. Counsel parents to continue topical antifungals for at least 1-2 weeks after clinical resolution to eradicate residual fungal elements and prevent relapse.
