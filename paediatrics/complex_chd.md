# Complex Congenital Heart Diseases (複雜性先天性心臟病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Complex Congenital Heart Diseases
* Structural cardiac malformations causing significant mixing of systemic/pulmonary blood flows
* Typically present with critical **Cyanosis (發紺)** or profound **Heart Failure (心臟衰竭)**
* Includes: **Ebstein's Anomaly (愛伯斯坦氏異常)**, **Truncus Arteriosus (動脈幹)**, **TAPVR (完全性肺靜脈異常回流)**, and **PAPVR (部分性肺靜脈異常回流)**

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Screening
* CHD incidence in HK: ~8 per 1000 live births
* Routine HK Department of Health (DH) prenatal screening: Morphology USG at 18-22 weeks detects major structural anomalies
* Postnatal screening: HA protocol mandates pre/post-ductal SpO2 screening prior to postnatal discharge to detect ductal-dependent lesions

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Ebstein's Anomaly
* Apical displacement of septal/posterior **Tricuspid Valve (三尖瓣)** leaflets
* → "Atrialization" of Right Ventricle (RV)
* → Severe Tricuspid Regurgitation (TR)
* → RV volume overload & functional hypoplasia
* Right-to-left shunt via ASD/PFO → **Cyanosis (發紺)**

### Genetics & Risk Factors of Ebstein's Anomaly
* Maternal Lithium exposure during 1st trimester
* Sporadic mutations (MYH7)

### Pathophysiology of Truncus Arteriosus
* Failure of truncoconal septation
* → Single great vessel overriding a large VSD
* → Supplies systemic, pulmonary, and coronary circulations
* → Pulmonary overcirculation & early **Heart Failure (心臟衰竭)**

### Genetics of Truncus Arteriosus
* Strongly associated with 22q11.2 microdeletion → **DiGeorge Syndrome (迪喬治症候群)** (~30-40% of cases)

### Pathophysiology of TAPVR
* All 4 pulmonary veins fail to connect to Left Atrium (LA)
* → Drain to Right Atrium (RA) via systemic veins (SVC/IVC/Coronary Sinus)
* Obligatory Right-to-Left shunt via ASD/PFO for survival
* Pathophysiology heavily depends on presence of venous obstruction

### Subtypes of TAPVR
* Supracardiac (Most common, ~50%): Drains to SVC
* Cardiac (~20%): Drains to Coronary Sinus
* Infracardiac (~20%): Drains to IVC/Portal vein → **Highest risk of obstruction**
* Mixed (~10%)

### Pathophysiology of PAPVR
* 1-3 pulmonary veins drain to RA/systemic veins
* → Left-to-Right shunt (similar to ASD)
* Strongly associated with Sinus Venosus ASD

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Signs of Ebstein's Anomaly
* Severe **Cyanosis (發紺)** (if severe TR + ASD right-to-left shunt)
* Massive cardiomegaly (often visible precordial bulge)
* Holosystolic murmur at Left Lower Sternal Border (LLSB)
* Multiple heart sounds (triple/quadruple rhythm)
* Hepatomegaly

### Clinical Signs of Truncus Arteriosus
* Mild **Cyanosis (發紺)**
* Tachypnoea & respiratory distress
* Failure to thrive
* Single loud S2
* Systolic ejection click
* Bounding peripheral pulses (due to diastolic runoff)

### Clinical Signs of Obstructed TAPVR (e.g., Infracardiac)
* Presents hours after birth
* Profound **Cyanosis (發紺)** unresponsive to O2
* Severe **Respiratory Distress (呼吸窘迫)**
* Poor perfusion / shock
* Often NO significant murmur

### Clinical Signs of Unobstructed TAPVR
* Presents weeks/months later
* Mild **Cyanosis (發紺)**
* Recurrent chest infections
* Right ventricular heave
* Fixed split S2 + systolic ejection murmur (flow across pulmonary valve)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### CXR Discriminators for Complex CHDs
| Condition | Classic CXR Appearance | Pathophysiological Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ebstein's Anomaly** | Massive "Wall-to-Wall" heart | Huge RA + atrialized RV |
| **Truncus Arteriosus** | Cardiomegaly + ↑ Pulmonary markings + Right Aortic Arch (~30%) | Pulmonary overcirculation + associated arch anomaly |
| **Obstructed TAPVR** | Small heart + Severe Pulmonary Edema | Venous backup into lungs without cardiomegaly |
| **Unobstructed TAPVR** | "Snowman" sign (Figure of 8) + ↑ Pulmonary markings | Dilated vertical vein & SVC (Only visible in older infants >4 months) |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Neonatal Cyanosis Differentials
| Category | Key Differentials | Distinguishing Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cardiac (Right-to-Left Shunts)** | Transposition of Great Arteries (TGA), Tetralogy of Fallot (ToF), Tricuspid Atresia | Fails Hyperoxia Test; Abnormal Echocardiogram |
| **Pulmonary** | Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS), Meconium Aspiration, Pneumothorax | Passes Hyperoxia Test (usually); Abnormal lung fields on CXR |
| **Vascular** | Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension of Newborn (PPHN) | >10% pre/post-ductal SpO2 difference |
| **Haematologic** | Methaemoglobinaemia | "Chocolate brown" blood; normal PaO2 with ↓ SpO2 |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Bedside Investigations
* Pre-ductal (Right Hand) vs Post-ductal (Either Foot) SpO2
* Hyperoxia Test (100% O2 for 10 mins → PaO2 fails to rise >150 mmHg suggests cardiac cyanosis)
* 4-limb Blood Pressure (rule out coarctation)
* 12-lead ECG (Ebstein's: Giant P waves, RBBB, WPW syndrome common)

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigations
* **Echocardiogram (超聲心動圖)**: Definitive diagnosis, defines anatomy, valve regurgitation, and shunts
* Chest X-Ray (CXR): Assesses cardiomegaly and pulmonary vascular markings

### Advanced Pre-Surgical Investigations
* Cardiac CT / MRI (Defines complex TAPVR/PAPVR anatomy)
* Cardiac Catheterization (Rarely needed for diagnosis now; used for pre-op pulmonary vascular resistance mapping)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Resuscitation Protocol
* Intubation and mechanical ventilation for severe distress
* HA Protocol for suspected ductal-dependent lesions: Initiate **Prostaglandin E1 (前列腺素E1)** (Alprostadil) infusion immediately (0.01 - 0.05 mcg/kg/min)
* Correct acidosis (NaHCO3) and hypoglycaemia
* Avoid 100% O2 in Truncus Arteriosus (↓ PVR → worsens pulmonary flooding/steal)

### Specific Surgical Management
* **Ebstein's Anomaly:**
  * Cone procedure (tricuspid valve repair)
  * ASD closure
  * Glenn/Fontan pathway if RV too hypoplastic
* **Truncus Arteriosus:**
  * Rastelli repair (VSD closure + RV-to-PA conduit) in neonatal period
* **TAPVR:**
  * Obstructed: EMERGENCY surgical re-anastomosis of common pulmonary vein to LA
  * Unobstructed: Elective repair at 1-6 months
* **PAPVR:**
  * Surgical baffling of anomalous vein to LA + ASD closure

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Structure
* Paediatric Cardiology (Diagnosis, Echo, Follow-up)
* Cardiothoracic Surgery (Definitive intervention)
* Paediatric Intensive Care (PICU) (Pre/Post-op stabilization)
* Clinical Genetics (22q11.2 screening for Truncus)

### Local Psychosocial Support
* Medical Social Worker (MSW): Assess for HA Samaritan Fund (撒瑪利亞基金) for expensive interventions or specific medical equipment
* Children's Heart Foundation (HK): Peer support, play therapy, and parental counseling

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect Indicators in Cardiac Care
* Repeated "Did Not Attend" (DNA) for crucial HA Cardiology follow-ups
* Failure to administer anti-failure medications (e.g., Diuretics, ACE inhibitors) leading to preventable decompensation
* Poor hygiene or faltering growth unexplained by the cardiac pathophysiology alone
* Parental resistance to life-saving surgical intervention without valid cultural/religious justification

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-Term Complications of Complex CHDs
* **Arrhythmias (心律不整):** High risk of SVT/WPW in Ebstein's; post-surgical scar-related VT
* **Heart Failure (心臟衰竭):** Chronic RV failure in Ebstein's; conduit failure in Rastelli
* Need for re-operation (Conduits in Rastelli repair do not grow with the child and require replacement)
* Pulmonary Vein Stenosis (Devastating complication post-TAPVR repair)
* Endocarditis (Requires lifetime antibiotic prophylaxis for dental procedures in some prosthetic repairs)

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic CXR finding for supracardiac TAPVR and when does it appear? Answer: The "snowman" sign (figure-of-8), which typically only becomes visible after 4-6 months of age as the vertical vein dilates.
* Viva Question: Which complex CHD is classically associated with Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) syndrome? Answer: Ebstein's Anomaly.
* Trap: Providing 100% oxygen to stabilize a neonate with Truncus Arteriosus in heart failure. Pearl: 100% oxygen is a potent pulmonary vasodilator. It will drastically drop pulmonary vascular resistance, worsening pulmonary overcirculation and stealing blood from the systemic circulation, leading to systemic shock. Keep saturations 75-85%.
* Trap: Delaying surgery for a neonate with infracardiac TAPVR to let them "grow and optimize". Pearl: Infracardiac TAPVR is almost always obstructed as it passes through the diaphragm/portal system. It is a surgical emergency requiring immediate intervention.
* Pitfall: Stopping Prostaglandin E1 infusion when a diagnosis of TAPVR is made. While TAPVR does not rely on the ductus arteriosus, if pulmonary venous return is highly obstructed, maintaining a patent ductus arteriosus can provide a pop-off for right-sided pressures; always consult pediatric cardiology before stopping.
