# Neonatal Jaundice (初生嬰兒黃疸)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Neonatal Jaundice
* **Yellowish discoloration** of skin, sclera, and mucous membranes
* Due to tissue deposition of bilirubin (hyperbilirubinaemia)
* Clinically visible cephalocaudally when **Total Serum Bilirubin (TSB) (總膽紅素)** > 85 µmol/L

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Neonatal Jaundice
* Extremely common in **Hong Kong (香港)**
* ~60-80% of Chinese newborns develop visible jaundice
* **G6PD Deficiency (葡萄糖六磷酸去氫酵素缺乏症)**: Highly prevalent in HK (4.5% males, 0.5% females) → routine cord blood screening
* **ABO Incompatibility (ABO血型不合)**: Common local cause of haemolytic pathological jaundice

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Physiological Jaundice
* ↑ Bilirubin load: Shorter RBC lifespan in neonates (70-90 days)
* ↓ Bilirubin clearance: Immature hepatic **UGT1A1 enzyme** activity → ↓ conjugation
* ↑ Enterohepatic circulation: Sterile gut & ↑ beta-glucuronidase in breast milk

### Pathophysiology of Pathological Unconjugated Hyperbilirubinaemia
* **↑ RBC Destruction (Haemolysis)**:
  * Immune: ABO/Rh incompatibility
  * Non-immune: G6PD deficiency, **Hereditary Spherocytosis (遺傳性球形紅血球增多症)**
* **↑ RBC Load**: Polycythaemia, cephalhaematoma, extensive bruising
* **↓ Conjugation**: Crigler-Najjar syndrome, Gilbert syndrome, Congenital Hypothyroidism
* **↑ Enterohepatic Circulation**: Breastfeeding jaundice (dehydration), bowel obstruction

### Pathophysiology of Conjugated Hyperbilirubinaemia
* Defined as conjugated fraction >20% of TSB
* ALWAYS pathological
* Causes: **Biliary Atresia (膽道閉鎖)**, Neonatal Hepatitis (TORCH), Choledochal cyst

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Onset Timing as a Diagnostic Clue
| Onset Time | Clinical Significance | Common Causes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **< 24 hours** | ALWAYS pathological | Haemolysis (ABO/Rh, G6PD), Sepsis (TORCH) |
| **24-72 hours** | Usually physiological | Physiological, Early onset breast milk/pathological |
| **> 72 hours to 2 weeks** | Late onset | Breast milk jaundice, Sepsis, Cephalhaematoma |
| **> 2 weeks (Prolonged)** | Needs investigation | Biliary atresia, Hypothyroidism, UTI, Breast milk |

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Physical Signs of Jaundice
* **Cephalocaudal Progression (從頭到腳的發展)**: Progresses head → toes
* **Kramer's Rule**: 
  * Zone 1 (head/neck) ~100 µmol/L
  * Zone 5 (palms/soles) >250 µmol/L

### Signs of Dehydration (Breastfeeding Jaundice)
* Sunken fontanelle
* Dry mucous membranes
* Poor skin turgor
* < 6 wet nappies/day
* Weight loss >10% of birth weight

### Signs of Acute Bilirubin Encephalopathy (ABE)
* **Early**: Lethargy, poor feeding, high-pitched cry
* **Late (Kernicterus)**: Opisthotonos, hypertonia, seizures, fever

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Breastfeeding Jaundice vs Breast Milk Jaundice
| Feature | Breastfeeding Jaundice | Breast Milk Jaundice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Early (1st week) | Late (peaks @ 2 weeks) |
| **Pathophysiology** | Suboptimal intake → dehydration → delayed meconium → ↑ enterohepatic circulation | Breast milk components (e.g. beta-glucuronidase) → ↑ enterohepatic circulation |
| **Clinical Status** | Dehydrated, weight loss >10%, poor output | Thriving, normal weight gain, normal output |

### Unconjugated vs Conjugated Jaundice
| Feature | Unconjugated Jaundice | Conjugated Jaundice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stool Colour** | Normal (yellow/green) | **Pale/Acholic (灰白大便)** |
| **Urine Colour** | Normal (clear/light yellow) | **Dark/Tea-coloured (茶色小便)** |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentials for Unconjugated Jaundice
* Physiological jaundice
* Breast milk / Breastfeeding jaundice
* G6PD deficiency
* ABO / Rh incompatibility
* Cephalhaematoma
* Sepsis / UTI
* Congenital Hypothyroidism

### Differentials for Conjugated Jaundice
* **Biliary Atresia (膽道閉鎖)**
* Neonatal Hepatitis (CMV, Rubella, Idiopathic)
* Alpha-1 Antitrypsin deficiency
* Galactosaemia
* Choledochal cyst

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **Transcutaneous Bilirubinometry (TcB)**: Non-invasive screening tool
* **Stool Colour Card (大便顏色卡)**: DH protocol for biliary atresia screening

### Routine Bloods (Jaundice Screen)
* Total and Direct Bilirubin (SBR)
* Maternal & infant blood group + Rh status
* **Direct Antiglobulin Test (DAT / Coombs test)**
* Complete Blood Count (CBC) + Reticulocyte count
* G6PD screening (Routine cord blood in HK)

### Prolonged Jaundice Screen (>14 days term, >21 days preterm)
* Split bilirubin (Total & Direct)
* Liver Function Tests (LFTs)
* Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs)
* Urine routine & culture (rule out UTI)

### Imaging Studies
* **Ultrasound Abdomen**: Look for biliary tree abnormalities, choledochal cyst, absent gallbladder (Biliary atresia)

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### HA Protocol for Jaundice Monitoring
* Plot TSB on **NICE / AAP Bilirubin Nomogram**
* Adjust threshold based on: Gestational age, hours of life, presence of risk factors (e.g., sepsis, haemolysis)

### 1st Line Management: Phototherapy (光照治療)
* **Indication**: TSB crosses phototherapy threshold
* **Mechanism**: Photoisomerization of unconjugated bilirubin → water-soluble **lumirubin** → excreted in urine/bile
* **Nursing Care**: Eye protection, maximal skin exposure, monitor temperature/hydration

### 2nd Line Management: Exchange Transfusion (換血治療)
* **Indications**: 
  * TSB ≥ 85 µmol/L above phototherapy line
  * Signs of ABE
  * Rapidly rising TSB despite intensive phototherapy
* **Mechanism**: Removes bilirubin & maternal antibody-coated RBCs

### Pharmacological Therapy
* **IVIG**: Consider in isoimmune haemolytic disease (ABO/Rh) if TSB rising rapidly to avoid exchange transfusion

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) Follow-up
* Routine Day 3-5 checks in HK for jaundice & weight
* Uses TcB screening; refers to HA Paediatric A&E/Ward if > threshold

### Lactation Support
* **Lactation Consultants**: Correct latch, optimize milk supply, prevent breastfeeding jaundice
* Educate mothers to continue breastfeeding even in breast milk jaundice

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect Indicators
* Delayed presentation to medical care despite severe jaundice/lethargy
* Non-compliance with MCHC follow-up / missing scheduled TcB checks
* Inconsistent history regarding feeding frequency and urine/stool output

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Kernicterus (Chronic Bilirubin Encephalopathy) (核黃疸)
* **Pathophysiology**: Permanent neurological damage from unconjugated bilirubin deposition in basal ganglia & brainstem
* **Clinical Features**: 
  * Choreoathetoid cerebral palsy
  * Sensorineural hearing loss
  * Upward gaze palsy
  * Dental enamel dysplasia

### Prognosis
* Excellent if treated promptly
* Physiological & breast milk jaundice resolve without sequelae
* Conjugated jaundice (e.g., Biliary Atresia) requires early Kasai procedure (< 60 days) to prevent liver failure

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Pitfall: Never ignore jaundice in the first 24 hours. It is haemolysis or sepsis until proven otherwise.
* Trap: Assuming all late-onset jaundice is breast milk jaundice. Pearl: Always do a prolonged jaundice screen (>14 days) to rule out Biliary Atresia and Hypothyroidism.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate breastfeeding from breast milk jaundice? Answer: Check weight gain and hydration status; breastfeeding jaundice has >10% weight loss and dehydration, breast milk jaundice has normal weight gain.
* Viva Question: What is the stool colour in biliary atresia? Answer: Pale/acholic. Mention the DH stool colour card given to all parents in HK.
* Trap: Stopping breastfeeding for breast milk jaundice. Pearl: Reassure the mother and encourage continued breastfeeding; the jaundice is benign and self-limiting.
* Pitfall: Discharging a baby with cephalhaematoma without early follow-up. Cephalhaematoma breakdown significantly increases bilirubin load, requiring close monitoring.
