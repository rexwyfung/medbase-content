# Paediatric Hypertension (小兒高血壓)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Paediatric Hypertension
* **Hypertension (HTN)**: BP ≥95th percentile for age, sex, & height on 3 separate occasions.
* **Normal BP**: <90th percentile.
* **Elevated BP (Pre-hypertension)**: 90th-95th percentile or ≥120/80 mmHg (whichever is lower).
* **Stage 1 HTN**: 95th to 99th percentile + 5 mmHg.
* **Stage 2 HTN**: >99th percentile + 5 mmHg.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* ↑ prevalence in HK parallel to ↑ childhood **Obesity (肥胖)**.
* **Primary HTN**: Increasingly common in adolescents.
* **Secondary HTN**: Predominates in prepubertal/young children; the younger the child & higher the BP, the more likely it is secondary.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Primary (Essential) Hypertension
* Most common in adolescents.
* Strongly associated with:
  * Obesity / **Metabolic Syndrome (代謝綜合症)**
  * +ve Family history
  * ↑ Salt intake
  * Sedentary lifestyle

### Secondary Hypertension (繼發性高血壓)
* **Renal Parenchymal Disease (70-80%)**:
  * **Glomerulonephritis (腎小球腎炎)** (e.g., APSGN, IgA nephropathy)
  * **Reflux Nephropathy (返流性腎病)** / Renal scarring
  * **Polycystic Kidney Disease (多囊腎)**
* **Renovascular Disease**:
  * **Renal Artery Stenosis (腎動脈狹窄)** (e.g., Fibromuscular dysplasia, Neurofibromatosis Type 1)
* **Cardiovascular**:
  * **Coarctation of the Aorta (主動脈縮窄)**
* **Endocrine**:
  * **Phaeochromocytoma (嗜鉻細胞瘤)**
  * **Cushing's Syndrome (庫茲綜合症)**
  * **Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (先天性腎上腺增生)**
  * **Hyperthyroidism (甲狀腺機能亢進)**
* **Medications/Toxins**:
  * Corticosteroids
  * Stimulants (e.g., Methylphenidate for ADHD)
  * Oral contraceptives
  * Illicit drugs (e.g., Cocaine)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Common Symptoms
* Often asymptomatic ("silent killer").
* Severe HTN symptoms:
  * **Headache (頭痛)**
  * **Epistaxis (鼻出血)**
  * Visual changes / Blurred vision
  * Dizziness / Tinnitus
  * Poor school performance / Irritability
  * **Seizures (抽搐)** / Encephalopathy (in hypertensive emergencies)

### Signs of Underlying Causes
* **Coarctation of Aorta**:
  * Radio-femoral delay
  * Weak/absent femoral pulses
  * BP right arm > lower limbs
  * Ejection systolic murmur
* **Renal Disease**:
  * **Oedema (水腫)**
  * **Haematuria (血尿)**
  * Palpable abdominal mass (Polycystic kidneys / Wilms tumour)
* **Endocrine**:
  * Cushingoid facies, truncal obesity, striae (Cushing's)
  * Tachycardia, tremor, goitre (Hyperthyroidism)
  * Episodic sweating, palpitations, pallor (Phaeochromocytoma)
  * Virilisation / Ambiguous genitalia (CAH)
* **Neurofibromatosis Type 1 (NF1)**:
  * Café-au-lait spots (>6)
  * Neurofibromas

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Primary vs Secondary Hypertension
| Feature | Primary HTN | Secondary HTN |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Age** | Adolescents (>12 yrs) | Infants & pre-pubertal children |
| **Severity** | Mild-Moderate (Stage 1) | Severe (Stage 2) |
| **Obesity** | Often +ve | Usually -ve |
| **Family Hx** | Often +ve for HTN/CVD | Usually -ve (except genetic renal diseases) |

### White Coat Hypertension (白袍高血壓)
* BP >95th percentile in clinic BUT normal at home or on **24-hr Ambulatory BP Monitoring (ABPM)**.
* No target organ damage.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Most Likely Diagnoses
* **Primary hypertension**: Obese adolescent.
* **Renal parenchymal disease**: Young child, +ve urinalysis.

### Must-Not-Miss Diagnoses (Red Flags)
* **Hypertensive Emergency (高血壓急症)**: Severe HTN + acute target organ damage (e.g., encephalopathy causing seizures/coma, heart failure, acute kidney injury).
* **Coarctation of the Aorta**: Critical congenital heart disease missed in infancy.
* **Phaeochromocytoma**: High risk of sudden fatal hypertensive crisis.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* **Accurate BP Measurement**: 
  * Use correct cuff size (bladder width = 40% of arm circumference, length = 80-100%).
  * Measure in all 4 limbs once → r/o Coarctation.
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: Dipstick for protein & blood → screen for glomerulonephritis.
* **Fundoscopy**: Look for hypertensive retinopathy (flame haemorrhages, papilloedema).

### Blood Investigations
* **Renal & Metabolic Screen**:
  * U&E, Creatinine, eGFR → Assess renal function.
  * Fasting lipid profile, HbA1c, Fasting glucose → Screen for metabolic syndrome.
* **Endocrine Screen** (if indicated):
  * Thyroid function tests (TFTs).
  * Plasma free metanephrines → Phaeochromocytoma.
  * Renin/Aldosterone ratio.

### Imaging Investigations
* **Renal Ultrasound with Doppler (腎臟超聲波及多普勒)**:
  * Assess renal size, scarring, cysts, asymmetry.
  * Doppler flow → check for renal artery stenosis.
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**:
  * Assess for **Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH)** → Target organ damage.
  * R/o Coarctation of Aorta.

### Gold Standard Investigation
* **24-hour Ambulatory BP Monitoring (ABPM) (24小時動態血壓監測)**:
  * Confirms diagnosis & rules out white-coat HTN.
  * Assesses "nocturnal dipping" (lack of BP dip during sleep strongly suggests secondary HTN).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management: Hypertensive Emergency
* **Admission**: PICU setting.
* **Principle**: ↓ BP gradually. Dropping BP too fast → cerebral ischaemia/infarction.
* **Target**: ↓ BP by max 25% of planned reduction in first 8 hrs, normalize over 24-48 hrs.
* **IV Pharmacotherapy**:
  * **Labetalol (拉貝洛爾)** IV infusion (1st line in HA).
  * **Sodium Nitroprusside (硝普鈉)** IV infusion.

### Chronic Management: Primary HTN
* **Lifestyle Modification (生活方式改變)** (1st Line):
  * Weight loss / BMI optimization.
  * **DASH diet**: ↓ Sodium, ↑ Potassium, ↑ Fruit/Veg.
  * Regular aerobic exercise (30-60 mins, 3-5 days/wk).
  * Avoid smoking, alcohol, caffeine.

### Chronic Management: Secondary / Symptomatic HTN
* **Indications for Pharmacotherapy**: Secondary HTN, symptomatic, target organ damage (LVH), failed lifestyle mod.
* **1st Line Pharmacotherapy**:
  * **ACE Inhibitors (ACEI)** (e.g., **Enalapril 依那普利**): Renoprotective, 1st line for proteinuria/diabetes.
  * **Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARB)** (e.g., Losartan).
  * **Calcium Channel Blockers (CCB)** (e.g., **Amlodipine 氨氯地平**): Good alternative.
* **Contraindications**:
  * ACEI/ARB strictly **CONTRAINDICATED** in Bilateral Renal Artery Stenosis.
* **Surgical Management**:
  * Balloon angioplasty/stenting for Renal Artery Stenosis.
  * Surgical repair for Coarctation of Aorta.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Approach
* **Dietitian (營養師)**: Crucial for primary HTN; weight management, DASH diet education.
* **Paediatric Nephrologist/Cardiologist**: For specialized workup of secondary causes.
* **Student Health Service (MCHC) / School Nurse**: Routine BP monitoring & lifestyle promotion.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Compliance & Medical Neglect
* **Medical Neglect**: Failure of caregivers to administer lifelong antihypertensives or attend follow-ups → risk of fatal target organ damage.
* **Severe Obesity**: Early-onset morbid obesity causing severe HTN may warrant MSW/SWD review if parents actively ignore medical/dietetic advice leading to severe harm.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Target Organ Damage (靶器官損害)
* **Cardiac**: Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH), early Coronary Artery Disease, Heart Failure.
* **Neurological**: Hypertensive encephalopathy, Stroke (CVA).
* **Renal**: Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD).
* **Ophthalmic**: Hypertensive retinopathy.

### Prognosis
* **Primary HTN**: Excellent if adherence to lifestyle modifications and weight loss is achieved.
* **Secondary HTN**: Depends on reversibility of underlying cause & compliance with pharmacotherapy.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Starting an ACE inhibitor in a child with a renal bruit.
* Pearl: A bruit suggests Renal Artery Stenosis. ACE inhibitors are contraindicated in bilateral renal artery stenosis as they precipitate acute renal failure by removing the efferent arteriolar constriction needed to maintain GFR.
* Viva Question: What is the most important physical examination step when you find high blood pressure in a child's arm?
* Answer: Measure the blood pressure in the legs and palpate the femoral pulses simultaneously with the radial pulse to rule out Coarctation of the Aorta.
* Pitfall: Using an inappropriately small BP cuff. This will falsely elevate the BP reading, leading to a misdiagnosis of hypertension. Always ensure the bladder width covers at least 40% of the arm circumference.
* Viva Question: What finding on 24-hour ABPM is highly suggestive of secondary hypertension?
* Answer: A loss of normal "nocturnal dipping" (normally BP drops by >10% during sleep).
