# Von Willebrand Disease (血管性血友病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Von Willebrand Disease
* Most common inherited bleeding disorder (遺傳性出血疾病)
* Caused by quantitative deficiency or qualitative defect in **Von Willebrand Factor (vWF)**
* Results in defective primary haemostasis (primary platelet plug formation) & secondary haemostasis (FVIII degradation)

### Local Epidemiology & Paediatric Context in Hong Kong
* Prevalence: ~1% of general population (most asymptomatic)
* HA Paediatric clinics: Often presents in childhood with recurrent **epistaxis (鼻出血)** or following dental extractions
* Frequently misdiagnosed/delayed diagnosis until menarche → severe **menorrhagia (月經量過多)** in adolescent girls

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Normal Physiology of Von Willebrand Factor (vWF)
* Synthesized in endothelial cells (Weibel-Palade bodies) & megakaryocytes (α-granules)
* **Function 1 (Primary Haemostasis):** Mediates platelet adhesion to subendothelial collagen via **Glycoprotein Ib (GpIb)** receptor
* **Function 2 (Secondary Haemostasis):** Carrier protein for **Factor VIII (FVIII)** → prevents rapid clearance/degradation of FVIII in circulation

### Classification & Genetics of Von Willebrand Disease
| Type | Incidence | Defect | Inheritance | vWF Level | FVIII Level | Clinical Severity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type 1** | ~75-80% | Partial quantitative deficiency | Autosomal Dominant (AD) | ↓ | ↓ or Normal | Mild to Moderate |
| **Type 2** | ~15-20% | Qualitative functional defect | AD (mostly) | Normal or ↓ | ↓ or Normal | Moderate |
| **Type 3** | <1% | Complete quantitative absence | Autosomal Recessive (AR) | Undetectable | ↓↓ (Very Low) | Severe (Haemophilia-like) |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Hallmark Clinical Presentation of Von Willebrand Disease
* Presentation dominated by **mucocutaneous bleeding (黏膜皮膚出血)**
* **Epistaxis (鼻出血):** Spontaneous, prolonged >10 mins, requiring medical attention
* **Menorrhagia (月經量過多):** Heavy menstrual bleeding, often leading to anaemia in adolescent girls
* **Gingival bleeding (牙齦出血):** Especially post-dental extraction
* Easy bruising, **ecchymoses (瘀斑)** out of proportion to trauma
* Prolonged bleeding post-minor surgery/tonsillectomy
* **Haemarthrosis (關節積血) / Muscle haematomas:** RARE (seen ONLY in severe Type 3 vWD due to markedly ↓ FVIII)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Von Willebrand Disease vs Haemophilia A
* **Inheritance:** vWD = AD/AR (affects boys & girls) vs Haemophilia = X-linked recessive (almost exclusively boys)
* **Bleeding Pattern:** vWD = Mucocutaneous (petechiae/epistaxis) vs Haemophilia = Deep tissue/joint (haemarthrosis/muscle haematomas)
* **Coagulation Profile:** vWD = Normal/prolonged APTT + ↑ Bleeding Time vs Haemophilia = Prolonged APTT + Normal Bleeding Time

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Childhood Bleeding Disorders
| Condition | Pathophysiology | Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Haemophilia A/B (血友病)** | FVIII / FIX deficiency | Males only, deep joint bleeds, isolated ↑ APTT |
| **Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP) (免疫性血小板減少症)** | Autoimmune platelet destruction | ↓↓ Platelet count, post-viral onset, normal coag profile |
| **Bernard-Soulier Syndrome (巨大血小板綜合症)** | GpIb deficiency | Giant platelets on smear, AR inheritance |
| **Glanzmann Thrombasthenia (血小板無力症)** | GpIIb/IIIa deficiency | Defective platelet aggregation, AR inheritance |
| **Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) (非意外傷害/虐兒)** | Physical abuse | Atypical bruise locations, inconsistent history, normal coag profile |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Screening Investigations for Bleeding Disorders
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC):** Normal platelet count (may be ↓ in Type 2B vWD) & microcytic hypochromic indices if iron deficient
* **Blood Smear (血塗片):** Normal morphology
* **Prothrombin Time (PT):** Normal
* **Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (APTT):** Normal or ↑ (prolonged ONLY if FVIII significantly ↓)
* **PFA-100 (Platelet Function Assay):** Prolonged closure time (replaces obsolete Bleeding Time test)

### Second-Line Diagnostic Panel for Von Willebrand Disease
* **vWF Antigen (vWF:Ag):** Measures total quantity of vWF protein (↓ in Type 1/3)
* **vWF Ristocetin Cofactor Activity (vWF:RCo):** Measures functional activity of vWF (↓ in all types; vWF:RCo to vWF:Ag ratio <0.7 suggests Type 2)
* **FVIII Coagulant Activity (FVIII:C):** Measures secondary haemostasis support (↓ or normal)

### Third-Line Specialist Investigations
* **vWF Multimer Analysis:** Gel electrophoresis to visualize size distribution of vWF multimers (differentiates Type 2 subtypes)
* **RIPA (Ristocetin-Induced Platelet Aggregation):** ↑ at low ristocetin doses in Type 2B
* **Genetic Testing:** Molecular characterization (useful for Type 2/3 and family screening)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### General Preventive Management
* Absolute avoidance of **NSAIDs (非類固醇消炎止痛藥)** & Aspirin → use Paracetamol
* Avoid intramuscular (IM) injections → give vaccines deep subcutaneously (SC)
* Strict dental hygiene to prevent extractions

### Acute Bleeding Management Protocol (HA Guidelines)
* **Local Haemostatic Measures:** Firm pressure, ice, topical thrombin
* **Antifibrinolytics:** **Tranexamic acid (氨甲環酸)** PO/IV/Mouthwash (Highly effective for mucosal/dental bleeds)
* **Desmopressin (DDAVP) (去氨加壓素):** 
    * 1st line for Type 1 vWD
    * Administered SC, IV, or Intranasal
    * Mechanism: Induces rapid release of endogenous vWF & FVIII from endothelial Weibel-Palade bodies
    * *Prerequisite:* Must document response via "DDAVP trial" beforehand
    * *Side effects:* Tachyphylaxis (diminished response after 3-4 doses), fluid retention, hyponatraemia (fluid restrict for 24h)
* **vWF/FVIII Concentrates (e.g., Haemate-P, Alphanate):**
    * Required for Type 3, most Type 2, severe bleeds, or major surgery
    * Derived from human plasma (virally inactivated)

### Chronic Management of Menorrhagia in Adolescents
* 1st line: **Tranexamic acid (氨甲環酸)** during menses
* 2nd line: Combined Oral Contraceptive Pill (COCP) or Progesterone-only pill
* 3rd line: Levonorgestrel Intrauterine System (**Mirena IUS**)

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Integration for vWD
* **Paediatric Haematologist (兒童血液科醫生):** Lead coordinator, maintains local bleeding disorder registry
* **Paediatric Gynaecologist:** Crucial for adolescent menorrhagia management to prevent school absenteeism
* **Dental Surgeon:** Pre-procedure planning (TXA cover ± DDAVP)
* **Clinical Geneticist:** Genetic counselling for prospective parents, especially in Type 3 (AR) families

### Psychosocial & Educational Support
* Provide HA explicit "Bleeding Disorder Alert Card"
* School Care Plan: Protocols for PE teachers regarding contact sports & managing epistaxis on campus
* Education: Instruct parents on home administration of TXA or intranasal DDAVP

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Differentiating vWD from Non-Accidental Injury (NAI)
* **Crucial Rule:** Always rule out coagulopathies (including mild vWD) before concluding bruising is due to child abuse
* **Red Flags for NAI (despite vWD diagnosis):** 
    * Bruising in non-mobile infants (TEN-4 rule: Torso, Ears, Neck in <4 months)
    * Patterned bruises (e.g., handprints, belt marks)
    * Delays in seeking medical care
    * Inconsistent mechanisms of injury provided by caregivers

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Common Complications of vWD
* **Iron Deficiency Anaemia (IDA) (缺鐵性貧血):** Extremely common in paediatric females secondary to menorrhagia → requires oral/IV iron replacement
* Severe post-operative/post-partum haemorrhage if unrecognized
* Joint damage (Arthropathy): RARE, isolated to Type 3

### Long-Term Prognosis
* Excellent for Type 1 & 2; normal life expectancy
* Type 3 requires rigorous prophylaxis but generally achieves good quality of life with specialized care

## 11. Examiner's Pearls

* Viva Question: Why might the APTT be prolonged in Von Willebrand Disease? Answer: Because vWF acts as a protective carrier protein for Factor VIII; without vWF, FVIII rapidly degrades, mimicking a mild Haemophilia A pattern.
* Viva Question: How does Desmopressin (DDAVP) work in vWD? Answer: It stimulates the V2 receptors on endothelial cells, causing exocytosis of Weibel-Palade bodies and a rapid, transient release of stored vWF and FVIII into the plasma.
* Trap: Prescribing DDAVP for a patient with Type 2B vWD. Pearl: DDAVP is contraindicated in Type 2B because releasing more of the mutant, hyper-adhesive vWF can cause spontaneous platelet clumping and exacerbate thrombocytopenia.
* Pitfall: Recommending unlimited DDAVP doses for prolonged bleeding. Tachyphylaxis occurs after 3-4 days of continuous use due to depletion of endothelial vWF stores.
