# Arthrogryposis Multiplex Congenita (先天性多發性關節攣縮症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Arthrogryposis
* Descriptive term, not a single disease entity
* Defined by presence of multiple, non-progressive joint contractures (關節攣縮)
* Present at birth (congenital)
* Must affect ≥2 different areas of the body

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Incidence: Occurs in ~1 in 3000 live births globally and locally

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Core Pathophysiology (Ultimate Common Pathway)
* Primary mechanism: **Fetal akinesia** (胎兒運動不能) / ↓ fetal movement in utero
* Normal joint development requires movement
* ↓ movement → lack of joint stretching → excessive peri-articular connective tissue deposition → fibrosis → contractures

### Neurological & Muscular Causes
* **Neurological**:
  * **Spinal Muscular Atrophy (脊髓性肌肉萎縮症)** (e.g., SMA type 0)
  * CNS malformations (e.g., holoprosencephaly)
* **Muscular**:
  * Congenital muscular dystrophies
  * Congenital myopathies

### Mechanical & Connective Tissue Causes
* **Mechanical (Maternal/Uterine constraints)**:
  * **Oligohydramnios** (羊水過少)
  * Bicornuate uterus
  * Multiple pregnancy
* **Connective Tissue Disorders**:
  * Diastrophic dysplasia
  * Osteogenesis imperfecta (成骨不全症)
* **Maternal conditions**:
  * Myasthenia gravis
  * Multiple sclerosis

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### General Neonatal Appearance
* Newborn appears "stiff" or "frozen"
* Fixed in distinct, rigid postures
* Extremities exhibit extremely ↓ range of motion (ROM)
* Limbs appear "tubular" and featureless
* Absent normal skin creases (due to absent underlying muscle bulk)
* Cutaneous fibrotic dimples present over affected joints

### Classic Amyoplasia Upper Limb Pattern
* Shoulders: Internally rotated
* Elbows: Extended
* Wrists: Flexed rigidly
* Hands: Classic **"policeman tip" stance**

### Classic Amyoplasia Lower Limb Pattern
* Hips: Dislocated
* Knees: Severe contractures (often hyperextended)
* Feet: Severe bilateral clubfoot / **Talipes equinovarus** (馬蹄內翻足)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Primary Amyoplasia vs. Lethal Neuromuscular Cascade
* **Primary Amyoplasia (肌肉發育不全)**:
  * Sporadic event
  * Normal brain function
  * Normal intellect
  * Non-progressive
* **Lethal Neuromuscular Cascade** (e.g., SMA Type 0):
  * Progressive weakness
  * Associated with respiratory failure
  * Severe generalized hypotonia
  * Absent deep tendon reflexes

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Arthrogryposis
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)** | Progressive LMN signs, severe hypotonia, absent reflexes, tongue fasciculations, normal intellect |
| **Trisomy 18 (Edwards Syndrome)** | Overlapping fingers, rocker-bottom feet, prominent occiput, micrognathia, severe cardiac defects |
| **Fetal Akinesia Deformation Sequence (FADS)** | Pulmonary hypoplasia, **polyhydramnios** (羊水過多), craniofacial anomalies, often lethal |
| **Congenital Myotonic Dystrophy** | Severe hypotonia, facial diplegia ("tent-shaped" mouth), maternal history of myotonia |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
* Comprehensive passive and active ROM assessment of all joints
* Neurological exam (tone, reflexes, cranial nerves)
* Neonatal developmental reflex screening

### Blood & Genetic Investigations
* **Creatine Kinase (CK)**:
  * Screen for underlying muscular dystrophies
* **Genetic Testing**:
  * Chromosomal microarray (CMA)
  * Whole Exome Sequencing (WES)
  * *SMN1* gene deletion test (to rule out SMA)

### Imaging Investigations
* **Ultrasound (US) Hips**:
  * Mandatory screening for **Developmental Dysplasia of the Hip (DDH)** (發育性髖關節發育不良)
  * DDH is almost universal in AMC
* **US Brain/Spine**:
  * Assess for CNS malformations
* **CXR & US Abdomen**:
  * Screen for associated cardiopulmonary or renal anomalies

### Special Investigations
* **EMG / Nerve Conduction Studies (NCS)**:
  * Differentiates neurogenic vs. myopathic causes
* **Muscle Biopsy**:
  * Reserved for atypical cases if genetic testing is inconclusive

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Neonatal Management & Airway Protocol
* **Airway Management**:
  * Immediate assessment of temporomandibular joint (TMJ)
  * If TMJ contracted → intubation is extremely difficult
  * Prepare fiberoptic or video laryngoscopy on standby
* **Resuscitation/Handling**:
  * Extremely gentle handling required
  * Avoid forced joint mobilization to prevent iatrogenic fractures

### Chronic Physical & Occupational Therapy
* **Physiotherapy (PT)**:
  * Aggressive, highly intensive daily passive stretching
  * Must start within days of birth
* **Occupational Therapy (OT)**:
  * Splinting and orthoses provision
  * Focus on maximizing Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)
* **Serial Casting**:
  * **Ponseti method** used strictly for clubfoot correction

### Orthopaedic Surgical Management
* **Timing**:
  * Surgeries timed to match developmental motor milestones
* **Procedures**:
  * Soft tissue releases
  * Corrective osteotomies
  * Tendon transfers
* **Goals**:
  * Enable bending of arms for self-feeding
  * Achieve plantigrade feet for weight-bearing and standing

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Roles
* **Paediatrician**: Overall medical coordination & genetic workup
* **Orthopaedic Surgeon**: Surgical correction of contractures/dislocations
* **Neurologist**: Diagnostic workup for underlying neuromuscular causes
* **PT/OT**: Daily rehabilitation and wheelchair/equipment access
* **Speech Therapist**: If bulbar involvement or TMJ ankylosis affects feeding

### Psychosocial & SWD Support Networks
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**:
  * Funding applications for customized wheelchairs and home modifications
* **Maternal & Child Health Centre (MCHC)**:
  * Ongoing developmental surveillance
* **Social Welfare Department (SWD)**:
  * Referrals to Early Education and Training Centre (EETC)
  * Special Child Care Centre (SCCC) placement

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Iatrogenic Fracture Risks vs. Non-Accidental Injury (NAI)
* Rigid joints + disuse osteopenia → ↑↑ susceptibility to fractures
* High risk during:
  * Difficult vaginal delivery
  * Overly aggressive physiotherapy by frightened/untrained parents
* **Safeguarding Protocol**:
  * Meticulously document all birth-related injuries immediately
  * Educate parents strictly on safe handling techniques
  * Avoid misdiagnosing genuine pathological fractures as NAI

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-Term Complications
* Extreme difficulty with ADLs and self-care
* Severe mobility impairment (many require customized wheelchairs)
* Secondary severe scoliosis
* Early-onset osteoarthritis in adulthood
* Airway/respiratory compromise (if severe scoliosis develops)

### Prognosis & Life Expectancy
* **Classic Amyoplasia/Mechanical causes**:
  * Typical lifespan
  * Normal intelligence
* **Syndromic/Neuromuscular causes** (e.g., SMA type 0, FADS):
  * Often lethal in early infancy due to respiratory failure

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why are there dimples over the joints in Arthrogryposis? Answer: Connective tissue tethers the skin directly to the bone in areas that didn't move in utero, forming classic fibrotic dimples over elbows and knees.
* Trap: Assuming all infants with Arthrogryposis Multiplex Congenita have severe intellectual disability or a lethal prognosis. Pearl: If the condition is due to primary amyoplasia or simple mechanical restriction, the child has completely normal intelligence and a normal lifespan.
* Pitfall: Forcefully manipulating contracted joints during the initial newborn physical examination. This frequently causes iatrogenic fractures in osteopenic limbs; joints must only be assessed with very gentle passive range of motion.
