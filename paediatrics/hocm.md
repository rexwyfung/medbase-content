# Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy (肥厚型梗阻性心肌病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy
* Unexplained **Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (左心室肥厚)** in absence of ↑ afterload (e.g., aortic stenosis, hypertension).
* Typically features **Asymmetric Septal Hypertrophy (ASH)**.
* **Obstructive** component involves **Systolic Anterior Motion (SAM)** of anterior mitral valve leaflet → **Left Ventricular Outflow Tract (LVOT)** obstruction.

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology
* Affects ~1 in 500 in general population; childhood presentation is rare but carries higher morbidity.
* **Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA)** data shows childhood onset often linked to **Noonan Syndrome (努南綜合症)** (N-HCM) or inborn errors of metabolism.
* Adolescent-onset is usually sarcomeric (S-HCM).
* Common S-HCM mutations in HK Chinese: **MYBPC3**, **MYH7**.
* Common N-HCM mutations in HK: **PTPN11**, **RAF1**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Basis of Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy
* **Autosomal Dominant** inheritance (most common).
* Sarcomeric protein gene mutations: **MYH7** (β-myosin heavy chain), **MYBPC3** (myosin-binding protein C).
* In infants/children: Often part of **RASopathies** (e.g., Noonan, LEOPARD) or metabolic (e.g., **Pompe Disease (龐貝氏症)**, Danon disease).

### Pathophysiological Mechanisms
* **Myocardial Fiber Disarray (心肌纖維排列紊亂)** + interstitial fibrosis → arrhythmogenic substrate → ↑ risk of **Sudden Cardiac Death (SCD) (心源性猝死)**.
* **Diastolic Dysfunction**: Stiff, non-compliant LV → ↑ filling pressure → left atrial dilation → ↑ risk of **Atrial Fibrillation (心房顫動)**.
* **LVOT Obstruction**: Hypertrophied septum + SAM of mitral valve → dynamic obstruction during systole (worsens with ↑ contractility or ↓ preload).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Characteristic Patient Symptoms
* Often asymptomatic (found on screening/murmur).
* **Dyspnea (呼吸困難)** on exertion.
* **Exertional Syncope (勞動性暈厥)** or presyncope.
* **Angina (心絞痛)** (due to microvascular dysfunction/myocardial ischemia).
* **Palpitations (心悸)**.
* Sudden Cardiac Arrest (often first presentation during vigorous sports).

### Key Clinical Signs on Examination
* **Double Apical Impulse (雙重跳動的心尖搏動)** (due to forceful left atrial contraction).
* **Jerky Carotid Pulse** (rapid upstroke, bifid).
* **Harsh Ejection Systolic Murmur** at lower left sternal border.
* **Pansystolic Murmur** at apex (if SAM causes mitral regurgitation).
* **S4 Gallop** (+ve 4th heart sound due to stiff LV).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Dynamic Murmur Changes
* Table summarizing dynamic maneuvers:

| Maneuver | Hemodynamic Effect | Murmur Intensity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Valsalva / Standing** | ↓ Venous return (Preload) → ↓ LV volume → Worsens LVOT obstruction | **↑ (Louder)** |
| **Squatting / Passive Leg Raise** | ↑ Venous return (Preload) & ↑ Afterload → ↑ LV volume → Relieves obstruction | **↓ (Softer)** |
| **Handgrip** | ↑ Afterload → ↑ Aortic pressure holding valve open | **↓ (Softer)** |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentiating Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy from Mimics
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Aortic Stenosis (主動脈瓣狹窄)** | Murmur radiates to carotids. Valsalva → **↓ Murmur**. Slow-rising pulse. |
| **Athlete's Heart (運動員心臟)** | Symmetric LVH. Normal diastolic function. LVH regresses with deconditioning. |
| **Ventricular Septal Defect (心室中隔缺損)** | Pansystolic murmur. No dynamic change with Valsalva. |
| **Hypertensive Heart Disease** | History of chronic ↑ BP. Symmetric LVH. Rare in pediatrics. |
| **Pompe Disease** | Hypotonia, macroglossia, massive cardiomegaly in infancy, severe ECG voltages. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### First-Line Bedside Investigations
* **ECG (心電圖)**: 
  * Massive LVH criteria +ve.
  * Deep, narrow **"Dagger" Q waves** in lateral/inferior leads (septal depolarization).
  * Repolarization abnormalities (T wave inversions).
![image|245](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/d0e53671af534b945ab6ea3a6357abcf.png)

### Gold Standard Imaging
* **Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE) (心臟超聲波)**:
  * Left ventricular wall thickness ≥15 mm (or Z-score >2.5 in children).
  * **Asymmetrical Septal Hypertrophy (ASH) ratio** ≥ 1.3:1 (Septum to Posterior Wall).
  * **SAM** of the anterior mitral valve leaflet.
  * Continuous wave Doppler → measures LVOT peak gradient.

### Specialist Diagnostics & Risk Stratification
* **24-48 hr Holter Monitor**: Detect non-sustained ventricular tachycardia (NSVT) or AF.
* **Cardiac MRI (CMR)**: **Late Gadolinium Enhancement (LGE)** indicates myocardial fibrosis (strong SCD predictor).
* **Cardiopulmonary Exercise Testing (CPET)**: Assess functional capacity & abnormal BP response (failure of BP to ↑ >20 mmHg during exercise = high risk).
* **Genetic Testing**: Gene panel sequencing for index patient & cascade screening for 1st-degree relatives.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management of LVOT Obstruction and Arrhythmia
* **Atrial Fibrillation**: Medical emergency in HOCM (loss of atrial kick → severe ↓ cardiac output). Immediate DC cardioversion or chemical control (Amiodarone).
* **Avoidance of Harmful Agents**:
  * STRICTLY AVOID **Nitrates / Diuretics** (↓ preload worsens obstruction).
  * STRICTLY AVOID **Positive Inotropes** e.g., Digoxin, Dobutamine (↑ contractility worsens obstruction).
* Aggressive IV hydration if volume depleted.

### Chronic Pharmacological Management
* **1st Line**: **Beta-blockers (β-受體阻滯劑)** (e.g., Propranolol, Metoprolol).
  * ↓ Heart rate → ↑ Diastolic filling time.
  * ↓ Contractility → ↓ LVOT gradient.
* **2nd Line**: Non-dihydropyridine Calcium Channel Blockers (e.g., **Verapamil**). Use with extreme caution in infants (risk of sudden cardiovascular collapse).
* Disopyramide (negative inotrope) if symptoms persist.

### Surgical & Procedural Interventions
* Indicated if severe symptoms (NYHA III/IV) despite max medical therapy & resting LVOT gradient > 50 mmHg.
* **Surgical Septal Myectomy (Morrow Procedure)**: Gold standard for pediatrics/young adults.
* **Alcohol Septal Ablation**: Percutaneous, usually reserved for adults/elderly.

### Prevention of Sudden Cardiac Death (SCD)
* **Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator (ICD) (植入式心臟除顫器)** indications:
  * Prior cardiac arrest / sustained VT (Secondary prevention).
  * Massive LVH (thickness ≥30 mm or severe Z-score).
  * Unexplained recent syncope.
  * Family history of premature SCD.
  * NSVT on Holter.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Pediatric Cardiologist**: Serial TTE and Holter monitoring.
* **Clinical Geneticist**: Pre-test and post-test counseling, cascade screening for family.
* **Specialist Cardiac Nurse**: Education on symptom monitoring and medication adherence.

### Psychosocial Support and Lifestyle Modifications
* **Clinical Psychologist**: Crucial for coping with chronic disease burden, ICD anxiety/shocks, and lifestyle restrictions.
* **Sports Restrictions**: MUST avoid competitive sports, burst exertion (sprinting), and isometric exercises (heavy weightlifting).
* Encourage low-intensity aerobic activities (e.g., casual walking, golf) per HA rehabilitation guidelines.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Recognizing Medical Neglect
* **Failure to Attend Medical Appointments**: Persistent missed cardiology follow-ups in a child with high SCD risk constitutes medical neglect.
* **Failure to Adhere to Restrictions**: Parents allowing/encouraging competitive sports participation despite explicit medical warnings.
* Requires prompt referral to HA Medical Social Worker (MSW) or Child Protection Team.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Major Complications
* **Sudden Cardiac Death (SCD) (心源性猝死)**: Highest risk during adolescence/young adulthood.
* **Heart Failure (心臟衰竭)**: "Burnout phase" where hypertrophied LV dilates and becomes globally hypokinetic (LVEF <50%).
* **Atrial Fibrillation (心房顫動)**: Leads to thromboembolism (requires anticoagulation) and acute decompensation.
* **Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎)**: Resulting from turbulent flow and mitral valve SAM injury.

### Long-Term Prognosis
* Varies heavily by genetic mutation.
* Sarcomeric HCM in adolescence carries significant SCD risk.
* Noonan-associated HCM often presents <1 year old, has high early morbidity (heart failure) but may stagnate in hypertrophy with lower malignant arrhythmia risk compared to S-HCM long-term.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of sudden cardiac death in young athletes? Answer: Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
* Viva Question: How do Valsalva and squatting affect the murmur of HOCM? Answer: Valsalva decreases preload, worsening obstruction and making the murmur louder. Squatting increases preload and afterload, reducing obstruction and making the murmur softer.
* Viva Question: What are the characteristic ECG findings in HOCM? Answer: Left ventricular hypertrophy and deep, narrow "dagger" Q waves in the inferior and lateral leads. ![image|569](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/4271a14397ae2171c3a332e10a8f686d.png)
* Trap: Assuming Digoxin is a good treatment because the patient is in heart failure. Pearl: Digoxin is a positive inotrope and will worsen the dynamic LVOT obstruction in HOCM; it is strictly contraindicated.
* Trap: Treating exertional dyspnea in HOCM with nitrates or diuretics. Pearl: Nitrates and diuretics reduce preload, which narrows the LV outflow tract and exacerbates the obstruction and symptoms. Use beta-blockers instead.
* Pitfall: Reassuring a patient with asymptomatic HOCM that they have zero risk of SCD. Always perform comprehensive risk stratification (Holter, MRI for LGE, family history, echo gradients) before determining the need for an ICD.
* Pitfall: Mistaking the pansystolic murmur of SAM-induced mitral regurgitation for a primary mitral valve prolapse or VSD. Always use dynamic maneuvers; an MR murmur from primary valve disease will not increase significantly with Valsalva, whereas the HOCM LVOT murmur will.
