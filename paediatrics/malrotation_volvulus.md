# Malrotation with Volvulus (腸旋轉不良伴腸扭轉)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Malrotation
* Failure of normal embryonic midgut rotation and fixation.
* Small intestine lies predominantly on RHS, large intestine on LHS.
* Narrow mesenteric base predisposes bowel to twist upon itself (**Volvulus (腸扭轉)**) → compromises **Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA) (腸繫膜上動脈)**.
### Local Epidemiology & Incidence
* Most devastating pediatric surgical emergency.
* Incidence: ~1 in 500 live births.
* Can present at any age, but highest risk in neonatal period.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Embryologic Rotation Failure
* Normal: Bowel rotates 270° counter-clockwise around SMA, fixing via broad mesentery.
* Malrotation: **Duodenojejunal (DJ) flexure** displaced downward/rightward. Caecum is highly mobile, usually in RUQ.
### Mechanisms of Obstruction
* **Ladd's Bands (拉德氏帶)**: Fibrous bands tethering caecum to posterior abdominal wall → cross over and compress duodenum → extrinsic obstruction.
* **Volvulus**: Poorly tethered midgut twists on narrow pedicle → venous engorgement → arterial occlusion → rapid midgut infarction.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Neonatal Presentation
* >50% present in 1st month of life (classically days 1-3).
* Sudden onset **Bilious vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)** in a previously healthy, full-term infant.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Early Clinical Signs
* Sudden green/yellow bilious emesis.
* Inconsolable crying (colicky abdominal pain).
* Abdomen initially soft, non-distended.
### Late Ischemic Signs
* Intractable **Shock (休克)** (tachycardia, ↑ capillary refill time, lethargy).
* Blood-stained stools / hematochezia.
* Abdominal distension, rigidity, erythema (implies established bowel necrosis & peritonitis).

## 5. Discriminatory Features
### The Neonatal Bilious Vomiting Rule
* ANY neonate with bilious vomiting = **Malrotation with Volvulus** until definitively excluded.
* Do not assume benign causes (e.g., gastro-oesophageal reflux never causes bilious vomiting).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentials for Neonatal Bilious Vomiting
| Differential | Key Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Duodenal Atresia (十二指腸閉鎖)** | "Double bubble" sign on AXR, polyhydramnios on antenatal US. Associated with **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症)**. |
| **Jejunal/Ileal Atresia (空腸/迴腸閉鎖)** | Multiple dilated bowel loops with air-fluid levels on AXR. |
| **Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸梗阻)** | Associated with **Cystic Fibrosis (囊性纖維化)**, "soap bubble" appearance on AXR. |
| **Hirschsprung's Disease (巨結腸症)** | Delayed meconium passage >48h, transition zone on contrast enema. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Laboratory Investigations
* **VBG/ABG**: Severe metabolic acidosis, ↑ lactate (indicates bowel ischemia).
* **Bloods**: FBC, U&E, Crossmatch (prep for emergency theatre).
### Imaging Modalities
* **Gold Standard Imaging**: **Upper GI Contrast Study (上消化道造影)**.
  * Findings: DJ flexure on RHS of spine & inferior to duodenal bulb. Classic "corkscrew" appearance of twisted small bowel loops.
* **Emergency Ultrasound (US)**: "Whirlpool sign" (SMA & SMV twisted). Operator-dependent; UGI contrast remains superior.
* **Abdominal X-Ray (AXR)**: Can be totally NORMAL early on. May show paucity of distal gas or gastric distension.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### HA Emergency Resuscitation Protocol
* Immediate **NBM (禁食)**.
* Pass wide-bore **Nasogastric (NG) tube (鼻胃管)** for gastric decompression.
* Large-bore IV access ×2.
* Fluid resuscitation: 20ml/kg 0.9% NaCl boluses for shock.
* IV Broad-spectrum antibiotics (e.g., **Cefotaxime + Metronidazole**).
### Surgical Intervention: Emergency Ladd's Procedure
* Emergency **Laparotomy (剖腹探查術)**.
* Untwisting the volvulus (counter-clockwise).
* Division of **Ladd's bands**.
* Widening the mesenteric base to prevent re-twisting.
* Bowel placement: Small bowel on RHS, large bowel on LHS (non-anatomical but safe).
* **Prophylactic appendicectomy (預防性闌尾切除術)** to prevent future diagnostic confusion.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Support
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Immediate intervention for families facing potential neonatal death or catastrophic lifestyle changes.
* **Dietitian/NICU**: Long-term nutritional planning if short bowel syndrome develops.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Clinical Governance & Safety Netting
* Extreme vigilance for delayed presentation. 
* Meticulous safety netting for previously discharged infants returning with non-specific vomiting that progresses to bilious.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Severe Surgical Complications
* Massive midgut necrosis → Extensive bowel resection.
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)**.
* Dependence on long-term **Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN) (全靜脈營養)**.
* TPN-induced liver failure → Multi-visceral transplant requirement.
* Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO) 2° to post-op adhesions.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls
* Trap: Ordering an AXR, seeing a normal gas pattern, and reassuring the parents. Pearl: Volvulus can have a completely NORMAL plain AXR. Never delay an Upper GI contrast.
* Viva Question: Why is an appendicectomy performed during a Ladd's Procedure? Answer: Because the final surgical placement puts the caecum (and appendix) in the left upper quadrant. A future appendicitis would present with highly atypical signs, confusing doctors.
* Pitfall: Waiting for peritonitis or bloody stools before consulting pediatric surgery. These are late signs of irreversible bowel necrosis; consult immediately upon presentation of bilious vomiting.
