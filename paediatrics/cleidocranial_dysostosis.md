# Cleidocranial Dysostosis (鎖骨顱骨發育異常)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A rare autosomal dominant skeletal dysplasia characterized by delayed ossification of midline structures, particularly the skull and clavicles.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Mutation in the **RUNX2** gene on chromosome 6p21 (a master transcription factor for osteoblast differentiation).
- Specifically impairs **intramembranous ossification** (predominantly clavicles, cranial vault, and facial bones).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Usually a normal birth weight and length, but an unusually large, soft head (delayed fontanelle closure) is noted by paediatricians.
- Parents may note they can fold the baby's shoulders together anteriorly.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Skull**: Exceptionally delayed closure of anterior/posterior fontanelles (can remain open into adulthood), wormian bones, frontal bossing.
- **Clavicular**: Hypoplastic or entirely absent clavicles. Allows extraordinary hypermobility of the shoulders (can approximate vividly in the midline).
- **Dental**: Highly characteristic issues. High arched palate, delayed eruption of deciduous and permanent teeth, multiple extra (supernumerary) teeth impacted in the gums.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Achondroplasia (limbs are relatively normal length here, primary defect is clavicle/skull/teeth).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Pyknodysostosis (also causes open fontanelles, but causes profound short stature and bone fragility).
- Mandibuloacral dysplasia.

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Imaging**: 
  - Skull X-ray: Wide sutures, massive open fontanelles, multiple calcified islands (Wormian bones).
  - Chest X-ray: absent or partially formed clavicles.
  - Panorex (Dental X-ray): Unerupted supernumerary teeth crowding the jaw.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Dental/Maxillofacial**: Major, complex, long-term dental surgery is the cornerstone of treatment. Includes removal of supernumerary teeth to allow permanent teeth to erupt, or pulling them down via orthodontics.
- **Orthopaedic**: Protective helmets in early childhood due to massive open fontanelles.
- **General**: No specific medical limits, life expectancy is normal.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Maxillofacial and Orthognathic surgical teams dominate the long-term MDT.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Brain injury vulnerability until the skull eventually closes.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Recurrent ear infections causing conductive hearing loss.
- Extensive dental caries and cosmetic impact from poor dentition.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Viva Q**: "If a child presents with Wormian bones on skull X-ray, what two conditions must you immediately consider?" Answer: Cleidocranial Dysostosis and Osteogenesis Imperfecta.
- **Trap**: Falsely attributing short stature to CD. While they are often slightly shorter, profound dwarfism points to an alternative diagnosis.
