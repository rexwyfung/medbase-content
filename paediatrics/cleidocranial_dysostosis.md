# Cleidocranial Dysostosis (鎖骨顱骨發育異常)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Cleidocranial Dysostosis
* Rare autosomal dominant (AD) skeletal dysplasia (骨骼發育不良)
* Characterized by delayed ossification of midline structures (skull, clavicles)

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Extremely rare in HK Hospital Authority (HA) settings
* No specific predilection for Chinese ethnicity
* Life expectancy is normal

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Mutation in Cleidocranial Dysostosis
* **RUNX2 (CBFA1)** gene mutation on chromosome 6p21
* Master transcription factor for osteoblast differentiation (成骨細胞分化)
* AD inheritance, ~30% de novo mutations

### Pathophysiological Mechanism
* Impaired intramembranous ossification (膜內骨化)
* Predominantly affects clavicles, cranial vault, facial bones, and teeth
* Endochondral ossification (軟骨內骨化) remains relatively intact → long bones usually normal length

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Neonatal & Infant Presentation
* Normal birth weight & length
* Unusually large, soft head (delayed fontanelle closure) noted by paediatricians or at MCHC
* Parents note hypermobility of shoulders (can fold anteriorly)

### Childhood & Adolescent Presentation
* Dental crowding, delayed eruption of permanent teeth
* Recurrent otitis media (中耳炎)

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Skull & Craniofacial Signs
* Exceptionally delayed closure of anterior/posterior fontanelles (前/後囟門) (can remain open into adulthood)
* Frontal bossing (前額突出), prominent parietal bones
* Depressed nasal bridge, hypertelorism (眼距過寬)

### Clavicular & Chest Signs
* Hypoplastic or completely absent clavicles (鎖骨)
* Extraordinary shoulder hypermobility (can approximate in midline)
* Narrow, bell-shaped thorax

### Dental Signs (Highly Characteristic)
* High arched palate (高拱顎)
* Delayed eruption of deciduous/permanent teeth
* Multiple supernumerary (extra) teeth impacted in gums
* Enamel hypoplasia, malocclusion (咬合不正)

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing Cleidocranial Dysostosis from Achondroplasia
* **Limbs**: Normal length in CD vs. rhizomelic shortening in Achondroplasia (軟骨發育不全)
* **Primary Defect**: Clavicles/skull/teeth in CD vs. endochondral ossification/long bones in Achondroplasia

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis of Delayed Fontanelle Closure & Skeletal Dysplasia

| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pyknodysostosis** (緻密骨發育不全) | Open fontanelles, profound short stature, osteosclerosis (bone fragility/fractures) |
| **Achondroplasia** (軟骨發育不全) | Rhizomelic dwarfism, trident hands, normal clavicles |
| **Osteogenesis Imperfecta** (成骨不全症) | Multiple fractures, blue sclerae, normal clavicles |
| **Mandibuloacral Dysplasia** | Mandibular hypoplasia, acro-osteolysis, skin changes |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Laboratory Investigations
* **Clinical Genetic Testing**: RUNX2 gene sequencing (Gold standard for confirmation)
* **Bone Profile (Ca/PO4/ALP)**: Normal (rules out rickets/metabolic bone disease)

### Imaging Investigations
* **Skull X-ray**: Wide sutures, massive open fontanelles, multiple calcified islands (**Wormian bones** (縫間骨))
* **Chest X-ray (CXR)**: Absent/hypoplastic clavicles, narrow upper thorax, small bell-shaped rib cage
* **Dental X-ray (Panorex)**: Unerupted supernumerary teeth crowding the jaw

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
* Protective helmets in early childhood due to massive open fontanelles
* No specific acute medical emergencies unless associated with unprotected brain trauma

### Chronic Dental/Maxillofacial Management (Cornerstone)
* Orthodontic & Maxillofacial surgery are mainstay of treatment
* Surgical removal of supernumerary teeth to allow permanent teeth to erupt
* Orthodontic traction & prosthodontics/implants for cosmetic & functional dentition

### Orthopaedic & General Management
* Conservative management for clavicular hypoplasia (surgery rarely needed)
* Screen for scoliosis (脊柱側彎) & osteoporosis
* Prompt treatment of acute otitis media to prevent hearing loss

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* **Maxillofacial/Dental Surgeons**: Dominate long-term care
* **ENT Surgeons**: Manage recurrent otitis media / hearing loss
* **Clinical Geneticist**: Genetic counselling for family planning (50% transmission risk)
* **Paediatrician**: Growth monitoring, coordination of care

### Psychosocial Support
* Medical Social Worker (MSW) referral for financial support regarding complex dental surgeries
* Psychological support for cosmetic impact of craniofacial/dental anomalies

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations in CD
* **Brain Injury Vulnerability**: High risk of intracranial injury from minor head trauma due to persistently open fontanelles
* Educate parents/caregivers on strict head protection
* Carefully distinguish true NAI from accidental injury secondary to absent protective skull vault

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Long-term Complications
* **ENT**: Recurrent acute otitis media → Conductive hearing loss (傳導性聽力受損)
* **Dental**: Extensive dental caries, severe malocclusion, cosmetic impact
* **Skeletal**: Mild short stature, scoliosis, pes planus, osteoporosis
* **Obstetric**: ↑ risk of cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD) requiring Caesarean section in affected females

### Prognosis
* Normal life expectancy
* Normal intellectual development

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: If a child presents with Wormian bones on skull X-ray, what conditions must you immediately consider? Answer: Cleidocranial Dysostosis, Osteogenesis Imperfecta, Down Syndrome, Rickets, Congenital Hypothyroidism.
* Trap: Falsely attributing profound short stature to Cleidocranial Dysostosis. Pearl: While CD patients may be slightly shorter, profound dwarfism points to an alternative diagnosis like Achondroplasia or Pyknodysostosis.
* Pitfall: Neglecting hearing screening. Conductive hearing loss secondary to recurrent otitis media is a common complication; regular ENT/audiology follow-up is mandatory.
* Viva Question: What is the genetic mutation and inheritance pattern of Cleidocranial Dysostosis? Answer: RUNX2 gene mutation, Autosomal Dominant inheritance.
