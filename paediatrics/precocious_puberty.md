# Precocious Puberty (性早熟)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Precocious Puberty
* Onset of 2° sexual characteristics < 8 yrs in girls.
* Onset of 2° sexual characteristics < 9 yrs in boys.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Gender: F >> M (ratio ~5-10:1).
* Girls: >90% **Idiopathic (原發性)**.
* Boys: >50% **Pathological (病理性)** → Always assume underlying CNS/adrenal lesion until proven otherwise.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Central Precocious Puberty (CPP) Pathophysiology
* Gonadotropin-dependent.
* Premature activation of Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal (HPG) axis.
* Pathogenesis: ↑ Pulsatile **GnRH (促性腺激素釋放激素)** → ↑ **LH/FSH** from anterior pituitary → ↑ gonadal sex steroids (Oestrogen/Testosterone).

### Peripheral Precocious Puberty (PPP) Pathophysiology
* Gonadotropin-independent.
* Excess sex steroids secreted from peripheral sources (adrenals/gonads) or exogenous sources.
* Pathogenesis: ↑ Sex steroids → -ve feedback on hypothalamus/pituitary → ↓/suppressed **LH/FSH**.

### Genetic & Syndromic Causes
* **McCune-Albright Syndrome (麥庫恩-奧爾布賴特綜合症)**: *GNAS* gene mutation → G-protein hyperactivation → autonomous ovarian cysts (PPP), café-au-lait spots, polyostotic fibrous dysplasia.
* **Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (先天性腎上腺增生症, CAH)**: *CYP21A2* mutation (21-hydroxylase deficiency) → ↑ adrenal androgens (PPP).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms in Girls
* **Thelarche (乳房發育)**: Usually 1st sign.
* **Pubarche/Adrenarche (陰毛發育)**.
* Rapid growth spurt (↑ growth velocity).
* Early **Menarche (初潮)**.

### Key Symptoms in Boys
* Testicular enlargement (Volume > 4 mL via Prader orchidometer).
* **Pubarche (陰毛發育)**.
* Deepening of voice.
* Rapid growth spurt & ↑ muscle mass.

### Associated Clinical Signs
* **Café-au-lait macules (咖啡牛奶斑)**: Suggests McCune-Albright Syndrome or Neurofibromatosis Type 1 (NF1).
* Midline facial defects / Optic atrophy: Suggests CNS lesions (septo-optic dysplasia, optic glioma).
* Adult body odour, severe acne: Suggests excess adrenal androgens.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features: Central vs Peripheral Precocious Puberty in Boys
| Feature | Central PP (中樞性) | Peripheral PP (外周性) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Testicular Volume** | Bilaterally enlarged (>4 mL) | Bilaterally small (<4 mL) OR unilateral mass |
| **Pathophysiology** | LH/FSH-driven testicular growth | Adrenal androgens / Leydig cell tumour |
| **Basal LH/FSH** | ↑ (Pubertal levels) | ↓ (Suppressed via -ve feedback) |
| **GnRH Stim Test** | Brisk LH peak | Flat LH response |

### Discriminatory Features: True Central PP vs Benign Variants
| Feature | True Central PP | Benign Premature Thelarche / Adrenarche |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Progression** | Rapid & sequential | Static or slowly regressing |
| **Growth Velocity** | ↑↑ (Crossing height centiles upwards) | Normal |
| **Bone Age** | Markedly advanced (>2 yrs ahead of chrono age) | Normal (matches chronological age) |
| **Pelvic USG** | ↑ Uterine length, visible endometrium | Prepubertal uterus |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Central Precocious Puberty
* Idiopathic (Most common in females).
* CNS Tumours: **Hypothalamic hamartoma (下丘腦錯構瘤)**, Optic glioma (associated with NF1), Astrocytoma.
* CNS Acquired Lesions: Post-meningitis, post-irradiation, hydrocephalus.

### Differential Diagnosis of Peripheral Precocious Puberty
* **Adrenal (腎上腺)**: CAH (late-onset), Adrenal cortical carcinoma.
* **Gonadal (性腺)**: Ovarian cyst/tumour (Granulosa cell), Testicular tumour (Leydig cell).
* **Syndromic (綜合症)**: McCune-Albright Syndrome.
* **Exogenous**: Contact with topical testosterone/estrogen creams.
* Severe Primary Hypothyroidism (Van Wyk-Grumbach syndrome → ↑ TSH cross-reacts with FSH receptors).

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
* **Tanner Staging (坦納階段)**: Document baseline puberty stage.
* **Bone Age X-ray (骨齡X光)**: Left hand & wrist. Assesses epiphyseal maturation. Markedly advanced in true PP.

### Basal Blood Investigations
* **Gonadotropins & Sex Steroids**: Basal LH, FSH, Oestradiol (girls), Testosterone (boys).
* **Adrenal Profile**: 17-OHP (screen for CAH), DHEAS, Androstenedione.
* **Thyroid Function Test**: TSH, free T4 (rule out primary hypothyroidism).
* **Tumour Markers**: bHCG, AFP (rule out germ cell tumours in boys).

### Dynamic Blood Investigations
* **GnRH Stimulation Test (促性腺激素釋放激素刺激試驗)**: *Gold Standard* for Central PP.
* Administer synthetic GnRH (LHRH).
* Interpretation: Brisk, pubertal rise in LH (peak > 5-8 IU/L) confirms Central PP. Flat response suggests Peripheral PP or prepubertal state.

### Radiological Investigations
* **MRI Brain (腦部磁力共振)**: MANDATORY in ALL boys with CPP, and girls with CPP onset < 6 yrs or +ve neuro signs. Target: Hypothalamic/pituitary region.
* **Pelvic USG (盆腔超聲波)** (Girls): Assess uterine size/length, endometrial echo, ovarian volume (> 1-2 mL indicates pubertal), rule out functional cysts.
* **Testicular USG (睾丸超聲波)** (Boys): Rule out asymmetric testicular tumours.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Chronic Management of Central Precocious Puberty
* **1st Line Pharmacotherapy**: **GnRH Agonists (促性腺激素釋放激素激動劑)** (e.g., Triptorelin, Leuprorelin).
* **Route**: Monthly or 3-monthly IM depot injections.
* **Mechanism**: Continuous, non-pulsatile stimulation down-regulates pituitary GnRH receptors → profound suppression of LH/FSH → halts puberty.
* **Goals**: Stop menses, delay early epiphyseal fusion to optimize final adult height, alleviate psychosocial distress.
* **Cessation**: Treatment stopped at normal pubertal age (typically ~11 yrs) to allow natural pubertal progression.

### Chronic Management of Peripheral Precocious Puberty
* Treat underlying pathology.
* CAH: Lifelong **Hydrocortisone (氫化可的松)** → suppresses ACTH → ↓ adrenal androgens.
* Ovarian/Testicular Tumours: Surgical resection.
* McCune-Albright Syndrome: Aromatase inhibitors (e.g., Letrozole) or Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators (SERMs, e.g., Tamoxifen).

### Management of Benign Pubertal Variants
* Premature Thelarche/Adrenarche: Reassurance & regular clinic observation.
* Monitoring: Serial height measurements (growth velocity) and Bone Age X-ray every 6-12 months to confirm non-progressive nature.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### MDT Roles in Precocious Puberty
* **Paediatric Endocrinologist (兒童內分泌科醫生)**: Formulate treatment plan, monitor growth velocity, titrate GnRH analogue doses.
* **Clinical Psychologist (臨床心理學家)**: Provide counseling for body image issues, distress from early menarche, and mismatched physical vs cognitive maturity.
* **Medical Social Worker (醫務社工)**: Liaise with schools to prevent peer bullying/isolation due to early physical development.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Red Flags
* Exogenous Hormone Exposure: Investigate neglect if child is exposed to parents' topical hormone creams (e.g., testosterone gel).
* Sexual Abuse Risk: Early physical maturity ↑ vulnerability to sexual exploitation. Caregivers must be strictly counseled on supervision.
* Parental Expectations: Educate parents that the child's emotional/cognitive maturity matches their chronological age, NOT their advanced physical appearance.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications of Untreated Precocious Puberty
* **Short Stature (身材矮小)**: Initial tall stature → premature epiphyseal fusion → significantly compromised final adult height.
* Psychosocial/Emotional Distress: Behavioral problems, poor self-esteem, early risky behaviors.
* Obstetric: Early risk of pregnancy in females.

### Prognosis with Treatment
* Excellent with timely GnRH agonist therapy.
* Bone age advancement halts, and final adult height is largely preserved.
* Normal HPG axis function restores quickly post-treatment (menses usually resume within 6-18 months of stopping GnRH agonists).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Assuming a boy with early pubic hair and a growth spurt has Central Precocious Puberty without formally assessing the testes.
* Pearl: Always use a Prader orchidometer. If testes are small (<4mL bilaterally), it is NOT Central PP; the androgens are peripheral (e.g., CAH or adrenal tumour).
* Viva Question: Why do we use a GnRH *agonist* to stop puberty instead of an antagonist?
* Answer: Continuous, non-pulsatile administration of a GnRH agonist paradoxically down-regulates and desensitizes pituitary GnRH receptors, leading to profound suppression of LH and FSH. Pulsatile release is required for activation.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing a 2-year-old girl with isolated breast development as having true precocious puberty. You will subject her to unnecessary MRI scans and dynamic tests. Benign premature thelarche is common < 2 years. Monitor growth velocity and bone age first.
* Viva Question: Which girls with Central Precocious Puberty absolutely require an MRI Brain?
* Answer: Any girl presenting with neurological signs, OR any girl with onset of Central PP before the age of 6. (Note: ALL boys with Central PP require an MRI regardless of age due to high risk of CNS lesions).
