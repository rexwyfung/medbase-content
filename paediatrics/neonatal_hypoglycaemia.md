# Neonatal Hypoglycaemia & Polycythaemia (新生兒低血糖與紅血球增多症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Hypoglycaemia Definition**: Blood glucose < 2.6 mmol/L in the first 48 hours of life. 
- **Polycythaemia Definition**: Central venous haematocrit (Hct) > 65%. 
- These phenomena frequently co-exist in specific populations, particularly the Infant of a Diabetic Mother (IDM), Small for Gestational Age (SGA), and Large for Gestational Age (LGA) babies.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **The IDM Model**: Maternal hyperglycaemia $ightarrow$ fetal hyperglycaemia $ightarrow$ massive fetal beta-cell hypertrophy & hyperinsulinism. At birth, maternal glucose is cut off, but the infant's massive insulin surge continues, causing rapid, profound hypoglycaemia. Constant fetal hyperinsulinism also acts as a growth factor (causing Macrosomia/LGA) and massively increases fetal oxygen demand, provoking chronic fetal hypoxia. The kidneys respond by producing erythropoietin, leading to Polycythaemia.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Most are asymptomatic and are detected through mandatory pre-feed glucose screening protocols.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Hypoglycaemia**: Jitteriness, lethargy, poor feeding, hypotonia, cyanosis, apnoeic spells, high-pitched cry, seizures.
- **Polycythaemia**: Plethoric ("ruddy/tomato red") appearance, sluggish capillary refill, priapism, poor feeding (hyperviscosity syndrome).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Seizures caused by hypoglycemia rapidly reverse with glucose administration, unlike those from HIE or structural defects.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Sepsis (common cause of unexplained hypoglycaemia)
- Inborn Errors of Metabolism (e.g. Glycogen storage diseases, G6PD isn't related but metabolic screens are critical)
- Congenital Hyperinsulinism

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**: Point-of-care capillary glucose. 
- **Bloods**: If recurrent or very severe (e.g. < 1.5 mmol/L), require a formal "Hypoglycaemia Screen" sent during the hypoglycaemic episode (Insulin, Cortisol, Growth Hormone, Ketones, Free Fatty Acids, Acylcarnitine profile) before administering treatment.
- Central venous sample needed to confirm polycythaemia (capillary heel pricks overestimate Hct due to stasis).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **HA Hypoglycaemia Pathway**: 
  - Asymptomatic but low: Encourage immediate breastfeeding/formula. Recheck in 30 mins. 
  - Can consider 40% buccal glucose gel (massively reduces NICU admissions).
  - Symptomatic or critically low (<1.5 mmol/L): Immediate IV Dextrose 10% bolus (2ml/kg) followed by continuous infusion.
- **Polycythaemia Management**: Ensure adequate hydration. If symptomatic (hyperviscosity syndrome), perform a Partial Exchange Transfusion (removing blood and replacing it with normal saline to dilute the Hct).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Preventative antenatal care via MCHC tracking maternal Gestational Diabetes ensures timely birth planning.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Not routinely applicable.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Prolonged asymptomatic hypoglycemia guarantees devastating, permanent irreversible brain injury (specifically occipital lobe injury affecting vision).
- Polycythaemia complications: Venous sinus thrombosis, renal vein thrombosis, Necrotizing Enterocolitis (due to sluggish gut perfusion).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Taking a peripheral capillary gas to diagnose Polycythaemia. A cold, squeezed heel yields artificially high Hct. Always confirm >65% on a free-flowing venous sample.
- **Viva Q**: "Why are we afraid of ketone-free hypoglycaemia?" Answer: It demonstrates the brain is absolutely starved. Hyperinsulinism strictly suppresses lipolysis, meaning no protective ketones are generated for the brain to use as alternative fuel during the hypoglycaemic crash.
