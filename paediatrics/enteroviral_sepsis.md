# Enteroviral Neonatal Sepsis Syndrome (腸病毒新生兒敗血綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- Devastating, life-threatening overwhelming systemic infection occurring in the first few weeks of life.
- Caused by non-polio **Enteroviruses** (腸病毒) (e.g., Echovirus, Coxsackievirus).

### Local Epidemiology
- Feared cause of rapid neonatal collapse.
- Outbreaks occur occasionally, sometimes clustering in newborn nurseries/NICUs.
- High mortality in severe forms.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Transmission & Pathophysiology
- Infection usually acquired perinatally (mother has a mild febrile "cold" or GI upset just before delivery) or postnatally.
- Transplacental/intrapartum infection → very high viral loads → severe symptoms mimicking bacterial sepsis.
- Neonates lack mature cellular immunity & neutralizing maternal antibodies.
- Virus disseminates widely → liver, heart, brain.

### Viral Pathogens & Tropism
- **Echovirus 11** (伊科病毒11型): Catastrophic fulminant hepatitis & coagulopathy.
- **Coxsackievirus B** (柯薩奇病毒B型): Fulminant myocarditis.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Sepsis-Like Presentation
- Fever (>38°C) or hypothermia.
- Profound lethargy, poor feeding.
- Poor perfusion, mottling, hypotension (cardiogenic/distributive shock).
- Apnea, respiratory distress.
- Rapid multiorgan failure.

### Hepatic Involvement
- Jaundice (黃疸).
- Huge hepatomegaly (肝腫大).
- Widespread petechiae, spontaneous bleeding (oozing from umbilical stump or venepuncture sites) due to sudden liver failure.

### Cardiac & Neurological Involvement
- Tachycardia out of proportion to fever, cardiogenic shock (myocarditis).
- Seizures, bulging fontanelle, irritability (meningoencephalitis).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing from Bacterial Sepsis
| Feature | Enteroviral Sepsis | Group B Strep (GBS) Sepsis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Organ Hit** | Massive liver failure, wildly deranged clotting, myocarditis | Lungs (pneumonia/sepsis) or brain (meningitis) |
| **Maternal History** | Mild flu/GI upset just before delivery | GBS colonization, prolonged rupture of membranes |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Main Differentials
- Bacterial neonatal sepsis (GBS, *E. coli*, *Listeria monocytogenes*).
- **Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)** (單純皰疹病毒) disseminated neonatal infection.
- Severe congenital heart disease (CHD) (先天性心臟病) presenting with shock/cyanosis.
- Inborn errors of metabolism (IEM) (先天性代謝缺陷) (e.g., Galactosemia → liver failure).

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Basic Bloods
- **Septic screen**: Blood culture, urine culture, CSF analysis & culture, CXR (rule out bacteria).
- **CBC**: Neutropenia/leukocytosis, severe thrombocytopenia (↓ platelets).
- **VBG/ABG**: Severe metabolic acidosis, ↑ lactate.

### Liver & Coagulation Profiling (CRITICAL)
- **LFTs**: Extreme transaminitis (AST/ALT in thousands), wildly ↑ bilirubin.
- **Coagulation**: PT/APTT heavily prolonged, ↓ fibrinogen, ↑ D-dimer (DIC).

### Cardiac Evaluation
- **Cardiac markers**: ↑ Troponin, ↑ CK-MB.
- **ECG & Echocardiogram**: Poor ventricular function, arrhythmias (if myocarditis suspected).

### Definitive Virological Diagnosis
- **Multiplex PCR**: Enterovirus PCR from blood, CSF, stool, & throat/NPA swabs.
- **HSV PCR**: Blood/CSF (CRITICAL to rule out HSV).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Resuscitation & Intensive Care
- Aggressive NICU/PICU support.
- Intubation & mechanical ventilation for respiratory failure/shock.
- Fluid resuscitation (cautious if myocarditis) + Inotropes for hypotension.

### Correcting Coagulopathy
- Massive repeated infusions: **Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)** (新鮮冷凍血漿), **Cryoprecipitate**, **Platelets**, & **Vitamin K** IV → stop fatal hemorrhage.

### Antimicrobial & Antiviral Therapy
- **Empirical Antibiotics**: IV Ampicillin + Cefotaxime/Gentamicin (until bacterial sepsis excluded).
- **Empirical Antiviral**: IV **Acyclovir** (阿昔洛韋) MUST be started to cover disseminated HSV until PCR results return.
- **Enterovirus-Specific Antiviral**: No definitive proven antiviral. Pleconaril (experimental) rarely obtained via compassionate use.

### Immunomodulation
- **IVIG** (靜脈注射免疫球蛋白): High-dose IVIG frequently given aiming to provide neutralizing antibodies (controversial but often used in severe cases).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Family Support
- Deeply distressing for parents.
- Exceedingly high mortality rate (>30%) for severe hepatic/myocarditis forms.
- MSW (Medical Social Worker) & psychological support crucial for grief/bereavement counseling.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### NAI Considerations
- N/A. Condition is entirely infectious.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Complications
- Death directly from acute liver failure, massive hemorrhage (DIC), or cardiac arrest.

### Long-Term Prognosis
- Survivors often suffer severe long-term neurodevelopmental impairment (cerebral palsy, seizure disorders).
- May require later liver transplant if surviving fulminant hepatic failure but developing cirrhosis.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Assuming all neonatal sepsis is bacterial and ignoring the bleeding. 
* Pearl: If a neonate with "sepsis" is bleeding from everywhere, think HSV or Enterovirus.
* Viva Question: A 6-day-old presents with fever, shock, an enlarged liver, and ALT of 4000. Mother had a mild flu the day before delivery. What MUST you treat for empirically while testing for Enterovirus?
* Answer: You must empirically give IV Acyclovir for disseminated Neonatal HSV, because the presentation of Enteroviral Hepatitis and Disseminated HSV Hepatitis is clinically identical, but HSV has a cure that you cannot delay.
* Pitfall: Waiting for PCR confirmation before starting Acyclovir. Delaying Acyclovir in a neonate with fulminant hepatitis can be fatal if the cause is HSV.
