# Enteroviral Neonatal Sepsis Syndrome (腸病毒新生兒敗血綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A devastating, life-threatening overwhelming systemic infection occurring in the first few weeks of life, caused by non-polio Enteroviruses (Echovirus, Coxsackievirus).
- **Local Epidemiology**: A feared cause of rapid neonatal collapse. Outbreaks occur occasionally, sometimes clustering in newborn nurseries.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Infection is usually acquired perinatally (mother has a mild febrile "cold" or GI upset just before delivery) or postnatally.
- Neonates lack mature cellular immunity and neutralizing maternal antibodies. The virus disseminates widely to the liver, heart, and brain.
- **Echovirus 11**: Famous for causing catastrophic fulminant hepatitis and coagulopathy.
- **Coxsackievirus B**: Famous for causing fulminant myocarditis.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A 5-day-old neonate who suddenly stops feeding, becomes terribly mottled, and develops rapid multi-organ failure.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Sepsis-like presentation: Fever or hypothermia, profound lethargy, poor perfusion, apnea.
- **Hepatic involvement**: Jaundice, huge hepatomegaly, widespread petechiae, spontaneous bleeding (oozing from the umbilical stump or venepuncture sites) due to sudden liver failure.
- **Cardiac involvement**: Tachycardia, cardiogenic shock, respiratory distress.
- **Neurological**: Seizures, bulging fontanelle (meningoencephalitis).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Group B Strep (GBS) sepsis: GBS typically hits the lungs (pneumonia/sepsis) or brain (meningitis). Massive liver failure with wildly deranged clotting is the hallmark of Echovirus sepsis.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Bacterial neonatal sepsis (GBS, E. coli, Listeria)
- Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV) disseminated neonatal infection
- Severe congenital heart disease
- Inborn errors of metabolism (e.g., Galactosemia causing liver failure)

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Rule out bacteria**: Septic screen (Blood, Urine, CSF, CXR).
- **Liver/Clotting (CRITICAL)**: LFTs (extreme transaminitis, AST/ALT in thousands), PT/APTT heavily prolonged, wildly high bilirubin.
- **Cardiac**: Troponin, ECG, Echocardiogram (if myocarditis suspected).
- **Definitive Diagnosis**: Enterovirus PCR from blood, CSF, stool, and throat swab.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Resuscitation**: Aggressive PICU/NICU support. Intubation, inotropes.
- **Coagulopathy correction**: Massive repeated infusions of Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP), Cryoprecipitate, platelets, and Vitamin K to stop fatal hemorrhage.
- **Antiviral**: There is no definitive proven antiviral for Enterovirus (unlike Acyclovir for HSV). Pleconaril (an experimental antiviral) is sometimes obtained on compassionate use. 
- **Immunomodulation**: High-dose IVIG is frequently given in hope of providing neutralizing antibodies.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Deeply distressing for parents as the mortality rate for the severe hepatic/myocarditis forms is exceedingly high (>30%).

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Death directly from acute liver failure, massive hemorrhage, or cardiac arrest.
- Survivors often have severe long-term neurodevelopmental impairment or require later liver transplant.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Assuming all neonatal sepsis is bacterial and ignoring the bleeding. If a neonate with "sepsis" is bleeding from everywhere, think HSV or Enterovirus.
- **Viva Q**: "A 6-day-old presents with fever, shock, an enlarged liver, and ALT of 4000. Mother had a mild flu the day before delivery. What MUST you treat for empirically while testing for Enterovirus?" Answer: You must empirically give IV Acyclovir for disseminated Neonatal HSV, because the presentation of Enteroviral Hepatitis and Disseminated HSV Hepatitis is clinically identical, but HSV has a cure that you cannot delay.
