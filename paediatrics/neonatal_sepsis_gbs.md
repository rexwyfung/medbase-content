# Neonatal Sepsis & Early Onset GBS Infection (新生兒敗血症與早發性B型鏈球菌感染)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Early-Onset vs Late-Onset Neonatal Sepsis
* **Early-Onset Sepsis (EOS) (早發性敗血症):** Bloodstream infection occurring **<72 hours** of life.
* **Late-Onset Sepsis (LOS) (遲發性敗血症):** Bloodstream infection occurring **>72 hours** of life.

### Epidemiology of Neonatal Sepsis in Hong Kong
* Incidence of EOS: ~1/1000 live births in HK.
* **Universal GBS Screening (全面B型鏈球菌篩查):** Implemented by HK Department of Health (DH) & Hospital Authority (HA). Vaginal-rectal swab taken at **35-37 weeks** gestation.
* Universal screening + Intrapartum Antibiotic Prophylaxis (IAP) → drastic ↓ in EOS GBS incidence.
* **Prematurity (早產)** & **Low Birth Weight (低出生體重)** → ↑ risk of both EOS & LOS.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathogenesis of Early-Onset Sepsis
* **Vertical Transmission (垂直傳播):** Ascending infection from colonized maternal genital tract.
* Fetal exposure via infected amniotic fluid (chorioamnionitis) or during vaginal delivery.
* Fetal aspiration of infected fluid → Congenital pneumonia → Bacteremia.

### Pathogenesis of Late-Onset Sepsis
* **Horizontal Transmission (水平傳播):** Nosocomial (NICU environment) or community-acquired.
* Pathogens enter via indwelling lines (UVC, UAC, PICC), endotracheal tubes, or breached skin barriers.

### Common Pathogens in Neonatal Sepsis
| Sepsis Type | Primary Pathogens | Sources |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **EOS** | **Group B Streptococcus (GBS) (B型鏈球菌)**, *E. coli* (大腸桿菌), *Listeria monocytogenes* | Maternal genital tract, GI tract flora |
| **LOS** | **Coagulase-Negative Staph (CONS)**, *S. aureus*, *Klebsiella*, *Pseudomonas*, *Candida* | Hospital environment, hands of HCWs, indwelling lines |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### General & Non-Specific Signs of Sepsis
* **Temperature Instability (體溫不穩):** Hypothermia (<36.5°C) more common than fever (>38°C) in premies.
* **Lethargy (嗜睡):** ↓ spontaneous movement, hypotonia.
* **Feeding Intolerance (餵哺困難):** Poor suck, vomiting, abdominal distension, bilious aspirates.

### Respiratory Signs of Sepsis
* **Tachypnea (氣促):** >60 breaths/min.
* **Apnea (窒息):** Cessation of breathing >20s or + bradycardia/desaturation.
* **Respiratory Distress (呼吸窘迫):** Grunting, nasal flaring, subcostal recession.

### Cardiovascular Signs of Sepsis
* **Tachycardia (心跳過速) / Bradycardia (心跳過緩)**
* **Poor Perfusion (血液灌注不良):** Prolonged capillary refill time (>3s), mottling, pallor.
* **Hypotension (低血壓):** Late sign of decompensated shock.

### Neurological Signs of Sepsis
* **Irritability (煩躁不安)**
* **Seizures (抽搐)**
* **Bulging Fontanelle (囟門凸起):** Indicates meningitis.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Sepsis vs Transient Tachypnea of Newborn (TTN)
| Feature | Neonatal Sepsis | TTN (新生兒短暫性氣促) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Birth to anytime | Immediately post-delivery |
| **Risk Factors** | Maternal GBS +ve, PROM >18h, maternal fever | C-section without labor, maternal diabetes |
| **CXR Findings** | Patchy infiltrates, consolidation (if pneumonia) | Fluid in fissures, hyperinflation, prominent vasculature |
| **Clinical Course** | Progressive deterioration without Abx | Self-limiting, resolves within 24-48 hours |

### Differentiating Sepsis vs Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS)
| Feature | Neonatal Sepsis | RDS (呼吸窘迫綜合症) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gestation** | Any gestation | Primarily premature (<32 weeks) |
| **CXR Findings** | Variable (normal to consolidation) | Uniform reticulogranular (ground-glass) pattern, air bronchograms |
| **Response to Surfactant**| Variable / Poor | Rapid ↑ in oxygenation |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differentials for Neonatal Collapse
| Differential Diagnosis (鑑別診斷) | Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Inborn Errors of Metabolism (先天性代謝缺陷)** | Encephalopathy, severe metabolic acidosis, hyperammonemia, normal CRP. |
| **Duct-Dependent Congenital Heart Disease (先天性心臟病)** | Collapse on Day 2-3 (ductus arteriosus closes), profound cyanosis unaffected by 100% O2, absent femoral pulses (Coarctation). |
| **Intracranial Hemorrhage (顱內出血)** | Extreme prematurity, sudden drop in Hb, bulging fontanelle, acute neurological deterioration. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Septic Screen (敗血症篩查)
* **Blood Culture (血液培養):** Gold standard. Take BEFORE Abx. Min 1mL required.
* **Complete Blood Count (全血細胞計數):** Look for absolute neutropenia (↑ specificity for sepsis), ↑ immature-to-total (I/T) neutrophil ratio >0.2, thrombocytopenia.
* **C-Reactive Protein (CRP) (C-反應蛋白):** May be normal at birth. Serial measurements (e.g., 12h, 24h) more useful to monitor response/exclude sepsis.
* **Blood Gas (血液氣體分析):** Metabolic acidosis, ↑ lactate.

### Second-Line Investigations
* **Lumbar Puncture (LP) (腰椎穿刺):** Mandatory if blood culture +ve, clinical signs of meningitis (seizures, altered conscious level), or late-onset sepsis. Look for ↑ WCC, ↑ protein, ↓ glucose, +ve culture/PCR.
* **Chest X-Ray (CXR):** If respiratory signs present.
* **Urine Culture:** via suprapubic aspirate (SPA) or in/out catheter. Highly relevant for LOS; rarely indicated for EOS <72h.

### Maternal & Placental Investigations
* **High Vaginal Swab (HVS) (高位陰道拭子):** Maternal GBS status.
* **Placental Histology:** Check for chorioamnionitis (絨毛膜羊膜炎).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### HA Intrapartum Antibiotic Prophylaxis (IAP) Protocol
* **Indication:** Universal screening GBS +ve, previous infant with GBS disease, GBS bacteriuria in current pregnancy, or unknown GBS status with risk factors (preterm labor <37w, PROM >18h, maternal fever >38°C).
* **1st Line IAP:** **IV Penicillin G** (3 million units stat → 1.5 million units q4h until delivery).
* **Penicillin Allergy (Low risk):** IV Cefazolin.
* **Penicillin Allergy (High risk/Anaphylaxis):** IV Clindamycin or IV Vancomycin (guided by sensitivities).

### Acute Management of Early-Onset Sepsis (EOS)
* **1st Line Empirical Abx:** **IV Penicillin** (or Ampicillin) + **IV Gentamicin** (慶大黴素).
* Covers GBS, *Listeria*, and *E. coli*.
* Stop Abx at 48h if blood cultures -ve and infant clinically well with normal CRP.

### Acute Management of Late-Onset Sepsis (LOS)
* **1st Line Empirical Abx (HA NICU context):** **IV Cloxacillin** + **IV Cefotaxime** OR **IV Vancomycin** + **IV Ceftazidime** (depending on local NICU antibiogram for MRSA/Pseudomonas).
* Remove/replace infected central lines if CONS/fungal infection confirmed.

### Supportive Intensive Care
* **Respiratory:** CPAP or invasive mechanical ventilation for respiratory failure.
* **Cardiovascular:** IV fluid bolus (10-20 mL/kg 0.9% NaCl). Inotropes (**Dopamine** / **Dobutamine**) if refractory shock.
* **Metabolic:** Maintain normoglycemia (IV D10W). Correct acidosis.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Parental Communication & Counselling
* Explain need for NICU admission (新生兒深切治療部) and separation from mother.
* Clear updates on culture results and intended duration of Abx therapy.
* Promote maternal expressed breast milk (EBM) provision and skin-to-skin care (kangaroo care) when hemodynamically stable.

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* **Neonatologist/Pediatrician:** Direct acute medical management.
* **Neonatal Nurses:** Continuous vital sign monitoring, IV line care, medication administration.
* **Clinical Microbiologist:** Advise on antibiotic rationalization and stewardship.
* **Medical Social Worker (醫務社工):** Financial assistance, family support during prolonged NICU stay.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Identifying Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Mimicking Sepsis
* **Red Flag:** Acute encephalopathy/seizures with normal infectious markers and no perinatal risk factors.
* **Action:** Exclude Shaken Baby Syndrome (Abusive Head Trauma). Perform urgent cranial US/CT to look for subdural hemorrhage (硬膜下出血).
* **Red Flag:** Unexplained fractures on CXR (done for respiratory distress).

### Maternal Social Risk Factors
* Maternal IV drug use, lack of antenatal care, or syphilis/HIV +ve status → ↑ risk of congenital infections and neonatal neglect.
* Refer to **Medical Social Services (MSS)** and initiate HA vulnerable infant protocols prior to discharge.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications of Sepsis
* **Septic Shock (敗血性休克)** → Multi-organ failure.
* **Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC) (彌散性血管內凝血):** Petechiae, oozing from venepuncture sites, pulmonary hemorrhage.
* **Meningitis (腦膜炎):** Occurs in 5-10% of neonates with bacteremia.

### Long-Term Prognosis & Sequelae
* Survivors of neonatal meningitis have high risk of neurodevelopmental impairment.
* **Cerebral Palsy (腦性麻痺):** Secondary to hypoxic-ischemic damage from shock/meningitis.
* **Sensorineural Hearing Loss (感音神經性聽力喪失):** Requires BAEP (Brainstem Auditory Evoked Potentials) screening before discharge.
* **Seizure Disorders (癲癇):** Chronic epilepsy requiring antiepileptic drugs.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most reliable hematological marker for early onset neonatal sepsis? Answer: An elevated Immature-to-Total (I/T) neutrophil ratio (>0.2) or absolute neutropenia is more predictive than leukocytosis.
* Trap: Waiting for a positive blood culture to start antibiotics in a symptomatic neonate. Pearl: Neonatal sepsis can be rapidly fatal; administer empirical IV antibiotics immediately after drawing blood cultures if sepsis is clinically suspected.
* Pitfall: Performing a urine culture as part of the routine workup for Early-Onset Sepsis (<72h). Urinary tract infections are extremely rare in the first 3 days of life; reserve urine cultures for Late-Onset Sepsis evaluations.
* Viva Question: A term baby presents with seizures at 24 hours of life. The mother had prolonged rupture of membranes. What must be included in the empirical antibiotic regimen? Answer: IV Cefotaxime (in addition to Ampicillin) to ensure adequate CNS penetration for suspected meningitis, while avoiding Ceftriaxone due to the risk of biliary sludging and displacing bilirubin.
* Trap: Assuming a normal CRP at birth rules out early-onset sepsis. Pearl: CRP takes 12-24 hours to mount an inflammatory response; serial CRP measurements are required to accurately guide the discontinuation of antibiotics.
* Pitfall: Using Ceftriaxone in a jaundiced neonate. Ceftriaxone displaces bilirubin from albumin binding sites, increasing the risk of kernicterus; use Cefotaxime instead.
