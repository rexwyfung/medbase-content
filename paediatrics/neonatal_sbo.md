# Small Bowel Obstruction (e.g., Jejunal/Ileal Atresia) (小腸阻塞)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Small Bowel Obstruction
* **Congenital Occlusion**: Complete or partial blockage of the **small bowel lumen** (小腸腸腔).
* **Level of Obstruction**: Most commonly involves the **duodenum** (十二指腸), **jejunum** (空腸), or **ileum** (迴腸).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Duodenal Atresia (十二指腸閉鎖)**: High local incidence; strongly associated with **Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症)** (Trisomy 21). Frequently detected antenatally via **MCHC** (母嬰健康院) morphology scans.
* **Jejunoileal Atresia (空腸迴腸閉鎖)**: Presents equally frequently but with distinct etiology; typically isolated anomalies.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Duodenal Atresia
* **Embryological Failure**: Failure of **recanalization** (重新管化) / vacuolization of embryonic bowel at 8-10 weeks gestation.
* **Genetics**: High association (~30%) with chromosomal anomalies, specifically **Trisomy 21** (唐氏綜合症).

### Pathophysiology of Jejunoileal Atresia
* **In-utero Vascular Accident (子宮內血管意外)**: Late ischemic event (e.g., localized ischemia, volvulus, or intussusception during embryonic life) → segmental necrosis → resorption → atretic segment.
* **Genetics**: Not typically associated with chromosomal anomalies.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Cardinal Presentation of Small Bowel Obstruction
* **Bilious Vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)**: Green/yellow vomit presenting in the 1st 24-48 hrs of life.
* **Surgical Emergency**: Bilious vomiting in a neonate is a **SURGICAL EMERGENCY** until proven otherwise (must exclude midgut volvulus).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Cardinal Signs of Small Bowel Obstruction
* **Abdominal Distension (腹部脹大)**: Severity depends on obstruction level (mild in duodenal, massive in ileal).
* **Failure to Pass Meconium (無法排出胎糞)**: Absent or delayed meconium passage >24 hrs.
* **Visible Peristalsis**: May be observed across the abdominal wall in severe or distal obstructions.
* **Dehydration & Shock (脫水與休克)**: Tachycardia (↑ HR), prolonged capillary refill time, ↓ urine output if presentation is delayed.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing Level of Obstruction
| Clinical Feature | Duodenal Atresia (十二指腸閉鎖) | Jejunoileal Atresia (空腸迴腸閉鎖) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset of Vomiting** | Very early (first few hours) | Later (24-48 hours) |
| **Vomit Content** | Bilious (if post-ampullary) or non-bilious (if pre-ampullary) | Always bilious/feculent |
| **Abdominal Distension**| Minimal / Epigastric only | Massive / Generalized |
| **Associated Anomalies**| Down syndrome, congenital cardiac defects | Rare (usually isolated vascular accident) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Critical Differential Diagnoses for Neonatal Bilious Vomiting
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Malrotation with Midgut Volvulus (腸扭轉)** | **Must-Not-Miss**. Sudden onset bilious vomiting + shock/hemodynamic instability. Upper GI contrast → "corkscrew" sign. |
| **Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸梗阻)** | Thick, sticky meconium obstructing terminal ileum. High association with **Cystic Fibrosis (囊性纖維化)**. Microcolon on enema. |
| **Hirschsprung's Disease (巨結腸症)** | Delayed meconium >48 hrs. Explosive stool release upon PR exam. Contrast enema → transition zone. |
| **Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC) (壞死性小腸結腸炎)**| Preterm infant. Bloody stools, abdominal erythema. AXR → pneumatosis intestinalis. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Laboratory Investigations
* **Blood Gas**: Assess for metabolic alkalosis (secondary to severe vomiting) or metabolic acidosis (indicates ischemic bowel/lactate ↑).
* **Renal Function (RFT) / Electrolytes**: Check for ↓ Na+, ↓ K+, ↓ Cl− (due to fluid loss) and pre-renal failure.

### Gold Standard Imaging Investigations
* **Abdominal X-Ray (AXR)** (腹部X光):
  * **Duodenal**: **"Double bubble" sign (雙氣泡徵)** (gas in stomach & proximal duodenum, airless distally).
  * **Jejunoileal**: Few dilated loops of bowel + air-fluid levels + absent distal gas.

### Specialized Imaging Investigations
* **Contrast Enema (造影灌腸)**: Identifies **Microcolon (微小結腸)** (strongly suggests complete unused distal bowel, typical in ileal atresia or meconium ileus); rules out Hirschsprung's.
* **Upper GI Contrast Study**: **Must-do** to exclude malrotation/volvulus if diagnosis is uncertain.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Resuscitation & Pre-operative Management
* **NBM (Nil By Mouth) (禁食)**: Strict fasting immediately.
* **Gastric Decompression**: Insert large-bore **Nasogastric (NG) Tube (胃喉)** on free drainage → stops vomiting → ↓ aspiration risk.
* **IV Fluid Resuscitation**: Aggressive rehydration (Normal Saline 10-20mL/kg bolus if shocked).
* **Electrolyte Correction**: Replace ↓ Cl− and ↓ K+ via maintenance IVF.
* **IV Antibiotics**: Broad-spectrum prophylactic cover (HA protocol: Ampicillin + Gentamicin + Metronidazole).

### Definitive Surgical Treatment
* **Pediatric Surgical Review**: Urgent consult for **Laparotomy (剖腹探查術)**.
* **Resection & Anastomosis**: Resection of atretic/dilated segment → primary anastomosis.
* **Temporary Stoma (造口)**: Formed if bowel is severely dilated, unhealthy, or neonate is hemodynamically unstable.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* **Pediatric Dietitians (營養師)**: Crucial for guiding post-op enteral feed advancement and managing specialized Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN).
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Assists with financial support for prolonged NICU/PICU stay.
* **Maternal & Child Health Centre (MCHC) (母嬰健康院)**: Facilitates long-term community monitoring of growth and developmental milestones.

### Psychosocial Support for Parents
* **Parental Training**: Extensive education required for home stoma care or long-term central venous catheter (CVC) care if TPN dependent.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations
* **Post-Op Wound Care**: Vigilant post-op wound/stoma care requires highly reliable parental capability; inability to cope triggers SWD (Social Welfare Department) referral.
* **Medical Neglect**: Repeatedly missing HA outpatient follow-ups or failing to adhere to strict dietary/TPN protocols mandates urgent safeguarding evaluation.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Post-operative Complications
* Anastomotic leak → peritonitis / sepsis.
* Prolonged postoperative ileus.

### Long-term Complications & Prognosis
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (SBS) (短腸綜合症)**: Occurs if large segments of bowel are resected → profound malabsorption → failure to thrive.
* **TPN Dependence**: Long-term reliance ↑ risk of **Total Parenteral Nutrition-Associated Liver Disease (PNALD)** and CVC line sepsis.
* **Adhesive Bowel Obstruction (黏連性腸阻塞)**: Lifelong risk following major abdominal surgery.
* **Prognosis**: Generally excellent (>90% survival) with early intervention, provided adequate residual bowel length is preserved.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Assuming bilious vomiting in a neonate is "just feeding intolerance".
* Pearl: Bilious vomiting in a newborn is a SURGICAL EMERGENCY until proven otherwise. You must urgently exclude midgut volvulus.
* Viva Question: Why does jejunal atresia happen vs duodenal atresia?
* Answer: Jejunal atresia is due to a late mesenteric vascular accident in-utero; Duodenal atresia is an early embryological failure of vacuolization/recanalization.
* Pitfall: Discharging a bilious vomiting neonate without a pediatric surgical review and contrast study. This delays the diagnosis of volvulus, leading to irreversible bowel necrosis.
* Viva Question: What classical finding is seen on the abdominal X-ray of a neonate with duodenal atresia?
* Answer: The "double bubble" sign, representing gas in the stomach and proximal duodenum with no distal gas.
* Trap: Waiting for contrast studies in a hemodynamically unstable neonate with bilious vomiting and a rigid abdomen.
* Pearl: If the neonate is shocked and exhibits signs of peritonitis, proceed directly to emergency laparotomy for suspected ischemic bowel/volvulus without delaying for imaging.
