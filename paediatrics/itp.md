# Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP) (免疫性血小板減少性紫癜)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of ITP
* Acquired, immune-mediated isolated thrombocytopenia (血小板減少症) 
* Platelet count < 100 x 10^9/L 
* Diagnosis of exclusion (must rule out other causes of ↓ platelets)

### Local Epidemiology of ITP
* Most common cause of acute onset thrombocytopenia in otherwise well children in HK HA hospitals
* Peak age: 2-5 years
* Seasonal variation: often follows viral peaks (winter/spring)

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Mechanism of Platelet Destruction
* Autoantibodies (usually IgG) target platelet surface antigens (e.g., GPIIb/IIIa (血小板糖蛋白IIb/IIIa))
* Antibody-coated platelets undergo premature destruction by macrophages (巨噬細胞)
* Primary site of destruction: Reticuloendothelial system → Spleen (脾臟)

### Triggers of ITP
* Preceding viral infection (e.g., URTI, Varicella (水痘), EBV) 1-4 weeks prior
* Post-vaccination (e.g., MMR) 1-4 weeks prior (rare)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Bleeding Manifestations in ITP
* Sudden onset in previously healthy, active child
* Cutaneous: Bruising (瘀斑), petechiae (瘀點), purpura (紫癜)
* Mucosal: 
  * Epistaxis (鼻出血)
  * Bleeding gums (牙齦出血)
  * Haematuria (血尿)
  * Menorrhagia (月經過多) in adolescent girls
  * GI bleeding (melaena/haematochezia) (rare but severe)

### Critical Negative Signs in ITP
* Child is otherwise completely well
* NO hepatosplenomegaly (肝脾腫大)
* NO lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)
* NO bone pain (骨痛)
* NO fever/lethargy (unless concurrent mild viral illness)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing ITP from Acute Lymphoblastic Leukaemia (ALL)
* **ALL**: Patient is unwell (fever, lethargy, bone pain, night sweats) +ve hepatosplenomegaly +ve lymphadenopathy. CBC shows abnormal WBC (blasts) & ↓ Hb.
* **ITP**: Patient is completely well. No organomegaly. Normal WBC & Hb.

### Distinguishing ITP from Henoch-Schönlein Purpura (HSP)
* **HSP (過敏性紫癜)**: Purpura is **palpable** & concentrated on gravity-dependent areas (buttocks/lower limbs). Accompanied by arthritis, colicky abdominal pain, nephritis. Platelet count = NORMAL or ↑.
* **ITP**: Purpura is **non-palpable** & generalized. Platelet count = ↓↓↓.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis Table for Acute Childhood Purpura/Thrombocytopenia
| Condition | Key Clinical Features | Diagnostic Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Acute ITP** | Well child, sudden petechiae, viral prodrome | Isolated ↓ Platelets, normal Hb/WBC |
| **Leukaemia (ALL/AML)** | Unwell, bone pain, organomegaly, fever | Pancytopenia, blasts on blood film/marrow |
| **Aplastic Anaemia (再生障礙性貧血)** | Insidious onset, pallor, frequent infections | Pancytopenia (↓ Hb, ↓ WBC, ↓ Plt), hypocellular marrow |
| **HUS (溶血性尿毒症綜合徵)** | Bloody diarrhoea prodrome, oliguria | Microangiopathic haemolytic anaemia (MAHA), schistocytes, ↑ Cr/Urea |
| **NAI (非意外傷害)** | Bruising in atypical sites (ears, neck, torso), inconsistent history | NORMAL platelet count, normal coagulation profile |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Blood Investigations for ITP
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: 
  * Isolated thrombocytopenia (<100 x 10^9/L)
  * Haemoglobin (Hb) MUST be normal (mild ↓ acceptable if heavy epistaxis)
  * White Cell Count (WBC) MUST be normal
* **Peripheral Blood Smear (周邊血液塗片)**: 
  * CRUCIAL to confirm true thrombocytopenia & rule out platelet clumping
  * Megathrombocytes (巨大血小板) commonly seen (indicates active marrow production)
  * MUST rule out blasts (Leukaemia) or schistocytes (HUS)
* **Blood Type & Screen**: Important if heavy bleeding or considering Anti-D therapy

### Indications for Bone Marrow Aspiration in ITP
* NOT routinely required for typical acute ITP
* Indicated if:
  * Atypical features present (abnormal WBC/Hb, hepatosplenomegaly, bone pain)
  * Failure to respond to 1st-line therapy (IVIG/Steroids)
  * **HA Protocol Mandate**: Prior to starting Corticosteroids (to ensure partial treatment doesn't mask ALL blasts)

### Monitoring Investigations for ITP
* Serial CBCs to monitor platelet count trajectory

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### First-Line Management of Mild ITP
* **Observation**: 
  * 1st-line for vast majority with mild/moderate skin bleeding only
  * Decision based on bleeding severity, NOT absolute platelet count
  * Spontaneous recovery in 80% within 6 months

### Medical Intervention for Severe ITP
* Indicated for significant mucosal bleeding (e.g., prolonged epistaxis) or life-threatening bleeding
* **IV Immunoglobulin (IVIG) (靜脈注射免疫球蛋白)**: 
  * 0.8–1 g/kg x 1-2 doses
  * Rapidly ↑ platelet count (blocks Fc receptors in spleen)
  * Expensive, requires inpatient admission
* **Corticosteroids (Oral Prednisolone) (皮質類固醇)**: 
  * Cheaper, effective
  * Requires Bone Marrow Aspirate first (local HA safeguard to rule out Leukaemia)
* **Anti-D Immunoglobulin**: 
  * Option for Rh-positive (Rh+ve) intact spleen patients
  * Risk of severe haemolysis (溶血); observe closely

### Management of Intracranial Haemorrhage (ICH) in ITP
* Feared, rare (<1%) emergency (headache, vomiting, altered GCS)
* Immediate CT Brain
* **Medical Resuscitation**:
  * **IVIG** + **IV Methylprednisolone** (pulse dose)
  * **Platelet Transfusion (血小板輸注)** (rapidly destroyed but provides transient life-saving haemostasis)
  * Emergency neurosurgical consult for evacuation

### Management of Chronic ITP (>12 months)
* **2nd-Line Therapies**:
  * Rituximab (Anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody)
  * Thrombopoietin (TPO) receptor agonists (Eltrombopag, Romiplostim)
* **Surgical**:
  * Splenectomy (脾臟切除術) (rarely performed now, delayed until >5 yrs old to ↓ sepsis risk)

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement in ITP
* Not generally required for acute self-limiting cases
* **Haematologist**: Referral mandatory for chronic ITP, atypical presentations, or refractory bleeding
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Support if severe financial burden from repeated admissions or expensive 2nd-line biologics

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safety Netting & Precautions in ITP
* Advise parents/school:
  * Avoid contact sports/trampolines (restrict physical activity)
  * Avoid NSAIDs/Aspirin (interfere with platelet function) → Use Paracetamol (撲熱息痛) for pain/fever
  * Avoid IM injections
* Provide open ward access/A&E card for immediate assessment if head trauma or prolonged bleeding occurs

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Red Flags in Purpura
* Bruising in unusual, protected locations (e.g., ears, neck, inner thighs, genitalia)
* Patterned bruising (e.g., handprints, belt marks)
* Normal CBC/Platelet count despite extensive clinical bruising

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Prognosis of Acute ITP
* Excellent prognosis
* ~80% resolve spontaneously within 6 months

### Complications and Chronic ITP
* **Chronic ITP**: ~20% progress to chronicity (>12 months). Higher risk in older children (>10 yrs) and adolescents
* **Severe Bleeding**: Intracranial Haemorrhage (ICH) (顱內出血) is very rare (<1%) but carries high mortality/morbidity

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Transfusing platelets for a well child with ITP and a platelet count of 5.
* Pearl: Platelet transfusions are generally CONTRAINDICATED in ITP because the circulating autoantibodies will immediately destroy the transfused platelets. They are strictly reserved for life-threatening emergencies (e.g., ICH) alongside IVIG/steroids.
* Viva Question: "A 3-year-old has petechiae and a platelet count of 10. Hb and WBC are normal. The blood film shows no blasts. He is playing happily. What is the treatment?"
* Answer: Observation and safety netting. Treatment in ITP is dictated by the severity of clinical bleeding symptoms, not the absolute numerical platelet count.
* Pitfall: Starting empirical oral prednisolone for ITP without confirming the diagnosis in HK HA hospitals. You risk partially treating and masking an underlying Acute Lymphoblastic Leukaemia (ALL), permanently delaying life-saving chemotherapy. Always perform a bone marrow aspirate before starting steroids if typical ITP criteria aren't perfectly met.
