# Cardiac Arrhythmias (心律不正)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Arrhythmias
* **Definition**: Any abnormality in the rate, regularity, origin, or conduction of the cardiac electrical impulse.
### Local Epidemiology of Paediatric Arrhythmias
* **Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT) (室上性心搏過速)**: The most common symptomatic arrhythmia in children requiring Hospital Authority (HA) Emergency Department attendance.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)
* **Mechanism**: Almost always driven by an accessory re-entry pathway (e.g., Atrioventricular Re-entrant Tachycardia [AVRT] in **Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) syndrome (沃夫-帕金森-懷特症候群)**) or Atrioventricular Nodal Re-entrant Tachycardia (**AVNRT**).
### Genetics of Long QT Syndrome (LQTS) (長QT綜合症)
* **Channelopathies**: Inherited genetic defects in myocardial ion channels causing delayed ventricular repolarization.
* **Romano-Ward Syndrome**: Autosomal dominant inheritance, purely cardiac phenotype.
* **Jervell and Lange-Nielsen Syndrome**: Autosomal recessive inheritance, +ve sensorineural deafness.
* **Pathophysiological Risk**: Prolonged QTc → **Torsades de Pointes (多形性心室性心動過速)** → Ventricular Fibrillation (VF) → Sudden Cardiac Death (SCD).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Clinical Presentation in Infants
* Often unrecognized until clinical decompensation and heart failure.
* **Symptoms**: Poor feeding, pallor, extreme lethargy, sweating, tachypnea.
### Clinical Presentation in Older Children
* **Symptoms**: Palpitations ("heart racing"), chest pain, dizziness, syncope (especially during exercise).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Vital Sign Thresholds for SVT
* **Infants**: HR continuously **>220 bpm**.
* **Older Children**: HR continuously **>180 bpm**.
### Heart Rate Characteristics in SVT
* **Rhythm**: Rigidly regular; does NOT vary or fluctuate with crying, sleeping, or activity.
### Signs of Cardiogenic Shock (心源性休克) in Prolonged SVT
* Occurs if the arrhythmia remains unrecognized for **>24-48 hours**.
* **Signs**: ↓ Perfusion, capillary refill time >2 seconds, mottling, absent/weak peripheral pulses, hepatomegaly, ↓ consciousness.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### SVT vs Sinus Tachycardia (竇性心搏過速)

| Discriminatory Feature | SVT (室上性心搏過速) | Sinus Tachycardia (竇性心搏過速) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **History** | Abrupt onset and termination | Gradual onset (e.g., +ve fever, pain, hypovolemia, sepsis) |
| **Infant Heart Rate** | > 220 bpm | < 220 bpm |
| **Child Heart Rate** | > 180 bpm | < 180 bpm |
| **Rate Variability** | Rigidly fixed rate | Fluctuates with activity/crying |
| **ECG P-waves** | Absent or retrograde | Normal P-wave visible before every QRS |

### Other Paediatric Tachycardias
* **Ventricular Tachycardia (VT) (心室性心搏過速)**: Incredibly rare in children unless +ve underlying structural heart disease, post-cardiac surgery, or severe systemic derangement.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Acute Bedside Investigations
* **Definitive Investigation**: **12-lead ECG DURING the event**. 
* **Classic SVT ECG Findings**: Narrow complex, rigidly regular, absent P waves, HR >220 (infants).
### Post-Termination Investigations
* **Post-Cardioversion ECG**: Crucial for identifying underlying structural/electrical anomalies.
* **Target Findings**: **Delta waves** and short PR interval (WPW syndrome), prolonged QTc (LQTS).
### Imaging & Special Investigations
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: Mandatory to rule out structural heart disease (e.g., **Ebstein anomaly** associated with WPW).
* **24-hour Holter Monitor**: For fleeting palpitations not captured in the AED.
* **Implantable Loop Recorder (ILR)**: For highly symptomatic, infrequent syncope/palpitations escaping Holter detection.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Unstable Management (Cardiogenic Shock)
* **Clinical Status**: Shock, poor perfusion, hypotension, ↓ GCS.
* **Gold Standard Action**: Immediate **Synchronized DC Cardioversion (同步直流電復律)**.
* **Dosing**: Start at **0.5 - 1 J/kg** (escalate to 2 J/kg if refractory).
### Acute Stable Management: Vagal Maneuvers
* **Infants**: Apply an ice-water bag directly to the face for ~10 seconds to simulate the diving reflex.
* **Older Children**: Valsalva maneuver (e.g., blowing hard into a 20ml syringe).
### Acute Stable Management: Medical Cardioversion
* **1st Line Medical Therapy**: **Adenosine (腺苷)** IV rapid push.
* **Dose**: Start **100-150 mcg/kg** (up to max 6mg initial dose).
* **Administration**: Fast IV push via a large vein closest to the heart → IMMEDIATELY followed by a rapid 0.9% NaCl flush.
* **HA Protocol Note**: Always warn parents beforehand: "The baby's heart will pause for a few seconds, they will turn grey, then it will restart normally."
### Chronic Management & Curative Therapy
* **Medical Prophylaxis**: **Amiodarone (胺碘酮)**, **Flecainide**, or **Sotalol**. 
* **Curative Therapy**: **Radiofrequency Catheter Ablation (射頻導管消融術)** of the accessory pathway.
* **Surgical Timing**: Usually deferred until the child is **>15kg** / older to ↓ complication risks (e.g., AV node damage).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Parent Education & Empowerment
* **Vigilance Training**: Train parents to check infant heart rates manually or via wearables.
* **Home Management**: Teach safe home vagal maneuvers (e.g., ice-water technique) for future episodes.
### MDT Involvement
* **Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) (母嬰健康院)**: Routine developmental monitoring and parental reassurance.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Psychosocial support for severe parental anxiety and financial assessment for ILR devices or ablation procedures outside HA standard safety net.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Toxicology & Substance Abuse Red Flags
* Always consider toxicology/drug screening in older children and teens presenting with new-onset, unexplained arrhythmias.
* **Specific Toxins**: Amphetamines, excessive caffeine (energy drinks), and OTC cold medicines containing **pseudoephedrine**.
* **Action**: Involve **Social Welfare Department (SWD) (社會福利署)** if illicit substance abuse or medical neglect is identified.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Acute Complications
* **Heart Failure / Cardiogenic Shock**: Rapid decompensation if SVT is unrecognized for >24-48 hours in infants.
* **Sudden Cardiac Death (SCD) (猝死)**: High risk in untreated Long QT syndrome or unchecked WPW (if AFib degenerates into VFib).
### Long-Term Prognosis
* **Infant-Onset SVT**: Excellent prognosis; the majority of infants will "grow out of it" by age 1 year due to spontaneous accessory pathway regression.
* **Adolescent-Onset SVT**: Rarely resolves spontaneously; typically requires definitive catheter ablation.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Attempting carotid massage or applying eyeball pressure in an infant for vagal maneuvers. Pearl: It is completely contraindicated due to risks of retinal detachment and carotid injury. Always use the facial ice-water technique.
* Viva Question: Why must Adenosine be given as a rapid IV bolus followed immediately by a rapid saline flush? Answer: Adenosine has an extraordinarily short half-life (< 10 seconds). If pushed slowly, it will be rapidly degraded by red blood cell enzymes before it reaches the AV node.
* Pitfall: Prescribing Digoxin or Verapamil for an infant with SVT and underlying Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) syndrome. These AV-nodal blocking agents can paradoxically force conduction down the accessory pathway, leading to fatal Ventricular Fibrillation (VF).
* Viva Question: What classic triad of ECG features is pathognomonic for Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) syndrome in sinus rhythm? Answer: A short PR interval, a widened QRS complex, and a slurred upstroke of the QRS complex known as a Delta wave.
