# Acne Vulgaris (尋常痤瘡)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Acne Vulgaris
* Chronic inflammatory disease of **pilosebaceous unit (毛囊皮脂腺)**
* Characterized by polymorphic lesions: **comedones (粉刺)**, **papules (丘疹)**, **pustules (膿疱)**, and **nodules/cysts (結節/囊腫)**

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Peak incidence in **adolescence (青春期)** (males 16-19 yrs; females 14-17 yrs)
* Extremely high prevalence (~80-90%) in HK teenagers
* Exacerbated by local **hot, humid climate (炎熱潮濕氣候)** 
* ↑ risk of **Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH) (炎症後色素沉著)** in HK's predominantly Fitzpatrick Type III/IV Asian skin

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiological Cascade of Acne
* **↑ Sebum production (皮脂分泌過多):** Driven by androgen surges during puberty
* **Altered follicular keratinization:** → hyperkeratosis → microcomedone formation
* ***Cutibacterium acnes* proliferation:** Commensal anaerobic bacteria multiply in sebum-rich environment
* **Inflammatory response:** *C. acnes* releases lipases/chemokines → follicular rupture → robust immune response (neutrophils/macrophages)

### Genetic & Hormonal Factors in Acne
* **Genetics:** Strong +ve family history highly predictive of severe/cystic phenotype
* **Hormones:** ↑ sensitivity of sebaceous glands to circulating androgens (e.g., DHT)
* Associated with **Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) (多囊卵巢綜合症)**

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Non-Inflammatory Lesions
* **Closed comedones (白頭粉刺):** Small, skin-colored bumps (1-2mm) without visible opening
* **Open comedones (黑頭粉刺):** Dilated follicular ostium filled with oxidized lipid/keratin (not dirt)

### Inflammatory Lesions
* **Papules (丘疹):** Erythematous, tender bumps (<5mm)
* **Pustules (膿疱):** Papules topped with visible pus
* **Nodules (結節):** Deep, painful, indurated lesions (>5mm)
* **Cysts (囊腫):** Fluctuant, deep nodules 

### Clinical Distribution
* Sebaceous-rich areas: **Face (臉部)**, **chest (胸部)**, **upper back (上背)**, **shoulders (肩膀)**

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Pathognomonic Features of Acne Vulgaris
* **Presence of Comedones:** Absolute requirement for definitive diagnosis of Acne Vulgaris
* **Polymorphic presentation:** Simultaneous mixture of comedones, papules, pustules, and scars in various stages of healing

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Key Differentials for Acneiform Eruptions
| Condition (English / Chinese) | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Malassezia Folliculitis (糠秕孢子菌毛囊炎)** | Pruritic, monomorphic papulopustules on chest/back; **No comedones**; Common in HK summers |
| **Rosacea (玫瑰痤瘡)** | Erythema, telangiectasia, flushing, older onset (30-50s); **No comedones**; Exacerbated by spicy food/alcohol |
| **Perioral Dermatitis (口周皮炎)** | Erythematous papules/vesicles around mouth; **Spares vermillion border**; Often triggered by topical steroids |
| **Drug-Induced Acne (藥物引發的痤瘡)** | Monomorphic eruption; Sudden onset; Associated with **Corticosteroids (類固醇)**, Lithium, Anticonvulsants |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Primary Investigations for Uncomplicated Acne
* **Clinical diagnosis (臨床診斷):** Predominantly visual assessment; no routine labs required

### Tiered Investigations for Suspected Underlying Pathology
* **Hormonal Profile:** If signs of hyperandrogenism (hirsutism, irregular menses, deep voice) → investigate for PCOS or Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia
* **Free & Total Testosterone**
* **LH/FSH ratio** (often >2:1 in PCOS)
* **DHEAS** (adrenal androgen)
* **Pelvic Ultrasound (盆腔超聲波):** To visualize polycystic ovaries

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Non-Pharmacological Management & Skincare
* Gentle cleansing BID with syndet (synthetic detergent) bars
* Avoid aggressive scrubbing → worsens inflammation
* Non-comedogenic cosmetics/sunscreens

### Topical Management of Mild Acne
| Medication Class | Examples | Mechanism & Clinical Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Topical Retinoids (外用維他命A酸)** | **Adapalene (阿達帕林)**, Tretinoin | Comedolytic & anti-inflammatory. Apply at night. Causes initial irritation/photosensitivity. |
| **Topical Antimicrobials** | **Benzoyl Peroxide (BPO) (過氧化苯甲酰)** | Antibacterial (*C. acnes*), comedolytic. Bleaches fabric. No bacterial resistance. |
| **Topical Antibiotics** | **Clindamycin 1% (克林黴素)** | Anti-inflammatory & antibacterial. **Must combine with BPO** to prevent resistance. |

### Systemic Management of Moderate Acne
* **Oral Antibiotics (口服抗生素):** 
  * 1st Line: **Doxycycline (多西環素)** 100mg daily OR **Lymecycline** 300mg daily
  * **Contraindicated <8 years old & pregnancy** (teeth staining / bone growth inhibition)
  * Max duration: 3-4 months to prevent antimicrobial resistance
  * *Always* co-prescribe with topical BPO/Retinoid
* **Hormonal Therapy (荷爾蒙治療):** Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCP) for females, esp. if acne flares premenstrually or PCOS present

### HA Dermatology Protocol for Severe/Nodulocystic Acne
* **Oral Isotretinoin (口服異維A酸):** 
  * Indicated for severe cystic acne, scarring acne, or moderate acne failing systemic antibiotics
  * Requires **HA Dermatologist (衛生署/醫管局皮膚科醫生)** prescription/supervision in public system
  * Teratogenic: Strict contraception required
  * Requires regular monitoring: LFTs, Fasting Lipids
  * Dose: 0.5 - 1.0 mg/kg/day (cumulative target 120-150 mg/kg)

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Psychosocial Impact of Acne
* ↑ Risk of **Depression (抑鬱)**, anxiety, and social isolation
* Severely impacts self-esteem during vulnerable adolescent years
* Monitor actively for **Suicidal Ideation (自殺傾向)** (particularly when initiating systemic treatments like isotretinoin, despite debated causality)

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Involvement
* General Practitioner / Paediatrician for initial management
* Dermatologist for severe/refractory cases
* Clinical Psychologist/Psychiatrist if severe affective symptoms present

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Endocrine & Safeguarding Red Flags
* **Pre-pubertal onset (青春期前發病):** Severe acne in a child <8 years old is abnormal → Must rule out precocious puberty, adrenal tumors, or virilizing conditions
* **Exogenous Steroid/Androgen Abuse:** Consider in adolescent athletes presenting with sudden, severe, monomorphic acne on trunk (anabolic steroid abuse)
* **Medical Neglect (醫療疏忽):** Severe, scarring, painful cystic acne with parents refusing standard medical therapies, causing profound psychological harm to the child

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Structural Complications of Acne
* **Atrophic Scarring (萎縮性疤痕):**
  * **Ice-pick (冰錐型):** Deep, narrow (<2mm) 
  * **Boxcar (車廂型):** Wider, sharply demarcated vertical edges
  * **Rolling (滾動型):** Broad depressions with sloping edges
* **Hypertrophic/Keloid Scars (增生性/蟹足腫疤痕):** Common on chest and upper back

### Pigmentary Complications
* **Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH):** 
  * Extremely prevalent complication in HK (Asian skin types)
  * Can take months/years to fade
  * Prevention: Early aggressive control of inflammation & daily sunscreen use

## 11. Examiner's Pearls 

* Viva Question: What is the absolute contraindication for prescribing oral tetracyclines in pediatric acne? Answer: Children under 8 years of age and pregnant/breastfeeding women, due to permanent dental discoloration and inhibition of fetal skeletal growth.
* Pitfall: Prescribing topical clindamycin monotherapy for chronic acne. This rapidy induces *C. acnes* antibiotic resistance; always co-prescribe with Benzoyl Peroxide.
* Trap: Assuming a sudden outbreak of monomorphic, itchy papulopustules on an athlete's back in summer is simply exacerbation of acne vulgaris. Pearl: This is classic for Malassezia (Pityrosporum) folliculitis; look for the absence of comedones and treat with topical antifungals.
* Viva Question: A female patient on oral isotretinoin presents with headache and blurred vision. What is the diagnosis and what common concomitant medication must be asked about? Answer: Benign Intracranial Hypertension (Pseudotumor Cerebri). Must ask if she is concurrently taking oral tetracyclines, as the combination massively increases this risk.
* Pitfall: Halting isotretinoin therapy immediately if mild, transient transaminitis occurs. Mild LFT elevation is expected; generally, treatment is only halted or dose-reduced if transaminases exceed 2.5-3 times the upper limit of normal.
