# Haemophilia A and B (血友病 甲型及乙型)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Haemophilia (血友病定義)
* Inherited bleeding disorders caused by congenital deficiency of specific coagulation factors.
* **Haemophilia A (甲型血友病)**: Deficiency of **Factor VIII (第八凝血因子)**.
* **Haemophilia B (乙型血友病)**: Deficiency of **Factor IX (第九凝血因子)** (Christmas Disease).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (香港本地流行病學)
* Haemophilia A is ~5-6x more common than B (~85% vs ~15%).
* Prevalence: ~1 in 5,000 live male births (Haem A); ~1 in 30,000 (Haem B).
* Centralised care in HK HA system: Managed primarily at **Comprehensive Haemophilia Care Centres (血友病綜合護理中心)** (e.g., Prince of Wales Hospital, Queen Elizabeth Hospital).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Inheritance (遺傳機制)
* **X-linked recessive (X染色體隱性遺傳)**.
* Exclusively affects males; females are typically asymptomatic carriers.
* ~30% of cases arise from *de novo* spontaneous mutations (no family history).
* Female carriers can rarely exhibit mild bleeding symptoms due to skewed **Lyonization / X-inactivation (X染色體去活化)**.

### Coagulation Cascade Defect (凝血級聯缺陷)
* Deficiency of FVIII or FIX disrupts the **Intrinsic Pathway (內在凝血途徑)**.
* Impaired Tenase complex formation → ↓ **Thrombin (凝血酶)** generation.
* Results in failure to form a stable cross-linked fibrin clot → delayed, prolonged, or spontaneous haemorrhage.

### Disease Severity Classification (疾病嚴重程度分類)

| Severity (嚴重程度) | Factor Level | Clinical Bleeding Phenotype (臨床出血表型) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Severe (重度)** | <1% | Frequent spontaneous bleeds (joints/muscles) from early childhood. |
| **Moderate (中度)** | 1-5% | Bleeds secondary to minor trauma; spontaneous bleeds are rare. |
| **Mild (輕度)** | 5-40% | Bleeds only after major trauma, surgery, or dental extractions. |

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Neonatal Presentation (新生兒表現)
* Often asymptomatic at birth if no trauma.
* Prolonged oozing from heel pricks, venepuncture, or circumcision.
* **Cephalhaematoma (頭顱血腫)** or subgaleal bleed post-instrumental delivery.
* **Intracranial Haemorrhage (ICH) (顱內出血)**: Rare but devastating; suspect in unexplained neonatal lethargy/seizures.

### Infant & Toddler Presentation (嬰幼兒表現)
* Onset coincides with increased mobility (crawling/walking).
* Prominent, easy bruising on buttocks/legs.
* Prolonged bleeding from frenulum tears or lip/tongue bites.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Haemarthrosis (關節積血) (Hallmark Feature)
* Spontaneous bleeding into synovial hinge joints (**knees, ankles, elbows**).
* Acute signs: Rapidly swollen, warm, exquisitely painful joint.
* Patient holds joint in flexion to ↓ capsular pressure; severely restricted ROM.
* **Target Joint (靶關節)**: Recurrent bleeds in the same joint → chronic inflammation.

### Deep Muscle Haematomas (深部肌肉血腫)
* Intramuscular bleeds expanding within fascial planes.
* **Iliopsoas Bleed (髂腰肌出血)**: Presents with groin/lower abdominal pain, hip flexion contracture, and severe pain on hip extension (lumbar plexus compression).
* Forearm/Calf bleeds: High risk for vascular compromise.

### Mucosal & Surgical Bleeding (黏膜及手術出血)
* Persistent oozing post-dental extraction, tonsillectomy, or trauma.
* Epistaxis and GI bleeding (less frequent than in vWD).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Haemophilia vs Von Willebrand Disease (血友病與類血友病之鑒別)

| Feature | Haemophilia A/B | Von Willebrand Disease (vWD) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inheritance** | X-linked recessive (Males) | Autosomal Dominant (Males = Females) |
| **Bleeding Type** | Deep tissue (Joints, Muscles) | Mucocutaneous (Epistaxis, Menorrhagia, Gums) |
| **Primary Defect** | Secondary Haemostasis (Coagulation) | Primary Haemostasis (Platelet Adhesion) |
| **Bleeding Time** | Normal | Prolonged |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Most Likely Differentials (最有可能的鑒別診斷)
* **Haemophilia A vs B**: Clinically indistinguishable; differentiation requires specific factor assays.
* **Von Willebrand Disease (類血友病)**: The most common inherited bleeding disorder; typically milder mucosal bleeds.

### Must-Not-Miss Emergencies (絕不能漏診的急症)
* **Intracranial Haemorrhage (顱內出血)**: Any head injury in a haemophiliac = high-risk. Treat before imaging.
* **Airway Compromise (呼吸道阻塞)**: Bleeding into neck, mouth floor, or retropharyngeal space can rapidly occlude the airway.
* **Compartment Syndrome (骨筋膜室綜合症)**: From deep muscle haematomas (esp. forearm/calf) causing neurovascular compromise.

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Coagulation Profile (床邊凝血功能檢查)
* **APTT (活化部分凝血活酶時間)**: ↑ Prolonged (reflects intrinsic pathway defect).
* **PT (凝血酶原時間)**: Normal (extrinsic pathway intact).
* **Platelet Count (血小板計數)**: Normal.
* **Bleeding Time / PFA-100 (出血時間)**: Normal (platelet plug formation is unaffected).

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Assays (黃金標準診斷化驗)
* **Factor VIII & IX Assays (第八/九因子檢測)**: Measures exact % activity to confirm diagnosis and stratify severity.
* **vWF Antigen & Activity**: Must be checked to rule out vWD (which can also cause mildly ↓ FVIII).

### Advanced & Genetic Testing (進階及基因檢測)
* **Inhibitor Screen (抗體篩查)**: Bethesda assay to detect IgG alloantibodies against replaced factor.
* **Molecular Genetic Testing (分子基因檢測)**: Identifies specific mutation (e.g., FVIII intron 22 inversion) for definitive diagnosis, carrier screening, and prenatal testing.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Bleed Management (急性出血急救處理)
* **GOLDEN RULE**: For suspected severe bleeds (Head, Neck, Chest, Abdomen, Pelvis, major joint) → **REPLACE FACTOR IMMEDIATELY (立即補充凝血因子)**. Do NOT delay treatment for imaging or lab results.
* **Factor Target Levels**:
  * Life-threatening/CNS bleed: Target 100% factor activity.
  * Joint/Muscle bleed: Target 40-50% factor activity.
* **First Aid**: RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) + immobilisation.

### HA Standard Prophylaxis (醫管局標準預防性治療)
* **Goal**: Convert severe phenotype to moderate phenotype by keeping trough factor levels >1-3% to prevent spontaneous haemarthroses.
* **Recombinant Factor VIII / IX (重組凝血因子)**: Regular IV infusions 2-3 times/week. Often requires Central Venous Access Device (Port-A-Cath) in young children.
* **Emicizumab / Hemlibra (艾美賽珠單抗)**: Bispecific monoclonal antibody mimicking FVIII. Given subcutaneously (weekly to monthly). Highly promoted in HK HA for Haem A (with or without inhibitors) to drastically ↓ IV infusion burden.

### Adjunctive Pharmacotherapy (輔助藥物治療)
* **Desmopressin / DDAVP (去氨加壓素)**: Stimulates release of endogenous FVIII and vWF from endothelial cells. Used ONLY for **Mild Haemophilia A**. Ineffective in severe Haem A or any Haem B. Fluid restrict to prevent hyponatraemia.
* **Tranexamic Acid (氨甲環酸)**: Antifibrinolytic agent. Highly effective adjunct for oral, dental, or mucosal bleeds.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Structure (跨專業醫療團隊)
* **Haematologist (血科醫生)**: Oversees factor protocols and inhibitor surveillance.
* **Physiotherapist (物理治療師)**: Crucial post-haemarthrosis to prevent muscle atrophy and restore joint ROM.
* **Orthopaedic Surgeon (骨科醫生)**: Manages chronic arthropathy (synovectomy, joint replacement).

### Psychosocial Support (心理社會支援)
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Financial assistance is critical. Prophylactic factors and Emicizumab are extremely expensive (supported by HA Samaritan Fund / Community Care Fund in HK).
* **Clinical Psychologist**: Coping with chronic illness, needle phobia.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) vs Bleeding Disorder (非意外損傷與出血性疾病)
* Always rule out severe bleeding disorders (check APTT/PT) in infants presenting with unexplained extensive bruising before diagnosing NAI.
* **NAI Red Flags**: Bruising in non-mobile infants, bruises in protected areas (ears, neck, torso), or recurrent bleeds despite adequate factor prophylaxis.

### Safety Netting & Prevention (安全網建議)
* Wear **MedicAlert Bracelet (醫療警報手帶)** at all times.
* Parents trained for home IV factor infusion via Port-A-Cath.
* Strictly **AVOID NSAIDs and Aspirin (避免使用非類固醇消炎藥及阿士匹靈)** → Use Paracetamol for pain.
* **AVOID Intramuscular (IM) Injections (避免肌肉注射)** → Administer routine childhood vaccines subcutaneously.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Factor Inhibitors (凝血因子抑制物)
* Most feared complication. Development of neutralising IgG alloantibodies against infused factor.
* Occurs in ~30% of severe Haemophilia A patients (rare in Haem B).
* **Clinical Clue**: Patient fails to respond to standard prophylactic or acute factor doses.
* **Management**: Requires bypass agents (e.g., Recombinant Factor VIIa / NovoSeven) or long-term Immune Tolerance Induction (ITI).

### Haemophilic Arthropathy (血友病性關節病)
* Irreversible, crippling joint destruction.
* Pathophysiology: Recurrent haemarthrosis → haemosiderin iron deposition in synovium → synovial hypertrophy and chronic inflammation → cartilage destruction and bone cysts.

### Transfusion-Transmitted Infections (輸血傳播感染)
* Historically: High rates of HIV, Hepatitis B, and Hepatitis C from pooled plasma.
* Modern Era: Risk virtually eliminated due to exclusive use of recombinant factor products in HK HA.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls

* Trap: Sending a child with haemophilia and a head injury for a CT scan before giving factor. Pearl: Factor first, CT second. Delaying factor replacement for imaging can be fatal.
* Viva Question: "What is the coagulation profile of a patient with Haemophilia A?" Answer: Prolonged APTT, normal PT, normal platelet count, normal bleeding time.
* Pitfall: Prescribing Ibuprofen for a painful swollen knee in a haemophiliac. Never prescribe NSAIDs as they inhibit platelet function and exacerbate bleeding; prescribe Paracetamol and immediate factor replacement.
* Viva Question: "How does Desmopressin (DDAVP) work and who is it for?" Answer: It induces endothelial release of stored FVIII and vWF; only useful for mild Haemophilia A and vWD, useless for Haemophilia B.
* Trap: Assuming Emicizumab (Hemlibra) is useful for acute bleeding episodes. Pearl: Emicizumab is purely for prophylaxis (mimics FVIII but has no acute "boost" effect); acute bleeds on Emicizumab still require standard recombinant factor or bypass agents.
