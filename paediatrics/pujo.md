# Pelviureteric Junction Obstruction (PUJO) (腎盂輸尿管交界處阻塞)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of PUJO
* Obstruction of urine flow from renal pelvis to proximal ureter → ↑ pelvic pressure → Hydronephrosis (腎積水)
* Functional or anatomical blockage without concurrent distal ureteric dilatation

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Incidence: 1 in 1000-1500 live births
* M:F = 2:1
* L:R = 2:1 (Bilateral in 10-40%)
* Most common cause of antenatal hydronephrosis in local HK HA obstetrics screening

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Intrinsic Pathophysiology
* Disruption of ureteric smooth muscle development → aperistaltic segment → failure of urine propulsion
* Mucosal folds or ureteric valves (rare)

### Extrinsic Pathophysiology
* Aberrant crossing lower pole renal vessel (迷走血管) compressing PUJ 
* More common cause in older children presenting acutely

### Genetic Factors
* Mostly sporadic
* Rare familial clustering; no single gene identified

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Antenatal Presentation
* Asymptomatic 
* Detected via routine maternal USG (↑ Anteroposterior Renal Pelvic Diameter [APD])

### Postnatal & Infant Presentation
* Mostly asymptomatic (detected on postnatal USG screen)
* Palpable abdominal/flank mass (enlarged kidney)
* Febrile Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) (尿道感染)
* Failure to thrive (生長遲緩)

### Older Child Presentation
* **Dietl's Crisis (迪特爾危象)**: Intermittent, severe periumbilical/loin pain + nausea/vomiting triggered by ↑ fluid intake (diuresis)
* Haematuria (血尿) following minor abdominal trauma

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing PUJO from Vesicoureteric Reflux (VUR)
* PUJO: Hydronephrosis ONLY (normal, non-dilated ureter)
* VUR: Hydroureteronephrosis (both pelvis and ureter are dilated)

### Distinguishing PUJO from Posterior Urethral Valves (PUV)
* PUJO: Usually unilateral, normal bladder wall, normal urination stream
* PUV: Exclusively male, strictly bilateral, thick-walled trabeculated bladder, dilated posterior urethra (Keyhole sign), poor stream

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Differentials for Pediatric Hydronephrosis
| Condition | Key Pathophysiology | Key USG / Imaging Finding | Clinical Clue |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PUJO** | Proximal obstruction | Dilated pelvis, normal ureter | Dietl's crisis, palpable flank mass |
| **VUR** | Retrograde urine flow | Dilated pelvis + dilated ureter | Recurrent febrile UTIs |
| **VUJO** | Distal ureteric obstruction | Megaureter down to UVJ | Usually asymptomatic |
| **PUV** | Urethral outflow obstruction | Bilateral hydro + thick bladder | Male, oligohydramnios, palpable bladder |
| **MCDK** | Dysplastic non-functioning kidney | Multiple non-communicating cysts | Absent renal function on MAG3/DMSA |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Initial Postnatal Imaging (USG)
* **Postnatal USG KUB**: Gold standard initial test
* Timing: Strictly after 48-72 hours of life (Day 3-7) to avoid false -ve from physiological neonatal oliguria
* Repeat USG at 4-6 weeks to assess progression

### Gold Standard Functional Investigation (MAG3)
* **MAG3 Diuretic Renogram (MAG3 放射性同位素腎圖)**: 
* Timing: Performed at 4-6 weeks of age (neonatal kidneys have immature tubular extraction)
* Assesses Differential Renal Function (DRF) & drainage half-life (T1/2) post-furosemide
* Obstructed curve: Continuously rising phase without excretion post-diuretic

### Adjunct Anatomical Investigation (MCUG)
* **Micturating Cystourethrogram (排尿性膀胱尿道造影)**
* Indicated if concurrent VUR suspected (co-exists in 10-15%), bilateral severe hydronephrosis, or ureteric dilatation seen on USG

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management
* Admit for IV antibiotics (e.g., Cefuroxime/Amikacin per HA antibiogram) if presenting with pyelonephritis/sepsis
* IV fluids and analgesia for Dietl's crisis

### Chronic Conservative Management (HA Protocol)
* Observation: >70% of mild/moderate cases resolve spontaneously
* Serial USG (monitoring APD) + MAG3 scans (monitoring DRF)
* **Prophylactic Antibiotics**: Trimethoprim or Cefalexin (2mg/kg nocte) in infants with severe hydronephrosis until MCUG excludes VUR or obstruction resolves

### Surgical Management Indications & Procedure
* **Indications**:
  * DRF <40% or progressive ↓ in DRF by >5-10% on serial scans
  * Progressive severe hydronephrosis (APD >30mm)
  * Recurrent breakthrough UTIs despite prophylaxis
  * Symptomatic (Dietl's crisis, stones)
* **Procedure**: **Anderson-Hynes Pyeloplasty (腎盂成形術)** (Open, Laparoscopic, or Robotic). Excision of stenotic segment → spatulation → primary anastomosis.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Roles
* **Paediatric Urology/Surgery**: Monitor serial imaging, determine surgical timing
* **Paediatric Nephrology**: Monitor BP, renal function, optimize CKD management if bilateral
* **Specialist Nurses**: Counsel on prophylactic ABX compliance and UTI symptom recognition

### Psychosocial Support
* Parental anxiety management regarding radiation exposure (MAG3/MCUG)
* Reassurance regarding the high spontaneous resolution rate in asymptomatic infants

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Concerns in Urological Care
* Repeated defaulting of HA clinic appointments and crucial imaging (USG/MAG3) leading to medical neglect and irreversible renal damage
* Late presentation of severe pyelonephritis coupled with profound hygiene neglect

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Medical Complications
* Renal cortical scarring (腎疤痕)
* Secondary Hypertension (高血壓) (due to RAAS activation from local ischaemia)
* Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) / ESRF (if bilateral or solitary kidney)
* Renal calculi (腎結石) secondary to urinary stasis

### Prognosis
* Excellent for mild/moderate cases managed conservatively
* Surgical success rate (pyeloplasty) >95% with stabilization or improvement of DRF

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: When should the first postnatal USG be performed for an antenatally diagnosed PUJO? Answer: Strictly after 48-72 hours of life to avoid false-negative results caused by relative physiological neonatal oliguria.
* Trap: Assuming a dilated ureter on USG is part of the PUJO spectrum. Pearl: PUJO strictly involves obstruction at the pelvic junction; the presence of a dilated ureter (hydroureter) strongly points toward VUR or VUJO.
* Pitfall: Ordering a MAG3 scan in the first few days of life. The immature neonatal kidney has poor tubular extraction and GFR; you must wait until 4-6 weeks of age for an accurate assessment of differential renal function.
