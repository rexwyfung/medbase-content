# Peptic Ulcer Disease (H. pylori) (胃潰瘍 / 幽門螺旋菌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Peptic Ulcer Disease
* **Definition**: Defect in gastric/duodenal mucosa extending through muscularis mucosae.
* **Primary (Idiopathic)**: Usually duodenal, chronic, strongly associated with **H. pylori (幽門螺旋菌)**.
* **Secondary**: Usually gastric, acute, secondary to stress (sepsis, burns, trauma) or drugs (**NSAIDs**, steroids).

### Local Epidemiology of H. pylori in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: ↓ incidence due to improved public hygiene/living standards in HK.
* **Transmission**: Fecal-oral or oral-oral route. 
* **Clustering**: High intrafamilial carriage rate (parents/siblings often +ve).
* **Demographics**: Higher prevalence in immigrants & lower socioeconomic status (SES).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of H. pylori Infection
* **Organism**: Gram-negative, spiral, flagellated, microaerophilic bacillus.
* **Mechanism of Injury**:
  * **Urease (尿素酶) production**: Converts urea → ammonia + CO2 → local acid neutralization → allows survival in acidic stomach.
  * **Flagella**: Allows penetration through gastric mucus layer.
  * **Toxins (CagA, VacA)**: Cytotoxic → severe mucosal inflammation (gastritis) → epithelial damage → ↑ susceptibility to acid.
  * **Result**: Hypergastrinemia & ↑ acid secretion (duodenal ulcers) OR impaired mucosal defense (gastric ulcers).

### Pathophysiology of NSAID-induced Ulcers
* **Mechanism**: Systemic inhibition of **Cyclooxygenase-1 (COX-1)**.
* **Effect**: ↓ mucosal **Prostaglandins (前列腺素) (PGE2)** → ↓ mucus/bicarbonate secretion, ↓ mucosal blood flow → acid-induced mucosal necrosis.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Insidious Presentation of Peptic Ulcer Disease
* Chronic, recurrent, dull/aching epigastric pain.
* Night-time waking due to pain.
* Unexplained **Iron Deficiency Anemia (缺鐵性貧血)** or occult GI blood loss.
* Failure to thrive (FTT) / poor weight gain in young children.

### Acute Catastrophic Presentation of Peptic Ulcer Disease
* Massive **Upper GI Bleed (上消化道出血)**.
* Sudden onset **Hematemesis (吐血)** ("coffee-ground" or fresh red blood).
* **Melena (黑糞症)** (black, tarry, foul-smelling stools).
* Hypovolemic shock (tachycardia, hypotension, prolonged CRT).
* Acute acute abdomen (if perforated).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Peptic Ulcer Disease
* **Pain**: Chronic dull, aching/burning **Epigastric pain (胃痛)**.
* **Nocturnal symptom**: Pain characteristically wakes child from sleep.
* **Associated GI symptoms**: Nausea, vomiting, bloating, early satiety.

### Differences Between Gastric and Duodenal Ulcers
| Feature | Gastric Ulcer (胃潰瘍) | Duodenal Ulcer (十二指腸潰瘍) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Location** | Stomach (lesser curvature) | Duodenum (first part) |
| **Pain & Meals** | Worsens *with* feeding (↑ acid secretion) | Improves *with* feeding (acid buffered), worsens hours later |
| **Etiology** | NSAIDs, stress, severe illness | H. pylori (majority) |
| **Weight** | Often ↓ (avoid eating due to pain) | Usually normal (eating relieves pain) |

### Key Clinical Signs & Alarm Features
* Epigastric tenderness on palpation.
* Pallor (due to anemia).
* Tachycardia / postural hypotension (suggests acute bleeding).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Peptic Ulcer from Functional Abdominal Pain
| Feature | Peptic Ulcer Disease | Functional Abdominal Pain (功能性腹痛) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Location** | Epigastric | Periumbilical |
| **Timing** | Wakes child at night | Never wakes child at night |
| **Systemic Signs** | Anemia, GI bleed, FTT present | Normal growth, no systemic signs |
| **Dietary effect** | Altered by meals | Unrelated to meals |
| **Psychosocial** | Usually non-contributory | Often linked to school stress/anxiety |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis for Epigastric Pain in Children
* **Functional Dyspepsia (功能性消化不良)**: Normal endoscopy, no alarm signs.
* **Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉)**: Malabsorption, chronic diarrhea, +ve tTG-IgA.
* **Crohn's Disease (克隆氏症)**: Can involve upper GI, weight loss, ↑ inflammatory markers.
* **Eosinophilic Oesophagitis (EoE)**: Dysphagia, food impaction, atopic history.
* **Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)**: Heartburn, regurgitation.
* **Biliary Colic / Pancreatitis**: RUQ/epigastric pain radiating to back, ↑ amylase/lipase.

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Non-Invasive Investigations for H. pylori
* **13C-Urea Breath Test (13C-UBT) (尿素呼氣試驗)**: 1st line non-invasive test for diagnosis & proving eradication. Detects active urease.
* **Stool H. pylori Antigen (糞便抗原測試)**: Alternative to UBT. Highly sensitive/specific for active infection.
* **Serology (IgG)**: Useless in children. Only indicates past exposure, cannot differentiate past vs. active infection.

### Gold Standard Invasive Investigations for Peptic Ulcer
* **Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD) (食道胃十二指腸內視鏡)**: Direct visualization + biopsies.
* **Rapid Urease Test (CLO test)**: Bedside test performed on biopsy sample. Color change (yellow → red/pink) indicates +ve urease activity.
* **Histology & Culture**: Confirms gastritis/ulcer, allows antibiotic sensitivity testing (vital in HK due to ↑ Clarithromycin resistance).

### Baseline Blood Investigations
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Check for microcytic anemia (↓ Hb, ↓ MCV).
* **Iron Studies**: ↓ Ferritin, ↓ Iron, ↑ TIBC.
* **Clotting Profile**: If acute GI bleed.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Upper GI Bleed
* **Resuscitation (ABC)**: 2x large-bore IV cannulae, fluid bolus (0.9% NaCl 20ml/kg), cross-match blood.
* **Medical**: IV **Proton Pump Inhibitor (質子泵抑制劑)** infusion (e.g., Omeprazole).
* **Endoscopic Intervention**: Urgent OGD for clipping, thermal coagulation, or adrenaline injection of bleeding ulcer.

### HA Standard 1st Line Eradication Protocol for H. pylori
* **Regimen**: "Triple Therapy" (三聯療法) for 10-14 days.
* **Goal**: Heal ulcer + eradicate bacteria to prevent relapse.

| Medication Type | Drug Name | Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PPI** | **Omeprazole** (奧美拉唑) OR Lansoprazole | ↓ Acid secretion, ↑ antibiotic efficacy |
| **Antibiotic 1** | **Amoxicillin** (阿莫西林) | Cell wall synthesis inhibitor |
| **Antibiotic 2** | **Clarithromycin** (克拉黴素) OR Metronidazole (if Penicillin-allergic) | Protein synthesis inhibitor (Macrolide) |

### Post-Eradication Confirmation Protocol
* **Confirmation is mandatory** due to rising resistance rates.
* **Timing**: MUST wait ≥ 4 weeks post-antibiotics AND ≥ 2 weeks post-PPI.
* **Method**: UBT or Stool Antigen. (Do NOT use serology).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement in Peptic Ulcer
* **Gastroenterologist**: Endoscopy & complex management.
* **Dietitian**: Nutritional support if FTT or iron deficiency.
* **Family Screening**: Consider testing asymptomatic parents/siblings (common reservoir for re-infection).

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Concerns in Peptic Ulcer Disease
* **Accidental Ingestion**: Ensure unsupervised access to adult NSAIDs/Aspirin is prevented.
* **Munchausen Syndrome by Proxy (FII)**: Rare, but consider if unexplained/recurrent deliberate poisoning with ulcerogenic drugs.
* **Medical Neglect**: Failure of parents to comply with strict 14-day Triple Therapy regimen.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications of Peptic Ulcer Disease
* **Hemorrhage**: Erosion into gastroduodenal artery → massive bleed → hypovolemic shock.
* **Perforation (腸胃穿孔)**: Transmural erosion → leakage of gastric contents → peritonitis.
  * **Sign**: Rigid, board-like abdomen.
  * **CXR**: Free gas under diaphragm (pneumoperitoneum) on erect CXR.

### Chronic Complications of Peptic Ulcer Disease
* **Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO)**: Due to acute edema or chronic fibrotic scarring at pylorus.
  * **Sign**: Non-bilious projectile vomiting, succussion splash.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why must a child stop PPIs 2 weeks before a Urea Breath Test? Answer: PPIs suppress H. pylori activity and urease production, leading to a false-negative result. Antibiotics must also be stopped for 4 weeks.
* Viva Question: What is the classic plain radiograph finding in a perforated peptic ulcer? Answer: Air under the right hemidiaphragm on an erect chest X-ray.
* Trap: Reassuring a parent of a child with nocturnal abdominal pain. Pearl: Functional abdominal pain does NOT wake a child from sleep. Nocturnal pain is a red flag necessitating investigation for organic pathology like peptic ulcer disease.
* Trap: Prescribing Ibuprofen for analgesia in a child with a history of recurrent epigastric pain. Pearl: NSAIDs severely exacerbate peptic ulcers by depleting protective prostaglandins. Paracetamol is the safe analgesic choice.
* Pitfall: Using H. pylori IgG serology to confirm eradication after Triple Therapy. Serology remains positive for months/years after successful eradication. Always use Urea Breath Test or Stool Antigen to confirm cure.
* Pitfall: Blaming diet/spicy foods as the primary cause of pediatric gastric ulcers. While irritants exacerbate symptoms, the vast majority of pediatric primary duodenal/gastric ulcers are caused by H. pylori infection.
