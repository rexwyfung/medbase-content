# Chronic Non-Specific Diarrhoea (Toddler Diarrhoea) (幼兒慢性非特異性腹瀉)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Toddler Diarrhoea
* Most common cause of chronic diarrhoea in healthy preschool children (ages 1-5 yrs).
* Benign, functional disorder of rapid intestinal transit without malabsorption.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Extremely common in local paediatric outpatient clinics; heavily anxiety-provoking for parents.
* Often exacerbated by local dietary habits, particularly ↑ consumption of packaged fruit juices and sweet probiotic drinks.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Maturation of Intestinal Motility
* Immaturity of the enteric nervous system → rapid intestinal transit time.
* Bowel hypermotility → insufficient time for water absorption in the colon → loose stools.

### Dietary Exacerbation (The 4 F's)
* **High Fluid (高水份)**: ↑ volume overwhelms the gut's absorptive capacity.
* **High Fruit Juice (高果汁)**: ↑ fructose and sorbitol → act as osmotic laxatives in the colon.
* **Low Fat (低脂肪)**: ↓ fat intake → rapid gastric emptying → accelerates bowel transit.
* **Low Fibre (低纖維)**: ↓ dietary fibre → lack of stool bulking and delayed transit.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Characteristic Stool Pattern
* 3-10 loose, very malodorous stools per day.
* Exclusively occurs during the day; **never** wakes the child at night.
* **"Peas and Carrots" sign**: visible undigested food particles (vegetables) in stool due to rapid transit.

### Clinical Status of the Child
* Child is brilliantly well, highly active, and energetic.
* **Normal Growth (生長正常)**: Height and weight track perfectly along their respective centiles.
* Normal physical examination with a soft, non-tender abdomen.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Absence of Red Flags
* Toddler Diarrhoea NEVER features:
  * Blood or mucus in stool (**Bloody Stool / 血便**)
  * Nocturnal diarrhoea (waking from sleep)
  * Weight loss or faltering growth (**生長遲緩**)
  * Severe abdominal pain
  * Fever or systemic signs of illness
* If ANY +ve → MUST aggressively investigate for organic pathology (e.g., IBD, Coeliac, Infection).

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Organic Causes of Chronic Diarrhoea
| Differential (鑑別診斷) | Distinguishing Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA) (牛奶蛋白敏感)** | Often bloody/mucoid stools, concurrent eczema, vomiting, presents <1 yr. |
| **Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)** | Explosive frothy stools, severe perianal excoriation, severe bloating/flatus. |
| **Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉)** | Rare in HK Chinese; causes faltering growth, abdominal distension, muscle wasting. |
| **Giardiasis / Parasitic Infection (寄生蟲感染)** | Recent travel history, foul-smelling greasy stools, weight loss. |
| **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) (炎症性腸病)** | Bloody diarrhoea, weight loss, fever, ↑ ESR/CRP, nocturnal symptoms. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Clinical Diagnosis
* Predominantly a clinical diagnosis based on classic history and a strictly normal growth curve.

### Baseline Bloods & Stool Tests (If Parental Anxiety is High or Atypical Features Present)
* **Stool Culture & Microscopy (糞便常規檢查)**: Rule out Ova, Cysts, Parasites (O&P) and bacterial pathogens (all will be -ve).
* **Stool Viral PCR**: Rule out chronic viral shedding.
* **Coeliac Screen**: Anti-tTG IgA + Total IgA (performed to rule out pathology; strictly normal).
* **FBC & Inflammatory Markers**: Check for anaemia and ↑ CRP/ESR (strictly normal).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Evidence-Based Reassurance
* Crucial 1st line: Reassure parents this is a harmless maturational phase, NOT a disease.
* Explain the physiology to alleviate parental anxiety and prevent unnecessary doctor-shopping.

### Dietary Modification (Resolving the 4 F's)
* **Normalize Fat (正常脂肪)**: Give full-fat milk (**全脂奶**); fat delays gastric emptying and slows transit. DO NOT restrict fat.
* **Reduce Fluid (減少水份)**: Restrict excessive water and drink intake.
* **Eliminate Fruit Juices (戒果汁)**: Strictly stop clear juices (especially apple/pear) which are high in sorbitol/fructose.
* **Normalize Fibre (正常纖維)**: Encourage complex carbohydrates and whole grains to bulk the stool.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Primary Care & MCHC Support
* Education and reassurance at the Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) is usually sufficient.
* Provide standardized dietary pamphlets.
* Reassure parents that the child will achieve normal toilet training (**如廁訓練**).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Parental Anxiety and Medical Abuse
* Monitor for parents imposing extreme, restrictive elimination diets that could inadvertently cause malnutrition.
* If parents refuse to accept reassurance and repeatedly demand invasive tests (e.g., colonoscopy) despite a thriving child → consider fabricated or induced illness.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Natural History
* Excellent prognosis.
* ~90% of children completely outgrow the condition spontaneously by the age of 4 to 5 years.
* Resolves naturally as the enteric nervous system matures and voluntary bowel control is achieved.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What are the cardinal red flags that would make you strongly doubt toddler's diarrhoea? Answer: Faltering growth/weight loss, nocturnal diarrhoea waking the child from sleep, blood/mucus in the stool, or severe joint pains/rashes.
* Trap: Recommending a low-fat diet to "rest the bowel" in toddler's diarrhoea. Pearl: Low fat actually accelerates gastric emptying and worsens the diarrhoea. You MUST encourage a normal/high-fat diet (e.g., whole milk) to slow intestinal transit.
* Pitfall: Ordering an invasive colonoscopy for classic Toddler's Diarrhoea. This represents a failure to recognize a basic benign condition and exposes a healthy child to unnecessary procedural risks.
* Viva Question: Which dietary sugars are most commonly implicated in exacerbating toddler's diarrhoea? Answer: Fructose and sorbitol (found in high concentrations in apple and pear juices), which act as unabsorbable osmotic laxatives.
