# Chronic Non-Specific Diarrhoea (Toddler Diarrhoea) (幼兒慢性非特異性腹瀉)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: The most common cause of chronic diarrhea in healthy preschool children (ages 1-5). It is a benign functional disorder of rapid intestinal transit.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Extremely common, often heavily anxiety-provoking for parents.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Immaturity of intestinal motility leads to rapid transit.
- Heavily exacerbated by the dietary **4 F's**:
  1. Low **F**at
  2. High **F**luid
  3. High **F**ruit juice (fructose and sorbitol act as osmotic laxatives)
  4. Low **F**iber
- Because transit is rapid, partially digested food passes straight through.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A 2-year-old child passing 3-10 loose, very smelly stools per day, often containing recognizable bits of undigested vegetables (peas, carrots).
- Crucially, the child is brilliantly well, highly active, and growing perfectly.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Loose stools mainly during the day, *never* waking the child at night.
- "Peas and carrots" sign (undigested food in stool).
- Normal physical examination. Normal growth curve (Height and Weight tracking nicely along centiles).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Toddler Diarrhea NEVER has: blood in stool, weight loss, severe abdominal pain, or nocturnal diarrhea. If any of these "Red Flags" are present, you MUST investigate for pathology (IBD, Coeliac). 

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Coeliac Disease (has growth failure)
- Giardiasis
- Cow's Milk Protein Allergy
- Lactose Intolerance

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Predominantly clinical**. 
- If parental anxiety is very high, a basic stool culture, stool microscopy (O&P), and Coeliac screen (Anti-tTG IgA) can be done to rule out pathology. All will be strictly normal.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Reassurance**: Reassure parents this is a harmless maturational phase not a disease.
- **Dietary Modification (The 4 F's)**:
  - Normalize **Fat** intake (fat delays gastric emptying and slows transit). Do NOT restrict fat.
  - Reduce excessive **Fluid**.
  - Strictly eliminate clear **Fruit juices** (especially apple/pear which are high in sorbitol).
  - Normalize dietary **Fiber**.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Education at the MCHC is sufficient. Reassure the child will be toilet-trainable.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Excellent. 90% of children completely outgrow the condition spontaneously by the age of 4 to 5 years as their gut matures and they achieve bowel control.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Ordering an invasive colonoscopy for classic Toddler's Diarrhea. This represents a failure to recognize a basic benign condition.
- **Viva Q**: "What are the red flags that would make you strongly doubt toddler's diarrhea?" Answer: Failure to thrive/weight loss, nocturnal diarrhea waking the child from sleep, blood in the stool, or severe joint pains/rashes.
