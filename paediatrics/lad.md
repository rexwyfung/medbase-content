# Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD) (白細胞黏附缺陷症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A rare, autosomal recessive primary immunodeficiency characterized by the inability of white blood cells (leukocytes) to leave the bloodstream and migrate into infected tissues.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Extremely rare, but universally tested in clinical exams due to its unique presentation.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **LAD Type 1** (The classic and most common form): Mutation in the **ITGB2** gene, leading to a defect or complete absence of **CD18** (the Beta-2 integrin subunit).
- **Function**: When a tissue is infected, endothelial cells display adhesion molecules. Neutrophils in the blood use CD18 integrins to "grab" the endothelium, roll to a stop, squeeze between the cells (diapedesis), and crawl into the tissue.
- **Pathology**: Without CD18, neutrophils just float helplessly past the infection in the bloodstream. They can't stop, and they can't get out of the blood vessels.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A 4-week-old neonate presents with a massive, angry red, spreading infection of the skin around the umbilicus (omphalitis), yet the umbilical cord stump has still not fallen off.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Delayed Separation of the Umbilical Cord**: Normally, neutrophils infiltrate the dying cord stump to break it down, causing it to fall off by day ~14. In LAD, neutrophils can't get there, so the cord famously stays attached for >30 days.
- **Infections WITHOUT PUS**: Severe, necrotic bacterial infections (skin, gums, lungs, perianal) that lack any purulence. (Pus is literally made of millions of dead neutrophils. No neutrophils in the tissue = no pus).
- **Severe Periodontitis**: Once teeth erupt, they get aggressive gum disease incredibly early, causing teeth to fall out in early childhood.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from CGD: In CGD, neutrophils get into the tissue easily but can't kill things, so they form huge granulomas and massive abscesses bursting with pus. In LAD, there is no pus at all.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Hyper-IgE Syndrome (also has "cold" non-inflammatory abscesses, but cords fall off on time).
- Chronic Granulomatous Disease.
- Neonatal sepsis from poor hygiene (often causes omphalitis, but usually with pus).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **CBC**: **Astounding Leukocytosis**. WBC counts are incredibly high (often 50,000 to 100,000 / $\mu$L), completely dominated by neutrophils. (Because the bone marrow is pumping them out due to infection, but none of them can leave the blood vessels to die in the tissues).
- **Definitive Diagnosis**: Flow cytometry of white blood cells demonstrating the **absence of CD18** and CD11 markers on the cell surface.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**: Aggressive, early, very broad-spectrum IV antibiotics for any suspected infection.
- **Dental**: Intensive, rigorous dental hygiene to prevent catastrophic periodontitis.
- **Curative**: **Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT)** is the only definitive cure and is usually required early in life.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Continuous surgical involvement for debridement of necrotic infections.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Overwhelming fatal sepsis in infancy if not transplanted.
- Severe necrotic scarring from skin infections.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Assuming a WBC count of 80,000 means the child has leukemia. In the context of a spreading, pus-free skin infection in a neonate, it is the physiologic hallmark of LAD.
- **Viva Q**: "A neonate with a 5-week-old attached umbilical cord develops omphalitis. Why is there no pus when you incise the red, swollen area?" Answer: Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (CD18 defect). Neutrophils lack the integrins required to adhere to the endothelium and migrate into the tissues. Since pus is an accumulation of dead neutrophils, the inability of neutrophils to reach the tissue guarantees an absence of pus.
