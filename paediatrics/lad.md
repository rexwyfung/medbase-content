# Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD) (白細胞黏附缺陷症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD)
**LAD (白細胞黏附缺陷症)**: Rare, autosomal recessive primary immunodeficiency → absolute inability of leukocytes (WBCs) to migrate from bloodstream into infected tissues.

### Local Epidemiology of LAD in Hong Kong
Extremely rare in Hong Kong (incidence <1/1,000,000) → but universally tested in MBBS exams due to unique and highly testable pathophysiology.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### LAD Type 1 Genetics
**LAD Type 1** (Classic & most common form): Mutation in **ITGB2 gene** → defect or complete absence of **CD18** (β-2 integrin subunit).

### Normal Neutrophil Migration Pathway
Normal tissue infection → endothelial cells display adhesion molecules → blood neutrophils use CD18 integrins to "grab" endothelium → rolling → firm adhesion → diapedesis → tissue infiltration.

### Pathological Defect in LAD
Pathology: ↓/absent CD18 → neutrophils cannot arrest or exit blood vessels → float helplessly in circulation → absent tissue immune response.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Classic Neonatal Presentation
Neonate (~4 weeks old) → presents with massive, spreading, angry red abdominal skin infection (**Omphalitis (臍炎)**) → BUT umbilical cord stump remains firmly attached.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Neonatal Umbilical Signs
**Delayed Cord Separation (臍帶脫落延遲)**: Cord attached for **>30 days** (Normal: neutrophils infiltrate dying cord stump to break it down by day ~14; LAD neutrophils cannot reach it).

### Characteristic Infectious Signs
**Infections WITHOUT Pus (無膿感染)**: Severe, necrotic bacterial infections (skin, gums, lungs, perianal) → strictly lack purulence.
*Pathophysiology note*: Pus = millions of dead neutrophils. No tissue neutrophils = NO pus.

### Paediatric Dental Manifestations
**Severe Periodontitis (嚴重牙周炎)**: Once teeth erupt → incredibly early, aggressive gingival disease → premature tooth loss in early childhood.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing LAD from Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)
* **LAD (白細胞黏附缺陷症)**: Neutrophils CANNOT reach tissue → **NO pus** at all.
* **CGD (慢性肉芽腫病)**: Neutrophils easily reach tissue but CANNOT kill catalase +ve organisms → form massive granulomas & bursting **pus-filled abscesses**.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differentials for Neonatal Omphalitis and Immunodeficiency
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD)** | Delayed cord >30 days, NO pus, astounding neutrophilia (↑↑↑ WBC). |
| **Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)** | Normal cord separation, massive pus/abscesses, granulomas. |
| **Hyper-IgE Syndrome (Job Syndrome)** | Normal cord separation, "cold" (non-inflammatory) abscesses, coarse facies. |
| **Neonatal Sepsis (新生兒敗血症)** | Usually secondary to poor hygiene, omphalitis typically WITH pus. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside and Blood Investigations
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC) (全血細胞計數)**: Astounding **Leukocytosis (白細胞增多)**.
* **WBC Count**: 50,000 to 100,000/μL.
* **Differential**: Completely dominated by **Neutrophils (中性粒細胞)** → bone marrow pumps them out due to infection, but zero can leave blood vessels to die in tissues.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigation
* **Flow Cytometry (流式細胞術)**: Definitive diagnosis → demonstrates absence/severe deficiency of **CD18** and **CD11** integrin markers on WBC surface.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Infection Management
* **Empirical IV Antibiotics (靜脈注射抗生素)**: Aggressive, early, very broad-spectrum IV antibiotics for any suspected infection (HA Protocol: early cover for MRSA & Gram-negatives).
* **Surgical Debridement (外科清創)**: Urgent and continuous surgical excision of necrotic tissue infections.

### Chronic & Preventive Management
* **Prophylactic Antimicrobials**: Long-term prophylactic antibiotics/antifungals to prevent recurrent sepsis.
* **Dental Care**: Intensive, rigorous dental hygiene to prevent catastrophic periodontitis.

### Definitive Curative Management
* **Haematopoietic Stem Cell Transplant (HSCT) (造血幹細胞移植)**: ONLY definitive cure → usually required early in life.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Network
* **Paediatric Immunology**: Lead medical management & HSCT planning.
* **Paediatric Surgery**: Debridement of necrotic infections.
* **Paediatric Dentistry**: Gingival preservation.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)**: Financial & psychological support for prolonged HA hospitalizations and transplant care.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) & Neglect Considerations
* N/A for direct disease mechanism.
* **Medical Neglect Red Flag**: Parental failure to maintain rigorous dental hygiene or administer life-saving prophylactic antibiotics → leads to severe preventable deterioration.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Life-Threatening Complications
* **Fatal Sepsis (致命性敗血症)**: Overwhelming systemic infection in infancy if not transplanted.
* **Dermatological**: Severe necrotic scarring from unhindered skin infections.
* **Dental**: Premature childhood edentulism (complete tooth loss).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Assuming a neonatal WBC count of 80,000 means the child has congenital leukaemia. Pearl: In the context of a spreading, pus-free skin infection in a neonate, astounding leukocytosis is the physiologic hallmark of LAD.
* Viva Question: A neonate with a 5-week-old attached umbilical cord develops omphalitis. Why is there no pus when you incise the red, swollen area? Answer: Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (CD18 defect). Neutrophils lack the integrins required to adhere to the endothelium and migrate into the tissues. Since pus is an accumulation of dead neutrophils, the inability of neutrophils to reach the tissue guarantees an absence of pus.
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents about a delayed umbilical cord separation without investigating. Delay >30 days is highly abnormal and demands a CBC to rule out LAD.
