# Food Allergy (IgE & Non-IgE) (食物過敏 - IgE 及 非IgE介導)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Food Allergy
* **Definition**: An adverse, reproducible immune response triggered by a specific food protein.
* **IgE-Mediated (IgE介導)**: Rapid onset hypersensitivity involving IgE antibodies against specific food antigens.
* **Non-IgE-Mediated (非IgE介導)**: Delayed onset, T-cell mediated response, mostly targeting the gastrointestinal tract and skin.

### Local Epidemiology & Common Allergens
* **Infants (嬰兒)**: **Cow's Milk Protein Allergy (CMPA) (牛奶蛋白過敏)** is the most common in Hong Kong infants, followed by egg (雞蛋).
* **Older Children (較大兒童)**: Peanut (花生), tree nuts (堅果), fish (魚), shellfish (甲殼類), and sesame (芝麻).
* **Cross-Reactivity (交叉反應)**: Pollen-food allergy syndrome (花粉-食物過敏綜合症) can develop in older children.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of IgE-Mediated Food Allergy
* **Sensitization (致敏)**: Prior exposure leads to production of food-specific **IgE antibodies** which bind to **mast cells (肥大細胞)**.
* **Early Phase (早期反應)**: Re-exposure causes cross-linking of IgE → massive degranulation of **histamine (組織胺)**, leukotrienes, and prostaglandins within 10-15 minutes → vasodilation, smooth muscle spasm, and mucosal edema.
* **Late Phase (晚期反應)**: Occurs 4-6 hours later, involving eosinophil and neutrophil infiltration, leading to prolonged symptoms.

### Pathophysiology of Non-IgE-Mediated Food Allergy
* **Mechanism**: T-cell mediated inflammatory process primarily targeting the gut mucosa.
* **Onset**: Takes hours to days to develop after ingestion.
* **Hygiene Hypothesis (衛生假說)**: Reduced microbial exposure in modern urban environments leads to an imbalance in immune development, increasing atopic susceptibility.

### Genetics of Atopy & Allergy
* **Atopy (特應性)**: A personal and/or familial tendency to produce IgE antibodies.
* **Genetic Defect**: Polymorphisms in genes controlling barrier function (e.g., **Filaggrin gene** mutations) increase susceptibility to both eczema (濕疹) and food allergy.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### IgE-Mediated Allergy Symptoms
* **Onset**: 10-15 minutes (up to 2 hours) post-ingestion.
* **Cutaneous (皮膚)**:
    * **Urticaria (蕁麻疹 / 風癩)**
    * Pruritus (痕癢)
    * Erythema (紅斑)
* **Facial**:
    * **Angioedema (血管性水腫)** (swollen lips, tongue, eyelids)
* **Respiratory (呼吸)**:
    * Bronchospasm (支氣管痙攣)
    * Wheezing (喘鳴)
    * Stridor (喘鳴音)
    * Cough (咳嗽)
* **Cardiovascular (心血管)**:
    * Hypotension (低血壓)
    * Tachycardia (心動過速)
    * Syncope (暈厥)

### Non-IgE-Mediated Allergy Symptoms
* **Onset**: Hours to days post-ingestion.
* **Gastrointestinal (腸胃)**:
    * Chronic diarrhea (慢性腹瀉)
    * Vomiting (嘔吐)
    * Abdominal pain (腹痛)
* **Allergic Proctocolitis (過敏性直腸結腸炎)**:
    * Blood-streaked stools ("red threads")
    * Mucus in stool in an otherwise well infant
* **FPIES (Food Protein-Induced Enterocolitis Syndrome)**:
    * Profuse, severe repetitive vomiting
    * **Hypovolemic shock (低血容量性休克)**
* **Cutaneous**:
    * Severe, refractory **atopic dermatitis (特應性皮炎)**
* **Systemic**:
    * Faltering growth (生長遲緩)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Discriminatory Features: CMPA vs Lactose Intolerance
* **Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)**: Purely enzymatic (lactase deficiency) causing bloating, flatulence, and watery diarrhea. It **NEVER** causes urticaria, angioedema, blood in stool, or anaphylaxis.
* **CMPA (牛奶蛋白過敏)**: Immune-mediated, causes systemic symptoms, cutaneous signs (hives, severe eczema), and blood/mucus in stool.

### Discriminatory Features: IgE vs Non-IgE Reactions
| Feature | IgE-Mediated | Non-IgE-Mediated |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset Time** | Rapid (Minutes to 2 hours) | Delayed (Hours to days) |
| **Primary System** | Skin, Respiratory, Cardiovascular | Gastrointestinal, Skin (Eczema) |
| **Key Signs** | Urticaria, Angioedema, Anaphylaxis | Bloody stools, chronic diarrhea, vomiting |
| **Testing** | SPT / sIgE Positive | SPT / sIgE Negative |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Gastrointestinal Differentials
* **Lactose Intolerance (乳糖不耐症)**
* **Acute Gastroenteritis (急性腸胃炎)**: Viral or Bacterial.
* **Intussusception (腸套疊)**: Suspect if presenting with red-currant jelly stool and intermittent severe crying.
* **Celiac Disease (乳糜瀉)**: Rare in HK, but presents with faltering growth and chronic diarrhea.
* **Gastro-esophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) (胃食管反流病)**

### Cutaneous & Systemic Differentials
* **Idiopathic Urticaria (特發性蕁麻疹)**: Usually post-viral.
* **Eosinophilic Esophagitis (嗜酸性食管炎)**: Difficulty swallowing, food impaction.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & In-Vivo Investigations
* **Skin Prick Test (SPT) (皮膚點刺測試)**:
    * Used for IgE-mediated allergies.
    * Highly sensitive, rapid result. Wheal ≥3mm is typically positive.
    * **Note**: Antihistamines must be stopped 48-72 hours prior.

### Laboratory Blood Investigations
* **Specific IgE (sIgE / RAST) (特異性IgE血清測試)**:
    * Used for IgE-mediated allergies.
    * The higher the level, the greater the likelihood of clinical allergy.
    * Component testing can identify specific allergenic proteins.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Test
* **Oral Food Challenge (OFC) (口服食物激發測試)**:
    * Used to confirm diagnosis if history/tests are equivocal or to check for outgrown allergy.
    * Conducted under strict medical supervision with resuscitation equipment.

### Non-IgE-Mediated Testing Protocol
* **Diagnostic Trial**:
    * SPT and sIgE are **NEGATIVE**.
    * Diagnosis is purely clinical.
    * **Trial of elimination** (2-4 weeks).
    * Followed by a **re-introduction challenge**.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management: Anaphylaxis
* **1st Line Life-Saving**: **IM Adrenaline (Epinephrine) (腎上腺素)** 1:1,000 into the anterolateral thigh.
    * **Dose**: 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.5 mg).
    * Repeat every 5 minutes if no clinical improvement.
* **Supportive (ABCDE)**:
    * High-flow O2
    * Lie patient flat, raise legs
    * IV fluid bolus (20ml/kg 0.9% NaCl)
* **Adjuncts**:
    * IV Hydrocortisone
    * IV Chlorphenamine
    * Note: Antihistamines and steroids DO NOT treat acute life-threatening compromise; they prevent biphasic reactions/cutaneous symptoms.
* **Observation**: Minimum 4-6 hours due to risk of late-phase (biphasic) reaction.

### Chronic Management: Dietary Avoidance
* **Strict Allergen Avoidance**: Mandates reading food labels carefully. Dietician involvement is **mandatory**.
* **Infant CMPA Protocol**:
    * Switch to **Extensively Hydrolyzed Formula (EHF) (深度水解配方奶)**.
    * If severe (e.g., FPIES, faltering growth) or fails EHF: Step up to **Amino Acid Formula (AAF) (氨基酸配方奶)**.
    * **Avoid Soy Formula (大豆配方奶)** in infants <6 months due to high risk of cross-reactivity and phytoestrogen content.

### Chronic Management: Pharmacological & Tolerance
* **EpiPen / Jext**: Auto-injectors must be provided to patients with a history of anaphylaxis or high-risk profiles.
* **Action Plan**: Provide a written Anaphylaxis Action Plan for parents and schools.
* **Immunotherapy (免疫療法)**: Oral immunotherapy (OIT) to induce tolerance is an evolving therapy conducted in specialist centers.
* **Heat Modification**: Some patients can tolerate baked milk/egg (heat degradation alters protein tertiary structure making it less allergenic).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Involvement
* **Dietician (營養師)**: Essential for ensuring adequate caloric/calcium intake during elimination diets.
* **School & Nursery**: Requires school preparation (e.g., nut-free zones) and staff training for EpiPen administration.
* **Psychosocial Impact**: Fear of eating, social isolation at parties/gatherings, and constant hypervigilance can severely impact the quality of life of the child and family.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Recognizing Neglect in Food Allergy
* **Medical Neglect**: Repeatedly ignoring strict dietary restrictions leading to frequent severe allergic reactions.
* **Intentional Harm**: Deliberate allergen exposure (a form of Fabricated or Induced Illness).
* **Red Flags**: Frequent presentations with anaphylaxis despite clear education, or discrepancies between parent's story and clinical findings.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Complications
* **Fatal Anaphylaxis (致命性過敏性休克)**: Especially in adolescents with asthma and peanut/tree nut allergy.
* **Nutritional Deficiencies**: Faltering growth, calcium/vitamin D deficiency due to overly restrictive elimination diets.
* **Severe Eczema**: Refractory atopic dermatitis due to ongoing untreated CMPA.

### Prognosis & Natural History
* **Milk and Egg Allergy**: High rate of resolution; often outgrown by school age.
* **Peanut, Tree Nut, Shellfish Allergy**: Tend to be lifelong and rarely outgrown.
* **Atopic March (特應性進程)**: Early food allergy and eczema strongly predict the subsequent development of asthma and allergic rhinitis.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Relying on "Total IgE" for food allergy diagnosis. Pearl: Total IgE is useless; it can be high in eczema/asthma without food allergy. You must request Specific IgE or do a Skin Prick Test.
* Viva Question: A mother breastfeeding her baby who has CMPA asks if she needs to change her diet? Answer: Yes. Intact cow's milk proteins cross into breastmilk. The mother must strictly eliminate dairy (and take calcium supplements) to continue breastfeeding.
* Pitfall: Recommending soy formula for a 3-month-old infant with CMPA. Avoid under 6 months due to high rate of concomitant soy allergy (cross-reactivity) and concerns over phytoestrogens.
* Viva Question: What is the most significant risk factor for fatal anaphylaxis in a child with food allergy? Answer: Co-existing, poorly controlled asthma, particularly in adolescents with peanut or tree nut allergy.
* Trap: Giving antihistamines as the first-line treatment for an acute anaphylactic reaction with stridor. Pearl: Antihistamines do not relieve mucosal edema or bronchospasm. IM Adrenaline is the ONLY life-saving first-line treatment.
