# Intraventricular Haemorrhage (腦室內出血)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Intraventricular Haemorrhage
* Bleeding originating in subependymal germinal matrix (室管膜下生發基質)
* Often ruptures → ventricular system
* Primary pathology of extreme prematurity (早產)

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Incidence inversely proportional to Gestational Age (GA) & birth weight
* Highest risk: GA <32 weeks or Very Low Birth Weight (VLBW) <1500g
* HA NICU data: ↑ survival of micro-premies → persistent clinical burden of IVH
* Most haemorrhages occur within first 72 hours of life

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Germinal Matrix Vulnerability
* Highly vascularized region active in neurogenesis/gliogenesis (up to 32-34 wks)
* Fragile capillary network: single endothelial layer, absent muscularis/collagen
* Highly susceptible to hypoxic-ischaemic injury (缺氧缺血性腦損傷)

### Haemodynamic Pathophysiology in Prematurity
* Premature infants have impaired cerebral autoregulation (腦血流自動調節)
* Cerebral blood flow becomes directly pressure-passive
* Systemic BP fluctuations (e.g., handling, ventilation, pain, pneumothorax) → rapid ↑ cerebral blood flow → capillary rupture
* Hypoxia/Hypercapnia → cerebral vasodilation → ↑ bleeding risk

### Genetic & Maternal Risk Factors
* Absent antenatal steroids
* Maternal chorioamnionitis (絨毛膜羊膜炎)
* Lack of specific genetic locus; primarily developmental/haemodynamic aetiology

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Asymptomatic Presentation of IVH
* 50%+ cases are clinically silent
* Picked up strictly via routine HA NICU cranial ultrasound screening

### Acute Neurological Signs of IVH
* Bulging fontanelle (囟門凸出)
* ↑ Head circumference
* ↓ Tone (hypotonia) / lethargy
* Altered consciousness / stupor
* Seizures (抽搐) (subtle: lip smacking, bicycling)
* Abnormal eye signs (e.g., fixed pupils, skew deviation)

### Systemic Signs of Acute Deterioration
* Unexplained sudden ↓ Haematocrit / Pallor (蒼白)
* Apnoea (呼吸暫停) & Bradycardia (心搏過緩)
* ↑ O2 requirement / sudden ventilator dependence
* Temperature instability
* Metabolic acidosis
* Refractory hypotension (低血壓)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating IVH from Subgaleal / Cephalhaematoma
* **Location:** IVH is intracranial (identified via US); Subgaleal/Cephalhaematoma are extracranial (palpable externally).
* **Timing:** Extracranial bleeds immediate post-instrumental delivery; IVH typically peaks 24-72 hours post-delivery.
* **GA Context:** IVH overwhelming in <32 wks; extracranial bleeds common in term macrosomia / operative deliveries.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Neonatal Encephalopathy & Apnoea
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features | Gold Standard Investigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Intraventricular Haemorrhage (IVH)** | GA <32 wks, sudden ↓ Hb, bulging fontanelle at 2-3 days life | Cranial US (papile grading) |
| **Hypoxic-Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE)** | Term/near-term infant, history of perinatal asphyxia, low APGARs | MRI Brain, continuous EEG |
| **Neonatal Meningitis (腦膜炎)** | Maternal GBS +ve, fever/hypothermia, ↑ CRP, late-onset >72h | Lumbar Puncture (CSF analysis) |
| **Inborn Errors of Metabolism (先天性代謝缺陷)** | Term infant, normal initially → deterioration with feeds, severe acidosis / ↑ ammonia | Plasma amino acids, urine organic acids |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Gold Standard Investigation for IVH
* **Bedside Cranial Ultrasound (顱腦超聲波)**
* Done via anterior fontanelle
* Highly sensitive for germinal matrix bleed & ventricular dilatation
* Can be done without moving unstable neonate

### Papile Staging System for IVH
| Grade | Cranial Ultrasound Findings | Prognosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grade I** | Haemorrhage restricted to germinal matrix | Excellent (>90% normal) |
| **Grade II** | IVH occupying <50% of ventricular volume; NO ventricular dilatation | Good |
| **Grade III** | IVH with ventricular dilatation (腦室擴大) | High risk of disability / hydrocephalus |
| **Grade IV** | IVH with parenchymal extension (venous infarction) | Poor (high rate of Cerebral Palsy) |

### Adjunct Blood Investigations
* **FBC:** ↓ Hb (acute bleed), ↓ Platelets (consumptive)
* **Coagulation Profile:** ↑ PT/APTT (DIC screening)
* **Blood Gas:** Metabolic acidosis (tissue hypoperfusion)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### HA Routine Screening Protocol for IVH
* All infants <32 weeks GA or <1500g must have screening Cranial US.
* Timing: Day 3-4 of life, Day 7, then Day 30 (or prior to discharge).

### Acute Medical Management of IVH
* **Strict Minimal Handling:** Elevate head 15-30°, keep head midline (prevent jugular venous obstruction).
* **Haemodynamic Stability:** Avoid rapid fluid boluses / hyperosmolar solutions (e.g., rapid NaHCO3). Use slow infusions.
* **Ventilation:** Maintain normocapnia (avoid hypocarbia → cerebral ischaemia; avoid hypercarbia → cerebral hyperaemia).
* **Correct Coagulopathy:** Transfuse FFP/Platelets if actively bleeding & deranged.
* **Haemoglobin Support:** Packed RBC transfusion for acute anaemia.

### Chronic Management of Post-Haemorrhagic Ventricular Dilatation (PHVD)
* Serial Cranial US to monitor ventricular index.
* **1st Line:** Serial Lumbar Punctures (LP) or Ventricular Taps (temporizing measure if symptomatic ↑ ICP).
* **2nd Line:** Neurosurgical intervention → Ommaya reservoir or Ventricular Access Device (VAD) insertion.
* **Definitive:** Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) Shunt (腦室腹腔引流) once CSF protein/blood clears and infant >2-2.5kg.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Early Intervention and Allied Health Support
* **Physiotherapy (物理治療):** Early motor stimulation, prevent contractures.
* **Occupational Therapy:** Visual tracking, ADL adaptations.
* **Speech Therapy:** Swallowing assessment, early communication.

### Psychosocial Support & Hong Kong Social Services
* Refer to Medical Social Worker (MSW) for financial assistance (e.g., CSSA, disability allowance).
* Early referral to Child Assessment Centre (CAC) / Department of Health for formal developmental tracking.
* Connect parents with pre-term support groups (e.g., Premature Baby Support Group in HA).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Differentiating IVH from Non-Accidental Injury (NAI)
* IVH in premature infants is a known physiological complication.
* **RED FLAG:** Unexplained intracranial haemorrhage (esp. Subdural Haemorrhage - SDH) in a *term* infant or an *older* infant >1 month.
* Suspect Shaken Baby Syndrome (搖晃嬰兒綜合症) if: SDH + Retinal Haemorrhages + unexplained fractures (e.g., rib, metaphyseal).
* HA Protocol: Mandatory MDT case conference + Police/Social Welfare Department (SWD) referral if NAI suspected.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Neurological Complications of IVH
* **Cerebral Palsy (腦性麻痺):** Particularly spastic diplegia (associated with periventricular leukomalacia - PVL) or quadriplegia (Grade IV).
* **Cognitive Impairment:** Global developmental delay.
* **Sensory Deficits:** Cortical visual impairment, sensorineural hearing loss.
* **Seizure Disorders:** Post-haemorrhagic epilepsy.

### Mechanical Complications
* **Post-Haemorrhagic Hydrocephalus (腦積水):** Blood clots block arachnoid villi → impaired CSF absorption.
* **Periventricular Leukomalacia (PVL):** Often co-exists with IVH, leading to cystic necrosis of white matter.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical preventative measure for IVH administered before birth? Answer: Antenatal maternal corticosteroids (e.g., dexamethasone) given >24 hours prior to preterm delivery.
* Viva Question: Which anatomical structure is the primary source of IVH in premature infants? Answer: The subependymal germinal matrix.
* Trap: Assuming a Grade IV IVH is simply a progression of bleeding pushing directly into the parenchyma. Pearl: Grade IV is actually a periventricular haemorrhagic venous infarction caused by compression of terminal veins by the distended ventricle, not direct extension.
* Trap: Using rapid fluid boluses to treat hypotension in an extreme premie. Pearl: Rapid shifts in intravascular volume lead to massive spikes in cerebral blood flow, directly precipitating germinal matrix rupture.
* Pitfall: Missing an expanding head circumference in an infant recovering from IVH. You must plot HC daily and perform serial cranial ultrasounds to catch post-haemorrhagic hydrocephalus before severe brain compression occurs.
