# Acute Haematogenous Osteomyelitis (急性血源性骨髓炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Acute Haematogenous Osteomyelitis
* **Acute Haematogenous Osteomyelitis (急性血源性骨髓炎)**: Acute bacterial infection of bone & bone marrow (骨髓).
* In children, almost always **haematogenous (血源性)** → bacteria reach bone via bloodstream.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Peak incidence: Children <5 years.
* Gender: ♂ > ♀.
* Common & serious paediatric orthopaedic emergency in **Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) (醫院管理局)** admissions.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathogenesis of Haematogenous Spread
* Transient asymptomatic **bacteraemia (菌血症)** (e.g., from minor skin abrasion, URTI, dental issue).
* Bacteria lodge in **metaphysis (乾骺端)** of long bones.
* Common sites: **Distal femur (股骨遠端)**, **proximal tibia (脛骨近端)**, **proximal humerus (肱骨近端)**.

### Role of the Metaphysis in Infection
* **Metaphyseal capillary loops (乾骺端微血管袢)**: Sluggish, turbulent blood flow + lack phagocytic cells → ideal environment for bacterial multiplication.
* Rigid bone structure → pus ↑ intraosseous pressure → blood vessel compression → **bone necrosis (骨壞死)**.
* Pus tracks outward via **Haversian canals (哈弗氏管)** → lifts periosteum → **subperiosteal abscess (骨膜下膿腫)**.

### Causative Organisms by Age and Condition
| Age/Risk Factor | Key Pathogens |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All ages (Most Common)** | ***Staphylococcus aureus* (金黃色葡萄球菌)** (>80%), Group A *Streptococcus* (S. pyogenes), *Kingella kingae* |
| **Neonates (<1 month)** | Group B *Streptococcus*, *E. coli* |
| **Sickle Cell Disease** | ***Salmonella* species (沙門氏菌)**, *S. aureus* |
| **Puncture Wounds (Foot)** | ***Pseudomonas aeruginosa* (綠膿桿菌)** |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Acute Systemic Symptoms
* Short history (days).
* **High fever (高燒)**, rigors, lethargy, toxic appearance.

### Localized Orthopaedic Signs
* **Severe, constant pain (劇痛)** localized to metaphysis.
* **Pseudoparalysis (假性癱瘓)**: Refusal to move limb/bear weight.
* **Exquisite point tenderness (局部壓痛)**: Most reliable early clinical sign.
* Late signs: Swelling, **erythema (紅斑)**, warmth (indicates cortical breach/subperiosteal abscess).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Osteomyelitis from Septic Arthritis
* **Septic Arthritis (化膿性關節炎)**: Infection *inside* joint space. ANY joint movement = excruciating pain.
* **Osteomyelitis (骨髓炎)**: Infection in bone (metaphysis). Gentle joint movement tolerated, but direct bone palpation = extreme pain.
* *Neonatal exception*: Blood vessels cross **physis (生長板)** → osteomyelitis often spreads to joint → concurrent septic arthritis.

### Differentiating Osteomyelitis from Bone Tumours
* **Bone Tumours (骨腫瘤)** (e.g., **Ewing Sarcoma (尤文氏肉瘤)**, **Osteosarcoma (骨肉瘤)**): Insidious (slow) onset over weeks/months. Usually afebrile initially.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Main Differentials for Acute Limb Pain and Fever
* **Septic Arthritis (化膿性關節炎)**
* **Cellulitis (蜂窩組織炎)**
* **Transient Synovitis (暫時性滑膜炎)** (if near hip)
* Bone tumours (**Ewing Sarcoma (尤文氏肉瘤)**, **Osteosarcoma (骨肉瘤)**, **Leukaemia (白血病)**)
* Trauma / Occult fracture

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Blood Investigations for Suspected Osteomyelitis
* **FBC (全血細胞計數)**: ↑ WBC (Leukocytosis with left shift).
* **ESR (紅血球沉降率) & CRP (C反應蛋白)**: Markedly ↑. **CRP** crucial for monitoring treatment response.
* **Blood Cultures (血液培養)**: Mandatory BEFORE antibiotics. +ve in ~50%.

### Imaging Modalities for Osteomyelitis
* **X-ray (X光)**: Initial test. Usually **NORMAL for first 10-14 days**. Late signs: **Periosteal reaction (骨膜反應)**, osteopenia, bone destruction. Used to rule out fractures/tumours.
* **MRI with Gadolinium (磁力共振掃描)**: **Gold standard (黃金標準)** & most sensitive. Detects bone marrow oedema & subperiosteal abscesses within 24-48 hrs.
* **Ultrasound (超聲波)**: Detects subperiosteal abscess or concurrent joint effusion.

### Special Surgical Investigations
* **Bone Aspirate/Biopsy (骨抽吸/活檢)**: Indicated if blood cultures -ve & no response to empirical antibiotics. Yields pus for culture.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Empirical Intravenous Antibiotics (HK HA Protocols)
* **Immediate initiation** after blood cultures.
* **1st Line (MSSA cover)**: High-dose **IV Cloxacillin (氯唑西林)** or **IV Cefazolin (頭孢唑林)**.
* **MRSA suspected** (critically ill, known carrier, HA local resistance patterns): **IV Vancomycin (萬古黴素)** or **IV Clindamycin (克林黴素)**.

### Antibiotic Duration and Oral Switch
* **Duration**: Prolonged course. 1-2 weeks IV, followed by oral switch.
* **Switch criteria**: Afebrile + significant ↓ CRP + clinical improvement.
* **Oral step-down**: High-dose **Cephalexin (頭孢氨苄)** or **Amoxicillin/Clavulanate (阿莫西林/克拉維酸)**.
* **Total duration**: 4-6 weeks.

### Indications for Surgical Management
* Presence of **subperiosteal abscess (骨膜下膿腫)** (requires I&D).
* Failure to respond to IV antibiotics within 48-72 hours.
* Concurrent **septic arthritis (化膿性關節炎)**.

### Orthopaedic Splinting
* Affected limb rested in a splint to prevent **pathological fractures (病理性骨折)** and relieve pain.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Paediatric Orthopaedics (兒童骨科)**: Surgical decision-making, I&D, long-term follow-up.
* **Infectious Diseases (傳染病科)**: Advice on complex cases/resistant organisms (e.g., MRSA, negative cultures).
* **Physiotherapy (物理治療)**: Post-acute mobilization to restore function and prevent contractures.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Considerations
* Osteomyelitis itself is rarely an NAI presentation, but delay in seeking medical care for a severely limping, febrile child warrants **Social Medical Worker (MSW) (醫務社工)** assessment for medical neglect.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Local Bone Complications
* **Chronic Osteomyelitis (慢性骨髓炎)**: Dead bone (**sequestrum (死骨)**) forms, surrounded by new bone (**involucrum (包殼)**). Causes chronic discharging sinuses; requires extensive debridement.
* **Growth Arrest (生長停滯)**: Physis (生長板) damage → limb length discrepancy or angular deformities.
* **Pathological Fracture (病理性骨折)**: Weakened bone fractures under normal stress.
* **Septic Arthritis (化膿性關節炎)**: Joint destruction (especially hip/shoulder).

### Overall Prognosis
* Excellent if diagnosed early and treated with full 4-6 week course of appropriate antibiotics.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: A 4-year-old presents with high fever and severe pain in the distal femur. An X-ray of the femur is completely normal. Does this rule out osteomyelitis? Answer: Absolutely not. X-rays are typically normal for the first 10-14 days. MRI is the gold standard for early diagnosis.
* Viva Question: What is the most common causative organism for acute haematogenous osteomyelitis in a healthy child? Answer: Staphylococcus aureus.
* Trap: Assuming joint movement will be severely restricted in isolated osteomyelitis. Pearl: In isolated osteomyelitis, gentle joint movement is usually tolerated, whereas exquisite point tenderness is elicited upon direct palpation of the metaphyseal bone.
* Pitfall: Stopping antibiotics too early (e.g., after 1 week when the child feels better). Osteomyelitis requires a prolonged 4-6 week course to eradicate bacteria from avascular bone and prevent chronic osteomyelitis.
