# Paediatric Heart Failure (小兒心臟衰竭)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
- Inability of heart to pump blood at rate commensurate with requirements of metabolizing tissues, or doing so only from ↑ filling pressure.

### Local Epidemiology & HK Context
- **Congenital Heart Disease (CHD) (先天性心臟病):** Leading cause in HK infants.
- **Cardiomyopathies (心肌病)** (e.g., viral myocarditis, genetic) & **Rheumatic Heart Disease (風濕性心臟病):** More common in older children/adolescents.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Aetiology by Age Group
| Age Group | Key Aetiologies |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Neonate (<1 week)** | Left heart obstruction (duct-dependent): **Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome (左心發育不良綜合症)**, critical aortic stenosis, severe coarctation of aorta. |
| **Infant (1-4 weeks)** | Left-to-right shunts: **VSD (室間隔缺損)**, PDA, AVSD. Shunt ↑ as pulmonary vascular resistance ↓ postnatally. |
| **Child/Adolescent** | Viral myocarditis, cardiomyopathies (dilated/hypertrophic), rheumatic fever, anthracycline toxicity (oncology). |

### Pathophysiological Mechanisms
- **L-to-R Shunts:** Postnatal ↓ in pulmonary vascular resistance → progressive ↑ in L-to-R shunt → ↑ pulmonary blood flow → pulmonary oedema.
- **Compensatory SNS Activation:** ↑ Sympathetic tone → tachycardia, diaphoresis.
- **RAAS Activation:** ↑ Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone → fluid retention → oedema/hepatic congestion.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Infant Presentation
- **Feeding Difficulties:** Tiring easily, prolonged feeding times, profuse sweating during feeds.
- **Growth:** **Failure to thrive / Faltering growth (生長遲緩)**.
- **Respiratory:** Tachypnoea, recurrent lower respiratory tract infections.

### Older Child Presentation
- **Functional:** Exercise intolerance, fatigue, orthopnoea.
- **GI/Systemic:** Abdominal pain (due to hepatic congestion), peripheral oedema.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Vital Signs & General
- **Tachycardia (心動過速) & Tachypnoea (呼吸急促):** Earliest & most sensitive signs.
- **Cool Peripheries (四肢冰冷):** Prolonged capillary refill time (>2 secs) in severe low cardiac output.

### Cardiac Signs
- **Cardiomegaly (心臟肥大):** Displaced apex beat.
- **Auscultation:** **Gallop rhythm (奔馬律)** (S3/S4), specific heart murmurs depending on structural defect.

### Congestive Signs
- **Hepatomegaly (肝腫大):** Most reliable sign of right-sided heart failure in infants (JVP is unassessable).
- **Peripheral Oedema / Ascites:** Rare in infants; indicates long-standing right heart failure or pulmonary hypertension in older children.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Heart Failure from Respiratory Pathologies
| Feature | Heart Failure | Respiratory Infection (e.g., Bronchiolitis) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hepatomegaly** | **Present** (Hepatic congestion) | Absent (Liver may be pushed down by hyperinflation, but not congested) |
| **Auscultation** | Gallop rhythm, murmurs, basal crepitations | Widespread wheeze, prominent crepitations |
| **Feeding** | Sweating during feeds, slow feeding | Poor intake due to breathlessness |
| **Fever** | Usually absent | Usually present |

### Differentiating Heart Failure from Sepsis
- **Sepsis:** Tachycardia & poor perfusion present, but usually febrile with an infective focus. No cardiomegaly or gallop rhythm.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Key Differentials
- **Pulmonary:** Bronchiolitis, severe pneumonia, asthma exacerbation.
- **Infective:** Sepsis / Septic shock.
- **Metabolic:** Mitochondrial disease (can present with cardiomyopathy), inborn errors of metabolism.
- **Rhythm:** Primary arrhythmias (e.g., SVT).

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations
- **ECG (心電圖):** Detect arrhythmias, ventricular hypertrophy, ischaemia.
- **Pre & Post-Ductal SpO2:** Screen for critical cyanotic CHD.

### Blood Investigations
- **Biomarkers:** ↑ BNP/NT-proBNP (ventricular stretch), ↑ Troponin (myocarditis).
- **Organ Perfusion:** U&E, LFTs (baseline & detect hepatic congestion/renal impairment).
- **VBG:** ↑ Lactate indicates poor tissue perfusion/acidosis.

### Imaging Studies
- **CXR:** **Cardiomegaly**, ↑ pulmonary vascular markings (plethora), pulmonary oedema.

### Gold Standard Investigation
- **Echocardiogram (超聲心動圖):** Defines structural anatomy, ventricular function (Ejection Fraction/Fractional Shortening), chamber dilatation, and estimates pulmonary artery pressures.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Resuscitation
- **Posture:** Prop up (semi-recumbent) to ↓ work of breathing.
- **Oxygen:** Titrate carefully. **CAUTION:** O2 closes the ductus arteriosus; avoid high-flow O2 in suspected duct-dependent lesions.
- **Duct-Dependent Neonates:** **IV Prostaglandin E1 (前列腺素E1) infusion** (5ng/kg/min) to maintain ductal patency. Watch for apnoea/hypotension.
- **PICU Support:** Inotropes (Milrinone, Dobutamine) for cardiogenic shock; mechanical ventilation if exhausted.

### Chronic Medical Management
- **Diuretics (利尿劑):** **Furosemide (呋塞米)** (↓ preload/pulmonary oedema), Spironolactone (K+-sparing).
- **ACE Inhibitors:** **Captopril** or Enalapril (↓ afterload, ↑ cardiac output).
- **Beta-Blockers:** Carvedilol or Bisoprolol (introduced carefully only when stable).

### Definitive Surgical Management
- Treat underlying cause (e.g., VSD/ASD closure, valve repair). Usually performed at 3-6 months to prevent irreversible pulmonary hypertension.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Dietetic & Nutritional Support
- **Dietitian:** Crucial for managing failure to thrive. Provide high-calorie formula (via NG tube if tiring during oral feeds) while restricting total fluid volume to prevent overload.

### Medical Social Worker (MSW) Support
- **MSW:** Essential for families facing chronic illness burden, repeated HA admissions, or financial stress from specialized equipment/transplant evaluation.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect Indicators
- Poor compliance with medications, specialized feeds, or missed HA follow-ups can rapidly precipitate acute heart failure.
- Consider non-organic failure to thrive (neglect) if growth fails to improve despite optimal medical/dietetic inpatient management.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Major Complications
- **Eisenmenger Syndrome (艾森曼格綜合症):** Irreversible pulmonary vascular disease from chronic L-to-R shunt → shunt reverses to R-to-L → cyanosis.
- **Systemic:** Failure to thrive, recurrent respiratory infections, arrhythmias, sudden cardiac death.

### Prognosis
- Dependent on underlying aetiology. Excellent following surgical repair of simple shunts. Guarded for severe cardiomyopathies or single-ventricle physiology.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Looking for raised JVP and ankle oedema in an infant. Pearl: Hepatomegaly is the most reliable sign of systemic venous congestion in infants.
* Viva Question: Why do infants with heart failure sweat during feeds? Answer: Feeding is their equivalent of exercise. The sympathetic nervous system is hyperactivated to maintain cardiac output, leading to diaphoresis.
* Pitfall: Administering high-flow oxygen to a collapsed neonate with suspected heart failure. High oxygen tension constricts the ductus arteriosus, precipitating fatal shock in duct-dependent systemic circulations (e.g., severe coarctation).
* Viva Question: What is Eisenmenger syndrome? Answer: Irreversibly raised pulmonary vascular resistance resulting from a chronic left-to-right shunt, leading to shunt reversal (right-to-left flow) and central cyanosis.
* Trap: Using normal adult heart rate ranges for paediatric patients. Pearl: Always interpret tachycardia relative to the child's age; a normal HR for a neonate (100-160 bpm) is severe tachycardia in an adolescent.
