# Pulmonary Hypertension (肺動脈高壓)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
- **Mean pulmonary arterial pressure (mPAP)** $\ge$ **20 mmHg** at rest, determined via **Right Heart Catheterization (RHC)** (右心導管檢查).
- **Normal mPAP**: 10–14 mmHg.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
- **Secondary PH**: Most common in HK. Usually secondary to **Congenital Heart Disease (CHD)** (先天性心臟病) with uncorrected left-to-right shunts (e.g., VSD, ASD), or **Chronic Lung Disease** like Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia (BPD) in extreme premature infants (NICU survivors).
- **Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension of the Newborn (PPHN)**: Seen locally in Hospital Authority (HA) NICUs, often post-meconium aspiration, sepsis, or Hypoxic-Ischemic Encephalopathy (HIE).
- **Idiopathic PAH (IPAH)**: Rare but highly fatal; female predominance. 

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Vascular Remodeling & Hemodynamics
- **Endothelial Dysfunction**: ↑ vasoconstrictors (endothelin, thromboxane) & ↓ vasodilators (nitric oxide, prostacyclin).
- **Vascular Remodeling**: Hypertrophy of smooth muscle and endothelial proliferation in small pulmonary arteries → lumen obliteration.
- **Right Heart Strain**: Leads to drastically ↑ **Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR)** (肺血管阻力).
- **Cor Pulmonale**: The right ventricle (RV) is forced to pump against extremely high pressures → **RV Hypertrophy (RVH)** → RV dilation → **Right Heart Failure** (右心衰竭).

### Eisenmenger Syndrome (艾森曼格綜合症)
- Progressive ↑ PVR exceeding Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR) → reversal of an initial left-to-right shunt into a **right-to-left shunt**.
- Results in severe, intractable **central cyanosis** (發紺); typically manifests by 10–15 years of age if large defects remain unoperated.

### Genetics
- **BMPR2 Gene Mutation**: Autosomal dominant with incomplete penetrance; responsible for ~70% of familial PAH cases.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Classic Clinical Presentation
- Older child with progressive, unexplained **Exertional Dyspnea** (勞動性氣促) and profound fatigue.
- **Exertional Syncope** (暈厥): A massive RED FLAG signifying a fixed RV cardiac output unable to meet exertional metabolic demands.

### Key Clinical Signs
- **Palpation**: **Left parasternal heave** (indicating RV hypertrophy), palpable **P2**.
- **Auscultation**: **Loud P2** (pulmonary component of 2nd heart sound), pansystolic murmur at the lower left sternal edge (Tricuspid Regurgitation), early diastolic murmur (Pulmonary Regurgitation).
- **Right Heart Failure Signs** (Late stage): ↑ JVP, tender **hepatomegaly** (肝臟腫大), peripheral edema, ascites.
- **Hypoxia Signs**: **Cyanosis** and **clubbing** (杵狀指) if Eisenmenger syndrome is established.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Syncope
| Discriminatory Feature | Pulmonary Hypertension (PH) | Vasovagal Syncope (迷走神經性暈厥) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Trigger** | **Peak exercise** (due to fixed cardiac output) | Prolonged standing, heat, pain, emotion |
| **Associated Symptoms** | Progressive dyspnea, chest pain, cyanosis | Visual blurring, diaphoresis, pallor, nausea |
| **Recovery Phase** | Slow recovery, persistent profound fatigue | Rapid recovery upon assuming supine position |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Main Differentials
- **Severe Asthma (嚴重哮喘)**: Causes SOB and wheeze, but NO loud P2, NO parasternal heave, and NO hepatomegaly. Responds to bronchodilators.
- **Severe Congenital Aortic Stenosis (AS) / Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)**: Both cause exertional syncope but present with left-sided heart signs (LV heave, apical murmurs, abnormal LV ECG).
- **Pulmonary Stenosis (PS)**: Features an ejection systolic murmur at the upper left sternal edge, but the P2 is soft/normal, not loud.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Bloods
- **Electrocardiogram (ECG)** (心電圖): **Right Axis Deviation (RAD)**, **RVH** (tall R waves in V1, deep S in V6), and Right Atrial Enlargement (peaked P pulmonale).
- **Blood Biomarkers**: ↑ BNP / NT-proBNP (indicates ventricular strain/failure), ABG (hypoxia, acidosis), FBC (compensatory polycythemia).

### Imaging
- **Echocardiogram** (心臟超聲波): 1st-line non-invasive screening tool. Estimates pulmonary pressures via Tricuspid Regurgitation (TR) jet velocity. Assesses RV function and screens for structural CHD.
- **Chest X-Ray (CXR)**: Enlarged central pulmonary arteries (prominent pulmonary bay), peripheral oligaemia ("pruning"), and cardiomegaly (RV enlargement).

### Gold Standard
- **Right Heart Catheterization (RHC)**: Mandatory for formal diagnosis.
- **Vasoreactivity Test**: Administering inhaled Nitric Oxide (iNO) during RHC to see if vessels can still dilate; dictates suitability for Calcium Channel Blocker therapy.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management (Pulmonary Hypertensive Crisis)
- **Avoid Triggers**: STRICT avoidance of dehydration, hypercarbia, hypoxia, and acidosis (powerfully constrict pulmonary vessels).
- **Immediate Resuscitation**:
  - **100% Oxygen**: Potent pulmonary vasodilator.
  - **Hyperventilation**: Induce mild alkalosis to ↓ PVR.
  - **Inhaled Nitric Oxide (iNO)**: First-line selective pulmonary vasodilator.
  - Inotropic support (e.g., Milrinone) to support failing RV.

### Chronic Management & HA Targeted Therapies
- Management is highly centralized (e.g., HK Children's Hospital).
- **Vasoreactive +ve**: High-dose Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs).
- **Vasoreactive -ve (Targeted Pulmonary Vasodilators)**:
  | Pathway Targeted | Drug Class | HA Formulary Examples | Route |
  | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
  | **Nitric Oxide** | Phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE-5) inhibitors | **Sildenafil** (西地那非) | PO / IV |
  | **Endothelin** | Endothelin Receptor Antagonists (ERAs) | **Bosentan** (波生坦), Macitentan | PO |
  | **Prostacyclin** | Prostacyclin Analogues | **Epoprostenol** (依前列醇), Iloprost | Continuous IV |
- **Surgical**: Atrial septostomy (palliative pop-off valve), Heart-Lung Transplant (refractory end-stage disease).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Coordination
- **Continuous IV Epoprostenol Support**: Requires a permanent central line. Parents must be meticulously trained by specialist nurses to mix the drug daily. **CRITICAL**: If the pump stops for even 5 minutes, the child can suffer fatal rebound PH.
- **School & Lifestyle**: Must avoid strenuous/competitive sports. Strict infection prevention required (annual influenza and pneumococcal vaccines). Coordination with Medical Social Workers (MSW) for financial aid regarding expensive targeted therapies.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect
- **Compliance Failure**: Failure of parents/carers to manage complex continuous IV therapies (Epoprostenol) or missing critical cardiology follow-ups represents severe medical neglect, warranting immediate MSW and Social Welfare Department (SWD) intervention due to the immediate risk of death.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Complications
- **PH Crises**: Often triggered by general anesthesia, crying, or pain → precipitates acute right heart failure and cardiovascular collapse.
- **Arrhythmias**: Atrial flutter/fibrillation secondary to severe right atrial dilation.

### Prognosis
- Idiopathic PH has historically poor survival (median <3 years if untreated).
- Modern triple targeted therapy has significantly improved 5–10 year outcomes.
- Patients with Eisenmenger syndrome may survive into adulthood but suffer severe morbidity, requiring eventual transplantation.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why is giving supplemental oxygen important in a child with a pulmonary hypertensive crisis? Answer: Because alveolar hypoxia is the most potent natural stimulus for pulmonary vasoconstriction. Oxygen directly dilates the pulmonary vascular bed, lowering the resistance the RV has to pump against.
* Trap: Forgetting to listen for the loud P2 in a breathless child. Pearl: A loud, palpable P2 in a child with unexplained dyspnea is a massive red flag for PH until proven otherwise.
* Pitfall: Prescribing Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs) without a prior Right Heart Catheterization (RHC) vasoreactivity test. CCBs can cause fatal systemic hypotension and cardiovascular collapse in non-responders.
* Viva Question: What is Eisenmenger Syndrome? Answer: The reversal of a long-standing left-to-right shunt into a right-to-left shunt due to progressive, irreversible pulmonary vascular disease and pulmonary hypertension, resulting in central cyanosis.
* Pitfall: Allowing a child with severe PH to undergo elective procedures under General Anesthesia (GA) at a peripheral hospital. GA strongly triggers fatal PH crises and must only be performed at a tertiary center by specialized pediatric cardiac anesthetists.
