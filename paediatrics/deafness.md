# Childhood Hearing Loss / Deafness (兒童聽力障礙 / 失聰)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Hearing Loss
* **Definition**: Partial or total inability to hear.
* **Categories**: 
  * **Conductive Hearing Loss (CHL)** (傳導性聽力障礙): Defect in outer/middle ear.
  * **Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL)** (感音神經性聽力障礙): Defect in inner ear (cochlea) or auditory nerve.
  * **Mixed Hearing Loss**: Combination of both.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: ~1-2 per 1000 newborns have significant bilateral SNHL.
* **HK Screening**: **Universal Neonatal Hearing Screening (UNHS)** (新生嬰兒聽力普查) implemented by HK Department of Health (DH) across all public hospitals.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Conductive Hearing Loss (CHL)
* Sound waves fail to reach inner ear.
* **Common Causes**: 
  * **Otitis Media with Effusion (OME)** (中耳積水) → Most common.
  * Impacted wax, foreign body.
  * Congenital atresia/microtia.
  * Eustachian tube dysfunction (associated with **Down syndrome**, cleft palate).

### Pathophysiology & Genetics of Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL)
* Damage to cochlear hair cells or **CN VIII** (auditory nerve).
* **Congenital/Genetic (80% in high-income countries)**: 
  * **Non-syndromic**: **Connexin 26 (GJB2)** mutations → Very common in HK Chinese.
  * **Syndromic**: Waardenburg, Usher, Pendred, Alport syndromes.
* **Acquired/Environmental**: 
  * **Congenital infections**: **Cytomegalovirus (CMV)** (leading non-genetic cause), Rubella.
  * Extreme prematurity / NICU admission (>48 hrs).
  * **Ototoxic drugs**: Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin), Loop diuretics (Furosemide).
  * Severe neonatal hyperbilirubinaemia (Kernicterus).
  * **Bacterial meningitis** → Rapid cochlear ossification.
  * Significant head injury / basal skull fracture.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Infant Symptoms & Signs of Hearing Loss
* Fails UNHS.
* ↓ or absent startle reflex to loud noises.
* Fails to turn to sound by 6 months.
* Absence of babbling by 12 months.

### Toddler and Child Symptoms & Signs of Hearing Loss
* **Speech and language delay** → Most common presentation if missed at birth.
* Inattentiveness / appearing to ignore instructions.
* ↑ TV/tablet volume.
* Poor school performance.
* Behavioural issues (frustration from inability to communicate).
* Articulation errors / unclear speech.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features vs Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
* Both present with poor response to name and speech delay.
* **Deafness**: +ve normal eye contact, +ve use of gestures to communicate, +ve seeking of social interaction.
* **ASD (自閉症譜系障礙)**: -ve eye contact, ↓ gestures, impaired joint attention, repetitive behaviours.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Common and Must-Not-Miss Differential Diagnoses

| Diagnosis | Type | Key Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Otitis Media with Effusion (OME)** | Conductive | **Most common**. Post-URTI. Dull/retracted tympanic membrane, flat tympanogram. Usually transient. |
| **Genetic SNHL (e.g., GJB2)** | Sensorineural | +ve Family history. Often non-syndromic. Confirmed by genetic panel. |
| **Congenital CMV** | Sensorineural | Progressive/late-onset SNHL. ± Petechiae, microcephaly, periventricular calcifications. |
| **Bacterial Meningitis** | Sensorineural | **Must-Not-Miss**. Post-infection complication. Urgent audiology required as cochlear ossification precludes cochlear implantation. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations for Hearing Loss
* **Otoscopy**: Check for wax, foreign body, signs of OME (dull, retracted, immobile tympanic membrane).
* **Distraction Test**: Conducted at Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC) level (~7-9 months).

### Objective Hearing Tests (Infants / Uncooperative)
* **Automated Otoacoustic Emissions (AOAE)**: Tests outer hair cell function (cochlea). 1st-line for UNHS.
* **Automated Auditory Brainstem Response (AABR)**: Tests auditory nerve pathway to brainstem. Definitive test for infants / NICU grads.

### Subjective Hearing Tests (Older Children)
* **Visual Reinforcement Audiometry (VRA)**: 6 months - 2.5 years. Conditions child to turn to visual reward when sound heard.
* **Play Audiometry**: 2.5 - 5 years. Child performs action (e.g., puts block in box) when sound heard.
* **Pure Tone Audiometry (PTA)**: > 5 years. Standard test using headphones and button press.

### Middle Ear Testing
* **Tympanometry**: Objective test of middle ear function/TM mobility. Flat curve = fluid (OME).

### Aetiological Yield Investigations
* **Bloods**: Genetic testing (GJB2 panel), CMV PCR (on newborn dried blood spot/urine if delayed presentation).
* **Imaging**: MRI Brain/Internal Auditory Meatus (IAM) (check CN VIII integrity, rule out structural anomalies).
* **Special**: ECG (Rule out **Jervell and Lange-Nielsen syndrome** → prolonged QT + severe SNHL).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Hearing Loss
* Generally not applicable unless presentation is sudden acute SNHL (requires urgent ENT referral/steroids) or acute otitis media (requires Abx).

### Chronic Management of Conductive Hearing Loss (OME)
* **Watchful Waiting**: Often resolves spontaneously.
* **Surgical**: If persistent > 3 months with significant hearing loss → **Myringotomy and Grommet (Tympanostomy tubes) insertion** ± Adenoidectomy.
* **Devices**: Bone conduction hearing aids if surgery contraindicated/delayed.

### Chronic Management of Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL)
* **Hearing Aids (助聽器)**: 1st-line for mild to severe SNHL. Fit as early as possible (< 6 months of age).
* **Cochlear Implants (CI) (人工耳蝸)**: 
  * Indication: Severe-to-profound SNHL where hearing aids are insufficient.
  * Funding: Supported by HA / Samaritan Fund in HK.
  * Timing: Early implantation (< 2 years) offers best chance for normal speech development.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Support
* **Audiologist**: Regular monitoring of hearing thresholds and device mapping.
* **Speech Therapist (ST)**: Intensive **Auditory-Verbal Therapy (AVT)** post-CI or hearing aid fitting.
* **Education Bureau (EDB)**: Provision of FM systems in mainstream classrooms, or placement in special schools for the deaf if using sign language.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding and Psychosocial Red Flags
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) Referral**: Essential for financial assistance (Hearing aids, CI batteries, and maintenance are expensive).
* **Medical Neglect**: Monitor for parental non-compliance with audiology appointments or failure to ensure the child wears prescribed hearing devices.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications of Unrecognised Hearing Loss
* Permanent, irreversible deficits in speech, language, cognitive, and social-emotional development.
* Poor academic attainment and social isolation.

### Prognosis with Early Intervention
* With early detection via UNHS and early intervention (<6 months) using CI/Hearing aids + ST, children can achieve mainstream education and age-appropriate speech equivalence.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Diagnosing a child with speech delay as having autism without checking hearing.
* Pearl: Audiology is the absolute mandatory first step in ANY child with speech delay.
* Viva Question: What is the most common non-genetic cause of sensorineural hearing loss?
* Answer: Congenital Cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection.
* Pitfall: Delaying audiology assessment after a child recovers from bacterial meningitis. Post-meningitis cochlear ossification can occur rapidly, precluding successful cochlear implantation if delayed.
