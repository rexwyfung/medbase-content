
# Childhood Hearing Loss / Deafness (兒童聽力障礙 / 失聰)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Partial or total inability to hear. Can be Conductive (outer/middle ear), Sensorineural (inner ear/auditory nerve), or Mixed.
- **Local Epidemiology**: ~1-2 per 1000 newborns have significant bilateral sensorineural hearing loss. Hong Kong has a Universal Neonatal Hearing Screening (UNHS) programme implemented by the Department of Health (DH).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Conductive (CHL)**: Sound waves cannot reach the inner ear.
  - *Causes*: Otitis Media with Effusion (OME - most common), impacted wax, foreign body, congenital atresia/microtia.
- **Sensorineural (SNHL)**: Damage to the cochlea or auditory nerve.
  - *Congenital/Genetic (50%)*: Non-syndromic (e.g., Connexin 26/GJB2 mutations - very common in Chinese), Syndromic (Waardenburg, Usher, Pendred, Alport).
  - *Acquired/Environmental*: Congenital infections (CMV is the leading non-genetic cause, Rubella), extreme prematurity, ototoxic drugs (Aminoglycosides like Gentamicin), neonatal hyperbilirubinaemia (Kernicterus), bacterial meningitis (causes cochlear ossification).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Infants**: Fails UNHS, lack of startle reflex to loud noises, failure to turn to sound by 6 months, absence of babbling by 12 months.
- **Toddlers/Children**: **Speech and language delay** (most common presentation if missed at birth), inattentiveness, turning the TV volume up high, poor school performance, behavioural issues (frustration).
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)**: Both can present with poor response to name and speech delay. *Crucial difference*: Deaf children usually have normal eye contact, use gestures to communicate, and seek social interaction, unlike children with ASD.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - OME (Conductive, usually transient).
  - Genetic SNHL (Connexin 26 mutation).
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Bacterial meningitis (requires urgent audiology assessment post-recovery as cochlear ossification can occur rapidly, precluding cochlear implantation).
  - Congenital CMV (can cause progressive, late-onset hearing loss).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **Otoscopy**: Check for wax, foreign body, signs of OME (dull, retracted, immobile tympanic membrane).
  - **Distraction Test**: (Health visitor/MCHC level, ~7-9 months).
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Objective Tests (Infants/Uncooperative)**:
    - **Otoacoustic Emissions (OAE)**: Tests outer hair cell function (used in UNHS).
    - **Auditory Brainstem Response (ABR)**: Tests auditory nerve pathway. Definitive test for infants.
    - **Subjective Tests (Older children)**:
    - Visual Reinforcement Audiometry (VRA) (6 months - 2.5 years).
    - Play Audiometry (2.5 - 5 years).
    - Pure Tone Audiometry (PTA) (> 5 years).
  - **Aetiological Yield**: MRI Brain/Internal Auditory Meatus, Genetic testing (GJB2 panel), CMV PCR (on newborn dried blood spot if delayed presentation), ECG (to rule out Jervell and Lange-Nielsen syndrome - prolonged QT).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Not applicable.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - **Conductive (OME)**: Often resolves spontaneously. If persistent > 3 months with significant hearing loss, consider Myringotomy and Grommet insertion.
  - **Sensorineural**:
    - **Hearing Aids**: First-line for mild to severe SNHL. Fit as early as possible (ideally < 6 months of age).
    - **Cochlear Implants (CI)**: For severe-to-profound SNHL where hearing aids are insufficient. Funded by HA/Samaritan Fund in HK. Early implantation (< 2 years) offers the best chance for normal speech development.
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **Speech Therapy**: Intensive Auditory-Verbal Therapy (AVT) post-CI or hearing aid fitting.
- **Education**: Support from EDB (e.g., FM systems in classrooms, special schools for the deaf if using sign language).
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- MSW referral for financial assistance (hearing aids can be expensive).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Unrecognised hearing loss leads to permanent deficits in speech, language, and cognitive development.
- With early detection (UNHS) and early intervention (CI/Hearing aids + Speech therapy), children can achieve mainstream education and normal speech.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Diagnosing a child with speech delay as having autism without checking hearing. *Pearl*: Audiology is the absolute mandatory first step in ANY child with speech delay.
- **Viva Question**: "What is the most common non-genetic cause of sensorineural hearing loss?" *Answer*: Congenital Cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection.
