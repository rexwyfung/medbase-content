# Cleft Lip and Palate (唇顎裂)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Cleft Lip and Palate
* **Cleft Lip (唇裂):** Congenital facial defect → failure of fusion of **maxillary processes (上頜突)** & **medial nasal processes (內鼻突)** at 5-6 wks gestation.
* **Cleft Palate (顎裂):** Congenital defect → failure of fusion of **palatal shelves (顎突)** at 8-10 wks gestation.
* Can be isolated or syndromic; unilateral or bilateral; complete or incomplete.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Incidence: **High in Asian populations** (incl. HK) → ~**1 in 500-700** live births.
* Cleft lip (+/- palate) → More common in **males**.
* Isolated cleft palate → More common in **females**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Embryological Pathophysiology
* **Primary palate defect (Lip/Alveolus):** Failure of mesodermal migration & fusion of nasal/maxillary prominences.
* **Secondary palate defect (Hard/Soft Palate):** Failure of palatine shelves to elevate, appose, and fuse.

### Genetic and Syndromic Associations
* **Multifactorial etiology:** Polygenic inheritance + environmental triggers.
* **Syndromic (15-30% of cases):**
    * **Pierre Robin Sequence (皮埃爾·羅賓序列症)**
    * **DiGeorge Syndrome (迪喬治症候群)** (22q11.2 microdeletion)
    * **Patau Syndrome (巴陶氏症候群)** (Trisomy 13)
    * **Edwards Syndrome (愛德華氏症候群)** (Trisomy 18)

### Environmental Teratogens
* **Maternal smoking** & **alcohol** consumption.
* Antiepileptics: **Phenytoin (苯妥英)**, **Valproate (丙戊酸)**.
* Maternal folate deficiency.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Antenatal and Neonatal Presentation
* Antenatal: Diagnosed via **20-week morphology ultrasound (結構超聲波)** (cleft lip easily seen; palate difficult).
* Neonatal: Diagnosed at birth during routine **Newborn Physical Examination (新生兒身體檢查)**.
* Feeding issues: Maternal reports of **milk pooling**, **nasal regurgitation (鼻腔反流)**, poor suckling.

### Physical Signs of Cleft Lip/Palate
* Visible gap in upper lip (unilateral/bilateral).
* Palpable/visible gap in roof of mouth (hard/soft palate).
* Nasal deformity: Depressed alar cartilage on the cleft side.

### Submucous Cleft Palate Triad
* Often missed; mucosa is intact but underlying muscle/bone is clefted.
* **Bifid uvula (懸雍垂裂)**.
* **Zona pellucida (透明帶)** (bluish central line across soft palate).
* **Notch** palpable in posterior hard palate.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Isolated vs. Syndromic Clefts
* **Isolated:** No extra-craniofacial anomalies; normal neurodevelopment.
* **Syndromic Red Flags:**
    * **Lower lip pits (下唇凹陷):** Pathognomonic for **Van der Woude syndrome**.
    * **Micrognathia (小頜畸形):** Suggests **Pierre Robin Sequence**.
    * **Cardiac murmurs / Hypocalcemia:** Suggests **22q11.2 deletion**.
    * **Polydactyly / Cutis aplasia:** Suggests **Trisomy 13**.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Syndromic Cleft Deformities

| Syndrome | Key Clinical Features | Underlying Genetics/Pathology |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pierre Robin Sequence (皮埃爾·羅賓序列症)** | Micrognathia + Glossoptosis + U-shaped cleft palate | Mandibular hypoplasia → tongue prevents palatal fusion |
| **Trisomy 13 (Patau Syndrome)** | Cleft lip/palate, holoprosencephaly, microphthalmia, polydactyly | Trisomy 13 (Nondisjunction) |
| **Treacher Collins Syndrome** | Down-slanting palpebral fissures, malar hypoplasia, microtia, cleft palate | *TCOF1* gene mutation (Autosomal Dominant) |
| **Van der Woude Syndrome** | Cleft lip/palate + bilateral lower lip pits | *IRF6* gene mutation (Autosomal Dominant) |
| **DiGeorge Syndrome (22q11.2DS)** | Cleft palate, thymic aplasia (immune deficiency), congenital heart defects (Tetralogy of Fallot), hypocalcemia | 22q11.2 microdeletion |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations for Cleft Deformities
* **Complete Neonatal Examination:** Rule out systemic malformations (cardiac, limb, neuro).
* **Palate Palpation:** Gloved finger insertion to exclude submucous cleft.

### Special Investigations & Genetics
* **Chromosomal Microarray (CMA) / Karyotype:** Indicated if dysmorphic features or multiple congenital anomalies +ve.
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波):** If cardiac murmur detected (r/o VSD, TOF associated with syndromic clefts).

### Audiological Screening for Cleft Palate
* **High risk of Eustachian tube dysfunction (咽鼓管功能障礙)** → recurrent **Otitis Media with Effusion (OME / 積液性中耳炎)**.
* **Otoacoustic Emissions (OAE)** or **Automated Auditory Brainstem Response (AABR)** screening mandatory at birth.
* Requires regular follow-up with **Audiologist (聽力學家)**.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Feeding Management
* Standard bottles fail → infants cannot generate -ve intraoral pressure.
* **Specialized bottles:** **Haberman feeder (哈伯曼奶樽)** / squeezable bottles with cross-cut teats.
* Upright feeding posture → ↓ nasal regurgitation & ↓ choking risk.
* NG tube feeding rarely needed unless Pierre Robin (severe airway/swallowing compromise).

### Surgical Timeline (Hong Kong HA Protocol)

| Procedure | Target Age | Criteria / Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cleft Lip Repair (Cheiloplasty)** | ~3 Months | **Rule of 10s:** 10 weeks old, 10 lbs weight, 10 g/dL Hb. |
| **Cleft Palate Repair (Palatoplasty)** | 6 - 12 Months | Balanced timing: Early enough for **speech development**, late enough for **maxillary bone growth**. |
| **Alveolar Bone Graft (牙槽骨植骨)** | 8 - 10 Years | Before eruption of the permanent maxillary canine tooth. |
| **Orthognathic Surgery / Rhinoplasty** | Post-puberty (16-18 Yrs) | Performed after facial skeletal growth is complete. |

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Roles
* **Plastic / Maxillofacial Surgeon:** Primary surgical repair.
* **ENT Surgeon:** Management of OME (may insert grommets during palate repair).
* **Speech Therapist (言語治療師):** Crucial for preventing/treating **Velopharyngeal Insufficiency (VPI)** & hypernasal speech.
* **Orthodontist (牙齒矯正科醫生) / Pediatric Dentist:** Management of malocclusion & dental anomalies.

### Psychosocial Support (MSW & SWD)
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW / 醫務社工):** Counseling for parental grief/guilt ("loss of the perfect baby").
* **Social Welfare Department (SWD / 社會福利署):** Financial assistance for specialized bottles or long-term therapy if needed.
* Connection to local patient support groups (e.g., HK Cleft Lip and Palate Association).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Maternal-Infant Bonding & Neglect Risks
* Visible facial deformities heavily impact maternal-infant attachment.
* Feeding frustrations ↑ maternal stress & risk of postnatal depression.
* **Maternal & Child Health Centre (MCHC / 母嬰健康院)** nurses must monitor closely for signs of neglect, failure to thrive (FTT), or poor bonding.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-Term Complications of Cleft Deformities
* **ENT / Hearing:** Recurrent AOM, persistent OME → **Conductive Hearing Loss (傳導性聽力受損)**.
* **Speech:** **Velopharyngeal Insufficiency (齶咽閉合不全)** → hypernasality, articulation errors.
* **Dental:** Missing teeth (lateral incisors), supernumerary teeth, severe malocclusion.
* **Psychological:** Poor self-esteem, bullying due to facial scarring or speech differences.

### Prognosis
* Overall prognosis for isolated cleft lip/palate is **excellent** with timely HA MDT intervention.
* Syndromic cleft prognosis depends heavily on underlying cardiac/neurological comorbidities.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the Rule of 10s for cleft lip repair? Answer: 10 weeks of age, 10 pounds in weight, and 10 g/dL of Hemoglobin.
* Viva Question: Why repair the cleft palate specifically at 6-12 months of age? Answer: To optimize the balance between allowing maximal mid-facial skeletal growth (favors delayed repair) and ensuring normal speech development before bad habits form (favors early repair).
* Viva Question: A neonate with a cleft palate presents with respiratory distress and a small jaw. What is the diagnosis and acute management? Answer: Pierre Robin Sequence. Manage with prone positioning or nasopharyngeal airway to prevent the tongue (glossoptosis) from obstructing the airway.
* Trap: Assuming a newborn with a cleft palate can breastfeed normally. Pearl: Cleft palate prevents the creation of negative suction pressure; they require specialized squeezable bottles (e.g., Haberman feeder) or maternal breast milk expression.
* Pitfall: Discharging a cleft palate neonate without an audiology referral. Cleft palate infants have abnormal tensor veli palatini insertion, leading to Eustachian tube dysfunction and a high risk of covert OME and permanent conductive hearing loss. Always screen hearing aggressively.
