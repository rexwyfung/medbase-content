# Cleft Lip and Palate (唇顎裂)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Congenital facial developmental defect resulting from failure of fusion of maxillary and medial nasal processes (lip) or palatal shelves (palate).
- **Local Epidemiology**: Incidence in Asia (including HK) is relatively high (approx. 1 in 500-700 live births).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Multifactorial etiology: Genetic (often isolated, but sometimes syndromic e.g. Pierre Robin, DiGeorge, Patau Trisomy 13) and Environmental (maternal smoking, alcohol, anti-epileptics like Phenytoin/Valproate).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Diagnosed antenatally (20-week morphology scan) or immediately at birth during newborn examination.
- Mother reports pooling of milk, nasal regurgitation during feeding.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Visible gap in upper lip (unilateral or bilateral) and/or roof of mouth (hard/soft palate).
- Submucous cleft palate (bifid uvula, zona pellucida, notch in posterior hard palate).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Must rule out complex syndromic associations (e.g., lower lip pits indicate Van der Woude syndrome).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Pierre Robin Sequence (micrognathia + glossoptosis + U-shaped cleft palate)
- Trisomy 13 (Patau) / Trisomy 18 (Edwards)
- Treacher Collins syndrome

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**: Complete neonatal physical exam.
- **Special**: Genetic screening (Microarray/Karyotype) if dysmorphic features present.
- **Auditory**: Hearing screening (Otoacoustic emissions / AABR) due to high risk of Eustachian tube dysfunction and otitis media with effusion.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**: Feeding support (Specialized squeezable bottles like Haberman feeder, Haberman teat).
- **Surgical Timeline (HA)**: 
  - Lip repair: Approx 3 months of age (Rule of 10s: 10 lbs, 10 weeks, 10g/dL Hb).
  - Palate repair: Approx 6-12 months (before speech development).
  - Alveolar bone graft: Approx 8-10 years (before eruption of permanent canine).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **MDT**: Cleft Surgeon (Plastic/Maxillofacial), Orthodontist, Speech Therapist (crucial), ENT surgeon, Audiologist.
- **Psychosocial**: MSW coordinates psychological support for parents grieving the 'perfect baby'. SWD can provide financial aid for long-term therapy.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Visible deformities can impact maternal-infant bonding. Health visitors/MCHC nurses monitor for bonding issues or neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Speech difficulties (velopharyngeal insufficiency), recurrent otitis media, hearing loss, dental anomalies, cosmetic and psychological issues.
- Overall prognosis is excellent with timely MDT care.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Viva Q**: "Why repair the palate at 6-12 months?" Answer: To balance allowing maximal facial growth (delayed repair is better for bone) against the need for normal speech development (early repair is required).
- **Trap**: Using normal feeding bottles. They rely on creating negative intraoral pressure, which a cleft baby cannot achieve. They need squeezable bottles.
