# Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) (心室中隔缺損)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
* Congenital defect in ventricular septum (心室中隔) → abnormal communication between LV & RV.
* Acquired VSD can occur post-MI (rare in paediatrics).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Most common congenital heart disease (CHD) globally & in HK.
* Accounts for ~30% of all CHD cases.
* Prevalence: ~2-3 per 1000 live births.
* HA screening protocol: Often detected incidentally by MCHC routine physical exam (neonatal & 6-8 weeks).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Anatomical Types of VSD
* **Perimembranous (80%)**: Membranous septum, near aortic/tricuspid valves. Can close via tricuspid valve septal leaflet aneurysm.
* **Muscular (15%)**: Muscular septum. Swiss-cheese appearance possible. High spontaneous closure rate.
* **Inlet (5%)**: Postero-inferior to perimembranous. Associated with AVSD & Down Syndrome (唐氏綜合症).
* **Outlet / Supracristal (<5%)**: Below pulmonary valve. Asian descent ↑ risk. High risk of aortic valve prolapse/regurgitation.

### Haemodynamic Pathophysiology
* LV pressure > RV pressure → L-to-R shunt during systole.
* Shunt volume depends on defect size & Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR).
* ↑ Pulmonary blood flow (pulmonary overcirculation) → ↑ venous return to LA & LV.
* LA & LV volume overload → LV dilation → Left-sided Heart Failure (左心衰竭).
* RV pressure remains normal unless defect is large (equalizes pressures).

### Associated Genetic Syndromes
* Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21) (唐氏綜合症) → Inlet VSD (part of AVSD).
* Edwards Syndrome (Trisomy 18) (愛德華氏綜合症).
* Patau Syndrome (Trisomy 13) (巴陶氏綜合症).
* DiGeorge Syndrome (22q11.2 deletion).

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Presentation by Defect Size
* **Small VSD (<3mm)**: 
  * Asymptomatic.
  * Incidental murmur picked up at MCHC check.
  * Normal growth & development.
* **Moderate/Large VSD**:
  * Asymptomatic at birth (PVR = SVR → minimal shunt).
  * Presents at 4-8 weeks as PVR naturally ↓.
  * Heart Failure signs: Poor feeding, diaphoresis (sweating) during feeds, Failure to Thrive (FTT) (生長遲緩).
  * Recurrent respiratory infections.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Auscultation Findings
* **Murmur**: Harsh, blowing, pan-systolic murmur at lower left sternal border (LLSB).
* **Paradox**: Small VSD → louder, harsher murmur (↑ turbulence). Large VSD → softer murmur.
* **S2**: Normal or split. Prominent P2 if Pulmonary Hypertension (PH) develops.
* **Apical Mid-diastolic Rumble**: Indicates massive L-to-R shunt (↑ relative flow across mitral valve).

### Signs of Heart Failure (心衰竭)
* Tachypnoea (氣促), tachycardia.
* Active precordium, palpable apical thrill.
* Displaced apex beat (LV dilation).
* Hepatomegaly (肝腫大) → secondary to systemic venous congestion.
* Diaphoresis during feeds.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing VSD from ASD and PDA
| Feature | VSD (心室中隔缺損) | ASD (心房中隔缺損) | PDA (動脈導管未閉) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Murmur Type** | Pan-systolic, harsh | Ejection systolic, soft | Continuous "machinery" |
| **Murmur Location** | LLSB | Upper left sternal edge | Left infraclavicular area |
| **S2 Finding** | Normal/prominent P2 | Fixed, widely split S2 | Normal |
| **Pulse** | Normal | Normal | Bounding pulse, wide pulse pressure |
| **Overload Chamber** | LA, LV | RA, RV | LA, LV |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differentials for Pan-systolic Murmur
* **Innocent Murmur (Still's)**: Soft, vibratory/musical, LLSB, loudest supine, ↓ intensity when sitting/standing. No HF signs.
* **Mitral Regurgitation (二尖瓣關閉不全)**: Pan-systolic, apex, radiates to axilla.
* **Tricuspid Regurgitation**: Pan-systolic, LLSB, ↑ intensity with inspiration (Carvallo's sign).
* **Tetralogy of Fallot (法洛氏四聯症)**: Large VSD present, but presents with cyanosis (發紺) & single S2 (right-to-left shunt due to severe PS).

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Blood Investigations
* **Pulse Oximetry**: Normal (>95%) in isolated L-to-R shunt. ↓ if Eisenmenger or concurrent infection.
* **ECG (心電圖)**:
  * Small VSD: Normal.
  * Large VSD: LV Hypertrophy (LVH) & LA Enlargement (LAE) (tall R waves in V5/V6, deep S waves in V1).
  * Late stage: Biventricular hypertrophy if PH develops.

### Imaging Investigations
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**:
  * Gold Standard.
  * Confirms anatomy (size, location, number of defects).
  * Assesses haemodynamic impact (chamber dilation, degree of shunting).
  * Estimates PA pressure.
* **CXR (胸部X光)**:
  * Small VSD: Normal.
  * Large VSD: Cardiomegaly (心臟肥大), ↑ pulmonary vascular markings (pulmonary plethora / lung congestion).

### Special / Advanced Investigations
* **Cardiac Catheterization**: Rare now for pure diagnosis. Used for transcatheter device closure or assessing reversibility of PH before surgery.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Medical Management of Heart Failure
* Bridge to surgery / optimization.
* **Diuretics**: Furosemide (Lasix) PO/IV + Spironolactone (Aldactone) to ↓ preload & pulmonary congestion.
* **ACE Inhibitors**: Captopril/Enalapril to ↓ systemic afterload → promotes forward flow into aorta → ↓ L-to-R shunt.

### Chronic & Nutritional Management
* **Small VSD**: Reassurance & MCHC/Paediatric SOPD follow-up. 75% muscular & many perimembranous close spontaneously by 2 yrs.
* **Nutrition (Crucial HK HA Focus)**:
  * High-calorie formula (e.g., Infatrini 1 kcal/ml) to counteract ↑ metabolic demand of HF.
  * NG tube feeding if tiring easily during oral feeds (prevents FTT).
* **Dental Hygiene**: Crucial to prevent Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎). Routine antibiotic prophylaxis no longer strictly recommended in HK for isolated simple VSDs unless history of IE or prosthetic material used.

### Surgical & Procedural Management
* **Indications**: FTT despite max medical therapy, symptomatic large VSD, or evolving Pulmonary Hypertension.
* **Timing**: Usually 3-6 months of age.
* **Transcatheter Device Closure**: For suitable perimembranous or muscular VSDs (minimally invasive).
* **Open Surgical Patch Closure**: Gold standard for large/complex defects or if transcatheter fails/unsuitable.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Paediatric Cardiologist / Cardiothoracic Surgeon**: Diagnostic imaging, medical management, definitive repair.
* **Dietitian**: Optimization of caloric intake (high-energy feeds) & growth monitoring.
* **Speech Therapist**: For feeding difficulties / swallowing coordination in severe HF.

### Psychosocial & SWD Support
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Financial assistance (e.g., Samaritan Fund for special formula or device costs), emotional support for parents coping with CHD diagnosis.
* **MCHC**: Routine growth & developmental surveillance post-discharge.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Recognizing Neglect in Failure to Thrive
* FTT is classic in large VSD, but MUST rule out non-organic causes (neglect, improper formula mixing to save money).
* Red Flag: FTT out of proportion to VSD size, or missed MCHC/SOPD appointments. Monitor closely for Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) or neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Disease Complications
* **Eisenmenger Syndrome (艾森曼格綜合症)**: Chronic large L-to-R shunt → irreversible pulmonary vascular remodelling → severe PH → RV pressure > LV pressure → shunt reversal (R-to-L) → Cyanosis. Inoperable & fatal.
* **Aortic Regurgitation (主動脈瓣關閉不全)**: Common in outlet/supracristal VSD. Venturi effect pulls right aortic cusp into defect.
* **Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎)**: Lifelong risk due to turbulent jet hitting RV wall.
* **Right Ventricular Outflow Tract Obstruction (RVOTO)**: Acquired "Gasul" phenomenon (hypertrophy of RV muscle bundle).

### Prognosis & Natural History
* Excellent for small defects (most close spontaneously).
* Excellent post-surgical repair if done early (<1 year) before irreversible PH sets in.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why does a baby with a large VSD not present with heart failure on day 1 of life, but rather at 4-6 weeks?
  * Answer: At birth, PVR is high (equal to SVR), limiting the L-to-R shunt. As PVR naturally falls over the first few weeks, the shunt ↑ massively, causing LV volume overload & HF.
* Viva Question: What is Eisenmenger syndrome?
  * Answer: Irreversible severe pulmonary hypertension causing reversal of a L-to-R shunt into a R-to-L shunt, leading to progressive cyanosis.
* Trap: A softer murmur in a known VSD patient always means the defect is closing.
  * Pearl: A softening murmur could indicate equalizing ventricular pressures due to worsening Pulmonary Hypertension (Eisenmenger's). Always check P2 intensity and O2 sats!
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents of an asymptomatic baby with a harsh LLSB murmur that no follow-up is needed because "loud murmurs mean small holes".
  * Action: While true that small holes are louder, all suspected VSDs must have a baseline Echocardiogram to confirm anatomy, rule out multiple defects, and establish a baseline.
