# Dengue Fever (登革熱)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Dengue
- Acute mosquito-borne viral infection.
- Caused by **Dengue virus** (Flavivirus).
- 4 distinct serotypes: **DENV 1-4**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
- HK highly susceptible to local outbreaks → ubiquitous *Aedes albopictus* (白紋伊蚊) mosquito population.
- Peak incidence: Late summer/autumn.
- Case clusters: Hiking trails, construction sites, imported cases (from SE Asia).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Viremic Phase Pathophysiology
- Direct viral replication → triggers intense systemic inflammation & cytokine cascade.

### Critical Phase (Plasma Leakage) Pathophysiology
- Starts precisely during **defervescence** (fever dropping).
- Intense cytokine storm → transient, massive dysfunction of endothelial glycocalyx → marked **plasma leakage (血漿滲漏)**.

### Antibody-Dependent Enhancement (ADE)
- Sequential infection with a *different* DENV serotype.
- Existing antibodies bind new virus but cannot neutralize → facilitate viral entry into macrophages (巨噬細胞).
- Result: Exponentially worse disease → **Severe Dengue / Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (登革出血熱)** & **Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS) (登革休克綜合症)**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Febrile Phase (Days 1-3)
- Sudden high fever.
- **Retro-orbital pain** (pain behind eyes).
- Severe myalgia/arthralgia (**"breakbone fever" (斷骨熱)**).
- Facial flushing.
- +ve **Tourniquet test (止血帶試驗)**.
- Fine erythematous rash.

### Critical Phase (Days 4-6)
- **FEVER DROPS** (defervescence) → patient clinical status *worsens*.
- **Plasma Leakage Signs**: Pleural effusion, ascites, hemoconcentration (↑ Hematocrit).
- **Bleeding Signs**: Petechiae (瘀點), epistaxis (鼻出血), GI bleeds, mucosal bleeding.

### Recovery Phase (Days 7+)
- Extravasated fluid reabsorbs → risk of fluid overload.
- Bradycardia.
- Classic convalescent rash: **"Isles of white in a sea of red"** with desquamation.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Dengue vs. Influenza
- **Dengue**: Retro-orbital pain, profound thrombocytopenia out of nowhere, +ve tourniquet test.
- **Influenza**: Predominantly respiratory symptoms (cough, coryza), normal/mildly ↓ platelets.

### Dengue vs. Malaria
- **Dengue**: Continuous fever for days → severe clinical crash during defervescence; significant rash.
- **Malaria**: Cyclical fevers (q48-72h), rigors, splenomegaly, absent typical rash.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Tropical & Febrile Differentials
| Condition | Key Differentiating Feature |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Malaria (瘧疾)** | Cyclical fever, travel to endemic area, hepatosplenomegaly, +ve blood smear. |
| **Typhoid Fever (傷寒)** | Step-ladder fever, rose spots, relative bradycardia, GI symptoms (constipation/diarrhea). |
| **Leptospirosis (鈎端螺旋體病)** | Animal urine exposure (rats), conjunctival suffusion, calf tenderness, jaundice. |
| **Zika / Chikungunya** | Zika: conjunctivitis, prominent rash. Chikungunya: debilitating, prolonged polyarthritis. |
| **Influenza (流行性感冒)** | Upper/lower respiratory tract symptoms prominent, absent plasma leak. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
- **Tourniquet Test**: Inflate BP cuff midway between systolic & diastolic for 5 mins. +ve = >10-20 petechiae per square inch.

### Blood Investigations
- **CBC**: Initial leukopenia → rapidly followed by profound **thrombocytopenia (血小板減少症)**.
- **Hematocrit (HCT)**: Distinctly ↑ HCT is the hallmark of plasma leakage.
- **LFTs**: Transaminitis (↑ ALT/AST) very common.

### Definitive Diagnostics
- **Early (Days 1-5)**: **Dengue NS1 Antigen** test (highly specific) or Dengue PCR.
- **Late (Days 5+)**: **Dengue IgM/IgG** serology.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Supportive & Acute Management
- **Cornerstone**: STRICT, meticulous fluid balance management.
- **Antipyretics**: ONLY **Paracetamol (撲熱息痛)**.
- **CONTRAINDICATED**: ABSOLUTELY NO NSAIDs or **Aspirin (阿司匹林)** → severe hemorrhagic risk.

### Critical Phase Protocol (Plasma Leakage)
- ↑ HCT *without* shock: Cautious oral rehydration or strictly titrated IV crystalloids matching the leak rate.

### Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS) Management
- Aggressive IV fluid resuscitation: **10-20 ml/kg boluses** of isotonic crystalloids (e.g., 0.9% NaCl).
- If refractory to crystalloids: Consider colloids.
- Monitor response via HCT, vital signs, and urine output.

### Recovery Phase Protocol
- **STOP all IV fluids immediately**.
- Rationale: Prevent fatal pulmonary edema when 3rd-space leaked plasma naturally reabsorbs into intravascular space.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Public Health & HA Protocols
- Statutory **Notifiable Infectious Disease** in HK.
- Must immediately notify **Centre for Health Protection (CHP) (衞生防護中心)**.
- Triggers urgent vector control measures (e.g., fogging by FEHD) around residence/workplace to prevent local clusters.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations in Dengue
- N/A for direct abuse.
- Ensure parental compliance with mosquito repellent and bed nets if traveling to endemic regions.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Major Complications
- **Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS)**: Life-threatening hypovolemic shock.
- **Severe Mucosal Bleeding**: GI, intracranial, pulmonary hemorrhage.
- **Organ Impairment**: Fulminant hepatic failure, myocarditis, encephalopathy.

### Prognosis
- Mortality is very low (<1%) IF fluid resuscitation is managed perfectly during the critical 48-hour plasma leakage window.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Relaxing and thinking the child is getting better because their fever has broken. Pearl: In Dengue, the moment the fever breaks (defervescence) is exactly when the lethal shock phase begins.
* Viva Question: Why is checking the Hematocrit (HCT) along with platelets so crucial in Dengue? Answer: Because the HCT indicates plasma leakage. The plasma leaks out of the vessels, but the red blood cells stay inside, making the blood thick and sludge-like (high HCT). A rising HCT + dropping platelets forecasts impending shock.
* Pitfall: Prescribing NSAIDs for severe bone/joint pain in Dengue. This drastically exacerbates the risk of catastrophic bleeding; strictly use Paracetamol.
