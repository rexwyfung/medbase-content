# Common Variable Immune Deficiency (CVID) (常見變異型免疫缺陷病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A highly variable, primary humoral immunodeficiency. It translates to essentially "Adult-onset XLA," though it can strike in childhood.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- The exact genetic cause is often unknown (polygenic), though some defects (like TACI mutations) are identified.
- **The Core Problem**: B-cells physically exist in normal or near-normal numbers, but they are "broken." They cannot undergo class-switching or differentiate into plasma cells.
- Therefore, they produce very little IgG, IgA, and/or IgM.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- A 15-year-old girl with a history of five pneumonias in the last three years, chronic sinus infections, and recently diagnosed with Idiopathic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Infections**: Exact same recurrent sinopulmonary bacterial infections as XLA (Pneumococcus, Haemophilus).
- **Autoimmunity**: Very high rate of autoimmune diseases (ITP, Autoimmune Hemolytic Anemia, Rheumatoid Arthritis, Vitiligo). Up to 25% of patients.
- **Lymphoproliferation**: Unlike XLA where nodes are empty, CVID patients often have ENLARGED lymph nodes, massive splenomegaly, and granulomas.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from XLA: CVID hits older children/adults (often age 10-30), hits females equally to males, B-cell count (CD19) is normal on flow cytometry, and they HAVE tonsils/lymph nodes.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- XLA (Bruton)
- Selective IgA deficiency
- Secondary Hypogammaglobulinemia (e.g., losing proteins via Nephrotic Syndrome or Protein-Losing Enteropathy, or due to Rituximab therapy).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Immunoglobulins**: Significantly decreased IgG + decreased IgA and/or IgM.
- **Vaccine Response (CRITICAL)**: Diagnosis REQUIRES demonstrating an impaired functional antibody response to vaccines (e.g., giving them the Pneumovax 23 vaccine and showing they failed to build IgG titers 4 weeks later).
- **Flow Cytometry**: Normal or slightly low CD19+ B-cells (proves they aren't XLA).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Replacement**: Lifelong IVIG or Subcutaneous IG (SCIG) replacement therapy to keep trough IgG levels > 800 mg/dL.
- **Screening**: High-resolution CT chest to monitor for bronchiectasis. Regular GI endoscopies if symptomatic to screen for gastric cancer.
- **Autoimmunity**: Treat concurrent ITP/AIHA with steroids or Rituximab as needed.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Chronic disease burden. Often requires respiratory physiotherapy to clear bronchiectatic sputum.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **Bronchiectasis**: The major cause of morbidity and eventual respiratory failure.
- **Malignancy Risk**: Markedly increased risk of Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma and Gastric Carcinoma (often driven by un-cleared H. pylori or pernicious anemia).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Missing CVID in a teenager purely presenting with "tough to treat asthma". If a teen's "asthma" involves constant green sputum and actual pneumonia consolidations, check their immunoglobulins.
- **Viva Q**: "Why do we test vaccine responses to diagnose CVID?" Answer: Because simply having low IgG isn't enough to prove the immune system is broken (they could just be losing IgG in their urine). Injecting a vaccine and failing to produce specific antibodies proves the B-cells are functionally defective at class-switching and memory formation.
