# Pulmonary Stenosis (肺動脈瓣狹窄)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Pulmonary Stenosis
* **Definition**: Narrowing of the pulmonary valve (valvar), below the valve (subvalvar/infundibular), or above the valve (supravalvar) → obstruction of blood flow from right ventricle (RV) to pulmonary artery (PA) → Right Ventricular Outflow Tract Obstruction (**RVOTO**).

### Local Epidemiology and Syndromic Associations
* Accounts for ~10% of congenital heart disease (CHD) in HK.
* Often isolated but frequently associated with genetic/chromosomal syndromes.
* **Key Associations**: 
  * **Noonan syndrome (努南氏綜合症)** (often features dysplastic valves).
  * **Congenital Rubella Syndrome (先天性風疹綜合症)** (often features peripheral PA stenosis).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Haemodynamic Consequences of RVOTO
* RVOTO → ↑ RV pressure to maintain cardiac output → **Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (右心室肥大)**.
* Progressive/severe obstruction → ↑ right atrial (RA) pressure → Right Heart Failure (**RHF**).

### Pathophysiology of Critical Pulmonary Stenosis
* Severe valvar stenosis in neonates = **Duct-Dependent Pulmonary Circulation (依賴導管的肺循環)**.
* RV cannot pump adequate blood to lungs → survival depends on:
  * Right-to-left shunting across **foramen ovale** → profound **Cyanosis (發紺)**.
  * Left-to-right shunting across Patent Ductus Arteriosus (**PDA**) to supply pulmonary circulation.
* Closure of PDA at 1-2 days of life → rapid onset of hypoxia, acidosis, and shock.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms by Severity of Pulmonary Stenosis
* **Mild**: Asymptomatic. Detected as incidental murmur during routine MCHC/school checks.
* **Moderate/Severe**: Exertional dyspnoea (勞動性氣促), fatigue, syncope, signs of RHF (hepatomegaly, peripheral oedema).
* **Neonatal Critical**: Profound **Cyanosis (發紺)** and shock rapidly following birth.

### Pathognomonic Auscultation Findings
* **Ejection Systolic Murmur (收縮期噴射性雜音)**: Loudest at Left Upper Sternal Border (**LUSB**), radiating to the back/left axilla.
* **Ejection Click (噴射性喀喇音)** (Valvar PS only): Only right-sided event that ↓ with inspiration (↑ venous return forces stiff valve open slightly before systole).
* **Heart Sounds**: Widely split S2 (delayed P2 due to prolonged RV ejection time). P2 may be soft/absent in severe disease.

### Palpation Findings in Severe Pulmonary Stenosis
* Prominent 'a' wave in JVP (forceful RA contraction against a stiff, hypertrophied RV).
* **Parasternal heave** (indicates RVH).
* Systolic thrill at LUSB.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing Pulmonary Stenosis from Similar Murmurs
| Feature | **Pulmonary Stenosis (肺動脈瓣狹窄)** | **Atrial Septal Defect (心房中隔缺損)** | **Aortic Stenosis (主動脈瓣狹窄)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Murmur Location** | LUSB | LUSB | RUSB |
| **Murmur Radiation** | Back / Left axilla | Back | Carotids / Neck |
| **S2 Split** | Widely split (varies with respiration) | Widely split & **Fixed** | Paradoxical split (if severe) |
| **Ejection Click** | Present (↓ with inspiration) | Absent | Present (no variation with inspiration) |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Most Likely Differential Diagnosis
* Isolated valvar pulmonary stenosis.

### Must-Not-Miss Differential Diagnoses
* **Tetralogy of Fallot (法洛四聯症)**: PS is a core component, but must identify VSD, overriding aorta, and RVH.
* **Critical Neonatal PS**: Duct-dependent emergency presenting with severe cyanosis & collapse.
* **Peripheral Pulmonary Stenosis**: Consider Congenital Rubella or Williams syndrome.

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations for Pulmonary Stenosis
* **ECG (心電圖)**: 
  * Right axis deviation (RAD).
  * Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (**RVH**): Tall R waves in V1, deep S waves in V6. Upright T wave in V1 (pathological > 1 month old).
  * Right atrial enlargement: Peaked P waves (if severe).

### Imaging Investigations for Pulmonary Stenosis
* **CXR (胸部X光)**: 
  * Normal heart size (unless RHF is present).
  * Prominent main pulmonary artery (post-stenotic dilatation).
  * Normal or ↓ pulmonary vascular markings (oligaemia if severe).

### Gold Standard Investigation
* **Echocardiogram (超聲心動圖)**: 
  * Diagnostic. Delineates site of stenosis (valvar, subvalvar, supravalvar).
  * Assesses RV function and hypertrophy.
  * Doppler measures transvalvular pressure gradient to determine severity.

### Echocardiographic Severity Grading
| Grade | Transvalvular Gradient |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Mild** | < 30 mmHg |
| **Moderate** | 30 - 50 mmHg |
| **Severe** | > 50 mmHg (Intervention strongly indicated if > 64 mmHg) |

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Critical Neonatal PS
* **Resuscitation (ABCDE)**: Secure airway, provide O2 (monitor sats, avoid hyperoxia if duct-dependent).
* **Medical Therapy**: **Prostaglandin E1 (Alprostadil/前列腺素)** IV infusion (e.g., 5 ng/kg/min) → maintains PDA patency → restores pulmonary blood flow. Monitor for apnoea/hypotension.
* Urgent transfer to paediatric cardiology/NICU (e.g., HKCH).

### Chronic and Definitive Management of PS
* **Mild (Gradient < 30 mmHg)**: Conservative observation. Regular cardiology OPD follow-up. Will often not progress.
* **Moderate/Severe (Gradient > 50-64 mmHg or Symptomatic)**:
  * **1st Line**: **Transcatheter Balloon Valvuloplasty (經皮球囊瓣膜成形術)**. Treatment of choice for typical valvar PS. High success rate, minimally invasive.
  * **2nd Line/Specific Indications**: **Surgical Valvotomy/Repair (外科瓣膜切開術)**. Indicated if balloon fails, or for subvalvar/supravalvar stenosis, or dysplastic valves (e.g., Noonan syndrome).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Clinical Geneticist (臨床遺傳學家)**: Crucial for syndromic screening (e.g., microarray/gene panel for Noonan syndrome) if dysmorphic features exist.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Support for families dealing with acute NICU admissions or navigating long-term congenital heart disease care in HK.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Considerations in Congenital Heart Disease
* Parental anxiety and guilt surrounding CHD diagnoses are high; psychological support is protective against neglect.
* Reassure parents that isolated PS treated with balloon valvuloplasty has excellent outcomes.
* **Red Flag**: Repeated defaulting of cardiology follow-ups for moderate/severe PS warrants active MSW or school nurse outreach to prevent life-threatening deterioration.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-Term Prognosis of Pulmonary Stenosis
* Excellent prognosis post-intervention (balloon or surgical).
* Restenosis or pulmonary regurgitation can occur long-term but is usually well-tolerated.

### Clinical Complications of Untreated Severe PS
* Right Ventricular Hypertrophy → Right Heart Failure.
* Atrial/Ventricular Arrhythmias (心律不正).
* **Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎)**: Note that HA/international guidelines no longer routinely recommend IE antibiotic prophylaxis for isolated PS unless there is a prior history of IE or prosthetic material used.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the significance of an upright T wave in V1 on a paediatric ECG? Answer: It is a sign of Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (RVH) if seen in a child older than 1 month, commonly seen in severe Pulmonary Stenosis.
* Trap: Assuming all right-sided heart sounds increase with inspiration. Pearl: The ejection click of valvar pulmonary stenosis is the ONLY right-sided acoustic event that decreases in intensity during inspiration.
* Pitfall: Forgetting the radiation pattern of ejection systolic murmurs. Aortic stenosis radiates to the neck/carotids; Pulmonary stenosis radiates to the back and left axilla.
* Trap: Routinely prescribing infective endocarditis prophylaxis for mild isolated pulmonary stenosis prior to dental work. Pearl: Modern guidelines do not recommend IE prophylaxis for isolated native valve PS unless there is a history of IE or recent surgical/catheter intervention with prosthetic material.
