# Rubella (德國麻疹)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Rubella Clinical Definition
* Mild, highly contagious viral exanthem caused by Rubella virus (RNA Togavirus)
* Characterized by mild prodrome, generalized maculopapular rash (斑丘疹), and prominent lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)
* Primary concern is teratogenic effect on fetus → Congenital Rubella Syndrome (CRS) (先天性風疹綜合症)

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology
* Incidence ↓↓ in HK due to universal childhood MMR vaccination
* HK Department of Health (DH) schedule: 1st dose at 12 months, 2nd dose at 18 months
* Outbreaks primarily occur in unvaccinated immigrants or young adults with waning immunity
* Statutory Notifiable Disease to Centre for Health Protection (CHP)

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Rubella Pathophysiology & Transmission
* **Transmission:** Respiratory droplet inhalation or direct contact with nasopharyngeal secretions
* **Incubation:** 14-21 days
* **Infectious Period:** 7 days before → 7 days after rash onset
* **Pathogenesis:** Virus replicates in respiratory epithelium → spreads to regional lymph nodes → Viraemia (days 5-7) → transplacental infection if pregnant (especially 1st trimester)

### Congenital Rubella Syndrome (CRS) Pathogenesis
* Maternal viraemia → placental infection → fetal viraemia
* Virus inhibits cell division & causes cytopathic damage in developing fetal organs → devastating congenital malformations
* Highest risk of CRS if infected <12 weeks gestation (>90% risk)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Rubella Prodrome Symptoms
* Often asymptomatic in children
* Mild low-grade fever (低燒)
* Malaise, mild coryza, conjunctivitis
* Sore throat

### Key Clinical Signs of Rubella
* **Lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大):** Prominent, tender. Typically suboccipital, postauricular, & posterior cervical (classic hallmark, precedes rash by 1 week)
* **Maculopapular Rash (斑丘疹):** Pink, non-confluent. Starts on face → spreads rapidly to trunk/limbs → fades in 3 days (often called "3-day measles")
* **Forschheimer Spots (福氏斑):** Pinpoint petechiae on soft palate (seen in 20% of patients)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Distinguishing Rubella from Measles (麻疹)
* **Severity:** Rubella is milder, lower fever, child appears less toxic
* **Rash Pattern:** Rubella rash clears faster (3 days), rarely confluent, does not leave prominent post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation/desquamation like measles
* **Nodes:** Severe postauricular/suboccipital lymphadenopathy strongly favours Rubella
* **Mucosa:** Koplik spots (-ve) in Rubella; Forschheimer spots (+ve) but non-specific

### Distinguishing Rubella from Parvovirus B19 (Erythema Infectiosum)
* **Rash Pattern:** B19 has distinct "slapped cheek" facial erythema followed by reticular (lacy) rash on limbs. Rubella rash is discrete macules/papules starting on face.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Childhood Viral Exanthems
| Disease | Pathogen | Key Clinical Features | Rash Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rubella (德國麻疹)** | Rubella virus | Postauricular nodes ↑, Forschheimer spots | Pink maculopapular, face → down, lasts 3 days |
| **Measles (麻疹)** | Measles virus | High fever, Cough, Coryza, Conjunctivitis, Koplik spots | Erythematous, confluent, face → down, lasts 7 days |
| **Erythema Infectiosum** | Parvovirus B19 | Mild prodrome | "Slapped cheek", lacy reticular rash on limbs |
| **Roseola Infantum** | HHV-6 | High fever x 3-5 days, abruptly ends | Maculopapular rash appears *after* fever breaks |
| **Scarlet Fever** | Group A Strep | Sore throat, Strawberry tongue, circumoral pallor | Sandpaper rash, prominent in skin folds (Pastia lines) |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### First-Line Investigations for Rubella
* **Clinical Diagnosis:** Often presumed clinically in classic presentations, but lab confirmation mandated by HK HA for public health tracking
* **Serology:** Rubella specific IgM (+ve) indicates acute infection

### Gold Standard / Confirmatory Investigations
* **Viral PCR:** Rubella RNA RT-PCR from throat swab, nasopharyngeal aspirate (NPA), or urine (most sensitive in early acute phase)
* **Paired Serology:** 4-fold ↑ in Rubella IgG titre between acute and convalescent sera (2-3 weeks apart)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Medical Management
* Mild & self-limiting; strictly supportive
* **Antipyretics/Analgesics:** PO Paracetamol / Ibuprofen for fever and arthralgia
* Ensure adequate oral hydration

### Hong Kong HA / CHP Public Health Protocols
* **Notification:** Statutory requirement to notify CHP via standard ND form immediately
* **Isolation:** Droplet precautions. Isolate patient (exclude from school/nursery) for **7 days** after onset of rash
* **Contact Tracing:** Identify exposed pregnant women urgently for serological testing/counselling

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Psychosocial & Community Care
* Reassure parents regarding the benign, self-limiting nature of the disease in children
* Counsel parents heavily on preventing contact with pregnant women
* Ensure family understands school exclusion policies (CHP guidelines)

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding & Vaccination Assessment
* **Vaccine Hesitancy:** If unvaccinated, explore parental reasons for missing MMR. Address misconceptions empathetically but firmly using scientific evidence
* Arrange catch-up vaccination for siblings/patient (post-recovery) per HK DH schedule

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Post-Natal Rubella Complications
* **Arthralgia/Arthritis (關節炎):** Symmetrical, involves wrists/fingers/knees. Common in adult females (up to 70%), rare in young children. Resolves in weeks
* **Thrombocytopenia:** Rare (1 in 3000). Presents with purpura/bleeding
* **Encephalitis (腦炎):** Extremely rare (1 in 6000)

### Congenital Rubella Syndrome (CRS) Complications
* **Classic Triad:** 
  1. Sensorineural deafness (感音神經性耳聾) (most common)
  2. Eye abnormalities (Cataracts 鰲內障, microphthalmia)
  3. Congenital Heart Disease (PDA, pulmonary artery stenosis)
* **Other:** Microcephaly, intellectual disability, hepatosplenomegaly, "blueberry muffin" rash (extramedullary haematopoiesis)

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of Congenital Rubella Syndrome? Answer: Sensorineural deafness, cataracts, and congenital heart disease (specifically PDA).
* Viva Question: How long should a child with rubella be excluded from school according to HK guidelines? Answer: 7 days from the onset of the rash.
* Trap: Confusing the rash of rubella with measles. Pearl: Rubella rash is lighter pink, rarely confluent, clears in 3 days, and the child appears generally well, unlike the toxic-appearing child with a confluent 7-day measles rash.
* Pitfall: Ignoring a pregnant contact of a child with rubella. Always actively ask about and screen pregnant contacts immediately, as primary rubella in the first trimester carries a >90% risk of severe fetal malformations.
