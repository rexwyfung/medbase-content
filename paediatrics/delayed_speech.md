# Delayed Speech and Language (言語及語言發展遲緩)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
- **Speech**: Motor execution of producing sounds/articulation.
- **Language**: Cognitive ability to understand (receptive) & express (expressive) thoughts.
- **Delayed Speech and Language (言語及語言發展遲緩)**: Failure to achieve expected speech/language milestones at appropriate chronological age.
- **Classification**:
  - **Expressive Delay**: Good comprehension, delayed vocalization.
  - **Receptive Delay**: Poor comprehension & expression (more severe).

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong)
- Most common reason for referral to **Child Assessment Service (CAS)** & **Maternal and Child Health Centre (MCHC)**.
- High prevalence (~5-10% of preschoolers).
- Often identified during routine **Comprehensive Child Development Service (CCDS)** surveillance.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Normal Language Prerequisites
- Intact hearing (auditory pathway).
- Intact cognitive ability (brain development).
- Intact anatomical structures (e.g., palate, lips, tongue).
- Stimulating psychosocial environment.

### Primary Language Impairment
- **Specific Language Impairment (SLI)**: Developmental language disorder without obvious cause (no hearing, cognitive, or anatomical deficit). Genetic predisposition common.

### Secondary Language Impairment
- **Hearing Loss (聽力障礙)**: Conductive (e.g., glue ear) or Sensorineural (SNHL).
- **Intellectual Disability (ID) / Global Developmental Delay (GDD) (整體發展遲緩)**.
- **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) (自閉症譜系障礙)**: Impaired social communication.
- **Anatomical Defects**: Cleft palate (顎裂) → hypernasal speech.
- **Environmental**: Psychosocial deprivation, excessive screen time.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Red Flags (MCHC Guidelines)
- **No babbling/pointing** by 12 months.
- **No single words** by 18 months.
- **No 2-word spontaneous phrases** by 24 months.
- **ANY loss/regression** of language/social skills at ANY age.

### Receptive vs Expressive Signs
- **Expressive Delay**:
  - Understands commands (e.g., "point to teddy").
  - Uses gestures/pointing to communicate.
  - Normal eye contact & joint attention.
- **Receptive Delay**:
  - Fails to follow simple commands (e.g., "give me the ball").
  - Echolalia (parroting) without comprehension.
  - Often associated with ASD or GDD.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating the Causes
| Condition | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hearing Impairment** | Visually alert, ignores sounds, delayed babbling/speech, absent startle reflex. |
| **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)** | Poor eye contact, absent joint attention, repetitive behaviors, lack of imaginative play. |
| **Global Developmental Delay (GDD)** | Delayed motor & cognitive milestones alongside speech delay. |
| **Specific Language Impairment (SLI)** | Normal non-verbal IQ, normal hearing, normal social interaction, isolated speech delay. |
| **Environmental Deprivation** | Poor stimulation, excessive screen time, improves rapidly with appropriate intervention. |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Most Likely
- **Specific Language Impairment (SLI)**.
- **Mild Conductive Hearing Loss**: e.g., Otitis Media with Effusion (OME) (積水性中耳炎).
- **Environmental Deprivation**: Lack of stimulation.

### Must-Not-Miss
- **Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL) (感音神經性聽力損失)**: Requires urgent audiology referral.
- **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) (自閉症譜系障礙)**.
- **Global Developmental Delay (GDD)**.
- **Landau-Kleffner Syndrome**: Acquired epileptic aphasia (sudden regression in receptive/expressive language + seizures/EEG abnormalities).

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Clinical Assessment
- **Developmental Screening**: MCHC CCDS tools (assessing all 4 domains: gross motor, fine motor/vision, speech/language, social/behavioral).
- **Physical Examination**:
  - Dysmorphic features (syndromes).
  - Oral cavity (cleft palate, tongue tie).
  - Skin (neurocutaneous stigmata, e.g., tuberous sclerosis).

### Audiology Assessment (MANDATORY)
- **Otoacoustic Emissions (OAE) / Auditory Brainstem Response (ABR)**: For infants.
- **Visual Reinforced Audiometry (VRA)**: 6-30 months.
- **Play Audiometry**: 30+ months.
- **Pure Tone Audiometry (PTA)**: >4 years.

### Gold Standard Formal Assessments
- **Formal Speech & Language Assessment**: By **Speech Therapist (ST)** (言語治療師).
- **Multidisciplinary Assessment**: At **Child Assessment Centre (CAC)** by Pediatrician, Clinical Psychologist, ST, OT to rule out ASD/GDD.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management
- Not applicable (unless acute regression → r/o Landau-Kleffner via urgent EEG).

### Definitive & Chronic Management
- **Treat Underlying Cause**:
  - **Hearing Loss**: Grommets (耳咽管置流管) for OME, hearing aids/cochlear implants for SNHL.
  - **Anatomical**: Surgical repair for cleft palate.
- **Speech Therapy (ST) (言語治療)**: Mainstay of treatment.
- **Parental Training**: Hanen program, encouraging reading, reducing screen time, interactive play.

### HA / Local Protocols
- **MCHC Referral**: Suspected cases refer to **Child Assessment Service (CAS)** for diagnosis.
- **Pre-school Rehabilitation Services**: Coordinate via **Social Welfare Department (SWD)**.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT)
- **Pediatrician**: Overall coordination, medical workup.
- **Speech Therapist (ST)**: Targeted language intervention.
- **Audiologist**: Hearing testing & aids.
- **Occupational Therapist (OT)**: If comorbid sensory/motor issues.
- **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Family support, financial assistance.

### Community & SWD Integration (Hong Kong)
- **Early Education and Training Centre (EETC) (早期教育及訓練中心)**.
- **On-site Pre-school Rehabilitation Services (OPRS) (到校學前康復服務)**.
- **Integrated Program in Kindergarten-cum-Child Care Centre (IP)**.
- **Special Child Care Centre (SCCC) (特殊幼兒中心)**: For moderate-severe GDD/ASD.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Psychosocial Deprivation
- Severe neglect/abuse can present as profound speech delay.
- **Red Flags**: Maternal depression, domestic violence, poor hygiene, missed MCHC appointments, unexplained bruising.
- **Action**: Mandatory **Medical Social Worker (MSW)** referral, consider **Care or Protection Order (CPO)** if imminent risk.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognosis
- **Expressive Delay Only**: Excellent prognosis. Often resolves by school age with ST.
- **Receptive Delay**: Guarded prognosis. Often persistent learning difficulties.
- **Secondary Delay (ASD/GDD)**: Depends on severity of primary condition.

### Long-Term Complications
- **Academic**: Poor literacy, reading difficulties (dyslexia), poor school performance.
- **Psychosocial**: Social isolation, low self-esteem, behavioral issues (frustration due to inability to communicate).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the absolute most important investigation for ANY child presenting with speech delay? Answer: Formal audiology assessment (hearing test) to rule out hearing loss.
* Trap: Reassuring parents that "boys talk late" (Einstein syndrome) without proper assessment. Pearl: While boys may be slightly slower, true red flags (e.g., no words at 18 months) MUST be formally investigated, starting with a hearing test.
* Pitfall: Missing an Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) diagnosis in a child with speech delay. ALWAYS ask about non-verbal communication (eye contact, pointing, joint attention) and repetitive behaviors.
* Viva Question: A 3-year-old child previously speaking in 3-word sentences suddenly stops speaking and stops understanding commands. What must you rule out? Answer: Landau-Kleffner Syndrome (acquired epileptic aphasia); investigate with an urgent EEG.
* Trap: Assuming a child who responds to loud noises does not have a hearing impairment. Pearl: A child with high-frequency sensorineural hearing loss may hear loud low-frequency noises (like a door slamming) but miss speech frequencies, leading to language delay.
