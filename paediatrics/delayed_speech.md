
# Delayed Speech and Language (言語及語言發展遲緩)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Failure to achieve speech and language milestones at the expected age. Can be expressive (producing speech), receptive (understanding speech), or both.
- **Local Epidemiology**: One of the most common reasons for referral to the Child Assessment Service (CAS) in Hong Kong. Often identified during MCHC developmental surveillance.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Language development requires intact hearing, cognitive ability, anatomical structures (cleft palate), and a stimulating environment.
- **Primary Language Impairment**: Specific Language Impairment (SLI) - delay without an obvious cause.
- **Secondary**: Due to hearing loss, Intellectual Disability (ID), Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), severe psychosocial deprivation, or anatomical defects.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Red Flags**:
  - No babbling or pointing by 12 months.
  - No single words by 16 months.
  - No 2-word spontaneous phrases by 24 months.
  - **ANY loss of language or social skills at ANY age.**
- **Receptive vs. Expressive**:
  - *Expressive delay*: Understands commands but cannot speak. Often uses gestures. Better prognosis.
  - *Receptive delay*: Does not understand commands (e.g., "give me the ball"). Often associated with global delay or ASD.
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **Hearing Loss**: Child may be visually very alert but ignores sounds.
- **Autism (ASD)**: Speech delay is accompanied by poor eye contact, lack of joint attention, and repetitive behaviours.
- **Global Developmental Delay (GDD)**: Speech delay is accompanied by delays in motor and cognitive domains.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Specific Language Impairment, Mild conductive hearing loss (e.g., Otitis Media with Effusion), Environmental deprivation (lack of stimulation, excessive screen time).
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Sensorineural hearing loss.
  - Autism Spectrum Disorder.
  - Landau-Kleffner Syndrome (acquired epileptic aphasia - suspect if sudden regression).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **Audiology Assessment**: **MANDATORY** for every child with speech delay. (Otoacoustic emissions, Auditory Brainstem Response for infants; Play audiometry for toddlers).
  - **Developmental Screening**: MCHC uses the Comprehensive Child Development Service (CCDS) tools.
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Formal Speech and Language Assessment**: By a Speech Therapist (ST).
  - **Multidisciplinary Assessment**: At CAS (Child Assessment Centre) to rule out ASD or GDD.
- **Monitoring**:
  - Not applicable.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Not applicable.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - Treat underlying cause (e.g., grommets for chronic otitis media with effusion).
  - **Speech Therapy**: The mainstay of treatment.
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **Early Intervention**: Refer to SWD for pre-school rehabilitation services (e.g., Early Education and Training Centre - EETC, or On-site Pre-school Rehabilitation Services - OPRS).
- **Parental Education**: Crucial. Advise on reducing screen time, reading to the child, and engaging in interactive play.
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Assess for severe psychosocial deprivation or neglect, which can cause profound speech delay. MSW referral if suspected.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Pure expressive delay often resolves with intervention.
- Receptive delay or delay associated with ASD/GDD has a more guarded prognosis and may lead to long-term learning difficulties and social isolation.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Reassuring parents that "boys talk late" without investigating. *Pearl*: While boys may be slightly slower, true red flags (e.g., no words at 18 months) MUST be investigated, starting with a hearing test.
- **OSCE Pearl**: In a history-taking station for speech delay, ALWAYS ask about hearing (response to sounds), social interaction (eye contact, pointing - to screen for ASD), and screen time exposure.
