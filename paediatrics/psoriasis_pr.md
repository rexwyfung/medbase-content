# Psoriasis & Pityriasis Rosea (銀屑病及玫瑰糠疹)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Psoriasis (銀屑病) Definition & Local Epidemiology
* **Definition**: Chronic, immune-mediated, genetically-predisposed inflammatory skin disease → epidermal hyperproliferation.
* **HK Epidemiology**: 
  * Prevalence: ~1-2% of children locally.
  * Bimodal onset (peaks at 15-20 yrs & 55-60 yrs).
  * Positive family history heavily predicts early childhood onset.

### Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) Definition & Local Epidemiology
* **Definition**: Acute, self-limiting, papulosquamous eruption triggered by viral reactivation.
* **HK Epidemiology**:
  * Very common in healthy adolescents/young adults (peak 10-35 yrs).
  * ↑ Incidence during spring & autumn seasonal changes in HK.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Psoriasis (銀屑病) Pathophysiology & Genetics
* **Immune Dysregulation**: Th1, Th17 & Th22 cell overactivation → massive cytokine cascade (**TNF-α, IL-17, IL-23**).
* **Keratinocyte (角質形成細胞) Hyperproliferation**: Normal skin turnover = 28 days; **Psoriatic skin = 3-5 days** → poor maturation & rapid cellular pile-up → thick silvery scales.
* **Genetics**: Strong genetic link → **HLA-Cw6** strongly associated with early-onset pediatric variant.

### Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) Pathophysiology
* **Viral Trigger**: Strongly associated with systemic reactivation of **Human Herpesvirus 6 (HHV-6)** or **HHV-7**.
* **Self-Limiting Immunity**: Host mounts delayed-type hypersensitivity response → viral clearance & spontaneous rash resolution in 6-8 weeks.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Psoriasis (銀屑病) Classic Presentation
* 12-year-old girl.
* **Persistent thick, red, intensely itchy scaly patches**. 
* **Location**: Chronically stuck over extensor surfaces (knees, elbows) & scalp.

### Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) Classic Presentation
* 15-year-old boy.
* **1 week ago**: Single large red scaly patch (**Herald Patch / 前驅斑**) on the abdomen.
* **Yesterday**: Sudden massive sweeping breakout of hundreds of smaller oval patches across back & chest. 
* **Systemic**: Feels perfectly well; completely afebrile.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Plaque Psoriasis (斑塊狀銀屑病) Key Signs
* **Morphology**: Well-demarcated, **erythematous plaques (紅斑)** covered with thick, **silvery-white scales (銀白色鱗屑)**.
* **Classic Distribution**: Extensors (knees/elbows), scalp margin, umbilicus, natal cleft.
* **Auspitz Sign (奧斯皮茨氏徵)**: +ve → peeling off scale reveals tiny, pinpoint bleeding spots (dilated superficial capillaries).
* **Köbner Phenomenon (同形反應)**: +ve → new psoriatic lesions develop precisely at sites of skin trauma/scratching.

### Psoriatic Nail Changes (銀屑病指甲病變)
* **Nail Pitting (甲凹陷)**: Tiny ice-pick holes on nail plate.
* **Onycholysis (甲床分離)**: Distal lifting of the nail from the bed.
* **Oil Drop Sign (油滴徵)**: Yellow-brown translucent discoloration beneath nail bed.

### Guttate Psoriasis (滴狀銀屑病) Key Signs
* **Trigger**: Universally preceded 1-3 weeks by **Group A Streptococcus (GAS)** tonsillopharyngitis.
* **Morphology**: Sudden "rain-drop" shower of 1-10mm scaly teardrop-shaped plaques.
* **Distribution**: Diffuse explosion over trunk & proximal extremities.

### Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) Key Signs
* **The Herald Patch (前驅斑)**: Initial lesion (80% of cases) → 2-5cm oval, salmon-pink scaly plaque on trunk.
* **The Secondary Eruption**: Erupts 1-2 weeks post-Herald Patch.
* **Morphology**: Smaller oval, salmon-pink macules featuring a **collarette of scale (領圈狀鱗屑)** (scale attached at periphery, loose centrally).
* **Distribution**: **Christmas Tree Pattern (聖誕樹圖案)** → oval plaques align precisely along Langer's skin cleavage lines on the back, angling downwards. 
* Spares face, palms, & soles.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing Pityriasis Rosea from Tinea Corporis (體癬)
| Feature | Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) | Tinea Corporis (體癬 / Ringworm) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Herald Patch vs Ringworm** | Salmon-pink center with peripheral **collarette scale**. | Central clearing with active, raised, strictly demarcated red scaly border. |
| **Evolution** | Explodes into systemic Christmas Tree pattern 1-2 weeks later. | Slowly enlarges peripherally; remains localized without massive sudden spread. |
| **KOH Scraping** | -ve (No hyphae). | +ve (Branching fungal hyphae). |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Psoriasis & Pityriasis Rosea Differentials
| Condition | Differentiating Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secondary Syphilis (二期梅毒)** | Diffuse scaly rash that **critically involves palms & soles**. Must check VDRL/RPR in sexually active adolescents. |
| **Atopic Eczema (異位性濕疹)** | Involves **flexures** (antecubital/popliteal fossae), lacks Auspitz sign, intensely pruritic, poorly demarcated borders. |
| **Seborrheic Dermatitis (脂溢性皮炎)** | Greasy, yellow scales strictly over sebum-rich areas (scalp, nasolabial folds, eyebrows). |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Clinical Diagnosis & Bedside Tests
* **Diagnosis**: Primarily **clinical** for both conditions based on history & morphology.
* **Skin Scraping (KOH Prep)**: Essential bedside test to rule out fungal infection (Tinea) if Herald patch presents alone.

### Targeted Bloods & Swabs
* **Guttate Psoriasis**: 
  * Throat swab (C&S) for Group A Streptococcus.
  * **ASO Titer (抗鏈球菌溶血素O效價)**: ↑ confirms recent Strep infection.
* **Atypical Pityriasis Rosea (Palm/Sole involvement)**:
  * **VDRL / RPR Serology**: Mandatory to rule out Secondary Syphilis in sexually active adolescents.

### Special Investigations / Biopsy
* **Skin Biopsy**: Rarely indicated. PR shows non-specific spongiosis. Psoriasis shows acanthosis, parakeratosis, absent granular layer, & Munro microabscesses.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) Management Protocol
* **Active Reassurance (1st Line)**: Condition is completely benign & non-contagious. Educate that it will strictly self-resolve in **6-8 weeks**.
* **Symptomatic Relief (If Pruritic)**:
  * Emollients (e.g., Aqueous cream, Emulsifying ointment).
  * Mild topical steroids (e.g., 1% Hydrocortisone).
  * Oral antihistamines (e.g., Cetirizine 10mg OM) to suppress night itch.

### Psoriasis (銀屑病) Topical Therapy (1st Line HA Protocol)
* **Emollients**: Apply liberally & frequently to soften scales & reduce pruritus.
* **Topical Corticosteroids**: Mometasone or Betamethasone valerate for acute flares (avoid long-term continuous use to prevent skin atrophy).
* **Vitamin D Analogues**: **Calcipotriol (卡泊三醇)** → normalizes epidermal keratinocyte proliferation. Safest for long-term maintenance.
* **Coal Tar Preparations**: Anti-mitotic/anti-inflammatory. Messy & malodorous but highly effective locally.

### Psoriasis (銀屑病) Systemic Therapy (Refractory/Severe)
* **Phototherapy**: **Narrowband UVB (窄頻中波紫外線)** → Gold standard for generalized Guttate and diffuse Plaque psoriasis.
* **Oral Immunosuppressants**: Methotrexate, Ciclosporin, or Acitretin (requires strict LFT/Renal/CBC monitoring).
* **Targeted Biologics (生物製劑)**: 
  * Anti-TNFα (Adalimumab).
  * Anti-IL-17A (Secukinumab).
  * *HA Protocol: Biologics strictly reserved for severe recalcitrant cases failing standard systemic therapies.*

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Psychosocial Impact & Interventions
* **Self-Esteem / Body Image**: Psoriasis causes massive psychological morbidity in adolescents due to visible flaking/erythema → ↑ risk of depression, bullying, & social withdrawal.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) / Clinical Psychologist**: Prompt referral vital for severe cases exhibiting mood changes or school refusal.
* **Support Groups**: Encourage joining local NGOs (e.g., Hong Kong Psoriasis Patients Association) for peer support.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Diagnostic Traps in Safeguarding
* **Medical Neglect**: No direct NAI linkage, but severe, untreated, or infected psoriatic lesions due to parental failure/refusal to apply prescribed topical therapies warrants MSW evaluation for medical neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Psoriasis (銀屑病) Complications & Prognosis
* **Prognosis**: Chronic, lifelong relapsing-remitting course. Complete permanent cure is currently impossible.
* **Psoriatic Arthritis (銀屑病關節炎)**: Develops in up to 30% of patients later in life (presents as asymmetric oligoarthritis, dactylitis/sausage digits, sacroiliitis).
* **Cardiometabolic Syndrome**: Severe systemic inflammation strongly linked to ↑ risk of obesity, hypertension, dyslipidemia, & premature cardiovascular disease.
* **Erythrodermic Psoriasis**: Life-threatening dermatological emergency (>90% BSA affected) → risks high-output cardiac failure, sepsis, & hypothermia.

### Pityriasis Rosea (玫瑰糠疹) Complications & Prognosis
* **Prognosis**: Excellent. Guaranteed spontaneous resolution.
* **Complications**: No permanent scarring. Often leaves temporary **post-inflammatory hypopigmentation (炎症後色素減退)** or hyperpigmentation lasting several months (highly prominent in darker Fitzpatrick skin types in HK).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: A 9-year-old child presents with a sudden, full-body 'raindrop' shower of tiny scaly plaques. What vital piece of history from the past 2 weeks must you elicit to confirm the diagnosis? Answer: You must ask if the child recently had a severe sore throat or tonsillitis. This triggers Guttate Psoriasis via Group A Streptococcus.
* Viva Question: An adolescent presents with a diffuse scaly maculopapular rash on the trunk. You suspect Pityriasis Rosea. What key physical examination step must you perform to rule out a dangerous differential? Answer: You must check the palms and soles. Involvement of palms/soles strongly suggests Secondary Syphilis, whereas PR classically spares them.
* Trap: Giving a teenager with Pityriasis Rosea intensive oral antifungals for weeks because you mistook the Herald Patch for severe Tinea Corporis. Pearl: Always take a thorough timeline history; the Herald Patch always precedes the secondary breakout by 1-2 weeks, and a bedside KOH scraping will be negative.
* Pitfall: Reassuring a parent that Psoriasis will completely disappear as the child grows up. This creates false hope and distrust later. Explain that Psoriasis is a chronic, lifelong genetic-immune condition with relapsing flares requiring ongoing management.
