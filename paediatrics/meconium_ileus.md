# Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸阻塞)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Meconium Ileus
* **Definition**: Neonatal lower intestinal obstruction secondary to impaction of abnormally thick, viscid meconium in the **terminal ileum** (末端迴腸).
* **Significance**: Earliest clinical manifestation of **Cystic Fibrosis (CF)** (囊腫性纖維化).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Incidence**: Exceedingly rare in local HK Chinese population compared to Caucasians (CF incidence ~1 in 2,500 in Caucasians vs. extremely rare in Asians).
* **Clinical Protocol**: Despite rarity, **CFTR** mutation must be highly suspected in any local neonate presenting with **Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸阻塞)** until proven otherwise.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### CFTR Gene Mutation & Pathophysiology
* **Genetics**: Autosomal recessive mutation of **CFTR gene** (commonly ΔF508) on Chromosome 7.
* **Mechanism**: Defective chloride channel → ↓ Cl⁻ & H₂O secretion into intestinal lumen.

### Intraluminal Pathophysiology of Meconium Impaction
* **Pancreatic Insufficiency**: ↓ pancreatic enzyme secretion *in utero* limits normal protein digestion.
* **Meconium Composition**: Intestinal contents become ultra-dehydrated, protein-rich, & excessively sticky (putty-like).
* **Mechanical Obstruction**: Tenacious meconium physically impacts & obliterates the lumen of the terminal ileum → **Bowel Obstruction** (腸阻塞).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Timing of Clinical Presentation
* **Onset**: Typically within **first 24-48 hours** of life.
* **Core Symptom**: Absolute failure to pass meconium within 48 hrs (Delayed meconium passage).

### Classical Triad of Neonatal Bowel Obstruction
* **Vomiting**: Explosive, persistent **bilious vomiting** (膽汁性嘔吐) (green/yellow).
* **Abdomen**: Immense **abdominal distension** (腹部脹大) → shiny, tense abdominal wall with visible veins.
* **Bowels**: Absolute constipation / absence of normal meconium.

### Specific Physical Examination Signs
* **Palpation**: Doughy, rubbery loops of bowel palpable in the **right iliac fossa (RIF)**.
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)**:
  * Empty rectal ampulla.
  * Normal anal tone.
  * Extraction of only small, dry, grey/white mucus pellets ("meconium plugs").

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Discriminatory Features: Meconium Ileus vs. Hirschsprung's Disease
| Feature | Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸阻塞) | Hirschsprung's Disease (先天性巨結腸症) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **DRE Findings** | Empty rectum; tight, dry mucus pellets | Empty rectum; explosive "squirt" of liquid stool/gas upon withdrawal |
| **Associated Condition** | **Cystic Fibrosis** (囊腫性纖維化) | Down Syndrome, MEN2 |
| **Maternal History** | None typically | None typically |
| **AXR Appearance** | "Soap-bubble" sign (Neuhauser's sign), NO air-fluid levels | Dilated proximal bowel, lack of gas in rectum, air-fluid levels present |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Causes of Neonatal Lower Bowel Obstruction
* **Hirschsprung's Disease** (先天性巨結腸症): Aganglionic distal colon.
* **Ileal Atresia** (迴腸閉鎖): Congenital obliteration of ileum; often secondary to *in utero* vascular accident (potentially caused by meconium volvulus).
* **Meconium Plug Syndrome** (胎糞塞綜合症): Functional immaturity of left colon; associated with prematurity or maternal diabetes (resolves post-enema; NOT CF).
* **Anorectal Malformations**: Imperforate anus (ruled out on initial physical exam).

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Laboratory Investigations
* **Blood Gas**: VBG to assess for dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, & metabolic acidosis (secondary to bowel ischemia/hypovolemia).
* **Bloods**: CBP (sepsis rule-out), U&Es (pre-hydration baseline), LFTs, Group & Save.

### Radiological Imaging for Meconium Ileus
* **Plain AXR (Abdominal X-Ray)**:
  * Dilated loops of bowel with varying calibres.
  * **Neuhauser's Sign** (Soap-bubble / Ground-glass appearance) in RIF → gas bubbles mixing with thick, tenacious meconium.
  * **Absence** of prominent air-fluid levels (thick meconium doesn't layer out with gravity).
* **Water-Soluble Contrast Enema (Gastrografin)**:
  * **Diagnostic & Therapeutic**.
  * Shows **microcolon** (disuse atrophy of colon distal to obstruction).
  * Reveals pellet-like impacted meconium in terminal ileum.

### Confirmatory Investigations for Cystic Fibrosis
* **Sweat Chloride Test**: Gold standard for CF (delayed until infant >2 weeks old & >2kg to ensure adequate sweat generation).
* **Genetics**: CFTR gene sequencing panel.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Medical Management (1st Line)
* **Resuscitation**: Keep NPO (Nil by mouth). NG tube insertion for free drainage/decompression. Aggressive IV fluid resuscitation (0.9% NaCl boluses) to correct hypovolemia.
* **Hyperosmolar Contrast Enema (Gastrografin)**:
  * Performed under fluoroscopy.
  * **Mechanism**: Hyperosmolar Gastrografin draws massive amounts of fluid into bowel lumen → softens/breaks up viscid meconium.
  * **Requirement**: Mandates intense IV hydration pre- and intra-procedure to prevent hypovolemic shock.
  * Often requires sequential daily enemas to clear the obstruction.
  * **Success Rate**: Clears obstruction in ~50-60% of uncomplicated cases.

### Surgical Management Indications & Procedures
* **Indications**: Failed Gastrografin enema, suspected perforation, meconium peritonitis, volvulus, or atresia.
* **Procedures**:
  * Enterotomy with intraoperative warm saline washout.
  * Resection of ischemic bowel with primary anastomosis.
  * Formation of temporary **ileostomy** (迴腸造口) (e.g., Bishop-Koop or Mikulicz stoma) if anastomosis unsafe.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement for Meconium Ileus
* **Neonatology & Paediatric Surgery**: Acute perioperative care.
* **Respiratory/Pulmonology**: Early baseline lung function & CF protocol initiation.
* **Dietitian**: Pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT) (CREON) and fat-soluble vitamin (ADEK) supplementation.
* **Physiotherapist**: Chest PT education for parents.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Crucial for psychosocial support; devastating lifelong genetic diagnosis requiring structured bereavement counseling.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Medical Neglect and Safeguarding Considerations
* Not directly linked to Non-Accidental Injury (NAI).
* **Vulnerability**: Extreme parental burden. HA protocols require strict monitoring of MCHC & clinic attendance to ensure adherence to intensive CF regimens (chest PT, medications, diet). Non-compliance requires immediate MSW / SWD intervention for medical neglect.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Immediate Neonatal Complications
* **Meconium Peritonitis** (胎糞性腹膜炎): Secondary to *in utero* or postnatal bowel perforation → massive inflammatory response & intra-abdominal calcifications.
* **Volvulus** (腸扭結) & Bowel Ischemia.

### Long-Term Prognosis
* **Bowel**: Post-operative adhesions (adhesion colic/obstruction), Short Bowel Syndrome (if massive resection needed).
* **Systemic**: Prognosis entirely dictated by underlying **Cystic Fibrosis** trajectory (progressive bronchiectasis, recurrent *Pseudomonas* pneumonias, CF-related diabetes, biliary cirrhosis).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why must a baby receiving a Gastrografin enema have extensive IV access and aggressive hydration? Answer: Gastrografin is highly concentrated (hyperosmolar). It rapidly shifts intravascular water into the bowel lumen, which can cause profound, life-threatening hypovolemic shock in a neonate if they are not aggressively pre-hydrated.
* Viva Question: What specific radiological sign on an AXR suggests Meconium Ileus? Answer: The "soap-bubble" or "ground-glass" appearance (Neuhauser's sign) in the right iliac fossa, combined with a lack of significant air-fluid levels.
* Trap: Misdiagnosing Meconium Ileus as Meconium Plug Syndrome. Pearl: Meconium Plug Syndrome occurs in the colon, is associated with prematurity/maternal diabetes, and resolves fully after an enema without underlying CF. Meconium Ileus involves the terminal ileum and is almost pathognomonic for CF.
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents fully after a successful surgical bowel resection for Meconium Ileus. You must initiate immediate CFTR genetic testing and sweat chloride testing, as >90% of these infants have underlying Cystic Fibrosis requiring lifelong MDT care.
