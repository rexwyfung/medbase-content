# Meningitis (腦膜炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Definition:** Acute inflammation of meninges (arachnoid & pia mater) surrounding the brain and spinal cord.
* **Nature:** Life-threatening medical emergency requiring immediate IV antibiotics.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong)
* **Bacterial Meningitis (細菌性腦膜炎):** Incidence ↓↓↓ dramatically due to Department of Health (DH) Childhood Immunisation Programme (covers **Hib** & **S. pneumoniae**).
* **Viral Meningitis (病毒性腦膜炎):** Now the most common aetiology (e.g., **Enterovirus (腸病毒)**).
* **Statutory Notification:** **Meningococcal (腦膜炎雙球菌)** & **Hib** infections are statutory notifiable diseases to DH.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathogenesis
* **Colonization:** Pathogens colonize nasopharynx → invade bloodstream (**Bacteraemia (菌血症)**).
* **BBB Penetration:** Pathogens cross blood-brain barrier (BBB) → enter **Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF (腦脊液))**.

### Inflammatory Cascade
* **Bacterial Multiplication:** Rapid multiplication in CSF triggers intense inflammatory cascade.
* **Physiological Impact:**
  * ↑ BBB permeability
  * ↑ **Cerebral oedema (腦水腫)**
  * ↑ **Intracranial pressure (ICP (顱內壓))**
  * ↓ Cerebral perfusion → neuronal ischaemia/damage

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Neonatal Presentation (< 1 month)
* Highly non-specific.
* Temperature instability (fever OR hypothermia).
* Poor feeding, irritability, lethargy, jaundice, apnoea, seizures.
* *Note:* Classic meningeal signs are often ABSENT.

### Infant Presentation (1 month - 2 years)
* Fever, vomiting, high-pitched cry.
* Irritability, lethargy.
* **Bulging anterior fontanelle (前囟門凸出)** (late sign).

### Older Children & Adolescents Presentation
* Classic triad: **Fever (發燒)**, **Headache (頭痛)**, **Neck Stiffness (頸部僵硬)**.
* Associated features: Photophobia, vomiting, altered mental status.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Meningeal Irritation Signs
* **Nuchal Rigidity (頸部僵硬):** Resistance to passive neck flexion.
* **Kernig's Sign:** Inability to fully extend knee when hip is flexed at 90° due to pain.
* **Brudzinski's Sign:** Passive neck flexion → involuntary hip/knee flexion.

### "Do Not Miss" Clinical Signs
* **Non-blanching Rash (不褪色皮疹):** Petechial/purpuric rash. Hallmark of **Meningococcal septicaemia (腦膜炎雙球菌敗血症)**.
* **Signs of ↑ ICP:**
  * **Cushing's triad (庫興氏三聯症)** (late sign): Bradycardia (↓ HR), Hypertension (↑ BP), irregular respirations.
  * Papilloedema (視乳頭水腫).
  * Focal neurological deficits.
  * ↓ GCS.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Meningitis vs. Encephalitis
* **Meningitis (腦膜炎):** Primarily affects meninges → headache, neck stiffness, photophobia. Normal higher cerebral function initially.
* **Encephalitis (腦炎):** Involves brain parenchyma directly → early profound altered mental status, bizarre behaviour, seizures, focal neuro signs.
* *(Note: Meningoencephalitis = combination of both).*

### Meningitis vs. Simple Febrile Convulsion
* **Febrile Convulsion (熱痙攣):** Rapid recovery, normal neurological exam post-ictal, non-toxic appearance.
* **Meningitis:** Child remains lethargic, toxic-looking, persistent altered sensorium, +ve meningeal signs.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Key Differentials
* **Viral Encephalitis** (e.g., HSV, Enterovirus).
* **Sepsis / Septicaemia (敗血症)**.
* **Space-Occupying Lesions:** Brain abscess / subdural empyema.
* **Vascular Events:** Subarachnoid haemorrhage.
* **Meningism (腦膜刺激症):** Severe systemic infections (e.g., Pyelonephritis, Pneumonia) mimicking meningeal irritation without actual CNS infection.

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Gold Standard Investigation
* **Lumbar Puncture (LP (腰椎穿刺)):** Gold standard for diagnosis. Send CSF for cell count, protein, glucose, Gram stain, culture, multiplex PCR (BioFire).

### Absolute Contraindications to Lumbar Puncture
* **CRITICAL:** Do NOT perform LP if:
  * Signs of ↑ ICP (focal neuro signs, papilloedema, GCS < 8, abnormal posturing).
  * Cardiovascular compromise / Shock.
  * Severe coagulopathy.
  * Infection at LP site.

### Blood Tests & Bedside
* **Bloods:** CBP, CRP, Blood cultures (draw BEFORE antibiotics, but do NOT delay Tx), Serum glucose (to compare with CSF), Coagulation profile.

### Neuroimaging
* **CT Brain:** NOT routine before LP. Indicated ONLY if LP contraindicated (to rule out mass lesion/severe cerebral oedema).

### CSF Interpretation Table
| Parameter | Bacterial Meningitis (細菌性腦膜炎) | Viral Meningitis (病毒性腦膜炎) | Tuberculous Meningitis (結核性腦膜炎) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Appearance** | Turbid / Purulent | Clear | Clear / Viscous |
| **White Cells** | ↑↑ Polymorphs (Neutrophils) | ↑ Lymphocytes (early may be polymorphs) | ↑ Lymphocytes |
| **Protein** | ↑↑ High | Normal / Mildly ↑ | ↑↑ High |
| **Glucose** | ↓ Low (< 50% serum glucose) | Normal | ↓↓ Very Low |

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management Rule
* **CRITICAL:** Do NOT delay antibiotics. If LP is contraindicated or delayed, draw blood cultures → immediately give IV empirical antibiotics.

### Empiric Antibiotic Therapy (HA Guidelines)
| Age Group | Likely Pathogens | 1st Line IV Antibiotics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Neonates (< 1 month)** | Group B Strep, *E. coli*, *Listeria monocytogenes* | **IV Ampicillin + Cefotaxime** |
| **Children (> 1 month)** | *N. meningitidis*, *S. pneumoniae*, *H. influenzae* type b | **IV Ceftriaxone or Cefotaxime** (+ **IV Vancomycin** if highly Pen-resistant pneumococcus suspected) |

### Adjuvant Therapies
* **Corticosteroids:** **IV Dexamethasone (地塞米松)**.
  * Must give BEFORE or WITH 1st dose of antibiotics.
  * ↓ Inflammatory response to bacterial lysis.
  * ↓ Risk of **sensorineural hearing loss (感音神經性聽力喪失)** (esp. *Hib*).
* **Antiviral:** Add **IV Acyclovir** if HSV encephalitis suspected.
* **Supportive:** Fluid restriction if SIADH; aggressive fluid resuscitation if septic shock.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Public Health & Contact Tracing
* **Notification:** Meningococcal & *Hib* infections are statutory notifiable to **DH (衛生署)**.
* **Chemoprophylaxis:** Close contacts of Meningococcal/*Hib* require eradication therapy (e.g., Oral **Rifampicin** or **Ciprofloxacin**).

### Multidisciplinary Follow-up
* **Audiology (聽力學):** Mandatory formal hearing assessment for all survivors before discharge or shortly after.
* **Paediatrics/Neurology:** Long-term surveillance for developmental delay, learning difficulties, and epilepsy.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Red Flags
* **Abusive Head Trauma (Shaken Baby Syndrome):** Can mimic meningitis (lethargy, vomiting, seizures). Always check for **retinal haemorrhages (視網膜出血)**.
* **Medical Neglect:** Unimmunized child presenting with vaccine-preventable disease (*Hib*, Pneumococcus) raises child protection concerns.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Neurological Complications
* **Sensorineural Hearing Loss (感音神經性聽力喪失):** Most common sequela (~10-20%).
* **Seizures / Epilepsy (癲癇)**.
* **Hydrocephalus (腦積水)**.
* **Cerebral Palsy (腦性麻痺)** / Cognitive impairment.

### Systemic Complications
* **Septic Shock (敗血性休克)**.
* **DIC (彌散性血管內凝血)**.
* **Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome:** Adrenal haemorrhage in meningococcaemia.
* **Limb Ischaemia:** May require amputation.

### Prognosis
* Mortality ~5-10% for bacterial meningitis in developed countries; significant long-term morbidity in survivors.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What are the absolute contraindications to performing a Lumbar Puncture?
  Answer: Signs of raised ICP (focal neuro deficits, GCS < 8), haemodynamic instability/shock, severe coagulopathy, or infection at the LP site.
* Viva Question: Why do we give Dexamethasone, and when must it be given?
  Answer: To reduce meningeal inflammation and risk of sensorineural hearing loss. It MUST be given before or concurrently with the 1st dose of antibiotics; it is ineffective if given later.
* Trap: Waiting for a CT head before giving antibiotics in a child with suspected meningitis and a low GCS.
  Pearl: Draw blood cultures and immediately administer IV antibiotics; do not delay life-saving treatment for imaging.
* Pitfall: Assuming a normal temperature or hypothermia in a neonate rules out meningitis. Neonatal presentations are highly atypical and hypothermia is a common sign of sepsis.
