# Transient Tachypnoea of the Newborn (TTN) (新生兒短暫性氣促)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of TTN
Benign, self-limiting respiratory distress in term/late-preterm neonates. 
Aetiology: Delayed clearance of fetal alveolar fluid.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Common in HA SCBU/NICU.
* Incidence: ~1-2% of all live births.
* ↑ Risk in **elective LSCS (剖腹產)** without onset of labour.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Normal Fetal Lung Fluid Clearance Pathophysiology
* Fetal lung: actively secretes fluid via $Cl^{-}$ pumps.
* Approaching term/during labour: ↑ catecholamines/glucocorticoids → switch to active fluid absorption via epithelial $Na^{+}$ channels (**ENaC**).

### Pathophysiology of TTN
* Lack of labour (e.g., elective LSCS) → ↓ catecholamine surge → ↓ ENaC activation.
* Result: Retained alveolar fluid → ↓ lung compliance → tachypnoea to compensate for ↑ work of breathing.

### Risk Factors for TTN
* **Elective LSCS** (Gold standard risk factor)
* Late preterm delivery (34-37 weeks)
* Maternal asthma or diabetes
* Male sex
* Macrosomia (巨大嬰兒)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Onset of TTN Symptoms
* Rapid onset: **< 2 hours** post-delivery.

### Key Clinical Signs of TTN
* **Tachypnoea (氣促)**: RR > 60/min (often 80-120/min)
* **Chest recession / Retractions (胸部凹陷)**: Subcostal, intercostal, sternal
* **Expiratory grunting (呼氣呻吟)**: Attempt to create PEEP and clear fluid
* **Nasal flaring (鼻翼煽動)**
* Mild **cyanosis (發紺)**: Usually resolves with minimal supplemental $O_{2}$ (< 40%)
* Auscultation: Usually clear, occasionally mild crackles.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Features vs Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS)
* **Gestation**: TTN = Term/late-preterm; RDS = Premature.
* **CXR**: TTN = Fluid in fissures/hyperinflation; RDS = Ground-glass opacities/low volume.

### Discriminatory Features vs Sepsis/Pneumonia
* **Clinical Course**: TTN improves rapidly (24-72h); Sepsis deteriorates.
* **Risk Factors**: Sepsis = Prolonged rupture of membranes (PROM), maternal fever, +ve maternal GBS.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differentials for Neonatal Respiratory Distress
| Condition | Gestation | Key Clues | Classic CXR Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **TTN (新生兒短暫性氣促)** | Term / Late-preterm | Elective LSCS, rapid onset, benign course | Fluid in horizontal fissure, hyperinflation, perihilar streaking |
| **RDS (呼吸窘迫綜合症)** | Premature | Surfactant deficiency | Ground-glass opacities, air bronchograms, low lung volume |
| **Neonatal Pneumonia / Sepsis** | Any | Maternal fever, PROM, GBS +ve | Patchy infiltrates, consolidation, indistinguishable from RDS/TTN |
| **Meconium Aspiration Syndrome (MAS) (胎糞吸入綜合症)** | Post-term / Term | Meconium-stained liquor, fetal distress | Patchy atelectasis & hyperinflation, coarse infiltrates |
| **Pneumothorax (氣胸)** | Any | Sudden deterioration, asymmetric chest movement | Translucent hemithorax, mediastinal shift |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations for TTN
* **Pulse oximetry ($SpO_{2}$)**: Continuous monitoring. Target > 92-95% (per local HA protocols).

### Blood Investigations for TTN
* **Blood Gas (CBG/ABG)**: 
  * Mild hypoxaemia
  * Normocapnia or mild respiratory alkalosis (compensatory ↑ RR)
  * *Note: Significant hypercapnia ($pCO_{2}$ ↑) suggests fatigue or alternative pathology (e.g., RDS).*
* **Septic Screen (感染篩查)** (Standard HA Protocol for respiratory distress):
  * **Blood culture**
  * **Complete Blood Count (CBC)** (Check WCC & Immature:Total neutrophil ratio)
  * **C-Reactive Protein (CRP) (C-反應蛋白)** (Baseline, repeat at 12-24h)

### Radiological Investigations for TTN (Gold Standard)
* **Chest X-Ray (CXR) (胸部X光)**:
  * Prominent perihilar vascular markings (Sunburst pattern)
  * Fluid in the horizontal fissure
  * Mild hyperinflation (flattened diaphragms)
  * Small pleural effusions (blunted costophrenic angles)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Respiratory Management of TTN
* Admit SCBU / NICU for continuous monitoring.
* **Oxygen Therapy**: Supplemental $O_{2}$ via nasal cannula or headbox to maintain $SpO_{2}$ > 92%. 
* **CPAP (持續氣道正壓通氣)**: Indicated if $FiO_{2}$ requirement > 30-40% or severe increased work of breathing. Helps push fluid into interstitium.

### Acute Nutritional Management & Fluid Protocol
* **NBM (Nil By Mouth)**: Strictly NBM if RR > 80/min (high risk of aspiration).
* **IV Fluids (靜脈輸液)**: Start 10% Dextrose (e.g., 60 ml/kg/day for day 1) if NBM.
* **Enteral Feeding**: Trial Nasogastric (NG) tube or cautious oral feeds if RR 60-80/min and work of breathing is mild.

### Acute Pharmacological Management of TTN
* **Empirical IV Antibiotics (經驗性抗生素)**: 
  * Impossible to definitively distinguish TTN from early-onset pneumonia initially.
  * Start **IV Ampicillin + Gentamicin** (Standard HA protocol).
  * Stop at 48 hours if blood cultures are -ve and CRP is normal.
* *Note: Diuretics (e.g., furosemide) or bronchodilators have NO proven benefit and are NOT recommended.*

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### MDT Involvement in TTN
* **Neonatologist/Paediatrician**: Lead clinical management & antibiotic stewardship.
* **NICU/SCBU Nurses**: Continuous monitoring of RR, effort, and $SpO_{2}$.
* **Lactation Consultant**: Assist mother in expressing breastmilk while infant is NBM to maintain supply.

### Psychosocial Care for TTN Parents
* Reassurance is paramount: Emphasize that TTN is a transient, self-limiting structural transition, not a permanent defect.
* Encourage Kangaroo Care (skin-to-skin) once respiratory status stabilizes.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Considerations in Neonatal Respiratory Distress
* TTN is a physiological condition. However, if respiratory depression/tachypnoea presents atypically or with altered mental status, consider maternal substance abuse (e.g., opiate withdrawal) and initiate medical social worker (MSW) involvement.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Prognosis of TTN
* **Excellent**. Self-limiting.
* Peak severity at 12-24 hours.
* Usually complete resolution within **24 to 72 hours**.
* No long-term pulmonary sequelae.

### Complications of TTN
* **Pneumothorax / Air leak**: Rare, usually secondary to CPAP use rather than TTN itself.
* **Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension of the Newborn (PPHN) (新生兒持續性肺動脈高壓)**: Extremely rare complication if hypoxia is severe and uncorrected.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the classic chest X-ray finding in Transient Tachypnoea of the Newborn? Answer: Fluid in the horizontal fissure, prominent perihilar streaking, and hyperinflation.
* Trap: Assuming mild respiratory distress in a term baby post-elective LSCS is definitively TTN without considering infection. Pearl: Always perform a baseline septic screen and start empirical IV antibiotics (Ampicillin + Gentamicin) until blood cultures are negative at 48 hours, as early-onset pneumonia mimics TTN.
* Pitfall: Allowing oral feeding in a neonate with a respiratory rate > 80/min. This significantly increases the risk of aspiration. Keep the infant NBM and start IV fluids until tachypnoea improves.
