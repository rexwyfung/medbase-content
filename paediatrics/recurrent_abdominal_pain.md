# Recurrent Abdominal Pain (反覆性腹痛)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition & Rome IV Criteria
* **Recurrent Abdominal Pain (反覆性腹痛):** Now classified under **Functional Gastrointestinal Disorders (FGIDs) (功能性腸胃病)** using Rome IV criteria.
* **Rome IV Criteria:** ≥4 episodes/month of abdominal pain sufficient to interrupt normal activities, lasting for ≥2 months.
* Replaces classical **Apley's criteria** (3 episodes over 3 months).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence:** ~10-15% of HK school-aged children.
* **HK Context:** Highly correlated with local intense academic pressure (e.g., TSAs, DSEs), parental anxiety, and extracurricular overloading.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Functional Gastrointestinal Disorders Pathophysiology (90%)
* **Brain-Gut Axis (腦腸軸) Dysfunction:** Altered neuro-immune interactions → **Visceral hypersensitivity (內臟過敏)** (normal bowel distension perceived as severe pain).
* **Altered GI Motility:** Neurological hypervigilance amplifying baseline peristalsis.
* **Psychosocial Triggers:** Stress, bullying, academic pressure, family discord, parental separation.

### Organic Disease Pathophysiology (10%)
* **GI:** **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) (炎症性腸病)**, **Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉)**, **Peptic Ulcer Disease (消化性潰瘍)** (↑ *H. pylori* prevalence in HK), Eosinophilic Oesophagitis, **Constipation (便秘)**.
* **Renal:** Recurrent UTI, Pelviureteric junction (PUJ) obstruction.
* **Gynaecological:** Endometriosis, ovarian cysts (adolescent females).

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Functional Abdominal Pain Phenotypes
* **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) (腸易激綜合症):** Pain related to defecation, altered stool frequency/form.
* **Abdominal Migraine (腹部偏頭痛):** Paroxysmal, intense periumbilical pain lasting ≥1 hr → interferes with activity. Interspersed with weeks of normal health.
* **Functional Dyspepsia (功能性消化不良):** Postprandial fullness, early satiety, epigastric burning. Not relieved by defecation.
* **Functional Abdominal Pain NOS:** Vague, periumbilical pain not meeting specific criteria above.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Typical Benign Features (Functional)
* Pain location: strictly **periumbilical** (臍周).
* Nature: dull/crampy, lasts <1 hour.
* Does **not** wake child from sleep.
* Normal growth parameters (weight/height centiles).
* Completely normal physical examination.

### Red Flag Symptoms (危險信號)
* Pain located away from umbilicus (e.g., RUQ, RLQ).
* Pain awakening child from sleep.
* Significant weight loss or faltering growth.
* Unexplained fever, joint pain, or rash.
* Persistent vomiting (especially bilious) or chronic severe diarrhoea.
* GI bleeding (haematemesis, melaena, haematochezia).
* +ve Family history of IBD, Coeliac, or Peptic Ulcer.
* Delayed puberty or mouth ulcers.
* Abnormal physical exam (e.g., RIF tenderness, perianal skin tags/fissures → Crohn's).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Functional Phenotype Differentiation
| Subtype | Key Discriminatory Features | Associated Symptoms |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **IBS** | Pain altered by defecation; change in stool frequency/form | Bloating, feeling of incomplete evacuation |
| **Abdominal Migraine** | Intense paroxysmal pain lasting 1-72 hrs; long symptom-free intervals | Anorexia, nausea, vomiting, pallor, photophobia |
| **Functional Dyspepsia** | Epigastric pain/burning related to meals | Postprandial fullness, early satiety, belching |
| **Constipation** | Pain improves immediately after large bowel movement | Palpable faecal mass in LIF, withholding posture |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Common vs Must-Not-Miss Differentials
| Category | Differentials | Clinical Clues |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Most Likely** | **Functional Abdominal Pain**, **Constipation (便秘)** | Periumbilical pain, normal growth, palpable LIF mass |
| **Must-Not-Miss (GI)** | **IBD (Crohn's/UC)**, **Coeliac Disease (乳糜瀉)**, Appendicitis | Poor growth, bloody stool, mouth ulcers, RIF pain |
| **Must-Not-Miss (Renal)** | UTI, PUJ Obstruction | Dysuria, loin pain, secondary enuresis |
| **Must-Not-Miss (Gynae)** | Ovarian torsion/cyst, Dysmenorrhoea | Sudden intense lower pelvic pain in pubertal females |

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Minimal Investigations (No Red Flags)
* **Reassurance:** Diagnosis of exclusion based on history/exam; avoid over-investigation.
* **Urinalysis/Culture:** Rule out UTI.
* **Stool:** Occult blood, culture (if diarrhoea).
* **Growth Monitoring:** Serial height/weight centile plotting.

### Gold Standard Laboratory Investigations (If Red Flags Present)
* **CBC & Smear:** Check for anaemia (chronic disease/blood loss).
* **ESR / CRP:** ↑ indicates organic inflammation (e.g., IBD).
* **Coeliac Screen:** Anti-tTG IgA + Total IgA.
* **Biochemistry:** U&Es, LFTs, Amylase, TFTs.
* **Stool Calprotectin (糞便鈣衛蛋白):** Excellent non-invasive marker for bowel inflammation (differentiates IBD from functional).

### Imaging & Endoscopic Investigations
* **Ultrasound Abdomen:** Low yield for functional pain. Used only if specific signs present (e.g., RUQ pain for gallstones, loin pain for PUJ obstruction).
* **Endoscopy/Colonoscopy (內窺鏡檢查):** Definitive test for IBD, Coeliac, or *H. pylori*. Reserved for cases with strong clinical suspicion or abnormal screening (e.g., ↑ Calprotectin).

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Biopsychosocial Approach & Validation
* **Validate Pain:** Acknowledge pain is real, not "faked" or "in their head".
* **Explain Brain-Gut Axis:** "Insides of the intestine become extra sensitive; normal digestion is felt as pain."
* **De-medicalization:** Differentiate "serious" (missed school) from "dangerous" (organ damage). Shift focus from "curing pain" to "restoring function".

### Pharmacological Therapy (藥物治療)
| Condition | 1st Line Options | HA Clinical Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **IBS / Cramping** | Peppermint oil, Antispasmodics (e.g., Mebeverine) | Use sparingly; target symptom control |
| **Abdominal Migraine** | Pizotifen, Sumatriptan, Propranolol | Use specific anti-migraine prophylaxis if missing school |
| **Functional Dyspepsia** | H2-blockers (Famotidine), PPIs (Omeprazole) | 4-8 week trial to suppress acid |
| **Constipation** | Macrogol (Laxido/Movicol), Lactulose | Aggressive disimpaction followed by long-term maintenance |

### Dietary & Lifestyle Modifications
* **IBS:** Low FODMAP diet trial (dietitian guided).
* **General:** Identify and manage school/social stressors. Maintain regular meals, hydration, and sleep hygiene.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Interventions
* **Clinical Psychologist (臨床心理學家):** Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) or hypnotherapy for severe functional pain impacting daily life.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) / School Social Worker:** Address peer bullying, academic expectations, family discord, or financial stress.
* **School Integration:** Liaise with teachers to allow bathroom passes and "time-out" spaces without sending the child home.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### School Refusal & Factitious Disorder
* **School Refusal (拒絕上學):** Common complication. Requires strict graded return to school plan. Emphasize child *must* attend school despite pain.
* **NAI / Neglect:** Consider if parent uses child's "illness" for secondary gain, or if child exhibits extreme anxiety around certain caregivers. Watch for repeated, unnecessary presentation to EDs (Munchausen by proxy).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Long-term Trajectory
* **Functional FGIDs:** Good overall prognosis for organ health, but symptoms persist into adulthood in ~30% (evolving into adult IBS). Chronic school absenteeism → educational failure and social isolation.
* **Organic Disease:** Varies by aetiology (e.g., IBD requires lifelong immunosuppression; Coeliac requires lifelong gluten-free diet).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What are the 'red flag' symptoms in a child presenting with recurrent abdominal pain? Answer: Pain waking from sleep, pain away from the umbilicus, weight loss/faltering growth, GI bleeding, chronic severe diarrhoea, unexplained fever, delayed puberty, and family history of IBD/Coeliac.
* Trap: Ordering an abdominal ultrasound routinely for typical periumbilical functional pain. Pearl: Ultrasound has an extremely low yield in the absence of red flags and often inadvertently increases parental anxiety by finding incidental, irrelevant anomalies.
* Pitfall: Recommending strict rest or keeping the child home from school during functional pain episodes. Encourage normal activities and school attendance; withdrawing the child reinforces the sick role and exacerbates school avoidance.
* Trap: Assuming nocturnal abdominal pain is functional. Pearl: Functional abdominal pain rarely wakes a child from sleep; night-time waking strongly suggests organic pathology like peptic ulcer disease or IBD.
* Viva Question: What non-invasive stool test helps differentiate IBD from functional abdominal pain? Answer: Faecal calprotectin.
