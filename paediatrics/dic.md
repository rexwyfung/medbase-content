# Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC) (彌散性血管內凝血)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition
* **Consumptive Coagulopathy (消耗性凝血病)**: Devastating systemic syndrome
* Simultaneous, uncontrolled activation of systemic coagulation → exhaustion of clotting factors & platelets
* Results in paradoxical massive thrombosis + massive hemorrhage

### Local Epidemiology (HK Context)
* Always 2° to underlying catastrophe; never a primary disease
* **Common triggers in HK HA (香港醫院管理局) PICU/NICU**:
  * Severe sepsis (esp. Gram -ve, *Streptococcus pneumoniae*)
  * Extreme trauma / crush injuries
  * Massive burns
  * Malignant leukemia (esp. APML / M3)
  * Severe perinatal asphyxia / meconium aspiration

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology
* **Trigger**: Massive release of **Tissue Factor (組織因子)** (e.g., via bacterial LPS/endotoxin, tissue damage) into blood
* **Phase 1 (Thrombosis)**: 
  * Widespread coagulation cascade activation
  * Microthrombi form in microvasculature → widespread organ ischemia (ARF, acute liver failure)
* **Phase 2 (Hemorrhage)**: 
  * Massive consumption of platelets & clotting factors (II, V, VIII, fibrinogen)
  * Secondary fibrinolysis kicks in (plasmin degrades cross-linked fibrin)
  * Result: Uncontrollable hemorrhage from all sites

### Genetics
* N/A (Acquired systemic syndrome)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Acute Clinical Presentation
* Critically ill neonate/child (NICU/PICU setting)
* Profound shock / sepsis
* Sudden, unprovoked bleeding from mucosal surfaces, IV line sites, ETT, heal-pricks
* Simultaneous ischemic changes (e.g., black toes/fingers)

### Hemorrhagic Signs
* **Petechiae (瘀點)**
* **Ecchymoses (瘀斑)**
* Continuous oozing from venepuncture / surgical wounds
* GI bleeds (hematemesis, melena)
* Massive pulmonary hemorrhage

### Thrombotic Signs
* **Acrocyanosis (肢端發紺)** (blue/black digits)
* Gangrene
* Oliguria/anuria (Acute Renal Failure)
* Altered mental status (microinfarcts in CNS)

### Systemic Signs
* Severe shock (tachycardia, hypotension, prolonged CRT)
* Multi-organ dysfunction syndrome (MODS)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### DIC vs. Similar Coagulopathies

| Feature | DIC (彌散性血管內凝血) | ITP (免疫性血小板減少性紫癜) | TTP (血栓性血小板減少性紫癜) | Liver Failure (肝衰竭) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Platelets** | ↓↓ | ↓↓↓ | ↓↓ | Normal / ↓ |
| **PT/APTT** | ↑↑ (Prolonged) | Normal | Normal | ↑↑ (Prolonged) |
| **Fibrinogen** | ↓↓ (Consumed) | Normal | Normal | ↓ (Decreased synthesis) |
| **D-Dimer** | ↑↑↑ (Massive) | Normal | Normal / ↑ | Normal / ↑ |
| **Schistocytes** | +ve | -ve | +ve | -ve |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Main Differentials
* **Severe Liver Failure (嚴重肝衰竭)**: ↓ factor synthesis → ↑ PT/APTT, ↓ fibrinogen. D-Dimer usually NOT massively elevated.
* **Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding (VKDB) (維生素K缺乏性出血)**: ↑ PT/APTT, normal platelets, normal D-Dimer.
* **Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP)**: ↓ platelets, normal coagulation profile.
* **Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)**: ↓ platelets, MAHA, ARF, but PT/APTT usually normal.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Investigations
* Urine output monitoring (strict hourly)
* Bedside clotting time (prolonged)
* Capillary blood gas (CBG/ABG): Metabolic acidosis (↑ lactate) from tissue ischemia

### Bloods (DIC Screen)
* **Platelets (血小板)**: ↓↓ (Consumptive)
* **PT / APTT (凝血酶原時間/活化部分凝血活酶時間)**: ↑↑ (Prolonged)
* **Fibrinogen (纖維蛋白原)**: ↓↓ (Converted to fibrin)
* **D-Dimer (D-二聚體)**: ↑↑↑ (Fibrin degradation product, highly sensitive)
* **Peripheral Blood Smear**: **Schistocytes (Helmet cells) (裂紅細胞)** → mechanical shearing of RBCs by fibrin meshes.

### Special Investigations
* FDPs (Fibrin Degradation Products): ↑↑
* Blood cultures / PCR (identify sepsis trigger)

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### 1st Line Management: Trigger Reversal
* **CRUCIAL**: Rapid identification & reversal of underlying trigger
* Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics (e.g., Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin per HA guidelines) for sepsis
* Source control (surgery/debridement)
* Aggressive shock management (IV crystalloid/colloid boluses, inotropes)

### Acute Replacement Therapy (If Actively Bleeding)
* **Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP) (新鮮冷凍血漿)**: 15-20 mL/kg → replaces exhausted clotting factors
* **Cryoprecipitate (冷凍沉澱品)**: 5-10 mL/kg → specifically to replace Fibrinogen (target >1.0 g/L)
* **Platelet Transfusion (血小板輸注)**: If platelets < 50x10^9/L & bleeding
* Packed RBCs (PRBC): If severe anemia / hemodynamic instability

### Controversial Management
* **Heparin (肝素)**: Generally contraindicated if actively bleeding. May be considered in pure thrombotic phase for specific triggers (e.g., purpura fulminans, APML) under strict pediatric hematologist guidance.
* **Vitamin K (維生素K)**: IV 1-5 mg (though slow onset, given to rule out concurrent deficiency).

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT Involvement
* Exclusively managed in PICU/NICU
* Pediatric Hematologist
* Pediatric Intensivist
* Microbiologist (if sepsis driven)

### Psychosocial Support
* High mortality → urgent Medical Social Worker (MSW) involvement
* Bereavement counseling / psychological support for parents

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding
* DIC purely secondary to trauma/burns requires clear history
* Unexplained extreme trauma → trigger NAI investigation & notify MSW / Police

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Complications
* **Intracranial Hemorrhage (ICH) (顱內出血)** → fatal or severe neurological deficit
* **Multi-organ Failure (MODS)** (ARF, liver failure, ARDS)
* **Autoamputation (自動截肢)**: Digits/limbs lost to microvascular thrombotic gangrene

### Prognosis
* Extremely guarded; very high mortality rate
* Dictated entirely by reversibility of the underlying cause

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Ordering Vitamin K in severe consumptive DIC and hoping it fixes the bleeding. Pearl: Vitamin K takes 12-24 hours for the liver to synthesize new factors; a patient in DIC will die in minutes to hours, needing immediate FFP and Cryoprecipitate.
* Viva Question: What specific blood smear finding is a hallmark of the microangiopathic hemolytic anemia seen in DIC? Answer: Schistocytes (fragmented red blood cells), formed when RBCs are sheared apart by the widespread fibrin meshes blocking the microvasculature.
* Pitfall: Giving prophylactic Heparin routinely in DIC. Do not give Heparin if the child is actively bleeding; it is highly controversial and only used in specific hyperthrombotic states under specialist guidance.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate Liver Failure from DIC using coagulation profiles? Answer: Both feature prolonged PT/APTT and low fibrinogen, but DIC presents with profoundly elevated D-Dimer due to massive clot breakdown.
