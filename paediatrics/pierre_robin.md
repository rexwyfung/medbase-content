# Pierre Robin Sequence (皮埃爾羅賓序列症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A sequence of congenital anomalies rather than a single syndrome, characterized by a specific triad of facial findings.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **The Sequence**: Mandibular hypoplasia (small jaw) $ightarrow$ tongue is pushed upwards and backwards (glossoptosis) $ightarrow$ tongue mechanically prevents the palatal shelves from fusing $ightarrow$ U-shaped cleft palate.
- Can be isolated or associated with syndromes (e.g., Stickler syndrome, DiGeorge syndrome).

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Severe upper airway obstruction and respiratory distress immediately after birth.
- Severe feeding difficulties, recurrent choking and aspiration.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Micrognathia (abnormally small mandible).
- Glossoptosis (downward displacement/retraction of the tongue).
- U-shaped (rather than V-shaped) cleft palate.
- Stridor, sternal retractions, cyanotic episodes.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- The U-shape of the cleft is highly discriminatory (indicates a mechanical barrier to fusion, unlike the V-shape in standard cleft palate).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Treacher Collins syndrome
- Stickler syndrome
- Isolated micrognathia

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**: Continuous SpO2 and cardiorespiratory monitoring. Airway assessment.
- **Special**: Polysomnography (Sleep study) to assess severity of obstructive apnoea. Genetics workup to rule out syndromic causes.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Airway Management**: 
  - Mild: Prone positioning (gravity pulls tongue forward).
  - Moderate: Nasopharyngeal airway (NPA) to bypass the tongue base.
  - Severe: Tongue-lip adhesion (glossopexy), Mandibular distraction osteogenesis, or Tracheostomy.
- **Feeding Support**: NG tube feeding is usually required initially to prevent aspiration and ensure weight gain.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **MDT**: Paediatric ENT, Plastic surgery, Respiratory, Dietitian.
- **Psychosocial**: MSW and comprehensive parental training (e.g., NPA care, CPR, feeding tube management) before discharge from HA hospital.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- High medical complexity increases parental burnout. Support via MSW and SWD is critical to prevent accidental neglect or severe caregiver exhaustion.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Hypoxia, cor pulmonale from chronic obstruction, failure to thrive.
- The jaw often undergoes "catch-up" growth in the first few years of life, eventually allowing airway independence.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Viva Q**: "Why is it called a sequence and not a syndrome?" Answer: Because a single primary embryological defect (mandibular hypoplasia) leads to a cascade of secondary defects (glossoptosis -> cleft palate).
- **Trap**: Intubating these infants is notoriously difficult (anesthetic emergency). Always call senior ENT/Anaesthesia early.
