# Scoliosis (脊柱側彎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Scoliosis
* 3D structural spinal deformity
* Coronal plane: **Cobb angle (柯布氏角度)** $\ge$ 10°
* Transverse plane: Vertebral axial rotation
* Sagittal plane: Frequently associated with thoracic hypokyphosis

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) (青少年原發性脊柱側彎)** is most common type (80-85%)
* HK prevalence: ~3-4% among adolescents
* Gender ratio: 1:1 for small curves (<10°), but $\ge$ 10:1 (F:M) for severe curves requiring treatment (bracing/surgery)
* Local screening: Universal school screening via **Student Health Service (SHS) (學生健康服務)** at Primary 4, Primary 6, Form 1, and Form 3

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis Pathophysiology
* Exact aetiology unknown (multifactorial)
* Involves uncoupled neuro-osseous growth
* Relative anterior spinal overgrowth → lordosis → biomechanical instability → lateral decompensation and rotation
* Exacerbated by rapid pubertal growth spurt

### Genetics of Scoliosis
* Polygenic inheritance pattern
* 30% of AIS patients have +ve family history
* Specific gene loci identified (e.g., CHD7, PAX1) but routine genetic testing not currently indicated in HK HA protocols

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Presenting Symptoms
* Mostly asymptomatic (painless)
* Detected via SHS screening or parental observation of postural asymmetry
* Back pain is a **Red Flag** in adolescents → dictates search for underlying pathology (e.g., syrinx, tumour, tethered cord)

### Physical Examination Signs
* **Adam's Forward Bend Test (亞當式前彎測試)**: Highlights **Rib Hump (肋骨突出)** due to vertebral rotation (pathognomonic for structural curve)
* Asymmetric shoulder height
* Prominent or asymmetric scapula
* Asymmetric waist crease / pelvic tilt
* Plumb line deviation from C7 spinous process to gluteal cleft
* **Neurological exam**: Must be normal in AIS. Check abdominal reflexes, lower limb tone/power, Babinski sign

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Structural vs Postural Scoliosis
| Feature | Structural Scoliosis (Idiopathic) | Non-Structural (Postural/Compensatory) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pathology** | Fixed bony/ligamentous deformity | Secondary to leg length discrepancy / pain |
| **Adam's Bend Test** | +ve (Rib hump persists) | -ve (Curve disappears on bending) |
| **Supine Position** | Curve persists | Curve corrects fully |
| **Vertebral Rotation** | Present | Absent |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Etiological Differentials Table
| Category | Condition | Distinguishing Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Idiopathic** | Infantile / Juvenile / AIS | Diagnosis of exclusion; asymptomatic; right thoracic curve most common |
| **Congenital** | Hemivertebra / Bar formation | Present at birth/early childhood; rapid progression; associated VACTERL anomalies |
| **Neuromuscular** | Cerebral Palsy / SMA / Duchenne | Large sweeping C-shaped curve; pelvic obliquity; severe neuromuscular deficits |
| **Syndromic** | Neurofibromatosis Type 1 (NF1) | Café-au-lait spots; sharp/short dystrophic curve |
| **Syndromic** | Marfan Syndrome | Tall stature; arachnodactyly; lens dislocation; aortic root dilatation |
| **Intraspinal** | Syringomyelia / Tethered Cord | **Left-sided** thoracic curve; absent abdominal reflexes; back pain; pes cavus |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Clinical Assessments
* **Scoliometer (脊柱側彎儀)**: Angle of Trunk Rotation (ATR). ATR $\ge$ 5-7° indicates need for radiographic evaluation
* Leg length measurement (Apparent vs True)

### Imaging Modalities
* **Standing Long-Cassette PA & Lateral Spine X-ray**: Gold standard.
* **Cobb Angle (柯布氏角度)**: Angle between superior endplate of upper end vertebra and inferior endplate of lower end vertebra
* **Risser Sign (里瑟標誌)**: Iliac apophysis ossification (Grade 0-5) to grade skeletal maturity
* **MRI Whole Spine**: Indicated ONLY for "Red Flags":
  * Left thoracic curve (AIS is typically right)
  * Abnormal neuro signs (e.g., absent abdominal reflexes)
  * Onset < 10 years old
  * Painful scoliosis
  * Rapid progression

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Observation & Conservative HA Protocols
* Target: Prevent progression >50° (threshold for lifelong progression)
* Curve < 25°: Observation with clinical exam + XR every 4-6 months until skeletal maturity
* Physiotherapy/Schroth method: Used adjunctively in HK, but unproven to halt curve progression independently

### Bracing Indications
* Curve 25°- 45° AND skeletally immature (Risser 0-2, pre-menarche or <1 year post-menarche)
* Rigid **TLSO brace (胸腰骶矯形器)** (e.g., Boston brace)
* Dose-dependent: Must wear $\ge$ 18-20 hours/day until skeletal maturity
* Brace does NOT correct curve; it prevents progression to surgical threshold

### Surgical Management Criteria
* Curve > 45-50° in growing children, or > 50° in skeletally mature patients
* HK Apex Centre: **Duchess of Kent Children's Hospital (DKCH) (大口環根德公爵夫人兒童醫院)**
* Procedure: Posterior Spinal Fusion (PSF) with pedicle screws and rods
* Neuromonitoring (SSEP/MEP) strictly required intraoperatively

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Interventions
* **Orthopaedic Surgeon**: Surgical planning, routine monitoring
* **Orthotist**: Brace fitting and adjustment, compliance monitoring
* **Physiotherapist**: Core strengthening, respiratory exercises, post-op mobility
* **Clinical Psychologist**: Body image counseling, brace-wearing compliance, anxiety management (high prevalence in adolescent girls)
* **School Nurse / SHS**: Early community detection and referral

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury & Neglect Indicators
* Medical neglect: Consistent failure to attend HK HA specialist appointments or orthotist fittings despite known severe curve
* Untreated severe curve (>60°) causing respiratory compromise due to parental refusal of standard care
* Physical signs of abuse discovered incidentally during Adam's forward bend test (e.g., concealed bruising on back)

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Untreated Curve Complications
* Progression: Curves >50° at skeletal maturity progress ~1°/year throughout adulthood
* **Cor Pulmonale (肺心病)**: Curves >90° → severe restrictive lung disease → pulmonary hypertension → right heart failure
* Chronic mechanical back pain
* Severe cosmetic deformity / psychological distress
* Pregnancy: Spinal curves may worsen slightly during pregnancy due to ligamentous laxity

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical physical exam finding that mandates an MRI in an adolescent with scoliosis? Answer: Absent abdominal reflexes or a left-sided thoracic curve, indicating potential syringomyelia or tethered cord.
* Viva Question: How do you assess skeletal maturity in a scoliosis patient? Answer: Risser sign (iliac apophysis ossification) on X-ray, menarchal status, and Tanner staging.
* Trap: Reassuring a patient that their back pain is simply due to their idiopathic scoliosis. Pearl: AIS is typically painless. Significant back pain in a scoliotic child is a red flag requiring investigation for osteoid osteoma, discitis, or intraspinal anomalies.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing structural scoliosis based solely on a standing X-ray without clinical examination. A leg length discrepancy can cause a compensatory (non-structural) curve on X-ray that disappears when the patient is seated or supine.
* Trap: Prescribing a brace for a patient with a 35° curve who is Risser 5 (skeletally mature). Pearl: Bracing is only effective in skeletally immature patients (Risser 0-2) to prevent progression during the growth spurt.
