# Drug Allergy, SJS & DRESS (藥物過敏、史提芬強生症候群及 DRESS)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS) / Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN)**: A horrific, life-threatening mucocutaneous hypersensitivity reaction with epidermal detachment. SJS (<10% body surface area), TEN (>30%), Overlap (10-30%).
- **DRESS**: Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms. A severe, idiosyncratic reaction targeting internal organs.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Culprit drugs are heavily prescribed: Antiepileptics (Carbamazepine, Phenytoin), Antibiotics (Penicillins, Bactrim), NSAIDs, and Allopurinol.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Deeply linked to HLA genetics. (e.g., HLA-B*1502 in Han Chinese strongly predicts Carbamazepine-induced SJS/TEN).
- **SJS/TEN**: Cytotoxic CD8+ T-cells kill keratinocytes via Fas-FasL pathway and granulysin release, causing the epidermis to completely slough off, akin to a massive full-thickness burn.
- **DRESS**: T-cell activation combined with viral reactivation (especially HHV-6, EBV, CMV) driving massive eosinophilia and internal organ infiltration.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **SJS/TEN**: Typically 1-3 weeks after a new drug. Starts with a flu-like prodrome, then excruciatingly painful target lesions develop, leading to large flaccid blisters and peeling skin.
- **DRESS**: Typically 2-8 weeks (later onset!) after starting a drug. Starts with a high-spiking fever, diffuse rash, massive facial swelling, and jaundice.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **SJS/TEN**:
  - Positive **Nikolsky sign** (light rubbing of the skin causes the top layer to peel off).
  - Severe mucosal involvement: Crusted/bleeding lips, conjunctivitis (can cause blindness), genital ulcers.
  - Target lesions (usually atypical, with 2 zones, unlike classic Erythema Multiforme which has 3).
- **DRESS**:
  - Widespread morbilliform rash.
  - Hallmarks: **Facial edema** and **lymphadenopathy**.
  - Internal: Hepatomegaly, jaundice, nephritis.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from simple Maculopapular Exanthem (MPE): A simple viral or mild drug rash DOES NOT have mucosal involvement, skin peeling, facial edema, or organ failure.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Staphylococcal Scalded Skin Syndrome (SSSS) - usually spares the mucous membranes and is superficial!
- Kawasaki Disease
- Erythema Multiforme (EM) - usually triggered by HSV, not drugs, and rarely progresses to TEN.
- Viral Exanthem

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **SJS/TEN**: Clinical diagnosis + Skin biopsy (showing full-thickness epidermal necrosis).
- **DRESS**: 
  - CBC: Massive **Eosinophilia** (>1.5 x 10^9/L), atypical lymphocytes.
  - Complete metabolic panel: Highly deranged LFTs (hepatitis), rising creatinine.
- **Pharmacogenetics**: HLA-B*1502 screening is mandatory in HK before starting Carbamazepine.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **CRITICAL STEP 1**: Immediately and permanently stop the suspected offending drug.
- **SJS/TEN**:
  - Treat exactly like a major burns victim in PICU/Burn unit.
  - Strict fluid replacement, temperature regulation, and pain control.
  - Aggressive ophthalmology involvement to prevent synechiae and blindness.
  - Medical: Controversial, but high-dose IVIG or Cyclosporine are often used early to halt the T-cell attack.
- **DRESS**:
  - Systemic Corticosteroids (e.g., Prednisolone 1mg/kg) with a VERY slow taper over weeks/months (to prevent deadly rebounds from HHV-6 reactivation).

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Devastating cosmetic and physical scarring in TEN. Long-term medical alert bracelets required.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **SJS/TEN**: Sepsis (loss of skin barrier), hypovolemic shock, permanent blindness, strictures in the esophagus/genitals. Very high mortality (up to 30% in TEN).
- **DRESS**: Fulminant hepatic failure. Autoimmune sequelae (e.g., Thyroiditis) can occur months later.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Using silver sulfadiazine cream routinely on the skin slough of SJS/TEN. Why? Because sulfadiazine is a sulfa-drug, which itself is a massive trigger for SJS/TEN!
- **Viva Q**: "A child with epilepsy develops SJS 2 weeks after starting a new medication. The skin peels off, but you also notice their eyes are terribly red and sticking together. Why MUST an ophthalmologist be heavily involved?" Answer: SJS causes severe mucosal inflammation. If the palpebral and bulbar conjunctiva are inflamed, they will scar and fuse together (symblepharon), permanently destroying the tear ducts and causing lifelong blindness.
