# Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) (杜興氏肌肉營養不良症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
Most common & severe childhood **muscular dystrophy (肌肉營養不良症)**. X-linked recessive disorder causing progressive, irreversible muscle weakness & wasting.

### Epidemiology of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* Incidence: ~1 in 3,000-6,000 live male births.
* Females: Usually asymptomatic carriers.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Genetic Basis of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Inheritance**: X-linked recessive.
* **Mutation**: Deletion in **DMD gene** on X chromosome (largest known human gene).
* **De Novo Rate**: ~1/3 cases are new mutations (no family history).

### Cellular Pathophysiology of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Dystrophin Defect**: Lack of **Dystrophin (抗肌萎縮蛋白)**, a structural protein anchoring cytoskeleton to extracellular matrix.
* **Membrane Damage**: Muscle cell membrane tears during contraction → calcium influx → muscle fiber necrosis.
* **Pseudohypertrophy (假性肥大)**: Dead muscle replaced by fat & fibrotic tissue.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Onset & Progression of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Infancy**: Normal appearance.
* **Early Childhood**: Average age of diagnosis ~5 years (though signs present earlier).
* **Initial Concerns**: **Language delay (語言遲緩)**, delayed motor milestones, frequent falls, clumsiness, difficulty climbing stairs (mounts one by one).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Motor Signs of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Proximal Muscle Weakness (近端肌肉無力)**: Pelvis & legs first → shoulder girdle & arms.
* **Gowers' Sign (高爾氏徵)**: Pathognomonic >3 years; child rolls prone, uses hands to "climb up own legs" to stand due to weak gluteal/quadriceps.
* **Waddling Gait (搖擺步態)**: Due to gluteus medius weakness (Trendelenburg gait).
* **Calf Pseudohypertrophy (小腿假性肥大)**: Large, firm calves (fat/scar tissue, not muscle).
* **Toe Walking (踮腳尖走路)**: Early Achilles tendon contractures.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

| Feature | Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) | Becker Muscular Dystrophy (BMD) | Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Protein Defect** | Absent dystrophin | Truncated/reduced dystrophin | SMN protein defect |
| **Onset** | Early childhood (~2-5 yrs) | Late childhood/adolescence | Infancy/early childhood |
| **Progression** | Rapid, wheelchair by 10-14 yrs | Slower, ambulation into adulthood | Variable, anterior horn cell disease |
| **Key Signs** | Gowers', pseudohypertrophy | Milder weakness | Profound hypotonia, fasciculations, absent reflexes |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Differentials for Progressive Muscle Weakness in Childhood
* **Becker Muscular Dystrophy (貝克氏肌肉營養不良症)**
* **Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA) (脊髓性肌肉萎縮症)**
* **Inflammatory Myopathies** (e.g., Dermatomyositis)
* **Cerebral Palsy (腦性麻痺)** (Note: presents with spasticity, non-progressive)

## 7. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Blood Investigations for Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Creatine Kinase (CK) (肌酸激酶)**: Best initial screen. Massively ↑ (often >10,000 U/L) even before clinical symptoms due to massive ongoing necrosis.

### Special & Definitive Investigations for Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Genetic Testing**: Definitive test. MLPA or sequencing of DMD gene to identify deletion/mutation.
* **Muscle Biopsy**: Historical gold standard (absent dystrophin staining). Rarely needed now if genetics confirmatory.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Medical Management of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Corticosteroids** (**Prednisolone** or **Deflazacort**): Proven to slow decline, prolong ambulation (2-3 yrs), delay severe scoliosis & respiratory failure. Start ~age 4-5.
* **Emerging Therapies**: Exon-skipping drugs (e.g., Eteplirsen) to produce functional dystrophin.

### Physical & Respiratory Management of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Physiotherapy & Orthotics**: Daily stretching to prevent contractures (Achilles). Ankle-foot orthoses (AFOs) for night. Surgical Achilles lengthening.
* **Respiratory Care**: Sleep studies for non-ambulant boys. **Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV/BiPAP)** for sleep-disordered breathing/hypoventilation. Cough assist devices. Eventual tracheostomy/full-time ventilation.

### Cardiac Management of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Cardiomyopathy Prevention**: Dilated cardiomyopathy ubiquitous. Prophylactic ACE inhibitors started early to delay heart failure.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team for Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* Requires Paediatric Neurologist, Respiratory Physician, Cardiologist, Orthopaedic Surgeon, PT, OT, Dietitian, & MSW.

### Family & Psychosocial Support for Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* Devastating diagnosis. Need support for coping with progressive loss of function & shortened lifespan (e.g., HK Neuromuscular Disease Association).
* **Genetic Counseling (遺傳諮詢)**: Crucial for mother (carrier status) & future pregnancies.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding in Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Major Complications of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* **Musculoskeletal**: Severe scoliosis (脊柱側彎) (needs spinal fusion once wheelchair-bound), joint contractures.
* **Respiratory**: Sleep-disordered breathing (daytime headache, irritability), pneumonia, respiratory failure.
* **Cardiac**: Dilated cardiomyopathy (擴張型心肌病).
* **Cognitive**: ~1/3 have learning difficulties.

### Prognosis of Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
* Loss of independent ambulation by 10-14 years.
* Death typically in late 20s from respiratory failure or intractable heart failure.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Describe Gowers' sign and explain its underlying pathophysiology? Answer: Child rolls prone and uses hands to 'climb up their own legs' to stand. Caused by severe proximal muscle weakness (gluteus maximus and quadriceps).
* Viva Question: A 4-year-old boy presents with language delay, frequent falls, and large calves. What is the best initial blood test, and what result do you expect? Answer: Creatine Kinase (CK). It will be massively elevated (>10,000 U/L) due to ongoing muscle necrosis.
* Trap: Assuming language delay is purely a primary developmental disorder. Pearl: Language delay can be an early presentation of DMD; check motor milestones and consider CK if suspicious.
* Pitfall: Assuming large calf muscles in a clumsy toddler are a sign of strength or athleticism. In DMD, this is pseudohypertrophy caused by fat and scar tissue replacing necrotic muscle.
