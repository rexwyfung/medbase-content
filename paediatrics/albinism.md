# Oculocutaneous Albinism (OCA) (眼皮膚白化症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A group of inherited disorders of melanin biosynthesis causing generalized reduction in pigmentation of hair, skin, and eyes.
- **Local Epidemiology**: Type 1 (OCA1 - absent tyrosinase) is most severe. Occurs worldwide.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Mostly autosomal recessive. OCA1a involves a complete lack of **tyrosinase** activity.
- Tyrosinase is the critical rate-limiting enzyme converting tyrosine to melanin. 
- Melanin is not just for pigment; it is mandatory for normal routing of optic nerve fibers at the chiasm and foveal development in embryonic life.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Obvious at birth: pure white hair, milky white skin, blue-pink irises.
- Distinct vision problems noticed early.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Skin/Hair**: Snow-white to pale blonde hair. Milky white skin that does not tan, but burns viciously.
- **Ocular anomalies (Mandatory for diagnosis)**:
  - Severe horizontal nystagmus (wobbly eyes).
  - Profound photophobia (lack of pigment in iris allows scattered light to blind the retina).
  - Iris transillumination (shining a light through the eyelid illuminates the whole red pupil).
  - Subnormal visual acuity due to foveal hypoplasia.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Must rule out deadly syndromic forms like Chediak-Higashi syndrome (albinism + profound immunodeficiency) or Hermansky-Pudlak syndrome (albinism + lethal bleeding disorders/pulmonary fibrosis).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Ocular Albinism (X-linked, only eyes affected, skin normal).
- Chediak-Higashi Syndrome
- Hermansky-Pudlak Syndrome
- Piebaldism (patchy loss of pigment).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**: Comprehensive ophthalmology exam.
- **Special**: VEP (Visual Evoked Potentials) showing classic misrouting of the optic pathways. 
- Genetic testing.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Dermatology**: Absolute, uncompromising UV protection. Broad spectrum SPF 50+, hats, UPF long clothing. Regular skin sweeping for pre-malignant lesions.
- **Ophthalmology**: Tinted/photochromic glasses for severely blinding photophobia. Correction of refractive errors. Strabismus surgery occasionally.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Social stigma is a massive issue. Close MSW follow-up needed. Visual impairment aids in school (large print, preferential seating) must be organized by special educational needs (SEN) coordinators.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Neglect in applying sun protection guarantees aggressive squamous and basal cell carcinomas at extremely young ages. Parental non-adherence here is a child protection issue.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Skin cancers in the 2nd and 3rd decades of life if unprotected.
- Permanent legal blindness.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Viva Q**: "Why do albinism patients have nystagmus and poor vision, considering the retina structure seems physically present?" Answer: Melanin is crucial in embryogenesis for the fovea to pit structurally, and for optic nerve fibers to decussate appropriately. Without melanin, the fovea never fully forms (foveal hypoplasia) and the visual cortex is "cross-wired", causing profound visual disruption.
