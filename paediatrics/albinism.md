# Oculocutaneous Albinism (OCA) (眼皮膚白化症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Oculocutaneous Albinism
* **Heterogeneous AR disorders** of **melanin biosynthesis (黑色素生合成)**
* → Generalized hypopigmentation of hair, skin & eyes.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: Rare in HK.
* **OCA1** (tyrosinase-related) & **OCA2** (P-protein related) = most common globally; local subtype data limited.
* **Lifelong follow-up**: Requires management in **Hospital Authority (HA) (醫院管理局)** Dermatology & Ophthalmology clinics.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Inheritance of OCA
* Strictly **Autosomal Recessive (AR) (體染色體隱性遺傳)**.

### Pathophysiology & Enzymatic Defects
* **Basic Mechanism**: Defect in melanin production pathway within melanocytes.
* **OCA1**: Mutation in *TYR* gene → **Tyrosinase (酪氨酸酶)** enzyme defect.
  * **OCA1a**: Complete absence of tyrosinase → zero melanin produced.
  * **OCA1b**: Reduced tyrosinase → minimal melanin accumulates over time.
* **OCA2**: Mutation in *OCA2* gene → **P-protein** (melanosomal transmembrane protein) defect → alters melanosomal pH & tyrosinase processing.
* **OCA3**: Mutation in *TYRP1* gene (rare).
* **OCA4**: Mutation in *SLC45A2* gene (rare).

### Developmental Impact of Melanin Absence
* **Neurological/Ophthalmic**: Melanin lack during embryogenesis → aberrant optic nerve fibre decussation at **optic chiasm (視交叉)** (excessive contralateral projection) + **foveal hypoplasia (黃斑中心凹發育不良)** → **failure to develop a fixation reflex (固視反射)**.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Congenital Presentation
* Striking hypopigmentation at birth (severity depends on subtype).
* **Ocular signs**: **Nystagmus (眼球震顫)** & poor visual acuity often primary complaints in infancy if skin hypopigmentation is subtle (e.g., OCA1b/OCA2).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Cutaneous Symptoms & Signs (Skin/Hair)
* **Hair**: Snow-white (OCA1a) to pale blonde/golden/light brown (OCA1b/OCA2).
* **Skin**: Milky white (OCA1a) to creamy white/light beige (OCA1b/OCA2).
* **Sun reaction**: Inability to tan, extreme **photosensitivity (光敏感)**, prone to severe sunburn.

### Ocular Symptoms & Signs (Mandatory for Diagnosis)
* **Congenital Nystagmus (先天性眼球震顫)**: Pendular (鐘擺型), horizontal, involuntary sensory defect nystagmus.
* **Constant Frowning (皺眉)**: Secondary to photophobia & visual effort.
* **Iris Transillumination (虹膜透照)**: Lack of pigment → red reflex visible through iris tissue.
* **Iris Colour**: Blue/pink (OCA1a) to hazel/light brown.
* **Profound Photophobia (畏光)**.
* **Foveal Hypoplasia (黃斑中心凹發育不良)**: Loss of foveal reflex on ophthalmoscopy.
* **Visual Acuity**: Significantly reduced (typically 6/60 or worse; legally blind).
* **Strabismus (斜視)** & reduced stereopsis.

## 5. Discriminatory Features
### Differentiating Isolated OCA from Syndromic Albinism
| Feature | Isolated OCA | Hermansky-Pudlak Syndrome (HPS) | Chediak-Higashi Syndrome (CHS) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pigmentation** | Generalized ↓ | Generalized ↓ | Generalized ↓ (often silvery hair) |
| **Hematology** | Normal | Platelet storage pool deficiency → Bleeding diathesis (易出血) | Giant lysosomal granules in WBCs; Neutropenia |
| **Immunology** | Normal | Normal | Severe Immunodeficiency → Recurrent pyogenic infections |
| **Other** | None | Pulmonary fibrosis (肺纖維化), Granulomatous colitis | Progressive neurologic degeneration, Hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis (HLH) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnoses for OCA
* **Ocular Albinism (OA) (眼白化症)**: X-linked recessive; strictly limited to eyes; skin/hair normal or slightly lighter than relatives.
* **Piebaldism (斑駁病)**: Autosomal dominant; localized stable leukoderma (depigmented skin) + white forelock; NO ocular involvement.
* **Vitiligo (白癜風)**: Acquired autoimmune melanocyte destruction; progressive, patchy; post-natal onset; NO congenital ocular signs.
* **Waardenburg Syndrome**: Localized hypopigmentation (white forelock) + sensory hearing loss, dystopia canthorum, heterochromia iridis.

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Clinical Investigations
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Generalized hypopigmentation + pathognomonic ocular signs.
* **Slit-lamp Exam**: Confirms iris transillumination.
* **Fundoscopy**: Confirms foveal hypoplasia.

### Special Ocular & Genetic Investigations
* **Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT)**: Gold standard imaging to confirm foveal hypoplasia.
* **Visual Evoked Potentials (VEP) (視覺誘發電位)**: Asymmetric cortical responses confirming optic pathway misrouting at chiasm (rarely required clinically).
* **Genetic Testing**: Gene panels to confirm subtype & guide genetic counselling.

### Systemic Screening (If Syndromic OCA Suspected)
* **Peripheral Blood Smear**: To exclude giant granules (CHS).
* **Platelet Aggregation Studies**: To exclude platelet storage pool deficiency (HPS).

## 8. Management
### Dermatological Management (HA Dermatology Protocol)
* **Strict Sun Protection (防曬)**: Lifelong adherence mandatory to prevent malignancy.
* **Topical**: Broad-spectrum, high-factor sunscreen (SPF 50+, PA+++) daily.
* **Clothing**: UV-protective clothing (UPF 50+), wide-brimmed hats.
* **Behavioural**: Strict avoidance of mid-day sun (10 AM - 4 PM).
* **Surveillance**: 6-12 monthly total body skin checks for pre-malignant **actinic keratoses (光化性角化症)** & skin cancers.

### Ophthalmological Management (HA Ophthalmology Protocol)
* **Refractive Correction**: Glasses/contacts to optimize residual vision.
* **Tinted Contact Lenses**: Fitting from early infancy allows development of normal fixation.
* **Photophobia Control**: Tinted glasses, UV filters, or photochromic lenses (變色鏡片).
* **Nystagmus/Strabismus**: Kestenbaum procedure (eye muscle surgery) occasionally used to shift null point if severe head turn present.
* **Low Vision Aids**: Magnifiers, high-contrast screens prescribed by low vision specialists.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### MDT & HA Support Networks
* **MCHC (母嬰健康院)**: Early detection & specialist referral during routine developmental surveillance.
* **MSW (醫務社會工作者) & SWD (社會福利署)**: Assessment for Disability Allowance due to severe visual impairment.
* **SEN Support in Schools**: High-contrast materials, large print textbooks, preferential front-row seating, assistive technology.
* **Psychosocial Care**: Clinical psychology for coping with visible difference/bullying. Connection with NGOs (e.g., Hong Kong Albinism Association).

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Concerns in OCA
* **Medical Neglect (醫療忽視)**: Parental failure to enforce strict sun protection.
* **Red Flag**: Child presenting with severe, recurrent sunburns requires urgent MSW/social care evaluation.
* **Consequence of Neglect**: Cutaneous malignancies presenting at extremely young ages (<20 years).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Cutaneous Complications of OCA
* **Premature Skin Aging**: Solar elastosis, pachyderma.
* **Actinic Keratoses (光化性角化症)**: Pre-malignant lesions.
* **Skin Cancers**: Extremely high risk of early-onset **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) (鱗狀細胞癌)** & **Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC) (基底細胞癌)**. Melanomas occur but often amelanotic (lacking pigment).

### Ophthalmic Complications & Prognosis
* **Permanent Visual Impairment (視力障礙)**: Usually legally blind; extremely poor stereopsis.
* **Prognosis**: Visual acuity & nystagmus remain stable throughout life (do not progressively deteriorate).
* **Systemic Prognosis**: Normal life expectancy (unless syndromic variants like CHS/HPS or fatal metastatic skin cancers).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls
* Viva Question: Why is vision poor in albinism? Answer: Due to foveal hypoplasia (lack of detail vision) and optic pathway misrouting (impaired binocular vision/stereopsis), as melanin is strictly required for normal embryological development of these structures.
* Trap: Believing OCA1a and OCA1b are clinically identical throughout life. Pearl: OCA1a has zero tyrosinase activity (hair/skin remain pure white forever), whereas OCA1b has residual activity (develops yellow/blonde hair and slight pigmentation with age).
* Pitfall: Reassuring a bleeding or bruising child with albinism. Explain why. You must urgently exclude Hermansky-Pudlak Syndrome (HPS), a syndromic albinism with platelet dysfunction; likewise, recurrent pyogenic infections strongly suggest Chediak-Higashi Syndrome (CHS).
* Pitfall: Underestimating UV risks in Hong Kong. Explain why. Assuming HK sun exposure is negligible is dangerous; HK's UV index frequently reaches "high" or "extreme" levels, mandating rigorous, year-round sun protection for OCA patients.
* Trap: Confusing Ocular Albinism (OA) with Oculocutaneous Albinism (OCA). Pearl: OCA affects skin, hair, and eyes (Autosomal Recessive). OA affects ONLY the eyes (X-linked Recessive).
