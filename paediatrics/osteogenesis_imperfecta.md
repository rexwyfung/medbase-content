# Osteogenesis Imperfecta (成骨不全症 / 玻璃骨)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Osteogenesis Imperfecta
* Genetic connective tissue disorder → extreme bone fragility (骨脆) & susceptibility to low-impact fractures (骨折).

### Local Epidemiology of OI in Hong Kong
* Incidence: ~1:15,000 - 1:20,000 live births globally.
* HK HA Context: Managed centrally by specialized pediatric metabolic and bone clinics (e.g., Duchess of Kent Children's Hospital). 

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Genetic Mutations in Osteogenesis Imperfecta
* Most commonly **Autosomal Dominant (AD)** inheritance.
* Mutations in **COL1A1** or **COL1A2** genes.

### Pathophysiology of Type I Collagen Defect
* Creates defect in **Type I collagen (骨膠原)**.
* **Type I (Mild):** Quantitative defect (↓ normal collagen).
* **Types II-IV (Severe):** Qualitative defect (abnormal collagen structure).
* Type 1 collagen = basic scaffold for bone, sclera, dentin, & ligaments → widespread connective tissue fragility and osteopenia.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Clinical Presentation by OI Type
* Varies significantly based on Sillence Classification.
* **Type I (Mild):** Presents in childhood. Fractures from trivial falls (e.g., stepping off a curb). Normal lifespan.
* **Type II (Perinatal Lethal):** Fetuses present with fractured limbs on anomaly scan or born with crushed ribcages. Fatal in utero or early infancy.
* **Type III (Severe):** Progressive deforming. Multiple fractures, severe kyphoscoliosis, wheel-chair bound.
* **Type IV (Moderate):** Intermediate severity. Bowing of long bones, moderate fracture rate.

### Misdiagnosis Presentation
* Commonly mistaken for **Non-Accidental Injury (NAI / 虐待兒童)** upon first presentation to HA Emergency Departments.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Skeletal Signs of Osteogenesis Imperfecta
* Recurrent low-velocity fractures (骨折).
* Progressive bowing of long bones (彎曲腿).
* Short stature (矮小症).
* Severe scoliosis/kyphoscoliosis (脊柱側彎).
* Joint hypermobility.

### Extraskeletal Signs of Osteogenesis Imperfecta
* **Blue sclerae (藍鞏膜):** Thin sclera reveals underlying blue choroidal veins.
* **Dentinogenesis imperfecta (牙質發育不全):** Amber/translucent, easily crumbling teeth.
* Progressive conductive/sensorineural hearing loss (聽力受損) (due to fractured ossicles/nerve compression, typically in 20s-30s).
* Easy bruising & thin skin.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing Osteogenesis Imperfecta from Child Abuse (NAI)
* Must be definitively distinguished from NAI.
* **Blue sclerae, Wormian bones (縫間骨)** on skull X-ray, and **Dentinogenesis Imperfecta** are virtually pathognomonic for OI over abuse.

### Distinguishing Osteogenesis Imperfecta from Rickets
* Rickets (佝僂病) features abnormal bone profile (↓ Phos, ↑ Alk Phos, variable Ca).
* OI features completely **normal** Calcium, Phosphate, and Alk Phos profiles.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Differential Diagnosis of Childhood Fractures and Bow Legs
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Accidental Injury (NAI)** | Inconsistent history, delayed presentation, metaphyseal corner fractures, rib fractures, normal sclerae/teeth. |
| **Rickets / Osteomalacia (佝僂病)** | Dietary deficiency history, ↑ Alk Phos, ↓ Phosphate, widened epiphyses/cupping on X-ray. |
| **Juvenile Idiopathic Osteoporosis** | Onset pre-puberty, back/bone pain, NO blue sclerae, NO family history of OI. |
| **Hypophosphatasia** | ↓ Alk Phos (diagnostic), premature tooth loss, defective mineralization. |
| **Blount Disease** | Isolated progressive tibia vara (proximal medial tibial epiphysis beaking), mostly in obese/African descent. |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside and Blood Investigations for OI
* **Bloods (Bone Profile):** Normal Calcium, Phosphate, Alk Phos, and PTH (Contrasts sharply with Rickets).
* **Vitamin D level:** Check baseline; co-existing deficiency needs correction to optimize bone health.

### Imaging Investigations for OI
* **Skeletal Survey:** Essential for mapping fractures/deformities and ruling out NAI.
* **X-ray Findings:** Osteopenia, old uniting fractures with massive hyperplastic callus formation, **Wormian bones (縫間骨)** in skull sutures, "codfish" vertebrae (compression fractures), bowing of long bones.
* **DEXA Scan:** To formally quantify bone mineral density (BMD) / osteopenia.

### Special Diagnostic Investigations for OI
* **Genetic Testing:** DNA sequencing of **COL1A1 / COL1A2** genes for unequivocal diagnosis (Gold Standard).

## 8. Management
### Acute Pharmacological & Surgical Management
* **Analgesia:** Adequate pain relief for acute fractures.
* **Orthopaedic Surgery:** Internal fixation of fractures; avoid prolonged cast immobilization to prevent rapid disuse osteopenia.

### Chronic Pharmacological Management (HA Protocol)
* **IV Bisphosphonates (雙磷酸鹽):** Cyclic IV infusions (e.g., Pamidronate or Zoledronic acid).
* *Mechanism:* Suppresses osteoclasts → wildly reduces fracture rates, ↑ bone density, and relieves chronic bone pain.

### Chronic Surgical & MDT Management
* **Orthopaedic rodding:** Fassier-Duval telescoping rods placed down the medullary canal of long bones to prevent bowing & provide internal scaffolding.
* **Dental & ENT:** Close monitoring for crumbling teeth (pediatric dentistry) and hearing deficits (audiology/ENT).
* **Physiotherapy:** Maximize mobility, muscle strengthening, prevent contractures.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team and Social Work Involvement
* Deep Medical Social Worker (MSW) involvement.
* Confirmed diagnosis officially shields family from continuous Social Welfare Department (SWD) investigations for suspected child abuse.
* Patient support groups (e.g., HK OI associations) for psychosocial peer support.
* Special schooling / integration support via Education Bureau if wheel-chair bound.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Protocols for Unexplained Fractures
* **MANDATORY HA Protocol:** Any child with unexplained fractures requires full safeguarding screen (Skeletal survey, ophthalmology review, SWD notification).
* OI must be formally excluded, but true abuse mathematically far outnumbers OI.
* **Crucial Rule:** If OI testing is pending, standard HA child protection protocols (SWD notification, keeping child safely in hospital via Place of Safety Order if needed) MUST proceed unaltered.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Complications of Osteogenesis Imperfecta
* Unrelenting chronic bone pain.
* Severe physical disability, immobility, wheel-chair reliance.
* Cardiopulmonary failure (2° to severe kyphoscoliosis destroying chest capacity - restrictive lung disease).
* Profound hearing loss.

### Prognosis of Osteogenesis Imperfecta
* Type II is universally fatal (perinatal).
* Type I has a normal lifespan but requires lifelong fracture management.
* Types III/IV have reduced lifespan 2° to respiratory complications from severe chest wall deformities.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls
* Trap: Assuming blue sclera completely confirms OI. Pearl: Sometimes totally normal neonates have slightly blue sclera because it is naturally thin in infancy; in OI, it must persist.
* Viva Question: How do the lab results differentiate OI from Rickets? Answer: OI features a genetic structural defect, so Calcium, Phosphate, and Alk Phos are completely normal. Rickets (nutritional) shows low Phos, high Alk Phos, and sometimes low Calcium.
* Pitfall: Discharging an infant with unexplained fractures because "it might be OI". Suspected NAI is a medical emergency; HA safeguarding protocols and SWD notification must proceed while genetics are pending to ensure the child's safety.
* Viva Question: What is the classic X-ray finding in the skull of an OI patient? Answer: Wormian bones (extra bone pieces within the cranial sutures).
* Trap: Using cast immobilization for too long in OI patients. Pearl: Prolonged immobilization rapidly exacerbates disuse osteopenia; early mobilization or internal rodding is strongly preferred to maintain bone mass.
