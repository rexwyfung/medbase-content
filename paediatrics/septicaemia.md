# Paediatric Sepsis & Septicaemia (小兒敗血症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Sepsis & Septicaemia Definition
* **Sepsis (敗血症)**: Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by dysregulated host response to infection (Sepsis-3 criteria adaptation).
* **Septicaemia (敗血症)**: Presence & active multiplication of pathogenic bacteria in bloodstream.
* **Severe Sepsis**: Sepsis + cardiovascular dysfunction OR Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) OR ≥2 organ dysfunctions.

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology & Pathogens
* **Neonates (<1 month)**: 
    * Group B *Streptococcus* (GBS)
    * *Escherichia coli* (*E. coli*)
    * *Listeria monocytogenes*
* **Infants & Children (>1 month)**: 
    * *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (肺炎鏈球菌)
    * *Neisseria meningitidis* (腦膜炎雙球菌)
    * *Staphylococcus aureus* (金黃葡萄球菌)
    * Group A *Streptococcus* (GAS)
* **Epidemiological Shift (HK)**: *Haemophilus influenzae* type b (Hib) is extremely rare due to widespread HK Childhood Immunisation Programme (HKCIP).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiological Mechanism of Sepsis
* **Trigger**: Pathogen-Associated Molecular Patterns (**PAMPs**, e.g., Gram-ve endotoxin/LPS) activate innate immune system via Toll-like receptors.
* **Cytokine Storm**: Massive release of pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6) → widespread systemic inflammation.
* **Haemodynamic Collapse**:
    * **Vasodilation**: Endothelial NO release → ↓ Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR) → profound hypotension ("warm shock").
    * **Capillary Leak**: Endothelial damage → fluid extravasation into interstitium → oedema, ARDS, intravascular volume depletion.
    * **Myocardial Depression**: Cytokine-mediated ↓ cardiac output (CO) → "cold shock" (more common in children than adults).
* **Coagulopathy**: Cytokines activate tissue factor + inhibit fibrinolysis → Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (**DIC**).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Non-Specific Signs
* Fever (發燒) OR Hypothermia (hypothermia more common in neonates)
* Lethargy & irritability
* Poor feeding (poor suckling in infants)
* Unexplained tachycardia (心跳過速)
* Tachypnoea (呼吸急促)

### Signs of Cold Shock (Common in Children)
* ↓ CO, ↑ SVR
* Cold/mottled peripheries
* Prolonged Capillary Refill Time (**CRT**) > 3 seconds
* Weak/thready peripheral pulses
* Oliguria (↓ urine output)
* Altered mental status / depressed conscious level

### Signs of Warm Shock
* ↑ CO, ↓ SVR
* Bounding pulses
* Flash CRT (< 1 second)
* Warm, flushed peripheries
* Wide pulse pressure

### Pathognomonic Red Flag Signs
* **Non-blanching petechial/purpuric rash**: Highly indicative of **Meningococcal Septicaemia (腦膜炎雙球菌敗血症)**.
* **Bulging fontanelle / Nuchal rigidity**: Indicates concurrent **Meningitis (腦膜炎)**.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Sepsis vs. Simple Viral Illness
| Feature | Simple Viral URTI | Paediatric Sepsis (小兒敗血症) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Appearance** | Alert, consolable, interactive | "Toxic", lethargic, inappropriate cry |
| **Perfusion** | Normal (CRT < 2s), pink skin | Abnormal (CRT > 3s), mottled/pale/ashen |
| **Heart Rate** | Tachycardia proportional to fever | Out-of-proportion tachycardia, persists post-antipyretics |
| **Mental Status** | Normal | Irritable, confused, drowsy, unresponsive |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Key Differential Diagnoses
| Condition | Differentiating Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bacterial Sepsis** | Fever, tachycardia, prolonged CRT, altered mental state. Pathogen varies by age. |
| **Meningococcal Septicaemia** | Rapid onset, shock, **non-blanching purpuric rash**. Can cause death within hours. Must not miss! |
| **Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)** | Staph/Strep origin. Fever, shock, **diffuse macular erythroderma** (sunburn-like), desquamation (late). |
| **Anaphylaxis** | Sudden onset, urticaria, wheeze, stridor, angioedema, hypotension. History of allergen exposure. |
| **Cardiogenic Shock** | Hepatomegaly, gallop rhythm, ↑ JVP, murmurs. Often secondary to myocarditis or congenital heart disease. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Immediate Bedside Investigations
* **DO NOT DELAY TREATMENT FOR INVESTIGATIONS.**
* **Bedside Glucose**: Check immediately. Hypoglycaemia (低血糖) is common & rapidly fatal in septic kids.
* **Blood Gas (VBG/ABG)**: 
    * **Lactate**: > 2 mmol/L → tissue hypoperfusion.
    * **pH/Base Excess**: Severe metabolic acidosis.

### Gold Standard Sepsis Bloods (Within 1 Hour)
* **Blood Cultures (血培養)**: Draw BEFORE antibiotics (but NEVER delay antibiotics if access is difficult).
* **CBC (Complete Blood Count)**: ↑ or ↓ WBC, ↓ Platelets (indicates DIC).
* **U&E (Renal Function)**: Assess Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) & electrolyte derangement.
* **LFT (Liver Function)**: Baseline organ function, hypoalbuminaemia (capillary leak).
* **Inflammatory Markers**: ↑ CRP (C-Reactive Protein), ↑ Procalcitonin (PCT).
* **Coagulation Screen**: PT/APTT, Fibrinogen, D-Dimer (Assess for DIC).

### Microbiological & Special Investigations
* **Urine Culture**: Clean catch, catheter, or suprapubic aspirate (SPA).
* **CSF Culture (Lumbar Puncture / 腰椎穿刺)**: ONLY if haemodynamically stable & NO coagulopathy/raised ICP.
* **Sputum/Swabs**: Throat swab, wound swab if clinically indicated.

### Continuous Monitoring Requirements
* Continuous ECG monitoring
* Continuous SpO2 (Pulse Oximetry)
* Frequent non-invasive BP (or arterial line if in PICU)
* Strict fluid balance: Insert indwelling urinary catheter (Goal output: > 1 ml/kg/hr).

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute "Golden Hour" Resuscitation
* **High-Flow Oxygen**: 100% O2 via non-rebreather mask (15 L/min).
* **Vascular Access**: Secure IV within 5 mins. If failed x2 or >5 mins → Insert **Intraosseous (IO) needle** immediately.

### Empirical Antibiotic Therapy (HA Protocols)
* *Administer within 1 hour of recognition.*
* **Neonates (<1 month)**: IV Ampicillin + IV Gentamicin (or Cefotaxime). (Ampicillin covers *Listeria*).
* **Children (>1 month)**: IV Ceftriaxone (頭孢曲松) or IV Cefotaxime.
* **If MRSA suspected**: Add IV Vancomycin.
* **If intra-abdominal source**: Add IV Metronidazole.

### Aggressive Fluid Resuscitation
* **1st Bolus**: 10-20 ml/kg of 0.9% NaCl (Normal Saline) given rapidly (over 5-10 mins).
* **Reassess**: HR, CRT, BP, conscious level. Check for fluid overload (hepatomegaly, basal crepitations).
* **Subsequent Boluses**: Repeat 10-20 ml/kg up to total 40-60 ml/kg in 1st hour if shock persists & no crackles/hepatomegaly.

### Inotropic / Vasopressor Support
* *Indication*: Fluid-refractory shock (persists post 40-60 ml/kg fluids).
* **1st Line**: Peripheral IV/IO Adrenaline (Epinephrine) or Noradrenaline infusion.
* **Action**: Call Paediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU) immediately.

### Definitive & Chronic Management
* **Source Control**: Surgical drainage of abscess, removal of infected central venous catheters (CVC), debridement of necrotic tissue.
* **Antimicrobial Stewardship**: De-escalate broad-spectrum antibiotics to targeted therapy once culture sensitivities are available.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **PICU (Paediatric Intensive Care Unit)**: Mandatory for fluid-refractory shock, need for inotropes, or multi-organ failure.
* **Infectious Disease (ID) Specialist**: For complex, resistant, or atypical pathogens.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) & Clinical Psychologist**: 
    * Provide crisis intervention & grief counselling for parents facing unexpected critical illness/death.
    * Support for long-term rehabilitation if complications occur (e.g., amputations, neurological deficits).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Public Health & Infection Control
* **Statutory Notification**: Notify Hong Kong Department of Health (DH) for notifiable diseases (e.g., *Neisseria meningitidis*, *Streptococcus pneumoniae*).
* **Contact Tracing & Prophylaxis**: Close contacts of Meningococcal cases MUST receive urgent chemoprophylaxis (Oral Rifampicin or Ciprofloxacin).
* **Isolation**: Droplet precautions for suspected Meningococcus or Haemophilus.

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) / Safeguarding
* Always assess for delayed presentation. If parents delayed seeking care despite obvious severe illness, investigate for medical neglect.
* Look for unexplained bruises that do not fit the typical purpuric rash pattern (e.g., linear bruises, varying ages of bruising).

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Major Clinical Complications
* **MODS**: Multi-Organ Dysfunction Syndrome.
* **DIC**: Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation → widespread microthrombi & massive haemorrhage.
* **Purpura Fulminans**: Severe microvascular thrombosis → ischaemic necrosis of extremities → requires limb amputations.
* **ARDS**: Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome.
* **AKI**: Acute Kidney Injury secondary to prolonged hypoperfusion.
* **Neurological**: Sensorineural hearing loss, seizures, developmental delay (if concurrent meningitis).

### Prognostic Factors
* Prognosis is entirely time-dependent. Survival drops linearly for every hour delay in administering the 1st dose of appropriate IV antibiotics and adequate fluid resuscitation.
* Mortality remains high (approx 5-10% in developed settings like HK), higher if septic shock present.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls

* Trap: Waiting for a lumbar puncture (LP) before giving antibiotics to a child with a purpuric rash and shock. Pearl: NEVER delay antibiotics in suspected sepsis/meningitis. If the child is in shock or has a coagulopathy (purpura), an LP is absolutely CONTRAINDICATED as it can cause fatal coning or bleeding. Give IV Ceftriaxone immediately.
* Viva Question: What is the most reliable clinical sign of shock in a child? Answer: Tachycardia and prolonged capillary refill time (>3 seconds).
* Pitfall: Relying on blood pressure to diagnose shock in paediatrics. Hypotension is a very LATE and pre-terminal sign in children, as their robust cardiovascular system compensates by ↑ HR and ↑ SVR until they suddenly collapse.
* Viva Question: Which fluid is preferred for initial bolus resuscitation in paediatric septic shock? Answer: Isotonic crystalloids, specifically 0.9% Normal Saline (or Plasmalyte/Hartmann's). 10-20 ml/kg bolus over 5-10 minutes.
* Trap: Assuming a normal temperature rules out sepsis. Pearl: Neonates and severely immunocompromised children often present with hypothermia (< 36°C) rather than fever during severe septic episodes.
