# Hodgkin & Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (霍奇金及非霍奇金淋巴瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Malignant clonal expansions of lymphocytes (B or T cells). Divided broadly into Hodgkin Lymphoma (HL) and Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHL).
- **Local Epidemiology**: Lymphoma is the 3rd most common childhood cancer (after Leukemias and Brain Tumors). HL peaks in adolescents; NHL often occurs slightly younger.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Hodgkin Lymphoma (HL)**: Highly associated with previous Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) infection. Malignant **Reed-Sternberg cells** (giant, binucleated, "owl-eye" B-cells) hide amidst a massive inflammatory background of totally normal reactive cells.
- **Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHL)**: Disseminates widely and grows extremely rapidly. 
  - *Burkitt Lymphoma*: B-cell origin. Driven by c-myc translocation t(8;14). Fastest growing human tumor. Endemic form associated with EBV (jaw mass in Africa); Sporadic form (abdominal mass).
  - *Lymphoblastic Lymphoma*: Usually T-cell origin. Basically a solid-tissue version of Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia. Often presents as a massive anterior mediastinal mass.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **HL**: A 15-year-old with a painless, rubbery lump in the lower neck that has been slowly growing for 2 months, complaining they sweat completely through their bedsheets at night.
- **NHL**: A 7-year-old with a rapidly expanding, massive surgical abdomen, vomiting, and acute kidney injury occurring over just a few days.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Painless Lymphadenopathy**: The classic defining feature (supraclavicular or cervical).
- **"B Symptoms" (Classic for Hodgkin)**: 
  - Unexplained Fever >38°C for 3 consecutive days.
  - Drenching night sweats (requires changing clothes).
  - Unexplained loss of >10% body weight in 6 months.
- **Mediastinal Mass (T-cell Lymphoblastic NHL)**: Causes SVC syndrome (facial plethora, engorged neck veins) and severe orthopnea (respiratory distress when lying flat as the tumor crushes the airway).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from reactive viral lymph nodes: Reactive nodes are tender, small (<2 cm), and go away in 2-3 weeks. Lymphoma nodes are rock hard/rubbery, completely painless, and steadily grow. Any enlarged Supraclavicular node is malignant until proven otherwise.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Epstein-Barr Virus (Infectious Mononucleosis) - nodes are tender and self-limit.
- Tuberculosis (TB lymphadenitis) - often matted, may have a sinus tract.
- Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia.

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Imaging**: 
  - CXR (critical to rule out mediastinal mass before giving any anesthesia!).
  - PET-CT for highly accurate functional staging.
- **Definitive Diagnosis**: **Excisional Lymph Node Biopsy**. Never do a fine needle aspirate (FNA); you need to see the entire lymph node architecture to diagnose lymphoma properly.
  - *Hodgkin*: **Reed-Sternberg cells**.
  - *Burkitt*: **"Starry-sky"** histological appearance (rapidly dividing cells interspersed with macrophages eating dead tumor cells).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Hodgkin Lymphoma**: Exceptional cure rates (>90%). Uses multi-agent chemotherapy (e.g., ABVD or OEPA) followed by low-dose local radiation to the affected nodes.
- **Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma**: NEVER radiation. Treated exclusively with intense, fast-paced systemic chemotherapy. 
- **CRITICAL ONCOLOGY EMERGENCY**: **Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS)**. 
  - Especially in Burkitt Lymphoma. The chemo causes massive, instant death of millions of tumor cells.
  - The cells burst open, dumping massive amounts of Potassium, Phosphate, and Uric Acid into the blood.
  - This causes acute renal failure and fatal ventricular arrhythmias. 
  - **Prevention**: Hyperhydration, Allopurinol, or Rasburicase BEFORE starting chemo.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Fertility preservation (sperm/egg banking) must be strongly discussed with adolescents before starting intense sterilizing chemotherapy/radiation.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Superior Vena Cava Syndrome (airway occlusion).
- Late Effects of therapy: Secondary cancers (e.g., Breast cancer in 30s after receiving chest radiation for HL at age 15), Cardiomyopathy (from Anthracycline chemo drugs).

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Laying a child with an anterior mediastinal mass flat on their back in the MRI scanner. The heavy tumor will crush their trachea while they are sedated, causing immediate lethal asphyxia.
- **Viva Q**: "A biopsy of a rapidly growing abdominal mass reveals a 'starry sky' appearance. What genetic translocation drives this cancer?" Answer: Burkitt Lymphoma, driven by the t(8;14) translocation which moves the c-myc oncogene next to the hyperactive Ig heavy chain promoter, causing explosive growth.
