# Hodgkin & Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (霍奇金及非霍奇金淋巴瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition
- **Malignant clonal expansions** of **lymphocytes (淋巴細胞)** (B or T cells).
- Broadly divided into **Hodgkin Lymphoma (HL) (霍奇金淋巴瘤)** and **Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHL) (非霍奇金淋巴瘤)**.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
- **Lymphoma (淋巴瘤)** is the 3rd most common childhood cancer (after Leukemias and Brain Tumors).
- **HL** peaks in **adolescents**.
- **NHL** occurs more frequently in **childhood** (younger overall demographic than HL).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Hodgkin Lymphoma
- Highly associated with prior **Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) (人類皰疹病毒第四型)** infection.
- Characterized by pathognomonic **Reed-Sternberg cells (里德-斯伯格細胞)** (giant, binucleated, "owl-eye" B-cells).
- Malignant cells hide within a massive **inflammatory background** of completely normal reactive cells.

### Pathophysiology & Genetics of Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma
- Disseminates widely and grows **extremely rapidly**.
- **Burkitt Lymphoma (伯基特淋巴瘤)**: 
  - B-cell origin. Fastest growing human tumor. 
  - Driven by **c-myc translocation t(8;14)**. 
  - **Endemic form**: Associated with EBV (jaw mass in Africa).
  - **Sporadic form**: Associated with massive abdominal mass.
- **Lymphoblastic Lymphoma (淋巴母細胞淋巴瘤)**: 
  - Usually T-cell origin. 
  - Essentially a solid-tissue variant of **Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)**. 
  - Often presents as a massive **anterior mediastinal mass (前縱隔腫塊)**.

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Classic Presentation of Hodgkin Lymphoma
- A 15-year-old with a **painless, rubbery lump** in the lower neck.
- Slowly growing for 2 months.
- Complaining of **drenching night sweats (夜間盜汗)** (sweating completely through bedsheets).

### Classic Presentation of Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma
- A 7-year-old with a **rapidly expanding massive surgical abdomen**.
- **Vomiting**, pain, or **acute kidney injury (AKI) (急性腎損傷)** occurring over just a few days.
- Alternatively presents with rapid onset **respiratory distress (呼吸窘迫)** due to a compressing **mediastinal mass (縱隔腫塊)**.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Signs of Hodgkin Lymphoma
- **Painless Lymphadenopathy (無痛性淋巴結腫大)**: Classic defining feature, nodes are much larger/firmer than benign nodes (usually **cervical or supraclavicular**).
- **"B Symptoms" (B期症狀)** (uncommon but critical for staging):
  - Unexplained **Fever >38°C** for 3 consecutive days.
  - **Drenching night sweats** (requires changing clothes).
  - Unexplained **weight loss >10%** in 6 months.
- **Pruritus (瘙癢)**: Generalized unexplained itching.

### Signs of Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma
- **Mediastinal Mass (T-cell NHL)**: 
  - Causes **Superior Vena Cava (SVC) Syndrome (上腔靜脈綜合症)** → facial plethora, engorged neck veins, facial swelling.
  - **Severe orthopnea**: Respiratory distress when lying flat (tumor crushes the airway).
- **Abdominal Mass (B-cell NHL)**: 
  - Pain, intestinal obstruction, palpable mass.
  - Can cause **intussusception (腸套疊)** if the ileum is involved.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Lymphoma vs. Reactive Viral Lymphadenopathy
| Feature | Reactive Viral Node | Lymphoma Node |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pain** | Tender (+ve) | **Painless (-ve)** |
| **Consistency** | Soft, mobile | **Rock hard/rubbery, firm** |
| **Size** | Small (<2 cm) | **Large, steadily growing** |
| **Duration** | Resolves in 2-3 weeks | **Persistent >4-6 weeks** |

- **CRITICAL RULE**: Any enlarged **Supraclavicular node (鎖骨上淋巴結)** is malignant until proven otherwise.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

### Common Differentials for Lymphadenopathy
- **Infectious Mononucleosis (傳染性單核細胞增多症)** (EBV): Nodes are tender, self-limiting, associated with pharyngitis and splenomegaly.
- **Tuberculosis (TB) Lymphadenitis (結核性淋巴結炎)**: Often matted nodes, may develop a sinus tract/caseating necrosis. High suspicion in HK.
- **Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL) (急性淋巴細胞白血病)**: Presence of circulating blasts, significant bone marrow failure (anemia, bruising, neutropenia).
- **Bacterial Adenitis**: Acute onset, hot, red, highly tender (e.g., Staphylococcus/Streptococcus).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)

### Imaging Studies
- **CXR (胸部X光)**: 
  - **CRITICAL** to rule out mediastinal mass before giving ANY anesthesia or sedation!
- **Ultrasound (超聲波)**: Assesses node architecture and solid abdominal masses.
- **PET-CT**: Gold standard for highly accurate **functional staging** and tracking treatment response.

### Hematology & Biochemistry Bloods
- **CBP & Smear**: Check for circulating blasts, cytopenias indicating marrow infiltration.
- **Renal & Liver Function**: Urate, K+, PO4-, Ca2+, LDH (markers of rapid cell turnover and Tumor Lysis Syndrome).
- **ESR / CRP**: Often elevated.

### Special & Definitive Diagnostic Tests
- **Excisional Lymph Node Biopsy (淋巴結切除活檢)**: 
  - **Definitive Diagnosis**.
  - **NEVER** do a fine needle aspirate (FNA); complete node architecture is required to grade lymphoma properly.
  - **Histology**: 
    - HL → **Reed-Sternberg cells**. 
    - Burkitt → **"Starry-sky" (星空)** appearance (macrophages eating dead tumor cells amidst rapidly dividing cells).
- **Bone Marrow Biopsy**: Essential for staging to check for dissemination.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Oncology Emergencies
- **Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS) (腫瘤溶解綜合症)**: 
  - Massive, instant death of millions of tumor cells (especially in Burkitt).
  - Dumps massive amounts of **K+, PO4-, and Uric Acid** into blood.
  - Causes AKI and fatal ventricular arrhythmias.
  - **Prevention/Treatment**: **Hyperhydration**, **Allopurinol (別嘌醇)**, or **Rasburicase (拉布立酶)** BEFORE starting chemo.
- **SVC / Airway Obstruction**: 
  - **NEVER lay flat**. Rapid PICU assessment required.

### Chronic Management Protocols
- **Hodgkin Lymphoma**:
  - **Chemotherapy (化療)**: Multi-agent (e.g., ABVD or OEPA).
  - **Radiotherapy (放療)**: Low-dose involved-field local radiation to affected nodes.
  - **Cure Rate**: Exceptional (>80-90%).
- **Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma**:
  - **Chemotherapy**: Intense, fast-paced systemic chemotherapy.
  - **Radiotherapy**: **NEVER** routinely used (due to widely disseminated nature).
  - **Targeted Therapy**: **Rituximab (利妥昔單抗)** (Anti-CD20) often used for B-cell NHL.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)

### Fertility Preservation & Psychosocial
- **Fertility Preservation (生育能力保存)**: Sperm/egg banking MUST be strongly discussed with adolescents before starting intense sterilizing chemotherapy/radiation.
- **Medical Social Worker (MSW)**: Vital for navigating compassionate funding for targeted therapies (e.g., Rituximab) not fully covered by HA standard fees.
- **Clinical Psychologist**: Assisting with altered body image (alopecia, cushingoid appearance from steroids) and treatment compliance.
- **Hospital School Support**: Ensuring continuity of education during prolonged inpatient stays.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Safeguarding Considerations
- **Treatment Refusal**: Parents refusing life-saving chemotherapy for alternative/traditional medicine. Untreated lymphoma is fatal; this requires an urgent **Care Order** application via SWD to act in the child's best interests.
- **Medical Neglect**: Failure to attend mandatory chemo appointments or ignoring signs of neutropenic sepsis.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications
- **Neutropenic Sepsis (中性粒細胞缺乏性敗血症)**: Absolute emergency requiring immediate IV empirical broad-spectrum antibiotics.
- **Airway Occlusion**: Due to rapidly expanding mediastinal mass.

### Late Effects & Prognosis
- **Secondary Cancers (繼發性癌症)**: High risk (e.g., Breast cancer in 30s after receiving chest radiation for HL at age 15).
- **Cardiomyopathy (心肌病)**: Due to cumulative doses of **Anthracycline (蒽環類)** chemo drugs.
- **Infertility/Endocrine Dysfunction**: From high-dose alkylating agents or radiation.
- **Overall Prognosis**: Very high cure rates overall, but late effects significantly impact long-term survivorship.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Laying a child with an anterior mediastinal mass flat on their back in the MRI scanner.
* Pearl: The heavy tumor will crush their trachea while they are sedated, causing immediate lethal asphyxia. Always image upright or with extreme caution if a large mediastinal mass is present.
* Viva Question: A biopsy of a rapidly growing abdominal mass reveals a 'starry sky' appearance. What genetic translocation drives this cancer?
* Answer: Burkitt Lymphoma, driven by the t(8;14) translocation which moves the c-myc oncogene next to the hyperactive Ig heavy chain promoter, causing explosive growth.
* Pitfall: Performing a Fine Needle Aspirate (FNA) on a suspicious lymph node to diagnose lymphoma. You will miss the diagnosis because FNA destroys the structural architecture needed to classify lymphomas properly; an excisional biopsy is mandatory.
* Viva Question: What are the classic metabolic derangements seen in Tumor Lysis Syndrome?
* Answer: Hyperkalemia, hyperphosphatemia, hyperuricemia, and hypocalcemia.
* Trap: Assuming night sweats and weight loss with a persistent cough is always Tuberculosis in Hong Kong.
* Pearl: Always keep Hodgkin Lymphoma high on your differential for "B symptoms" with a mediastinal mass or persistent lymphadenopathy, even in a TB-endemic region.
