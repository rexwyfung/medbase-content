# Cerebral Palsy (CP) (腦性麻痺)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition (臨床定義)
* **Permanent, non-progressive (非進行性)** disorder of movement & posture → activity limitation.
* Attributed to non-progressive disturbances (injury/malformation) in the developing fetal/infant brain.
* Motor disorders are frequently accompanied by disturbances of cognition, communication, vision, perception, behaviour, & seizure disorders (癲癇).

### Local Epidemiology (本地流行病學)
* Most common cause of severe physical disability in childhood.
* **Incidence:** ~2 per 1000 live births globally & in Hong Kong.
* **Prematurity (早產):** Risk ↑ exponentially with ↓ gestational age (<28 weeks) & ↓ birth weight.
* In HK, routine developmental surveillance at **Maternal & Child Health Centres (MCHC) (母嬰健康院)** is primary detection route → triggers referral to **Child Assessment Centre (CAC) (兒童體能智力測驗中心)**.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology (病理生理學)
* Brain lesion is **static** (static encephalopathy).
* Clinical manifestations (e.g., spasticity (痙攣), contractures (關節攣縮)) **evolve** as the child grows & nervous system matures.

### Aetiology: Timing of Injury (受傷時間分類)
* **Prenatal (產前) (80%):** Most common. Cortical migration disorders, structural brain malformations, intrauterine infections (TORCH), cerebrovascular events (fetal stroke), genetic syndromes.
* **Perinatal (圍產期) (10%):** **Hypoxic-Ischaemic Encephalopathy (HIE) (缺氧缺血性腦病)** at term, neonatal stroke, severe neonatal jaundice → Kernicterus (核黃疸).
* **Postnatal (產後) (10%):** Brain injury <2 years old. Meningitis, encephalitis, traumatic brain injury (TBI), near-drowning, symptomatic hypoglycaemia.

### Prematurity & White Matter Injury (早產與白質損傷)
* Preterm brains are highly susceptible to **Periventricular Leukomalacia (PVL) (腦室周圍白質軟化)**.
* Ischaemic damage/venous infarction affects descending pyramidal tracts near lateral ventricles → classically causes **Spastic Diplegia (痙攣性雙癱)**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Infant Presentation (嬰兒期早期表現)
* **Oromotor Incoordination:** Poor feeding, poor latch, gagging, vomiting.
* **Tone Abnormalities:** Initial hypotonia (floppy) → later hypertonia/spasticity (stiff).
* **Abnormal Posture:** Extensor posturing (opisthotonus).
* **Hand Preference (慣用手):** Handedness <18 months is a major red flag for **Hemiplegia (偏癱)**.

### Delayed Motor Milestones (運動發展遲緩)
* Not sitting unsupported by 9 months.
* Not walking by 18 months.
* Abnormal gait patterns (e.g., toe-walking, scissoring gait).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Non-Progressive Nature (非進行性特徵)
* **CP is static.** Child slowly gains milestones or plateaus.
* **Regression (能力倒退):** Loss of previously acquired milestones is **NEVER** CP → indicates a progressive neurodegenerative/metabolic disorder.

### Red Flags for Alternative Diagnoses (替代診斷危險信號)
* Absence of perinatal/prenatal risk factors.
* Strong family history of progressive neurological disorders.
* Development of focal, progressive neurological signs.
* Atypical dysmorphism not fitting CP.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Progressive Neurological Disorders (進行性神經系統疾病)
* **Leukodystrophies (腦白質營養不良).**
* **Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA) (脊髓性肌萎縮症).**

### Spinal Cord & Structural Lesions (脊髓與結構性病變)
* Tethered cord syndrome (脊髓栓系綜合症).
* Spinal cord tumours.

### Inborn Errors of Metabolism & Genetic Syndromes (遺傳代謝病與基因綜合症)
* Aminoacidopathies, mitochondrial diseases.
* Rett syndrome, Angelman syndrome (presents with severe delay, ataxia, epilepsy).

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside & Clinical Evaluation (床邊與臨床評估)
* Serial neurological exams evaluating posture, tone (velocity-dependent catch, clasp-knife), deep tendon reflexes (↑), Babinski sign (+ve).
* Full developmental assessment (Motor, Vision, Hearing, Speech/Language).

### Neuroimaging (神經影像學)
* **MRI Brain (磁力共振掃描):** Gold standard. Recommended for all suspected CP to confirm structural abnormality & rule out progressive lesions.
  * *Diplegia:* PVL.
  * *Hemiplegia:* Middle cerebral artery (MCA) infarct.
  * *Dyskinetic:* Basal ganglia/thalamus lesions (e.g., from severe HIE or Kernicterus).

### Special & Genetic Investigations (特殊與基因檢查)
* **Genetic/Metabolic Testing:** Microarray, targeted gene panels, metabolic workup (lactate, ammonia, amino acids) IF MRI is normal or atypical features present (regression, family history).
* **Screening:** Formal audiology (聽力測試), ophthalmology (眼科檢查), EEG (腦電圖) if clinical seizures.

## 7. Classification by Motor Type & GMFCS

### Motor Type Classification (運動分型)
| Type (Type) | % | Pathophysiology | Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Spastic (痙攣型)** | 80% | Pyramidal tract / Motor cortex damage | ↑ Tone (velocity-dependent), hyperreflexia, clonus, +ve Babinski. |
| - *Hemiplegia (偏癱)* | | Unilateral | Arm > Leg affected. Face spared. Often 2° to perinatal MCA stroke. |
| - *Diplegia (雙癱)* | | Bilateral | Legs >> Arms. Scissoring gait, toe-walking. Strongly associated with Prematurity & PVL. |
| - *Quadriplegia (四肢癱)* | | Bilateral | All 4 limbs + trunk/bulbar affected. Severe HIE/structural anomaly. High rate of severe ID & epilepsy. |
| **Dyskinetic (運動障礙型)**| 6% | Basal ganglia / Extra-pyramidal damage | Involuntary movements: Chorea (jerky), Athetosis (writhing), Dystonia (twisting). Fluctuating tone. Often 2° to Kernicterus or term HIE. |
| **Ataxic (共濟失調型)** | 4% | Cerebellar damage | Early hypotonia, poor balance, intention tremor, wide-based ataxic gait. Often genetic. |
| **Mixed (混合型)** | 10% | Multiple areas | Usually Spastic + Dyskinetic. |

### Gross Motor Function Classification System (GMFCS) (粗大運動功能分級系統)
| Level | Description (Aged 6-12 yrs) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **I** | Walks without limitations. |
| **II** | Walks with limitations (e.g., outdoors/stairs). |
| **III** | Walks using a hand-held mobility device. |
| **IV** | Self-mobility with limitations; may use powered mobility. |
| **V** | Transported in a manual wheelchair; dependent for all mobility. |

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Multidisciplinary Approach (多學科綜合治療)
* **Goal:** Maximize function, independence, prevent deformities, optimize quality of life. NO cure.
* **Core Team:** Paediatrician, Orthopaedic Surgeon, Physiotherapist (PT), Occupational Therapist (OT), Speech Therapist (ST).

### Medical Management of Spasticity (痙攣的藥物治療)
* **Oral Agents:** Baclofen (GABA agonist), Diazepam, Trihexyphenidyl (for dyskinesia/dystonia). Limited by systemic side effects (sedation, drooling).
* **Botulinum Toxin A (Botox) (肉毒桿菌毒素):** Targeted IM injections into spastic muscles (e.g., gastrocnemius) to relieve spasticity temporarily (3-6 months), delay surgery, improve orthotic fit. *HA Protocol:* Highly utilized in HK, often subvented for specific criteria.
* **Intrathecal Baclofen Pump (ITB):** For severe, generalized, refractory spasticity/dystonia.

### Surgical Interventions (外科手術)
* **Orthopaedic Surgery:** Tendon lengthening (e.g., Achilles), osteotomies to prevent/correct hip subluxation/dislocation (frequent in quadriplegia), spinal fusion for severe scoliosis.
* **Selective Dorsal Rhizotomy (SDR) (選擇性脊神經後根切斷術):** Neurosurgical division of sensory nerve rootlets in spinal cord to permanently ↓ spasticity. *Best candidates:* Spastic diplegia, age 4-8, good underlying strength/cognition.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Hong Kong Referral Pathways & Education (香港轉介途徑與教育)
* **Child Assessment Centre (CAC):** Crucial for formal diagnosis & bridging to education/social resources.
* **Early Training:** Early Education & Training Centre (EETC) (早期教育及訓練中心), Special Child Care Centre (SCCC) (特殊幼兒中心).
* **Schooling:** Mainstream schools (with integration support) vs. Special Schools for Physical Disability / Severe Intellectual Disability (ID).

### Comorbidity Management (共病管理)
* **Epilepsy (癲癇):** Seen in up to 50% (especially Quadriplegia/Hemiplegia). Requires AEDs.
* **Feeding/Nutrition:** Bulbar palsy → recurrent aspiration, failure to thrive. Often requires **Gastrostomy/PEG tube (胃造口)**.
* **Vision/Hearing:** Cortical visual impairment, strabismus, sensorineural hearing loss. Requires routine screening.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Vulnerability & Caregiver Burnout (脆弱性與照顧者過勞)
* Children with severe physical/intellectual disabilities are highly vulnerable to abuse & neglect.
* **Caregiver Burnout:** Massive risk factor. Assess family stress continuously.
* **Respite Care (暫託服務):** Essential MSW (Medical Social Worker) referral.

### NAI (Non-Accidental Injury) Evaluation (非意外傷害評估)
* Unexplained fractures in a non-ambulatory child (though consider osteopenia of CP).
* Poor hygiene, severe severe untreated pressure sores, missed medical appointments.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis

### Common Complications (常見併發症)
* **MSK:** Joint contractures, hip subluxation/dislocation, severe progressive scoliosis.
* **Bone Health:** Osteopenia, pathological fractures (due to non-weight bearing, AED use).
* **Respiratory:** Recurrent aspiration pneumonia (major cause of mortality in severe Quadriplegia).
* **GI:** Severe constipation, Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

### Prognostic Indicators for Walking (步行的預後指標)
* **Good Prognosis:** Hemiplegia (almost all walk by age 2).
* **Predictors:** If a child can **sit independently by age 2**, they will likely walk unaided by age 6.
* If a child cannot sit or roll over by age 2, independent walking is highly unlikely.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the classic MRI finding in a 28-week premature infant who develops spastic diplegic cerebral palsy? Answer: Periventricular Leukomalacia (PVL) – ischaemic damage to white matter tracts descending adjacent to the lateral ventricles.
* Viva Question: A 2-year-old child presents with exclusively right-sided fisting and poor right leg movement. What is the most likely cause seen on MRI? Answer: Perinatal stroke affecting the left middle cerebral artery (MCA) territory, causing spastic hemiplegia.
* Viva Question: Which type of CP is classically associated with severe neonatal jaundice (Kernicterus) or term HIE? Answer: Dyskinetic CP (choreoathetoid/dystonic), due to basal ganglia damage.
* Trap: Assuming handedness at 10 months of age is a sign of advanced development. Pearl: Early hand preference (before 18 months) is pathological and a massive red flag for contralateral hemiplegia.
* Trap: Diagnosing CP in a child who was walking at 14 months but has become increasingly stiff and unable to walk at 24 months. Pearl: CP is a static, non-progressive disorder. Loss of milestones signifies a progressive neurodegenerative or spinal cord disorder requiring urgent investigation.
* Pitfall: Focusing solely on treating motor spasticity while ignoring other domains. CP is a multisystem disorder; you must aggressively screen for and manage the massive burden of comorbidities including epilepsy, cortical visual impairment, and silent aspiration.
