# Spina Bifida (Neural Tube Defects) (脊柱裂)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Neural Tube Defects
* **Congenital neural tube defects (NTDs) (神經管缺陷)**: A spectrum of congenital anomalies caused by the failure of the embryonic neural tube to close completely during the first month of pregnancy (days 21-28).
* Involves exposed neural tissue, meninges, and overlying bone/skin defects.
### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology
* Incidence ↓ significantly globally and in **Hong Kong** due to routine maternal **Folic Acid (葉酸)** supplementation.
* Widespread HA antenatal ultrasound screening and **Maternal Serum Alpha-Fetoprotein (MSAFP) (母體血清甲胎蛋白)** screening → early detection → provides the option for termination of pregnancy (TOP).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Embryological Pathophysiology
* **Neural tube** forms the brain and spinal cord.
* Failure of the caudal end to close leaves the spinal cord and meninges exposed. 
* **Amniotic fluid toxicity**: Exposed neural tissue is progressively damaged by the amniotic fluid during pregnancy → irreversible neurological deficits below the level of the lesion.
### Multifactorial Aetiology & Risk Factors
* **Nutritional**: **Folic acid (Vitamin B9) deficiency** is the most significant preventable risk factor.
* **Medications**: Maternal use of antiepileptics (e.g., **Sodium Valproate (丙戊酸鈉)**, Carbamazepine) during the 1st trimester (act as folate antagonists).
* **Maternal conditions**: Pre-gestational diabetes, severe obesity, and hyperthermia in early pregnancy.
* **Genetics**: ↑ risk with a +ve family history or a previous affected pregnancy.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Spina Bifida Occulta (隱性脊柱裂)
* **Mildest form**: Failure of the vertebral arches to fuse, but the spinal cord and meninges are normal.
* Usually asymptomatic; incidental finding on X-ray.
* **Cutaneous markers (Skin stigmata)**: Tuft of hair, sacral dimple, lipoma, or haemangioma over the lower spine.
### Meningocele (腦膜膨出)
* A sac of **meninges and CSF** protrudes through the vertebral defect.
* The spinal cord remains in its normal position within the spinal canal.
* Neurological deficits are usually minimal or completely absent.
### Myelomeningocele (脊髓腦膜膨出)
* **Most severe & common clinical form**: Spinal cord and nerve roots herniate into the protruding sac, exposing the raw neural placode.
* **Motor Deficits**: Flaccid paralysis, muscle atrophy, and absent reflexes below the level of the lesion (usually lumbosacral).
* **Sensory Loss**: Complete anaesthesia below the lesion.
* **Sphincter Dysfunction**: **Neurogenic bladder (神經性膀胱)** (incontinence, urinary retention) and bowel incontinence are almost universal.
* **Orthopaedic Deformities**: **Talipes equinovarus (Clubfoot/馬蹄內翻足)**, hip dislocation, scoliosis (due to muscle imbalance).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating Myelomeningocele from Sacrococcygeal Teratoma
| Feature | Myelomeningocele (脊髓腦膜膨出) | Sacrococcygeal Teratoma (骶尾部畸胎瘤) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pathology** | Herniated neural elements & meninges | Germ cell tumour at the base of the coccyx |
| **Neurological Deficit** | Profound lower limb flaccid paralysis | Usually absent or extremely mild |
| **Sphincter Tone** | Universal neurogenic bladder/bowel | Normal early on; altered only if mass effect ↑ |
| **Location** | Lumbosacral spine (midline) | Sacrococcygeal region (often a buttock mass) |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Main Differentials for Spinal Lesions/Paralysis
* **Sacrococcygeal teratoma (骶尾部畸胎瘤)**.
* **Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) (脊髓性肌肉萎縮症)**: Causes progressive flaccid paralysis and absent reflexes, but has no visible back lesion.
* **Caudal regression syndrome**: Sacral agenesis and motor deficits, strongly associated with maternal diabetes.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Antenatal Screening & Diagnosis
* **Bloods**: ↑ **Maternal serum AFP (MSAFP)** at 15-20 weeks gestation.
* **Imaging (Gold Standard)**: Detailed fetal US anomaly scan (18-20 weeks) → visualizes the spinal defect, "lemon sign" (frontal bone scalloping), and "banana sign" (cerebellar herniation).
* **Special**: Amniocentesis for amniotic AFP and Acetylcholinesterase (AChE) if US findings are equivocal.
### Postnatal Bedside & Imaging Investigations
* **MRI Spine & Brain**: Defines the exact anatomy of the spinal lesion; detects associated brain anomalies (e.g., **Chiari II malformation**, hydrocephalus).
* **Renal US**: Essential baseline to assess for hydronephrosis or renal scarring caused by the neurogenic bladder.
* **Urodynamic Studies**: Assesses bladder function, capacity, and detrusor pressures to guide urological management.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Neonatal Management
* **Delivery**: Elective LSCS if the lesion is large to prevent rupture/trauma during vaginal delivery.
* **Infection Prevention**: The exposed placode MUST be covered with sterile, warm saline-soaked non-adherent dressings to prevent meningitis and tissue drying.
* **Positioning**: The infant must be nursed strictly **prone** to avoid pressure on the sac.
* **Surgical Closure**: Urgent neurosurgical closure of the defect within 24-48 hours of birth.
### Chronic Multidisciplinary Management
* **Neurosurgery**: Monitor and treat **Hydrocephalus (腦積水)** → Insertion of a **Ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt (腦室腹腔分流術)**.
* **Urology (Crucial for survival)**: Prevent renal failure. **Clean Intermittent Catheterization (CIC) (間歇性導尿)** to routinely empty the neurogenic bladder. Prophylactic antibiotics (e.g., Trimethoprim) and anticholinergics (e.g., Oxybutynin) to ↓ detrusor pressure and prevent reflux.
* **Orthopaedics**: Surgical release or serial casting for clubfoot (Ponseti method), hip reduction, and spinal bracing/surgery for scoliosis.
* **Physiotherapy**: Maximize mobility via standing frames, ankle-foot orthoses (AFOs), and early wheelchair provision.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Prevention & Community Support
* **Prevention via MCHC (母嬰健康院)**: 
  * Low-risk women: **400 mcg Folic Acid** daily (starting 1 month pre-conception to 12 weeks gestation).
  * High-risk women (prior child with NTD, taking Valproate, BMI >30): **5 mg Folic Acid** daily.
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) / SWD (社會福利署)**: Financial assistance (Disability Allowance), housing adaptations, and special school placement coordination.
* **Clinical Psychology**: Support for family coping strategies, managing chronic disability, and transition to independent adolescent care.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Recognizing Abuse or Neglect in Disabled Children
* **General NAI Flags**: Delayed presentation for VP shunt complications, severe poor hygiene, recurrent severe UTIs indicating neglect of the strict CIC protocol, or unexplained fractures (paralyzed limbs have severe osteopenia and are highly susceptible to handling fractures).

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Neurological & Neurosurgical Complications
* **Hydrocephalus (腦積水)**: Develops in >80% of children with myelomeningocele due to the associated **Chiari II Malformation** (downward herniation of the cerebellar vermis and brainstem through the foramen magnum, blocking CSF flow).
* **Tethered Cord Syndrome**: The spinal cord adheres to surgical scar tissue as the child grows → leads to worsening gait, ↑ scoliosis, and newly altered bladder function. Requires surgical release.
### Systemic Complications
* **Latex Allergy**: Extremely common (>50%) due to frequent mucosal exposure during surgeries and daily catheterizations. A **strict latex-free environment** MUST be used.
* **Renal Failure**: The leading cause of death in adulthood if the neurogenic bladder and VUR are poorly managed.
* **Prognosis**: Survival >90% to adulthood with modern HA MDT care, though substantial lifelong physical and urological morbidities persist.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most important preventable risk factor for neural tube defects, and what is the recommended prophylactic dose for a low-risk woman? Answer: Folic acid deficiency. The dose is 400 micrograms daily, starting at least 1 month before conception until 12 weeks gestation.
* Viva Question: A newborn with a myelomeningocele has a rapidly increasing head circumference. What is the most likely cause, and what is the underlying anatomical defect? Answer: Hydrocephalus. It is caused by the associated Chiari II malformation blocking the flow of CSF at the foramen magnum.
* Viva Question: What is the leading cause of mortality in adults with spina bifida? Answer: Renal failure secondary to chronic, poorly managed neurogenic bladder and recurrent urinary tract infections.
* Trap: Using standard latex gloves or regular catheters during routine examinations for Spina Bifida patients. Pearl: Always use a strict latex-free environment, as these patients have a remarkably high incidence of severe latex allergy and anaphylaxis due to lifelong, repeated mucosal exposures.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to ask about maternal anti-epileptic drug use (especially Sodium Valproate) when taking a history for a child with a neural tube defect. Always review 1st-trimester teratogenic exposures.
* Pitfall: Assuming a patient with a meningocele will have the same severe neurological deficits as one with a myelomeningocele. Remember that in a meningocele, the spinal cord remains within the normal canal and neurological deficits are usually absent or minimal.
