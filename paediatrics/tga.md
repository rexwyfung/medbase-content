

# Transposition of the Great Arteries (TGA) (大動脈轉位)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A cyanotic congenital heart defect where the aorta arises from the right ventricle and the pulmonary artery arises from the left ventricle (ventriculoarterial discordance).
- **Local Epidemiology**: The most common cause of cyanotic congenital heart disease presenting in the *neonatal* period in HK.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Parallel Circulations**: Deoxygenated systemic blood returns to the RA -> RV -> Aorta -> Body. Oxygenated pulmonary blood returns to the LA -> LV -> Pulmonary Artery -> Lungs.
- **Survival Dependency**: Survival is impossible without mixing between the two parallel circuits. Mixing occurs via an Atrial Septal Defect (ASD), Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD), or Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA).
- Risk factors: Maternal diabetes.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **Profound Cyanosis**: Presents within the first few hours to days of life as the ductus arteriosus begins to close.
- Cyanosis is unresponsive to supplemental oxygen (failed hyperoxia test).

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Cyanosis**: Central cyanosis, often severe.
- **Tachypnoea**: Without significant respiratory distress (comfortable tachypnoea).
- **Murmur**: Often NO murmur if the ventricular septum is intact (simple TGA). A pansystolic murmur may be present if there is a VSD.
- Single loud S2 (aorta is anterior).

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF)**: Usually presents later (weeks to months) with a harsh ejection systolic murmur.
- **Respiratory Causes of Cyanosis**: Usually have significant respiratory distress (grunting, retractions) and respond to oxygen.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Other cyanotic CHDs: Tetralogy of Fallot, Tricuspid Atresia, Truncus Arteriosus, Total Anomalous Pulmonary Venous Return (TAPVR).
- Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension of the Newborn (PPHN).
- Severe neonatal sepsis.

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
**Bedside**: Hyperoxia test (PaO2 fails to rise significantly on 100% O2). Pre- and post-ductal SpO2 (may show reverse differential cyanosis if PDA is present and pulmonary vascular resistance is high).
- **Imaging**: **CXR**: "Egg on a string" appearance (narrow mediastinum due to anteroposterior relationship of great vessels, globular heart).
**Gold Standard**: **Echocardiogram**: Confirms the diagnosis and assesses the sites of mixing (ASD, VSD, PDA).

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute Resuscitation (NICU)**:
  - **Prostaglandin E1 (PGE1) Infusion**: (Alprostadil) LIFESAVING. Keeps the ductus arteriosus patent to allow mixing. *Side effect to watch for: Apnoea (may require intubation).*
  - **Balloon Atrial Septostomy (Rashkind Procedure)**: Performed urgently in the cardiac cath lab or bedside under echo guidance if mixing is inadequate despite PGE1 (creates or enlarges an ASD).
- **Definitive Surgical**:
  - **Arterial Switch Operation (Jatene procedure)**: The standard of care. Usually performed within the first 1-2 weeks of life at HKCH. The great arteries are transected and switched, and the coronary arteries are reimplanted into the neo-aorta.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Intensive support for parents dealing with a critically ill neonate requiring urgent surgery.
- Long-term follow-up by paediatric cardiology for neurodevelopmental monitoring and cardiac function.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Not directly applicable.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Without surgery, mortality is >90% in the first year.
- Post-arterial switch: Excellent long-term survival. Potential late complications include coronary artery stenosis, neo-aortic root dilation, and right ventricular outflow tract obstruction.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Giving 100% oxygen and expecting the cyanosis to improve. *Pearl*: TGA is a mixing lesion; oxygen will not significantly improve systemic PaO2 and may actually promote ductal closure, worsening the condition.
- **Viva**: "What is the most common side effect of Prostaglandin E1 infusion?" *Answer*: Apnoea. Always have intubation equipment ready when starting PGE1.
