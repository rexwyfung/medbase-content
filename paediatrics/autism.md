
# Autism Spectrum Disorder (自閉症譜系障礙)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: A neurodevelopmental disorder characterised by persistent deficits in social communication and social interaction, along with restricted, repetitive patterns of behaviour, interests, or activities (DSM-5 criteria).
- **Local Epidemiology**: Prevalence in Hong Kong is increasing, estimated at ~1-2%. Often picked up by Maternal and Child Health Centres (MCHC) during routine developmental surveillance (e.g., 18-month or 4-year checks) or by kindergarten teachers.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Highly heritable (up to 80-90% concordance in monozygotic twins).
- Polygenic inheritance, but also associated with specific genetic syndromes (e.g., Fragile X, Tuberous Sclerosis, Rett syndrome).
- Advanced parental age and prematurity are risk factors.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- See below.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Social Communication Deficits**: Poor eye contact, lack of joint attention (not pointing to share interest), delayed speech, echolalia, literal understanding of language, difficulty making friends.
- **Restricted/Repetitive Behaviours**: Hand-flapping, spinning, lining up toys, intense preoccupation with specific subjects (e.g., train schedules, dinosaurs), insistence on sameness, hyper- or hypo-reactivity to sensory input (covering ears for normal sounds).
- **Red Flags**: No babbling by 12 months, no pointing by 12 months, no single words by 16 months, loss of any language or social skills at any age.
## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- **Global Developmental Delay / Intellectual Disability**: ASD specifically involves a *discrepancy* between social/communication skills and other cognitive abilities. However, they frequently co-occur.
- **Hearing Impairment**: Can mimic poor response to name. (Always check hearing!).

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- **Most Likely**:
  - Autism Spectrum Disorder.
- **Must-Not-Miss**:
  - Severe hearing loss.
  - Landau-Kleffner syndrome (acquired epileptic aphasia - suspect if there is a sudden regression in language).
  - Underlying genetic syndrome (Fragile X, TSC).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Bedside**:
  - **Audiology Assessment**: Mandatory for any child with speech delay or suspected ASD to rule out hearing loss.
  - **Developmental Screening**: MCHC uses tools like the Comprehensive Child Development Service (CCDS) screening.
- **Gold Standard**:
  - **Multidisciplinary Assessment**: Conducted at Child Assessment Centres (CAC) under the Department of Health. Involves Paediatricians, Clinical Psychologists (using tools like ADOS-2, ADI-R), Speech Therapists, and Occupational Therapists.
- **Monitoring**:
  - Genetic testing (Chromosomal Microarray, Fragile X DNA testing) is increasingly offered as first-line investigation for ASD to identify syndromic causes.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Acute**:
  - Not applicable.
- **Chronic/Definitive**:
  - **No medical cure for core symptoms**.
  - Medications may be used for co-morbidities: Melatonin for sleep, Risperidone/Aripiprazole for severe irritability/aggression, Methylphenidate for co-morbid ADHD.
## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- **Early Intervention**: Crucial. Refer to Social Welfare Department (SWD) for pre-school rehabilitation services (e.g., Early Education and Training Centre - EETC, Special Child Care Centre - SCCC, or On-site Pre-school Rehabilitation Services - OPRS).
- **Therapies**: Speech therapy (communication), Occupational therapy (sensory integration, fine motor), Behavioural therapy (Applied Behaviour Analysis - ABA).
## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- **Parental Support**: Diagnosis is often devastating. Connect parents with local NGOs (e.g., Heep Hong Society, SAHK) and parent support groups. MSW referral for caregiver stress.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Lifelong condition. Prognosis depends on cognitive ability (IQ) and language development by age 5.
- High rates of co-morbidities: ADHD (up to 50%), anxiety, epilepsy (up to 30%), sleep disorders.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Forgetting to check hearing. *Pearl*: "Before diagnosing autism in a child who doesn't respond to their name, I must arrange a formal audiology assessment."
- **Local Pearl**: Be aware of the long waiting times for CAC assessments in HK. Advise parents that private early intervention can be started while waiting for the public assessment.
