# Foreign Body Aspiration (異物吸入)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Foreign Body Aspiration
* Inhalation of organic/inorganic material into larynx, trachea, or bronchial tree
* Causes partial/complete airway obstruction
* Medical emergency requiring prompt intervention to prevent asphyxia (窒息)

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Demographics
* Peak age: 1-3 years (poor dentition, high oral exploration)
* Common local organic objects: 
  * Watermelon seeds (西瓜子) - extremely common during Lunar New Year
  * Peanuts (花生)
  * Nuts/seeds
* Common local inorganic objects:
  * Small toy parts
  * Stationery caps
* Male > Female

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Anatomical Predilection
* **Right main bronchus (右主支氣管)**: Most common site
* Anatomical reasons:
  * Wider diameter
  * Shorter length
  * Steeper/more vertical angle from trachea

### Airway Obstruction Mechanisms
* **Bypass valve**: Partial obstruction → air flows in and out → localized wheeze
* **Check-valve**: Air enters during inspiration (bronchial dilation) but cannot exit during expiration (bronchial constriction) → distal **Hyperinflation (肺過度充氣)**
* **Stop-valve**: Complete obstruction → air trapped distally resorbs → distal **Atelectasis (肺不張)**

### Mucosal Inflammatory Response
* Organic bodies (peanuts) → severe chemical pneumonitis (化學性肺炎)
* Swelling/granulation tissue formation
* Obstruction worsens rapidly over hours/days

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Historical Symptoms
* History of choking episode (highly sensitive)
* Sudden onset of symptoms
* Persistent dry cough (咳嗽)
* Acute dyspnoea (呼吸困難)
* Cyanosis (發紺)

### Key Clinical Signs
* **Stridor (喘鳴)**: Indicates upper airway/laryngeal/tracheal obstruction
* **Wheeze (喘息)**: Indicates lower airway/bronchial obstruction
* Asymmetrical chest expansion
* Localized ↓ breath sounds (呼吸音減弱)
* Dullness to percussion (if atelectasis)
* Hyperresonance to percussion (if hyperinflation)

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Discriminatory Clinical Features
* Sudden onset in a previously well, afebrile child
* Unilateral auscultatory findings (unilateral wheeze is FBA until proven otherwise)
* Non-responsive to inhaled bronchodilators (salbutamol)
* Positive history of witnessed choking (哽噎)
* No preceding upper respiratory tract infection (URTI) prodrome

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Differential Diagnosis of Foreign Body Aspiration Table
| Condition | Differentiating Clinical Features | Differentiating Investigations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Childhood Asthma (兒童哮喘)** | Bilateral wheeze, recurrent episodes, +ve family history, +ve response to salbutamol | Normal CXR (or bilateral hyperinflation), spirometry reversibility |
| **Acute Bronchiolitis (急性細支氣管炎)** | URTI prodrome, bilateral fine crackles, low-grade fever, age < 1 year | NPA +ve for RSV, bilateral hyperinflation on CXR |
| **Croup (哮吼)** | Barking cough, inspiratory stridor, fever, voice hoarseness | Steeple sign on AP neck X-ray |
| **Bacterial Pneumonia (細菌性肺炎)** | High fever, productive cough, toxic appearance | Focal consolidation, ↑ WCC, ↑ CRP |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Tiered Radiological Investigations Table
| Investigation | Indication/Timing | Key Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PA Chest X-Ray (CXR)** | 1st line (if stable) | Radiopaque object (rare), unilateral hyperinflation, atelectasis, consolidation |
| **Expiratory/Decubitus CXR** | 2nd line (if PA normal but high suspicion) | Affected side remains hyperinflated during expiration, mediastinal shift away from affected side |
| **Fluoroscopy** | Diagnostic adjunct | Mediastinal shift during dynamic respiration |
| **CT Thorax** | Difficult/chronic cases | Identifies radiolucent objects, assesses granulation tissue/bronchiectasis |

### Gold Standard Investigation
* **Rigid Bronchoscopy (硬式支氣管鏡檢查)**
* Both diagnostic and therapeutic
* Indicated if history +ve for FBA, even if CXR is completely normal

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Basic Life Support Protocol (Choking Child)
* Effective cough present:
  * Encourage coughing
  * Monitor continuously
* Ineffective cough/Unconscious:
  * Call for help (Resuscitation team)
  * Commence pediatric BLS/CPR
* Ineffective cough/Conscious (Infant <1 yr):
  * 5 back blows (拍背)
  * 5 chest thrusts (推胸)
* Ineffective cough/Conscious (Child >1 yr):
  * 5 back blows
  * 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)

### Hospital Authority (HA) Inpatient Management Protocol
| Step | Intervention | Target/Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **O2 Therapy** | Maintain SpO2 >94% |
| 2 | **NBM (Nil By Mouth)** | Strict fasting for emergency GA |
| 3 | **IV Access & Fluids** | Maintenance hydration (0.9% NaCl + 5% Dextrose) |
| 4 | **Consultation** | Urgent referral to ENT / Pediatric Surgery for Rigid Bronchoscopy |

### Post-Operative Management
* IV Corticosteroids (Dexamethasone) → ↓ airway edema
* IV Antibiotics → Treat secondary post-obstructive pneumonia
* Chest physiotherapy → Facilitate secretion clearance

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **ENT / Pediatric Surgeons**: Procedural extraction
* **Anesthetist**: Specialized pediatric airway management
* **Pediatric ICU**: Post-operative airway monitoring
* **Play Therapist**: Relieve procedural anxiety

### Parental Education & Preventive Care
* Chop food into small pieces
* Avoid high-risk foods (peanuts, whole grapes) < 4 years old
* Supervise eating (no walking/running while chewing)
* Keep small objects/batteries out of reach

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Non-Accidental Injury (NAI) Red Flags
* Unexplained delay in seeking medical attention after choking event
* History inconsistent with child's developmental age (e.g., non-mobile infant aspirating a floor object)
* Object found is highly unusual or inappropriate
* Conflicting stories between caregivers

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Acute Complications
* Total airway obstruction → Hypoxic cardiac arrest → Death
* Laryngeal edema (喉頭水腫)
* Pneumothorax (氣胸)
* Pneumomediastinum

### Chronic Complications (Missed FBA)
* Post-obstructive pneumonia
* Lung abscess (肺膿腫)
* Bronchiectasis (支氣管擴張)
* Chronic granuloma formation

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most reliable clinical indicator of a foreign body aspiration? Answer: A witnessed history of a choking episode by a caregiver.
* Viva Question: A 2-year-old with a strong history of choking on a peanut has a completely normal inspiratory CXR. What is the next step? Answer: Proceed to rigid bronchoscopy; a normal CXR does not rule out a radiolucent foreign body.
* Trap: Assuming a negative response to salbutamol in a wheezing toddler implies severe asthma. Pearl: Unilateral wheeze unresponsive to bronchodilators is a foreign body until proven otherwise.
* Pitfall: Performing a blind finger sweep in a choking child. This can push the foreign body deeper and turn a partial obstruction into a complete laryngeal obstruction.
* Pitfall: Ordering a soft tissue neck X-ray to rule out lower airway foreign bodies. Neck X-rays only identify radiopaque objects in the upper airway, missing the majority of bronchial foreign bodies.
