# Urticaria & Hereditary Angioedema (蕁麻疹及遺傳性血管性水腫)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Urticaria
**Urticaria (Hives) (蕁麻疹)**: Raised, intensely erythematous, pruritic wheals that migrate & resolve individually within 24 hrs without scarring.

### Clinical Definition of Angioedema
**Angioedema (血管性水腫)**: Swelling of the deeper dermis/subcutaneous tissue.

### Clinical Definition of Hereditary Angioedema
**Hereditary Angioedema (HAE) (遺傳性血管性水腫)**: Rare, life-threatening genetic disorder resulting in episodic, unpredictable massive swelling without hives.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Pathophysiology of Allergic Urticaria
Mast-cell mediated. Release of pure **Histamine (組織胺)** → superficial leaky vessels (hives) or deep leaky vessels (allergic angioedema).

### Genetics of Hereditary Angioedema
Autosomal dominant (AD). Deficiency (Type 1) or dysfunction (Type 2) of the **C1-Esterase Inhibitor (C1-INH)** protein.

### Pathophysiology of Hereditary Angioedema
Unchecked activation of the contact system → massive overproduction of **Bradykinin (緩激肽)**. Bradykinin is a powerful vasodilator → tissue edema. **NO** itch or hives (not histamine-mediated).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Clinical Presentation of Urticaria
* Sudden onset of intensely itchy pink/red migrating blanching wheals.
* Often triggered by a viral illness or food allergen.
* Allergic angioedema (if present) is often facial/periorbital & responds to adrenaline.

### Clinical Presentation of Hereditary Angioedema
* Massive asymmetric limb swelling (often triggered by minor physical trauma or psychological stress).
* Submucosal edema → severe colicky abdominal pain (bowel swelling).
* Laryngeal edema → stridor, airway obstruction (develops over hours, subsides over days).
* **NO** itch, **NO** rash.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Distinguishing HAE from Anaphylaxis
* **HAE**: Lacks urticaria, lacks bronchospasm (upper airway swelling only), normal BP (no systemic shock), **unresponsive** to adrenaline/steroids/antihistamines.
* **Anaphylaxis**: Hives, bronchospasm, hypotension, responsive to adrenaline.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Differential Diagnoses
| Condition | Key Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ACE-Inhibitor Angioedema** | Bradykinin-mediated. Common in adults, rare in kids. |
| **Anaphylaxis (過敏性休克)** | IgE/Mast-cell mediated. Hives, wheeze, shock. |
| **Erythema Multiforme (多形性紅斑)** | Fixed target lesions (do not migrate rapidly). |
| **Acute Surgical Abdomen (急腹症)** | Often misdiagnosed in HAE due to massive bowel wall edema on imaging. |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Acute Urticaria Investigations
No baseline investigations needed unless anaphylaxis is suspected.

### Screening Investigation for HAE
**Low C4 level**: Classic screening test. Persistently low (even between attacks) because uninhibited C1 constantly consumes the C4 supply.

### Definitive Investigation for HAE
Low **C1-INH antigenic level** and/or low **functional C1-INH assay**.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Acute Management of Urticaria
* 2nd generation oral antihistamines.
* ↑ up to 4x standard dose if refractory.

### Acute Airway Management in HAE
* **CRITICAL**: Secure airway extremely early (can swell shut instantly).

### Medical Therapy for Acute HAE Attacks
* **1st Line**: IV C1-Inhibitor concentrate (**Berinert, Cinryze**) OR **Icatibant** (SC Bradykinin B2 receptor antagonist) OR **Ecallantide**.
* **Remote Setting Alternative**: FFP (Fresh Frozen Plasma) contains C1-INH, but controversial (contains kininogens → theoretically worsens attack).

### Chronic Prophylaxis for HAE
* **Danazol** (attenuated androgen) OR prophylactic IV C1-INH.
* Mandatory prophylaxis before dental/surgical procedures.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Emergency Preparedness
Must carry emergency C1-INH/Icatibant everywhere.

### Psychosocial Impact
Massive anxiety regarding dental work (classic trigger for fatal laryngeal edema).

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Safeguarding Considerations
Unexplained, recurrent swelling in HAE could mimic trauma; differentiate via careful history (swelling vs. hematoma/bruising) & C4 levels.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Complications of Hereditary Angioedema
* Death via asphyxiation (laryngeal edema).
* Unnecessary laparotomies (misdiagnosed bowel edema).

### Complications of Urticaria
Chronic spontaneous urticaria (>6 weeks) → severe ↓ quality of life.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Giving Adrenaline, Steroids, and Cetirizine to a child with HAE airway swelling and assuming they are safe. Pearl: The bradykinin pathway is totally untouched by these allergy drugs. You must give specific C1-inhibitor or secure the airway.
* Viva Question: Why is C4 checked as a screening test for HAE? Answer: C1-inhibitor usually prevents the spontaneous activation of C1. Without the inhibitor, C1 is constantly active and burns through the C4 supply, making C4 consistently low.
* Pitfall: Misdiagnosing severe HAE abdominal pain as an acute surgical abdomen. Avoid unnecessary laparotomies by checking for a family history of HAE or prior unexplained swelling episodes.
