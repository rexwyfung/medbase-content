# Urticaria & Hereditary Angioedema (蕁麻疹及遺傳性血管性水腫 - HAE)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Urticaria (Hives)**: Raised, intensely erythematous, pruritic wheals that migrate and resolve individually within 24 hours without leaving a scar.
- **Angioedema**: Swelling of the deeper dermis/subcutaneous tissue.
- **Hereditary Angioedema (HAE)**: A rare, life-threatening genetic disorder resulting in episodic, unpredictable massive swelling without hives.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- **Urticaria/Allergic Angioedema**: Mast-cell mediated. Release of purely *Histamine*. Causes leaky vessels superficially (hives) or deeply (angioedema).
- **Hereditary Angioedema (HAE)**: Autosomal dominant. Deficiency (Type 1) or dysfunction (Type 2) of the **C1 Inhibitor (C1-INH)** protein. Leads to unchecked activation of the contact system and massive overproduction of **Bradykinin**. Bradykinin is a powerful vasodilator but does NOT cause itching or hives.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **Urticaria**: Sudden onset of incredibly itchy pink/red migrating bumps after a viral illness or eating a food.
- **HAE**: A child presenting with massive asymmetric swelling of a limb (often after minor trauma like a bumped knee), severe colicky abdominal pain (bowel swelling), or stridor (laryngeal swelling). NO itch, NO rash.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- **Mast-Cell (Allergic)**: Intensely itchy, erythematous blanching wheals. Angioedema is often facial/periorbital and responds to adrenaline.
- **Bradykinin (HAE)**: 
  - Subcutaneous edema (non-pitting, non-itchy, skin-colored).
  - Submucosal edema: Gastrointestinal swelling mimicking surgical acute abdomen.
  - Laryngeal edema: The leading cause of death. Asphyxiation can occur in hours.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Distinct from Anaphylaxis: HAE attacks lack urticaria, lack bronchospasm (it's upper airway swelling, not lower), maintain normal blood pressure (no systemic shock), and most importantly, **HAE does NOT respond to Adrenaline, steroids, or antihistamines.**

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- ACE-Inhibitor induced angioedema (also bradykinin mediated, common in adults, rare in kids).
- Anaphylaxis.
- Erythema Multiforme (target lesions, fixed).
- Acute surgical abdomen (often misdiagnosed in HAE because imaging shows massive bowel wall edema).

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Acute Urticaria**: No investigations needed unless part of anaphylaxis.
- **HAE Screener**: Low **C4 level** (the classic screening test, low even between attacks because C1 isn't there to stop the cascade consuming C4).
- **Definitive HAE**: Low C1-INH antigenic level and/or low functional C1-INH assay.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Urticaria**: 2nd generation oral antihistamines (Up to 4x standard dose if refractory).
- **HAE Acute Attack**:
  - Secure the airway extremely early. They can swell shut instantly.
  - **Specific Therapy**: IV C1-Inhibitor concentrate (Berinert, Cinryze) OR Icatibant (Subcutaneous Bradykinin B2 receptor antagonist) OR Ecallantide.
  - *Note*: If specific therapies are completely unavailable in a remote setting, FFP (Fresh Frozen Plasma) can be carefully used as it contains C1-INH, though it's controversial as it also contains kininogens which could theoretically worsen the attack.
- **HAE Prophylaxis**: Danazol (attenuated androgen) or prophylactic IV C1-INH.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Must carry emergency C1-INH or Icatibant everywhere. Massive anxiety surrounding dental work (a classic trigger for fatal laryngeal edema). Prophylaxis provided before any surgery.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- N/A.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- Death by asphyxiation in HAE.
- Chronic spontaneous urticaria (lasting >6 weeks) causes significant quality-of-life impairment.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Giving Adrenaline, Steroids, and Cetirizine to a child with HAE airway swelling and assuming they are safe. Bradykinin pathway is totally untouched by these allergy drugs. You must give specific C1-inhibitor or secure the airway.
- **Viva Q**: "Why is C4 checked as a screening test for HAE?" Answer: C1-inhibitor usually prevents the spontaneous activation of C1. Without the inhibitor, C1 is constantly active and burns through the C4 supply, making C4 consistently low.
