# Myocarditis & Pericarditis (心肌炎及心包炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Myocarditis
* Inflammation of cardiac muscle (myocardium) (心肌).
* → Myocyte necrosis (心肌細胞壞死).
* → Ventricular dilation & severe cardiac dysfunction.

### Clinical Definition of Pericarditis
* Inflammation of the fibroserous sac surrounding the heart (pericardium) (心包).
* Often accompanied by fluid accumulation (effusion).

### Local Epidemiology & Etiology
* Typically triggered by seasonal viral infections in Hong Kong.
* **Key Viral Pathogens:** Enteroviruses (Coxsackie B), Parvovirus B19, Adenovirus.
* **Vaccine-induced:** Historically noted as a rare side-effect of mRNA COVID-19 vaccines in adolescent males.
* **Autoimmune/Inflammatory:** Can occur secondary to Kawasaki Disease (川崎病) or PIMS-TS (Pediatric Inflammatory Multisystem Syndrome).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics

### Pathophysiology of Viral Myocarditis
* **Phase 1 (Direct viral damage):** Viral entry into myocytes → replication → direct cytotoxicity & cell lysis.
* **Phase 2 (Immune response):** T-cell mediated autoimmune attack acting against heart tissue cross-reactive antigens.
* **Phase 3 (Remodeling):** Edema, necrosis, replacement fibrosis → ↓ contractility, ↑ arrhythmias.

### Pathophysiology of Pericarditis
* Inflammatory fibrinous exudate → roughens smooth visceral and parietal pericardial layers.
* Layers rub together during cardiac cycle → pleuritic chest pain.
* Fluid accumulation → Pericardial Effusion (心包積液).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Prodromal Symptoms
* Preceding 1-2 week viral prodrome.
* URTI (coryza) or GI symptoms (vomiting, diarrhea).

### Key Symptoms of Myocarditis
* Exertional dyspnea (氣促).
* Severe fatigue.
* Palpitations (心悸).
* New-onset heart failure (心臟衰竭) symptoms.

### Key Signs of Myocarditis
* **Inappropriate tachycardia** (心跳過速) (fast heart rate out of proportion to fever).
* **Gallop rhythm** (S3 heart sound).
* Muffled heart sounds.
* Hepatomegaly (肝臟腫大).
* Raised Jugular Venous Pressure (JVP).

### Key Symptoms & Signs of Pericarditis
* Acute, sharp, pleuritic chest pain (胸痛).
* **Worsening factors:** Lying flat (supine), deep inspiration.
* **Relieving factors:** Sitting up & leaning forward.
* **Pericardial friction rub** (心包摩擦音) (harsh, scratching sound best heard at left lower sternal border).

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)

### Differentiating Pericarditis vs Costochondritis
* **Costochondritis (肋軟骨炎):** Chest wall has reproducible point tenderness upon physical palpation.
* **Pericarditis:** No pain upon pressing the chest wall; pain is positional.

### Differentiating Myocarditis vs Respiratory Illness
* **Myocarditis:** Heart failure signs (hepatomegaly, S3 gallop, extreme tachycardia).
* **Asthma/Pneumonia:** Wheeze, focal crepitations, normal cardiac output signs.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

### Table of Differential Diagnoses for Acute Pediatric Chest Pain / Dyspnea
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Distinguishing Features | Investigation Clues |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Asthma/Pneumonia (哮喘/肺炎)** | Wheeze, focal crepitations, productive cough | Focal consolidation on CXR, normal ECG |
| **Costochondritis (肋軟骨炎)** | Reproducible point tenderness on chest wall | Normal ECG & Echo |
| **Cardiac Arrhythmia (e.g., SVT)** | Abrupt onset palpitations, HR >220 bpm | Narrow QRS, absent P waves on ECG |
| **Kawasaki Disease (川崎病)** | Prolonged fever ≥5 days, bilateral non-purulent conjunctivitis, polymorphous rash | Coronary artery aneurysms on Echo |

## 6. Tiered Investigations

### Bedside Investigations (ECG)
* **Myocarditis ECG:** Non-specific ST-T wave changes, ventricular ectopics, AV heart blocks, low voltage QRS.
* **Pericarditis ECG:** Widespread, diffuse saddle-shaped ST elevations (ST段提升). PR segment depressions (sparing aVR/V1).

### Blood Investigations
* **Cardiac Biomarkers:** ↑↑ Troponin I or T (marks myocyte necrosis).
* **Heart Failure Marker:** ↑ NT-proBNP (marks ventricular stretch/failure).
* **Inflammatory Markers:** ↑ ESR, ↑ CRP.
* **Microbiology:** Viral PCR (Enterovirus, Adenovirus, Parvovirus) & respiratory pathogen panel.

### Imaging Investigations
* **CXR:** Cardiomegaly, pulmonary edema (Myocarditis); water-bottle shaped heart (Large Pericardial Effusion).
* **Echocardiogram (超聲心動圖):**
  * Myocarditis: ↓ Ejection Fraction (EF), global hypokinesia, dilated ventricles.
  * Pericarditis: Echo-free space surrounding the heart indicating fluid/effusion.

### Gold Standard & Special Investigations
* **Gold Standard Imaging:** Cardiac MRI (心臟磁力共振) → shows patchy late gadolinium enhancement (LGE) and myocardial edema.
* **Endomyocardial Biopsy:** Rarely performed in pediatrics unless fulminant/refractory to establish specific viral/inflammatory etiology.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management of Myocarditis (Supportive)
* Purely supportive.
* Strict bed rest (to ↓ myocardial oxygen demand).
* Continuous ECG & SpO2 monitoring in PICU.

### Pharmacological Management of Myocarditis
* **Inotropes:** IV Milrinone (米力農) / Dobutamine for cardiogenic shock.
* **Diuretics:** IV Furosemide if in fluid overload/heart failure.
* **Immunomodulation:** High-dose IVIG (靜脈注射免疫球蛋白) 2g/kg (HA protocol for suspected autoimmune/fulminant/PIMS-TS cases) ± IV Corticosteroids.

### Mechanical Support for Fulminant Myocarditis
* **VA-ECMO (Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation):** Used in pediatric cardiac centers (e.g., HK Children's Hospital) for refractory cardiogenic shock as a bridge to recovery or heart transplant.

### Evidence-Based Management of Pericarditis
* **1st Line:** High-dose NSAIDs (e.g., Ibuprofen) for 2-4 weeks.
* **Adjunct:** Colchicine (秋水仙鹼) for 3 months to ↑ response and prevent recurrence.
* **Gastric Protection:** Co-prescribe PPI (e.g., Omeprazole) while on high-dose NSAIDs.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care

### Rehabilitation & Activity Restriction Protocol
* Absolute restriction from competitive sports for 3 to 6 months post-recovery.
* Prevents sudden cardiac death (SCD) during strenuous exercise.
* Require normal ECG, Holter monitor, and Echo prior to sports clearance.

### Psychosocial & MDT Support
* **Medical Social Worker (MSW) / Clinical Psychologist:** Critical for families facing prolonged PICU admissions or ECMO.
* **School Liaison:** To arrange graded return to school and excuse from physical education (PE) classes.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags

### Medical Neglect Screening
* N/A directly for disease etiology, but must assess for medical neglect if parents severely delayed seeking care despite obvious respiratory distress, cyanosis, or loss of consciousness.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications of Myocarditis
* **Acute:** Fulminant cardiogenic shock (心源性休克) → Fatal.
* **Rhythm:** Malignant arrhythmias (VT/VF, complete heart block).
* **Chronic:** Progression to Dilated Cardiomyopathy (擴張型心肌病) requiring lifelong HF medications or cardiac transplantation.

### Complications of Pericarditis
* **Pericardial Tamponade (心包填塞):** Effusion rapidly accumulates → physically crushes the right heart → prevents ventricular filling.
* **Tamponade Signs (Beck's Triad):** Hypotension, muffled heart sounds, raised JVP.
* **Tamponade Management:** Requires emergency pericardiocentesis (心包穿刺術).
* **Chronic:** Constrictive pericarditis (rare in pediatrics).

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Trap: Giving beta-blockers for the "tachycardia" in acute myocarditis. Pearl: Tachycardia is a necessary compensatory mechanism. Beta-blockers are negative inotropes; if the heart is already failing, you will throw the child into instant cardiogenic shock.
* Viva Question: Why does leaning forward relieve the pain of pericarditis? Answer: Leaning forward pulls the inflamed anterior visceral and parietal pericardium away from the diaphragmatic pleura, reducing the painful friction generated by breathing and gravity.
* Pitfall: Missing cardiac tamponade in a deteriorating child with pericarditis. Look out for Beck's Triad, assess for pulsus paradoxus (drop in systolic BP >10mmHg on inspiration), and request an urgent bedside echocardiogram.
* Viva Question: What is the classic ECG manifestation of acute pericarditis? Answer: Widespread, diffuse saddle-shaped ST elevations and PR segment depressions, characteristically sparing leads aVR and V1.
