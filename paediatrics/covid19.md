# COVID-19 in Children (兒童 2019 冠狀病毒病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
- **Definition**: Infection by the SARS-CoV-2 virus in the pediatric population. 
- **Local Epidemiology**: Massive spikes during HK's 5th wave (Omicron), revealing that while usually mild, the sheer volume of cases resulted in significant pediatric morbidity globally and locally.

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
- Virus binds to ACE2 receptors (which are less densely expressed in children's upper airways compared to adults, potentially explaining milder initial disease).
- Omicron variants prominently affected the upper airway glottic machinery, causing a surge in viral croup.

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- Usually an asymptomatic or mild upper respiratory tract infection (URTI) with rhinitis, sore throat, and a few days of fever.
- However, specific distinct pediatric presentations include: Severe acute COVID croup, COVID-induced encephalopathy, and subsequent PIMS-TS.

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
- Mild: Fever, cough, anosmia (loss of smell, less reported in toddlers), diarrhea.
- Severe (Acute): "Barking" cough with stridor and chest indrawing (Croup).
- Seizures: Seizures out of proportion to fever, pointing to potential COVID encephalitis.

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
- Clinically indistinguishable from Influenza, RSV, or Parainfluenza without PCR testing. Can only be firmly diagnosed via antigen/PCR.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
- Influenza A/B
- RSV Bronchiolitis
- Parainfluenza (classic croup)
- Adenovirus

## 7. Tiered Investigations (Bedside, Bloods, Imaging, Special)
- **Definitive**: Nasopharyngeal or throat/saliva PCR for SARS-CoV-2.
- **Bloods/Imaging**: Only required if the child is hospitalized with moderate/severe disease. CXR for pneumonia. FBC, CRP, LFT, Troponin if suspecting systemic involvement or myocarditis.

## 8. Evidence-Based Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
- **Mild**: Home isolation. Symptomatic control with Paracetamol and Ibuprofen.
- **Moderate/Croup**: Nebulized adrenaline and oral dexamethasone for croup exactly as per non-COVID protocols.
- **Severe (Pneumonia/Hypoxia)**: Oxygen therapy, IV Dexamethasone, and potentially antivirals (e.g., Remdesivir) if instituted early in high-risk groups (e.g., immunocompromised, severe neurodisability).
- **Prophylaxis**: COVID-19 vaccination strongly endorsed by local pediatric societies for all children > 6 months to prevent severe acute disease and dramatically reduce the risk of PIMS-TS.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care (MSW, MCHC, SWD)
- Massive psychosocial impact of quarantine mandates, school closures, and parental anxiety. Focus on rehabilitating "Long COVID" symptoms (chronic fatigue, brain fog, return to school).

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
- Severe social isolation during lockdowns led to documented spikes in undetected NAI and severe deterioration in child mental health (eating disorders, depression).

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
- **Post-acute**: PIMS-TS / MIS-C (developing 2-6 weeks later).
- **Acute**: Viral encephalitis, severe croup requiring pediatric intensive care.
- Overall mortality in healthy children is exceptionally low.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
- **Trap**: Assuming COVID doesn't cause severe acute disease in kids. During the Omicron wave, the incidence of severe, ICU-level croup skyrocketed.
- **Viva Q**: "A mother asks if her child should get the COVID vaccine, seeing as the acute infection is usually mild. What is your strongest medical argument for vaccination?" Answer: While acute disease is mild, the vaccine is highly effective at preventing the deadly post-infectious complication, PIMS-TS, which strikes previously completely healthy children weeks after a mild or silent infection.
