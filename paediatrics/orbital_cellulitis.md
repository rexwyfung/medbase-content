# Orbital & Periorbital Cellulitis (眼眶及眶周蜂窩組織炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Periorbital vs Orbital Infection
*   **Periorbital (Pre-septal) Cellulitis (眶前蜂窩組織炎):** Infection of eyelid & soft tissue **anterior** to the orbital septum (眼眶中隔).
*   **Orbital (Post-septal) Cellulitis (眼眶蜂窩組織炎):** Infection of orbital tissues **posterior** to the orbital septum. Medical emergency!
### Hong Kong Epidemiology Context
*   Predominantly pediatric presentation in HK HA AEDs.
*   Peak incidence: Winter/Spring (correlates with URTI/sinusitis seasons).
*   Most common age: <5 yrs (pre-septal), older children (post-septal).

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Anatomical Pathophysiology
*   **Orbital Septum (眼眶中隔):** Fibrous sheet acting as an anatomical barrier.
*   **Spread mechanism:** Orbit surrounded by paranasal sinuses. Thinnest bone = **Lamina papyracea** (紙樣板) (medial wall).
*   Ethmoid sinusitis (篩竇炎) → local spread via lamina papyracea → post-septal orbit.
### Common Causative Organisms
*   ***Staphylococcus aureus* (金黃葡萄球菌):** Including MRSA.
*   ***Streptococcus pneumoniae* (肺炎鏈球菌)**
*   ***Haemophilus influenzae* type b (Hib) (流感嗜血桿菌):** ↓ incidence post-HK childhood immunization.
*   ***Streptococcus pyogenes* (化膿性鏈球菌)**

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Signs of Pre-Septal Cellulitis
*   **Unilateral eyelid erythema (紅斑)**
*   **Eyelid edema (水腫)**
*   **Warmth & Induration (硬結)**
*   Normal eye examination underneath.
### Red Flag Signs of Post-Septal (Orbital) Cellulitis
*   **Proptosis (凸眼):** Globe pushed forward/downward.
*   **Ophthalmoplegia (眼肌麻痺):** Restricted/painful extraocular movements.
*   **↓ Visual Acuity (視力下降)**
*   **RAPD (+ve) (相對性傳入性瞳孔缺陷):** Indicates optic nerve compression.
*   **Chemosis (結膜水腫)**
*   **Systemic toxicity:** High fever, lethargy.

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Pre-Septal vs Post-Septal Cellulitis Differentiation
| Clinical Feature (臨床特徵) | Pre-Septal (眶前) | Post-Septal (眼眶) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Proptosis (凸眼)** | Absent | **Present** |
| **Eye Movement (眼球運動)** | Normal, painless | **Restricted, painful** |
| **Vision (視力)** | Normal | **↓ or Double vision (Diplopia)** |
| **Pupillary Reflex** | Normal | **Abnormal (RAPD +ve)** |
| **Chemosis (結膜水腫)** | Rare/Mild | **Severe** |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Common Ocular and Periorbital Differentials
*   **Allergic reaction / Insect bite (過敏反應/蚊蟲叮咬):** Bilateral or unilateral, sudden onset, intense pruritus, afebrile.
*   **Viral Conjunctivitis (病毒性結膜炎):** Bilateral, watery discharge, mild lid swelling.
*   **Idiopathic Orbital Inflammatory Disease / Pseudotumor:** Painful proptosis, recurrent, rapid response to steroids.
### Severe/Oncological Differentials
*   **Retinoblastoma (視網膜母細胞瘤):** Leukocoria (白瞳症), strabismus.
*   **Rhabdomyosarcoma (橫紋肌肉瘤):** Rapidly progressive unilateral proptosis, no fever/inflammation.

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside Clinical Evaluation
*   **Visual Acuity Test (視力測試):** Snellen chart / fixation.
*   **Pupillary Light Reflex:** Swinging flashlight test for RAPD.
*   **Colour Vision (色覺):** Red desaturation (紅綠色覺異常) = early optic nerve compromise.
### First-Line Laboratory Investigations
*   **CBC:** ↑ WBC (neutrophilia).
*   **Inflammatory Markers:** ↑ CRP & ↑ ESR.
*   **Blood Cultures (血培養):** Prior to IV Abx.
*   **Eye Swab:** If discharging wound or conjunctivitis present.
### Gold Standard Imaging
*   **Contrast CT Orbit & Paranasal Sinuses (電腦掃描):**
    *   Indication: Suspected post-septal, CNS signs, inability to assess vision (severe swelling), no improvement after 24-48h IV Abx.
    *   Look for: Subperiosteal abscess (骨膜下膿腫), sinus opacification, fat stranding.

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Outpatient Management for Mild Pre-Septal Cellulitis
*   **PO Co-amoxiclav (Augmentin) (阿莫西林/克拉維酸)**
*   Daily clinical review in HA AED or GOPC to ensure no progression.
### Inpatient HA Protocol for Post-Septal / Severe Pre-Septal
| Intervention | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Admission** | Paediatric ward + urgent Ophthalmology/ENT consult. |
| **Positioning** | Head elevation at 30° to ↓ orbital venous congestion. |
| **IV Antibiotics (1st Line)** | **IV Ceftriaxone (頭孢曲松)** + **IV Flucloxacillin** (or IV Augmentin) to cover MRSA/Anaerobes. |
| **Adjuncts** | Nasal decongestants (e.g., Xylometazoline) for sinusitis. |
### Surgical Management Indications
*   **FESS (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery) / Abscess Drainage:**
    *   Subperiosteal abscess > 10mm.
    *   Deterioration in vision / RAPD +ve.
    *   No improvement after 48h of optimal IV Abx.

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
*   **Paediatrics (兒科):** Primary admission, systemic Abx management.
*   **Ophthalmology (眼科):** Daily visual acuity and optic nerve monitoring.
*   **ENT (耳鼻喉科):** Surgical drainage of paranasal sinuses / abscess.
### Patient & Family Education
*   Explain need for repeated, uncomfortable eye examinations.
*   Counsel on the risk of rapid deterioration necessitating emergency surgery.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Trauma and Neglect Indicators
*   **Unexplained periorbital trauma:** Bruising without consistent mechanism (rule out NAI).
*   **Delayed presentation:** Parents seeking medical care only after severe visual loss or systemic sepsis develops.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Vision-Threatening Complications
*   **Optic Neuritis / Ischaemia (視神經炎):** → Permanent blindness (失明).
*   **Corneal Ulceration (角膜潰瘍):** Due to severe proptosis and exposure.
### Life-Threatening CNS Complications
*   **Cavernous Sinus Thrombosis (海綿竇血栓形成):** Bilateral eye signs, cranial nerve palsies (III, IV, V1, V2, VI).
*   **Meningitis (腦膜炎)**
*   **Intracranial Abscess (顱內膿腫):** Frontal lobe extension.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most critical anatomical barrier differentiating the severity of periorbital infections? Answer: The orbital septum (眼眶中隔).
* Viva Question: What is the most common anatomical source of infection for orbital cellulitis in children? Answer: The ethmoid sinus (篩竇) via the thin lamina papyracea.
* Trap: Assuming normal visual acuity rules out orbital cellulitis. Pearl: Check color vision (red desaturation) and RAPD, as optic nerve compromise can occur before overt visual acuity drop.
* Trap: Discharging a toddler with severe eyelid swelling on oral antibiotics without a successful eye exam. Pearl: If the eyelid cannot be opened manually to assess eye movement and vision, admit for IV antibiotics and consider CT imaging.
* Pitfall: Missing bilateral signs. Bilateral eye involvement or contralateral spread highly suggests Cavernous Sinus Thrombosis, a life-threatening complication requiring immediate aggressive intervention.
