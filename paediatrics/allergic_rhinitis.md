# Allergic Rhinitis & Conjunctivitis (過敏性鼻炎及結膜炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Allergic Rhinoconjunctivitis
* IgE-mediated type I hypersensitivity reaction
* Chronic inflammation of nasal and conjunctival mucosa
* Triggered by environmental aeroallergens
### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology
* **Prevalence**: ↑↑ (up to 40% of HK children)
* **Primary Triggers**: House dust mites (HDM - *Dermatophagoides pteronyssinus*, *Blomia tropicalis*), cockroaches, pet dander, mould
* **Exacerbating Factors**: ↑ HK humidity, ↑ air pollution (PM2.5)
* Core component of the **"Atopic March" (過敏進行曲)** (Eczema → Food Allergy → Allergic Rhinitis → Asthma)

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Early Phase Response (Minutes)
* Inhaled allergens cross nasal epithelium → bind to specific **IgE** on mast cells/basophils
* Cross-linking of IgE → Mast cell degranulation
* Release of preformed mediators (Histamine, Tryptase) → Vasodilation, ↑ vascular permeability, ↑ glandular secretion
* **Clinical Result**: Sneezing, intense pruritus, profuse clear rhinorrhea
### Late Phase Response (4-6 Hours)
* Release of newly synthesized mediators (Leukotrienes, Prostaglandins) & cytokines (IL-4, IL-5, IL-13)
* Chemotaxis & infiltration of **Eosinophils (嗜酸性粒細胞)**, Basophils, T-cells (Th2 profile)
* **Clinical Result**: Mucosal edema, hypertrophy, chronic severe nasal congestion/blockage
### Genetic Predisposition
* High concordance in monozygotic twins
* Filaggrin (FLG) gene mutations ↑ skin barrier permeability → predisposes to early allergen sensitization

## 3. Clinical Presentation
### Classic Paediatric Phenotype
* School-aged child presenting with chronic throat clearing, loud snoring, & sleep fragmentation
* Constant upward rubbing of the nose
* Chronic mouth-breathing (口呼吸) leading to dry lips & subsequent craniofacial changes (adenoid facies)

## 4. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Nasal Symptoms (鼻部症狀)
* Paroxysmal sneezing (打噴嚏)
* Profuse, clear, watery rhinorrhea (流鼻水)
* Severe, bilateral nasal congestion/blockage (鼻塞)
* Intense nasal pruritus (鼻痕癢)
### Ocular Symptoms (眼部症狀)
* Bilateral intensely itchy, red, watery eyes
* Gritty/burning sensation
### Pathognomonic Clinical Signs
* **Allergic Salute (過敏性敬禮)**: Upward rubbing of the nose
* **Transverse Nasal Crease**: Permanent horizontal line across the nasal bridge from chronic allergic salute
* **Allergic Shiners (過敏性黑眼圈)**: Dark infraorbital venous pooling due to chronic nasal congestion impeding venous return
* **Dennie-Morgan Lines**: Prominent extra skin folds/creases under the lower eyelids
* **Otoscopy/Rhinoscopy**: Pale, boggy (swollen), bluish-grey nasal turbinates (鼻甲); clear uninfected secretions
* **Ocular Signs**: Conjunctival injection, cobblestone papillae on upper tarsal conjunctiva

## 5. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Allergic Rhinitis vs. Viral URI (Common Cold)
* **Allergic Rhinitis**: Lasts weeks/months, strictly clear/watery secretions, prominent severe itching, paroxysmal sneezing, recurrent seasonal or perennial pattern, no fever.
* **Viral URI**: Lasts 7-10 days, secretions evolve from clear → thick/purulent (yellow/green), minimal itching, often accompanied by low-grade fever/sore throat/cough.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
### Diagnostic Differentials Table
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Viral URI (上呼吸道感染)** | Acute onset, resolves in 7-10 days, thick/coloured secretions, +ve fever/sore throat |
| **Adenoidal Hypertrophy (腺樣體肥大)** | Chronic snoring, mouth breathing, sleep apnea, **no** significant itching/sneezing |
| **Nasal Foreign Body (鼻腔異物)** | **Unilateral**, foul-smelling, purulent/bloody discharge, common in toddlers |
| **Chronic Rhinosinusitis (慢性鼻竇炎)** | Facial pain/pressure, purulent post-nasal drip, hyposmia (↓ smell), lasting >12 weeks |
| **Non-Allergic Rhinitis** | Vasomotor triggers (temperature, strong smells, spicy food), absent ocular symptoms, -ve allergy tests |

## 7. Tiered Investigations
### Bedside & Clinical Investigations
* Primarily a **clinical diagnosis** based on characteristic history & physical signs
### Specialised Allergy Testing
* **Skin Prick Test (皮膚點刺測試)**: Gold standard for aeroallergens. Highly sensitive & specific. Must stop oral antihistamines 3-5 days prior.
### Blood Tests
* **Serum Specific IgE (RAST/ImmunoCAP)**: Quantifies specific IgE against aeroallergens (HDM, pollens, pet dander). Safe in severe eczema/dermographism or if unable to stop antihistamines.
* **Total IgE & Eosinophil Count**: Non-specific; rarely used for primary diagnosis alone.
### Imaging
* **Lateral Neck X-ray**: To rule out adenoidal hypertrophy if profound snoring/OSA suspected.

## 8. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### Environmental Control (Non-Pharmacological)
* **HDM Avoidance (塵蟎控制)**: Wash bedding in hot water (>60°C) weekly, use anti-mite mattress/pillow encasements, remove carpets/stuffed toys, maintain indoor humidity <50% (use dehumidifiers).
* **Pet Dander**: Remove pets from bedroom, HEPA air filters.
### Stepwise Pharmacological Management
| Step / Severity | 1st Line Pharmacotherapy | Mechanism & Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Step 1: Mild/Intermittent** | **Oral 2nd Gen Antihistamines** (e.g., Cetirizine, Loratadine, Fexofenadine) | Blocks H1 receptors. Rapid onset. Non-sedating. Controls itch/sneezing but poor efficacy for severe congestion. |
| **Step 2: Moderate/Persistent** | **Intranasal Corticosteroids (INCS)** (e.g., Fluticasone, Mometasone) | **Gold Standard**. ↓ mucosal inflammation. Delayed onset (takes days). Highly effective for congestion. Minimal systemic absorption. |
| **Step 3: Refractory/Comorbid** | **Leukotriene Receptor Antagonists (LTRA)** (e.g., Montelukast) | Blocks leukotriene pathway. Highly effective if patient has concurrent **asthma**. |
| **Ocular Symptoms** | **Topical Antihistamines/Mast-cell stabilizers** (e.g., Olopatadine eye drops) | Rapid relief of allergic conjunctivitis. |
| **Step 4: Severe/Unresponsive** | **Allergen Immunotherapy (脫敏治療)** (Sublingual SLIT or Subcutaneous SCIT) | Disease-modifying. Induces immune tolerance (shifts Th2 → Th1/Treg). Takes 3-5 years of daily/regular therapy. |
### Additional HA Prescribing Practices
* **Nasal Saline Irrigation (洗鼻)**: Mechanical flushing of mucus & allergens; improves INCS mucosal penetration and efficacy.

## 9. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Impact on Quality of Life & Education
* Chronic nasal congestion → Sleep fragmentation & mild Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)
* Consequence: Chronic daytime fatigue, irritability, ↓ concentration, ADHD-like behavioral presentation
* Significantly impairs school performance and academic trajectory
* **Psychosocial Intervention**: Reversing the blockage transforms the child's academic trajectory. Ensure school nurses/teachers understand it is a chronic medical issue, not behavioral defiance.

## 10. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Recognizing Medical Neglect
* While primary non-accidental injury (NAI) is rarely applicable, repeated failure of parents to provide fundamental preventative medications (like INCS) leading to severe morbidity (e.g., severe OSA, growth failure, deafness from recurrent glue ear) may warrant assessment for medical neglect.

## 11. Complications & Prognosis
### Local Airway Complications
* **Chronic Rhinosinusitis (慢性鼻竇炎)**: Blocked ostia lead to secondary bacterial infections.
* **Eustachian Tube Dysfunction**: Chronic inflammation → Recurrent Acute Otitis Media (AOM) → Otitis Media with Effusion (OME / Glue Ear) → Conductive hearing loss & speech delay.
* **Adenoidal Hypertrophy**: Due to chronic lymphoid stimulation → Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA).
* **Asthma Exacerbation**: "One airway, one disease" concept. Uncontrolled AR directly triggers or worsens asthma control.
### Prognosis
* Chronic condition; symptoms often fluctuate environmentally.
* Proper INCS adherence yields excellent symptom control and prevents structural airway changes.

## 12. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Prescribing a 1st-generation antihistamine (e.g., Chlorpheniramine) for school-going children. Pearl: 1st-generation antihistamines cross the blood-brain barrier, causing profound daytime somnolence and cognitive impairment, severely affecting a child's ability to learn. Always prescribe 2nd-generation antihistamines (e.g., Cetirizine, Loratadine).
* Viva Question: A child on an intranasal corticosteroid (INCS) spray complains of frequent epistaxis (nosebleeds). What is the likely cause and how do you manage it? Answer: Poor spray technique. Spraying directly onto the highly vascular nasal septum (Kiesselbach's plexus) causes mucosal thinning and bleeding. Advise the patient to point the nozzle laterally, away from the septum (towards the ear on the same side) while spraying.
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents that a toddler with a unilateral, foul-smelling, purulent nasal discharge just has "severe allergic rhinitis". You must always exclude a nasal foreign body in this specific presentation.
