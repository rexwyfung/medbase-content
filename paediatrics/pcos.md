# Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) (多囊卵巢綜合症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of PCOS
* Endocrine d/o characterized by chronic anovulation (無排卵) & hyperandrogenism (雄激素過多)
* Diagnosis strictly requires exclusion of other aetiologies (e.g., CAH, Cushing's, thyroid dysfx, hyperprolactinaemia)
### HA Diagnostic Criteria (Rotterdam Criteria 2003)
* Requires $\ge$ 2 of 3 features for dx:
    1. Oligo- and/or anovulation (月經稀發)
    2. Clinical and/or biochemical signs of hyperandrogenism
    3. Polycystic ovaries on US ($\ge$ 12 follicles measuring 2-9mm, or ovarian volume > 10mL in at least one ovary)
### Local Epidemiology
* Affects ~5-15% of reproductive-age women globally; prevalent in HK Chinese population
* ↑ risk in South Asian populations
* Often presents in adolescence / early 20s

## 2. Pathophysiology & Genetics
### Core Pathophysiological Drivers
* **Insulin Resistance (IR) (胰島素阻抗)**: Central driver → hyperinsulinaemia → ↑ ovarian androgen production & ↓ hepatic Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG) synthesis → ↑ free (active) testosterone
* **Neuroendocrine Dysfx**: ↑ GnRH pulse frequency → ↑ LH secretion relative to FSH → LH:FSH ratio > 2:1 (common but not mandatory for dx)
* **Ovarian Dysfx**: ↑ LH stimulates theca cells → ↑ androstenedione & testosterone. Relative ↓ FSH → impaired follicle maturation & poor aromatization of androgens to oestrogens → follicular arrest (cysts)
### Genetic & Environmental Factors
* Polygenic inheritance pattern; high familial concordance
* Exacerbated by obesity (adipose tissue ↑ peripheral conversion of androgens to oestrone via aromatase)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Menstrual Irregularities
* Oligomenorrhoea (cycle length > 35 days) or secondary amenorrhoea (> 6 months)
* Infertility (不育) / subfertility due to anovulation
### Hyperandrogenic Features
* **Hirsutism (多毛症)**: Terminal hair growth in male-pattern areas (Ferriman-Gallwey score)
* Acne (暗瘡) - persistent, cystic, often jawline/lower face
* Male-pattern alopecia (脫髮)
### Metabolic Signs
* Obesity (肥胖) (centripetal) - ~50-60% of pts
* **Acanthosis Nigricans (黑棘皮症)**: Velvety, hyperpigmented plaques on neck/axillae (marker of severe IR)
* Skin tags

## 4. Discriminatory Features (How to distinguish from similar conditions)
### Differentiating PCOS vs. Late-Onset CAH (先天性腎上腺增生症)
* CAH: ↑↑ 17-OH progesterone (17-OHP) > 6 nmol/L (requires synacthen test for confirmation)
* PCOS: Normal or borderline ↑ 17-OHP
### Differentiating PCOS vs. Cushing's Syndrome
* Cushing's: Central obesity + striae + proximal myopathy + moon facies. Requires overnight dexamethasone suppression test or 24h urinary free cortisol
* PCOS: Lacks classical Cushingoid striae/myopathy
### Differentiating PCOS vs. Androgen-Secreting Tumour
* Tumour: Rapid onset (<1 year) & severe virilization (clitoromegaly, voice deepening, male muscle mass). Serum testosterone typically markedly elevated (> 5 nmol/L)
* PCOS: Gradual onset, peripubertal, testosterone slightly elevated but < 5 nmol/L

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
### Comprehensive DDx Table
| Condition | Distinguishing Clinical/Lab Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Late-Onset CAH** | ↑ 17-OH progesterone (diagnostic) |
| **Cushing's Syndrome** | Cushingoid habitus, abnormal dexamethasone suppression |
| **Ovarian/Adrenal Tumour**| Rapid virilization, markedly ↑ testosterone (>5 nmol/L) or DHEAS |
| **Hyperprolactinaemia** | Galactorrhoea, markedly ↑ prolactin, MRI brain (adenoma) |
| **Thyroid Dysfunction** | Hypo/Hyperthyroid signs, abnormal TSH / free T4 |
| **Hypothalamic Amenorrhoea**| Low BMI, extreme exercise, ↓ LH, ↓ FSH, ↓ Oestradiol |

## 6. Tiered Investigations
### First-Line Biochemical Profile
* Total Testosterone & SHBG → calculate Free Androgen Index (FAI)
* LH & FSH (day 2-5 of cycle) → look for LH:FSH ratio > 2:1
* Prolactin, TSH, Free T4 (rule out DDx)
* 17-OH Progesterone (follicular phase, ideally morning) to rule out CAH
### Metabolic Screening (Crucial in HA setting)
* Fasting glucose & HbA1c (糖化血紅素) (rule out T2DM/Prediabetes)
* Fasting lipid profile (dyslipidaemia common)
* OGTT (Oral Glucose Tolerance Test) if BMI > 25 kg/m² or high-risk ethnicity
### Imaging Studies
* **Transvaginal Ultrasound (TVUS) (陰道超聲波)**: Gold standard for checking ovarian morphology (string of pearls sign). *Note: Transabdominal US used for virgins/adolescents, but lower sensitivity.*

## 7. Management (Acute, Chronic, HA Protocols)
### 1st Line: Lifestyle Modifications
* **Weight loss (減重)** (Target 5-10% body weight) → restores ovulation, ↓ hyperandrogenism, ↓ IR. Primary therapy for obese pts.
* Exercise & dietary modification
### Symptom-Directed Pharmacotherapy: Menstrual Control & Endometrial Protection
* **Combined Oral Contraceptive Pill (COCP) (綜合口服避孕藥)**: 1st line. Induces regular shedding, ↓ LH, ↑ SHBG (binds free androgens). HA preferred: low-dose estrogen with non-androgenic progestin (e.g., desogestrel) or anti-androgenic (e.g., cyproterone acetate - Dianette).
* **Intermittent Progestogen**: E.g., Medroxyprogesterone acetate (Provera) 10mg daily for 10-14 days every 1-3 months. Induces withdrawal bleed to prevent endometrial hyperplasia if pt declines COCP.
### Symptom-Directed Pharmacotherapy: Hyperandrogenism
* COCP (as above)
* Anti-androgens (Add-on to COCP if refractory after 6 months): Spironolactone (螺內酯), Cyproterone acetate. *Teratogenic: must ensure effective contraception.*
* Topical Eflornithine (Vaniqa) for facial hirsutism
### Symptom-Directed Pharmacotherapy: Metabolic & Infertility
* **Metformin (二甲雙胍)**: Off-label but widely used in HK HA. Improves IR, modest weight loss, helps restore ovulation.
* **Ovulation Induction (排卵刺激)**:
    * 1st Line: **Letrozole** (Aromatase inhibitor) - currently preferred over Clomiphene due to better live birth rates and mono-follicular development.
    * Alt 1st Line: **Clomiphene citrate** (SERM)
    * 2nd Line: Gonadotropins or Laparoscopic Ovarian Drilling (LOD)

## 8. MDT & Psychosocial Care
### Multidisciplinary Team Involvement
* **Gynaecologist/Endocrinologist**: Disease monitoring, fertility rx
* **Dietitian (營養師)**: Weight management, low glycaemic index diets
* **Dermatologist**: Severe acne / hirsutism rx
### Psychosocial Support
* High prevalence of depression, anxiety, & disordered eating.
* Screen for body image distress (hirsutism/obesity) & refer to Clinical Psychologist if indicated.

## 9. Safeguarding & NAI Red Flags
### Recognizing Vulnerabilities in PCOS
* NAI less directly applicable, but adolescents with PCOS are highly vulnerable to **Eating Disorders** (Bulimia/Binge Eating) due to weight-related distress.
* Guard against inappropriate cosmetic procedures in minors without proper medical management first.

## 10. Complications & Prognosis
### Long-Term Metabolic Complications
* **Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) (二型糖尿病)** & Gestational Diabetes
* Dyslipidaemia & ↑ Cardiovascular disease risk
* Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA)
### Gynaecological & Obstetric Complications
* **Endometrial Hyperplasia/Carcinoma (子宮內膜癌)**: Due to unopposed oestrogen from chronic anovulation. (Requires minimum 3-4 bleeds per year to protect endometrium).
* Pregnancy complications: ↑ risk of miscarriage, pre-eclampsia, GDM.

## 11. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What are the three components of the Rotterdam criteria? Answer: Oligo/anovulation, clinical/biochemical hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on ultrasound (need 2 of 3).
* Viva Question: Why do women with PCOS have an increased risk of endometrial cancer? Answer: Chronic anovulation leads to continuous unopposed oestrogen stimulation of the endometrium without the protective shedding effect of progesterone.
* Trap: Assuming every patient with polycystic ovaries on ultrasound has PCOS. Pearl: Up to 20% of normal women have polycystic ovaries on US. Diagnosis requires the syndrome (menstrual or androgenic symptoms) plus exclusion of other causes.
* Pitfall: Prescribing anti-androgens (like spironolactone) to a woman actively trying to conceive or without adequate contraception. Explanation of how to avoid: Anti-androgens can feminize a male fetus. Always ensure highly effective contraception (like a COCP or IUD) before initiating.
* Pitfall: Diagnosing PCOS based solely on US findings in a 15-year-old. Explanation of how to avoid: Polycystic ovarian morphology is common and normal in normal puberty. Avoid US for PCOS diagnosis within 8 years of menarche; rely on clinical signs of hyperandrogenism and persistent oligomenorrhoea.
