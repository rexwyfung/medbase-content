# Urethral Trauma (尿道創傷)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Urethral Trauma
* **Injury to the urethra (尿道)** 
* **Classification:** 
* **Anterior Urethral Injury (前尿道創傷):** Involves bulbar (球部) or pendulous/penile (陰莖部) urethra.
* **Posterior Urethral Injury (後尿道創傷):** Involves membranous (膜部) or prostatic (前列腺部) urethra.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Aetiology
* **Gender:** Heavily Male > Female (due to anatomical length & pelvic fracture mechanisms).
* **Posterior Injuries:** ~90% associated with **Pelvic Fractures (骨盆骨折)**.
* **Common HK Mechanisms:** High-speed MVAs (e.g., mini-bus accidents), industrial falls in construction sites (HA trauma registry data).
* **Anterior Injuries:** Typically **Straddle Injuries (跨騎傷)** (e.g., falling on bicycle crossbars/fences) or iatrogenic (traumatic Foley catheterization/endoscopy).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Anterior vs Posterior Urethral Anatomy
| Feature | Anterior Urethra (前尿道) | Posterior Urethra (後尿道) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Components** | Penile (pendulous), Bulbar | Membranous, Prostatic |
| **Location** | Distal to urogenital diaphragm | Proximal to/within urogenital diaphragm |
| **Mechanism** | Crush injury against symphysis pubis | Shearing forces from pelvic ring disruption |

### Fascial Layers & Haematoma Pathophysiology
* **Buck's Fascia (陰莖深筋膜) Intact:** 
* **Patho:** Haematoma/urine confined to penile shaft.
* **Sign:** "Penile sleeve" haematoma.
* **Buck's Fascia Ruptured (Colles' Fascia Intact):** 
* **Patho:** Extravasation into perineum, scrotum, anterior abdominal wall.
* **Limit:** Bounded by fascial attachments (fascia lata of thigh, Dartos of scrotum).
* **Sign:** Classic **Butterfly Haematoma (蝴蝶狀血腫)** in perineum.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Classic Triad of Urethral Injury
* **Blood at urethral meatus (尿道口滴血)** (Most consistent sign)
* **Inability to void (無法排尿)**
* **Palpable distended bladder (膀胱脹滿)**

### Examination Findings for Anterior Urethral Injury
* **Perineal haematoma (會陰血腫)** 
* **Scrotal ecchymosis/swelling (陰囊瘀血)**
* **Sleeve haematoma (陰莖血腫)**

### Examination Findings for Posterior Urethral Injury
* **Pelvic instability/crepitus (骨盆不穩)**
* **High-riding prostate (前列腺上移)** (on Digital Rectal Exam / DRE; +/- boggy haematoma in prostatic bed)
* **Perineal bruising (會陰瘀傷)** (Late sign)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis for Post-Traumatic Lower Urinary Symptoms
| Diagnosis | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Urethral Trauma (尿道創傷)** | +ve Blood at meatus, +ve High-riding prostate, Normal bladder contour |
| **Extraperitoneal Bladder Rupture (膀胱破裂)** | -ve Blood at meatus, Gross haematuria (if catheterized), Pelvic fracture +ve |
| **Intraperitoneal Bladder Rupture** | Signs of peritonitis (腹膜炎), ↑ Urea/Creatinine (peritoneal reabsorption) |
| **Renal Trauma (腎創傷)** | Loin ecchymosis (Grey Turner's sign), Flank mass, Gross haematuria |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Primary Diagnostic Imaging for Suspected Urethral Trauma
* **Gold Standard:** **Retrograde Urethrogram (RGU) (逆行性尿道造影)**
* **Timing:** MUST be performed **BEFORE** any attempt at urethral catheterization.
* **Method:** 20-30mL water-soluble contrast injected slowly via meatus with 15-20mL Foley balloon inflated in fossa navicularis.
* **Finding:** Extravasation of contrast (造影劑外滲) +/- non-opacification of proximal urethra/bladder.

### Secondary Trauma Workup Imaging
* **CT Pelvis (骨盆電腦掃描):** 
* **Indication:** Assess associated pelvic ring fractures/haematoma.
* **CT Cystogram (電腦掃描膀胱造影):** 
* **Indication:** To rule out concomitant bladder rupture (can be done if suprapubic catheter is placed first).

### AAST Urethral Injury Grading Scale
| Grade | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **I** | Contusion: Blood at meatus, normal RGU |
| **II** | Stretch injury: Elongation of urethra without extravasation |
| **III** | Partial disruption: Extravasation of contrast at injury site with contrast in bladder |
| **IV** | Complete disruption (<2cm): Extravasation with NO contrast in bladder |
| **V** | Complete disruption (>2cm) or extension into prostate/vagina |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Acute Emergency Management (HA Trauma Protocol)
* **CRITICAL RULE:** **DO NOT** attempt blind urethral Foley catheterization if triad present (risk of converting partial → complete tear, introducing infection).
* **Resuscitation:** ATLS protocol (ABCDE) → stabilize pelvic fracture (pelvic binder).

### Immediate Urological Management Algorithm
* **Grade I-II (Contusion/Stretch):**
* **Action:** Trial of gentle Foley catheter insertion by Urologist (1 attempt only). If successful, leave for 7-14 days.
* **Grade III-V (Partial/Complete Disruption):**
* **1st Line (HK HA Standard):** **Suprapubic Catheterization (SPC) (恥骨上膀胱造口術)** under USG guidance.
* **Alternative (Selected Centres):** Primary early endoscopic realignment (within 72 hours) via flexible cystoscopy + RGU if patient haemodynamically stable.

### Delayed Management (Definitive Repair)
* **Timing:** Wait 3-6 months post-injury for pelvic haematoma resolution & scar stabilization.
* **Pre-op Assessment:** Combined antegrade cystourethrogram (via SPC) + RGU to define stricture length.
* **Procedure:** **Delayed Anastomotic Urethroplasty (延期尿道吻合術)**.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Suprapubic Catheter (SPC) Insertion Principles
* **Requirement:** Must have a distended, palpable bladder or USG guidance.
* **Location:** 2 finger-breadths above symphysis pubis in midline.
* **Caution:** Avoid bowel injury (USG ensures no bowel loops anterior to bladder).

### Delayed Anastomotic Urethroplasty Principles
* **Position:** Exaggerated lithotomy position.
* **Incision:** Midline perineal incision.
* **Key Steps:**
* **Excision:** Complete excision of the fibrotic stricture segment.
* **Mobilization:** Extensive bulbar mobilization to achieve tension-free anastomosis.
* **Anastomosis:** Spatulated, mucosa-to-mucosa, tension-free anastomosis over a silicone catheter.

### Consent for Urethral Trauma Interventions
* **Immediate Risks (SPC):** Bowel perforation, bleeding, blockage, recurrent UTI.
* **Long-Term Trauma/Surgical Risks:** 
* **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄):** ~100% in complete transection managed with SPC alone; recurrent stricture post-urethroplasty.
* **Erectile Dysfunction (勃起功能障礙) (ED):** Damage to cavernous nerves during original trauma (rarely from delayed surgery).
* **Urinary Incontinence (尿失禁):** Damage to sphincter mechanisms.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Post-SPC Insertion Care
* **Catheter Care:** Regular flushing to prevent clot retention; change every 4-6 weeks.
* **Infection Control:** Oral antibiotics (e.g., Cephalexin) per HA biogram; adequate hydration.

### Post-Urethroplasty Care
* **Catheter Duration:** Urethral catheter left in situ for 2-3 weeks.
* **Voiding Trial:** Performed only AFTER a **Pericatheter Urethrogram** confirms no contrast extravasation at the anastomosis site.
* **Follow-up:** Uroflowmetry (尿流率測試) at 3, 6, 12 months to monitor for stricture recurrence (Qmax <15 mL/s warrants investigation).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications
* **Pelvic abscess / sepsis:** From infected haematoma or extravasated urine.
* **Severe haemorrhage:** From initial pelvic fracture/venous plexus tear.

### Late Complications & Prognosis
* **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄):** Most common. May require repeated direct visual internal urethrotomy (DVIU) or redo urethroplasty.
* **Erectile Dysfunction:** Incidence ranges from 20-60% depending on initial crush severity. Often unresponsive to PDE5 inhibitors; may require penile prosthesis (陰莖假體).
* **Urinary Incontinence:** Up to 10% risk, particularly if both internal (bladder neck) and external sphincters are injured.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of posterior urethral injury? Answer: Blood at the urethral meatus, inability to void, and a palpable distended bladder.
* Viva Question: What is the definitive initial diagnostic test for a patient with suspected urethral injury? Answer: Retrograde urethrogram (RGU) prior to any catheterization attempts.
* Trap: A trauma patient has a pelvic fracture and needs urine output monitoring, so the ED resident inserts a Foley catheter. Pearl: Blind catheterization is strictly contraindicated if there is blood at the meatus or a high-riding prostate; an RGU must be done first to avoid completing a partial urethral tear.
* Pitfall: Attempting blind SPC insertion in a hypovolaemic trauma patient with an empty bladder. This risks bowel perforation. Always use ultrasound guidance to confirm bladder distension.
* Viva Question: What determines the anatomical boundaries of the "butterfly" perineal haematoma in anterior urethral trauma? Answer: Rupture of Buck's fascia with an intact Colles' fascia restricts fluid to the perineum, scrotum, and anterior abdominal wall, strictly bound by the fascia lata of the thighs and Dartos fascia.
