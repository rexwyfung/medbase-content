# Hemorrhoids (痔瘡)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Hemorrhoids
* **Hemorrhoids (痔瘡)** = Symptomatic enlargement & distal displacement of normal anal cushions (肛門墊)
* Consist of blood vessels, smooth muscle (Treitz's muscle), & connective tissue

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Risk Factors
* Highly prevalent in HK adult population (up to 30-50%)
* ↑ Risk with:
	* **Chronic constipation (便秘)** (low-fiber diet common in HK)
	* **Prolonged straining (用力排便)**
	* **Pregnancy (懷孕)**
	* **Portal hypertension (門脈高壓)**
	* Aging (↓ connective tissue support)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Surgical Anatomy of Anal Cushions
* **Location**: Right anterior (11 o'clock), right posterior (7 o'clock), left lateral (3 o'clock) (lithotomy position)
* **Blood Supply**:
	* **Superior Rectal Artery (直腸上動脈)** → Internal hemorrhoids (內痔)
	* **Middle & Inferior Rectal Arteries (直腸中/下動脈)** → External hemorrhoids (外痔)
* **Innervation**:
	* **Above Dentate Line (齒狀線)**: Visceral (painless)
	* **Below Dentate Line**: Somatic (pudendal nerve → painful)

### Pathophysiology of Hemorrhoidal Disease
* **Sliding Anal Cushion Theory**:
	* Breakdown of suspensory ligaments (Parks' ligament) → ↓ structural support
	* ↑ Shearing force during defecation → Venous engorgement & prolapse
	* Overlying mucosa susceptible to trauma → Bleeding

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Key Clinical Symptoms
* **Painless Fresh Per-Rectal Bleeding (無痛鮮血便)** (Classic internal hemorrhoid presentation)
* **Blood Splashing in Toilet Bowl** or on toilet paper
* **Anal Prolapse (肛門脫垂)** during/after defecation
* **Pruritus Ani (肛門瘙癢)** (Mucus discharge from prolapsed mucosa)
* **Severe Anal Pain (劇烈肛門痛)** (Only if thrombosed external hemorrhoid or strangulated internal hemorrhoid)

### Important Clinical Signs on Physical Examination
* **Inspection**: Skin tags, external hemorrhoids, perianal dermatitis, prolapsed purple/red masses
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE, 直腸指檢)**:
	* Uncomplicated internal hemorrhoids often NOT palpable (too soft)
	* Essential to exclude low rectal masses/tumors
* **Proctoscopy (直腸鏡檢查)**: Visualizes bulging, engorged purplish anal cushions

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Differential Diagnosis of Per-Rectal Bleeding

| Differential Diagnosis                 | Discriminatory Features                                         |
| :------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌)**            | Altered bowel habit, weight loss, older age, admixed dark blood |
| **Anal Fissure (肛裂)**                  | Tearing pain *during* defecation, sentinel tag, tight sphincter |
| **Diverticular Bleeding (憩室出血)**       | Large volume, painless, dark/maroon blood, older patient        |
| **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (炎症性腸病)** | Diarrhea, mucus, abdominal pain, systemic symptoms              |
| **Rectal Prolapse (直腸脫垂)**             | Concentric mucosal rings (vs radial in hemorrhoids)             |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Tiered Investigations for Hemorrhoids Workup
* **1st Line**: **Proctoscopy (直腸鏡檢查)** (Gold standard for direct visualization/grading)
* **Blood Tests**: **Complete Blood Count (全血細胞計數)** (Assess for anemia from chronic bleeding)
* **Colorectal Cancer Screening (HK HA Protocol)**:
	* **Flexible Sigmoidoscopy (乙狀結腸鏡)** or **Colonoscopy (大腸鏡)**
	* Indicated if: Age >50, +ve family history, altered bowel habit, iron deficiency anemia, +ve FIT test

### Goligher's Classification of Internal Hemorrhoids (Grading)

| Grade | Clinical Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Grade I (第一期)** | Bleed but do NOT prolapse |
| **Grade II (第二期)** | Prolapse with defecation → Spontaneous reduction |
| **Grade III (第三期)** | Prolapse with defecation → Requires manual reduction |
| **Grade IV (第四期)** | Permanently prolapsed → Irreducible, risk of strangulation |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Conservative Management (First-Line for Grade I-II)
* **Dietary Modification**: ↑ Fluid intake, ↑ Dietary fiber (纖維) (Psyllium husk)
* **Bowel Habit Training**: Avoid prolonged sitting on toilet (>5 mins), avoid straining
* **Sitz Baths (溫水坐浴)**: Relieves sphincter spasm

### Medical Management Options
* **Topical Agents**: Corticosteroid/local anesthetic creams (e.g., Ultraproct, Proctosedyl) (Short-term use <7-10 days)
* **Phlebotonics**: Daflon (Micronized purified flavonoid fraction) → ↓ venous tone/capillary hyperpermeability
* **Laxatives (瀉藥)**: Lactulose or Macrogol to soften stool

### Outpatient Procedures (For Grade I-III)
* **Rubber Band Ligation (RBL, 橡皮圈結紮術)**:
	* 1st line for Grade II/III
	* Must place band ≥2cm *above* dentate line (avoid severe pain)
* **Injection Sclerotherapy (注射硬化治療)**:
	* 5% Phenol in almond oil injected into submucosa → Fibrosis & fixation

### Definitive Surgical Indications
* Grade III-IV failing conservative/outpatient therapy
* Mixed internal & external hemorrhoids
* Strangulated/gangrenous hemorrhoids
* Patient preference for definitive treatment

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Key Operative Principles: Milligan-Morgan Open Hemorrhoidectomy (開放式痔瘡切除術)
* **Positioning**: Lithotomy or Jackknife (prone)
* **Incision**: V-shaped incision around hemorrhoidal pedicle
* **Dissection**: Dissect hemorrhoid off internal anal sphincter (內括約肌) to avoid incontinence
* **Ligation**: Transfix and ligate the vascular pedicle at the apex
* **Wound Care**: Leave mucosal/skin defects open to heal by secondary intention (standard in HK HA)
* **Preservation of Skin Bridges**: MUST leave adequate muco-cutaneous bridges between excision sites to prevent stricture

### Key Operative Principles: Stapled Hemorrhoidopexy (PPH, 痔瘡環狀切除術)
* **Mechanism**: Circumferential excision of redundant rectal mucosa above dentate line + lifting (pexy) of prolapsed tissue
* **Advantage**: Less post-op pain (incision is in insensate zone)
* **Disadvantage**: Higher recurrence rate for prolapse

### Pre-operative Consent Complications
* **Post-operative Bleeding (術後出血)** (Primary or Secondary at 7-10 days)
* **Severe Pain (劇烈疼痛)**
* **Urinary Retention (尿瀦留)**
* **Anal Stricture (肛門狹窄)** (Rare but severe)
* **Fecal Incontinence (大便失禁)** (Damage to internal sphincter)

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative Care Protocols
* **Analgesia (止痛)**: Multi-modal (Paracetamol + NSAIDs + weak opioids). Avoid codeine (causes constipation)
* **Spasm Relief**: Metronidazole (associated with ↓ post-op pain), warm sitz baths
* **Bowel Regulation**: Routine bulk-forming and osmotic laxatives starting Day 0
* **Monitoring**: Observe for urinary retention (common due to reflex pudendal spasm)

### Discharge & Rehabilitation Criteria
* Ability to void urine spontaneously
* Pain well-controlled on oral analgesia
* Tolerating oral diet
* Safe discharge with outpatient follow-up in Surgical SOPD

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Surgical Complications (<30 Days)
* **Acute Urinary Retention (急性尿瀦留)**: Most common (~10-15%) → requires temporary catheter
* **Primary Hemorrhage (原發性出血)**: Slipped ligature → requires return to OT
* **Secondary Hemorrhage (繼發性出血)**: Days 7-14 due to sloughing of eschar/infection → Admit, IV antibiotics, rarely requires OT
* **Fecal Impaction (糞便嵌塞)**: Due to fear of defecation from pain

### Late Surgical Complications (>30 Days)
* **Anal Stricture / Stenosis (肛門狹窄)**: Due to over-excision of anoderm (loss of skin bridges)
* **Fecal Incontinence (大便失禁)**: Sphincter injury or impaired anal cushion flatus sealing
* **Recurrence (復發)**: ~5% for open hemorrhoidectomy, higher for RBL/Stapled
* **Chronic Anal Pain (慢性肛門痛)**: Unresolved sphincter spasm or neuroma

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What anatomical landmarks define the position of primary internal hemorrhoids? Answer: 3, 7, and 11 o'clock positions in the lithotomy position.
* Viva Question: Why do internal hemorrhoids present with painless bleeding, while external hemorrhoids cause pain? Answer: Internal hemorrhoids are located above the dentate line (visceral innervation), whereas external hemorrhoids are below it (somatic pudendal innervation).
* Trap: Assuming PR bleeding in an elderly patient is strictly from their known, visually obvious hemorrhoids. Pearl: Always exclude colorectal malignancy with flexible sigmoidoscopy or colonoscopy in patients >50 or with any red flag symptoms.
* Pitfall: Banding an external hemorrhoid or placing the band on an internal hemorrhoid too close to the dentate line. This causes excruciating somatic pain and requires immediate removal of the band.
* Viva Question: A patient presents on post-op day 10 after a Milligan-Morgan hemorrhoidectomy with sudden heavy PR bleeding. What is the diagnosis and cause? Answer: Secondary hemorrhage due to sloughing of the eschar or local wound infection.
* Pitfall: Excising all redundant perianal tissue circumferentially during open hemorrhoidectomy. This reliably leads to severe post-operative anal stricture; always preserve sufficient muco-cutaneous skin bridges.
