# Breast Reconstruction (乳房重建)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Breast Reconstruction
* **Surgical restoration** of breast volume, contour, symmetry, and nipple-areolar complex post-**Mastectomy (乳房切除術)** or severe trauma
* **Goals**: ↑ Psychosocial well-being, ↑ body image, restore anatomical symmetry

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Breast Cancer (乳癌)**: #1 most common female cancer in HK (~5000 new cases/year)
* **Reconstruction rate**: Traditionally low (~10-20%) due to cultural factors, but ↑ rapidly in **Hospital Authority (HA)** centers (e.g., Queen Mary Hospital, Prince of Wales Hospital) due to ↑ joint oncoplastic clinics
* **Demographics**: ↑ demand in younger patients (< 55 years) and those with **BRCA1/2 gene mutations (BRCA基因突變)** opting for prophylactic mastectomy

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Relevant Anterior Chest Wall Anatomy
* **Pectoralis Major Muscle (胸大肌)**: Key coverage for subpectoral implants. Blood supply: **Thoracoacromial artery (胸肩峰動脈)**
* **Serratus Anterior Muscle (前鋸肌)**: Lateral coverage for implants
* **Infarction zones**: Mastectomy skin flaps rely on subdermal plexus; excessive thinning → skin necrosis

### Deep Inferior Epigastric Perforator (DIEP) Flap Anatomy
* **Donor site**: Lower abdomen (infraumbilical fat/skin)
* **Blood supply**: **Deep inferior epigastric artery/vein (腹壁下深動靜脈)**
* **Muscle sparing**: Preserves **Rectus abdominis (腹直肌)** → ↓ donor site morbidity

### Latissimus Dorsi (LD) Flap Anatomy
* **Donor site**: Back (skin/muscle island)
* **Blood supply**: **Thoracodorsal artery/vein (胸背動靜脈)**
* **Innervation**: Thoracodorsal nerve (often divided to prevent flap twitching)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Indications for Reconstruction Consultation
* **Primary**: Diagnosis of operable breast cancer requiring mastectomy
* **Secondary**: Severe breast asymmetry post-Breast Conserving Surgery (BCS)
* **Prophylactic**: High-risk genetic carriers (BRCA1/2) planning risk-reducing mastectomy

### Pre-operative Physical Assessment Findings
* **Skin quality**: Prior irradiation → thick, fibrotic skin (poor for implants)
* **Contralateral breast**: Assess size, ptosis, and need for symmetrization surgery (reduction/mastopexy)
* **Abdominal donor site (for DIEP/TRAM)**: Assess pinchable fat, prior scars (e.g., Pfannenstiel scar is safe, Kocher/midline scar may disrupt perforators)
* **Back donor site (for LD)**: Assess tissue bulk, shoulder mobility

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Comparison of Reconstructive Modalities
| Feature | **Implant-Based (植入物)** | **Autologous Flap (自體組織)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Material** | Silicone/Saline | DIEP, TRAM, LD Flap |
| **Operative Time** | Short (1-2 hrs) | Long (4-8 hrs for microsurgery) |
| **Recovery** | Fast (2-4 weeks) | Slow (4-8 weeks) |
| **Donor Site Morbidity** | None | Yes (Abdomen, Back) |
| **Feel / Naturalness** | Firmer, fixed shape | Soft, ages naturally with patient |
| **Impact of Radiotherapy** | High risk of **Capsular Contracture (包膜攣縮)** | Better tolerance, volume remains stable |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Pre-operative Blood Workup
* **Complete Blood Picture (CBP)**
* **Liver & Renal Function Tests (LRFT)**
* **Coagulation Profile (PT/APTT)**
* **Type & Screen (T&S)**: Crucial for free flap surgeries

### Pre-operative Flap Vascular Imaging
* **CT Angiogram (CTA) Abdomen/Pelvis**: Gold standard for DIEP flap
* **Purpose**: Map perforators originating from Deep Inferior Epigastric Artery
* **Doppler Ultrasound**: Alternative for perforator mapping if CTA contraindicated (e.g., severe contrast allergy)

### Oncological Clearance & Staging
* **Mammogram / Breast Ultrasound**: Assess bilateral breast disease
* **PET-CT / Bone Scan + CT Thorax/Abdomen**: Exclude distant metastasis (Stage IV disease is a relative contraindication to complex reconstruction)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Timing of Reconstruction Algorithm
* **Immediate Reconstruction (立即重建)**:
  * Done at time of mastectomy
  * **Indications**: Early-stage disease (Stage I/II), prophylactic mastectomy, skin-sparing/nipple-sparing mastectomy (SSM/NSM)
* **Delayed Reconstruction (延遲重建)**:
  * Done months/years after oncology treatments
  * **Indications**: Inflammatory breast cancer, locally advanced disease (Stage III) requiring **Post-Mastectomy Radiotherapy (PMRT - 術後放射治療)**, patient preference

### HA Guidelines for Modality Selection
* **Adequate Skin + No PMRT** → Implant / Tissue Expander
* **Prior PMRT + Poor Skin Quality** → Autologous Flap (LD or DIEP)
* **Large Contralateral Breast** → Autologous Flap (better volume matching)
* **Inadequate Abdominal Tissue / Prior Abdominal Surgery** → LD Flap + Implant

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Steps for Tissue Expander / Implant
* **Pocket Creation**: Subpectoral (classic) vs Prepectoral (modern HA trend)
* **Acellular Dermal Matrix (ADM - 脫細胞真皮基質)**: Used as a sling to support lower pole of prepectoral implant
* **Expansion**: Saline injected periodically over weeks in outpatient clinic
* **Exchange**: 2nd stage surgery swapping expander for permanent silicone implant

### Operative Steps for DIEP Free Flap
* **Harvest**: Dissect infraumbilical skin/fat, isolate perforators through rectus muscle
* **Recipient Vessels**: Prepare Internal Mammary Artery/Vein (IMA/IMV)
* **Microsurgery**: Arterial and venous anastomosis under microscope
* **Insetting**: Shape flap into breast pocket, monitor vascular pedicle

### Critical Consent Issues in Breast Reconstruction
* **Risk of Flap Loss**: < 2% total failure rate (requires immediate re-operation)
* **Donor Site Complications**: Abdominal weakness, bulge, hernia
* **Implant Risks**: Rupture, infection, **Breast Implant-Associated Anaplastic Large Cell Lymphoma (BIA-ALCL)** (rare but mandatory to mention)
* **Need for Revisions**: Nipple reconstruction and tattooing are separate, future procedures

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Free Flap Monitoring Protocol
* **Frequency**: Q1H for first 24-48 hours
* **Clinical signs**: Skin color, temperature, capillary refill time (ideal: 1-2 seconds)
* **Doppler**: Handheld surface Doppler to confirm arterial/venous signal
* **Red Flags**:
  * Pale, empty, absent pulse → **Arterial Thrombosis (動脈血栓)**
  * Congested, purple, rapid brisk refill → **Venous Congestion (靜脈充血)**

### Post-operative Drain & Wound Management
* **Suction Drains**: Kept in axilla, breast, and donor site
* **Removal Criteria**: Output < 30ml / 24 hours
* **Abdominal Binder**: Worn for 6 weeks post-DIEP/TRAM to support rectus fascia

### ERAS and Mobility Protocol
* **Positioning**: "Beach chair" position initially to ↓ abdominal wound tension
* **Mobilization**: Day 1 dangle legs, Day 2 ambulate with assistance
* **DVT Prophylaxis**: LMWH (Clexane) started 12-24 hours post-op + TED stockings

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (< 2 weeks)
* **Flap Ischemia / Thrombosis**: Surgical emergency, requires re-exploration
* **Hematoma**: Common in large raw surface areas (LD flap back site)
* **Mastectomy Skin Flap Necrosis**: Dusky skin edges, requires debridement

### Intermediate Complications (2-6 weeks)
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**: Salvageable with IV antibiotics; implant may require removal if refractory
* **Seroma (血清腫)**: Accumulation of fluid at donor site (esp. LD back site), requires serial aspiration

### Late Complications (> 6 weeks)
* **Capsular Contracture**: Hard, painful implant. Risk ↑↑ with PMRT
* **Fat Necrosis (脂肪壞死)**: Firm, painless lump in autologous flap; benign but mimics cancer recurrence (requires core biopsy/USG to differentiate)
* **Abdominal Hernia / Bulge**: Rare in DIEP, higher risk in full TRAM flaps

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the optimal timing for reconstruction if a patient needs Post-Mastectomy Radiotherapy (PMRT)? Answer: Delayed autologous reconstruction is preferred to avoid radiation-induced flap volume loss, fibrosis, or severe implant capsular contracture.
* Trap: Assuming a previous C-section via Pfannenstiel incision contraindicates a DIEP flap. Pearl: Pfannenstiel scars are low and usually spare the superiorly oriented deep inferior epigastric perforators, so DIEP flap is generally still possible.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate between arterial compromise and venous congestion in a free flap? Answer: Arterial compromise presents as pale, cool skin with slow/absent capillary refill and no Doppler signal. Venous congestion presents as purple/blue, swollen tissue with brisk capillary refill (<1 sec) and dark blood on pinprick.
* Pitfall: Performing an immediate prepectoral implant reconstruction in a heavy smoker. Avoid by delaying reconstruction or utilizing an autologous flap, as smoking significantly ↑ risk of mastectomy skin flap necrosis and subsequent implant exposure.
* Trap: Confusing TRAM flap and DIEP flap anatomy. Pearl: TRAM harvests the entire rectus abdominis muscle, risking hernias; DIEP strictly dissects the perforators and spares the muscle, minimizing abdominal morbidity.
* Viva Question: What is the significance of the Internal Mammary Artery (IMA) in breast reconstruction? Answer: It is the most reliable and commonly used recipient vessel for microsurgical anastomosis in free flap (e.g., DIEP) breast reconstructions.
