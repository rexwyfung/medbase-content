# Thoracic & Cardiac Transplantation (胸腔與心臟移植)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Cardiothoracic Transplantation
* **Heart Transplantation (心臟移植)**: Surgical replacement of a failing native heart with a healthy cadaveric donor heart for end-stage heart failure refractory to maximal medical/device therapy.
* **Lung Transplantation (肺移植)**: Surgical replacement of one or both diseased lungs to restore pulmonary function in irreversible, progressive end-stage respiratory disease.

### Local Epidemiology and HA Logistics in Hong Kong
* **Designated Centre**: **Queen Mary Hospital (QMH, 瑪麗醫院)** is the *sole* tertiary referral centre for all cardiothoracic transplantations in Hong Kong.
* **Organ Allocation**: Governed by the **Centralised Organ Donation Register (CODR, 中央器官捐贈登記名冊)**. Allocation is strictly ABO-blood group matched, size-matched, and prioritised by medical urgency (e.g., patients on ECMO/VAD support).
* **Severe Donor Shortage**: HK has one of the lowest cadaveric organ donation rates globally (~5 per million population), heavily necessitating **Bridge-to-Transplant (BTT)** strategies like **Ventricular Assist Devices (VAD, 心室輔助裝置)**.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Orthotopic Heart Transplantation
* **Biatrial Anastomosis (雙心房吻合術)**: Older technique. Native posterior atrial walls + SA node left intact. Donor heart anastomosed via atria. → High risk of atrial arrhythmias and AV valve regurgitation.
* **Bicaval Anastomosis (雙腔靜脈吻合術)**: Current standard. Complete excision of native right atrium. Anastomoses at:
  * **Superior Vena Cava (SVC, 上腔靜脈)**
  * **Inferior Vena Cava (IVC, 下腔靜脈)**
  * **Left Atrial Cuff (左心房袖)** (containing 4 pulmonary veins)
  * **Ascending Aorta (升主動脈)**
  * **Pulmonary Artery (PA, 肺動脈)**

### Surgical Anatomy of Lung Transplantation
* Access via **Clamshell incision (蛤殼式切口)** or bilateral anterolateral thoracotomies.
* Sequential anastomoses (Posterior to Anterior):
  * 1. **Main Stem Bronchus (主支氣管)**: Anastomosed close to secondary carina to preserve vascularity.
  * 2. **Pulmonary Artery (PA, 肺動脈)**.
  * 3. **Pulmonary Vein (PV) / Left Atrial Cuff (左心房袖)**.

### Pathophysiology of Allograft Rejection
* **Hyperacute Rejection (超急性排斥)**: Minutes-hours. Pre-formed donor-specific antibodies (DSA) against ABO or HLA → complement activation → widespread graft thrombosis. (Prevented by negative crossmatch).
* **Acute Cellular Rejection (急性細胞性排斥)**: Weeks-months. T-cell mediated (CD4/CD8) cytotoxicity against donor HLA antigens → myocardial necrosis / perivascular infiltrates.
* **Chronic Rejection (慢性排斥)**: Years. Fibrointimal hyperplasia → **Cardiac Allograft Vasculopathy (CAV, 心臟移植物血管病變)** or **Bronchiolitis Obliterans Syndrome (BOS, 閉塞性細支氣管炎綜合症)**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of End-Stage Heart Failure
* **Left-Sided Failure**: **Orthopnoea (端坐呼吸)**, Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnoea (PND), resting dyspnoea, extreme lethargy.
* **Right-Sided Failure**: Raised JVP, profound **pitting oedema (凹陷性水腫)**, congestive hepatomegaly, medically refractory **ascites (腹水)**.
* **Cardiac Cachexia (心源性惡病質)**: Severe muscle wasting due to chronic low cardiac output and systemic inflammation.

### Clinical Presentation of End-Stage Lung Disease
* Intractable resting dyspnoea, severe exercise intolerance (walking distance <150m).
* Refractory hypoxaemia requiring continuous Long-Term Oxygen Therapy (LTOT) or Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV).
* Recurrent severe exacerbations/hospitalisations.
* Secondary **Cor Pulmonale (肺源性心臟病)** → Right ventricular failure signs.

### Signs of Acute Allograft Rejection Post-Transplant
* **Heart**: Often asymptomatic early. Later: unexplained fever, newly elevated JVP, S3 gallop, arrhythmias, rapidly declining Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF).
* **Lung**: Fever, worsening dyspnoea, cough, crackles, ↓ FEV1/FVC by >10% from baseline, new opacities on Chest X-Ray.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentials for Post-Transplant Allograft Dysfunction
| Differential | Onset Timing | Discriminatory Clinical Features | Diagnostic Investigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Graft Dysfunction (PGD)** | < 72 hours | Acute severe hypoxaemia/hypotension immediately off bypass. Due to ischemia-reperfusion injury. | CXR (Diffuse infiltrates), ECHO (Severe biventricular failure). |
| **Acute Cellular Rejection** | 1-6 months | Fever, gradual clinical decline, new arrhythmias/dyspnoea. | **Endomyocardial Biopsy / Transbronchial Biopsy** (+ve lymphocytic infiltrate). |
| **Cytomegalovirus (CMV) Infection** | 1-6 months | Fever, leukopenia, pneumonitis, hepatitis, enteritis. Occurs when prophylaxis stops. | CMV Quantitative PCR (blood/BAL). |
| **Cardiac Allograft Vasculopathy (CAV)** | > 1 year | Silent ischemia, sudden cardiac death, HF signs. No angina due to denervated heart. | Coronary Angiography / Intravascular Ultrasound (IVUS). |
| **Bronchiolitis Obliterans (BOS)** | > 1 year | Insidious onset of progressive dyspnoea, dry cough. | PFTs (Irreversible obstructive pattern). |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Recipient Pre-operative Cardiopulmonary Workup
* **Echocardiography (超聲心動圖)**: Confirms irreversible structural/functional failure (LVEF <20%).
* **Cardiopulmonary Exercise Testing (CPET)**: Objective functional assessment. **Peak VO2 <12-14 mL/kg/min** is the threshold for transplant listing.
* **Right Heart Catheterisation (RHC, 右心導管檢查)**: **CRITICAL**. Assesses Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR). **Fixed PVR >3-5 Wood Units** is an absolute contraindication for isolated heart transplant → requires heart-lung block.

### Immunological Workup for Transplantation
* **ABO Blood Typing**: Strict matching required.
* **HLA Typing**: HLA-A, B, and DR loci mapping.
* **Panel Reactive Antibodies (PRA)**: Estimates recipient's pre-formed HLA antibodies. High PRA (>10-20%) implies a highly sensitized patient → high risk of positive crossmatch.
* **Prospective Lymphocytotoxicity Crossmatch**: Recipient serum mixed with donor lymphocytes. Must be -ve before surgery.

### Donor Assessment Investigations
* **Brain Death Confirmation**: Strictly performed by two independent senior clinicians in HA.
* **Cardiac Assessment**: Bedside ECHO, ECG, +/- Coronary angiogram if donor >45 years or has CAD risk factors.
* **Pulmonary Assessment**: Bronchoscopy (rule out aspiration/purulence), ABG (PaO2 >300 mmHg on 100% FiO2), clear CXR.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Surgical Indications for Heart Transplantation
* End-Stage **Dilated Cardiomyopathy (擴張性心肌病)** or Ischaemic Cardiomyopathy.
* **NYHA Class III/IV** symptoms refractory to maximal optimal medical therapy (OMT) + CRT/ICD.
* Peak VO2 <14 mL/kg/min (or <12 if on beta-blockers).
* Refractory cardiogenic shock requiring continuous inotropes or IABP/ECMO/VAD.

### Surgical Indications for Lung Transplantation
* **Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis (IPF)**: Rapid decline in FVC (>10% in 6 months) or diffusing capacity (DLCO) <39%.
* **COPD (慢性阻塞性肺病)**: BODE index 7-10, FEV1 <20%, history of severe exacerbations + hypercapnia.
* **Cystic Fibrosis (囊性纖維化)** / Bronchiectasis: FEV1 <30%, recurrent massive haemoptysis.

### Absolute Contraindications for Cardiothoracic Transplantation
* **Irreversible End-Organ Failure**: e.g., Cirrhosis, eGFR <30 mL/min (unless combined multiorgan transplant).
* **Active Malignancy**: Generally require 2-5 years disease-free interval (except non-melanoma skin cancer).
* Active systemic infection (untreated HIV/HBV/HCV/TB).
* Active substance abuse, smoking, or uncontrolled severe psychiatric illness (non-compliance risk).
* High Fixed Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (for isolated heart transplant).

### Bridge to Transplant (BTT) Therapies in Hong Kong
* **Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO, 體外膜氧合)**: VA-ECMO for short-term support (days-weeks).
* **Left Ventricular Assist Device (LVAD, 左心室輔助裝置)**: Implantable mechanical pump (e.g., HeartMate III) draining left ventricle to ascending aorta. Used as BTT or Destination Therapy (DT).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Heart Transplantation
* Approach via **Median Sternotomy (正中胸骨切開術)**.
* Institution of **Cardiopulmonary Bypass (CPB, 體外循環)**.
* **Cold Ischaemic Time**: MUST be **<4 hours** to prevent severe Primary Graft Dysfunction.
* Explantation of native heart leaving posterior atrial cuffs (biatrial) or just venous orifices (bicaval).
* Graft reperfusion, de-airing, and weaning from CPB. Temporary epicardial pacing wires routinely placed due to frequent post-op bradycardia.

### Operative Principles of Lung Transplantation
* Single (posterolateral thoracotomy) vs Bilateral Sequential (Clamshell).
* **Cold Ischaemic Time**: MUST be **<6-8 hours**.
* Donor lung flushed with cold preservation solution (e.g., Perfadex) + Prostaglandin E1.
* Careful bronchial anastomosis (telescoping or end-to-end) wrapped with local tissue (e.g., omentum/pericardium) to prevent ischemia/dehiscence.

### Informed Consent and Ethical Issues
* **Major Haemorrhage**: Extreme risk due to dense adhesions (if prior sternotomy) and coagulopathy from CPB/ECMO.
* **Lifetime Commitment**: Strict adherence to immunosuppressants and regular invasive surveillance (endomyocardial biopsies).
* High risk of perioperative mortality, opportunistic infections, and secondary malignancies.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Intensive Care Management Post-Transplant
* **Hemodynamic Support**: Isoproterenol or Dobutamine frequently used for chronotropic support (denervated heart resting rate is typically 90-110 bpm, but early bradycardia is common).
* **Pulmonary Management (Lung Transplant)**: Early extubation when possible. Aggressive bronchoscopy clearance of secretions. Maintain negative fluid balance to minimize pulmonary oedema. Inhaled Nitric Oxide (iNO) if elevated PVR.
* Strict reverse isolation protocol.

### Post-Transplant Immunosuppression Protocol
* **Induction Therapy**: **Basiliximab** (IL-2 receptor antagonist) or **Anti-Thymocyte Globulin (ATG)** given intra-op/early post-op to aggressively deplete T-cells.
* **Maintenance Triple Therapy (HA Protocol)**:
  1. **Calcineurin Inhibitor (CNI)**: **Tacrolimus (他克莫司)** or Cyclosporine.
  2. **Anti-metabolite**: **Mycophenolate Mofetil (MMF, 霉酚酸酯)**.
  3. **Corticosteroids**: Prednisolone (tapered to lowest effective dose).

### Post-Transplant Antimicrobial Prophylaxis
* **PCP (Pneumocystis jirovecii)**: Co-trimoxazole (Bactrim) for 6-12 months (or lifelong in lung).
* **CMV Prophylaxis**: Valganciclovir for 3-6 months based on Donor/Recipient CMV serostatus (D+/R- is highest risk).
* **Fungal Prophylaxis**: Itraconazole or inhaled Amphotericin B (especially for lung recipients).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<30 Days) Post-Cardiothoracic Transplant
* **Primary Graft Dysfunction (PGD)**: Leading cause of early mortality. Presents as severe ARDS (lung) or acute cardiogenic shock (heart). Requires ECMO rescue.
* **Massive Bleeding / Cardiac Tamponade**: Coagulopathy from CPB + hepatic congestion.
* **Bronchial Anastomotic Dehiscence**: Unique to lung transplant due to tenuous airway vascularity.
* Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) secondary to CPB, hypotension, and CNI nephrotoxicity.

### Late Complications (>1 Year) of Heart Transplantation
* **Cardiac Allograft Vasculopathy (CAV)**: Accelerated, diffuse concentric intimal hyperplasia of coronary arteries. **Angina is absent** due to cardiac denervation. Detected via annual coronary angiogram/IVUS. Often requires re-transplantation; stenting is generally ineffective.
* **Calcineurin Inhibitor Nephrotoxicity**: Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine cause afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction → irreversible chronic kidney disease (CKD).

### Late Complications (>1 Year) of Lung Transplantation
* **Bronchiolitis Obliterans Syndrome (BOS)**: Chronic rejection manifesting as progressive airflow obstruction (FEV1 drop) non-responsive to bronchodilators. Leading cause of late mortality.

### Post-Transplant Malignancy Risks
* **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD)**: EBV-driven B-cell lymphoma directly related to intensity of immunosuppression.
* **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) of Skin**: Markedly ↑ incidence. Requires strict sun protection and annual dermatology review.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why do heart transplant recipients NOT experience typical angina pectoris during an acute myocardial infarction or active Cardiac Allograft Vasculopathy? Answer: The donor heart is completely surgically denervated during excision/implantation. Afferent pain fibres do not regenerate, meaning ischemia is clinically silent or presents atypically as sudden heart failure or arrhythmias.
* Trap: Prescribing Atropine for severe bradycardia in a post-heart transplant patient. Pearl: Atropine acts via the vagus nerve, which is permanently severed in a transplanted heart. It will have ZERO effect. You MUST use direct-acting beta-agonists like Isoprenaline (Isoproterenol) or Adrenaline, or use epicardial pacing.
* Pitfall: Discharging a transplant patient on a new macrolide antibiotic (e.g., Clarithromycin/Erythromycin) without adjusting their regular medications. Macrolides are potent CYP3A4 inhibitors and will cause Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine levels to spike to toxic, kidney-destroying levels.
* Viva Question: What is the defining absolute contraindication for isolated orthotopic heart transplantation during Right Heart Catheterization, and what is the surgical alternative? Answer: A fixed Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR) of >5 Wood Units that does not reverse with vasodilators (e.g., Nitric Oxide). The donor right ventricle will acutely fail against this high resistance. The only alternative is an En-bloc Heart-Lung Transplant.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with previous cardiac surgeries (e.g., prior CABG or mechanical valve) cannot receive a heart transplant. Pearl: Prior sternotomies are *not* absolute contraindications, but they severely increase surgical complexity and the risk of catastrophic haemorrhage upon re-entry.
