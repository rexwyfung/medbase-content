# Anorectal Abscess & Fistula (肛門直腸膿腫與瘻管)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Anorectal Abscess & Fistula
* **Anorectal Abscess (肛門直腸膿腫)**: Acute purulent collection in pararectal/perianal spaces.
* **Fistula-in-Ano (肛瘻)**: Chronic abnormal epithelised tract communicating between anorectal canal (internal opening) & perianal skin (external opening).

### Local Epidemiology & Hong Kong Context
* Incidence ↑ in males (M:F = 2:1), peak age 30-50 yrs.
* Overall recurrence rate in HK Chinese population is ~13.3% (HA CDARS data).
* Associated w/ ↑ BMI, smoking, DM, **Crohn's Disease (克隆氏症)**, HIV, local TB.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Cryptoglandular Hypothesis of Pathophysiology
* **Anal glands** drain into **anal crypts** at the **Dentate Line (齒狀線)**.
* Obstruction of crypts (feces/trauma) → stasis → bacterial overgrowth.
* Infection penetrates internal sphincter → spreads to intersphincteric space.
* Acute phase = **Abscess (膿腫)**. Chronic phase = **Fistula (瘻管)**.

### Surgical Anatomy & The Anal Sphincter Complex
* **Internal Anal Sphincter (IAS)**: Smooth muscle, involuntary (maintains resting tone).
* **External Anal Sphincter (EAS)**: Striated muscle, voluntary (maintains squeeze tone).
* **Ischioanal Fossa**: Fat-filled space laterally, prone to extensive horseshoe abscess expansion.

### Parks Classification of Anal Fistulae
| Type | Incidence | Tract Pathway |
|---|---|---|
| **Intersphincteric** | 70% | Tract between IAS & EAS → exits perianal skin. |
| **Transsphincteric** | 20% | Tract crosses BOTH IAS & EAS → exits into ischioanal fossa. |
| **Suprasphincteric** | 5% | Tract courses UP intersphincteric space, OVER puborectalis, DOWN into ischioanal fossa. |
| **Extrasphincteric** | 1% | Tract bypasses sphincters completely; connects rectum directly to skin (trauma/Crohn's). |

### Goodsall's Rule for Fistula Trajectory
* **Anterior External Openings**: Tract runs straight (radially) to dentate line.
* **Posterior External Openings**: Tract curves to posterior midline at dentate line.
* *Exception*: Anterior openings >3cm from anal margin may curve to posterior midline (horseshoe).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Typical Symptoms of Anorectal Abscess
* Constant, throbbing perianal **pain**; ↑ w/ sitting, coughing, defecation.
* Purulent discharge (if spontaneous rupture).
* Fever, chills, malaise (systemic toxicity).

### Typical Symptoms of Fistula-in-Ano
* Intermittent purulent/serosanguinous perianal **discharge**.
* Pruritus ani (skin irritation).
* Intermittent swelling/pain (when tract blocks → recurrent abscess).

### Physical Signs & Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)
* **Inspection**: Erythematous, swollen, fluctuant perianal lump. Note position of external opening.
* **Palpation**: Induration, local ↑ temperature, tenderness.
* **DRE (肛門直腸指檢)**: Assess sphincter tone, palpable internal opening (indurated dimple at dentate line). Exclude rectal masses.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Anorectal Swelling & Pain Differentials
| Diagnosis | Discriminatory Features |
|---|---|
| **Thrombosed External Hemorrhoid** | Bluish, firm, painful lump at anal verge. No fluctuance. |
| **Pilonidal Abscess** | Located at natal cleft (sacrococcygeal). Contains hair follicles. |
| **Hidradenitis Suppurativa** | Recurrent multiple abscesses/sinuses in apocrine-rich areas (axillae, groins). |
| **Anal Fissure (肛裂)** | Tearing pain *during* defecation +ve fresh blood. Spasm of IAS. |

### Inflammatory & Neoplastic Differentials for Fistulae
| Diagnosis | Discriminatory Features |
|---|---|
| **Crohn's Disease (克隆氏症)** | Multiple/complex fistulae, skin tags, painless ulcers, GI symptoms. |
| **Genitourinary TB (結核病)** | Pale granulation tissue, undermined edges, concomitant lung/renal TB. |
| **Anal Squamous Cell Carcinoma** | Hard, everted edges, indurated tract, inguinal lymphadenopathy. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Baseline Laboratory Workup
* **CBC**: ↑ WBC (neutrophilia in acute abscess).
* **CRP/ESR**: ↑ in active infection or Crohn's flare.
* **Fasting Glucose/HbA1c**: Screen for DM (poor wound healing, ↑ risk of Fournier's gangrene).

### Gold Standard Imaging for Complex Fistulae
* **MRI Pelvis (磁力共振掃描)**: Gold standard for mapping high/complex tracts, horseshoe extensions, missing internal openings. *During clinical placements at UK centres like John Radcliffe Hospital, anticipate earlier and routine pre-op MRI utilization for fistulae compared to standard HA emergency pathways.*
* **Endoanal Ultrasound (EAUS)**: Cheaper, useful for intersphincteric/low transsphincteric tracts. 3D-EAUS w/ H2O2 enhancement ↑ accuracy.

### Outpatient Endoscopic Workup
* **Proctosigmoidoscopy / Colonoscopy**: Mandatory in complex/recurrent fistulae or age >40 to exclude IBD or synchronous colorectal malignancy.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Acute Abscess Management Algorithm
* **Absolute Indication**: Surgical emergency → **Incision & Drainage (I&D)** ASAP to prevent sepsis/Fournier's.
* **Conservative Rx**: NEVER appropriate for fluctuant abscess.
* **Adjuvant Antibiotics**: Only if systemic sepsis, valvular heart disease, immunosuppression, or extensive surrounding cellulitis.

### Simple vs Complex Fistulae Parameters
| Feature | Simple Fistula (低位瘻管) | Complex Fistula (高位瘻管) |
|---|---|---|
| **EAS Involvement** | <30% of external sphincter | >30% of external sphincter |
| **Location** | Intersphincteric, low transsphincteric | High transsphincteric, suprasphincteric, extrasphincteric |
| **Patient Factors** | Normal anatomy | Crohn's, prior radiation, recurrent, anterior female |
| **Definitive Rx** | Fistulotomy (瘻管切開術) | Seton + LIFT / Advancement Flap |

### Simple Fistula (Low-Type) Management Algorithm
* **Fistulotomy (瘻管切開術)**: Lay open the tract. Secondary healing. Excellent cure rate (95%), minimal incontinence risk.

### Complex Fistula (High-Type) Management Algorithm
* **1st Line HA Protocol**: Placement of loose **Seton (線掛手術)** for 6-12 weeks to resolve sepsis, drain cavity, and mature tract.
* **Definitive Rx**: 
  * **LIFT (Ligation of Intersphincteric Fistula Tract)**: HA & CUHK preferred sphincter-sparing technique.
  * **Mucosal Advancement Flap**: Closes internal opening, retains sphincter.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Abscess Incision & Drainage (I&D) Operative Steps
* Lithotomy/Prone Jackknife position under GA/Spinal.
* Cruciate incision over maximally fluctuant point, close to anal verge (minimizes future tract length).
* Excise skin edges to prevent premature superficial closure.
* Break loculations with finger/forceps. Copious washout.
* Loosely pack w/ alginate/gauze.

### Seton Suture Placement Steps
* Identify internal opening (gently inject H2O2/methylene blue via external opening).
* Probe tract gently via external to internal opening (avoid creating false tracts).
* Pass non-absorbable heavy tie (Silastic loop/silk) through tract.
* Tie loosely outside anus to act as wick.

### Surgical Consent & Specific Operative Risks
* **Bleeding / Sepsis**.
* **Recurrence (復發)**: ~13% despite successful initial surgery.
* **Fecal Incontinence (大便失禁)**: Critical consent item. Risk ↑ w/ high tracts, multiple surgeries, or female anterior fistulae.
* Multiple staged procedures required (Seton → LIFT).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative Care
* **Diet**: Normal diet post-op. Encourage ↑ fluid and ↑ fibre.
* **Analgesia**: Paracetamol + NSAIDs. Avoid opioids to prevent constipation.
* **Laxatives**: Lactulose + Senna (ensure soft, bulky stools).

### Discharge & Wound Rehabilitation Protocols
* **Sitz Baths (溫水坐浴)**: 3-4x daily, especially post-defecation, for hygiene and sphincter relaxation.
* Daily dressing changes; avoid tight packing to allow healing from base outwards.
* HA Wound Clinic follow-up.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early & Intermediate Complications
* **Post-op Hemorrhage**: May require re-packing/theatre.
* **Acute Urinary Retention**: ↑ risk in older males post-spinal anaesthesia.
* **Premature Wound Closure**: Skin heals over cavity → recurrent abscess.

### Late Complications & Prognosis
* **Recurrence (復發)**: Usually due to missed internal opening or undiagnosed horseshoe tract.
* **Fecal Incontinence**: IAS damage → passive incontinence; EAS damage → urge incontinence.
* **Keyhole Deformity**: From large posterior fistulotomy → chronic mucous leakage.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is Goodsall's Rule? Answer: Anterior fistulae run radially to the dentate line; posterior fistulae curve to the posterior midline. An exception is anterior fistulae >3cm from the anal margin, which often track to the posterior midline.
* Viva Question: What is the primary indication for a loose Seton? Answer: To provide continuous drainage, resolve acute sepsis, and induce fibrosis around the tract prior to definitive sphincter-sparing surgery (e.g., LIFT) in complex fistulae.
* Trap: Treating a fluctuant perianal abscess with oral antibiotics alone. Pearl: A fluctuant abscess is a surgical emergency; antibiotics cannot penetrate the abscess cavity effectively. I&D is strictly required to prevent expansion into the ischioanal fossa or Fournier's gangrene.
* Pitfall: Aggressively probing an acute anorectal abscess to find an internal opening during emergency I&D. This can easily create a false tract or damage the sphincter. Keep emergency I&D simple; definitive fistula mapping is performed electively once inflammation subsides.
* Trap: Performing a fistulotomy on an anterior fistula in a female patient. Pearl: The anterior anal sphincter complex in females is significantly shorter and weaker. A fistulotomy here carries an unacceptably high risk of fecal incontinence; use sphincter-sparing techniques instead.
