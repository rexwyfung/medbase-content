# Head and Neck Reconstruction (頭頸重建)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Head and Neck Reconstruction
* **Objective**: Restoration of aero-digestive tract function (swallowing, speech, breathing) + aesthetic form post-extirpation (usually oncological) or severe trauma
* **Modality**: Ranges from simple closure → microvascular free tissue transfer (游離皮瓣)

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (HA Context)
* **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)**: Endemic in HK/Southern China. Radiation-induced tissue fibrosis (放射性纖維化) → ↑ need for vascularized tissue in salvage surgery
* **Oral Cavity Squamous Cell Carcinoma (口腔鱗狀細胞癌)**: High incidence → dictates frequent need for tongue/mandible reconstruction
* **Patient Demographics**: Often elderly, ↑ comorbidities (smokers, alcoholics, poor nutrition)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Key Neck Recipient Vascular Anatomy (頸部受區血管解剖)
* **Arterial Targets**:
* **Facial artery (面動脈)**
* **Superior thyroid artery (甲狀腺上動脈)**
* **Transverse cervical artery (頸橫動脈)**
* **Venous Targets**:
* **Internal jugular vein (IJV) (頸內靜脈)** branches (e.g., facial vein)
* **External jugular vein (EJV) (頸外靜脈)**

### Pathophysiology of Irradiated Tissue (放射組織病理生理)
* **Post-Radiotherapy (RT) Changes**:
* Endarteritis obliterans → severe local tissue hypoxia
* Dense fibrosis → ↓ wound healing, ↑ risk of wound breakdown/fistula
* **Surgical Implication**: Primary closure often fails → absolute need for importing healthy, well-vascularized tissue (free flaps) from outside radiation field

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Functional Deficits Requiring Reconstruction
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**: Loss of pharyngeal propulsion or tongue base bulk
* **Aspiration (吸入性肺炎)**: Incompetent glottic closure or loss of sensation
* **Dysphonia/Aphonia (發音困難)**: Loss of vocal cords or resonating cavities
* **Trismus (牙關緊閉)**: Scarring of masticator space

### Structural & Aesthetic Defect Signs
* **Bony Defect**: Mandibular/maxillary discontinuity → malocclusion, lower facial collapse
* **Soft Tissue Defect**: Salivary fistula (唾液瘻管), exposed carotid artery (頸動脈外露), exposed hardware

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Reconstructive Ladder Options (重建階梯比較)
| Reconstructive Option | Indications | Pros / Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Closure** | Small mucosal defects (<2cm) | Simple, quick / Risk of tethering, stricture |
| **Healing by 2nd Intention** | Small oral cavity defects | No donor site / Prolonged healing, contracture |
| **Skin Graft (SSG/FTSG)** | Superficial defects, no exposed bone | Low morbidity / Poor bulk, contracture, fails in RT field |
| **Local/Regional Flaps** | Moderate defects, unfit for free flap (e.g., Pectoralis Major flap) | Reliable, fast / Bulky, limited reach, donor deformity |
| **Free Tissue Transfer** | Large/complex defects, irradiated tissue | Gold standard, tailored / Prolonged OR time, 1-5% total failure risk |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Donor Site Vascular Imaging (供區血管造影)
* **Lower Limb CT Angiogram (CTA) (電腦斷層血管造影)**: Mandatory before **Fibula Free Flap** to confirm 3-vessel runoff to foot (prevent foot ischemia)
* **Allen's Test (艾倫氏測試)**: Clinical test ± Doppler mandatory before **Radial Forearm Free Flap (RFFF)** to confirm intact ulnar artery collateral flow to hand

### Pre-operative Optimization Workup
* **Nutritional Assessment**: **Serum Albumin (血清白蛋白)** >30g/L desired for healing
* **Dental Assessment (牙科評估)**: Caries extraction pre-RT to prevent osteoradionecrosis
* **Cardiopulmonary**: ECG, Echo (Echo if poor effort tolerance), CXR → anticipate 8-12 hr surgery

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Defect-Specific Flap Selection Algorithm (皮瓣選擇指引)
| Defect Type | Flap of Choice | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mandible (Bony)** | **Fibula Free Flap (腓骨游離皮瓣)** | Length up to 25cm, accepts osseointegrated dental implants |
| **Tongue Base / Pharynx** | **Anterolateral Thigh (ALT) (大腿前外側皮瓣)** | Good bulk, long pedicle, large skin paddle |
| **Anterior Tongue / Floor of Mouth** | **Radial Forearm (RFFF) (橈前臂游離皮瓣)** | Thin, pliable for mobility, sensate potential |
| **Vocal Cord / Larynx** | **Vertical Rectus Abdominis / Local** | Bulk provision, strap muscles |
| **Unfit for prolonged OR (ASA IV)** | **Pectoralis Major Regional Flap (胸大肌皮瓣)** | Pedicled, reliable, shorter operative time (<3 hrs) |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Microvascular Operative Principles (微血管手術原則)
* **2-Team Approach**: Extirpation team (ENT/MaxFax) + Reconstruction team (Plastic Surgery)
* **Ischemia Time**: Aim <2-3 hours for free flaps
* **Anastomosis**: Typically 1 artery (hand-sewn) + 1-2 veins (venous coupler device often used in HA)
* **Geometry**: Avoid pedicle kinking/tension; ensure adequate vessel length

### Key Informed Consent Elements (術前同意書要點)
* **Flap Failure Risk**: ~1-5% risk of complete flap loss necessitating re-operation/salvage
* **Donor Site Morbidity**: E.g., skin graft requirement at forearm/leg, weakness, sensory loss
* **Airway/Feeding**: High likelihood of temporary **Tracheostomy (氣管造口)** and **Nasogastric (NG) / PEG tube**

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Free Flap Monitoring Protocol (游離皮瓣監測)
* **Frequency**: Hourly (q1h) flap checks for 1st 24-48 hours, then q2h, then q4h
* **Clinical Parameters**:
* **Color**: Pink (normal) vs Pale (arterial block) vs Congested/Blue (venous block)
* **Capillary Refill**: 1-2 sec (normal) vs brisk/absent
* **Temperature**: Warm vs Cold
* **Doppler**: Handheld pencil Doppler to map marked perforator signal
* **Prick Test**: Brisk bright red blood = normal; Dark blood = venous; No blood = arterial

### Post-Operative Rehabilitation (術後康復)
* **Airway**: Tracheostomy weaning once swelling ↓ (usually post-op day 5-7)
* **Swallow**: **Speech & Swallow Therapy (言語及吞嚥治療)** assessment (Videofluoroscopy/VFS) before oral intake
* **Positioning**: Head elevation 30°, avoid neck ties (tracheostomy secured with sutures, not tapes) to prevent pedicle compression

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Complications (<72 hours)
* **Venous Congestion (靜脈瘀血)**: Most common cause of flap compromise. Flap is purple, swollen, brisk capillary refill, bleeds dark blood. → **Immediate re-exploration**
* **Arterial Thrombosis (動脈血栓)**: Flap is pale, cool, empty capillary refill.
* **Neck Hematoma (頸部血腫)**: Compresses pedicle → leads to venous congestion

### Intermediate/Late Complications
* **Pharyngocutaneous Fistula (咽皮瘻管)**: Saliva leak into neck. ↑ risk with prior RT. → Mgt: NBM, NG feeding, local wound care, surgical closure if persists
* **Donor Site Complications**: Poor graft take, tendon exposure (RFFF), foot drop (Fibula flap - common peroneal nerve injury)
* **Stricture**: Dysphagia requiring serial balloon dilatations

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate arterial vs venous compromise in a free flap? Answer: Arterial is pale, cool, with absent capillary refill and no bleeding on pinprick. Venous is bluish/purple, engorged, brisk/instant capillary refill, with dark blood on pinprick.
* Viva Question: What is the most crucial pre-operative investigation before a fibula free flap? Answer: Lower limb CT angiogram to confirm triphasic flow and intact anterior tibial, posterior tibial, and peroneal arteries to prevent distal foot ischemia upon harvest.
* Trap: Tying a tracheostomy tube with a neck strap circumferentially in a post-op free flap patient. Pearl: Always secure the tracheostomy tube directly to the skin with heavy non-absorbable sutures to avoid external compression of the microvascular pedicle.
* Pitfall: Delaying return to theatre for a congested free flap. Venous thrombosis causes irreversible no-reflow phenomenon much faster than arterial occlusion. Immediate re-exploration within 4 hours is critical for salvage.
