# Ischaemic Heart Disease (缺血性心臟病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Ischaemic Heart Disease
* **Ischaemic Heart Disease (IHD)**: Imbalance between myocardial O2 supply & demand → myocardial ischaemia (心肌缺血).
* **Stable Angina (穩定性心絞痛)**: Transient, predictable ischaemia triggered by exertion, relieved by rest/nitrates.
* **Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS - 急性冠心病綜合症)**: Unstable Angina (UAP), NSTEMI, STEMI. Driven by acute plaque rupture & thrombosis.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* 3rd leading cause of death in HK (after cancer & pneumonia).
* High burden on **Hospital Authority (HA - 醫院管理局)** acute medical/cardiology wards.
* Major risk factors in HK: ↑ Age, **Diabetes Mellitus (糖尿病)**, Hypertension, Hyperlipidaemia, Smoking (吸煙).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Coronary Artery Surgical Anatomy
* **Left Main Stem (LMS - 左主冠狀動脈)**: Bifurcates into LAD & LCx.
* **Left Anterior Descending (LAD - 左前降支)**: Supplies anterior wall, anterior 2/3 of septum, apex.
* **Left Circumflex (LCx - 左迴旋支)**: Supplies lateral & posterior left ventricle.
* **Right Coronary Artery (RCA - 右冠狀動脈)**: Supplies right ventricle, SA node (60%), AV node (90%), posterior 1/3 of septum.
* **Coronary Dominance**: Determined by origin of Posterior Descending Artery (PDA). 85% Right-dominant (from RCA).

### Pathophysiology of Atherosclerosis
* Endothelial injury → subendothelial LDL deposition → oxidation → macrophage phagocytosis → **foam cells**.
* Fatty streak progression → fibrous cap formation → **Atheromatous Plaque (粥樣斑塊)**.
* Plaque rupture/erosion → exposure of thrombogenic core → platelet aggregation & coagulation cascade activation → **Thrombosis (血栓形成)**.

### Pathophysiology of Myocardial Infarction
* Complete occlusion → anaerobic metabolism → lactic acidosis → angina.
* Prolonged ischaemia (>20-30 mins) → irreversible myocyte necrosis → **Myocardial Infarction (心肌梗塞)**.
* Necrosis progression: Subendocardial (NSTEMI) → Transmural (STEMI).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Classic Symptoms of IHD & ACS
* **Chest Pain (胸痛)**: Central, crushing/heavy, radiating to left arm, neck, or jaw.
* **Angina Pectoris**: Exertional, <15 mins, relieved by GTN.
* **ACS Pain**: Occurs at rest, >20 mins, NOT relieved by GTN.
* **Autonomic Symptoms**: **Diaphoresis (出汗)**, nausea, vomiting, profound pallor.
* **Dyspnoea (呼吸困難)**: Equivalent to angina in elderly/diabetic patients (silent ischaemia).

### Clinical Signs of Complicated Myocardial Infarction
* Often unremarkable in uncomplicated cases.
* **Sympathetic activation**: Tachycardia, hypertension.
* **Vagal activation** (often inferior MI): Bradycardia, hypotension.
* **Heart Failure (心臟衰竭) signs**: ↑ JVP, bilateral basal crepitations, S3/S4 gallop.
* **Mechanical Complications**: New pan-systolic murmur (papillary muscle rupture/VSD).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Differentials for Acute Chest Pain
| System | Condition | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cardiac** | **Aortic Dissection (主動脈夾層)** | Sudden tearing pain radiating to back, pulse deficits, widened mediastinum. |
| **Cardiac** | **Pericarditis (心包炎)** | Pleuritic pain, relieved by leaning forward, diffuse concave ST-elevation. |
| **Pulmonary** | **Pulmonary Embolism (肺栓塞)** | Sudden dyspnoea, pleuritic pain, hypoxia, haemoptysis, right heart strain. |
| **Pulmonary** | **Tension Pneumothorax (張力性氣胸)** | Unilateral absent breath sounds, hyper-resonant percussion, tracheal deviation. |
| **GI** | **GORD (胃酸倒流) / Spasm** | Burning pain, related to meals/posture, relieved by antacids. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Bedside Investigations
* **12-lead ECG (心電圖)**: Crucial for HA rapid triage.
* **STEMI**: ST-elevation in contiguous leads, new LBBB.
* **NSTEMI/UAP**: ST-depression, T-wave inversion.
* **Bedside Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: Assesses LVEF, Regional Wall Motion Abnormalities (RWMA), rules out mechanical complications / pericardial effusion.

### Essential Laboratory Investigations
* **High-sensitivity Troponin I/T (高敏度肌鈣蛋白)**: ↑ in myocardial necrosis (MI). Normal in UAP. Serial testing (0h, 1h/3h HA protocols) to detect dynamic rise/fall.
* **Lipid Profile (血脂)**: Fasting total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, Triglycerides.
* **HbA1c & Fasting Glucose**: Evaluate DM status.
* **Renal Function Tests (RFT)**: Baseline creatinine before contrast exposure (angiogram).
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Exclude anaemia triggering ischaemia.

### Non-Invasive Functional & Anatomical Imaging
* **Treadmill Exercise Tolerance Test (ETT)**: Detects inducible ischaemia.
* **CT Coronary Angiography (CTCA - 電腦掃描冠狀動脈造影)**: Excellent negative predictive value for ruling out CAD in low-intermediate risk stable patients.
* **Myocardial Perfusion Scan (MIBI)**: Identifies reversible ischaemia vs fixed scar.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigation
* **Invasive Coronary Angiography (冠狀動脈造影)**: Defines coronary anatomy, localises stenoses, quantifies severity, and guides revascularisation strategy (PCI vs CABG).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Acute Medical Management (HA Protocol for ACS)
* **MONA**: **M**orphine (for pain), **O**xygen (if SpO2 <90%), **N**itrates (GTN sublingual), **A**spirin (300mg stat).
* **Dual Antiplatelet Therapy (DAPT - 雙重抗血小板治療)**: Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (Ticagrelor or Clopidogrel).
* **Anticoagulation**: LMWH (Enoxaparin) or Unfractionated Heparin.
* **High-dose Statins**: Atorvastatin/Rosuvastatin stat to stabilize plaque.

### STEMI Reperfusion Strategy
* **1st Line**: Primary **Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI - 經皮冠狀動脈介入治療 / 通波仔)**. Target door-to-balloon time <90 mins.
* **2nd Line**: Thrombolysis (Streptokinase / Tenecteplase) if PCI unavailable within 120 mins.

### Indications for CABG vs PCI Revascularisation
| Revascularisation Modality | Key Indications |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PCI (通波仔)** | 1-2 vessel disease, acute STEMI, high surgical risk (frail/elderly), simple discrete lesions (Low SYNTAX score). |
| **CABG (搭橋手術)** | Significant **LMS disease**, 3-vessel disease + **Diabetes Mellitus**, diffuse complex disease (High SYNTAX score >22), concurrent valve surgery needed. |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Principles of Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG)
* **Access**: Median sternotomy.
* **Myocardial Protection**: Cardiopulmonary Bypass (CPB - 心肺機) with cardioplegic arrest (心臟停頓液) → ensures bloodless, motionless field.
* **Off-Pump CABG (OPCAB - 不停跳搭橋)**: Alternative utilizing tissue stabilizers to bypass without CPB (↓ systemic inflammatory response, controversial long-term graft patency vs on-pump).
* **Goal**: Complete revascularisation of all ischemic territories with viable myocardium.

### Graft Conduit Selection
* **Left Internal Mammary Artery (LIMA - 左內乳動脈)**: Graft of choice for LAD. Excellent long-term patency (>90% at 10 years) due to active NO production and matching caliber.
* **Saphenous Vein Graft (SVG - 大隱靜脈)**: Harvested from leg. Used for RCA and LCx targets. Prone to late atherosclerosis (50% occlusion at 10 years).
* **Radial Artery (橈動脈)**: Requires positive Allen's test before harvest. Prone to vasospasm; requires post-op calcium channel blockers.

### Pre-operative Consent Issues for CABG
* **Mortality**: 1-2% (higher in emergency/poor LVEF).
* **Neurological**: Stroke (中風) (1-2%) due to aortic cross-clamping / calcification emboli.
* **Cardiac**: Peri-operative MI, arrhythmias (Post-op AF in ~30%).
* **Surgical**: Bleeding requiring re-exploration (2-3%), deep sternal wound infection / mediastinitis (1-2%, ↑ risk in diabetics/bilateral IMA use).
* **Renal**: Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) requiring temporary dialysis.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate ICU Post-operative Care
* **Haemodynamic Monitoring**: Arterial line (continuous BP), CVP, Swan-Ganz catheter (if poor LV function).
* **Ventilatory Support**: Early extubation protocol (<6 hours) if haemodynamically stable.
* **Drain Management**: Monitor mediastinal/pleural chest drain output closely. Target <100ml/hr. Suspect surgical bleeding if excessive.
* **Medical Therapy**: Recommence Aspirin within 24 hours. Beta-blockers to prevent AF.

### Long-term Cardiac Rehabilitation & Secondary Prevention
* **Phase 1-4 Rehabilitation**: Gradual mobilization, supervised exercise training.
* **Strict Risk Factor Modification**: Smoking cessation (戒煙) is mandatory. Optimize DM control (HbA1c <7%).
* **Lipid Targets**: LDL <1.4 mmol/L (Statins +/- Ezetimibe).
* **Medications**: Lifelong Aspirin. Beta-blockers, ACE-inhibitors (especially if LVEF <40%), Statins.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<30 days)
* **Post-op Haemorrhage**: Risk of **Cardiac Tamponade (心包填塞)** → sudden ↓ BP, ↑ CVP, pulsus paradoxus, ↓ chest drain output (clotted). Requires urgent re-sternotomy.
* **Peri-operative MI**: Due to graft failure or incomplete revascularisation.
* **Low Cardiac Output Syndrome (LCOS)**: Requires inotropic support (Adrenaline/Milrinone) or Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump (IABP).

### Intermediate to Late Complications
* **Surgical Site**: Sternal non-union, chronic incisional pain.
* **Graft Failure**: Intimal hyperplasia (months) → Atherosclerosis (years). SVG failure is the primary cause of recurrent angina late post-CABG.
* **Heart Failure**: Ischaemic cardiomyopathy if myocardium was non-viable prior to surgery.

### Prognostic Determinants
* **Pre-operative LVEF**: Most critical determinant of long-term survival.
* **Completeness of Revascularisation**: Leaving ischaemic territories un-grafted worsens prognosis.
* **LIMA to LAD graft**: Confers the greatest survival benefit.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most crucial prognostic determinant in a patient with IHD undergoing CABG? Answer: Pre-operative Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF).
* Viva Question: Why is the LIMA preferred over the SVG for grafting the LAD? Answer: LIMA has superior long-term patency (>90% at 10 years) due to its resistance to atherosclerosis and native nitric oxide production.
* Trap: Treating all elevated troponins as ACS. Pearl: Troponin indicates myocardial injury, not necessarily ischaemia. Always consider Type 2 MI, PE, sepsis, or renal failure if the clinical picture does not match ACS.
* Trap: Prescribing DAPT indefinitely post-CABG for all patients. Pearl: Post-CABG patients typically only need single antiplatelet therapy (Aspirin) long-term, unless they had a recent ACS or concurrent PCI stents requiring DAPT.
* Pitfall: Ignoring a sudden cessation of chest drain output post-CABG accompanied by hypotension. This is the classic presentation of impending cardiac tamponade due to clotted drains. Immediate bedside evaluation and potential emergency re-sternotomy are required.
* Pitfall: Harvesting the radial artery without checking collateral circulation. Always perform an Allen's test or use Doppler ultrasound to confirm adequate ulnar collateral flow to the hand before harvesting.
