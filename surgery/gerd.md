# Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (胃食管反流病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease
* **Definition:** Pathological condition where reflux of stomach contents → troublesome symptoms and/or complications (e.g., esophagitis, stricture).

### Local Epidemiology of GERD in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence:** ~4-5% in HK (lower than West but ↑ rapidly due to obesity & westernized diet).
* **Barrett's Esophagus (巴雷特食管):** Rare in HK (<1% vs. 10-15% in West); routine population screening NOT recommended by HA.
* **Gastric Cancer Overlap:** Due to high regional upper GI malignancy, threshold for early endoscopy (OGD) is much lower in HK than US/UK.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Anti-Reflux Barrier
* **Lower Esophageal Sphincter (下食管括約肌, LES):** High-pressure zone at gastroesophageal junction (GEJ).
* **Crural Diaphragm (膈肌腳):** External pinchcock mechanism; encircles LES.
* **Angle of His (希氏角):** Acute angle between cardia & fundus → flap valve effect.
* **Phrenoesophageal Ligament:** Anchors GEJ to diaphragm; degeneration → hiatal hernia.

### Pathophysiology of Reflux
* **Transient LES Relaxations (TLESRs):** Most common cause; unprovoked LES relaxation not preceded by swallowing.
* **Mechanical LES Failure:** Hypotensive LES (<10 mmHg) or short intra-abdominal LES segment (<1 cm).
* **Hiatal Hernia (食管裂孔疝):** Displacement of LES into thorax → ↓ pressure barrier & loss of crural pinchcock.
* **Delayed Gastric Emptying:** ↑ intragastric pressure → pushes contents upward.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Typical Symptoms of GERD
* **Heartburn (胃灼熱):** Retrosternal burning; postprandial & exacerbated by supine posture.
* **Regurgitation (反酸):** Effortless return of acidic fluid/food into pharynx.
* **Water Brash:** Hypersalivation reflex in response to distal acid.

### Atypical/Extra-esophageal Symptoms of GERD
* **Respiratory:** Chronic cough, asthma exacerbation (micro-aspiration).
* **ENT:** Hoarseness, globus sensation, laryngitis, dental erosions.

### Alarm Symptoms Requiring Urgent Endoscopy
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難):** Suspect stricture or esophageal adenocarcinoma (食管腺癌).
* **Odynophagia:** Suspect ulceration or candidiasis.
* **Weight Loss:** Unintentional; high concern for malignancy.
* **GI Bleeding:** Hematemesis, melena, or iron deficiency anemia.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnoses of Dyspepsia and Chest Pain

| Condition | Discriminatory Clinical Features | Key Diagnostic Finding |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GERD** | Burning retrosternal pain, worse supine, relief with PPI. | +ve pH monitoring, esophagitis on OGD. |
| **Achalasia (賁門失弛緩症)** | Dysphagia to BOTH liquids & solids, regurgitation of undigested food. | "Bird-beak" on barium, aperistalsis on manometry. |
| **Peptic Ulcer Disease (消化性潰瘍)** | Epigastric pain, related to meals (gastric = worse; duodenal = better). | Ulcer crater on OGD, +ve *H. pylori*. |
| **Ischemic Heart Disease** | Exertional crushing chest pain, radiation to jaw/arm, diaphoresis. | ECG changes, ↑ Troponin. |
| **Eosinophilic Esophagitis** | Young male, atopy/asthma history, episodic food impaction. | >15 eosinophils/HPF on biopsy. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Investigation for GERD with Alarm Features
* **Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD):** Mandatory for age >50, alarm symptoms, or PPI failure (HA standard). Evaluates esophagitis, strictures, Barrett's, malignancy.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigation for GERD
* **24-hour Ambulatory pH-Impedance Monitoring:** Quantifies acid/non-acid reflux events. DeMeester Score >14.72 = abnormal acid exposure. Required before surgery.

### Pre-operative Workup for Anti-reflux Surgery
* **High-Resolution Manometry (HRM):** **Mandatory** pre-op. Rules out achalasia/scleroderma; guides wrap choice (partial vs. 360°).
* **Barium Swallow:** Assesses anatomy, hiatal hernia size, and esophageal length (identifies shortened esophagus).

### Los Angeles Classification for Erosive Esophagitis

| Grade | Description on Endoscopy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Grade A** | ≥1 mucosal break(s) <5 mm, not bridging two mucosal folds. |
| **Grade B** | ≥1 mucosal break(s) >5 mm, not bridging two mucosal folds. |
| **Grade C** | Mucosal breaks bridging ≥2 folds, involving <75% of circumference. |
| **Grade D** | Mucosal breaks involving ≥75% of esophageal circumference. |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management for GERD
* **Lifestyle Modification:** Weight loss (BMI optimization critical), head-of-bed elevation.
* **Dietary Changes:** Avoid late meals (<3 hrs pre-bed). Avoid triggers (caffeine, chocolate, alcohol, high-fat, spicy foods).

### Medical Management for GERD (HA Protocol)
* **1st Line:** Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPI) daily for 4-8 weeks (e.g., Omeprazole 20mg or Pantoprazole 40mg).
* **Refractory:** Double-dose PPI (BD).
* **Adjuncts:** H2-receptor antagonists (Famotidine) at night for nocturnal acid breakthrough; Prokinetics (Domperidone) rarely used due to cardiac side effects.

### Indications for Surgical Management of GERD
* **Refractory:** Failure of optimal medical therapy.
* **Patient Preference:** Young patient unwilling to commit to lifelong PPI therapy.
* **Volume Regurgitation:** Severe regurgitation/aspiration risk (PPI stops acid, not fluid).
* **Complications:** Refractory strictures, large symptomatic hiatal hernia.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Steps for Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication (腹腔鏡尼森胃底摺疊術)
* **Dissection:** Divide pars flaccida, preserve hepatic branch of vagus.
* **Mobilization:** Dissect crura; circumferential mobilization of distal esophagus (~3 cm intra-abdominal length).
* **Short Gastric Vessels:** Divide to mobilize gastric fundus (prevents wrap tension).
* **Crural Repair (Cruroplasty):** Suture posterior crura to narrow hiatus.
* **Wrap:** 360° fundoplication around distal esophagus (Nissen) over a large bougie (e.g., 56-60 Fr) to prevent stricture.

### Pre-operative Consent Issues for Anti-reflux Surgery
* **Dysphagia:** Common early post-op; 10% risk of long-term persistence.
* **Gas-Bloat Syndrome:** Inability to belch/vomit → flatulence, abdominal distension.
* **Recurrence:** ~10-15% need PPI resumption at 10 years.
* **Conversion:** Risk of conversion to open laparotomy (<1%).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Diet for Fundoplication
* **Day 0-14:** Clear liquids → Full liquids.
* **Weeks 2-4:** Pureed/soft diet. Strict avoidance of bread, meat chunks, and carbonated beverages (prevents wrap distension/disruption).
* **Week 6+:** Gradual return to normal solid diet.

### Post-operative Discharge Criteria
* **Vitals:** Afebrile, hemodynamically stable.
* **Diet:** Tolerating liquid diet without severe dysphagia/vomiting.
* **Timing:** Usually discharged post-op Day 1-2 in HA setting.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications Post-Fundoplication
* **Esophageal/Gastric Perforation:** Potentially fatal; suspect if early tachycardia/fever.
* **Bleeding:** From short gastric vessels or splenic capsule injury.
* **Pneumothorax:** Pleural breach during mediastinal dissection.

### Late Complications Post-Fundoplication
* **Wrap Migration/Herniation:** Wrap slips into chest ("slipped Nissen") → dysphagia, pain.
* **Severe Dysphagia:** Wrap too tight or un-recognized underlying motility disorder.
* **Vagus Nerve Injury:** Delayed gastric emptying, diarrhea.

### Prognosis and Reflux Recurrence Rate
* **Efficacy:** 85-90% symptom relief at 5 years.
* **Failure:** 10-15% recurrence requiring medical therapy; 2-5% require revisional surgery.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical pre-operative investigation before performing a fundoplication? Answer: High-resolution esophageal manometry to exclude achalasia and severe dysmotility.
* Viva Question: If manometry shows absent esophageal peristalsis, what surgical modification is made? Answer: A partial wrap (e.g., Toupet 270° posterior or Dor 180° anterior) is performed instead of a 360° Nissen to prevent severe postoperative dysphagia.
* Trap: Assuming isolated atypical symptoms (chronic cough, asthma) without typical heartburn will resolve with fundoplication. Pearl: Atypical symptoms have a much lower surgical response rate (~50%) compared to typical regurgitation/heartburn. 
* Trap: Recommending population screening for Barrett's Esophagus in Hong Kong due to GERD symptoms. Pearl: Incidence of Barrett's and Esophageal Adenocarcinoma is very low in the local Chinese population; guidelines do not recommend screening without alarm symptoms or multiple risk factors.
* Pitfall: Discharging a post-fundoplication patient without strict dietary counseling. Patient eats solid food too early, leading to severe food bolus impaction at the wrap or acute wrap disruption.
* Pitfall: Performing OGD while the patient is on active PPI therapy during a diagnostic workup. This masks mucosal disease; PPI must be stopped 2-4 weeks prior to pH monitoring and baseline diagnostic OGD.
