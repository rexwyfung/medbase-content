# Head and Neck Cancer (頭頸癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Head and Neck Cancers
* **Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma (HNSCC) (頭頸鱗狀細胞癌)**: Malignancies arising from the mucosal epithelium of the upper aerodigestive tract (oral cavity, pharynx, larynx, nasal cavity, paranasal sinuses).
* **Histology**: >90% are squamous cell carcinomas. Others include salivary gland adenocarcinomas, lymphomas, and sarcomas.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC)
* **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)**: Highly endemic in Southern China and Hong Kong (Cantonese population).
* **Incidence**: Top 10 most common cancers in HK males; peak incidence in middle age (40-60 years).
* **Hospital Authority (HA) Priority**: Managed via specialized multidisciplinary Head & Neck clinics; fast-track for unexplained neck masses >3 weeks.

### Local Risk Factors & Pathogenesis Triggers
* **Traditional Risk Factors**:
	* Tobacco smoking.
	* Excessive alcohol consumption (synergistic effect ↑ risk 40x).
	* Betel nut chewing (oral cavity SCC).
* **Viral Etiologies**: 
	* **Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) (人類疱疹病毒第四型)**: Primary driver for endemic NPC.
	* **Human Papillomavirus (HPV) (人類乳頭瘤病毒)**: Specifically **HPV-16/18**; driving rising incidence of **Oropharyngeal SCC (口咽癌)** in younger, non-smoking cohorts.
	* **Dietary Factors**: Ingestion of nitrosamine-rich preserved foods (e.g., salted fish) during childhood strongly linked to NPC.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Cervical Lymph Node Levels
| Level   | Anatomical Region                   | Key Boundaries & Drainage                                         |
| :------ | :---------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **I**   | Submental (Ia) & Submandibular (Ib) | Above hyoid; drains oral cavity, lip, submandibular gland.        |
| **II**  | Upper Jugular                       | Skull base to hyoid; primary drainage for oral cavity, pharynx.   |
| **III** | Middle Jugular                      | Hyoid to cricoid; drains larynx, hypopharynx, thyroid.            |
| **IV**  | Lower Jugular                       | Cricoid to clavicle; drains larynx, hypopharynx, thyroid.         |
| **V**   | Posterior Triangle                  | Post to SCM, ant to trapezius; drains nasopharynx (NPC hallmark). |
| **VI**  | Anterior / Central Compartment      | Between carotids; drains thyroid, larynx, cervical trachea.       |

### Pathophysiology of Field Cancerization
* **Field Cancerization (區域癌化)**: Chronic exposure of entire aerodigestive tract mucosa to carcinogens (smoke/alcohol) → diffuse genetic alterations.
* **Clinical Consequence**: High risk of synchronous (simultaneous) or metachronous (subsequent) primary aerodigestive tumours.

### Pathophysiology of EBV-Driven Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma
* **Pathogenesis**: EBV infection + genetic susceptibility (specific HLA alleles) + environmental carcinogens (nitrosamines).
* **Location**: Typically arises from the **Fossa of Rosenmüller (羅森穆勒氏窩)** in the nasopharynx.
* **Spread**: Early aggressive lymphatic spread to retropharyngeal and Level II/V cervical nodes; high propensity for distant metastasis (bone, lung, liver).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC)
* **Cervical Lymphadenopathy (頸淋巴結腫大)**: Painless, firm, enlarging neck mass (often bilateral/Level V) is the most common presentation.
* **Otologic Symptoms**: Unilateral hearing loss, tinnitus, aural fullness secondary to **Eustachian Tube (耳咽管)** obstruction → **Otitis Media with Effusion (積水性中耳炎)** in adults (Red Flag).
* **Nasal Symptoms**: Blood-stained nasal discharge, epistaxis, nasal obstruction.
* **Neurological Signs**: Cranial nerve palsies (CN III, IV, V, VI) due to superior skull base invasion.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/b6eba2c95dccdde18865f31c643dbeb4.png)

### Clinical Signs of Oral Cavity and Oropharyngeal Tumours
* **Oral Lesions**: Painless, non-healing ulcer >2 weeks, mucosal induration, leukoplakia (white patch), erythroplakia (red patch - higher malignant potential).
* **Functional Deficits**: **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**, odynophagia, restricted tongue mobility (hypoglossal nerve involvement), trismus (medial pterygoid invasion).
* **Referred Pain**: Unilateral **Otalgia (耳痛)** with normal ear exam (referred via CN IX/X).
![image|543](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/ff58d09fa2f1d92c96edd5a47e86c1f8.png)

### Clinical Signs of Laryngeal Tumours
* **Glottic SCC**: Early presentation with persistent **Hoarseness (聲音沙啞)** >3 weeks.
* **Supraglottic SCC**: Late presentation (few early symptoms) → dysphagia, foreign body sensation, bulky metastatic neck nodes.
* **Late Signs**: Stridor (airway compromise), haemoptysis, weight loss.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of a Cervical Neck Mass
| Category                       | Common Differentials                                         | Discriminatory Clinical Features                                                                              |
| :----------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Neoplastic (Malignant)**     | Metastatic SCC, NPC, Lymphoma, Thyroid Carcinoma             | Hard, fixed, painless, rapid growth, age >40, risk factors (smoking/EBV).                                     |
| **Inflammatory / Infectious**  | Reactive adenopathy, **TB Lymphadenitis (結核性淋巴結炎)**, Abscess | Tender, fluctuant, erythema, cold abscess (TB), slow resolution.                                              |
| **Congenital (Developmental)** | Thyroglossal Duct Cyst, Branchial Cleft Cyst                 | Midline, moves with swallowing (Thyroglossal); lateral, anterior to SCM, present since childhood (Branchial). |
| **Benign Neoplasm**            | Lipoma, Schwannoma, Salivary Pleomorphic Adenoma             | Soft/rubbery, slow growing, mobile, discrete margins.                                                         |

### Rule of 80s for Neck Masses
* 80% of non-thyroid neck masses in adults >40 years are neoplastic.
* 80% of those neoplastic masses are malignant.
* 80% of those malignancies are metastatic.
* 80% of the primary sources arise from within the aerodigestive tract (HNSCC).

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Clinical & Endoscopic Investigations
* **Flexible Nasoendoscopy (FNE) (內窺鏡檢查)**: Mandatory clinic exam to visualize nasopharynx, oropharynx, and larynx.
* **Panendoscopy**: Examination under anesthesia (EUA) including laryngoscopy, esophagoscopy, and bronchoscopy to map tumour extent and rule out synchronous primaries.

### Tissue Diagnosis Modalities
* **Ultrasound-guided Fine Needle Aspiration (USG-FNA) (超聲波引導細針抽吸活檢)**: Gold standard for evaluating a suspicious neck mass.
* **Primary Site Biopsy**: Endoscopic punch/forceps biopsy of the visible primary mucosal lesion (e.g., nasopharynx, vocal cord).
* **Contraindicated**: Open excision biopsy of an undiagnosed enlarged cervical node (disrupts lymphatic drainage, seeds tumour cells, compromises definitive neck dissection).

### Staging Imaging Protocols (HA Standards)
* **MRI Neck and Face**: Modality of choice for local staging; superior soft-tissue resolution for assessing perineural spread, skull base invasion, and deep fascial space extension.
* **CT Neck with Contrast**: Alternative for assessing bone destruction (e.g., mandible) and gross nodal disease.
* **PET-CT**: Indicated for advanced disease (stage III/IV) to identify distant metastases or unknown primary origins.

### Biomarkers for Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma
* **Plasma EBV DNA Titer**: Highly sensitive local marker used for screening, monitoring treatment response, and detecting early NPC recurrence.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Approach
* **Core Members**:
	* Head & Neck Surgeon (ENT/Plastics).
	* Clinical Oncologist.
	* Radiologist.
	* Pathologist.
	* Speech Therapist (SALT).
	* Dietitian.

### Management Algorithm for Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC)
* **Primary Modality**: Non-surgical. **Concurrent Chemoradiotherapy (CCRT)** or primary intensity-modulated radiotherapy (IMRT) due to profound anatomical inaccessibility and high radiosensitivity of NPC.
* **Surgical Salvage**: Indicated ONLY for persistent/recurrent localized disease (e.g., salvage nasopharyngectomy) or isolated residual neck nodes (salvage neck dissection) post-radiotherapy.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/7aface34edc840ee6d2b51e35f068e99.png)

### Management Algorithm for Laryngeal Cancer
* **Early Stage (T1-T2)**: Single-modality organ preservation → Primary Radiotherapy OR **Transoral Laser Microsurgery (TLM) (經口激光顯微手術)**.
* **Locally Advanced (T3-T4)**: Neoadjuvant chemotherapy + RT (organ preservation trial) OR primary **Total Laryngectomy (全喉切除術)** + post-operative RT.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/d69fbea9b62a3fe79139ea758c2e7372.png)

### Surgical Indications for Oral Cavity Cancers
* **Primary Modality**: Upfront surgical resection with 1-1.5cm clear margins is the gold standard for oral cavity SCC (tongue, floor of mouth, buccal mucosa).
* **Neck Management**: Concurrent elective or therapeutic neck dissection to address regional micrometastases.
* **Adjuvant Therapy**: Post-operative RT/CRT indicated for positive margins, perineural invasion, extracapsular nodal extension.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Principles of Neck Dissection
* **Radical Neck Dissection (RND)**: Clearance of Level I-V nodes + deliberate resection of the **Sternocleidomastoid (SCM)**, **Internal Jugular Vein (IJV)**, and **Spinal Accessory Nerve (CN XI)**.
* **Modified Radical Neck Dissection (MRND)**: Clearance of Level I-V nodes but sparing ≥1 of the non-lymphatic structures (SCM, IJV, CN XI) to preserve function.
* **Selective Neck Dissection (SND)**: Sparing of specific lymph node levels based on primary tumour drainage patterns (e.g., Supraomohyoid dissection for oral cavity).

### Reconstructive Operative Principles
* **Goal**: Restore form (bulk, mucosal lining, facial contour) and function (swallowing, speech, airway).
* **Free Tissue Transfer (Microvascular Flaps)**:
  * **Radial Forearm Free Flap (RFFF)**: Thin, pliable fasciocutaneous flap ideal for tongue/oral mucosa.
  * **Anterolateral Thigh (ALT) Flap**: Bulky flap for massive soft tissue defects.
  * **Free Fibula Osteocutaneous Flap**: Gold standard for reconstructing segmental mandibular defects.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/a531b0a603994f00813c244f13e4de3a.png)

### Tracheostomy Operative Principles
* **Prophylactic Tracheostomy (氣管造口術)**: Often performed concurrently with major composite H&N resections to secure the airway against predictable post-operative soft tissue edema and bleeding.

### Surgical Consent for Major Head and Neck Surgery
* **Cranial Nerve Injury**: Risk of injury to CN VII (Marginal mandibular → asymmetric smile), CN XI (shoulder drop), CN XII (tongue deviation), CN X (recurrent laryngeal → hoarseness/aspiration).
* **Chyle Leak**: Disruption of the **Thoracic Duct (胸導管)** (usually left-sided Level IV dissection) → chylous fistula.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Tracheostomy and Airway Care Protocol
* **Humidification**: Essential to prevent mucous plugging, as upper airway is bypassed.
* **Inner Tube Care**: Regular inspection and cleaning of inner cannula to prevent crusting/obstruction.
* **Suctioning**: Frequent sterile aspiration of secretions.

### Free Flap Monitoring Protocol
* **High-Frequency Monitoring**: Every 1-2 hours for the first 48 hours (highest risk of vascular thrombosis).
* **Clinical Checks**: Assess flap colour, temperature, capillary refill time, and tissue turgor.
* **Instrumental Checks**: Handheld Doppler ultrasound strictly marking the perforator signal; implantable Cook-Swartz venous Doppler for buried flaps.

### Speech and Swallowing Rehabilitation
* **Speech and Language Therapist (SALT)**: Early assessment of dysphagia via **Videofluoroscopic Swallowing Study (VFSS) (吞嚥造影檢查)**.
* **Tracheostomy Weaning**: Downsizing tubes → spigotting (capping) trial → decannulation once upper airway is patent and swallowing is safe.
* **Post-Laryngectomy Speech**: Training with **Surgical Voice Restoration (SVR)** via tracheoesophageal puncture (TEP) valve or electrolarynx.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Immediate / Early Post-operative Complications (<7 days)
* **Airway Obstruction**: Secondary to progressive hematoma or profound mucosal edema compressing the trachea.
* **Flap Failure**: Arterial or venous thrombosis requiring immediate return to theatre for microvascular salvage.
* **Chyle Leak (乳糜漏)**: Milky drainage from neck drains; managed by zero-fat (MCT-only) diet or surgical re-exploration/ligation.
* **Salivary Fistula**: Breakdown of pharyngeal/mucosal repair → saliva leaks into the neck, risking vessel blowout.

### Late Radiotherapy / Surgical Complications
* **Osteoradionecrosis (ORN) of the Jaw (放射性骨壞死)**: Devitalization of irradiated mandible → chronic exposed bone, pathological fractures.
* **Xerostomia (口乾症)**: Radiation damage to salivary glands → severe dry mouth, rampant dental caries.
* **Hypothyroidism**: Due to radiation scatter or surgical removal of thyroid lobes during laryngectomy.
* **Carotid Blowout Syndrome**: Catastrophic rupture of the carotid artery due to exposure, infection, or post-radiation necrosis; high mortality.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: An adult male presents with unilateral serous otitis media. What must you explicitly rule out? Answer: Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) invading or obstructing the Eustachian tube. Direct flexible nasoendoscopy is mandatory.
* Trap: Performing an excisional biopsy on an undiagnosed firm lateral neck mass in a 55-year-old smoker. Pearl: Never perform an open biopsy on a suspected metastatic cervical node. This disrupts tissue planes, seeds tumour cells, and complicates future curative neck dissection. Always perform USG-FNA first.
* Pitfall: Reassuring a patient with hoarseness lasting more than 3 weeks without further imaging. A persistent change in voice is the hallmark of glottic squamous cell carcinoma; an urgent ENT referral for vocal cord visualization is strictly required.
* Viva Question: What is the significance of the Thoracic Duct during a left-sided neck dissection? Answer: The thoracic duct arches into the confluence of the left internal jugular and subclavian veins (Level IV). Injury causes a chyle leak, identified by milky fluid accumulating in surgical drains, especially after enteral feeding commences. ![image|276](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/9199c67d978e522b54530d827ab48375.png)
* Trap: Assuming radical surgery is the primary treatment for Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma. Pearl: NPC is treated primarily with concurrent chemoradiotherapy due to its difficult anatomical location (skull base) and high sensitivity to radiation. Surgery is strictly reserved for salvage of localized recurrence.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate a thyroglossal duct cyst from a branchial cleft cyst? Answer: A thyroglossal duct cyst is located in the midline and moves upwards upon swallowing or tongue protrusion. A branchial cleft cyst is located laterally, typically anterior to the upper third of the sternocleidomastoid muscle, and does not move with swallowing. ![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/c9daa2f958a36cdacf952daed35a06c9.png)
