# Pilonidal Sinus (藏毛竇)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Pilonidal Sinus
* **Acquired condition**
* **Chronic inflammation** of hair follicles in the **natal cleft (臀溝)**
* **Foreign body reaction** → subcutaneous sinus tract containing hair

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology of Pilonidal Sinus
* **Incidence**: Historically lower in Asian populations vs Caucasians, but ↑ locally due to sedentary lifestyles
* **Demographics**: Peak age **15-30 years**
* **Gender**: Male > Female (3:1)
* **Risk Factors (HA Clinical Cohorts)**:
* **Hirsutism (多毛症)**
* **Obesity (肥胖)**
* **Deep natal cleft**
* **Prolonged sitting** (e.g., IT professionals, professional drivers)
* **Poor local hygiene**

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Sacrococcygeal Region
* **Location**: Midline, overlying **sacrococcygeal fascia (骶尾骨筋膜)**
* **Upper limit**: Sacral promontory
* **Lower limit**: Anal verge (~5cm posterosuperior to anus)
* **Tract direction**: Primary sinus is midline; secondary tracts usually branch **cephalad (頭側)** and lateral

### Pathophysiology of Pilonidal Sinus Formation
* **Friction/Mechanical forces** (sitting/walking) → hair breaks off
* **Negative pressure** in deep **natal cleft (臀溝)** → sucks hair and debris into midline pores
* **Foreign body giant cell reaction** → inflammation
* **Bacterial colonization** (skin flora/anaerobes) → micro-abscess formation
* **Sinus tract formation** → lined by **granulation tissue (肉芽組織)** (not true epithelium)
* **Secondary infection** → frank **pilonidal abscess (藏毛竇膿腫)**

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Asymptomatic Presentation of Pilonidal Sinus
* **Painless midline pits** in the **natal cleft**
* **Incidental finding** during routine physical exam

### Acute Presentation (Pilonidal Abscess)
* **Severe localized pain** in sacrococcygeal region
* **Erythema (紅斑)**
* **Swelling (腫脹)**
* **Fluctuant mass (波動性腫塊)** lateral to the midline (usually)
* **Purulent discharge (膿性分泌物)**
* **Systemic signs**: ±ve low-grade fever

### Chronic Presentation (Discharging Sinus)
* **Recurrent pain**
* **Intermittent discharge**: **Blood-stained** or **purulent**
* **Primary openings**: Midline pits in **natal cleft** (often containing protruding hair tufts)
* **Secondary openings**: Lateral to midline (typically cranial to primary pit)
* **Induration (硬結)** around the tracts

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Pilonidal Sinus
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pilonidal Sinus (藏毛竇)** | Midline pits, natal cleft location, tracks cephalad, hair tufts +ve |
| **Perianal Abscess (肛周膿腫)** | Closer to anal verge, severe perianal pain, painful DRE |
| **Fistula-in-Ano (肛瘺)** | Communicates with anorectum (internal opening +ve), tracks from dentate line |
| **Hidradenitis Suppurativa (化膿性汗腺炎)** | Multiple apocrine gland sites (axillae, groin, perineum), severe scarring |
| **Sacrococcygeal Teratoma (骶尾部畸胎瘤)** | Congenital, pediatric population, cystic/solid mass, no hair pits |
| **Furuncle/Carbuncle (癰)** | Staphylococcal skin infection, central necrotic plug, no sinus tract |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Pre-operative Blood Investigations
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Primarily based on Hx and PE
* **Routine Pre-op Bloods (HA Protocol)**:
* **CBP**: ↑ WBC (if acute abscess)
* **LRFT**: Baseline
* **Clotting Profile**: If planning major flap closure
* **HbA1c**: Crucial for wound healing optimization

### Imaging Modalities for Complex Pilonidal Sinus
* **Usually unnecessary** for simple/primary cases
* **Fistulogram/USG Buttock**: Poor yield, rarely used
* **MRI Pelvis (Pelvic Floor Protocol)**:
* **Indication**: Recurrent/complex disease, differentiating from high fistula-in-ano
* **Findings**: Delineates tract extent, branching, and relation to sphincter complex

### Microbiological Swabs
* **Pus Swab for C/ST**:
* **Timing**: During I&D of acute abscess
* **Common Pathogens**: *Bacteroides*, *Peptostreptococcus*, *S. aureus*, *E. coli*

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management for Asymptomatic Pilonidal Sinus
* **Indication**: Incidental pits, no history of abscess/discharge
* **Protocol**:
* **Meticulous perineal hygiene**
* **Hair control**: Regular shaving or **Laser Epilation (激光脫毛)**
* **Weight reduction**

### Acute Abscess Management Protocol
* **1st Line Action**: Urgent **Incision & Drainage (I&D) (切開引流術)**
* **Approach**: Off-midline incision (avoids non-healing midline wound)
* **Procedure**:
* **Drain pus**
* **Curettage** to remove hair nests/granulation tissue
* **Leave open** to heal by secondary intention
* **Antibiotics**: Only if +ve systemic sepsis, cellulitis, or immunosuppression (e.g., Augmentin)

### Chronic Pilonidal Sinus Management Options
| Surgical Option | Description | Indication | Pros / Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Excision & Lay Open** | Excise tract, wound left to granulate | Standard HA primary case | Pro: Low recurrence. Con: Long healing time (weeks), daily packing |
| **Excision & Primary Midline Closure** | Excise tract, close midline | Small, clean sinus | Pro: Fast healing. Con: Highest recurrence rate, high dehiscence risk |
| **Bascom Cleft Lift / Off-Midline Flap** | Excise pit, mobilize skin flap, close off-midline | Complex/recurrent disease | Pro: Flattens cleft, low recurrence. Con: Technically demanding |
| **Limberg (Rhomboid) Flap** | Rhomboid excision, fasciocutaneous rotation flap | Extensive recurrent disease | Pro: Excellent off-midline closure. Con: Cosmetically poor, major surgery |
| **EPSiT (Endoscopic Pilonidal Sinus Treatment)** | Fistuloscope insertion, tract debridement, diathermy ablation | Minimally invasive option | Pro: No large wound, rapid recovery. Con: Variable recurrence, requires specialized equipment |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Patient Positioning and Preparation
* **Position**: **Prone jackknife position (俯臥摺刀位)**
* **Preparation**:
* **Shave buttocks** wide margin pre-operatively
* **Tape buttocks apart** for maximal natal cleft exposure
* **IV Prophylactic Antibiotics** at induction

### Core Operative Steps for Excision
* **Probe tract**: Gentle insertion of lacrimal probe via primary pit
* **Methylene Blue injection**: Maps secondary tracts (optional)
* **Elliptical Excision**: Incorporate all pits and tracts en bloc
* **Depth**: Down to, but NOT including, **sacrococcygeal fascia** (avoid exposing bare bone)
* **Hemostasis**: Meticulous electrocautery

### Surgical Consent and Risks
* **Bleeding (出血)**
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**
* **Wound dehiscence (傷口裂開)**
* **Recurrence (復發)**: Highly stressed (up to 30% depending on technique)
* **Pain** and prolonged daily dressings (if laid open)
* **Ugly scar / altered contour** (if flap used)

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Wound Care
* **Open Wounds (Lay Open)**:
* **Daily packing** with alginate or gauze (e.g., Kaltostat, Aquacel)
* **Shower daily**: Wash wound with lukewarm water
* **Closed Wounds (Flaps)**:
* **Suction drain**: Kept until output < 15-20ml/day
* **Avoid sitting directly** on the wound (use pressure-relieving cushions)

### Long-term Preventive Rehabilitation Measures
* **Crucial Step**: **Strict hair control** around the natal cleft
* **Methods**: Shaving every 1-2 weeks, depilatory creams, or **Laser epilation (gold standard)**
* **Duration**: Continue until age 30-35 (when hair softens and natal cleft depth alters)
* **Hygiene**: Daily scrubbing of natal cleft
* **Lifestyle**: Weight loss, frequent standing breaks for sedentary workers

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications
* **Post-operative Bleeding**: Often from sacrococcygeal fascia vessels
* **Acute SSI**: Cellulitis or frank abscess requiring re-drainage
* **Wound Dehiscence**: Common in midline primary closures due to tension and shear forces

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications
* **Delayed wound healing**: Unhealed open wound > 12 weeks
* **Hypergranulation tissue**: Requires silver nitrate cautery

### Late Post-operative Complications
* **Recurrence (復發)**:
* **Causes**: Missed tract, retained hair, deep unflattened cleft, poor post-op hair control
* **Management**: Requires re-operation (often step-up to flap closure like Limberg)
* **Marjolin's Ulcer (Squamous Cell Carcinoma)**:
* **Rare but lethal** complication of decades-long chronic discharging sinus
* **Suspicion**: Hard, everted edges, sudden ↑ pain/bleeding
* **Action**: Edge biopsy required

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why do pilonidal sinus tracts characteristically branch cephalad (towards the head)? Answer: The physical structure of the natal cleft and the rolling motion of the buttocks during walking create a vacuum-like mechanical force that drives hair and debris upwards.
* Viva Question: During an I&D for an acute pilonidal abscess, where should the incision be placed and why? Answer: Off-midline (lateral to the natal cleft). An incision directly in the midline heals very poorly due to constant friction, maceration, and tension.
* Trap: Classifying a pilonidal sinus as a true cyst. Pearl: It is NOT a true cyst. It is an acquired pseudo-cyst or sinus tract lined by granulation tissue, not true epithelium, formed by a foreign body giant cell reaction to hair.
* Trap: Assuming a discharging perianal lesion is a pilonidal sinus without performing a Digital Rectal Examination (DRE). Pearl: Always perform a DRE. A high fistula-in-ano can present similarly; a pilonidal sinus will NOT have an internal opening communicating with the anal canal.
* Pitfall: Discharging the patient after excision without a strict hair removal protocol. High recurrence rate is guaranteed if the surrounding skin is not kept completely hair-free (ideally via laser epilation) during and after the healing phase.
* Viva Question: What is the primary anatomical objective of a Bascom Cleft Lift or Limberg flap? Answer: To obliterate and flatten the deep natal cleft and move the surgical incision off the midline, removing the mechanical environment that causes recurrence.
