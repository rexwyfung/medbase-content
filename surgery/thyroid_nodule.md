# Thyroid Nodule (甲狀腺結節)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Thyroid Nodule
* **Discrete lesion** within **thyroid gland (甲狀腺)** radiologically distinct from surrounding parenchyma
* Non-palpable nodules discovered on imaging = **Incidentalomas (偶然發現的腫瘤)**

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Thyroid Nodules
* **Prevalence**: ↑ common in HK; palpable in ~5% ♀, ~1% ♂
* **Incidental detection**: USG detects nodules in ~50% of adult HK population
* **Malignancy Risk**: ~5-15% of all evaluated nodules
* **Endemicity**: Historical iodine deficiency in Southern China; modern cases predominantly sporadic or autoimmune (Hashimoto's)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Thyroid Gland Surgical Anatomy
* **Location**: Anterior neck, deep to strap muscles, C5-T1 level
* **Arterial Supply**: Superior thyroid a. (from external carotid), Inferior thyroid a. (from thyrocervical trunk)
* **Venous Drainage**: Superior/Middle (→ IJV), Inferior (→ Brachiocephalic v.)
* **Nerve Relations (CRITICAL)**:
	* **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) (喉返神經)**: Ascends in tracheoesophageal groove, enters larynx deep to inferior constrictor; intimate with **Ligament of Berry (貝里氏韌帶)**
	* **Superior Laryngeal Nerve (SLN) External Branch (喉上神經外支)**: Descends alongside superior thyroid artery; supplies cricothyroid muscle (tenses vocal cords)
* **Parathyroid Glands (甲狀旁腺)**: Usually 4; superior (posterior capsule, constant), inferior (variable, often anterior to RLN)

### Thyroid Nodule Pathophysiology
* **Hyperplastic/Colloid**: Focal overgrowth of normal tissue, ± fluid degeneration
* **Inflammatory**: Focal granuloma/lymphocytic infiltrate (e.g., Hashimoto's thyroiditis)
* **Neoplastic (Benign)**: Follicular adenoma (encapsulated)
* **Neoplastic (Malignant)**: Papillary (PTC), Follicular (FTC), Medullary (MTC), Anaplastic (ATC), Lymphoma

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Clinical History of Thyroid Nodule
* Mostly **asymptomatic**, noticed incidentally or by peers
* **Compressive Symptoms**: Dysphagia (食道受壓), dyspnea/stridor (氣管受壓), hoarseness (RLN infiltration)
* **Endocrine Status**: Symptoms of hyperthyroidism (palpitations, heat intolerance, ↓ weight) or hypothyroidism (lethargy, ↑ weight)
* **Red Flags for Malignancy**: Rapid growth, fixed hard mass, concurrent hoarseness, family history (MEN2), previous neck irradiation

### Physical Examination of Neck
* **Inspection**: Anterior neck swelling, moves +ve with swallowing (**吞嚥時移動**)
* **Palpation**: Characterize size, consistency (hard = suspect malignancy), fixity
* **Lymph Nodes**: Palpate cervical chains (Levels I-VI) for **lymphadenopathy (頸部淋巴結腫大)**
* **Special Tests**:
	* **Pemberton's Sign (彭伯頓氏徵)**: Bilateral arm elevation → facial plethora/stridor (indicates retrosternal extension/thoracic inlet obstruction)
	* **Vocal Cord Assessment**: Listen to voice (bovine cough/hoarseness = RLN palsy)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Differential Diagnosis of Anterior Neck Mass
| Category | Conditions | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thyroidal** | Multinodular Goiter (MNG), Solitary Nodule, Thyroiditis | Moves with swallowing, central/lower neck |
| **Congenital** | Thyroglossal Duct Cyst (甲狀舌管囊腫) | Midline, moves +ve with tongue protrusion |
| **Lymph Node** | Reactive, Lymphoma, Metastatic (e.g., from head/neck SCC) | Usually lateral neck (Levels II-V), ± tender |
| **Other** | Dermoid cyst, Lipoma, Branchial cleft cyst | Branchial cyst lateral (anterior border of SCM) |

### Differentiating Benign vs Malignant Thyroid Nodules
| Feature          | Benign Nodule (良性結節)                  | Malignant Nodule (惡性結節)                            |
| :--------------- | :------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------- |
| **History**      | Slow growth, soft/spongy              | Rapid growth, childhood neck radiation, MEN2       |
| **Examination**  | Mobile, non-tender                    | Hard, fixed, cervical lymphadenopathy +ve          |
| **USG Findings** | Purely cystic, spongiform, comet-tail | Hypoechoic, microcalcifications, irregular margins |
| **Voice**        | Normal                                | Hoarseness (RLN involvement)                       |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### First-Line Laboratory Investigations
* **Serum TSH (促甲狀腺激素)**: Gold standard initial test
	* If ↓ TSH (Hyperthyroid) → Radionuclide scan indicated
	* If normal/↑ TSH → Proceed to USG Neck
* **Thyroid Autoantibodies**: Anti-TPO, Anti-Tg (suspected Hashimoto's)
* **Serum Calcitonin (降鈣素)**: If family history of Medullary Thyroid Cancer (MTC) or MEN2

### Radiological Imaging (USG & Scans)
* **USG Neck (頸部超聲波)**: Primary modality to assess nodule characteristics & guide FNAC
	* **TIRADS Scoring (USG Risk Stratification)**:
		* TR1: Benign (0 points)
		* TR2: Not suspicious (2 points)
		* TR3: Mildly suspicious (3 points) → FNAC if ≥2.5cm
		* TR4: Moderately suspicious (4-6 points) → FNAC if ≥1.5cm
		* TR5: Highly suspicious (≥7 points) → FNAC if ≥1cm
* **Radionuclide Thyroid Scan (Tc-99m or I-123)**: Indicated ONLY if TSH ↓. Detects "hot" (hyperfunctioning, rarely malignant) vs "cold" nodules.

### Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC)
* **FNAC (細針抽吸細胞檢查)**: Gold standard tissue sampling for normal/↑ TSH or "cold" nodules.
* **Bethesda System for Reporting Thyroid Cytopathology (TBSRTC)**:

| Category | Diagnosis                              | Risk of Malignancy (%) | Standard HA Management                              |
| :------- | :------------------------------------- | :--------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- |
| **I**    | Non-diagnostic                         | 5-10%                  | Repeat USG-guided FNAC                              |
| **II**   | Benign                                 | 0-3%                   | Clinical/USG follow-up                              |
| **III**  | AUS / FLUS                             | 10-30%                 | Repeat FNAC or molecular testing/diagnostic surgery |
| **IV**   | Follicular Neoplasm                    | 25-40%                 | Diagnostic Hemithyroidectomy                        |
| **V**    | Suspicious for Malignancy (Likely PTC) | 50-75%                 | Hemithyroidectomy or Total Thyroidectomy            |
| **VI**   | Malignant                              | 97-99%                 | Total Thyroidectomy ± Neck Dissection               |

### Pre-operative Staging & Assessment
* **Indirect Laryngoscopy / Nasoendoscopy**: Mandatory pre-op to document baseline vocal cord function
* **CT Neck/Thorax (Contrast)**: Indicated for retrosternal goiter, fixed masses, or extensive lymphadenopathy (Assess for airway compression/invasion)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Conservative & Medical Management
* **Benign Nodules (Bethesda II)**: Routine clinical and USG surveillance (e.g., 12-24 months)
* **Hot Nodules (Hyperfunctioning)**: Medical therapy (Carbimazole), Radioiodine (I-131) ablation, or surgery
* **Cystic Nodules**: Simple aspiration; if recur, consider surgical excision or ethanol/radiofrequency ablation (RFA)

### Indications for Thyroid Surgery
* **Absolute Indications**:
	* Confirmed malignancy (Bethesda VI)
	* Suspicious cytology (Bethesda IV, V)
* **Relative Indications**:
	* Compressive symptoms (dysphagia, stridor, choking)
	* Retrosternal extension
	* Cosmetic deformity
	* Patient preference / anxiety
	* Toxic adenoma/MNG failing medical therapy

### HA Protocol for Thyroid Nodules
1. TSH checked at GOPC → refer Surgical/ENT SOPD.
2. USG neck + FNAC via one-stop clinic.
3. Discuss in Multi-Disciplinary Team (MDT) if malignant.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Types of Surgical Intervention
* **Hemithyroidectomy / Lobectomy (半甲狀腺切除術)**: For Bethesda IV/V, isolated benign nodules, or low-risk unifocal PTC <4cm.
* **Total Thyroidectomy (全甲狀腺切除術)**: For Bethesda VI (most cancers), bilateral MNG, marked compressive symptoms.
* **Prophylactic Central Neck Dissection (Level VI)**: Controversial; selectively done for PTC based on MDT decision.

### Key Operative Steps (Hemithyroidectomy)
* **Positioning**: Supine, neck extended (sandbag under shoulders)
* **Incision**: Transverse cervical collar incision (Kocher's incision) in a natural skin crease
* **Exposure**: Elevate subplatysmal flaps; open investing fascia vertically in midline between strap muscles
* **Mobilization**: Retract strap muscles laterally; ligate middle thyroid vein
* **Superior Pole**: Divide superior thyroid vessels close to gland (prevent SLN external branch injury)
* **Inferior Pole & Capsular Dissection**: Identify & preserve parathyroids on vascular pedicles. Identify RLN in tracheoesophageal groove.
* **Resection**: Divide ligament of Berry; dissect isthmus off trachea.

### Informed Consent (CRITICAL)
* **General**: Bleeding (hematoma), infection, scarring (keloid/hypertrophic scar)
* **Specific**:
* **RLN Palsy (喉返神經受損)**: ~1-2% permanent. Unilateral → hoarseness. Bilateral → airway obstruction requiring tracheostomy.
* **Hypoparathyroidism/Hypocalcemia (低血鈣症)**: Transient (~20%), permanent (~1-2%).
* Requirement for lifelong Thyroxine (T4) replacement (if Total Thyroidectomy).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Ward Care
* **Airway Monitoring**: Keep tracheostomy tray / stitch cutter at bedside for 24 hrs
* **Position**: Head elevation 30-45° (↓ venous engorgement, ↓ edema)
* **Drain Management**: Removal once output <20-30mL/24h (often drainless in modern ERAS)

### Biochemical Monitoring Protocol
* **Serum Calcium & PTH**: Monitor post-op day 1 & 2 (especially after Total Thyroidectomy)
* Monitor for clinical signs of **Hypocalcemia**:
	* Peri-oral/fingertip numbness/tingling
	* **Chvostek's Sign**: Tapping facial nerve → facial muscle twitch
	* **Trousseau's Sign**: Inflating BP cuff above systolic for 3 mins → carpopedal spasm

### Long-term Rehabilitation
* **Thyroid Replacement Therapy**: L-thyroxine (Levothyroxine) for life (Total) or if hypothyroid (Hemi)
* TSH suppression therapy targeted based on risk of recurrence (for malignancies)
* Scar massage/silicone gel to prevent keloids

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Complications (<24 hours)
* **Tension Hematoma (頸部血腫)**: SURGICAL EMERGENCY. Deep to strap muscles → rapidly compresses airway → stridor/hypoxia.
* **Bilateral RLN Injury**: Immediate post-extubation stridor.

### Intermediate Complications (Days to Weeks)
* **Hypocalcemia (Tetany)**: Due to inadvertent removal or devascularization of parathyroids. Treat with PO Calcium + Vitamin D (Calcitriol) or IV Calcium Gluconate if severe.
* **Unilateral RLN Palsy**: Hoarse, breathy voice; weak cough.
* **SLN Injury**: Inability to hit high-pitched notes (noticeable in singers/teachers).
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**: Rare in clean neck surgery.

### Late Complications (Months to Years)
* **Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能低下)**
* **Keloid / Hypertrophic Scar**
* **Tumor Recurrence** (in cases of malignancy)

### Prognosis of Thyroid Cancers
* **Papillary (PTC)**: Excellent (>95% 10-year survival)
* **Follicular (FTC)**: Good (hematogenous spread to bone/lungs possible)
* **Medullary (MTC)**: Variable (depends on stage, MEN2 association)
* **Anaplastic (ATC)**: Dismal (<10% 1-year survival, rapid local invasion)

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most immediate life-threatening complication post-thyroidectomy? Answer: Tension hematoma leading to acute airway compromise, requiring immediate bedside opening of the wound (cut sutures down to the strap muscles) before transferring to OT.
* Trap: Assuming a hyperfunctioning ("hot") nodule on a radionucleotide scan is highly likely to be malignant. Pearl: Hot nodules are rarely malignant (<1%); "cold" (non-functioning) nodules carry a much higher malignancy risk (~15-20%).
* Pitfall: Removing the parathyroid glands inadvertently during a total thyroidectomy. Always dissect tightly on the thyroid capsule (capsular dissection technique) to preserve the parathyroid glands on their vascular pedicle. If devascularized, mince and auto-transplant them into the sternocleidomastoid (SCM) muscle.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate between a Follicular Adenoma and a Follicular Carcinoma? Answer: They cannot be differentiated on cytology (FNAC). Diagnostic hemithyroidectomy is required for histological assessment to identify capsular or vascular invasion, which defines Follicular Carcinoma.
* Trap: Dissecting too far laterally when isolating the superior thyroid pole, risking injury to adjacent structures. Pearl: Always ligate the superior thyroid vessels as close to the thyroid capsule as possible to avoid injuring the external branch of the superior laryngeal nerve.
* Pitfall: Discharging a post-total thyroidectomy patient without checking calcium levels. Always protocolize serum calcium/PTH checks on Post-Op Day 1 and monitor for early clinical signs like peri-oral paresthesia or a positive Chvostek's sign.
