# Heart Valve Disease (心瓣疾病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definitions
* **Valvular Stenosis (心瓣狹窄):** Incomplete valve opening → forward blood flow obstruction → pressure overload hypertrophy.
* **Valvular Regurgitation (心瓣關閉不全):** Incomplete valve closure → backward blood flow leakage → volume overload dilation.
* **Surgical Heart Valve Disease:** Haemodynamically severe structural lesions requiring **Surgical Valve Replacement (心瓣置換手術)** or **Repair (心瓣修補手術)** to prevent irreversible myocardial damage and death.

### Local Epidemiology & HA Context
* **Degenerative Calcific Aortic Stenosis:** Most common indication for valve replacement in HKHA (driven by aging population).
* **Rheumatic Heart Disease (風濕性心臟病):** Still prevalent in HK among elderly and immigrants; predominantly affects Mitral > Aortic valves.
* **Mitral Valve Prolapse (二尖瓣脫垂):** Most common cause of primary Mitral Regurgitation (MR) in HK.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Heart Valves
* **Aortic Valve (主動脈瓣):** 3 cusps (Left, Right, Non-coronary). Located at aortic root. Critically close relation to **Bundle of His** (high risk of conduction block during AVR/TAVI).
* **Mitral Valve (二尖瓣):** 2 leaflets (Anterior, Posterior). Anchored to papillary muscles via **Chordae Tendineae (腱索)**.
* **Tricuspid & Pulmonary Valves:** Right-sided valves. Less commonly require isolated surgery; often operated on concomitantly due to secondary right heart failure.

### Pathophysiology of Left-Sided Valve Lesions
* **Aortic Stenosis (AS):** LV outflow tract obstruction → ↑ LV pressure → **LV Hypertrophy (LVH)** → ↓ Coronary perfusion & Diastolic dysfunction → Ischaemia & Heart Failure.
* **Aortic Regurgitation (AR):** Diastolic backflow to LV → LV volume overload → Eccentric hypertrophy → Progressive LV dilation & systolic failure.
* **Mitral Stenosis (MS):** LA outflow obstruction → ↑ LA pressure → LA dilation (↑ AFib risk) → Pulmonary venous HTN → Pulmonary oedema → **Right Heart Failure (RHF)**.
* **Mitral Regurgitation (MR):** Systolic backflow to LA → LA volume overload → ↑ LA pressure + LV volume overload → LV eccentric hypertrophy.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Aortic Valve Disease Symptoms & Signs
* **AS Symptoms:** Classic triad = **SAD** (Syncope, Angina, Dyspnoea). Indicates severe disease with poor survival without surgery.
* **AS Signs:** Slow-rising pulse, narrow pulse pressure, heaving apex beat, **Ejection Systolic Murmur (ESM)** radiating to carotids.
* **AR Symptoms:** Exertional dyspnoea, palpitations (awareness of forceful heartbeat).
* **AR Signs:** Collapsing (water-hammer) pulse, wide pulse pressure, displaced apex, **Early Diastolic Murmur (EDM)** best heard at left lower sternal edge leaning forward.

### Mitral Valve Disease Symptoms & Signs
* **MS Symptoms:** Dyspnoea, haemoptysis, systemic thromboembolism (due to concurrent AFib).
* **MS Signs:** Malar flush, irregularly irregular pulse (AFib), tapping apex, **Mid-Diastolic Murmur (MDM)** with opening snap.
* **MR Symptoms:** Progressive dyspnoea, orthopnoea, extreme fatigue.
* **MR Signs:** Displaced thrusting apex, **Pansystolic Murmur (PSM)** radiating to axilla.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Clinical Features
| Valve Lesion | Pulse Character | Apex Beat | Classic Murmur (Auscultation) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aortic Stenosis (主動脈瓣狹窄)** | Slow-rising (Pulsus parvus et tardus) | Heaving (pressure overload) | **ESM** @ Aortic area → Carotids |
| **Aortic Regurgitation (主動脈瓣關閉不全)** | Collapsing (Water-hammer) | Displaced (volume overloaded) | **EDM** @ L-sternal edge (leaning fwd) |
| **Mitral Stenosis (二尖瓣狹窄)** | Normal / Irregular (AFib) | Tapping (palpable S1) | **MDM** @ Apex (L-lateral decubitus) |
| **Mitral Regurgitation (二尖瓣關閉不全)** | Normal / Irregular (AFib) | Displaced, thrusting | **PSM** @ Apex → Axilla |

### Differential Diagnosis of Valve Murmurs
* **Systolic Murmurs:** AS, MR, Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy (HOCM), Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD), Tricuspid Regurgitation.
* **Diastolic Murmurs:** AR, MS, Pulmonary Regurgitation (Graham Steell murmur).

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Bedside & Laboratory Investigations
* **ECG:** Assess rhythm (AFib in mitral disease) & voltage (LVH strain pattern in AS).
* **Blood Tests:** CBC, LRFT, Coagulation profile (baseline INR), Troponin, **NT-proBNP** (strong prognostic marker for heart failure).
* **Crossmatch:** Group & Screen, prepare 2-4 units PRBC for open-heart surgery.

### Gold Standard Imaging (Echocardiography)
* **Transthoracic Echocardiogram (TTE):** 1st-line diagnostic tool. Quantifies valve area, transvalvular pressure gradients, and **Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF)**.
* **Transoesophageal Echocardiogram (TOE):** Superior posterior resolution. Crucial for assessing mitral valve anatomy (for repair planning), detecting LA appendage thrombus, and diagnosing **Infective Endocarditis (感染性心內膜炎)** vegetations.

### Pre-Operative Workup for Valve Surgery
* **CXR:** Evaluates cardiomegaly, pulmonary venous congestion, aortic calcification.
* **Coronary Angiogram (冠狀動脈造影):** Mandatory in HKHA for men >40yo, post-menopausal women, or those with CAD risks to rule out concurrent ischemia requiring **CABG (冠狀動脈搭橋手術)**.
* **Dental Clearance:** Orthopantomogram (OPG) + dental review to eradicate oral sepsis before prosthetic material insertion.
* **Carotid Doppler:** Exclude significant carotid stenosis to mitigate stroke risk during cardiopulmonary bypass.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### General Medical Optimization
* **Heart Failure Tx:** Loop Diuretics (Furosemide) for pulmonary congestion. ACEi/ARBs for afterload reduction in MR/AR (*Strictly avoid vasodilators in severe AS*).
* **Arrhythmia Control:** Rate control (Beta-blockers/Digoxin) for AFib in mitral disease.
* **Anticoagulation:** Warfarin or DOACs for AFib. (*Note: DOACs are strictly contraindicated in moderate-to-severe MS or with mechanical valves*).

### Surgical Indications for Aortic Stenosis (AS)
* **Class I Surgical Triggers:**
* 1. Symptomatic severe AS (Mean gradient ≥40 mmHg, Peak velocity ≥4.0 m/s, Valve Area ≤1.0 cm²).
* 2. Asymptomatic severe AS with **LVEF <50%**.
* 3. Severe AS undergoing other cardiac surgery (e.g., CABG).

### Surgical Indications for Mitral Regurgitation (MR)
* **Class I Surgical Triggers (Primary MR):**
* 1. Symptomatic severe MR with LVEF >30%.
* 2. Asymptomatic severe MR with LV dysfunction (**LVEF 30-60%** or **LV End-Systolic Dimension ≥40mm**).

### HA Protocol: TAVI vs SAVR Pathway
* **SAVR (Surgical Aortic Valve Replacement):** Gold standard for low surgical risk, age <65-70, or concurrent cardiac disease requiring open approach.
* **TAVI/TAVR (Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation - 經導管主動脈瓣植入術):** Standard of care for high/prohibitive surgical risk, age >80, or severe frailty. Rapidly expanding to intermediate-risk patients in HKHA.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Operative Principles (Open Surgery)
* **Access & Support:** Median sternotomy + **Cardiopulmonary Bypass (CPB)** via ascending aorta & right atrium cannulation. Myocardial protection via **Cardioplegia (心臟停搏液)**.
* **Mitral Valve:** **Repair > Replacement** whenever feasible. Preserving the chordal apparatus protects LV geometry and contractility. Annuloplasty ring inserted to prevent future annular dilation.
* **Aortic Valve:** Excision of heavily calcified leaflets + rigorous decalcification of annulus + Replacement (Repair is rarely durable for calcific AS).

### Prosthetic Valve Selection (Mechanical vs Bioprosthetic)
| Feature | Mechanical Valve (機械心瓣) | Bioprosthetic Valve (生物心瓣) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Material** | Pyrolytic carbon / Titanium | Porcine / Bovine pericardium |
| **Durability** | Lifelong (Excellent) | Degenerates in 10-15 years |
| **Anticoagulation** | **Lifelong Warfarin Required** (Target INR 2.5-3.5) | Short-term (3-6mo) Aspirin/Warfarin |
| **Target Age (HK)** | **< 60-65 years old** | **> 65-70 years old** |
| **Drawbacks** | Bleeding risk, audible clicking | Structural Valve Deterioration (SVD) requiring redo surgery |

### Informed Consent & Critical Risks
* **Mortality:** ~1-3% for elective single-valve surgery.
* **Neurological:** Stroke (embolism of calcium, air, or atheroma during cross-clamping).
* **Cardiac:** **Complete Heart Block (完全性心臟傳導阻滯)** requiring permanent pacemaker (high risk in AVR/TAVI due to conduction system proximity).
* **Valve-specific:** Paravalvular leak, Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis (PVE).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-Operative Care (ICU)
* **Haemodynamic Targets:** Maintain MAP 65-80 mmHg. Strict blood pressure control to protect aortic suture lines.
* **Drain Monitoring:** Mediastinal and pleural tubes. Alert surgeon if blood loss >200ml/hr for 2 consecutive hours (suggests surgical bleeding).
* **Early Extubation:** Target extubation within 6 hours post-op (Fast-track protocol).

### Long-Term Management & Rehabilitation
* **Anticoagulation Bridging:** Initiate IV Heparin post-op for mechanical valves, transition to oral Warfarin once drains are removed and haemostasis is secure.
* **Endocarditis Prophylaxis:** Lifelong strict oral hygiene. **Prophylactic antibiotics (Amoxicillin or Clindamycin)** 1 hour prior to high-risk invasive dental procedures.
* **Surveillance:** Baseline echocardiogram at 2-3 months post-op, then routine annual follow-up.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (<30 Days)
* **Cardiac Tamponade (心包填塞):** Clotted mediastinal drains → blood accumulates around heart → ↓ cardiac output.
* **Bleeding:** Surgical site bleeding requiring urgent return to theatre.
* **Arrhythmias:** Post-op AFib (peaks day 2-3), Bradycardia/Heart Block.
* **Low Cardiac Output Syndrome (LCOS):** Requiring prolonged inotropic support or Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump (IABP).

### Late Surgical Complications (>30 Days)
* **Structural Valve Deterioration (SVD):** Progressive calcification and tearing of tissue bioprostheses.
* **Valve Thrombosis / Pannus:** Mechanical valves seizing up due to subtherapeutic INR or tissue overgrowth.
* **Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis (PVE):** High mortality complication requiring 6 weeks IV antibiotics ± urgent redo root replacement.
* **Haemolysis:** Mechanical shearing of RBCs causing anaemia, ↑ LDH, and schistocytes on blood smear.

### Prognosis & Survival Rates
* **Severe Symptomatic AS:** Untreated median survival is **< 2-3 years**.
* **Post-AVR:** Restores normal life expectancy matching the age-matched population.
* **Mitral Repair:** Excellent long-term survival and freedom from reoperation if performed prior to irreversible LV systolic dysfunction.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why is Mitral Valve Repair preferred over Mitral Valve Replacement? Answer: Repair preserves the subvalvular apparatus (chordae tendineae and papillary muscles), which is vital for maintaining Left Ventricular geometry and optimal systolic function.
* Viva Question: What is the classic symptom triad of severe Aortic Stenosis? Answer: Syncope, Angina, and Dyspnoea (SAD).
* Trap: Prescribing DOACs (e.g., Apixaban or Rivaroxaban) for a patient with a mechanical heart valve. Pearl: DOACs are absolutely contraindicated for mechanical valves; these patients MUST be managed on lifelong Warfarin with a strict target INR.
* Pitfall: Waiting for LVEF to drop below 50% before referring severe Mitral Regurgitation for surgery. In MR, the left ventricle ejects freely into the low-pressure left atrium, which falsely elevates the measured LVEF. True LV dysfunction begins when LVEF falls below 60%; surgery must be performed before reaching this threshold.
* Trap: Failing to request a preoperative coronary angiogram in an elderly patient scheduled for elective valve surgery. Pearl: You must always rule out occult coronary artery disease in patients >40 years old; concomitant CABG must be performed if significant coronary stenosis is identified.
* Viva Question: Where is the AV node located in relation to the aortic valve, and why is this anatomically significant during surgery? Answer: The Bundle of His runs extremely close to the membranous septum near the right and non-coronary cusps; surgical debridement or excessive suture traction in this area carries a high risk of inducing Complete Heart Block requiring a permanent pacemaker.
