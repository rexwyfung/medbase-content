# Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Prostate Cancer
* Malignant transformation of prostatic epithelial cells.
* Predominantly **Adenocarcinoma (腺癌)** (>95%).
* Arises mostly within the **Peripheral Zone (外周帶)**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* 3rd most common male cancer in HK.
* Incidence ↑ rapidly due to aging population & westernized diet.
* Median age at diagnosis: ~60–70 years old.
* Local mortality rates remain stable due to early detection via **Prostate-Specific Antigen (前列腺特異抗原)** tracking.


## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy and McNeal Zones
* Prostate divided into 4 zones: **Peripheral Zone (外周帶)** (70% volume, site of 75–80% of cancers), **Transition Zone (過渡帶)** (site of **Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (良性前列腺增生)** & 10–15% of cancers), **Central Zone (中央帶)**, and **Anterior Fibromuscular Stroma (前纖維肌肉基質)**.
* Neurovascular bundle runs posterolaterally at 5 and 7 o'clock positions relative to the prostatic capsule; crucial landmark for nerve-sparing **Radical Prostatectomy (根治性前列腺切除術)**.
* Lymphatic drainage: Primarily to **Internal Iliac (內髂)** and **Obturator Lymph Nodes (閉孔淋巴結)**, followed by External Iliac and Common Iliac nodes.

### Advanced Pathophysiology of Malignant Transformation
* Androgen receptor (AR) signalling drives luminal epithelial cell proliferation and survival.
* Somatic mutations: **TMPRSS2-ERG gene fusion**, **PTEN loss** (leads to PI3K/Akt pathway activation), and **TP53 inactivation**.
* Grading based on **Gleason Score (格里森评分)** (Primary + Secondary pattern architectural patterns 1–5) or the updated **ISUP Grade Groups (國際泌尿病理協會分級群組)** (Grade 1 to 5).


## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Storage Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms
* **Frequency (頻尿)** due to local bladder irritation or bladder outlet narrowing.
* **Nocturia (夜尿)** leading to sleep disruption.
* **Urgency (急尿)** and occasionally urge incontinence.

### Voiding Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms
* **Hesitancy (排尿困難)** due to mechanical **Bladder Outlet Obstruction (膀胱出口梗阻)**.
* **Reduced Urinary Stream (尿流細弱)** or prolonged voiding time.
* **Intermittency (間斷排尿)** and terminal dribbling.

### Advanced and Metastatic Signs and Symptoms
* **Visible Haematuria (肉眼血尿)** or **Haematospermia (血精症)** due to invasion into the bladder neck, urethra, or seminal vesicles.
* Bone pain (typically lumbar spine, pelvis, ribs) due to osteoblastic **Bone Metastases (骨轉移)**.
* Spinal cord compression signs: Bilateral lower limb weakness, saddle anaesthesia, hyperreflexia, acute urinary retention.
* Cachexia, weight loss, and bilateral lower limb lymphoedema due to extensive pelvic lymphadenopathy.

### Physical Examination Findings
* **Digital Rectal Examination (直腸指檢)** revealing an asymmetrical, hard, nodular, irregular prostate gland with loss of the median sulcus.
* Palpable distended bladder indicating **Chronic Urinary Retention (慢性尿 retention)**.


## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia Versus Prostate Cancer
| Feature | Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌) | Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (良性前列腺增生) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **DRE Findings** | Hard, irregular, nodular gland, fixed, lost median sulcus | Smooth, firm, elastic, symmetrically enlarged gland |
| **Zone of Origin** | **Peripheral Zone (外周帶)** | **Transition Zone (過渡帶)** |
| **PSA Kinetics** | Rapid, sustained velocity rise (>0.75 ng/mL/year) | Slow, stable, volume-dependent rise |
| **Free/Total PSA Ratio** | ↓ Free/Total PSA ratio (<15%) | Normal or ↑ Free/Total PSA ratio (>25%) |

### Prostatitis Versus Prostate Cancer
| Feature | Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌) | Prostatitis (前列腺炎) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clinical Presentation** | Often asymptomatic early, progressive LUTS, systemic features | Acute perineal/suprapubic pain, dysuria, fevers, chills |
| **DRE Findings** | Nodular, firm, painless | Exquisitely tender, boggy, hot gland (avoid vigorous massage) |
| **PSA Trend** | Persistent elevation | Spikes acutely; takes **6 weeks** to baseline after antibiotic resolution |


## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-line Blood and Urine Laboratories
* **Total PSA (tPSA)**: Screen screening/diagnostic threshold traditionally >4.0 ng/mL (adjusted lower for younger HK patients).
* **PSA Derivatives**: **PSA Density (PSAD)** >0.15 ng/mL/cc or low **Free/Total PSA ratio (<15%)** raises malignancy suspicion.
* **Renal Function Tests**: Serum **Creatinine (肌酐)** & **eGFR** to rule out obstructive uropathy or hydronephrosis.
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析) & MSU Culture**: Rule out concurrent UTI before any instrumentation or PSA testing interpretation.

### Advanced Imaging Workup
* **Multiparametric MRI (mpMRI)**: Preferred pre-biopsy tool. Evaluated using **PI-RADS (前列腺影像報告和數據系統) v2.1 scoring** (Score 1–5; 4 or 5 indicates high likelihood of clinically significant cancer).
* **Transrectal Ultrasound (TRUS)** or **Transperineal Template Biopsies (TPTBP)**: Core biopsy confirmation. TPTBP preferred in HK Hospital Authority due to **lower sepsis risk** and better anterior zone sampling.
* **Technetium-99m Bone Scan**: Indicated if tPSA >20 ng/mL, Gleason score ≥8, or bone pain present to look for osteoblastic lesions.
* **Ga-68 PSMA PET-CT**: Advanced molecular imaging utilized for staging high-risk patients or pinpointing biochemically recurrent disease.

### TNM Staging Breakdown
| Stage (T) | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **T1** | Clinically inapparent tumor, not palpable by DRE or visible on imaging (T1a/b incidental TURP chips, T1c biopsy due to ↑ PSA). |
| **T2** | Tumor confined within the prostate gland (T2a ≤half of 1 lobe, T2b >half of 1 lobe, T2c both lobes). |
| **T3** | Tumor extends through the prostatic capsule (T3a extra-capsular extension, T3b invades **Seminal Vesicles (精囊)**). |
| **T4** | Tumor fixed or invades adjacent structures other than seminal vesicles (e.g., bladder neck, rectum, levator ani). |


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Localized Low-Risk Disease Protocols
* **Active Surveillance (主動監測)**: Regular monitoring via serial PSA (every 3–6 months), DRE (every 6 months), and repeat mpMRI/biopsies. Preferred for low-volume ISUP Grade Group 1 disease to avoid treatment morbidity.
* **Watchful Waiting (觀察等待)**: Symptomatic conservative management deferred until clinical progression; reserved for elderly patients with <10-year life expectancy.

### Localized Intermediate and High-Risk Disease Management
* **Radical Prostatectomy (根治性前列腺切除術)**: Definitively indicated for localized disease with >10-year life expectancy. Can be open, laparoscopic, or **Robot-Assisted Laparoscopic Prostatectomy (RALP)**.
* **Radical External Beam Radiotherapy (EBRT)**: Often combined with 6–36 months of **Androgen Deprivation Therapy (ADT)** for high-risk patients.

### Advanced and Metastatic Disease Protocols (HK HA Formulations)
* First-line ADT: Medical castration via **LHRH Agonists** (e.g., Goserelin, Leuprorelin) or **LHRH Antagonists** (e.g., Degarelix). 
* Note: LHRH agonists cause an initial testosterone surge; must co-prescribe an anti-androgen (e.g., Cyproterone acetate or Bicalutamide) for 2–4 weeks to prevent **Tumor Flare Symptom (腫瘤耀斑現象)**.
* **Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer (CRPC)**: Progression despite castrate levels of testosterone (<50 ng/dL). Manage with docetaxel chemotherapy or novel hormonal agents (Abiraterone, Enzalutamide).


## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Radical Prostatectomy
* Patient in Trendelenburg position.
* Dissection of the **Endopelvic Fascia (盆腔內筋膜)** and incision of puboprostatic ligaments.
* Control and ligation of the **Dorsal Venous Complex (背側靜脈叢)** to minimize intraoperative bleeding.
* Transection of the bladder neck, mobilization of seminal vesicles, and division of vasa deferentia.
* Careful preservation of posterolateral neurovascular bundles if oncologically clear (**Nerve-Sparing Surgery**).
* Apical transection of the urethra and creation of a watertight **Vesicourethral Anastomosis (膀胱尿道吻合術)** over a Foley catheter.
* Standard or extended pelvic lymph node dissection for intermediate/high-risk patients.

### Consent and Major Surgical Risks
* **Urinary Incontinence (尿失禁)**: Sphincter damage/weakness; resolves over 6–12 months in majority, but requires pelvic floor exercises.
* **Erectile Dysfunction (勃起功能障礙)**: Injury to cavernous nerves; risk ↑ if non-nerve-sparing approach required.
* Rectal injury (<1%): Risk of rectourethral fistula.
* Standard risks: Pelvic lymphocele, deep vein thrombosis, anastomotic leak, bladder neck contracture.


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Management
* Early mobilization on post-op Day 1 to reduce thromboembolic events.
* Graduated pneumatic compression stockings and low-molecular-weight heparin as per HK HA thromboprophylaxy standards.
* Maintenance of continuous low-pressure bladder irrigation if gross haematuria present; secure Foley catheter carefully to abdominal wall to avoid tension on the vesicourethral anastomosis.
* Early transition to oral fluids and regular diet to reduce post-op ileus.

### Catheter Care and Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation
* Catheter remains in situ for **7–14 days** post-op to allow anastomotic healing.
* Perform a **Retrograde Cystogram (逆行性膀胱造影)** prior to removal if there is a high clinical suspicion of an anastomotic leak.
* Initiate aggressive **Pelvic Floor Muscle Training (盆底肌肉鍛煉)** (Kegel exercises) immediately upon catheter removal to accelerate urinary continence recovery.


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications
* Primary or reactionary haemorrhage from the dorsal venous complex requiring re-exploration.
* Pelvic collections or urinoma secondary to an incomplete or leaking vesicourethral anastomosis.
* Thromboembolism: DVT or pulmonary embolism.
* Urinary tract infection or acute pelvic pelvic sepsis.

### Intermediate and Late Complications
* **Anastomotic Leakage (吻合口漏)** causing prolonged drain output or localized peritonitis.
* **Bladder Neck Contracture (膀胱頸攣縮)** causing progressive voiding LUTS months after surgery; managed with endoscopic incision.
* Persistent **Stress Urinary Incontinence (壓力性尿失禁)** beyond 12 months (may require an artificial urinary sphincter if refractory).
* Permanent erectile dysfunction.

### Prognosis and Biochemical Recurrence Tracking
* Regular follow-up with tPSA monitoring every 3 months.
* Successful radical surgery should lead to undetectable tPSA (<0.1 or <0.2 ng/mL).
* **Biochemical Recurrence (生化復發)** defined as two consecutive PSA readings **≥0.2 ng/mL** after prostatectomy.
* 5-year survival for localized disease exceeds 95–99%; drops significantly if distant or visceral metastases are present at baseline.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why must a patient be co-administered an anti-androgen when initiating an LHRH agonist for metastatic prostate cancer? Answer: LHRH agonists cause an initial transient surge in LH and testosterone before down-regulating receptors; this "tumor flare" can cause catastrophic acute spinal cord compression or severe bone pain if not blocked by an anti-androgen like cyproterone acetate.
* Trap: Assuming a normal digital rectal examination excludes prostate cancer. Pearl: Up to 15–20% of prostate cancers present with a normal DRE because the tumor is either small (T1c) or located deep within the anterior transition zone, making PSA and mpMRI indispensable diagnostic tools.
* Pitfall: Ordering a PSA test immediately after a patient presents with an acute urinary tract infection or urinary retention. This will cause a falsely elevated PSA reading leading to unnecessary biopsies. Wait at least 6 weeks following full eradication of the UTI or resolution of retention before checking baseline PSA.
* Viva Question: What is the clinical definition of biochemical recurrence following a radical prostatectomy versus definitive radiotherapy? Answer: Post-prostatectomy recurrence is defined as a confirmed PSA ≥0.2 ng/mL, whereas post-radiotherapy recurrence (Phoenix Criteria) is defined as a PSA rise of 2.0 ng/mL or more above the nadir value.
