# Biliary Tract Malignancy (膽道惡性腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Biliary Tract Malignancy
* Malignant tumors arising from the biliary epithelium or gallbladder mucosa.
* **Cholangiocarcinoma (CCA) (膽管癌)**: Adenocarcinoma of bile ducts. Subdivided into **Intrahepatic (iCCA)**, **Perihilar/Klatskin (pCCA)**, and **Distal (dCCA)**.
* **Gallbladder Carcinoma (GBC) (膽囊癌)**: Malignant tumor of the gallbladder, usually adenocarcinoma.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Endemic region for **Clonorchis sinensis (中華肝吸蟲)** infection (via raw freshwater fish), driving high local incidence of CCA.
* Strong association with recurrent **Pyogenic Cholangitis (復發性化膿性膽管炎)** & **Hepatolithiasis (肝內膽管結石)** in the local population.
* GBC shows higher prevalence in females; CCA slightly male-predominant.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Biliary Tract Surgical Anatomy (膽道外科解剖)
* **Perihilar Region**: Confluence of Left & Right Hepatic Ducts to form Common Hepatic Duct (CHD).
* **Calot's Triangle (卡洛氏三角)** borders: Cystic duct (inferior), CHD (medial), Inferior border of liver (superior). Contains cystic artery & Lund's node.
* **Blood Supply**: Extrahepatic bile duct has purely axial blood supply (3 o'clock & 9 o'clock arteries). Aggressive dissection → ischemia → anastomotic leak/stricture.
* **Caudate Lobe (尾狀葉) (Segment I)**: Biliary drainage directly into left & right main hepatic ducts at the hilum. 

### Pathophysiology & Risk Factors of Cholangiocarcinoma
* Chronic biliary inflammation → biliary epithelial dysplasia → invasive adenocarcinoma.
* High-yield risk factors: **Clonorchis sinensis**, **Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC) (原發性硬化性膽管炎)**, **Choledochal cysts (膽管囊腫)**, HBV/HCV cirrhosis, Thorotrast exposure.

### Pathophysiology & Risk Factors of Gallbladder Carcinoma
* Chronic cholecystitis → mucosal dysplasia → carcinoma in situ → invasive carcinoma.
* High-yield risk factors: Large **Gallstones (膽結石)** (>3cm), **Gallbladder polyps (膽囊息肉)** (>1cm), **Porcelain gallbladder (瓷化膽囊)**, anomalous pancreaticobiliary duct junction (APBDJ).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Cholangiocarcinoma
* Painless, progressive **Obstructive Jaundice (阻塞性黃疸)** (classic in pCCA & dCCA).
* Severe **Pruritus (痕癢)** (often precedes jaundice due to bile salt deposition).
* **Pale stools (灰白便)** & **Dark urine (茶色尿)**.
* Constitutional symptoms: **Weight loss (體重下降)**, anorexia, fatigue.
* iCCA often asymptomatic until large (presents with vague RUQ pain/mass).

### Symptoms of Gallbladder Carcinoma
* Often asymptomatic early (discovered incidentally post-cholecystectomy).
* Late-stage mimics chronic cholecystitis: Right Upper Quadrant (RUQ) pain, nausea, weight loss.
* Jaundice indicates direct invasion of biliary tree or hilar node compression.

### Clinical Signs on Examination
* **Scleral icterus (鞏膜黃疸)** & generalized cutaneous jaundice.
* Scratch marks (excoriations) from intense pruritus.
* **Hepatomegaly (肝腫大)** (liver palpable due to biliary stasis/obstruction).
* **Courvoisier's Sign (庫瓦西耶徵)**: Palpable, non-tender gallbladder in a jaundiced patient.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis for Obstructive Jaundice
| Category | Condition (Chinese Translation) | Key Discriminatory Features |
|---|---|---|
| Malignant | Pancreatic Head Cancer (胰臟頭癌) | Painless jaundice, +ve Courvoisier's sign, "Double duct sign" on imaging |
| Malignant | Periampullary Carcinoma (壺腹周圍癌) | Fluctuating jaundice, GI bleeding, occult blood +ve stool |
| Benign | Choledocholithiasis (膽總管結石) | Biliary colic (painful), fluctuating jaundice, deranged LFTs resolving post-pain |
| Benign | Mirizzi Syndrome (米里齊綜合症) | Large stone in GB neck compressing CHD, history of recurrent cholecystitis |
| Benign | Benign Biliary Stricture (良性膽管狹窄) | History of prior biliary surgery/iatrogenic trauma, recurrent cholangitis |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Laboratory Investigations for Biliary Malignancy
* **Liver Function Tests (LFTs)**: ↑ **Bilirubin (膽紅素)** (conjugated), ↑↑ **ALP/GGT** (obstructive pattern), mildly ↑ ALT/AST.
* **Tumor Markers**: ↑ **CA 19-9**, ↑ **CEA**. (Rule out hepatocellular carcinoma with AFP).
* **Coagulation Profile**: ↑ PT/INR due to ↓ Vitamin K absorption (lack of gut bile).

### Imaging Modalities for Biliary Tumors
* **USG Abdomen (腹部超聲波)**: 1st line. Identifies intra/extra-hepatic biliary dilatation, exact level of obstruction, GB masses/stones.
* **Triphasic CT Abdomen/Pelvis (電腦斷層掃描)**: Assesses tumor extent, vascular invasion (Portal Vein/Hepatic Artery), regional lymphadenopathy, and distant metastasis.
* **MRCP (磁力共振膽胰管造影)**: Gold standard non-invasive imaging for mapping biliary tree anatomy and tumor extent (crucial for pCCA staging).
* **PTC / ERCP**: Diagnostic (brush cytology/biopsy for tissue diagnosis) & Therapeutic (biliary stenting/drainage).

### Bismuth-Corlette Staging System for Perihilar Cholangiocarcinoma
| Type | Extent of Biliary Involvement |
|---|---|
| Type I | Tumor below confluence; L/R main hepatic ducts intact |
| Type II | Tumor involves confluence; L/R ducts NOT isolated |
| Type IIIa | Tumor extends into Right secondary intrahepatic ducts |
| Type IIIb | Tumor extends into Left secondary intrahepatic ducts |
| Type IV | Tumor involves bilateral secondary intrahepatic ducts OR is multifocal |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Pre-operative Biliary Drainage (PBD) Indications
* Routine PBD is NOT recommended (↑ risk of cholangitis).
* HA Protocol Indications for PBD: Concurrent acute cholangitis, severe jaundice (Bilirubin >250 µmol/L) awaiting major hepatectomy, malnutrition requiring prolonged pre-op optimization.
* Modalities: Endoscopic stenting (ERCP) or Percutaneous Transhepatic Biliary Drainage (PTBD).

### Surgical Resectability Criteria
* Curative intent is achieved ONLY via complete (R0) surgical resection.
* Criteria for Resectability: 
  * Absence of distant metastases or peritoneal disease.
  * Absence of bilateral hepatic artery / main portal vein involvement (unless vascular reconstruction planned).
  * Adequate Future Liver Remnant (FLR) (>20% normal liver, >30-40% if underlying fibrosis/PSC).

### Medical & Palliative Management Protocols
* Unresectable disease: Palliative biliary drainage to relieve jaundice/pruritus (Self-expanding metallic stents preferred if life expectancy >3 months).
* Systemic Chemotherapy: 1st line is Gemcitabine + Cisplatin (HA standard oncology protocol).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles for Intrahepatic Cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA)
* Major **Hepatectomy (肝切除術)** to achieve negative margins (R0).
* Regional lymphadenectomy (hilar pedicle).

### Operative Principles for Perihilar Cholangiocarcinoma (pCCA)
* En bloc resection of extrahepatic bile duct + Major hepatectomy (usually extended Right or Left hepatectomy).
* Mandatory **Caudate lobectomy (尾狀葉切除術)** (due to direct biliary drainage into hilum).
* Regional lymphadenectomy + Roux-en-Y **Hepaticojejunostomy (肝管空腸吻合術)** reconstruction.

### Operative Principles for Distal Cholangiocarcinoma (dCCA)
* **Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple's procedure) (胰十二指腸切除術)**.
* Excision of CBD, head of pancreas, duodenum, distal stomach/pylorus, followed by triple reconstruction (pancreaticojejunostomy, hepaticojejunostomy, gastrojejunostomy).

### Operative Principles for Gallbladder Carcinoma (GBC)
* Incidental T1a (mucosa only, detected post-cholecystectomy): Simple Cholecystectomy is curative.
* T1b, T2, T3: **Extended Cholecystectomy (擴大膽囊切除術)** (En bloc resection of GB + wedge resection of liver segments IVb & V + portal lymphadenectomy).

### Informed Consent for Major Biliary Resection
* Risks: Massive hemorrhage requiring transfusion, **Bile leak (膽汁滲漏)**, Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF), severe intra-abdominal infection, deep vein thrombosis, mortality (<5% in specialized high-volume centers).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care for Biliary Resection
* ICU/HDU admission for first 24-48 hours.
* Continuous monitoring of hemodynamics and central venous pressure (CVP).
* Strict monitoring of surgical drain output (Assess for volume, frank blood, or bilious fluid indicating leak).
* Daily LFTs, Lactate, and Coagulation profile (PT/INR) to monitor liver synthetic function.

### ERAS Protocols for Hepato-biliary Surgery
* **Early Enteral Feeding**: Sips of clear fluids on Post-Op Day 1, advance as tolerated. Avoid prolonged NGT.
* **Early Mobilization**: Sit out of bed on Day 1 to ↓ risk of atelectasis and DVT.
* **Analgesia**: Multimodal (Epidural, PCA, paracetamol); minimize opioid use to prevent ileus.
* **VTE Prophylaxis**: LMWH + graduated compression stockings (TEDS).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (0-30 days)
* **Bile Leak (膽汁滲漏)**: From cut liver surface or hepaticojejunostomy breakdown. Managed by maintaining drains, ERCP stenting (if applicable), or re-operation if generalized peritonitis.
* **Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF) (肝切除術後肝衰竭)**: Characterized by ↑↑ Bilirubin, coagulopathy, encephalopathy.
* Intra-abdominal hemorrhage (slippage of vascular ties).
* Subphrenic abscess / intra-abdominal collection.

### Late Surgical Complications
* Biliary stricture at the hepaticojejunostomy anastomosis.
* Recurrent pyogenic cholangitis.

### Prognosis of Biliary Tract Malignancy
* Generally poor due to late presentation and aggressive tumor biology.
* 5-year survival for resected pCCA is roughly 20-40%.
* High rate of locoregional and distant recurrence even after R0 resection.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the anatomical rationale for performing a mandatory caudate lobectomy in Perihilar Cholangiocarcinoma resections? Answer: The biliary ducts of the caudate lobe (Segment I) drain directly into the left and right main hepatic ducts at the hilum, making it highly susceptible to direct microscopic tumor invasion; removal is essential for an R0 margin.
* Trap: Assuming a palpable gallbladder in a severely jaundiced patient is due to acute cholecystitis with impacted gallstones. Pearl: Courvoisier's Law dictates that a palpable, non-tender gallbladder in the presence of painless jaundice is unlikely to be caused by gallstones; it strongly suggests a malignant obstruction of the distal biliary tree or pancreatic head.
* Pitfall: Recommending routine pre-operative biliary drainage via PTBD or ERCP for all patients with resectable malignant biliary obstruction. Routine drainage increases the risk of severe cholangitis and complicates surgical resection; it should be strictly reserved for patients with concomitant cholangitis, severe malnutrition needing optimization, or profound hyperbilirubinemia before a major liver resection.
* Viva Question: What is the surgical management for a Gallbladder Carcinoma staged as T1b or higher? Answer: An extended cholecystectomy, which involves en bloc removal of the gallbladder along with a wedge resection of the adjacent liver bed (Segments IVb and V) and a regional portal lymphadenectomy.
* Pitfall: Proceeding directly to surgery for a suspected biliary tract malignancy without correcting the patient's coagulation profile. Prolonged obstructive jaundice causes fat malabsorption, leading to Vitamin K deficiency and elevated PT/INR; always correct with IV Vitamin K or Fresh Frozen Plasma before invasive procedures.
