# Post-operative Head and Neck Surgery Care (頭頸外科手術術後護理)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Scope of Post-operative Head and Neck Surgery Care
* **Definition**: Specialized multi-disciplinary management of patients following major ablative, reconstructive, or endocrine head and neck procedures.
* **Core Focus**: Airway protection, wound dynamic stabilization, **free flap monitoring (游離皮瓣監測)**, fluid/electrolyte homeostasis, and early detection of life-threatening complications.

### Local Epidemiology of Head and Neck Oncology in Hong Kong
* **Disease Burden**: High prevalence of **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)** requiring salvage **neck dissection (頸部淋巴結清掃術)** post-radiotherapy failure.
* **Subsite Incident Rates**: Increasing incidence of **Oral Cavity Squamous Cell Carcinoma (OSCC) (口腔鱗狀細胞癌)** linked to historical habits, requiring complex resections and microvascular reconstructions in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) tertiary centers.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Neck Zones and Major Vessels
* **Anatomical Landmarks**: Neck divided into Levels I to VII for systematic lymphadenectomy.
* **Carotid Sheath (頸動脈鞘)**: Contains **Common Carotid Artery (CCA) (總頸動脈)**, **Internal Jugular Vein (IJV) (內頸靜脈)**, and **Vagus Nerve (CN X) (迷走神經)**.
* **Thoracic Duct Anatomy**: Located at left Level IV junction of IJV and subclavian vein; anatomical variations high → vulnerable to iatrogenic transection.

### Pathophysiology of Post-operative Airway Obstruction
* **Mechanical Compression**: Arterial/venous hemorrhage within unyielding deep cervical fascial planes → rapid tissue tension ↑ → lateral compression of compliant tracheal wall.
* **Mucosal Oedema**: Surgical manipulation, disruption of lymphatic drainage, or aggressive fluid resuscitation → **laryngeal oedema (喉頭水腫)** → luminal narrowing.

### Pathophysiology of Post-operative Chyle Leak
* **Mechanism**: Structural failure of thoracic duct or right lymphatic duct ligation → uncontained extravasation of chyle into Level IV neck space.
* **Dietary Impact**: Initiation of long-chain triglyceride enteral feeding → chylomicron formation in enterocytes → thoracic duct flow ↑ up to 100 mL/hr → unmasked or exacerbated high-volume **chyle leak (乳糜漏)**.

### Microvascular Flap Physiology and Ischaemia
* **Arterial Insufficiency**: Microvascular thrombosis at arterial anastomosis → complete cessation of nutrient blood inflow → acute coagulative necrosis of flap tissue within 4-6 hours.
* **Venous Congestion**: Venous anastomotic thrombosis → blood outflow occlusion → retroactive microvascular hydrostatic pressure ↑ → tissue edema, capillary rupture, and secondary arterial inflow failure.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Post-operative Neck Haematoma
* **Airway Distress**: Progressive **stridor (喘鳴)**, tachypnoea, hypoxia, and use of accessory respiratory muscles.
* **Local Signs**: Tense, ecchymotic neck swelling, asymmetrical neck contours, and **tracheal deviation (氣管偏位)**.
* **Systemic Indicators**: Agitation, tachycardia, and blood-soaked dressings.

### Clinical Presentation of Post-operative Chyle Leak
* **Drain Output Evolution**: Sudden change from serosanguinous fluid to milky-white or cloudy fluid upon commencement of enteral feeds.
* **Volume Sign**: Rapidly escalating drain output volumes, often exceeding >500 mL/24 hours in severe cases.
* **Local Tissue Manifestations**: Delayed wound healing, skin flap maceration, or supraclavicular fullness.

### Clinical Presentation of Free Flap Vascular Compromise
* **Arterial Thrombosis Signs**:
  * **Flap Paleness (皮瓣蒼白)** or mottling.
  * Decreased tissue temperature.
  * Prolonged or absent **capillary refill time (CRT) (毛細血管充盈時間)** (>3 seconds).
  * Loss of arterial Doppler signals.
* **Venous Congestion Signs**:
  * Dark purple, cyanotic discoloration.
  * Rapidly swelling, tense skin paddle.
  * Hyper-brisk CRT (<1 second) with **bleeding dark blood** upon pinprick test.

### Clinical Signs of Post-operative Cranial Nerve Injuries
* **Spinal Accessory Nerve (CN XI) (副神經)**: Shoulder drop, inability to shrug, limited shoulder abduction.
* **Hypoglossal Nerve (CN XII) (舌下神經)**: Tongue deviation **towards the side of injury**, dysarthria.
* **Marginal Mandibular Nerve (Branch of CN VII) (面神經下頜緣支)**: Asymmetrical smile, drooping of ipsilateral lower lip corner.
* **Vagus/Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (CN X / RLN) (喉返神經)**: Hoarseness, breathy voice, bovine cough, or acute airway compromise if bilateral.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Post-operative Acute Dyspnoea
| Feature | Neck Haematoma (頸部血腫) | Laryngeal Oedema (喉頭水腫) | Mucus Plugging (粘液栓塞) | Tension Pneumothorax (張力性氣胸) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Sudden (0-24 hrs post-op) | Gradual (12-48 hrs post-op) | Sudden (anytime post-op) | Sudden (intra-op or early post-op) |
| **Neck Exam** | Tense, swollen, ecchymotic | Soft neck, no masses | Soft neck, patent incisions | Tracheal deviation to contralateral side |
| **Tracheostomy** | Free flow or compressed | Intact if below edema | Occluded/resists suction catheter | Fully patent tube |
| **Auscultation** | Stridor heard over neck | Inspiratory stridor | Coarse upper airway noise | **Absent breath sounds** unilaterally |

### Differential Diagnosis of Free Flap Anastomotic Failure
| Assessment | Normal Flap (正常皮瓣) | Arterial Thrombosis (動脈血栓) | Venous Congestion (靜脈淤血) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Colour** | Pink, matches donor site | Pale, white, ghostly | Purple, blue, cyanotic |
| **Temperature** | Warm (within 1-2°C of core) | Cold (↓ >2°C relative to control) | Cold or rapidly cooling |
| **Turgor** | Soft, compressible | Flaccid, flat | Tense, edematous, swollen |
| **Capillary Refill** | 1-2 seconds | Delayed (>3 secs) or absent | Hyper-brisk (<1 second) |
| **Pinprick Test** | Bright red bleeding | **No bleeding** | Immediate **dark/maroon bleeding** |
| **Doppler Signal** | Pulsatile arterial + hum | Loss of arterial signal | Loss of venous hum |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Tier 1 Bedside and Laboratory Investigations
* **Drain Fluid Analysis**: Fluid **triglyceride level (甘油三酯水平)** measurement if chyle leak suspected (+ve if >1.2 mmol/L or greater than serum levels).
* **Serial Haemoglobin & Haematocrit**: Rapid trend ↓ indicates active, uncontained concealed hemorrhage.
* **Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)**: Quantifies severity of hypoxia, hypercapnia, and metabolic strain in evolving airway failure.

### Tier 2 Bedside Imaging Modalities
* **Handheld Acoustic Doppler Ultrasound**: Routine hourly bedside mapping of microvascular arterial and venous pedicle signals.
* **Bedside Chest X-ray (CXR)**: Indicated for post-operative neck resections involving Level IV/V to rule out iatrogenic **pneumothorax (氣胸)** or **chylothorax (乳糜胸)**.

### Tier 3 Advanced Imaging and Endoscopy
* **Flexible Nasendoscopy (FNE) (纖維鼻咽喉鏡)**: Direct visualization of supraglottic, glottic, and subglottic spaces to grade **laryngeal oedema** prior to planned extubation.
* **CT Angiography (CTA) of Neck**: Reserved for stable patients with suspected late arterial pseudoaneurysm or flap pedicle kinking without acute airway distress.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Emergency Management Protocol for Post-operative Airway Obstruction
* **Step 1**: Immediate activation of emergency response team; deliver 100% high-flow oxygen.
* **Step 2**: If tense hematoma present, **evacuate immediately at bedside** by removing skin sutures/staples to decompress the airway. Do not wait for operating theatre.
* **Step 3**: Direct laryngoscopy or emergency tracheostomy if bedside decompression fails to restore airway patency.
