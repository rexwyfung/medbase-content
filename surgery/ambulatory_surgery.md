# Ambulatory Surgery (日間手術)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Ambulatory Surgery
* Admission and discharge occur on the exact same calendar day
* Excludes 23-hour stay (extended recovery) or overnight observation
* Represents a shift from traditional inpatient **Elective Surgery (預約手術)** to streamlined, protocol-driven care

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (HA Context)
* Hospital Authority (HA) strategic direction → Maximizing day surgery rates to ↓ inpatient bed pressure
* Target: >60-70% of all elective procedures performed as day cases (e.g., implemented heavily at Queen Mary Hospital Day Surgery Centre)
* Key benefit: ↓ **Nosocomial Infections (院內感染)**, ↑ bed turnover rate, ↓ healthcare costs

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Physiological Response to Ambulatory Surgery
* Goal is minimal physiological derangement → Rapid return to baseline homeostasis
* Minor surgical stress → Transient ↑ **Cortisol (皮質醇)**, ↑ **Catecholamines (兒茶酚胺)**
* Requires minimal fluid shifts and minimal risk of major post-operative bleeding

### Anatomical Considerations for Day Case Suitability
* Favour superficial, peripheral, or minimally invasive anatomical approaches
* **Inguinal Canal (腹股溝管)** → Suitable for localized hernia repairs
* **Breast (乳房)** → Superficial dissections with predictable haemostasis
* Avoid procedures requiring major body cavity entry (e.g., Thoracotomy, major laparotomy) due to ↑ risk of visceral injury and fluid third-spacing

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Pre-assessment Symptoms Dictating Unsuitability
* Severe **Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (阻塞性睡眠窒息症)** symptoms
* Unstable **Angina (心絞痛)** or recent Myocardial Infarction (<6 months)
* Poor effort tolerance (<4 METs; inability to climb two flights of stairs)
* Severe **Gastro-oesophageal Reflux Disease (胃食管反流病)** → ↑ Aspiration risk

### Physical Signs Dictating Unsuitability
* Morbid obesity (BMI >35-40 kg/m²)
* Difficult airway signs:
* Mallampati Class III or IV
* Thyromental distance <6.5 cm
* Limited neck extension or restricted mouth opening

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Ambulatory vs Inpatient Surgery Discriminators
| Selection Criteria | Ambulatory Surgery (Day Case) | Inpatient Surgery |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Patient Factors** | ASA I or II (stable III) | ASA III, IV, or poorly controlled disease |
| **Surgical Factors** | Procedure <2 hours, superficial/endoscopic | Complex, prolonged, major fluid shifts |
| **Social Factors** | Escort present, resides <1 hr from hospital | Lives alone, lacks telephone/transport |
| **Pain/PONV Risk** | Predictably low to moderate, oral meds suffice | High risk requiring IV PCA or close monitoring |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Tiered Pre-operative Investigations
* Goal: Avoid routine blanket testing; investigate solely based on clinical indication and HA protocols.
* **Complete Blood Count (全血細胞計數)**
* **Renal Function Test (腎功能測試)**
* Group & Save (G&S)
* **Electrocardiogram (心電圖)**

### Social & Logistical Workup Requirements
* Must have a responsible adult escort for transport home
* Must have an adult staying with the patient for the first 24 hours post-operatively
* Residence must be within 1 hour of the surgical facility via Hong Kong public transport or taxi
* Access to a working telephone for emergency contact

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Common Indications for Ambulatory Surgery
* **Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (腹腔鏡膽囊切除術)**
* **Inguinal Hernia Repair (腹股溝疝修補術)**
* **Excision of Lipoma/Sebaceous Cyst (脂肪瘤/皮脂腺囊腫切除術)**
* Haemorrhoidectomy / Perianal procedures
* Varicose vein avulsion / Endovenous ablation

### Absolute Contraindications to Ambulatory Surgery
* Lack of responsible adult escort
* Poorly controlled systemic disease (e.g., brittle diabetes, uncontrolled asthma)
* Known susceptibility to **Malignant Hyperthermia (惡性高熱)**
* High risk of massive acute blood loss

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles in Day Surgery
* Absolute meticulous **Haemostasis (止血)** to prevent post-op haematoma
* Prioritize minimally invasive or endoscopic approaches
* Strict fluid management to avoid volume overload and post-operative urinary retention

### Anaesthetic Principles for Rapid Recovery
* Utilize short-acting anaesthetic agents:
* **Propofol (異丙酚)**
* **Desflurane (地氟醚)**
* **Remifentanil (瑞芬太尼)**
* Avoid long-acting muscle relaxants
* Maximize use of **Local Anaesthesia (局部麻醉)** infiltration (e.g., Bupivacaine) at surgical site

### Informed Consent Issues in Day Cases
* Must explicitly consent for potential **Unplanned Admission (意外入院)**
* Advise on the legal prohibition of driving, signing legal documents, or operating machinery for 24 hours post-General Anaesthesia

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care & Discharge Criteria (PADSS)
| PADSS Parameter | Score 2 | Score 1 | Score 0 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vital Signs** | Within 20% of baseline | 20-40% of baseline | >40% of baseline |
| **Ambulation** | Steady gait/no dizziness | Requires assistance | Unable to ambulate |
| **N/V** | Minimal | Moderate (treatable) | Severe (intractable) |
| **Pain** | Acceptable | Requires oral analgesia | Severe (requires IV) |
| **Bleeding** | Minimal | Moderate | Severe |
* *Note: A score of ≥9 is typically required for safe discharge.*

### ERAS Protocols in Ambulatory Surgery
* Pre-emptive **Analgesia (止痛藥)**
* Pre-emptive **Anti-emetics (止吐藥)**
* Early mobilization in the Post-Anaesthetic Care Unit (PACU)
* Early oral intake of clear fluids

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (PACU)
* **Post-operative Nausea and Vomiting (PONV) (術後噁心及嘔吐)**
* Severe breakthrough **Pain (痛楚)**
* Hypoxia or airway compromise

### Intermediate Complications & Unplanned Admissions
* Primary or reactionary **Bleeding (出血)**
* Post-operative **Urinary Retention (尿瀦留)**
* Unplanned hospital admission (Most common causes: intractable PONV, severe pain, bleeding, surgical complication)

### Late Complications
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染)**
* Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) / Pulmonary Embolism (PE) (Rare in day surgery due to early ambulation)

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What are the Apfel criteria used for predicting Post-operative Nausea and Vomiting (PONV)? Answer: Female gender, non-smoker, history of PONV or motion sickness, and post-operative use of opioids.
* Trap: Assuming every patient requires a pre-operative ECG and chest X-ray before day surgery. Pearl: Blanket testing is obsolete; investigations must be strictly tailored to the patient's comorbidities (e.g., ECG only for males >40 or females >50, or those with cardiac history).
* Pitfall: Discharging a post-hernia repair patient before they have voided urine. Always ensure spontaneous voiding before discharge after pelvic, perianal, or groin surgeries to rule out acute post-operative urinary retention.
* Viva Question: What is the most common reason for a failed same-day discharge (unplanned admission) following ambulatory surgery? Answer: Intractable post-operative nausea and vomiting (PONV) and inadequate pain control.
