# Gastrointestinal Endoscopy (胃腸內視鏡)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Upper Gastrointestinal Endoscopy
* **Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD) (食道胃十二指腸內視鏡)**: Diagnostic & therapeutic visualization of mucosa from esophagus to 2nd part of duodenum (**D2**).

### Definition of Lower Gastrointestinal Endoscopy
* **Colonoscopy (大腸鏡)**: Diagnostic & therapeutic visualization of large bowel from rectum to **cecum (盲腸)**, often including **terminal ileum (末端迴腸)**.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of GI Malignancies
* **Colorectal Cancer (CRC) (大腸癌)**: 2nd most common cancer in HK, 2nd leading cause of cancer deaths.
* **Gastric Cancer (胃癌)**: 6th most common cancer in HK.
* **HA CRC Screening Programme**: Subsidized **Faecal Immunochemical Test (FIT) (大便隱血測試)** for asymptomatic residents aged 50-75 → +ve FIT triggers subsidized colonoscopy.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Upper GI Endoscopic Anatomy Landmarks
* **Upper Esophageal Sphincter (UES)**: 15cm from incisors.
* **Z-line (Squamocolumnar Junction)**: ~40cm from incisors; transition from squamous (pale) to columnar (red) epithelium.
* **Diaphragmatic Hiatus**: ~40cm from incisors; pinch effect on inspiration.
* **Cardia (賁門)** → **Fundus (胃底)** → **Body (胃體)** → **Antrum (胃竇)**.
* **Pylorus (幽門)**: Sphincter entering duodenum.
* **Ampulla of Vater (十二指腸乳頭)**: Medial wall of D2.

### Lower GI Endoscopic Anatomy Landmarks
* **Anal Canal (肛管)** → **Rectum (直腸)**: 15cm long, Houston's valves.
* **Sigmoid Colon (乙狀結腸)**: Tortuous, common site for **diverticular disease (憩室病)** & perforation risk.
* **Descending Colon** → **Splenic Flexure (脾彎)**: Bluish hue from spleen.
* **Transverse Colon** → **Hepatic Flexure (肝彎)**: Liver shadow.
* **Ascending Colon** → **Cecum (盲腸)**: Identified by **appendiceal orifice (闌尾孔)** & **ileocecal valve (ICV) (迴盲瓣)**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Indications for Diagnostic OGD
* **Upper GI Bleeding (UGIB) (上消化道出血)**: Hematemesis, melena.
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)** / Odynophagia.
* Unexplained weight loss with abdominal pain.
* Refractory **Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) (胃酸倒流)**.
* Persistent vomiting.

### Indications for Diagnostic Colonoscopy
* **Lower GI Bleeding (LGIB) (下消化道出血)**: Hematochezia.
* Positive **FIT (大便隱血測試)**.
* Altered bowel habit (esp. older adults).
* Unexplained iron deficiency anemia (IDA).
* Surveillance for **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) (炎症性腸病)**.

### Alarm Symptoms Requiring Urgent Endoscopy
* **Weight loss (體重下降)**
* **Anemia (貧血)**
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**
* Palpable abdominal mass.
* Age > 50 with new-onset dyspepsia.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Differential Diagnosis of Upper GI Bleeding
| Pathology | Endoscopic Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Peptic Ulcer Disease (消化性潰瘍)** | Ulcer crater in gastric antrum or duodenal bulb; Forrest classification used for bleeding risk. |
| **Esophageal Varices (食道靜脈曲張)** | Dilated submucosal veins in distal esophagus; red wale signs indicate high rupture risk. |
| **Mallory-Weiss Tear** | Linear mucosal laceration at gastroesophageal junction; associated with severe retching. |
| **Gastric Malignancy (胃癌)** | Irregular, friable mass with raised edges; malignant ulcers often have nodular margins. |

### Differential Diagnosis of Lower GI Bleeding
| Pathology | Endoscopic Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Diverticular Bleeding (憩室出血)** | Blood pumping from diverticulum or adherent clot in diverticular orifice (usually left colon). |
| **Hemorrhoids (痔瘡)** | Engorged anal vascular cushions on retroflexion in rectum. |
| **Colorectal Polyps/Cancer (大腸瘜肉/癌)** | Protruding mucosal lesions or stricturing, friable masses. |
| **Angiodysplasia (血管發育不良)** | Flat, red, fern-like vascular lesions; commonly in right colon. |
| **Ischemic Colitis (缺血性腸炎)** | Segmental mucosal edema, erythema, sloughing; often at splenic flexure (watershed area). |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Pre-Endoscopy Laboratory Investigations
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Assess Hb for anemia, Plt for bleeding risk.
* **Coagulation Profile (PT/INR/APTT)**: Crucial for therapeutic endoscopy.
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: Assess urea (↑ in UGIB) and safety for bowel prep.
* **Type & Screen (T&S)**: If active bleeding suspected.

### Bowel Preparation Protocol for Colonoscopy
* **Polyethylene Glycol (PEG) (聚乙二醇)**: e.g., Klean Prep. High volume (3-4L).
* **Sodium Picosulfate/Magnesium Citrate**: e.g., Picolax. Low volume, better tolerated but risk of electrolyte imbalance (avoid in renal failure).
* Split-dose regimen: Half dose evening before, half dose 4-6h before procedure (gold standard for mucosal visualization).

### Pre-Procedural Fasting Guidelines (HA Standard)
* Solid food: Fasting for 6 hours pre-procedure.
* Clear fluids: Fasting for 2 hours pre-procedure.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### HA Anticoagulant / Antiplatelet Management Algorithm
* **Aspirin**: Generally safe to continue for diagnostic scopes & biopsies; stop 5-7 days for high-risk therapeutic procedures (e.g., large EMR).
* **Clopidogrel**: Stop 5-7 days before therapeutic endoscopy.
* **Warfarin**: Stop 5 days prior; bridge with LMWH if high thromboembolic risk; target INR < 1.5.
* **DOACs (e.g., Apixaban)**: Stop 24-48 hours depending on renal function & procedure risk.

### Endoscopic Therapeutic Interventions (OGD)
* **Variceal Bleeding**: Endoscopic Variceal Ligation (EVL) / Banding.
* **Non-Variceal Bleeding (Ulcers)**: Dual therapy → Epinephrine injection + Thermal coagulation (heater probe) OR Mechanical clipping.
* **Strictures**: Balloon dilatation, stent insertion.

### Endoscopic Therapeutic Interventions (Colonoscopy)
* **Polyps**: **Polypectomy (瘜肉切除術)** via cold snare (small) or hot snare (large).
* **Large sessile lesions**: Endoscopic Mucosal Resection (EMR) or Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD).
* **Bleeding**: Adrenaline injection, clipping, Argon Plasma Coagulation (APC) for angiodysplasia.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Patient Positioning and Sedation Setup
* Left lateral decubitus position.
* Continuous monitoring: SpO2, NIBP, ECG (if comorbidities exist).
* Sedation: **Midazolam** + **Fentanyl** / **Pethidine** (conscious sedation); Propofol (Monitored Anesthesia Care - MAC) for complex/prolonged cases.
* Oxygen supplementation via nasal cannula.

### OGD Operative Steps
* Insert mouthguard.
* Intubate esophagus under direct vision (avoid blind intubation).
* Traverse stomach to pylorus.
* Intubate D1 and D2; perform biopsies if indicated (e.g., CLO test for *H. pylori*).
* Retroflexion (J-maneuver) in stomach to visualize fundus and cardia.

### Colonoscopy Operative Steps
* Perform Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) first.
* Advance scope via direct mucosal visualization; use torque and air insufflation sparingly.
* Identify cecum via appendiceal orifice and ICV.
* Intubate terminal ileum to rule out IBD.
* Careful inspection during withdrawal (minimum 6 minutes withdrawal time for adequate adenoma detection rate - ADR).

### Informed Consent & Complication Risks
* **Bleeding**: < 1% (diagnostic), ↑ with polypectomy/therapeutic maneuvers.
* **Perforation**: ~1:1000 for colonoscopy; ↑ in IBD, strictures, or therapeutic resection.
* **Cardiopulmonary events**: Related to sedation (hypoxia, aspiration, arrhythmia).
* **Failure of procedure**: Inadequate bowel prep, obstructing tumor, or severe looping.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-Endoscopy Monitoring
* Monitor in recovery room until fully awake (Aldrete score criteria).
* NPO until gag reflex returns (if topical local anesthetic throat spray used for OGD).
* Vital signs monitoring q15min for first hour.

### Discharge Criteria and Instructions
* Strict instruction: No driving, operating machinery, or signing legal documents for 24 hours (due to sedation).
* Must be accompanied by a responsible adult.
* Advise patient to return to A&E for: severe abdominal pain, massive hematemesis, hematochezia, fever, or shortness of breath.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Procedural Complications (< 24 hours)
* **Aspiration Pneumonia (吸入性肺炎)**: Esp. in UGIB scopes; consider pre-intubation in massive bleed.
* **Perforation (腸穿孔)**: Acute severe abdominal pain, peritonitis, free air under diaphragm on erect CXR.
* **Primary Post-Polypectomy Bleeding**: Occurs immediately during or right after resection.

### Intermediate to Late Complications (> 24 hours)
* **Delayed Post-Polypectomy Bleeding**: 1-14 days post-procedure; secondary to sloughing of eschar.
* **Post-Polypectomy Coagulation Syndrome (Transmural Burn)**: Localized peritonitis/pain without frank perforation; due to thermal injury.

### Management of Endoscopic Perforation
* Small, clean OGD/colon perf (noticed immediately) → Endoscopic clipping if possible, broad-spectrum IV antibiotics, NPO.
* Large perf, delayed presentation, or generalized peritonitis → Urgent **Laparotomy (剖腹手術)** / Laparoscopy with primary repair or resection + stoma.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the Forrest Classification for bleeding peptic ulcers? Answer: I (Active bleed: Ia spurting, Ib oozing), II (Recent bleed: IIa visible vessel, IIb adherent clot, IIc hematin spot), III (Clean base).
* Viva Question: What constitutes an adequate Adenoma Detection Rate (ADR) for a screening colonoscopy? Answer: An ADR of at least 25% (often cited as >30% in males, >20% in females) is required to ensure quality screening.
* Trap: Assuming normal vital signs rule out a significant colonic perforation post-colonoscopy. Pearl: Early perforation may only present with localized pain. Always have a high index of suspicion and request an urgent CT abdomen with water-soluble contrast or erect CXR.
* Pitfall: Blindly pushing the endoscope against resistance during colonoscopy to overcome a loop. You risk colonic perforation, particularly at the rectosigmoid junction. Always pull back, apply abdominal pressure, and advance only with clear luminal view.
* Trap: Recommending low-volume bowel prep (e.g., Picolax) for an elderly patient with Chronic Kidney Disease. Pearl: Osmotic laxatives with magnesium can precipitate severe acute renal failure or electrolyte disturbances. Use PEG-based solutions instead.
