# Malignant Melanoma (惡性黑色素瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Malignant Melanoma Clinical Definition
* **Malignant Melanoma (惡性黑色素瘤)**: Aggressive cutaneous/mucosal malignancy arising from **melanocytes (黑色素細胞)** (neural crest-derived pigment cells).
* Characterised by ↑ tendency for early lymphatic and haematogenous metastasis.

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology
* Incidence: Lower in HK compared to Caucasians (~1 in 100,000) but gradually ↑.
* Most common subtype in HK/Asians: **Acral Lentiginous Melanoma (肢端小痣性黑色素瘤)** (~50-60%).
* Primary anatomical locations in HK: Soles of feet, palms, subungual (甲下) regions.
* Age peak: 60-70 years.
* Prognosis in HK: Often worse than Western countries due to late presentation of foot/nail lesions.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Cutaneous Surgical Anatomy
* **Epidermis (表皮)**: Outermost layer. Melanocytes reside in the **stratum basale (基底層)**.
* **Dermis (真皮)**: Contains lymphatics and blood vessels → conduit for metastasis.
* **Subcutaneous fat (皮下脂肪)**: Deep margin for excision.

### Malignant Melanoma Pathophysiology & Growth Phases
* **Radial Growth Phase (水平生長)**: Initial superficial, lateral spread within epidermis/papillary dermis. Lacks metastatic potential.
* **Vertical Growth Phase (垂直生長)**: Penetration deep into reticular dermis/subcutis. Angiogenesis ↑, direct correlation with metastatic risk.

### Malignant Melanoma Histological Subtypes
* **Acral Lentiginous (肢端小痣性)**: Palms, soles, nails. Unrelated to UV exposure. Most common in HK/Asians.
* **Superficial Spreading (淺表蔓延性)**: Most common in Caucasians. Intermittent sun exposure. Usually trunk/legs.
* **Nodular (結節性)**: Aggressive. Rapid vertical growth from onset (minimal radial phase).
* **Lentigo Maligna (惡性雀斑樣)**: Elderly face/sun-damaged skin. Slow-growing.

### Melanoma Genetic Mutations
* **BRAF V600E**: ~50% of cutaneous melanomas. Key target for systemic therapy.
* **NRAS / c-KIT**: c-KIT mutations ↑ in acral and mucosal melanomas.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### The ABCDE Rule for Melanoma Assessment
* **A - Asymmetry**: One half unlike the other.
* **B - Border**: Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined margin.
* **C - Colour**: Varied shades of brown, black, blue, red, or white.
* **D - Diameter**: >6mm (size of a pencil eraser), though can be smaller.
* **E - Evolution**: Recent change in size, shape, colour, or symptom (bleeding, itching).

### Key Clinical Signs of Advanced Melanoma
* **Ugly Duckling Sign (醜小鴨徵)**: A nevus that stands out and looks distinctly different from patient's other moles.
* **Hutchinson's Sign (哈欽森徵)**: Periungual pigmentation spreading to proximal/lateral nail folds (pathognomonic for subungual melanoma).
* **Satellite Lesions (衛星病灶)**: Pigmented nodules ≤2cm from primary tumour.
* **In-transit Metastases (途中轉移)**: Dermal/subcutaneous mets >2cm from primary but before regional node.
* **Regional Lymphadenopathy (局部淋巴結腫大)**: Firm, non-tender palpable nodes.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Melanoma vs Common Pigmented Lesions
| Condition | Discriminatory Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Malignant Melanoma (惡性黑色素瘤)** | ABCDE +ve, rapid evolution, bleeding, Hutchinson's sign. |
| **Benign Melanocytic Nevus (良性痣)** | Symmetrical, uniform colour, stable over years. |
| **Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)** | Pearly edge, telangiectasia, central ulceration, slow growth. |
| **Pigmented Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)** | Hyperkeratotic, crusting, non-healing ulcer, sun-exposed areas. |
| **Seborrheic Keratosis (脂溢性角化病)** | "Stuck-on" appearance, warty, uniform waxy surface, keratin pearls. |
| **Subungual Haematoma (甲下血腫)** | History of trauma, grows outwards with nail growth, uniform colour. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Primary Diagnostic Investigation
* **1st Line: Excisional Biopsy (切除活檢)**.
* Margins: Strictly **2mm** clinical margin into upper subcutis.
* Orientation: Longitudinal alignment parallel to lymphatic drainage (facilitates subsequent wide local excision).

### Investigations to Avoid
* **CONTRAINDICATED: Shave biopsy or superficial punch biopsy**.
* Reason: Transects tumour → destroys **Breslow thickness (布雷斯洛厚度)** calculation → ruins clinical staging.

### Histopathological Workup
* **Breslow Thickness**: Depth in mm from granular layer to deepest tumour cell. Most important prognostic factor.
* **Ulceration**: Presence independently worsens prognosis (upstages T category).
* **Mitotic Rate**: Mitoses/mm². Indicates tumour aggressiveness.
* **Mutation Profiling**: **BRAF V600E / c-KIT** testing if stage III/IV.

### Locoregional & Systemic Staging Investigations
* **Regional USG (超聲波)**: Evaluate draining lymph node basins.
* **FNA Cytology (幼針抽吸)**: Of suspicious nodes identified on USG.
* **PET-CT / CT Thorax/Abdo/Pelvis / MRI Brain**: Indicated for Stage III (macroscopic nodes) or Stage IV disease. Not routine for Stage I/II.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Wide Local Excision (WLE) Margins Protocol
* WLE depends strictly on Breslow thickness (based on HA/international consensus):
| Breslow Thickness | Recommended WLE Margin |
| :--- | :--- |
| **In situ (Melanoma in situ)** | 0.5 - 1.0 cm |
| **< 1.0 mm** | 1.0 cm |
| **1.0 - 2.0 mm** | 1.0 - 2.0 cm |
| **> 2.0 mm** | 2.0 cm |

### Lymph Node Management Algorithm
* **Clinically node -ve & Breslow <0.8mm**: Observation.
* **Clinically node -ve & Breslow ≥0.8mm (or ulcerated)**: **Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (前哨淋巴結活檢 - SLNB)**.
* **Clinically node +ve (FNA proven)**: **Therapeutic Lymph Node Dissection (TLND - 治療性淋巴結清掃術)**.
* **SLNB +ve**: HA standard shifting away from routine Completion LND. Multidisciplinary discussion for TLND vs nodal USG surveillance ± Adjuvant therapy.

### Advanced Disease Adjuvant & Systemic Therapies
* **Targeted Therapy**: **BRAF/MEK inhibitors** (e.g., Dabrafenib + Trametinib) for BRAF V600E +ve.
* **Immunotherapy**: **PD-1 inhibitors** (e.g., Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab). First-line for BRAF -ve or adjuvant for high-risk Stage II/III.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Wide Local Excision (WLE) Operative Principles
* **En bloc excision**: Excision down to, but not including, the deep fascia (unless fascia involved).
* **Reconstruction**: Primary closure if possible. **Split-thickness skin graft (SSG)** or local flap if tension excessive (very common for acral melanomas on sole of foot).

### Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB) Principles
* **Dual Tracer Technique**: Pre-op **Technetium-99m lymphoscintigraphy** + intra-op **Patent Blue V dye** (or ICG).
* Identify first node(s) receiving lymphatic drainage. Send for permanent section (intra-op frozen section contraindicated as it destroys node architecture).

### Specific Surgical Consent Issues
* Incomplete excision requiring re-operation.
* Need for skin graft (SSG) → donor site pain, scarring, graft failure.
* **Lymphedema (淋巴水腫)**: Significant risk (esp. block dissection of groin/axilla).
* Nerve injury (e.g., long thoracic nerve in axillary LND).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care
* **Skin Graft Care**: Tie-over bolster dressing for 5-7 days to prevent hematoma/shearing.
* **Drain Management**: For lymph node dissections, maintain suction drains until output <30ml/24 hours.
* **Mobilisation**: Strict elevation for lower limb grafts (acral WLE) to ↓ venous pressure. Avoid early weight-bearing.

### Long-Term Surveillance & Rehabilitation Protocol
* Regular HA dermatology/surgery joint clinic follow-up.
* Physical exam: Total body skin check + regional lymph node palpation.
* **Frequency**: Q3M for years 1-2 → Q6M for years 3-5 → Annually.
* **Rehabilitation**: Physiotherapy for lymphedema prevention/management (compression garments, manual lymphatic drainage).
* Patient education: Sun protection (SPF 50+), routine self-skin checks.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications
* Hemorrhage / Hematoma (often causes graft failure).
* Wound infection / Necrosis of skin flaps.
* Seroma formation (common post-axillary/groin dissection).

### Late Surgical Complications & Oncological Failure
* **Lymphedema (淋巴水腫)**: Chronic, debilitating swelling post-TLND.
* **Local Recurrence**: Tumour regrowth at WLE scar.
* **In-transit Metastasis**: Cutaneous/subcutaneous recurrence between primary site and node basin.
* **Distant Metastasis**: Lungs, Liver, Brain, Bone, GI tract.

### Prognosis & TNM Survival Data
| AJCC Stage | Clinical Features | Approximate 5-Year Survival |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage I** | Localised, ≤2mm, node -ve | > 95% |
| **Stage II** | Localised, >2mm or ulcerated, node -ve | 70 - 80% |
| **Stage III** | Regional lymph node +ve or in-transit mets | 50 - 60% |
| **Stage IV** | Distant metastasis | 20 - 30% (improving with Immunotherapy) |

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most important prognostic factor for localised malignant melanoma? Answer: Breslow thickness.
* Trap: Performing an incisional or shave biopsy for a suspected melanoma. Pearl: Always perform an excisional biopsy with a 2mm margin to preserve tumour architecture for accurate Breslow thickness measurement.
* Viva Question: An elderly patient in Hong Kong presents with a painless, spreading black patch under the thumbnail. What is the diagnosis until proven otherwise? Answer: Acral lentiginous melanoma (subungual melanoma).
* Pitfall: Neglecting to examine the patient's soles, palms, and nail beds during a routine skin check in Hong Kong. Local epidemiology dictates acral lentiginous melanoma is the most common subtype in Asians.
* Viva Question: What margins are required for a wide local excision of a melanoma with a Breslow thickness of 1.5mm? Answer: 1 to 2 cm margins.
* Trap: Closing a primary melanoma biopsy site with a complex local flap before histology returns. Pearl: Always orient excision parallel to lymphatics and avoid complex closures that alter lymphatic drainage pathways, as this can severely compromise subsequent sentinel lymph node biopsy mapping.
* Viva Question: What is the significance of the BRAF V600E mutation? Answer: Present in ~50% of melanomas and allows for systemic targeted therapy with BRAF/MEK inhibitors in advanced disease.
