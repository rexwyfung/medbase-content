# Stoma & Related Problems (造口及相關問題)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Stoma (造口的臨床定義)
* **Stoma (造口)**: Artificially created opening connecting a body cavity to exterior surface.
* **Bowel Stoma (腸造口)**: Exteriorization of bowel on anterior abdominal wall for fecal diversion.
* **Urostomy (泌尿造口)**: Urinary diversion, commonly via **Ileal Conduit (迴腸導管)**.
* Categorized by:
* Purpose: **Defunctioning/Protective (保護性)** vs **Therapeutic (治療性)**.
* Duration: **Temporary (暫時性)** vs **Permanent (永久性)**.
* Anatomy: **End (端式)** vs **Loop (環式)** vs **Double-barrel (雙腔式)**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (香港的流行病學)
* **Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌)**: Top 2 most common cancer in HK → major cause for stoma creation.
* **Anterior Resection (前切除術)**: Defunctioning loop ileostomy common to protect low colorectal/coloanal anastomosis.
* **Abdominoperineal Resection (腹會陰切除術)**: Permanent end colostomy mandatory for low rectal cancers involving sphincter.
* Aging population in HK → ↑ prevalence of diverticular disease → ↑ **Hartmann's procedure (哈特曼氏手術)** → end colostomy.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Anatomy of Colostomy (結腸造口的解剖學)
* Usually sited in **Left Iliac Fossa (LIF) (左下腹)**.
* Brings out large bowel (usually sigmoid or descending colon).
* **Mucosa (黏膜)**: Pink, moist, flush with skin or slightly raised.
* Output: Solid/semi-solid feces, malodorous, low volume.

### Anatomy of Ileostomy (迴腸造口的解剖學)
* Usually sited in **Right Iliac Fossa (RIF) (右下腹)**.
* Brings out small bowel (terminal ileum).
* Requires **Spout (突起)** (typically 2-3cm) → prevents excoriation from caustic small bowel enzymes.
* Output: Liquid/mushy, rich in pancreatic/biliary enzymes, higher volume (500-1000ml/day).

### Anatomy of Urostomy (泌尿造口的解剖學)
* Usually sited in **RIF (右下腹)**.
* **Ileal Conduit (迴腸導管)**: Isolated segment of terminal ileum (blood supply preserved) → ureters implanted into proximal end → distal end forms stoma.
* Spouted to drain urine continuously into bag.

### Pathophysiology of High Output Stoma (高輸出量造口的病理生理學)
* Defined as output **>1.5L/24hr** (or >2L/day in HK HA standard guidelines).
* Pathophysiology: Loss of large bowel water absorption + small bowel hypersecretion.
* Sequelae: → **Dehydration (脫水)** → **Acute Kidney Injury (急性腎損傷)** → **Electrolyte Imbalance (電解質失衡)** (↓Na+, ↓K+, ↓Mg2+).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Normal Physical Signs of Stoma (正常造口的體徵)
* **Color (顏色)**: Beefy red/pink.
* **Moisture (濕潤度)**: Moist mucosa.
* **Skin (皮膚)**: Intact peristomal skin, no erythema.
* **Effluent (排出物)**: Appropriate consistency for bowel level.

### Symptoms of Stoma Complications (造口併發症的症狀)
* **Abdominal Pain (腹痛)**: Colicky (obstruction) or constant (ischemia).
* **Parastomal Swelling (造口旁腫脹)**: Suspect hernia or abscess.
* **Bleeding (出血)**: Mucosal trauma vs portal hypertension (**Caput Medusae/海蛇頭**).
* **Skin Irritation (皮膚刺激)**: Burning, severe pruritus, weeping skin.

### Signs of Stomal Ischemia (造口缺血的體徵)
* Dusky/cyanotic/black mucosa.
* Loss of normal mucosal sheen.
* Sloughing of tissue.
* Malodorous discharge.
* Failure to bleed upon gentle pinprick.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Features: End vs Loop Stoma (端式與環式造口的區別)
| Feature | End Stoma (端式造口) | Loop Stoma (環式造口) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lumen (管腔)** | Single lumen. | Two lumens (afferent & efferent). |
| **Supporting Rod (支撐棒)** | Absent. | Present (in early post-op period). |
| **Function (功能)** | Permanent diversion or post-resection. | Usually temporary defunctioning. |
| **Appearance (外觀)** | Circular. | Often oval/larger. |

### Differential Diagnosis of Parastomal Swelling (造口旁腫脹的鑑別診斷)
| Differential | Key Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Parastomal Hernia (造口旁疝氣)** | Reducible mass, expansile cough impulse, contains bowel/omentum. |
| **Stomal Prolapse (造口脫垂)** | Telescoping of bowel OUT of stoma opening. |
| **Parastomal Abscess (造口旁膿腫)** | Erythematous, indurated, tender, fever, purulent discharge. |
| **Granuloma (肉芽腫)** | Small, friable, bleeding nodules at mucocutaneous junction. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Pre-operative Stoma Siting (術前造口定位)
* Mandatory for all elective stomas.
* Performed by **Stoma Nurse/ET Nurse (造口護士)**.
* Patient examined supine, sitting, and standing.
* Crucial landmarks to avoid:
* Bony prominences (costal margin, ASIS).
* Umbilicus (肚臍).
* Skin creases/scars.
* Belt line.
* Placed through belly of **Rectus Abdominis (腹直肌)** → ↓ hernia risk.

### Laboratory Investigations for High Output Stoma (高輸出量造口的血液化驗)
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: ↑ Hct (hemoconcentration).
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: ↑ Urea/Cr ratio (pre-renal AKI), ↓ Na+, ↓ K+.
* **Bone Profile**: ↓ Mg2+, ↓ Ca2+.
* **Venous Blood Gas (VBG)**: Metabolic acidosis (loss of HCO3- in ileostomy fluid).

### Imaging for Stomal Complications (造口併發症的影像學檢查)
* **CT Abdomen/Pelvis with IV Contrast (電腦掃描)**:
* Assesses bowel viability.
* Identifies parastomal hernia sac contents.
* Detects parastomal collections/abscesses.
* Checks for intra-abdominal recurrence of primary malignancy.
* **Gastrografin Enema (水溶性顯影劑灌腸)**:
* Evaluates distal limb patency and anastomotic integrity before reversal.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Surgical Indications for Stoma Creation (建立造口的手術指徵)
| Category | Examples |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Defunctioning (保護性)** | Protect low anterior resection anastomosis, protect pelvic sepsis, distal obstruction. |
| **Diversion (改道)** | Fournier's gangrene, perianal Crohn's, enterocutaneous fistula. |
| **Resection (切除後)** | Abdominoperineal Resection (APR), Hartmann's procedure, total proctocolectomy. |

### Medical Management of High Output Stoma (高輸出量造口的藥物治療)
* **Fluid Restriction (限制水分)**: Limit hypotonic oral fluids (water/tea) to <500ml/day.
* **Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) (口服補液鹽)**: WHO formula or St. Mark's solution (high Na+).
* **Anti-diarrheals (止瀉藥)**:
* **Loperamide (洛哌丁胺)**: 4mg-16mg QDS (take 30-60 mins BEFORE meals).
* **Codeine Phosphate (可待因)**: 15-60mg QDS.
* **Anti-secretory (抗分泌藥物)**:
* **PPI (質子泵抑制劑)**: e.g., Omeprazole 40mg BD → ↓ gastric secretions.
* **Octreotide (奧曲肽)**: For refractory cases (SC injection).

### HA Protocols for Parastomal Hernia Management (醫管局造口旁疝氣管理指引)
* **Conservative (保守治療)**: Asymptomatic → Reassurance, stoma binder/support belt.
* **Surgical (手術治療)**: Indicated for obstruction, strangulation, skin breakdown, appliance failure.
* Surgical options: Local fascial repair (high recurrence), stoma relocation, mesh repair (Sugarbaker or Keyhole technique).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Stoma Creation (建立造口的手術原則)
* Pre-operative marking by Stoma Nurse.
* Trephine through **Rectus Abdominis Muscle (腹直肌)** to prevent herniation.
* Defect size: Two fingers snug fit.
* Adequate mobilization of bowel to ensure tension-free exteriorization.
* Ensure excellent vascular supply to the exteriorized segment.
* **Brooke's Eversion (布魯克氏外翻)**: Eversion of mucosa (especially ileostomy) to create spout using 3-point absorbable sutures (e.g., Vicryl).

### Key Steps for Loop Ileostomy (環式迴腸造口的關鍵步驟)
* Identify distal ileum (usually 15-20cm proximal to ileocecal valve).
* Pass vascular sling through mesentery close to bowel wall.
* Bring loop through abdominal wall trephine.
* Open distally (efferent limb) longitudinally to preserve proximal blood supply.
* Insert stoma rod (optional, dependent on surgeon preference, usually removed post-op D3-5).
* Mature stoma with eversion of afferent limb.

### Informed Consent for Stoma Surgery (造口手術的知情同意)
* **General risks**: Bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding structures, anesthetic risks.
* **Specific stoma risks**:
* Ischemia/Necrosis.
* Retraction/Prolapse.
* High output → AKI.
* Parastomal hernia (very common long-term).
* Skin excoriation.
* Altered body image & psychosocial impact.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Stoma Care (術後即時造口護理)
* Transparent stoma bag applied in OT to monitor mucosal color and early output.
* Assess viability daily (color, bleeding).
* Monitor output strictly (volume, consistency).
* Fluid balance charting strictly enforced.
* Stoma rod removal on Post-Op Day 3 to 5 (if loop stoma).

### ERAS Protocols for Stoma Patients (造口患者的術後加速康復指引)
* **Early mobilization (及早下床活動)**.
* **Early enteral feeding (及早進食)**: Start fluids once stoma functions (flatus/stool).
* Chew food thoroughly to prevent stoma blockage.
* Stoma education initiated early by ward nurses and Stoma Nurse.
* Teaching patient/family appliance changes and emptying.

### Long-term Stoma Rehabilitation (長遠造口復康)
* Dietitian referral for nutritional advice (avoid stringy, high-fiber foods that cause blockage e.g., mushrooms, sweetcorn).
* Psychological support (body image counseling).
* Join patient support groups (e.g., HK Stoma Association).
* Certification for medical fee waiving (if applicable in HA).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (<30 days) (早期手術併發症)
* **Ischemia/Necrosis (缺血/壞死)**: Due to tight fascial defect or damaged mesenteric vessels. Needs immediate surgical revision if extends below fascia.
* **Hemorrhage (出血)**: From mucosal edge (manage with adrenaline gauze/sutures) or mesentery.
* **Retraction (回縮)**: Tension on bowel or ischemia. Often requires revision.
* **High Output (高輸出量)**: Leading to AKI/dehydration.
* **Mucocutaneous Separation (黏膜皮膚分離)**: Tension or local infection.

### Late Surgical Complications (>30 days) (晚期手術併發症)
* **Parastomal Hernia (造口旁疝氣)**: Most common late complication.
* **Stomal Prolapse (造口脫垂)**: Telescoping of bowel. Common in loop colostomies.
* **Stomal Stenosis (造口狹窄)**: Fibrosis at fascial or skin level due to previous ischemia/infection.
* **Peristomal Dermatitis (造口旁皮膚炎)**: Chemical (leakage), allergic (adhesive), or infective (fungal).
* **Stoma Varices (造口靜脈曲張)**: In patients with portal hypertension (Caput Medusae). Can cause massive bleeding.

### Prognosis & Reversal Considerations (預後及還原考量)
* Temporary defunctioning stomas typically reversed at **8-12 weeks** post-primary surgery.
* Prerequisites for reversal:
* Patient clinically fit.
* Primary anastomosis healed (confirmed by Gastrografin enema or endoscopy).
* Normal distal bowel function without stricture.
* Morbidity of reversal is significant (wound infection, anastomotic leak, incisional hernia).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most reliable sign of stoma viability post-operatively? Answer: Bright red/pink mucosal color and brisk bleeding of the mucosa upon gentle pinprick or rubbing.
* Viva Question: Why must an ileostomy be spouted but a colostomy can be flush? Answer: Ileostomy effluent contains active, caustic proteolytic/digestive enzymes that cause severe skin excoriation, whereas colostomy effluent is mostly solid waste.
* Viva Question: How do you treat bleeding from stoma varices in a cirrhotic patient? Answer: Direct pressure, Adrenaline-soaked gauze, suture ligation. Refractory cases require TIPS (Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt) or stoma relocation.
* Trap: Confusing stoma output definitions. Pearl: Normal ileostomy output is ~500-800ml/day. High output is defined as >1.5L-2L/day. Colostomy output should generally be low volume and semi-solid.
* Trap: Reversing a defunctioning stoma blindly. Pearl: Always request a water-soluble contrast enema (Gastrografin) or flexible endoscopy to rule out anastomotic leak or stricture before booking the reversal.
* Pitfall: Prescribing standard loperamide PRN for high output stoma. You must prescribe it 30-60 minutes BEFORE meals, often at high doses (e.g., 4mg QDS), to slow gut transit when the gastrocolic reflex is stimulated by food.
* Pitfall: Superficial necrosis of the stoma. If only the mucosa sloughs but the deeper tissue at the fascial level is viable, conservative management may suffice. You must examine the stoma with a glass test tube and penlight to assess the level of ischemia; if necrosis extends below the fascia, urgent re-laparotomy is required.
