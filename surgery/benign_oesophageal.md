# Benign Oesophageal Diseases (良性食道疾病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) (胃食道逆流症)
* **Definition**: Abnormal reflux of gastric contents into **oesophagus (食道)** → mucosal damage or debilitating symptoms
* **Classification**: Non-erosive reflux disease (NERD) vs Erosive oesophagitis

### Clinical Definition of Achalasia (食道失弛緩症)
* **Definition**: Primary oesophageal motility disorder characterized by:
* Absent oesophageal peristalsis
* Failure of **lower oesophageal sphincter (LES) (下食道括約肌)** relaxation during swallowing

### Clinical Definition of Zenker's Diverticulum (岑克氏憩室)
* **Definition**: Pulsion-type **false diverticulum (假性憩室)** (mucosa + submucosa only) protruding through posterior pharyngeal wall

### Local Epidemiology of Benign Oesophageal Diseases in Hong Kong
* **GERD**: Prevalence rapidly ↑ in HK (~5-10%) mimicking Western trends
* **Drivers**: ↑ Obesity/BMI, Westernized high-fat diet, aging population
* **Achalasia**: Rare (incidence ~1/100,000), typically presents aged 30-50
* **Zenker's**: Predominantly elderly males (>70 years)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Oesophagus (食道)
* 25cm muscular tube from **cricopharyngeus (環咽肌)** (C6) to **cardia (賁門)** (T11)
* **Musculature**: Upper 1/3 striated, middle 1/3 mixed, lower 1/3 smooth
* **Natural Constrictions (Common sites of food impaction/iatrogenic injury)**:
* 15cm from incisors: Cricopharyngeus (narrowest point)
* 25cm from incisors: Aortic arch / Left main bronchus crossing
* 40cm from incisors: Diaphragmatic hiatus (T10)
* **Z-line (Squamocolumnar junction)**: Normal transition from squamous (oesophagus) to columnar (stomach) epithelium

### Pathophysiology of GERD (胃食道逆流症)
* **Primary Mechanism**: Transient LES relaxations (TLESRs)
* **Secondary Factors**:
* ↓ LES resting tone (<10 mmHg)
* **Hiatus hernia (食管裂孔疝)** (loss of diaphragmatic crus pinchcock effect)
* ↓ Oesophageal clearance (poor peristalsis)
* Delayed gastric emptying

### Pathophysiology of Achalasia (食道失弛緩症)
* **Cellular mechanism**: Autoimmune/idiopathic degeneration of inhibitory neurons (nitric oxide/VIP) in **Auerbach's (myenteric) plexus (腸肌神經叢)**
* **Result**: Unopposed cholinergic (excitatory) stimulation → continuous LES contraction & aperistalsis

### Pathophysiology of Zenker's Diverticulum (岑克氏憩室)
* Incoordination between pharyngeal contraction and cricopharyngeus relaxation
* ↑ Intraluminal pressure forces mucosa out through **Killian's dehiscence (基利安氏裂隙)** (weak spot between thyropharyngeus and cricopharyngeus muscles)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of GERD (胃食道逆流症)
* **Heartburn (胃灼熱)** (retro-sternal burning)
* Regurgitation (acid/sour taste)
* Water brash (hypersalivation)
* Extra-oesophageal: Chronic cough, asthma exacerbation, hoarseness, dental erosions

### Key Symptoms of Achalasia (食道失弛緩症)
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)** (solids = liquids simultaneously)
* Regurgitation of undigested, bland (non-acidic) food
* Retrosternal chest pain
* Progressive weight loss

### Key Symptoms of Zenker's Diverticulum
* Cervical **dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**
* **Halitosis (口臭)** (rotting food trapped in pouch)
* Regurgitation of food eaten days ago
* Gurgling sound in neck
* Chronic aspiration pneumonia

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)

| Condition | Discriminatory Clinical Features | Key Diagnostic Finding |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Achalasia (食道失弛緩症)** | Dysphagia to solids = liquids simultaneously | "Bird's beak" on Barium, aperistalsis on manometry |
| **Oesophageal Cancer (食道癌)** | Progressive dysphagia (solids → liquids), elderly, marked weight loss | Mass/stricture on OGD, +ve biopsy |
| **Peptic Stricture (消化性狹窄)** | History of severe GERD, dysphagia to solids | Smooth stricture at lower oesophagus on OGD |
| **Zenker's Diverticulum** | High cervical dysphagia, halitosis, regurgitation of old food | Posterior pouch on Barium Swallow |
| **Systemic Sclerosis** | Associated CREST syndrome, severe reflux | Absent peristalsis, *low* LES tone on manometry |
| **Diffuse Oesophageal Spasm** | Severe chest pain mimicking AMI, intermittent dysphagia | "Corkscrew" appearance on Barium Swallow |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Investigation for Dysphagia & GERD Red Flags
* **Oesophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD) (胃鏡檢查)**
* Excludes malignancy (mandatory in HK for new dysphagia or dyspepsia >40 years)
* Identifies complications: **Barrett's oesophagus (巴雷特食道)**, strictures, erosive oesophagitis
* *Note*: OGD may be normal in early Achalasia or NERD

### Diagnostic Imaging for Benign Oesophageal Motility Disorders
* **Barium Swallow (鋇劑吞嚥造影)**
* Achalasia: Dilated oesophagus, **"Bird's beak" (鳥嘴狀)** tapering at gastroesophageal junction, absent gastric air bubble
* Zenker's: Posterior pooling of contrast in neck pouch
* Hiatus Hernia: Shows size and type (sliding vs rolling)

### Gold Standard Investigation for Achalasia
* **High-resolution Oesophageal Manometry (高解像度食道測壓)**
* Essential pre-operative test
* Diagnostic criteria: Incomplete LES relaxation (↑ Integrated Relaxation Pressure [IRP]) + absent peristalsis

### Gold Standard Investigation for GERD
* **24-hour Ambulatory pH Monitoring (24小時pH監測)**
* Quantifies acid exposure time (DeMeester score >14.7 = +ve)
* Indicated if OGD normal but symptoms persist, or mandatory prior to anti-reflux surgery

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Management Algorithm for GERD
* **Step 1: Conservative/Lifestyle**
* Weight loss, ↑ head of bed, avoid meals <3 hrs before sleep
* Avoid triggers: Caffeine, alcohol, chocolate, spicy food
* **Step 2: Medical**
* **Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) (質子泵抑制劑)** (e.g., Omeprazole 20mg OM/BD) for 4-8 weeks
* H2-receptor antagonists if PPI intolerant
* **Step 3: Surgical Intervention**
* Considered for refractory cases

### Surgical Indications for GERD
* Refractory symptoms despite maximal PPI therapy
* Complications: Stricture, recurrent aspiration, severe mucosal damage
* Young patient preferring to avoid lifelong PPIs
* Large symptomatic hiatus hernia (Type II-IV)

### Management Algorithm for Achalasia
* **Low Surgical Risk Patients**:
* **Peroral Endoscopic Myotomy (POEM) (經口內視鏡肌肉切開術)** (Emerging standard in HK HA clusters) OR
* **Laparoscopic Heller Myotomy (腹腔鏡海勒氏肌肉切開術)** + Partial Fundoplication
* **High Surgical Risk/Elderly Patients**:
* **Pneumatic Dilation (氣球擴張術)** (Effective but carries 2-5% perforation risk)
* **Botulinum Toxin (Botox) Injection (肉毒桿菌注射)** into LES (Short-acting, lasts ~6 months, causes scarring making future surgery difficult)

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Operative Principles of Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication (腹腔鏡尼森胃底摺疊術)
* Mobilize gastric fundus (divide short gastric vessels)
* Dissect crura and clear lower oesophagus (restore intra-abdominal oesophagus length >2cm)
* **Crural repair**: Approximate diaphragmatic crura posteriorly with non-absorbable sutures
* **Fundoplication**: Create 360-degree, short (1.5-2cm), floppy wrap around lower oesophagus
* Calibrate wrap with intra-oesophageal bougie (prevent over-tightening)

### Key Operative Principles of Laparoscopic Heller Myotomy
* Anterior longitudinal incision of LES circular muscle fibers
* Extend myotomy 6cm onto oesophagus and 2-3cm onto gastric side
* Mandatory addition of anterior (Dor) or posterior (Toupet) partial fundoplication to prevent severe post-op GERD

### Key Operative Principles of POEM (Peroral Endoscopic Myotomy)
* Purely endoscopic, no abdominal incisions
* Create submucosal tunnel in oesophagus
* Divide inner circular muscle layer while preserving outer longitudinal layer
* Close mucosal entry point with endoclips

### Key Consent Issues for Anti-Reflux & Achalasia Surgery
* Post-operative **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)** (usually transient due to oedema, but may persist if wrap too tight)
* **Gas-bloat syndrome (氣脹綜合症)** (inability to belch or vomit)
* Risk of visceral perforation (oesophagus, stomach, spleen)
* Symptom recurrence (wrap slippage or migration)
* Potential conversion to open laparotomy

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care Diet Progression
* **Day 0**: Nil by mouth (NBM), IV fluids
* **Day 1**: Contrast swallow (optional in some HA protocols) to rule out leak → start clear fluids
* **Weeks 1-2**: Full liquid diet
* **Weeks 3-4**: Soft/puréed diet (avoid bread, tough meat)
* **>1 Month**: Gradual return to normal diet

### ERAS Protocols for Foregut Surgery
* Aggressive post-op anti-emetics (Ondansetron, Metoclopramide) to prevent retching (can disrupt new fundoplication wrap)
* Early mobilization (Day 0) to ↓ VTE and pneumonia risk
* Early catheter removal
* Multi-modal non-opioid analgesia (Paracetamol, NSAIDs if not contraindicated)

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications of Foregut Surgery (<30 Days)
* **Visceral Perforation (內臟穿孔)**: Undetected oesophageal mucosal breach → mediastinitis / peritonitis
* **Bleeding**: Splenic capsular tear, short gastric vessel bleeding
* Acute dysphagia (wrap oedema/too tight)
* Pneumothorax (pleural breach during crural dissection)

### Late Complications of Foregut Surgery (>30 Days)
* **Wrap failure/migration**: "Slipped Nissen" (stomach herniates through wrap)
* Persistent **Gas-bloat syndrome (氣脹綜合症)**
* Recurrent reflux or dysphagia
* Incisional hernia at trocar sites

### Prognosis & Long-term Surveillance for Achalasia
* Surgery/POEM achieves >90% symptom relief but does *not* cure underlying neuropathy
* ↑ Risk of **Oesophageal Squamous Cell Carcinoma (食道鱗狀細胞癌)** (due to chronic food stasis & inflammation)
* Surveillance OGD recommended 10-15 years post-diagnosis

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the defining manometric feature distinguishing Achalasia from Scleroderma? Answer: Both have aperistalsis, but Achalasia has HIGH/non-relaxing LES pressure, while Scleroderma has LOW/incompetent LES pressure.
* Trap: Prescribing Botox injection as a harmless first-line trial for young Achalasia patients. Pearl: Botox causes submucosal scarring and obliterates tissue planes, significantly increasing the difficulty and complication rate of a subsequent definitive Heller myotomy or POEM.
* Pitfall: Performing a Heller myotomy without an anti-reflux procedure. Severe GERD is a guaranteed complication; always perform a partial fundoplication (e.g., Dor or Toupet).
* Viva Question: A patient with long-standing GERD undergoes OGD which shows salmon-pink mucosa extending 4cm above the Z-line. What is the diagnosis and histological hallmark? Answer: Barrett's oesophagus. The histological hallmark is intestinal metaplasia (presence of goblet cells).
* Trap: Assuming dysphagia in an 80-year-old is benign presbyesophagus. Pearl: New-onset progressive dysphagia in the elderly is oesophageal cancer until proven otherwise; mandatory urgent OGD is required.
* Pitfall: Treating a patient with Zenker's diverticulum with an OGD without prior imaging. Blindly passing an endoscope can easily intubate the thin-walled pouch and cause fatal perforation. Always confirm with Barium Swallow first.
