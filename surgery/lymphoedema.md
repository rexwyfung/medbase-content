# Lymphoedema (淋巴水腫)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Lymphoedema
* **Lymphoedema (淋巴水腫):** Chronic interstitial accumulation of protein-rich fluid.
* Arises secondary to mechanical failure or congenital maldevelopment of the lymphatic system.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Demographics
* **Secondary Lymphoedema (繼發性淋巴水腫):** Most common etiology locally.
* Primary HK cause (Upper Limb): **Breast cancer (乳癌)** tx (axillary clearance + radiotherapy).
* Primary HK cause (Lower Limb): Pelvic malignancies (cervical/endometrial/prostate) tx.
* **Filariasis (絲蟲病):** Rare locally but ↑ index of suspicion for foreign domestic workers from SEA.
* **Primary Lymphoedema (原發性淋巴水腫):** Very rare (congenital hypoplasia/aplasia).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Normal Lymphatic Anatomy
* Blind-ended initial lymph capillaries → pre-collectors.
* Afferent collectors (contain valves + smooth muscle) → lymph nodes.
* Efferent trunks → **Thoracic duct (胸導管)** (left) / Right lymphatic duct → subclavian veins.

### Pathophysiological Mechanism
* Mechanical obstruction or destruction of lymphatic pathways.
* ↓ Lymphatic transport capacity falls below normal physiological lymphatic load.
* Extravasation & accumulation of macromolecular, high-protein fluid in interstitium.

### Cellular and Tissue Alterations
* High interstitial protein → osmotic pull → ↑ fluid retention.
* Chronic fluid stasis → macrophage activation → **Fibroblast proliferation (纖維母細胞增生)** → dense fibrosis.
* Adipocyte hypertrophy → ↑ subcutaneous fat deposition.
* Impaired local immune surveillance → ↑ susceptibility to recurrent **Cellulitis (蜂窩組織炎)**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Classical Symptoms
* Progressive heaviness, aching, or tension in the affected limb.
* Noticeable tightness of clothing, watches, or jewelry.
* Asymmetrical swelling typically progressing from distal to proximal.

### Pathognomonic Clinical Signs
* **Stemmer's Sign (斯特默氏徵) +ve:** Inability to pinch/lift a fold of skin at the base of the 2nd toe or finger.
* Early disease: **Pitting edema (凹陷性水腫)**.
* Late disease: **Non-pitting edema (非凹陷性水腫)** (due to fibrosis).
* **Peau d'orange (橘皮樣外觀):** Dimpled skin appearance.
* **Hyperkeratosis (角化過度):** Thickening of stratum corneum.
* Deepened natural skin creases & "Buffalo hump" on the dorsum of the foot.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Table of Differentials for Unilateral Limb Swelling
| Feature | **Lymphoedema (淋巴水腫)** | Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) | Chronic Venous Insufficiency (CVI) | Lipoedema (脂肪水腫) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Gradual (months/years) | Acute (hours/days) | Gradual | Gradual (puberty onset) |
| **Pain** | Heaviness/aching, rarely acute pain | Acute pain, tenderness | Aching, worse dependency | Tender to palpation |
| **Distribution** | Often unilateral | Unilateral | Often bilateral | Strictly bilateral, spares feet |
| **Stemmer's Sign** | **+ve** | -ve | -ve | -ve |
| **Skin Changes** | Hyperkeratosis, Peau d'orange | Erythema, warmth | Hemosiderin staining, ulcers | Normal skin texture |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### International Society of Lymphology (ISL) Staging System
| Stage | Clinical Presentation | Pathophysiology |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 0** | Subclinical. No visible swelling. Heaviness reported. | Impaired transport, capacity still > load. |
| **Stage I** | **Pitting edema**. Swelling reduces with elevation. | Fluid accumulation. Minimal fibrosis. |
| **Stage II** | **Non-pitting edema**. No reduction with elevation. | Significant fibrosis & fat deposition. |
| **Stage III** | Lymphostatic Elephantiasis. Severe skin changes. | Massive fibrosis, hyperkeratosis, papillomatosis. |

### First-Line Clinical Investigations
* Diagnosis is primarily clinical.
* **Limb Volume Measurement (肢體體積測量):** Serial circumferential tape measurements or water displacement.
* **Bioimpedance Spectroscopy:** Detects early/subclinical extracellular fluid shifts.

### Gold Standard Imaging Modality
* **Lymphoscintigraphy (淋巴核子掃描):** Injects 99mTc-nanocolloid into web spaces.
* Findings: Delayed transit time, dermal backflow, asymmetrical or absent node uptake.

### Ancillary Imaging and Pathological Workup
* **Duplex Ultrasound (多普勒超聲波):** Essential to rule out DVT or venous incompetence.
* **CT/MRI (電腦掃描/磁力共振):** Exclude recurrent axillary/pelvic malignancy causing obstruction.
* **Indocyanine Green (ICG) Lymphography (螢光淋巴攝影):** Used peri-operatively to map functional vs non-functional superficial lymphatics.

### Laboratory Workup
* Renal/Liver/Thyroid function tests: Exclude systemic causes of edema.
* **Wuchereria bancrofti (班氏絲蟲)** serology or midnight blood smear: If endemic travel/residence history.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative First-Line: Complete Decongestive Therapy (CDT)
* Phase 1 (Intensive reduction): **Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD - 手動淋巴引流)** + multi-layer short-stretch bandaging.
* Phase 2 (Maintenance): Custom-fitted flat-knit **Compression garments (壓力衣)** + meticulous skin care + specific exercise regimens.

### Medical Management & HA Protocols
* **Meticulous Skin Hygiene:** Daily moisturizers (emollients) to prevent entry portals for bacteria.
* **Cellulitis Management (HA Protocol):** Prompt empirical PO **Augmentin (阿莫西林/克拉維酸)** or **Flucloxacillin (氟氯西林)**.
* **Prophylactic Antibiotics:** Low-dose PO Penicillin V or Erythromycin if ≥2 episodes of cellulitis/year.

### Contraindicated Medical Therapies
* **Diuretics:** STRICTLY AVOID. Removes water but leaves interstitial protein → accelerates fibrosis.

### Indications for Surgical Intervention
* Failure of conservative CDT (typically >6 months trial).
* Recurrent severe cellulitis despite prophylaxis.
* Severe functional impairment or intractable pain.
* Lymphorrhea (weeping of lymph fluid through skin).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Physiological Surgical Operations (Reconstructive)
* Indicated for early-stage disease (ISL Stage I/early II) with functional lymphatics on ICG.
* **Lymphovenous Anastomosis (LVA - 淋巴靜脈吻合術):** Supermicrosurgery connecting superficial lymphatic vessels (<0.8mm) to adjacent subdermal venules to bypass proximal obstruction.
* **Vascularized Lymph Node Transfer (VLNT - 血管化淋巴結轉移):** Free flap transfer of functional lymph nodes (e.g., supraclavicular, groin) with blood supply to the affected limb. Acts as a lymphatic "pump" or sponge.

### Excisional Surgical Operations (Debulking)
* Indicated for late-stage disease (ISL Stage II/III) with heavy fat deposition and fibrosis.
* **Suction-Assisted Protein Lipectomy (SAPL):** Specialized tourniquet-controlled liposuction. Removes solid fat/fibrosis. Requires absolute, strict, lifelong compression post-op.
* **Charles Procedure (查爾斯手術):** Radical excision of all skin and subcutaneous tissue down to deep fascia, followed by Split-Thickness Skin Graft (STSG). Reserved for extreme elephantiasis.

### Pre-operative Counseling & Consent Issues
* Curative expectation management: Surgery reduces volume but is rarely a total cure.
* Lifelong commitment to CDT/compression garments is generally still required.
* VLNT specific risk: Iatrogenic donor-site lymphoedema.
* Charles Procedure specific risk: Severe cosmetic deformity, chronic ulceration, graft failure.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care
* Strict elevation of the affected limb.
* Free flap monitoring (VLNT): Handheld Doppler checks Q1H for first 24-48 hours.
* Avoid venipuncture, IV lines, or BP cuffs on the operated limb.

### Post-operative Compression Protocol
* **Debulking (SAPL):** Immediate application of rigid compression garments to prevent fluid re-accumulation in the newly created dead space.
* **Physiological (LVA/VLNT):** Compression strictly delayed for 3-4 weeks to avoid mechanical pressure on delicate microvascular anastomoses, then resumed.

### Long-Term Rehabilitation Strategy
* Early referral to Allied Health (Physiotherapy/Occupational Therapy) for specialized lymphoedema care.
* Strict weight management (Obesity independently worsens lymphatic load).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications
* Microvascular thrombosis → flap loss or anastomosis failure (LVA/VLNT).
* Hematoma or Seroma formation.
* Surgical site infection / Cellulitis flare.

### Late Surgical Complications
* Recurrence of limb swelling (due to poor compression compliance).
* Hypertrophic scarring or skin graft contractures (Charles procedure).
* Iatrogenic lymphoedema at the VLNT donor site.

### Severe Oncological Complication
* **Stewart-Treves Syndrome (斯圖爾特-特雷維斯綜合徵):** Rare, highly aggressive **Lymphangiosarcoma (淋巴管肉瘤)** arising in chronic lymphoedematous limbs (usually post-mastectomy).
* Prognosis: Extremely poor (5-year survival <10%).
* Clinical presentation: Sudden ↑ swelling, new purplish nodules, or non-healing ulcers.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of secondary lymphoedema in Hong Kong upper limbs? Answer: Iatrogenic, specifically post-axillary lymph node dissection and adjuvant radiotherapy for breast cancer.
* Trap: Prescribing loop diuretics to rapidly reduce limb swelling in a lymphoedema patient. Pearl: Diuretics only clear the water component but leave the high-protein exudate behind in the interstitium, which accelerates tissue fibrosis and worsens the disease long-term.
* Pitfall: Ignoring a newly developed purplish, bruise-like nodule in a patient with a 10-year history of breast cancer-related lymphoedema. Pearl: Maintain a high index of suspicion for Stewart-Treves syndrome (lymphangiosarcoma) and perform an urgent punch biopsy.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate clinically between lipoedema and lymphoedema? Answer: Lipoedema strictly spares the feet (no buffalo hump), is invariably bilateral and symmetrical, is painful/tender to palpation, and presents with a negative Stemmer's sign.
* Trap: Allowing nursing staff to take blood pressure or perform venipuncture on the swollen arm of a post-mastectomy patient. Pearl: Always strictly use the contralateral normal limb to prevent micro-trauma, which can easily trigger severe cellulitis in the immunocompromised, lymphoedematous limb.
