# Carcinoma of Pancreas (胰腺癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
* **Clinical Definition**:
	* Malignant neuroendocrine or exocrine epithelial tumor arising from the pancreas, predominantly **adenocarcinoma (腺癌)** (>85%) originating from the ductal epithelium.
	* Highly aggressive with rapid local invasion and early systemic dissemination.
* **Hong Kong Epidemiology**:
	* Represents a significant cause of cancer-related mortality in Hong Kong, tracking closely behind lung, colorectal, liver, and breast cancers.
	* Incidence peaks between **60–80 years**.
* **Local Risk Factors**:
	* Chronic tobacco smoking (吸煙)
	* **Long-standing diabetes mellitus (糖尿病)** (especially new-onset diabetes in an elderly patient)
	* **Chronic pancreatitis (慢性胰腺炎)**
	* High dietary fat intake

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Pancreatic Surgical Anatomy
* **Anatomical Subdivisions**:
	* Divided into the **head (胰頭)**, **neck (胰頸)**, **body (胰體)**, and **tail (胰尾)**.
	* The head sits within the C-loop of the duodenum; the tail abuts the splenic hilum.
* **Vascular Relationships**:
	* The **superior mesenteric vein (SMV)** and **portal vein (PV)** run posterior to the pancreatic neck and anterior to the uncinate process.
	* The **superior mesenteric artery (SMA)** lies immediately left of the SMV.
	* The **celiac axis** arises superiorly, giving off the **common hepatic artery**.
* **Lymphatic Drainage**: Extensive and multidirectional networks draining into the pancreaticoduodenal, pyloric, celiac, and superior mesenteric lymph node stations.
* **Biliary Confluence**: The common bile duct (CBD) traverses the posterior aspect of the pancreatic head to join the main pancreatic duct at the **Ampulla of Vater (瓦特氏壺腹)**.

### Advanced Pathophysiology
* **Genetics**: Driven by predictable somatic mutations:
	* **KRAS** oncogene activation (>90%).
	* Inactivation of tumor suppressor genes **CDKN2A/p16**, **TP53**, and **SMAD4/DPC4**.
* **Precursor Lesions**: Progresses from **Pancreatic Intraepithelial Neoplasia (PanIN)** grading from low-grade to high-grade carcinoma *in situ* before breaking the basement membrane.
* **Tumor Location Impact**: 
	* Head of pancreas tumors cause early compression of the intrapancreatic CBD, leading to profound obstructive jaundice.
	* Body and tail tumors grow insidiously without biliary obstruction, presenting late with extensive local infiltration and cachexia.


## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Storage and Infiltration Symptoms
* **Obstructive Jaundice (阻塞性黃疸)**: Progressive, relentless, and classically **painless jaundice** when associated with pancreatic head tumors.
* **Abdominal Pain**: Dull, deep-seated epigastric pain radiating directly to the back; characteristically relieved by sitting forward or bending over.
* **Constitutional Distress**: Severe anorexia, rapid **profound weight loss (體重減輕)**, and cachexia out of proportion to tumor size due to malabsorption and systemic inflammation.
* **Steatorrhea (脂肪瀉)**: Pale, bulky, foul-smelling stools that float, caused by exocrine pancreatic insufficiency and exocrine duct occlusion.

### Physical and Pathognomonic Signs
* **Courvoisier's Law (庫瓦西耶法則)**:
	* In a patient with jaundice, a **palpable, non-tender gallbladder (可觸及的無痛膽囊)** indicates the obstruction is unlikely to be a gallstone (as chronic cholelithiasis fibroses the gallbladder wall, preventing distension).
	* It highly points to pancreatic head or periampullary malignancy.
* **Hepatomegaly**: Enlargement of the liver due to long-standing biliary congestion or extensive hepatic metastasis.
* **Trousseau's Sign of Malignancy**: Migratory superficial **thrombophlebitis (血栓性靜脈炎)** caused by tumor-induced hypercoagulability.
* **Cachectic Facies**: Temporal wasting and obvious profound subcutaneous fat loss.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Differential Diagnosis Matrix

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup

### First-Line/Screening Investigations
* **Ultrasound of Abdomen (腹部超聲波)**:
  * Detects dilated intrahepatic and extrahepatic biliary ducts.
  * Identifies a hypoechoic mass in the pancreatic head if >2 cm.
  * Assists in ruling out cholelithiasis as the cause of jaundice.
* **Baseline Liver Function Tests (肝功能測試)**:
  * Obstructive pattern: Marked ↑ **conjugated bilirubin**, highly ↑ **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)** and **Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase (GGT)**.
* **Serum Tumor Marker**:
  * **CA 19-9 (糖鏈抗原19-9)**: Highly sensitive for pancreatic cancer but can be falsely elevated in benign biliary obstruction. Must be interpreted alongside bilirubin levels and **Lewis antigen status** (Lewis-negative individuals do not synthesize CA 19-9).

### Definitive Staging and Characterization
* **Triple-Phase Pancreatic Protocol CT (三相胰腺電腦掃描)**:
  * **Gold Standard Imaging**: Consists of non-contrast, arterial/pancreatic, and portal venous phases.
  * Identifies the primary mass as a hypoattenuating lesion during the pancreatic phase.
  * Evaluates **vascular encroachment**: Assesses the degree of contact (>180° or <180°) with the **SMA**, **celiac axis**, and **SMV/PV** confluence to determine resectability.
* **Magnetic Resonance Cholangiopancreatography (MRCP)**:
  * Non-invasive evaluation of biliary and pancreatic ductal anatomy.
  * Demonstrates the pathognomonic **"Double Duct Sign" (雙管徵)**: Simultaneous dilation of the CBD and main pancreatic duct.

### Advanced and Pre-procedural Interventions
* **Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS) (內鏡超聲)**:
  * Superior modality for detecting small tumors (<2 cm) and evaluating local nodal involvement.
  * Facilitates **EUS-guided Fine-Needle Aspiration (FNA)** or biopsy for histopathological confirmation without seeding the peritoneal cavity.
* **Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography (ERCP)**:
  * Not routinely used for diagnostic purposes due to pancreatitis risk.
  * Indicated strictly for therapeutic **biliary decompression** (insertion of a self-expanding metal stent) in patients with severe cholangitis, intractable pruritus, or those undergoing neoadjuvant chemotherapy.


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications

### Surgical Indications & Resectability Classification
* **Resectable Disease**: No contact with the SMA, celiac axis, or hepatic artery; <180° contact with the SMV/PV without contour deformity.
* **Borderline Resectable Disease**: Limited vascular contact with the SMA/celiac axis (<180°) or SMV/PV encroachment segmentally amendable to surgical reconstruction.
* **Unresectable Disease**: >180° encasement of the SMA or celiac axis; unreconstructible SMV/PV occlusion; distant metastatic disease (M1).

### Management of Curative Intent (Resectable/Borderline)
-  Surgical resection is the only curative modality.
-  For borderline resectable cases, administer **Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy** (FOLFIRINOX protocol) to downstage the tumor, followed by restaging and surgery.
-  All resected patients receive adjuvant chemotherapy (e.g., **Gemcitabine** or **modified FOLFIRINOX**) within 12 weeks post-surgery if functional status permits.

### Management of Palliative Intent (Unresectable/Metastatic)
-  Decompress biliary obstruction via ERCP-placed **Metallic Stents**.
-  Manage duodenal obstruction via endoscopic duodenal stenting or surgical **Gastrojejunostomy**.
-  Aggressive pain control via **Celiac Plexus Neurolysis** and systemic opioids.
-  Palliative chemotherapy to prolong survival.


## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Techniques
* **Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple's Procedure) (惠普爾手術)**:
  * Indicated for tumors involving the pancreatic head, uncinate process, or periampullary region.
  * **Resection Phase**: Removal of the pancreatic head, duodenum, proximal 15 cm of jejunum, gallbladder, distal CBD, and antrum of stomach (unless performing a **Pylorus-Preserving Pancreaticoduodenectomy [PPPD]**).
  * **Reconstruction Phase (The Three Anastomoses)**:
    1. **Panaticojejunostomy (胰腺空腸吻合術)**: Anastomosis of the pancreatic remnant to the jejunum (highest risk of failure).
    2. **Hepaticojejunostomy (肝門空腸吻合術)**: Anastomosis of the bile duct to the jejunum.
    3. **Gastrojejunostomy/Duodenojejunostomy**: Restoration of gastrointestinal continuity.
* **Distal Pancreatectomy and Splenectomy (遠端胰腺及脾臟切除術)**:
  * Indicated for tumors localized to the pancreatic body and tail.
  * Requires simultaneous **splenectomy** due to the intimate relationship of the splenic vessels to the pancreatic parenchyma.

### Pre-operative Consent and High-Yield Risks
* **Mortality and Morbidity Rates**: Local perioperative mortality is <5% in specialized high-volume Hong Kong Hospital Authority tertiary centers, but morbidity remains high (30–40%).
* **Postoperative Pancreatic Fistula (POPF)**: Leakage of enzyme-rich pancreatic fluid from the pancreaticojejunostomy.
* **Post-pancreatectomy Hemorrhage (PPH)**: Catastrophic bleeding from eroded pseudoaneurysms (often secondary to a pancreatic leak digesting the gastroduodenal artery stump).
* **Delayed Gastric Emptying (DGE)**: Transient functional gastric outlet obstruction requiring prolonged nasogastric decompression.
* **New-Onset Diabetes**: Exocrine and endocrine insufficiency resulting from parenchymal loss.


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Protocols
* **Early Mobilization**: Out-of-bed ambulation within **24 hours** post-surgery to reduce thromboembolic complications and atelectasis.
* **Prophylactic Somatostatin Analogues**: Administration of **Octreotide (奧曲肽)** or **Pasireotide** subcutaneously in high-risk soft pancreas glands to decrease exocrine secretions and minimize fistula rates.
* **Drain Management**:
	* Routine placement of intraoperative surgical drains adjacent to the pancreaticojejunostomy.
	* Check drain fluid **Amylase levels** on postoperative day 3; an amylase level >3 times the upper limit of serum normal defines a pancreatic leak.
* **Thromboembolism Prophylaxis**: Strict adherence to mechanical (IPC devices) and pharmacological (low-molecular-weight heparin, **Enoxaparin**) thromboprophylaxis due to the hypercoagulable state of pancreatic malignancy.

### Long-Term Rehabilitation and Exocrine Replacement
* **Pancreatic Enzyme Replacement Therapy (PERT)**: Mandatory administration of oral **Creon (胰酶膠囊)** with all meals and snacks to correct malabsorption, steatorrhea, and fat-soluble vitamin (A, D, E, K) deficiencies.
* **Glycemic Optimization**: Close endocrinology follow-up to manage brittle pancreaticogenic diabetes (Type 3c diabetes) resulting from loss of both islet cells and glucagon-producing cells.


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (Days 1–7)
- **Primary Post-operative Hemorrhage**: Due to technical failure or slippage of surgical clips.
- **Acute Portal Vein Thrombosis**: Secondary to vascular manipulation or reconstruction.

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications (Days 7–30)
- **Postoperative Pancreatic Fistula (POPF)**: Manifests as persistent output of amylase-rich fluid, fever, and leukocytosis.
- **Delayed Gastric Emptying (DGE)**: Inability to tolerate oral intake by postoperative day 14.
- **Intra-abdominal Abscess**: Secondary to low-grade pancreatic or biliary anastomotic leaks.

### Late Post-operative Complications (>30 Days)
- **Anastomotic Stricture**: Progressive narrowing of the hepaticojejunostomy leading to recurrent cholangitis or jaundice.
- **Malabsorption and Cachexia**: Persistent exocrine insufficiency if PERT is under-dosed.

### Prognosis
* **Overall Survival**: Pancreatic adenocarcinoma carries an exceptionally poor prognosis.
* **Five-Year Survival Rate**:
	* Approximately **8–10%** overall.
	* For the select subgroup undergoing successful R0 curative margin resection without nodal involvement, 5-year survival increases to **20–25%**.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: Why is a patient with pancreaticogenic (Type 3c) diabetes at higher risk for severe hypoglycemia compared to a Type 1 diabetic? Answer: Pancreatic resection removes both insulin-producing beta cells and glucagon-producing alpha cells, eliminating the body's primary counter-regulatory hormone response to hypoglycemia.
* Viva Question: What constitutes a positive "double duct sign" on imaging and what pathology does it suggest? Answer: Concurrent dilation of the common bile duct and the main pancreatic duct, strongly signifying a mechanical mass obstruction at the head of the pancreas or ampulla of Vater.  ![image|231](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/05390ae6d46a61f855616080ffd18038.png)
* Trap: Assuming that a normal CA 19-9 level rules out pancreatic adenocarcinoma. Pearl: Approximately 5–10% of the population lack the Lewis blood group antigen (futility to synthesize sialylated Lewis a antigen) and will never display elevated CA 19-9 levels despite a high tumor burden.
* Trap: Performing an urgent diagnostic ERCP on every patient who presents with a pancreatic head mass and jaundice. Pearl: ERCP should be avoided if immediate surgery is planned, as it introduces bacteria into the biliary system, significantly increasing postoperative infectious complications and hepaticojejunostomy leak rates.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to screen and treat for post-splenectomy sepsis risks following a distal pancreatectomy. Prevent by ensuring the patient receives timely vaccinations against encapsulated organisms (Streptococcus pneumoniae, Neisseria meningitidis, Haemophilus influenzae type b) either 2 weeks pre-operatively or 2 weeks post-operatively.
