# Hyperparathyroidism (甲狀旁腺功能亢進症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Hyperparathyroidism Categories
* **Primary Hyperparathyroidism (PHPT) (原發性甲狀旁腺功能亢進):** Autonomous, unregulated ↑PTH secretion → hypercalcemia (高血鈣).
* **Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (SHPT) (繼發性甲狀旁腺功能亢進):** Compensatory ↑PTH due to chronic hypocalcemia/hyperphosphatemia (e.g., Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)).
* **Tertiary Hyperparathyroidism (THPT) (三發性甲狀旁腺功能亢進):** Autonomous ↑PTH secretion following prolonged SHPT, persisting even after cause (e.g., renal transplant) is corrected.

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology
* **PHPT Prevalence:** ~1-4/1000 in HK; predominates in post-menopausal women (F:M = 3:1).
* **SHPT/THPT Burden:** Extremely high in HK HA due to massive local End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) population (esp. diabetic nephropathy). High rates of parathyroidectomy performed for refractory SHPT in local HA hospitals.
* **Diagnosis Shift:** Historically presented with severe bone/stone disease; currently, >80% in HK diagnosed incidentally via routine HA health screening (asymptomatic hypercalcemia).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Parathyroid Gland Embryological Anatomy
* **Superior Glands (上甲狀旁腺):** Derived from 4th pharyngeal pouch. Constant location: posterior to recurrent laryngeal nerve (RLN) (喉返神經) and superior to inferior thyroid artery (ITA).
* **Inferior Glands (下甲狀旁腺):** Derived from 3rd pharyngeal pouch (descends with thymus). Highly variable location: anterior to RLN, inferior to ITA, often ectopic (intrathymic, mediastinal, intrathyroidal).

### Primary Hyperparathyroidism Pathophysiology
* **Aetiology:** 
* **Solitary Adenoma (單發性腺瘤):** 80-85% (monoclonal proliferation, commonly *MEN1* or *PRAD1* gene mutation).
* **Four-gland Hyperplasia (四腺增生):** 10-15% (often associated with MEN1 or MEN2A).
* **Multiple Adenomas:** 4-5%.
* **Parathyroid Carcinoma (甲狀旁腺癌):** <1% (suspect if marked hypercalcemia >3.5 mmol/L, palpable neck mass, RLN palsy).

### Pathophysiological Effects of Elevated PTH
* **Bone:** ↑Osteoclast activity → ↑Ca2+ & ↑PO4- resorption.
* **Kidney:** ↑Ca2+ reabsorption (distal tubule), ↓PO4- reabsorption, ↑1α-hydroxylase activity (converts 25-OH-Vit D → active 1,25-(OH)2-Vit D).
* **GI Tract:** ↑Ca2+ & ↑PO4- absorption (indirectly via ↑active Vit D).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Classic Hypercalcemia Symptoms (高血鈣症狀)
* **Bones (Bony involvement):** Osteitis fibrosa cystica, bone pain, pathological fractures, osteoporosis.
* **Stones (Renal involvement):** Nephrolithiasis (腎結石) (calcium oxalate/phosphate), nephrocalcinosis, polyuria, polydipsia.
* **Abdominal Groans (GI involvement):** Constipation, nausea, vomiting, peptic ulcer disease (↑gastrin), acute pancreatitis.
* **Psychiatric Overtones (Neuro/Psych involvement):** Lethargy, depression, confusion, coma, proximal myopathy.

### Clinical Signs on Physical Examination
* **Neck:** Usually normal. Palpable mass → suspect parathyroid carcinoma or concurrent thyroid goitre.
* **Ophthalmological:** Band keratopathy (calcium deposition in cornea - rare).
* **Neurological:** ↓Deep tendon reflexes, hypotonia.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Hypercalcemia Differential Diagnosis Table
| Condition | PTH Level | Serum Ca2+ | Serum PO4- | Key Discriminatory Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary HPT** | ↑ or Inappropriately N | ↑ | ↓ or Low-N | ↑24h Urine Ca2+ (>10 mmol/d). |
| **FHH (Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcemia)** | Normal or slightly ↑ | ↑ | Normal | **↓24h Urine Ca2+ (<5 mmol/d).** Ca2+/Cr clearance ratio <0.01. Autosomal dominant. **Surgery contraindicated.** |
| **Malignancy (PTHrP mediated)** | ↓ (Suppressed) | ↑↑ (Rapid onset) | ↓ | e.g., Squamous cell lung Ca. Weight loss, known primary. |
| **Bone Metastasis / Multiple Myeloma** | ↓ (Suppressed) | ↑↑ | ↑ or Normal | Lytic bone lesions, Bence-Jones protein +ve, ↑Globulin. |
| **Secondary HPT** | ↑↑ | ↓ or Low-N | ↑ (if CKD) | Known ESRD. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Laboratory Investigations
* **Serum Profile:** ↑Ca2+, ↓PO4-, ↑ALP (indicates bone turnover), Normal/↓25-OH-Vit D.
* **Intact PTH (iPTH):** Elevated or inappropriately normal despite ↑Ca2+.
* **24-hour Urine Calcium & Creatinine:** Essential to exclude FHH (Ca/Cr clearance ratio <0.01 = FHH; >0.02 = PHPT).

### Pre-operative Localization Imaging (HK HA Standard)
* **High-Resolution Ultrasound Neck (頸部超聲波):** Operator-dependent. Identifies enlarged glands (hypoechoic, hypervascular on Doppler). Detects concurrent thyroid nodules.
* **Tc99m-Sestamibi Scintigraphy (MIBI scan) (核子醫學掃描):** Taken up by thyroid and parathyroid. Washes out of thyroid rapidly. Delayed phase (2 hours) shows retention in hyperactive parathyroid tissue.
* **Concordance:** If US + MIBI pinpoint identical location → proceed to Minimally Invasive Parathyroidectomy (MIP).

### Second-Line & Advanced Imaging (For Ectopic/Recurrent Cases)
* **4D-CT Neck/Thorax:** Adds functional (perfusion) data to anatomical CT. Excellent for ectopic glands (mediastinum, retroesophageal). Higher radiation dose.
* **Venous Sampling:** Highly specialized; reserved for re-operative cases with negative standard imaging.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Asymptomatic PHPT Surgical Indications Table (NIH 2014 Guidelines)
| Criteria Category | Threshold for Surgery |
|---|---|
| **Age** | <50 years old |
| **Serum Calcium** | >0.25 mmol/L (1.0 mg/dL) above upper limit of normal |
| **Bone Density (DEXA)** | T-score <-2.5 (any site) OR vertebral fracture on imaging |
| **Renal Function** | eGFR <60 mL/min |
| **Urine Calcium** | >10 mmol/day (400 mg/day) AND ↑stone risk profile, OR presence of nephrolithiasis/nephrocalcinosis |

### Conservative & Medical Management
* **Indication:** Mild asymptomatic PHPT not meeting NIH criteria, or unfit for surgery.
* **Surveillance Protocol:** Annual serum Ca2+, creatinine; DEXA scan every 1-2 years.
* **Medical Therapy:** 
* **Cinacalcet (Calcimimetic):** ↑Sensitivity of CaSR to Ca2+ → ↓PTH. Used for severe hypercalcemia unfit for surgery.
* **Bisphosphonates (Alendronate):** Improves bone mineral density, transient effect on Ca2+.
* **General:** Avoid dehydration, avoid thiazide diuretics (↑Ca2+), maintain moderate dietary calcium (do not restrict).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Minimally Invasive Parathyroidectomy (MIP) Principles
* **Indication:** Concordant localization (US + MIBI) of a solitary adenoma.
* **Approach:** Targeted small incision (1.5 - 2 cm) directly over localized adenoma.
* **Advantages:** ↓Scar, ↓pain, ↓operative time, same-day discharge possible, lower risk to contralateral RLN.

### Bilateral Neck Exploration (BNE) Principles
* **Indication:** Discordant/negative imaging, suspect multi-gland disease (MGD), MEN syndrome, secondary/tertiary HPT.
* **Procedure:** Systematic exploration of all 4 parathyroid anatomical locations.
* **For SHPT/THPT/Hyperplasia:** Perform Subtotal Parathyroidectomy (remove 3.5 glands) OR Total Parathyroidectomy with autotransplantation (implant minced tissue into sternocleidomastoid or brachioradialis muscle).

### Intraoperative PTH (ioPTH) Monitoring (Miami Criteria)
* **Protocol:** Baseline PTH, pre-excision PTH, 10-minute post-excision PTH.
* **Miami Criterion (Success):** >50% drop in ioPTH from the highest pre-excision baseline at 10 minutes post-excision. Confirms cure and absence of multi-gland disease.

### Pre-operative Surgical Consent
* **Key Risks to Discuss:** Bleeding, infection, RLN injury (hoarseness, aspiration), persistent/recurrent hypercalcemia, transient/permanent hypocalcemia (requiring lifelong Ca2+/Vit D).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care Ward Protocol
* **Airway Monitoring:** Keep tracheostomy kit at bedside for first 24h (risk of expanding compressive hematoma).
* **Calcium Monitoring (HA Protocol):** Check serum Ca2+ every 6-12 hours for 24-48 hours. Monitor for perioral numbness, tingling, positive Chvostek’s sign, positive Trousseau’s sign.
* **Discharge Criteria:** Normocalcemic for 24 hours, stable vocal cord function.

### Hungry Bone Syndrome (飢餓骨症候群) Pathophysiology & Management
* **Pathophysiology:** Profound, rapid remineralization of bone following sudden withdrawal of PTH in patients with severe pre-op osteitis fibrosa cystica or chronically high PTH.
* **Clinical Picture:** Severe, prolonged hypocalcemia, hypophosphatemia, hypomagnesemia.
* **Management:** Aggressive IV Calcium Gluconate infusion, high-dose oral Calcium Carbonate, Calcitriol (active Vit D).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Complications Table
| Phase | Complication | Management/Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Early (<24h)** | **Compressive Neck Hematoma (頸部血腫)** | **Surgical Emergency.** Immediate bedside suture release to secure airway → emergency OT for hemostasis. |
| **Early** | **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) Palsy** | Unilateral = hoarseness. Bilateral = stridor/airway loss. Refer Speech Therapy / ENT if persisting >6 months. |
| **Early/Int** | **Transient Hypocalcemia** | Very common (up to 30%). Usually resolves in weeks. Treat with oral Ca2+ and Calcitriol. |
| **Late (>6 mo)** | **Permanent Hypoparathyroidism** | <1-2% in expert hands. Lifelong oral calcium and active Vitamin D required. |
| **Late** | **Recurrent Hyperparathyroidism** | Hypercalcemia >6 months post-op. May require re-exploration (venous sampling, 4D CT). |

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: How do you differentiate Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcemia (FHH) from Primary Hyperparathyroidism biochemically? Answer: Calculate the calcium-to-creatinine clearance ratio. A ratio < 0.01 indicates FHH, whereas > 0.02 indicates primary hyperparathyroidism.
* Trap: Recommending low-calcium diets for patients with Primary Hyperparathyroidism to treat hypercalcemia. Pearl: Severe calcium restriction can lead to negative calcium balance, worsening bone disease, and may stimulate further PTH secretion; normal dietary calcium intake is advised.
* Pitfall: Proceeding with a minimally invasive parathyroidectomy (MIP) solely based on a positive Sestamibi scan without checking intraoperative PTH. You may miss multi-gland disease. Always use ioPTH monitoring (Miami criteria >50% drop at 10 mins) when performing targeted surgery.
* Viva Question: What is the embryological origin of the inferior parathyroid glands and why is this clinically relevant? Answer: They arise from the 3rd pharyngeal pouch along with the thymus. They are clinically relevant because their descent is highly variable, making them the most common ectopic glands found in the anterior mediastinum or within the thymus.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and elevated calcium has Secondary Hyperparathyroidism. Pearl: Secondary HPT typically presents with low or normal calcium. Elevated calcium in a long-standing CKD/Secondary HPT patient indicates progression to Tertiary Hyperparathyroidism (autonomous secretion).
* Pitfall: Discharging a post-parathyroidectomy patient complaining of peri-oral numbness without checking an urgent calcium level. Always suspect and actively exclude post-operative hypocalcemia before discharge.
