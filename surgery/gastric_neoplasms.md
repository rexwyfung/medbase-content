# Carcinoma of Stomach (胃癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Gastric Carcinoma
* **Malignant epithelial tumor** originating from the gastric mucosal lining.
* Over 90% are **Adenocarcinomas (腺癌)**; rarer types include Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors (GIST), Lymphomas, and Neuroendocrine Tumors (NET).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Ranked as the **6th leading cause of cancer deaths** in Hong Kong.
* **Hong Kong Cancer Registry** data demonstrates a higher prevalence in males (M:F ratio ~1.5–2:1).
* Median age of diagnosis is **65–70 years old**.
* Strong correlation with chronic **Helicobacter pylori (幽門螺旋桿菌)** infection within the local population.


## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Gastric Vascular Anatomy and Lymphatic Drainage
* Lesser curvature supplied by **Left Gastric Artery (LGA)** & **Right Gastric Artery (RGA)**.
* Greater curvature supplied by **Left Gastroepiploic Artery (LGEA)** & **Right Gastroepiploic Artery (RGEA)**.
* Fundus supplied by **Short Gastric Arteries** from the splenic artery.
* **Japanese Gastric Cancer Association (JGCA)** Lymph Node Stations:
  * **D1 Lymphadenectomy**: Clears perigastric lymph nodes (Stations 1–6).
  * **D2 Lymphadenectomy**: Clears D1 nodes plus those along the main coeliac trunk branches (Stations 7–12a). Considered the standard of care for radical curative intent.

### Lauren Classification of Gastric Adenocarcinoma
* **Intestinal Type (腸型)**:
	* Well-differentiated, forms cohesive glandular structures.
	* Strongly associated with environmental triggers (H. pylori, smoking, high-salt diet) and precancerous pathways like **Chronic Atrophic Gastritis (慢性萎縮性胃炎)** and **Intestinal Metaplasia (腸上皮化生)**.
	* Common in older males; better relative prognosis.
* **Diffuse Type (瀰漫型)**:
	* Poorly differentiated, infiltrative discohesive growth pattern.
	* Characterized by **Signet Ring Cells (印戒細胞)** where intracellular mucin displaces the nucleus.
	* Strongly linked to genetic alterations, specifically **CDH1 gene mutation** leading to a loss of **E-cadherin**.
	* Occurs in younger patients with an equal M:F distribution; carries a worse prognosis.

### Borrmann Macroscopic Classification of Advanced Gastric Cancer
| Borrmann Type | Macroscopic Description                    | Clinical Growth Characteristics                                            |
| :------------ | :----------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Type I**    | Polypoid / Fungating lesion                | Sharply demarcated, well-circumscribed, minimal wall infiltration          |
| **Type II**   | Ulcerated lesion with elevated borders     | Clear, distinct margins; circumscribed local ulceration                    |
| **Type III**  | Ulcerated lesion with infiltrative borders | Poorly defined margins; aggressive lateral infiltration into adjacent wall |
| **Type IV**   | Diffusely infiltrative lesion              | **Linitis plastica (皮革胃)**; completely non-compliant, rigid wall           |

### Siewert Classification of Gastroesophageal Junction (GEJ) Adenocarcinoma
* **Siewert Type I**: Adenocarcinoma of the distal esophagus. Epicenter located 1 cm to 5 cm proximal to the anatomical Z-line.
* **Siewert Type II**: True cardia carcinoma. Epicenter located between 1 cm proximal and 2 cm distal to the Z-line.
* **Siewert Type III**: Subcardial gastric carcinoma. Epicenter located 2 cm to 5 cm distal to the Z-line, infiltrating the GEJ from below.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Gastric Cancer Symptoms
* Frequently **asymptomatic** or presents with vague, non-specific dyspepsia.
* Postprandial fullness, early satiety, or mild epigastric discomfort.

### Advanced Gastric Cancer Symptoms
* **Weight loss (體重減輕)** and anorexia.
* Persistent, unremitting **epigastric pain (上腹痛)**.
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**: Prominent in proximal tumors or Siewert Type I/II/III lesions.
* **Vomiting (嘔吐)**: Non-bilious, projectile vomiting indicating **Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO) (胃出口梗阻)** in distal/pyloric tumors.
* **Melaena (黑便)** or chronic fatigue secondary to **Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA)** from occult mucosal bleeding.

### Advanced Disease Physical Signs
* **Epigastric mass**: Palpable, hard, irregular mass that moves vertically with respiration.
* **Virchow's node (左鎖骨上淋巴結腫大)**: Enlarged left supraclavicular lymph node via lymphatic spread through the thoracic duct.
* **Sister Mary Joseph nodule (臍部結節)**: Periumbilical metastasis traveling along the falciform ligament.
* **Irish's node**: Enlarged left anterior axillary lymph node.
* **Krukenberg tumor (卵巢轉移瘤)**: Bilateral ovarian masses caused by transcoelomic drop metastasis (classically containing signet ring cells).
* **Blumer's shelf**: Palpable drop metastasis in the rectovesical or rectouterine pouch on digital rectal examination.
* **Succussion splash**: Auscultated over the stomach >4 hours post-meal, indicating severe **Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO)**.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis Matrix
| Differential Diagnosis                            | Discriminatory Features vs Gastric Carcinoma                                                                                                                                               |
| :------------------------------------------------ | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Benign Gastric Ulcer (胃潰瘍)**                    | Periodic pain relieved by food/antacids; completely heals post-PPI therapy; smooth, regular ulcer margins with radiating mucosal folds on OGD.                                             |
| **Gastric Lymphoma (胃淋巴瘤)**                       | MALT or DLBCL types; presents with systemic B-symptoms (fever, night sweats); diffuse mucosal thickening on OGD; primary management relies on H. pylori eradication or chemo-radiotherapy. |
| **Gastric Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor (GIST)** | Subepithelial lesion with a normal overlying mucosa; arises from the Interstitial Cells of Cajal; demonstrates classic immunohistochemical staining for **c-KIT (CD117)**.                 |
| **Chronic Gastritis (慢性胃炎)**                      | Absence of profound weight loss, cachexia, or palpable masses; OGD reveals diffuse mucosal erythema or atrophy without discrete masses or malignant ulcerations.                           |


## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Primary Diagnostic Modality
* **Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD) (胃鏡檢查) + Biopsy**: The definitive gold standard. Requires **at least 6–8 tissue biopsies** taken from the suspicious ulcer rim and base to maximize diagnostic yield.

### Radiographic and Surgical Staging
* **CT Thorax, Abdomen, and Pelvis (CT TAP)**: Primary modality for systemic TNM staging. Identifies solid organ distant metastasis (e.g., liver, lung), gross lymphadenopathy, ascites, and direct invasion into adjacent structures.
* **Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS) (超聲胃鏡)**: Most accurate modality to determine local T-stage depth of invasion and regional N-stage status. Guides decision-making for endoscopic versus surgical resection.
* **Staging Laparoscopy (分期腹腔鏡檢查)**: Indicated for all potentially resectable advanced tumors (≥T3 or N+) lacking clear distant metastasis on CT. Crucial for detecting **occult peritoneal metastasis (腹膜轉移)** and obtaining peritoneal washings for fluid cytology.

### Routine Pre-operative Laboratory Workup
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Evaluates for microcytic, hypochromic anemia reflecting chronic occult blood loss.
* **Renal Function Tests & Electrolytes (RFT)**: Evaluates for **hypokalemic hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis** in patients presenting with persistent vomiting due to GOO.
* **Liver Function Tests (LFT)**: Assesses for hypoalbuminemia (nutritional status marker) and elevated ALP/GGT suggesting hepatic metastasis.
* **Tumor Markers**: **CEA (癌胚抗原)** and **CA 19-9**. Checked at baseline; they lack diagnostic sensitivity/specificity but serve as markers to monitor therapeutic response and recurrence.


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Curative Resection Criteria
* **Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD) / Endoscopic Mucosal Resection (EMR)**: Strictly reserved for **Early Gastric Cancer (EGC)** confined entirely to the mucosa (T1a), <2 cm in size, well-differentiated, non-ulcerated, and lacking lymphovascular space invasion.
* **Radical Surgical Resection**: Mandatory for fit patients with stage IB–III resectable tumors. Requires achieving a **>5 cm proximal margin** for intestinal-type tumors and a **>8 cm proximal margin** for diffuse-type tumors, combined with a standard **D2 lymphadenectomy**.
![image|359](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/1e9267d6b46cc3aa2541ae29b14b89f8.png)

### Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) Oncological Protocols
* **Perioperative Chemotherapy (FLOT Regimen)**:
	* Standard of care within HK HA hospitals for patients with resectable tumors staged as ≥T2 or node-positive (+ve).
	* Consists of 4 cycles of **Fluorouracil**, **Leucovorin**, **Oxaliplatin**, and **Docetaxel** pre-operatively, followed by 4 cycles post-operatively.
* **Adjuvant Chemotherapy**: Single-agent **S-1** or combination **XELOX (Capecitabine + Oxaliplatin)** for patients who proceeded directly to upfront surgery without receiving neoadjuvant chemotherapy.

### Palliative Management for Metastatic Disease (Stage IV)
* **Palliative Gastrectomy**: Avoided unless required as a last resort to control life-threatening hemorrhage or intractable obstruction refractory to conservative measures.
* **Endoscopic Self-Expanding Metal Stent (SEMS)**: Preferred less-invasive palliative choice for managing malignant gastric outlet obstruction or severe distal esophageal blockage.
* **Palliative Gastrojejunostomy (GJ) (胃空腸吻合術)**: Surgical bypass reserved for patients with mechanical obstruction who possess a good performance status but harbor unresectable locoregional or metastatic disease.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Surgical Resection Strategies Based on Tumor Topography
* **Total Gastrectomy (全胃切除術)**:
	* Indicated for proximal-third tumors, mid-body tumors, and all diffuse-type variants (e.g., linitis plastica).
	* Alimentary reconstruction achieved via a **Roux-en-Y Esophagojejunostomy**.
* **Subtotal Gastrectomy (遠端胃大部切除術)**:
	* Indicated for distal-third and antral tumors where a negative proximal margin of >5 cm can be successfully obtained.
	* Reconstruction achieved via a **Billroth II gastrojejunostomy** or a **Roux-en-Y gastrojejunostomy**.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/9257f88512f3017e6d3d25072fb34746.png)

### Operative Steps of Radical D2 Lymphadenectomy
* Systematic clearance of all perigastric lymph nodes (Stations 1–6).
* En-bloc dissection of nodes along the left gastric artery (Station 7), common hepatic artery (Station 8), coeliac axis (Station 9), splenic artery trunk (Stations 10 and 11), and proper hepatic artery (Station 12a).
* Preservation of the spleen and pancreatic tail is maintained as the default standard unless direct tumor invasion is confirmed intra-operatively.
![image|524](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/cfdd31ad79a831eecd385526b7552ebb.png)

### Specific Complications for Informed Consent
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口瘻)**: High-morbidity complication; can cause fulminant peritonitis, mediastinitis (in proximal reconstructions), sepsis, and multi-organ failure.
* **Duodenal Stump Leak (十二指腸殘端瘻)**: Leads to chemical peritonitis and localized fistulization due to the high-volume outflow of unbuffered bile and pancreatic juices.
* **Adjacent Organ Injury**: Risk of damage to the pancreas (resulting in acute postoperative pancreatitis or a pancreatic fistula) and the spleen (potentially necessitating an unintended splenectomy).
* **Systemic Surgical Risks**: Massive intraoperative hemorrhage necessitating blood transfusions, Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT), Pulmonary Embolism (PE), and severe hospital-acquired pneumonia.


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Protocol
* **Early Mobilization**: Out-of-bed ambulation starting on Post-operative Day 1 (POD 1) to lower the incidence of atelectasis and thromboembolic events.
* **Early Oral Intake**: Prompt step-up introduction of clear oral fluids and liquid nutrition as tolerated, avoiding routine prolonged nil-by-mouth (NBM) durations.
* **Multimodal Analgesia**: Scheduled utilization of epidural analgesia or patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) to limit systemic opioid dependency and accelerate the resolution of postoperative ileus.
* Routine prolonged placement of nasogastric tubes (NGT) and abdominal drains is actively discouraged.

### Long-term Metabolic and Nutritional Rehabilitation
* **Megaloblastic Anemia (Pernicious Anemia)**: Total gastrectomy eliminates gastric parietal cells → absolute loss of **Intrinsic Factor (內因子)** production → complete failure of ileal Vitamin B12 absorption. Demands **lifelong intramuscular Vitamin B12 supplementation** (1000 mcg administered every 3 months).
* **Iron and Calcium Malabsorption**: Caused by the absence of gastric acid secretion (required to reduce ferric iron to its absorbable ferrous form) and the anatomical bypass of the primary duodenal absorptive bed. Requires lifelong oral replacements.
* **Dietary Modification**: Patients must shift permanently to small, frequent, high-protein, low-carbohydrate meals while avoiding concurrent liquid intake during solid feeding.


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (POD 1–14)
* **Anastomotic Leak**: Characterized clinical presentation features unexplained tachycardia, spiking fevers, acute abdominal pain, tachypnea, and purulent or enteric-stained output from abdominal drains.
* **Duodenal Stump Blowout**: Classically manifests between POD 5–7. Presents with sudden, severe right upper quadrant (RUQ) pain, localized or generalized peritonitis, and an abrupt surge of bile-rich fluid in the surgical drains.
* **Secondary Hemorrhage**: Stems from slipped surgical ligatures or acute stress-induced ulceration; manifests as hematemesis, melaena, or rapid hypovolemic shock.

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications
* **Delayed Gastric Emptying / Gastroparesis**: Functional motility failure occurring after subtotal resections, requiring prolonged decompression and prokinetic therapy.
* **Acute Afferent Loop Syndrome (輸入畔綜合征)**: Mechanical loop obstruction of the afferent limb following a Billroth II reconstruction. Upstream accumulation of pancreaticobiliary secretions creates high intraluminal pressure, manifesting as severe RUQ pain followed by sudden **projectile, non-bilious vomiting** that immediately decompresses the limb and relieves the pain.

### Late Post-operative Complications
* **Early Dumping Syndrome (早期傾倒綜合征)**: Manifests 15–30 minutes following a meal. Hypertonic chyme is dumped directly into the jejunum → major osmotic fluid shifts from the intravascular space into the bowel lumen → rapid jejunal distension and systemic vasoactive peptide release. Signs include tachycardia, dizziness, diaphoresis, abdominal cramping, and acute diarrhea.
* **Late Dumping Syndrome (晚期傾倒綜合征)**: Manifests 1–3 hours following a meal. Rapid carbohydrate delivery into the small bowel → transient systemic hyperglycemia → triggers an exaggerated, dysregulated insulin surge → results in severe **Reactive Hypoglycemia (反應性低血糖)**. Signs include tremors, diaphoresis, confusion, and syncope.
* **Blind Loop Syndrome**: Chronic bacterial overgrowth within an aperistaltic afferent limb, causing bile salt deconjugation, steatorrhea, and macrocytic anemia.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why is a D2 lymphadenectomy preferred over D1 in fit patients undergoing curative gastric cancer surgery? Answer: Large randomized controlled trials show that D2 lymphadenectomy significantly reduces long-term locoregional recurrence rates and improves cancer-specific survival compared to D1, provided it is performed in high-volume specialized units with low postoperative morbidity.
* Viva Question: What is the clinical significance of finding positive peritoneal cytology (CY1) during staging laparoscopy in the absence of macroscopic peritoneal implants? Answer: According to the AJCC staging system, positive peritoneal cytology is classified as M1 (Stage IV) disease because it portends an exceptionally high risk of early peritoneal recurrence; this finding generally contraindicates upfront radical surgical resection in favor of systemic chemotherapy.
* Trap: Assuming that a negative biopsy result from an initial endoscopy completely rules out gastric cancer in a patient presenting with a suspicious-looking gastric ulcer. Pearl: All gastric ulcers require a repeat OGD with extensive re-biopsying of the ulcer rim after 6–8 weeks of intensive PPI therapy to document complete macroscopic and microscopic healing and to rule out occult malignancy.
* Trap: Attempting to treat post-gastrectomy B12 deficiency anemia with high-dose oral Vitamin B12 supplements. Pearl: Total gastrectomy entirely removes the source of intrinsic factor, making oral absorption impossible; treatment must consist of lifelong intramuscular Vitamin B12 injections.
* Pitfall: Directing a subtotal gastrectomy for a diffuse-type gastric cancer using standard 5 cm macroscopic margins. Diffuse-type adenocarcinomas exhibit extensive submucosal microscopic infiltration far beyond the visible borders of the tumor, requiring a minimal proximal margin of 8 cm or an intraoperative conversion to a total gastrectomy, which should always be verified by intraoperative frozen section analysis of the margins.
