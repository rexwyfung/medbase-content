# Carcinoma of Stomach (胃癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Gastric Cancer (胃癌的臨床定義)
* **Malignant neoplasm** arising from gastric mucosa.
* Histologically predominant type (>90-95%) is **Gastric Adenocarcinoma (胃腺癌)**.
* Less common types include **Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor (GIST, 胃腸道基質瘤)**, **Gastric Lymphoma (胃淋巴瘤)**, and **Neuroendocrine Tumor (NET, 神經內分泌腫瘤)**.

### Local Epidemiology and Incidence in Hong Kong (香港本地流行病學與發病率)
* Ranked as **6th most common cancer** in Hong Kong.
* **6th leading cause of cancer deaths** locally.
* Median age of presentation is around **65-70 years old**.
* Male-to-female ratio is approximately **1.5:1 to 2:1**.
* Highest incidence rates are clustered in East Asian regions (including China, Japan, Korea).

### Major Etiological Factors and Risk Factors (主要病因與風險因素)
* **Helicobacter pylori (幽門螺桿菌)** infection (+ve in >70% distal gastric cancers) → chronic inflammation.
* Dietary habits: High intake of **salted, smoked, or pickled foods** (high nitrosamines) vs low intake of fresh fruits/vegetables (vitamin C/E protective).
* Behavioral factors: **Cigarette smoking** (↑ risk by 1.5x) and **alcohol consumption**.
* Premalignant conditions: **Chronic atrophic gastritis (慢性萎縮性胃炎)**, **intestinal metaplasia (腸上皮化生)**, **gastric adenomatous polyps (胃腺瘤性息肉)**, **pernicious anemia (惡性貧血)**, and history of partial gastrectomy (stump cancer after 15-20 years).
* Genetic syndromic risk: **Hereditary Diffuse Gastric Cancer (HDGC, 遺傳性彌漫性胃癌)** via **CDH1 gene mutation** (E-cadherin loss), **Lynch syndrome (林奇綜合徵)**, and **Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP, 家族性腺瘤性息肉病)**.


## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Gastric Surgical Anatomy and Blood Supply (胃部外科解剖與血液供應)
* Divided anatomically into **cardia (賁門)**, **fundus (胃底)**, **body (胃體)**, **antrum (胃竇)**, and **pylorus (幽門)**.
* Lesser curvature supply: **Left Gastric Artery (左胃動脈)** (from celiac trunk) and **Right Gastric Artery (右胃動脈)** (from hepatic artery proper).
* Greater curvature supply: **Left Gastroepiploic Artery (左胃網膜動脈)** (from splenic artery), **Right Gastroepiploic Artery (右胃網膜動脈)** (from gastroduodenal artery), and **Short Gastric Arteries (胃短動脈)** (from splenic artery supplying fundus).

### Gastric Lymphatic Drainage Zones and D2 Dissection (胃淋巴引流區域與D2切除術)
* Lymph node (LN) stations classified by Japanese Gastric Cancer Association (JGCA) into 16 stations across 3 compartments.
* **Perigastric LNs (D1 stations)**: Stations 1-6 (along lesser/greater curvatures, cardia, pylorus).
* **Extra-perigastric LNs (D2 stations)**: Stations 7-12 (along left gastric, common hepatic, celiac trunk, splenic splenic artery, hepatic proper artery).
* Standard of care in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) hospitals is **D2 Lymphadenectomy (D2淋巴結清掃)** → removal of both D1 and D2 stations to optimize regional control.

### Lauren Classification of Gastric Adenocarcinoma (胃腺癌的Lauren分型)
* **Intestinal Type (腸型)**:
  * Well-differentiated, cohesive cells forming glandular structures.
  * Driven by environmental factors via **Correa pathway** (Normal → Atrophic Gastritis → Intestinal Metaplasia → Dysplasia → Carcinoma).
  * Predominantly affects older males; located distally.
* **Diffuse Type (彌漫型)**:
  * Poorly differentiated, discohesive cells infiltrating gastric wall singly or in small groups.
  * Characterized by **Signet-Ring Cells (印戒細胞)** containing intracellular mucin displacing the nucleus.
  * Driven by genetic alterations (e.g., **CDH1 loss**); affects younger patients, equal gender distribution.
  * Tends to cause **Linitis Plastica (皮革胃)** (rigid, thickened stomach due to extensive desmoplasia) with poor prognosis.

### World Health Organization Histological Classification (世界衛生組織組織學分型)
* Adenocarcinoma (tubular, papillary, mucinous, signet-ring cell).
* Adenosquamous carcinoma.
* Squamous cell carcinoma.
* Undifferentiated carcinoma.

### Pathophysiology of Premalignant Gastric Lesions (癌前病變的病理生理學)
* **Chronic H. pylori colonization** → cytotoxin-associated gene A (CagA) and vacuolating cytotoxin A (VacA) release.
* Persistent mucosal injury → **hypochlorhydria (低胃酸)** → bacterial overgrowth → conversion of dietary nitrates into carcinogenic **nitrosamines**.
* Mucosal atrophy causes loss of parietal/chief cells, replaced by mucus-secreting intestinal-type epithelium (**intestinal metaplasia**).
* Accumulation of genetic mutations (p53 inactivation, APC mutation, microsatellite instability) drives progress to **high-grade dysplasia** and **invasive carcinoma**.


## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early-stage Gastric Cancer Symptoms (早期胃癌臨床症狀)
* Frequently **asymptomatic** (discovered on screening endoscopy).
* Non-specific dyspepsia (indigestion) unrelieved by antacids.
* Epigastric discomfort or mild pain.
* Postprandial fullness or early satiety.

### Advanced-stage Gastric Cancer Symptoms (晚期胃癌臨床症狀)
* **Weight loss (體重減輕)** (involuntary and progressive).
* Anorexia (loss of appetite).
* Persistent **epigastric pain (上腹痛)** radiating to the back.
* Persistent vomiting (suggests **Gastric Outlet Obstruction [GOO, 胃出口梗阻]** by antral tumor).
* Dysphagia (suggests tumor involving **cardia or gastroesophageal junction [GEJ]**).
* Melena (黑便) or chronic hematemesis (吐血) causing iron deficiency anemia features (fatigue, dyspnea).

### Signs of Metastatic Spread and Eponymous Signs (轉移性擴散體徵與命名體徵)
* **Virchow's Node (維爾肖氏淋巴結)**: Left supraclavicular lymphadenopathy via thoracic duct.
* **Sister Mary Joseph's Nodule (瑪麗約瑟夫修女結節)**: Palpable periumbilical mass indicating peritoneal metastasis.
* **Krukenberg Tumor (庫肯勃氏瘤)**: Palpable adnexal mass due to transcoelomic drop metastasis to ovaries (usually bilateral signet-ring cells).
* **Blumer's Shelf (布魯默氏架)**: Palpable mass on digital rectal examination (DRE) in the rectovesical or rectouterine pouch.
* **Irish's Node**: Left anterior axillary lymph node metastasis.
* **Hepatomegaly (肝腫大)** or ascites (腹水) implying advanced liver or peritoneal dissemination.

### Paraneoplastic Syndromes Associated with Gastric Cancer (胃癌相關副腫瘤綜合徵)
* **Leser-Trélat sign (萊瑟-特雷拉特徵)**: Sudden eruption of multiple seborrheic keratoses with pruritus.
* **Acanthosis Nigricans (黑棘皮症)**: Hyperpigmented, velvety plaques in flexural areas (axillae, neck).
* **Trousseau Sign of Malignancy (特魯索氏徵)**: Migratory superficial thrombophlebitis due to hypercoagulability.
* **Microangiopathic Hemolytic Anemia (MAHA)**.


## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Gastric Lesions (胃部病變鑑別診斷表)

| Differential Diagnosis | Clinical Presentation | Endoscopic Findings | Histological / Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gastric Adenocarcinoma** | Age >60, profound weight loss, chronic anemia, cachexia. | Irregular, heaped-up margins, friable base, loss of rugal folds. | Mucin+ve, **signet-ring cells** or atypical glandular sheets; malignant cells breach basement membrane. |
| **Benign Gastric Ulcer** | Epigastric pain exacerbated by food; history of NSAID use or H. pylori. | Smooth, regular, punched-out borders; clean base; radiating mucosal folds. | No cytological atypia; **fibrous tissue and acute/chronic inflammatory cell infiltrates**. |
| **Gastric Lymphoma (MALT / DLBCL)** | B-symptoms (fever, night sweats), abdominal pain, H. pylori +ve history. | Large fleshy rugal folds, diffuse infiltrative submucosal plaques. | Monoclonal B-cell infiltrate; **lymphoepithelial lesions** pathognomonic for MALToma. |
| **Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor (GIST)** | Often asymptomatic, presents with acute upper GI bleed or mass effect. | Subepithelial lesion with normal overlying mucosa, central umbilication/ulceration. | Spindle or epithelioid cells; **CD117 (c-kit) +ve** and **DOG1 +ve** immunohistochemistry. |
| **Gastric Neuroendocrine Tumor (NET)** | Associated with Type A chronic gastritis/pernicious anemia (Type 1) or Zollinger-Ellison (Type 2). | Multiple small polypoid submucosal nodules, smooth surface. | **Chromogranin A +ve** and **Synaptophysin +ve**; salt-and-pepper chromatin pattern. |

### Distinguishing Gastric Ulcer from Ulcerated Carcinoma (良性胃潰瘍與潰瘍性胃癌的鑑別)
* Benign ulcers have **smooth, regular margins**, and surrounding mucosal folds extend cleanly to the ulcer edge.
* Malignant ulcers have **irregular, elevated, beaded, or nodular margins**, and surrounding rugal folds terminate abruptly or fuse before reaching the crater.
* Every gastric ulcer **MUST be biopsied** (minimum 6-8 biopsy bites from ulcer margins) to rule out malignancy before initiating medical healing therapy.


## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Diagnostic Endoscopy and Tissue Biopsy (診斷性內視鏡與組織活檢)
* **Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD, 胃鏡檢查)** is the **gold standard diagnostic investigation**.
* Allows direct visualization of tumor location, size, macroscopic type (Borrmann classification).
* **Multiple targeted tissue biopsies (>=6 bites)** required from the ulcer/mass border to maximize diagnostic yield.
* Brush cytology can be added to improve sensitivity.

### Laboratory Workup and Tumor Markers (實驗室檢查與腫瘤標記物)
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Confirms **microcytic hypochromic anemia** (due to chronic blood loss).
* **Renal Function Tests & Electrolytes (RFT)**: Assesses baseline hydration status, especially if patient is vomiting from GOO.
* **Liver Function Tests (LFT)**: Hypoalbuminemia indicates **malnutrition**; elevated ALP/GGT suggests **liver metastases**.
* **Coagulation Profile (PT/APTT)**: Baseline for surgical clearance.
* **Tumor Markers**:
  * **CEA (Carcinoembryonic Antigen, 癌胚抗原)**: ↑ in 30-40% cases; useful for post-op monitoring.
  * **CA 19-9**: Associated with advanced stage and peritoneal dissemination.
  * **CA 72-4**: High specificity for gastric cancer recurrence monitoring.
  * *Note: Tumor markers have low diagnostic sensitivity/specificity; never used in isolation for diagnosis.*

### Radiological Staging via CT and PET-CT (電腦斷層與正電子掃描分期)
* **Contrast-Enhanced CT Abdomen and Pelvis (CECT)**:
  * Identifies **gastric wall thickening**, regional LN enlargement, and distant metastases (liver, lung, bone, peritoneum).
  * Evaluates **ascites** or presence of pelvic masses (Krukenberg tumor / Blumer's shelf).
* **CT Thorax**: Routinely included to rule out pulmonary metastases.
* **FDG PET-CT (正電子掃描)**: Optional; highly effective for detecting occult distant metastases, but has **low sensitivity for diffuse-type/signet-ring cell carcinomas** due to low FDG-avidity.

### Endoscopic Ultrasound for T and N Staging (內視鏡超聲波T與N分期)
* **Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS, 內視鏡超聲波)**:
  * Most accurate modality for evaluating **locoregional T stage** (depth of wall invasion across individual histological layers).
  * Evaluates **N stage** (visualizes perigastric lymph nodes; allows Fine Needle Aspiration [FNA] biopsy if required).
  * Crucial for determining eligibility for **Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD)** in early-stage disease.

### Staging Laparoscopy and Peritoneal Cytology (分期腹腔鏡與腹膜細胞學)
* **Staging Laparoscopy (分期腹腔鏡檢查)**:
  * Indicated for all locoregionally advanced resectable diseases (≥T3 or N+ve) without distant organ metastasis on CT.
  * Detects **occult peritoneal micrometastases (miliary deposits)** missed by CT scans in up to 20-30% of cases.
* **Peritoneal Lavage Cytology**: Instillation and re-aspiration of normal saline to check for free malignant cells (+ve cytology = M1 disease, ruling out immediate curative resection).

### AJCC TNM Staging System Table (AJCC TNM分期系統表)

| Stage Element | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **T1a** | Tumor invades **lamina propria or muscularis mucosae** (candidate for endoscopic resection). |
| **T1b** | Tumor invades **submucosa**. |
| **T2** | Tumor invades **muscularis propria**. |
| **T3** | Tumor invades **subserosal connective tissue** without breach of visceral peritoneum. |
| **T4a** | Tumor perforates **serosa (visceral peritoneum)**. |
| **T4b** | Tumor invades **adjacent structures** (e.g., pancreas, spleen, colon, liver). |
| **N1** | Metastasis in **1 to 2** regional lymph nodes. |
| **N2** | Metastasis in **3 to 6** regional lymph nodes. |
| **N3a** / **N3b** | Metastasis in **7 to 15** / **16 or more** regional lymph nodes. |
| **M0** / **M1** | No distant metastasis / **Distant metastasis present** (including +ve peritoneal cytology). |


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Endoscopic Resection Indications and Criteria (內視鏡切除指徵與標準)
* **Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD, 內視鏡黏膜下剥離術)** or **Endoscopic Mucosal Resection (EMR)**.
* **Absolute Indications**:
  * Differentiated adenocarcinoma.
  * Confined to **mucosa (T1a)**.
  * Size **≤2 cm**.
  * **Non-ulcerated** (-ve for ulceration).
  * No lymphovascular invasion.
* *Expanded criteria allow ESD for larger or ulcerated mucosal tumors and small (<2 cm) undifferentiated lesions without lymphovascular invasion.*

### Multidisciplinary Team Protocol for Resectable Disease (可切除疾病的多學科團隊方案)
* All cases discussed in joint Surgical Oncology MDT meeting.
* **Early Gastric Cancer (Stage I)**: Direct surgical resection (or ESD if meeting specific criteria).
* **Locoregionally Advanced Gastric Cancer (Stage II to III, ≥T2 or N+ve)**: **Peri-operative Chemotherapy (術前術後雙向化療)** + Radical Resection.

### Neoadjuvant and Adjuvant Chemotherapy Protocols (術前與術後輔助化療方案)
* **Neoadjuvant (Pre-operative) Chemotherapy**:
  * Local HA hospitals widely utilize **FLOT regimen** (Fluorouracil, Leucovorin, Oxaliplatin, Docetaxel) for 3-4 cycles pre-op, or **XELOX/SOX** (Capecitabine/S-1 + Oxaliplatin).
  * Aim: Downstage tumor, treat micrometastases, and ↑ R0 resection rate.
* **Adjuvant (Post-operative) Chemotherapy**:
  * Continued post-operatively for 3-4 cycles (FLOT) or up to 6 months to 1 year (S-1 / XELOX based on CLASSIC and ACTS-GC trials).
  * Indicated for patients who had upfront surgery and were pathologically staged as ≥T3 or N+ve.

### Indications for Definitive Surgical Resection (確定性手術切除指徵)
* Absence of distant organ metastases (M0) and -ve peritoneal cytology.
* Patient medically fit for major upper abdominal laparotomy/laparoscopy (ECOG performance status 0-1, acceptable cardiopulmonary reserve).
* Intent is **R0 Resection (complete macroscopic and microscopic clearance with -ve margins)**.

### Palliative Management Strategies for Advanced Disease (晚期疾病的姑息性治療策略)
* Indicated for Stage IV disease (distant metastases, extensive peritoneal seeding, encasement of major vessels).
* **Palliative Surgery**: **Palliative Gastrectomy** reserved for persistent tumor hemorrhage or uncontrolled pain; **Gastrojejunostomy (GJ, 胃空腸吻合術)** or **Palliative Roux-en-Y Bypass** for gastric outlet obstruction.
* **Endoscopic Interventions**: **Self-Expanding Metallic Stent (SEMS, 自張式金屬支架)** placement for relief of malignant pyloric or esophageal obstruction; endoscopic hemostasis (APC/coagulation) for bleeding lesions.
* **Medical Palliation**: Systemic palliative chemotherapy +/- targeted therapy based on biomarker profiles:
  * **Trastuzumab (曲妥珠單抗)** if **HER2-positive** (+ve).
  * **Nivolumab (納武利尤單抗)** if **PD-L1 positive** (CPS ≥5).


## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Subtotal Gastrectomy (根治性胃次全切除術原則)
* Indicated for **distal tumors (antrum or lower body)** where a **>3-5 cm proximal resection margin** can be safely achieved.
* Removes distal **4/5ths of the stomach**, including pylorus and first part of duodenum.
* Omentectomy (greater and lesser omentum removal) performed en-bloc.

### Operative Principles of Total Gastrectomy (根治性全胃切除術原則)
* Indicated for **proximal tumors (cardia, fundus), middle body tumors, or diffuse-type/linitis plastica** involving the whole stomach.
* Entire stomach resected from distal esophagus (2 cm above GEJ) to proximal duodenum (2 cm distal to pylorus).
* Complete regional D2 lymphadenectomy included.

### Lymphadenectomy Extent and D2 Dissection Technique (淋巴結清掃範圍與D2切除技術)
* Clearance of **D1 stations** (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6) and **D2 stations** (7: left gastric, 8: common hepatic, 9: celiac axis, 11: splenic artery, 12a: hepatic proper artery).
* Requires skeletonization of major upper abdominal vessels.
* Spleen and pancreatic tail preserving D2 lymphadenectomy is standard (avoid routine splenectomy unless tumor directly invades).

### Gastrointestinal Reconstruction Options (胃腸道重建方案選項)
* **Following Subtotal Gastrectomy**:
  * **Billroth I (畢氏I式)**: Gastroduodenostomy (direct anastomosis of gastric stump to duodenum). Low risk of dumping, more physiological, but high tension/leak rate if mobilization insufficient.
  * **Billroth II (畢氏II式)**: Gastrojejunostomy (loop of jejunum brought up to gastric stump). High risk of **alkaline reflux gastritis** and afferent loop syndrome.
  * **Roux-en-Y Gastrojejunostomy (Roux-en-Y胃腸吻合術)**: Preferred to eliminate bile reflux. Jejunum divided 40 cm distal to ligament of Treitz; distal limb anastomosed to gastric stump, proximal limb anastomosed 40-50 cm downstream (Y-loop).
* **Following Total Gastrectomy**:
  * **Roux-en-Y Esophagojejunostomy (食道空腸Roux-en-Y吻合術)**: Essential reconstruction modality. Direct jejunal loop to esophagus anastomosis, often utilizing a circular stapler.

### Pre-operative Informed Consent and Specific Surgical Risks (術前知情同意與特異性手術風險)
* Anastomotic Leak (吻合口漏) (Esophagojejunostomy leak leads to high mortality mediastinitis/peritonitis; duodenal stump blow-out leads to biliary peritonitis).
* Injury to adjacent structures (pancreatic tail trauma → **pancreatic fistula**, splenic injury → emergency splenectomy, bile duct injury).
* Secondary hemorrhage from skeletonized major vessels.
* Long-term nutritional impairments (profound weight loss, dumping syndrome).


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery Protocol for Gastrectomy (胃切除術後加速康復方案)
* **Pre-operative**: No prolonged fasting; carbohydrate loading drinks up to 2 hours pre-op; optimization of nutritional status via immunonutrition if cachectic.
* **Intra-operative**: Avoidance of routine abdominal surgical drains (or early removal); restrictive fluid management to prevent gut edema; maintenance of normothermia.
* **Post-operative**: Effective multimodal opioid-sparing analgesia (epidural or transversus abdominis plane block); **early mobilization** (out of bed on Post-op Day 1); avoidance of routine nasogastric tube (NGT) decompression (early removal in theatre or Day 1).

### Immediate Post-operative Monitoring and Complication Surveillance (術後即時監測與併發症監控)
* Monitor vital signs for tachycardia or hypotension (indicates early internal hemorrhage or sepsis).
* Strict monitoring of fluid balance and surgical drain output (sudden high amylase level in drain fluid confirms **pancreatic fluid leak** or **anastomotic/duodenal leak**).
* Early introduction of clear oral fluids (Post-op Day 1-2) progressing to soft diet as tolerated by Day 4-5 based on bowel function return.

### Long-term Nutritional Rehabilitation and Vitamin Supplementation (長期營養康復與維他命補充)
* Small, frequent, high-protein meals (5-6 small meals/day) to compensate for loss of gastric reservoir.
* **Vitamin B12 supplementation**: Essential lifelong intramuscular injections (1000 mcg every 3 months) after total gastrectomy due to complete loss of **Intrinsic Factor (內因子)** from gastric parietal cells → prevents pernicious megaloblastic anemia and subacute combined degeneration of spinal cord.
* **Iron supplementation**: Oral/IV iron needed due to loss of gastric acid (HCl required to reduce dietary $Fe^{3+}$ to absorbable $Fe^{2+}$).
* **Calcium and Vitamin D supplementation**: Early monitoring to prevent metabolic bone disease (osteomalacia/osteoporosis) due to bypass of duodenum (primary calcium absorption site).


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (術後早期併發症)
* **Anastomotic Leakage (吻合口滲漏)**: Occurs on Post-op Day 5-7. Presents with severe abdominal pain, persistent tachycardia, fever, peritonitis, or purulent/biliary drain output. Require immediate CT with oral contrast, NPO, IV antibiotics, or emergency re-laparotomy/stenting.
* **Duodenal Stump Blow-out (十二指腸殘端破裂)**: Specific to Billroth II / Roux-en-Y subtotal gastrectomy. Caused by high intraluminal pressure in the afferent loop. Presents as sudden severe right upper quadrant pain and peritonitis.
* **Secondary Hemorrhage (繼發性出血)**: Bleeding from stapled lines or slipped ligatures on major vessels.
* **Acute Pancreatitis / Pancreatic Fistula (胰腺廔管)**: Secondary to thermal or mechanical trauma during D2 lymph node dissection around celiac axis and splenic artery.

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications (術後中期併發症)
* **Delayed Gastric Emptying (DGE, 延遲胃排空)** / Gastric Atony: Occurs after subtotal gastrectomy due to vagal denervation. Treats with prokinetics and temporary NGT decompression.
* **Afferent Loop Syndrome (輸入畔綜合徵)**: Specific to Billroth II reconstruction. Mechanical obstruction of afferent jejunal loop → accumulation of bile and pancreatic secretions → loop distension. Presents as severe cramping abdominal pain 20-30 minutes postprandially, relieved completely by **projectile, non-bilious vomiting**.

### Late Post-operative Complications and Dumping Syndromes (術後晚期併發症與傾倒綜合徵)
* **Early Dumping Syndrome (早期傾倒綜合徵)**:
  * Occurs **10-30 minutes post-meals**.
  * Pathophysiology: Hyperosmolar chyme dumps rapidly into jejunum → massive fluid shift from intravascular space into bowel lumen → bowel distension and release of vasoactive peptides (serotonin, bradykinin).
  * Symptoms: Abdominal cramps, diarrhea, nausea, palpitations, diaphoresis (sweating), syncope.
  * Management: Small dry meals, low carbohydrates, avoid liquids during meals; **Octreotide (奧曲肽)** for refractory cases.
* **Late Dumping Syndrome (晚期傾倒綜合徵)**:
  * Occurs **1-3 hours post-meals**.
  * Pathophysiology: Rapid delivery of simple carbohydrates into small bowel → rapid glucose absorption → transient systemic hyperglycemia → triggers exaggerated, massive **insulin surge** → delayed reactive **reactive hypoglycemia**.
  * Symptoms: Tremors, sweating, confusion, dizziness, syncope.
  * Management: Dietary modification (complex carbohydrates, high protein, high fiber), acarbose.
* **Alkaline Reflux Gastritis (鹼性反流性胃炎)**: Chronic bile reflux into stomach stump after Billroth II → mucosal erythema, burning pain unrelieved by antacids. Treated by converting Billroth II to Roux-en-Y.
* **Nutritional Deficiency Anemias**: Megaloblastic (B12 deficiency) and Iron-deficiency anemia.

### Prognostic Factors and 5-year Survival Rates (預後因素與五年生存率)
* Most critical prognostic factor is **Stage at Diagnosis** (depth of wall invasion and number of positive lymph nodes).
* **Early Gastric Cancer (T1, any N)**: Excellent prognosis, 5-year survival rate **>90-95%**.
* **Stage II**: 5-year survival approx **60-70%**.
* **Stage III**: 5-year survival drops to **20-30%**.
* **Stage IV (Metastatic disease)**: Dismal prognosis, 5-year survival **<5%**; median survival 10-12 months.
* High **Lymph Node Ratio (LNR)** (+ve LNs divided by total LNs harvested) implies higher recurrence. Minimally **15 LNs must be examined** for accurate staging.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why is a staging laparoscopy mandatory in advanced gastric cancer before scheduling curative gastrectomy? Answer: Because contrast-enhanced CT scans fail to detect subtle peritoneal or miliary omental metastases (micrometastases) in up to 20-30% of cases, which would signify Stage IV disease and render a morbid radical gastrectomy non-beneficial.
* Viva Question: A patient presents with severe cramping abdominal pain 30 minutes after eating, followed by projectile bilious vomiting which completely relieves the pain. What is the diagnosis and surgical anatomy? Answer: Afferent loop syndrome post-Billroth II reconstruction, caused by acute mechanical obstruction of the afferent jejunal limb, leading to stasis and high-pressure build-up of bile/pancreatic juices until the obstruction is overcome and contents forcefully regurgitate into the stomach.
* Trap: Assuming that a negative biopsy from a gastric ulcer completely rules out gastric cancer. Pearl: Gastric carcinomas can have necrotic ulcer bases; you must always take at least 6-8 biopsy samples specifically from the raised, irregular margins of the ulcer, and repeat the endoscopy to confirm complete healing after a course of PPIs.
* Trap: Prescribing lifelong oral Vitamin B12 tablets to a patient who has undergone a radical total gastrectomy. Pearl: Total gastrectomy removes all gastric parietal cells, causing a complete lack of Intrinsic Factor; oral B12 cannot be absorbed in the terminal ileum without intrinsic factor, so the patient requires lifelong parenteral (intramuscular) Vitamin B12 injections.
* Pitfall: Performing a routine splenectomy during a standard D2 lymphadenectomy for a middle-body gastric adenocarcinoma to clear Station 10 nodes. Routine splenectomy increases post-operative morbidity and infectious risks without providing any survival benefit, and should only be performed if the tumor directly invades the splenic hilum or parenchyma.
* Pitfall: Managing early dumping syndrome by advising patients to drink a large glass of water during meals to wash down food. Drinking liquids during meals accelerates gastric emptying and worsens the rapid hyperosmolar fluid shifts in the jejunum; patients must separate fluid intake from solid food by at least 30-45 minutes.
