# Anal Cancer (肛門癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Anal Cancer
* **Anal Cancer (肛門癌):** Malignancy arising from the anal canal (肛管) or anal margin (肛緣).
* **Histology Types:** 
* **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) (鱗狀細胞癌):** >80% of all cases.
* **Adenocarcinoma (腺癌):** Rare, arises from upper anal canal/anal glands; treated similar to low rectal cancer.
* **Malignant Melanoma (惡性黑色素瘤):** <2% cases, highly aggressive, poor prognosis.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Incidence:** Rare in HK (accounts for ~1-2% of all GI malignancies) but incidence is ↑.
* **High-Risk Demographics:** 
* **MSM (男男性接觸者):** Highest risk group.
* **Immunocompromised:** HIV +ve patients (CD4 count correlates with risk), solid organ transplant recipients.
* **Gender:** Generally more common in females (cervical/vulvar dysplasia association), but male predominance in HIV/MSM cohorts in HK.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Anal Canal
* **Anal Canal (肛管):** ~3-4 cm long, from anorectal ring (puborectalis) to anal verge.
* **Dentate Line (齒狀線) / Pectinate Line:** Critical anatomical watershed dividing upper & lower canal.
* **Above Dentate Line:**
* **Epithelium:** Columnar / Transitional (Cloacogenic).
* **Embryology:** Endoderm (Hindgut).
* **Arterial:** Superior rectal artery (IMA branch).
* **Venous:** Portal system (Superior rectal vein → IMV).
* **Lymphatics:** Internal iliac nodes (髂內淋巴結) & Mesorectal nodes.
* **Nerve:** Autonomic (painless).
* **Below Dentate Line:**
* **Epithelium:** Non-keratinized stratified squamous.
* **Embryology:** Ectoderm (Proctodeum).
* **Arterial:** Inferior rectal artery (Internal pudendal branch).
* **Venous:** Systemic system (Inferior rectal vein → Internal pudendal vein → IVC).
* **Lymphatics:** Superficial inguinal nodes (腹股溝淋巴結).
* **Nerve:** Somatic via pudendal nerve (exquisitely painful).

### Pathophysiology of Anal Squamous Cell Carcinoma
* **HPV Pathogenesis:** High-risk HPV 16 & 18 (人類乳頭瘤病毒) → viral DNA integrates into host genome → E6 & E7 oncoproteins produced.
* **Molecular Mechanism:** E6 inactivates **p53** tumor suppressor; E7 inactivates **Rb** (Retinoblastoma) protein.
* **Disease Progression:** HPV infection → Anal Intraepithelial Neoplasia (AIN) / High-grade Squamous Intraepithelial Lesion (HSIL) → Invasive SCC.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Anal Cancer
* **Bleeding PR (直腸出血):** Most common presenting symptom (often dismissed as hemorrhoids).
* **Perianal Pain (肛門痛):** Especially with defecation, implies sphincter or somatic nerve invasion (below dentate line).
* **Perianal Mass (肛門腫塊):** Patient may notice a growing, non-resolving lump.
* **Pruritus Ani (肛門瘙癢):** Secondary to discharge or direct skin involvement.
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Tenesmus (裏急後重), change in bowel habits, fecal incontinence (大便失禁) (sphincter destruction).

### Key Clinical Signs of Anal Cancer
* **Visual Inspection:** Exophytic, ulcerated, or nodular lesion at the anal verge. Hard, rolled, everted edges.
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) (直腸指檢):** Indurated stricture or palpable mass; assess fixity to sphincter/prostate/vagina.
* **Inguinal Lymphadenopathy:** Palpable, hard, fixed superficial inguinal nodes (腹股溝淋巴結腫大).
* **Vaginal Exam:** Rule out rectovaginal fistula or posterior vaginal wall invasion.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Anal Mass/Pain
| Condition | Discriminatory Clinical Features | Diagnostic Clue |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Anal Cancer (肛門癌)** | Indurated edge, chronic progressive pain, inguinal nodes, HIV/HPV risk. | Biopsy +ve for SCC. |
| **Hemorrhoids (痔瘡)** | Soft, compressible, painless (unless thrombosed). No induration. | Proctoscopy: engorged venous cushions. |
| **Anal Fissure (肛裂)** | Sharp tearing pain during defecation, spasms. Usually midline (posterior > anterior). | Sentinel pile, visible tear. (Lateral/chronic fissure demands biopsy to r/o SCC). |
| **Perianal Abscess / Fistula (肛瘻)** | Acute throbbing pain, fever, fluctuant swelling, purulent discharge. | Pus on aspiration; internal opening on MRI/EUA. |
| **Rectal Adenocarcinoma (直腸癌)** | Usually higher up, mucinous discharge, less perianal pain (unless invading downwards). | Biopsy +ve for Adenocarcinoma. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Bedside Investigations and Initial Workup for Anal Cancer
* **Examination Under Anesthesia (EUA) (麻醉下檢查):** Essential for accurate assessment of size, sphincter involvement, and adequate deep incisional/punch biopsy.
* **Proctoscopy / Rigid Sigmoidoscopy:** To assess proximal extent of tumor.
* **Cervical / Vulvar Screening:** Women require Pap smear/colposcopy due to field effect of HPV.

### Laboratory Workup for Anal Cancer
* **Viral Serology:** HIV test (mandatory), Hepatitis B/C status.
* **Immune Status:** CD4 count & Viral load (if HIV +ve) to optimize HAART before chemoradiation.
* **Routine Labs:** CBC, U&Es, LFTs (r/o distant organ dysfunction).
* **Tumor Markers:** SCC Ag (Squamous Cell Carcinoma Antigen) - sometimes monitored locally, though not strictly standard in all HA guidelines.

### Radiological Staging for Anal Cancer
* **MRI Pelvis (盆腔磁力共振):** Gold standard for **Local (T & N) Staging**. Assesses sphincter invasion, mesorectal/internal iliac/inguinal node involvement.
* **CT Thorax, Abdomen, Pelvis (CT CAP):** Gold standard for **Distant (M) Staging** (Liver, Lung mets).
* **PET-CT (正電子發射掃描):** Highly sensitive for nodal staging and detecting occult distant metastases; increasingly used in HA oncology protocols for definitive radiotherapy planning.

### TNM Staging System (8th Ed) for Anal Carcinoma
| Stage | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **T1** | Tumor ≤ 2 cm. |
| **T2** | Tumor > 2 cm but ≤ 5 cm. |
| **T3** | Tumor > 5 cm. |
| **T4** | Tumor invades adjacent organs (e.g., vagina, urethra, bladder). *Note: Sphincter invasion alone is NOT T4.* |
| **N1a** | Metastasis in inguinal, mesorectal, or internal iliac lymph nodes. |
| **N1b** | Metastasis in external iliac lymph nodes. |
| **N1c** | Metastasis in external iliac AND (inguinal, mesorectal, or internal iliac) nodes. |
| **M1** | Distant metastasis. |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Primary Management Algorithm for Anal SCC (Nigro Protocol)
* **Gold Standard 1st Line:** Definitive Chemoradiotherapy (CRT) (化學放射治療) via the **Nigro Protocol**.
* **Radiotherapy:** External Beam Radiotherapy (EBRT) to anal canal + prophylactic irradiation to bilateral inguinal and pelvic nodes (45-50.4 Gy).
* **Concurrent Chemotherapy:** **5-Fluorouracil (5-FU)** (or Capecitabine) + **Mitomycin C (MMC)**.
* **Efficacy:** Achieves clinical complete response (cCR) in 80-90% of patients; sphincter-preserving.

### Management of Clinically Positive Inguinal Nodes
* **Standard Approach:** Treat definitively with high-dose Radiotherapy (included in radiation field).
* **Surgical Avoidance:** Primary block dissection is NOT recommended due to high morbidity (lymphedema) and efficacy of CRT.

### Surgical Indications in Anal Cancer
* **Small Anal Margin Tumors (T1N0, well-differentiated):** Wide Local Excision (WLE) with 1 cm margins (must not compromise sphincter function).
* **Persistent Disease:** Failure to achieve cCR at 3-6 months post-CRT (confirmed by biopsy).
* **Recurrent Disease:** Local recurrence after initial cCR post-CRT.
* **Palliative:** Intractable pain, bleeding, or major fecal incontinence destroying quality of life (requires diversion stoma or palliative resection).

### Management Algorithm for Anal Adenocarcinoma
* **Protocol:** Treated strictly following rectal cancer protocols (Neoadjuvant long-course CRT followed by surgical resection: LAR or APR).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Operative Principles of Salvage Abdominoperineal Resection (APR)
* **Indication:** Salvage surgery for recurrent/persistent anal SCC.
* **Positioning:** Lloyd-Davies position (lithotomy).
* **Abdominal Phase:** High ligation of IMA, total mesorectal excision (TME) down to pelvic floor.
* **Perineal Phase:** Wide/cylindrical excision of anus, sphincters, and ischioanal fat. 
* **Reconstruction:** Extralevator APR (ELAPE) leaves a massive pelvic floor defect → requires plastic surgery closure via **Vertical Rectus Abdominis Myocutaneous (VRAM) Flap** (腹直肌皮瓣) or Gluteal flap to prevent perineal hernia.
* **Stoma Formation:** Permanent end colostomy (結腸造口) in the Left Iliac Fossa (LIF).

### Consent Issues and Risks for Salvage APR
* **Irreversible Anatomical Change:** Permanent stoma formation (requires stoma nurse counseling).
* **Sexual Dysfunction:** Damage to nervi erigentes (parasympathetic) → erectile dysfunction, vaginal dryness/dyspareunia.
* **Urinary Dysfunction:** Bladder atony (requires prolonged catheterization).
* **Perineal Wound Complications:** High risk (up to 30-50%) of delayed healing, dehiscence, or flap necrosis, worsened by prior high-dose pelvic radiotherapy.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care and ERAS Protocols
* **Mobilization:** Strictly no prolonged sitting for 2-4 weeks to protect perineal flap (nursed flat or on side).
* **DVT Prophylaxis:** LMWH + TED stockings (high pelvic surgery = ↑ VTE risk).
* **Flap Monitoring:** Frequent Doppler checks of VRAM flap viability (color, capillary refill, temperature).
* **Drain Management:** Pelvic and perineal drains left in situ until output is minimal (<30ml/day) to prevent seroma/abscess.

### Long-term Rehabilitation and Stoma Care
* **Stoma Therapy:** Education by Stoma Nurse (造口護士) for appliance management and parastomal skin care.
* **Psychological Support:** Adjustment to altered body image and psychosexual counseling.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications of Radical Pelvic Surgery (APR)
* **Hemorrhage:** Presacral venous plexus bleeding (notoriously difficult to control; may require thumbtacks/muscle packing).
* **Flap Ischemia/Necrosis:** Venous congestion or arterial insufficiency of VRAM flap.
* **Pelvic Sepsis:** Abscess formation in the empty pelvis.
* **Ureteral Injury:** Intraoperative damage requiring stenting/repair.

### Late Complications of Chemoradiotherapy (Nigro Protocol Toxicity)
* **Radiation Proctitis/Dermatitis:** Chronic pain, bleeding, mucosal atrophy, perianal skin excoriation.
* **Vaginal Stenosis:** Requires routine use of vaginal dilators post-RT.
* **Anal Stricture:** Fibrosis leading to obstructed defecation.
* **Chemotherapy Toxicity:** Bone marrow suppression (neutropenia) from Mitomycin C.

### Prognosis of Anal Cancer
* **Early Stage (I-II):** Excellent; 5-year OS >80-90% with Nigro protocol.
* **Node-Positive (Stage III):** 5-year OS ~60%.
* **Salvage APR:** 5-year OS ~40-50% for patients requiring surgical salvage.
* **Surveillance:** Clinical exam + DRE + anoscopy every 3-6 months for 5 years; annual CT CAP.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the primary lymphatic drainage for a tumor located below the dentate line? Answer: Superficial inguinal lymph nodes.
* Viva Question: What is the Nigro Protocol? Answer: The standard 1st line definitive chemoradiotherapy for Anal SCC, consisting of EBRT (45-50.4 Gy) concurrent with 5-Fluorouracil and Mitomycin C.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with an indurated, non-healing lateral anal fissure simply needs conservative management or a sphincterotomy. Pearl: An atypical, lateral, or indurated fissure must always be biopsied under anesthesia to rule out an underlying squamous cell carcinoma.
* Trap: Recommending a block dissection for clinically palpable inguinal nodes at initial presentation of Anal SCC. Pearl: Inguinal node metastases are treated with definitive radiotherapy included in the initial RT field; surgery is rarely indicated upfront.
* Pitfall: Planning immediate salvage surgery (APR) for a residual mass found 4 weeks post-chemoradiotherapy. Tumor regression can be slow; clinical complete response (cCR) assessment should be delayed until 3 to 6 months post-treatment before declaring CRT failure and proceeding to salvage surgery.
