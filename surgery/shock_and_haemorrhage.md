# Shock and Haemorrhage (休克與出血)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Shock (休克定義)
* Inadequate systemic tissue perfusion → ↓ cellular oxygen delivery (DO2) relative to demand
* → Anaerobic metabolism → ↑ **Lactic acid (乳酸)** accumulation → cellular death
* **Hypotension (血壓低)** is a late sign; shock can exist with normal BP

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Trauma (創傷)**: Leading cause of haemorrhagic shock in young adults (RTA, falls from height) managed at major HA trauma centres (e.g., PWH, QEH, QMH)
* **Non-traumatic haemorrhage**: Upper GI bleeding (variceal/peptic ulcer) highly prevalent in HK elderly/cirrhotic populations
* **Septic shock**: Common in HA surgical wards 2° to biliary sepsis or perforated viscus

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Cellular Pathophysiology of Shock
* ↓ O2 delivery → failure of oxidative phosphorylation
* ↓ ATP production → failure of Na+/K+ pump → cellular swelling & lysis
* Anaerobic metabolism → ↑ **Lactate (乳酸)** → metabolic acidosis
* Acidosis → myocardial depression & diminished response to catecholamines

### Macroscopic Compensatory Mechanisms
* **Sympathetic NS activation**: ↑ Catecholamines → peripheral vasoconstriction (maintains central BP) + **Tachycardia (心跳過速)**
* **RAAS activation**: ↓ Renal perfusion → ↑ Renin → ↑ Angiotensin II & Aldosterone → Na+/H2O retention
* **ADH release**: Posterior pituitary → ↑ H2O reabsorption

### ATLS Haemorrhagic Shock Classification
| Parameter | Class I | Class II | Class III | Class IV |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blood Loss (mL)** | < 750 | 750 - 1500 | 1500 - 2000 | > 2000 |
| **Blood Loss (%)** | < 15% | 15 - 30% | 31 - 40% | > 40% |
| **Heart Rate (bpm)** | < 100 | > 100 | > 120 | > 140 |
| **Blood Pressure** | Normal | Normal | ↓ (Hypotension) | ↓↓ |
| **Pulse Pressure** | Normal / ↑ | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ |
| **Resp Rate** | 14 - 20 | 20 - 30 | 30 - 40 | > 35 |
| **Urine Output (mL/h)**| > 30 | 20 - 30 | 5 - 15 | Negligible |
| **Mental Status** | Mildly anxious | Anxious | Confused | Lethargic / Unconscious |
| **Fluid Resuscitation**| Blood not req | Blood may be req | Blood strongly req | Massive transfusion protocol |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Symptoms & Signs of Shock
* **Tachycardia (心跳過速)** (First measurable sign)
* Narrowed pulse pressure
* Delayed capillary refill (> 2 secs)
* **Tachypnoea (呼吸急促)**
* Cool, clammy skin (peripheral vasoconstriction)
* Mild anxiety / agitation

### Late Symptoms & Signs of Shock
* **Hypotension (血壓低)** (Indicates decompensation, >30% blood volume lost)
* **Oliguria (少尿)** (< 0.5 mL/kg/hr)
* Altered mental status / Coma (昏迷)
* Kussmaul breathing (compensatory for severe metabolic acidosis)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Types of Shock Differential
| Shock Type | Pathophysiology | Key Discriminatory Features / Signs | Haemodynamic Profile |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hypovolaemic** | Loss of circulating volume | Flat neck veins, bleeding source, trauma | ↓ CVP, ↓ CO, ↑ SVR |
| **Cardiogenic** | Pump failure | ↑ JVP, crepitations, gallop rhythm, ↑ trop | ↑ CVP, ↓↓ CO, ↑ SVR |
| **Distributive** (Sepsis, Anaphylaxis, Neurogenic) | Vasodilation / loss of vascular tone | Warm extremities (early), bounding pulse, fever/rash | ↓ CVP, ↑ CO (early), ↓↓ SVR |
| **Obstructive** | Extracardiac flow obstruction | Muffled heart sounds (tamponade), absent breath sounds (tension PTX) | ↑ CVP, ↓ CO, ↑ SVR |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Primary Resuscitation Investigations (ATLS Adjuncts)
* **ABG/VBG**: Assess pH, base excess (BE), and **Lactate (乳酸)** (prognostic indicator)
* **Bedside FAST scan (重點超聲波)**: Check for pericardial, perihepatic, perisplenic, pelvic free fluid
* **eFAST**: Assesses for pneumothorax/haemothorax
* Trauma series X-rays: CXR, Pelvis XR (AP)

### Laboratory Investigations for Shock Workup
* **Type & Screen / Crossmatch**: Urgent (O-negative uncrossmatched blood if unstable)
* **CBC**: Baseline Hb (may be normal initially due to haemoconcentration), ↓ Platelets
* **Coagulation profile**: PT, APTT, Fibrinogen (check for TIC - Trauma-Induced Coagulopathy)
* **Renal function (RFT)**: ↑ Urea/Cr (pre-renal AKI), ↓ K+ (early) or ↑ K+ (late/crush injury)
* **LFT / Amylase**: Baseline end-organ assessment

### Advanced Radiological Investigations
* **CT Angiogram (CTA)**: Gold standard if patient is hemodynamically stable or responsive to initial fluids
* Identifies active arterial blush / extravasation (requires embolization or surgery)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Initial Resuscitation Algorithm (ATLS)
* **C**: Control massive external bleeding (direct pressure, tourniquet, pelvic binder)
* **A**: Airway maintenance + cervical spine restriction
* **B**: Breathing (High flow O2, decompress tension PTX)
* **C**: Circulation (2x large bore 14G/16G IV access)
* **D**: Disability (GCS, pupils)
* **E**: Exposure & Environment (prevent hypothermia)

### HA Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP) (大量輸血方案)
* **Trigger**: Persistent shock, active bleeding, estimated loss > 4 units in 4 hours
* **Target Ratio**: 1:1:1 (PRBC : FFP : Platelets) to prevent dilutional coagulopathy
* **Adjuncts**: 
  * **Tranexamic acid (TXA)**: 1g loading over 10 mins, then 1g infusion over 8 hrs (MUST give within 3 hrs of injury)
  * IV Calcium Gluconate (citrate in PRBC chelates Ca2+ → hypocalcaemia)

### Surgical Indications in Haemorrhage
* Persistent haemodynamic instability despite resuscitation (non-responder)
* +ve FAST with hypotension (Immediate laparotomy)
* Massive haemothorax (> 1500mL initially or > 200mL/hr for 2-4 hrs)
* Pelvic fracture with ongoing shock despite binder (+ve blush on CT → Angioembolization)

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Damage Control Surgery (DCS) Principles (損害控制手術)
* **Goal**: Survive the physiological insult, not anatomical perfection
* **Phase 1 (Theatre)**: 
  * Rapid arrest of haemorrhage (packing, vessel ligation, shunting)
  * Control of contamination (staple off bowel ends, no anastomosis)
  * Temporary abdominal closure (VAC dressing / Bogota bag)
* **Phase 2 (ICU)**: Physiological restoration (warmth, coagulation, perfusion)
* **Phase 3 (Theatre)**: Planned re-exploration (48-72 hrs later) for definitive repair and anastomosis

### Emergency Consent Issues
* Life-saving procedures in HA often proceed under **two-doctor signature** if patient is incapacitated and next-of-kin unavailable
* Must document necessity to save life / prevent permanent disability

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate ICU Post-operative Care
* Invasive monitoring: Arterial line, Central Venous Catheter (CVC), Foley catheter
* Goal-directed therapy: MAP > 65 mmHg, Urine Output > 0.5 mL/kg/hr
* Serial monitoring of Lactate clearance and Base Excess improvement

### Correction of the "Lethal Triad" (死亡三角)
* **Hypothermia (低溫症)**: Bair hugger, warm IV fluids/blood, warm room
* **Acidosis (酸中毒)**: Optimize perfusion, judicious ventilation
* **Coagulopathy (凝血障礙)**: MTP, targeted factor replacement (Cryoprecipitate for Fibrinogen < 1.5 g/L), correct hypocalcaemia

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications of Shock
* **Abdominal Compartment Syndrome (ACS)**: 2° to massive fluid resuscitation and bowel oedema (requires decompressive laparotomy)
* **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)**: Pre-renal failure progressing to Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN)
* **TRALI / TACO**: Transfusion-related acute lung injury / circulatory overload

### Intermediate to Late Complications & Prognosis
* **ARDS (急性呼吸窘迫綜合症)**: 2° to systemic inflammatory response (SIRS)
* **MODS (多器官功能衰竭)**: Multi-organ dysfunction syndrome
* **DIC (彌散性血管內凝血)**: Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation
* Prognosis strictly correlates with duration and depth of shock (serial lactate is the best predictor of mortality)

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the earliest clinical sign of hypovolaemic shock? Answer: Tachycardia, not hypotension.
* Viva Question: What is the fluid of choice for initial resuscitation in a bleeding trauma patient? Answer: Warmed blood products (1:1:1), limiting crystalloids to avoid dilutional coagulopathy and worsening the lethal triad.
* Trap: Relying on a normal hemoglobin level to rule out acute major hemorrhage. Pearl: Initial Hb reflects baseline; it takes hours for interstitial fluid shifts to cause a drop in Hb (haemodilution). 
* Trap: Giving large volumes of normal saline for hemorrhagic shock. Pearl: Normal saline causes hyperchloraemic metabolic acidosis, exacerbating the lethal triad. Use blood products.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to administer calcium during massive transfusion. Citrate toxicity from transfused blood chelates serum calcium, leading to severe myocardial depression and coagulopathy.
* Pitfall: Delaying surgery to get a CT scan in a hemodynamically unstable patient with a positive FAST. Unstable patients with free intra-abdominal fluid must go straight to the operating theatre for laparotomy.
