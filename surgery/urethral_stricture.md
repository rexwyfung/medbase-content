# Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Urethral Stricture
* **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)**: Abnormal narrowing of urethral lumen due to cicatricial cicatrization/scarring of urethral epithelium and surrounding **corpus spongiosum (尿道海綿體)** (spongiofibrosis).
* Primary pathology involves fibrotic tissue replacing normal vascular spaces of **corpus spongiosum**, causing luminal constriction and fixed **Bladder Outlet Obstruction (BOO, 膀胱出口梗阻)**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Dominant demographic: Elderly males presenting to **Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) urology specialist outpatient clinics (SOPD)**, secondary to previous **Transurethral Resection of the Prostate (TURP, 經尿道前列腺切除術)** or prolonged instrumentation.
* Younger cohort: Associated with straddle trauma, pelvic fractures from road traffic accidents, or **Balanitis Xerotica Obliterans (BXO, 閉塞性乾燥性龜頭炎)**.
* Female strictures: Rare (<5% of cases), typically localized to distal urethra/meatus, secondary to recurrent **Urinary Tract Infections (UTI, 尿路感染)** or pelvic radiation.


## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Urethral Anatomical Zones
* **Anterior Urethra (前尿道)**: Comprises **meatus (尿道口)**, **fossa navicularis (舟狀窩)**, **pendulous/penile urethra (陰莖尿道)**, and **bulbar urethra (球尿道)**; entirely surrounded by **corpus spongiosum**.
* **Posterior Urethra (後尿道)**: Comprises **membranous urethra (膜部尿道)** (traverses external sphincter) and **prostatic urethra (前列腺尿道)**; lacks **corpus spongiosum**.

### Pathophysiology of Anterior Strictures
* Urethral epithelial injury → extravasation of urine into surrounding tissues → intense inflammatory response.
* Fibroblast proliferation and collagen deposition in **corpus spongiosum** → **spongiofibrosis (海綿體纖維化)**.
* Contraction of scar tissue → progressive narrowing of urethral lumen → ↑ upstream hydrodynamic pressure during voiding phase.

### Pathophysiology of Posterior Strictures
* Rarely isolated cicatricial strictures; typically **pelvic fracture urethral distraction defects (PFUDD)**.
* Severe pelvic trauma → shearing force at **prostatomembranous junction** → complete disruption of urethra.
* Gap fills with dense fibroconnective scar tissue → complete obliteration of luminal continuity without true **spongiofibrosis**.


## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Voiding Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms
* **Hesitancy (排尿困難)**: Delayed initiation of micturition due to ↑ resistance at stricture site.
* **Reduced Urinary Stream (尿流細小)**: Fixed narrowing → thin, split, or spraying stream.
* **Straining (用力排尿)**: Abdominal musculature recruitment to overcome fixed proximal obstruction.
* **Intermittency (斷續排尿)**: Interrupted urine flow due to detrusor fatigue against high resistance.

### Storage and Post-micturition Symptoms
* **Frequency (頻尿)** and **Nocturia (夜尿)**: Secondary to incomplete emptying or compensatory detrusor overactivity.
* **Incomplete Emptying (排尿不淨)**: Persistent post-void residual urine volume.
* **Post-micturition Dribble (尿後餘瀝)**: Pooling of urine within dilated segment proximal to stricture, slowly leaking after voiding.

### Physical Signs
* **Palpable Urethral Induration (可觸及的尿道硬結)**: Scar tissue felt along ventral aspect of penile shaft or perineum.
* **Meatal Stenosis / BXO Changes**: Whitish, scarred, depigmented prepuce and narrowed meatal orifice.
* **Palpable Distended Bladder (可觸及的膀胱腫脹)**: Signifies chronic **Urinary Retention (尿瀦留)**.


## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Common Differentials for Male Voiding LUTS
| Differential Diagnosis | Discriminatory History | Physical Exam Findings | Investigative Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)** | History of urethral trauma, instrumentation, or **TURP**. Continuous, fixed poor stream. | Normal prostate size on **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)**; palpable urethral scar. | **Uroflowmetry** shows plateau pattern; **Retrograde Urethrogram (RGU)** shows luminal narrowing. |
| **Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH, 前列腺增生)** | Progressively worsening LUTS in elderly males; fluctuant stream severity. | Enlarged, smooth, non-tender prostate on **DRE**. | **Uroflowmetry** shows parabolic/descending curve; **Transrectal Ultrasound (TRUS)** shows prostate volume >30 mL. |
| **Bladder Neck Contracture (膀胱頸攣縮)** | Occurs post-prostatectomy or radical pelvic surgery. | Normal or absent prostate tissue on **DRE**. | **Cystoscopy** confirms rigid, narrowed bladder neck orifice. |
| **Detrusor Sphincter Dyssynergia (DSD)** | Neurogenic history (e.g., spinal cord injury, multiple sclerosis). | Signs of upper motor neuron lesion (hyperreflexia). | **Urodynamics** demonstrates simultaneous detrusor contraction and external sphincter activation. |


## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup

### First-Line/Screening Non-Invasive Tests
* **Uroflowmetry (尿流率檢查)**: Diagnostic peak flow rate ($Q_{max} < 10\text{ mL/s}$) with characteristic **plateau-shaped curve** indicating fixed mechanical obstruction.
* **Post-Void Residual (PVR) Ultrasound**: Quantifies incomplete emptying; volume $>100\text{ mL}$ warrants close monitoring to prevent renal impairment.
* **Urinalysis & Midstream Specimen of Urine (MSU)**: Rules out active **UTI** prior to any instrumentation; +ve culture requires targeted antibiotic eradication.

### Definitive Diagnostic Imaging
* **Retrograde Urethrogram (RGU, 逆行性尿道造影)**: Radiocontrast instilled via meatus; delineates stricture location, length, and severity in the **anterior urethra**.
* **Voiding Cystourethrogram (VCUG, 排尿期膀胱尿道造影)**: Combined with **RGU** to image proximal patency and evaluate **posterior urethra** during voiding phase.
* **B模式尿道超聲 (MUG)**: High-resolution ultrasound to measure depth and extent of **spongiofibrosis** to guide urethroplasty planning.

### Endoscopic Evaluation
* **Flexible Cystoscopy (彈性膀胱鏡檢查)**: Confirms diagnosis under local anesthesia; directly visualizes distal extent of stricture but cannot bypass tight blocks.


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications

### Acute Urinary Retention Management
* **Suprapubic Cystostomy (SPC, 恥骨上膀胱穿刺造廔術)**: Preferred method in acute retention with dense strictures; avoids forcing urethral catheters which can create a **false passage (假通道)** and worsen scarring.
* **Urethral Catheterization**: Attempted only with soft, low-friction hydrophilic catheters by experienced staff; abandoned immediately if resistance met.

### Minimally Invasive Endoscopic Interventions
* **Direct Visual Internal Urethrotomy (DVIU, 直視下尿道內切開術)**: Cold-knife or laser incision of stricture scar at 12 o'clock position to achieve expansion.
* **Indications for DVIU**: Short ($<1.5\text{ cm}$), single, bulbar urethral strictures with minimal **spongiofibrosis**.
* **Urethral Dilation (尿道擴張術)**: Sequential passage of sounds or balloons; high recurrence rate; strictly temporary palliation.

### Open Reconstruction (Urethroplasty) Indications
* **Indications for Open Surgery**: Stricture length $>1.5-2\text{ cm}$, recurrence after failed **DVIU**, extensive **spongiofibrosis**, or complex posterior distraction defects.


## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Direct Visual Internal Urethrotomy (DVIU) Steps
1. Patient positioned in lithotomy; **rigid cystoscope** advanced under direct vision to face of stricture.
2. Introduce urethrotome blade; perform a single longitudinal incision at **12 o'clock position** to avoid dorsal vessels and corpus cavernosum injury.
3. Advance scope into bladder; irrigate clots; insert 16-18Fr silicone urethral catheter to template lumen.

### Urethroplasty Techniques
* **Excision and Primary Anastomosis (EPA, 切除並吻合術)**: For short ($<2\text{ cm}$), isolated bulbar strictures; complete excision of scar followed by tension-free spatulated anastomosis.
* **Substitution Urethroplasty (替代性尿道成形術)**: For strictures $>2\text{ cm}$; augmentation of urethral lumen using a **Buccal Mucosa Graft (BMG, 口腔黏膜移植物)** harvested from inner cheek.

### Essential Informed Consent Risks
* **Recurrence (復發)**: Up to 50% risk within 2-5 years for long/complex strictures post-DVIU.
* **Erectile Dysfunction (ED, 勃起功能障礙)**: Due to injury to cavernous nerves or branches of common penile artery during deep perineal dissection.
* **Urinary Incontinence (尿失禁)**: Secondary to external sphincter damage, particularly in proximal membranous strictures.
* **Fistula Formation (尿道廔管)**: Breakdown of graft or anastomosis site leading to perineal urinary leakage.


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Management
* **Catheter Care**: Maintain indwelling silicone urethral catheter for **7-14 days** post-DVIU, or **14-21 days** post-urethroplasty to protect graft healing.
* **Perineal Care**: Ice packs to perineum for 48 hours to minimize hematoma; avoid direct pressure on perineal wound.

### Discharge Protocols & Monitoring
* **Antibiotic Prophylaxis**: Short course of oral fluoroquinolone or co-amoxiclav to reduce risk of catheter-associated sepsis based on HA antibiograms.
* **Catheter Removal Protocol**: Perform a **Pericatheter Retrograde Urethrogram (佈膜周圍尿道造影)** at 3 weeks for urethroplasty cases; confirm absence of contrast extravasation before pulling catheter.

### Long-Term Surveillance
* Follow-up at **HA Urology Clinic** at 3, 6, and 12 months with **Uroflowmetry** and **PVR** checks to monitor for asymptomatic early recurrence.


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (0 - 48 Hours)
* **Perineal Hematoma (會陰血腫)**: Bleeding from transected **corpus spongiosum**; managed with compression or surgical re-exploration.
* **Urosepsis (尿路敗血症)**: Bacteria translocation into prostatic venous plexus; requires immediate IV antibiotics and fluid resuscitation.

### Intermediate Complications (48 Hours - 30 Days)
* **Wound Infection / Graft Loss**: Sloughing of **BMG** due to poor vascular bed incorporation or underlying hematoma.
* **Urinary Extravasation (尿液外滲)**: Occurs if catheter is displaced early; leads to perineal cellulitis or **Fournier's Gangrene (福尼爾氏壞疽)**.

### Late Complications (>30 Days) & Prognosis
* **Stricture Recurrence (尿道狹窄復發)**: Highest risk within first 12 months due to scar contracture.
* **Chordee (陰莖彎曲)**: Ventral chordee from extensive scar tissue contraction along the pendulous urethra.
* **Prognosis**: **EPA** and **BMG Urethroplasty** have long-term success rates of 80-90%; DVIU success drops below 20% if repeated for a recurrent stricture.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic flow pattern seen on uroflowmetry for a urethral stricture? Answer: A flat, low-amplitude plateau-shaped curve with an elongated voiding time, contrasting with the rapidly declining parabolic curve of BPH.
* Viva Question: Why is a single incision at the 12 o'clock position strictly maintained during DVIU? Answer: To minimize the risk of serious hemorrhage, as the corpus spongiosum is thinnest dorsally, and to avoid damaging the paired cavernous arteries located laterally.
* Trap: Assuming that a recurrent stricture can be successfully managed with sequential, repeated internal urethrotomies. Pearl: Success drops to near 0% after a second failed DVIU; repeated incisions worsen spongiofibrosis and compromise the vascular bed for subsequent urethroplasty.
* Pitfall: Attempting blind, forceful urethral catheterization with stiff plastic coudé catheters in a patient with suspected traumatic stricture disruption. Forceful insertion creates a false passage, converts a partial disruption into a complete obliteration, and exacerbates pelvic hematoma infection.
