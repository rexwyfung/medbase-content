# Inflammatory Bowel Disease (炎症性腸病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Inflammatory Bowel Disease
* **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (炎症性腸病)**: Chronic, relapsing idiopathic inflammation of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract.
* **Ulcerative Colitis (潰瘍性結腸炎) (UC)**: Confluent mucosal inflammation limited to **colon** & **rectum** (直腸).
* **Crohn's Disease (克隆氏症) (CD)**: Transmural, patchy granulomatous inflammation affecting any part of GI tract (mouth to anus).

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of IBD
* **Incidence**: Rapidly ↑ in Hong Kong & Asia over past 2 decades.
* **Prevalence**: UC historically > CD in HK, but CD incidence catching up.
* **Demographics**: Bimodal age distribution (Peak 1: 15-30 yrs; Peak 2: 60-80 yrs).
* **Genetics**: Polygenic, **NOD2/CARD15** mutation linked to CD (lower prevalence in Chinese vs Caucasians).
* **Smoking**: 
* **UC**: Protective (non-smokers/ex-smokers at ↑ risk).
* **CD**: Detrimental (smokers ↑ risk of strictures/fistulas).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Pathophysiology of Ulcerative Colitis
* **Inflammation depth**: Mucosa & submucosa ONLY.
* **Distribution**: Starts at **rectum** (proctitis), extends proximally in continuous fashion.
* **Pathognomonic features**:
* **Crypt abscesses** (隱窩膿腫)
* **Pseudopolyps** (假性息肉)
* Mucosal friability & superficial ulcers.
* **Cure**: Total **Proctocolectomy** (直腸結腸切除術) is curative.

### Pathophysiology of Crohn's Disease
* **Inflammation depth**: **Transmural** (全層) → leads to fibrotic strictures & fistulas.
* **Distribution**: **Skip lesions** (跳躍性病灶); most common site = **terminal ileum** (末端迴腸) & caecum.
* **Pathognomonic features**:
* **Non-caseating granulomas** (非乾酪性肉芽腫) (microscopic)
* **Cobblestone appearance** (鵝卵石樣外觀) (macroscopic)
* **Creeping fat** (蔓延性脂肪)
* **Deep fissuring ulcers** (rose-thorn ulcers).
* **Cure**: Surgery is NEVER curative; strictly reserved for complications.

### Surgical Anatomy for Restorative Proctocolectomy (IPAA)
* **Blood supply**: Pouch depends on **marginal artery of Drummond** & **superior mesenteric artery** (腸繫膜上動脈).
* **Division**: **Ileocolic artery** (迴結腸動脈) divided to allow ileal loop to reach pelvis without tension.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Intestinal Symptoms of Ulcerative Colitis
* **Bloody diarrhoea** (血便) (hallmark)
* **Tenesmus** (裡急後重) (due to proctitis)
* **Mucus discharge** (黏液排出)
* **Colicky lower abdominal pain** (絞痛) (relieved by defecation)

### Intestinal Symptoms of Crohn's Disease
* **Chronic diarrhea** (usually non-bloody unless colonic involvement)
* **Right iliac fossa (RIF) pain** (右下腹痛) (mimics appendicitis)
* **Weight loss** (體重下降) & malabsorption
* **Perianal disease** (肛周疾病): **Fistula-in-ano** (肛瘻), fissures, abscesses (up to 30% of CD patients).

### Extraintestinal Manifestations (EIMs) of IBD
* **Skin**: **Erythema Nodosum** (結節性紅斑) (mirrors disease activity), **Pyoderma Gangrenosum** (壞疽性膿皮症) (independent of activity).
* **Joints**: Sacroiliitis, ankylosing spondylitis (HLA-B27 +ve).
* **Eyes**: Episcleritis, anterior uveitis.
* **Hepatobiliary**: **Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC)** (原發性硬化性膽管炎) (strongly associated with UC → ↑ risk of cholangiocarcinoma).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Features: Crohn's vs Ulcerative Colitis
| Feature | Crohn's Disease (CD) | Ulcerative Colitis (UC) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Site** | Mouth to anus (Terminal ileum > colon) | Colon & Rectum only |
| **Distribution** | Skip lesions | Continuous, starts at rectum |
| **Depth** | Transmural | Mucosal / Submucosal |
| **Macroscopic** | Cobblestone, strictures, creeping fat | Pseudopolyps, friable mucosa, continuous ulcers |
| **Microscopic** | Non-caseating granulomas | Crypt abscesses, goblet cell depletion |
| **Perianal Disease** | Common (fistulas, fissures) | Rare |
| **Strictures/Fistulas**| Common | Rare |
| **Malignancy Risk** | ↑ (if colonic involvement) | ↑↑ (duration & extent dependent) |

### Infective Differential Diagnoses
* **Infective gastroenteritis**: *Salmonella*, *Shigella*, *Campylobacter*.
* *Clostridioides difficile* colitis (recent antibiotics).
* Intestinal tuberculosis (結核病) (mimics CD; high index of suspicion in HK; check for caseating granulomas).
* Amoebiasis.

### Non-Infective Differential Diagnoses
* Ischaemic colitis (elderly, watershed areas).
* Radiation enteritis (hx of pelvic RT for cervical/prostate CA).
* Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎) (mimics terminal ileitis).
* Colorectal carcinoma (大腸癌).
* Diverticulitis.

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Blood Investigations for IBD Workup
* **CBC**: ↓ Hb (anemia of chronic disease/bleeding), ↑ WBC, ↑ Platelets.
* **RFT/LRFT**: Dehydration, ↓ Albumin (malnutrition/protein-losing enteropathy).
* **Inflammatory markers**: ↑ **CRP**, ↑ **ESR** (correlates with disease activity).
* **Hematinics**: ↓ Iron, ↓ B12, ↓ Folate (esp. in CD terminal ileitis).

### Stool Investigations for IBD Workup
* **Stool cultures & microscopy**: R/o *C. diff*, ova/cysts/parasites.
* **Fecal Calprotectin** (糞便鈣衛蛋白): Sensitive non-invasive marker for intestinal inflammation; used to distinguish IBD from IBS and monitor response.

### Endoscopic Investigations for IBD Workup
* **Oesophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD)**: For CD upper GI symptoms.
* **Colonoscopy (結腸鏡) + Terminal Ileoscopy**: 
* **Gold standard** for diagnosis.
* Multiple biopsies from all segments (even if macroscopically normal) for histological mapping.
* **Contraindicated** in acute severe colitis/toxic megacolon (risk of perforation).
* **Flexible Sigmoidoscopy**: Safe alternative in acute severe flares.

### Radiological Imaging for IBD Workup
* **AXR**: Rule out **Toxic Megacolon** (毒性巨結腸) (transverse colon diameter >6cm), thumbprinting (mucosal edema), pneumoperitoneum.
* **CT Abdomen/Pelvis (with contrast)**: Assess strictures, intra-abdominal abscesses, perforation.
* **MR Enterography (MRE)** (磁力共振腸造影): Radiation-free, optimal for assessing small bowel strictures vs active inflammation in CD.
* **Pelvic MRI**: Gold standard for assessing complex perianal fistulas in CD.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Hospital Authority Medical Step-Up Protocol
* **Mild/Moderate Disease**:
* **5-ASAs** (e.g., Mesalazine): 1st line for UC induction & maintenance.
* **Corticosteroids** (e.g., Prednisolone, IV Hydrocortisone, Budesonide): Acute flares ONLY. Never for maintenance.
* **Severe/Refractory Disease (Immunomodulators)**:
* **Thiopurines** (Azathioprine / 6-MP): Steroid-sparing maintenance. Monitor CBC/LFTs (risk of myelosuppression, hepatotoxicity, pancreatitis).
* **Biologics**: Anti-TNFα (**Infliximab**, Adalimumab). Requires prior TB screening (CXR + latent TB test) due to high local endemicity in HK.

### Surgical Indications for Ulcerative Colitis
* **Emergency**:
* Toxic megacolon unresponsive to medical therapy (24-48 hrs).
* Massive uncontrollable hemorrhage.
* Colonic perforation.
* **Elective**:
* Medical refractoriness (failure of biologics/steroid dependence).
* Biopsy shows high-grade dysplasia or Colorectal CA.
* Intolerable medication side effects.

### Surgical Indications for Crohn's Disease
* **Failure of medical therapy**.
* **Complications (Surgical triad)**:
* **Strictures** → Bowel obstruction.
* **Fistulas** (Enterocutaneous, enterovesical, enterovaginal).
* **Abscesses** / Perforation.
* Severe perianal disease (failure of seton drainage + biologics).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Options for Ulcerative Colitis
* **Emergency**: **Subtotal Colectomy + End Ileostomy** (次全結腸切除術 + 迴腸造口術). Leaves rectal stump; safest in acutely unwell, toxic patients.
* **Elective (Curative)**: **Restorative Proctocolectomy with Ileal Pouch-Anal Anastomosis (IPAA)**.
* Eradicates disease & removes cancer risk.
* Preserves anal sphincter mechanism.
* Created using distal ileum (usually J-pouch).
* Protective loop ileostomy often done temporarily.

### Operative Principles for Crohn's Disease
* **Rule of thumb**: "Bowel conservation is paramount." Prevent Short Bowel Syndrome.
* **Resection**: Limited resection with macroscopically clear margins (microscopic disease at margin does NOT ↑ recurrence risk).
* **Strictureplasty** (狹窄成形術): Used for short fibrotic strictures to widen lumen WITHOUT sacrificing bowel length (e.g., Heineke-Mikulicz for <10cm, Finney for 10-20cm).
* **Perianal CD**: Drainage of abscesses + **Seton insertion** (掛線). Avoid aggressive sphincter division (high risk of permanent incontinence).

### Pre-operative Consent and Stoma Siting
* **Consent**: Risk of bleeding, infection, anastomotic leak, pelvic nerve damage (sexual dysfunction, urinary retention), permanent stoma, Short Bowel Syndrome (in CD).
* **Stoma Siting**: MUST involve Stoma Nurse Pre-op. Mark site away from bony prominences, umbilicus, and skin folds (usually RIF for ileostomy).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### ERAS Protocol for IBD Surgery
* **Pre-op**: Optimization of nutrition (ensure ↑ albumin), anemia correction, pre-habilitation, carbohydrate loading.
* **Intra-op**: Minimally invasive (laparoscopic) approach preferred, avoid routine drains, goal-directed fluid therapy, opioid-sparing analgesia.
* **Post-op**:
* Early mobilization (Day 1).
* Early enteral feeding (Day 1).
* Early catheter removal.
* VTE prophylaxis (LMWH + TED stockings) as IBD is a pro-thrombotic state.

### Stoma Care and Rehabilitation
* Intensive education by HA Stoma Care Nurses.
* Monitor stoma output (High output ileostomy >2L/day → risk of AKI, ↓ K+, ↓ Na+).
* Skin care around stoma (barrier creams).
* Dietary advice: Avoid high-fiber blockages, ensure adequate hydration.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications
* **Hemorrhage**: Intraluminal or intraperitoneal.
* **Anastomotic Leak** (吻合口漏): Presents with tachycardia, fever, localized peritonitis. Requires urgent re-laparotomy.
* **Stoma complications**: Ischaemia/necrosis (venous congestion initially, then arterial failure), retraction.
* **Infection**: Surgical site infection (SSI), intra-abdominal collection.

### Late Complications of Ileoanal Pouch
* **Pouchitis** (儲袋炎) (up to 50%): Inflammation of the ileal pouch. Presents with ↑ stool frequency, urgency, bleeding. Tx: Metronidazole / Ciprofloxacin.
* **Pouch dysfunction / failure**: Leading to permanent end ileostomy.
* **Pelvic sepsis**: From occult leak.
* **Fecundity**: Decreased female fertility due to pelvic adhesions.

### Long-term Prognosis and Surveillance
* **Colorectal Cancer Risk in UC**: Correlates with duration (>8-10 years) and extent (pancolitis).
* **Surveillance Colonoscopy**: HA protocol requires regular colonoscopies + random 4-quadrant biopsies every 10cm to screen for dysplasia.
* **CD Recurrence**: 50% require second surgery within 10 years. Endoscopic recurrence at anastomosis almost universal.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the defining radiological feature of toxic megacolon on AXR? Answer: Transverse colon diameter > 6cm, with loss of haustrations and mucosal thumbprinting.
* Viva Question: Which extraintestinal manifestations of IBD run independently of bowel disease activity? Answer: Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC) and Pyoderma Gangrenosum.
* Viva Question: What is the standard emergency operation for acute severe, medically refractory Ulcerative Colitis? Answer: Subtotal colectomy with end ileostomy.
* Trap: Recommending a restorative proctocolectomy (IPAA) in the emergency setting for toxic megacolon. Pearl: IPAA is contraindicated in emergency settings due to high morbidity; subtotal colectomy is the safe initial step.
* Trap: Assuming Crohn's disease can be cured by radical surgical resection. Pearl: Surgery in CD is palliative for complications (strictures, fistulas) and must be bowel-preserving to prevent Short Bowel Syndrome.
* Pitfall: Performing an urgent colonoscopy in a patient suspected of toxic megacolon. Risk of iatrogenic colonic perforation.
* Pitfall: Prescribing anti-motility agents (e.g., loperamide) to a patient with acute severe colitis. Risk of precipitating toxic megacolon.
* Trap: Insisting on macroscopically and microscopically negative margins when resecting Crohn's disease strictures. Pearl: Only macroscopic clearance is required; microscopic inflammation at margins does not increase anastomotic recurrence rates.
