# Surgical Infections and Surgical Site Infection (外科感染與手術部位感染)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染定義)
* Infection occurring within **30 days** post-op
* If implant/prosthesis placed → within **90 days** post-op
* Requires ≥1 of:
  * Purulent drainage (**膿液**)
  * +ve culture isolated aseptically
  * Pain/tenderness, localized swelling, redness, or heat + surgeon deliberately opens wound (unless culture -ve)
  * Diagnosis of SSI by surgeon

### Classification of SSI (CDC Criteria)
| SSI Category (SSI分類) | Anatomical Depth | Clinical Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Superficial Incisional (淺層)** | Skin & subcutaneous tissue | Cellulitis, superficial wound breakdown |
| **Deep Incisional (深層)** | Deep soft tissues (fascia, muscle) | Fascial dehiscence, deep abscess |
| **Organ/Space (器官/腔隙)** | Any anatomical part opened/manipulated other than incision | Intra-abdominal abscess, anastomotic leak, empyema |

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology & Resistance Patterns
* Major burden in HA hospitals; ↑ length of stay & costs
* High prevalence of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in HK:
  * **MRSA (耐甲氧西林金黃色葡萄球菌)**: Endemic in HA wards
  * **ESBL-producing Enterobacteriaceae**: High rates in GI/urology wards
  * **Carbapenemase-producing Enterobacteriaceae (CPE)**: Rising incidence → strictly requires isolation
  * **VRE (耐萬古黴素腸球菌)**: Monitored strictly in local ICUs

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Pathogenesis of Wound Infection
* SSI occurs when: **Inoculum dose × Virulence / Host Resistance > 10⁵ CFU/g tissue**
* Exception: Presence of foreign body (e.g., mesh, graft) → **10² CFU/g** sufficient to cause SSI
* **Biofilm (生物膜)** formation: Glycocalyx matrix protecting bacteria from host immunity & antibiotics (esp. on implants)

### Common Pathogens by Surgical Site
| Surgical Site | Dominant Flora | Common SSI Pathogens |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clean (Skin/Breast/Hernia)** | Skin flora | *S. aureus*, *Coagulase-negative Staph (CoNS)*, *Strep* spp. |
| **Upper GI / Biliary** | Oral/enteric | *E. coli*, *Klebsiella*, *Enterococcus* |
| **Lower GI / Colorectal** | Dense enteric + anaerobes | *E. coli*, *Bacteroides fragilis*, *Clostridium*, *Enterococcus* |
| **Urological** | Urethral/bladder | *E. coli*, *Proteus*, *Klebsiella*, *Enterococcus* |

### Wound Classification System (Altemeier Classification)
| Class | Description | Expected SSI Rate | Prophylactic Abx |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I. Clean (清潔)** | Uninfected, no inflammation, resp/GI/GU tracts NOT entered. | <2% | Ortho/vasc/implants only |
| **II. Clean-Contaminated (清潔-受污染)** | Resp/GI/GU tracts entered under controlled conditions. No unusual spillage. | <10% | Yes |
| **III. Contaminated (受污染)** | Open/fresh accidental wounds, major break in sterile tech, gross spillage from GI. | ~20% | Yes (Therapeutic) |
| **IV. Dirty/Infected (骯髒/受感染)** | Old traumatic wounds, purulence present, pre-op perforation of viscera. | ~40% | Yes (Therapeutic) |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Local Signs of Superficial & Deep SSI (局部感染表徵)
* **Erythema / Rubor (紅斑)**
* **Calor (局部發熱)**
* **Induration / Tumor (硬結 / 腫脹)**
* **Dolor (劇痛/觸痛)**
* **Purulent Discharge (膿性分泌物)**
* Wound dehiscence (**傷口裂開**)
* Crepitus (**皮下氣腫**) → Suggests necrotizing infection/gas-forming organisms

### Systemic Signs of Surgical Infection
* **Fever (發燒)**: >38°C
* **Tachycardia (心跳過速)**: >90 bpm
* **Tachypnea (呼吸急促)**: >20 breaths/min
* **SIRS / Sepsis (敗血症)**: Hypotension, altered mental status, oliguria

### Timing of Post-Operative Fever Presentation
* **Post-op Day 1-2**: Atelectasis, severe necrotizing soft tissue infection (Strep/Clostridium)
* **Post-op Day 3-5**: UTI, pneumonia, phlebitis
* **Post-op Day 5-7**: **SSI (Superficial/Deep)**, anastomotic leak
* **Post-op Day >7**: DVT/PE, drug fever, deep abscess

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Causes of Post-operative Fever (The 5 Ws)
| The "W" | Condition | Timing | Discriminatory Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wind** | Atelectasis/Pneumonia | POD 1-2 | ↓ breath sounds, basal crepitations, CXR changes |
| **Water** | UTI (尿道炎) | POD 3-5 | Dysuria, cloudy urine, urinary catheter presence |
| **Walking** | DVT / PE (深層靜脈血栓) | POD 5-7 | Calf swelling/tenderness, sudden hypoxia, tachycardia |
| **Wound** | **SSI (手術部位感染)** | POD 5-9 | Wound erythema, purulence, localized pain |
| **Wonder Drugs** | Drug Fever, Blood Tx | Anytime | Diagnosis of exclusion, rash, eosinophilia |

### Differentials for Wound Swelling/Discharge
* **Seroma (血清腫)**: Clear yellowish fluid, no erythema/fever. Common post-mastectomy/hernia.
* **Hematoma (血腫)**: Dark blood, ecchymosis, sudden painful swelling. Risks: poorly controlled BP, coagulopathy.
* **Wound Dehiscence (Non-infectious)**: Technical failure (suture breakage), knot failure, poor tissue quality.
* **Allergic Contact Dermatitis**: Pruritic, blistering erythema strictly mirroring dressing tape distribution.

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Bedside & Laboratory Investigations for Suspected SSI
* **Wound Swab (傷口拭子)**: For Culture & Sensitivity (C&ST). *Crucial: Take deep swab or aspirate pus after cleaning surface to avoid colonizer contamination.*
* **Complete Blood Count (全血細胞計數)**: ↑ WBC (Neutrophilia), left shift.
* **Inflammatory Markers**: ↑ CRP (C反應蛋白), ↑ Procalcitonin (降鈣素原 - highly specific for bacterial sepsis).
* **Blood Cultures (血液培養)**: Take 2 sets (aerobic + anaerobic) *before* Abx if temp >38.5°C or septic shock.

### Imaging Studies for Deep/Organ-Space SSI
* **Bedside Ultrasound (超聲波)**:
  * 1st line for locating superficial/deep subcutaneous fluid collections.
  * Allows real-time image-guided aspiration.
* **CT Scan with IV Contrast (電腦掃描)**:
  * **Gold Standard** for Organ/Space SSI (e.g., intra-abdominal abscess, anastomotic leak).
  * Look for: rim-enhancing fluid collection, extraluminal free air, stranding.
  * Essential for planning percutaneous drainage (PTD).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Immediate Wound Management Algorithm (Superficial/Deep SSI)
* **Step 1:** Remove sutures/staples over the infected area.
* **Step 2:** Open the wound (**打開傷口**) to allow free drainage of pus.
* **Step 3:** Probe wound gently with sterile instrument to break up loculations & assess depth (ensure fascia intact).
* **Step 4:** Pack wound with saline-soaked gauze (Wet-to-Dry dressing) or apply advanced alginate dressing.
* **Step 5:** Allow to heal by **Secondary Intention (二期癒合)**.

### Empiric Antibiotic Therapy according to HA IMPACT Guidelines
* *Note: Routine Abx NOT indicated for minor superficial SSI without surrounding cellulitis/systemic signs (source control is sufficient).*
* **Indications for Systemic Abx:** Cellulitis (>5cm margin), systemic inflammatory response, deep/organ-space infection, immunocompromised.
* **1st Line (Community/Clean wounds):** PO/IV Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin) OR Cloxacillin.
* **1st Line (GI/Contaminated):** IV Amoxicillin-Clavulanate OR IV Cefuroxime + Metronidazole.
* **Suspected MRSA:** IV Vancomycin OR Linezolid (Requires Micro consult in HA).
* **Suspected ESBL:** IV Meropenem OR Ertapenem (Targeted therapy strictly).

### Indications for Surgical Re-intervention (Operative Management)
* Evisceration / Burst Abdomen (**爆裂性腹部創傷**).
* Necrotizing Fasciitis (Requires EMERGENT radical debridement).
* Deep fascial dehiscence.
* Organ/Space abscess inaccessible to radiological Percutaneous Drainage (PTD) or failing PTD.
* Ongoing sepsis despite initial source control.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Source Control Principles
* **Source Control (感染源控制)** is the paramount principle: Drain pus, remove necrotic tissue, control leaks.
* **Debridement (清創手術)**: Excise non-viable tissue until bleeding edges are reached.
* **Copious Irrigation (大量沖洗)**: Normal saline washout (often >3L for intra-abdominal sepsis).
* Remove infected foreign bodies (e.g., infected mesh) if salvage fails.

### Wound Closure Strategies Post-Debridement
* **Never close a grossly infected wound primarily.**
* **Secondary Intention:** Leave open, pack daily.
* **Delayed Primary Closure (延遲一期縫合):** Pack open initially → review in OT at day 3-5 → close if clean and granulating.
* **Vacuum-Assisted Closure (VAC / 負壓傷口治療)**: -125 mmHg continuous pressure. ↑ angiogenesis, ↓ edema, clears exudate. Contraindicated over exposed bowel (fistula risk).

### Prophylactic Antibiotic Principles (Pre-operative)
* **Timing:** Administer within **60 minutes** prior to skin incision (or 120 mins for Vancomycin).
* **Redosing:** Indicated if surgery > 4 hours or massive blood loss (>1.5L).
* **Duration:** Stop within 24 hours post-op (prolonged use ↑ risk of *C. difficile* and AMR without reducing SSI).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Local Wound Care & Advanced Dressings
* **Normal Saline Cleansing:** Avoid harsh antiseptics (H2O2, undiluted Betadine) in granulating wounds as they are cytotoxic to fibroblasts.
* **Alginate/Hydrofiber:** For heavily exuding wounds.
* **Silver-impregnated dressings (銀離子敷料):** Antimicrobial properties for critically colonized wounds.
* Stoma Care Nurse (SCN) referral for complex abdominal wounds / fistulae management.

### Systemic Optimization in ERAS (Enhanced Recovery After Surgery)
* **Glycemic Control:** Strict peri-op BSL control (6-10 mmol/L). Hyperglycemia directly impairs neutrophil phagocytosis.
* **Normothermia:** Warm IV fluids, Bair Hugger. Hypothermia causes vasoconstriction → ↓ tissue oxygenation.
* **High-Protein Nutrition:** Crucial for wound healing (Vitamin C, Zinc, Arginine).
* **Smoking Cessation:** Essential to improve microvascular tissue perfusion.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Systemic Complications (< 1 week)
* **Sepsis & Septic Shock (敗血性休克)**: Multisystem organ failure, ARDS, AKI.
* **Necrotizing Soft Tissue Infection (NSTI)**: Rapid spread along fascial planes.
* Hemorrhage secondary to vessel erosion by infection.

### Late Local Complications (> 1 week to months)
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝)**: SSI is the strongest independent risk factor for incisional hernia due to fascial necrosis.
* **Chronic Sinus/Fistula Tract:** Think foreign body (stitch sinus), retained necrotic tissue, or undiagnosed Crohn's/TB/Malignancy.
* **Hypertrophic Scar / Keloid (蟹足腫)**: Secondary to prolonged inflammation.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: When are prophylactic antibiotics NOT indicated? Answer: Clean elective procedures (Class I) without implant placement, e.g., uncomplicated lipoma excision or thyroidectomy.
* Viva Question: What is the most critical perioperative factor in preventing SSI? Answer: Administering the correct prophylactic antibiotic within 60 minutes before the surgical incision to ensure peak tissue levels during exposure.
* Trap: Prescribing PO antibiotics for a mild stitch abscess or seroma. Pearl: Source control (removing the stitch or opening the wound) is sufficient for superficial wound infections without cellulitis; antibiotics are unnecessary and promote AMR.
* Trap: Swabbing a dry, sloughy wound surface for culture. Pearl: Surface swabs represent colonization. True C&ST requires cleaning the wound and taking a deep tissue swab, tissue biopsy, or aspirating frank pus.
* Pitfall: Primary closure of an Altemeier Class III/IV wound (e.g., perforated appendicitis). This virtually guarantees a severe SSI. Always leave the skin and subcutaneous tissue open for delayed primary closure or secondary healing.
* Pitfall: Blaming fever on Post-op Day 1 on a wound infection. SSI rarely presents before POD 5. Early post-op fever is physiologically related to cytokines (IL-1, IL-6, TNF-alpha) from tissue trauma, atelectasis, or pre-existing infection.
