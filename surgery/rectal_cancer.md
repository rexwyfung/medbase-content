# Rectal Cancer (直腸癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Rectal Cancer
* Malignant epithelial tumor arising from **rectal mucosa** (直腸黏膜).
* Anatomically defined as tumor within **15 cm** from **anal verge** (肛門緣) via rigid sigmoidoscopy.

### Local Epidemiology and Burden in Hong Kong
* **Colorectal cancer** (大腸癌) is the 2nd most common malignancy and 2nd leading cause of cancer death in Hong Kong.
* **Rectal cancer** accounts for ~30-35% of all colorectal cancer cases locally.
* Male-to-female ratio is ~1.5:1; peak incidence at **60-70 years old**.
* Managed via standardized multidisciplinary team (MDT) pathways across **Hong Kong Hospital Authority** (醫院管理局) clusters.


## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy and Landmarks
* Rectum begins at **sacral promontory** (S3 level / rectosigmoid junction) and ends at **anorectal ring** (肛門直腸環).
* **Peritoneal reflection** (腹膜反折): Covers anterior and lateral surfaces of upper 1/3; anterior surface of middle 1/3; lower 1/3 is entirely extraperitoneal.
* **Mesorectum** (直腸系膜): Fatty tissue surrounding rectum containing lymph nodes and vessels; enveloped by **visceral pelvic fascia** (盆腔臟層筋膜).

### Arterial Supply and Venous Drainage
* Upper rectum: **Superior rectal artery** (直腸上動脈) ← branch of **inferior mesenteric artery (IMA)** (下腸繫膜動脈).
* Middle/Lower rectum: **Middle rectal artery** (直腸中動脈) ← branch of **internal iliac artery** (髂內動脈).
* Lower rectum/Anal canal: **Inferior rectal artery** (直腸下動脈) ← branch of **internal pudendal artery** (陰部內動脈).
* Venous drainage: Upper rectum → **inferior mesenteric vein (IMV)** → **portal system** (門靜脈系統) (causes liver metastasis); Middle/Lower rectum → **internal iliac veins** → **systemic circulation** (體循環) (causes lung metastasis).

### Lymphatic Drainage Pathways
* Upper/Middle rectum: Follows **superior rectal vessels** to **IMA lymph nodes** (下腸繫膜動脈淋巴結).
* Lower rectum: Follows **superior rectal vessels** AND laterally along **middle rectal vessels** to **internal iliac lymph nodes** (髂內淋巴結).

### Innervation and Autonomic Nerves
* **Sympathetic nerves**: Derived from **hypogastric plexus** (腹下神經叢) (T12-L2) → controls ejaculation; damage leads to retrograde ejaculation.
* **Parasympathetic nerves**: Derived from **nervi erigentes** (S2-S4) → controls penile/clitoral erection and bladder detrusor function; damage leads to erectile dysfunction and urinary retention.

### Molecular Pathophysiology Pathways
* **Adenoma-carcinoma sequence** (腺瘤-癌序列) (~80%): Driven by **APC gene** mutation/loss → **KRAS** activation → **TP53** loss.
* **Microsatellite Instability (MSI) pathway** (~15%): Defective mismatch repair (**dMMR**) genes (**MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2**); associated with **Lynch syndrome** (林奇綜合症).


## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Storage and Evacuation Symptoms
* **Hematochezia** (便血): Altered blood mixed with stool or passage of fresh blood + clots.
* **Tenesmus** (裏急後重): Sensation of incomplete defecation due to tumor mass mimicking stool in rectal ampulla.
* **Change in bowel habit** (排便習慣改變): ↑ frequency, spurious diarrhea, or worsening constipation.
* Narrowing of stool caliber ("pencil-shaped stools").

### Constitutional and Advanced Disease Symptoms
* Unexplained **weight loss** (體重減輕) and anorexia.
* Lethargy and shortness of breath secondary to **iron deficiency anemia** (缺鐵性貧血).
* Deep pelvic pain: Indicates invasion into sacral plexus or pelvic floor.
* Urinary frequency/urgency: Indicates anterior invasion into bladder/prostate.

### Physical and Digital Rectal Examination Signs
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)** (直腸指檢): Evaluates distance from anal verge, exophytic or ulcerative mass, mobility vs fixation, and sphincter tone.
* Rigid sigmoidoscopy: Identifies exact height of tumor from anal verge and biopsy confirmation.
* General signs: Cachexia, palpable abdominal mass, hepatomegaly (liver metastases), left supraclavicular lymphadenopathy (**Virchow's node**).


## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Common Differentials and Comparison
| Differential Diagnosis | Discriminatory Clinical Features | Diagnostic Investigations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hemorrhoids** (痔瘡) | Painless fresh bright red blood dripping into bowl; not mixed with stool; no tenesmus; soft anal cushions on DRE. | Anoscopy / Flexible Sigmoidoscopy |
| **Ulcerative Colitis** (潰瘍性結腸炎) | Chronic bloody diarrhea + mucus; diffuse colonic involvement; systemic features (+ve p-ANCA). | Colonoscopy + biopsy showing continuous mucosal inflammation / crypt abscesses |
| **Amoebic Colitis / Dysentery** (阿米巴痢疾) | History of travel; offensive bloody diarrhea; "flask-shaped" ulcers. | Stool microscopy for trophozoites / PCR |
| **Solitary Rectal Ulcer Syndrome** (孤立性直腸潰瘍綜合症) | Associated with chronic straining; straining at stool + mucus discharge; ulcer usually anterior. | Biopsy showing fibromuscular obliteration of lamina propria |
| **Diverticular Disease** (結腸憩室病) | Typically affects sigmoid colon; left lower quadrant pain; presentation is usually acute diverticulitis or brisk painless hemorrhage. | CT Abdomen and Pelvis |


## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup

### First-line Diagnostic Investigations
* **Colonoscopy with Biopsy** (結腸鏡檢查及活檢): Gold standard to confirm tissue diagnosis and exclude synchronous colonic lesions (~3-5%).
* **Rigid Sigmoidoscopy**: Essential for precise determination of tumor height relative to anorectal ring for sphincter preservation planning.

### Laboratory Workup and Tumor Markers
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Confirms microcytic hypochromic **iron deficiency anemia**.
* **Renal Function Tests & Electrolytes**: Baseline assessment prior to contrast imaging and major surgery.
* **Liver Function Tests**: Screen for liver metastases (↑ ALP / Gamma-GT / Bilirubin).
* **Carcinoembryonic Antigen (CEA)** (癌胚抗原): Baseline tumor marker; useful for post-operative monitoring and tracking recurrence, NOT for primary screening.

### Local Staging and Locoregional Imaging
* **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) Pelvis**: Gold standard for local staging. Assesses **T-stage**, lymph node involvement, **Extramural Vascular Invasion (EMVI)**, and relationship to the **Circumferential Resection Margin (CRM)** (環周切緣).
* **Endorectal Ultrasound (ERUS)**: Utilized for early superficial lesions (**T1 vs T2**) if local excision (e.g., TEM/TAMIS) is considered.

### Systemic Staging and Metastatic Evaluation
* **CT Chest, Abdomen, and Pelvis (Contrast-enhanced)**: Detects distant metastases (liver, lungs, peritoneal seeding) and evaluates retroperitoneal adenopathy.
* **PET-CT Scan**: Indicated for clarifying equivocal lesions on conventional imaging or evaluating suspected recurrence with rising CEA.

### Standard TNM Staging Classification
| TNM Stage | T (Primary Tumor) | N (Regional Lymph Nodes) | M (Distant Metastasis) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage I** | T1 (lamina propria/submucosa) or T2 (muscularis propria) | N0 (No regional node metastasis) | M0 (No distant metastasis) |
| **Stage II** | T3 (through muscularis propria into subserosa/mesorectum) or T4 (invades visceral peritoneum/organs) | N0 | M0 |
| **Stage III** | Any T | N1 (1-3 nodes) or N2 ($\ge$ 4 nodes) | M0 |
| **Stage IV** | Any T | Any N | M1 (Distant metastasis present) |


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications

### Early-stage Rectal Cancer Management
* **Stage I (T1N0)**: Candidates for local excision if favorable pathology (well-differentiated, no lymphovascular invasion).
* Otherwise, undergo primary radical surgical resection.

### Locally Advanced Rectal Cancer Protocol (Hong Kong Hospital Authority Standards)
* **Indications for Neoadjuvant Therapy**: **Stage II / III (T3-T4, or N+ve)**, or threatened **CRM $\le$ 1 mm** on baseline pelvic MRI.
* **Regimen Options**:
  * **Long-course Chemoradiotherapy (LCCRT)**: 45-50.4 Gy external beam radiation over 5 weeks + concurrent radiosensitizing Capecitabine (口服化療藥), followed by surgery in 8-12 weeks.
  * **Short-course Radiotherapy (SCRT)**: 25 Gy total delivered over 5 consecutive days, followed by immediate surgery (within 1 week) or delayed surgery (4-8 weeks).
  * **Total Neoadjuvant Therapy (TNT)**: Delivery of both systemic chemotherapy and chemoradiotherapy upfront prior to surgery to minimize systemic micrometastases and achieve maximum **Pathological Complete Response (pCR)**.

### Surgical Indications and Fitness Assessment
* Intent: Curative resection for Stage I-III; palliative resection for symptomatic Stage IV disease (obstruction/uncontrolled bleeding).
* Pre-operative optimization: Correction of anemia (hemoglobin Target > 10 g/dL), cardiopulmonary optimization, and nutritional assessment via **Nutritional Risk Screening (NRS-2002)**.


## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Essential Operative Principles
* **Total Mesorectal Excision (TME)** (全直腸系膜切除術): Sharp, meticulous dissection within the avascular "holy plane" between visceral pelvic fascia and parietal pelvic fascia. Ensures intact removal of mesorectum to reduce local recurrence rates from ~30% to <5%.
* **Autonomous Nerve Preservation (ANP)**: Identification and preservation of hypogastric nerves and pelvic splanchnic nerves to safeguard urogenital function.
* **High Ligation of IMA**: Ligation at its origin from aorta to ensure adequate lymphadenectomy (apical lymph node harvest) and maximize conduit mobility.

### Primary Radical Surgical Procedures
* **Anterior Resection (AR) / Low Anterior Resection (LAR)**: For upper and middle rectal tumors. Sphincter-preserving; rectal anastomosis created using circular end-to-end staplers.
* **Ultra-low Anterior Resection + Inter sphincteric Resection (ISR)**: For very low tumors; dissects between internal and external anal sphincters to preserve external sphincter.
* **Abdominoperineal Resection (APR)** (腹陰莖聯合切除術): Indicated if tumor invades external anal sphincter, levator ani, or if sphincter preservation is oncologically impossible. Resulting in a permanent end-colostomy.

### Defunctioning Stoma Indications
* **Loop Ileostomy** (迴腸造口術): Routinely performed during LAR/Ultra-low AR to protect the distal anastomosis from consequences of **anastomotic leak** (吻合口廔) by diverting fecal flow.

### Key Consent Issues and Risks to Disclose
* Permanent end-colostomy risk (specifically for APR, or if LAR cannot be safely completed).
* Defunctioning temporary loop stoma and future reversal surgery risks.
* Autonomic nerve injury leading to **erectile dysfunction** (陽痿) (~30-40%), retrograde ejaculation, and **urinary retention** (尿瀦留).
* Anastomotic leakage risk ($\sim$ 5-11%).
* **Anterior Resection Syndrome (ARS)**: Increased bowel frequency, urgency, and occasional fecal incontinence post-sphincter preservation.


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Protocol
* **Pre-operative**: Mechanical bowel preparation selectively paired with oral antibiotics; carbohydrate loading 2 hours prior to induction; avoid prolonged fasting.
* **Intra-operative**: Avoidance of fluid overload (goal-directed fluid therapy); maintenance of normothermia; utilizing minimally invasive approaches (laparoscopic/robotic).
* **Post-operative**: Early mobilization within 24 hours; early introduction of oral fluids and diet on Post-op Day 1; multimodal non-opioid analgesia to reduce ileus risk; restrictive use of nasogastric tubes and abdominal drains.

### Stoma Care and Rehabilitation
* Early review by dedicated **Stoma and Wound Care Nurse Specialist** (造口專科護士).
* Comprehensive patient education on appliance management, skin care, and dietary adjustments to prevent parastomal dermatitis or dehydration from high-output ileostomy.


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (Within 30 Days)
* **Anastomotic Leakage**: Typically manifests on Post-op Days 5-9 with pelvic pain, fever, peritonitis, purulent/fecaloid drain output, or rising inflammatory markers.
* **Pelvic Abscess / Sepsis**: Secondary to micro-leak or contaminated hematoma; managed via CT-guided percutaneous drainage and systemic antibiotics.
* **Post-operative Ileus** (術後腸麻痺): Delayed return of bowel function; managed conservatively with bowel rest, intravenous fluids, and electrolyte optimization.
* Hemorrhage: Pelvic bleeding from presacral venous plexus or mesenteric pedicle vessels.

### Intermediate Complications
* **Urinary Retention / Neurogenic Bladder**: Due to intra-operative traction or injury to pelvic splanchnic nerves.
* Wound site infection / Perineal wound breakdown (frequent after APR).

### Late Complications
* **Anastomotic Stricture**: Presents with worsening constipation or narrow stools; managed via endoscopic dilatation.
* **Parastomal Hernia** or stoma prolapse.
* **Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO)** (粘連性腸梗阻).
* **Anterior Resection Syndrome (ARS)**: Chronic bowel dysfunction characterized by clustering of bowel movements, urgency, and fecal soilage.

### Prognosis and Post-operative Surveillance
* 5-year survival rates by Stage: Stage I >90%, Stage II 70-80%, Stage III 50-65%, Stage IV <15%.
* **HA Surveillance Protocol**:
  * Clinical review and **CEA** every 3-6 months for the first 3 years, then every 6 months for years 4 and 5.
  * **CT Chest/Abdomen/Pelvis** annually for up to 5 years.
  * Surveillance **Colonoscopy** at 1 year post-op; repeat at 3 years and then every 5 years if normal.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the single most critical landmark to evaluate on a pelvic MRI before planning rectal cancer surgery? Answer: The relationship of the tumor to the Circumferential Resection Margin (CRM), as a threatened CRM ($\le 1\text{ mm}$) mandates neoadjuvant chemoradiotherapy to reduce local recurrence.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically manage a patient on Post-op Day 6 following a low anterior resection who presents with tachycardia, low-grade fever, and new pelvic pain? Answer: Suspect an anastomotic leak; stabilize hemodynamically, check inflammatory markers, order a contrast-enhanced CT of the abdomen and pelvis to assess the anastomosis, and keep the patient nil-by-mouth with broad-spectrum antibiotics.
* Trap: Assuming that a high CEA value is diagnostic for primary rectal cancer screening. Pearl: CEA has low sensitivity and specificity for early-stage screening; its primary role is as a baseline reference value and a highly specific tool for tracking post-operative recurrence.
* Trap: Believing that all low rectal tumors within 5 cm of the anal verge require an abdominoperineal resection (APR). Pearl: If the tumor does not invade the external anal sphincter or levator ani mechanism, an ultra-low anterior resection with intersphincteric resection (ISR) can be safely performed to achieve clear margins and preserve sphincteric function.
* Pitfall: Omitting rigid sigmoidoscopy assessment because a flexible colonoscopy has already identified the rectal tumor. Flexible colonoscopes can loop in the rectum, leading to highly inaccurate measurements of tumor height, which may critically alter the decision between sphincter-preserving surgery and a permanent colostomy.
