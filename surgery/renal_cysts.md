# Renal Cysts (腎囊腫)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Renal Cysts
* **Simple Renal Cysts (單純性腎囊腫)**: Benign, fluid-filled sacs within **Renal Cortex (腎皮質)** or **Medulla (腎髓質)**
* **Complex Renal Cysts (複雜性腎囊腫)**: Cysts with septations, calcifications, solid components, or enhancement → ↑ risk of **Renal Cell Carcinoma (腎細胞癌, RCC)**
* **Polycystic Kidney Disease (多囊腎病)**: Genetic disorder characterized by progressive formation of multiple bilateral cysts

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Renal Cysts
* **Incidence**: Extremely common incidentaloma in Hong Kong **Hospital Authority (HA)** urology and SOPD clinics
* **Age Correlation**: Prevalence ↑ with age (present in >50% of HK population >50 years old)
* **ADPKD (常染色體顯性多囊腎)**: Prevalence ~1:1000 in HK; accounts for ~5-10% of End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) requiring dialysis in HA

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Kidney
* **Location**: Retroperitoneal organs (T12-L3 levels)
* **Coverings**: Surrounded by **Gerota's Fascia (吉氏筋膜)** and perinephric fat
* **Blood Supply**: **Renal Artery (腎動脈)** (branch of abdominal aorta)
* **Venous Drainage**: **Renal Vein (腎靜脈)** → IVC (Left renal vein is longer, crosses over aorta)

### Pathophysiology of Simple Renal Cysts
* **Origin**: Arise from distal convoluted tubules or collecting ducts
* **Mechanism**: Weakening of tubular basement membrane + tubular obstruction → diverticulum formation → detachment from parent tubule → isolated fluid accumulation

### Pathophysiology of ADPKD
* **Genetics**: Mutations in **PKD1** (Chromosome 16, 85%) or **PKD2** (Chromosome 4, 15%)
* **Mechanism**: Dysregulation of primary cilia in tubular epithelial cells → abnormal intracellular Ca2+ signaling → ↑ cAMP → cellular proliferation & fluid secretion

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Simple Renal Cysts
* **Usually Asymptomatic (無症狀)**: Incidental finding on imaging
* **Loin Pain (腰痛)**: Due to capsular stretch from massive size
* **Macroscopic Hematuria (肉眼血尿)**: If cyst ruptures into collecting system
* **Fever/Chills (發燒/發冷)**: If infected

### Symptoms of Complex Cysts or ADPKD
* **Constitutional Symptoms**: Weight loss, anorexia, night sweats (suggests malignant transformation to RCC)
* **ADPKD Systemic Features**: Subarachnoid hemorrhage (berry aneurysms), early-onset hypertension

### Clinical Signs of Renal Cysts
* **Palpable Mass (可觸及腫塊)**: Ballottable flank mass (especially in ADPKD)
* **Hypertension (高血壓)**: RAAS activation from localized renal ischemia
* **Renal Angle Tenderness**: +ve in infected cysts or hemorrhage

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Renal Masses
| Condition | Key Discriminatory Features | Imaging Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Cyst** | Asymptomatic, normal BP, normal renal function | Thin wall, anechoic, no enhancement |
| **Complex Cyst/RCC** | Hematuria, weight loss, flank pain | Thick septations, solid nodules, +ve contrast enhancement |
| **ADPKD** | Family Hx, early HTN, bilateral palpable kidneys | Innumerable bilateral cysts, enlarged kidneys |
| **Renal Abscess** | High fever, severe flank pain, sepsis, ↑ WBC | Thick irregular wall, surrounding fat stranding |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Laboratory Investigations for Renal Cysts
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: Check Cr, Urea, eGFR (Baseline function, ADPKD progression)
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: ↑ WBC in infected cyst; Polycythemia in RCC (paraneoplastic)
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析) & Culture**: Microscopic hematuria, rule out UTI
* **Coagulation Profile**: PT/APTT/INR (Essential pre-op workup)

### Radiological Investigations for Renal Cysts
* **USG KUB (泌尿系統超聲波)**: 1st line investigation. Confirms cystic nature (anechoic, posterior acoustic enhancement)
* **Triphasic CT Abdomen/Pelvis (三相電腦掃描)**: **Gold Standard** for characterizing complex cysts. Non-contrast, corticomedullary, and nephrographic phases needed to assess **Enhancement (顯影)** (>15-20 Hounsfield Units)

### Bosniak Classification System Table
| Grade | Description | Malignancy Risk | HK HA Management Protocol |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I** | Simple cyst: hairline thin wall, no septa/calcification/enhancement | ~0% | Reassure & Discharge |
| **II** | Few hairline septa, fine calcification, high-attenuation <3cm | ~0% | Reassure & Discharge |
| **IIF** | Multiple hairline septa, minimal thickening, thick calcification | ~5% | Surveillance (USG/CT at 6, 12, 24 mos) |
| **III** | Thick/irregular walls or septa with measurable enhancement | ~50% | Surgical Excision |
| **IV** | Grade III features + enhancing soft-tissue nodular components | ~100% | Surgical Excision (Treat as RCC) |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management in HA Clinics
* **Bosniak I & II**: Absolute reassurance. Discharge from urology SOPD
* **Bosniak IIF**: Enter HA surveillance protocol. Patient counseling on importance of follow-up

### Medical Management of ADPKD
* **Strict BP Control**: Target <130/80 mmHg (ACE inhibitors / ARBs preferred)
* **Tolvaptan (Vasopressin V2 receptor antagonist)**: Slows cyst growth/renal decline. Subject to strict HA nephrology prescribing criteria (monitor LFTs for hepatotoxicity)

### Surgical Indications for Renal Cysts
* **Suspected Malignancy**: Bosniak III and IV cysts
* **Symptomatic Simple Cysts**: Intractable pain, obstructive uropathy (causing hydronephrosis), unremitting hypertension (compression of renal parenchyma)
* **Infected Cysts**: Refractory to IV antibiotics, requires percutaneous drainage or deroofing

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Principles of Laparoscopic Cyst Deroofing (腹腔鏡囊腫去頂術)
* **Indication**: Large symptomatic benign cysts
* **Procedure**: Excision of the extra-parenchymal dome of the cyst
* **Key Step**: Must send excised cyst wall for histopathology to rule out occult malignancy
* **Key Step**: Cauterize or apply argon plasma coagulation to cyst edges to prevent bleeding/recurrence

### Principles of Partial / Radical Nephrectomy (部分/根治性腎切除術)
* **Indication**: Bosniak III / IV (Treat as localized RCC)
* **Partial Nephrectomy**: Nephron-sparing surgery. Clamping of renal hilum (warm ischemia time ideally <25 mins)
* **Radical Nephrectomy**: For large Grade IV cysts or non-favorable anatomy. Includes removal of Gerota's fascia

### Informed Consent for Renal Cyst Surgery
* **General Risks**: Bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding structures (bowel, spleen, liver)
* **Specific Risks**: Urine leak (urinoma), recurrence of cyst
* **Nephrectomy Risks**: Need for renal replacement therapy (dialysis) if contralateral kidney fails

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* **Vitals Monitoring**: Q1H BP/Pulse to detect concealed retroperitoneal hemorrhage
* **Urine Output**: Strict Hourly I/O charting (>30ml/hr). Monitor for gross hematuria
* **Analgesia**: Multi-modal (Paracetamol, NSAIDs if normal RFT, judicious opioids)

### Post-operative Drain & Catheter Management
* **Retroperitoneal Drain**: Monitor volume and nature (serous, blood, or urine)
* **Fluid Creatinine Check**: If high output, check drain fluid creatinine vs serum creatinine to rule out **Urine Leak (尿漏)**
* **Foley Catheter**: Usually removed Post-Op Day 1 or 2 to encourage mobilization

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications
* **Hemorrhage**: From renal parenchyma or slipped hilar pedicle clip (requires urgent re-exploration)
* **Urinoma**: Secondary to collecting system injury during partial nephrectomy/deroofing
* **Paralytic Ileus**: Due to retroperitoneal hematoma or intra-abdominal handling

### Late Surgical Complications & Prognosis
* **Cyst Recurrence**: Higher risk if inadequate deroofing performed
* **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)**: Progressive decline post-nephrectomy
* **Prognosis**: Excellent for simple cysts. For Bosniak III/IV, depends on final TNM staging of RCC

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the defining radiological feature that upgrades a cyst to Bosniak III or IV on CT? Answer: The presence of measurable contrast enhancement in the septa, walls, or solid components.
* Trap: Assuming a hyperdense cyst on non-contrast CT is malignant. Pearl: A high-attenuation cyst (>20 HU) that is homogeneous and non-enhancing is a benign hemorrhagic or proteinaceous cyst (Bosniak II) and requires no surgery.
* Pitfall: Recommending percutaneous aspiration for a suspected malignant complex cyst. Doing so risks needle-tract seeding and cyst rupture. Surgery is the definitive diagnostic and therapeutic step.
* Viva Question: What is the most critical intra-operative time metric during a partial nephrectomy? Answer: Warm Ischemia Time (WIT). It should be kept strictly under 25-30 minutes to prevent irreversible acute tubular necrosis.
* Trap: Diagnosing a young patient with ADPKD based purely on a single renal cyst. Pearl: A single cyst in a patient <30 years old requires thorough evaluation and genetic history, as simple cysts are exceedingly rare in this age group.
