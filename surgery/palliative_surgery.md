# Palliative Care for Surgical Oncology (外科腫瘤學紓緩治療)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Surgical Palliative Care (外科紓緩治療定義)
* **Goal**: Relief of physical/psychological suffering, ↑ **Quality of Life (QoL) (生活質素)**
* **Surgical Role**: Strict symptom control rather than oncological cure
* **Key Targets**:
  * Relief of obstruction
  * Control of malignant bleeding
  * Palliation of intractable pain

### Local Epidemiology of End-Stage Cancers in Hong Kong (香港晚期癌症流行病學)
* High-burden malignancies requiring surgical palliation in HK:
  * **Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌)**
  * **Lung Cancer (肺癌)**
  * **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) (肝癌)**
  * **Breast Cancer (乳癌)**
* **Hospital Authority (HA)** Context: Multidisciplinary management via **Palliative Care Consultative Team (PCCT) (紓緩治療會診團隊)**
* Integration with local hospice networks:
  * **Bradbury Hospice (白普理寧養中心)**
  * **Haven of Hope Hospital (靈實醫院)**

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Pathophysiology of Malignant Bowel Obstruction (惡性腸梗阻病理生理學)
* Extrinsic compression via **Peritoneal Carcinomatosis (腹膜轉移)**
* Intrinsic luminal occlusion via primary tumor growth
* Pathological cascade:
  * ↑ Intraluminal pressure
  * → Venous/lymphatic congestion
  * → Bowel wall ischemia
  * → ↑ Risk of perforation/sepsis

### Pathophysiology of Malignant Biliary Obstruction (惡性膽道梗阻病理生理學)
* Tumor compression at biliary tree:
  * **Cholangiocarcinoma (膽管癌)**
  * **Carcinoma of Pancreas (胰臟癌)**
* Pathological cascade:
  * Biliary stasis
  * → ↑ Serum Bilirubin
  * → Pruritus & Malabsorption
  * → ↑ Risk of **Ascending Cholangitis (急性化膿性膽管炎)**

### Cancer Cachexia Syndrome (癌症惡病質綜合症)
* Pro-inflammatory cytokine-driven metabolic state:
  * ↑ **Tumor Necrosis Factor-alpha (TNF-α) (腫瘤壞死因子-α)**
  * ↑ **Interleukin-6 (IL-6) (白細胞介素-6)**
* Clinical consequences:
  * Severe muscle wasting
  * Refractory anorexia
  * ↑ Surgical morbidity/mortality

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Advanced Surgical Oncology Patients (晚期外科腫瘤病徵)
* **Intractable Pain (頑固性痛楚)**:
  * **Somatic Pain (軀體痛)**
  * **Visceral Pain (內臟痛)**
  * **Neuropathic Pain (神經痛)**
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**
* **Nausea & Vomiting (噁心及嘔吐)**
* **Severe Constipation (嚴重便秘)**

### Clinical Signs of End-Stage Malignancy (晚期惡性腫瘤體徵)
* **Malignant Ascites (惡性腹水)**:
  * +ve Shifting dullness
  * +ve Fluid thrill
* **Malignant Pleural Effusion (惡性胸腔積液)**:
  * ↓ Breath sounds
  * +ve Stony dullness on percussion
* **Cachexia (惡病質)**:
  * Temporal wasting
  * Sunken facies
  * Palpable lymphadenopathy (e.g., Virchow's node)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentials for Bowel Obstruction in Cancer Patients (癌症患者腸梗阻之鑑別診斷)

| Etiology (病因) | Discriminatory Features (鑑別特徵) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Malignant Bowel Obstruction (惡性腸梗阻)** | Gradual onset, palpable mass, multi-level strictures on imaging. |
| **Adhesions (腸黏連)** | History of prior abdominal surgery, clear solitary transition point. |
| **Radiation Enteritis (放射性腸炎)** | History of pelvic/abdominal RT, diffuse thickened bowel wall. |
| **Opioid-Induced Constipation (鴉片類藥物引致之便秘)** | History of high-dose opioids, extensive fecal loading, no clear transition point. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Laboratory Investigations for Surgical Palliation (外科紓緩治療之血液檢驗)
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**:
  * ↓ Hb (Bleeding/Anemia of chronic disease)
  * ↑ WBC (Concurrent infection)
* **Renal Function Tests (RFT)**:
  * ↑ Urea/Creatinine (Dehydration)
* **Liver Function Tests (LFT)**:
  * ↑↑ Bilirubin (Biliary obstruction)
  * ↑ ALP/GGT (Hepatic metastases)
  * ↓ Albumin (Severe malnutrition)

### Imaging Modalities for Palliative Surgical Planning (紓緩外科手術之影像學檢查)
* **CT Abdomen/Pelvis w/ Contrast (腹部及盆腔電腦掃描)**:
  * Identifies exact transition points
  * Assesses extent of **Carcinomatosis (腹膜轉移)**
  * Evaluates ascites volume
* **Chest X-Ray (CXR) / Ultrasound (USG)**:
  * Confirms effusions
  * Guides percutaneous drainage

### Pre-operative Prognostic Assessment (術前預後評估)
* **ECOG Performance Status (ECOG 體能狀況評分)**:
  * ECOG 0-2: May tolerate palliative resection/diversion
  * ECOG 3-4: Strict non-operative/minimally invasive palliation indicated
* Assessment of life expectancy via multidisciplinary tumor board

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Indications for Palliative Surgical Intervention (紓緩外科手術之指徵)
* Impending or actual viscus perforation
* Massive tumor hemorrhage unresponsive to embolization/RT
* Refractory mechanical obstruction failing non-operative therapy

### Medical Management of Malignant Bowel Obstruction in HA (醫管局惡性腸梗阻之藥物治療)
* **Octreotide (奧曲肽)**:
  * ↓ GI secretions
* **Dexamethasone (地塞米松)**:
  * ↓ Peritumoral edema
  * Acts as anti-emetic
* **Hyoscine Butylbromide (Buscopan) (丁基東莨菪鹼)**:
  * Anti-spasmodic for colicky pain
* **Haloperidol (氟哌啶醇)**:
  * Centrally acting anti-emetic

### Endoscopic & Interventional Radiology Palliation (內視鏡及介入放射紓緩治療)
* **Self-Expanding Metal Stents (SEMS) (自膨脹金屬支架)**:
  * Indications: Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO), Colorectal obstruction, Esophageal strictures
* **ERCP + Biliary Stenting (內視鏡逆行胰膽管造影及支架置入)**:
  * Indication: Malignant obstructive jaundice
* **Percutaneous Transhepatic Cholangiography (PTCD) (經皮穿肝膽道造影及引流)**:
  * Indication: Failed ERCP for biliary obstruction

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles in Palliative Surgery (紓緩外科之手術原則)
* **Minimal Morbidity > Curative Resection**:
  * Avoid complex multivisceral resections
  * Avoid high-risk primary anastomoses
* **Stoma Formation (造口術)**:
  * Loop colostomy or ileostomy
  * Safest method for malignant distal obstruction
* **Damage Control (損害控制)**:
  * Ligation/packing for bleeding
  * Source control for sepsis

### Informed Consent and Advanced Care Planning (知情同意及預設照顧計劃)
* Explicitly state surgery is **NOT** curative
* Establish ceiling of care pre-operatively
* Initiate **Advance Directive (AD) (預設醫療指示)** discussions
* Implement **DNACPR (不作心肺復甦術)** protocols per HA guidelines

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Palliative Care (術後即時紓緩護理)
* **Pain Control (痛楚控制)**:
  * Patient-Controlled Analgesia (PCA) initially
  * Rapid transition to transdermal **Fentanyl patch (芬太尼貼片)** or oral opiates
* **Fluid Management**:
  * Careful hydration
  * Avoid fluid overload (minimizes recurrent ascites/effusions)

### Rehabilitation and Nursing Support (復康及護理支援)
* **Stoma Care (造口護理)**:
  * Stoma nurse input for appliance fitting
  * Patient/family education for independence
* **Dietitian Input (營養師支援)**:
  * Clear fluids → small frequent meals
  * High-calorie nutritional supplements

### Community and Hospice Transition (社區及寧養過渡)
* Direct referral to **Community Nursing Services (CNS) (社康護理服務)**
* Fast-track transfer to HA Palliative Care wards for end-of-life (EOL) symptom management
* Psychological support for patient and family

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Palliative Surgical Complications (早期紓緩手術併發症)
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口漏)**:
  * ↑↑ Risk due to cachexia/prior RT
* **Wound Dehiscence (傷口裂開)**:
  * Poor tissue healing
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染)**

### Late Palliative Surgical Complications (後期紓緩手術併發症)
* **Stoma Complications (造口併發症)**:
  * Parastomal hernia
  * Prolapse
  * Retraction
* **Re-obstruction (再次梗阻)**:
  * Disease progression
  * New stricture formation

### Prognosis and Survival Outcomes (預後及存活率)
* Overall survival strictly limited (weeks to months)
* Interventions prioritized strictly on immediate symptom relief rate
* Quality of EOL phase heavily dependent on successful community palliative integration

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the primary surgical strategy for a completely obstructing, unresectable rectosigmoid tumor in a cachectic patient? Answer: Formation of a diverting loop colostomy without tumor resection.
* Trap: Prescribing prokinetics (e.g., Metoclopramide) for bowel obstruction. Pearl: Metoclopramide is contraindicated in complete mechanical obstruction as it ↑ risk of perforation; use Haloperidol or Cyclizine instead.
* Pitfall: Performing a primary anastomosis after bowel resection in a patient with severe carcinomatosis and hypoalbuminemia. This risks catastrophic anastomotic leak; an end stoma with a mucous fistula or Hartmann's procedure is always safer in palliative settings.
* Viva Question: Which palliative intervention is preferred for malignant Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO) in a patient with an ECOG score of 3? Answer: Endoscopic insertion of a Self-Expanding Metal Stent (SEMS) rather than surgical gastrojejunostomy.
* Trap: Continuing routine screening labs and aggressive vitals monitoring in the terminal EOL phase post-surgery. Pearl: Cease all non-essential investigations; focus strictly on comfort care and symptom palliation pathways.
