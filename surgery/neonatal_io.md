# Neonatal Intestinal Obstruction (新生兒腸梗阻)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Neonatal Intestinal Obstruction
* **Neonatal Intestinal Obstruction (新生兒腸梗阻)**: Mechanical or functional blockage of GI tract occurring within first 28 days of life.
* Characterized by cardinal triad: **Bilious vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)**, **Abdominal distension (腹部脹大)**, ↓/absent **Meconium (胎糞)**.

### Local Epidemiology and Hospital Authority (HA) Incidence
* Incidence: ~1 in 2000 live births in HK.
* Centralized care model: Complex cases transferred to **Hong Kong Children's Hospital (HKCH)** (香港兒童醫院) or regional HA tertiary pediatric surgery units (QMH/PWH).
* Leading local etiologies: **Duodenal Atresia (十二指腸閉鎖)**, **Hirschsprung Disease (巨結腸症)**, **Anorectal Malformations (肛直腸畸形)**.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Anatomical Classification (High vs Low Obstruction)
* **High Obstruction (高位梗阻)**: Proximal to **Ligament of Treitz (屈氏韌帶)** (Duodenum, proximal jejunum).
* **Low Obstruction (低位梗阻)**: Distal to Ligament of Treitz (Distal ileum, colon, rectum).

### Embryological Pathophysiology by Etiology
* **Duodenal Atresia**: Failure of GI tract recanalization at 8-10 weeks gestation.
* **Jejunoileal Atresia (空迴腸閉鎖)**: Intrauterine mesenteric vascular accident → ischemic necrosis → resorption of bowel segment.
* **Malrotation with Midgut Volvulus (腸旋轉不良伴中腸扭轉)**: Incomplete 270° counterclockwise midgut rotation → narrow mesenteric base → prone to torsion → rapid midgut ischemia.
* **Hirschsprung Disease**: Failure of neural crest cell (神經嵴細胞) migration to distal hindgut → aganglionic segment → functional obstruction.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Antenatal Presentation
* **Polyhydramnios (羊水過多)** (High obstructions prevent fetal swallowing/absorption of amniotic fluid).
* Dilated fetal bowel loops on antenatal USG.
* Echogenic bowel.

### Cardinal Postnatal Symptoms
* **Bilious vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)** (Absolute surgical red flag).
* **Failure to pass meconium (胎糞排出延遲)** (>24-48 hrs post-birth).
* Refusal to feed / Poor suckling.
* Excessive crying / Irritability.

### Key Physical Examination Signs
* **Abdominal distension (腹部脹大)** (Marked in low obstruction).
* Scaphoid abdomen (舟狀腹) (Proximal obstruction e.g., severe duodenal atresia).
* Visible peristalsis across abdominal wall.
* Hemodynamic instability / Shock (Tachycardia, ↓ capillary refill) → high suspicion for bowel necrosis/perforation.
* Tight anal sphincter + "Blast sign" (explosive stool release) on DRE (Hirschsprung's).
* Absent or ectopic anus on perineal exam.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Discriminatory Features of Common Obstructions
| Condition | Level | Pathognomonic Sign / Clue | Associated Syndromes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Duodenal Atresia (十二指腸閉鎖)** | High | **Double bubble sign (雙氣泡徵)** | Trisomy 21 (唐氏綜合症) (~30%) |
| **Midgut Volvulus (中腸扭轉)** | High | Sudden shock, bloody stool, "Corkscrew" sign | Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia |
| **Jejunoileal Atresia (空迴腸閉鎖)** | Low | Multiple air-fluid levels, "Triple bubble" | Cystic Fibrosis (Rare in HK) |
| **Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸梗阻)** | Low | Neuhauser's sign (Soap bubble appearance) | Cystic Fibrosis |
| **Hirschsprung Disease (巨結腸症)** | Low | "Blast sign" on PR exam, Transition zone | MEN 2A, Down Syndrome |
| **Anorectal Malformation (肛直腸畸形)** | Low | Absent/ectopic anus on perineal exam | VACTERL association |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Immediate Bedside Workup & Labs
* **VBG**: Assess pH, lactate (↑ lactate = late sign of ischemia).
* **U&E**: Assess electrolyte derangement (Hypokalemic hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis typical in high obstruction).
* **CBP**: Leukocytosis or neutropenia (signs of sepsis).
* **Group & Screen**: Crucial prep for emergent OT.

### Gold Standard First-Line Imaging
* **Erect & Supine AXR (腹部X光)**
* Screen for pneumoperitoneum (氣腹) (Rigler's sign, football sign).
* Characterize bowel gas: Double bubble vs diffuse dilatation.

### Confirmatory Contrast Imaging Studies
* **Upper GI Contrast Study (上消化道造影)**
* Indication: Rule out Malrotation/Volvulus (Emergent).
* Finding: Ligament of Treitz on right side, "Corkscrew" duodenum.
* **Contrast Enema (灌腸造影)**
* Indication: Evaluate low obstruction.
* Finding: Microcolon (Ileal atresia/Meconium ileus), Transition zone (Hirschsprung's).
* **Bedside USG Abdomen**
* Finding: "Whirlpool sign" (SMA/SMV inversion in Volvulus).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Initial Resuscitation & Optimization (HA NICU Protocol)
* STRICT NPO (禁食).
* Insert **Orogastric Tube (OGT/胃管)** (Replogle tube 10Fr) for continuous decompression.
* Aggressive IV Fluid resuscitation (0.9% NaCl bolus 10-20ml/kg if shocked).
* Maintenance IVF (10% Dextrose + electrolytes).
* Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics (Ampicillin + Cefotaxime + Metronidazole per HA antibiogram).

### Definitive Surgical Indications & Timing
* **Emergent OT (<6 hrs)**: Suspected Volvulus, Perforation/Pneumoperitoneum, Closed-loop obstruction.
* **Urgent OT (24-48 hrs)**: Atresias (once metabolically optimized), Imperforate anus (stoma creation).
* **Elective OT**: Hirschsprung's (after initial washouts/leveling colostomy).

### Medical & Conservative Management Options
* **Meconium Ileus**: Hyperosmolar Gastrografin enema (diagnostic & therapeutic) under fluoroscopy.
* **Hirschsprung's**: Daily warm saline rectal washouts (直腸灌洗) to decompress until definitive pull-through.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Standard Operative Approaches by Etiology
* **Midgut Volvulus**: **Ladd's Procedure (拉德氏手術)**
* Derotation (counterclockwise).
* Division of Ladd's bands.
* Widening of mesenteric base.
* **Prophylactic Appendicectomy (預防性闌尾切除術)**.
* Placement of small bowel on right, large bowel on left.
* **Duodenal Atresia**: **Duodenoduodenostomy (十二指腸吻合術)** (Diamond-shaped Kimura anastomosis).
* **Jejunoileal Atresia**: Resection of dilated proximal bulb + primary end-to-end/end-to-oblique anastomosis.

### Pre-operative Counseling & Consent (知情同意)
* Risk of major bowel resection & temporary stoma creation (造口手術).
* Risk of **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)**.
* Need for prolonged TPN & central line access.
* General risks: Bleeding, infection, anastomotic leak, general anesthetic risks in neonates.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate NICU Post-operative Care
* Strict incubator temperature regulation.
* Continued OGT decompression until return of bowel function (↓ output volume, clear/non-bilious color).
* Strict strict I/O and daily weight charting.

### Nutritional Rehabilitation Protocol
* **Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN/全靜脈營養)** via PICC/Central line initially.
* Trophic enteral feeds (微量餵養) initiated once gut activity returns.
* Gradual increment of breast milk or extensively hydrolyzed formula to prevent Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Post-operative Complications
* **Anastomotic leak (吻合口瘺)**.
* Intra-abdominal sepsis / Wound dehiscence.
* Prolonged post-operative ileus.

### Late Complications & Prognosis
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)** → TPN dependence, TPN-associated liver disease.
* Adhesive small bowel obstruction (粘連性腸梗阻).
* Blind loop syndrome / Bacterial overgrowth.
* Anastomotic stricture requiring serial dilatations.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most critical condition to rule out immediately in a neonate with bilious vomiting? Answer: Midgut volvulus secondary to malrotation.
* Trap: Assuming bilious vomiting in a neonate can be managed conservatively as simple "feeding intolerance". Pearl: Bilious vomiting in a neonate is a surgical emergency indicating mechanical obstruction until malrotation/volvulus is actively ruled out by an Upper GI contrast study.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to check the perineum during the initial physical examination. Always perform a thorough perineal exam to rule out imperforate anus and note the exact anatomical position of meconium passage.
* Viva Question: Why must we perform a prophylactic appendicectomy during a Ladd's procedure? Answer: To avoid future diagnostic confusion, as the surgery relocates the cecum to the left upper quadrant, making the classic clinical signs of appendicitis absent.
* Trap: Giving oral contrast for a suspected lower GI obstruction. Pearl: Use a water-soluble contrast enema (e.g., Gastrografin) via the rectum to differentiate between Hirschsprung's disease, meconium ileus, and other distal functional obstructions.
