# Thyroid Cancer (甲狀腺癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Thyroid Malignancy
* **Thyroid Carcinoma (甲狀腺癌)**: Malignant neoplasm arising from thyroid follicular cells (Papillary, Follicular, Anaplastic) or parafollicular C-cells (Medullary).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: Top 5 most common cancer in Hong Kong females (Department of Health/HK Cancer Registry).
* **Gender Ratio**: F:M ≈ 4:1.
* **Incidence Trend**: Steadily ↑ over past decade (due to ↑ detection of microcarcinomas via high-resolution USG).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Thyroid Gland
* **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) (喉返神經)**: Ascends in tracheoesophageal groove, intimately related to **Ligament of Berry** (posterior suspensory ligament) → supplies all intrinsic laryngeal muscles except cricothyroid.
* **External Branch of Superior Laryngeal Nerve (EBSLN) (喉上神經外支)**: Runs close to superior thyroid vessels → supplies cricothyroid muscle (tenses vocal cords).
* **Parathyroid Glands (副甲狀腺)**: Typically 4 glands; blood supply predominantly from **Inferior Thyroid Artery** branches.
* **Venous Drainage**: Superior/Middle → IJV; Inferior → Brachiocephalic vein.

### Pathophysiology of Papillary Thyroid Carcinoma (PTC)
* **Incidence**: Most common (80-85%).
* **Cell of Origin**: Follicular cells.
* **Spread Pattern**: Primarily lymphatic (cervical lymph nodes).
* **Histology**: **Psammoma bodies** (calcifications), **Orphan Annie eye nuclei** (optically clear), intranuclear inclusions/grooves.
* **Genetics**: **BRAF V600E** (associated with aggressive course in HK populations), RET/PTC rearrangements.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/e7ba46b2ed9232c5c186e7c324bba104.png)

### Pathophysiology of Follicular Thyroid Carcinoma (FTC)
* **Incidence**: 10-15%. More common in iodine-deficient areas.
* **Cell of Origin**: Follicular cells.
* **Spread Pattern**: Hematogenous (lungs, bone).
* **Histology**: Must show **capsular or vascular invasion** to distinguish from benign adenoma.
* **Genetics**: RAS mutations, PAX8-PPARγ translocations.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/6ec5e867bdbf21cfdad801f5d601d31e.png)

### Pathophysiology of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC)
* **Incidence**: 5%.
* **Cell of Origin**: Parafollicular C-cells.
* **Markers**: ↑ **Calcitonin (降鈣素)**, ↑ CEA.
* **Histology**: Solid pattern, **amyloid stroma** (Congo red +ve, apple-green birefringence).
* **Genetics**: 20% familial (MEN 2A, MEN 2B, Familial MTC) → **RET proto-oncogene** germline mutation.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/5990ce85af01d04591a696f48d37c86f.png)

### Pathophysiology of Anaplastic Thyroid Carcinoma (ATC)
* **Incidence**: <1%. Occurs in elderly (>65 yrs).
* **Pathology**: Undifferentiated cells, highly aggressive, rapid local invasion (trachea, esophagus).
* **Prognosis**: Dismal (months); largely palliative.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Primary Presenting Symptoms of Thyroid Cancer
* **Neck Mass**: Painless, gradually enlarging anterior neck lump (頸部腫塊).
* **Incidentally found**: Detected on USG or PET-CT for other reasons.

### Symptoms of Local Invasion (Red Flags)
* **Hoarseness (聲音沙啞)** → RLN infiltration.
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)** → Esophageal compression/infiltration.
* **Dyspnea/Stridor (呼吸困難/喘鳴)** → Tracheal compression/invasion.
* **Pain**: Rare, suggests rapid growth (Anaplastic) or bleeding into a nodule.

### Key Physical Signs of Thyroid Malignancy
* **Nodule Characteristics**: Hard, irregular, fixed to surrounding tissues.
* **Mobility**: Moves upwards with swallowing (吞嚥時隨之上下移動) (tethered to pretracheal fascia).
* **Lymphadenopathy**: Palpable cervical lymph nodes (頸淋巴結腫大), especially Level VI (central) and Levels II-IV, V (lateral).
* **Pemberton's Sign (+ve)**: Facial congestion/cyanosis upon raising arms (indicates thoracic inlet obstruction / retrosternal extension).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis for Anterior Neck Mass
| Pathology | Distinguishing Clinical / Histological Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Multinodular Goiter (MNG) (多結節性甲狀腺腫)** | Multiple nodules, soft/firm, functionally usually euthyroid, no capsular invasion. |
| **Follicular Adenoma (濾泡狀腺瘤)** | Benign, encapsulated mass. Indistinguishable from FTC on FNA. Needs histology. |
| **Hashimoto's Thyroiditis (橋本氏甲狀腺炎)** | Diffusely enlarged, rubbery. Anti-TPO Ab +ve. Hypothyroid. ↑ risk of primary thyroid lymphoma. |
| **Thyroid Cyst (甲狀腺囊腫)** | Fluid-filled on USG. Sudden painful enlargement if hemorrhagic. |
| **Thyroglossal Duct Cyst (甲狀舌管囊腫)** | Midline mass, moves upwards with tongue protrusion. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Laboratory Investigations for Thyroid Nodules
* **TFT (TSH, free T4)**: Usually normal (euthyroid) in cancer. Suppressed TSH suggests autonomous nodule (rarely malignant).
* **Tg (Thyroglobulin) (甲狀腺球蛋白)**: NOT for initial diagnosis. Used post-operatively for PTC/FTC surveillance.
* **Serum Calcitonin & CEA**: Checked if MTC is suspected or if family history of MEN2.
* **Serum Calcium/PTH**: Baseline check prior to total thyroidectomy.

### Gold Standard Imaging for Thyroid Nodules
* **USG Neck (頸部超聲波)**: Assesses size, solid/cystic, vascularity, cervical nodes.
* **Malignant USG Features**: Hypoechoic, microcalcifications, ill-defined margins, taller-than-wide shape, ↑ central vascularity.
* **Hong Kong HA Protocol**: Uses TIRADS/U-scoring to dictate need for biopsy.

### Tissue Diagnosis for Thyroid Nodules
* **USG-guided FNA (幼針抽吸生檢)**: 1st line tissue diagnosis.
* **Cytology (Bethesda System)**: Classifies risk of malignancy (I to VI).
* **Limitation**: Cannot distinguish Follicular Adenoma from Follicular Carcinoma (requires histology for invasion).

### Staging & Pre-operative Planning Investigations
* **CT Neck & Thorax**: Indicated if large fixed mass, suspected retrosternal extension, or advanced disease (evaluates tracheal/esophageal invasion and lung mets). HA Note: Iodinated contrast may delay Radioactive Iodine therapy (RAI) by 4-8 weeks.
* **Laryngoscopy (喉內窺鏡)**: Mandatory pre-op to document baseline vocal cord mobility.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Surgical Indications based on Bethesda Cytology Algorithm
| Bethesda Category | Description | HA Standard Management |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I. Non-diagnostic** | Insufficient cells | Repeat USG-guided FNA. |
| **II. Benign** | Colloid nodule, thyroiditis | Clinical observation, annual USG. |
| **III. AUS/FLUS** | Atypia of undetermined sig. | Repeat FNA in 3-6 months or genetic testing. |
| **IV. Follicular Neoplasm** | Suspicious for FTC | Diagnostic Hemithyroidectomy. |
| **V. Suspicious for Malignancy** | Likely PTC | Total Thyroidectomy or Hemithyroidectomy. |
| **VI. Malignant** | Confirmed carcinoma | Total Thyroidectomy (usually). |

### Management Algorithm for Well-Differentiated Thyroid Cancer (PTC/FTC)
* **Hemithyroidectomy (單側甲狀腺切除術)**: Indicated for low-risk, unifocal tumor < 4cm, no extrathyroidal extension, no clinical lymph node mets (cN0).
* **Total Thyroidectomy (全甲狀腺切除術)**: Indicated for tumor > 4cm, gross extrathyroidal extension (T4), bilateral nodules, clinically +ve nodes (cN1), distant mets (M1), or prior radiation exposure.
* **Prophylactic Central Neck Dissection (Level VI)**: Highly debated; frequently done in HA for T3/T4 tumors or clinically aggressive PTC.
* **Therapeutic Modified Radical Neck Dissection (MRND)**: For biopsy-proven lateral neck nodes (Levels II-V).

### Adjuvant Radioactive Iodine (RAI) Therapy (放射性碘治療) Protocol
* **Goal**: Ablate remnant normal thyroid tissue & treat microscopic/macroscopic metastasis.
* **Timing**: Post-Total Thyroidectomy.
* **Preparation**: Patient must be hypothyroid (withdraw T4 for 4 weeks) OR receive rhTSH (Thyrogen) to ↑ TSH > 30 mU/L to stimulate iodine uptake. Start low-iodine diet 2 weeks prior.
* **Indications**: High-risk features (gross invasion, distant mets, nodal mets >3cm).

### Post-Operative Endocrine Management Algorithm
* **Thyroid Hormone Suppression**: Lifelong **Levothyroxine (左旋甲狀腺素)** replacement.
* **Target TSH**:
* **High-risk disease**: Suppress TSH to < 0.1 mU/L.
* **Low/Intermediate-risk**: Suppress TSH to 0.1 - 0.5 mU/L.
* **Surveillance**: Serial serum Thyroglobulin (Tg) + Anti-Tg Ab, and USG Neck every 6-12 months.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Operative Steps for Thyroidectomy
* **Position**: Rose position (neck extended with sandbag under shoulders).
* **Incision**: Kocher's collar incision (科赫爾氏切口) in lower neck skin crease.
* **Flap Creation**: Subplatysmal flaps raised superiorly to thyroid cartilage, inferiorly to sternal notch.
* **Midline Opening**: Separate strap muscles (sternohyoid, sternothyroid) vertically along linea alba cervicalis.
* **Ligation of Superior Pole**: Ligate superior thyroid vessels strictly **close to the gland** (↓ risk to EBSLN).
* **Ligation of Inferior Pole**: Ligate branches of inferior thyroid artery **on the thyroid capsule** (preserve blood supply to parathyroids).
* **RLN Identification**: Mandatory identification in tracheoesophageal groove; trace upwards to insertion under cricopharyngeus at Berry's ligament.

### Specific Consent Issues for Thyroid Surgery
* **Permanent Hoarseness (聲音沙啞)**: Due to RLN injury (1-2%).
* **Voice Fatigue / Pitch Loss**: Due to EBSLN injury (difficulty hitting high notes).
* **Hypoparathyroidism / Hypocalcemia (低血鈣症)**: Transient (20-30%), Permanent (1-2%). Requires lifelong Calcium/Vitamin D.
* **Life-threatening Hematoma**: Compresses trachea, requiring emergency bedside opening.
* **General Surgical Risks**: Wound infection, ugly scar/keloid (疤痕增生), GA risks.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Airway Monitoring
* **Airway Watch**: Strict observation for stridor, respiratory distress, and expanding neck swelling.
* **Emergency Tray**: Keep suture removal kit and tracheostomy tray at bedside for 1st 24 hours.

### Post-operative Calcium Monitoring Algorithm
* **Monitoring**: Check serum Calcium and PO4 on Post-Op Day 1 (or PTH at 4 hours post-op).
* **Clinical Assessment**: Check for perioral numbness, tingling, **Chvostek’s sign** (facial twitching on tapping facial nerve), **Trousseau’s sign** (carpopedal spasm with BP cuff).
* **Intervention for Hypocalcemia**:
* **Mild/Asymptomatic**: PO Calcium Carbonate + PO Alfacalcidol (Vitamin D analogue).
* **Severe/Symptomatic/ECG changes (prolonged QT)**: IV 10% Calcium Gluconate slowly via central line or large bore IV.

### Long-term Rehabilitation & Follow-up
* **Medication Compliance**: Ensure patient understands lifelong need for Thyroxine on empty stomach.
* **Voice Rehabilitation**: Speech therapy referral if temporary RLN neuropraxia.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (< 24 hours)
* **Tension Hematoma (血腫)**: Bleeding deep to strap muscles causing venous engorgement, laryngeal edema, and acute airway obstruction.
* **Bilateral RLN Palsy**: Vocal cords paralyzed in paramedian position → acute stridor immediately post-extubation. Needs re-intubation or urgent tracheostomy.

### Intermediate Surgical Complications (Days to Weeks)
* **Transient Hypocalcemia**: Resolves as stunned parathyroids recover vascularity.
* **Wound Infection (傷口感染)**: Rare due to excellent blood supply of neck.
* **Seroma**: Fluid collection, usually self-limiting.

### Late Surgical Complications (Months to Years)
* **Permanent Hypoparathyroidism**: Requires lifelong high-dose Calcium and Vitamin D.
* **Permanent Unilateral RLN Palsy**: Compensated by contralateral cord crossing midline; may need vocal cord medialization (injection laryngoplasty).
* **Hypertrophic Scar / Keloid**: Treat with silicone gel/patches or steroid injections.

### Prognosis and Survival Rates
* **PTC/FTC**: Excellent prognosis. 10-year survival > 90-95%.
* **Age Factor**: Age > 55 (AJCC 8th Ed) represents a sharp drop in prognosis for differentiated thyroid cancer.
* **ATC**: Near 100% mortality within 6-12 months.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: How do you definitely differentiate a follicular adenoma from a follicular carcinoma? Answer: Only by histology demonstrating capsular or vascular invasion; FNA cytology cannot differentiate the two.
* Trap: Relying on FNA to diagnose follicular carcinoma. Pearl: FNA only shows follicular cells; you must perform a diagnostic hemithyroidectomy to assess for capsular/vascular invasion on permanent tissue section.
* Pitfall: Waiting for an anesthesiologist if a patient develops severe stridor and an expanding neck hematoma post-thyroidectomy. Cut the skin and deep fascial sutures to open the wound immediately on the ward bed to relieve airway compression before transferring to OT.
* Viva Question: What is the most critical anatomical consideration when ligating the superior and inferior thyroid vessels to prevent nerve and gland injury? Answer: Ligate superior vessels close to the upper pole to protect the EBSLN, and ligate inferior vessels on the thyroid capsule to preserve the blood supply to the parathyroid glands.
* Trap: Assuming a midline anterior neck mass moving with swallowing is always a thyroid nodule. Pearl: A thyroglossal duct cyst also moves with swallowing, but uniquely, it also moves upwards with tongue protrusion.
* Viva Question: Which histological variant of thyroid cancer is associated with amyloid stroma and which serum marker should you check? Answer: Medullary thyroid carcinoma; you must check serum calcitonin and CEA.
