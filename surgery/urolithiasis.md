# Urolithiasis (尿石症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Urolithiasis
* **Urolithiasis (尿石症)**: Solid crystalline masses (結石) forming within the urinary tract (泌尿道) leading to obstruction, pain, and potential infection.

### Hong Kong Local Epidemiology
* Lifetime risk ~10% in HK population.
* Peak onset: 30-50 years old.
* Gender: M:F = 2-3:1.
* ↑ incidence in summer months (HK hot/humid climate → dehydration → highly concentrated urine).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Surgical Anatomy of Ureteric Constrictions
* **Pelvi-ureteric junction (PUJ) (腎盂輸尿管交界處)**: Renal pelvis transitions into ureter.
* **Pelvic brim (骨盆緣)**: Ureter crosses anterior to common iliac vessels.
* **Vesico-ureteric junction (VUJ) (膀胱輸尿管交界處)**: Narrowest physiological segment traversing bladder wall.

### Pathophysiology of Stone Formation
* Supersaturation of urinary solutes → nucleation → crystal growth → aggregation.
* Promoters: ↑ Ca, ↑ oxalate (草酸), ↑ urate (尿酸), ↓ urinary volume.
* Inhibitors: ↓ citrate (枸櫞酸鹽), ↓ magnesium, ↓ nephrocalcin.

### Stone Composition & Characteristics
| Stone Type (結石類型) | Frequency | Radiopacity | Pathophysiology & Causes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Calcium Oxalate (草酸鈣)** | 75-80% | Opaque | ↑ Ca in urine, hyperparathyroidism, dehydration, high dietary oxalate. |
| **Calcium Phosphate (磷酸鈣)** | 5-10% | Opaque | Renal tubular acidosis, ↑ Ca, alkaline urine. |
| **Struvite / Triple Phosphate (鳥糞石)** | 10% | Moderately Opaque | Staghorn calculi. Urea-splitting bacteria (Proteus, Klebsiella) → ↑ pH. |
| **Uric Acid (尿酸)** | 5-10% | Lucent | Gout, high purine diet, persistent acidic urine (pH <5.5). |
| **Cystine (胱氨酸)** | <1% | Semi-opaque | Autosomal recessive defect in basic amino acid transport (COLA). |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Classical Symptoms of Urolithiasis
* **Renal colic (腎絞痛)**: Sudden onset, agonizing, intermittent spasmodic pain.
* Pain radiation: Loin-to-groin / ipsilateral testicle or labia.
* **Macroscopic / Microscopic Hematuria (血尿)**.
* Nausea & vomiting (↑ vagal tone sharing splanchnic innervation).
* LUTS: Frequency, dysuria, urgency (if stone impacted at VUJ).

### Clinical Signs on Examination
* Patient restless, writhing constantly to find a comfortable position.
* Tachycardia, pallor, diaphoresis.
* +ve **Loin tenderness (腰部壓痛)**.
* +ve **Renal angle percussion pain (腎區叩擊痛)**.
* Abdomen usually soft, non-peritonitic (distinguishes from acute abdomen).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Discriminatory Features for Urolithiasis Differentials
| Differential Diagnosis (鑑別診斷) | Discriminatory Features (鑑別特徵) |
|---|---|
| **Ruptured AAA (腹主動脈瘤破裂)** | Elderly, pulsatile mass, hypotensive shock, back pain. **CRITICAL TO EXCLUDE**. |
| **Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎)** | Pain shifts periumbilical → RIF, peritonism, immobile patient (worsened by movement). |
| **Pyelonephritis (腎盂腎炎)** | High fever, rigors, pyuria, continuous dull ache rather than colicky pain. |
| **Biliary Colic (膽絞痛)** | RUQ pain radiating to right shoulder/scapula, provoked by fatty meals. |
| **Ectopic Pregnancy (宮外孕)** | Female of childbearing age, missed menses, +ve pregnancy test, shock. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Baseline Laboratory Investigations
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: Dipstick (+ve blood in 90%), check pH, nitrites/leukocytes to rule out UTI.
* **MSU (中段尿)**: Microscopy, Culture & Sensitivity (M/C/S) to exclude co-existing infection.
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: Cr, Urea (assess baseline renal function, rule out AKI).
* **Bone Profile**: Ca, PO4, ALP, Albumin (rule out primary hyperparathyroidism).
* **Serum Urate**: Rule out gout/hyperuricemia.
* **CBC**: ↑ WCC suggests super-added infection.

### Gold Standard Imaging
* **Non-contrast CT KUB (平掃電腦掃描泌尿系統)**: >99% sensitivity/specificity. Determines stone size, location, degree of **Hydronephrosis (腎積水)**, and perinephric fat stranding.

### Adjunct Imaging Modalities
* **KUB X-ray (腹部平片)**: Useful for tracking radiopaque stones (Ca oxalate/phosphate) post-diagnosis.
* **USG KUB (超聲波)**: 1st line for pregnant women & children. Detects hydronephrosis well but poor at visualizing mid-ureteric stones.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Acute Symptomatic Management
* **Analgesia**: NSAIDs are 1st line in HK HA (e.g., IM Diclofenac 75mg or PR Indomethacin 50mg) → ↓ ureteric prostaglandin synthesis → ↓ spasm. Avoid if AKI.
* IV fluids if severe vomiting prevents oral intake.

### Indications for Emergency Decompression (Urological Emergency)
* Obstructed infected kidney (pyonephrosis).
* Bilateral ureteric obstruction.
* Obstruction in solitary functioning kidney.
* Intractable pain / vomiting failing maximal medical therapy.
* Progressive AKI secondary to post-renal obstruction.

### Emergency Decompression Procedures
* **Retrograde JJ Stent insertion (雙J導管植入術)**.
* **Percutaneous Nephrostomy (PCN) (經皮腎造瘻術)**: Preferred if retrograde fails or patient in florid sepsis.

### Conservative Management & Medical Expulsive Therapy (MET)
* Indicated for uncomplicated stones <5mm (high spontaneous passage rate).
* Hydration (drink >2.5L/day).
* **MET**: Alpha-1-blockers (e.g., **Tamsulosin** 0.4mg OM) for distal ureteric stones → relaxes ureteric smooth muscle.

### Definitive Surgical Management Algorithm
| Stone Location & Size       | Recommended 1st Line Intervention                  |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Renal Stone < 2cm**       | **ESWL (體外震波碎石術)** or FURS (Flexible Ureteroscopy) |
| **Renal Stone > 2cm**       | **PCNL (經皮穿刺腎碎石術)**                                |
| **Proximal Ureteric Stone** | ESWL or Flexible URS/RIRS                          |
| **Distal Ureteric Stone**   | **Rigid URS (輸尿管鏡碎石術)**                            |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### ESWL (Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy) Principles
* Focuses external acoustic shockwaves to fragment the stone.
* Contraindications: Pregnancy, uncorrected bleeding diathesis, untreated UTI, AAA in shockwave path.

### URS (Ureteroscopy) Principles
* Rigid URS for distal ureter; Flexible URS (FURS; aka. Retrograde Intrarenal Surgery (RIRS)) for proximal ureter/renal pelvis.
* Laser lithotripsy (Holmium:YAG laser) used to dust/fragment stones.
* Often requires temporary JJ stent insertion post-op to prevent ureteric edema obstruction.
![image](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/80bb9249bf19d298bf61d96710355a26.png)

### PCNL (Percutaneous Nephrolotomy) Principles
* Direct fluoroscopic/US-guided puncture via flank into the renal collecting system.
* Best modality for large (>2cm), complex, or staghorn calculi.

### Surgical Consent Key Risks
* Bleeding (血尿) / Infection (sepsis).
* Failure of procedure (need for multiple sessions or ancillary procedures).
* Ureteric injury (perforation, avulsion requiring open repair or stricture formation).
* Stent-related symptoms (frequency, dysuria, flank pain during micturition).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative Care
* Strict monitoring of vitals (rule out early urosepsis).
* Assess urine output and degree of hematuria via foley catheter.
* Aggressive IV/oral hydration to flush residual stone fragments.

### JJ Stent Management
* Confirm JJ stent position via post-op KUB X-ray.
* Book routine stent removal (usually 2-4 weeks post-op via flexible cystoscopy under LA).

### Long-term Preventive Rehabilitation
* High fluid intake >2.5-3L daily to maintain dilute urine.
* Normal dietary calcium (do NOT restrict Ca → prevents ↑ gut oxalate absorption).
* ↓ dietary sodium, ↓ animal protein intake.
* Thiazide diuretics for confirmed hypercalciuria.
* Potassium citrate for hypocitraturia or uric acid stones (alkalinizes urine).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Surgical Complications
* **Urosepsis (泌尿道感染敗血症)**: Life-threatening, requires prompt broad-spectrum IV Abx + source control.
* Gross hematuria leading to clot retention.
* Intra-operative ureteric injury (perforation, complete avulsion).
* Adjacent organ injury (colon, pleura injury during PCNL tract dilation).

### Intermediate Surgical Complications
* **Steinstrasse (石街)**: Column of fragmented stones obstructing the ureter post-ESWL.
* Stent migration or severe lower urinary tract stent symptoms.
* Post-obstructive diuresis (volume depletion following relief of chronic obstruction).

### Late Surgical Complications & Prognosis
* **Ureteric stricture (輸尿管狹窄)**: Ischemia from impacted stone or intra-op scope trauma.
* Renal scarring → progressive CKD / loss of renal unit function.
* High recurrence: ~50% at 5 years without formal metabolic evaluation and strict lifestyle modification.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most critical condition to exclude in an elderly male presenting with acute left-sided renal colic? Answer: Leaking or ruptured Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA).
* Trap: Recommending a low-calcium diet to prevent calcium oxalate stones. Pearl: Normal dietary calcium must be maintained; restricting calcium increases free intestinal oxalate, paradoxically increasing oxalate absorption and stone risk.
* Pitfall: Performing ESWL or definitive ureteroscopy in a patient with an obstructed, infected kidney. Proceeding with lithotripsy in a purulent system will cause fatal urosepsis; the immediate action must be emergency decompression via PCN or JJ stent.
* Viva Question: Which type of urinary stone is classically radiolucent on a plain KUB X-ray? Answer: Uric acid stones.
* Viva Question: What is the medical expulsive therapy of choice for a 4mm distal ureteric stone? Answer: Alpha-blockers such as Tamsulosin 0.4mg daily to relax ureteric smooth muscle.
