# Per Rectal Bleeding (便血)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Per Rectal Bleeding
* **Lower Gastrointestinal Bleeding (LGIB) (下消化道出血)**: Bleeding originating distal to the **Ligament of Treitz (屈氏韌帶)**.
* **Haematochezia (便血)**: Passage of fresh/bright red blood per rectum → typically implies LGIB, but can occur in massive Upper GI Bleeding (UGIB) with rapid transit.
* **Melaena (黑糞)**: Black, tarry, foul-smelling stools → implies bleeding proximal to right colon (degraded by bacteria).

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Lower GI Bleeding
* **Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌)**: 2nd most common cancer in HK → index of suspicion must be extremely ↑.
* **Diverticular Disease (憩室病)**: Increasing incidence in HK due to westernized diet.
* **Asian Phenotype**: Right-sided diverticulosis (右側憩室病) is significantly more common in HK/Asian populations compared to predominantly left-sided in Western populations.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Vascular Anatomy of the Lower Gastrointestinal Tract
* **Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA)**: Supplies distal duodenum to proximal 2/3 of transverse colon (via ileocolic, right colic, middle colic).
* **Inferior Mesenteric Artery (IMA)**: Supplies distal 1/3 of transverse colon to upper rectum (via left colic, sigmoid branches, superior rectal).
* **Marginal Artery of Drummond (邊緣動脈)**: Crucial collateral anastomosis between SMA & IMA.
* **Rectal Blood Supply**:
  * **Superior Rectal Artery** (from IMA).
  * **Middle Rectal Artery** (from Internal Iliac).
  * **Inferior Rectal Artery** (from Internal Pudendal).

### Pathophysiology of Diverticular Bleeding
* Mucosal herniation at point of entry of **vasa recta (直血管)** into muscularis propria.
* Chronic trauma/stretching → asymmetric intimal thickening of vasa recta → segmental weakness → rupture.
* Usually massive, painless, arterial bleeding.

### Pathophysiology of Angiodysplasia (血管發育不良)
* Chronic intermittent low-grade venous obstruction (due to muscular contraction) → dilation of submucosal veins → formation of fragile **arteriovenous malformations (AVM)**.
* Associated with **Aortic Stenosis (主動脈瓣狹窄)** (**Heyde's Syndrome**).

### Pathophysiology of Haemorrhoidal Bleeding (痔瘡出血)
* Downward displacement & engorgement of **Corpus Cavernosum Recti ( anal cushions - 肛墊)**.
* Blood is bright red and typically drips *after* defecation (spillage of arterialised venous blood).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Characteristic Symptoms of Per Rectal Bleeding
* **Fresh blood dripping on pan**: Hemorrhoids (痔瘡).
* **Blood on toilet paper with severe pain**: Anal Fissure (肛裂).
* **Blood mixed with stool + altered bowel habit**: Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌).
* **Painless massive fresh bleeding**: Diverticular bleed / Angiodysplasia.
* **Bloody diarrhea + mucus + tenesmus**: Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD - 炎症性腸病) or Ischemic Colitis (缺血性腸炎).

### Vital Signs and Hemodynamic Status
* **Early Shock**: Tachycardia (心跳過速) (>100 bpm), tachypnea.
* **Late Shock**: Hypotension (低血壓) (SBP <90 mmHg), altered mental state, oliguria.
* **Postural Drop**: SBP drop >20 mmHg or HR ↑ >20 bpm upon standing → indicates >15-20% blood volume loss.

### Physical Examination Findings
* **General**: Pallor (蒼白), stigmata of chronic liver disease (if concurrent portal hypertension).
* **Abdomen**: Scars, palpable mass, tenderness.
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) (直腸指檢)**: **MANDATORY**. Assess for palpable low rectal mass, inspect glove for fresh blood / melaena, assess anal tone.
* **Proctoscopy / Rigid Sigmoidoscopy**: Bedside visual assessment of hemorrhoids and distal proctitis.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis Table for Lower Gastrointestinal Bleeding

| Aetiology | Pain | Bleeding Pattern | Key Clinical/HK Demographic Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌)** | -ve | Intermittent, mixed with stool | ↑ age, altered bowel habit, weight loss, +ve family history. |
| **Diverticular Disease (憩室病)** | -ve | Sudden, massive | Right-sided in HK, usually stops spontaneously (80%). |
| **Hemorrhoids (痔瘡)** | -ve | Dripping, post-defecation | Constipation, prolonged sitting. Painful *only* if thrombosed. |
| **Anal Fissure (肛裂)** | +ve (severe) | Streaks on paper | Spasm, sentinel pile, searing pain during/after defecation. |
| **Angiodysplasia (血管發育不良)**| -ve | Low-grade or massive | Right colon, elderly, associated with ESRF or Aortic Stenosis. |
| **Ischemic Colitis (缺血性腸炎)** | +ve (mild) | Bloody diarrhea | Elderly, AF (心房顫動), watershed areas (Splenic flexure). |
| **IBD (UC/Crohn's)** | +ve (crampy)| Bloody diarrhea + mucus | Young patients, fever, extra-intestinal manifestations. |
| **Massive UGIB** | +/- | Haematochezia (if transit fast) | Peptic ulcer disease, liver cirrhosis, ↑ Urea. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Initial Laboratory Investigations
* **CBC**: Check Hb (may be normal in acute bleed due to hemoconcentration), MCV (microcytic → chronic loss), Platelets.
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: ↑ Urea:Creatinine ratio (>100:1) highly suggestive of UGIB (blood digestion).
* **Coagulation Profile**: PT/APTT/INR (identify coagulopathy, warfarin/DOAC use).
* **Type & Screen (血型及抗體篩查)**: Crossmatch (交叉配血) 2-4 units depending on severity.

### Endoscopic Investigations
* **Oesophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD) (胃鏡)**: **MANDATORY** in massive PR bleeding with hemodynamic instability to rule out brisk UGIB (accounts for 10-15% of severe "LGIB").
* **Colonoscopy (大腸鏡)**: Gold standard. Diagnostic & therapeutic. Requires rapid bowel prep (PEG) if done urgently.

### Advanced Radiological Imaging for Obscure Bleeding
* **CT Mesenteric Angiography (CTMA) (電腦掃描血管造影)**:
  * Minimum bleeding rate: **0.3 - 0.5 mL/min**.
  * Excellent for localizing bleeding site (active contrast extravasation/blush).
* **Catheter Angiography (DSA)**:
  * Minimum bleeding rate: **0.5 mL/min**.
  * Diagnostic and therapeutic (embolization).
* **RBC Scintigraphy (Tc-99m labeled RBC scan)**:
  * Minimum bleeding rate: **0.1 mL/min**.
  * Highly sensitive, poorly localizing. Rarely used acutely in HK HA setting due to time constraints.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Initial Resuscitation Protocol (HK Hospital Authority)
* **Airway/Breathing**: Supplemental O2. Intubate if airway compromised.
* **Circulation**:
  * 2x Large bore IV access (14G/16G).
  * Rapid crystalloid resuscitation (Normal Saline / Plasmalyte).
  * Activate **Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP) (大量輸血方案)** if hemodynamically unstable: PRBC:FFP:Platelets in 1:1:1 ratio.
  * Correct coagulopathy (Reverse Warfarin with Prothrombin Complex Concentrate / Vitamin K).
* **Foley Catheter**: Monitor hourly urine output.

### Endoscopic and Radiological Management
* **Colonoscopic Hemostasis**: 
  * Injection (Adrenaline 1:10000).
  * Mechanical (Endoclips - 內視鏡止血夾).
  * Thermal (Argon Plasma Coagulation - APC).
* **Angiographic Embolization (血管栓塞治療)**:
  * Indicated if CTMA +ve and patient unfit for surgery.
  * Risk of bowel ischemia/stricture (especially if non-superselective).

### Surgical Indications for Lower GI Bleeding
* Hemodynamic instability despite aggressive resuscitation.
* Transfusion requirement >6 units PRBCs.
* Re-bleeding after failed endoscopic or angiographic control.
* Undetectable source with life-threatening persistent bleed.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles for Localized Bleeding
* **Segmental Colectomy (節段性結腸切除術)**:
  * Performed if the exact bleeding site is marked (by India ink, CTMA, or endoscopy).
  * E.g., Right Hemicolectomy with primary anastomosis.
  * Blind segmental resection is strictly contraindicated (high risk of missing the lesion → fatal re-bleed).

### Operative Principles for Unlocalized Massive Bleeding
* **Emergency Subtotal Colectomy (緊急次全結腸切除術)**:
  * Indicated for massive, life-threatening LGIB where source cannot be localized despite OGD/CTMA.
  * Removal of colon from ileocecal valve to upper rectum.
  * **Reconstruction**: End Ileostomy (迴腸造口術) is preferred in unstable/shocked patients to avoid high-risk anastomotic leak. Primary ileorectal anastomosis only if patient is hemodynamically perfectly stable.

### Pre-operative Consent Issues (手術同意書重點)
* Death / Mortality (↑ risk due to hemorrhagic shock).
* Need for temporary or permanent Stoma (造口).
* Risk of Re-bleeding (if segmental).
* Anastomotic leak (吻合口漏) and subsequent peritonitis/sepsis.
* Intra-abdominal collections, wound infection.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* Admission to **ICU / HDU (深切治療部 / 高依賴護理病房)** for continuous hemodynamic monitoring.
* Monitor drains (if placed) for fresh blood / enteric content.
* DVT prophylaxis (LMWH / TED stockings) once bleeding risk is confidently stabilized.

### ERAS and Stoma Rehabilitation
* **Early Mobilization & Enteral Feeding**: Shift from NPO to clear fluids on POD1 as tolerated.
* **Stoma Care**: Early involvement of **Stoma Nurse (造口護士)**. Train patient/relatives on stoma appliance changing, output monitoring (prevent high output dehydration).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (< 30 days)
* **Re-bleeding (再出血)**: Especially if incorrect source identified or missed synchronous lesions.
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口漏)**: Highest risk between POD 5-7. Presents with fever, tachycardia, peritonitis.
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**: High risk in emergency contaminated surgeries.
* **Ileus (腸梗阻)**: Prolonged failure to pass flatus/stool.

### Late Post-operative Complications (> 30 days)
* **Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (黏連性小腸阻塞)**.
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝)**.
* **Parastomal Hernia (造口旁疝)**.

### Prognosis and Re-bleeding Risk
* Diverticular bleeds stop spontaneously in 70-80% of cases.
* Re-bleeding risk is ~25% after 1st episode, up to 50% after 2nd episode.
* Angiodysplasia tends to have chronic, recurrent, low-volume bleeding.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of massive lower gastrointestinal bleeding? Answer: Diverticular disease.
* Viva Question: An elderly patient presents with massive haematochezia and shock. What is the very first diagnostic procedure to perform after stabilization? Answer: An emergency OGD to rule out a massive upper GI bleed.
* Trap: Assuming bright red blood per rectum is always due to hemorrhoids. Pearl: Always perform a DRE and schedule a colonoscopy to rule out left-sided colorectal cancer, regardless of the presence of hemorrhoids.
* Trap: Doing a blind segmental colectomy when you "think" the bleeding is from the left colon based on probability. Pearl: Blind segmental resection for unlocalized bleeding has an unacceptably high re-bleeding and mortality rate; a subtotal colectomy is the standard of care for unlocalized massive LGIB.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to check the Urea:Creatinine ratio. A significantly elevated Urea:Creatinine ratio (>100:1) strongly points towards an upper GI source of the bleeding.
* Pitfall: Requesting a RBC scan for an unstable patient with massive bleeding. RBC scans are too slow and lack precise anatomical localization for surgical planning in an acute emergency; use CT Mesenteric Angiography instead.
