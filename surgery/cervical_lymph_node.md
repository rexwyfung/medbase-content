# Cervical Lymph Node Management (頸部淋巴結處理)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Cervical Lymphadenopathy
* **Cervical Lymphadenopathy (頸部淋巴結病變):** Enlargement of cervical lymph nodes (>1cm) or alteration in normal architectural morphology (loss of fatty hilum).
* **Pathological Significance:** Represents reactive hyperplasia, granulomatous infection, or malignant infiltration (primary lymphoma vs metastatic carcinoma).

### Hong Kong Epidemiological Context
* **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌):** Endemic in Southern China/HK due to high Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) prevalence and dietary factors (salted fish). NPC classically presents as painless upper cervical lymphadenopathy.
* **Tuberculosis (TB) (結核病):** Remains endemic in HK. Tuberculous cervical lymphadenitis is a leading cause of chronic benign neck masses.
* **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) (鱗狀細胞癌):** Common in the elderly, strongly associated with smoking, alcohol, and increasingly HPV (Human Papillomavirus) infection for oropharyngeal SCC.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Robbins Anatomical Levels of Cervical Lymph Nodes (I-VI)
| Level | Anatomical Boundaries | High-Yield Primary Tumor Sources |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level I** | Submental (Ia) & Submandibular (Ib) | Oral cavity SCC, lip, anterior tongue, submandibular gland |
| **Level II** | Upper jugular (skull base to hyoid) | Oropharynx, **NPC (鼻咽癌)**, parotid |
| **Level III** | Mid jugular (hyoid to cricoid) | Larynx, hypopharynx, thyroid |
| **Level IV** | Lower jugular (cricoid to clavicle) | Thyroid, larynx, cervical esophagus |
| **Level V** | Posterior triangle (post to SCM) | **NPC**, scalp/skin SCC |
| **Level VI** | Central compartment (hyoid to suprasternal notch) | Thyroid, parathyroid, subglottic larynx |

### Pathophysiology of Lymphatic Metastasis
* **Metastatic SCC:** Tumor microemboli travel via lymphatics → trapped in subcapsular sinus of node → proliferation replaces nodal architecture → capsular breach (**Extranodal Extension/ENE**) → tethering to adjacent neurovascular structures (e.g., IJV, SCM, SAN).
* **NPC Spread:** The nasopharynx has a rich, midline-crossing lymphatic plexus → early bilateral or contralateral cervical node metastasis is classic.

### Pathophysiology of Granulomatous Infection (TB)
* **Tuberculous Lymphadenitis:** Mycobacterial seeding → caseating granulomas → coalescing nodes (matting) → liquefaction necrosis (cold abscess) → ruptures through deep cervical fascia → **Collar Stud Abscess (領扣狀膿腫)** → spontaneous skin breakdown (discharging sinus).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### General Compressive & Systemic Symptoms
* **Mass Effect:** Dysphagia (esophageal compression), stridor/dyspnea (tracheal compression), hoarseness (Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve/RLN palsy).
* **B Symptoms (B類症狀):** Unexplained fever >38°C, drenching night sweats, weight loss >10% over 6 months → strongly suggests **Lymphoma (淋巴瘤)**.

### Malignant vs Benign Clinical Signs
* **Malignant Features:** Painless, hard/stony consistency, progressive growth, fixed to overlying skin or underlying muscle, irregular margins.
* **Inflammatory/Infective Features:** Painful/tender (rapid capsular distension), soft/fluctuant (abscess), overlying erythema, mobile.
* **Lymphoma Features:** Firm, "rubbery" consistency, often matted, multi-station or bilateral involvement.

### Specific Eponymous & Pathognomonic Signs
* **Troisier's Sign:** Enlarged left supraclavicular node (**Virchow's Node (魏爾嘯淋巴結)**) → signifies metastatic spread via the thoracic duct from an intra-abdominal or pelvic primary (e.g., gastric, ovarian, renal).
* **Ear/Nose Signs:** Unilateral middle ear effusion (OME), epistaxis, or cranial nerve palsies associated with a neck mass → highly pathognomonic for **NPC**.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### The "Rule of 80s" in Adult Neck Masses
* **80%** of non-thyroid neck masses in adults are neoplastic.
* **80%** of neoplastic masses are malignant.
* **80%** of malignant masses are metastatic (usually SCC).
* **80%** of metastatic primary tumors originate above the clavicle (aerodigestive tract).

### Differential Diagnosis by Etiology (KITTENS)
| Category | Key Differentials |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Infective/Inflammatory** | **TB (結核病)**, Reactive lymphadenitis, Kikuchi disease, HIV, EBV/CMV, Toxoplasmosis |
| **Malignant (Primary)** | Hodgkin/Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma, Thyroid Carcinoma, Salivary Tumours |
| **Malignant (Secondary)** | Metastatic SCC (Head & Neck), **NPC**, Adenocarcinoma (GI/Lung/Breast) |
| **Congenital** | Branchial cleft cyst, Thyroglossal duct cyst, Cystic hygroma |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Workup (Triple Assessment for Neck)
* **Clinical Endoscopy:** Mandatory **Flexible Nasendoscopy (鼻咽內窺鏡)** in clinic to evaluate post-nasal space, base of tongue, and larynx.
* **Imaging:** **Ultrasound Neck (頸部超聲波)** (Evaluates size, loss of fatty hilum, cystic necrosis, microcalcifications, and vascularity).
* **Cytology:** **Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC) (細針抽吸細胞學檢查)** under USG guidance.

### Advanced Imaging & Staging Modalities
* **Contrast CT Neck/Thorax:** Identifies deep structure invasion, defines extranodal extension (ENE), and screens for pulmonary metastases.
* **MRI Head & Neck:** Superior soft-tissue resolution for assessing skull base invasion, intracranial extension, and perineural spread (critical for NPC staging).
* **PET-CT (正電子掃描):** Gold standard for locating an unknown primary tumor (**CUP Syndrome**) and ruling out distant metastasis before radical surgery.

### Endoscopic & Laboratory Protocols
* **Panendoscopy (Examination Under Anesthesia):** Direct laryngoscopy, esophagoscopy, and bronchoscopy with directed random biopsies if FNAC shows SCC but primary is clinically occult.
* **EBV DNA Titres:** High sensitivity/specificity blood test for screening, monitoring, and detecting recurrence of NPC in HK.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### HA Protocol for Suspected NPC (鼻咽癌)
* **Diagnosis:** Endoscopic biopsy of post-nasal space + Plasma EBV DNA.
* **Primary Modality:** **Radiotherapy (RT)** for early-stage or **Concurrent Chemoradiotherapy (CCRT) (同步放射化療)** for advanced stage.
* **Surgical Role:** Strictly limited to salvage surgery (Radical Neck Dissection) for isolated residual or recurrent cervical nodes post-irradiation. *Never* operate as 1st line.

### Tuberculous Cervical Lymphadenitis Management
* **1st Line Medical:** 6-9 months of Anti-TB medication via DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course) clinic.
* **Procedural Role:** USG-guided needle aspiration for tense, painful cold abscesses. Formal excision is only indicated for paradoxical upgrading reactions resistant to medication or chronically discharging sinuses.

### Surgical Indications for Neck Dissection
* **Therapeutic Neck Dissection:** Clinically or radiologically +ve nodes (N+) in operable Head & Neck SCC (e.g., oral cavity, larynx).
* **Elective Neck Dissection:** Clinically -ve nodes (N0) but the primary tumor carries >20% risk of occult microscopic metastasis (e.g., tongue thickness >4mm, advanced T-stage oral cancers).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Types of Neck Dissection (頸部淋巴結清除術)
| Classification | Resected Levels | Preserved Key Structures | Clinical Indication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Radical (RND)** | I-V | None. Resects SCM, IJV, SAN. | Massive N3 disease invading non-lymphatic structures. |
| **Modified Radical (MRND)** | I-V | Preserves ≥1 of: **S**CM, **I**JV, **S**AN. | Clinically +ve neck (N1-N2) without gross invasion. |
| **Selective (SND)** | Specific subsets (e.g., I-III or II-IV) | Preserves SCM, IJV, SAN. | Elective (N0) neck or highly selected minimal N1 disease. |

### Key Anatomical Landmarks & Operative Steps
* **Incision:** Modified Blair or apron flap based on primary tumor location. Raise subplatysmal flaps.
* **Nerve Identification & Protection:**
  * **Marginal Mandibular Nerve (CN VII):** Protected by identifying and ligating the facial vein, sweeping it superiorly (Hayes Martin maneuver).
  * **Spinal Accessory Nerve (CN XI):** Identified in the posterior triangle (Level V); essential for trapezius function.
* **Vascular Control:**
  * **Internal Jugular Vein (IJV):** Ligated low at the root of the neck and high at the skull base during a Radical Neck Dissection.

### Pre-operative Consent & Complication Risks
* **Cranial Nerve Injuries:** CN VII (lower lip drop/asymmetry), CN X (vocal cord palsy/hoarseness), CN XI (shoulder drop/inability to abduct >90°), CN XII (tongue deviation).
* **Major Vascular Injury:** Carotid artery blowout (fatal), massive haematoma.
* **Lymphatic Injury:** **Chyle Leak (乳糜漏)** (specifically in left-sided Level IV dissection due to Thoracic Duct).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* **Airway Monitoring:** Strict observation for stridor or respiratory distress. High risk of compressive haematoma compromising the trachea.
* **Positioning:** Nurse head elevated at 30° to ↓ venous congestion and ↓ dependent edema.

### Drain Management Protocols
* **Closed Suction Drains:** Routinely placed deep to skin flaps to prevent seroma and haematoma formation.
* **Chyle Leak Surveillance:** Monitor drain fluid characteristics. If fluid turns milky white upon starting enteral feeding → immediate suspicion of thoracic duct injury.
* **Removal:** Drains removed when output is <20-30mL/24h for 2 consecutive days.

### Physiotherapy & Functional Rehab
* **Shoulder Syndrome Prevention:** Early intensive physiotherapy required if CN XI is stretched or resected to prevent trapezius atrophy and secondary adhesive capsulitis (frozen shoulder).
* **Swallowing/Speech:** Early intervention by speech therapists if concurrent primary mucosal resection (e.g., glossectomy) was performed.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (<24h)
* **Compressive Haematoma (血腫):** Surgical emergency. If acute airway compromise occurs, requires immediate bedside opening of the wound to relieve pressure, followed by formal theater exploration for hemostasis.
* **Chyle Leak:** Fluid accumulation from disrupted thoracic duct.

### Intermediate Complications (Days-Weeks)
* **Management of Chyle Leak:**
  * **Conservative:** Strict non-fat or **Medium Chain Triglyceride (MCT) diet**, or TPN + Somatostatin/Octreotide.
  * **Surgical:** Thoracic duct ligation if output >500mL/day or failure of conservative tx >5-7 days.
* **Wound Infection & Flap Necrosis:** High risk in malnourished patients, smokers, or previously irradiated fields.

### Late Complications & Prognosis
* **Neuropathic Pain & Numbness:** Division of the Greater Auricular Nerve results in permanent numbness over the earlobe and lower neck.
* **Prognosis & ENE:** The presence of **Extranodal Extension (ENE)** on final histology halves survival rates and is an absolute indication for adjuvant Concurrent Chemoradiotherapy (CCRT).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why is excisional biopsy strictly contraindicated as a 1st line investigation for an enlarging, painless cervical lymph node? Answer: It violates fascial planes, disrupts physiological lymphatic drainage, upstages the tumor, and significantly increases the risk of local recurrence and complications in subsequent curative surgery or radiotherapy.
* Trap: Assuming a massive, fixed, left supraclavicular node originates from a primary head and neck squamous cell carcinoma. Pearl: This represents Troisier's sign (Virchow's node) and is highly indicative of metastatic spread from an intra-abdominal, pelvic, or thoracic primary (e.g., gastric or lung cancer).
* Pitfall: Failing to recognize a milky drain output on post-op day 2 after starting the patient on a normal diet. This indicates a chyle leak from an unrecognized thoracic duct injury. Immediately switch the patient to a non-fat or Medium Chain Triglyceride (MCT) diet and monitor drain volumes strictly.
* Viva Question: What specific mechanism defines a "Collar Stud" abscess in tuberculous cervical lymphadenitis? Answer: A deep infected lymph node undergoes caseous necrosis and ruptures through the deep cervical fascia, forming a superficial subcutaneous abscess cavity that remains connected to the deep node via a narrow tract.
* Trap: Planning a primary radical neck dissection for a patient newly diagnosed with Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) and bulky bilateral neck nodes. Pearl: NPC is highly radiosensitive; 1st line treatment is strictly definitive RT/CCRT. Surgery is reserved exclusively for the salvage of isolated recurrent or residual disease post-radiation.
