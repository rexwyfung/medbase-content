# Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Acute Pancreatitis
* **Definition**: Acute inflammatory process of the **pancreas (胰臟)** with variable involvement of peripancreatic tissues/remote organ systems.
* **Diagnostic Criteria (Atlanta 2012)**: Requires 2 of 3:
  * Typical acute onset, persistent, severe epigastric pain (上腹痛) radiating to back.
  * Serum **amylase (澱粉酶)** or **lipase (脂肪酶)** >3x Upper Limit of Normal (ULN).
  * Characteristic findings of acute pancreatitis on **CECT (對比增強電腦掃描)**, MRI, or USG.

### Local Epidemiology of Acute Pancreatitis in Hong Kong
* **Incidence**: Common acute surgical admission in **HA hospitals (醫管局醫院)**.
* **Biliary Tract Disease (Gallstones - 膽石)**: Most common etiology in HK (~60%).
* **Alcoholism (酒精)**: 2nd most common (~20%), ↑ frequency in younger males.
* **Mortality**: Overall ~5%; Severe necrotizing pancreatitis (嚴重壞死性胰臟炎) up to 15-30%.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Pancreas
* **Location**: Retroperitoneal organ (except tail at splenic hilum).
* **Arterial Supply**: Superior Pancreaticoduodenal Artery (from Gastroduodenal Artery), Inferior Pancreaticoduodenal Artery (from SMA), Splenic Artery (體部及尾部).
* **Venous Drainage**: Portal system (Splenic Vein + SMV → Portal Vein).
* **Ductal System**: Main pancreatic duct (Wirsung) joins common bile duct (CBD) at **Ampulla of Vater (法特氏壺腹)**.

### Pathophysiology of Acute Pancreatitis
* **Initiating Event**: Acinar cell injury/ductal obstruction.
* **Intracellular Mechanism**: Premature activation of zymogens (Trypsinogen → **Trypsin (胰蛋白酶)**) within acinar cells.
* **Cascade**: Autodigestion by proteases/lipases → local inflammation, edema, hemorrhage, necrosis.
* **Systemic Response**: Release of cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6) → **SIRS (全身炎症反應綜合症)** → ARDS, AKI, Multiorgan Failure (MOF).

### Etiology Mnemonic (I GET SMASHED)
* **I**diopathic
* **G**allstones (膽石) (Most common in HK)
* **E**thanol (酒精)
* **T**rauma (Blunt abdominal trauma)
* **S**teroids
* **M**umps (Mumps/Malignancy)
* **A**utoimmune (IgG4-related)
* **S**corpion sting (Rare in HK)
* **H**ypercalcemia / Hypertriglyceridemia (高血脂) (>11.3 mmol/L)
* **E**RCP (Post-ERCP pancreatitis - PEP)
* **D**rugs (Azathioprine, Thiazides, Valproate)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Acute Pancreatitis
* **Pain**: Sudden onset, severe, unrelenting **epigastric pain (上腹痛)**.
* **Radiation**: Radiates directly through to the back (~50% cases).
* **Relief**: Partially relieved by leaning forward.
* **GI Symptoms**: Profound nausea (噁心), recurrent non-bilious/bilious vomiting (嘔吐) that does not relieve pain.

### Key Physical Signs of Acute Pancreatitis
* **General**: Tachycardia, tachypnea, fever, dehydration, hypovolemia (SIRS criteria).
* **Abdominal Inspection**: Distension (ileus), generalized guarding but classically *less* rigid than peptic ulcer perforation.
* **Palpation**: Severe epigastric tenderness, localized guarding.
* **Late Hemorrhagic Signs (Rare but poor prognosis)**:
  * **Cullen's sign (庫倫氏徵)**: Periumbilical ecchymosis (Hemoperitoneum).
  * **Grey Turner's sign (格雷特納氏徵)**: Flank ecchymosis (Retroperitoneal hemorrhage).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis for Acute Epigastric Pain
| Differential Diagnosis | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)** | ↑↑ Lipase/Amylase, pain radiating to back. |
| **Peptic Ulcer Perforation (消化性潰瘍穿孔)** | Sudden rigid "board-like" abdomen (板狀腹), free gas under diaphragm on CXR. |
| **Acute Cholecystitis (急性膽囊炎)** | Positive Murphy's sign (墨菲氏徵), pain radiating to right shoulder, thickened GB wall on USG. |
| **Mesenteric Ischemia (腸繫膜缺血)** | Pain out of proportion to physical signs, ↑ Lactate, AF history. |
| **Myocardial Infarction (心肌梗塞)** | Inferior MI presents as epigastric pain. ECG changes (ST elevation), ↑ Troponin. |
| **AAA Rupture (腹主動脈瘤破裂)** | Pulsatile expansile mass, profound hypotension, shock. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Laboratory Investigations
* **Lipase (脂肪酶)**: Most specific & sensitive. Stays ↑ longer than amylase. >3x ULN is diagnostic.
* **Amylase (澱粉酶)**: Sensitive but less specific (↑ in perforation, ischemia, salivary gland disease). Level does *not* correlate with severity.
* **CBC**: ↑ WBC (Leukocytosis), ↑ Hct (Hemoconcentration due to 3rd space loss - poor prognostic sign).
* **LFT**: ↑ ALT/AST >3x ULN highly predictive of **gallstone etiology (膽石成因)**. ↑ ALP, Bilirubin (Cholestasis).
* **Renal Function (RFT)**: ↑ Urea/Creatinine (AKI, hypovolemia). ↑ BUN is a severe prognostic marker.
* **Calcium**: ↓ Ca2+ (Fat saponification in retroperitoneum).
* **CRP**: >150 mg/L at 48h indicates severe necrotizing disease.
* **ABG**: ↓ PaO2 (ARDS), metabolic acidosis (Lactate).

### Diagnostic Imaging for Acute Pancreatitis
* **USG Abdomen (腹部超聲波)**:
  * 1st line imaging for *all* patients.
  * Identifies gallstones/biliary tree dilatation. Pancreas often obscured by overlying bowel gas.
* **CECT Abdomen & Pelvis (對比增強電腦掃描)**:
  * Do NOT perform immediately unless diagnostic uncertainty (may exacerbate AKI).
  * Optimal timing: **72-96 hours** post-onset to accurately define extent of **pancreatic necrosis (胰臟壞死)**.
* **MRCP (磁力共振膽胰管造影)**:
  * Indicated if suspected choledocholithiasis (CBD stone) but USG -ve and LFTs deranged.

### Prognostic Scoring Systems (Modified Glasgow Score)
| Modified Glasgow Score (PANCREAS) - Evaluate at 48h | Criteria (Score ≥3 indicates Severe Pancreatitis) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **P**aO2 | < 8 kPa (60 mmHg) |
| **A**ge | > 55 years |
| **N**eutrophils (WBC) | > 15 x 10^9/L |
| **C**alcium | < 2 mmol/L |
| **R**enal Function (Urea) | > 16 mmol/L |
| **E**nzymes (LDH / AST) | LDH > 600 U/L or AST > 200 U/L |
| **A**lbumin | < 32 g/L |
| **S**ugar (Glucose) | > 10 mmol/L (No prior DM) |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Revised Atlanta Classification of Severity
| Severity Level | Organ Failure (OF) & Local Complications |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Mild (輕度)** | No OF, no local/systemic complications. |
| **Moderate (中度)** | Transient OF (<48h) OR local complications (e.g., fluid collection) without persistent OF. |
| **Severe (重度)** | Persistent OF (>48h). Can be single or multiple organ. High mortality. |

### Initial Resuscitation & Conservative Management Protocol
* **Fluid Resuscitation**: Aggressive IV crystalloids. **Ringer's Lactate (乳酸林格氏液)** preferred over Normal Saline to ↓ hyperchloremic acidosis & SIRS. Goal-directed (Urine Output >0.5ml/kg/h).
* **Analgesia**: IV Opioids (Fentanyl/Morphine via PCA). Avoid NSAIDs if ↑ Cr.
* **Oxygen**: Supplemental O2 to maintain SpO2 >95%.
* **Monitoring**: Foley catheter (hourly UO), CVP line if severe, frequent vital signs.

### Medical Management & Nutritional Support
* **Enteral Feeding**: Early oral feeding (within 24h) if pain improves. If unable, fine-bore Nasogastric/Nasojejunal (NG/NJ) feeding. Strictly preferred over TPN to maintain gut mucosal barrier and ↓ bacterial translocation.
* **Antibiotics**: Prophylactic Abx are **NOT** recommended for sterile necrosis. Only indicated for proven **infected necrosis (受感染壞死)** or concurrent **acute cholangitis (急性膽管炎)** (e.g., IV Cefuroxime + Metronidazole, or Meropenem).

### Surgical & Endoscopic Indications in Acute Pancreatitis
* **Concurrent Cholangitis / Biliary Obstruction**: Urgent **ERCP + Sphincterotomy (內視鏡逆行膽胰管造影及括約肌切開術)** within 24h.
* **Infected Pancreatic Necrosis**: Step-up approach (Drainage → Necrosectomy).
* **Gallstone Pancreatitis (Mild)**: Index admission **Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (腹腔鏡膽囊切除術)** within 2 weeks.
* **Abdominal Compartment Syndrome (ACS)**: Decompressive laparotomy if bladder pressure >20 mmHg with new OF.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles for Pancreatic Necrosectomy (Step-Up Approach)
* **Philosophy**: Delay intervention ideally until >4 weeks to allow necrosis to become Walled-Off Necrosis (WON).
* **Step 1: Minimally Invasive Drainage**: Percutaneous radiologically guided catheter drainage or Endoscopic Transgastric Drainage. (Success rate ~35-50% without surgery).
* **Step 2: VARD (Video-Assisted Retroperitoneal Debridement)**: If Step 1 fails, dilate percutaneous tract, insert laparoscope, and mechanically debride necrosis under direct vision with continuous post-op lavage.
* **Step 3: Open Necrosectomy**: Last resort. Midline laparotomy, access lesser sac, blunt manual debridement of necrotic slough, insert large bore drains for continuous continuous lavage.

### Operative Principles for Gallstone Pancreatitis Management
* **Mild Attack**: Laparoscopic cholecystectomy + Intraoperative Cholangiogram (IOC) during the *same* admission once pain resolves and amylase ↓. Prevents 30% risk of recurrent attack within weeks.
* **Severe Attack**: Delay cholecystectomy until acute inflammation subsides and fluid collections resolve (often >6 weeks).

### Consent Issues for Pancreatic Surgery
* Bleeding (Major vessels nearby: Splenic artery/vein, Portal vein).
* Pancreatic fistula (胰瘻管) formation requiring prolonged drainage.
* Exocrine/Endocrine insufficiency (Brittle Diabetes Mellitus, Malabsorption requiring Creon).
* Bowel perforation, recurrent sepsis, need for multiple re-operations / ICU stay.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care & Monitoring
* **ICU Admission**: Mandatory for open necrosectomy or severe SIRS.
* **Drain Management**: Continuous large-volume lavage (e.g., 1L Normal Saline/hour) if VARD/Open necrosectomy performed to flush out residual necrotic debris. Monitor effluent character (purulent vs clear).
* **Glycemic Control**: Strict blood glucose monitoring (sliding scale insulin) due to ↑ risk of endocrine failure.

### ERAS Protocol for Pancreatic Surgery
* **Early Mobilization**: Prevent DVT/PE and basal atelectasis.
* **Nutrition**: Resume EN via NJ tube ASAP post-necrosectomy to support hypercatabolic state.
* **Drain Removal**: Remove retroperitoneal/peripancreatic drains only when output is non-purulent, amylase-poor, and imaging confirms resolution of collections.

### Long-term Rehabilitation & Follow-up
* **Pancreatic Exocrine Insufficiency (PEI)**: Prescribe **Pancreatic Enzyme Replacement Therapy (PERT - e.g., Creon)** if steatorrhea or severe weight loss develops.
* **Endocrine Insufficiency**: Monitor HbA1c; high risk of developing Type 3c Diabetes Mellitus.
* **Lifestyle**: Absolute cessation of alcohol (戒酒) and smoking.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Systemic Complications (< 1 week)
* **Pulmonary**: ARDS, pleural effusion (classically left-sided).
* **Renal**: AKI (hypovolemia/SIRS).
* **Cardiovascular**: Hypovolemic shock, distributive shock.
* **Metabolic**: Hypocalcemia, hyperglycemia, DIC.

### Intermediate Local Complications (1-4 weeks)
* **Acute Peripancreatic Fluid Collection (APFC)**: Sterile fluid, unencapsulated. Most resolve spontaneously.
* **Acute Necrotic Collection (ANC)**: Fluid + necrotic debris. Can become infected (diagnosed by gas on CT or +ve FNA gram stain/culture).

### Late Local Complications (> 4 weeks)
* **Pancreatic Pseudocyst (假性胰臟囊腫)**: Encapsulated fluid collection (no epithelial lining, fibrous wall).
  * Complications: Rupture, bleeding (pseudoaneurysm), infection, gastric/biliary outlet obstruction.
  * Management: Observe if <6cm/asymptomatic. If symptomatic >6 weeks → Endoscopic cystogastrostomy or Surgical Roux-en-Y cystojejunostomy.
* **Walled-Off Necrosis (WON)**: Encapsulated necrotic tissue. Requires debridement if infected/symptomatic.
* **Vascular**: Splenic vein thrombosis (導致左側門脈高壓 / Gastric varices). Splenic artery pseudoaneurysm (risk of massive hemorrhage).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the single most important initial management step in a patient presenting with severe acute pancreatitis? Answer: Aggressive goal-directed IV fluid resuscitation (Ringer's Lactate preferred) to counter 3rd space loss and maintain pancreatic microcirculation.
* Trap: Serum amylase level correlates with the severity of acute pancreatitis. Pearl: False. Amylase and lipase levels only confirm the diagnosis. Severity is determined by physiological parameters (e.g., Modified Glasgow score, CRP, organ failure).
* Pitfall: Starting broad-spectrum prophylactic antibiotics for all patients with acute necrotizing pancreatitis to prevent infection. Routine prophylaxis does not prevent infected necrosis and increases the risk of fungal/resistant infections. Antibiotics should only be given if there is confirmed infection (gas on CT, positive FNA) or concurrent cholangitis.
* Viva Question: A patient with gallstone pancreatitis recovers fully within 48 hours. When should they undergo laparoscopic cholecystectomy? Answer: During the index admission (same hospital stay), ideally within 2 weeks, to prevent recurrent attacks.
* Trap: Performing an immediate CT scan on admission for a patient with typical epigastric pain and lipase >3x ULN. Pearl: Early CT (within 48h) often underestimates the extent of pancreatic necrosis. CT should be delayed to 72-96 hours if performed for severity staging.
* Pitfall: Rushing to perform open necrosectomy on a patient who spikes a fever on day 10 with confirmed infected necrosis. Surgical debridement should ideally be delayed until at least 4 weeks post-onset to allow the necrosis to encapsulate (Walled-Off Necrosis), making the "step-up approach" much safer and minimizing bleeding risk.
