# Diseases of the Groin, Scrotum & Genitalia (腹股溝、陰囊及生殖器疾病)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Scrotal Emergencies (陰囊急症定義)
* **Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉)**: Twisting of spermatic cord → venous outflow obstruction → arterial compromise → acute ischaemia.
* **Epididymo-orchitis (副睪睪丸炎)**: Acute inflammation/infection of epididymis ± testis.
* **Fournier's Gangrene (福尼爾氏壞疽)**: Synergistic polymicrobial necrotizing fasciitis of perineum/scrotum.

### Clinical Definition of Benign Scrotal Swellings (良性陰囊腫塊定義)
* **Hydrocele (陰囊水腫)**: Abnormal fluid accumulation between parietal & visceral layers of **Tunica Vaginalis (鞘膜)**.
* **Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張)**: Abnormal dilatation & tortuosity of **Pampiniform Plexus (蔓狀靜脈叢)** within spermatic cord.
* **Epididymal Cyst / Spermatocele (副睪囊腫/精液囊腫)**: Fluid-filled sac within epididymis.

### Local Epidemiology of Groin & Scrotal Conditions (本地流行病學)
* **Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張)**: Occurs in ~10-15% of general male population; 90% left-sided.
* **Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉)**: Bimodal peak: neonates & peri-pubertal boys (12-18 years).
* **Testicular Tumours (睪丸腫瘤)**: Most common solid malignancy in young men (20-35 years); primary HA presentation is painless mass.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Scrotum & Testicular Coverings (陰囊及睪丸包膜解剖學)
* **Skin & Dartos Muscle (皮膚及肉膜)**: Outermost layer; wrinkling effect.
* **External Spermatic Fascia (外精索筋膜)**: Derived from external oblique aponeurosis.
* **Cremasteric Fascia & Muscle (提睪肌筋膜)**: Derived from internal oblique.
* **Internal Spermatic Fascia (內精索筋膜)**: Derived from transversalis fascia.
* **Tunica Vaginalis (鞘膜)**: Visceral (adherent to testis) & Parietal layers.
* **Tunica Albuginea (白膜)**: Tough fibrous capsule directly enveloping testis.

### Surgical Anatomy of Testicular Lymphatic Drainage (睪丸淋巴引流解剖學)
* **Testis (睪丸)**: Drains primarily via gonadal vessels to **Para-aortic Lymph Nodes (主動脈旁淋巴結)** (L2 level).
* **Scrotum (陰囊)**: Drains via superficial lymphatics to **Superficial Inguinal Lymph Nodes (腹股溝淺淋巴結)**.
* *Note*: This dictates surgical approach for cancer → never biopsy via scrotum to prevent altering lymphatic drainage.

### Pathophysiology of Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉病理生理學)
* **Bell-Clapper Deformity (鐘擺畸形)**: High attachment of Tunica Vaginalis → lack of posterior fixation to scrotum.
* Allows testis to freely rotate on spermatic cord pedicle.
* 1st event: Venous congestion & edema.
* 2nd event: Arterial occlusion → haemorrhagic infarction (irreversible damage post 6-8 hours).

### Pathophysiology of Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張病理生理學)
* Left internal spermatic vein inserts perpendicularly into left renal vein (high pressure).
* Right internal spermatic vein inserts obliquely into IVC (low pressure).
* Incompetent venous valves → retrograde flow → venous stasis → ↑ scrotal temperature → potential infertility.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Acute Scrotum (急性陰囊症狀)
* **Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉)**: Sudden onset, excruciating, unilateral pain. Often wakes patient from sleep. Associated lower abdominal/groin pain, nausea/vomiting.
* **Epididymo-orchitis (副睪睪丸炎)**: Gradual onset unilateral pain. Associated with LUTS (dysuria, frequency) or fever.
* **Torsion of Testicular Appendage (睪丸附件扭轉)**: Gradual to acute pain localized to superior pole.

### Signs of Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉徵象)
* **High-Riding Testis (高位睪丸)**: Retracted upwards due to shortened, twisted cord.
* **Transverse Lie (橫向位置)**: Testis lies horizontally rather than vertically.
* **Absent Cremasteric Reflex (提睪肌反射消失)**: Highly sensitive but not 100% specific.
* **Prehn's Sign (普雷恩氏徵)**: -ve (Elevation of scrotum does NOT relieve pain; may worsen it).

### Signs of Benign Scrotal Swellings (良性陰囊腫塊徵象)
* **Hydrocele (陰囊水腫)**: Fluctuent, non-tender, +ve transillumination. Cannot "get above" swelling.
* **Epididymal Cyst (副睪囊腫)**: +ve transillumination, able to palpate testis separately from the cyst.
* **Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張)**: "Bag of worms" sensation. Enlarges with Valsalva/standing; collapses when supine.

### Signs of Testicular Malignancy (睪丸惡性腫瘤徵象)
* Hard, craggy, painless, irregular mass arising *within* the testis.
* Does NOT transilluminate.
* Often associated with secondary hydrocele.
* Supraclavicular or abdominal masses if advanced metastatic disease.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Acute Scrotal Pain (急性陰囊疼痛鑑別診斷)
| Diagnosis | Onset | Key Clinical Signs | Doppler USG Findings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉)** | Sudden | High-riding, transverse lie, -ve cremasteric reflex | ↓ or absent arterial flow |
| **Epididymo-orchitis (副睪睪丸炎)** | Gradual | Tender swollen epididymis, fever, +ve Prehn's sign | ↑ hyperaemic flow |
| **Torsion of Appendage (附件扭轉)** | Gradual/Acute | Localized tenderness, "Blue Dot Sign" | Normal testicular flow |
| **Strangulated Hernia (箝頓性疝氣)** | Acute | Bowel sounds in scrotum, overlying erythema | Bowel loops in canal/scrotum |

### Differential Diagnosis of Painless Scrotal Swelling (無痛陰囊腫塊鑑別診斷)
| Diagnosis | Swelling Location | Transillumination | Can Palpate Above? | Can Feel Testis Separately? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hydrocele (陰囊水腫)** | Surrounds testis | +ve | Yes | No |
| **Epididymal Cyst (副睪囊腫)** | Superior/posterior | +ve | Yes | Yes |
| **Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張)** | Cord (superior) | -ve | Yes | Yes |
| **Testicular Tumour (睪丸腫瘤)** | Within testis | -ve | Yes | No |
| **Inguinoscrotal Hernia (腹股溝陰囊疝氣)**| Groin to scrotum | -ve | No | Yes (usually) |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Laboratory Workup for Acute Scrotum (急性陰囊實驗室檢查)
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: Nitrites/Leukocytes point to UTI/Epididymo-orchitis.
* **MSU (中段尿液)**: Culture & sensitivity to guide antibiotic therapy.
* **Urethral Swab / First Void Urine (尿道拭子/首段尿液)**: For NAAT (Chlamydia/Gonorrhoea) in sexually active young men.

### Laboratory Workup for Testicular Tumour (睪丸腫瘤實驗室檢查)
* **Alpha-fetoprotein (αFP)**: Elevated in Non-Seminomatous Germ Cell Tumours (NSGCT); NEVER elevated in pure seminoma.
* **Beta-Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (βHCG)**: Elevated in NSGCT & ~15% of seminomas.
* **Lactate Dehydrogenase (LDH)**: Non-specific marker of tumour burden / tissue necrosis.

### Imaging Modalities for Scrotal Conditions (陰囊疾病影像學檢查)
* **Doppler Ultrasound (多普勒超聲波)**: 1st line for almost all scrotal pathology.
* Differentiates solid vs cystic.
* Assesses vascularity (↑ in infection, ↓ in torsion).
* Gold standard for confirming/characterizing varicocele (retrograde flow >2mm vessels).

### Staging Investigations for Testicular Cancer (睪丸癌分期檢查)
* **CT Thorax/Abdomen/Pelvis (電腦掃描)**: Mandatory for staging to assess para-aortic lymph nodes & pulmonary metastases.
* *Note*: Do NOT biopsy the testis percutaneously due to risk of seeding scrotal skin lymphatics.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### HA Management Algorithm for Acute Testicular Torsion (醫管局急性睪丸扭轉處置流程)
* High clinical suspicion → NPO → prepare for immediate emergency surgery.
* Golden window: <6 hours from pain onset for >90% salvage rate.
* Do NOT delay surgery for USG if clinical picture is classic.

### Management Algorithm for Epididymo-orchitis (副睪睪丸炎處置流程)
* Age <35 (STI suspected): IM Ceftriaxone + PO Doxycycline.
* Age >35 (Enteric flora suspected): PO Fluoroquinolone (e.g., Ciprofloxacin/Levofloxacin) or Co-trimoxazole.
* Supportive: Scrotal elevation, NSAIDs, ice packs.

### Surgical Indications for Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張手術指徵)
* Documented male infertility with abnormal semen analysis.
* Intractable testicular pain/discomfort.
* Progressive testicular atrophy.
* Paediatric/Adolescent varicocele with >20% volume discrepancy between testes.

### Surgical Indications for Hydrocele (陰囊水腫手術指徵)
* Symptomatic (heavy, dragging sensation affecting quality of life).
* Cosmetic concerns (patient request).
* Inability to adequately palpate underlying testis to exclude malignancy.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Scrotal Exploration for Torsion (睪丸扭轉探查手術原則)
* Midline scrotal raphe or transverse incision.
* Deliver testis → untwist (detorsion) → wrap in warm saline swabs.
* Assess viability (colour, bleeding on nicking tunica albuginea).
* Viable → **Bilateral Orchidopexy (雙側睪丸固定術)** (3-point non-absorbable fixation to dartos).
* Non-viable → **Orchidectomy (睪丸切除術)** + contralateral orchidopexy.

### Operative Principles of Radical Inguinal Orchidectomy (根治性腹股溝睪丸切除術原則)
* Incision in inguinal crease (never scrotal).
* Open external oblique aponeurosis.
* Mobilize spermatic cord → early clamping/ligation of cord at deep inguinal ring to prevent tumour embolization.
* Deliver testis from scrotum into groin wound.
* Excise en bloc.

### Operative Principles of Hydrocelectomy (陰囊水腫切除術原則)
* Jaboulay procedure: Excision/eversion of redundant tunica vaginalis.
* Lord's plication: Plication of tunica vaginalis (reduces haematoma risk).
* Crucial step: MUST meticulously inspect underlying testis to rule out underlying tumour.

### Surgical Consent Issues for Scrotal Surgery (陰囊手術知情同意重點)
* **Orchidopexy**: Risk of recurrent torsion, future testicular atrophy, infertility.
* **Orchidectomy**: Infertility, need for lifelong testosterone replacement (if bilateral), psychological impact (offer testicular prosthesis).
* **Varicocelectomy**: Recurrence (up to 10%), hydrocele formation post-op, testicular artery injury → atrophy.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Post-operative Care for Scrotal Surgery (陰囊手術後護理)
* Elevate scrotum using scrotal support or folded towel to ↓ edema.
* Cold compress/ice packs for 24-48 hours.
* Avoid strenuous activity / heavy lifting for 2-4 weeks.

### Rehabilitation & Follow-up for Testicular Tumour (睪丸腫瘤復健與隨訪)
* Post-orchidectomy MDT review for adjuvant chemo/radiotherapy.
* Serial tumour markers (AFP, hCG, LDH) to ensure normalization.
* Surveillance CT scans to monitor retroperitoneal nodes.
* Sperm banking should be discussed *prior* to starting adjuvant therapies.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Complications of Scrotal Surgery (陰囊手術併發症)
* **Early**: Reactive scrotal haematoma/swelling (very common due to loose dartos tissues), surgical site infection.
* **Intermediate**: Delayed wound healing, chronic epididymitis.
* **Late**: **Chronic Orchialgia (慢性睪丸痛)** (especially post-vasectomy or varicocelectomy), testicular atrophy (due to vascular injury), disease recurrence.

### Complications of Radical Orchidectomy (根治性睪丸切除術併發症)
* Ilioinguinal nerve injury → loss of sensation to base of penis/scrotum.
* Haematoma in empty hemiscrotum.
* Psychological distress.

### Prognosis of Testicular Cancer (睪丸癌預後)
* Excellent prognosis overall.
* Stage I Seminoma: >99% 5-year survival.
* Even advanced/metastatic disease is highly curable with cisplatin-based chemotherapy (BEP regimen).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the defining characteristic that separates an epididymal cyst from a hydrocele on clinical examination? Answer: In an epididymal cyst, you can palpate the testis separately from the fluid-filled swelling; in a hydrocele, the testis is enveloped and cannot be felt separately.
* Trap: Assuming a new-onset varicocele is benign without further thought. Pearl: A new right-sided varicocele or a varicocele that does not collapse when the patient lies supine is a "red flag" for a retroperitoneal mass (e.g., Renal Cell Carcinoma invading the IVC). Urgent abdominal USG/CT is required.
* Pitfall: Delaying surgery for a young male with sudden extreme scrotal pain to wait for an ultrasound. Do not do this. Time is testicle; proceed to emergency scrotal exploration to prevent irreversible ischaemia.
* Viva Question: Why do we perform a radical inguinal orchidectomy rather than a trans-scrotal orchidectomy for suspected testicular cancer? Answer: To prevent disruption of natural lymphatic drainage and avoid seeding tumour cells into the scrotal skin lymphatics, which drain to superficial inguinal nodes rather than para-aortic nodes.
* Trap: Reassuring a patient with a "classic" epididymo-orchitis without following up. Pearl: Approximately 10% of testicular tumours present with secondary inflammation mimicking epididymo-orchitis; always arrange a follow-up USG 4-6 weeks after antibiotic treatment to ensure complete resolution and exclude underlying malignancy.
* Pitfall: Biopsying a suspected testicular mass percutaneously. Do not stick a needle in it. Direct biopsy alters lymphatic drainage; the correct diagnostic procedure is a radical inguinal orchidectomy.
