# Pancreatic Pseudocyst & Complications (胰臟假性囊腫及併發症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Pancreatic Pseudocyst
* **Pancreatic Pseudocyst (胰臟假性囊腫)**: Localized collection of amylase-rich pancreatic juice enclosed by a wall of fibrous/granulation tissue.
* Lacks true epithelial lining (-ve epithelium) → distinguishes from true pancreatic cysts.
* Mature wall typically takes **>4 weeks** to form post-**Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)** or trauma.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Most common cystic lesion of the pancreas (75-80% of all pancreatic cysts).
* In HK Hospital Authority (HA) network, primarily follows severe biliary pancreatitis (gallstones) or alcohol-induced pancreatitis.
* Affects ~10-15% of patients post-severe acute pancreatitis.
* Male > Female (linked to alcohol/smoking demographics locally).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Pancreas and Lesser Sac
* Fluid typically accumulates in the **Lesser Sac (小網膜囊)**.
* Anterior boundary: **Stomach (胃)** and **Gastrocolic omentum (胃結腸大網膜)**.
* Posterior boundary: **Pancreas (胰臟)**.
* Inferior boundary: **Transverse mesocolon (橫結腸繫膜)**.
* Close proximity to **Splenic Artery (脾動脈)** (superiorly) and **Gastroduodenal Artery (胃十二指腸動脈)** (head of pancreas) → ↑ risk of pseudoaneurysm.

### Pathophysiology of Pseudocyst Formation
* Severe inflammation/trauma → **Pancreatic duct disruption (胰管破裂)**.
* Extravasation of pancreatic enzymes (amylase/lipase/proteases) into peripancreatic space.
* Acute fluid collection (0-4 weeks) → elicits intense local inflammatory response.
* Surrounding structures (stomach, colon, mesocolon) wall off the fluid.
* Maturation (>4 weeks) → thick fibrous/granulation capsule forms → **Pseudocyst**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Pancreatic Pseudocyst
* Persistent/recurrent **Epigastric pain (上腹痛)** post-pancreatitis.
* Gastric outlet obstruction symptoms: **Nausea (噁心)**, **Vomiting (嘔吐)**, early satiety.
* Weight loss (↓ nutritional intake + malabsorption).
* **Obstructive Jaundice (阻塞性黃疸)** (if cyst compresses common bile duct).

### Key Clinical Signs of Pancreatic Pseudocyst
* Palpable, smooth, tender **Epigastric mass (上腹腫塊)**.
* Fullness in the upper abdomen.
* Sepsis signs (Fever, ↑ HR) → suggests **Infected Pseudocyst / Abscess (膿腫)**.
* Signs of acute hemorrhage (Shock, pallor) → suggests ruptured pseudoaneurysm.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Pancreatic Cystic Lesions
| Condition | Epithelium | Communication with Pancreatic Duct | Malignant Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pseudocyst** | -ve | Often +ve | None |
| **Serous Cystadenoma (SCA)** | +ve (Cuboidal, glycogen-rich) | -ve | Extremely rare (<1%) |
| **Mucinous Cystic Neoplasm (MCN)** | +ve (Columnar, mucin) | -ve | High (Premalignant) |
| **Intraductal Papillary Mucinous Neoplasm (IPMN)** | +ve (Columnar, mucin) | +ve | High (esp. Main duct type) |

### Discriminatory Features of Walled-Off Necrosis (WON)
* **Walled-Off Necrosis (WON)** also occurs >4 weeks post-necrotizing pancreatitis.
* Key difference: Contains **solid necrotic debris** (fat, pancreatic tissue), whereas pseudocyst contains **purely liquid/fluid**.
* Requires aggressive necrosectomy (VARD/endoscopic) rather than simple drainage.

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Laboratory Investigations
* **Serum Amylase (血清澱粉酶) / Lipase**: Persistently ↑ suggests ongoing leak/pseudocyst.
* **CBC**: ↑ WBC (infection/abscess), ↓ Hb (hemorrhage).
* **LFTs**: ↑ Bilirubin, ↑ ALP/GGT (CBD compression).
* **CRP**: Tracks inflammatory response.

### Gold Standard Imaging for Pancreatic Pseudocyst
* **Contrast-Enhanced CT Pancreas (電腦掃描)**: Modality of choice.
* Findings: Well-circumscribed, round/oval fluid density collection.
* Enhancing thick capsule.
* Assesses size, number, anatomical relations, and complication (pseudoaneurysm, gastric/CBD compression).

### Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS) and Cyst Fluid Analysis
* **EUS (內視鏡超聲波)**: Crucial in HA protocol for distinguishing pseudocyst from neoplasms & guiding drainage.
* **FNA (Fine Needle Aspiration)** fluid analysis discriminates cyst type:

| Biomarker / Feature | Pseudocyst | Serous Cystadenoma | Mucinous Neoplasm (MCN/IPMN) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Amylase** | Very ↑ (>250 U/L) | Low | Low (↑ in IPMN if duct communicates) |
| **CEA** | Low (<192 ng/mL) | Low | Very ↑ (>192 ng/mL) |
| **Cytology** | Inflammatory cells | Glycogen-rich cells | Mucin-producing cells |
| **Viscosity** | Low (Watery) | Low (Watery) | High (Viscous) |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management Criteria
* **Indications**: Asymptomatic, uncomplicated cysts, regardless of size. (Historical size cut-off >6cm is obsolete in modern HA guidelines if asymptomatic).
* **Approach**: Watchful waiting, serial CT/USG monitoring.
* Optimization of nutrition (enteric feeding) and strict alcohol abstinence.

### Indications for Pancreatic Pseudocyst Intervention
* Symptoms (persistent pain, early satiety, vomiting).
* Complications (Infection/abscess, bleeding, rupture, CBD/gastric outlet obstruction).
* Progressive enlargement.
* Diagnostic uncertainty (rule out malignancy).

### Endoscopic and Surgical Management Algorithm
| Modality | Indication / Context | HA Preference / Efficacy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Endoscopic Transmural Drainage** | Thick-walled cyst adherent to stomach/duodenum. | **1st Line (Gold Standard)**. EUS-guided Cystogastrostomy via LAMS (Lumen-Apposing Metal Stent). |
| **Surgical Drainage** | Endoscopic failure, distance >1cm from stomach, suspicion of malignancy. | 2nd Line. Roux-en-Y Cystojejunostomy or surgical Cystogastrostomy. |
| **Percutaneous Drainage (Radiological)** | Septic, unstable patient unfit for endoscopy/surgery. | Avoid if possible. High risk of **Pancreaticocutaneous fistula (胰臟皮瘻管)**. |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Operative Principles for Endoscopic Cystogastrostomy
* Use **EUS guidance** to locate cyst, confirm lack of intervening vessels (avoid varices/gastroduodenal artery).
* Puncture cyst wall via posterior gastric wall (transgastric).
* Deploy **LAMS (Lumen-Apposing Metal Stent)** or double-pigtail plastic stents.
* Ensure cyst wall is mature (otherwise risk of free perforation into peritoneal cavity).

### Key Operative Principles for Surgical Cystojejunostomy / Cystogastrostomy
* Intraoperative USG to locate cyst.
* **Biopsy the cyst wall (囊腫壁活檢)**: Mandatory to rule out cystic neoplasm.
* Dependent drainage: Anastomosis must be at the lowest point of the cyst.
* Roux-en-Y loop ensures no tension and prevents reflux.

### Surgical Consent and Counseling Issues
* Specific risks: Bleeding, perforation, stent migration, infection.
* Recurrence risk (~10%).
* Possibility of open conversion (if laparoscopic).
* Need for future stent removal (typically 4-6 weeks post-LAMS placement).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-Operative Care and ERAS
* Fasting initially, progress to clear fluids on POD 1 if no clinical signs of leak.
* Monitor drains (if surgical). Test drain fluid for amylase to detect anastomotic leak.
* Routine use of PPIs (↓ gastric acid secretion to protect cystogastrostomy site).
* Avoid NSAIDs if high bleeding risk; rely on multimodal analgesia (Paracetamol + regional block).

### Long-Term Rehabilitation and Follow-Up
* Follow-up CT/EUS at 4-6 weeks to document cyst resolution.
* Schedule LAMS removal via OGD to prevent stent embedding or delayed bleeding.
* Management of underlying cause (e.g., Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for gallstone disease).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications
* **Bleeding (出血)**: From puncture site, gastric wall, or underlying pseudoaneurysm.
* **Perforation (穿孔)**: Misplaced stent → intra-abdominal spillage → peritonitis.
* **Stent Migration (支架移位)**: Into the cyst or stomach.

### Intermediate and Late Disease Complications (If left untreated)
* **Infection/Abscess**: Secondary bacterial seeding.
* **Rupture**: Into peritoneal cavity (causing generalized peritonitis/ascites) or GI tract (can be therapeutic or cause GI bleed).
* **Pseudoaneurysm (假性動脈瘤)**: Enzymatic digestion of adjacent splenic, gastroduodenal, or pancreaticoduodenal arteries.
* **Fistulization**: To pleura (pancreaticopleural fistula → massive pleural effusion).

### Prognosis of Pancreatic Pseudocyst
* Spontaneous resolution occurs in ~40-50% of acute pseudocysts within 6 weeks.
* EUS-guided drainage has success rate >90% with low recurrence (<10%).
* Long-term prognosis depends primarily on the progression of the underlying Chronic Pancreatitis or recurrent Acute Pancreatitis.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most feared life-threatening complication of a pancreatic pseudocyst? Answer: Pseudoaneurysm rupture (usually splenic or gastroduodenal artery) secondary to enzymatic digestion of the arterial wall, presenting with sudden cyst expansion and massive hemorrhage (hemosuccus pancreaticus).
* Viva Question: How do you definitively diagnose a ruptured splenic artery pseudoaneurysm associated with a pseudocyst, and what is the treatment? Answer: CT Angiogram. First-line management is urgent interventional radiology (IR) angioembolization; if it fails, emergency laparotomy and surgical ligation.
* Viva Question: A patient with a pancreatic cyst has fluid aspirated showing very high CEA and low amylase. What is the likely diagnosis? Answer: Mucinous cystic neoplasm (MCN) or a macrocystic Serous Cystadenoma (though SCA usually has low CEA). It is definitively a mucinous neoplasm, requiring surgical resection due to malignant potential.
* Trap: Recommending immediate percutaneous drainage for an asymptomatic 7cm pancreatic pseudocyst. Pearl: Size criteria alone no longer mandate drainage in modern HA protocols; asymptomatic pseudocysts should be managed conservatively with serial imaging.
* Trap: Stating percutaneous drainage is a good first-line option for symptomatic pseudocysts. Pearl: Percutaneous drainage should be avoided unless the patient is critically ill/septic, as it carries a very high risk of creating a permanent pancreaticocutaneous fistula.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to biopsy the cyst wall during an open or laparoscopic surgical drainage (e.g., cystojejunostomy). Always send a full-thickness biopsy of the cyst wall to pathology to definitively rule out an underlying cystic neoplasm (like MCN) that may have been misdiagnosed as a pseudocyst.
