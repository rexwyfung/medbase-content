# Testicular Tumours (睾丸腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Testicular Tumour
* **Solid neoplasm** arising from testicular tissue
* **Primary classification**: **Germ Cell Tumours (GCTs) (生殖細胞瘤)** account for 95% of cases
* **Non-Germ Cell Tumours** (Leydig/Sertoli cell tumours) account for remaining 5%

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Testicular Tumours
* **Incidence**: Relatively uncommon in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) compared to Caucasian populations, but remains the **most common solid malignancy** in young men
* **Peak Age**: Bimodal distribution → 15–35 years (NSGCTs) & 30–40 years (Seminomas)
* **Risk Factors**: 
* **Cryptorchidism (隱睪症)** (↑ risk by 4-6x, even post-orchidopexy)
* **Family History** in first-degree relatives
* **Prior Contralateral Testicular Cancer**
* **Klinefelter's Syndrome** (↑ risk for extragonadal GCTs)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy and Lymphatic Drainage of the Testis
* **Embryological Origin**: Genital ridge (intra-abdominal) → descends via inguinal canal
* **Arterial Supply**: **Testicular Artery** (branch of abdominal aorta)
* **Venous Drainage**: 
* **Right Testicular Vein** → Inferior Vena Cava (IVC)
* **Left Testicular Vein** → Left Renal Vein
* **Lymphatic Drainage (CRITICAL)**: Follows gonadal vessels → **Retroperitoneal / Para-aortic lymph nodes (旁主動脈淋巴結)** (L1-L2 level)
* **Pathological Drainage**: Scrotal skin involvement or prior scrotal surgery (violating tunica vaginalis) diverts lymphatic spread → **Superficial Inguinal Lymph Nodes (腹股溝淋巴結)**

### Pathophysiology and Classification of Germ Cell Tumours
| Classification | Subtypes | Pathophysiology & Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Seminoma (精原細胞瘤)** | Classic, Anaplastic, Spermatocytic | Slow growing, highly radiosensitive, originates from seminiferous tubules. Typical in 30-40 yr olds. |
| **Non-Seminomatous GCT (NSGCT) (非精原細胞生殖細胞瘤)** | Embryonal, Yolk Sac, Choriocarcinoma, Teratoma | Aggressive, early haematogenous/lymphatic spread, mixed histology common. Typical in 15-35 yr olds. |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Testicular Tumours
* **Painless Testicular Mass (無痛性硬塊)** → Classic presentation (most common)
* **Scrotal Heaviness/Dragging Sensation** → Due to ↑ testicular weight
* **Acute Scrotal Pain** (10% of cases) → Due to spontaneous intra-tumoral haemorrhage or infarction
* **Gynaecomastia (男性女乳症)** → Systemic endocrine effect from ↑ **βHCG** (esp. choriocarcinoma)
* **Metastatic Symptoms** → Back pain (para-aortic nodal mass), haemoptysis/dyspnoea (lung metastases), supraclavicular mass

### Clinical Signs on Scrotal Examination
* **Palpation**: Hard, irregular, non-tender intra-testicular lump
* **Location**: Arises *from* the testis (cannot separate from testis, unlike epididymal cysts)
* **Transillumination**: **-ve** (solid tumour does not transilluminate)
* **Secondary Signs**: 
* Reactive **Hydrocele (陰囊水腫)** (makes palpation difficult; warrants mandatory imaging)
* Palpable abdominal mass or supraclavicular lymphadenopathy (Troisier's sign)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of a Scrotal Mass
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Testicular Tumour (睾丸腫瘤)** | Painless, hard, intra-testicular, -ve transillumination. |
| **Epididymal Cyst (附睪囊腫)** | Separate from testis (superior/posterior), +ve transillumination, fluid-filled. |
| **Hydrocele (陰囊水腫)** | Can "get above it", +ve transillumination. Testis usually impalpable. |
| **Epididymo-orchitis (附睪睾丸炎)** | Acute severe pain, erythema, fever, +ve prehn's sign, ↑ Doppler flow. |
| **Testicular Torsion (睾丸扭轉)** | Sudden severe pain, high-riding testis, absent cremasteric reflex, ↓/absent Doppler flow. |
| **Varicocele (精索靜脈曲張)** | "Bag of worms" sensation, mostly left-sided, decompresses when supine. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Laboratory Investigations and Tumour Markers
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC) & Renal/Liver Function (RFT/LFT)** → Pre-op baseline, assess for hepatic metastases
* **Serum Tumour Markers (CRITICAL for diagnosis, staging, & monitoring)**:
* **Alpha-Fetoprotein (αFP)**: ↑ in Yolk Sac & Embryonal. **NEVER** ↑ in pure Seminoma. (Half-life: 5-7 days)
* **Beta-Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (βHCG)**: ↑ in Choriocarcinoma (100%), Embryonal, & some Seminomas (15%). (Half-life: 24-36 hrs)
* **Lactate Dehydrogenase (LDH)**: Non-specific marker of high tumour burden/tissue necrosis. Prognostic utility in IGCCCG classification.

### Radiological Imaging for Primary Testicular Evaluation
* **Scrotal Ultrasound (USS)**: **Gold Standard 1st Line Imaging**
* Differentiates solid vs cystic lesions
* Characteristic finding: Hypoechoic intra-testicular mass with internal vascularity
* Crucial for assessing testis obscured by tense reactive hydrocele

### Staging Imaging for Metastatic Disease
* **Contrast CT Chest, Abdomen, & Pelvis (CT C/A/P)**: 
* Standard HA staging protocol for all confirmed/suspected testicular malignancies
* Assesses retroperitoneal (para-aortic) lymphadenopathy and pulmonary metastases

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Immediate HA Clinical Protocols
* **Sperm Banking (精子儲存)**: **Mandatory** offering prior to any surgical or oncological intervention due to irreversible infertility risks.
* **Tumour Marker Kinetics**: Must draw markers pre-operatively and post-operatively to track clearance according to biological half-lives.

### Primary Surgical Management
* **Radical Inguinal Orchiectomy (根治性腹股溝睾丸切除術)**: **Gold Standard** diagnostic & therapeutic initial step for ALL suspected cases.
* **Avoid Scrotal Biopsy**: Strictly contraindicated due to risk of seeding tumour cells into scrotal skin & altering lymphatic drainage to superficial inguinal nodes.

### Adjuvant Oncological Management Strategies
| Tumour Type & Stage | Adjuvant Management Algorithm |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 1 Seminoma** | Active surveillance (preferred HA protocol) OR single-agent Carboplatin |
| **Stage >1 Seminoma** | Radiotherapy to para-aortic nodes OR BEP Chemotherapy (Bleomycin, Etoposide, Platinum) |
| **Stage 1 NSGCT** | Active surveillance OR primary Retroperitoneal Lymph Node Dissection (RPLND) OR single-cycle BEP |
| **Stage >1 NSGCT** | Multi-agent BEP Chemotherapy ± post-chemo RPLND for residual masses |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Steps for Radical Inguinal Orchiectomy
* **Incision**: Inguinal incision (parallel to inguinal ligament). NEVER scrotal.
* **Exposure**: Open external oblique aponeurosis to expose inguinal canal.
* **Early Ligation**: Mobilize spermatic cord and apply atraumatic clamp at the **deep inguinal ring** FIRST → prevents haematogenous/lymphatic dissemination during testicular manipulation.
* **Delivery**: Deliver testis from scrotum into the inguinal wound.
* **Excision**: Ligate spermatic cord structures (vas deferens and gonadal vessels) separately with non-absorbable sutures at the deep ring; excise testis and cord *en bloc*.

### Pre-operative Consent and Counseling
* **Infertility/Subfertility**: Risk from loss of testicle and downstream chemo/radiotherapy.
* **Hypogonadism**: Need for lifelong testosterone replacement if contralateral testis is abnormal or absent.
* **Testicular Prosthesis**: Offer insertion of silicone prosthesis at the time of primary surgery or delayed procedure.
* **Cosmesis & Nerve Injury**: Risk of ilioinguinal nerve injury → numbness over inner thigh/base of penis.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* **Scrotal Support**: Provide scrotal support undergarments and ice packs to ↓ scrotal haematoma/oedema.
* **Analgesia**: Multimodal PO analgesics (Paracetamol + NSAIDs); discharge on post-op day 0 or 1.
* **Wound Care**: Keep inguinal incision dry; monitor for surgical site infection (SSI).

### Long-term Rehabilitation and Survivorship
* **Psychological Support**: Referral to clinical psychology/HA cancer support groups for body image and sexual dysfunction coping.
* **Oncological Surveillance**: 5-10 year strict follow-up schedule (Clinical exam, Tumour markers, CXR, CT C/A/P) to monitor for recurrence.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early and Intermediate Surgical Complications
* **Scrotal Haematoma**: Most common early complication; ↓ risk with strict haemostasis and scrotal support.
* **Wound Infection (SSI)**: Requires PO antibiotics.
* **Ilioinguinal Nerve Neuropathy**: Chronic neuropathic groin pain or localized paresthesia.

### Late Oncological Complications and Prognostic Stratification
* **Bleomycin Toxicity**: Pulmonary fibrosis (monitor with pulmonary function tests).
* **Cisplatin Toxicity**: Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity (tinnitus), peripheral neuropathy.
* **Secondary Malignancy**: ↑ risk of secondary leukaemias or solid tumours decades later due to radiotherapy/chemotherapy.
* **Prognosis**: Stratified by **IGCCCG** (International Germ Cell Cancer Collaborative Group) into Good, Intermediate, and Poor risk categories. Overall 5-year survival >95% for early-stage disease.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why do we never biopsy a testicular mass via the scrotum? Answer: A trans-scrotal approach violates the tunica vaginalis, seeding tumour cells into the scrotal subcutaneous tissue and altering the lymphatic drainage from the retroperitoneal (para-aortic) nodes to the superficial inguinal nodes, upstaging the cancer.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with a pure seminoma can have an elevated AFP. Pearl: Pure seminomas NEVER secrete alpha-fetoprotein (αFP). If a biopsy shows "pure seminoma" but αFP is elevated, the tumour MUST be treated clinically as a Non-Seminomatous Germ Cell Tumour (NSGCT) because occult yolk sac or embryonal elements are present.
* Pitfall: Reassuring a young man with a tense hydrocele and no palpable testicular mass without imaging. Pearl: A tense hydrocele can mask an underlying primary testicular neoplasm. You must order a scrotal ultrasound to exclude malignancy in any hydrocele where the testis cannot be adequately palpated.
* Viva Question: What is the primary lymphatic drainage of the right and left testicles? Answer: Both drain to the para-aortic/retroperitoneal lymph nodes at the L1-L2 level, following the embryological descent of the gonadal vessels.
* Trap: Forgetting to draw baseline tumour markers before radical orchiectomy. Pearl: Pre-operative αFP, βHCG, and LDH are mandatory. Their post-operative decline must be tracked against their biological half-lives (βHCG: ~1 day, αFP: ~5-7 days) to confirm complete excision or indicate residual metastatic disease.
