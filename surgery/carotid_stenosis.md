# Carotid Stenosis (頸動脈狹窄)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Carotid Stenosis (頸動脈狹窄定義)
* **Definition:** Pathological narrowing of extracranial **Internal Carotid Artery (ICA) (頸內動脈)**
* **Location:** Typically occurs at carotid bifurcation / carotid bulb
* **Aetiology:** 90% 2° to **Atherosclerosis (動脈粥樣硬化)**
* **Consequence:** → Ischemic stroke (缺血性中風) or **Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA) (短暫性腦缺血發作)**

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (香港本地流行病學)
* **Stroke Burden:** Stroke is 4th leading cause of death in HK
* **Carotid Contribution:** Extracranial carotid stenosis accounts for ~15-20% of ischemic strokes in HK HA hospitals
* **Risk Factors (HA Data):**
* **Hypertension (高血壓)** (Most prevalent)
* **Diabetes Mellitus (糖尿病)**
* **Hyperlipidaemia (高脂血症)**
* **Smoking (吸煙)**
* **Age:** ↑ incidence in males > 65 years

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Carotid Bifurcation (頸動脈分叉處外科解剖)
* **Common Carotid Artery (CCA):** Bifurcates at C4 level (upper border of thyroid cartilage)
* **Internal Carotid Artery (ICA):** Posterolateral, NO cervical branches, larger bulb
* **External Carotid Artery (ECA):** Anteromedial, multiple cervical branches (first branch = Superior Thyroid Artery)
* **Key Adjacent Nerves:**
* **Hypoglossal Nerve (CN XII) (舌下神經):** Crosses ICA/ECA anteriorly
* **Vagus Nerve (CN X) (迷走神經):** Lies posterolateral in carotid sheath
* **Marginal Mandibular Branch of CN VII (面神經下頜緣支):** At risk during high neck incisions

### Pathophysiology of Atherosclerotic Plaque Formation (動脈粥樣硬化斑塊形成病理生理學)
* **Hemodynamics:** Carotid bulb has ↑ cross-sectional area → low shear stress & flow reversal
* **Endothelial Injury:** Shear stress + metabolic factors (LDL, glucose) → endothelial dysfunction
* **Plaque Formation:** Lipid deposition + macrophage infiltration (foam cells) + smooth muscle proliferation
* **Plaque Instability:** Thin fibrous cap + necrotic lipid core + intraplaque hemorrhage → rupture

### Mechanisms of TIA and Stroke (短暫性腦缺血發作與中風機制)
* **Atheroembolism (Microembolisation):** Plaque ruptures → platelet/fibrin aggregate or cholesterol crystals dislodge → embolize to cerebral/retinal circulation (Most common mechanism)
* **Thrombosis:** Ruptured plaque exposes thrombogenic core → acute in situ vessel occlusion
* **Hypoperfusion:** Severe stenosis (>90%) + inadequate collateral flow via Circle of Willis → global hemodynamic failure (Watershed infarcts)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Classic Neurological Symptoms of Carotid Stenosis (頸動脈疾病神經症狀)
* **Hemiparesis/Hemiplegia (偏癱):** Contralateral motor weakness (Face/Arm > Leg)
* **Hemisensory Loss (偏側感覺喪失):** Contralateral numbness/tingling
* **Dysphasia/Aphasia (失語症):** Expressive (Broca) or Receptive (Wernicke) if dominant hemisphere involved (usually Left)
* **Symptom Duration:** TIA resolves <24h (usually <1h), Stroke persists >24h

### Characteristics of Amaurosis Fugax (短暫性黑蒙特徵)
* **Visual Phenomenon:** Ipsilateral transient monocular blindness
* **Patient Description:** "Curtain falling down" over one eye
* **Duration:** Usually lasts minutes, total recovery
* **Mechanism:** Microemboli (Hollenhorst plaques) to **Ophthalmic Artery (眼動脈)** / Central Retinal Artery

### Key Clinical Signs on Physical Examination (體格檢查關鍵臨床體徵)
* **Carotid Bruit (頸動脈雜音):** Auscultated at angle of jaw
* **Bruit Paradox:** Disappears when stenosis >90% (flow too low to create turbulence)
* **Fundoscopy:** +ve **Hollenhorst plaques (霍倫霍斯特斑塊)** (cholesterol crystals in retinal arterioles)
* **Neurological Deficits:** Contralateral upper motor neuron signs (+ve Babinski, hyperreflexia)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnoses for Acute Neurological Deficits (神經功能缺損鑑別診斷)
* **Cardioembolic Stroke:** Atrial fibrillation, valvular heart disease
* **Lacunar Infarct:** Small vessel lipohyalinosis 2° to severe hypertension
* **Hemorrhagic Stroke:** Intracerebral hemorrhage, Subarachnoid hemorrhage
* **Intracranial Neoplasm:** Primary brain tumor or metastases
* **Hypoglycemia:** Important metabolic mimic (always check Dxt)

### Differentiating Carotid vs Vertebrobasilar Ischemia (頸動脈與椎基底動脈缺血鑑別)

| Clinical Feature | Carotid Territory (Anterior Circulation) | Vertebrobasilar Territory (Posterior Circulation) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Visual Loss** | Monocular (Ipsilateral Amaurosis Fugax) | Binocular (Homonymous hemianopia, Cortical blindness) |
| **Motor Weakness** | Unilateral (Contralateral) | Bilateral (Drop attacks, quadriparesis) |
| **Cranial Nerves** | Dysphasia (Dominant hemisphere) | Diplopia, Dysarthria, Dysphagia, Vertigo |
| **Coordination** | Normal | Ataxia, Cerebellar signs |
| **Consciousness** | Usually preserved | Altered / Syncope |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Imaging for Carotid Stenosis (頸動脈狹窄第一線影像學檢查)
* **Carotid Duplex Ultrasound (DUS) (頸動脈多普勒超聲):**
* **Role:** Initial screening & surveillance (non-invasive, bedside)
* **Measurement:** Peak Systolic Velocity (PSV) ↑ proportionally with stenosis severity
* **Plaque Morphology:** Identifies echolucent (unstable) vs calcified plaques

### Confirmatory Imaging & Brain Assessment (確診影像與大腦評估)
* **CT Angiography (CTA) (電腦斷層血管攝影):**
* **Role:** Gold standard anatomical roadmap for pre-operative planning
* **Information:** Accurate stenosis sizing, tandem lesions, aortic arch anatomy
* **Brain CT/MRI:**
* **Role:** Rule out hemorrhage, identify previous silent infarcts, assess collateral circulation

### Pre-operative Cardiovascular Workup (術前心血管檢查)
* **ECG:** Rule out silent MI, AF
* **Echocardiogram:** Rule out mural thrombus, valvular vegetations
* **Fasting Bloods:** Glucose, HbA1c, Lipid panel, Renal function (CTA contrast prep)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Medical Management Protocol (香港醫管局內科治療方案)
* **Antiplatelet Therapy (抗血小板治療):** Dual antiplatelet (Aspirin + Clopidogrel) acute post-TIA, then single agent lifelong
* **Lipid Modification:** High-intensity Statin (e.g., Atorvastatin) targeting LDL <1.8 mmol/L
* **Blood Pressure Control:** Strict HA targets <140/90 mmHg (caution: avoid abrupt BP drops preventing watershed ischemia)
* **Lifestyle:** Smoking cessation, stringent glycemic control

### Surgical Indications for Symptomatic Carotid Stenosis (有症狀狹窄手術指徵)
| Stenosis Severity (NASCET) | Management Protocol (HA Guidelines) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **70% - 99%** | **Strongly Indicated** for Carotid Endarterectomy (CEA). Maximum benefit. Target surgery <14 days of symptom onset. |
| **50% - 69%** | **Consider CEA**. Marginal benefit. Dependent on patient age, gender, plaque morphology. |
| **< 50%** | **Not Indicated**. Medical therapy only. Surgical risk outweighs benefit. |

### Surgical Indications for Asymptomatic Carotid Stenosis (無症狀狹窄手術指徵)
* **> 70% Stenosis:** Highly selected patients only
* **Criteria for Surgery:** >5 year life expectancy + low surgical risk (<3% perioperative stroke/death)
* **Trend:** Increasingly managed medically with intensive statin/antiplatelet therapy due to improving medical outcomes

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Carotid Endarterectomy (CEA) Operative Steps (頸動脈內膜切除術手術步驟)
* **Anesthesia:** General (GA) or Local/Regional (allows awake neurological monitoring)
* **Incision:** Anterior to Sternocleidomastoid (SCM)
* **Mobilization:** Divide common facial vein, identify/protect CN X and CN XII
* **Systemic Heparinization:** Administer IV Heparin before clamping
* **Clamping:** Clamp sequence → ICA, then CCA, then ECA
* **Cerebral Protection:** Insert intraluminal shunt if contralateral flow inadequate (measured by stump pressure <50mmHg or awake patient neuro changes)
* **Arteriotomy & Endarterectomy:** Remove atheromatous plaque at correct cleavage plane
* **Closure:** **Patch angioplasty (補片血管成形術)** (Dacron/Bovine pericardium) to prevent re-stenosis
* **Flushing:** Flush out air/debris before releasing ICA clamp

### Carotid Artery Stenting (CAS) Principles (頸動脈支架植入術原則)
* **Approach:** Endovascular via femoral or radial artery
* **Indications:** High open surgical risk ("Hostile neck" - previous neck radiation, radical neck dissection, recurrent stenosis, tracheostomy)
* **Mandatory Adjunct:** Embolic Protection Device (EPD) deployed distal to plaque before stenting

### Informed Consent and Key Operative Risks (知情同意與主要手術風險)
* **Stroke/Death Risk:** 2-3% risk during CEA (must document)
* **Cranial Nerve Palsy:** ~5% risk (usually transient)
* **Bleeding/Hematoma:** Risk of airway compromise
* **Myocardial Infarction:** Most common cause of perioperative mortality

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Neurological Monitoring (術後即時神經功能監測)
* **Setting:** Intensive Care Unit (ICU) / High Dependency Unit (HDU) for 24 hours
* **Neurological Checks:** Hourly Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) and pupil checks
* **Surgical Site:** Frequent monitoring for neck swelling / hematoma formation

### Hemodynamic Control Post-Carotid Endarterectomy (術後血流動力學控制)
* **Avoid Hypertension (SBP >160):** Prevents neck hematoma and **Cerebral Hyperperfusion Syndrome (腦過度灌注綜合徵)**. Use IV Labetalol/GTN if necessary.
* **Avoid Hypotension (SBP <120):** Prevents graft thrombosis. Use IV fluids or Vasopressors if necessary.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Cranial Nerve Injuries in Carotid Surgery (頸動脈手術腦神經損傷)
* **Hypoglossal Nerve (CN XII):** Tongue deviates *towards* the side of injury
* **Vagus Nerve (CN X):** Vocal cord paralysis → Hoarseness (recurrent laryngeal branch)
* **Marginal Mandibular Nerve (CN VII branch):** Drooping of ipsilateral corner of mouth
* **Glossopharyngeal Nerve (CN IX):** Dysphagia, impaired gag reflex (rare, high ICA lesions)

### Early Post-operative Complications (< 30 days) (早期術後併發症)
* **Acute Neck Hematoma:** Medical emergency. Tracheal deviation → requires immediate bedside wound opening before return to theater.
* **Stroke:** Embolization during surgery or acute post-op thrombosis.
* **Cerebral Hyperperfusion Syndrome:** Loss of cerebral autoregulation + sudden ↑ flow → severe unilateral headache, seizures, focal deficits, intracerebral hemorrhage.

### Late Complications and Long-term Prognosis (晚期併發症與長期預後)
* **Re-stenosis:** Occurs in 5-10%. Usually asymptomatic myointimal hyperplasia (<2 years) or recurrent atherosclerosis (>2 years).
* **Prognosis:** CEA significantly ↓ stroke risk in symptomatic >70% stenosis (absolute risk reduction ~16% at 5 years).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the target population for Carotid Endarterectomy according to NASCET? Answer: Symptomatic patients with 70-99% ICA stenosis.
* Trap: Assuming a loud carotid bruit indicates the most severe stenosis. Pearl: A bruit disappears when stenosis is >90% because flow volume is insufficient to generate audible turbulence; a silent neck can be a critical stenosis.
* Pitfall: Rushing a patient to CT brain when they develop a rapidly expanding post-operative neck hematoma with stridor. Bedside wound opening is immediately required to secure the airway.
* Viva Question: During CEA, which artery is clamped first, and which is unclamped last? Answer: ICA is clamped first (to prevent distal embolization) and unclamped last (after flushing debris into ECA).
* Trap: Prescribing aggressive antihypertensives to normalize a patient's BP of 170/90 immediately upon admission for an acute symptomatic carotid stenosis. Pearl: Permissive hypertension is required acutely to maintain collateral cerebral perfusion pressure across the stenotic segment.
* Pitfall: Discharging a patient post-TIA without confirming their antiplatelet and high-dose statin adherence. Stroke risk is highest in the first 48 hours post-TIA.
