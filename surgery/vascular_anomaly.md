# Vascular Anomaly (血管異常)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition and ISSVA Classification of Vascular Anomalies
* **ISSVA Classification (國際血管異常研究學會分類)** differentiates lesions by cellular kinetics:
* **Vascular Tumors (血管腫瘤):** Endothelial hyperplasia (e.g., **Infantile Hemangioma (嬰兒血管瘤)**)
* **Vascular Malformations (血管畸形):** Structural dysmorphogenesis with normal endothelial turnover (classified by vessel type: Capillary, Venous, Lymphatic, Arteriovenous)

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Vascular Anomalies
* **Infantile Hemangioma (IH):** ~4-5% in Asian infants (slightly ↓ than Caucasians)
* F:M ratio ≈ 3:1
* ↑ incidence in premature/low birth weight infants in HA neonatal units
* **Vascular Malformations:** Present at birth, grow proportionally with patient, 1% prevalence

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Pathophysiology of Vascular Tumors (Infantile Hemangioma)
* Endothelial cell proliferation → **GLUT-1 +ve**
* **Triphasic Life Cycle:**
* 1. Proliferation Phase (0-1 yr): Rapid growth
* 2. Involution Phase (1-5 yrs): Spontaneous regression
* 3. Involuted Phase (>5 yrs): Residual fibrofatty tissue/telangiectasia

### Pathophysiology of High-Flow Vascular Malformations
* **Arteriovenous Malformation/AVM (動靜脈畸形):** Direct arterial-venous communication without capillary bed → **Nidus (畸形血管巢)**
* Arterial pressure transmits directly to veins → venous engorgement/varicosities
* Shunting → distal tissue ischemia (steal syndrome)
* ↑ venous return → ↑ cardiac output → Heart failure (rare but fatal)

### Pathophysiology of Low-Flow Vascular Malformations
* **Venous Malformation/VM (靜脈畸形):** Spongy, dysplastic venous channels with ↓ smooth muscle
* Blood stagnation → localized coagulopathy → calcification forming **Phleboliths (靜脈石)**
* **Lymphatic Malformation/LM (淋巴管畸形):** Abnormal lymphatic channel development (Macrocystic >1cm vs Microcystic <1cm)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Infantile Hemangioma
* Absent at birth (often precursor pale/erythematous macule)
* **Superficial:** Bright red, lobulated, raised nodule ("Strawberry")
* **Deep:** Bluish soft tissue swelling, normal overlying skin
* Firm during proliferation, compressible during involution

### Clinical Presentation of Venous Malformation
* Soft, compressible, bluish mass
* ↑ size with dependency or Valsalva maneuver
* Episodic pain 2° to thrombosis or phlebolith formation

### Clinical Presentation of Arteriovenous Malformation (AVM)
* Warm, pulsatile mass
* Palpable thrill & audible bruit
* Skin changes: Erythema, ulceration, bleeding, necrosis

### Clinical Presentation of Lymphatic Malformation
* Soft, non-compressible, transilluminating mass
* Commonly located in cervicofacial or axillary regions
* Sudden ↑ size/pain if intralesional hemorrhage or infection occurs

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Features of Vascular Tumors vs Malformations

| Clinical Feature | **Vascular Tumors (e.g., IH)** | **Vascular Malformations** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Post-natal (1-4 weeks) | Present at birth |
| **Growth Pattern** | Rapid proliferation → Involution | Proportional to child's growth |
| **Endothelial Turnover** | ↑ (Hyperplastic) | Normal |
| **Immunohistochemistry**| **GLUT-1 +ve** | **GLUT-1 -ve** |
| **Clinical Signs** | Firm, compressible later | +/- Bruit/Thrill (AVMs), Phleboliths (VMs) |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Imaging for Vascular Anomalies
* **Doppler Ultrasound (多普勒超聲波):**
* **IH:** Solid mass, high vessel density, high-flow arterial signal
* **VM:** Hypoechoic, compressible spaces, low/no flow, acoustic shadowing from **Phleboliths (靜脈石)**
* **AVM:** High-flow, low-resistance arteries, pulsatile veins

### Advanced Imaging for Complex Vascular Anomalies
* **MRI with Contrast (磁力共振掃描):** Gold standard for deep tissue mapping pre-intervention
* **VM:** T2 hyperintense, T1 isointense
* **AVM:** Signal flow voids
* **Diagnostic Angiography (血管造影):** Reserved for mapping high-flow AVMs immediately pre-embolization

### Schobinger Clinical Staging System for AVMs

| Stage | Clinical Features | Management Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I (Quiescence)** | Pink/bluish stain, warmth, AV shunting on Doppler | Observation |
| **II (Expansion)** | Enlargement, pulsations, thrill, bruit, tortuous veins | Intervene if symptomatic |
| **III (Destruction)** | Dystrophic skin, ulceration, bleeding, necrosis, pain | Embolization +/- Resection |
| **IV (Decompensation)**| High-output cardiac failure | Urgent intervention |

### Baseline Pre-operative Laboratory Workup
* **Coagulation Profile (凝血指標):** Mandatory for large VMs to rule out **Localized Intravascular Coagulopathy (LIC)**
* Check D-dimer (↑) and Fibrinogen (↓)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management of Vascular Anomalies
* **IH:** Active observation for small, asymptomatic, non-cosmetically sensitive lesions
* **Capillary Malformation (Port-wine stain):** Camouflage makeup, pulsed dye laser (titrated for Asian skin types to avoid hyperpigmentation)

### Medical Management of Infantile Hemangioma (HA Protocol)
* **Indications:** Life/vision-threatening (airway, periorbital), ulceration, functional impairment
* **1st Line:** Oral **Propranolol (普萘洛爾)** (2-3 mg/kg/day divided BD)
* Pre-treatment mandatory screening: ECG (exclude heart block), baseline blood glucose (hypoglycemia risk), BP
* **Topical:** Timolol maleate 0.5% drops for small, superficial lesions

### Interventional Management of Low-Flow Malformations (VM/LM)
* **1st Line:** Percutaneous **Sclerotherapy (硬化治療)** (e.g., Bleomycin, Sodium Tetradecyl Sulfate, Absolute Alcohol)
* Requires ultrasound/fluoroscopic guidance
* Surgical excision reserved for well-defined lesions or post-sclerotherapy residuum

### Management Algorithm for High-Flow Arteriovenous Malformations (AVMs)
* **1st Line:** Transarterial/Transvenous **Embolization (血管栓塞術)** (e.g., Onyx, coils)
* **Surgical Resection:** Performed 24-72 hours post-embolization to minimize blood loss
* Proximal ligation of feeding artery is strictly **CONTRAINDICATED** (→ collateralization → unresectable)

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Principles of Embolization and Resection for AVMs
* Complete eradication of the **Nidus (畸形血管巢)** is mandatory
* Embolization must penetrate the nidus, not just block feeding arteries
* Radical surgical margins (oncologic-style clearance) required to prevent recurrence

### Informed Consent Details for Vascular Interventions
* Risk of torrential intra-operative hemorrhage (cross-match blood, cell saver setup)
* Iatrogenic nerve injury (lesions frequently engulf neurovascular bundles)
* Need for multiple, staged procedures
* High risk of recurrence (especially AVMs)
* Skin necrosis / non-healing ulcers post-sclerotherapy

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Hemodynamic Monitoring
* Strict fluid input/output monitoring
* Vigilance for delayed hemorrhage post-resection/embolization
* Routine neurovascular checks to rule out compartment syndrome in extremities

### Post-Sclerotherapy Care Protocols
* Anticipate severe post-procedural swelling and pain → aggressive analgesia protocol
* Apply firm compression garments to ↑ sclerosant efficacy and ↓ dead space
* Monitor renal function (if large volumes of sclerosant/contrast used)

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications of Sclerotherapy and Embolization
* Non-target embolization → distal tissue ischemia / stroke / nerve palsy
* Systemic sclerosant toxicity (Bleomycin → pulmonary fibrosis; Alcohol → cardiovascular collapse)
* Severe anaphylaxis

### Surgical Complications of Vascular Resection
* Intra-operative torrential hemorrhage
* Damage to vital structures (e.g., Facial nerve in parotid hemangiomas)
* Flap necrosis or wound dehiscence

### Late Complications and Recurrence Prognosis
* **AVMs:** Extremely high recurrence rate if nidus incompletely excised; recurrences often more aggressive
* **IH:** Excellent prognosis post-involution; may require elective excision of redundant fibrofatty tissue

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Trap: Ligating the feeding artery of an AVM to control growth or bleeding. Pearl: Never ligate the feeding artery; it blocks future endovascular access and stimulates rapid recruitment of collateral vessels, making the lesion surgically unresectable.
* Viva Question: What is the most critical pre-operative check before starting oral Propranolol for an infantile hemangioma? Answer: An ECG to rule out congenital heart blocks (e.g., PHACE syndrome) and baseline blood glucose to monitor for profound hypoglycemia.
* Pitfall: Confusing a rapidly growing vascular mass in a 4-week-old newborn as a vascular malformation. Rapid post-natal proliferation is the absolute hallmark of an Infantile Hemangioma, which will be GLUT-1 positive.
* Viva Question: A patient with a massive venous malformation presents for surgery. What specific pre-operative blood test is critical? Answer: Coagulation profile, specifically checking D-dimers and fibrinogen, to rule out Localized Intravascular Coagulopathy (LIC) which can precipitate severe peri-operative hemorrhage.
