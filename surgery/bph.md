# Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (良性前列腺增生)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definitions of Prostate Pathology
* **Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) (良性前列腺增生):** Histological diagnosis indicating ↑ proliferation of stromal & epithelial cells in the prostate.
* **Benign Prostatic Enlargement (BPE):** Macroscopic ↑ in prostate size.
* **Bladder Outlet Obstruction (BOO):** Urodynamic def indicating mechanical obstruction.
* **Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (LUTS) (下泌尿道症狀):** Clinical symptom complex. *Note: The term "prostatism" is obsolete as LUTS are not specific to BPH.*

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence:** Highly prevalent in HK men >60 yrs; incidence ↑ exponentially with age.
* **HA Burden:** Leading cause of elective urological operations (e.g., TURP) & emergency admissions for Acute Urinary Retention (AUR) in HK public hospitals.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Prostate
* **Zonal Anatomy:** BPH strictly arises from the **Transition Zone (TZ) (移行區)** (periurethral area).
* **Comparison:** Prostate cancer (前列腺癌) typically arises from the Peripheral Zone (PZ) (邊緣區).
* **Key Landmark:** Verumontanum (精阜) → distal limit of resection during surgery to prevent external sphincter damage.

### Pathophysiology of Bladder Outlet Obstruction (BOO)
* **Static Component (Anatomic):** Androgen-driven (testosterone → DHT via $5\alpha$-reductase) → cellular proliferation → mechanical compression of prostatic urethra.
* **Dynamic Component (Physiologic):** ↑ $\alpha_1$-adrenergic sympathetic tone in prostatic stroma & bladder neck → smooth muscle contraction.
* **Detrusor Response:** BOO → detrusor hypertrophy → bladder wall trabeculation (小樑形成) / saccules (小囊) / diverticula (憩室) → end-stage detrusor failure & decompensation.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Storage Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (LUTS)
* **Frequency:** Voiding too often during daytime.
* **Nocturia (夜尿):** Waking at night $\geq 1$ time to void.
* **Urgency:** Sudden, compelling desire to pass urine.
* **Urge Incontinence:** Involuntary leakage preceded by urgency.

### Voiding Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (LUTS)
* **Hesitancy:** Delay in initiating micturition.
* **Reduced Stream:** Weak flow compared to previous baseline.
* **Straining:** Use of abdominal muscles to initiate/maintain flow.
* **Intermittency:** Urine flow stops & starts.

### Post-Micturition Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (LUTS)
* **Incomplete Emptying:** Sensation of residual urine post-void.
* **Post-Micturition Dribble:** Involuntary terminal leakage.

### Clinical Signs on Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)
* **Size:** Enlarged.
* **Consistency:** Smooth, firm, rubbery.
* **Contour:** Symmetrical, non-nodular.
* **Anatomy:** Palpable median sulcus (typically preserved).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Differential Diagnosis for LUTS

| Condition | Discriminatory Symptoms | Discriminatory Signs / DRE Findings | Initial Diagnostic Test |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **BPH** | Gradual onset LUTS, mixed storage/voiding | Symmetrical, smooth, rubbery enlargement | Uroflowmetry, US KUB |
| **Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)** | Bone pain (mets), wt loss, acute retention | Hard, nodular, asymmetrical, absent sulcus | ↑ PSA, mpMRI, TRUS biopsy |
| **Prostatitis** | Perineal pain, dysuria, fever, pelvic pain | Boggy, exquisitely tender, warm prostate | Urine culture, Expressed Prostatic Secretion |
| **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)** | Hx of trauma/STI/catheter, poor stream | Normal DRE, visible stricture on scope | Retrograde Urethrogram (RGU) |
| **Overactive Bladder (OAB)** | Pure storage LUTS (urgency, frequency) | Normal DRE | Urodynamics |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Laboratory Investigations for LUTS
* **Urinalysis (Dipstick/Microscopy):** R/O UTI (nitrites/leukocytes) & Haematuria (blood).
* **Renal Function (Urea/Creatinine/eGFR):** Assess for obstructive uropathy.
* **Urine Culture (MSU):** If dipstick +ve for infection.

### Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) (前列腺特異抗原) Interpretation
* **Baseline Measurement:** Risk stratification for BPH progression & screening for prostate cancer.
* **Confounders:** Falsely ↑ by UTI, instrumentation, AUR. *Must wait 6 weeks post-UTI to check.*
* **Medication Effects:** Falsely ↓ (by ~50%) in patients on $5\alpha$-reductase inhibitors (e.g., Finasteride, Dutasteride) for $>6$ months.

### Imaging Studies for BPH and BOO
* **US KUB (Ultrasound Kidneys-Ureters-Bladder):**
* **Findings:** Hydronephrosis (bilateral), Bladder wall thickening/trabeculation, Post-Void Residual (PVR) volume (>100mL = significant), Intravesical prostatic protrusion (IPP).
* **TRUS (Transrectal Ultrasound):** Used to accurately measure prostate volume prior to specific surgeries (e.g., HoLEP).

### Urodynamic and Endoscopic Pre-operative Workup
* **Uroflowmetry:** Non-invasive flow rate. $Q_{max} < 10$ mL/s strongly indicates BOO.
* **Urodynamic Studies (UDS):** Differentiates BOO from detrusor underactivity.
* **Flexible Cystoscopy (膀胱鏡檢查):** R/O bladder tumours (CIS), strictures, or bladder stones.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management for Mild LUTS
* **Lifestyle Modifications:** Evening fluid restriction, ↓ caffeine/alcohol, scheduled voiding.
* **Monitoring:** "Watchful waiting" with annual IPSS (International Prostate Symptom Score) & DRE.

### Medical Therapy for Moderate to Severe LUTS
* **$\alpha_1$-Blockers (e.g., Tamsulosin, Alfuzosin):**
* **Action:** Relaxes prostatic smooth muscle (dynamic component). Fast onset (days).
* **Side Effects:** Postural hypotension, Intraoperative Floppy Iris Syndrome (IFIS), retrograde ejaculation.
* **$5\alpha$-Reductase Inhibitors (5-ARIs) (e.g., Finasteride, Dutasteride):**
* **Action:** Blocks T → DHT conversion. Shrinks prostate volume (static component) by 20-30%.
* **Efficacy:** Slow onset (6 months). Prevents disease progression & ↓ risk of AUR.
* **Combination Therapy:** $\alpha_1$-Blocker + 5-ARI indicated for large prostates ($>30$ cc) & severe LUTS.

### Absolute Surgical Indications for BPH Intervention (HK HA Criteria)
* **Refractory Acute Urinary Retention (AUR) (急性尿瀦留):** Failed Trial Without Catheter (TWOC).
* **Recurrent UTI:** Due to static residual urine.
* **Recurrent Gross Haematuria:** Refractory to 5-ARIs.
* **Renal Impairment:** Bilateral hydronephrosis secondary to BOO.
* **Bladder Stones (膀胱結石):** Secondary to urinary stasis.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Gold Standard Operative Intervention: TURP
* **Procedure:** Transurethral Resection of the Prostate (TURP) (經尿道前列腺切除術).
* **Principle:** Endoscopic electrocautery loop resects hyperplastic TZ adenoma piece-by-piece, creating a wide funnel from bladder neck to verumontanum.
* **Fluid:** Uses non-conductive glycine irrigation (monopolar) or normal saline (bipolar).

### Minimally Invasive Surgical Alternatives
* **HoLEP (Holmium Laser Enucleation of the Prostate):** Enucleation of entire TZ adenoma. Better for very large glands ($>80$ cc), lower bleeding risk.
* **Greenlight PVP (Photoselective Vaporization):** Vaporizes tissue. Safe for patients on active anticoagulation.
* **UroLift / REZUM:** Office-based procedures preserving ejaculatory function.

### Pre-Operative Consent and Patient Counselling
* **Retrograde Ejaculation:** Most common (~70-90%). "Dry orgasm" due to open bladder neck.
* **Bleeding/Clot Retention:** May require return to theatre (washout/diathermy).
* **Urethral Stricture / Bladder Neck Contracture:** Long-term risk (1-5%).
* **Incontinence:** Stress incontinence due to external sphincter damage (<1%).
* **Erectile Dysfunction:** Rare, but must be counselled.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-Operative Ward Care
* **Catheterization:** 3-way 22/24Fr Foley catheter placed on traction.
* **Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI) (連續膀胱沖洗):** Uses 0.9% Normal Saline.
* **Titration:** Rate titrated by nursing staff to keep effluent light pink to clear.
* **Clot Management:** If blocked, perform manual bladder washout via Toomey syringe under strict asepsis.

### Rehabilitation and Catheter Management
* **Mobilization:** Early ambulation on Post-Op Day (POD) 1.
* **Trial Without Catheter (TWOC):** Stop CBI when urine clear → remove catheter POD 2-3.
* **Discharge Criteria:** Successful spontaneous voiding, minimal PVR on US, acceptable haematuria.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-Operative Complications
* **Primary/Secondary Haemorrhage:** Clot retention.
* **TUR Syndrome (經尿道前列腺切除綜合症):** Fluid overload + dilutional hyponatremia due to systemic absorption of hypotonic glycine irrigation (monopolar TURP). Presents with confusion, visual disturbances, bradycardia, hypertension.
* **Sepsis / UTI:** Prophylactic antibiotics must be optimized.
* **Failure to Void:** Requiring re-catheterization.

### Late Post-Operative Complications
* **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄):** From rigid instrument trauma.
* **Bladder Neck Contracture:** Scarring of the bladder neck.
* **Recurrence:** Tissue regrowth requiring re-operation (10% at 10 years).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of TUR syndrome? Answer: Dilutional hyponatremia, fluid overload (hypertension/bradycardia), and neurological changes (confusion/visual disturbances/seizures).
* Viva Question: A patient on Tamsulosin is scheduled for cataract surgery. What must the ophthalmologist be warned about? Answer: Intraoperative Floppy Iris Syndrome (IFIS).
* Trap: Mislabeling non-specific lower urinary symptoms as "prostatism". Pearl: Strictly use the term LUTS and systematically categorize into storage, voiding, and post-micturition phases, as BPH is only one of many potential aetiologies.
* Pitfall: Checking a serum PSA immediately after an acute urinary retention episode or acute UTI. The PSA will be falsely elevated and misleading. You must wait at least 6 weeks post-resolution to obtain a true baseline value.
* Pitfall: Performing a blind urethral catheterization forcefully in a man with suspected BPH and a stricture. This can create a false passage. Always escalate to a coudé (Tiemann) tip catheter or flexible cystoscopy-guided wire insertion.
