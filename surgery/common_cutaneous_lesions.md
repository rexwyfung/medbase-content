# Common Cutaneous Lesions (皮膚常見病變)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Benign vs Malignant Cutaneous Lesions
* **Benign Cutaneous Lesions (良性皮膚病變)**: Non-cancerous localized proliferations of epidermal, dermal, or subcutaneous tissue components (e.g., keratinocytes, adipocytes, fibroblasts) that do not breach the basement membrane or metastasize.
* **Malignant Cutaneous Lesions (惡性皮膚病變)**: Neoplastic transformations of skin cellular elements characterized by autonomous growth, local tissue invasion, destruction, and potential for regional or systemic metastasis.

### Local Epidemiology of Basal Cell Carcinoma in Hong Kong
* Most common **Skin Malignancy (皮膚惡性腫瘤)** in Hong Kong (accounting for >60% of non-melanoma skin cancers).
* Highest incidence in elderly population (>60 years old).
* Predominantly affects **Sun-exposed Areas (日光照射部位)** like the face, head, and neck.
* Equal gender distribution or slight male predominance.

### Local Epidemiology of Squamous Cell Carcinoma in Hong Kong
* Second most common non-melanoma skin cancer in Hong Kong (approx. 20-25%).
* Strongly linked to cumulative UV radiation, chronic non-healing wounds, or **Marjolin's Ulcer (馬熱蘭潰瘍)**.
* Frequently arises from pre-cancerous lesions such as **Actinic Keratosis (日光性角化病)** or **Bowen's Disease (鮑溫氏病)**.
* Higher incidence in outdoor workers and immunosuppressed patients (e.g., post-renal transplant).

### Local Epidemiology of Cutaneous Melanoma in Hong Kong
* Rare in Hong Kong compared to Western countries (<1% of all skin malignancies).
* **Acral Lentiginous Melanoma (肢端雀斑樣黑色素瘤)** is the most common subtype in Chinese patients (>50% of local cases).
* Frequently occurs on non-sun-exposed areas including palms, soles, and **Subungual Regions (甲下區)**.
* Often diagnosed at an advanced stage locally due to low public awareness and misdiagnosis as benign nevi or hematomas.

### Local Epidemiology of Common Benign Cutaneous Lesions
* **Epidermoid Cysts (表皮囊腫)** and **Lipomas (脂肪瘤)** constitute the vast majority of minor surgical skin lumps in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) general outpatient clinics (GOPC) and specialist outpatient clinics (SOPD).
* High prevalence of **Seborrheic Keratosis (脂溢性角化病)** in local elderly population (commonly known as "age spots").
* **Keloids (瘢痕疙瘩)** and **Hypertrophic Scars (增生性瘢痕)** have a significantly higher incidence and severity in Asian/Chinese skin compared to Caucasians due to hyperactive fibroblast response.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Skin Layer Anatomy for Surgical Excision
* **Epidermis (表皮)**: Outermost avascular layer; surgical cuts must reach the dermis to allow secure suturing.
* **Dermis (真皮)**: Contains collagen, elastic fibers, and the **Subdermal Plexus (皮下血管網)**; key layer for anchoring dermal sutures (**Dermal-Epidermal Junction (皮真皮交界)**).
* **Subcutaneous Fat (皮下脂肪)**: Contains superficial vessels and nerves; superficial lipomas are situated here.
* **Deep Fascia (深筋膜)**: Surgical plane boundary for benign lesions; malignant lesions require excision of this layer if invaded.
* **Langer's Lines (皮紋線)**: Lines of skin tension; surgical incisions placed parallel to Langer's lines minimize tension, leading to optimal healing and minimal scarring.

### Pathophysiology of Epidermoid Cysts
* Derived from the **Infundibulum of Hair Follicle (毛囊漏斗部)**.
* Implantation of epidermal elements into the dermis via trauma or follicular occlusion.
* Cyst wall lined by stratified squamous epithelium.
* Continuous desquamation of keratin inside the cyst cavity → progressive accumulation of **Cheesy Keratinous Material (乾酪樣角質物)**.
* Rupture of cyst wall → release of keratin into dermis → severe **Sterile Inflammatory Response (無菌性炎症反應)** mimicking bacterial infection.

### Pathophysiology of Lipomas
* Benign mesenchymal tumor composed of mature adipocytes.
* Arranged in lobules and enclosed by a thin, distinct **Fibrous Capsule (纖維包膜)**.
* Pathogenesis linked to chromosomal rearrangements (e.g., 12q13-15 mutations).
* Possesses a central feeding blood vessel; rarely undergoes malignant transformation to **Liposarcoma (脂肪肉瘤)** unless deep-seated or retroperitoneal.

### Pathophysiology and Genetics of Basal Cell Carcinoma
* Arises from pluripotential cells in the **Basal Layer of Epidermis (表皮基底層)**.
* Driven by **Ultraviolet (UV) Radiation (紫外線輻射)** causing DNA damage (thymine dimers).
* Key genetic pathway alteration involves the **Hedgehog Signaling Pathway (刺蝟信號通路)**.
* Mutations in **PTCH1 (Patched 1)** or **SMO (Smoothened)** genes → uncontrolled proliferation of basal keratinocytes.

### Pathophysiology of Squamous Cell Carcinoma
* Arises from malignant transformation of **Epidermal Keratinocytes (表皮角質形成細胞)**.
* Induced by cumulative UV-B radiation or chronic irritation (e.g., chronic osteomyelitis sinuses, burn scars).
* Stepwise progression: Normal skin → Actinic Keratosis → SCC in situ (Bowen's disease) → Invasive SCC.
* Associated with **p53 Tumor Suppressor Gene (p53抑癌基因)** mutations and inactivation.
* Breaks through the basement membrane to invade the underlying dermis, carrying a risk of lymphatic spread.

### Pathophysiology of Cutaneous Melanoma Subtypes in Asians
* Malignant transformation of **Melanocytes (黑色素細胞)** at the basal layer or dermo-epidermal junction.
* In Chinese populations, pathogenetic drivers are less related to UV light and more associated with **Mechanical Trauma (機械性損傷)** or friction (for acral sites).
* Low frequency of **BRAF V600E** mutations (<20% in acral types vs >50% in Western superficial spreading types).
* High frequency of **KIT (c-KIT)** or **CCND1** gene amplifications and copy number alterations.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Physical Signs of Epidermoid Cysts
* **Punctum (中央小孔)**: Pathognomonic central dilated follicular opening; may exude foul-smelling keratinous material.
* **Fluctuant (波動感)**: Palpable fluid-like consistency due to soft keratin content.
* Tethered to the overlying **Epidermis (表皮)** at the punctum but highly mobile over deep fascia.
* Erythema, exquisite tenderness, and rapid increase in size indicate **Acute Rupture/Infection (急性破裂/感染)**.

### Physical Signs of Lipomas
* **Slip Sign (滑動徵)**: Positive when the edge of the lump slips away from the examining finger upon palpation.
* **Lobulated Configuration (分葉狀)**: Soft, smooth, lobulated mass on palpation.
* Completely mobile over the overlying skin and underlying deep structures.
* Usually asymptomatic; pain suggests **Angiolipoma (血管脂肪瘤)** or compression of adjacent peripheral nerves.

### Clinical Presentation of Basal Cell Carcinoma Subtypes
* **Nodular BCC (結節型)**: Most common; pearly or translucent papule/nodule with **Telangiectasia (毛細血管擴張)**, rolled borders, and central ulceration (**Rodent Ulcer (侵蝕性潰瘍)**).
* **Superficial BCC (表淺型)**: Erythematous, scaly plaque with a fine pearly border; resembles eczema or psoriasis.
* **Morphoeic/Sclerosing BCC (硬斑型)**: Indurated, scar-like white/yellowish plaque; ill-defined borders, highly aggressive local invasion.
* **Pigmented BCC (色素型)**: Contains melanin; dark brown/black color mimicking melanoma or seborrheic keratosis.

### Clinical Presentation of Squamous Cell Carcinoma
* **Hyperkeratotic Nodule (角化過度結節)**: Firm, indurated flesh-colored or erythematous plaque/nodule.
* **Everted Edges (翻花狀邊緣)**: Ulcerated lesion with raised, everted, irregular margins.
* **Foul-smelling Exudate (惡臭滲出物)**: Secondary to necrotic tissue breakdown or bacterial superinfection.
* **Induration (硬結)**: Palpable firmness extending beyond the visible margin of the tumor, signifying deep dermal invasion.

### ABCDE Criteria for Cutaneous Melanoma Assessment
* **A - Asymmetry (不對稱性)**: One half of the lesion does not match the other half.
* **B - Border Irregularity (邊緣不規則)**: Notched, ragged, blurred, or poorly defined margins.
* **C - Color Variation (顏色多樣性)**: Shades of black, brown, tan, blue, white, or red within the same lesion.
* **D - Diameter (直徑)**: Greater than **6mm** (though melanomas can sometimes be smaller).
* **E - Evolving (進展演變)**: Any rapid change in size, shape, color, elevation, or new symptoms like bleeding, itching, or crusting.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Table of Key Discriminatory Features for Cutaneous Lesions

| Feature | Epidermoid Cyst | Lipoma | Basal Cell Carcinoma | Squamous Cell Carcinoma | Melanoma (Acral) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Site** | Trunk, neck, face | Limbs, trunk | Face, head, neck | Sun-exposed limbs, face | Palms, soles, subungual |
| **Consistency** | Firm, fluctuant | Soft, doughy | Firm | Hard, indurated | Variable, firm |
| **Skin Attachment** | Tethered at punctum | Not tethered | Integral to epidermis | Integral to epidermis | Integral to epidermis |
| **Mobility** | Mobile over deep tissues | Highly mobile | Moves with skin | Fixed to deep tissue late | Moves with skin |
| **Surface** | Smooth + **Punctum** | Smooth, lobulated | **Telangiectasia**, pearly | **Keratotic crust**, ulcer | Asymmetric pigment |
| **Growth Rate** | Slow | Very slow | Slow (years) | Rapid (months) | Rapid/Evolving |
| **Ulceration** | Only if infected | Absent | Late (**Rodent ulcer**) | Common (**Everted edge**) | Variable |
| **LN Metastasis** | Never | Never | Extremely rare (<0.1%) | Moderate (approx. 5%) | High risk |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup

### Bedside Assessment and Dermoscopy
* **Dermoscopy (皮膚鏡檢查)**: Non-invasive tool used in SOPD to differentiate pigmented lesions.
* **BCC Dermoscopic Findings**: Leaf-like areas, spoke-wheel areas, large blue-gray ovoid nests, arborizing telangiectasias, lack of pigment network.
* **Melanoma Dermoscopic Findings**: Atypical pigment network, irregular streaks, asymmetric blotches, blue-white veil, atypical vascular patterns.

### 1st Line Diagnostic Gold Standard for Cutaneous Malignancies
* **Excision Biopsy (切除活檢)**: Diagnostic procedure of choice for suspected melanoma or small carcinomas.
* Incision must include a narrow margin (**1-3mm**) of normal skin and extend down to subcutaneous fat.
* Incision orientation must align with definitive wide local excision parameters (longitudinal on limbs).
* **Incisional/Punch Biopsy (切開/打孔活檢)**: Indicated *only* for very large lesions, or when definitive excision would cause severe cosmetic deformity (e.g., eyelid, nose tip) before a tissue diagnosis is established.

### Pre-operative Imaging Indications for Deep Cutaneous Lesions
* **Ultrasonography (超聲波檢查)**: Used to delineate deep boundaries of large soft tissue lumps, confirming fluid content (cyst) vs fat tissue (lipoma).
* **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) (磁共振成像)**: Indicated if a suspected lipoma is **>5cm** ("giant lipoma"), fixed to deep structures, or located within muscles, to rule out **Liposarcoma (脂肪肉瘤)**.
* MRI features suspicious for liposarcoma: Thick septa (>2mm), nodular contrast enhancement, non-adipose tissue components.

### Staging Workup for High-Risk Cutaneous Malignancies
* **Computed Tomography (CT) Thorax/Abdomen/Pelvis (電腦斷層掃描)**: Indicated for invasive SCC with suspected nodal involvement, or Melanoma with Breslow thickness **>1mm** to screen for visceral metastasis.
* **Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB) (前哨淋巴結活檢)**: Staging tool for Cutaneous Melanoma without clinically palpable lymph nodes if:
  * Breslow thickness **>0.8mm**
  * Breslow thickness **<0.8mm** with **Ulcerating Features (潰瘍)** or high mitotic rate.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications

### Hong Kong Hospital Authority Triage Protocols
* **Routine Triage (例行)**: Asymptomatic benign lesions (lipomas, stable cysts) placed on routine SOPD waiting list (can exceed 1-2 years).
* **Priority 2 (P2 / 次緊急)**: Rapidly growing benign lesions, recurrently infected cysts, or clinically suspected BCCs; seen within 8 weeks.
* **Priority 1 (P1 / 緊急)**: Highly suspected SCC or Melanoma; evaluated within 2 weeks at a specialist **Dermato-Oncology Clinic (皮膚腫瘤專科門診)**.

### Management Algorithm for Benign Cutaneous Lesions
* **Conservative Management (保守治療)**: Reassurance and observation for small, asymptomatic lipomas or stable epidermoid cysts.
* **Surgical Excision under Local Anesthesia (局麻下手術切除)**: Primary definitive management strategy when surgical indications are met.

### Surgical Indications for Benign Cutaneous Lesions
* **Symptomatic Distress (症狀明顯)**: Pain, local tenderness, mechanical restriction of joint movement.
* **Recurrent Infection (反覆感染)**: Recurrent abscess formation in epidermoid cysts requiring repeated incision and drainage.
* **Rapid Growth (生長迅速)**: Rapid enlargement raising clinical concern for alternative diagnoses or malignancy.
* **Cosmetic Disfigurement (影響外觀)**: Significant cosmetic concern, especially on facial or visible areas.
* **Diagnostic Uncertainty (診斷不明)**: Inability to clinically rule out a soft tissue sarcoma or malignant skin lesion.

### 1st Line Management for Basal Cell Carcinoma
* **Standard Surgical Excision (標準手術切除)**: Primary treatment for low-risk tumors.
* Clear surgical margins required: **4-5mm** of normal skin around the visible tumor border, extending down to deep subcutaneous fat.
* **Mohs Micrographic Surgery (MMS) (莫氏顯微手術)**: Form of intraoperative real-time margin control.
* Indications for MMS in HK: Recurrent BCC, ill-defined borders, or tumors located in high-risk cosmetic zones (**H-zone of face**: periocular, perinasal, perioral, ears).

### 1st Line Management for Squamous Cell Carcinoma
* **Wide Local Excision (WLE) (廣泛局部切除)**: Standard gold-standard approach.
* **Low-risk SCC (<2cm, well-differentiated)**: Excision with **4mm** peripheral clinical margin.
* **High-risk SCC (>2cm, poorly differentiated, perineural invasion)**: Excision with **6-10mm** peripheral clinical margin, taking the deep fascial layer.
* **Adjuvant Radiotherapy (放療)**: Indicated for positive surgical margins where re-excision is impossible, deep perineural invasion, or regional lymph node involvement.

### Management Algorithm for Cutaneous Melanoma
* **Wide Local Excision Margin Guidelines Based on Breslow Thickness**:
  * Melanoma in situ: **0.5cm - 1.0cm** margin.
  * Breslow thickness $\le 1.0\text{ mm}$: **1.0cm** margin.
  * Breslow thickness $1.01\text{ mm} - 2.0\text{ mm}$: **1.0cm - 2.0cm** margin.
  * Breslow thickness $> 2.0\text{ mm}$: **2.0cm** margin.
* **Therapeutic Lymph Node Dissection (淋巴結清掃术)**: Indicated if biopsy or SLNB confirms regional lymph node metastasis.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Steps for Epidermoid Cyst Excision
* Infiltrate **1% Lignocaine + 1:200,000 Adrenaline (利多卡因加腎上腺素)** around the lesion (**Local Anesthesia (局部麻醉)**).
* Design an **Elliptical Incision (橢圓形切口)** oriented parallel to Langer’s lines, encompassing the central **Punctum (中央小孔)**.
* Incise through skin into the dermis, avoiding puncturing the underlying cyst capsule.
* Use blunt dissection with curved **Mesenbaum Scissors (組織剪)** to separate the cyst wall from surrounding subcutaneous tissue.
* Deliver the **Intact Cyst (完整囊腫)** to reduce the risk of recurrence.
* Flush the wound with normal saline; check for hemostasis.
* Close the dead space with absorbable dermal sutures (**3-0 or 4-0 Vicryl**); close skin with non-absorbable sutures (**4-0 or 5-0 Prolene/Ethilon**).

### Operative Steps for Lipoma Excision
* Mark the borders of the lipoma and plan a straight incision over the maximum convexity of the mass.
* Inject local anesthetic into the subcutaneous layer above the capsule.
* Incise through skin and subcutaneous fat until the shiny white **Lipoma Capsule (脂肪瘤包膜)** is visualized.
* Create a plane between the capsule and surrounding fat using blunt finger dissection or a gauze peanut swab.
* Pop or enucleate the lipoma out of the surgical wound.
* Clamp, divide, and ligate the **Central Vascular Pedicle (中央血管蒂)** at the base of the lipoma.
* Obliterate the large residual **Dead Space (死腔)** using interrupted absorbable sutures to prevent hematoma formation.
* Perform skin closure.

### Surgical Margin Guidelines Summary

| Lesion Type | Recommended Peripheral Clinical Margin | Deep Margin Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Epidermoid Cyst / Lipoma** | Close shave around capsule (0mm) | Immediately beneath capsule |
| **Basal Cell Carcinoma (Low risk)** | 4mm | Subcutaneous fat layer |
| **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (Low risk)** | 4mm | Subcutaneous fat layer |
| **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (High risk)** | 6mm - 10mm | Deep fascia layer |
| **Melanoma (Breslow <1mm)** | 1cm | Deep fascia layer |
| **Melanoma (Breslow >2mm)** | 2cm | Deep fascia layer |

### High-Yield Consent Risks Specific to Cutaneous Surgery
* **Scarring and Keloid Formation (瘢痕及瘢痕疙瘩)**: High clinical relevance in Hong Kong due to Asian skin phenotypes; risk of wide, raised, dark scars.
* **Recurrence (復發)**: Cysts can recur if any part of the epithelial wall is left behind; lipomas can recur if satellite lobes are missed.
* **Hematoma/Seroma Accumulation (血腫/血清腫)**: Secondary to failure to close large anatomical dead spaces.
* **Wound Dehiscence (傷口裂開)**: Caused by excessive skin tension or premature removal of sutures.
* **Nerve Injury (神經損傷)**: Avoid injury to the **Marginal Mandibular Nerve (下頜緣支)** during submandibular excisions, or the **Accessory Nerve (副神經)** in the posterior triangle of the neck.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Wound Care Protocols
* Apply a firm **Pressure Dressing (加壓敷料)** over the wound for the first **24-48 hours** to compress dead space and minimize hematoma risk.
* Instruct the patient to keep the surgical site completely dry for the first 48 hours.
* After 48 hours, dressings can be changed to simple water-resistant plasters.
* Showering is permissible if the wound is protected, but swimming or soaking the wound is prohibited until suture removal.

### Local Suture Removal Timelines by Anatomical Site
* **Face / Neck**: **3 - 5 days** (to minimize cosmetic railtracking marks).
* **Scalp**: **7 - 10 days** (highly vascular but high tension).
* **Trunk / Upper Limbs**: **10 - 12 days**.
* **Lower Limbs / Back / Over Joints**: **12 - 14 days** (high tension sites require delayed removal to prevent dehiscence).

### Hypertrophic Scar and Keloid Prevention Protocols
* Start therapy immediately after suture removal for high-risk patients.
* Apply **Silicone Gel (矽膠膏)** or **Silicone Sheeting (矽膠貼)** twice daily for at least 3-6 months.
* Avoid direct UV exposure on the healing scar for 6 months (use sunscreen) to prevent permanent **Hyperpigmentation (色素沉著)**.
* **Intralesional Triamcinolone Acetonide (TA) Injection (病損內類固醇注射)**: Initiated if the scar becomes abnormally raised, itchy, or erythematous.


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (Within 1 week)
* **Hematoma (血腫)**: Presents as rapid swelling, pain, and dark discoloration under the flap; requires immediate suture removal, evacuation of clot, and hemostasis if airway or skin flap perfusion is threatened.
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染)**: Presents as erythema, warmth, purulent discharge; treat with oral antibiotics (e.g., **Augmentin** or **Cloxacillin** based on local HA resistance profiles) or wound opening.
* **Skin Flap Necrosis (皮瓣壞死)**: Caused by excessive tension, poor design, or compromised subdermal plexus during dissection.

### Intermediate and Late Post-operative Complications
* **Suture Sinus (縫線竇道)**: Foreign body reaction against retained deep dermal sutures; presents as a non-healing focal discharging point; resolves upon removal of the offending suture material.
* **Hypertrophic Scar / Keloid (增生性瘢痕/瘢痕疙瘩)**: Appears 1-6 months post-op; keloids expand beyond the boundaries of the original surgical incision.
* **Tumor Recurrence (腫瘤復發)**: Due to incomplete excision margins (indicated on pathology reports as positive margins).

### Prognostic Indicators for Skin Malignancies
* **Basal Cell Carcinoma**: Excellent prognosis; >99% 5-year survival rate with complete surgical excision; rarely impacts overall life expectancy.
* **Squamous Cell Carcinoma**: Good prognosis if caught early (5-year survival >90%); drops significantly if perineural invasion or regional lymph node metastasis occurs.
* **Cutaneous Melanoma**: Governed by **Breslow Thickness (布雷斯洛厚度)** and staging.
  * $\le 1\text{ mm}$ thick: 5-year survival >95%.
  * $> 4\text{ mm}$ thick or nodal involvement: 5-year survival drops below 50-60%.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls

* Viva Question: An elderly patient presents with a suspected infected epidermoid cyst on the back showing acute erythema and fluctuance. What is your immediate surgical management? Answer: Perform incision and drainage (I&D) or a hot unroofing procedure to evacuate the pus and keratin. Do not attempt a formal capsule excision during the acute inflammatory phase because the capsule is friable, boundaries are obscured, and local anesthesia will be ineffective in acidic infected tissue. Schedule a formal excision 4-6 weeks after the inflammation resolves.
* Trap: Assuming that a black subungual or plantar lesion in a Chinese patient is always a simple subungual hematoma or benign melanocytic nevus. Pearl: Always maintain a high index of suspicion for Acral Lentiginous Melanoma in Asian patients with new or changing pigmented lesions on the hands or feet, and perform an excision biopsy if the lesion evolves.
* Pitfall: Removing a facial skin lump via an incision that runs perpendicular to Langer's lines. This creates excessive wound tension, leading to wide, cosmetically disfiguring scars or keloid formation. Always mark and plan skin incisions parallel to relaxed skin tension lines.
* Viva Question: If a histopathology report for a basal cell carcinoma excision states "tumor extends to the deep margin", what is your next clinical action? Answer: Arrange for wide local re-excision of the surgical scar to ensure adequate deep margins down to the next clear anatomical plane, as incomplete excision carries a high risk of deep local recurrence and tissue destruction.
* Trap: Performing a wide local excision with a 2cm margin immediately for a highly suspicious pigmented skin lesion before getting a tissue diagnosis. Pearl: Always perform a narrow-margin excision biopsy (1-3mm) first to confirm the diagnosis and determine the precise Breslow thickness, which dictates the correct oncological margin required for definitive wide local excision.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to close the anatomical dead space after removing a giant subcutaneous lipoma. This inevitably leads to a large postoperative hematoma or seroma collection. Avoid this by placing multiple interrupted absorbable sutures to approximate the deep tissue layers before skin closure.
