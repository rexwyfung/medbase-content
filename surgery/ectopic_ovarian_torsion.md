# Ectopic Pregnancy & Ovarian Torsion (宮外孕與卵巢扭轉)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Ectopic Pregnancy (宮外孕定義)
* **Ectopic Pregnancy**: Implantation of fertilized ovum outside the uterine cavity.
* **Tubal Pregnancy** (輸卵管妊娠): Accounts for >95% of cases.
* **Heterotopic Pregnancy** (複合妊娠): Simultaneous intrauterine and extrauterine pregnancy (rare, but ↑ risk with IVF).

### Definition of Ovarian Torsion (卵巢扭轉定義)
* **Ovarian Torsion**: Partial or complete rotation of the ovary and/or fallopian tube (adnexal torsion) around its vascular axis (infundibulopelvic and utero-ovarian ligaments).
* **Ischaemic Necrosis** (缺血性壞死): Final irreversible consequence if untwisted promptly.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology of Gynaecological Emergencies (香港婦科急症流行病學)
* **Ectopic Pregnancy**: Incidence ~1-2% of all pregnancies. Leading cause of 1st-trimester maternal mortality.
* **Ovarian Torsion**: 5th most common gynaecological emergency. Bimodal age distribution (reproductive years and post-menopausal).
* **Risk Factors (Ectopic)**: Previous ectopic, Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) (盆腔炎), IVF/ART, IUCD failure, tubal surgery.
* **Risk Factors (Torsion)**: Ovarian cyst/mass >5cm (especially dermoid cysts/teratomas), Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS) (卵巢過度刺激綜合症), pregnancy.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Anatomy of Ectopic Pregnancy Sites (宮外孕位置解剖)
* **Ampulla** (壺腹部): ~70% (most common).
* **Isthmus** (峽部): ~12% (highest risk of early rupture).
* **Fimbria** (傘部): ~11%.
* **Interstitial/Cornual** (間質部): ~2-3% (rupture presents late at 8-12 weeks, causes massive/fatal haemorrhage due to proximity to uterine artery anastomoses).
* **Others**: Cervical, ovarian, abdominal scar (Caesarean scar pregnancy - ↑ incidence locally).

### Pathophysiology of Tubal Rupture (輸卵管破裂病理生理)
* Implantation in fallopian tube lacks supportive decidual layer → trophoblastic invasion erodes tubal muscularis and blood vessels.
* → Tubal distension → localized ischaemia/necrosis.
* → **Tubal Rupture** (輸卵管破裂) → massive intraperitoneal haemorrhage (haemoperitoneum) → hypovolaemic shock.

### Anatomy of Ovarian Torsion (卵巢扭轉解剖)
* **Infundibulopelvic Ligament** (骨盆漏斗韌帶): Contains ovarian artery and vein.
* **Utero-ovarian Ligament** (卵巢固有韌帶): Connects ovary to uterus.
* Right-sided torsion more common (asymmetric space, left colon prevents left torsion).

### Pathophysiology of Ovarian Ischaemia (卵巢缺血病理生理)
* Torsion twists vascular pedicle → thin-walled venous and lymphatic outflow obstructed first.
* → Intense ovarian congestion and oedema (capsule stretches → severe pain).
* → ↑ Interstitial pressure → secondary arterial inflow obstruction.
* → Ischaemia, infarction, and irreversible necrosis (usually takes >4-8 hours).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Presentation of Unruptured Ectopic Pregnancy (未破裂宮外孕臨床表現)
* **Classic Triad**: Amenorrhoea (停經) + Lower abdominal pain + Vaginal bleeding (usually dark/scanty).
* **Abdominal Examination**: Mild localized iliac fossa tenderness.
* **Pelvic Examination**: Cervical motion tenderness (cervical excitation), unilateral adnexal tenderness, uterus size smaller than gestational age.

### Presentation of Ruptured Ectopic Pregnancy (破裂宮外孕臨床表現)
* **Vitals**: Tachycardia, hypotension, tachypnoea (Hypovolaemic shock).
* **Abdomen**: Acute abdomen (guarding, rigidity, rebound tenderness).
* **Shoulder Tip Pain** (肩膊痛): Referred pain from diaphragmatic irritation by haemoperitoneum (Kehr's sign) (often via Phrenic nerve).
* **Syncope** (暈厥): Due to severe internal haemorrhage.

### Presentation of Ovarian Torsion (卵巢扭轉臨床表現)
* **Onset**: Sudden, sharp, unilateral lower abdominal/pelvic pain.
* **Associated Symptoms**: Severe nausea and vomiting (70% of cases, vagal reflex).
* **Pain Characteristics**: Often colicky/intermittent initially (partial twisting/untwisting), becoming constant.
* **Palpation**: Tender, firm adnexal mass palpable in 50% of cases.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Matrix: Acute Pelvic Pain in Females (女性急性盆腔痛鑑別診斷表)
| Condition | Pain Onset | β-hCG Status | Ultrasound Findings | Key Discriminator |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ectopic Pregnancy** | Gradual (sudden if ruptured) | +ve | Empty uterus, adnexal mass, free fluid | Amenorrhoea + PV bleeding |
| **Ovarian Torsion** | Sudden, sharp | -ve (unless pregnant) | Enlarged oedematous ovary >4cm, ↓ Doppler flow | Severe nausea/vomiting, palpable mass |
| **Ruptured Ovarian Cyst** | Sudden (mid-cycle/post-coital) | -ve | Collapsed cyst, free pelvic fluid | Pain peaks at onset, then improves |
| **PID / TOA** | Gradual | -ve | Thickened tubes, complex adnexal mass | Bilateral pain, fever, vaginal discharge |
| **Acute Appendicitis** | Gradual, migratory | -ve | Dilated appendix >6mm, fat stranding | Migrates to RIF, anorexia, low-grade fever |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Blood Investigations for Gynaecological Emergencies (婦科急症血液檢查)
* **Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT)**: Mandatory 1st line for ALL women of childbearing age with abdominal pain.
* **Serum Quantitative β-hCG** (定量絨毛膜促性腺激素): Essential to map against ultrasound findings. Normal pregnancy β-hCG doubles every 48 hours; ectopic usually has suboptimal rise (<66% in 48hrs) or plateau.
* **CBC**: Check baseline Hb (haemorrhage) and WCC (infection/necrosis).
* **Type & Screen (GXM)**: Essential for ruptured ectopic (prepare crossmatched blood).
* **Rhesus Status**: To determine need for Anti-D immunoglobulin.

### Imaging for Ectopic Pregnancy (宮外孕影像學檢查)
* **Transvaginal Ultrasound (TVUS)** (陰道超聲波): Gold standard.
* **Findings**:
  * Empty uterine cavity.
  * Inhomogeneous adnexal mass (blob sign) or tubal ring sign (bagel sign).
  * Fluid in Pouch of Douglas (POD) (echogenic fluid = haemoperitoneum).
* **Discriminatory Zone**: Serum β-hCG level (usually >1500-2000 IU/L in HA guidelines) where an intrauterine gestational sac MUST be visible on TVUS. If absent → presumed ectopic.

### Imaging for Ovarian Torsion (卵巢扭轉影像學檢查)
* **Pelvic Ultrasound with Doppler** (多普勒超聲波): 1st line.
* **Findings**:
  * Unilaterally enlarged ovary (>4cm) due to oedema.
  * **String of Pearls Sign**: Peripherally displaced follicles.
  * **Whirlpool Sign**: Twisted vascular pedicle.
  * ↓ or absent venous/arterial flow on Doppler.
  * *Crucial Note*: Presence of Doppler flow DOES NOT exclude torsion (dual blood supply from ovarian and uterine arteries).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Medical Management of Ectopic Pregnancy (宮外孕藥物治療)
* **Agent**: Systemic **Methotrexate** (氨甲蝶呤) (folic acid antagonist).
* **Indications (Strict Criteria)**:
  * Haemodynamically stable.
  * Unruptured ectopic.
  * Adnexal mass < 3.5 cm.
  * No fetal heartbeat.
  * Initial β-hCG < 1500 - 5000 IU/L.
  * Patient compliant with close follow-up.
* **Contraindications**: Immunodeficiency, renal/hepatic impairment, active peptic ulcer, breastfeeding.

### Surgical Indications for Ectopic Pregnancy (宮外孕手術指徵)
* Haemodynamic instability (immediate surgery).
* Signs of rupture (acute abdomen, large haemoperitoneum).
* Failure of or contraindication to medical management.
* Heterotopic pregnancy.
* **Surgical Options**:
  * **Laparoscopic Salpingectomy** (腹腔鏡輸卵管切除術): Treatment of choice (removes diseased tube).
  * **Laparoscopic Salpingotomy** (腹腔鏡輸卵管切開術): Considers if contralateral tube is absent or severely damaged (preserves fertility but carries 5-8% risk of persistent trophoblastic tissue).

### Surgical Algorithm for Ovarian Torsion (卵巢扭轉手術流程)
* **Emergency Laparoscopy** (急症腹腔鏡): Definitive diagnostic and therapeutic tool.
* **Ovarian Detorsion** (卵巢復位術): Untwist the ovary. Observe for return of perfusion (pink colour).
* **If viable**: Perform **Ovarian Cystectomy** (卵巢囊腫切除術) to prevent recurrence. Alternatively, oophoropexy (fixation) if no cyst.
* **If necrotic/gangrenous**: Perform **Salpingo-oophorectomy** (輸卵管卵巢切除術) (removal of dead tissue to prevent peritonitis/sepsis).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Steps for Laparoscopic Salpingectomy (輸卵管切除術手術步驟)
* Entry: Palmer's point or umbilicus (Hasson/Veress) depending on prior surgeries.
* Evacuate haemoperitoneum to optimize visualization.
* Identify the ectopic pregnancy in the fallopian tube.
* Elevate tube. Use bipolar diathermy and dissecting scissors to divide the mesosalpinx progressively from fimbrial end to the isthmus.
* Amputate tube at the isthmus (leave cornua intact to avoid uterine bleeding).
* Extract tube in an endobag.
* Thorough peritoneal lavage.

### Operative Steps for Ovarian Detorsion (卵巢復位術手術步驟)
* Assess direction of twist (usually clockwise on right, counter-clockwise on left).
* Use blunt graspers to gently untwist the pedicle.
* Apply warm saline irrigation and wait 10-15 minutes to assess viability (re-establishment of arterial flow).
* Do NOT clamp the pedicle before detorsion if attempting to save the ovary.

### Consent Issues in Gynaecological Emergencies (婦科急症知情同意事項)
* **Ectopic Consent**: Risk of conversion to laparotomy (if massive adhesions/bleeding), loss of tube (decreased future fertility), need for blood transfusion, risk of persistent ectopic (if salpingotomy).
* **Torsion Consent**: Explicit consent for possible oophorectomy if ovary is found to be non-viable/necrotic. Risk of cyst recurrence.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Monitoring (術後監測)
* Monitor vital signs and urine output (fluid resuscitation for ruptured ectopic).
* Anti-D immunoglobulin injection within 72 hours for all Rhesus D negative women who had ectopic pregnancy surgery/bleeding.
* Early mobilization and diet resumption (ERAS).

### Follow-up Protocol (覆診準則)
* **Post-Salpingotomy/Medical Management**: Serial serum β-hCG monitoring weekly until levels drop to <5 IU/L (ensures no persistent trophoblast).
* **Post-Salpingectomy**: Routine outpatient clinic follow-up for wound check and histopathology review.
* Pre-conception counselling: Advise early early-pregnancy TVUS for future pregnancies due to 10-15% recurrence risk of ectopic pregnancy.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Complications of Ectopic Pregnancy Surgery (宮外孕手術併發症)
* **Early**: Haemorrhage, injury to surrounding organs (bladder, bowel, ureter).
* **Intermediate**: Pelvic infection, persistent trophoblastic disease (if salpingotomy performed → requires Methotrexate rescue).
* **Late**: Adhesion formation → chronic pelvic pain, recurrent ectopic pregnancy (10-15%), secondary infertility.

### Complications of Ovarian Torsion Surgery (卵巢扭轉手術併發症)
* **Early**: Thromboembolism (rare but historically feared: theoretical risk of dislodging pelvic vein thrombus during detorsion).
* **Intermediate**: Pelvic collection / abscess if necrotic ovary left in situ.
* **Late**: Premature ovarian failure (if bilateral or solitary ovary lost), recurrence of torsion (if oophoropexy/cystectomy not performed).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical first step in evaluating a female of reproductive age with acute abdominal pain? Answer: Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT) to rule out ectopic pregnancy.
* Trap: Believing normal Doppler arterial flow rules out ovarian torsion. Pearl: Ovaries have a dual blood supply (ovarian and uterine arteries); torsion initially compromises venous/lymphatic drainage, so arterial flow may persist on ultrasound. Clinical picture dictates surgery.
* Pitfall: Discharging a stable patient with a positive UPT and empty uterus on TVUS without follow-up. This is a Pregnancy of Unknown Location (PUL) and could be an early ectopic. Serial β-hCG monitoring in 48 hours is mandatory.
* Viva Question: Why do patients with a ruptured ectopic pregnancy complain of shoulder tip pain? Answer: Haemoperitoneum irritates the diaphragm, and the pain is referred via the phrenic nerve (C3, C4, C5 dermatomes) to the shoulder (Kehr's sign).
* Trap: Removing the ipsilateral ovary during a routine salpingectomy for ectopic pregnancy. Pearl: The ovary should be strictly preserved unless it is heavily involved in the mass or bleeding cannot be controlled, to maintain hormonal function and future fertility.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to administer Anti-D immunoglobulin. All Rhesus-negative women presenting with ectopic pregnancy (surgically or medically managed) must receive Anti-D to prevent alloimmunization in future pregnancies.
