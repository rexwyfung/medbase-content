# Acute Abdomen (急腹症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Acute Abdomen
* **Acute Abdomen (急腹症)**: Sudden onset of severe abdominal pain typically lasting <24-72 hours
* Requires rapid clinical evaluation to determine need for urgent **surgical intervention (外科手術)**
* Encompasses a broad spectrum of intra-abdominal, retroperitoneal, and extra-abdominal pathologies

### Hong Kong Epidemiology for Acute Abdomen
* Most common surgical emergencies presenting to HK Hospital Authority (HA) A&E departments:
* **Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎)**: Peaks in young adults (20-30s)
* **Biliary Tract Pathologies (膽道疾病)**: e.g., **Acute Cholecystitis (急性膽囊炎)**, ↑ incidence in HK due to rapidly aging population
* **Perforated Peptic Ulcer Disease (穿孔性消化性潰瘍)**: High prevalence linked to **H. pylori (幽門螺旋桿菌)** and widespread NSAID/Aspirin use in HK elderly
* **Bowel Obstruction (腸阻塞)**: Often 2° to prior pelvic/abdominal surgeries or malignant tumors (e.g., Colorectal Cancer)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Pathophysiology of Visceral Pain (內臟痛)
* Originates from stretching/distension of hollow viscus organs or solid organ capsules
* Mediated by autonomic nerve fibers (C-fibers)
* Characteristics: Dull, crampy, poorly localized, generally midline
* Localization matches embryological origin:
* **Foregut (前腸)** (stomach to D2): Epigastric
* **Midgut (中腸)** (D2 to prox 2/3 transverse colon): Periumbilical
* **Hindgut (後腸)** (distal 1/3 transverse colon to rectum): Suprapubic

### Pathophysiology of Somatic/Parietal Pain (體壁痛)
* Originates from direct irritation of parietal peritoneum (e.g., pus, blood, gastric acid, feces)
* Mediated by somatic nerve fibers (A-delta fibers)
* Characteristics: Sharp, constant, severe, precisely localized to specific dermatomes
* Exacerbated by movement, coughing, deep breathing

### Pathophysiology of Referred Pain (轉移痛)
* Pain perceived at a site distant from the pathological organ due to shared central neural pathways
* **Diaphragmatic Irritation (橫膈膜刺激)**: → **Kehr's Sign (克爾氏徵)** (shoulder tip pain via C3, C4, C5)
* **Biliary Tract (膽道)**: → Right subscapular area
* **Ureteric Colic (輸尿管絞痛)**: → Groin / Testicles / Labia

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms in Acute Abdomen
* **Pain (痛)**: Assess SOCRATES (Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Assoc. symptoms, Timing, Exac/Relieving factors, Severity)
* **Vomiting (嘔吐)**:
* Bilious (綠色/膽汁性) → Proximal bowel obstruction
* Feculent (糞便性) → Distal bowel obstruction
* **Bowel Alterations (排便習慣改變)**:
* Absolute constipation (絕對便秘) (no flatus/feces) → **Intestinal Obstruction (腸阻塞)**
* Bloody diarrhea → **Ischemic Colitis (缺血性腸炎)** / IBD
* **Systemic (全身性)**: Fever, chills, rigors, syncope

### Key Physical Signs in Acute Abdomen
* **Vital Signs (生命表徵)**: ↑HR, ↓BP, ↑Temp, ↑RR (signs of sepsis/shock)
* **Inspection (視診)**: Distension, surgical scars, visible peristalsis, Cullen's/Grey Turner's signs
* **Palpation (觸診)**:
* **Guarding (腹肌緊繃)**: Voluntary contraction 2° to pain
* **Rigidity (腹肌僵直)**: Involuntary "board-like" reflex contraction (indicates generalized peritonitis)
* **Rebound Tenderness (反跳痛)**: Pain ↑ upon sudden release of pressure
* **Percussion (叩診)**:
* Loss of liver dullness → Pneumoperitoneum (氣腹)
* Shifting dullness → Ascites (腹水) / Hemoperitoneum
* **Auscultation (聽診)**:
* Absent/Tinkling bowel sounds (腸鳴音)
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) (直腸指檢)**:
* Blood, melena, pelvic mass, tenderness (pelvic appendicitis)

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Bedside Investigations for Acute Abdomen
* **Venous Blood Gas (VBG) (靜脈血氣分析)**: Check lactate (↑ indicates ischemia/sepsis), pH, base excess
* **Urine Dipstick (尿液快速測試)**: Leukocytes/nitrites (UTI), blood (stones)
* **Urine hCG (尿液懷孕測試)**: MANDATORY for all females of childbearing age
* **ECG (心電圖)**: Rule out inferior Myocardial Infarction (心肌梗塞) presenting as epigastric pain

### Laboratory Investigations for Acute Abdomen
* **CBP (全血細胞計數)**: ↑ WBC (Neutrophilia), ↓ Hb (Bleeding)
* **LRFT (肝腎功能測試)**: Assess hydration (Urea/Cr), hepatobiliary derangement (Bilirubin, ALP, ALT)
* **Amylase/Lipase (澱粉酶/脂肪酶)**: >3x normal upper limit = **Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)**
* **Clotting Profile (凝血功能)**: PT/aPTT/INR for pre-op baseline
* **Type & Screen (G&S) / Crossmatch**: Prepare for massive transfusion if bleeding suspected

### Imaging Modalities for Acute Abdomen
* **Erect CXR (站立位胸部X光)**: Look for **Pneumoperitoneum (氣腹)** (free gas under diaphragm) indicating hollow viscus perforation
* **AXR (腹部X光)**: Look for dilated bowel loops, air-fluid levels, volvulus, toxic megacolon
* **USG Abdomen/Pelvis (腹部超聲波)**: 1st line for biliary tract, solid organs, gynecological pathology
* **CT Abdomen & Pelvis with IV Contrast (電腦掃描)**:
* **Gold Standard (黃金標準)** for undifferentiated acute abdomen
* High sensitivity/specificity for appendicitis, diverticulitis, ischemia, leaking AAA

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Initial Resuscitation (Hong Kong HA Protocol)
* **NBM (禁食)**: Nil by mouth immediately
* **IV Access (靜脈導管)**: Insert 2 large-bore (14G/16G) IV cannulas
* **Fluid Resuscitation (液體復甦)**: Warmed crystalloids (e.g., Normal Saline/Hartmann's) titrated to urine output (>0.5ml/kg/hr)
* **Analgesia (止痛)**: IV Paracetamol, short-acting opioids (e.g., Fentanyl) - does *not* mask signs!
* **Empiric Antibiotics (經驗性抗生素)**:
* Per HA guidelines for intra-abdominal sepsis: IV Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin) + IV Metronidazole OR IV Ceftriaxone + IV Metronidazole
* **NG Tube (胃喉)**: Decompression if vomiting or bowel obstruction
* **Urinary Catheter (尿喉)**: Strict fluid balance monitoring

### Indications for Urgent Laparotomy
* Generalized Peritonitis (廣泛性腹膜炎)
* Hemodynamic instability with suspected intra-abdominal hemorrhage (e.g., AAA, ruptured ectopic, trauma)
* Bowel Ischemia (腸缺血) (clinical suspicion + severe lactic acidosis)
* Pneumoperitoneum (氣腹) with signs of sepsis
* Strangulated Hernia (絞窄性小腸氣)

### Medical vs Conservative Management of Acute Abdomen
* **Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)**: Primarily supportive (aggressive hydration, analgesia, nutrition)
* **Uncomplicated Diverticulitis (無併發症憩室炎)**: IV Antibiotics, bowel rest
* **Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (粘連性小腸阻塞)**: "Drip and Suck" (IV fluids + NG tube) for 48-72 hrs unless signs of strangulation occur

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Pre-operative Preparation and Consent
* **Optimization**: Correct coagulopathy, electrolyte imbalances (if time permits)
* **Informed Consent (知情同意)**: Must detail specific procedure + potential for **Laparotomy (剖腹探查術)**, stoma formation (造口), bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding structures, ICU admission, death.

### Key Operative Principles (Exploratory Laparotomy/Laparoscopy)
* **Incision**: Midline laparotomy offers fastest, most versatile access to all quadrants
* **Control contamination**: Clamp bleeding vessels, limit enteric spill (suction/packs)
* **Identify Pathology**: Systematic 4-quadrant exploration
* **Definitive Treatment**: Resect ischemic/necrotic bowel, repair perforations, remove inflamed organs (e.g., appendicectomy)
* **Peritoneal Lavage (腹腔沖洗)**: Washout with copious warm saline
* **Closure**: Mass closure technique (e.g., PDS loop), consider leaving skin open if grossly contaminated to prevent SSI

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care for Acute Abdomen
* **Level of Care**: HDU/ICU for continuous monitoring in septic/shocked patients
* **Respiratory Care**: Incentive spirometry, chest physio to prevent atelectasis (肺不張) / pneumonia
* **DVT Prophylaxis**: LMWH (e.g., Enoxaparin) + TED stockings / SCDs

### ERAS (Enhanced Recovery After Surgery) Protocol in HK
* **Early Mobilization (盡早下床活動)**: Prevents DVT, improves respiratory function, stimulates bowel
* **Early Enteral Nutrition (盡早腸道營養)**: Reduces bacterial translocation, shortens ileus
* **Multimodal Analgesia (多模式止痛)**: Minimize opioids to reduce post-op ileus (use Paracetamol, NSAIDs if renal fxn ok, TAP blocks)
* **Catheter/Drain Removal**: Remove ASAP to reduce infection risk and aid mobilization

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (0-30 Days)
* **Bleeding (出血)**: Slipped ligature, unrecognized vessel injury
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染)**: High risk in dirty/contaminated abdominal wounds
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口滲漏)**: Typically presents day 5-7 post-op with fever, ↑ WBC, peritonitis
* **Paralytic Ileus (麻痺性腸梗阻)**: Failure of bowel motility recovery
* **Cardiopulmonary (心肺)**: MI, PE, Atelectasis, Pneumonia

### Late Surgical Complications (>30 Days)
* **Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (粘連性小腸阻塞)**: Most common long-term complication of abdominal surgery
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝)**: Due to fascial breakdown, risk ↑ in obesity, infection, malnutrition
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸症候群)**: If massive bowel resection required (e.g., mesenteric ischemia)

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most important initial test to rule out in an elderly patient presenting with sudden, severe diffuse abdominal pain but relatively benign physical findings? Answer: Mesenteric Ischemia (check ABG/VBG for profound lactic acidosis) and Ruptured AAA.
* Trap: Believing that administering opioid analgesia to a patient with an undiagnosed acute abdomen will mask physical signs and delay diagnosis. Pearl: Modern surgical teaching mandates prompt, adequate analgesia; it relieves distress and often makes the abdominal examination easier and more reliable.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to perform a bedside urine pregnancy test (hCG) in a female of childbearing age with abdominal pain. Unruptured/ruptured ectopic pregnancy is a life-threatening surgical emergency that must be excluded before pursuing other GI differentials.
* Viva Question: An upright CXR is requested to look for a pneumoperitoneum. If the patient is too sick to stand, what is the alternative radiographic view? Answer: Left lateral decubitus AXR (free air will outline the lateral border of the liver).
* Trap: Discharging an elderly patient with "constipation" based purely on non-specific abdominal pain and mild distension without fully evaluating for mechanical causes. Pearl: Always maintain a high index of suspicion for underlying colorectal malignancy causing obstruction in older HK demographics.
* Pitfall: Assuming a normal serum amylase rules out acute pancreatitis. Amylase levels can be normal in late presentations, acute-on-chronic pancreatitis, or hypertriglyceridemia. Lipase is more sensitive and specific.
