# Pneumothorax and Pleural Effusion (氣胸與胸腔積液)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Pneumothorax (氣胸)
* **Pneumothorax (氣胸)**: Abnormal accumulation of air in the **pleural space (胸膜腔)** → loss of negative pressure → partial/complete lung collapse.
* **Primary Spontaneous Pneumothorax (PSP)**: Occurs without underlying lung disease (often apical subpleural blebs).
* **Secondary Spontaneous Pneumothorax (SSP)**: Occurs due to underlying lung pathology [e.g., **COPD (慢性阻塞性肺病)**, **Tuberculosis (結核病)**].
* **Tension Pneumothorax (張力性氣胸)**: Life-threatening one-way valve air leak → progressive intrapleural pressure ↑ → mediastinal shift → venous return ↓ → obstructive shock.

### Definition of Pleural Effusion (胸腔積液)
* **Pleural Effusion (胸腔積液)**: Pathological fluid accumulation in the **pleural space (胸膜腔)**.
* **Transudate (漏出液)**: Ultrafiltrate of plasma (intact capillaries), due to systemic hydrostatic/oncotic pressure imbalances.
* **Exudate (滲出液)**: Protein-rich fluid due to localized capillary permeability ↑ or lymphatic obstruction.

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology
* **TB-related Pathology**: Endemic in HK; high index of suspicion for **pulmonary tuberculosis (肺結核)** as a primary cause for both SSP and exudative effusions.
* **PSP Demographics in HK**: Classically affects tall, thin, young males (20-30 years).
* **SSP Demographics in HK**: Commonly elderly male smokers with underlying COPD or active/past TB.
* **Malignant Effusion**: Lung cancer (肺癌) is the leading cancer in HK; frequently presents with malignant pleural effusion (MPE).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Pleura and Chest Wall
* **Visceral Pleura (臟層胸膜)**: Adherent to lung parenchyma, insensitive to somatic pain (autonomic innervation).
* **Parietal Pleura (壁層胸膜)**: Lines the inner chest wall, diaphragm, mediastinum; heavily innervated by intercostal/phrenic nerves (exquisitely pain-sensitive).
* **Normal Pleural Pressure**: Rests at -5 cmH2O (maintains lung expansion).
* **Intercostal Neurovascular Bundle (肋間神經血管束)**: Vein, Artery, Nerve (VAN) runs tightly along the **inferior border (下緣)** of the corresponding rib.

### Pathophysiology of Spontaneous Pneumothorax
* **Apical Bleb Rupture**: High negative intrapleural pressure at lung apices → mechanical stress → subpleural bleb/bulla formation & rupture.
* **Air Leak Dynamics**: Alveolar gas enters pleural space → transpulmonary pressure gradient equalizes → lung elastic recoil → lung collapse.

### Pathophysiology of Pleural Effusion
* **Transudative Mechanism**: ↑ Capillary hydrostatic pressure [e.g., **Congestive Heart Failure (心臟衰竭)**] or ↓ plasma oncotic pressure [e.g., **Liver Cirrhosis (肝硬化)**, **Nephrotic Syndrome (腎病綜合症)**].
* **Exudative Mechanism**: ↑ Pleural vascular permeability [e.g., **Pneumonia (肺炎)**, TB] or impaired lymphatic drainage (e.g., Malignancy).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Pneumothorax
* **Sudden onset pleuritic chest pain (胸膜性胸痛)**: Sharp, worse on inspiration.
* **Dyspnea (呼吸困難)**: Severity proportional to size and underlying lung reserve.
* **Asymmetrical chest expansion**: ↓ movement on affected side.
* **Hyperresonant percussion note (叩診過清音)** over affected hemithorax.
* **Decreased/absent breath sounds (呼吸音減弱)** on auscultation.

### Clinical Signs of Tension Pneumothorax
* **Severe respiratory distress** and hypoxia.
* **Tracheal deviation (氣管偏移)**: Shifted *away* from the affected side.
* **Elevated Jugular Venous Pressure (JVP↑)**: Distended neck veins due to ↓ venous return.
* **Haemodynamic Instability**: Tachycardia, hypotension (obstructive shock).

### Clinical Presentation of Pleural Effusion
* **Progressive dyspnea** (exertional → resting).
* **Dry cough** and heaviness in the chest.
* **Stony dullness to percussion (叩診木實音)** over fluid level.
* **Decreased/absent breath sounds** over fluid level.
* **Decreased vocal resonance/tactile fremitus (語顫減弱)** (fluid insulates sound transmission).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Unilateral Lung Pathology

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Imaging for Pneumothorax and Effusion
* **CXR PA Erect (直立後前位胸部X光)**:
* **Pneumothorax findings**: Visible visceral pleural edge (lung margin), devoid of lung markings peripherally. Deep sulcus sign (on supine films).
* **Effusion findings**: Blunting of costophrenic angle (肋膈角變鈍) (>200mL fluid), meniscus sign, homogenous opacification.

### Advanced Imaging and Workup
* **CT Thorax (胸部電腦掃描)**: Gold standard for localizing apical bullae prior to VATS, assessing underlying parenchymal disease (emphysema, ILD), and differentiating empyema from lung abscess.
* **USG Thorax (胸部超聲波)**: Highly sensitive for detecting small effusions, identifying loculations (septations), and providing real-time guidance for safe aspiration/drainage.

### Diagnostic Thoracentesis and Light's Criteria for Pleural Effusion
* **Diagnostic Thoracentesis (胸腔穿刺)**: Send fluid for Biochemistry (Protein, LDH, Glucose, pH), Microbiology (Gram stain, Culture, AFB smear/culture), Cytology.
* **Light's Criteria (萊特準則)** (Exudate if ≥1 criteria met):
| Criterion | Transudate | Exudate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pleural Fluid/Serum Protein Ratio** | < 0.5 | **≥ 0.5** |
| **Pleural Fluid/Serum LDH Ratio** | < 0.6 | **≥ 0.6** |
| **Pleural Fluid LDH Level** | < 2/3 upper limit normal serum | **≥ 2/3 upper limit normal serum** |

### Specific Pleural Fluid Biomarkers
* **pH < 7.2**: Suggests complicated parapneumonic effusion / **Empyema (膿胸)** → absolute indication for chest drain.
* **Glucose < 3.3 mmol/L**: Infection (Empyema), Rheumatoid Arthritis, Malignancy, TB.
* **ADA (Adenosine Deaminase) ↑**: Highly suggestive of TB pleurisy in HK context.
* **Triglycerides > 1.24 mmol/L**: Chylothorax (乳糜胸).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Acute Management Algorithm for Primary Spontaneous Pneumothorax (HK HA Protocols)
* **Size < 2cm (visible rim on CXR at level of hilum)** AND asymptomatic: Observation, supplemental O2 (accelerates nitrogen resorption 4x), early discharge with outpatient follow-up.
* **Size > 2cm** OR symptomatic: **Needle Aspiration (針刺抽氣)** (14-16G cannula at 2nd ICS MCL).
* **Failed Aspiration** (>2.5L aspirated or no clinical/radiological improvement): **Chest Tube Insertion (胸腔引流)** (10-14Fr Seldinger drain).

### Acute Management Algorithm for Secondary Spontaneous Pneumothorax
* **All SSP > 1cm** OR symptomatic: Require active intervention. Needle aspiration is rarely successful.
* **First-line**: **Chest Tube Insertion** + admission.
* **If < 1cm** AND asymptomatic: Observe closely in-patient with O2.

### Emergency Management of Tension Pneumothorax
* **Immediate Intervention**: DO NOT wait for CXR.
* **Needle Decompression (針刺減壓)**: 14G cannula at 2nd Intercostal Space (ICS) Mid-Clavicular Line (MCL) OR 5th ICS Mid-Axillary Line (MAL) immediately.
* **Definitive**: Follow immediately with formal Chest Tube insertion.

### Surgical Indications for Pneumothorax (VATS Bullectomy & Pleurodesis)
* **2nd episode** of ipsilateral PSP/SSP.
* **1st episode** of contralateral pneumothorax.
* **Synchronous bilateral pneumothoraces**.
* **Persistent air leak** (> 3-5 days despite chest drain).
* **Failure of lung re-expansion**.
* **High-risk professions**: Pilots, commercial divers (done after 1st episode).
* **Hemopneumothorax (血氣胸)**.

### Management Algorithm for Pleural Effusion
* **Transudate**: Treat underlying medical cause (e.g., diuretics for CHF). Therapeutic aspiration only if severely breathless.
* **Exudate (Parapneumonic)**: IV Antibiotics.
* **Complicated Parapneumonic/Empyema (pH < 7.2, frank pus, positive Gram stain)**: Urgent Chest Tube drainage. If loculated → intrapleural fibrinolytics (tPA/DNase) OR VATS decortication.
* **Malignant Pleural Effusion (MPE)**: Symptomatic relief via repeated aspiration, **Indwelling Pleural Catheter (IPC)**, or chemical pleurodesis (Talc).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Chest Drain Insertion Principles (Triangle of Safety)
* **Anatomical Landmarks (Triangle of Safety / 安全三角)**:
  * Anterior: Lateral border of Pectoralis Major.
  * Posterior: Lateral border of Latissimus Dorsi.
  * Inferior: Horizontal line from the nipple (5th ICS).
* **Technique**: Infiltrate local anesthetic (Lignocaine) down to pleura. Insert needle/trocar strictly **above the superior border** of the lower rib to avoid the intercostal VAN bundle.
* **Confirmation**: Fogging of the tube, fluid return, swinging of the fluid column.

### VATS Bullectomy and Pleurodesis Principles
* **VATS (Video-Assisted Thoracoscopic Surgery / 單孔/多孔胸腔鏡手術)**: Minimally invasive approach.
* **Bullectomy**: Stapled wedge resection of apical blebs/bullae using endo-staplers.
* **Pleurodesis (胸膜固定術)**: Obliteration of pleural space to prevent recurrence.
  * **Mechanical (Apical pleurectomy / pleural abrasion)**: Scrubbing parietal pleura to induce inflammation/fibrosis.
  * **Chemical**: Instillation of sterile graded Talc (滑石粉), Tetracycline, or Iodine.

### Operative Consent for VATS
* **General Risks**: Bleeding, infection (empyema), severe post-operative pain, injury to adjacent organs (lung, diaphragm, heart).
* **Specific Risks**: Intercostal neuralgia (chronic neuropathic pain), persistent air leak, recurrence of pneumothorax (1-5%).
* **Conversion**: 1-2% risk of conversion to open thoracotomy (開胸手術) if bleeding cannot be controlled.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Chest Drain Management and Monitoring
* **Underwater Seal (水封引流瓶)**: Maintain system below patient's chest level.
* **Swinging (波動)**: Fluid level moves with respiration → indicates tube is patent and in the pleural space.
* **Bubbling (氣泡)**: Indicates ongoing air leak (bronchopleural fistula). Must differentiate patient air leak from loose tubing connection.
* **Removal Criteria**: Lung fully expanded on CXR, no air leak (no bubbling on coughing), fluid drainage < 100-200 mL/day, no purulence.

### Post-Operative ERAS and Rehabilitation Protocol
* **Pain Management**: Multi-modal analgesia is crucial (Paravertebral block, intercostal nerve block, PCA, oral NSAIDs/Paracetamol) to prevent atelectasis.
* **Chest Physiotherapy**: Deep breathing exercises, **Incentive Spirometry (誘發性肺活量計)**, early mobilization (Day 1 post-op).
* **DVT Prophylaxis**: TED stockings, LMWH.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Complications of Chest Tube Insertion
* **Early**: Hemothorax (laceration of intercostal/internal mammary artery), lung parenchymal injury, malposition (subcutaneous, intra-abdominal into liver/spleen).
* **Re-expansion Pulmonary Edema (REPE / 復張性肺水腫)**: Rare but fatal complication caused by rapid lung re-expansion (draining > 1.5L fluid rapidly or high-pressure suction on chronially collapsed lung). → ↑ vascular permeability → flash edema.

### Post-Operative Complications (VATS)
* **Early (< 30 days)**: Persistent air leak, bleeding (hemothorax), surgical site infection, pneumonia/atelectasis.
* **Late (> 30 days)**: Recurrent pneumothorax, chronic incisional pain / **Intercostal Neuralgia (肋間神經痛)**.

### Prognosis and Recurrence Rates
* **Conservative (No Surgery)**: Recurrence risk after 1st PSP is ~30%, after 2nd is ~60%, after 3rd is >80%.
* **Post-VATS Bullectomy + Pleurodesis**: Recurrence rate drops dramatically to < 5%.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of signs in a tension pneumothorax? Answer: Tracheal deviation away from the affected side, absent breath sounds on the affected side, and haemodynamic instability (hypotension/tachycardia).
* Viva Question: Why do we insert a chest drain exactly over the superior border of the rib? Answer: To avoid the intercostal neurovascular bundle (vein, artery, nerve), which runs along the costal groove at the inferior border of the rib.
* Viva Question: What fluid parameters mandate the immediate insertion of a chest tube in a parapneumonic effusion? Answer: Pleural fluid pH < 7.2, glucose < 3.3 mmol/L, positive Gram stain or culture, or the presence of frank pus (empyema).
* Trap: Waiting for a portable CXR to confirm a suspected tension pneumothorax before intervening. Pearl: Tension pneumothorax is a clinical diagnosis; you must perform immediate needle decompression before ordering any imaging.
* Trap: Assuming continuous bubbling in an underwater seal drain always represents a persistent internal broncho-pleural air leak. Pearl: Always temporarily clamp the tube close to the patient's skin; if bubbling continues, the leak is external (e.g., loose connection in the tubing system), not from the lung.
* Pitfall: Draining more than 1.5 Liters of pleural fluid rapidly in a single setting during thoracentesis. This significantly ↑ the risk of Re-expansion Pulmonary Edema (REPE) due to sudden reperfusion injury and increased capillary permeability in the chronically collapsed lung.
* Pitfall: Discharging a patient immediately after a successful needle aspiration for PSP without observation. A post-aspiration observation period (e.g., 2-4 hours) with a repeat CXR is mandatory to ensure the pneumothorax does not rapidly re-accumulate.
