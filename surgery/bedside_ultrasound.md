# Bedside Ultrasound (床邊超聲波)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS)
* Bedside imaging performed and interpreted simultaneously by the treating clinician.
* Rapid, dynamic, repeatable assessment → guides immediate diagnosis and procedural intervention.
* Focused answering of binary clinical questions (e.g., "Is there free fluid?" / "Is there a pneumothorax?").

### Epidemiology and Usage in Hong Kong HA Hospitals
* **Standard of Care**: Mandatory adjunct in Advanced Trauma Life Support (ATLS) protocols across all HK Hospital Authority (HA) trauma centers (e.g., QMH, PWH, KWH).
* Heavily utilized in AED resuscitation rooms (急症室), Surgical ICUs, and surgical wards for undifferentiated shock, acute abdomen, and central venous catheter (CVC) insertion.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Physics of Ultrasound (超聲波物理) and Probe Selection
* **Linear Probe (高頻線陣探頭)**: ↑ frequency (7-13 MHz), ↓ depth, ↑ resolution. 
  * Use: Vascular access, pleural line, soft tissue/skin, appendicitis in thin patients.
* **Curvilinear Probe (凸陣探頭)**: ↓ frequency (2-5 MHz), ↑ depth, ↓ resolution. 
  * Use: eFAST, hepatobiliary, AAA, deep abdominal structures.
* **Phased Array Probe (相控陣探頭)**: ↓ frequency, ↓ footprint (fits between ribs). 
  * Use: Echocardiography, thoracic windows.
* **Pathophysiologic Principle**: Fluid is anechoic (black) because it transmits sound waves perfectly; bone/air scatter or reflect sound waves → casting acoustic shadows or artifacts.

### eFAST Anatomical Windows (腹部創傷超聲波重點評估)
* **RUQ (Hepatorenal Recess / Morison's Pouch)**: Evaluates space between liver and right kidney, right pleural space, and subdiaphragmatic space.
* **LUQ (Splenorenal Recess)**: Evaluates space between spleen and left kidney, left pleural space.
* **Pelvic Window (Pouch of Douglas / Rectovesical Pouch)**: Suprapubic view evaluating space posterior to bladder.
* **Subxiphoid / Parasternal**: Evaluates pericardial sac for effusion.
* **Anterior Thorax**: Evaluates bilateral 2nd-4th intercostal spaces at mid-clavicular line for pneumothorax.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Indications Prompting Bedside Ultrasound
* Blunt or penetrating torso trauma.
* Undifferentiated hypotension/shock (RUSH exam protocol).
* Acute abdominal pain (RUQ pain, RIF pain, flank pain).
* Acute dyspnea or hypoxia in post-op setting.

### Pathognomonic Sonographic Signs (特徵性超聲波徵象)
* **Anechoic Stripe (無回聲帶)**: +ve for free fluid (游離液體).
* **Seashore Sign (海岸徵)**: Normal lung sliding on M-mode (rules out pneumothorax at that exact intercostal space).
* **Barcode / Stratosphere Sign (條碼徵)**: Absent lung sliding on M-mode → +ve Pneumothorax (氣胸).
* **Lung Point (肺點)**: Transition point between normal sliding and absent sliding → 100% specific for pneumothorax.
* **Mickey Mouse Sign**: Normal portal triad cross-section (Portal vein = face; CBD = right ear; Hepatic artery = left ear).
* **Target Sign (靶徵)**: Concentric rings seen in cross-section → +ve for Intussusception (腸套疊) or Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Anechoic Free Fluid
* Hemoperitoneum (腹腔積血) (Trauma, ruptured AAA, ectopic pregnancy).
* Ascites (腹水) (Cirrhosis, heart failure, intra-abdominal malignancy).
* Urine (Ruptured bladder).
* Bile (Biliary leak, perforated gallbladder).
* Bowel contents (Perforated viscus).

### POCUS Discriminatory Features in Acute Abdomen
| Clinical Suspicion (臨床懷疑) | Discriminatory POCUS Findings | False Positives / Pitfalls |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Acute Cholecystitis (急性膽囊炎)** | Gallstones (acoustic shadowing), gallbladder wall >3mm, pericholecystic fluid, sonographic Murphy's +ve | Hypoalbuminemia/ascites causing physiological wall thickening |
| **Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎)** | Non-compressible blind-ended tubular structure >6mm diameter, target sign, appendicolith | Normal appendix mimicking thickened bowel; mesenteric adenitis |
| **Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (腹主動脈瘤)** | Aorta diameter >3cm (outer-to-outer wall limit) | Bowel gas obscuring view; measuring IVC instead of Aorta |
| **Hydronephrosis (腎積水)** | Dilated anechoic renal pelvis and calyces (bear paw appearance) | Prominent renal cysts; extrarenal pelvis |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Adjuncts to Bedside Ultrasound
* VBG (Lactate) for tissue perfusion deficit.
* Type & Screen / Crossmatch for potential massive transfusion.
* Serial Hb/Hct levels.

### Escalation Imaging Following Initial POCUS
* **Hemodynamically STABLE +ve eFAST**: MUST proceed to urgent contrast CT Abdomen/Pelvis for precise grading of solid organ injury.
* **Hemodynamically STABLE -ve eFAST + High Clinical Suspicion**: Serial FAST scans vs CT Abdomen/Pelvis.
* **Equivocal Biliary/Appendiceal POCUS**: Formal USG by Radiology Department.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### HA Trauma Management Algorithm (eFAST Integration)
* **Unstable + Blunt Trauma + (+ve) eFAST** → Direct to Operating Theater (OT) for Emergency Laparotomy (緊急剖腹探查術).
* **Unstable + Blunt Trauma + (-ve) eFAST** → Search for extra-abdominal source (Pelvic fracture, severe long bone fractures, intrathoracic bleed) → Resuscitate → Consider pelvic binder/angioembolization.
* **Stable + Blunt Trauma + (+ve) eFAST** → Resuscitate → CT Abdomen/Pelvis to plan possible conservative management (NOM - Non-Operative Management).

### Surgical Indications Derived from POCUS
* **Pericardial Effusion (心包積液) + Cardiac Tamponade (RV diastolic collapse)** → Emergency Pericardiocentesis / Resuscitative Thoracotomy.
* **Massive Hemothorax (血胸) / Tension Pneumothorax** → Immediate Needle Thoracostomy (針刺減壓) → Intercostal Chest Drain (胸管置入).
* **AAA Rupture (>3cm aorta + free fluid + shock)** → Permissive hypotension → Emergency EVAR or Open Repair.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### POCUS-Guided Interventional Principles
* **Real-time Visualization**: Needle tip must be continuously visualized during CVC insertion, paracentesis, or thoracentesis.
* **Doppler Interrogation**: Always apply Color Doppler prior to incision/puncture to identify and avoid aberrant vessels (e.g., Inferior Epigastric Artery during ascitic tap).
* **Asepsis**: Mandatory use of sterile probe covers and sterile ultrasound gel for invasive procedures.

### Ultrasound Procedural Consent Issues in HA
* **Diagnostic POCUS**: Implied consent; legally documented as part of standard physical examination.
* **Elective Procedural POCUS (e.g., CVC, Ascitic Tap)**: Requires formal written informed consent detailing specific risks (bleeding, infection, visceral injury).
* **Emergency Life-Saving POCUS (e.g., Tamponade, Trauma)**: Proceed under HA emergency doctrine; document "Medical Dominance" / sign-off by two medical officers if patient is incapacitated.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Post-operative POCUS Monitoring Indications
* **Bleeding Detection**: Serial FAST scans in recovery room to monitor for expanding hematoma or new free fluid post-laparotomy.
* **Urinary Function**: Bladder scan to assess for post-op urinary retention (POUR). Volume >400ml → in-out catheterization.
* **GI Function**: Bedside USG to assess bowel peristalsis, differentiating physiological post-op ileus (腸梗阻) from mechanical small bowel obstruction (SBO).

### POCUS for ERAS Respiratory Screening
* Early lung USG to screen for post-op basal atelectasis (肺不張) and pleural effusions.
* Guides targeted aggressive chest physiotherapy and incentive spirometry within the ERAS pathway.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Complications of POCUS Misinterpretation
* **False -ve eFAST** → Delayed operative intervention → ↑ mortality/morbidity. 
  * Note: eFAST misses hollow viscus injuries and retroperitoneal bleeds.
* **False +ve eFAST** → Misinterpreting physiological ascites or fluid in bowel loops as hemoperitoneum → Unnecessary non-therapeutic laparotomy.

### Complications of Ultrasound-Guided Procedures
* Hemorrhage / Hematoma (血腫) at puncture site.
* Iatrogenic visceral perforation (e.g., bowel loop puncture, pneumothorax during CVC).
* Intravascular injection or nerve injury during regional blocks.

### Prognostic Value of Ultrasound Findings
* Massive free fluid in ≥3 windows on primary eFAST in a hypotensive patient dictates a very high probability of needing the Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP) and damage-control surgery.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most sensitive anatomical window in eFAST for detecting early intraperitoneal free fluid in a supine trauma patient? Answer: Morison's pouch (Hepatorenal recess) in the Right Upper Quadrant.
* Trap: Assuming a negative eFAST definitively rules out all intra-abdominal injuries. Pearl: eFAST only detects macroscopic free fluid (requires minimum ~200-500ml); it routinely misses hollow viscus perforations, retroperitoneal injuries (e.g., pancreatic tear), and early solid organ lacerations without significant active bleeding.
* Pitfall: Mistaking the inferior vena cava (IVC) for the abdominal aorta during a bedside AAA scan. Confirm the aorta by identifying its thicker, pulsatile echogenic walls, lack of respiratory variation, location anterior to the spine, and by confirming arterial flow with pulsed wave Doppler.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate a true hydronephrosis from multiple renal cysts on bedside ultrasound? Answer: Hydronephrosis demonstrates interconnected anechoic spaces that communicate directly with the renal pelvis (bear paw sign), whereas renal cysts appear as isolated, spherical anechoic structures in the renal cortex.
* Trap: Placing the ultrasound probe parallel to the ribs during an eFAST lung scan to look for pneumothorax. Pearl: The probe must be placed perpendicular to the ribs to clearly visualize the two adjacent rib shadows and the pleural line between them (the "Bat sign") to properly assess for lung sliding.
