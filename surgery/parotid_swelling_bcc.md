# Parotid Swelling & Basal Cell Carcinoma (腮腺腫脹與基底細胞癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Parotid Swellings
* **Parotid Neoplasms (腮腺腫瘤):** Abnormal growths within parotid gland. 80% benign (Pleomorphic Adenoma most common). 20% malignant (Mucoepidermoid Carcinoma most common).
* **Secondary Metastasis:** Parotid nodes drain scalp, face, and external ear → can present as parotid swelling secondary to advanced facial skin cancers.

### Clinical Definition of Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)
* **Basal Cell Carcinoma (基底細胞癌):** Slow-growing, locally invasive non-melanoma skin cancer (NMSC) arising from basal layer of epidermis.
* **Link:** Aggressive/neglected facial BCCs can directly invade parotid or metastasize to intra-parotid lymph nodes (rare but high-yield for exams).

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong Context)
* **NMSC Incidence:** BCC is the most common skin cancer in HK (approx 60% of all skin cancers). ↑ incidence in elderly patients presenting to HA dermatology/surgery clinics.
* **Parotid Tumours:** Pleomorphic adenoma (多形性腺瘤) primarily affects middle-aged females in HK. Warthin's tumour strongly associated with male smokers.
* **Malignancy Risk:** Rule of 80s for parotid → 80% of salivary tumours are in parotid, 80% are benign, 80% of benign are pleomorphic adenomas.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Parotid Gland
* **Location:** Anterior & inferior to external acoustic meatus, wedged between mandible ramus and sternocleidomastoid (SCM).
* **Parotid Fascia:** Dense unyielding fascia (derived from investing layer of deep cervical fascia) → parotitis causes severe pain due to capsular stretch.
* **Deep vs Superficial Lobes:** Artificially divided by Facial Nerve (CN VII) (面神經). 80% of gland is superficial.
* **Stensen's Duct (腮腺管):** Exits anterior border, pierces buccinator, opens opposite 2nd upper molar.

### Facial Nerve (CN VII) Anatomy & Landmarks
* **Exit:** Stylomastoid foramen.
* **Trunk:** Enters posteromedial surface of parotid.
* **Pes Anserinus:** Bifurcates into temporofacial & cervicofacial divisions → then 5 terminal branches (Temporal, Zygomatic, Buccal, Marginal Mandibular, Cervical).
* **Surgical Landmarks (High-Yield):**
* **Tragal pointer:** 1cm deep & inferior to tragal cartilage.
* **Tympanomastoid suture:** Leads directly to stylomastoid foramen.
* **Posterior belly of digastric:** Nerve lies superior to its origin.

### Pathophysiology of BCC & Parotid Invasion
* **BCC Pathogenesis:** UV-induced DNA damage (PTCH1 mutation in Hedgehog pathway).
* **Local Invasion:** Spreads via path of least resistance. Invades dermis, fascia, cartilage, bone.
* **Parotid Involvement:** Direct extension from preauricular/cheek BCC, or lymphatic spread to superficial/deep parotid nodes.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Parotid Swellings
* **Benign:** Painless, slow-growing, unilateral lump.
* **Malignant:** Rapid growth, pain (perineural invasion), facial weakness (CN VII palsy), overlying skin changes.
* **Inflammatory/Infectious:** Sudden onset, severe pain, fever, pus from Stensen's duct, bilateral (Mumps).

### Signs of Parotid Neoplasms
* **Palpation:** Firm/rubbery, lobulated (Pleomorphic adenoma) vs Soft/cystic (Warthin's).
* **Mobility:** Superficial lesions are mobile; deep lobe lesions may present as parapharyngeal mass (medial displacement of tonsil).
* **CN VII Exam:** Assess all 5 branches (wrinkle forehead, close eyes tight, puff cheeks, show teeth, platysma).

### Symptoms & Signs of BCC
* **Nodular BCC:** Pearly nodule, telangiectasia (毛細血管擴張), rolled edges.
* **Ulcerative (Rodent Ulcer):** Central necrotic ulceration with pearly raised margin.
* **Sclerosing/Morphoeic:** Plaque-like, scar-like, ill-defined margins (high risk of deep invasion).
* **Pigmented:** Mimics melanoma (less common in Chinese/HK population but occurs).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Table: Differential Diagnosis of Parotid Mass
| Category | Condition | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Benign Neoplasm** | Pleomorphic Adenoma | Middle-aged female, slow growth, lobulated, cartilaginous/myxoid stroma. |
| **Benign Neoplasm** | Warthin's Tumour | Elderly male smoker, bilateral in 10%, lower pole, cystic spaces. |
| **Malignancy** | Mucoepidermoid Carcinoma | Most common parotid malignancy, CN VII palsy, hard, fixed. |
| **Malignancy** | Adenoid Cystic Carcinoma | High rate of perineural invasion (skip lesions), painful. |
| **Malignancy** | Metastatic Nodes | Secondary to head/neck BCC, SCC, or Melanoma. |
| **Infective** | Acute Bacterial Parotitis | Dehydrated elderly (post-op), *S. aureus*, fever, pus at duct. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Imaging for Parotid
* **Ultrasound (USG) Neck:** Gold standard initial test. Differentiates solid vs cystic, assesses local nodes, guides FNAC.

### Tissue Diagnosis for Parotid
* **USG-guided FNAC (細針抽吸細胞學檢查):** Mandatory for all parotid masses before surgery. Accurately diagnoses benign vs malignant (sensitivity >80%).
* **Core Needle Biopsy:** Used if FNAC is inconclusive or suspect lymphoma, but requires extreme caution to avoid CN VII injury.

### Advanced Imaging for Staging
* **MRI Neck with Contrast:** Modality of choice for parotid malignancy, deep lobe involvement, parapharyngeal space extension, or perineural invasion (CN VII, CN V3).
* **CT Neck/Thorax:** If MRI contraindicated or assessing bone invasion (mandible/temporal bone) and pulmonary metastasis.

### Investigation of BCC
* **Dermoscopy:** Arborising vessels, blue-grey ovoid nests, ulceration.
* **Incisional/Punch Biopsy:** For large lesions to confirm histology before major resection.
* **Excisional Biopsy:** For small lesions (<1cm) where biopsy and treatment are simultaneous.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Management of Benign Parotid Neoplasms
* **Observation:** Rarely indicated (risk of malignant transformation in pleomorphic adenoma → Carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma).
* **Surgical Resection:** Superficial Parotidectomy (淺層腮腺切除術) or Extracapsular Dissection. Enucleation is STRICTLY CONTRAINDICATED (↑ recurrence risk).

### Management of Parotid Malignancy
* **Radical/Total Parotidectomy:** Indicated for malignant tumours.
* **CN VII Resection:** Only if the nerve is directly infiltrated or paralyzed pre-operatively. Requires immediate nerve grafting (e.g., sural nerve, great auricular nerve).
* **Neck Dissection:** Modified radical neck dissection if clinically +ve nodes (N+) or high-grade primary tumour.
* **Adjuvant Radiotherapy:** Indicated for high-grade, +ve margins, deep lobe, perineural invasion, or node +ve disease (Standard HA Oncology protocol).

### Management Algorithm for Facial BCC
* **Low-Risk BCC (Small, nodular, non-critical area):** Standard surgical excision (4mm clinical margins).
* **High-Risk/Morphoeic BCC or Critical Area (Eyelid, Nose, Lip):** Mohs Micrographic Surgery (MMS) or Excision with frozen section control.
* **Unresectable/Medically Unfit:** Primary Radiotherapy.
* **Advanced/Metastatic BCC (e.g., Parotid node invasion):** Vismodegib (Hedgehog pathway inhibitor) under HA targeted therapy protocols + palliative RT.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Superficial Parotidectomy Steps
* **Incision:** Modified Blair incision (preauricular, curving down behind ear lobe into cervical crease).
* **Flap Elevation:** Raise superficial musculoaponeurotic system (SMAS) flap.
* **Identify Main Trunk CN VII:** Use landmarks (tragal pointer, tympanomastoid suture, posterior belly digastric). Use nerve stimulator.
* **Dissection:** Carefully dissect parotid tissue off the nerve branches using fine bipolar diathermy and mosquito forceps.
* **Haemostasis & Closure:** Insert surgical drain (Radivac).

### BCC Excision & Reconstruction
* **Excision Margins:** 4mm for standard BCC; >5mm for high-risk/morphoeic.
* **Reconstruction Ladder:** Primary closure → Local flaps (advancement, rotation, transposition - e.g., Rhomboid flap, Bilobed flap) → Skin grafts (FTSG from pre-auricular/supraclavicular area for face color match).

### Consent Issues (High-Risk Complications)
* **Parotidectomy:** Explicitly consent for temporary/permanent CN VII palsy (facial asymmetry, inability to close eye → exposure keratitis), Frey's syndrome, salivary fistula, earlobe numbness (Great Auricular Nerve sacrifice).
* **BCC Excision:** Consent for scarring, flap failure, recurrence, requirement for further surgery if margins +ve.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care
* **Head Elevation:** 30 degrees to ↓ venous congestion and facial oedema.
* **Drain Management:** Keep vacuum drain until output <15-20ml/24 hours.
* **Eye Care (If CN VII weak):** Prescribe artificial tears, lubricating eye ointment at night, consider eye patch/taping to prevent exposure keratitis (暴露性角膜炎).

### Rehabilitation & Follow-up
* **Facial Physiotherapy:** For temporary neuropraxia of CN VII (recovery takes weeks to months).
* **Oncology Referral:** Discuss all malignancies in Head & Neck Multi-Disciplinary Team (MDT) / Tumour Board for adjuvant RT.
* **Dermatology/Surgical Follow-up:** Annual skin checks for BCC patients (30-50% risk of developing a second NMSC within 5 years).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (Parotidectomy)
* **Haematoma/Haemorrhage:** Requires urgent re-exploration to prevent airway compromise or flap necrosis.
* **Facial Nerve Palsy:** Usually temporary (neuropraxia from traction). Immediate complete palsy requires urgent assessment.
* **Salivary Fistula / Sialocele:** Fluid collection under flap. Management: Pressure dressing, serial aspiration, anticholinergics (Botulinum toxin injection to remnant gland in refractory cases).

### Late Complications (Parotidectomy)
* **Frey's Syndrome (Auriculotemporal Syndrome) (味覺性出汗):** Aberrant re-innervation of sweat glands by parasympathetic salivary fibers. Present as gustatory sweating/flushing over parotid bed during eating. Management: Reassurance, anti-perspirants, Botox injections.
* **Tumour Recurrence:** Especially if Pleomorphic adenoma was enucleated or capsule ruptured (multi-nodular seeding).

### BCC Prognosis
* **Standard BCC:** Excellent prognosis (cure rate >95% with complete excision).
* **Metastatic BCC (to parotid/nodes):** Poor prognosis, requires aggressive multi-modal therapy (Vismodegib + Surgery + RT).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most consistent bony landmark for the facial nerve trunk during parotidectomy? Answer: The tympanomastoid suture.
* Trap: Performing an open incisional biopsy for a suspected parotid pleomorphic adenoma. Pearl: Never do an open biopsy; it disrupts the capsule and causes multifocal tumor seeding. Always use USG-guided FNAC.
* Pitfall: Discharging a patient post-parotidectomy with a facial nerve palsy without assessing the eye. You must actively prescribe eye lubrication and taping to prevent permanent visual loss from exposure keratitis.
* Viva Question: Which nerve is almost always sacrificed during a parotidectomy, leading to numbness of the earlobe? Answer: The Great Auricular Nerve.
* Trap: Reassuring a patient that their facial skin lesion is benign just because it is slow-growing and painless. Pearl: A slow-growing, painless, pearly nodule with telangiectasia is the classic presentation of a Basal Cell Carcinoma and requires biopsy/excision.
* Pitfall: Inadequate excision margins for a morphoeic BCC. Morphoeic BCCs have subclinical microscopic extensions far beyond their visible borders; they require Mohs Micrographic Surgery or wider excision margins.
* Viva Question: What is the anatomical divide between the superficial and deep lobes of the parotid gland? Answer: The facial nerve (CN VII) artificially divides the gland; there is no true anatomical fibrous septum.
