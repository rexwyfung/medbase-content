# Skin & Flaps (皮膚與皮瓣)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Classification of Skin Grafts and Flaps
* **Skin Graft (皮膚移植)**: Segment of epidermal and dermal tissue completely severed from its blood supply and transferred to a recipient site.
* **Split-Thickness Skin Graft (SSG) (刃厚皮片)**: Contains epidermis and a variable portion of the upper dermis.
* **Full-Thickness Skin Graft (FTSG) (全厚皮片)**: Contains epidermis and the entire dermis, excluding subcutaneous fat.
* **Flap (皮瓣)**: Unit of tissue transferred with its own intact or reconnected blood supply.
* **Random Pattern Flap (隨機皮瓣)**: Perfused by the subdermal vascular plexus without a specific named axial artery.
* **Axial Pattern Flap (軸型皮瓣)**: Perfused by a specific, named direct cutaneous axial artery.
* **Free Flap (游離皮瓣)**: Tissue completely detached from its native donor site and revascularized via microvascular anastomosis to recipient vessels.

### Local Epidemiological Context in Hong Kong
* **Major Indications**: Most reconstructions in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) centers are driven by post-wide local excision (WLE) of skin malignancies (e.g., **Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC) (基底細胞癌)**, **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) (鱗狀細胞癌)**), trauma, and diabetic foot ulcers.
* **Burn Injury Distribution**: Managed predominantly in regional burns units (e.g., Prince of Wales Hospital, Queen Mary Hospital).
* **Head and Neck Reconstruction**: High volume of free flaps (e.g., **Anterolateral Thigh (ALT) flap (股外側肌皮瓣)**, **Radial Forearm Flap (前臂游離皮瓣)**) utilized due to endemic **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)** osteoradionecrosis or advanced oral cavity SCC.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Microvascular Anatomy of the Skin
* **Epidermis (表皮)**: Avascular outer layer; relies entirely on diffusion from the dermis.
* **Dermis (真皮)**: Contains dense vascular networks; crucial for graft survival and structural integrity.
* **Subdermal Plexus (皮下血管網)**: Primary vascular network at the dermal-subcutaneous junction; supplies random flaps.
* **Fasciocutaneous Perforators (筋膜皮穿支)**: Vessels traveling along intermuscular septa to supply the deep fascia and skin.
* **Musculocutaneous Perforators (肌皮穿支)**: Vessels passing vertically through skeletal muscle to reach the overlying skin.
* **Angiosome (血管區)**: Three-dimensional block of tissue supplied by a specific source artery and its companion veins.

### Pathophysiology of Skin Graft Take
* **Plasmatic Imbibition (血漿吸收期) (0–48 hours)**: Graft absorbs nutrients and fluid passively from the recipient bed via capillary action; graft weight ↑ by ~40%.
* **Inosculation (血管吻合期) (48–72 hours)**: Alignment and anastomosis of capillaries between the recipient bed and the donor graft.
* **Revascularization / Neovascularization (血管再生期) (>72 hours)**: New blood vessels sprout from the recipient bed and grow into the graft to establish definitive perfusion.
* **Recipient Bed Requirements**: Must be well-vascularized (e.g., granulation tissue, periosteum, perichondrium, paratenon). Graft will fail on bare bone, bare cartilage, bare tendon, or avascular fat.

### Pathophysiology of Flap Ischaemia and Congestion
* **Arterial Insufficiency (動脈供血不足)**: Inadequate inflow → tissue hypoxia → pale, cold flap with delayed capillary refill time (CRT) → ischaemic necrosis.
* **Venous Congestion (靜脈淤血)**: Inadequate outflow → blood accumulation in subdermal plexus → ↑ hydrostatic pressure → mechanical thrombosis of microcirculation → dusky/blue, warm or cold flap with brisk/instant CRT → congestive necrosis.
* **Flap Delay Phenomenon (皮瓣延遲現象)**: Incising a flap in stages prior to complete transfer → induces controlled hypoxia → stimulates angiogenesis and realignment of vessels along the axial orientation → ↑ flap survival length.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Signs of Normal Skin Graft Take
* **Color**: Initially pale (0–48 hours) → pink and well-perfused (>72 hours).
* **Adherence**: Firmly adherent to the recipient bed by day 5 due to fibrin deposition and fibrovascular ingrowth.
* **Moisture**: Maintains normal skin turgor without excessive sloughing or desquamation.

### Clinical Signs of Graft Failure
* **Color Changes**: Dark brown, black, or chalky white appearance.
* **Fluid Accumulation**: Fluctuance beneath the graft surface indicating a **Haematoma (血腫)** or **Seroma (血清腫)**.
* **Sloughing**: Complete separation or liquefaction of the epidermal layer.

### Flap Perfusion Monitoring Parameters
* **Color**: Pink (normal) vs Pale (arterial insufficiency) vs Dusky/Blue/Purple (venous congestion).
* **Temperature**: Warm (normal) vs Cold (arterial or severe venous failure).
* **Capillary Refill Time (CRT) (毛細血管充盈時間)**: 1–2 seconds (normal) vs >3 seconds/absent (arterial failure) vs <1 second/instantaneous (venous congestion).
* **Dermal Prick Test (皮瓣針刺試驗)**: Bright red bleeding within 1–2 seconds (normal) vs No bleeding (arterial failure) vs Dark/maroon bleeding instantly (venous congestion).
* **Handheld Doppler Signal**: Sonorous, triphasic arterial flow over the pedicle marking vs Loss of signal or continuous low-pitch venous hum.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Comparison Between Split-Thickness and Full-Thickness Skin Grafts
| Feature | Split-Thickness Skin Graft (SSG) (刃厚皮片) | Full-Thickness Skin Graft (FTSG) (全厚皮片) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dermal Components Included** | Epidermis + upper part of dermis only | Epidermis + complete layer of dermis |
| **Donor Site Healing Method** | Re-epithelialization from adnexal structures | Primary surgical closure or SSG coverage |
| **Recipient Bed Perfusion Needs** | Lower metabolic demand; takes more easily | High metabolic demand; requires excellent bed |
| **Primary Contraction (Immediate)** | Minimal (recoils less upon harvesting) | High (significant recoil due to elastin fibers) |
| **Secondary Contraction (Late)** | High (significant wound contracture over time) | Minimal (excellent long-term dimensional stability) |
| **Aesthetic & Texture Match** | Poor; often shiny, hypopigmented, or hairless | Excellent; matches color, texture, and hair growth |

### Comparison of Flap Classifications by Vascular Pattern
| Flap Type | Vascular Supply Mechanism | Length-to-Width Ratio Limits | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Random Pattern Flap** | Subdermal vascular plexus only | Strict 1:1 or 2:1 limits in lower limbs | Local transposition/rotation flaps |
| **Axial Pattern Flap** | Direct cutaneous source artery | Highly flexible; limited only by angiosome | Groin flap (superficial circumflex iliac) |
| **Fasciocutaneous Flap** | Perforators along intermuscular septa | Long, reliable design limits | Anterolateral Thigh (ALT) flap |
| **Musculocutaneous Flap** | Perforators passing through muscle body | Broad, bulky volume limits | Latissimus Dorsi (LD) flap |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Pre-operative Laboratory Profiling
* **Full Blood Count (FBC)**: Optimize **Haemoglobin (Hb)** levels (target >10 g/dL) to maximize systemic oxygen delivery to the graft/flap.
* **Coagulation Profile (PT/INR/APTT)**: Baseline screening to prevent postoperative haematoma (causes graft failure) and rule out hypercoagulability (causes free flap microvascular thrombosis).
* **Renal and Glycaemic Profiles**: Strict **HbA1c** and fasting glucose optimization required; poorly controlled diabetes compromises microvascular circulation and wound healing.

### Pre-operative Vascular Imaging Pathways
* **Handheld Acoustic Doppler**: Locates and maps cutaneous perforators on the skin surface prior to design of local or regional flaps.
* **CT Angiography (CTA) (電腦斷層血管造影)**: Gold standard for mapping perforator location, caliber, and course in complex free flaps (e.g., mapping deep inferior epigastric perforators for **DIEP flap (深腹壁下動脈穿支皮瓣)**).
* **Color Duplex Ultrasound**: Evaluates patency and caliber of donor and recipient source vessels (e.g., verifying patency of the palmar arch via modified Allen's test confirmed on ultrasound before **Radial Forearm Free Flap** harvest).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### The Reconstructive Ladder (修復階梯方式)
* **Step 1**: Healing by secondary intention (spontaneous epithelialization).
* **Step 2**: Primary closure (direct suturing).
* **Step 3**: Delayed primary closure.
* **Step 4**: Split-thickness skin graft (SSG).
* **Step 5**: Full-thickness skin graft (FTSG).
* **Step 6**: Local flap (rotation, transposition, advancement).
* **Step 7**: Regional flap.
* **Step 8**: Free tissue transfer / Free flap.
* *Note*: The **Reconstructive Elevator (修復電梯)** concept may bypass steps to select the optimal functional/aesthetic modality immediately.

### Absolute Surgical Indications for Skin Grafts
* Large raw wounds or burns where primary closure is impossible.
* Post-excision defects of skin cancers with well-vascularized beds.
* Temporary or permanent coverage of clean, non-infected granulating wounds.

### Absolute Surgical Indications for Flaps
* Coverage of exposed vital structures (e.g., bare bone, bare cartilage, bare tendon, major nerves, or blood vessels).
* Reconstruction of full-thickness structural defects (e.g., cheek, abdominal wall, eyelid).
* Coverage of wounds over joints where skin grafts would cause disabling **Joint Contracture (關節攣縮)**.
* Coverage of areas subjected to high pressure or weight-bearing (e.g., sacral pressure ulcers, plantar aspect of the foot).
* Coverage of ischemic beds or tissue beds damaged by previous radiotherapy.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Split-Thickness Skin Grafting (SSG)
* **Recipient Bed Preparation**: Ensure meticulous haemostasis via electrocautery or topical adrenaline; eradicate all active infection.
* **Graft Harvesting**: Use a mechanical **Dermatome (取皮機)** adjusted to the desired thickness (typically 0.010 to 0.015 inches). Common donor site: anterior/lateral thigh.
* **Graft Meshing**: Pass the harvested skin through a mesher (e.g., 1:1.5 or 1:2 ratio) to allow expansion, prevent fluid collection (haematoma/seroma) via mesh holes, and increase surface area coverage.
* **Fixation and Dressing**: Secure graft edges with staples or sutures. Apply a **Tie-over Bolster Dressing (打包加壓包紮)** to exert constant, uniform downward pressure, maximizing contact between the graft and the bed.

### Operative Principles of Full-Thickness Skin Grafting (FTSG)
* **Harvesting**: Cut an exact template of the defect out of the donor site (common sites: post-auricular, supraclavicular, groin).
* **Defatting (去脂肪)**: Manually trim all subcutaneous fat from the dermal underside using tenotomy scissors until the glistening white dermis is fully exposed. Residual fat prevents plasmatic imbibition.
* **Donor Site Management**: Close the donor site defect primarily via direct suturing.

### Specific Consent Risks and Morbidities
* **Graft/Flap Failure**: Partial or complete necrosis requiring return to theater, debridement, or alternative reconstruction.
* **Donor Site Morbidity**: Permanent scarring, contour deformity, pain, and sensory loss (e.g., lateral femoral cutaneous nerve injury during ALT flap harvest).
* **Haematoma and Infection**: Risks at both the recipient and donor surgical sites.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Standardized Post-operative Flap Monitoring Protocol (HA Wards)
* **Frequency Schedule**: Hourly for the first 24 hours → 2-hourly for the next 24 hours → 4-hourly until day 5.
* **Environment Controls**: Maintain a warm room temperature (24–26°C) to prevent sympathetic vasoconstriction and vasospasm of the flap pedicle.
* **Positioning Protocols**: Ensure zero external pressure on the flap or its vascular pedicle. Elevate the reconstructed area to promote venous drainage and decrease oedema.
* **Hydration Management**: Maintain strict normovolemia via IV fluids to preserve microvascular perfusion pressures.

### Management of Skin Graft Post-operative Sites
* **Bolster Removal**: Keep the tie-over bolster dressing completely undisturbed for 5–7 days unless signs of infection (odour, purulent discharge, unexplained fever) develop.
* **Donor Site Care**: Dress the SSG donor site with non-adherent hydrophilic dressings; allow it to re-epithelialize undisturbed over 10–14 days.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (0–72 hours)
* **Haematoma and Seroma**: Accumulation of blood or serum beneath a skin graft, separating it mechanically from its nutrient bed → leads to focal or total graft failure.
* **Shearing Forces**: Micro-motion or sliding of the graft relative to the bed → tears delicate capillary buds during inosculation.
* **Pedicle Thrombosis (血管蒂血栓形成)**: Acute arterial or venous occlusion of a free flap anastomosis. Requires **Emergency Surgical Exploration (緊急手術探查)** within a 4–6 hour warm ischemia window for flap salvage.

### Intermediate Complications (3 days to 3 weeks)
* **Infection**: Bacterial proliferation (especially *Streptococcus pyogenes* or *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*) leading to enzymatic destruction of the graft or flap margins.
* **Epidermolysis (表皮剝脫)**: Sloughing of the superficial epidermal layer of a flap or FTSG; often resolves with underlying dermis remaining intact.

### Late Complications (>3 weeks)
* **Secondary Contracture**: Severe shortening and tightening of an SSG, leading to cosmetic disfigurement or functional restriction across joints.
* **Hyperpigmentation**: Darkening of the transferred graft, particularly common when using SSG or distant FTSG on facial defects.
* **Hypertrophic Scarring (增生性疤痕) / Keloids (瘢痕疙瘩)**: Excessive collagen deposition at the donor or recipient margins, highly prevalent in Asian skin types.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What are the three chronological stages of skin graft take? Answer: Plasmatic imbibition (0–48 hours), Inosculation (48–72 hours), and Revascularization/Neovascularization (>72 hours).
* Viva Question: Why must a full-thickness skin graft be meticulously defatted prior to transfer? Answer: Subcutaneous fat is poorly vascularized and acts as a mechanical barrier that prevents plasmatic imbibition from reaching the dermal plexus, leading to total graft necrosis.
* Trap: A flap that turns dusky blue and exhibits instant capillary refill requires observation and elevation only. Pearl: Brisk, instant capillary refill combined with a blue/purple color indicates acute venous congestion, which is a surgical emergency; the patient must be taken back to theater immediately for pedicle exploration or revision of the venous anastomosis.
* Trap: Skin grafts can be reliably placed directly onto exposed cortical bone or bare tendon. Pearl: Skin grafts require a vascularized recipient bed to survive; they will fail completely on cortical bone stripped of periosteum or tendon stripped of paratenon.
* Pitfall: Removing a tie-over bolster dressing prematurely on postoperative day 2 to inspect an SSG. This destroys the fragile capillary bridges forming during the inosculation phase, causing total graft failure. Keep the dressing intact for 5–7 days.
* Pitfall: Allowing a postoperative free flap patient to become hypothermic or hypovolemic in the ward. Hypothermia and hypovolemia trigger intense sympathetic vasoconstriction and peripheral vasospasm, directly inducing pedicle thrombosis and flap death. Ensure aggressive warming and normovolemia.
