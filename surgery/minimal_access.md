# Minimal Access Surgery (微創手術)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Minimal Access Surgery
* Also known as **Laparoscopic Surgery (腹腔鏡手術)** or **Keyhole Surgery (匙孔手術)**.
* Surgical modality utilizing small incisions (typically 0.5–1.5 cm), endoscopes, and specialized elongated instruments.
* Replaces traditional open laparotomy to ↓ trauma, ↓ pain, and ↑ recovery speed.

### Local Epidemiology & HA Context
* Standard of care in **Hospital Authority (HA) (醫院管理局)** for appendectomy, cholecystectomy, and most elective colorectal/hernia operations.
* Highly favored in HK public system to ↓ length of stay (LOS) and optimize HA bed turnover.
* **Robotic Surgery (機械臂輔助手術)** (e.g., Da Vinci) is available in HA but usually requires patient co-payment (實報實銷) as a self-financed item, except for selected urology/gynecology cases.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Cardiovascular Pathophysiology of Pneumoperitoneum (氣腹的心血管生理)
* Requires **CO2 insufflation (二氧化碳充氣)**.
* ↑ Intra-abdominal pressure (IAP) (usually 12-15 mmHg) → ↓ inferior vena cava (IVC) venous return.
* → ↓ Preload → ↓ Cardiac Output (CO) initially.
* → Compensatory ↑ Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR) and ↑ Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP).

### Respiratory Pathophysiology of Pneumoperitoneum (氣腹的呼吸生理)
* ↑ IAP → splinting/elevation of diaphragm (橫膈膜).
* → ↓ Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) and ↓ lung compliance.
* → ↑ Peak airway pressures, V/Q mismatch → ↑ risk of hypoxia/hypercapnia.
* **Hypercapnia (高碳酸血症)**: CO2 absorption across peritoneum → respiratory acidosis (呼吸性酸中毒).

### Surgical Anatomy for Abdominal Access (通道解剖學)
* **Umbilicus (肚臍)**: Natural scar, thinnest abdominal wall layer. Linea alba (白線) and umbilical fascia merge. Safest and most common primary port site.
* **Palmer's Point (帕默氏點)**: Left mid-clavicular line, 3cm below costal margin. Used for access if extensive peri-umbilical adhesions suspected.
* **Inferior Epigastric Vessels (腹壁下血管)**: Run along posterior surface of rectus abdominis (腹直肌). Must visualize via laparoscope (transillumination) before placing lateral ports to avoid severe bleeding.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Signs of Intra-operative Vascular Injury (術中血管損傷)
* Sudden unexplained hypotension out of proportion to estimated blood loss.
* Retroperitoneal hematoma (腹膜後血腫) expanding rapidly on monitor.
* Blood rapidly filling the operative field.

### Signs of Intra-operative Gas Embolism (術中氣體栓塞)
* Pathognomonic: **"Mill-wheel" murmur (水車樣雜音)** on cardiac auscultation.
* Sudden ↓ End-tidal CO2 (EtCO2) (due to ↓ right ventricular outflow).
* Sudden severe hypotension, arrhythmias, right heart failure.

### Signs of Post-operative Visceral Injury (術後內臟損傷)
* Often presents late (post-op day 3-5) due to unrecognized thermal bowel injury.
* **Tachycardia (心跳過速)**, fever, ↑ WBC.
* Diffuse **peritonitis (腹膜炎)** (guarding/rigidity).
* ↑ Output of feculent/biliary fluid from abdominal drains.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Comparison of Open vs Minimal Access Modalities
| Feature | Open Surgery (傳統開腹手術) | Laparoscopic Surgery (腹腔鏡手術) | Robotic Surgery (機械臂輔助手術) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Incision/Trauma** | Large | Small (0.5-1.5cm) | Small (0.8-1.2cm) |
| **Visual Field** | 3D, Direct | 2D (typically), Monitor-based | 3D, High-Definition, Magnified |
| **Instruments** | Full articulation | Rigid (Straight sticks), 4 degrees of freedom | Fully articulating (EndoWrist), 7 degrees of freedom |
| **Surgeon Ergonomics**| Variable | Poor (fulcrum effect, fatigue ↑) | Excellent (seated console, tremor filtration) |
| **Cost in HK HA** | Standard | Standard | ↑↑↑ (Self-financed item) |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Cardiorespiratory Pre-operative Assessment
* Must ensure fitness for pneumoperitoneum and Trendelenburg/Reverse-Trendelenburg positions.
* **ECG (心電圖)**: Rule out ischemic heart disease (IHD) / arrhythmias.
* **CXR (胸部X光)**: Exclude severe bullous emphysema (risk of pneumothorax).
* **Pulmonary Function Tests (肺功能測試)**: Indicated for severe COPD/Asthma patients.
* **ABG (動脈血氣)**: Baseline CO2 for patients with chronic respiratory failure.

### Adhesion Risk Assessment Imaging
* **History**: Ask about prior laparotomies, localized peritonitis, or mesh repairs.
* **CT Abdomen/Pelvis (腹部及盆腔電腦掃描)**: Identifies previous ventral hernia meshes, gross adhesions, or distorted anatomy that may necessitate open access.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Absolute Contraindications to Laparoscopic Surgery
* Inability to tolerate pneumoperitoneum (Severe heart failure, severe COPD).
* Uncorrectable coagulopathy (凝血障礙).
* Massive abdominal distension/bowel obstruction with ↑↑ risk of bowel perforation.
* Hypovolemic shock (低血容量性休克) requiring immediate life-saving laparotomy.

### Relative Contraindications to Laparoscopic Surgery
* Extensive intra-abdominal adhesions (粘連) from multiple previous open surgeries.
* Advanced pregnancy (3rd trimester) (↑ risk of uterine injury; modify port sites).
* Massive organomegaly.

### Common HA Laparoscopic Indications
* **Biliary**: Symptomatic cholelithiasis (膽石) → Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (腹腔鏡膽囊切除術).
* **Colorectal**: Colorectal malignancy → Laparoscopic Colectomy / Anterior Resection.
* **Hernia**: Bilateral or recurrent inguinal hernias → TEP / TAPP repairs.
* **Emergency**: Suspected Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎), perforated peptic ulcer (PPU).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Primary Port Insertion Techniques
* **Open (Hasson) Technique (哈森法)**: 
  * Direct cut-down to linea alba, incise fascia/peritoneum under direct vision.
  * Standard of care in HA for maximum safety; minimal risk of vascular/visceral injury.
* **Closed (Veress Needle) Technique (氣腹針)**:
  * Blind insertion. Rely on tactile "double click" and saline drop test.
  * ↑ risk of retroperitoneal vascular injury.
* **Optical Trocar**: Camera inserted into trocar with transparent tip to visualize layers passing through.

### Pneumoperitoneum Operative Maintenance
* **Gas Used**: **Carbon Dioxide (CO2)**.
* **Why CO2?**: Highly soluble in blood (↓ risk of fatal gas embolism), non-combustible (safe with diathermy).
* **Target IAP**: Maintained continuously at **12-15 mmHg**.

### Essential Laparoscopic Consent Issues
* **MANDATORY**: Explicit consent for **Conversion to Open Surgery (轉為開腹手術)**.
* **Specific Laparoscopic Risks**: Visceral injury (腸道損傷) (1-2/1000), major vascular injury (大血管損傷) (0.5/1000), post-op shoulder tip pain (肩膊痛), port-site hernia (傷口疝氣).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) in Laparoscopy
* **Early Mobilization (早期下床活動)**: Same day / POD 1. ↓ risk of DVT/PE, ↓ atelectasis.
* **Early Enteral Nutrition (早期進食)**: Promotes return of bowel function, ↓ ileus.
* **Catheter Removal**: Early removal of Foley catheter to ↓ CAUTI.

### Multimodal Analgesia Strategy (多模式鎮痛)
* Minimal access inherently requires less opioid analgesia.
* Intra-op: Local anesthetic (LA) infiltration (Bupivacaine) into all port-site fascia/skin.
* Post-op: Regular Paracetamol (撲熱息痛) + NSAIDs (e.g., Celecoxib).
* Avoid excessive opioids to prevent delayed bowel function (ileus).

### Wound and Port-Site Care
* Dressings usually removed at 48 hours.
* Sutures/clips (if non-absorbable) removed at 7-10 days.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Severe Intra-operative Complications
* **Major Vascular Injury**: Aorta, IVC, or iliac vessels. 
  * Management: Immediately leave instrument in place to tamponade, convert to urgent open laparotomy, call vascular surgeon.
* **Bowel Thermal Injury (腸道灼傷)**: Capacitive coupling or direct diathermy burn. Often missed intra-op.
* **Gas Embolism**: CO2 directly into a vessel.
  * Management: Stop insufflation, 100% O2, **Durant's maneuver** (Left lateral decubitus + Trendelenburg to trap gas in RV apex).

### Early Post-operative Complications (POD 1-7)
* **Shoulder Tip Pain (肩膊痛)**: Referred pain via **Phrenic Nerve (膈神經) (C3,4,5)** from residual CO2 irritating the diaphragm. Self-limiting.
* **Surgical Emphysema (外科皮下氣腫)**: Crepitus around ports/neck. Usually resolves spontaneously with O2 therapy.
* **Unrecognized Visceral Injury**: Presents as sepsis/peritonitis days later as ischemic bowel wall sloughs off.

### Late Post-operative Complications
* **Port-site Hernia (切口疝氣)**: Bowel/omentum herniating through fascial defect. 
  * Prevention: Must routinely close fascia for any port **≥10 mm**.
* **Port-site Metastasis (切口轉移)**: Extremely rare. Prevented by pulling malignant specimens out inside an **Endobag (標本袋)**.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the preferred gas for pneumoperitoneum and why? Answer: Carbon Dioxide (CO2). It is highly soluble in blood (minimizes severity of gas embolism), non-combustible (safe for diathermy), and inexpensive.
* Viva Question: A patient collapses immediately upon insertion of the Veress needle and insufflation. EtCO2 drops to zero and a "mill-wheel" murmur is heard. What is the diagnosis and immediate management? Answer: Massive CO2 gas embolism. Stop insufflation, desufflate abdomen, 100% O2, place patient in left lateral decubitus and Trendelenburg position (Durant's maneuver), and consider central line aspiration of RV.
* Viva Question: Which port sizes require formal fascial closure at the end of a laparoscopic procedure to prevent herniation? Answer: Any port ≥10 mm (e.g., 10 mm, 12 mm, or 15 mm ports). 5 mm ports generally do not require fascial closure.
* Trap: Reassuring a patient with severe day 4 post-op abdominal pain and tachycardia that "laparoscopic pain is normal". Pearl: Delayed presentation of severe pain and sepsis 3-5 days after laparoscopy is the classic presentation of unrecognized thermal bowel injury. Immediate CT with contrast or re-laparoscopy is required.
* Pitfall: Inserting lateral trocars strictly based on surface landmarks. Always visualize the abdominal wall internally with the laparoscope (transillumination) to identify and avoid the inferior epigastric vessels before lateral port insertion.
