# Patient Blood Management in Surgery (外科病人血液管理)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Patient Blood Management
* **Patient Blood Management (病人血液管理)**: Multidisciplinary, evidence-based approach to optimize patient's endogenous blood mass, minimize surgical blood loss, and harness patient-specific physiological tolerance of anemia.
* Goal: ↓ reliance on **Allogeneic Blood Transfusion (異體輸血)** → ↓ morbidity, mortality, and healthcare costs.

### Local Epidemiology and Blood Usage in Hong Kong
* Managed centrally by **Hong Kong Red Cross Blood Transfusion Service (香港紅十字會輸血服務中心)**.
* Aging population (高齡化人口) in HK → ↑ demand for blood products (e.g., major orthopedic/oncological surgeries) + ↓ eligible donor pool.
* **Hospital Authority (醫院管理局)** promotes restrictive transfusion strategies to preserve limited local blood inventory.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Pathophysiology of Pre-operative Anemia
* **Iron Deficiency Anemia (缺鐵性貧血)**: Most common. Absolute (dietary/GI bleed) vs. Functional (chronic inflammation/hepcidin ↑).
* Tissue hypoxia → ↑ cardiac output (tachycardia) → ↑ risk of perioperative **Myocardial Infarction (心肌梗塞)**.

### Coagulation Cascade and Surgical Bleeding
* **Primary Hemostasis (初級止血)**: Platelet adhesion + aggregation → temporary plug.
* **Secondary Hemostasis (次級止血)**: Clotting factor cascade (Intrinsic/Extrinsic) → stable fibrin clot.
* **Fibrinolysis (纖維蛋白溶解)**: Plasmin breaks down fibrin. Hyperfibrinolysis in trauma/major surgery → massive bleeding.

### Pathophysiology of Massive Transfusion
* **Lethal Triad (死亡三角)**: **Hypothermia (低溫症)** + **Acidosis (酸中毒)** + **Coagulopathy (凝血障礙)**.
* Rapid administration of cold, citrate-rich packed RBCs (PRBCs) → binds ionized calcium (Ca2+) → **Hypocalcemia (低血鈣)** → worsening coagulopathy + myocardial depression.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Signs of Acute Surgical Bleeding
* Tachycardia (心跳過速) (Earliest sign)
* Hypotension (低血壓) (Late sign, indicates >30% blood volume loss)
* Pallor (蒼白)
* Oliguria (少尿) (↓ renal perfusion)
* Altered mental status (意識改變)

### Symptoms of Pre-operative Anemia
* Fatigue/Lethargy (疲倦)
* Exertional dyspnea (勞動性氣促)
* Palpitations (心悸)
* Angina (心絞痛) (in susceptible patients)

### Signs of Acute Transfusion Reactions
* Fever (發燒) / Chills (發冷)
* Urticaria/Rash (蕁麻疹)
* Dyspnea/Hypoxia (呼吸困難)
* Hematuria (血尿) (Hemoglobinuria from hemolysis)
* Flank pain (腰痛)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentials for Post-operative Bleeding: Surgical vs. Medical Causes
| Feature | **Surgical Bleeding (外科出血)** | **Medical Coagulopathy (凝血障礙)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Site of bleeding** | Localized (e.g., drain, wound bed) | Diffuse (e.g., oozing from IV sites, mucosal) |
| **Drain output** | Frank red blood, large volume, sudden ↑ | Serosanguinous, slow continuous ooze |
| **Coagulation profile (PT/APTT/Plt)** | Normal | Abnormal (Prolonged PT/APTT, ↓ Plt) |
| **Hemodynamic status** | Rapid deterioration if major vessel | Gradual deterioration |
| **Management principle** | Urgent re-exploration / Angioembolization | Transfusion of specific blood products / Correction of underlying cause |

### Differentials for Acute Respiratory Distress Post-Transfusion
| Condition | **TRALI (輸血相關急性肺損傷)** | **TACO (輸血相關循環超載)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pathophysiology** | Immune-mediated capillary leak | Volume overload |
| **Patient Profile** | Any patient | Elderly, known CHF/CKD |
| **Blood Pressure** | Normal or ↓ | ↑ (Hypertension) |
| **Echocardiogram** | Normal ejection fraction | ↓ EF, fluid overload |
| **BNP level** | Normal | ↑↑ Elevated |
| **Management** | Supportive (O2, Ventilation), DO NOT give diuretics | O2, Diuretics (Lasix), Upright position |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Hematological Investigations
* **Complete Blood Count (全血細胞計數)**: Check Hb, MCV, Platelets.
* **Iron Profile (鐵質組合)**: Ferritin, Transferrin saturation (TSAT), TIBC.
* **Coagulation Profile (凝血指標)**: PT, APTT, INR.
* **Renal Function Test (腎功能檢查)**: Cr, Urea (assess EPO production capability/TACO risk).

### Advanced Coagulation Workup
* **Thromboelastography (TEG)** / **Rotational Thromboelastometry (ROTEM)**:
* Evaluates real-time global hemostasis (clot formation, strength, lysis).
* R-time/CT prolonged → needs FFP.
* Maximum Amplitude (MA)/MCF ↓ → needs Platelets/Cryoprecipitate.
* LY30 ↑ → needs **Tranexamic Acid (氨甲環酸)**.

### Pre-operative Type and Screen Protocols
* **Group and Screen (G&S) (血型及抗體篩查)**: Checks ABO/Rh(D) + atypical antibodies. Valid for 72 hrs. For surgeries with low expected blood loss (e.g., Lap Chole).
* **Type and Crossmatch (血型配對)**: Allocates specific PRBC units for the patient. For surgeries with high expected blood loss (>10% blood volume, e.g., Whipple, AAA repair).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Three Pillars of Patient Blood Management Algorithm
* Pillar 1: Optimize erythropoiesis (Pre-op).
* Pillar 2: Minimize blood loss and bleeding (Intra-op/Peri-op).
* Pillar 3: Harness and optimize physiological tolerance of anemia (Post-op).

### Pre-operative Anemia Optimization (Pillar 1 HA Protocols)
* Goal: Detect anemia >4 weeks pre-op.
* Iron deficiency (Ferritin <30 µg/L or TSAT <20%): Give **Oral Iron (口服鐵劑)**. If intolerant or surgery <4 weeks: **Intravenous Iron (靜脈注射鐵劑)** (e.g., Ferric Carboxymaltose).
* Renal anemia / Chronic disease: **Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (EPO)** + IV Iron.
* Stop antiplatelets/anticoagulants per HA perioperative guidelines.

### Intra-operative Blood Conservation Strategies (Pillar 2 Principles)
* Meticulous surgical hemostasis (Cautery, topical hemostats/Surgicel).
* Mainstay medication: **Tranexamic Acid (TXA) (氨甲環酸)** (Antifibrinolytic). 1g IV loading → 1g IV infusion.
* **Intraoperative Cell Salvage (IOCS) (術中血液回收)**: Collects, washes, and returns patient's own shed blood. Indicated if anticipated blood loss >1000ml.
* Maintenance of normothermia (Bair hugger) to prevent coagulopathy.
* Permissive hypotension (Target MAP 60-65 mmHg) during specific phases (e.g., hepatic parenchymal transection) to ↓ venous bleeding.

### Post-operative Transfusion Triggers (Pillar 3 HA Guidelines)
* Restrictive strategy is gold standard in HK HA.
* **Hb < 7 g/dL**: Threshold for healthy, hemodynamically stable patients.
* **Hb < 8 g/dL**: Threshold for patients with pre-existing cardiovascular disease, symptomatic anemia, or acute coronary syndrome (ACS).
* Transfuse 1 unit of PRBC at a time → reassess clinical status and Hb.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Hemostasis Principles
* Anticipate major vessel anatomy.
* Proximal and distal control before entering high-risk vascular beds.
* Use appropriate energy devices (Harmonic scalpel, LigaSure).

### Consent for Blood Transfusion (HA Protocols)
* Must obtain explicit, documented consent for receiving blood products.
* Explain risks: Allergic reactions, fever, infections (HBV, HCV, HIV - rare in HK due to NAT testing), TRALI, TACO.
* **Jehovah's Witnesses (耶和華見證人)**: Refuse primary blood components (Red cells, white cells, platelets, plasma). May accept fractions (albumin, immunoglobulins) or cell salvage. Must respect autonomy. Requires extensive Pre-op MDT planning (EPO, iron) and strict bloodless surgery techniques.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Post-operative Hemodynamic Monitoring
* Continuous continuous vital signs monitoring (BP, HR, SpO2).
* Serial Hb monitoring ONLY if active bleeding suspected; avoid routine daily phlebotomy to ↓ iatrogenic blood loss.
* Monitor surgical drain character and volume.

### Post-operative Nutritional Support for Anemia
* Early enteral feeding per ERAS.
* Iron-rich diet / Oral iron supplementation upon discharge if Hb remains low but patient is asymptomatic.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Transfusion Complications (<24 hrs)
* **Acute Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction (急性溶血性輸血反應)**: ABO incompatibility (Clerical error). Triad of fever, flank pain, hemoglobinuria. → Stop transfusion immediately, aggressive IV fluids, maintain urine output.
* **Febrile Non-Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction (發熱性非溶血性輸血反應)**: Cytokine release from donor WBCs. Most common. Treat with Paracetamol.
* Allergic Reactions: Mild (urticaria → antihistamines) to Anaphylaxis (hypotension/bronchospasm → Adrenaline).
* TRALI and TACO (See Section 4).

### Late Transfusion Complications (>24 hrs)
* **Delayed Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction**: 5-14 days post-transfusion. Extravascular hemolysis due to minor RBC antigens (e.g., Kidd, Duffy).
* **Transfusion-Transmitted Infections (輸血傳播感染)**: Bacterial (most common in platelets due to room temp storage), Viral (HBV, HCV, HIV).
* **Alloimmunization (同種免疫)**: Formation of antibodies complicating future crossmatches.
* **Iron Overload (鐵質積聚)**: In chronically transfused patients.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the mechanism of action of Tranexamic Acid (TXA)? Answer: It is a synthetic analog of lysine that reversibly binds to plasminogen, preventing its conversion to plasmin, thereby inhibiting fibrinolysis and stabilizing clots.
* Viva Question: What is the formula for massive transfusion protocol (MTP) component therapy in severe trauma? Answer: Provide PRBC, FFP, and Platelets in a 1:1:1 ratio to mimic whole blood and prevent dilutional coagulopathy.
* Trap: Automatically giving 2 units of PRBCs for a non-bleeding patient with Hb 6.8 g/dL. Pearl: Current HA standard is single-unit transfusion followed by clinical reassessment and a repeat Hb check to minimize unnecessary exposure and volume overload.
* Trap: Attempting to reverse Apixaban (NOAC) with Vitamin K or FFP in an acutely bleeding surgical patient. Pearl: FFP/Vit K is for Warfarin. For factor Xa inhibitors like Apixaban, use Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (PCC) or specific reversal agents like Andexanet alfa if available.
* Pitfall: Continuing transfusion when a patient develops sudden dyspnea and hypertension. This is likely TACO. The transfusion must be stopped immediately, the patient sat up, and IV diuretics administered.
* Pitfall: Neglecting to check ionized calcium during a massive hemorrhage protocol. Citrate toxicity rapidly depletes ionized Ca2+, leading to refractory hypotension and worsening bleeding; IV calcium chloride or gluconate must be actively replaced.
