# Spinal Cord Compression (脊髓壓迫症)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Spinal Cord Compression
* **Malignant Spinal Cord Compression (MSCC) (惡性脊髓壓迫症)**: Pathological compression of dural sac + contents (spinal cord/cauda equina) by extradural tumor mass
* **Non-Malignant Compression**: Compression secondary to trauma, infection (**Spinal Epidural Abscess (脊柱硬膜外膿腫)**), degenerative disease, or disc herniation
* **Neurological Surgical Emergency (神經外科急症)** requiring intervention <24-48h to prevent irreversible paraplegia/tetraplegia

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) Settings
* Most common cause in HK = **Metastatic disease (轉移性疾病)**
* Most common primary tumors in HK causing MSCC:
  * **Lung Cancer (肺癌)** (Highest incidence)
  * **Breast Cancer (乳癌)**
  * **Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)**
  * **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) (肝細胞癌)** (Endemic in HK d/t HBV)
  * **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)** (Endemic in South China/HK)
* Age peak: 50-70 years
* Anatomical distribution: Thoracic (70%) > Lumbar (20%) > Cervical (10%)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Spinal Cord Anatomy Relevant to Compression
* Spinal cord terminates at **L1/L2 intervertebral disc** (Conus Medullaris)
* Lesions above L1/L2 → **Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) (上運動神經元)** signs
* Lesions below L1/L2 → **Lower Motor Neuron (LMN) (下運動神經元)** signs (Cauda Equina)
* Blood supply: Anterior spinal artery (1) + Posterior spinal arteries (2) + Artery of Adamkiewicz (T9-T12)

### Pathophysiological Mechanisms of Spinal Cord Compression
* **Direct mechanical compression (直接機械性壓迫)**: Tumor expansion in epidural space → neuronal deformation
* **Vascular compromise (血管受壓)**: Venous plexus compression → vasogenic edema → ↑ interstitial pressure → arterial occlusion → spinal cord ischemia/infarction
* **Location Classification**:
  * **Extradural (硬膜外)** (95%): Metastases, abscess, hematoma, disc
  * **Intradural Extramedullary (硬膜內髓外)** (4%): Meningioma, schwannoma
  * **Intramedullary (髓內)** (1%): Ependymoma, astrocytoma

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Symptoms of Spinal Cord Compression
* **Back Pain (背痛)** (90-95%): Usually first symptom, precedes neuro deficits by weeks/months
  * Character: Progressive, severe, unremitting
  * Worsening factors: Recumbency (night pain), coughing, straining (Valsalva)
  * Radicular pain (放射痛): Band-like/girdle pain around chest/abdomen

### Late Symptoms of Spinal Cord Compression
* **Motor Weakness (運動無力)** (60-85%): Progressive symmetric/asymmetric leg weakness → difficulty walking
* **Sensory Deficit (感覺缺損)**: Ascending numbness, tingling
* **Autonomic/Sphincter Dysfunction (括約肌功能障礙)**: Urinary retention, fecal incontinence (Late & poor prognostic sign)

### Upper Motor Neuron Signs (上運動神經元體徵) (Lesion > L1/L2)
* **Tone**: ↑ (Spasticity)
* **Reflexes**: ↑ Hyperreflexia
* **Clonus**: +ve
* **Babinski Sign**: Extensor (Upgoing plantar)
* **Sensory Level**: Well-defined dermatomal sensory loss corresponding to spinal level

### Lower Motor Neuron Signs (下運動神經元體徵) (Lesion < L1/L2)
* **Tone**: ↓ (Flaccidity)
* **Reflexes**: ↓ Hyporeflexia/Areflexia
* **Muscle Bulk**: Atrophy/Wasting
* **Fasciculations**: Present

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnoses for Acute Spinal Cord Syndromes
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Malignant Extradural Compression** | Hx of primary malignancy, insidious localized pain, progressive UMN signs |
| **Spinal Epidural Abscess (脊柱硬膜外膿腫)** | Fever, ↑ WCC, ↑ CRP/ESR, IVDU hx, recent bacteremia, excruciating spinal tenderness |
| **Prolapsed Intervertebral Disc (PID)** | Sudden onset after lifting, pain > weakness, usually LMN signs (radiculopathy/cauda equina) |
| **Transverse Myelitis (橫貫性脊髓炎)** | Rapid onset (hours/days), post-viral hx, MS association, severe inflammatory response on CSF |
| **Epidural Hematoma (硬膜外血腫)** | Sudden onset, anticoagulant use, coagulopathy, recent lumbar puncture/trauma |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Gold Standard Imaging for Spinal Cord Compression
* **MRI Whole Spine (全脊柱磁力共振)** with & without Gadolinium
  * **Mandatory (必須)** for all suspected MSCC
  * Rationale: Accurately delineates cord compression, soft tissue extension, and identifies skip lesions (present in 10-30% cases)

### Adjunct Imaging for Spinal Workup
* **CT Spine**: Assesses bone stock, trabecular destruction, spinal instability (useful for pre-op surgical planning)
* **X-ray Spine (Plain films)**: Winking owl sign (missing pedicle), vertebral collapse, wedge fractures (Late signs, low sensitivity)
* **CT Thorax/Abdomen/Pelvis (CT TAP)**: For staging and identifying unknown primary

### Pre-operative Laboratory Investigations
* **CBP**: WCC (infection), Hb (anemia of chronic disease/bleeding risk), Plt
* **LRFTs**: Alk Phos (↑ in bone mets), Albumin (nutritional status)
* **Coagulation Profile**: PT/APTT/INR (crucial before surgery/biopsy)
* **Tumor Markers**: AFP (HCC), PSA (Prostate), CEA (Colorectal), EBV DNA (NPC)

### Spinal Metastasis Scoring Systems (Surgical Decision Making)
| Tokuhashi Scoring System (Prognosis & Survival) | Points Range | Surgical Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **General Condition (KPS)** | 0 - 2 | Score 0-8 (Life exp < 6 mo): Conservative / Palliative RT |
| **No. of Extraspinal Bone Mets** | 0 - 2 | Score 9-11 (Life exp >= 6 mo): Palliative Surgery |
| **No. of Vertebral Mets** | 0 - 2 | Score 12-15 (Life exp >= 1 yr): Excisional Surgery |
| **Metastases to Major Organs** | 0 - 2 | |
| **Primary Site of Cancer** | 0 - 5 | |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Immediate Medical Management in Hong Kong HA
* **Dexamethasone (地塞米松)** (High dose):
  * Bolus: 16mg IV/PO immediately STAT
  * Maintenance: 8mg BD (or 4mg QDS) PO/IV
  * Rationale: ↓ vasogenic edema → relieves pressure → neuroprotection
  * Note: Co-prescribe PPI (e.g., Pantoprazole) for GI protection
* **Analgesia**: WHO analgesic ladder (Paracetamol → Tramadol/NSAIDs → Morphine/Oxycodone)
* **Spinal Immobilization**: Bed rest flat until spinal stability cleared by orthopedics/neurosurgery

### Indications for Surgical Decompression
* Spinal instability (Spinal Instability Neoplastic Score [SINS] >= 7)
* Bony compression causing the neural deficit (RT cannot shrink bone)
* Radioresistant tumors (e.g., Renal cell ca, Thyroid ca, HCC, Melanoma)
* Progression of neurological deficits during/after Radiotherapy
* Unknown primary tumor requiring histological diagnosis (Biopsy)
* Good general prognosis (Life expectancy > 3-6 months)

### Indications for Radiotherapy (放射治療)
* Radiosensitive tumors (e.g., Myeloma, Lymphoma, Prostate Ca, Breast Ca)
* Life expectancy < 3 months (palliative intent)
* Surgically unfit patients or multi-level diffuse disease
* Post-operative adjuvant therapy (2-3 weeks post-surgery)

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Common Surgical Procedures for Decompression
* **Posterior Decompressive Laminectomy (椎板切除術) +/- Instrumentation**:
  * Removal of lamina + spinous process
  * Used for posterior compression or limited life expectancy
  * Often requires pedicle screw fixation (instrumentation) to prevent iatrogenic instability
* **Anterior Corpectomy (椎體切除術) and Reconstruction**:
  * Removal of vertebral body + adjacent discs
  * Used for anterior compression (most metastases occur in vertebral body)
  * Reconstruction with titanium cage + bone graft + anterior plating
* **Separation Surgery (分離手術)**: Minimally invasive decompression to create a gap between tumor and spinal cord, followed by stereotactic radiosurgery (SRS)

### Pre-operative Consent and Risks
* **Infection**: Wound infection, meningitis (if dural tear)
* **Bleeding**: High volume blood loss (especially highly vascular tumors like HCC, Renal Ca, Thyroid Ca) → Pre-op embolization may be needed
* **Neurological Injury**: Worsening of paraplegia, CSF leak (dural tear)
* **Hardware Failure**: Screw loosening, cage subsidence
* **General**: DVT/PE, pneumonia, MI, mortality

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Monitoring
* **Neurological Charting**: Hourly neuro observations (power, sensation, GCS)
* **Drain Management**: Monitor Hemovac output (Remove when <30-50ml/24h)
* **Steroid Weaning**: Gradual taper of Dexamethasone over 1-2 weeks to prevent adrenal crisis and ↓ infection risk
* **VTE Prophylaxis**: Mechanical (TED/Flowtrons) ± Chemical (LMWH, after ruling out post-op hematoma on Day 1-2)

### Spinal Rehabilitation Protocol
* **Mobilization**: Early mobilization within 24-48h if spine stable
* **Orthosis**: TLSO (Thoracolumbosacral orthosis) or Cervical collar depending on surgical level and construct stability
* **Multidisciplinary Team (MDT)**: Physiotherapy (PT) for mobility/transfers, Occupational Therapy (OT) for ADLs, Medical Social Worker (MSW) for placement (e.g., convalescent hospital/hospice)
* **Bladder/Bowel Care**: Regular catheterization regimens, laxatives for neurogenic bowel

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (< 30 days)
* Epidural hematoma causing acute re-compression
* Surgical site infection (SSI) (↑ risk d/t high-dose steroids, prior RT, malnutrition)
* CSF leak / Pseudomeningocele
* DVT / Pulmonary Embolism (PE) (Highest risk in malignancy + immobility)

### Late Post-operative Complications (> 30 days)
* Construct failure / pseudoarthrosis
* Adjacent segment disease
* Tumor recurrence
* Radiation myelopathy (if post-op RT given)

### Prognostic Factors in Spinal Cord Compression
* **Pre-treatment Neurological Status (Crucial)**: Ambulatory pre-op → >80% remain ambulatory post-op. Non-ambulatory >48h pre-op → <10% regain ambulation.
* **Tumor Biology**: Myeloma/Lymphoma/Breast/Prostate (Better) vs Lung/HCC/Gastric (Worse)
* **Rapidity of Onset**: Slower onset of deficits = better potential for recovery

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical prognostic factor for functional recovery in MSCC? Answer: The patient's ambulatory and neurological status immediately prior to therapeutic intervention.
* Viva Question: Why do we co-prescribe a PPI when starting Dexamethasone for spinal cord compression? Answer: High-dose steroids significantly increase the risk of peptic ulcer disease and GI bleeding, which is exacerbated by concurrent physiological stress.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with newly diagnosed spinal metastases requires immediate surgical fixation. Pearl: Assess spinal stability using the SINS criteria; many mechanically stable spine metastases can be managed primarily with radiotherapy and bisphosphonates.
* Trap: Discharging an elderly patient with new-onset, severe, unremitting thoracic back pain without neurological deficits. Pearl: Back pain without a clear mechanical trigger in the elderly or those with a cancer history is a red flag and warrants an urgent whole spine MRI to rule out early MSCC.
* Pitfall: Delaying high-dose steroids while waiting for MRI confirmation. If MSCC is highly suspected clinically, administer STAT Dexamethasone (16mg) immediately to halt neurological deterioration, then arrange urgent imaging.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to examine the anal sphincter tone and perianal sensation. Autonomic sphincter dysfunction is a critical late sign of spinal cord or cauda equina compression and dictates extreme surgical urgency.
