# Liver Tumours (肝臟腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Liver Tumours
* **Liver Tumours (肝臟腫瘤)** encompass a heterogeneous group of benign and malignant neoplasms originating from primary hepatic parenchymal cells, biliary epithelium, or stromal tissue, or arising secondary to metastatic spread from distant primary malignancies.
* Primary malignant neoplasms include **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC, 原發性肝細胞癌)** and **Cholangiocarcinoma (CCA, 膽管癌)**.
* Primary benign neoplasms include **Hepatic Hemangioma (肝血管瘤)**, **Focal Nodular Hyperplasia (FNH, 局灶性結節性增生)**, and **Hepatic Adenoma (肝腺瘤)**.

### Local Epidemiology of Hepatocellular Carcinoma in Hong Kong
* **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)** ranks as the 5th most common cancer and the 3rd leading cause of cancer deaths in Hong Kong.
* Age-standardized incidence rates show a significant male predominance with a male-to-female ratio of approximately 3:1 to 4:1.
* Median age of presentation in Hong Kong is between 60 and 65 years.
* Chronic **Hepatitis B Virus (HBV, 乙型肝炎病毒)** infection accounts for over 75% to 80% of all HCC cases locally due to historical vertical transmission patterns prior to the introduction of universal neonatal vaccination in 1988.

### Risk Factors for Primary Liver Malignancies
* Chronic **HBV Infection** with high viral load ($HBV\ DNA > 2000\ IU/mL$) is the primary driver in Hong Kong.
* Chronic **Hepatitis C Virus (HCV, 丙型肝炎病毒)** infection is an increasing risk factor among specific high-risk cohorts.
* **Liver Cirrhosis (肝硬化)** of any etiology, including alcoholic liver disease and non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), now termed metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH).
* Dietary exposure to **Aflatoxin B1** derived from contaminated peanuts, grains, and soy products stored in humid environments.
* Concomitant metabolic comorbidities including obesity, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and metabolic syndrome which synergize with viral hepatitis to amplify oncogenic risk.


## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Couinaud Liver Segmentation System
* The **Couinaud Classification (庫伊諾分段法)** divides the liver into 8 anatomically independent segments based on portal vascular inflow, hepatic arterial supply, and biliary drainage.
* Each segment contains its own central **Portal Triad (門脈三聯件)** bundle consisting of a portal vein branch, hepatic artery branch, and bile duct radicle.
* The segments are separated by the three major hepatic veins:
* The **Middle Hepatic Vein (肝中靜脈)** runs in **Cantlie's Line (坎特利線)**, extending from the middle of the gallbladder fossa anteriorly to the left side of the inferior vena cava (IVC) posteriorly, dividing the liver into functional **Right Lobe** and **Left Lobe**.
* The **Right Hepatic Vein (肝右靜脈)** divides the right lobe into **Anterior Segments (Segments V and VIII)** and **Posterior Segments (Segments VI and VII)**.
* The **Left Hepatic Vein (肝左靜脈)** divides the left lobe into **Medial Segment (Segment IV)** and **Lateral Segments (Segments II and III)**.
* **Segment I (Caudate Lobe, 尾狀葉)** is anatomically distinct, receiving vascular inflow directly from both left and right portal triads and draining directly into the IVC via multiple independent short hepatic veins.

### Hepatic Vascular Anatomy and Inflow Control
* Total hepatic blood inflow is approximately $1500\ mL/min$.
* The **Portal Vein (門靜脈)** delivers 70% to 75% of total blood volume but supplies only 50% to 55% of available oxygen to normal parenchyma.
* The **Hepatic Artery (肝動脈)** delivers 25% to 30% of total blood volume but provides 45% to 50% of oxygen supply to normal parenchyma.
* Primary malignant liver tumours (HCC) derive 90% to 95% of their vascular supply from the **Hepatic Artery** via neo-angiogenesis, providing the physiological rationale for transarterial locoregional interventions.
* The **Portal Triad** components are enveloped by the **Glissonian Sheath (格利森氏鞘)**, which enables en bloc extrahepatic or intrahepatic isolation during anatomical resections.

### Pathophysiology of Cirrhosis-Driven Carcinogenesis
* Chronic necroinflammation mediated by HBV viral integration or toxic insults drives repetitive cycles of hepatocyte apoptosis and compensatory cellular regeneration.
* Accumulation of somatic mutations and epigenetic alterations targets key tumor suppressor genes (e.g., *TP53*, *RB1*) and activates oncogenic pathways (e.g., Wnt/$\beta$-catenin, PI3K/Akt/mTOR).
* Deposition of dense extracellular matrix by activated **Hepatic Stellate Cells (肝星狀細胞)** alters architectural microenvironments, inducing **Portal Hypertension (門脈高壓)** and mechanical cellular stress.
* Progression tracks through a predictable histological continuum: Chronic Hepatitis → Regenerative Nodule → Low-Grade Dysplastic Nodule → High-Grade Dysplastic Nodule → Early HCC → Advanced HCC.

### Pathophysiology of Benign Liver Tumours
* **Hepatic Hemangioma**: The most common benign liver tumor; a vascular malformation consisting of endothelium-lined cavernous spaces filled with slow-moving blood; does not carry malignant potential.
* **Focal Nodular Hyperplasia (FNH)**: A hyperplastic parenchymal response to a pre-existing congenital local vascular anomaly or arteriovenous malformation; characterized histologically by a pathognomonic **Central Stellate Scar (中央星形瘢痕)** containing a large malformed artery without bile ducts.
* **Hepatic Adenoma**: A benign monoclonal hepatocyte proliferation strongly associated with exogenous **Oral Contraceptive Pill (OCP)** usage or anabolic steroid consumption; carries distinct molecular subtypes (HNF1A-mutated, $\beta$-catenin-activated, Inflammatory, Unclassified); $\beta$-catenin-activated subtypes carry a high risk of **Malignant Transformation** to HCC, while large adenomas ($> 5\ cm$) carry an acute risk of spontaneous **Spontaneous Intratumoral Hemorrhage** and rupture.


## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early-Stage Clinical Presentations
* Early primary and secondary liver tumours are predominantly **Asymptomatic** and discovered incidentally via routine surveillance ultrasound or cross-sectional imaging performed for unrelated indications.
* Mild, vague right upper quadrant (RUQ) discomfort or epigastric fullness due to stretching of **Glisson's Capsule (肝包膜)**.
* Nonspecific dyspeptic symptoms including early satiety, bloating, or mild nausea.

### Advanced-Stage Symptoms and Constitutional Signs
* Right upper quadrant pain characterized as dull, aching, and persistent, occasionally radiating to the right shoulder due to diaphragmatic irritation.
* Rapidly progressive **Profound Weight Loss (體重減輕)** and anorexia.
* Persistent low-grade pyrexia driven by tumor necrosis or release of pyrogenic cytokines (e.g., IL-1, IL-6, TNF-$\alpha$).
* Acute, severe, tearing abdominal pain associated with hypotension and tachycardia, indicating **Spontaneous Tumor Rupture (腫瘤破裂)** with hemoperitoneum.

### Physical Signs of Portal Hypertension and Liver Failure
* **Icterus/Jaundice (黃疸)** seen in sclera and under the tongue, indicating advanced biliary obstruction by tumor thrombus or terminal hepatic parenchymal failure.
* **Hepatomegaly (肝臟腫大)** with a firm, irregular, or nodular liver edge on palpation.
* An audible arterial **Hepatic Bruit (肝臟血管雜音)** or friction rub auscultated over the hepatic surface due to extreme hypervascularity.
* Clinical signs of decompensated chronic liver disease:
  * **Ascites (腹水)** detected via shifting dullness or fluid thrill.
  * **Splenomegaly (脾臟腫大)** secondary to portal venous congestion.
  * **Caput Medusae (海蛇頭樣腹壁靜脈曲張)** indicating prominent abdominal wall collateral veins.
  * **Spider Angiomas (蜘蛛痣)** and **Palmar Erythema (掌紅斑)** due to systemic hyperestrogenism.
  * **Asterixis (flapping tremor, 撲翼樣震顫)** indicating metabolic **Hepatic Encephalopathy (肝性腦病)**.


## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Table Comparing Benign and Malignant Liver Tumours
| Feature | Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) | Intrahepatic Cholangiocarcinoma | Metastatic Liver Disease | Hepatic Hemangioma | Focal Nodular Hyperplasia (FNH) | Hepatic Adenoma |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Demographics** | Elderly males; chronic HBV/HCV carriers | Age $> 60$ years; history of PSC or choledochal cysts | History of colorectal, gastric, or pancreatic cancer | Young to middle-aged females ($20-50$ years) | Young females ($20-40$ years) | Females ($20-40$ years); strong history of OCP use |
| **Serum Markers** | Elevated **AFP** | Elevated **CA19-9** and **CEA** | Elevated **CEA** (colorectal primary) | Normal tumor markers | Normal tumor markers | Normal tumor markers |
| **Background Liver** | **Cirrhotic/Fibrotic** in $> 80\%$ of local cases | Typically non-cirrhotic | Typically non-cirrhotic | Normal healthy liver | Normal healthy liver | Normal healthy liver |
| **Arterial Phase CT/MRI** | **Hyperenhancement** (Non-rim) | Mixed, faint peripheral rim enhancement | **Hypovascular** rim enhancement (target sign) | Peripheral **globular centripetal enhancement** | **Intense homogeneous enhancement** | Variable enhancement; often hypervascular |
| **Venous/Washout Phase** | Portal venous/delayed **Washout** | Progressive, delayed centripetal filling | Persistent hypointensity | Gradual **centripetal filling** to equilibrium | Iso/hyperattenuating; **Central scar** remains dark | Rapid washout or becomes isointense |
| **Malignant Potential** | Highly malignant | Highly malignant | Malignant | **Zero** | **Zero** | **High** in $\beta$-catenin subtype ($5-10\%$) |
| **Risk of Rupture** | High if exophytic or large | Low | Low | Extremely low | Zero | **High** if size $> 5\ cm$ |

### Differentiating HCC from Metastatic Liver Disease
* HCC classically presents as a solitary mass or multinodular lesions in a **Cirrhotic Liver**, whereas metastatic disease characteristically presents as **Multiple Bilobar Nodules** with umbilical necrosis ("central umbilication") distributed throughout a non-cirrhotic liver.
* HCC demonstrates direct portal vein tumor thrombus invasion, whereas metastatic disease rarely invades the portal vein lumens directly, instead causing extrinsic compression.
* Underlying viral hepatitis serology (+ve HBsAg) and significantly elevated serum **Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP, 甲胎蛋白)** point definitively to HCC, whereas gastrointestinal metastases exhibit elevated **Carcinoembryonic Antigen (CEA, 癌胚抗原)**.


## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Laboratory Investigations and Serum Markers
* **Viral Serology**: **HBsAg**, **HBsAb**, **HBV DNA quantitative PCR**, **Anti-HCV**, **HCV RNA**.
* **Tumor Markers**:
  * **Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP)**: Diagnostic if $> 400\ ng/mL$ in the presence of a hypervascular hepatic mass; also used for postoperative recurrence surveillance.
  * **Protein Induced by Vitamin K Absence or Antagonist-II (PIVKA-II)**: Alternative/complementary biomarker used in Hong Kong HA clinics for AFP-negative HCC.
* **Liver Function Tests (LFTs)**: Serum albumin, total bilirubin, alkaline phosphatase (ALP), alanine aminotransferase (ALT), aspartate aminotransferase (AST).
* **Coagulation Profile**: **Prothrombin Time (PT)** and **International Normalized Ratio (INR)** to evaluate the hepatic synthetic capacity for clotting factors.
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: To detect **Thrombocytopenia** (Platelets $< 100 \times 10^9/L$), which serves as an indirect clinical marker for portal hypertension and hypersplenism.

### Dynamic Imaging Protocols for Liver Tumours
* **Multiphasic Contrast-Enhanced Computed Tomography (CT) or Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)**: Must include unenhanced, late arterial, portal venous, and delayed phases.
* **Pathognomonic Imaging Criteria for HCC**:
  * Non-rim **Arterial Phase Hyperenhancement (APHE)** due to neo-arterialization.
  * Portal venous or delayed phase **Portal/Delayed Washout (洗脫現象)**.
  * Presence of an enhancing **Tumor Capsule**.
* **Hepatocyte-Specific Contrast MRI (Gd-EOB-DTPA / Primovist)**: Utilized extensively in Hong Kong for identifying sub-centimeter nodules ($< 1\ cm$) and differentiating FNH from adenomas based on hepatocyte uptake in the hepatobiliary phase.
* **Chest CT and Bone Scan**: Staging workup to rule out extrahepatic pulmonary or osseous metastases before planning curative resections.

### Child-Pugh Scoring System for Hepatic Functional Reserve
* Used to classify the severity of cirrhosis and predict perioperative mortality.

| Clinical & Biochemical Parameter | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Encephalopathy Grade** | None | Grade 1-2 (Mild/Moderate) | Grade 3-4 (Severe) |
| **Ascites** | None | Mild / Easily controlled | Moderate to severe / Diuretic-resistant |
| **Total Bilirubin ($\mu mol/L$)** | $< 34$ | $34 - 51$ | $> 51$ |
| **Serum Albumin ($g/L$)** | $> 35$ | $28 - 35$ | $< 28$ |
| **INR** | $< 1.7$ | $1.7 - 2.3$ | $> 2.3$ |

* **Interpretation of Child-Pugh Classes**:
  * **Class A**: 5 to 6 points (Excellent hepatic reserve; candidates for major surgical resection).
  * **Class B**: 7 to 9 points (Borderline function; acceptable for localized minor resection or ablation).
  * **Class C**: 10 to 15 points (Decompensated liver failure; absolute contraindication for surgical resection).

### Indocyanine Green (ICG) Clearance Test for Remnant Function
* Performed routinely across Hong Kong surgical units (pioneered by the HKU-Queen Mary Hospital team) to objectively quantify functional hepatic reserve prior to major hepatectomy.
* A standard dose of $0.5\ mg/kg$ of **Indocyanine Green (ICG)** dye is injected intravenously.
* **ICG Retention Rate at 15 Minutes ($ICG-R_{15}$)** is measured:
  * $ICG-R_{15} < 14\%$: Safe for major hepatectomy (up to 3 contiguous segments or hemihepatectomy) in non-cirrhotic or well-compensated cirrhotic livers.
  * $ICG-R_{15}$ between 14% and 22%: Restricts surgery to minor resections (segmentectomy or subsegmentectomy).
  * $ICG-R_{15} > 23\%$: Surgical resection is contraindicated due to an unacceptable risk of postoperative liver failure; patient redirected to locoregional or systemic therapies.

### Hong Kong Liver Cancer (HKLC) Staging System
* Developed locally to guide aggressive multi-modality treatment pathways, often selecting patients for resection or locoregional therapy who would be deemed unresectable under the western Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) system.

| HKLC Stage | ECOG Performance Status | Child-Pugh Score | Tumor Status / Burden | Recommended HKLC Primary Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage I** | 0 to 1 | A | Solitary $\le 5\ cm$; No vascular invasion | **Surgical Resection** (1st line) or Ablation |
| **Stage IIa** | 0 to 1 | A | Solitary $> 5\ cm$ OR $\le 3$ nodules all $\le 3\ cm$; No vascular invasion | **Surgical Resection** (1st line) or Ablation |
| **Stage IIb** | 0 to 1 | A | $\le 3$ nodules all $> 3\ cm$ OR $> 3$ nodules; No vascular invasion | **Surgical Resection** or TACE |
| **Stage IIIa** | 0 to 1 | A | Intrahepatic venous invasion (Portal Vein branch) OR diffuse tumor | **Surgical Resection** or TACE or SIRT |
| **Stage IIIb** | 0 to 1 | A | Extrahepatic metastasis or Main Portal Vein invasion | **Systemic Therapy** or Clinical Trials |
| **Stage IV** | 2 | A or B | Any tumor burden | Locoregional therapy or Supportive Care |
| **Stage V** | 3 to 4 OR Any ECOG | C | Any tumor burden | **Best Supportive Care** |


## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Surgical Resection Indications for Hepatocellular Carcinoma
* **Absolute Criteria for Hepatectomy**:
  * Patient must have a **Child-Pugh Class A** score.
  * Acceptable **ICG-R15** clearing capability (typically $< 14\%$ for major resections).
  * Adequate volume of **Future Liver Remnant (FLR)** quantified via CT volumetry:
    * $> 25\% - 30\%$ of total liver volume in a completely normal, non-cirrhotic liver.
    * $> 40\% - 50\%$ of total liver volume in a severely cirrhotic or damaged liver.
  * Absence of extrahepatic distant metastases.
  * Absence of main portal vein or main hepatic vein tumor thrombus trunk invasion.

### Liver Transplantation Selection Criteria
* Reserved for patients with early-stage HCC but with underlying **Decompensated Cirrhosis (Child-Pugh B/C)** who cannot tolerate anatomical resections.
* **The Milan Criteria**:
  * Solitary tumor $\le 5\ cm$ in maximal diameter, OR
  * Up to 3 separate nodules, each $\le 3\ cm$ in maximal diameter.
  * Absence of macrovascular invasion and absence of extrahepatic nodal or systemic spread.
* **The UCSF Criteria** (often applied as extended criteria in Hong Kong centers):
  * Solitary tumor $\le 6.5\ cm$, OR
  * Maximum of 3 nodules with largest nodule $\le 4.5\ cm$ and a total cumulative tumor diameter $\le 8\ cm$.

### Locoregional Therapies for Unresectable HCC
* **Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA) / Microwave Ablation (MWA)**: Curative-intent local thermal destruction indicated for small tumors ($\le 3\ cm$) in patients who are poor surgical risks or have anatomically deep-seated solitary lesions.
* **Transarterial Chemoembolization (TACE, 經導管動脈化療栓塞術)**: Palliative locoregional therapy for intermediate-stage (HKLC Stage IIb/IIIa) multi-nodular tumours without macrovascular invasion. Combines targeted hepatic arterial infusion of chemotherapeutic agents (e.g., Cisplatin or Doxorubicin) emulsified in Lipiodol followed by embolic particles (e.g., Gelfoam) to induce ischemic tumor necrosis.
* **Selective Internal Radiation Therapy (SIRT / Yttrium-90 Radioembolization)**: Delivery of resin or glass microspheres loaded with the beta-emitting isotope **Yttrium-90 ($^{90}Y$)** via the hepatic artery; highly effective for large tumors with branch portal vein thrombosis.

### Systemic Therapy Protocols in Hong Kong Hospital Authority
* Managed via HA oncology specialist clinics for advanced, non-resectable, non-ablatable cases (HKLC Stage IIIb).
* **First-Line Combined Immunotherapy Protocol**: Combination of **Atezolizumab** (anti-PD-L1 antibody) plus **Bevacizumab** (anti-VEGF antibody). This has largely replaced tyrosine kinase inhibitor monotherapy due to superior overall survival data.
* **Alternative First-Line Monotherapy**: **Sorafenib** or **Lenvatinib** (oral multi-kinase inhibitors) utilized if patients have absolute contraindications to immunotherapy (e.g., severe auto-immune conditions or a high risk of variceal hemorrhage).


## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Anatomical vs Non-Anatomical Hepatectomy
* **Anatomical Hepatectomy (解剖性肝切除術)**: Complete systematic removal of the tumor along with its entire underlying corresponding portal vein segment and subsegment boundaries. This approach is preferred for HCC because it eradicates microscopic intrahepatic **Portal Venous Micrometastases** and satellite nodules spread via the segmental venous outflow.
* **Non-Anatomical Hepatectomy (Wedge Resection, 非解剖性楔形切除術)**: Removal of the tumor mass with a clear macro/microscopic parenchymal margin ($> 1\ cm$) without regard to segmental boundaries. Reserved for patients with severely limited functional parenchymal reserve where maximizing parenchymal preservation is critical.

### Vascular Inflow Occlusion (Pringle Manoeuvre)
* The **Pringle Manoeuvre (普林格爾手法)** is a critical surgical technique employed to minimize intraoperative parenchymal blood loss during liver transection.
* **Method**: The **Hepatoduodenal Ligament (肝十二指腸韌帶)** is clamped using a soft vascular atraumatic clamp or a tourniquet to compress the **Common Hepatic Artery** and the **Main Portal Vein** simultaneously.
* **Ischemia Time Parameters**:
  * Intermittent clamping cycles of **15 minutes of ischemia followed by 5 minutes of reperfusion** are strictly timed.
  * Total cumulative ischemic time should ideally not exceed 60 to 90 minutes in normal livers, and must be significantly curtailed in cirrhotic livers to prevent ischemic liver failure.
* Does not control retrograde back-bleeding from the **Hepatic Veins**, which must be minimized by maintaining a **Low Central Venous Pressure (CVP $< 5\ mmHg$)** maintained by the anesthetic team during parenchymal transection.

### Operative Management of the Future Liver Remnant
* If CT volumetry reveals an inadequate **Future Liver Remnant (FLR)**, steps must be taken preoperatively to stimulate contralateral parenchymal hypertrophy:
* **Portal Vein Embolization (PVE)**: Interventional radiological occlusion of the portal vein branch supplying the tumor-bearing segments 4 to 6 weeks prior to planned surgery. This redirects portal flow to the healthy liver segments, inducing robust hypertrophy.
* **Associating Liver Partition and Portal Vein Ligation for Staged Hepatectomy (ALPPS)**: A two-stage operative procedure. Stage 1 involves ligating the portal vein branch supplying the diseased lobe and physically transecting the liver parenchyma along the planned line of resection while leaving the arterial and biliary connections intact. Stage 2 is performed 7 to 14 days later, resecting the diseased lobe once rapid accelerated hypertrophy of the FLR is confirmed via interval CT.

### Key Consent Risks and Pre-operative Counseling
* **Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF)**: The most lethal specific risk; manifested by progressive intractable jaundice, coagulopathy, and encephalopathy.
* **Massive Intractable Intraoperative Hemorrhage**: Requiring major blood transfusions, carrying risk of air embolism into the hepatic veins.
* **Bile Leak (膽汁漏)**: Occurring from raw cut parenchymal surfaces or structural damage to major biliary radicals.
* **Perioperative Mortality**: Approximately 1% to 3% in specialized high-volume Hong Kong centers for Child-Pugh A patients.


## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Monitoring and Fluid Protocols
* **Admittance to Intensive Care Unit (ICU)** or specialized High Dependency Unit (HDU) for the first 24 to 48 hours.
* **Strict Fluid Restriction Protocol**: Avoid aggressive crystalloid volume resuscitation post-transection. Maintaining a state of mild hypovolemia or fluid neutrality minimizes hepatic sinusoidal congestion, reducing the risk of delayed parenchymal oozing and preserving microvascular perfusion.
* **Hourly Output Surveillance**: Monitoring urine output ($> 0.5\ mL/kg/hour$) and monitoring volume and color of abdominal surgical drains (looking for fresh blood or golden-brown bile staining).

### Post-hepatectomy Pain Management and Mobilization
* **Avoidance of Continuous Epidural Analgesia** in patients undergoing major resections or those with borderline pre-existing cirrhosis due to post-operative coagulopathy (INR elevation), which poses an unacceptable risk of epidural hematoma formation during catheter removal.
* **Patient-Controlled Analgesia (PCA)** using intravenous opioids (e.g., Morphine or Fentanyl) combined with scheduled around-the-clock paracetamol; NSAIDs are strictly contraindicated due to hepatorenal syndrome and gastric ulceration risks.
* **Early Physical Mobilization**: Target sitting out of bed on Post-Operative Day 1 (POD 1) and active assisted ambulation by POD 2 to minimize thromboembolic events and hypostatic hypoventilation pneumonia.

### Nutritional Support and Metabolic Monitoring
* Early initiation of clear oral fluids and light diet within 24 hours post-surgery to maintain intestinal mucosal barrier integrity and attenuate systemic translocation of endotoxins.
* **Daily Monitoring of Biochemical Parameters**: Liver function tests, PT/INR, and **Serum Glucose Level**.
* Supplemental intravenous **Dextrose Infusions (10% to 20%)** are routinely administered in the first 48 hours because the recovering remnant liver has severely depleted glycogen storage and impaired gluconeogenesis capabilities, posing an acute risk of profound **Refractory Hypoglycemia**.
* Correction of electrolyte derangements, particularly hypophosphatemia, hypokalemia, and hypomagnesemia, which occur rapidly during the peak phase of liver regeneration (POD 2 to POD 5).


## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (Days 0-7)
* **Primary Intra-abdominal Hemorrhage**: Occurs within hours of surgery due to slippage of vascular ties, dislodgement of clips on major hepatic vein branches, or severe diffuse oozing from the raw parenchymal transection bed secondary to hypothermia and consumptive coagulopathy.
* **Acute Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF)**: Defined by the **International Study Group of Liver Surgery (ISGLS) "50-50 Criteria"**: **Bilirubin $> 50\ \mu mol/L$** combined with an **INR $> 50\%$ prolongation** (or $INR > 1.7$) occurring concurrently on or after **Post-Operative Day 5**. Clinical manifestations include progressive encephalopathy, intractable ascites, and development of hepatorenal syndrome.
* **Air Embolism**: Rare intraoperative or early post-operative event caused by negative intrathoracic pressure drawing atmospheric air directly into open, non-collapsed hepatic venous branches during deep breathing or straining.

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications (Week 1-4)
* **Bile Leak (膽汁漏)**: Characterized by persistent drainage of high-volume fluids with a bilirubin concentration $> 3 \times$ concurrent serum bilirubin levels. Managed initially via keeping the surgical drains on continuous dependent drainage; persistent high-output leaks require **Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography (ERCP)** combined with biliary sphincterotomy and placement of a trans-papillary biliary stent to lower intraductal pressure.
* **Perihepatic Abscess / Subphrenic Fluid Collection**: Developed from infected localized hematomas or chronic bile loculations. Presents with spiking fevers, leukocytosis, and right-sided pleuritic chest pain. Diagnosed via ultrasound or abdominal CT and treated with **Image-Guided Percutaneous Catheter Drainage** and targeted systemic antibiotics.
* **Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) & Pulmonary Embolism (PE)**: Paradoxical risk despite prolonged INR due to a concurrent reduction in liver-synthesized natural anticoagulants (Protein C, Protein S, and Antithrombin III).

### Late Complications and Long-term Survival Prognosis
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝氣)**: Particularly common after the classic right subcostal "Mercedes-Benz" or reverse-L laparotomy incisions due to mechanical disruption of abdominal wall fascia and potential damage to intercostal nerve branches.
* **Tumor Recurrence (腫瘤復發)**: The single greatest barrier to long-term cure. Intrasegmental or extrahepatic recurrence occurs in up to 50% to 70% of patients within 5 years post-resection. Recurrence within the first 2 years typically represents **Metastatic Seedlings** from the primary resected tumor, whereas recurrence after 2 years represents **De Novo Multicentric Carcinogenesis** arising independently within the remaining diseased, cirrhotic parenchymal field.
* **Prognosis**: For early-stage HCC (HKLC Stage I/IIa) undergoing optimal anatomical R0 resection in Hong Kong, the 5-year overall survival rate ranges from 50% to 65%. Once macrovascular invasion or extrahepatic systemic spread is established, 5-year survival drops to $< 10\%$.


## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why do we prioritize the Hong Kong Liver Cancer (HKLC) staging system over the Western Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) system in local clinical practice? Answer: The HKLC system was derived from a large-scale local cohort of predominantly HBV-infected patients. It prescribes significantly more aggressive treatment guidelines, recommending surgical resection or locoregional therapies for intermediate and advanced stages (such as tumors with branch portal vein thrombus) that the BCLC system automatically relegates to systemic therapy or palliative care alone, thereby yielding superior survival outcomes in Asian populations.
* Viva Question: A patient develops progressive confusion, a flapping tremor, and marked abdominal distention on post-operative day 6 following a right hemihepatectomy. What is the definitive diagnostic parameter you must check? Answer: I must check the serum total bilirubin and INR values to see if they meet the ISGLS "50-50 criteria" (Bilirubin $> 50\ \mu mol/L$ and $INR > 1.7$ on or after POD 5), which confirms Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF), and immediately initiate supportive therapy including lactulose, microvascular perfusion optimization, and avoid sedatives.
* Trap: Believing that a normal serum Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP) level rules out the diagnosis of Hepatocellular Carcinoma. Pearl: Up to 30% to 40% of histologically proven HCC lesions are completely non-secretory and present with normal baseline AFP levels; diagnosis in these AFP-negative cases relies strictly on characteristic hypervascular enhancement with delayed washout seen on multiphasic contrast CT or Primovist MRI.
* Trap: Assuming that a large hepatic hemangioma should be resected prophylacticly due to an imminent risk of spontaneous rupture. Pearl: Asymptomatic hepatic hemangiomas carry an incredibly low risk of spontaneous rupture regardless of size and should be managed conservatively; surgical intervention is indicated only if they become highly symptomatic, demonstrate rapid rapid size progression, or cause compressive features like gastric outlet obstruction.
* Pitfall: Removing an epidural catheter in a patient on post-operative day 3 following a major hepatectomy without checking the concurrent coagulation profile. This mistake can lead to the formation of a catastrophic spinal epidural hematoma causing permanent paraplegia if the patient has silent post-operative coagulopathy. Always check that the INR is safely $< 1.5$ before any epidural catheter manipulation.
* Pitfall: Routine ordering of a percutaneous needle core biopsy for a resectable, highly suspicious hypervascular liver mass in a cirrhotic patient. This action can lead to tumor tract seeding along the biopsy path or precipitate acute intra-abdominal hemorrhage. Avoid pre-operative biopsy if the non-invasive imaging criteria on contrast CT/MRI are entirely pathognomonic for HCC.
