# Liver Tumours (肝臟腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Liver Tumours
* **Primary Liver Malignancy**: Tumours originating from liver parenchyma, most commonly **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) (肝細胞癌)** (>90%), followed by **Cholangiocarcinoma (膽管癌)**.
* **Secondary Liver Metastases**: Tumours spreading from extrahepatic primaries (e.g., colorectal, breast, neuroendocrine).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: 5th most common cancer, 3rd leading cause of cancer-related mortality in HK.
* **Gender Predilection**: Male predominant (M:F ≈ 3:1).
* **Viral Aetiology**: >70-80% of HK cases are driven by chronic **Hepatitis B Virus (HBV) (乙型肝炎病毒)** infection.
* **Screening Protocol**: HK Department of Health recommends 6-monthly USG + Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) for high-risk cohorts (HBV/HCV carriers, cirrhosis).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Liver
* **Couinaud Segments (庫伊諾分段)**: 8 functionally independent segments based on vascular inflow/outflow and biliary drainage.
* **Dual Blood Supply**: 
  * Portal Vein (PV): 75% volume, 50% oxygen.
  * Hepatic Artery (HA): 25% volume, 50% oxygen.
* **Tumour Vascularisation**: Normal parenchyma relies mainly on PV; HCC relies ≈100% on HA (Basis for arterial embolic therapies).
* **Venous Drainage**: Right, Middle, and Left Hepatic Veins drain into the Inferior Vena Cava (IVC).

### Pathophysiology of Hepatocellular Carcinoma
* **HBV Oncogenesis**: HBV DNA integrates into host hepatocyte genome → proto-oncogene activation + tumour suppressor gene inactivation.
* **Cirrhotic Pathway**: Chronic inflammation → hepatocyte necrosis → regeneration → dysplasia → macroscopic nodule → HCC.
* **Metabolic Aetiologies**: ↑ incidence of Metabolic dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease (MASLD) → HCC independent of cirrhosis.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Clinical Symptoms
* **Asymptomatic**: Often incidental finding on routine HK HA screening.
* **Right Upper Quadrant (RUQ) Pain (右上腹痛)**: Due to stretching of Glisson's capsule.
* **Constitutional Symptoms**: Unintentional weight loss, anorexia, profound fatigue.
* **Decompensated Liver Symptoms**: Jaundice, abdominal distension (ascites), lower limb oedema, melaena/haematemesis (ruptured varices).
* **Acute Acute Abdomen**: Severe, sudden pain + shock → indicates **Ruptured HCC (破裂性肝癌)**.

### Key Clinical Signs
* **General**: Cachexia, **Jaundice (黃疸)**, scleral icterus.
* **Abdominal**: 
  * Tender **Hepatomegaly (肝脾腫大)** with hard/irregular liver edge.
  * Hepatic bruit (↑ arterial flow to tumour).
  * Shifting dullness (+ve ascites).
* **Stigmata of Chronic Liver Disease**:
  * Palmar erythema
  * Spider naevi
  * Gynaecomastia
  * Caput medusae
  * Asterixis (hepatic encephalopathy)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Liver Tumours
| Tumour Type | Imaging Characteristics (Triphasic CT/MRI) | Key Clinical & Laboratory Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (肝細胞癌)** | Arterial hyperenhancement, portal/delayed washout | ↑ AFP, Cirrhosis/HBV background |
| **Colorectal Liver Metastases (結直腸癌肝轉移)** | Peripheral rim enhancement (Target sign) | ↑ CEA, Known primary history |
| **Haemangioma (血管瘤)** | Peripheral nodular enhancement → centripetal fill-in | Most common benign, usually asymptomatic |
| **Focal Nodular Hyperplasia (局灶性結節性增生)** | Central stellate scar, intense arterial enhancement | Female, normal liver, asymptomatic |
| **Hepatic Adenoma (肝腺瘤)** | Heterogeneous enhancement, possible haemorrhage | OCP use, risk of rupture/malignant change |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Tiered Pre-operative Blood Investigations
* **Tumour Markers**: **Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) (甲胎蛋白)** (>400 ng/mL highly suggestive of HCC).
* **Hepatitis Serology**: HBsAg, Anti-HCV, HBV DNA viral load.
* **Liver Function Tests (LFTs)**: Bilirubin (obstruction/failure), ALT/AST (inflammation), ALP/GGT, Albumin (synthetic function).
* **Coagulation Profile**: Prothrombin Time (PT) / INR (synthetic function).
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: Thrombocytopenia (indicates portal hypertension/hypersplenism).

### Liver Function Reserve Assessment (HK HA Protocols)
* **Child-Pugh Score (肝功能評估)**: Assesses generalized cirrhosis severity.
* **Indocyanine Green (ICG) Clearance Test (靛綠排泄試驗)**: Measures exact functional reserve.
  * **ICG-R15**: Retention rate at 15 minutes.
  * **Cut-offs**: <14% required for safe major hepatectomy; <22% for minor hepatectomy in HK.

### Child-Pugh Scoring System
| Parameter                     | 1 point   | 2 points                            | 3 points            |
| ----------------------------- | --------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Bilirubin (μmol/L)**        | <34       | 34-50                               | >50                 |
| **Albumin (g/L)**             | >35       | 28-35                               | <28                 |
| **PT prolongation (s) / INR** | <4 / <1.7 | 4-6 / 1.7-2.3                       | >6 / >2.3           |
| **Ascites**                   | None      | Mild/Moderate (Diuretic responsive) | Severe (Refractory) |
| **Encephalopathy**            | None      | Grade I-II                          | Grade III-IV        |
* *Class A: 5-6 points (Good reserve). Class B: 7-9 points. Class C: 10-15 points (Contraindication to resection).*

### Gold Standard Radiological Investigations
* **Triphasic Contrast CT Liver**: Demonstrates classical arterial enhancement + portal washout.
* **MRI Liver with Primovist (Gadoxetic acid)**: Superior for detecting small nodules <1cm.
* **CT Thorax/Abdomen/Pelvis**: Exclude extrahepatic metastases.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Hong Kong Liver Cancer (HKLC) Staging System Overview
* HKLC (developed locally) is preferred over BCLC in HK HA hospitals.
* HKLC broadens surgical indications, recommending aggressive resection even for multiple tumours or intrahepatic vascular invasion, resulting in improved survival for Asian HBV-driven HCC.

### Management Algorithm by HKLC Stage
| HKLC Stage | Tumour Profile & Liver Function | Recommended HA First-Line Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Early (I, IIa)** | Small/multiple, Child-Pugh A/B, No extrahepatic spread | **Surgical Resection (肝切除術)** or RFA |
| **Intermediate (IIb, IIIa)** | Large/vascular invasion, Child-Pugh A/B | Aggressive Resection / TACE |
| **Advanced (IIIb, IV)** | Extrahepatic spread, Child-Pugh A/B | Systemic therapy (Targeted/Immunotherapy) |
| **Terminal (V)** | Child-Pugh C / Poor performance status | Best Supportive Care |

### Local Ablative Therapies
* **Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA) (射頻消融術)**: First-line alternative for solitary HCC <3cm or up to 3 nodules <3cm, especially for centrally located tumours to preserve parenchyma.
* **Microwave Ablation (MWA)**: Higher temperature, faster ablation, less susceptibility to heat-sink effect from adjacent blood vessels.

### Transarterial & Radiotherapy Modalities
* **Transarterial Chemoembolization (TACE) (經動脈化療栓塞)**: Palliative for large/multifocal unresectable HCC. Injects chemotherapeutic agent + embolic particles into HA.
* **Selective Internal Radiation Therapy (SIRT) / Yttrium-90**: Microparticles emitting beta-radiation via HA.
* **Stereotactic Body Radiation Therapy (SBRT)**: Precise high-dose external beam radiation for locally advanced tumours.

### Systemic Medical Therapies
* **1st Line Immunotherapy Combination**: Atezolizumab (Anti-PD-L1) + Bevacizumab (Anti-VEGF).
* **Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors (TKIs)**: Sorafenib, Lenvatinib used if immunotherapy contraindicated.
* **Antiviral Therapy**: Entecavir / Tenofovir mandatory for HBV-driven HCC to prevent reactivation and protect remaining liver.

### Liver Transplantation Indications
* Considered when tumours are unresectable but confined to the liver with poor liver reserve (Child-Pugh B/C).
* **UCSF Criteria**: Solitary tumour ≤6.5 cm, OR ≤3 nodules with largest ≤4.5 cm and total diameter ≤8 cm.
* Extremely limited by deceased donor pool in HK; Living Donor Liver Transplantation (LDLT) relies on willing/compatible relatives.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Pre-operative Optimisation Principles
* **Portal Vein Embolization (PVE)**: Performed 4-6 weeks pre-op to induce compensatory hypertrophy of the Future Liver Remnant (FLR) if initial volume is inadequate.
* Control ascites and correct coagulopathy (Vitamin K, FFP).

### Key Operative Steps for Hepatectomy
* **Incision**: Mercedes-Benz, J-shaped, or pure Laparoscopic approach.
* **Mobilisation**: Division of falciform, coronary, and triangular ligaments.
* **Intraoperative USG (IOUS)**: Confirm tumour margins and vascular relationships.
* **Inflow Control**: **Pringle Maneuver (入肝血流阻斷術)** (clamping hepatoduodenal ligament) to minimise blood loss. Usually 15 mins on, 5 mins off.
* **Anterior Approach**: For large right liver tumours, parenchymal transection before right lobe mobilisation to avoid tumour rupture/venous embolisation.
* **Parenchymal Transection**: Using CUSA (Cavitron Ultrasonic Surgical Aspirator) and energy devices.
* **Haemostasis**: Argon beam coagulation, surgical sealants.

### Key Elements of Surgical Consent
* **Specific Risks Discussed**:
  * Massive intra-operative haemorrhage requiring transfusion.
  * Post-hepatectomy liver failure (PHLF).
  * Bile leak / Biliary stricture.
  * High risk of disease recurrence.
  * Need for intensive care admission post-op.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care
* **Monitoring**: ICU/HDU monitoring for major hepatectomies. Strict urine output, CVP, and drain output monitoring.
* **Laboratory Tracking**: Daily LFTs, PT/INR, Lactate to monitor liver regeneration and function. Peak transaminitis expected at Day 1-2.
* **Glycaemic Control**: Hypoglycaemia is an early sign of liver failure. IV Dextrose infusion if required.

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Protocols
* **Analgesia**: Epidural or continuous wound infiltration. Avoid NSAIDs (renal/bleeding risk) and caution with hepatically metabolised opiates.
* **Early Mobilisation**: Day 1 mobilisation to prevent DVT and pulmonary complications.
* **VTE Prophylaxis**: Mechanical prophylaxis (TEDs/SCDs). LMWH introduced cautiously depending on post-op INR and drain output.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (<30 Days)
* **Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF)**: Most lethal complication. Defined by worsening bilirubin and INR on Day 5.
* **Haemorrhage**: Secondary bleeding from the resection surface or slipped ligatures.
* **Bile Leak**: Detected by bilious output from abdominal drains. Managed with ERCP/stenting or percutaneous drainage.
* **Infection**: Subphrenic abscess, pneumonia, wound infection.

### Intermediate & Late Surgical Complications
* **Tumour Recurrence**: High rate (up to 70% at 5 years) due to underlying cirrhotic field defect.
* **Portal Vein Thrombosis**: Can precipitate acute liver decompensation or variceal bleeding.
* **Biliary Strictures**: Resulting from ischaemic injury to bile ducts.

### Prognosis and Survival Outcomes
* Resection offers 5-year survival of 50-70% for early-stage HCC.
* BCLC C / HKLC IV stage median survival is <12-18 months even with systemic therapy.
* Continued HBV viral suppression significantly improves long-term disease-free survival.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic imaging hallmark of Hepatocellular Carcinoma on a triphasic CT scan? Answer: Arterial hyperenhancement followed by portal venous and delayed phase washout.
* Viva Question: How do we determine if a patient has sufficient liver reserve for a major hepatectomy in Hong Kong? Answer: Child-Pugh Class A with an ICG-R15 clearance of less than 14%.
* Viva Question: What is the purpose of the Pringle maneuver during a hepatectomy? Answer: To minimise intra-operative bleeding by temporarily occluding the portal triad (hepatic artery and portal vein) within the hepatoduodenal ligament.
* Trap: Relying solely on normal AFP levels to rule out HCC. Pearl: Up to 30-40% of HCCs are non-AFP secreting; diagnosis must rely on the integration of patient risk factors (HBV) and definitive cross-sectional imaging features.
* Trap: Assuming a massively elevated ALT/AST indicates poor liver synthetic reserve. Pearl: ALT/AST indicate acute hepatocellular necrosis/inflammation. Synthetic reserve is evaluated by Albumin, Bilirubin, and PT/INR.
* Pitfall: Performing a percutaneous biopsy on a highly suspicious resectable HCC nodule in a cirrhotic patient. The biopsy risks track seeding and tumour rupture; if classic triphasic CT features are present with elevated AFP, biopsy is not required prior to resection.
