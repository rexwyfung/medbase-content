# Metabolic Response to Injury (創傷代謝反應)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Metabolic Response to Injury
- **Metabolic Response to Injury (創傷代謝反應)** = A systemic, graded physiological cascade triggered by local tissue damage (surgery/trauma) → alters vital organ function to preserve life and initiate healing.
- Unregulated or prolonged responses drive pathological hypermetabolism, immune suppression, and detrimental fluid shifts.

### Local Epidemiology & Clinical Burden
- A universal physiological process; response severity is directly proportional to the magnitude of the insult (e.g., major trauma > open surgery > minimally invasive surgery).
- **Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) Priority**: Aggressive implementation of **Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS)** (術後加速康復) protocols across HA clusters aims to actively suppress this metabolic response → ↓ stress hormones, ↓ post-operative complications, ↓ length of stay (LOS).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Phases of the Metabolic Response
| Feature | Ebb Phase (休克期 / Catabolic) | Flow Phase (高代謝期 / Hypermetabolic) | Anabolic Phase (合成代謝期 / Recovery) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Timing** | 0-48 hrs post-injury | 48 hrs - weeks | Weeks - months |
| **Physiological Goal** | Conserve volume & energy | Mobilise stores for repair | Tissue rebuilding |
| **Clinical State** | Hypovolaemia, ↓ cardiac output (CO), hypothermia | ↑ CO, tissue oedema, pyrexia, hypermetabolism (↑ BMR) | Normalisation of BMR & diuresis |
| **Metabolism** | ↓ BMR, lactic acidosis | ↑ BMR (15-25%), catabolism, weight loss | Restored protein synthesis |
| **Key Mediators** | Catecholamines, Cortisol, Aldosterone | Cytokines, Insulin Resistance | Insulin, Growth Factors |

### Immune & Inflammatory Axis (Cytokine Release)
- **Tissue Damage** → release of intracellular debris known as **DAMPs (損傷相關分子模式 / Damage-Associated Molecular Patterns)** (e.g., HMGB1, heat shock proteins).
- DAMPs bind **Pattern Recognition Receptors (PRRs)** on macrophages → activates **Inflammasomes**.
- Triggers massive release of pro-inflammatory cytokines: **IL-1, IL-6, TNFα** (腫瘤壞死因子-α) within the first 24 hrs.
- This drives **SIRS (全身炎症反應綜合症)** → fever, acute-phase protein synthesis in liver, and severe skeletal muscle breakdown.
- Concurrently, anti-inflammatory mediators (IL-4, IL-10, TGFβ, SPMs) attempt to control the inflammation, causing **CARS (代償性抗炎反應綜合症)**. Unbalanced CARS leads to profound immunosuppression.

### Neuroendocrine Axis (Hormonal Shift)
- **Afferent Nociceptive Pain** → Hypothalamus → Pituitary → Adrenal axis.
- ↑ **Counter-regulatory hormones**: **Adrenaline, Cortisol, Glucagon** → drives lipolysis, hepatic gluconeogenesis, and skeletal muscle proteolysis.
- ↑ **Aldosterone & ADH** → Na+ & H2O retention → obligate post-operative oliguria & extracellular volume expansion.

### Post-operative Insulin Resistance (胰島素阻抗)
- Pro-inflammatory cytokines cause failure of **GLUT4** transporter activation in peripheral muscle.
- Result: **Hyperglycaemia** despite hyperinsulinaemia.
- Consequence: Muscle cannot utilise glucose → body forced into continuous "auto-cannibalism" of muscle protein to feed hepatic gluconeogenesis.

### Hepatic Acute-Phase Response
- Liver reprioritises protein synthesis, primarily driven by **IL-6**.
- **+ve Acute Phase Reactants** ↑: **CRP (C-反應蛋白)**, Fibrinogen.
- **-ve Acute Phase Reactants** ↓: **Albumin (白蛋白)** (drop is primarily due to ↑ transcapillary escape rate from leaky microvasculature, not purely ↓ synthesis).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of the Flow Phase
- **Vital Signs**: ↑ Temp (early pyrexia is often a sterile cytokine response, not infection), ↑ HR, ↑ RR.
- **Fluid Status**: 
  - Paradoxical weight gain (+6-10L extracellular fluid) despite massive lean tissue loss.
  - Peripheral oedema.
  - Transient post-operative oliguria (ADH/aldosterone driven).
- **Musculoskeletal**: Profound fatigue and extreme muscle wasting (ubiquitin-proteasome pathway can destroy up to 500g of wet muscle/day if severe).

### Laboratory & Bedside Signs of Severe Stress
- **Blood**: Hyperglycaemia (insulin resistance), ↑ WBC (leukocytosis), ↑ CRP, ↓ Albumin.
- **Urine**: ↑ Urinary nitrogen excretion (10-20g N/day).
- **Vascular**: Vasodilatation, hyperdynamic circulation (bounding pulse, warm peripheries post-resuscitation).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentiating Normal Response vs. Pathological Complications
| Clinical State | Pathophysiology | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Normal Flow Phase** | Sterile cytokine release | Transient pyrexia (day 1-2), normalising HR/urine output, clean sterile wounds. |
| **Post-op Sepsis (敗血症)** | Superadded infection driving a secondary inflammatory hit | Swinging fevers > day 3, purulent wound, ↑ procalcitonin, +ve cultures, hypotension. |
| **Persistent Inflammation, Immunosuppression & Catabolism (PICS)** | Failed resolution of CARS | Chronic critical illness, unremitting muscle wasting, recurrent opportunistic nosocomial infections. |
| **Surgical Haemorrhage** | Uncontrolled mechanical volume loss | Failure to transition to flow phase; persistent Ebb phase (hypotensive, tachycardic, cold, oliguric), ↓ Hb. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Pre-operative Baseline Assessment
- **Nutritional Screening**: MUST tool (Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool) used routinely in HA.
- **Cardiopulmonary Fitness (CPET)**: Gold standard objective measure. An Anaerobic Threshold (AT) < 11 mL/kg/min or VO2 peak < 15 mL/kg/min indicates the patient cannot physically meet the massive O2 demands of the Flow phase → ↑↑ mortality risk.
- **Bloods**: Baseline Hb, HbA1c (identify pre-existing metabolic syndrome/insulin resistance), Albumin (marker of chronic disease state/inflammation, not pure nutrition).

### Post-operative Monitoring of Metabolic Response
- **Glycaemic Control**: Serial capillary blood glucose (CBG). Keep 6-10 mmol/L in critical care; avoid strict normoglycaemia due to high mortality risk from hypoglycaemia.
- **Inflammatory Markers**: Serial **CRP** & WBC. (CRP expected to peak day 2-3; failure to halve by day 4/5 strongly indicates a secondary insult like an anastomotic leak or pneumonia).
- **Metabolic Acidosis Evaluation**: Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) → Lactate & Base Excess (BE) monitor tissue perfusion and resolution of the Ebb phase.
- **Renal & Fluid Monitoring**: Strict I/O charting, Daily U&E (Urea & Electrolytes), monitor K+ and PO4 (risk of refeeding syndrome).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Avoidance of Exacerbating Factors (Secondary Insults)
- Prevent secondary triggers that amplify DAMP release and prolong catabolism:
  - **Hypothermia**: Use forced-air warmers (Bair Hugger) → prevents coagulopathy and shivering-induced ↑ BMR.
  - **Tissue Hypoxia**: O2 supplementation, goal-directed fluid therapy.
  - **Uncontrolled Pain**: Drives massive catecholamine release; requires multimodal analgesia.
  - **Starvation**: Triggers massive glycogenolysis and muscle breakdown.

### HA ERAS (Enhanced Recovery After Surgery) Protocol 
- **Pre-operative**:
  - Prehabilitation (exercise, optimise Hb, smoking cessation).
  - Abolish prolonged fasting: clear fluids allowed up to 2 hrs pre-op.
  - **Carbohydrate Loading**: Oral complex carb drink 2 hrs pre-op → switches body from fasting to fed state → significantly blunts post-op **Insulin Resistance**.
- **Intra-operative**:
  - Goal-directed fluid therapy (avoid excess 0.9% NaCl to prevent hyperchloraemic acidosis & gut oedema which delays return of bowel function).
- **Post-operative**:
  - **Early Enteral Feeding**: Trophic feeding within 24 hrs stimulates gut hormones, preserves Gut-Associated Lymphoid Tissue (GALT), and prevents bacterial translocation.
  - **Early Mobilisation**: Reverses immobility-induced muscle proteolysis.

### Nutritional Intervention Algorithm
- Indicated if oral intake inadequate for > 5 days (earlier if pre-existing malnutrition).
- **Enteral Nutrition (EN) is always 1st line**: via NGT/NJT/PEG. Maintains mucosal integrity.
- **Parenteral Nutrition (PN/TPN)**: Reserved strictly for intestinal failure, prolonged ileus, or high-output fistulae. High risk of central line-associated bloodstream infections (CLABSI) and refeeding syndrome.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Minimising the Primary Surgical Insult
- **Minimal Access Surgery (MIS)**: Laparoscopic/robotic approaches ↓ structural tissue trauma → ↓ DAMP release → drastically ↓ magnitude of SIRS and catabolism.
- **Damage Control Surgery (損傷控制手術)** (For severe trauma/sepsis):
  - Rule: Physiology > Anatomy.
  - Abbreviated laparotomy (pack, control haemorrhage, clip bowel, NO anastomoses) → Temporary abdominal closure → ICU resuscitation (restore temp, correct acidosis/coagulopathy) → Definitive repair 24-48 hrs later.
- **Regional Anaesthesia**: Blocks afferent nociceptive signals from reaching the hypothalamus → completely blunts cortisol & catecholamine spikes. 
  - *HA Contextual Note: Epidurals are largely abandoned for simple laparoscopic surgeries due to fluid shifts and hypotension; target local blocks/wound catheters/lidocaine infusions instead.*

### Consent Issues Regarding Stress Response
- High-risk/frail patients must be explicitly counselled on the risk of **MODS (Multiple Organ Dysfunction Syndrome)**. Their heart/lungs may fail not from direct surgical injury, but because they lack the physiological reserve to survive the systemic hypermetabolic Flow phase.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Metabolic Support & ERAS Ward Care
- **Pain Management**: Opiate-sparing regimens (regular paracetamol/NSAIDs/regional blocks). Avoid systemic opiates as they cause paralytic ileus, delaying enteral nutrition.
- **Fluid Strategy**: "Zero balance" approach. Stop IV maintenance fluids immediately once tolerating oral intake to prevent visceral oedema.

### Rehabilitation & Catabolism Reversal
- **Physiotherapy**: Artificial nutritional support alone CANNOT stop muscle wasting as long as the stress response continues. Active muscle contraction (exercise) is the *only* physiological mechanism to overcome the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway and rebuild lean mass.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (Days 1-3)
- **Shock / Circulatory Collapse**: Failure of the Ebb phase to resolve, leading to refractory hypotension and irreversible lactic acidosis.
- **SIRS-induced Organ Injury**: Massive capillary leak → Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) and Acute Kidney Injury (AKI).

### Intermediate Complications (Days 4-14)
- **Sepsis & MODS (多器官功能障礙綜合症)**: Intense initial hypermetabolism + subsequent immunosuppression (CARS) → opportunistic nosocomial infections (VAP, UTI, CRBSI).
- **Wound Dehiscence / Anastomotic Leak**: Direct consequence of extreme catabolism, failed collagen synthesis, and severe hypoproteinaemia.

### Late Complications & Prognosis
- **Critical Illness Myopathy**: Profound acquired weakness delaying ventilator weaning and functional rehab.
- **Prognosis**: MODS remains the leading cause of late surgical death (mortality ~25%).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What are the primary counter-regulatory hormones mediating the catabolic stress response to surgery? Answer: Adrenaline, cortisol, and glucagon.
* Trap: Believing the rapid post-operative drop in serum albumin is primarily due to decreased hepatic synthesis or malnutrition. Pearl: It is a negative acute-phase reactant; the acute drop is primarily caused by increased microvascular permeability and rapid transcapillary escape of albumin into the interstitium.
* Pitfall: Administering massive volumes of 0.9% normal saline post-operatively to "flush" the kidneys. This causes hyperchloraemic metabolic acidosis, exacerbates visceral oedema, and severely delays gut motility. Use balanced crystalloids and a goal-directed "zero-balance" fluid strategy.
* Viva Question: Why do we administer a carbohydrate drink 2 hours before elective surgery in ERAS protocols? Answer: To switch the body from a fasted to a fed state, which significantly blunts post-operative insulin resistance and reduces catabolic muscle breakdown.
* Trap: Believing that aggressively over-feeding (hyperalimentation) a severely injured catabolic patient will reverse their muscle wasting. Pearl: You cannot feed a patient out of the stress response. Artificial nutrition must be modest to avoid metabolic stress; reversal of muscle wasting requires resolution of the underlying inflammation and active physical mobilisation.
* Viva Question: What is the primary intracellular pathway responsible for skeletal muscle wasting during the surgical stress response? Answer: The ATP-dependent ubiquitin-proteasome pathway.
* Pitfall: Prolonging pre-operative fasting unnecessarily (e.g., "nil by mouth from midnight"). This depletes hepatic glycogen stores before the surgical insult even begins, forcing the body to instantly break down skeletal muscle for gluconeogenesis during surgery.
