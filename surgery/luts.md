# Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (下尿路症狀)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms (下尿路症狀)
* **Definition**: Constellation of non-specific symptoms affecting **Storage (儲尿)**, **Voiding (排尿)**, & **Post-micturition (排尿後)** phases
* **Pathological correlate**: Usually implies **Bladder Outlet Obstruction (BOO) (膀胱出口阻塞)** or **Detrusor Overactivity (逼尿肌過度活躍)**
* **Syndrome**: Often grouped under **Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) (良性前列腺增生)** in men

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Prevalence**: Highly common in aging HK male population; >50% of men >60 yrs & >80% of men >80 yrs experience LUTS
* **HA Primary Care Burden**: Major driver of GOPC (General Outpatient Clinic) & SOPC (Specialist Outpatient Clinic) Urology referrals
* **Risk Factors**: ↑ Age, metabolic syndrome, obesity, family history

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Prostate (前列腺解剖)
* **Zonal Anatomy (McNeal's Zones)**:
* **Transitional Zone (移行區)**: Surrounds urethra → primary site of **BPH (良性前列腺增生)** → causes BOO
* **Peripheral Zone (周邊區)**: Posterior/lateral aspect → primary site of **Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)** (70-80%) → palpable on DRE
* **Central Zone (中央區)**: Surrounds ejaculatory ducts (10-20% of CaP)
* **Neurovascular Bundles**: Run posterolaterally (5 & 7 o'clock) → critical preservation to prevent Erectile Dysfunction (ED)
* **Arterial Supply**: Prostatic arteries via **Inferior Vesical Artery (髂內動脈分支)**

### Pathophysiology of BOO and LUTS
* **Static Component**: Glandular/stromal hyperplasia ↑ prostate volume (PV) → mechanical urethral compression
* **Dynamic Component**: ↑ Alpha-1 adrenergic tone in prostatic stroma/capsule/bladder neck → smooth muscle contraction → ↑ urethral resistance
* **Detrusor Response**: 
* **Early**: Detrusor hypertrophy → ↓ compliance → **Detrusor Overactivity (DO)** (Storage LUTS)
* **Late**: Detrusor decompensation → ↓ contractility → **Detrusor Underactivity** (Voiding LUTS, ↑ PVR, Retention)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Storage Symptoms (儲尿期症狀)
* **Frequency (尿頻)**: ↑ Daytime voiding
* **Nocturia (夜尿)**: Waking ≥1 time/night to void
* **Urgency (尿急)**: Sudden compelling desire to pass urine
* **Urge Incontinence (急迫性尿失禁)**: Involuntary leakage accompanied by urgency

### Voiding Symptoms (排尿期症狀)
* **Hesitancy (排尿遲緩)**: Delay in initiating micturition
* **Poor Stream (尿流細弱)**: ↓ Caliber/force of urine flow
* **Intermittency (尿流中斷)**: Flow stops & starts
* **Straining (用力排尿)**: Need to use abdominal musculature

### Post-Micturition Symptoms (排尿後症狀)
* **Incomplete Emptying (尿不盡感)**: Sensation of residual urine
* **Terminal Dribbling (滴尿)**: Prolonged final part of micturition

### Key Clinical Signs on Physical Examination
* **Abdominal Exam**: Palpable/percussible bladder (Retention)
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) (直腸指檢)**: Assess size, symmetry, nodularity, sulcus, anal tone
* **Neuro Exam**: Perineal sensation, lower limb reflexes (exclude neurogenic bladder)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Table of LUTS Differential Diagnoses
| Condition | Key Differentiating Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **BPH (良性前列腺增生)** | Symmetrical smooth enlarged prostate, older age, mixed storage/voiding LUTS |
| **Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)** | Hard nodule on DRE, asymmetric, ↑ PSA, bone pain (if metastatic) |
| **Overactive Bladder (OAB)** | Pure storage symptoms (Urgency +++), no prostate enlargement, no BOO |
| **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)** | Dysuria, acute onset, +ve nitrites/leukocytes, fever/hematuria possible |
| **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)** | Hx of urethral trauma/catheterization/STI, young pt, poor stream |
| **Neurogenic Bladder** | Hx of stroke/spinal cord injury/DM, ↓ anal tone, abnormal perineal sensation |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Investigations for LUTS in HA Clinics
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: Rule out UTI, microscopic hematuria
* **Serum PSA (前列腺特異抗原)**: Screen for CaP (Counsel before testing); Age-adjusted cutoffs (usually <4.0 ng/mL)
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: Check Cr/eGFR to exclude obstructive uropathy
* **Bladder Diary (排尿日記)**: Differentiate polyuria vs nocturia (3-day record)

### International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS) Severity Table
| IPSS Grade | Score Range | Indication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mild (輕度)** | 0 - 7 | Watchful waiting, lifestyle modifications |
| **Moderate (中度)** | 8 - 19 | Medical therapy |
| **Severe (重度)** | 20 - 35 | Medical therapy or Surgical intervention |
* **Quality of Life (QoL) Score**: 0 (Delighted) to 6 (Terrible)

### 2nd Line & Pre-operative Imaging for LUTS
* **Uroflowmetry (尿流率檢查)**: Qmax <10 mL/s indicates significant obstruction; normal >15 mL/s
* **Post-Void Residual (PVR) (殘餘尿量)**: via Bladder Ultrasound. PVR >100-150 mL indicates poor emptying
* **Transrectal Ultrasound (TRUS)**: Assess accurate prostate volume (PV) to guide surgical modality
* **USG KUB**: Exclude hydronephrosis/bladder stones if ↑ Cr or severe symptoms

### Advanced Urodynamic Studies (UDS) Indications
* **Pressure-Flow Study**: Gold standard to prove BOO vs Detrusor Underactivity
* **Indications**: Young pt (<50 yrs), neurogenic disease, failed surgical/medical treatment, large PVR

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management of Mild LUTS
* **Fluid Restriction**: Especially evening/night-time (minimize nocturia)
* **Dietary Mods**: ↓ Caffeine/alcohol (bladder irritants)
* **Bladder Retraining**: Timed voiding, double voiding
* **Medication Review**: Avoid anti-cholinergics, sympathomimetics, optimize diuretics timing

### Medical Therapy for Moderate-to-Severe LUTS (HA Protocol)
* **Alpha-1 Blockers (α受體阻滯劑)**: Tamsulosin, Alfuzosin, Doxazosin. Fast onset (days). Target dynamic component. SE: Postural hypotension, Retrograde ejaculation, IFIS.
* **5-Alpha Reductase Inhibitors (5-ARI)**: Finasteride, Dutasteride. Shrinks prostate by 20-30%. Target static component. Prevents Acute Urinary Retention (AUR) & progression. Slow onset (3-6 mos). SE: ↓ Libido, ED, ↓ PSA by 50%.
* **Combination Therapy**: Alpha-blocker + 5-ARI for PV >30cc & Mod/Severe symptoms.
* **Anti-cholinergics / Beta-3 Agonists**: (Tolterodine / Mirabegron) Add if pure storage symptoms persist (ensure PVR <150mL to avoid AUR).

### Absolute Surgical Indications for BPH
* **Refractory AUR (急性尿瀦留)**: Failed Trial Without Catheter (TWOC) x2
* **Recurrent UTI**
* **Recurrent Gross Hematuria**
* **Bladder Stones (膀胱結石)**
* **Renal Impairment (Obstructive Uropathy / Post-renal AKI)**
* **Medically Refractory LUTS / Intolerable Side Effects**

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Gold Standard Surgical Modalities for BOO/BPH
* **TURP (經尿道前列腺切除術)**: For PV 30-80cc. 
* **Monopolar TURP**: Uses Glycine irrigation. Risk of TUR syndrome.
* **Bipolar TURP**: Uses Normal Saline. ↓ Risk of TUR syndrome. Current HA standard.
* **Open/Robotic Simple Prostatectomy**: For PV >80-100cc or concomitant large bladder stones.
* **Laser Enucleation (HoLEP / ThuLEP)**: Size-independent, better hemostasis, steep learning curve.

### Minimally Invasive Surgical Therapies (MIST)
* **Rezum (Water Vapor Therapy)**: Steam induces necrosis. Preserves sexual function.
* **UroLift (Prostatic Urethral Lift)**: Implants to retract prostatic lobes. No thermal energy.

### Pre-operative Consent Protocol for TURP
* **Bleeding / Transfusion (出血/輸血)**: ~1-5% risk
* **Retrograde Ejaculation (逆行射精)**: 65-75% risk (Destruction of bladder neck)
* **Erectile Dysfunction (勃起功能障礙)**: ~10% risk
* **Urinary Incontinence (尿失禁)**: <1% risk (Sphincter injury)
* **TUR Syndrome**: Very rare with bipolar
* **Urethral Stricture / Bladder Neck Contracture**: ~5% risk long-term

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care Protocol
* **Catheterization**: Insert 3-way Foley catheter (20-22 Fr)
* **Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI) (連續膀胱沖洗)**: Use Normal Saline. Titrate speed to keep effluent light pink/clear. Prevent clot retention.
* **Fluid Balance**: Strict I/O monitoring
* **DVT Prophylaxis**: Early mobilization, TEDS (avoid early chemical prophylaxis due to bleeding risk)

### Trial Without Catheter (TWOC) Protocol
* **Timing**: Usually Day 2-3 post-TURP when urine is clear
* **Procedure**: Stop CBI → Remove catheter → Monitor voiding volume & post-void residual (PVR)
* **Success Criteria**: Voided volume > 200mL AND PVR < 100-150mL
* **Failure (Recurrent Retention)**: Re-catheterize, ensure alpha-blocker coverage, attempt TWOC again in 1-2 weeks or teach Intermittent Self-Catheterization (ISC)

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications of TURP (< 30 days)
* **Clot Retention (血塊積聚)**: Acute pain, no urine output despite CBI. Requires manual bladder washout (Toomey syringe).
* **Hematuria / Hemorrhage**: May require return to theatre for diathermy hemostasis.
* **TUR Syndrome**: Dilutional hyponatremia + fluid overload.
* **UTI / Urosepsis**: Due to instrumentation.
* **Capsular Perforation**: Extravasation of irrigation fluid.

### Late Complications of TURP (> 30 days)
* **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)**: Due to mucosal trauma from large resectoscope.
* **Bladder Neck Contracture**: Fibrosis at resection margin.
* **Retrograde Ejaculation**: Permanent.
* **Re-treatment**: 1-2% per year require re-resection due to BPH regrowth.

### Pathophysiology & Management of TUR Syndrome
* **Pathophysiology**: Venous sinusoids opened during resection → systemic absorption of hypotonic irrigation fluid (1.5% Glycine used in Monopolar TURP) → hypervolemic hyponatremia.
* **Signs/Symptoms**: Bradycardia, Hypertension → Hypotension, Nausea/Vomiting, Confusion, Visual disturbances (glycine toxicity), Seizures, Coma.
* **Management**: 
* Abort procedure immediately
* Diuretics (Furosemide) for hypervolemia
* Check serum Na+
* Hypertonic saline (3%) ONLY for severe symptomatic hyponatremia (correct slowly to prevent Central Pontine Myelinolysis)

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of painless, progressive LUTS in an elderly male? Answer: Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH).
* Trap: Assuming a massively enlarged prostate on DRE directly correlates with severe LUTS. Pearl: Prostate size correlates poorly with symptom severity; BOO is dependent on the transition zone encroaching the urethra, not total volume.
* Pitfall: Prescribing Tamsulosin without checking the patient's planned eye surgeries. Intraoperative Floppy Iris Syndrome (IFIS) complicates cataract surgery; always ask before initiating alpha-blockers.
* Viva Question: A patient with BPH is prescribed Finasteride. How will this affect his PSA levels? Answer: 5-ARIs halve the baseline PSA level after 6 months; you must double the measured PSA to interpret his actual cancer risk.
* Trap: Performing TURP on a patient whose primary symptom is only urgency and frequency with a PVR of 20mL. Pearl: This is likely pure OAB/Detrusor Overactivity. TURP may worsen storage symptoms; optimize anticholinergics/mirabegron first.
* Pitfall: Rapidly correcting hyponatremia in TUR syndrome. Rapid correction risks osmotic demyelination syndrome (Central Pontine Myelinolysis).
* Viva Question: Patient develops acute abdominal pain and absolute constipation 1 day after starting an anti-cholinergic for OAB. What is the diagnosis? Answer: Acute Urinary Retention precipitating sympathetic overactivity and ileus, or anti-cholinergic-induced bowel dysmotility; immediately check bladder scan and stop the medication.
* Trap: Diagnosing BOO solely based on Uroflowmetry showing Qmax < 10mL/s. Pearl: A low Qmax can also be caused by Detrusor Underactivity; only Urodynamic Pressure-Flow Studies can definitively prove obstruction (High pressure, low flow).
