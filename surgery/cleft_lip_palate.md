# Cleft Lip and Palate (唇顎裂)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Cleft Lip and Palate
* **Cleft Lip (CL) (兔唇/裂唇):** Congenital non-fusion of frontonasal & maxillary prominences.
* **Cleft Palate (CP) (裂齶):** Congenital non-fusion of lateral palatal shelves.
### Hong Kong Epidemiology
* Incidence: ~1.3–1.5 per 1,000 live births in HK.
* Asians → ↑ risk compared to Caucasians or Africans.
* CL +/- CP: Males > Females (2:1).
* Isolated CP: Females > Males (2:1).
* Unilateral CL: Left side > Right side (2:1).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Embryological Pathophysiology
* **Lip:** Failure of fusion of medial nasal & maxillary prominences (Weeks 4–7 gestation).
* **Palate:** Failure of fusion of lateral palatine processes (Weeks 8–12 gestation).
* **Primary Palate:** Structures anterior to incisive foramen (lip, premaxilla/alveolus).
* **Secondary Palate:** Structures posterior to incisive foramen (hard & soft palate).
### Muscular Anatomy in Cleft Lip (裂唇肌肉解剖)
* **Orbicularis Oris (口輪匝肌):** Sphincter ring disrupted.
* Fibers run parallel to cleft margin.
* Abnormal lateral insertion → Alar base.
* Abnormal medial insertion → Columella base.
### Muscular Anatomy in Cleft Palate (裂齶肌肉解剖)
* **Levator Veli Palatini (齶帆提肌):** Key muscle for velopharyngeal closure.
* Normal insertion → Midline palatine aponeurosis.
* Cleft insertion → Abnormal anterior insertion into posterior border of hard palate.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Physical Signs
* Visible unilateral or bilateral facial cleft.
* **Nasal Deformity (鼻部畸形):**
* Depressed/flattened lower lateral cartilage (alar dome).
* Deficient/short columella (especially in bilateral CL).
* Deviated nasal septum.
### Functional Impairment Symptoms
* **Feeding Difficulties (餵食困難):** 
* Inability to generate -ve intraoral pressure.
* Inadequate suckling.
* **Recurrent Otitis Media (復發性中耳炎):** 
* Tensor veli palatini dysfunction.
* Poor Eustachian tube opening.
* **Speech Abnormalities (言語異常):** 
* Velopharyngeal insufficiency (VPI) (齶咽閉合不全).
* Hypernasal speech.
* Articulation errors.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Syndromic vs Non-Syndromic Classification
| Classification | Proportion | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Syndromic (非綜合症)** | 70% | Isolated defect, multifactorial etiology (genetic + maternal smoking/folate ↓/phenytoin). |
| **Syndromic (綜合症)** | 30% | Associated with chromosomal/monogenic disorders. Requires full genetic workup. |
### Key Syndromic Differentials
* **Pierre Robin Sequence (皮埃爾·羅賓綜合症):**
* Micrognathia (小頜畸形)
* Glossoptosis (舌下垂)
* U-shaped Cleft Palate
* **Treacher Collins Syndrome:**
* Mandibulofacial dysostosis
* Malar hypoplasia
* Down-slanting palpebral fissures
* **Patau Syndrome (Trisomy 13):**
* Microcephaly
* Holoprosencephaly
* Polydactyly
* Severe CL/CP

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Antenatal Imaging Workup
* **Fetal Anomaly USG (胎兒超聲波):** Routinely detects CL at 18–20 weeks (CP harder to visualize).
* **Fetal MRI (胎兒磁力共振):** Reserved for complex syndromic suspicion.
### Postnatal & Pre-operative Workup
* **Genetic Screening:** Karyotype / chromosomal microarray if syndromic features +ve.
* **Cardiology:** Echocardiogram (rule out ASD/VSD prior to general anesthesia).
* **Audiology:** Tympanometry / Brainstem Auditory Evoked Response (BAER) (assess hearing / middle ear effusion).
### Anatomical Staging & Classification (Veau Classification)
| Veau Class | Anatomical Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Group I** | Defect of soft palate only. |
| **Group II** | Defect of soft & hard palate (stops at incisive foramen). |
| **Group III** | Unilateral complete CL + CP (involves alveolus). |
| **Group IV** | Bilateral complete CL + CP. |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Immediate Neonatal Management (HA Protocol)
* **Airway:** Prone/lateral positioning. Nasopharyngeal airway (NPA) if Pierre Robin Sequence +ve.
* **Feeding:** 
* Specialist lactation input.
* Haberman feeder (SpecialNeeds bottle) or squeeze bottles.
* Upright feeding at 45° angle.
* **Taping / NAM:** Nasoalveolar molding (NAM) (鼻槽塑形) by orthodontist to narrow cleft gap pre-op.
### Multidisciplinary Surgical Timeline (HK Standard)
| Age | Surgical / Clinical Intervention |
| :--- | :--- |
| **3 - 6 Months** | Cleft Lip Repair (Rule of 10s MUST be met). |
| **9 - 12 Months** | Cleft Palate Repair (Before speech habituation). |
| **1 - 3 Years** | Speech therapy, ongoing audiology (Grommet insertion if needed). |
| **8 - 11 Years** | Alveolar Bone Graft (牙槽骨移植) (Before permanent canine eruption). |
| **14 - 18 Years** | Definitive Orthognathic Surgery (頜骨矯正) & Rhinoplasty (鼻部整形). |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Pre-operative Fitness (Rule of 10s for CL Repair)
* Body weight > **10 lbs** (4.5 kg).
* Age > **10 weeks**.
* Hemoglobin > **10 g/dL**.
* WBC < **10,000/mm³**.
### Key Operative Principles for Cleft Lip
* **Unilateral CL:** Millard Rotation-Advancement Flap (Standard in HK). 
* Reorients orbicularis oris.
* Lengthens lip.
* Closes defect.
* **Bilateral CL:** Modified Mulliken technique.
### Key Operative Principles for Cleft Palate
* **Techniques:** Furlow double-opposing Z-plasty OR Bardach two-flap palatoplasty.
* **Muscle Sling Release:** MUST completely release & retroposition levator veli palatini to restore velopharyngeal sphincter.
* **Tension-Free Closure:** Crucial to prevent post-op fistula.
### Informed Consent Counseling
* **General Surgical Risks:**
* Hemorrhage
* Surgical site infection
* General anesthesia risks
* **Specific Cleft Risks:**
* Wound dehiscence
* Oronasal fistula (口鼻瘻管)
* Need for secondary speech surgeries
* Midface growth restriction

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative Care
* **Airway Monitoring:** Strict SpO2 monitoring (high risk of airway edema post-palatoplasty).
* **Restraints:** Arm splints (肘部固定夾) ("No-No" arm bands) × 3 weeks to prevent face touching.
* **Analgesia:** Regular paracetamol + NSAIDs (syrup format).
### Wound & Nutritional Care
* **Diet Progression:** 
* Clear fluids initially.
* Pureed/soft diet via cup or spoon.
* NO pacifiers or rigid bottle nipples.
* **Suture Care:** Chlorhexidine gentle dabbing + topical antibiotic ointment along lip suture line.
### Long-Term Rehabilitation
* **Speech Therapy (言語治療):** Crucial from age 1.5 to correct articulation & monitor VPI.
* **Orthodontics (牙齒矯正):** Maxillary expansion pre-alveolar bone graft.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Complications (0-30 Days)
* Airway compromise (hypoxia / upper airway edema).
* Post-operative hemorrhage.
* Wound dehiscence.
### Intermediate Complications (Months)
* **Oronasal Fistula (口鼻瘻管):** Breakdown of palatal repair → nasal regurgitation of fluids (requires re-operation).
* **Otitis Media with Effusion (OME) (積水性中耳炎):** High risk of conductive hearing loss (requires grommets).
### Late Complications (Years)
* **Velopharyngeal Insufficiency (VPI) (齶咽閉合不全):** Occurs in ~20%; requires secondary pharyngoplasty if speech therapy fails.
* **Maxillary Hypoplasia (上頜發育不全):** Midface retrusion secondary to palatal scar restriction → Class III malocclusion.
* **Dental Abnormalities:** Missing lateral incisors.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the embryological basis of a cleft lip? Answer: Failure of fusion between the medial nasal prominence and the maxillary prominence during weeks 4-7 of gestation.
* Trap: Delaying palate repair to let the child grow larger to make surgery technically easier. Pearl: Palate repair MUST be performed before 12-18 months of age (ideally 9-12 months in HK) to ensure normal speech development and prevent permanent hypernasal speech habituation.
* Pitfall: Overlooking syndromic associations in an infant with a cleft palate. Always perform a thorough genetic, cardiac (ECHO), and hearing evaluation, as 30% of cases are syndromic (e.g., Pierre Robin sequence).
* Viva Question: What are the criteria for the "Rule of 10s" before cleft lip repair? Answer: At least 10 weeks of age, 10 pounds of weight, Hemoglobin of 10 g/dL, and a WBC count less than 10,000/mm³.
* Trap: Using regular infant bottles to feed a newborn with an unrepaired cleft palate. Pearl: Infants with a cleft palate cannot generate negative intraoral pressure; they require specialized bottles (e.g., Haberman feeder) or squeeze bottles for assisted feeding.
