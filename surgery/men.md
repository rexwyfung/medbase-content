# Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia (多發性內分泌腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia
* **Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia (MEN)** = Group of autosomal dominant (體染色體顯性遺傳) inherited cancer syndromes causing hyperplasias/neoplasias in multiple endocrine glands.
* Characterized by high penetrance, younger age of onset vs sporadic counterparts, and multifocal/multiglandular involvement.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & HA Context
* **Prevalence**: Rare (~1-3 per 100,000 globally). Similar incidence in HK.
* **Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) Protocol**: Index cases & 1st-degree relatives MUST be referred to **HA Clinical Genetic Service (CGS)** at HKCH/QMH for predictive testing & multidisciplinary management.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### MEN 1 (Wermer's Syndrome) Pathophysiology
* **Gene**: **MEN1 gene** on **Chr 11q13**.
* **Protein**: **Menin** (tumor suppressor protein).
* **Mutation Type**: Inactivating (loss-of-function) mutation → Requires "Two-Hit" Knudson hypothesis for tumorigenesis.
* **Classic Triad (3 Ps)**:
* **Parathyroid (副甲狀腺)**: Multiglandular hyperplasia (>90% penetrance).
* **Pancreaticoduodenal (胰臟/十二指腸)**: Neuroendocrine tumors (NETs) (30-70%). Gastrinoma (Zollinger-Ellison) > Insulinoma.
* **Pituitary (腦下垂體)**: Adenomas (15-50%). Prolactinoma > GH-secreting.

### MEN 2A (Sipple's Syndrome) Pathophysiology
* **Gene**: **RET proto-oncogene** on **Chr 10q11.2**.
* **Protein**: Transmembrane receptor tyrosine kinase.
* **Mutation Type**: Activating (gain-of-function) missense mutation → Spontaneous dimerization/activation.
* **Classic Triad**:
* **Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (甲狀腺髓質癌 - MTC)**: Arises from parafollicular C-cells (>90%).
* **Pheochromocytoma (嗜鉻細胞瘤)**: Adrenal medullary tumor (40-50%). Usually bilateral.
* **Parathyroid**: Hyperplasia (10-20%).

### MEN 2B Pathophysiology
* **Gene**: **RET proto-oncogene** (specifically **codon M918T** mutation in >95%).
* **Mutation Type**: Gain-of-function.
* **Phenotype**:
* **MTC**: Highly aggressive, presents in infancy.
* **Pheochromocytoma**: Bilateral (50%).
* **NO Parathyroid involvement**.
* **Extra-endocrine features**: Marfanoid habitus, mucosal neuromas, intestinal ganglioneuromatosis.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### MEN 1 Clinical Presentation
* **Primary Hyperparathyroidism (原發性副甲狀腺功能亢進)**:
* Asymptomatic hypercalcemia (most common).
* Nephrolithiasis (腎結石) (renal colic, hematuria).
* Bone pain (osteitis fibrosa cystica).
* **Pancreatic NETs (胰臟神經內分泌腫瘤)**:
* **Gastrinoma**: Refractory peptic ulcer disease (消化性潰瘍), severe GERD, secretory diarrhea.
* **Insulinoma**: Whipple's triad (fasting hypoglycemia, neuroglycopenic symptoms, relief with glucose).
* **Pituitary Adenoma (腦下垂體腺瘤)**:
* Bitemporal hemianopsia (visual field defect).
* Galactorrhea, amenorrhea (Prolactinoma).

### MEN 2A Clinical Presentation
* **Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC)**:
* Palpable neck mass (cervical lymphadenopathy).
* Dysphagia, hoarseness (if locally advanced).
* Flushing & diarrhea (systemic effect of ↑ Calcitonin).
* **Pheochromocytoma**:
* Episodic triad: Headache, sweating, palpitations.
* Refractory hypertension (高血壓).

### MEN 2B Clinical Presentation
* **Extra-endocrine Signs (Pathognomonic)**:
* **Mucosal neuromas (粘膜神經瘤)**: Bumpy lips, tongue tip nodules.
* **Marfanoid habitus**: Tall, long extremities, arachnodactyly, high-arched palate.
* **Intestinal ganglioneuromatosis**: Chronic constipation, megacolon in infants.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Discriminatory Comparison of MEN Syndromes
| Feature | MEN 1 | MEN 2A | MEN 2B |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gene Defect** | *MEN1* (Chr 11) | *RET* (Chr 10) | *RET* (M918T) |
| **Parathyroid** | +++ (>90%) | + (10-20%) | - |
| **Pancreatic NETs**| +++ (30-70%) | - | - |
| **Pituitary** | +++ (15-50%) | - | - |
| **Thyroid (MTC)** | - | +++ (>90%) | +++ (100%, Aggressive) |
| **Pheochromocytoma**| - | ++ (50%) | ++ (50%) |
| **Physical Habitus**| Normal | Normal | Marfanoid, Neuromas |

### Differentiating Familial vs Sporadic Endocrine Tumors
* **Age of Onset**: MEN tumors present 10-20 years earlier than sporadic tumors.
* **Multifocality**: MEN parathyroid disease is invariably 4-gland hyperplasia; sporadic is typically a single adenoma. MEN pheochromocytomas are frequently bilateral; sporadic are unilateral.

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Genetic Workup & Screening Protocol
* **1st Line**: Germline DNA sequencing (Targeted *RET* for MEN2; *MEN1* for MEN1).
* **Indication**: Any patient with MTC, bilateral pheochromocytoma, multiglandular parathyroid disease, or 2+ MEN-associated tumors.

### MEN 1 Biochemical & Imaging Workup
* **Labs**: Fasting serum Ca2+, PO4-, Intact PTH (iPTH), Fasting gut hormones (Gastrin, Insulin, C-peptide), Prolactin, IGF-1.
* **Imaging**:
* **Neck**: US Neck, Tc-99m Sestamibi scan (for parathyroid).
* **Abdomen**: Triple-phase CT Pancreas / MRI Abdomen, Endoscopic US (EUS) for small insulinomas, **Ga-68 DOTATATE PET-CT** (gold standard for NETs).
* **Brain**: MRI Pituitary with gadolinium.

### MEN 2 Biochemical & Imaging Workup
* **Labs**: Fasting serum **Calcitonin (降鈣素)** (MTC marker), CEA, **Plasma free metanephrines** (Pheo marker).
* **Imaging**:
* **Neck**: US Neck (evaluate thyroid nodules & cervical LN mapping).
* **Abdomen**: CT/MRI Adrenals (rule out pheochromocytoma). **MIBG Scan** or **Ga-68 DOTATATE** for extra-adrenal/metastatic pheo.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### MEN 2 Surgical Sequencing Protocol (CRITICAL)
* **Golden Rule**: If a patient has both MTC and Pheochromocytoma, **MUST operate on Pheochromocytoma FIRST**.
* **Reasoning**: Proceeding with thyroidectomy first risks lethal intra-operative hypertensive crisis (高血壓危機) from anesthesia/stress triggering catecholamine release.

### Prophylactic Thyroidectomy Protocol for MEN 2 (HA / ATA Guidelines)
* Stratified by *RET* mutation codon risk:
* **Highest Risk (MEN2B, codon 918)**: Total thyroidectomy in **1st year of life** (often within 1st month).
* **High Risk (MEN2A, e.g., codon 634)**: Total thyroidectomy **≤ 5 years old**.
* **Moderate Risk (MEN2A)**: Surgery in childhood/young adulthood, guided by stimulated calcitonin levels.

### Management Algorithm for MEN 1
* **Hyperparathyroidism**: Surgery indicated for symptomatic hypercalcemia, renal stones, ↓ bone density.
* **Gastrinoma**: High-dose PPIs (Medical 1st line). Surgical resection if resectable & no liver mets.
* **Insulinoma**: Enucleation or distal pancreatectomy (curative intent).
* **Prolactinoma**: Dopamine agonists (Cabergoline/Bromocriptine) (Medical 1st line). Transsphenoidal surgery if resistant/visual compromise.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Parathyroidectomy in MEN 1 & 2A
* **Operative Strategy**: Cervical exploration with identification of ALL 4 glands.
* **Procedure Options**:
* **Subtotal Parathyroidectomy**: Resect 3.5 glands (leave 50mg of viable tissue marked with clip).
* **Total Parathyroidectomy with Autotransplantation**: Resect all 4 glands, mince 1 gland, autotransplant into brachioradialis muscle or SCM. (HA preference varies by surgeon experience; autotransplantation allows easier re-exploration in arm if recurrence).
* **Routine Concurrent Thymectomy**: Removes supernumerary parathyroid glands and prevents future thymic carcinoids.

### Total Thyroidectomy for Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MEN 2)
* **Procedure**: **Total Thyroidectomy + Bilateral Central Compartment Neck Dissection (Level VI)**.
* **Lateral Neck Dissection (Levels II-V)**: Indicated if US shows suspicious lateral LNs or Calcitonin > 200 pg/mL.
* **Consent Issues**: Permanent hypoparathyroidism (Require lifelong Ca/Vit D), Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) injury (聲帶麻痺/hoarseness), lifelong Thyroxine replacement.

### Adrenalectomy for Pheochromocytoma (MEN 2)
* **Pre-operative Prep**: Alpha-blockade (**Phenoxybenzamine**) for 10-14 days until orthostatic hypotension achieved, followed by Beta-blockade (**Propranolol**) to control tachycardia. *Never beta-block before alpha-block!*
* **Procedure**: Laparoscopic/Robotic Bilateral **Cortical-Sparing Subtotal Adrenalectomy**.
* **Rationale**: Preserves adrenal cortex to prevent obligate Addisonian crisis and need for lifelong steroid replacement, despite ~10-20% risk of pheo recurrence.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Post-operative Hormone Replacement Therapy
* **Post-Thyroidectomy**: Lifelong Levothyroxine (T4) targeted to maintain TSH in normal range (TSH suppression NOT needed for MTC, unlike differentiated thyroid cancer).
* **Post-Total Adrenalectomy (if cortex not spared)**: Lifelong Glucocorticoid (Hydrocortisone) + Mineralocorticoid (Fludrocortisone). Strict "Sick Day" rules (double dose during illness/stress).
* **Post-Parathyroidectomy**: Monitor serial Ca2+. Oral Calcium + Calcitriol for temporary/permanent hypocalcemia.

### Surveillance and Long-term Follow-up
* **MEN 1**: Annual Fasting Ca, PTH, Gastrin, Prolactin. MRI Pituitary & CT Pancreas every 1-3 years.
* **MEN 2**: Annual Calcitonin & CEA (for MTC recurrence). Annual Plasma free metanephrines (for Pheo recurrence).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Immediate & Early Post-operative Complications
* **Post-Parathyroid/Thyroidectomy**:
* **Neck Hematoma**: Surgical emergency! Can cause airway compromise. Require immediate bedside opening of surgical clips.
* **Hypocalcemia (低血鈣)**: Peri-oral numbness, Chvostek's sign (+ve), Trousseau's sign (+ve). Treat with IV Calcium Gluconate.
* **Bilateral RLN Injury**: Stridor, requires immediate intubation/tracheostomy.
* **Post-Adrenalectomy**:
* **Addisonian Crisis (Acute Adrenal Insufficiency)**: Refractory hypotension, hypoglycemia, hyperkalemia. Treat with IV Hydrocortisone.

### Late Complications & Prognosis
* **MEN 1 Prognosis**: Decreased life expectancy primarily due to malignant progression of Pancreatic NETs (metastatic disease) or thymic carcinoid.
* **MEN 2 Prognosis**: Determined strictly by MTC stage at presentation.
* MEN 2B MTC: Highly aggressive, often fatal if prophylactic thyroidectomy not performed in infancy.
* MEN 2A MTC: Better prognosis, especially if operated before extra-thyroidal spread.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of death in MEN 1? Answer: Malignant pancreaticoduodenal neuroendocrine tumors (e.g., metastatic gastrinoma) and thymic carcinoid tumors.
* Viva Question: What is the genetic inheritance pattern of MEN syndromes? Answer: Autosomal dominant with high penetrance.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with MEN 2 and a thyroid nodule needs urgent thyroidectomy. Pearl: You MUST rigorously screen for and resect any pheochromocytoma first to prevent a fatal intraoperative hypertensive crisis.
* Trap: Starting a patient with pheochromocytoma on propranolol for tachycardia immediately. Pearl: You must establish adequate alpha-blockade (phenoxybenzamine) first; unopposed alpha stimulation leads to paradoxical severe hypertension.
* Pitfall: Doing a simple lumpectomy for a parathyroid adenoma in MEN 1. In MEN 1, parathyroid disease is inherently multiglandular. You must perform a subtotal parathyroidectomy (3.5 glands) or total parathyroidectomy with autotransplantation.
* Pitfall: Relying on TSH suppression post-operatively for Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma. MTC arises from C-cells, not follicular cells, so it does not possess TSH receptors. Replace thyroxine only to physiological (euthyroid) levels.
