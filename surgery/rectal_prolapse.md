# Rectal Prolapse (直腸脫垂)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Rectal Prolapse
* **Rectal Prolapse (直腸脫垂)**: Circumferential, full-thickness descent of **Rectal Wall (直腸壁)** through the **Anal Verge (肛門緣)**
* **Internal Intussusception (內套疊)**: Prolapse of rectal mucosa remaining internal, failing to breach anal verge
* **Mucosal Prolapse (黏膜脫垂)**: Partial-thickness descent, typically associated with prolapsing **Haemorrhoids (痔瘡)**

### Epidemiology & Hong Kong Context
* **Demographics**: ↑ Incidence in **Elderly Females (長者女性)** (>60 yrs, F:M ratio 6:1)
* **HK HA Context**: Progressive ↑ case volume parallel to HK's rapidly ageing demographic profile
* **Paediatric Presentation**: Rare, must exclude **Cystic Fibrosis (囊腫性纖維化)**, Ehlers-Danlos, or malnutrition

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Anatomical Defects in Prolapse
* **Pelvic Floor Weakness**: Attenuation of **Levator Ani (提肛肌)** & puborectalis sling
* **Pouch of Douglas (直腸子宮陷凹)**: Abnormally deep cul-de-sac
* **Sphincter Dysfunction**: Patulous **External Anal Sphincter (肛門外括約肌)**
* **Redundant Anatomy**: Elongated **Sigmoid Colon (乙狀結腸)** with lost sacral attachments

### Pathophysiological Theories
* **Sliding Hernia Theory (滑動疝理論)**: Anterior rectal wall herniates through pelvic fascia defect
* **Intussusception Theory (腸套疊理論)**: Circumferential internal intussusception originating at rectosigmoid junction → evolves to full external prolapse

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Patient Presentation
* **Protruding Mass (脫出腫塊)**: Initially post-defaecation → progresses to spontaneous/continuous with ambulation
* **Faecal Incontinence (大便失禁)**: Present in 50-75%, due to chronic internal sphincter stretch & pudendal neuropathy
* **Obstructed Defaecation (排便阻塞)**: Constipation (25-50%), manual digitation often required
* **Mucous Discharge & Bleeding**: Consequence of mucosal desiccation and trauma

### Physical Examination Signs
* **Pathognomonic Sign**: Visible prolapse with **Concentric Rings (同心環)** of mucosa
* **Digital Rectal Examination (PR, 直腸指檢)**: ↓ Resting tone, ↓ voluntary squeeze pressure
* **Dynamic Assessment**: Ask pt to bear down/strain on commode to visualize full extent
* **Perineal Descent**: Sags >3cm during Valsalva manoeuvre

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Prolapse Differentiation Table
| Clinical Feature | **Full-Thickness Prolapse (直腸脫垂)** | **Haemorrhoids / Mucosal Prolapse (痔瘡)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mucosal Folds** | **Concentric (同心)** | **Radial (放射狀)** |
| **Prolapse Length** | Usually > 5cm | Usually < 2cm |
| **Anal Sulcus** | Present (between prolapse & perianal skin) | Absent (continuous with perianal skin) |
| **Palpation** | Thick, muscular, double-wall feel | Thin, single mucosal layer |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Bedside Clinical Investigations
* **Proctoscopy (直腸鏡) / Rigid Sigmoidoscopy**: Assess mucosa, exclude **Solitary Rectal Ulcer Syndrome (孤立性直腸潰瘍綜合症)**

### Endoscopic Evaluation
* **Colonoscopy (結腸鏡檢查)**: Mandatory to exclude proximal **Colorectal Malignancy (結直腸癌)** acting as lead point or causing chronic straining

### Pelvic Floor Physiological Testing
* **Anorectal Manometry (肛門直腸測壓)**: Quantifies resting/squeeze pressures → guides procedure choice (e.g. avoid resection if severe ↓ tone)
* **Defaecography (排糞造影)**: Differentiates internal vs external prolapse, identifies rectocoele/enterocoele
* **Pudendal Nerve Terminal Motor Latency (PNTML)**: Assesses neuropathic sphincter damage (prognostic for post-op incontinence recovery)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Conservative Management Indications
* **Target Group**: High-risk frail elderly, asymptomatic/mild internal prolapse
* **Interventions**: High-fibre diet, osmotic laxatives, **Biofeedback (生物反饋治療)**, **Pelvic Floor Exercises (骨盆底肌肉運動)**

### Surgical Algorithm based on Operative Fitness
* **Young / Fit (Low ASA)**: **Abdominal Approach (腹部手術)** → ↑ durability, ↓ recurrence
* **Elderly / Frail (High ASA)**: **Perineal Approach (會陰手術)** → ↓ physiological stress, tolerates regional/local anaesthesia, but ↑ recurrence

### Bowel Function-Driven Surgical Selection
* **Constipation Dominant**: Favour resection rectopexy (to remove redundant colon)
* **Incontinence Dominant**: Strictly avoid resection, favour ventral mesh rectopexy

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Perineal Procedures (High ASA Patients)
* **Delorme's Procedure (德洛姆術式)**: Mucosal stripping + plication of denuded muscularis (Indication: short prolapse <5cm)
* **Altemeier's Procedure (阿爾特邁爾術式)**: Perineal rectosigmoidectomy + hand-sewn coloanal anastomosis (Indication: long prolapse >5cm)

### Abdominal Procedures (Low ASA Patients)
* **Laparoscopic Ventral Mesh Rectopexy (LVMR, 腹腔鏡腹側網片直腸固定術)**: Current HA preferred standard. Mesh fixed to anterior rectum & sacral promontory. Avoids posterior dissection → preserves autonomic nerves → ↓ de novo constipation
* **Suture Rectopexy (縫合直腸固定術)**: Full posterior mobilisation + suturing mesorectum to presacral fascia
* **Resection Rectopexy (Frykman-Goldberg)**: Suture rectopexy + sigmoid colectomy (Indication: severe slow-transit constipation)

### Pre-operative Consent Pearls
* **Recurrence Risk**: ~10-20% for perineal approaches vs <5% for abdominal approaches
* **Functional Outcomes**: Warn regarding unpredictable bowel function (persistent incontinence, de novo constipation)
* **Mesh Complications**: Specifically consent for mesh erosion/infection if VMR planned

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative Protocol
* **Bowel Regime**: Aggressive scheduled laxatives (e.g., Macrogol) to strictly prevent straining
* **Analgesia**: Multi-modal sparing opioids to avoid **Opioid-Induced Constipation (阿片類藥物引起的便秘)**
* **Mobility**: Early mobilisation, avoid prolonged sitting/straining on commode

### Long-term Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation
* **Physiotherapy**: Post-operative biofeedback crucial for retraining overstretched sphincters
* **Lifestyle Modification**: Adopt squatting position for defaecation (optimises anorectal angle)

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Operative Complications
* **Bleeding / Pelvic Haematoma (骨盆血腫)**: From presacral venous plexus injury
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口漏)**: Specific to Altemeier's or Resection Rectopexy
* **Pelvic Sepsis**: Highest risk with concurrent bowel resection + mesh placement

### Late Surgical Complications
* **Recurrence (復發)**: Peaks within 2-3 years, highly correlated with perineal approaches
* **De Novo Constipation**: Up to 30% after posterior mobilisation procedures (nerve damage)
* **Mesh Erosion (網片侵蝕)**: Erosion into rectum or vagina requiring explantation
* **Stricture (狹窄)**: Fibrotic stricture at coloanal anastomosis (Altemeier's)

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate rectal prolapse from 3rd/4th degree haemorrhoids? Answer: Rectal prolapse has concentric mucosal folds; haemorrhoids have radial folds.
* Trap: Recommending primary sphincter repair for faecal incontinence in a patient with a known rectal prolapse. Pearl: Always repair the prolapse first. Sphincter tone often improves spontaneously once the chronic stretching from the prolapsing tissue is eliminated.
* Pitfall: Omitting pre-operative colonoscopy in an adult patient with rectal prolapse. A proximal colorectal carcinoma acting as a lead point or causing chronic straining (tenesmus) must be excluded before any prolapse repair.
* Viva Question: What is the preferred abdominal surgical option in Hong Kong for a young, fit female with rectal prolapse to preserve sexual and bowel function? Answer: Laparoscopic Ventral Mesh Rectopexy (LVMR), because it avoids posterior rectal mobilisation, thereby protecting the pelvic autonomic nerves and reducing de novo constipation.
* Viva Question: An 85-year-old female with multiple comorbidities (ASA IV) presents with a 6cm rectal prolapse. What is the safest surgical option? Answer: Altemeier's procedure (perineal rectosigmoidectomy) as it can be performed safely under regional or local anaesthesia.
