# Transplant Immunology (移植免疫學)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Classification of Graft Types
* **Autograft (自體移植)**: Tissue transferred from one site to another on the same individual (e.g., split-thickness skin graft) $\rightarrow$ 0% rejection risk.
* **Isograft / Syngraft (同基因移植)**: Transfer between genetically identical twins $\rightarrow$ essentially 0% rejection risk.
* **Allograft (同種異體移植)**: Transfer between genetically disparate individuals of the same species $\rightarrow$ requires baseline **Immunosuppression (免疫抑制)**.
* **Xenograft (異種移植)**: Transfer between different species (e.g., porcine valves) $\rightarrow$ provokes profound hyperacute rejection.

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology & HA Organ Donation Context
* HK uses a strict **Opt-in System (自願捐贈機制)** via the Centralised Organ Donation Register (CODR) $\rightarrow$ results in chronically low deceased donor rates (~5-6 per million population).
* Heavy reliance on **Living Related Donors (LRD) (活體親屬捐贈)**, particularly for **Renal Transplant (腎移植)** and **Liver Transplant (肝移植)**.
* End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) incidence $\uparrow\uparrow$ driven by diabetic nephropathy $\rightarrow$ waitlist for deceased donor kidneys in HK HA network exceeds 5-7 years.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Major Histocompatibility Complex (MHC) & HLA System
* **MHC (主要組織相容性複合體)** genes located on Chromosome 6.
* **Class I HLA (人類白細胞抗原第一類)** (A, B, C): Expressed on almost ALL nucleated cells $\rightarrow$ presents endogenous antigens to **CD8+ Cytotoxic T cells**.
* **Class II HLA (人類白細胞抗原第二類)** (DR, DP, DQ): Expressed only on Antigen-Presenting Cells (APCs) (e.g., dendritic cells, B cells, macrophages) $\rightarrow$ presents exogenous antigens to **CD4+ Helper T cells**.
* Renal transplantation prioritizes matching at HLA-A, HLA-B, and HLA-DR loci (total 6 alleles).

### Mechanisms of Allorecognition (同種異體識別)
* **Direct Allorecognition**: Recipient T cells directly recognize intact *donor* MHC molecules on the surface of *donor* passenger dendritic cells $\rightarrow$ drives vigorous **Acute Cellular Rejection**.
* **Indirect Allorecognition**: *Recipient* APCs phagocytose donor antigens, process them, and present donor MHC peptides bound to *recipient* MHC molecules $\rightarrow$ slower, drives **Chronic Rejection** and antibody production.

### The Three-Signal Model of T-Cell Activation
* **Signal 1**: T-Cell Receptor (TCR) binding to the Antigen-MHC complex on APC $\rightarrow$ triggers intracellular calcium release.
* **Signal 2 (Co-stimulation)**: CD28 on T cell binds to B7 (CD80/86) on APC $\rightarrow$ essential for full activation; without it, T cell undergoes anergy.
* **Signal 3**: IL-2 binds to the IL-2 Receptor (CD25) on the T cell surface $\rightarrow$ activates the **mTOR (哺乳動物雷帕黴素靶蛋白)** pathway $\rightarrow$ drives T cell clonal proliferation.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Hyperacute Rejection (超急性排斥)
* Occurs minutes to hours post-anastomosis.
* Intraoperative signs: Graft rapidly becomes cyanotic, mottled, flaccid, and anuric immediately after clamp release.
* Driven by pre-existing cytotoxic antibodies (Anti-ABO or Anti-HLA) $\rightarrow$ triggers massive complement cascade, endothelial damage, and microvascular thrombosis.

### Clinical Presentation of Acute Rejection (急性排斥)
* Occurs weeks to months post-transplant (typically within first 3-6 months).
* Systemic signs: Low-grade fever, malaise, graft swelling, graft tenderness.
* Renal specific: Oliguria (urine output $\downarrow$), fluid retention, weight $\uparrow$, abrupt serum **Creatinine (肌酸酐)** $\uparrow$.
* Liver specific: Jaundice, altered mental status, ascites $\uparrow$, deranged **Liver Function Tests (LFTs) (肝功能測試)** (bilirubin $\uparrow$, transaminases $\uparrow$).

### Clinical Presentation of Chronic Rejection (慢性排斥)
* Occurs months to years post-transplant.
* Insidious, progressive decline in organ function without prominent systemic symptoms.
* Renal: Slowly progressive uremia, proteinuria $\uparrow$, new-onset refractory hypertension.
* Liver: Vanishing bile duct syndrome, progressive cholestasis.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentiating Post-Transplant Allograft Dysfunction
| Feature | Acute Rejection (急性排斥) | Calcineurin Inhibitor (CNI) Toxicity | Opportunistic Infection (e.g., CMV) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Weeks to months | Dose-dependent, variable | Typically 1-6 months post-op |
| **Systemic Signs** | Fever, graft tenderness | Fine tremor, gum hypertrophy, hirsutism | High fever, neutropenia, pneumonitis |
| **Urine Output** | Oliguria, acute decline | Preserved or mildly decreased | Variable |
| **Diagnostic Gold Standard** | Core needle biopsy (tubulitis, endarteritis) | Normal/ischemic biopsy + High trough CNI levels | PCR for viral DNA load |
| **Management** | Pulse IV Steroids, Anti-Thymocyte Globulin (ATG) | Reduce CNI dosage (Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine) | Antivirals (Ganciclovir), $\downarrow$ Immunosuppression |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Donor-Recipient ABO Compatibility Testing
* Absolute prerequisite for solid organ transplant (except in specific pediatric heart/liver protocols or advanced desensitization programs).
* O is universal donor; AB is universal recipient. Incompatible ABO $\rightarrow$ guarantees **Hyperacute Rejection**.

### HLA Tissue Typing and Panel Reactive Antibody (PRA)
* **Tissue Typing**: Genomic PCR tests to match HLA-A, B, and DR.
* **PRA (群體反應性抗體)**: Tests recipient's serum against a panel of random donor lymphocytes in the local population. High PRA (>80%) $\rightarrow$ recipient is "highly sensitized" (due to prior transfusions, pregnancies, or previous transplants) $\rightarrow$ severely $\downarrow$ chance of finding a compatible organ.

### Pre-Transplant Crossmatching Techniques
* **Complement-Dependent Cytotoxicity (CDC) Crossmatch**: Recipient serum mixed with specific donor lymphocytes + complement. If cells lyse (+ve crossmatch) $\rightarrow$ absolute contraindication to transplant.
* **Flow Cytometry Crossmatch**: Highly sensitive test to detect low-level donor-specific antibodies (DSA) not picked up by CDC.

### Post-Transplant Diagnostic Investigations
* **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM)**: Daily morning trough levels of **Tacrolimus (他克莫司)** or **Cyclosporine (環孢素)** to balance efficacy vs. toxicity.
* **Doppler Ultrasound**: 1st line imaging for acute graft dysfunction to rule out surgical causes (e.g., renal artery thrombosis, ureteric leak/stenosis, biliary stricture).
* **Percutaneous Core Biopsy (穿刺活組織檢查)**: The definitive gold standard for differentiating acute cellular rejection, antibody-mediated rejection (AMR), and CNI toxicity.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### HA Organ Allocation Protocols & Indications
* Referrals reviewed by HA specialized boards (e.g., Queen Mary Hospital for liver/heart, multiple centers for renal).
* Absolute contraindications: Active systemic infection, active malignancy (within 5 years), active substance abuse, irreversible severe extra-organ disease.

### Induction Immunosuppression (誘導免疫抑制)
* Administered intra-operatively and immediately post-op to aggressively deplete T-cells and prevent early rejection.
* **Basiliximab (巴利昔單抗)**: IL-2 receptor (CD25) monoclonal antagonist $\rightarrow$ standard risk patients.
* **Anti-Thymocyte Globulin (ATG)**: Polyclonal lymphocyte depleting agent $\rightarrow$ high-risk patients (high PRA, re-transplants).

### Maintenance Immunosuppression (維持免疫抑制)
* Standard HA Triple Therapy Regimen:
* **1. Calcineurin Inhibitor (CNI)**: **Tacrolimus** (1st line) or **Cyclosporine**. Blocks Signal 1/TCR pathway via calcineurin inhibition $\rightarrow$ prevents IL-2 transcription.
* **2. Antimetabolite**: **Mycophenolate Mofetil (MMF) (霉酚酸酯)**. Inhibits inosine monophosphate dehydrogenase $\rightarrow$ prevents purine synthesis in T/B cells.
* **3. Corticosteroid**: Low-dose **Prednisolone (潑尼松龍)**. Broad anti-inflammatory effect.

### Management of Acute Rejection Episodes
* **Acute Cellular Rejection (ACR)**: 1st line $\rightarrow$ High-dose IV **Methylprednisolone** pulses x 3 days. 2nd line (steroid-resistant) $\rightarrow$ ATG.
* **Antibody-Mediated Rejection (AMR)**: Diagnosed via +ve C4d staining on biopsy. Treated with **Plasmapheresis (血漿置換)**, IVIG, and **Rituximab (利妥昔單抗)** (Anti-CD20).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Cold Ischemia Time (CIT) and Preservation Solutions
* Organs flushed and preserved in hyperosmolar, high-potassium cold solutions (e.g., **University of Wisconsin (UW) solution** at 4°C).
* Hypothermia $\downarrow$ cellular metabolism by ~10% for every 1°C drop.
* Maximum acceptable CIT: Heart (<4-6 hours), Liver (<12 hours), Kidney (<24 hours). Prolonged CIT $\rightarrow$ risk of **Delayed Graft Function (DGF)**.

### Medico-Legal Consent via Human Organ Transplant Ordinance (HOTO)
* All living donor transplants in HK MUST be approved by the Human Organ Transplant Board.
* Ensures no commercial coercion/organ trading.
* Independent assessment required for psychological readiness and clear understanding of surgical risks by the donor.

### Brain Stem Death (腦幹死亡) Certification Protocol
* Prerequisite for deceased donation.
* Requires 2 independent senior doctors (not part of the transplant team).
* Must demonstrate absence of all brainstem reflexes (pupillary, corneal, vestibulo-ocular, cranial nerve motor, gag/cough) and a +ve **Apnoea Test (呼吸暫停測試)**.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-Operative Isolation and Monitoring
* Protective isolation in highly ventilated rooms (HEPA filters).
* Strict input/output (I/O) charting; replace urine output 1:1 with IV fluids in renal transplants to prevent hypovolemia-induced graft hypoperfusion.

### Pharmacological Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM)
* Narrow therapeutic index for CNIs.
* Toxicity of Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine: **Nephrotoxicity (腎毒性)**, neurotoxicity, new-onset diabetes after transplantation (NODAT), hypertension.
* Side effects of MMF: GI intolerance (diarrhea), bone marrow suppression (leukopenia).

### Long-Term Opportunistic Infection Prophylaxis
* Post-transplant immune nadir typically occurs between 1 to 6 months.
* **Pneumocystis Jirovecii Pneumonia (PJP)**: Prophylaxis with **Co-trimoxazole** for 3-6 months.
* **Cytomegalovirus (CMV) (巨細胞病毒)**: Prophylaxis with **Valganciclovir (纈更昔洛韋)** if mismatch present (Donor +ve / Recipient -ve).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical & Immunological Complications (<1 month)
* **Hyperacute Rejection**: Necessitates immediate graft nephrectomy/excision.
* **Vascular Thrombosis**: Renal artery/vein or hepatic artery thrombosis $\rightarrow$ catastrophic graft loss.
* **Anastomotic Leaks**: Urine leak (urinoma) or biliary leak at the surgical reconstruction site.

### Intermediate Infectious Complications (1-6 months)
* Peak window for opportunistic infections due to maximum immunosuppression maintenance.
* Viral: CMV, EBV, BK virus (causes polyomavirus-associated nephropathy).
* Fungal: Candida, Aspergillus.

### Late Malignant Complications (>6 months)
* Overall malignancy risk $\uparrow$ 3-5x compared to general population due to depressed immune surveillance.
* **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD) (移植後淋巴增生性疾病)**: Strongly driven by EBV reactivation $\rightarrow$ B-cell lymphoma.
* Skin cancers: Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) risk highly elevated.
* **Cardiovascular Disease**: Leading cause of mortality in long-term transplant survivors (exacerbated by steroid/CNI metabolic side effects).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the defining histological hallmark of Antibody-Mediated Rejection (AMR) on a renal allograft biopsy? Answer: Diffuse C4d complement deposition in peritubular capillaries.
* Viva Question: Which immunosuppressant is famously associated with causing gingival hyperplasia and hirsutism? Answer: Cyclosporine.
* Viva Question: A post-renal transplant patient presents with progressive graft dysfunction, and biopsy shows intranuclear viral inclusions (decoy cells) in the tubular epithelium. What is the likely diagnosis? Answer: BK Virus nephropathy.
* Trap: Assuming that a hyperacute rejection is driven by cell-mediated T-lymphocyte immunity. Pearl: Hyperacute rejection is purely humoral, mediated by pre-formed recipient antibodies activating the complement cascade and destroying donor endothelium within minutes.
* Trap: Treating all rising creatinine post-renal transplant aggressively with IV steroids assuming acute cellular rejection. Pearl: Rising creatinine could be due to Calcineurin Inhibitor (CNI) toxicity. Giving steroids would not fix this and further immunosuppresses the patient; always check Trough Levels and consider Doppler ultrasound/biopsy first.
* Pitfall: Ignoring the specific HLA mismatch profile when assigning CMV prophylaxis. A D+/R- (Donor positive, Recipient negative) status is the highest risk scenario for primary severe CMV disease and mandates rigorous antiviral prophylaxis (e.g., Valganciclovir).
* Pitfall: Performing a transplant without securing a recent Panel Reactive Antibody (PRA) or CDC crossmatch. Using outdated serum may miss recently formed Donor-Specific Antibodies (DSA), guaranteeing immediate hyperacute graft loss.
