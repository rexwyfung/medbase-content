# Painless Visible Haematuria (無痛性肉眼血尿)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Painless Visible Haematuria
* **Visible/Macroscopic Haematuria**: Frank blood in urine noticed by patient. 
* **Painless**: Absence of renal colic, dysuria, or suprapubic pain.
* **Red Flag**: Painless visible haematuria = **Urological Malignancy** (泌尿道惡性腫瘤) until proven otherwise.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Urothelial Carcinoma (尿路上皮癌)**: Top 10 most common cancers in HK.
* **Demographics**: Predominantly older males (Male:Female ≈ 3:1).
* **Risk Factors**: **Smoking** (吸煙) is the most significant risk factor in HK, followed by occupational exposure (aromatic amines in dye/rubber industries), and aristolochic acid (馬兜鈴酸) in traditional Chinese medicine.
* **HK Hospital Authority (HA) Guidelines**: All adults >40 years with painless visible haematuria mandate urgent 2-week fast-track urology referral.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Urinary Tract
* **Upper Tract**: **Kidneys (腎臟)**, **Renal Pelvis (腎盂)**, **Ureters (輸尿管)**. Lined by urothelium from calyces down.
* **Lower Tract**: **Bladder (膀胱)**, **Prostate (前列腺)**, **Urethra (尿道)**. 
* **Vascular Supply (Bladder)**: Superior and inferior vesical arteries (branches of internal iliac artery).

### Pathophysiology of Tumour Bleeding
* **Malignant Neoangiogenesis**: Tumours secrete VEGF → aberrant, fragile neovessels.
* **Spontaneous Rupture**: Fragile vessels rupture spontaneously into urinary tract → visible haematuria.
* **Intermittent Nature**: Bleeding can spontaneously cease due to local clot formation, leading to false reassurance.

### Pathophysiology of Clot Retention (血塊瀦留)
* Heavy bleeding → clot formation in bladder → obstructs **bladder neck (膀胱頸)** / urethra.
* Acute urinary retention (急性尿瀦留) → stretching of detrusor → severe suprapubic pain (transforms *painless* to *painful* presentation secondarily).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Painless Visible Haematuria
* **Timing of Bleeding**: 
  * Initial haematuria → urethral origin.
  * Terminal haematuria → bladder neck/prostatic origin.
  * Total haematuria → bladder/upper tract origin.
* **Clot Morphology**:
  * **Vermiform (worm-like) clots**: Upper tract origin (cast of ureter).
  * **Amorphous clots**: Bladder origin.
* LUTS (下尿路症狀): Irritative symptoms (frequency, urgency) if Carcinoma in Situ (CIS) (原位癌) is present.

### Key Clinical Signs on Physical Examination
* **General**: Pallor (anaemia from chronic blood loss), cachexia (metastatic disease).
* **Abdomen**: Palpable flank mass (e.g., advanced **Renal Cell Carcinoma (腎細胞癌)**), palpable distended bladder (if clot retention).
* **Genitalia**: 
  * **Left Varicocele (左側精索靜脈曲張)**: Non-reducing in supine position → pathognomonic for left renal vein tumour thrombus (RCC).
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) (直腸指檢)**: Nodular, hard prostate → **Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)** invading bladder neck; clinically benign enlarged prostate → **BPH (良性前列腺增生)**.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Comprehensive Differential Diagnosis of Painless Visible Haematuria
| Aetiology | Specific Condition | Discriminatory Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Neoplastic (Must Exclude)** | **Bladder Cancer (膀胱癌)** | Most common. Associated with smoking. Amorphous clots. |
| **Neoplastic** | **Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)** | Flank mass, paraneoplastic syndromes (polycythaemia, hypercalcaemia), vermiform clots. |
| **Neoplastic** | **Upper Tract Urothelial Carcinoma (UTUC)** | Vermiform clots. Similar risk factors to bladder cancer. |
| **Neoplastic** | **Prostate Cancer (前列腺癌)** | Hard nodular prostate on DRE, ↑ PSA, terminal haematuria. |
| **Benign Prostatic** | **BPH (良性前列腺增生)** | LUTS, enlarged smooth prostate. Diagnosis of exclusion for bleeding. |
| **Iatrogenic/Medical** | **Anticoagulant Use** | DOACs/Warfarin. **Rule**: Unmasks underlying pathology; do not attribute solely to drugs. |
| **Iatrogenic** | **Radiation Cystitis (放射性膀胱炎)** | Hx of pelvic radiotherapy (e.g., for cervical/rectal Ca). Telangiectasia on cystoscopy. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First Line Laboratory Investigations
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: Confirm RBCs (exclude hemoglobinuria/myoglobinuria).
* **Urine Culture (尿液細菌培養)**: Rule out concurrent UTI.
* **Urine Cytology (尿液細胞學)**: 3 consecutive early morning samples. +ve for high-grade Urothelial Ca / CIS. High specificity, low sensitivity for low-grade tumors.
* **Blood Tests**: CBC (assess anaemia), Renal Function Panel (baseline Crea/eGFR before contrast imaging), Clotting profile (PT/APTT).

### Gold Standard Imaging for Upper Tract
* **CT Urogram (CTU) (電腦斷層尿路造影)**: Gold standard. Triple-phase (non-contrast, nephrographic, excretory). Detects radiolucent filling defects in renal pelvis/ureters and renal masses.
* **USG Kidneys & Bladder (KUB)**: 2nd line if CTU contraindicated (e.g., severe CKD, contrast allergy). Misses small urothelial lesions.

### Gold Standard Endoscopic Investigation for Lower Tract
* **Flexible Cystoscopy (軟式膀胱鏡)**: Gold standard to visualize urethra and bladder mucosa. 
* **HA Protocol**: Performed under Local Anaesthesia (LA) in outpatient clinic. Allows direct visualization of tumors, CIS patches, or prostatic bleeding.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Acute Resuscitation and Medical Management
* **ABCDE Approach**: Large bore IV access, fluid resuscitation if tachycardic/hypotensive.
* **Medication Review**: Withhold anticoagulants/antiplatelets if clinically safe (consult cardiology if recent stents).
* **Catheterization**: Insert large bore **3-way Foley catheter (22-24Fr)**.
* **Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI) (連續膀胱沖洗)**: Use 0.9% Normal Saline at a rate sufficient to keep effluent light pink/clear. Prevents clot formation.

### Surgical Indications for Urgent Intervention
* **Refractory Clot Retention**: CBI fails to clear clots, causing severe pain/obstruction.
* **Haemodynamic Instability**: Ongoing massive haematuria despite CBI.
* **Definitive Treatment**: Tumour identified requiring histopathological diagnosis and local staging.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Clot Evacuation (血塊清除術)
* **Instrumentation**: **Rigid Cystoscopy (硬式膀胱鏡)** under General/Spinal Anaesthesia.
* **Evacuation**: Use **Ellik Evacuator (艾力克抽吸器)** or Toomey syringe to aggressively wash out established clots.
* **Haemostasis**: Systematic inspection and **Diathermy Fulguration (電刀燒灼)** of bleeding points or tumour bed.

### Operative Principles of Transurethral Resection of Bladder Tumour (TURBT) (經尿道膀胱腫瘤切除術)
* **Diagnostic & Therapeutic**: Excisional biopsy of the bladder mass.
* **Depth of Resection**: MUST include underlying **detrusor muscle (逼尿肌)** layer to accurately assess T-stage (superficial vs muscle-invasive).
* **Obturator Nerve Reflex (閉孔神經反射)**: Resection on lateral bladder walls stimulates obturator nerve → sudden adductor spasm → risk of bladder perforation.
  * **Prevention**: General Anaesthesia with muscle relaxants OR specific Obturator Nerve Block.

### Key Consent Issues for TURBT/Clot Evacuation
* **Bleeding**: Need for blood transfusion.
* **Infection**: UTI, epididymo-orchitis, urosepsis.
* **Bladder Perforation (膀胱穿孔)**: Extra-peritoneal (managed conservatively with catheter) vs Intra-peritoneal (requires emergency open laparotomy).
* **Urethral Stricture (尿道狹窄)**: Due to rigid instrument trauma.
* **Further Procedures**: Need for second-look TURBT, intravesical therapy, or radical cystectomy based on histology.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* **CBI Maintenance**: Continue CBI post-op. Wean speed as effluent clears. 
* **Single-dose Intravesical Chemotherapy**: **Mitomycin C (絲裂黴素C)** administered within 24 hours post-TURBT. 
  * **Rationale**: Destroys floating tumour cells, ↓ recurrence rate by 30-40%.
  * **Contraindication**: Suspected bladder perforation.

### Catheter Management and Discharge Planning
* **Trial Without Catheter (TWOC) (拔除導尿管測試)**: Once urine is clear for 24 hours without irrigation.
* **Discharge Education**: Warn patient of secondary hemorrhage (scab sloughing off at ~10-14 days post-op); ensure high fluid intake.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (In-hospital)
* **Haemorrhage**: Primary or reactionary.
* **TUR Syndrome (TUR綜合症)**: Fluid overload and dilutional hyponatraemia if monopolar diathermy + glycine irrigation used. (Less common in HK HA now due to bipolar TURBT + Saline).
* **Bladder Perforation**: Extravasation of irrigation fluid.

### Intermediate and Late Complications
* **Urethral Stricture**: Presents as poor urinary stream months later.
* **Contracted Bladder**: ↓ capacity from repeated resections or intravesical BCG therapy.

### Prognosis and Surveillance Protocols
* **Non-Muscle Invasive Bladder Cancer (NMIBC)**: High risk of recurrence. Requires adjuvant intravesical BCG and rigid surveillance protocol (Flexible cystoscopy every 3-6 months).
* **Muscle-Invasive Bladder Cancer (MIBC)**: Requires systemic neoadjuvant chemotherapy + **Radical Cystectomy (根治性膀胱切除術)** or radical radiotherapy.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the gold standard initial imaging and endoscopic workup for a 65-year-old male presenting with painless visible haematuria? Answer: Triphasic CT Urogram (CTU) and Flexible Cystoscopy.
* Trap: Attributing painless visible haematuria in a patient on Warfarin or NOACs entirely to over-anticoagulation. Pearl: Anticoagulation frequently unmasks occult urological malignancies; a complete haematuria workup (CTU + Cystoscopy) is absolutely mandatory regardless of INR levels.
* Pitfall: Reassuring a patient with painless haematuria and a normal Ultrasound KUB. USG has poor sensitivity for detecting small upper tract urothelial carcinomas (UTUC) and flat carcinoma in situ (CIS) in the bladder. CTU and cystoscopy must be ordered.
* Viva Question: During history taking, the patient describes passing "worm-like" blood clots in their urine. What does this pathognomonic sign indicate? Answer: Vermiform clots indicate an upper urinary tract source of bleeding (kidney or ureter), as the clot forms a cast of the ureteral lumen.
* Viva Question: Why is it critical to resect deep into the detrusor muscle layer during a TURBT? Answer: To accurately differentiate between Non-Muscle Invasive (Ta/T1) and Muscle-Invasive (T2) Bladder Cancer, which dictates entirely different treatment pathways (local therapy vs radical cystectomy).
* Pitfall: Administering post-operative intravesical Mitomycin C when a deep resection was performed and bladder perforation is suspected. Extravasation of Mitomycin C into the perivesical/intraperitoneal space causes severe chemical peritonitis and tissue necrosis.
