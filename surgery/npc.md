# Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (鼻咽癌)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of NPC
* **Malignant epithelial carcinoma** arising from the epithelial lining of the nasopharynx (鼻咽).
* Predominantly originates within the **Fossa of Rosenmüller** (羅氏窩) (lateral pharyngeal recess).
* Characterized by a strong etiologic link to **Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV)** (艾斯特林-巴爾病毒) infection.
* Exhibits distinct geographic, ethnic, and clinicopathological variations compared to other head and neck squamous cell carcinomas.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Endemic in Southern China and Hong Kong; historically termed **"Guangdong Tumor"** (廣東瘤).
* Annual incidence in Hong Kong is among the highest globally, peak incidence occurs at **40–60 years** of age.
* Marked gender predisposition with a **Male:Female ratio of approximately 3:1**.
* **Age-standardized incidence** has been steadily ↓ over recent decades due to dietary shifts, but it remains a leading cause of cancer death in young to middle-aged adult males locally.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Anatomical Relations and Fossa of Rosenmüller
* **Nasopharynx Boundaries**: Superiorly bounded by the skull base (sphenoid sinus/clivus); inferiorly continuous with the oropharynx at the level of the soft palate; anteriorly opens to the nasal cavity via the choanae; laterally bounded by the **Eustachian tube orifice** (耳咽管開口) and the **Fossa of Rosenmüller**.
* **Fossa of Rosenmüller**: A deep recesses posterior to the cartilaginous medial pterygoid plate; the most common site of primary NPC origin; narrow dimensions allow microscopic tumors to easily penetrate deep fascial planes before appearing endoscopically prominent.
* **Routes of Local Spread**:
  * Superior spread: Through the **Foramen Lacerum** (破裂孔) or **Foramen Ovale** (卵圓孔) into the middle cranial fossa, directly involving the **Cavernous Sinus** (海綿竇).
  * Lateral spread: Penetrates the pharyngobasilar fascia into the parapharyngeal space, compressing the Eustachian tube and neurovascular structures.
  * Posterior spread: Into the retropharyngeal space, prevertebral muscles, and upper cervical vertebrae.

### WHO Pathological Classification
* **WHO Type 1: Keratinizing Squamous Cell Carcinoma** (角化性鱗狀細胞癌): Rare in Hong Kong (<2% of local cases); weak correlation with EBV; lower radiosensitivity; associated with tobacco and alcohol use.
* **WHO Type 2: Non-keratinizing Differentiated Carcinoma** (非角化性分化癌): Stratified epithelium without distinct keratinization; intermediate radiosensitivity; moderately associated with EBV.
* **WHO Type 3: Non-keratinizing Undifferentiated Carcinoma** (非角化性未分化癌): The predominant subtype in Hong Kong (>95% of cases); also known lymphoepithelioma due to intense T-cell lymphocytic infiltrate; extremely strong clonal association with **EBV infection**; highly radiosensitive and chemosensitive.

### Pathophysiology and EBV Oncogenesis
* **Genetic Susceptibility**: Strongly linked to specific **HLA genotypes** (e.g., HLA-A2, B17, B46) highly prevalent in Southern Chinese populations.
* **Environmental Co-factors**: Consumption of Cantonese-style **salted fish** (鹹魚) and preserved foods during early childhood introduces carcinogenic **volatile nitrosamines**.
* **EBV Molecular Mechanisms**: Latent EBV infection (Type II latency) drives oncogenesis via expression of **LMP1** (Latent Membrane Protein 1, mimics constitutive CD40 signaling → activates NF-κB and MAPK pathways → inhibits apoptosis and promotes proliferation) and **LMP2A** (prevents viral lytic activation, allowing evasion of host immune surveillance).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Otological Symptoms
* **Conductive Hearing Loss** (傳導性聽力損失): Unilateral, progressive; caused by tumor occlusion of the Eustachian tube pharyngeal ostium → persistent negative middle ear pressure → **Otitis Media with Effusion (OME)** (滲出性耳炎) / **Serous Otitis Media** (漿液性耳炎).
* **Aural Fullness**: Sensation of ear blockage or pressure, often mimicking simple Eustachian tube dysfunction.
* **Tinnitus** (耳鳴): Low-pitched or buzzing, unilateral, persistent.

### Rhinological Symptoms
* **Epistaxis** (鼻出血): Classically presents as **blood-stained post-nasal drip** (回吸性血涕) or blood-flecked saliva/sputum in the early morning.
* **Nasal Obstruction** (鼻塞): Unilateral or bilateral; late feature indicating massive exophytic tumor extension filling the nasopharyngeal vault.
* **Anosmia / Hyposmia** (嗅覺減退): Rare; secondary to total nasal airway occlusion.

### Cervical Lymphadenopathy Signs
* **Cervical Metastasis** (頸部淋巴結腫大): The single most common presenting symptom (up to 70% of patients).
* **Nodal Characteristics**: Characteristically painless, stony-hard, fixed, slowly enlarging.
* **Anatomical Distribution**: Typically involves **Level II (Upper Jugular)**, **Level III (Middle Jugular)**, and **Level V (Posterior Triangle)** nodes; frequently spreads to bilateral neck chains due to rich, midline-crossing nasopharyngeal lymphatic networks.
* **Rouviere Node Involvement**: Metastasis to the topmost retropharyngeal lymph node (Node of Rouviere) is highly characteristic and easily detected on cross-sectional imaging.

### Neurological and Cranial Nerve Deficits
* **Cranial Nerve VI (Abducens) Palsy**: Most commonly affected cranial nerve; occurs via skull base invasion into the cavernous sinus → loss of lateral rectus function → **lateral gaze diplopia** (複視).
* **Cranial Nerve III, IV, VI Involvement**: Total ophthalmoplegia, ptosis, and loss of pupillary reflexes due to extensive cavernous sinus syndrome.
* **Cranial Nerve V (Trigeminal) Palsy**: Invasion of Foramen Ovale or Gasserian ganglion → facial numbness, loss of corneal reflex, or wasting of the temporalis/masseter muscles.
* **Trotter's Triad** (特羅特三聯症): Pathognomonic triad indicating deep parapharyngeal space extension:
  * Unilateral conductive hearing loss (Eustachian tube compression).
  * Unilateral temporofacial neuralgia / pain (CN V2/V3 irritation).
  * Ipsilateral palatal immobility / deviation (CN X or levator veli palatini infiltration).
* **Lower Cranial Nerve Palsies (CN IX, X, XI, XII)**: Occurs via compression within the posterior parapharyngeal space (Jugular Foramen / Hypoglossal Canal syndrome) → dysphagia, hoarseness, bovine cough, loss of gag reflex, shoulder drop, and ipsilateral tongue deviation/atrophy.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

| Differential Diagnosis | Key Distinguishing Features vs. NPC |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Lymphoma** (淋巴瘤) | Diffuse, rubbery, generalized lymphadenopathy; associated systemic B-symptoms (fever, night sweats, weight loss); nasopharynx may show diffuse lymphoid enlargement rather than an epicentered ulcerated mass. |
| **Cervical Tuberculous Lymphadenitis** (頸部淋巴結結核) | Matched, fluctuant nodes ("cold abscess"); sinus tract or skin changes; systemic constitutional symptoms; +ve Mantoux/IGRA; biopsy shows caseating granulomatous inflammation. |
| **Branchial Cleft Cyst** (鰓裂囊腫) | Congenital lesion; typically lateral neck mass along anterior border of sternocleidomastoid; fluctuant, non-fixed; smooth-walled and cystic on imaging; cytologic evaluation yields anucleated squamous cells without malignant features. |
| **Benign Adenoid Hypertrophy** (腺樣體肥大) | Occurs mostly in children/adolescents; rare as new-onset asymmetric mass in adults; smooth, symmetric lobulated appearance on endoscopy; no deep tissue invasion or cranial nerve palsies. |
| **Angiofibroma** (鼻咽血管纖維瘤) | Exclusively adolescent males; massive, recurrent epistaxis; hypervascular benign tumor expanding through sphenopalatine foramen; biopsy strictly contraindicated due to catastrophic bleeding risk. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Diagnostic Endoscopy and Tissue Biopsy
* **Flexible/Rigid Nasendoscopy** (鼻咽鏡檢查): Direct visualization of nasopharyngeal mucosa; looks for asymmetric mucosal bulging, exophytic masses, or necrotic ulceration centered on the Fossa of Rosenmüller.
* **Endoscopic Punch Biopsy** (內視鏡活檢): Definitive diagnostic gold standard; performed in outpatient clinic under local anesthesia via the nasal cavity; must sample deep tissue to avoid necrotic surface debris.
* **Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC)** (細針穿刺細胞學檢查): Indicated for any palpable cervical lymph node if the primary nasopharyngeal lesion is microscopic or indeterminate; demonstrates metastatic non-keratinizing carcinoma cells in a lymphoid background.

### Biomarkers and Serological Screening
* **Plasma EBV DNA Quantification via Real-Time PCR** (血漿EBV DNA定量):
  * **Screening**: Standard HA protocol utilizes plasma EBV DNA for asymptomatic high-risk screening (e.g., first-degree relatives).
  * **Prognostication**: Pre-treatment baseline viral load correlates directly with total tumor burden and risk of distant micrometastases.
  * **Monitoring**: Checked mid-radiotherapy and post-radiotherapy; failing to clear plasma EBV DNA to zero indicates residual disease; serial post-treatment spikes are highly sensitive for detecting **subclinical systemic recurrence**.
* **EBV Serology**: IgA against Viral Capsid Antigen (**VCA-IgA**) and IgA against Early Antigen (**EA-IgA**); historically useful for screening but largely supplanted by quantitative plasma DNA PCR in modern Hong Kong HA settings.

### Staging Imaging Protocols
* **MRI Brain and Nasopharynx with Contrast**: The preferred modality for primary tumor T-staging; superior soft-tissue contrast allows precise mapping of parapharyngeal space extension, retropharyngeal nodal involvement, skull base bone marrow infiltration, and cavernous sinus invasion.
* **CT Skull Base to Upper Thorax**: Excellent for evaluating cortical bone erosion at the skull base; used for planning radiation therapy fields.
* **Distant Metastasis Workup (M-Staging)**:
  * **PET-CT (FDG)**: Highly recommended in Hong Kong HA protocols for advanced locoregional disease (Stage III-IV) to rule out occult distant osseous, hepatic, or pulmonary metastases.
  * **Chest CT / Abdominal US or CT / Whole-body Bone Scan**: Traditional tiered alternative if PET-CT is inaccessible; checks for lung metastases (cannonball lesions), liver metastases, and osteolytic/osteoblastic skeletal lesions.

### TNM Staging System Table (AJCC/UICC 8th Edition)

| Stage | T Criteria (Primary Tumor) | N Criteria (Regional Lymph Nodes) | M Criteria |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage I** | **T1**: Confined to nasopharynx, oropharynx, or nasal cavity without parapharyngeal extension | **N0**: No regional lymph node metastasis | **M0**: No distant metastasis |
| **Stage II** | **T2**: Parapharyngeal extension or infiltration of adjacent soft tissues (e.g., pterygoid muscles) | **N1**: Unilateral metastasis in cervical node(s) and/or unilateral/bilateral retropharyngeal node(s), $\le$ 6 cm, above caudal border of cricoid cartilage | **M0**: No distant metastasis |
| **Stage III** | **T3**: Infiltration of bony skull base structures, cervical vertebrae, pterygoid structures, or paranasal sinuses | **N2**: Bilateral metastasis in cervical node(s), $\le$ 6 cm, above caudal border of cricoid cartilage | **M0**: No distant metastasis |
| **Stage IVA** | **T4**: Intracranial extension, involvement of cranial nerves, hypopharynx, orbit, parotid gland, or extensive soft tissue infiltration | **N3**: Metastasis in cervical node(s) > 6 cm and/or extension below the caudal border of the cricoid cartilage | **M0**: No distant metastasis |
| **Stage IVB** | Any T | Any N | **M1**: Distant metastasis present (most common sites: bone, lung, liver) |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Early-Stage Disease Management Protocol
* **Stage I (T1 N0 M0)**:
  * Primary treatment modality is **Definitive Radiotherapy (RT) alone** (根治性放射治療).
  * Modern standard utilizes **Intensity-Modulated Radiotherapy (IMRT)** (調強放射治療).
  * IMRT delivers highly conformal radiation doses ($\approx$ 70 Gy in 33–35 fractions over 6–7 weeks) to the primary tumor volume while minimizing radiation scatter to surrounding critical organs (e.g., parotid glands, optic chiasm, brainstem, spinal cord).

### Locoregionally Advanced Disease Treatment Algorithm
* **Stage II, III, IVA (Locoregionally Advanced)**:
  * Primary treatment modality is **Concurrent Chemoradiotherapy (CCRT)** (同步化學放射治療).
  * **Chemotherapy Regimen**: Intravenous **Cisplatin** (順鉑) administered either weekly ($30\text{--}40 \text{ mg/m}^2$) or every 3 weeks ($100 \text{ mg/m}^2$) concurrently with standard IMRT.
  * **Induction/Adjuvant Chemotherapy**: For bulky Stage III or IVA disease with high baseline plasma EBV DNA, HA protocols often add **Induction Chemotherapy** (吉西他濱 Gemcitabine + 順鉑 Cisplatin) prior to CCRT to shrink tumor volume and eliminate systemic micrometastases.

### Surgical Indications for Salvage Therapy
* NPC is primarily a non-surgical disease due to its extreme radiosensitivity and anatomic inaccessibility. Surgery is strictly reserved for **salvage therapy** (挽救性手術) in the following scenarios:
* **Persistent or Recurrent Local Disease (rT1-rT2)**: Isolated failure at the primary nasopharyngeal site following full-dose radical radiotherapy, provided there is no distant metastasis.
* **Persistent or Recurrent Regional Nodal Disease (rN1-rN2)**: Residual palpable or imaging-positive neck nodes persisting $\ge$ 3 months post-radiotherapy, or subsequent isolated nodal relapse in a previously irradiated neck.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Surgical Approaches for Salvage Nasopharyngectomy
* **Endoscopic Nasopharyngectomy** (內視鏡鼻咽切除術):
  * Indicated for small, localized recurrent tumors (rT1 or early rT2) restricted to the nasopharyngeal mucosa or superficial muscle layers.
  * Performed transnasally using rigid endoscopes; utilizes mucosal margins validated by intraoperative frozen sections.
  * Associated with significantly lower morbidity compared to open approaches.
* **Open Maxillary Swing Approach** (上頜骨翻轉術):
  * The classic open surgical gold standard popularized by major Hong Kong academic centers for extensive recurrent tumors (rT2–rT3) involving the parapharyngeal space or skull base.
  * **Operative Steps**: Weber-Becker facial incision → anterior osteotomy of the maxilla → unilateral maxilla is swung laterally while remaining pedicled on the cheek skin flap to provide direct, wide visualization of the nasopharyngeal vault → radical tumor clearance with margin control → maxilla swung back and stabilized with titanium plates and screws.

### Radical Neck Dissection Principles for Nodal Recurrence
* **Salvage Neck Dissection** (挽救性頸淋巴結清掃術):
  * Indicated for persistent or recurrent cervical lymphadenopathy post-radiotherapy.
  * Preferentially performs a **Modified Radical Neck Dissection (MRND)** or **Radical Neck Dissection (RND)** depending on the infiltration of non-lymphatic structures.
  * **Anatomical Landmarks & Structures Resected in Classical RND**: Removal of lymph node Levels I–V, along with the **Internal Jugular Vein (IJV)**, **Sternocleidomastoid Muscle (SCM)**, and **Spinal Accessory Nerve (CN XI)**.
  * Fibrotic, irradiated neck tissues feature loss of normal anatomical planes; extreme care is mandatory to avoid catastrophic injury to the **Carotid Artery** and the **Vagus Nerve (CN X)**.

### Informed Consent and Operative Risks
* **Maxillary Swing Risks**: Permanent facial scarring, trismus (jaw stiffness due to pterygoid fibrosis), palatal fistula formation, malocclusion of teeth, infraorbital nerve numbness, osteoradionecrosis of the maxilla.
* **Neck Dissection Risks**: Injury to CN XI (→ shoulder drop and inability to abduct arm >90°), injury to CN XII (→ tongue deviation/dysphagia), marginal mandibular nerve injury (→ asymmetric smile), chylous fistula (due to thoracic duct injury during left Level IV/V dissection), internal jugular vein thrombosis, carotid artery rupture (especially in heavily irradiated fields).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Acute Post-operative Management and Airway Care
* **Airway Protection**: High risk of post-operative airway compromise due to pharyngeal edema; temporary **prophylactic tracheostomy** (氣管切開術) or delayed extubation in ICU/HDU is often required after open maxillary swing procedures.
* **Flap Monitoring & Wound Care**: Close monitoring of facial skin flap perfusion; meticulous oral hygiene with antiseptic rinses to prevent surgical site infection of the osteotomy lines.
* **Nutritional Support**: Total nil-by-mouth (NBM) in the immediate post-operative phase; enteral feeding maintained via a temporary **Nasogastric Tube (NGT)** (鼻胃管) or percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy (PEG) until palatal and pharyngeal healing is confirmed (typically 7–14 days).

### Long-term Post-Radiotherapy Rehabilitation
* **Trismus Management**: Aggressive jaw-opening exercises (using wooden tongue spatulas or specialized mechanical jaw trainers) to combat progressive radiation-induced fibrosis of the temporomandibular joint and pterygoid muscles.
* **Xerostomia Care** (口乾症護理): Radiation damage to the parotid and submandibular glands results in permanent saliva loss; managed with frequent water sips, artificial saliva substitutes, and strict compliance with **topical fluoride application** to prevent rampant radiation caries.
* **Swallowing & Speech Therapy**: Long-term rehabilitation required for patients with pharyngeal constrictor fibrosis or lower cranial nerve deficits to minimize chronic aspiration risks.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Acute and Early Complications
* **Hemorrhage**: Severe epistaxis or neck hematoma; primary concern is carotid blow-out in an irradiated, infected wound bed.
* **Flap Necrosis**: Ischemic breakdown of facial skin flaps or mucosal coverings.
* **Palatal Dehiscence**: Separation of the soft/hard palate incisions after open access, resulting in a persistent oronasal fistula.

### Late Complications and Radiation-Induced Sequelae
* **Osteoradionecrosis (ORN) of Skull Base / Maxilla** (骨放射性壞死): Severe, chronic complication characterized by exposed, avascular, necrotic bone; presents with foul odor, chronic pain, and recurrent local infections; requires hyperbaric oxygen therapy or radical debridement.
* **Hypopituitarism and Endocrine Deficiencies**: Radiation damage to the hypothalamus-pituitary axis; results in growth hormone, thyroid-stimulating hormone, or ACTH deficiencies; requires lifelong hormone replacement therapy.
* **Radiation-Induced Temporal Lobe Necrosis (TLN)** (時間葉壞死): Cognitive decline, memory impairment, personality changes, or focal seizures due to radiation necrosis of brain tissue; visible on MRI as cyst formation and "finger-like" edema.
* **Internal Carotid Artery (ICA) Stenosis**: Accelerated atherosclerosis within the radiation field, markedly ↑ the long-term risk of ischemic stroke.

### Prognostic Indicators and Survival Outcomes
* **Tumor Staging**: 5-year overall survival varies sharply by stage (Stage I >90%, Stage IVA $\approx$ 60%).
* **Post-Radiation Plasma EBV DNA**: The single most powerful independent prognostic indicator; patients with detectable plasma EBV DNA 6 weeks post-RT exhibit vastly higher rates of local and distant recurrence.
* **Histological Subtype**: WHO Type 3 has a significantly better prognosis than WHO Type 1 due to its superior response to radiation and chemotherapy protocols.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why do adult patients in Hong Kong presenting with new-onset unilateral otitis media with effusion require mandatory nasendoscopy? Answer: Unilateral Eustachian tube obstruction by an occult Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma must be definitively excluded before assuming a benign etiology.
* Viva Question: What is the clinical significance of a rising plasma EBV DNA level in a patient who completed radical chemoradiotherapy for NPC 2 years ago? Answer: It indicates subclinical systemic or local tumor recurrence, which mandates urgent restaging via PET-CT and fiberoptic nasendoscopy.
* Trap: Performing an open incisional biopsy on a suspicious upper cervical lymph node before examining the nasopharynx. Pearl: Always perform fiberoptic nasendoscopy and direct punch biopsy of the nasopharynx first; open biopsy of a metastatic cervical node violates tissue planes and significantly ↑ regional recurrence rates.
* Trap: Assuming that a completely normal-looking nasopharyngeal mucosa on nasendoscopy rules out NPC in a symptomatic patient. Pearl: NPC can infiltrate submucosally without causing exophytic mucosal changes; if clinical suspicion is high or EBV DNA is elevated, cross-sectional MRI must be ordered.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to check dental health and arrange pre-radiation dental clearance for NPC patients. Delayed dental extractions in a post-irradiated jaw can trigger catastrophic, non-healing osteoradionecrosis of the mandible or maxilla.
* Pitfall: Attributing diplopia and a lateral gaze deficit in an NPC patient to simple rectus muscle strain. This sign indicates Cranial Nerve VI palsy from skull base erosion into the cavernous sinus, designating the patient as T4 (Stage IVA) and altering the entire prognosis.
