# Perioperative Care & Metabolism (圍手術期護理與代謝)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Perioperative Care & Metabolism
* **Perioperative Care (圍手術期護理)**: Multidisciplinary management of patients from surgical decision to complete recovery
- **Surgical Metabolism (手術代謝)**: Neuroendocrine & inflammatory cascades triggered by tissue trauma → aims to preserve vital organ function & provide energy for wound healing
* **Goal**: Minimize surgical stress, maintain physiological homeostasis (生理恆定), and accelerate functional recovery

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) (術後加速康復) Overview
* Evidence-based paradigm shift from traditional "nil by mouth & bed rest"
* **Aim**: ↓ surgical stress response, ↓ morbidity, ↓ Length of Stay (LOS)
* **Hong Kong HA Epidemiology/Context**: 
    * Routinely implemented across HA clusters for **Colorectal**, **Hepatobiliary (HPB)**, **Upper GI**, and **Orthopaedic** major surgeries
    * Requires dedicated ERAS nurse coordinators & multidisciplinary input (Surgeons, Anaesthetists, Dietitians, Physiotherapists)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### The Neuroendocrine Stress Response Axis
* **Afferent signaling**: Pain + tissue injury → spinothalamic tracts → Hypothalamus
* **Endocrine response**:
    * ↑ **Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal (HPA) axis**: ↑ ACTH → ↑ **Cortisol (皮質醇)** (catabolic, insulin resistance, gluconeogenesis)
    * ↑ **Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS)**: ↑ **Catecholamines (兒茶酚胺)** (tachycardia, HTN, glycogenolysis)
    * ↑ **Pituitary**: ↑ **ADH (抗利尿激素)** → H2O retention (oliguria)
    * ↑ **Pancreas**: ↑ **Glucagon (升糖素)**, ↓ Insulin → Hyperglycemia (高血糖)
    * ↑ **RAAS system**: ↑ Aldosterone → Na+ & H2O retention, K+ excretion

### Cuthbertson’s Phases of Metabolic Response
| Phase | Timeframe | Pathophysiology & Clinical Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ebb Phase (退潮期)** | 0–24 hours | **Goal**: Conserve energy & volume.<br>↓ Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), ↓ Temp, ↓ Cardiac Output, ↓ Tissue perfusion, Insulin resistance. |
| **Flow Phase (Catabolic) (漲潮期-分解代謝)** | Days to Weeks | **Goal**: Mobilize energy for healing.<br>↑ BMR, ↑ Temp, ↑ O2 consumption, severe muscle protein breakdown (nitrogen loss), Hyperglycemia. |
| **Anabolic Phase (合成代謝)** | Weeks to Months | **Goal**: Restore tissue mass.<br>Positive nitrogen balance, fat and protein synthesis, weight gain. |

### Pathophysiology of Fasting & Bowel Prep
* **Traditional prolonged fasting**: Depletes hepatic glycogen → exacerbates post-op insulin resistance & muscle catabolism
* **Mechanical Bowel Preparation (MBP)**: Causes severe dehydration (脫水), electrolyte imbalance (↓ K+, ↓ Mg2+), and disrupts gut microbiome

### Anatomy of Surgical Positioning Nerve Injuries
* **Brachial Plexus (臂叢神經)**: Superficial in axilla/posterior triangle. Injured by arm abduction >90° or head turned away forcefully
* **Ulnar Nerve (尺神經)**: Exposed at cubital tunnel (medial epicondyle). Injured by compression against hard operating table/armboards
* **Common Peroneal Nerve (腓總神經)**: Wraps around fibular neck. Exquisitely vulnerable to compression from lithotomy stirrups (截石位支架)

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Physiological Signs of Uncomplicated Surgical Stress
* **Vitals**: 
    * Mild tachycardia (輕度心跳過速)
    * Low-grade fever <38.0°C in first 24-48h (due to atelectasis & inflammatory cytokines like IL-6)
* **Urine**: 
    * Mild oliguria (少尿) (expected ~0.5 ml/kg/hr due to ADH/Aldosterone)
* **Metabolic**: 
    * Hyperglycemia (even in non-diabetics)

### Signs of Maladaptive / Complicated Stress Response
* **Vitals**: 
    * Sustained HR >100 bpm
    * Fever >38.5°C or persistent >48h post-op
    * Hypotension (Systolic <90 mmHg)
* **CNS**: 
    * Post-operative Delirium (POD) (術後譫妄)
* **GI**: 
    * Prolonged Post-operative Ileus (POI) (術後腸梗阻) (>3-5 days)
* **Renal**: 
    * Anuria (<50 ml/day) or persistent oliguria unresponsive to fluid challenge

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentiating Post-operative Fever (The "W"s)
| Timeframe | Source ("W") | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Days 1-2** | **Wind** (Atelectasis / Pneumonia) | Mild fever, ↓ breath sounds bases. Pneumonia: Purulent sputum, CXR consolidations. |
| **Days 3-5** | **Water** (UTI) | Dysuria, frequency, cloudy urine. Heavy catheter use. |
| **Days 5-7** | **Walking** (DVT / PE) | Calf swelling, unilateral pain, hypoxia, pleuritic chest pain. |
| **Days 7-10** | **Wound** (SSI / Anastomotic Leak) | Erythema, purulent discharge. Leak: Tachycardia, severe abd pain, peritonitis. |
| **Anytime** | **Wonder Drugs** (Drug fever) | Diagnosis of exclusion. Penicillins, cephalosporins. |

### Differentiating Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS) vs Sepsis
| Condition | Diagnostic Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SIRS** | ≥2 of: <br>• Temp >38°C or <36°C <br>• HR >90 bpm <br>• RR >20 or PaCO2 <32 mmHg <br>• WBC >12 or <4 |
| **Sepsis (敗血症)** | SIRS + Confirmed or suspected infection source |
| **Severe Sepsis** | Sepsis + End-organ dysfunction (e.g., AKI, altered mental status) |
| **Septic Shock** | Sepsis + Hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg + Lactate >2 mmol/L |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Pre-operative Risk Stratification: ASA Physical Status Classification
| ASA Grade | Definition | Local Clinical Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ASA I** | Normal healthy patient | Fit 25M, non-smoker for appendicectomy |
| **ASA II** | Mild systemic disease | Well-controlled HTN/DM, smoker, BMI <35 |
| **ASA III** | Severe systemic disease | Poorly controlled HTN/DM, ESRF on HD, previous MI (>3mo) |
| **ASA IV** | Severe disease that is a constant threat to life | Recent MI/CVA (<3mo), symptomatic valve disease, sepsis |
| **ASA V** | Moribund patient, not expected to survive 24h | Ruptured AAA with profound shock |
| **ASA VI** | Brain-dead organ donor | N/A |
*Note: Append 'E' (e.g., ASA IIIE) for Emergency procedures.*

### Routine Pre-operative Laboratory Workup Algorithm
* **Minor Surgery (ASA I/II)**: 
    *   No routine labs required unless clinically indicated
* **Major Surgery / ASA III+**:
    * **CBP (全血細胞計數)**: Check baseline Hb, PLT
    * **LRFT (肝腎功能)**: Crucial for IV contrast decisions and drug dosing
    * **Coagulation (PT/aPTT)**: Only if on anticoagulants, known liver disease, or neuroaxial anaesthesia planned
    * **G&S (血型及抗體篩查) / Crossmatch**: If expected blood loss >500ml

### Routine Pre-operative Cardiopulmonary Evaluation
* **ECG (心電圖)**: 
    * Age >65
    * History of Cardiac disease / DM / HTN
* **CXR (胸部X光)**: 
    * History of cardiopulmonary disease
    * New respiratory symptoms
    * Malignancy staging
* **Echocardiogram (心臟超聲波)**: 
    * Murmur on exam
    * Heart failure history with worsening symptoms

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Pre-operative Protocol
* **Counseling**: Manage expectations, stoma education
* **Optimization**: 
    * Smoking cessation (戒煙) strictly >4 weeks prior (↓ pulmonary complications)
    * Anemia correction (IV Iron / EPO)
* **Nutrition & Fasting**:
    * **NO prolonged fasting**: Solid food up to **6 hours** pre-op
    * Clear fluids up to **2 hours** pre-op
    * **Carbohydrate Loading (碳水化合物負荷)**: 800ml clear carb drink night before + 400ml 2h pre-op (Prevents post-op insulin resistance)
* **Bowel Prep**: Avoid routine Mechanical Bowel Prep (MBP) unless left-sided colonic anastomosis or pelvic surgery

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Intra-operative Protocol
* **Anaesthesia**: Short-acting agents (Propofol, Remifentanil) + depth monitoring (BIS)
* **Surgical Approach**: Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS) (微創手術) preferred
* **Fluid Management**: **Goal-Directed Fluid Therapy (GDFT)** using stroke volume variation (SVV) via arterial line → avoid salt/water overload
* **Temperature**: Active warming (Bair Hugger / Fluid warmers) to maintain **Normothermia (>36°C)** (Prevents coagulopathy & SSI)
* **Tubes & Drains**: Avoid routine NG tubes and prophylactic abdominal drains (↑ infection risk, hinders mobilization)

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS) Post-operative Protocol
* **Feeding**: Early oral feeding within 24h (stimulates gut motility, ↓ POI)
* **Mobilization**: Out of bed on Day 0 (↓ DVT, ↓ atelectasis)
* **Analgesia**: Multi-modal, opioid-sparing strategy
* **Catheters**: Early removal of Urinary Catheter (尿喉) within 24-48h (↓ CAUTI risk)

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### General Principles of Patient Positioning
* **Goals**: Optimum surgical access, maintain airway/circulation, prevent pressure sores (壓瘡) and nerve palsies
* **Pressure Points**: Must be heavily padded (Gel pads)
* **Eyes/Ears**: Taped closed (prevent corneal abrasion), ears flat (prevent cartilage necrosis)

### Common Surgical Positions and Specific Risks
| Position | Surgical Use | Physiological/Anatomical Risks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Supine (仰臥位)** | Laparotomy, Hernia, Breast | ↓ FRC, pressure on heels/sacrum/occiput. |
| **Lithotomy (截石位)** | Perineal, Lower GI, Gynae | **Common Peroneal Nerve** palsy (stirrups). Compartment syndrome in calves. ↓ Vital capacity. |
| **Trendelenburg (頭低腳高位)** | Pelvic surgery, central lines | ↑ Intracranial Pressure (ICP), ↑ Intraocular Pressure (IOP), ↓ diaphragmatic excursion. |
| **Reverse Trendelenburg** | Upper GI, HPB, Bariatric | Venous pooling in legs → ↓ Venous return → Hypotension. High DVT risk. |
| **Prone (俯臥位)** | Spine, Posterior Rectum | Ocular pressure (blindness risk), massive vena cava compression if abdomen not free. |
| **Lateral (側臥位)** | Thoracotomy, Nephrectomy | VQ mismatch (Dependent lung overperfused/underventilated). Axillary nerve stretch. |

### Informed Consent for Perioperative Risks
* Must specifically mention positioning risks (nerve palsy, pressure sores) for prolonged surgeries (>4 hours)
* Must consent for potential conversion from Laparoscopic to Open approach (轉開腹手術)
* Consent for blood transfusion risks

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Post-operative Intravenous Fluid Therapy Protocol (NICE / HA Guidelines)
* **Rule**: Prescribe based on the "5 Rs" (Resuscitation, Routine maintenance, Replacement, Redistribution, Reassessment)
* **Routine Maintenance Formula (per 24h)**:
    * **Water**: 25-30 ml/kg/day
    * **Sodium (Na+)**: 1 mmol/kg/day
    * **Potassium (K+)**: 1 mmol/kg/day
    * **Glucose**: 50-100g/day (to prevent starvation ketosis, NOT for complete nutrition)
* **Choice of Fluid**: Avoid excessive 0.9% Normal Saline (causes hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis). Prefer balanced crystalloids (Plasmalyte / Hartmann's)

### Post-operative VTE Prophylaxis Strategy (HK HA Guidelines)
* **Mechanical Prophylaxis**: 
    * Graduated compression stockings (TEDs)
    * Intermittent Pneumatic Compression (IPC) boots (used intra-op and post-op)
* **Pharmacological Prophylaxis**:
    * **LMWH** (e.g., Enoxaparin / Clexane 40mg SC daily)
    * **Timing**: Start 6-12h post-op once hemostasis secured
    * **Renal Impairment**: Reduce dose if CrCl <30 ml/min, or use Unfractionated Heparin (UFH)

### Post-operative Multi-modal Analgesia (多模式鎮痛)
* **Base (Scheduled)**: Paracetamol 1g QID (PO/IV)
* **Adjunct (Scheduled)**: NSAIDs (e.g., Celecoxib / Ibuprofen) IF no contraindications (renal failure, active GI bleed, anastomotic leak risk debatable)
* **Regional Block**: Transversus Abdominis Plane (TAP) block, Epidural (for open laparotomy)
* **Rescue**: Weak opioids (Tramadol) → Strong opioids (Morphine/Oxycodone) PRN
* **Opioid Sparing Rationale**: Prevents sedation, respiratory depression, and POI

### Post-Operative Nausea and Vomiting (PONV) Management
* **Apfel Score Risk Factors**: Female, Non-smoker, Hx of PONV/Motion sickness, Post-op opioid use
* **Prophylaxis/Treatment**: Ondansetron (5HT3 antagonist), Dexamethasone, Metoclopramide (avoid in bowel obstruction)

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Intraoperative / Immediate Complications (0-24h)
* **Surgical**: Primary Hemorrhage (slip of ligature, unrecognized bleeder)
* **Anaesthetic**: Difficult airway, Malignant Hyperthermia (惡性高熱)
* **General**: Hypothermia (<36°C) → coagulopathy, severe PONV, Acute Urinary Retention (AUR)

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications (Days 1-7)
* **Respiratory**: Atelectasis, Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia (HAP)
* **Cardiovascular**: DVT, Pulmonary Embolism (PE), Myocardial Infarction (Type 2 - supply/demand mismatch)
* **GI**: Post-operative Ileus (POI), Anastomotic Leak (吻合口瘻) (typically Day 5-7)
* **Infection**: Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染), CAUTI, CLABSI

### Late Post-operative Complications (Weeks to Years)
* **Wound**: Incisional Hernia (切口疝), Hypertrophic scar / Keloid
* **GI**: Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO) (粘連性腸梗阻)
* **Psychosocial**: Chronic post-surgical pain

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What are the three phases of the metabolic response to surgery described by Cuthbertson? Answer: Ebb phase (0-24h, hypometabolic), Flow phase (catabolic, hypermetabolic), and Anabolic phase (recovery).
* Viva Question: Why do we avoid using 0.9% Normal Saline for prolonged routine post-operative fluid maintenance? Answer: It contains supraphysiological chloride (154 mmol/L) which causes hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis and reduces renal cortical blood flow.
* Viva Question: What is the primary nerve injury risk when a patient is placed in the lithotomy position? Answer: Common peroneal nerve injury due to direct compression against the lateral metal stirrups, resulting in foot drop.
* Viva Question: According to ERAS protocols, what are the fasting guidelines for clear fluids and solid foods before surgery? Answer: Clear fluids up to 2 hours pre-op, and solid foods up to 6 hours pre-op.
* Trap: Automatically diagnosing a fever of 37.8°C on Post-Op Day 1 as a Surgical Site Infection (SSI). Pearl: Low-grade fever in the first 24-48h is a normal physiological stress response usually secondary to atelectasis and inflammatory cytokines (IL-6); SSI typically presents after Day 4.
* Trap: Believing that prolonged fasting (NBM from midnight) reduces aspiration risk and improves outcomes. Pearl: Prolonged fasting induces preoperative glycogen depletion, exacerbates postoperative insulin resistance, and is explicitly condemned by modern ERAS guidelines.
* Pitfall: Prescribing routine IV potassium supplementation in the first 24 hours post-operatively. The surgical stress response (aldosterone and tissue breakdown) naturally releases potassium; adding IV potassium too early can precipitate dangerous hyperkalemia.
* Pitfall: Removing the armboard abruptly or allowing the arm to drop >90 degrees during patient transfer. This causes severe traction injury to the brachial plexus.
