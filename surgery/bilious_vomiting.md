# Bilious Vomiting in Newborn (新生兒膽汁性嘔吐)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Neonatal Bilious Vomiting
* **Bilious Vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)**: Emesis containing green/yellow bile in the 1st 28 days of life.
* **Core Principle**: Considered a life-threatening surgical emergency until proven otherwise.
* **Primary Objective**: Immediate exclusion of **Midgut Volvulus (中腸扭轉)** secondary to **Malrotation (腸旋轉不良)** to prevent catastrophic bowel necrosis.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Demographics
* Incidence of malrotation: ~1 in 2500-3000 live births in HK.
* Presentation timeline: 50% present in 1st week of life; 80% within 1st month.
* Male-to-female ratio: 2:1 in symptomatic neonatal presentations.
* HK Hospital Authority (HA) context: Cases require urgent transfer via NETS (Newborn Emergency Transport Service) to tertiary pediatric surgical centers (e.g., HKCH, QMH, PWH).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Embryological Anatomy of Midgut
* Normal midgut development (Weeks 5-10): Herniation into umbilical cord → 270° counterclockwise rotation around **Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA)** axis → return to abdominal cavity.
* Normal fixation: Broad mesenteric base extending from **Duodenojejunal (DJ) Flexure** (Ligament of Treitz) in LUQ to cecum in RLQ.

### Pathophysiology of Malrotation with Volvulus
* Incomplete rotation → narrow mesenteric base (DJ flexure and cecum lie close together).
* Twisting of small bowel around narrow SMA axis → venous/lymphatic engorgement → arterial occlusion → transmural ischemia → irreversible bowel gangrene within 6 hours.
* **Ladd's Bands (拉德束)**: Abnormal peritoneal bands running from undescended cecum (often in RUQ/epigastrium) across duodenum to lateral abdominal wall → extrinsic duodenal compression & obstruction.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Early Clinical Presentation
* Sudden onset **Bilious Vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)** (usually a previously well neonate).
* Feeding intolerance & excessive crying (colicky pain).
* Abdomen initially soft & non-distended (due to high/proximal level of obstruction).

### Late/Ominous Signs of Bowel Ischemia
* Progressive abdominal distension & rigidity.
* **Hematochezia (便血)**: Blood per rectum indicating mucosal sloughing/ischemia.
* Erythema and edema of abdominal wall → underlying peritonitis.
* Hemodynamic collapse: Tachycardia, hypotension, mottling, lethargy (Septic/Hypovolemic Shock).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Diagnostic Classification of Neonatal Intestinal Obstruction
| Pathology | Typical Onset | Distinguishing Clinical Features | Classic AXR Finding |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Midgut Volvulus** | 1st week | Sudden decompensation, hematochezia | Paucity of distal gas |
| **Duodenal Atresia (十二指腸閉鎖)** | Day 1 (birth) | Polyhydramnios in utero, Down syndrome | **Double Bubble Sign (雙氣泡徵)** |
| **Jejunoileal Atresia** | Day 1-2 | Maternal cocaine/smoking, cystic fibrosis | Multiple dilated loops, air-fluid levels |
| **Meconium Ileus (胎糞性腸梗阻)** | Day 1-2 | Failure to pass meconium >48h, Cystic Fibrosis | Soap bubble appearance (Neuhauser sign) |
| **Hirschsprung Disease (巨結腸症)** | 1st week | Explosive stool on PR exam, distension | Dilated proximal bowel, narrow distal |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Screening Imaging
* **Abdominal X-Ray (AXR) AP & Lateral**:
* Primarily to rule out perforation (pneumoperitoneum) & duodenal atresia.
* Findings in volvulus: Often normal early on. May show paucity of distal gas or gasless abdomen.

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigation
* **Upper GI (UGI) Contrast Study (上消化道造影)**:
* Indication: Hemodynamically stable neonate with bilious vomiting.
* Pathognomonic signs:
* **Corkscrew Sign (螺旋徵)**: Contrast spiraling down the distal duodenum/jejunum.
* Abnormal DJ flexure location: Displaced to the right of midline & inferior to duodenal bulb.
* Beak sign: Abrupt termination of contrast at proximal duodenum (complete obstruction).

### Pre-operative Laboratory Workup
* Capillary/Venous Blood Gas (VBG): Severe metabolic acidosis & ↑ lactate (late indicator of ischemia).
* CBC: Leukocytosis/leukopenia, ↓ platelets (sepsis/DIC).
* Renal Function Test (RFT): Pre-renal AKI (dehydration).
* Group & Screen (G&S), Crossmatch: Essential for emergency laparotomy.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Immediate HKHA Resuscitation Protocol
* Strict NPO (Nil per os).
* Insert large-bore **Orogastric/Nasogastric Tube (OGT/NGT)** (Size 8Fr-10Fr) → continuous free drainage to decompress stomach & prevent aspiration.
* Secure IV access → fluid resuscitation: 20 mL/kg 0.9% NaCl bolus.
* IV broad-spectrum antibiotics: Ampicillin + Gentamicin + Metronidazole (HA neonatal sepsis protocol).
* Correct coagulopathy (Vitamin K 1mg IV/IM).

### Indications for Emergency Surgery
* **Absolute Indications**:
* Shock, hemodynamic instability, or peritonitis.
* AXR showing pneumoperitoneum.
* UGI contrast study confirming midgut volvulus.
* **Time-Critical Rule**: Target OT within 1-2 hours of suspected diagnosis; bowel necrosis is irreversible after ~6 hours of ischemia.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Ladd's Procedure (拉德手術) Steps
* Incision: Transverse supraumbilical laparotomy.
* **Evisceration & Detorsion**: Deliver all bowel, untwist mass in **Counterclockwise** direction ("turn back the clock").
* **Division of Ladd's Bands**: Sever bands compressing duodenum to fully mobilize right colon & cecum.
* **Broadening Mesentery**: Dissect along SMA axis to splay out the narrow mesenteric base.
* **Prophylactic Appendectomy (預防性闌尾切除術)**: Prevent future diagnostic confusion since cecum is permanently placed in LUQ.
* **Final Positioning**: Small bowel placed entirely on the right, large bowel entirely on the left.

### Damage Control for Ischemic Bowel
* If bowel is frankly necrotic: Resect dead segment. Avoid primary anastomosis if massive contamination or borderline viability; form double-barrel stoma.
* If bowel viability is questionable after detorsion/warming: Leave in situ, close abdomen temporarily (Bogota bag/VAC), perform mandatory **Second-Look Laparotomy (二次剖腹探查術)** in 24-48 hours.

### Pre-operative Consent Focus
* High mortality risk if bowel is gangrenous.
* Risk of massive bowel resection → **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)**.
* Need for long-term Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN) and central lines.
* Risk of stoma creation.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate NICU Care
* Invasive hemodynamic monitoring (Arterial line, CVP).
* Mechanical ventilation (splinted diaphragm from bowel edema/closure).
* Pro-kinetic agents (e.g., Erythromycin) often required for post-operative ileus.

### Nutritional Rehabilitation Strategy
* Central Venous Access (PICC/Hickman) for prolonged TPN.
* Trophic enteral feeds (expressed breast milk preferred) introduced slowly once NGT output ↓ and bowel sounds +ve.
* Goal: Promote intestinal adaptation & prevent mucosal atrophy.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<30 days)
* Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS) & multi-organ failure (if massive ischemia).
* Prolonged paralytic ileus.
* Anastomotic leak / Stoma necrosis.

### Intermediate to Late Complications
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)**: Due to massive resection of absorptive surface (especially ileum/ileocecal valve) → chronic malabsorption, diarrhea.
* **Intestinal Failure-Associated Liver Disease (IFALD)**: Cholestasis/cirrhosis secondary to long-term TPN reliance.
* Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO).
* Recurrent Volvulus: Rare (~1-2%) but possible if adhesions fail to maintain broad mesentery.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical first step in managing a neonate presenting with bilious vomiting? Answer: Immediate cessation of enteral feeds and insertion of a large-bore orogastric/nasogastric tube for gastric decompression.
* Viva Question: During a Ladd's procedure, in which direction must the surgeon untwist the volvulus? Answer: Counterclockwise, as the midgut almost universally twists in a clockwise direction around the SMA.
* Trap: Waiting for a pediatric surgery consultation before initiating fluid resuscitation and ordering an UGI contrast study in a stable patient. Pearl: The primary team must instantly decompress the stomach, resuscitate, and immediately arrange the UGI study to rule out malrotation.
* Pitfall: Reassuring parents because the initial abdominal X-ray is normal. AXR is frequently completely normal in the early stages of midgut volvulus; normal plain films never exclude the diagnosis.
* Viva Question: Why is an incidental appendectomy routinely performed during a Ladd's procedure? Answer: The procedure leaves the cecum in the left upper quadrant; removing the appendix prevents future life-threatening delays in diagnosing appendicitis due to atypical left-sided pain.
* Trap: Performing a primary anastomosis on bowel with questionable viability during the initial emergency laparotomy. Pearl: Leave questionable bowel in situ and plan for a mandatory second-look laparotomy at 24-48 hours to preserve maximal bowel length and prevent anastomotic failure.
