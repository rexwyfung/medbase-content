# Facial Fracture (面部骨折)
## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Facial Fractures
* **Facial Fracture (面部骨折)**: Structural disruption of the facial skeleton framework
* Involves **Upper Third** (Frontal), **Middle Third** (Maxilla, Zygoma, Nasal, Orbit), or **Lower Third** (Mandible)

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Demographics**: Predominantly young males (M:F ratio ~3:1)
* **Common Mechanisms in HK**:
* Falls (跌倒) (especially elderly population)
* Sports injuries (運動創傷) (e.g., cycling, rugby, contact sports)
* Interpersonal violence/Assault (毆打)
* Industrial/Construction accidents (工業意外)
* **Low MVA Rate**: Motor Vehicle Accidents (MVA) ↓ common in HK vs US/UK due to high public transport use, but cause severe high-energy trauma when they occur

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Surgical Anatomy of the Mandible (下頜骨)
* U-shaped bone → forces transmit along the arch
* **Ring Principle**: Fracture in one location often → contralateral fracture (e.g., Symphysis + Bilateral Condyles)
* **Weak Points**: Condylar neck, angle (especially with impacted 3rd molar), mental foramen

### Surgical Anatomy of Midfacial Buttresses (中面部支柱)
* Facial skeleton resists masticatory forces via thick bone pillars (Buttresses)
* **Vertical Buttresses**: Nasomaxillary, Zygomaticomaxillary, Pterygomaxillary
* **Horizontal Buttresses**: Frontal bar, Infraorbital rim, Maxillary alveolus
* **Pathophysiology**: Fracture implies disruption of buttresses → loss of vertical height & AP projection (dish-face deformity)

### Surgical Anatomy of the Orbit (眼眶)
* **Floor**: Maxillary sinus roof (extremely thin bone)
* **Lamina Papyracea**: Medial wall (Ethmoid bone)
* **Blow-out Mechanism**: ↑ Intra-orbital hydraulic pressure → fractures floor/medial wall → protects globe from rupture but → herniation of orbital contents into maxillary sinus

### Le Fort Classification of Maxillary Fractures (上頜骨骨折)
* **Le Fort I (Guerin/Horizontal)**: Separates palate from maxilla (Floating Palate)
* **Le Fort II (Pyramidal)**: Involves maxilla, nasal bones, medial orbit
* **Le Fort III (Craniofacial Disjunction)**: Separates entire midface from cranial base, involves zygomatic arch & lateral orbit

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### General Signs of Facial Trauma
* **Soft Tissue**: Swelling (腫脹), Ecchymosis (瘀斑), Lacerations
* **Bony**: Palpable step deformity (階梯狀變形), bony crepitus (骨擦音), mobility of maxilla

### Clinical Signs of Mandibular Fracture (下頜骨骨折)
* **Malocclusion (咬合不正)**: Teeth do not align perfectly (patient reports bite feels "off")
* **Trismus (牙關緊閉)**: Restricted mouth opening (<35mm)
* **Sublingual Hematoma**: Pathognomonic sign for mandible body/symphysis #
* **Nerve Deficit**: Anesthesia/paresthesia of lower lip & chin (Mental nerve injury, V3)

### Clinical Signs of Orbital Blow-out Fracture (眼眶爆裂性骨折)
* **Diplopia (複視)**: Specially on upgaze (Inferior rectus muscle entrapment)
* **Enophthalmos (眼球內陷)**: Sunken eye due to enlarged orbital volume
* **Nerve Deficit**: Cheek/upper lip anesthesia (Infraorbital nerve injury, V2)
* **Ophthalmic**: Subconjunctival hemorrhage (結膜下出血) with no posterior limit

### Clinical Signs of Zygomaticomaxillary Complex (ZMC) Fracture
* **Deformity**: Flattened malar eminence (顴骨隆起變平)
* **Ocular**: Lateral canthal dystopia (anti-mongoloid slant of the eye)
* **Trismus**: Impingement of depressed zygomatic arch on coronoid process of mandible

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Discriminatory Features of Facial Traumas
| Condition | Key Symptoms | Discriminatory Signs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Soft Tissue Contusion (軟組織挫傷)** | Pain, localized swelling | Normal occlusion, -ve bony steps, normal eye movements |
| **Isolated Nasal Bone # (鼻骨骨折)** | Epistaxis, nasal deformity | -ve malocclusion, normal vision, septal hematoma possible |
| **Mandibular # (下頜骨骨折)** | Jaw pain, lower lip numbness | Sublingual hematoma, malocclusion, bony step at lower border |
| **ZMC # (顴骨上頜骨複合體骨折)** | Cheek numbness, flattened cheek | V2 anesthesia, limited mouth opening, palpable infraorbital step |
| **Orbital Floor # (眼眶底骨折)** | Diplopia, sunken eye | Restricted upgaze, +ve forced duction test, intact maxilla |
| **Le Fort # (勒福氏骨折)** | Mobile midface | Grasping upper incisors moves palate (I), nose (II), or whole face (III) |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Primary Survey (ATLS Protocol)
* **Airway (氣道)**: Assess patency. Clear blood/teeth/secretions. Mandible # → tongue fallback (requires jaw thrust / oropharyngeal airway / intubation)
* **C-Spine**: High-energy facial trauma → 10% risk of concurrent C-spine injury. Apply rigid collar.
* **Circulation**: Profuse bleeding from maxillary artery branches. Control via direct pressure or Foley catheter balloon tamponade in nasal cavity.

### Gold Standard Imaging for Facial Fractures
* **CT Facial Bones (面部骨骼電腦掃描)**
* **Views**: Axial, Coronal, Sagittal + 3D Reconstruction
* **Findings**: Precise fracture lines, displacement, orbital volume, soft tissue entrapment
* **Rule**: Replaces plain radiographs (e.g., Water's view) in all HA trauma centers

### Targeted Imaging for Mandibular Fractures
* **Orthopantomogram (OPG) (全景牙科X光)**
* **Benefit**: Excellent overview of entire mandible, complete dentition, and tooth roots in relation to fracture lines

### Pre-operative Ophthalmic Assessment
* **Visual Acuity (視力)**: Exclude traumatic optic neuropathy/globe rupture (immediate priority)
* **Pupils**: Assess for Relative Afferent Pupillary Defect (RAPD)
* **Ocular Motility**: Document degrees of restriction in all gazes (Hess chart)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Indications for Conservative Management
* Undisplaced fractures (無移位骨折)
* Normal pre-morbid occlusion perfectly intact
* No functional deficits (normal vision, normal mouth opening)
* Patient medically unfit for general anesthesia

### Medical Management & Ward Protocols
* **Analgesia**: Paracetamol + NSAIDs (if no major active bleeding risk)
* **Antibiotics**: IV Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin) for compound # (e.g., involving tooth-bearing areas or sinuses)
* **Tetanus Prophylaxis**: Indicated for all open wounds/mucosal lacerations
* **Steroids**: IV Dexamethasone (↓ edema, especially in optic nerve compression, requires Ophthalmology consult)

### Surgical Indications for Open Reduction & Internal Fixation (ORIF)
* **Functional Impairment**: Malocclusion, severe trismus, symptomatic diplopia with +ve muscle entrapment
* **Aesthetic Deformity**: Significant facial asymmetry, enophthalmos >2mm
* **Displacement**: Widely separated fracture segments, unstable/mobile midface

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Timing of Surgical Intervention
* **Rule**: Avoid immediate surgery (unless compromised airway or impending optic nerve ischemia)
* **Optimal Timing**: Delay 5-7 days to allow soft tissue edema to subside (↑ accuracy of bony reduction), but fix before 14 days (before early bone consolidation begins)

### Operative Principles for Mandible Fractures
* **Maxillomandibular Fixation (MMF) (頜間固定)**: Wire upper & lower teeth together via arch bars/Erich bars to perfectly re-establish pre-morbid occlusion *before* internal fixation
* **ORIF (切開復位內固定術)**: Transoral approach (avoids facial scars). Fixation with Titanium miniplates (鈦合金骨板) & screws along ideal lines of osteosynthesis (Champy's lines)

### Operative Principles for Midface & ZMC Fractures
* **Exposure**: Gillies temporal approach (zygomatic arch elevation), Subciliary/Transconjunctival incision (orbital floor), Upper buccal sulcus incision (maxilla)
* **Sequence**: Re-establish outer facial frame first (Zygomatic arch/frontal bone) → then reconstruct inner buttresses
* **Orbital Floor Reconstruction**: Release entrapped inferior rectus muscle → restore floor with Titanium mesh (鈦網) or PDS plate (可吸收骨板)

### Informed Consent Issues for Facial Trauma Surgery
* **Nerve Injury**: Damage to Facial nerve (CN VII, → facial palsy) or Trigeminal nerve branches (V2/V3, → permanent localized numbness)
* **Functional Failure**: Persistent malocclusion requiring secondary orthodontics, persistent diplopia
* **Hardware Issues**: Plate exposure through mucosa, infection requiring implant removal
* **Cosmetic**: Unfavorable scarring (ectropion from subciliary incision), residual asymmetry

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Dietary & Nutritional Rehabilitation
* **Initial Diet**: Pureed/Liquid diet (流質飲食) for 4-6 weeks (minimizes masticatory stress on healing facial bones)
* **Nutrition Review**: Dietitian referral (ensure adequate caloric/protein intake to prevent rapid weight loss during MMF)

### Oral Hygiene Protocols
* **Mouthwash**: 0.2% Chlorhexidine (洗必泰) mouthwash QDS (prevents hardware infection, crucial post-transoral approach)
* **Brushing**: Soft-bristled baby toothbrush (avoiding direct contact with surgical incisions)
* **Dental Care**: Waterpik (low pressure setting) to clear food debris from MMF arch bars

### Specific Post-operative Instructions
* **Activity Restrictions**: Strictly avoid nose blowing (擤鼻涕) for 4 weeks in midface/orbital # (prevents surgical emphysema/orbital compartment syndrome)
* **Positioning**: Sleep with head elevated 30° (↓ post-operative facial edema)
* **Eye Care**: Lubricating eye drops/ointment if lagophthalmos (inability to close eye completely post-op) is present

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Surgical Complications (<1 week)
* **Airway Compromise**: Secondary to massive post-op edema or expanding hematoma
* **Bleeding/Hematoma**: Retrobulbar hematoma (球後血腫) → surgical emergency (requires immediate lateral canthotomy to save vision)
* **Infection**: Wound infection or sinusitis (especially if hardware placed in contaminated sinus spaces)

### Intermediate Surgical Complications (1-4 weeks)
* **Hardware Exposure**: Plate/screws eroding through oral mucosa (requires removal if infected)
* **Wound Dehiscence**: Breakdown of intra-oral or delicate facial incisions
* **Ocular**: Ectropion (外翻) (eyelid pulls away from globe) following lower eyelid approaches

### Late Surgical Complications & Prognosis (>1 month)
* **Bone Healing Issues**: Malunion (錯位癒合) → facial asymmetry, Non-union (不癒合)
* **Functional Issues**: Persistent malocclusion (TMJ dysfunction), permanent diplopia
* **Neurological**: Permanent anesthesia of V2 (cheek) or V3 (lower lip/chin)
* **Prognosis**: Excellent bone union if adequately fixed. Soft tissue re-draping and final cosmetic outcome takes up to 6-12 months.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: How do you clinically test for a Le Fort fracture? Answer: Grasp the anterior maxillary alveolus and rock it gently while palpating the nasal bridge and zygomaticofrontal sutures to assess the level of midface mobility.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with isolated facial trauma only has facial injuries. Pearl: Always treat high-energy facial trauma as polytrauma. Exclude C-spine injury (ATLS protocol) before assessing the face.
* Pitfall: Discharging a patient with a "black eye" without assessing ocular motility. Always perform the forced duction test and assess upgaze to rule out inferior rectus entrapment from a blow-out fracture.
* Viva Question: Why must a sublingual hematoma raise high suspicion for a specific facial fracture? Answer: It is highly pathognomonic for a mandibular fracture (especially body or symphysis) due to tearing of the lingual mucoperiosteum.
* Trap: Fixing a mandibular fracture before re-establishing the patient's bite. Pearl: Always apply Maxillomandibular Fixation (MMF) first to lock the teeth into pre-morbid occlusion, then plate the fracture.
* Pitfall: Allowing a patient with an orbital wall or maxillary fracture to blow their nose. This forces air from the paranasal sinuses into the orbit, causing orbital emphysema and potential optic nerve compression.
