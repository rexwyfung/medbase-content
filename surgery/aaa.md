# Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (腹主動脈瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm
* **Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (腹主動脈瘤)**: Permanent localized dilation of the abdominal aorta **>3.0 cm** (or >50% normal vessel diameter)
* **>90%** of AAAs arise **infrarenally (腎下)**
* **True Aneurysm (真性動脈瘤)**: Involves all 3 layers of vessel wall (intima, media, adventitia)

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology & Risk Factors
* **Demographics**: ↑ incidence in **males >65 years**
* **Major Risk Factors**: **Smoking (吸煙)** (strongest predictor for development/rupture), ↑ age, male gender, hypertension, hypercholesterolemia, family history, Caucasian ethnicity
* **Negative Association**: Diabetes Mellitus (DM) paradoxically ↓ progression rate
* **HK HA Context**: Opportunistic screening; no universal population screening programme in HK (unlike UK NHS)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Infrarenal Aorta
* **Anatomical Borders**: From **renal arteries (腎動脈)** superiorly to **aortic bifurcation (主動脈分叉)** (L4 vertebra) inferiorly
* **Crucial EVAR Landmarks**: Proximal aortic neck (length, angulation, diameter) and iliac artery tortuosity/calcification
* **Important Neighbouring Structures**: Duodenum (3rd part crosses anteriorly → risk of **aortoenteric fistula (主動脈腸瘻)**), Left renal vein (crosses anteriorly), Inferior Vena Cava (IVC) (lies on the right → risk of aortocaval fistula)

### Pathophysiology of Aneurysmal Dilation
* **Vessel Wall Degradation**: Driven by ↑ Matrix Metalloproteinases (**MMP**) activity → Destruction of **elastin** & **collagen** in tunica media
* **Atherosclerotic Inflammatory Infiltrate**: Macrophages & lymphocytes infiltrate adventitia/media → ↑ inflammatory cytokines
* **Laplace’s Law (拉普拉斯定律)**: Wall Tension = (Pressure × Radius) / Wall Thickness. As radius ↑, wall tension ↑ exponentially → ↑ risk of rupture

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Asymptomatic AAA
* **Most Common Presentation**: **Incidental finding (偶然發現)** on US/CT abdomen for unrelated issues
* **Physical Exam**: Palpable, non-tender, **expansile pulsatile abdominal mass (擴張性搏動性腹部腫塊)** (above umbilicus)

### Clinical Presentation of Symptomatic Unruptured AAA
* **Pain**: Deep, continuous abdominal pain, back pain, or flank pain (indicates rapid expansion or impending rupture)
* **Microembolization**: "Trash foot" (blue toe syndrome) due to atheroemboli to lower limbs

### Clinical Presentation of Acute Ruptured AAA
* **Classic Triad (經典三聯症)** (Seen in <50%):
	* **1. Sudden severe abdominal/back/flank pain (突發劇烈腹痛/背痛)**
	* **2. Hypotension / Hypovolaemic Shock (低血容量性休克)** (systolic BP <90 mmHg, tachycardia)
	* **3. Pulsatile abdominal mass (搏動性腹部腫塊)**
- **Grey Turner’s Sign (格雷特納徵)** / **Cullen’s Sign**: Flank/periumbilical ecchymosis (late sign of retroperitoneal hemorrhage)

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis for Acute Ruptured AAA
| Condition | Discriminatory Features from Ruptured AAA |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Renal Colic (腎絞痛)** | Colicky pain, writhing patient, +ve hematuria (Note: AAA can also cause hematuria if compressing ureter) |
| **Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰腺炎)** | Epigastric pain radiating to back, ↑ Amylase/Lipase, Hx of gallstones/alcohol |
| **Ischemic Bowel (缺血性腸病)** | Pain out of proportion to exam, bloody diarrhea, AF risk, lactate ↑↑ |
| **Perforated Peptic Ulcer (消化性潰瘍穿孔)** | Sudden onset, rigid board-like abdomen, free gas under diaphragm on CXR |
| **Diverticulitis (憩室炎)** | LIF pain, fever, ↑ WCC, localized peritonism |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line & Screening Imaging
* **Ultrasound Abdomen (腹部超聲波)**:
	* **Gold Standard** for screening & surveillance.
	* Highly sensitive/specific, non-invasive, zero radiation
	* Evaluates maximum AP diameter (outer-to-outer wall)

### Definitive Imaging & Pre-operative Planning
* **CT Aortogram (CTA 主動脈電腦掃描造影)**: Mandatory for operative planning
	* Determines exact size, extent, relationship to renal/iliac arteries (EVAR suitability)
	* **Signs of Impending Rupture on CT**: "Crescent sign" (high-attenuation acute hematoma within thrombus), aortic blebs, focal wall discontinuity
![image|287](https://pub-f0606dedf6d94e4bb5b1999cbd065914.r2.dev/paediatrics/ad6d89ac80176804234401dee41dcb46.png)

### Pre-operative Baseline & Fitness Workup
* **Bloods**: CBC (baseline Hb), RFT (pre-contrast check), LFT, Coagulation profile, **Group & Screen / Crossmatch (配血) 6 units**
* **Cardiac Assessment**: ECG, Echocardiogram (Assess EF, ischemia risk - major cause of peri-op mortality)
* **Respiratory Assessment**: CXR, PFTs / Spirometry (fitness for GA/open surgery)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative & Medical Management
* **Indications**: Asymptomatic AAA not meeting surgical threshold
* **Surveillance Protocol**:
	* 3.0-4.4 cm: Annual US
	* 4.5-5.4 cm: 3-6 monthly US
* **Risk Modification**:
	* Strict **Smoking Cessation (戒煙)**
	* BP control
	* Statin therapy
	* Antiplatelet (Aspirin)

### Surgical Indications for Elective AAA Repair
* **Size Criteria**: **>5.5 cm (Males)** or **>5.0 cm (Females)**
* **Growth Rate**: Rapid expansion **>0.5 cm in 6 months** or >1.0 cm in 1 year
* **Symptoms**: Any symptomatic AAA (pain, tenderness) regardless of size
* **Complications**: Distal embolization, infection

### Emergency Protocol for Ruptured AAA
* **Permissive Hypotension (容許性低血壓)**: Target systolic BP 70-90 mmHg (maintain consciousness/urine output, prevent blowing the clot)
* **Massive Transfusion Protocol (大量輸血方案)**: 1:1:1 ratio (RBC:FFP:Platelets)
* **Immediate Transfer**: Straight to operating theatre (OT); IF unstable + known AAA → NO delay for CT; IF stable/unknown → Urgent CTA

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Endovascular Aneurysm Repair (EVAR 腔內主動脈瘤修復術)
* **Principle**: Transfemoral insertion of stent-graft to exclude aneurysm sac from systemic pressure
* **Anatomical Requirements**: Proximal neck length ≥10-15 mm, neck angulation <60°, adequate iliac artery caliber without excessive tortuosity
* **Pros/Cons**:
	* ↓ perioperative mortality, ↓ LOS, local/regional anesthesia possible.
	* BUT ↑ reintervention rate, requires lifelong surveillance, ↑ risk of endoleaks

### Open Surgical Repair (OSR 傳統開腹主動脈瘤修復術)
* **Principle**: Midline laparotomy, systemic heparinization, aortic cross-clamping (infrarenal/suprarenal), sac opened, insertion of synthetic graft (Dacron/PTFE), sac closed over graft
* **Pros/Cons**:
	* For **Connective Tissue Disease**, **Inflammatory/Mycotic Aneurysm**.
	* Durable (young patients), definitive, no endoleaks.
	* BUT ↑ physiological stress, ↑ perioperative mortality/morbidity, larger scar

### Surgical Consent & Key Risks
* **General**:
	* Bleeding (massive)
	* Infection
	* Death (Elective <5%, Ruptured >50%)
* **Specific**:
	* Cardiac events (MI)
	* Renal failure (due to suprarenal clamping or atheroemboli)
	* Ischemic colitis / Bowel ischemia (ligation of IMA)
	* Lower limb ischemia
	* Spinal cord ischemia (paraplegia due to Artery of Adamkiewicz involvement)
	* **Endoleak (內漏)** (EVAR only)

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-Operative ICU/HDU Care
* **Hemodynamic Monitoring**: Strict BP control (avoid hypertension → graft blowout; avoid hypotension → graft thrombosis/renal ischemia)
* **Perfusion Checks**: Hourly lower limb neurovascular obs (pulses, doppler, cap refill, temperature)
* **End-Organ Monitoring**: Strict I/O, hourly urine output (monitor for AKI), abdominal examination (monitor for bowel ischemia - severe pain, bloody diarrhea, acidosis)

### Long-Term Post-Operative Surveillance
* **EVAR Surveillance**: Lifelong imaging required to detect endoleaks and sac expansion
* **Protocol**: CTA at 1 month and 12 months post-op; if sac stable/shrinking without endoleak, transition to annual Duplex US + non-contrast CT

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (<30 Days)
* **Cardiopulmonary**: MI, Arrhythmias, Pneumonia
* **Renal**: AKI (contrast-induced or ischemia-induced)
* **Gastrointestinal**: **Ischemic Colitis (缺血性結腸炎)** (classically involves left colon/rectum post IMA ligation)
* **Vascular**: Graft thrombosis, distal atheroembolization, hemorrhage

### Late Surgical Complications & Endoleaks (EVAR)
* **Graft Infection**: Rare but devastating. Presents with fever, weight loss, ↑ inflammatory markers. Requires graft excision + extra-anatomic bypass (Axillobifemoral)
* **Aortoenteric Fistula (主動脈腸瘻)**: Late complication. GI bleed (herald bleed → massive hematemesis/melena). Diagnosis via endoscopy/CTA
* **Endoleaks (EVAR only)**: Persistent blood flow outside stent-graft but within aneurysm sac

### Classification of EVAR Endoleaks (內漏)
| Type         | Mechanism                                                       | Severity & Management                                           |
| :----------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Type I**   | Leak at attachment site (1a Proximal, 1b Distal)                | **High pressure**. High rupture risk. Needs urgent repair       |
| **Type II**  | Retrograde flow from collateral branches (IMA, Lumbar arteries) | **Most common**. Often benign. Observe; embolize if sac expands |
| **Type III** | Structural failure (graft disconnection, fabric tear)           | **High pressure**. Needs urgent repair                          |
| **Type IV**  | Graft porosity (blood oozes through graft mesh)                 | Self-limiting. Resolves once coagulation normalizes             |
| **Type V**   | Endotension (expanding sac, NO visible leak on CTA)             | Continual expansion. May require conversion to open repair      |

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is Laplace's Law and its relevance to abdominal aortic aneurysms? Answer: Wall tension = (Pressure × Radius) / Wall thickness. It explains why larger AAAs expand faster and have an exponentially increased risk of rupture.
* Viva Question: What is "permissive hypotension" and why is it used in ruptured AAA? Answer: Maintaining systolic BP at 70-90 mmHg with restricted fluids to preserve consciousness and organ perfusion while preventing the dislodgement of the hemostatic clot prior to surgical control.
* Trap: Assuming normal hemoglobin rules out acute ruptured AAA. Pearl: In hyperacute hemorrhage, hemoglobin levels may initially appear normal because whole blood is lost; the Hb only drops after hemodilution from fluid resuscitation or intracellular fluid shifts.
* Trap: Believing microscopic hematuria definitively rules out ruptured AAA and confirms renal colic. Pearl: A ruptured AAA or rapidly expanding AAA can compress the renal vein or directly irritate the ureter, causing microscopic hematuria that mimics renal colic.
* Pitfall: Delaying operative intervention to obtain a CT scan in a hemodynamically unstable patient with a known AAA. An unstable patient with a known AAA and pulsatile mass must be transferred immediately to the operating theatre for emergent exploration and proximal aortic clamping; imaging delays are lethal.
