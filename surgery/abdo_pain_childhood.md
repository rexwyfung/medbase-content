# Abdominal Pain in Childhood (兒童腹痛)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Paediatric Acute Abdomen
* **Acute Abdomen (急腹症)**: Sudden onset, severe abdominal pain requiring urgent surgical evaluation <24 hrs.
* **Surgical vs Medical**: Crucial to differentiate surgical emergencies (e.g., appendicitis, volvulus) from self-limiting medical conditions (e.g., gastroenteritis, mesenteric adenitis).

### Local Epidemiology of Paediatric Abdominal Pain in Hong Kong
* **Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎)**: Most common pediatric surgical emergency. Peak incidence 10-12 yrs.
* **Intussusception (腸套疊)**: Most common cause of intestinal obstruction in infants. Peak 3-9 months. ↑ incidence in HK post-viral (Adenovirus/Rotavirus) seasons.
* **Mesenteric Adenitis (腸繫膜淋巴結炎)**: Extremely common benign cause mimicking appendicitis, usually post-URI.
* **Incarcerated Inguinal Hernia (嵌頓性腹股溝疝)**: Common cause of obstruction in neonates/infants.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Pathophysiology of Intussusception (腸套疊)
* **Definition**: Invagination of proximal bowel (intussusceptum) into distal bowel (intussuscipiens).
* **Most Common Type**: Ileocolic (90%).
* **Mechanism**: Mesentery dragged into lumen → venous congestion → bowel wall edema → arterial compromise → ischemia/necrosis → mucosal sloughing + blood = **Red currant jelly stool (紅莓果醬樣糞便)**.
* **Lead Points (引導點)**: 
* Idiopathic (90%): Enlarged Peyer's patches (hypertrophy post-viral infection).
* Pathological (10%, usually >2 yrs or neonates): **Meckel's Diverticulum (梅克爾憩室)**, polyps, lymphoma, Henoch-Schönlein Purpura (HSP) haematoma.

### Pathophysiology of Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎) in Children
* **Mechanism**: Luminal obstruction → closed-loop obstruction → bacterial overgrowth → venous engorgement → arterial compromise → gangrene/perforation.
* **Obstruction Causes**: Lymphoid hyperplasia (common in kids), faecolith (糞石), parasites (Enterobius vermicularis).
* **Paediatric Vulnerability**: Omentum underdeveloped (short) in young children → fails to wall off perforation → early generalized **Peritonitis (腹膜炎)**.

### Embryology and Pathophysiology of Meckel's Diverticulum (梅克爾憩室)
* **Embryology**: Failure of vitelline duct (omphalomesenteric duct) obliteration.
* **Rule of 2s**: 2% population, 2 feet from ileocaecal valve, 2 inches long, 2 types of ectopic tissue (gastric, pancreatic), presents <2 yrs.
* **Pathophysiology**: Ectopic gastric mucosa secretes acid → ulceration of adjacent normal ileum → painless lower GI bleeding / perforation. Can act as lead point for intussusception.

### Pathophysiology of Malrotation with Volvulus (腸旋轉不良伴扭轉)
* **Embryology**: Failure of normal 270° counter-clockwise midgut rotation around Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA).
* **Anatomy**: Narrow mesenteric base + Ladd's bands (crossing duodenum).
* **Pathophysiology**: Narrow base twists (**Volvulus (腸扭轉)**) → SMA occlusion → catastrophic midgut ischemia → gangrene.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Key Symptoms of Paediatric Surgical Abdomen
* **Bilious Vomiting (膽汁性嘔吐)**: Green vomit. SURGICAL EMERGENCY (Volvulus) until proven otherwise.
* **Colicky Pain (絞痛)**: Intermittent severe pain + drawing up legs (Intussusception).
* **Migration of Pain**: Periumbilical → RIF (Appendicitis).

### Pathognomonic Signs of Intussusception
* **Sausage-shaped mass (香腸狀腫塊)**: Palpable in RUQ/epigastrium.
* **Dance's Sign (丹斯徵)**: Empty RIF on palpation (caecum drawn superiorly).
* **PR Bleeding**: **Red currant jelly stool (紅莓果醬樣糞便)** (late sign of ischemia).

### Clinical Signs of Paediatric Appendicitis
* **McBurney's Point Tenderness (麥氏點壓痛)**: Junction of lateral 1/3 and medial 2/3 of line from ASIS to umbilicus.
* **Rovsing's Sign (羅夫辛徵)**: LIF palpation causes RIF pain.
* **Psoas Sign**: RIF pain on passive right hip extension.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Differential Diagnosis by Age Group Table
| Age Group | Top Surgical Causes | Top Medical Causes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Neonates (<1 mo)** | Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC), Hirschsprung's, Malrotation/Volvulus, Incarcerated Hernia | Sepsis, Gastroenteritis, Metabolic errors |
| **Infants (1 mo - 2 yrs)** | **Intussusception (腸套疊)**, Incarcerated Hernia, Meckel's Diverticulum | Gastroenteritis, Colic, Otitis Media, UTI |
| **Preschool (2-5 yrs)** | Appendicitis, Intussusception (think lead point) | Gastroenteritis, **Mesenteric Adenitis (腸繫膜淋巴結炎)**, Constipation, HSP |
| **School Age (>5 yrs)** | **Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎)**, Testicular/Ovarian Torsion | Constipation, UTI, Gastroenteritis, DKA |

### Medical vs Surgical Causes of Paediatric Abdominal Pain
* **Mesenteric Adenitis**: High fever + generalized lymphadenopathy + URI symptoms + milder RIF tenderness (diffuse).
* **Henoch-Schönlein Purpura (HSP) (過敏性紫癜)**: Purpuric rash on buttocks/legs + joint pain + colicky abdominal pain.
* **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) (糖尿病酮症酸中毒)**: Kussmaul breathing + fruity breath + polyuria + severe pseudo-surgical abdominal pain.

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### First-Line Laboratory Investigations for Paediatric Abdomen
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: R/O UTI. (Note: mildly ↑ WBCs/RBCs can occur in appendicitis due to ureteric irritation).
* **CBC**: ↑ WBC (neutrophilia) in appendicitis.
* **CRP**: ↑ in late/perforated appendicitis. Normal CRP does NOT rule out early appendicitis.
* **VBG**: Check lactate for bowel ischemia (e.g., volvulus, intussusception). R/O DKA.

### Imaging Modalities for Paediatric Abdominal Pain (ALARA Principle)
* **Ultrasound Abdomen (腹部超聲波)**:
* **1st Line HA Protocol**. No radiation.
* **Intussusception**: Target sign (靶環徵) / Doughnut sign (transverse), Pseudokidney sign (longitudinal).
* **Appendicitis**: Non-compressible appendix >6mm, +ve fluid collection, appendicolith.
* **Abdominal X-Ray (AXR)**: Only if suspect obstruction/perforation. May show double bubble (duodenal atresia), absent distal gas (volvulus), or free air.
* **Upper GI Contrast Study (UGI)**: Gold standard for suspected **Malrotation/Volvulus** (shows corkscrew appearance of duodenum).
* **CT Abdomen/Pelvis**: RARELY used in children in HK due to radiation risk (ALARA). Reserved for complex abscesses or atypical presentations.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Management Algorithm for Suspected Intussusception
* **Resuscitation**: NPO, IVF, NGT decompression if distended.
* **1st Line (Non-Operative)**: **Pneumatic Reduction (氣體灌腸復位)** under fluoroscopy/USS guidance.
* Contraindications for pneumatic reduction: Shock, peritonitis, free air (perforation).
* **2nd Line (Surgical)**: Laparotomy/Laparoscopy reduction if pneumatic fails or contraindicated.

### Management Algorithm for Paediatric Appendicitis in HA Hospitals
* **Early Appendicitis**: NPO, IVF, IV Antibiotics (e.g., Cefuroxime + Metronidazole) → urgent Appendicectomy.
* **Appendiceal Mass/Phlegmon (闌尾包塊)** (presented >3-5 days):
* **Ochsner-Sherren Regimen**: Conservative management. NPO, NGT, IV Abx, strict vital signs + abdominal girth monitoring.
* Interval appendicectomy 6-8 weeks later (controversial, but still practiced in some HA clusters).
* If fails (↑ fever, ↑ pain, expanding mass) → Urgent surgery.

### Emergency Management for Malrotation with Volvulus
* IMMEDIATE fluid resuscitation.
* **Ladd's Procedure (拉德手術)**: SURGICAL EMERGENCY. Do not delay for imaging if patient is unstable with bilious vomiting.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Operative Principles for Intussusception Reduction
* **Incision**: Right transverse supra-umbilical incision.
* **Technique**: Gentle retrograde milking of intussuscipiens. NEVER pull the intussusceptum (risk of iatrogenic tearing).
* **Resection Indication**: If bowel irreducible or gangrenous → segmental resection + primary anastomosis.
* **Appendicectomy**: Often performed routinely to avoid future diagnostic confusion, even if appendix normal.

### Operative Principles for Paediatric Laparoscopic Appendicectomy
* **Ports**: 3-port technique (umbilicus, LIF, suprapubic).
* **Technique**: Identify base of appendix at caecum (convergence of taeniae coli) → skeletonize appendiceal artery in mesoappendix → clip/diathermy → ligate base (endoloop) → retrieve via endobag (prevent port site infection).
* **Peritoneal Lavage**: Thorough irrigation if perforated, aspirate all pus.

### Operative Principles for Ladd's Procedure
* 1. Untwist bowel (counter-clockwise).
* 2. Divide Ladd's bands to relieve duodenal obstruction.
* 3. Widen mesenteric base to prevent re-volvulus.
* 4. Appendicectomy (prophylactic, as caecum will be placed in LUQ).
* 5. Place small bowel on right, large bowel on left.

### Paediatric Surgical Consent Issues
* **Consent Provider**: Parents or legal guardian.
* **Stoma Consent**: Must ALWAYS consent for possible temporary stoma in gangrenous intussusception/volvulus.
* **Unexpected Findings**: Consent for normal appendix removal if acting as lead point or to prevent future confusion.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Post-operative Care for Paediatric Abdominal Surgery
* **Diet**: Sips of water once fully awake/extubated → upgrade to solid diet as tolerated (early feeding encouraged in ERAS).
* **Analgesia**: Multimodal. IV Paracetamol + NSAIDs. Avoid strong opioids if possible to prevent post-op ileus.
* **Antibiotics**: Stop within 24 hrs for simple appendicitis. Continue 5-7 days for perforated appendicitis/gangrenous bowel.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Complications of Paediatric Abdominal Surgery
* **Post-operative Ileus (術後腸梗阻)**: Delayed return of bowel function.
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**: High risk in perforated appendicitis.
* **Intra-abdominal Abscess (腹腔膿腫)**: Recurrent fever/pain days after perforated appendicitis surgery (requires USS/CT guided drainage).

### Late Complications of Paediatric Abdominal Surgery
* **Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (粘連性小腸梗阻)**: Lifetime risk after any laparotomy/laparoscopy.
* **Recurrent Intussusception**: ~10% recurrence post-pneumatic reduction; ~1% post-surgical reduction.
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)**: Severe complication post-massive bowel resection for midgut volvulus → requires long-term TPN.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most common cause of bilious vomiting in a neonate until proven otherwise? Answer: Malrotation with midgut volvulus.
* Viva Question: What is the classic ultrasound finding of intussusception? Answer: Target sign or Doughnut sign.
* Trap: Assuming bilious vomiting in an infant is just gastroenteritis. Pearl: Bilious vomiting in pediatric patients is a surgical emergency indicating obstruction distal to the ampulla of Vater; mandate urgent surgical consult and UGI contrast study to rule out volvulus.
* Trap: Ruling out appendicitis because the child's white blood cell count is normal. Pearl: Up to 10-20% of early appendicitis cases can present with a normal WBC and CRP; clinical diagnosis remains paramount.
* Pitfall: Pulling the intussusceptum during manual reduction of intussusception. Always use gentle retrograde milking to push the bowel out; pulling causes high risk of iatrogenic perforation.
* Pitfall: Ordering a CT scan as a first-line test for pediatric abdominal pain. Always use the ALARA principle (As Low As Reasonably Achievable); Ultrasound is the strictly preferred first-line imaging in Hong Kong to avoid radiation risk.
