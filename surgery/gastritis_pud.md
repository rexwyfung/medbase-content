# Gastritis & Peptic Ulcer Disease (胃炎與消化性潰瘍)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Gastritis and Peptic Ulcer Disease
* **Gastritis (胃炎):** Histological infammation of gastric mucosa (acute/chronic)
* **Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD) (消化性潰瘍):** Mucosal defect extending through muscularis mucosae into submucosa/deeper, occurring in stomach or duodenum

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **H. pylori Prevalence:** ~40-50% in adult HK population
* **Incidence Trends:** ↓ Elective ulcer surgeries due to effective Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI) (質子泵抑製劑) & *H. pylori* eradication
* **Emergency Presentations:** ↑ Bleeding/perforation rates in elderly population secondary to polypharmacy (NSAIDs/Aspirin/DOACs)
* **Gender Ratio:** DU more common in males; GU equal gender distribution

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Gastroduodenal Blood Supply
* **Anterior Duodenal Wall:** Thin, prone to perforation → peritonitis
* **Posterior Duodenal Wall:** Adjacent to **Gastroduodenal Artery (GDA) (胃十二指腸動脈)** → prone to massive haemorrhage
* **Lesser Curvature of Stomach:** Supplied by **Left Gastric Artery (LGA) (胃左動脈)** → site of Type I GU & major bleeding
* **Innervation:** Vagus nerve (迷走神經) (stimulates parietal cell acid secretion)

### Pathophysiology of Mucosal Breakdown
* **Mechanism:** Imbalance between mucosal defenses (mucus, bicarbonate, prostaglandins, blood flow) & aggressive factors (acid, pepsin)
* ***H. pylori* Pathogenesis:** Produces urease (neutralizes acid locally) + cytotoxins (CagA/VacA) → chronic mucosal inflammation
* **NSAID Pathogenesis:** Inhibits COX-1 → ↓ mucosal prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) synthesis → ↓ mucus/bicarbonate secretion & ↓ mucosal blood flow

### Modified Johnson Classification of Gastric Ulcers
| Type | Location | Acid Secretion | Pathogenesis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type I** | Lesser curvature (incisura) | Normal/Low | Mucosal defense defect |
| **Type II** | Body of stomach + Active/Healed DU | High | Acid hypersecretion |
| **Type III** | Prepyloric | High | Acid hypersecretion |
| **Type IV** | High on lesser curve (near GEJ) | Normal/Low | Mucosal defense defect |
| **Type V** | Anywhere (often multiple) | Normal/Low | NSAID-induced |

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Uncomplicated PUD
* **Duodenal Ulcer (DU):** Epigastric pain relieved by food/antacids, wakes patient at night
* **Gastric Ulcer (GU):** Epigastric pain exacerbated by food, associated with weight loss/anorexia
* **Dyspepsia (消化不良):** Nausea, bloating, early satiety

### Clinical Signs of Bleeding Peptic Ulcer
* **Haematemesis (吐血):** Fresh red blood or 'coffee-ground' emesis
* **Melaena (黑糞):** Tarry, foul-smelling black stool
* **Haemodynamic Instability:** Tachycardia, hypotension, pallor, prolonged capillary refill

### Clinical Signs of Perforated Peptic Ulcer (穿孔性消化性潰瘍)
* **Sudden Onset:** Agonizing, sudden sharp epigastric pain spreading globally
* **Peritonism:** Board-like abdominal rigidity, guarding, rebound tenderness
* **Systemic Signs:** Shallow rapid breathing, tachycardia, absent bowel sounds

### Clinical Signs of Gastric Outlet Obstruction (GOO)
* **Vomiting:** Projectile, non-bilious, contains undigested food
* **Physical Signs:** Visible gastric peristalsis, **Succussion splash (振水音)**
* **Metabolic Disturbance:** Hypochloremic hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis + paradoxical aciduria

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Discriminatory Table for Epigastric Pain
| Condition                            | Key Discriminatory Features                                                |
| :----------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Peptic Ulcer Disease**             | Pain related to meals, responds to antacids/PPI, history of NSAID use      |
| **Gastro-oesophageal Reflux (GERD)** | Retrosternal burning, worse supine, acid brash                             |
| **Gastric Carcinoma (胃癌)**           | Progressive weight loss, palpable epigastric mass, Virchow's node, anaemia |
| **Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)**       | Pain radiating to back, severe continuous pain, ↑↑ Amylase/Lipase          |
| **Biliary Colic / Cholecystitis**    | RUQ pain, Murphy's sign +ve, post-prandial (fatty foods), abnormal LFTs    |
| **Acute Coronary Syndrome**          | Chest pain radiating to jaw/arm, diaphoresis, ECG changes, ↑ Troponin      |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### First-Line Laboratory Workup
* **Complete Blood Picture (CBP):** ↓ Hb (bleeding), ↑ WCC (perforation/infection)
* **Renal Function Test (RFT):** ↑ Urea out of proportion to creatinine (upper GI bleed)
* **Liver Function Test (LFT) & Amylase:** Rule out biliary/pancreatic pathology
* **Coagulation Profile & Type/Screen:** Essential for bleeding/pre-op prep
* **Venous Blood Gas (VBG):** Assess lactate (ischemia/shock) & pH

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Investigation
* **Oesophagogastroduodenoscopy (OGD) (胃鏡):** Direct visualization of ulcer, mandatory for GU to rule out malignancy (multiple edge biopsies)
* **Campylobacter-Like Organism (CLO) Test:** Rapid urease test on biopsy for *H. pylori*

### Diagnostic Imaging for PUD Complications
* **Erect Chest X-Ray (CXR):** +ve for subdiaphragmatic **Pneumoperitoneum (氣腹)** in 70% of perforations
* **CT Abdomen with IV Contrast:** High sensitivity for perforation (free air/fluid), excludes alternative acute abdomen causes

### Prognostic Scoring Systems for PUD Complications
| Score                  | Application                          | Key Variables Included                                                                |
| :--------------------- | :----------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Boey Score**         | Predicts mortality in perforated PUD | 1. Major medical illness 2. Pre-op shock (BP<90) 3. Duration >24h               |
| **Glasgow-Blatchford** | Pre-endoscopy risk for bleeding      | Urea, Hb, Sys BP, HR, Melaena, Syncope, Hepatic/Cardiac failure                       |
| **Rockall Score**      | Post-endoscopy risk for bleeding     | Age, Shock, Comorbidities, Endoscopic diagnosis, SRH (Stigmata of Recent Haemorrhage) |

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Acute Resuscitation Protocol (HK HA Guidelines)
* **A/B/C:** Secure airway, high-flow O2
* **IV Access:** 2x large bore IV cannulae (16G or 18G)
* **Fluid Resuscitation:** Crystalloids (Normal Saline/Plasmalyte)
* **Medications:** High-dose IV Omeprazole (80mg bolus → 8mg/hr infusion for bleeding), Broad-spectrum IV Antibiotics (for perforation)
* **Catheterization:** Foley catheter for strict urine output monitoring
* **Transfusion:** Target Hb >7 g/dL (or >9 g/dL in ischemic heart disease)

### Medical Management & H. pylori Eradication
* **HA First-Line Bismuth Quadruple Therapy (14 days):**
	* PPI
	* Bismuth Subcitrate
	* Tetracycline
	* Metronidazole
	* → replacing triple therapy due to macrolide resistance
* **NSAID cessation:** Mandatory; if impossible, transition to COX-2 inhibitor + continuous PPI

### Endoscopic Management Algorithm for Bleeding PUD
* **Timing:** Urgent OGD within 24 hours of presentation
* **Dual Haemostasis Therapy:** Injection (Adrenaline 1:10000) + Mechanical (Endoclips) OR Thermal (Heater probe/Argon plasma coagulation)
* **Forrest Classification:** Guides intervention (Ia: spurting, Ib: oozing, IIa: visible vessel, IIb: adherent clot require dual therapy)

### Forrest Classification of Peptic Ulcer Bleeding
| Forrest Grade | Endoscopic Findings                                      | Risk of Rebleeding | Management                                   |
| :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------- | :------------------------------------------- |
| **Ia**        | Arterial **spurting** hemorrhage                         | High               | Endoscopic therapy                           |
| **Ib**        | **Oozing** hemorrhage                                    | High               | Endoscopic therapy                           |
| **IIa**       | Non-bleeding **visible** **vessel**                      | High               | Endoscopic therapy                           |
| **IIb**       | **Adherent** **clot**                                    | Intermediate       | Clot removal & treat underlying stigmata     |
| **IIc**       | Flat pigmented haematin / **"coffee-ground" spot**       | Low                | Medical management                           |
| **III**       | **Clean-based ulcer** (no stigmata of recent hemorrhage) | Lowest             | Medical management, safe for early discharge |

### Absolute Surgical Indications
* **Perforation:** Generalized peritonitis
* **Refractory Bleeding:** Haemodynamic instability despite active resuscitation, failure of 2x therapeutic OGD attempts
* **Gastric Outlet Obstruction:** Refractory to conservative management (NPO, NGT decompression, PPI)
* **Malignancy:** GU biopsy returning +ve for carcinoma

### Surgical Management
* **Perforated DU**: Laparoscopic Omental Patch Repair (Graham Patch) (大網膜覆蓋術)
* **Bleeding PUD**: Laparotomy + Under-running of bleeding vessel (縫紮術)
* **Refractory GOO**:
	* Truncal Vagotomy + Gastrojejunostomy (Bypass)
	* Alternative: Endoscopic balloon dilatation (requires multiple sessions)

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles for Perforated DU
* **Procedure:** Laparoscopic Omental Patch Repair (Graham Patch) (大網膜覆蓋術)
* **Steps:**
  * Diagnostic laparoscopy + massive peritoneal lavage (warm saline)
  * Identify perforation (usually anterior DU)
  * Mobilize pedicled omentum
  * Place 3 interrupted absorbable sutures (e.g., Vicryl) across defect
  * Tie sutures over omental plug
* **Note:** Routine definitive anti-ulcer surgery (e.g., Vagotomy) is obsolete due to PPIs

### Operative Principles for Bleeding PUD
* **Procedure:** Laparotomy + Under-running of bleeding vessel (縫紮術)
* **Posterior DU Bleed Steps:** Longitudinal duodenotomy → 3-point U-stitch (figure-of-8) using non-absorbable suture (e.g., Silk) to ligate GDA proximally, distally, and transverse pancreatic branch → Transverse closure (pyloroplasty) to prevent stricture
* **Bleeding GU Steps:** Gastrotomy → Excision/Biopsy of ulcer (must rule out cancer) → primary closure

### Informed Consent & Operative Risks
* **General:** Bleeding, infection, damage to adjacent organs (biliary tree, pancreas)
* **Specific:** Leak from patch repair/duodenotomy, re-bleeding, conversion to open laparotomy, post-op ileus, mortality (high in elderly/delayed presentation)

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care Pathway
* **Fasting Status:** NPO (Nil per os) initially
* **Tubes:** Nasogastric tube (NGT) strictly on free drainage (protects repair), Foley catheter, surgical drains
* **Medications:** IV PPI, IV broad-spectrum antibiotics (e.g., Cefuroxime + Metronidazole) for 3-5 days
* **DVT Prophylaxis:** TEDS, intermittent pneumatic compression, LMWH once bleeding risk mitigated

### Diet Advancement & ERAS Protocols
* **Water Soluble Contrast Study (Gastrografin):** Typically performed on post-op Day 3-5 to confirm no leak before feeding
* **Feeding Progression:** Clear fluids → Free fluids → Soft diet → Normal diet
* **Mobilization:** Early ambulation to prevent chest infections and DVT

### Long-Term Follow-up Requirements
* ***H. pylori* Testing:** Urea Breath Test (UBT) 4-6 weeks post-eradication to confirm cure
* **Repeat Endoscopy:** Mandatory OGD in 6-8 weeks for all Gastric Ulcers to document complete healing and repeat biopsy to definitively exclude underlying Gastric Cancer

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Post-operative Complications (0-7 days)
* **Repair Leak:** Presents as worsening peritonitis, systemic sepsis, purulent drain output
* **Re-bleeding:** Drop in Hb, bloody NGT output, melaena
* **Intra-abdominal Collection:** Pelvic or subphrenic abscess → swinging pyrexia, paralytic ileus

### Intermediate Post-operative Complications (1-4 weeks)
* **Respiratory:** Basal atelectasis, hospital-acquired pneumonia (poor pain control limits coughing)
* **Wound:** Surgical site infection (SSI), wound dehiscence
* **Vascular:** Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT), Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

### Late Surgical Complications (Months to Years)
* **Mechanical:** Incisional hernia, adhesions causing small bowel obstruction
* **Recurrence:** Recurrent ulceration if *H. pylori* not eradicated or NSAIDs continued
* **Post-Vagotomy Syndromes (Rare now):** Dumping syndrome, post-vagotomy diarrhoea, biliary gastritis

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic triad of Boey's score predicting mortality in perforated peptic ulcer? Answer: Major medical comorbidity, pre-operative shock, and duration of perforation greater than 24 hours.
* Viva Question: Why do we perform a biopsy on all gastric ulcers but rarely on duodenal ulcers? Answer: Gastric ulcers carry a significant risk of harboring underlying malignancy (gastric carcinoma), whereas duodenal ulcers are almost invariably benign.
* Trap: Assuming a patient with a perforated ulcer does not need H. pylori eradication therapy post-operatively. Pearl: All patients with perforated PUD must undergo H. pylori testing and eradication therapy, as it drastically reduces the lifetime risk of ulcer recurrence.
* Trap: Discharging a patient post-gastric ulcer repair without arranging further endoscopic follow-up. Pearl: A follow-up OGD at 6-8 weeks is mandatory to document ulcer healing and take further biopsies to definitively rule out occult gastric malignancy.
* Pitfall: Removing an NGT too early after an omental patch repair. The stomach must remain decompressed to prevent tension on the delicate duodenal/gastric repair, risking a post-operative leak.
* Pitfall: Attempting to biopsy a bleeding posterior duodenal ulcer during emergency laparotomy. This ulcer base lies on the pancreas and GDA; aggressive manipulation can provoke massive, uncontrollable haemorrhage.
