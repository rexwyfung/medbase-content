# Renal Transplantation (腎臟移植)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Renal Transplantation
* **Renal Transplantation (腎臟移植)**: The surgical placement of a healthy functioning human kidney (allograft) into a patient with **End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) (末期腎衰竭)** to replace lost physiological renal functions (excretory, endocrine, metabolic).
* **Treatment Superiority**: Gold standard treatment for ESRD, offering superior quality of life and survival benefit compared to long-term **Haemodialysis (HD) (血液透析)** or **Peritoneal Dialysis (PD) (腹膜透析)**.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Primary Etiologies**: **Diabetic Nephropathy (糖尿病腎病)** is the leading cause of ESRD in HK, followed closely by **IgA Nephropathy (IgA腎病)** and chronic glomerulonephritis.
* **Organ Shortage**: HK suffers from a chronic shortage of deceased donor organs due to low societal donation rates.
* **Waiting Times**: Patients on the **Hospital Authority (HA) Central Renal Registry (醫院管理局中央腎臟輪候冊)** face an average wait time of 5-7 years for a deceased donor kidney.
* **Living Donation**: Highly encouraged by HA protocols (Living Related or Living Unrelated) to bypass long wait times and optimize graft survival via pre-emptive transplantation.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Heterotopic Allograft Anatomy
* **Heterotopic Placement (異位移植)**: The allograft is placed in the **Iliac Fossa (髂窩)** rather than the native anatomical renal bed.
* **Right Iliac Fossa (RIF) Preference**: The right side is typically preferred because the **Right External Iliac Vein (右髂外靜脈)** is more superficial and horizontal, facilitating easier venous anastomosis.
* **Retroperitoneal Approach (腹膜後入路)**: The graft is placed strictly extraperitoneally to prevent intra-abdominal adhesions, protect the graft, and allow percutaneous access for future biopsies.

### Pathophysiology of Allograft Rejection
* **Hyperacute Rejection (超急性排斥反應)**: Occurs within minutes/hours. Mediated by pre-formed recipient anti-donor antibodies (ABO incompatibility or pre-sensitized HLA). $\rightarrow$ Complement activation $\rightarrow$ Endothelial necrosis $\rightarrow$ Immediate graft thrombosis.
* **Acute Rejection (急性排斥反應)**: Occurs weeks to months post-op. Can be **T-Cell Mediated Rejection (TCMR) (T細胞介導排斥)** (tubulointerstitial cellular infiltrate) or **Antibody-Mediated Rejection (ABMR) (抗體介導排斥)** (donor-specific antibodies attacking endothelium).
* **Chronic Rejection (慢性排斥反應)**: Occurs over months to years. Characterized by **Interstitial Fibrosis and Tubular Atrophy (IFTA) (間質纖維化及腎小管萎縮)** and transplant glomerulopathy, leading to gradual, irreversible decline in eGFR.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Signs of End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD)
* **Fluid Overload (體液超載)**: Peripheral edema, elevated JVP, pulmonary edema.
* **Uraemic Syndrome (尿毒症)**: Pruritus, uraemic frost, encephalopathy, asterixis, pericarditis.
* **Metabolic Derangements (代謝紊亂)**: Refractory hypertension, severe anemia, renal osteodystrophy.

### Clinical Presentation of Acute Graft Rejection
* **Oliguria or Anuria (少尿或無尿)**: Sudden $\downarrow$ in urine output.
* **Graft Tenderness (移植物觸痛)**: Swollen, enlarged, and painful kidney palpable in the iliac fossa.
* **Systemic Signs (全身症狀)**: Low-grade fever, malaise, fluid retention.
* **Biochemical Signs (生化指標)**: Acute $\uparrow$ in serum creatinine and urea, worsening hypertension.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Early Post-transplant Oliguria
| Pathology | Timing | Pathophysiology | Discriminatory Diagnostic Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN) (急性腎小管壞死)** | Day 1 to Weeks | Ischaemic injury from prolonged Cold Ischemia Time (CIT) | Most common cause of **Delayed Graft Function (DGF)**; normal perfusion on USS. |
| **Hyperacute Rejection (超急性排斥)** | Intra-op to 24h | Pre-formed cytotoxic antibodies (Type II Hypersensitivity) | Intra-op graft turns blue/mottled; anuria on table. Requires immediate nephrectomy. |
| **Renal Artery Thrombosis (腎動脈血栓)** | Day 1 to Day 3 | Surgical technique error or hypercoagulability | Sudden catastrophic anuria; absent flow on Doppler USS. |
| **Urine Leak (尿液漏出)** | Day 3 to Weeks | Ischaemic necrosis of distal ureter (watershed area) | Fluid collection on USS; drain fluid creatinine $\gg$ serum creatinine. |
| **CNI Toxicity (鈣調磷酸酶抑制劑毒性)** | Variable | Afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction from Tacrolimus | High serum Tacrolimus trough levels; $\uparrow$ creatinine resolves with dose $\downarrow$. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Tier 1 Immunological Workup (Recipient & Donor)
* **ABO Blood Typing (ABO血型配對)**: Absolute prerequisite (unless specialized desensitization protocol utilized).
* **HLA Tissue Typing (人類白細胞抗原配對)**: HLA-A, HLA-B, and HLA-DR matching to predict graft survival and rejection risk.
* **Panel Reactive Antibodies (PRA) (群體反應性抗體)**: Assesses degree of recipient pre-sensitization to the general population's HLA antigens.
* **Complement-Dependent Cytotoxicity (CDC) Crossmatch (淋巴細胞毒性交叉配對)**: Final pre-op check. Mix recipient serum + donor lymphocytes. A +ve crossmatch is an absolute contraindication to surgery (predicts hyperacute rejection).

### Tier 2 Radiological and Urological Workup
* **CT Angiogram of Donor (電腦血管造影)**: Maps renal arterial anatomy, identifies multiple accessory renal arteries, and determines venous length to select the optimal kidney for procurement.
* **Echocardiogram & Cardiac Stress Test**: Strict cardiopulmonary workup for recipient due to high incidence of silent ischemic heart disease in ESRD.
* **Non-Contrast CT KUB (平掃電腦掃描)**: Screens recipient for severe iliofemoral vascular calcifications that could preclude safe vascular anastomosis.

### Tier 3 Post-operative Graft Surveillance Investigations
* **Doppler Ultrasound Scan (多普勒超聲波)**: 1st-line investigation for delayed graft function. Checks arterial patency, venous outflow, and **Resistive Index (RI) (阻力指數)** ($\uparrow$ RI >0.8 suggests rejection, ATN, or toxicity).
* **MAG3 / DTPA Isotope Renogram (同位素腎圖)**: Differentiates ATN (good perfusion, poor excretion) from acute rejection/infarction (poor perfusion).
* **Ultrasound-Guided Percutaneous Graft Biopsy (超聲波引導移植腎穿刺活檢)**: Gold standard for diagnosing and differentiating rejection versus drug toxicity (graded via **Banff Classification**).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Surgical Indications for Renal Transplantation
* **Absolute Indication**: Irreversible ESRD with eGFR $< 15 \text{ mL/min/1.73m}^2$.
* **Pre-emptive Transplantation (預防性移植)**: Performing transplantation before the initiation of dialysis (optimizes long-term outcomes).

### Absolute Contraindications to Transplantation
* **Active Malignancy (活躍期癌症)**: Requires specific cancer-free waiting periods depending on tumor histology.
* **Active Systemic Infection (活躍期系統性感染)**: e.g., active untreated TB or severe bacterial sepsis.
* **Severe Irreversible Comorbidities**: Severe cardiovascular/pulmonary disease rendering the patient unfit for general anesthesia.
* **Psychosocial Absolute Contraindications**: Documented severe medical non-compliance or active substance abuse.

### Hong Kong HA Triple Immunosuppression Protocol
* **Induction Therapy (誘導治療)**: Administered intra-operatively. **Basiliximab (巴利昔單抗)** (IL-2 receptor antagonist) for standard-risk; **Antithymocyte Globulin (ATG) (抗胸腺細胞球蛋白)** for high-risk/sensitized patients.
* **Maintenance - CNI (鈣調磷酸酶抑制劑)**: **Tacrolimus (他克莫司)** is the 1st-line cornerstone. Requires strict therapeutic drug monitoring (trough levels).
* **Maintenance - Antiproliferative (抗增殖劑)**: **Mycophenolate Mofetil (MMF) (嗎替麥考酚酯)**.
* **Maintenance - Corticosteroids (皮質類固醇)**: **Prednisolone (潑尼松龍)** (often tapered down slowly over months).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Donor Procurement and Bench Surgery Principles
* **Organ Preservation (器官保存)**: Graft is flushed with ice-cold preservation solution (e.g., **University of Wisconsin [UW] solution**) to slow metabolic rate and minimize hypoxic injury.
* **Cold Ischemia Time (CIT) (冷缺血時間)**: Must be strictly minimized (ideally $<18$ hours for deceased donors) to prevent severe ATN and DGF.
* **Bench Surgery Setup (工作台手術)**: Allograft is kept in an ice bath. Surrounding perinephric fat is excised, vessels are skeletonized, and accessory vessels are reconstructed before implantation.

### Sequential Anastomosis Operative Steps
* **Vascular Control**: Extraperitoneal exposure of the iliac vessels. Systemic heparinization administered prior to clamping.
* **Venous Anastomosis (1st)**: Donor renal vein anastomosed end-to-side to the recipient **External Iliac Vein (髂外靜脈)** using continuous non-absorbable prolene sutures.
* **Arterial Anastomosis (2nd)**: Donor renal artery anastomosed end-to-side to the recipient **External Iliac Artery (髂外動脈)** (or occasionally end-to-end to internal iliac artery).
* **Reperfusion (血管打通)**: Clamps removed. Graft should immediately become pink, firm, and ideally produce urine on the table.

### Ureteric Anastomosis Operative Steps
* **Lich-Gregoir Extravesical Ureteroneocystostomy (輸尿管膀胱吻合術)**: Distal donor ureter is implanted into the anterior dome of the recipient bladder.
* **Anti-reflux Mechanism**: The ureter is tunneled submucosally to create a one-way valve effect, preventing vesicoureteral reflux and subsequent allograft pyelonephritis.
* **Ureteric Stenting**: A **Double-J (JJ) Stent (雙J輸尿管支架)** is routinely deployed across the anastomosis to protect against strictures and urine leaks. Removed via flexible cystoscopy at 4-6 weeks.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Fluid and Hemodynamic Management
* **Strict Fluid Replacement Protocol**: Hourly urine output is matched 1:1 with IV fluids (typically 0.45% NaCl + Sodium Bicarbonate or Plasma-Lyte) to prevent hypovolemia and graft hypoperfusion.
* **Hemodynamic Targeting**: Maintain systolic BP $>120 \text{ mmHg}$ and adequate Central Venous Pressure (CVP) to ensure optimal allograft perfusion.
* **Post-operative Polyuria**: Expected due to urea-driven osmotic diuresis and clearance of retained fluid overload.

### Prophylactic Pharmacological Management
* **PJP Prophylaxis**: **Co-trimoxazole (復方新諾明)** daily for 6-12 months to prevent Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia.
* **CMV Prophylaxis**: **Valganciclovir (纈更昔洛韋)** for 3-6 months (especially if Donor is CMV +ve and Recipient is CMV -ve).
* **Fungal Prophylaxis**: Oral Nystatin drops or Fluconazole to prevent oral/esophageal candidiasis.
* **Surgical Drain Management**: Closed suction drain removed when output is $<50 \text{ mL/day}$ and drain fluid creatinine matches serum creatinine (rules out urine leak).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical/Vascular Complications (< 1 month)
* **Delayed Graft Function (DGF) (移植物延遲功能恢復)**: Defined as the requirement for dialysis within the first 7 days post-transplant. Primarily caused by ATN.
* **Renal Artery Thrombosis (RAT)**: Devastating early complication $\rightarrow$ sudden complete anuria. Almost always results in graft loss.
* **Renal Vein Thrombosis (RVT)**: Presents with sudden swelling of graft, ipsilateral leg edema, and gross hematuria.

### Intermediate Urological Complications (1 to 6 months)
* **Urine Leak (尿液漏出)**: Usually occurs at the vesicoureteral anastomosis due to ischemia of the distal ureter. Presents with $\downarrow$ urine output and clear fluid pouring from surgical drains.
* **Lymphocele (淋巴囊腫)**: Disruption of recipient iliac lymphatics. Can cause extrinsic compression of the transplant ureter/vein $\rightarrow$ obstructive uropathy or leg edema. Treated with percutaneous drainage or laparoscopic fenestration.

### Late Medical & Oncological Complications (> 6 months)
* **Opportunistic Infections (機會性感染)**: High risk of CMV, BK Polyomavirus (causes BK nephropathy), and atypical mycobacteria due to chronic immunosuppression.
* **Transplant Renal Artery Stenosis (TRAS) (移植腎動脈狹窄)**: Presents as refractory new-onset hypertension and unexplained graft dysfunction. Treated with percutaneous angioplasty.
* **Malignancy (癌症)**: Significantly $\uparrow$ risk of **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD) (移植後淋巴增殖性疾病)** (EBV-driven) and non-melanoma skin cancers (squamous cell carcinomas).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: Why is the donor right kidney typically associated with a shorter renal vein than the left? Answer: Because the inferior vena cava lies on the right side of the aorta, making the native right renal vein anatomically much shorter than the left.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically and biochemically differentiate a post-operative urine leak from a lymphocele? Answer: Aspirate the fluid. In a urine leak, the fluid creatinine and potassium will be exponentially higher than serum levels. In a lymphocele, the fluid creatinine will be identical to serum levels.
* Trap: Assuming that anuria on post-operative day 1 is definitive evidence of acute graft rejection. Pearl: The most common cause of early oliguria/anuria and Delayed Graft Function is Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN) secondary to prolonged cold ischemia time.
* Pitfall: Removing the surgical drain simply because the output volume is high. Always test high-volume drain output for creatinine to ensure you are not missing a catastrophic ureteric anastomotic breakdown/urine leak before pulling the drain.
* Trap: Prescribing standard nephrotoxic drugs to a transplant patient without adjusting for their solitary functional kidney status. Pearl: Always strictly avoid NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, and carefully dose-adjust all renally excreted medications to preserve the allograft.
* Viva Question: What is the classic presentation of BK Polyomavirus nephropathy in a transplant recipient? Answer: An asymptomatic, progressive rise in serum creatinine months post-transplant; urinalysis may show pathognomonic "decoy cells," and definitive diagnosis requires a renal biopsy showing viral inclusion bodies.
