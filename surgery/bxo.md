# Balanitis Xerotica Obliterans (閉塞性乾燥性龜頭炎)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Balanitis Xerotica Obliterans
* **Definition**: Genital variant of **Lichen Sclerosus (硬化性苔蘚)** affecting male external genitalia.
* **Characteristics**: Chronic, progressive, sclerosing inflammatory dermatosis → scarring & depigmentation of **Prepuce (包皮)** & **Glans penis (龜頭)**.
* **Pathological Outcome**: Leads to intractable **Phimosis (包莖)**, meatal stenosis, & potential urethral stricture.

### Local Epidemiology of BXO in Hong Kong
* **HA Incidence**: Common presentation to local HA Urology SOPC; accounts for significant % of adult circumcisions.
* **Demographics**: Bimodal peak (boys aged 5-15, uncircumcised men >50 yrs).
* **Malignancy Risk**: Pre-malignant condition; ~5% progress to **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (鱗狀細胞癌)**.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Male External Genitalia
* **Prepuce (包皮)**: Double-layered fold of smooth muscle, vessels, nerves, mucous membrane, & skin.
* **Glans penis (龜頭)**: Distal expansion of corpus spongiosum containing highly vascular erectile tissue.
* **Navicular fossa**: Distal urethral dilation within glans; primary site of stricture extension in severe BXO.

### Pathophysiology and Histological Changes in BXO
* **Aetiology**: Idiopathic. Postulated: Autoimmune, chronic infection, local trauma, genetic (HLA-DQ7).
* **Macroscopic Patho**: Chronic inflammation → loss of tissue elasticity → rigid cicatricial ring formation.
* **Histopathology**: 
* **Hyperkeratosis**: Thickened stratum corneum.
* **Epidermal Atrophy**: Thinning of epidermis, loss of rete ridges.
* **Collagen Alteration**: Homogenization/hyalinization of collagen in upper dermis.
* **Inflammatory Infiltrate**: Dense, band-like lymphocytic infiltrate in lower dermis.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Cardinal Symptoms of BXO
* **Pruritus (痕癢)**: Often severe & unremitting.
* **Dyspareunia (性交疼痛)**: Pain during erection/intercourse due to rigid foreskin.
* **LUTS (下尿路症狀)**: Voiding symptoms (hesitancy, poor stream, straining) → implies meatal/urethral involvement.
* **Recurrent Infection**: Recurrent balanoposthitis or UTI.

### Classic Clinical Signs of BXO
* **Visual Appearance**: Sclerotic, ivory-white, atrophic plaques on **Glans penis (龜頭)** or **Prepuce (包皮)**.
* **Phimosis (包莖)**: Non-retractile foreskin forming a thickened, fibrotic ring.
* **Fissuring**: Radial cracks & bleeding upon attempted foreskin retraction.
* **Meatal Stenosis (尿道口狹窄)**: Pinpoint meatus obscured by white fibrotic tissue.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Diagnostic Differentials for Penile Plaques and Phimosis

| Condition | Key Clinical Features | Discriminatory Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **BXO (閉塞性乾燥性龜頭炎)** | White sclerotic plaques, cicatricial phimosis, meatal stenosis | Depigmented scarring, stricture formation |
| **Zoon's Balanitis (漿細胞性龜頭炎)** | Orange-red, glazed patches on glans/inner prepuce | "Cayenne pepper" spots, middle-aged/elderly men |
| **SCC / CIS (鱗狀細胞癌/原位癌)** | Erythematous, velvety, or ulcerated lesions, painful/painless | Raised indurated margins, palpable inguinal LN |
| **Candidal Balanitis (念珠菌龜頭炎)** | Erythematous rash, satellite papules, intense pruritus | +ve response to antifungals, frequent in DM patients |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Bedside and Laboratory Investigations for Suspected BXO
* **Clinical Diagnosis**: Classic macroscopic appearance usually diagnostic.
* **Urinalysis/MSU**: Exclude concurrent UTI & check for glucosuria (DM screening).
* **Skin Biopsy (活組織檢查)**: Indicated if atypical appearance, failure of conservative Tx, or high index of suspicion for **SCC (鱗狀細胞癌)**.

### Urological Investigations for Advanced BXO
* **Uroflowmetry (尿流速測試)**: Assess for BOO if meatal involvement suspected (Qmax ↓, prolonged plateau curve).
* **Bladder Scan**: Assess post-void residual (PVR) urine.
* **Flexible Cystoscopy/Urethroscopy (膀胱鏡檢查)**: Indicated *only* if meatus permits & complex stricture suspected; evaluate proximal extent of urethral involvement.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Medical Management and HA Protocols for BXO
* **1st Line Medical Tx**: Ultra-potent topical corticosteroids (e.g., **Clobetasol propionate (氯倍他索) 0.05%**).
* **HA Prescribing Protocol**: Apply OD/BD for 1-3 months. Reverses early disease & halts progression.
* **Adjuncts**: Strict genital hygiene, liberal use of emollients, avoidance of soap.

### Surgical Indications for BXO
* **Refractory Disease**: Failure of topical steroid therapy.
* **Complicated Phimosis**: Pathological **Phimosis (包莖)** with recurrent balanoposthitis.
* **Obstructive LUTS**: Meatal stenosis or urethral stricture causing compromised voiding.
* **Oncological Concern**: Suspicious lesions requiring excision/biopsy to rule out **SCC**.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Surgery for BXO
* **Circumcision (割包皮)**: Curative for isolated prepuce disease. Requires radical excision of *all* diseased preputial tissue to prevent recurrence.
* **Meatoplasty / Meatotomy (尿道口成形術)**: Ventral slit or extended meatoplasty if meatal stenosis present.
* **Urethroplasty (尿道成形術)**: Required for extensive urethral strictures. 
* **Graft Principle**: MUST use Buccal Mucosal Graft (BMG). DO NOT use genital skin flaps/grafts (high failure rate due to disease recurrence in genital tissue).

### Pre-operative Consent and Complications Counseling
* **Standard Risks**: Bleeding, haematoma, wound infection.
* **Specific Risks**: Disease recurrence on glans/meatus, requirement for further reconstructive surgeries.
* **Cosmesis**: Altered penile appearance, potential loss of penile skin length.
* **Oncological Surveillance**: Require lifelong awareness due to persistent 5% risk of **SCC**.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* **Dressings**: Compressive penile dressing (remove after 24-48 hrs).
* **Analgesia**: Regular Paracetamol + NSAIDs.
* **Oedema Control**: Scrotal support, ice packs, elevate penis.

### Discharge Planning and Rehabilitation
* **Wound Care**: Keep wound dry for 48 hrs. Apply topical antibiotic ointment or petroleum jelly to glans/meatus to prevent crusting & synechiae.
* **Activity**: Avoid strenuous physical exertion for 1-2 weeks.
* **Sexual Function**: Strict abstinence from intercourse/masturbation for 4-6 weeks to allow suture line healing.
* **Follow-up**: Arrange HA SOPC review to monitor wound, check histology, & assess urinary stream.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early and Intermediate Surgical Complications
* **Early (0-30 days)**: Post-operative bleeding, expanding haematoma, SSI, painful nocturnal erections causing suture rupture.
* **Intermediate (1-6 months)**: Wound dehiscence, delayed healing, redundant skin requiring revision, asymmetric cosmesis.

### Late Complications and Long-Term Prognosis
* **Progression of Disease**: Glanular/meatal disease can progress despite successful circumcision.
* **Stricture Formation**: Meatal stenosis & anterior urethral strictures causing BOO & renal impairment.
* **Malignancy**: Long-term risk of malignant transformation into **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (鱗狀細胞癌)**.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the classic histological hallmark of BXO? Answer: Homogenization and hyalinization of collagen in the upper dermis with a band-like lymphocytic infiltrate below.
* Trap: Recommending a genital skin flap for urethroplasty in a BXO patient. Pearl: Genital skin is affected by the BXO disease process; buccal mucosal graft (BMG) is the gold standard for BXO urethral strictures.
* Pitfall: Discharging a BXO patient post-circumcision without long-term counseling. Always advise patients on the ~5% lifetime risk of SCC and the need to report any new glanular ulcers or voiding difficulties immediately.
* Viva Question: What is the first-line medical therapy for early uncomplicated BXO? Answer: Ultra-potent topical steroids, such as 0.05% Clobetasol propionate, applied daily for up to 3 months.
* Trap: Confusing BXO with Zoon's balanitis on inspection. Pearl: Zoon's presents as a red, glazed, non-scarring patch with "cayenne pepper" spots in older men, whereas BXO features white, sclerotic, scarring plaques.
