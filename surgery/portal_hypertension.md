# Portal Hypertension (門靜脈高壓)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Portal Hypertension
* **Portal Hypertension (門靜脈高壓)**: Defined by **Hepatic Venous Pressure Gradient (HVPG)** >5 mmHg
* **Clinically Significant Portal Hypertension (CSPH)**: HVPG ≥10 mmHg (↑ risk of decompensation)
* **Variceal Bleeding Threshold**: HVPG ≥12 mmHg

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* Endemic **Hepatitis B Virus (乙型肝炎)** is the leading driver of **Liver Cirrhosis (肝硬化)** in HK
* 8-10% of HK population are HBV carriers; major burden on HA hepatobiliary/transplant services
* Secondary causes: **Hepatitis C (丙型肝炎)**, Alcoholic Liver Disease, **Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/NASH)** (rapidly ↑ incidence in HK)

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Portal Venous System
* **Portal Vein (門靜脈)** formation: **Superior Mesenteric Vein (SMV)** + **Splenic Vein (脾靜脈)**
* Confluence location: Posterior to the neck of the pancreas
* Contributes ~75% of hepatic blood flow, ~50% of hepatic oxygen supply

### Porto-Systemic Anastomoses Locations
* **Gastro-esophageal**: Left gastric vein ↔ Azygos/Hemiazygos veins → **Esophageal Varices (食道靜脈曲張)**
* **Anorectal**: Superior rectal vein ↔ Middle/Inferior rectal veins → Anorectal varices (not internal hemorrhoids)
* **Umbilical**: Paraumbilical veins ↔ Epigastric veins → **Caput Medusae**
* **Retroperitoneal**: Veins of Retzius (lumbar/gonadal veins)

### Pathophysiology of Portal Hypertension
* **Ohm's Law Analogy**: Pressure = Flow × Resistance (ΔP = Q × R)
* **↑ Resistance (R)**: Structural distortion (fibrosis/regenerative nodules) + Dynamic intrahepatic vasoconstriction (↓ NO, ↑ Endothelin)
* **↑ Flow (Q)**: Splanchnic vasodilatation (driven by NO/prostacyclin) → Hyperdynamic circulation → Exacerbates portal pressure

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Portal Hypertension
* **Hematemesis/Melena (吐血/黑糞)**: Asymptomatic until variceal rupture
* **Abdominal Distension**: Rapid onset weight gain from **Ascites (腹水)**
* **Altered Mental Status**: Lethargy, confusion, sleep inversion from **Hepatic Encephalopathy (肝性腦病)**

### Key Clinical Signs of Chronic Liver Disease and Portal Hypertension
* **Portal Hypertension Specific**:
  * **Splenomegaly (脾腫大)**
  * **Ascites (腹水)**: Shifting dullness, fluid thrill
  * **Caput Medusae**: Dilated periumbilical veins
* **Cirrhosis/Hepatic Failure Specific**:
  * **Spider Naevi**: SVC distribution
  * **Palmar Erythema**
  * **Jaundice (黃疸)**
  * **Hepatic Flap (Asterixis)**
  * **Fetor Hepaticus**
  * **Gynecomastia / Testicular Atrophy**

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Classification and Causes of Portal Hypertension
| Classification | Anatomical Site | Common Etiologies | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pre-hepatic** | Splenic/Portal Vein | Portal vein thrombosis (PVT), Splenic vein thrombosis (pancreatitis) | Normal LFTs, normal liver biopsy, isolated gastric varices (splenic vein thrombosis) |
| **Intra-hepatic (Pre-sinusoidal)**| Portal tracts / Sinusoids | Schistosomiasis, Primary Biliary Cholangitis (PBC), Congenital Hepatic Fibrosis | Normal/slightly ↑ HVPG (wedge pressure often normal), specific autoantibodies (+ve AMA) |
| **Intra-hepatic (Sinusoidal)** | Sinusoids | **Cirrhosis (肝硬化)** (HBV, HCV, Alcohol, NAFLD) | ↑↑ HVPG, signs of chronic liver disease, abnormal LFTs |
| **Post-hepatic** | Hepatic Vein / IVC | Budd-Chiari Syndrome, Right-sided Heart Failure, Constrictive Pericarditis | Hepatomegaly, rapidly progressive ascites, prominent venous collaterals on back |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Laboratory Investigations
* **CBC**: Thrombocytopenia (earliest sign of hypersplenism), ↓ Hb (bleeding/anemia of chronic disease), leukopenia
* **Renal / LFT**: ↑ Bilirubin, ↓ Albumin, ↑ AST/ALT (viral/toxin), ↑ ALP/GGT (biliary)
* **Coagulation Profile**: ↑ PT / ↑ INR (impaired synthetic function)
* **Virology**: HBsAg, Anti-HBs, Anti-HCV (HA mandatory screening)

### Diagnostic Imaging for Portal Hypertension
* **USG Abdomen + Doppler (1st Line)**:
  * Reversal of portal flow (hepatofugal)
  * Portal vein diameter >13 mm
  * Patency of PV/HV (r/o Budd-Chiari or PVT)
  * Screening for **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)**
* **Triphasic CT Abdomen**: Detailed portosystemic collateral anatomy, preoperative planning for TIPS/shunts, accurate HCC staging

### Endoscopic Evaluation
* **OGD (Esophagogastroduodenoscopy)**: Gold standard for variceal screening/grading
* Red wale marks, cherry red spots = high risk of impending rupture

### Staging Systems for Liver Cirrhosis (Child-Pugh Score)
| Parameter | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bilirubin (μmol/L)** | <34 | 34 - 50 | >50 |
| **Albumin (g/L)** | >35 | 28 - 35 | <28 |
| **PT (sec prolonged) or INR**| <4 or <1.7 | 4-6 or 1.7-2.3 | >6 or >2.3 |
| **Ascites** | None | Mild/Moderate (Diuretic-responsive) | Tense/Refractory |
| **Encephalopathy (Grade)** | None | Grade I-II | Grade III-IV |
* **Class A**: 5-6 points (Good prognosis)
* **Class B**: 7-9 points
* **Class C**: 10-15 points (Poor prognosis, transplant candidate)

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Acute Variceal Bleeding Management Algorithm (HA Protocol)
* **Airway/Breathing**: Endotracheal intubation if massive bleed or Grade III/IV encephalopathy to protect airway
* **Resuscitation**: 2x large-bore IV, Type & Screen. **Restrictive Transfusion**: Target Hb 7-8 g/dL (avoid ↑ splanchnic pressure)
* **Pharmacotherapy (Immediate)**:
  * Vasoactive drugs: **IV Terlipressin** 2mg Q4H or **IV Octreotide** (constricts splanchnic vessels)
  * Prophylactic Abx: **IV Ceftriaxone** 1g daily (prevents SBP, ↓ rebleeding mortality)
* **Endoscopy (OGD)**: Within 12h. **Endoscopic Variceal Ligation (EVL)** for esophageal; **Cyanoacrylate glue** for gastric varices
* **Rescue Therapy**: Balloon tamponade (**Sengstaken-Blakemore tube**) for max 24h as bridge to TIPS

### Prophylaxis for Variceal Bleeding
* **Primary Prophylaxis**: Non-selective β-blockers (NSBB) (e.g., **Propranolol**, Carvedilol) OR EVL for medium/large varices
* **Secondary Prophylaxis**: EVL every 2-4 weeks until obliteration + NSBB indefinitely

### Management of Ascites and Encephalopathy
* **Ascites (腹水)**:
  * Sodium restriction (<2g/day)
  * Dual diuretics: **Spironolactone** + **Furosemide** (ratio 100mg:40mg to maintain normokalemia)
  * Refractory: Large Volume Paracentesis (LVP) + IV Albumin replacement (8g per liter of ascites removed >5L)
* **Hepatic Encephalopathy**: Oral **Lactulose** (titrate to 2-3 soft stools/day) + Oral **Rifaximin**

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles of Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt (TIPS)
* **Indication**: Refractory variceal bleeding, refractory ascites
* **Mechanism**: Creates low-resistance channel between Hepatic Vein (systemic) and Portal Vein (portal) bypassing fibrotic liver parenchyma
* **Technique**: Access via Right Internal Jugular Vein → IVC → Right Hepatic Vein → Puncture through liver tract to Portal Vein → Deploy **PTFE-covered stent** (reduces shunt dysfunction vs bare-metal)

### Operative Principles of Surgical Portosystemic Shunts
* **Current Status**: Rarely performed in HA hospitals due to TIPS and Liver Transplantation
* **Selective Shunt (Distal Splenorenal/Warren Shunt)**: Decompresses gastro-esophageal varices via splenic vein to left renal vein; preserves mesenteric flow to liver (↓ encephalopathy risk)
* **Non-selective Shunt (Portocaval Shunt)**: Complete diversion; high rate of fatal encephalopathy

### Key Consent Issues for Portal Hypertension Interventions (TIPS)
* **Hepatic Encephalopathy (肝性腦病)**: 30-50% risk post-TIPS due to bypassing hepatic clearance of gut toxins
* **Right Heart Failure**: Sudden ↑ in venous return can precipitate cardiac decompensation
* **Stent Thrombosis/Stenosis**: Requires re-intervention
* **Capsular Puncture / Intraperitoneal Hemorrhage**: Fatal procedural complication

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care (Post-TIPS/Shunt)
* **ICU/HDU Admission**: Strict hemodynamic monitoring (CVP, Art line)
* **Neurological Monitoring**: Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) and flapping tremor checks for early detection of encephalopathy
* **Imaging Follow-up**: Doppler USG at 24h, 1 week, and 3 months to confirm shunt patency and flow velocity

### Long-term Rehabilitation and Monitoring
* **Dietary Modification**: High protein (1.2-1.5 g/kg/day), frequent small meals + late evening snack to prevent muscle wasting (sarcopenia)
* **HCC Screening Program**: HA protocol mandates 6-monthly USG Liver + serum AFP for all cirrhotic patients
* **Transplant Evaluation**: Workup for Orthotopic Liver Transplantation (OLT) if MELD score >15

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications of Interventions
* **Failure to Control Bleeding**: ~10-15% of acute variceal bleeds despite OGD/TIPS
* **Acute Hepatic Decompensation**: Ischemic hepatitis from sudden ↓ in portal flow
* **Acute Kidney Injury / Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)**: Precipitated by bleeding or large volume paracentesis without albumin

### Late Complications & Disease Progression
* **TIPS Dysfunction**: Intimal hyperplasia causing stenosis; presents with recurrent ascites/bleeding
* **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)**: Progressive fibrotic environment triggers malignant transformation
* **Chronic Hepatic Encephalopathy**: Irreversible cognitive decline

### Prognosis of Portal Hypertension
* **Child-Pugh C**: 1-year survival <50% without transplant
* **Acute Variceal Bleed Mortality**: ~15-20% at 6 weeks
* **Hepatorenal Syndrome (Type 1)**: Median survival <2 weeks without intervention/terlipressin

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the normal Hepatic Venous Pressure Gradient (HVPG) and at what level do varices bleed? Answer: Normal is 1-5 mmHg. Clinically significant portal hypertension is ≥10 mmHg. Variceal bleeding risk ↑ significantly when HVPG is ≥12 mmHg.
* Trap: Over-transfusing a patient with acute variceal bleeding. Pearl: Aim for a restrictive target hemoglobin of 7-8 g/dL; over-transfusion ↑ splanchnic pressure and triggers rebound bleeding.
* Pitfall: Forgetting prophylactic antibiotics in acute variceal bleeding. Prescribe IV Ceftriaxone 1g/day to ↓ mortality and re-bleeding rates, as bacterial infections (e.g., SBP) are a major trigger for re-bleeding.
* Viva Question: How do you differentiate a pre-hepatic vs intra-hepatic cause of portal hypertension clinically and on biopsy? Answer: Pre-hepatic (e.g., Portal Vein Thrombosis) will have normal liver function tests and normal liver histology, whereas intra-hepatic (cirrhosis) will show impaired synthetic function and fibrotic bridging/nodules on biopsy.
* Trap: Prescribing non-selective beta-blockers (NSBB) in a patient with refractory ascites and hypotension. Pearl: Stop or reduce NSBB in end-stage cirrhotics with refractory ascites and systolic BP <90 mmHg, as it decreases renal perfusion and precipitates Hepatorenal Syndrome.
* Pitfall: Misdiagnosing isolated gastric varices without esophageal varices. Always suspect and rule out splenic vein thrombosis secondary to pancreatic pathology (e.g., chronic pancreatitis or pancreatic cancer).
