# Intestinal Transplantation (小腸移植)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Intestinal Transplant
* **Intestinal Transplantion (小腸移植)**: Surgical replacement of irreversibly failed small intestine to restore enteral autonomy & eliminate dependence on **Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN, 全靜脈營養)**.

### Local Epidemiology & Hong Kong Context
* Extremely rare procedure globally & locally.
* **Queen Mary Hospital (QMH, 瑪麗醫院)** is the sole designated transplant centre for intestinal/multivisceral grafts under the **Hospital Authority (HA, 醫院管理局)**.
* Extremely selective criteria due to high morbidity, severe immunosuppression requirements, & scarcity of size-matched paediatric/adult donors in HK.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Small Intestine Blood Supply
* **Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA, 腸繫膜上動脈)**: Primary arterial supply to graft.
* **Superior Mesenteric Vein (SMV, 腸繫膜上靜脈)**: Primary venous drainage; anatomically preferable to drain into recipient portal circulation to mimic physiological 1st-pass hepatic metabolism.
* High concentration of **Gut-Associated Lymphoid Tissue (GALT)**: Makes intestine highly immunogenic → ↑ risk of **Acute Cellular Rejection (ACR)** & **Graft-versus-Host Disease (GVHD, 移植物抗宿主病)**.

### Pathophysiology of Intestinal Failure (IF)
* Loss of functional absorptive surface area → impaired absorption of macronutrients, water, electrolytes → inability to maintain protein-energy balance.
* Requires prolonged intravenous supplementation (TPN).

### Pathophysiology of Intestinal Failure-Associated Liver Disease (IFALD)
* Prolonged TPN toxicity (lipid emulsions/phytosterols) + lack of enteral stimulation (↓ enterohepatic circulation) + recurrent line sepsis → hepatic steatosis → cholestasis (膽汁淤積) → progressive biliary cirrhosis (肝硬化).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Presentation of Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)
* Intractable watery diarrhoea (腹瀉) / high stoma output.
* Profound weight loss, muscle wasting, cachexia (惡病質).
* Severe dehydration & hypovolaemia.
* Refractory electrolyte derangements (↓ K+, ↓ Mg2+, ↓ Ca2+).

### Physical Signs of Chronic TPN Complications
* **Jaundice (黃疸)** & hepatosplenomegaly (signs of IFALD / impending liver failure).
* Multiple central venous catheter (CVC) scars or active line sepsis signs (erythema, purulence).
* Engorged superficial chest veins (+ve SVC syndrome) → indicates central venous thrombosis.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Adult Causes of Intestinal Failure
* **Mesenteric Ischaemia (腸繫膜缺血)**: SMA thrombosis/embolism leading to massive resection.
* **Crohn's Disease (克隆氏症)**: Multiple recurrent resections (short bowel syndrome).
* **Radiation Enteritis (放射性腸炎)**: Post-pelvic/abdominal radiotherapy.
* Severe abdominal trauma / volvulus.

### Paediatric Causes of Intestinal Failure
* **Necrotising Enterocolitis (NEC, 壞死性小腸結腸炎)**: Premature neonates.
* **Gastroschisis (腹裂)** / Omphalocele.
* Intestinal atresia.
* **Hirschsprung's Disease (赫希斯普倫氏症)**: Total colonic/small bowel aganglionosis.

### Classification of Intestinal Failure (Type I to III)
| IF Type | Duration | Clinical Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type I** | Acute (<28 days) | Self-limiting, perioperative (e.g., post-op ileus). |
| **Type II** | Prolonged (Weeks-Months) | Metabolically unstable, needs complex multi-disciplinary TPN/surgery. |
| **Type III** | Chronic (Months-Years) | Irreversible IF, permanently TPN dependent; true candidates for transplant evaluation. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Baseline Laboratory Workup for Transplant Candidates
* **LFTs**: ↑ Bilirubin, ↑ ALP/GGT (early markers of IFALD).
* **Renal Function (RFTs)**: Crucial as calcineurin inhibitors (Tacrolimus) are nephrotoxic.
* **Nutritional Panel**: Albumin, Prealbumin, B12, Folate, Iron profile, Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K), Trace elements (Zinc, Selenium).
* **Coagulation Profile**: INR/PT to assess hepatic synthetic function.

### Radiological Assessment of Vascular Anatomy
* **Doppler US**: Assess portal vein patency & hepatic echotexture.
* **CT Angiography (CTA, 電腦掃描血管造影) Abdomen**: Map donor/recipient SMA & SMV anatomy; rule out mesenteric root thrombosis.
* **Venous Mapping**: Duplex US/MRV to document patency of internal jugular, subclavian, & femoral veins (must ensure sufficient access for transplant surgery).

### Pre-operative Immunological Staging & Infectious Disease Workup
* **HLA Typing** & Panel Reactive Antibodies (PRA).
* **Viral Serology**: CMV, EBV (high risk of Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD) if Donor +ve / Recipient -ve), HIV, HBV, HCV.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Medical Management & Bowel Rehabilitation 
* Maximize enteral nutrition (trophic feeds to stimulate adaptation).
* Anti-diarrhoeals (Loperamide, Codeine) + PPIs (reduce gastric hypersecretion).
* **GLP-2 Analogues (e.g., Teduglutide)**: ↑ intestinal mucosal hyperplasia & absorption.
* Optimize custom TPN to minimize liver toxicity (e.g., Omegaven/fish oil lipid formulations).

### Surgical Indications for Intestinal Transplant (Life-Threatening TPN Failure)
* Irreversible IF **AND** presence of life-threatening TPN complications:
* **Impending Liver Failure**: Progressive IFALD (Bilirubin >50-100 µmol/L, portal hypertension, splenomegaly, coagulopathy).
* **Recurrent Central Line Sepsis**: ≥2 episodes of systemic sepsis/yr, or 1 episode of fungaemia/ARBs.
* **Loss of Central Venous Access**: Thrombosis of ≥2 major central veins (internal jugular, subclavian, femoral).
* Frequent severe episodes of dehydration/electrolyte imbalance despite maximal medical therapy.

### Absolute Contraindications to Transplant
* Active systemic malignancy.
* Severe cardiopulmonary comorbidities.
* Profound irreversible neurological disability.
* Active, untreatable sepsis.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Types of Intestinal Grafts
| Graft Type | Components | Indications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Isolated Small Bowel (ISB)** | Small intestine only | IF with normal liver function & healthy stomach/duodenum. |
| **Modified Multivisceral** | Stomach, Duodenum, Pancreas, Small Bowel | Severe GI motility disorders; diffuse GI disease without liver failure. |
| **Full Multivisceral** | Stomach, Duodenum, Pancreas, Small Bowel + **Liver** | IF with irreversible liver failure (IFALD) / cirrhosis. |

### Key Operative Steps of Graft Implantation
* **Arterial Anastomosis**: Donor SMA to Recipient Infrarenal Aorta.
* **Venous Anastomosis**: Donor SMV to Recipient Portal Vein (preferred for physiological hepatic drainage) OR to Recipient IVC (if portal vein thrombosed).
* **Bowel Continuity**: Proximal enteric anastomosis (recipient jejunum to donor jejunum); Distal anastomosis (donor ileum to recipient colon).

### Essential Stoma Formation Principle
* **Terminal/Loop Ileostomy (迴腸造口) Formation**: Routine exteriorisation of the distal donor bowel is **MANDATORY**.
* Purpose: Allows direct visual endoscopic monitoring & frequent mucosal biopsies to detect early Acute Cellular Rejection (ACR).

### Crucial Pre-Operative Consent Issues
* **Extreme Surgical Risk**: High intra-operative mortality, massive blood loss.
* **Severe Immunosuppression**: Lifelong requirement, extremely high risk of opportunistic infections (CMV, fungal) & PTLD.
* **High Rejection Rate**: Intestine is the most immunogenic organ; frequent readmissions required.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Immunosuppression Protocol
* **Induction Therapy**: Lymphocyte-depleting agents (Alemtuzumab, Thymoglobulin) to aggressively knock out T-cells.
* **Maintenance Therapy**: High-dose **Tacrolimus (FK506)** + Corticosteroids. Mycophenolate Mofetil (MMF) or Sirolimus as adjuncts.

### Endoscopic Monitoring for Rejection Surveillance
* **Protocol Ileoscopy (迴腸內視鏡)**: Direct visualization of stoma mucosa twice weekly for the 1st month, then weekly, then monthly.
* Mucosal biopsies taken at every scope to look for blunting of villi, crypt apoptosis, & lymphocytic infiltrates (markers of ACR).

### Post-operative Nutritional Rehabilitation Protocol
* Maintain IV TPN immediately post-op.
* Initiate dilute enteral trophic tube feeds early (stimulates graft mucosa).
* Gradual transition: Tube feeds → oral liquid diet → solid food.
* Wean TPN as enteral tolerance improves & stoma output normalizes.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<1 month)
* **Acute Cellular Rejection (ACR, 急性細胞排斥)**: Fever, ↑ stoma output (watery/bloody), mucosal sloughing on endoscopy.
* **Vascular Thrombosis**: SMA/SMV thrombosis → fatal graft necrosis.
* Anastomotic leak → intra-abdominal sepsis.
* Primary non-function of the graft.

### Intermediate Complications (1-6 months)
* **Cytomegalovirus (CMV, 巨細胞病毒) Enteritis**: Mimics rejection (ulcerations, fever). Requires IV Ganciclovir.
* **Opportunistic Infections**: Fungaemia, Pneumocystis jirovecii (PJP).
* **Graft-versus-Host Disease (GVHD, 移植物抗宿主病)**: Donor graft lymphocytes attack recipient skin (rash), liver, & native GI tract.

### Late Complications (>6 months)
* **Chronic Rejection**: Progressive mucosal fibrosis, strictures, chronic malabsorption.
* **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD, 移植後淋巴增生性疾病)**: EBV-driven B-cell lymphoma 2° to intense immunosuppression.
* Renal failure: Secondary to lifelong Tacrolimus nephrotoxicity.

### Prognosis & Survival Rates
* 1-year patient survival: ~70-80%.
* 5-year patient survival: ~50-60%.
* Highest morbidity and mortality among all solid organ transplants.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What are the absolute indications to refer a Short Bowel Syndrome patient for Intestinal Transplant evaluation? Answer: Irreversible intestinal failure accompanied by life-threatening TPN complications (impending liver failure, loss of major venous access, or recurrent severe central line sepsis).
* Trap: Assuming isolated small bowel transplant is sufficient for a patient with established TPN-induced cirrhosis. Pearl: A patient with irreversible IFALD and cirrhosis mandates a combined liver-intestinal (multivisceral) transplant, as isolated intestine will not rescue the failing native liver.
* Pitfall: Mistaking watery, high-volume stoma output in the second post-operative week as simple infectious gastroenteritis. Any sudden increase in stoma output or change in effluent colour (bloody/sloughy) must be treated as Acute Cellular Rejection until proven otherwise with an urgent protocol stoma biopsy.
* Viva Question: Why is the donor intestine routinely brought out as an ileostomy rather than just forming a primary anastomosis to the native colon? Answer: To allow frequent, non-invasive endoscopic visualization and mucosal biopsies for rejection surveillance, which is uniquely critical due to the high immunogenicity of the bowel.
* Trap: Prescribing standard oral medications immediately post-transplant. Pearl: Graft absorptive function is initially erratic; crucial immunosuppressants (like Tacrolimus) must be given IV or meticulously monitored via trough levels if given enterally to prevent subtherapeutic dosing and rejection.
