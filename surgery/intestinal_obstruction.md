# Intestinal Obstruction (腸阻塞)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Intestinal Obstruction
* **Mechanical Obstruction**: Physical blockage preventing normal transit of intraluminal contents.
* **Functional Obstruction (Paralytic Ileus)**: Failure of normal intestinal motility in absence of mechanical lesion.
* **Closed-Loop Obstruction (閉袢性阻塞)**: Bowel segment occluded at two points along its length → ↑↑ risk of rapid ischemia & perforation.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Small Bowel Obstruction (小腸阻塞) (SBO)**: 
* **Most common cause**: **Post-operative Adhesions (腸粘連)** (60-70%).
* **2nd most common cause**: **Incarcerated Hernias (嵌頓疝)** (10-20% - notably inguinal/femoral).
* **Large Bowel Obstruction (大腸阻塞) (LBO)**: 
* **Most common cause**: **Colorectal Cancer (大腸癌)** (>60%) → Highly prevalent in Hong Kong Department of Health registries.
* **2nd most common cause**: **Volvulus (腸扭轉)** (Sigmoid > Cecal).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Bowel
* **Small Bowel**: Valvulae conniventes (Plicae circulares) completely cross lumen.
* **Large Bowel**: Taeniae coli form Haustra (結腸袋) → incomplete septa.
* **Ileocecal Valve (回盲瓣)**: Competent in up to 20% of population → converts LBO into dangerous **Closed-Loop Obstruction**.
* **Blood Supply**: SMA supplies midgut (up to proximal 2/3 transverse colon); IMA supplies hindgut.

### Pathophysiology of Simple Obstruction
* **Luminal occlusion** → Accumulation of gas (swallowed air) & fluid (saliva, gastric, biliary, pancreatic secretions).
* **Bowel dilatation** proximal to block → ↑ intramural pressure.
* **Third-Space Loss**: Fluid transudation into bowel lumen & peritoneal cavity → **Hypovolemia (低血容量)** & electrolyte imbalance (↓K+, ↓Cl-, ↓Na+, Metabolic Alkalosis from vomiting).

### Pathophysiology of Strangulation (絞窄)
* ↑↑ Intramural hydrostatic pressure > Capillary perfusion pressure.
* **Venous congestion** → Edema → **Arterial compromise (動脈缺血)**.
* **Mucosal ischemia** → Breakdown of gut barrier → **Bacterial translocation (細菌易位)** → Sepsis (敗血症) → **Transmural Necrosis (壞死)** & Perforation.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Cardinal Symptoms of Intestinal Obstruction
* **Abdominal Pain (腹痛)**: Colicky, intermittent (SBO: central, 3-5 min intervals; LBO: lower abdominal, 10-15 min intervals).
* **Vomiting (嘔吐)**: Early/bilious in proximal SBO; Late/feculent in distal SBO/LBO.
* **Abdominal Distension (腹脹)**: More prominent in distal SBO & LBO.
* **Absolute Constipation (絕對便秘)**: Failure to pass flatus or feces (may be delayed if distal bowel empties).

### Key Physical Signs of Simple Obstruction
* **Inspection**: Distension, visible peristalsis, surgical scars (adhesions).
* **Palpation**: Guarding, mass (malignancy/intussusception).
* **Percussion**: Tympanic over distended bowel.
* **Auscultation**: Hyperactive, high-pitched "tinkling" bowel sounds (腸鳴音亢進).
* **Hernial Orifices**: MUST palpate all groin/umbilical orifices for lumps.
* **Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)**: Empty rectum, blood/mucus (malignancy/ischemia), fecal impaction.

### Signs of Strangulation or Ischemia
* **Vital Signs**: Tachycardia (心跳過速), Hypotension (低血壓), Fever (發燒).
* **Pain**: Changes from colicky to **constant, severe pain**.
* **Abdominal Examination**: localized **Tenderness (壓痛)**, Rebound tenderness (反跳痛), Guarding/Rigidity (Peritonitis).
* **Sepsis**: Altered mental status, ↓ urine output.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### SBO vs LBO Discriminatory Features

| Feature | Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO) | Large Bowel Obstruction (LBO) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pain** | Central, frequent colic (3-5 mins) | Lower abdomen, infrequent (10-15 mins) |
| **Vomiting** | Early, often profuse & bilious | Late, may be feculent or absent |
| **Distension**| Moderate (unless distal SBO) | Marked (especially with competent IC valve) |
| **Most Common Cause**| **Adhesions (腸粘連)** | **Malignancy (惡性腫瘤)** |
| **AXR Findings**| Central, valvulae conniventes | Peripheral, haustra, often $>5$cm diameter |

### Differential Diagnosis of Paralytic Ileus (麻痺性腸梗阻) vs Mechanical Obstruction

| Feature | Mechanical Obstruction | Paralytic Ileus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pathology** | Physical blockage | Dysmotility (post-op, sepsis, ↓K+) |
| **Pain** | Colicky | Vague, mild, non-colicky |
| **Bowel Sounds** | Hyperactive, high-pitched "tinkling" | Absent or extremely hypoactive |
| **Radiology** | Transition point, collapsed distal bowel | Generalized gas in small & large bowel |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Initial Laboratory Investigations
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: ↑ WBC (leukocytosis suggests ischemia/strangulation), ↑ Hct (hemoconcentration from dehydration).
* **Renal Function Test (RFT)**: ↑ Urea, ↑ Creatinine (Pre-renal AKI), ↓ K+, ↓ Na+, ↓ Cl-.
* **Venous Blood Gas (VBG)**: Metabolic alkalosis (early, due to vomiting), High Lactate (Metabolic acidosis → highly suspicious for **Bowel Ischemia (腸缺血)**).
* **Group & Screen (G&S)** / Crossmatch: Essential pre-operative preparation.
* **Coagulation Profile**: PT/APTT/INR (preparation for OR).

### First-Line Imaging: Abdominal X-Ray (AXR)
* **Erect & Supine Views**: Standard baseline (though CT is superseding in HA).
* **SBO Findings**: Central loops, valvulae conniventes completely cross lumen, multiple air-fluid levels (Rule of 3s: >3cm width, >3 air-fluid levels, plicae >3mm thick).
* **LBO Findings**: Peripheral location, haustra do NOT cross entire lumen, cecal diameter >9cm indicates imminent perforation.
* **Volvulus Specifics**: "Coffee bean" sign (Sigmoid), "Fetal anomaly" sign (Cecal).

### Gold Standard Imaging: CT Abdomen & Pelvis with IV Contrast
* **Diagnostic Yield**: 90-95% sensitivity/specificity for obstruction.
* **Crucial Findings**:
* **Transition Point (過渡點)**: Dilated proximal bowel suddenly collapsing to decompressed distal bowel.
* **Cause**: Mass, hernia, volvulus "whirl sign".
* **Ischemia Signs (CRITICAL)**: **Pneumatosis intestinalis (腸壁積氣)** (gas in bowel wall), Portal venous gas, Lack of wall enhancement, "Target sign" (submucosal edema), Free intraperitoneal fluid/gas.

### Additional Local HA Protocols (Water-Soluble Contrast)
* **Gastrografin Follow-Through (SBO)**: Diagnostic (distinguishes complete vs partial) & Therapeutic (hyperosmolar, draws fluid into lumen, decreases bowel wall edema).
* **Water-Soluble Contrast Enema (WSCE)**: To differentiate LBO from acute colonic pseudo-obstruction (Ogilvie's syndrome).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Initial Resuscitation & Conservative Management (Drip and Suck)
* **NPO (Nil Per Os)**: Strict bowel rest.
* **Nasogastric Tube (NGT/Ryle's Tube)**: Decompression of stomach to prevent aspiration & relieve symptoms.
* **IV Fluid Resuscitation**: Aggressive crystalloids (Normal Saline or Hartmann's) to correct deficit & ongoing third-space losses.
* **Electrolyte Correction**: Aggressive K+ replacement once urine output is established.
* **Urinary Catheter**: Strict Input/Output (I/O) monitoring.
* **Analgesia**: IV Paracetamol or cautious opioids (avoid worsening ileus).

### Gastrografin Challenge Protocol for Adhesive SBO (HA Context)
* **Indication**: Uncomplicated adhesive SBO after initial resuscitation.
* **Administration**: 100mL neat Gastrografin (or diluted per local protocol) via NGT, clamp tube for 2-4 hours.
* **Assessment**: AXR at 4-24 hours.
* **Success**: Contrast reaches cecum/colon → implies partial obstruction → Diet escalation.
* **Failure**: Contrast retained in small bowel at 24 hrs → Indication for surgical exploration.

### Absolute Indications for Emergency Laparotomy
* **Clinical/Radiological signs of Strangulation/Ischemia**: Peritonitis, persistent tachycardia, fever, lactic acidosis, pneumatosis intestinalis.
* **Closed-Loop Obstruction**: Volvulus, complete LBO with competent ileocecal valve.
* **Incarcerated/Strangulated Hernia**: Irreducible with severe pain.
* **Failure of Conservative Management**: No clinical improvement after 48-72 hours (or failure of Gastrografin challenge).
* **Virgin Abdomen**: SBO in patient with NO previous abdominal surgeries (high suspicion of underlying tumor or complicated hernia).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles for Small Bowel Obstruction
* **Incision**: Midline laparotomy (Laparoscopy only if early, mild distension, single band adhesion expected).
* **Adhesiolysis (腸粘連鬆解術)**: Sharp/blunt dissection to release bands.
* **Assessment of Viability**: Check bowel color, peristalsis, and arterial pulsation (mesenteric border).
* **Ischemia Test**: Wrap in warm saline packs + 100% O2 for 10 mins. If non-viable → **Bowel Resection & Primary Anastomosis (腸切除吻合術)**.

### Operative Principles for Large Bowel Obstruction
* **Right-sided Malignancy**: **Right Hemicolectomy (右半結腸切除術)** + primary ileocolic anastomosis.
* **Left-sided Malignancy**: 
* **Hartmann's Procedure (哈特曼氏手術)**: Resection of lesion, end colostomy (結腸造口), closure of rectal stump (safest in emergencies/sepsis).
* **Resection & Primary Anastomosis**: With/without defunctioning loop ileostomy (if patient is stable, bowel well-perfused).
* **Endoscopic Stenting**: Palliative for advanced disease, or as "Bridge to Surgery" to convert emergency surgery to elective surgery (allowing pre-op staging/optimization).
* **Volvulus**: Sigmoidoscopy & flatus tube decompression (if no peritonitis) → Elective resection later. If peritonitis → Laparotomy & resection.

### Informed Consent for Bowel Resection
* **Procedure Details**: Laparotomy, ± Bowel resection, ± Stoma formation (CRITICAL to consent for stoma in all obstruction cases).
* **General Risks**: Bleeding, infection, DVT/PE, cardiorespiratory complications, death.
* **Specific Risks**: **Anastomotic leak (吻合口漏)** (life-threatening), Stoma complications (necrosis, retraction, prolapse, high-output), Paralytic ileus, Short bowel syndrome (if massive resection), Recurrent adhesions.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Ward Care
* **Hemodynamics**: Strict I/O, maintain IV fluids until oral intake established.
* **Analgesia**: PCA (Patient-Controlled Analgesia) or Epidural (reduces opioid use, promotes return of bowel function).
* **Tubes**: NGT on free drainage, remove when drainage significantly decreases (<200ml/day) & non-bilious.
* **VTE Prophylaxis**: LMWH (e.g., Clexane) + TED stockings.

### Diet Escalation & ERAS Protocols
* **Early Mobilization**: Crucial to prevent DVT, atelectasis, and stimulate peristalsis.
* **Sips of Water**: Start once NGT is removed and patient has no nausea.
* **Gradual Escalation**: Clear fluids → Free fluids → Soft diet → Normal diet, guided by passage of flatus/stool and absence of vomiting.
* **Stoma Care**: Stoma nurse involvement early for education on bag changing and output monitoring.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (Days 1-7)
* **Systemic**: Pneumonia, DVT/PE, Myocardial Infarction.
* **Wound**: Surgical Site Infection (SSI), Wound dehiscence/burst abdomen.
* **Gastrointestinal**: **Prolonged Paralytic Ileus (麻痺性腸梗阻)**.
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口漏)**: Usually presents Day 5-7 with fever, tachycardia, localized/generalized peritonitis, purulent/feculent drain output. Requires urgent re-laparotomy.

### Intermediate to Late Surgical Complications (Weeks to Years)
* **Stoma Complications**: High-output stoma (especially ileostomy → dehydration/AKI), parastomal hernia, prolapse, skin excoriation.
* **Recurrent Obstruction**: Adhesions are a lifelong risk.
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝)**: Due to fascial failure at midline laparotomy site.
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸綜合症)**: If massive enterectomy was required → malabsorption, requiring long-term TPN.

### Prognostic Factors in Intestinal Obstruction
* **Delay to Surgery**: Time from onset to OR directly correlates with mortality.
* **Strangulation/Necrosis**: Presence of dead bowel significantly ↑ mortality.
* **Age & Comorbidities**: ↑ risk in elderly with cardiopulmonary disease.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the rule of 3s in diagnosing Small Bowel Obstruction on AXR? Answer: Valvulae conniventes >3cm in diameter, >3 air-fluid levels on erect film, plicae circulares >3mm thick.
* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate between simple and strangulated bowel obstruction? Answer: Strangulation presents with constant (non-colicky) pain, tachycardia, localized tenderness, peritonitis, fever, and leukocytosis.
* Viva Question: What is a closed-loop obstruction and why is it a surgical emergency? Answer: The bowel is occluded at two adjacent points. It rapidly progresses to massive dilatation, ↑ intramural pressure, venous infarction, gangrene, and perforation because the gas/fluid has nowhere to escape.
* Trap: Assuming the passage of flatus or feces completely rules out an intestinal obstruction. Pearl: In early, partial, or high small bowel obstruction, the patient may still pass flatus/stool as the distal decompressed bowel empties its residual contents.
* Trap: Assuming all LBOs require emergency laparotomies with a colostomy. Pearl: Right-sided tumors often allow for primary anastomosis, and left-sided tumors can increasingly be managed with colonic stenting as a "bridge to surgery" in stable patients.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to meticulously examine all hernial orifices in a patient presenting with an acute abdomen. You must fully expose and palpate the inguinal, femoral, and umbilical regions to rule out an incarcerated hernia as the cause of SBO.
* Pitfall: Inadequately consenting an emergency obstruction patient. Always obtain consent for a possible stoma (colostomy/ileostomy) even if you plan a primary anastomosis, as intra-operative findings of sepsis or ischemia may mandate a stoma.
