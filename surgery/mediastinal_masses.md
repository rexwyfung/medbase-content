# Mediastinal Masses (縱膈腫塊)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Mediastinal Masses
* **Definition**: Any abnormal space-occupying lesion (tumor, cyst, or inflammatory mass) located within the **mediastinum (縱膈)**.
* **Classification**: Categorized anatomically into **Anterior**, **Middle**, and **Posterior** compartments to guide differential diagnosis and management.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Tuberculosis (TB) (結核病)**: Endemic in HK; ↑ incidence of **tuberculous mediastinal lymphadenopathy**.
* **Thymoma (胸腺瘤)**: Most common primary anterior mediastinal tumor in adults. Strong association with **Myasthenia Gravis (MG) (重症肌無力)**.
* **Lung Cancer (肺癌)** & **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)**: Common in HK; frequent causes of metastatic middle mediastinal lymphadenopathy.
* **Age Bimodality**: 
  * **Children/Young Adults**: ↑ Neurogenic tumors (posterior), Lymphoma, Germ Cell Tumors (GCT).
  * **Adults > 40yo**: ↑ Thymoma, Metastatic lymphadenopathy, Retrosternal goitre.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Mediastinum (縱膈)
* **Boundaries**: Thoracic inlet (superior) → Diaphragm (inferior); Sternum (anterior) → Vertebrae (posterior); Pleural cavities (lateral).
* **Anterior Compartment**: Sternum to anterior aspect of pericardium/great vessels. Contains: Thymus, lymph nodes, connective tissue.
* **Middle Compartment**: Anterior aspect of pericardium to anterior aspect of vertebral bodies. Contains: Heart, great vessels, trachea, bronchi, esophagus, lymph nodes.
* **Posterior Compartment**: Paravertebral gutters. Contains: Sympathetic chain, intercostal nerves, spinal nerve roots.

### Pathophysiology of Anterior Mediastinal Masses
* **The "4 Ts"**: 
  * **Thymoma/Thymic Carcinoma (胸腺瘤/胸腺癌)**: Epithelial neoplasm. Paraneoplastic autoimmunity (MG, pure red cell aplasia).
  * **Teratoma/Germ Cell Tumors (畸胎瘤/生殖細胞瘤)**: Extragonadal germ cell rests. Can be benign (mature) or malignant (seminoma/non-seminoma).
  * **Thyroid (Retrosternal Goitre) (胸骨後甲狀腺腫)**: Downward extension of cervical goitre into superior/anterior mediastinum.
  * **Terrible Lymphoma (淋巴瘤)**: Hodgkin's or Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma (NHL).

### Pathophysiology of Middle Mediastinal Masses
* **Lymphadenopathy (淋巴結腫大)**: Metastatic (lung/esophageal Ca), infectious (TB, histoplasmosis), inflammatory (Sarcoidosis).
* **Cystic Lesions**: 
  * **Bronchogenic Cyst**: Foregut duplication anomalies.
  * **Pericardial Cyst**: Mesothelial anlage defect.

### Pathophysiology of Posterior Mediastinal Masses
* **Neurogenic Tumors (神經源性腫瘤)**: 
  * **Nerve sheath**: Schwannoma (benign, most common), Neurofibroma.
  * **Autonomic ganglia**: Ganglioneuroma (benign), Neuroblastoma (malignant, pediatric).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Mass Effect Symptoms of Mediastinal Masses
* **Tracheal/Bronchial Compression**:
  * **Cough (咳嗽)**
  * **Dyspnea (呼吸困難)**
  * **Stridor (喘鳴)**
* **Esophageal Compression**:
  * **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**
* **Nerve Invasion/Compression**:
  * **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN)** → **Hoarseness (聲音沙啞)**
  * **Phrenic Nerve** → **Diaphragmatic paralysis (橫膈膜癱瘓)** (elevated hemidiaphragm on CXR)

### Specific Syndromic Presentations
* **Superior Vena Cava (SVC) Syndrome (上腔靜脈綜合症)**: 
  * **Facial/neck swelling (面部/頸部腫脹)**
  * **Plethoric facies**
  * **Distended neck veins**
  * **Pemberton's sign +ve**
* **Myasthenia Gravis (MG) (重症肌無力)** (Thymoma):
  * **Ptosis (眼瞼下垂)**
  * **Diplopia (複視)**
  * **Fatiguable proximal muscle weakness**
* **Horner's Syndrome (霍納氏綜合症)** (Apical/Posterior lesions involving sympathetic chain):
  * **Ptosis** (partial)
  * **Miosis (瞳孔縮小)**
  * **Anhidrosis (無汗症)**

### Systemic Symptoms of Mediastinal Masses
* **B Symptoms** (Lymphoma/TB):
  * **Fever**
  * **Night sweats**
  * **Unintentional weight loss**
* **Hyperthyroidism Signs** (Toxic retrosternal goitre):
  * **Tremor**
  * **Tachycardia**
  * **Heat intolerance**

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Compartmental Differential Diagnosis of Mediastinal Masses
| Compartment | Key Differentials | Distinguishing Clinical/Imaging Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Anterior** | **Thymoma** | Adult + MG; solid mass. |
| **Anterior** | **Teratoma / GCT** | Young male; calcification/fat/teeth on CT; ↑ AFP/β-hCG (non-seminoma). |
| **Anterior** | **Retrosternal Goitre** | Continuous with cervical thyroid; moves with swallowing; heterogenous enhancement. |
| **Anterior** | **Lymphoma** | B symptoms; bulky lobulated LN mass; responsive to steroids. |
| **Middle** | **Metastatic LN** | Known primary (e.g., Lung Ca); necrotic LNs on CT. |
| **Middle** | **TB Lymphadenitis** | Endemic in HK; rim-enhancing necrotic LNs; +ve QuantiFERON/T-SPOT. |
| **Middle** | **Bronchogenic Cyst** | Smooth, thin-walled, water-density paratracheal/subcarinal cyst. |
| **Posterior** | **Schwannoma** | Paravertebral sulcus; homogenous, well-circumscribed. |
| **Posterior** | **Neurofibroma** | Associated with NF1; "dumbbell" shape through intervertebral foramen. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Initial Blood Investigations & Tumor Markers
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**: R/O infection, hematological malignancy.
* **Tumor Markers (MANDATORY for anterior masses before biopsy)**:
  * **Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP)**: ↑ in Non-seminomatous GCT (NSGCT).
  * **Beta-human chorionic gonadotropin (β-hCG)**: ↑ in NSGCT (choriocarcinoma) and some seminomas.
* **Thyroid Function Tests (TFTs)**: If retrosternal goitre suspected.
* **Acetylcholine Receptor (AChR) Antibodies**: If Thymoma/MG suspected.

### First-line Imaging Modalities
* **Chest X-Ray (CXR) (PA & Lateral)**: 
  * Identifies mass location (anterior vs posterior on lateral view).
  * **Hilum overlay sign**: Differentiates hilar vs mediastinal mass.
  * Assesses tracheal deviation and diaphragmatic elevation (phrenic nerve palsy).

### Gold Standard Diagnostic Imaging
* **Contrast-Enhanced CT Thorax (對比增強電腦掃描)**:
  * Determines exact location, size, cystic vs solid components.
  * Assesses invasion into adjacent structures (vascular, pericardial, pleural).
* **MRI Thorax**:
  * Superior for assessing **neurovascular invasion**.
  * Gold standard for **posterior neurogenic tumors** (evaluates intraspinal "dumbbell" extension).
* **PET-CT**:
  * Staging for suspected lymphoma, lung cancer, or aggressive thymic carcinoma.

### Tissue Diagnosis & Biopsy Approaches
* **CT-guided Percutaneous Biopsy**:
  * Useful for middle/posterior masses or unresectable anterior masses.
  * **CONTRAINDICATED** in operable thymoma (risk of pleural seeding).
* **Endobronchial Ultrasound (EBUS) (支氣管內視鏡超聲波)**:
  * Gold standard for sampling paratracheal and subcarinal (Middle) LNs.
* **Video-Assisted Thoracoscopic Surgery (VATS) Biopsy**:
  * For pleural/mediastinal masses inaccessible by EBUS/CT-guided methods.
* **Excisional Biopsy**:
  * Performed for suspected benign cysts or operable thymoma directly.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Medical Management & Oncology Referrals
* **Lymphoma**: **Oncology referral**. Treatment is Chemo/RT. Surgery limited to diagnostic lymph node biopsy.
* **Non-Seminomatous GCT**: Primary platinum-based chemotherapy. Surgery ONLY for residual post-chemo mass.
* **Seminoma**: Highly radiosensitive/chemosensitive.
* **TB Lymphadenitis**: 6-9 months Anti-TB chemotherapy (HA DOTS protocol).

### Surgical Indications for Anterior Masses
* **Thymoma**: **Gold Standard = Upfront Surgical Resection** (Total Thymectomy) if locally resectable. Pre-op chemo if locally advanced/unresectable.
* **Mature Teratoma**: Surgical excision (prevents malignant transformation or rupture).
* **Retrosternal Goitre**: Total thyroidectomy (usually via cervical approach; sternotomy rarely needed unless massive or recurrent).

### Surgical Indications for Posterior Masses
* **Neurogenic Tumors**:
  * Surgical excision via VATS.
  * **Dumbbell tumors**: Require joint Thoracic + Neurosurgical approach (laminectomy + thoracic resection).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Approaches for Mediastinal Resection
* **Median Sternotomy (正中胸骨切開術)**:
  * Traditional gold standard for large anterior masses, invasive thymomas, retrosternal goitres requiring great vessel control.
* **VATS / Robotic-Assisted Thoracoscopic Surgery (RATS)**:
  * HA preferred approach for early-stage thymomas, small neurogenic tumors, and cysts.
  * Benefits: ↓ pain, ↓ LOS, quicker recovery.

### Oncological Principles of Thymectomy (胸腺切除術)
* **En Bloc Resection**: Total removal of thymus + tumor + surrounding mediastinal fat (phrenic nerve to phrenic nerve).
* **"No-Touch" Technique**: Avoid tumor capsule breach to prevent pleural seeding (Thymoma is highly prone to drop metastases).
* **Nerve Preservation**: Meticulous identification and preservation of bilateral phrenic nerves unless directly invaded by aggressive tumor.

### Surgical Consent & Specific Risks
* **Bleeding/Major Vessel Injury**: Proximity to SVC, aorta, pulmonary artery.
* **Nerve Injury**:
  * **Phrenic Nerve**: Unilateral → elevated diaphragm, dyspnea. Bilateral → ventilator dependence.
  * **RLN**: Hoarseness.
* **Chylothorax (乳糜胸)**: Injury to thoracic duct (especially posterior mediastinal dissection).
* **Conversion to Open Sternotomy/Thoracotomy**: Always consent for VATS/RATS.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Monitoring
* **Chest Drain Management**: Monitor for hemorrhage (>200ml/hr requires re-exploration) and air leak.
* **Analgesia**: Thoracic epidural or PCA (Paravertebral block for VATS). Crucial to prevent sputum retention and atelectasis.
* **CXR**: Day 1 post-op to check lung expansion and drain position.

### Specific Myasthenia Gravis Post-operative Care
* **ICU/HDU Monitoring**: High risk of **Myasthenic Crisis** (respiratory failure requiring mechanical ventilation) or **Cholinergic Crisis**.
* **Medication Management**: Coordinate with Neurologist. Restart anticholinesterases (e.g., Pyridostigmine) cautiously. Post-op pain/stress ↑ MG exacerbation risk.
* **Avoid Certain Drugs**: Aminoglycosides, beta-blockers, muscle relaxants (can precipitate MG crisis).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications
* **Hemorrhage**: From great vessels or internal mammary artery.
* **Respiratory Failure**: Extubation failure, especially in MG patients.
* **Arrhythmias**: Atrial fibrillation (due to pericardial irritation).

### Late Surgical Complications
* **Post-thoracotomy Pain Syndrome**: Chronic neuropathic pain.
* **Chylothorax**: Presents as milky pleural effusion once enteral feeding commences.

### Prognostic Factors in Thymoma (Masaoka-Koga Staging)
| Stage | Description | Management Principles |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I** | Macroscopically completely encapsulated, no microscopic capsular invasion. | Complete surgical resection alone. Excellent prognosis. |
| **II** | Microscopic transcapsular invasion or macroscopic invasion into surrounding fatty tissue. | Surgery + Consider post-op RT. |
| **III** | Macroscopic invasion into neighboring organs (pericardium, great vessels, lung). | Surgery (if resectable) + Post-op RT + Chemo. |
| **IVa** | Pleural or pericardial dissemination (drop metastases). | Chemo + Debulking surgery. |
| **IVb** | Lymphogenous or hematogenous metastasis. | Palliative Chemotherapy. |

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What are the "4 Ts" of anterior mediastinal masses? Answer: Thymoma, Teratoma (Germ Cell Tumor), Thyroid (Retrosternal goitre), and Terrible Lymphoma.
* Viva Question: A young male presents with a large anterior mediastinal mass. What blood tests must be checked before attempting any biopsy? Answer: Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) and beta-hCG to rule out a non-seminomatous germ cell tumor.
* Trap: Performing a percutaneous CT-guided core biopsy on a resectable anterior mediastinal mass suspected to be a thymoma. Pearl: Directly proceed to upfront surgical resection. Biopsying a thymoma risks violating the capsule and causing permanent pleural seeding.
* Pitfall: Rushing to resect a biopsy-proven lymphoma in the anterior mediastinum. Lymphomas are strictly managed medically with chemotherapy and radiotherapy; major surgery does not improve survival and delays systemic treatment.
* Viva Question: During a thymectomy, what are the anatomical boundaries of clearance to ensure complete en bloc resection? Answer: From the thoracic inlet superiorly, down to the diaphragm inferiorly, and strictly from one phrenic nerve to the contralateral phrenic nerve laterally.
* Trap: Assuming a patient's Myasthenia Gravis will immediately resolve following thymectomy. Pearl: Improvement is often gradual and unpredictable; some patients may even experience a transient post-operative myasthenic crisis requiring mechanical ventilation.
* Pitfall: Prescribing routine post-operative antibiotics like Gentamicin for a thymectomy patient. Aminoglycosides can severely block the neuromuscular junction and precipitate a life-threatening myasthenic crisis.
* Viva Question: An MRI of a posterior mediastinal mass shows a "dumbbell" appearance. What does this signify and what is the management implication? Answer: It signifies a neurogenic tumor extending through the intervertebral foramen into the spinal canal. It requires a joint thoracic and neurosurgical approach (laminectomy + resection) to safely remove.
