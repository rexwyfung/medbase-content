# Wound Healing, Scarring & Closure (傷口癒合、疤痕形成與縫合)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Wound Healing (傷口癒合)
* Complex biological sequence aimed at restoring tissue integrity & function post-injury.
* **Acute wound**: Proceeds through normal orderly phases → anatomical & functional restoration <4 weeks.
* **Chronic wound**: Arrested in one of the healing phases (usually inflammatory) → failure to heal >4 weeks (e.g., **Diabetic foot ulcer (糖尿病足部潰瘍)**).

### Types of Wound Closure Mechanisms
* **Primary Intention (第一期癒合)**: Direct apposition of clean wound edges (e.g., surgical incision closed w/ sutures/staples). Minimal scarring.
* **Secondary Intention (第二期癒合)**: Wound left open to heal via granulation tissue formation, contraction & re-epithelialization (e.g., abscess cavity post-I&D, pressure ulcers). Prominent scarring.
* **Tertiary Intention / Delayed Primary Closure (第三期癒合 / 延遲一期縫合)**: Wound deliberately left open for 3-5 days to clear heavy contamination/infection, then formally closed.

### Local Hong Kong Epidemiology & Risk Factors
* **Keloid (蟹足腫)** predisposition: ↑ incidence in Asian/Southern Chinese populations (approx. 15-20% higher risk vs Caucasians).
* **Diabetic Wounds**: Very high burden in HK HA hospitals due to ↑ prevalence of T2DM in elderly population.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Skin Anatomy Relevant to Wound Closure
* **Epidermis (表皮)**: Avascular, composed of keratinocytes. Heals by re-epithelialization.
* **Dermis (真皮)**: Contains collagen, elastin, blood vessels, appendages. Provides structural strength. Submucosal/dermal layer dictates tensile strength of closure.
* **Langer’s Lines (蘭格氏線)**: Natural skin tension lines. Incisions parallel to Langer's lines → ↓ tension → optimal cosmetic scar.

### Phase 1: Haemostasis & Inflammatory Phase (發炎期) (Days 0-5)
* **Haemostasis**: Vasoconstriction → Platelet aggregation → Fibrin clot formation.
* **Inflammation**: Vasodilation + ↑ capillary permeability. 
* Key cells: **Neutrophils (嗜中性白血球)** (days 1-2, phagocytosis) → **Macrophages (巨噬細胞)** (days 3-5, crucial for orchestrating healing via release of VEGF, TGF-β, PDGF).

### Phase 2: Proliferative Phase (增生期) (Days 3-21)
* **Angiogenesis**: Endothelial cell proliferation driven by VEGF.
* **Fibroplasia**: Fibroblasts lay down **Type III Collagen (第三型膠原蛋白)**.
* **Granulation**: Formation of beefy red, highly vascular tissue.
* **Epithelialization**: Basal keratinocytes migrate across wound bed.

### Phase 3: Remodelling Phase (重塑期) (Day 21 - up to 2 years)
* **Collagen Replacement**: Type III collagen replaced by stronger **Type I Collagen (第一型膠原蛋白)**.
* **Wound Contraction**: Driven by **Myofibroblasts (肌纖維母細胞)**.
* **Tensile Strength**: 20% at 3 weeks → Max 80% of uninjured skin at 1 year (NEVER reaches 100%).

### Pathophysiology of Abnormal Scarring
* Prolonged inflammation / ↑ tension → Excessive TGF-β & PDGF → Hyperactive fibroblasts → Overproduction of dense collagen network → **Hypertrophic/Keloid scar**.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Clinical Signs of Normal Wound Healing
* Post-op Days 1-4: Mild erythema (rubor), swelling (tumor), warmth (calor), minimal serosanguinous exudate.
* Post-op Days 5-14: Wound edges opposed, healing ridge palpable (collagen deposition), ↓ erythema.

### Signs of Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (手術部位感染)
* **Systemic**: Fever, ↑ HR.
* **Local**: 
  * Spreading erythema (>2cm beyond wound margin)
  * Purulent discharge (pus)
  * Increasing localized pain/tenderness
  * Wound breakdown/dehiscence
  * Malodour

### Signs of Wound Dehiscence (傷口裂開)
* **Superficial**: Separation of skin/subcuticular layer only.
* **Deep/Fascial**: "Popping" sensation felt by patient.
* **Pathognomonic Sign**: Profuse, suddenly ↑ **salmon-coloured (pink) serous discharge** from laparotomy wound (heralds fascial dehiscence / incipient evisceration).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Hypertrophic Scar (增生性疤痕) vs Keloid Scar (蟹足腫)
| Feature | Hypertrophic Scar (增生性疤痕) | Keloid (蟹足腫) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Borders** | Remains **WITHIN** boundaries of original wound | Extends **BEYOND** original wound margins (claw-like) |
| **Onset** | Early (within 1-2 months) | Late (months to years post-injury) |
| **Location** | Anywhere (often extensor surfaces across joints) | Sternal chest, earlobes, shoulders, upper back |
| **Progression** | May spontaneously regress over time | Rarely regresses, progressive |
| **Genetics/Race**| Less genetic link | ↑↑ Strong familial/racial predilection (Asians/Africans) |
| **Symptoms** | Less symptomatic | Often intensely pruritic (itchy) or painful |

### Differential Diagnosis for Chronic Non-healing Wounds
| Ulcer Type | Location | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Venous (靜脈性)** | Gaiter area (medial malleolus) | Shallow, exudative, irregular margins, lipodermatosclerosis |
| **Arterial (動脈性)** | Toes, lateral malleolus, pressure points | Punched out, necrotic/pale base, painful, absent pulses |
| **Neuropathic (神經性)**| Plantar surface (metatarsal heads) | Deep, calloused edges, painless, loss of protective sensation |
| **Malignant (惡性)** | Anywhere (often chronic burn/scar) | Raised everted edges, **Marjolin's Ulcer (SCC)**, bleeds easily |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Bedside Workup for Chronic/Infected Wounds
* **Wound Swab for C/ST**: Taken strictly AFTER debridement/cleaning from deep tissue (avoid superficial colonizers).
* **Ankle-Brachial Index (ABI)**: Mandatory for leg ulcers to assess arterial supply before applying compression therapy (Do NOT compress if ABI < 0.8).
* **Monofilament Test**: Assess neuropathy in diabetic feet.

### Laboratory Investigations for Poor Wound Healing
* **Infection markers**: CBC (↑ WCC), CRP, ESR.
* **Nutritional status**: Albumin, Prealbumin (better reflection of acute nutrition).
* **Diabetic control**: HbA1c, Fasting Glucose (poor control → ↓ leukocyte function).
* **Renal function**: RFT (uremia impairs healing).

### Imaging & Invasive Workup for Complex Wounds
* **X-ray / MRI**: To rule out underlying **Osteomyelitis (骨髓炎)** if bone is probed or chronic ulcer.
* **Tissue Biopsy**: Wedge biopsy of chronic ulcer edge to rule out **Marjolin’s Ulcer (馬喬林潰瘍)** (Squamous Cell Carcinoma).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Suture Material Selection Algorithm (縫線選擇)
| Material Type | Examples | Key Properties | Surgical Indications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Absorbable, Braided** | Vicryl (Polyglactin) | Good handling, moderate reactivity | Subcutaneous tissue, bowel anastomosis |
| **Absorbable, Monofilament** | Monocryl, PDS | Smooth passage, longer tensile retention (PDS) | Subcuticular closure, biliary/fascial closure (PDS) |
| **Non-Absorbable, Monofilament**| Prolene, Nylon | Inert, ↓ tissue reaction, requires removal | Skin closure, vascular anastomosis (Prolene) |
| **Non-Absorbable, Braided** | Silk | Excellent knot security, highly reactive | Securing drains, mucosal ties |

### HK Hospital Authority (HA) Protocol for Tetanus Prophylaxis (破傷風預防)
| Vaccination History | Clean Wound | Tetanus-Prone Wound (Contaminated, deep, crush, >6h) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Complete course (last dose <5 yrs)** | Nothing | Nothing |
| **Complete course (last dose 5-10 yrs)**| Nothing | Give **Tetanus Toxoid (TT)** vaccine |
| **Complete course (last dose >10 yrs)**| Give TT vaccine | Give **TT vaccine** + **Human Tetanus Immunoglobulin (HTIG)** |
| **Unknown / Incomplete** | Give TT vaccine | Give **TT vaccine** + **HTIG** |

### Management Algorithm for Abnormal Scarring (Keloids/Hypertrophic)
1. **1st Line (Preventative/Conservative)**: Silicone gel sheets (↑ hydration, ↓ capillary activity), pressure garments (esp. burns).
2. **2nd Line (Medical)**: Intra-lesional **Triamcinolone (類固醇注射)** injections (repeated every 3-4 weeks) → flattens scar, ↓ pruritus.
3. **3rd Line (Surgical)**: Surgical excision + IMMEDIATE post-op radiotherapy or steroid injection (excising keloid alone has >50% recurrence rate).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Core Principles of Wound Closure (傷口縫合原則)
* **Tension-free closure**: Undermine wound edges to minimize tension (prevents necrosis & wide scarring).
* **Tissue Eversion**: Slight eversion of skin edges with sutures (compensates for subsequent scar retraction).
* **Elimination of Dead Space**: Meticulous deep dermal/subcutaneous suturing → prevents seroma/haematoma formation (nidus for infection).
* **Atraumatic technique**: Use fine forceps (e.g., Adson/toothed), avoid crushing skin edges.

### Wound Debridement Principles (清創手術)
* **Objective**: Convert chronic wound to acute wound.
* **T.I.M.E. Concept**:
  * **T**issue: Remove necrotic, non-viable slough.
  * **I**nfection/Inflammation: Control bioburden.
  * **M**oisture balance: Use appropriate dressings (absorptive vs hydrating).
  * **E**dge of wound: Excision of hyperkeratotic edges to promote epithelial advancement.

### Consent Issues in Wound Management & Scarring
* **Mandatory Disclosures**: 
  * Infection, bleeding, wound breakdown.
  * Unpredictability of final cosmetic result.
  * Explicitly mention risk of **hypertrophic/keloid scarring**, especially in high-risk zones (sternum, deltoid) and local Asian demographics.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Standard Post-operative Wound Care Protocols
* **Dressings**: Keep clean and dry. First dressing typically removed at 48 hours to inspect wound, unless signs of systemic sepsis dictate earlier inspection.
* **Showering**: Allowed after 48h if wound is closed and dry.

### Suture / Staple Removal Timing Algorithm
* Removal timing balances tensile strength vs. risk of "railroad track" scarring.
* **Face / Neck**: 3 - 5 days (excellent blood supply, ↑ cosmetic priority).
* **Scalp**: 7 days.
* **Chest / Abdomen**: 7 - 10 days.
* **Back / Limbs**: 10 - 14 days.
* **Over extensor joints**: 14 days (subjected to high mechanical stress).

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Wound Complications (<30 days)
* **Haematoma / Seroma**: Fluid collection due to inadequate haemostasis or unclosed dead space. Requires aspiration or opening of wound.
* **Surgical Site Infection (SSI)**: Usually Day 3-7 post-op. Pathogens: *S. aureus* (most common), *Streptococcus*, enteric gram-negatives (if GI surgery).
* **Wound Dehiscence / Burst Abdomen (腹部爆裂)**: Surgical emergency. Requires immediate coverage w/ saline-soaked gauze → return to OT for tension-free mass closure (retention sutures).

### Late Surgical Wound Complications (>30 days)
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝氣)**: Fascial breakdown leading to peritoneal sac protrusion. Requires mesh hernioplasty.
* **Contractures**: Severely limits ROM across a joint (common in burns or wounds crossing flexor creases perpendicular to Langer's lines).
* **Sinus Tract / Fistula Formation**: Due to retained foreign body (e.g., unabsorbed deep suture, mesh) or chronic deep infection.

### Prognostic Factors for Poor Wound Healing (The "DID NOT HEAL" mnemonic)
* **D**iabetes, **I**nfection, **D**rugs (Steroids, Immunosuppressants).
* **N**utritional deficit (↓ Vit C, ↓ Zinc, ↓ Protein), **O**xygenation/hypoxia, **T**issue trauma/tension.
* **H**aematoma, **E**lderly age, **A**rterial insufficiency, **L**ocal recurrence of malignancy.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the most critical cellular factor required for collagen synthesis during the proliferative phase? Answer: Vitamin C (Ascorbic acid) is essential for the hydroxylation of proline and lysine residues in collagen.
* Trap: Prescribing antibiotics for all wounds presenting with erythema. Pearl: Post-operative wound erythema in the first 24-48 hours is usually the normal physiological inflammatory phase, not an infection requiring antibiotics.
* Pitfall: Re-suturing an infected surgical wound that has broken down. Always leave infected dehisced wounds open to heal by secondary intention or use delayed primary closure once clinically clean.
* Viva Question: A patient complains of sudden copious pink fluid leaking from their laparotomy wound on post-op day 5. What is the diagnosis and immediate management? Answer: This is fascial dehiscence (burst abdomen). The management is urgent application of sterile saline-soaked gauze, abdominal binder, IV fluids, and emergent return to theater for definitive mass closure.
* Trap: Treating a keloid scar with simple surgical excision. Pearl: Simple excision of a keloid has a massive recurrence rate (>50%) and often results in an even larger keloid. It must be combined with adjuvant therapy like intra-lesional steroids or localized radiotherapy.
* Pitfall: Removing facial sutures at 10 days. Facial sutures must be removed early (3-5 days) to prevent permanent punctate "railroad track" scarring, as the face heals very rapidly due to robust vascularity.
* Viva Question: What are the key properties of Prolene (Polypropylene) and when is it indicated? Answer: Prolene is a non-absorbable, monofilament synthetic suture. It elicits minimal tissue reaction and maintains high tensile strength indefinitely, making it the gold standard for vascular anastomoses and fine skin closure.
