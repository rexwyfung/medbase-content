# Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor (胃腸道基質腫瘤)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of GIST
* **Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor (GIST)**: Most common mesenchymal tumor of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract.
* Arises from the **Interstitial cells of Cajal (Cajal間質細胞)** (the pacemaker cells of the gut).
* Primarily driven by activating mutations in receptor tyrosine kinases (**c-KIT** or **PDGFRA**).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Incidence**: ~1.5 per 100,000 population in Hong Kong.
* **Age**: Median onset 60-65 years.
* **Gender**: Male = Female.
* **Malignancy Potential**: All GISTs possess malignant potential; categorized into risk groups rather than simply "benign" or "malignant".

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Anatomical Distribution of GIST
* **Stomach (胃)**: 60% (Most common, best prognosis).
* **Small Intestine (小腸)**: 30% (Worse prognosis than gastric).
* **Colorectum (結直腸)**: <5%.
* **Esophagus (食道)** / Others: <5%.

### Cellular Origin and Lineage
* Originates from **Interstitial cells of Cajal (ICC)** located within the **muscularis propria** layer of the GI wall.

### Molecular Pathophysiology
* **c-KIT (CD117) Mutation**: ~80%. Constitutive activation of KIT tyrosine kinase → unchecked cell proliferation & apoptosis evasion.
* **PDGFRA Mutation**: ~10%. Often in gastric GISTs, specifically exon 18 (D842V) mutation → intrinsic resistance to Imatinib.
* **Wild-type GIST**: ~10%. Lacks KIT/PDGFRA mutations. Often SDH (Succinate Dehydrogenase) deficient or involves BRAF/NF1 mutations.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Common Clinical Presentations of GIST
* **GI Bleeding (胃腸道出血)**: Most common presentation. Mucosal ulceration over the submucosal tumor → melena, hematemesis, or occult bleeding.
* **Anemia (貧血)**: Secondary to chronic occult blood loss → lethargy, exertional dyspnea.
* **Abdominal Pain (腹痛)**: Vague, non-specific discomfort.
* **Mass Effect**: Early satiety (gastric), bowel obstruction (small bowel), intussusception.
* **Acute Acute Abdomen**: Severe peritonitis if tumor rupture (腫瘤破裂) occurs (rare but catastrophic).

### Physical Examination Findings
* **Palpable Abdominal Mass (腹部腫塊)**: Often smooth, mobile, non-tender.
* **Signs of Anemia**: Conjunctival pallor (蒼白), tachycardia.
* **Incidental Finding**: ~30% asymptomatic, found during endoscopy/imaging for other reasons.

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differentials for GI Submucosal Mass
| Condition | Discriminatory Features | Endoscopy / EUS Findings | IHC/Markers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GIST** | Most common. Bleeding risk. | Submucosal, central ulceration. 4th layer (muscularis propria). | **CD117 +ve, DOG1 +ve** |
| **Leiomyoma (平滑肌瘤)** | Asymptomatic. Esophagus/Stomach. | Hypoechoic on EUS. 4th layer. | Desmin +ve, CD117 -ve |
| **Schwannoma (神經鞘瘤)** | Slow growing. | Hypoechoic on EUS. 4th layer. | S-100 +ve, CD117 -ve |
| **Neuroendocrine Tumor (NET)** | Carcinoid syndrome (rare). | 2nd/3rd layer. Yellowish appearance. | Chromogranin A +ve |
| **Lymphoma (淋巴瘤)** | Systemic B symptoms. | Thickened mucosal folds. | CD20 +ve |
| **Gastric Adenocarcinoma** | Weight loss, cachexia. | Irregular mucosal ulcer/mass. | Cytokeratin +ve |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Blood Tests and Laboratory Investigations
* **CBC**: Check Hb level (↓ Hb if bleeding).
* **RFT / LFT**: Baseline organ function, check for liver metastases.
* **Coagulation Profile / Group & Screen**: Prep for potential transfusion/surgery.
* **Tumor Markers**: Not useful (CEA, CA19-9 are usually normal).

### Endoscopic Investigations
* **OGD (胃鏡) / Colonoscopy**: Identifies submucosal bulge with normal overlying mucosa. "Target sign" (central umbilication/ulceration).
* **Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS) (內視鏡超聲波)**: Differentiates layer of origin. GIST classically appears as a **hypoechoic, homogeneous mass arising from the 4th layer (muscularis propria)**.

### Biopsy Guidelines for GIST
* **EUS-FNA (EUS-guided Fine Needle Aspiration)**: Preferred modality for tissue diagnosis.
* **Percutaneous Image-Guided Biopsy**: **CONTRAINDICATED** if tumor is primarily resectable (high risk of tumor rupture and intraperitoneal seeding). Reserved for metastatic disease or pre-neoadjuvant confirmation.

### Radiological Staging Imaging
* **Contrast-Enhanced CT Abdomen & Pelvis**: Gold standard. Assesses size, local invasion, and distant metastases. Typical appearance: Hypervascular, exophytic solid mass.
* **MRI Pelvis**: Mandatory for rectal GIST to assess relationship to sphincter complex.
* **PET-CT Scan**: Highly sensitive. Used for assessing early response to neoadjuvant Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitor (TKI) therapy or evaluating ambiguous metastatic lesions.

### Pathological & Immunohistochemistry (IHC) Profile
* **CD117 (c-KIT)**: +ve (>95%).
* **DOG1 (Discovered on GIST-1)**: +ve (>98%). High sensitivity/specificity.
* **CD34**: +ve (~70%).
* **Desmin / S-100**: -ve (helps rule out leiomyoma/schwannoma).
* **Mitotic Index**: Crucial for risk stratification (measured per 50 HPF or 5mm²).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Surgical Indications for Resection
* **All symptomatic GISTs** (bleeding, pain, obstruction) regardless of size.
* **All localized GISTs ≥2cm**.
* **Suspicious EUS features**: Irregular borders, cystic spaces, heterogeneous echo.

### Management of Small Incidental GISTs (<2cm)
* **Gastric GISTs <2cm**: Conservative HA protocol → Active surveillance via EUS/OGD every 1-2 years.
* **Non-Gastric (Small Bowel/Colorectal) GISTs <2cm**: Immediate surgical resection (inherently higher risk of aggressive behavior).

### Medical Therapy: Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors (TKI)
* **1st Line**: **Imatinib (伊馬替尼) / Glivec**. (Standard dose: 400mg daily).
* **2nd Line**: **Sunitinib** (for Imatinib failure or exon 9 mutations).
* **3rd Line**: **Regorafenib**.
* **Mutation Testing Requirement**: Mandatory before initiating TKI (e.g., PDGFRA D842V mutation is completely resistant to Imatinib).

### Neoadjuvant Imatinib Indications
* Marginally resectable tumors.
* Large tumors where shrinkage allows organ preservation (e.g., avoiding total gastrectomy or Abdominoperineal Resection for rectal GIST).
* Duration: 6-9 months, with response monitored via CT/PET-CT before surgery.

### Adjuvant Imatinib Guidelines (HK HA Standard)
* Indicated strictly for **High-Risk GISTs** post-resection (based on Miettinen criteria).
* **Duration**: **3 years** of Imatinib (400mg daily) significantly improves Recurrence-Free Survival (RFS) and Overall Survival (OS) compared to 1 year.
* Ruptured GIST requires lifelong Imatinib.

### Metastatic GIST Management Algorithm
* Primary treatment is medical: **Systemic Imatinib**.
* Palliative surgery/RFA/TACE considered only for limited focal progression, symptomatic bleeding, or bowel obstruction.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Core Surgical Principles for GIST
* **R0 Resection**: Aim for macroscopically and microscopically negative margins.
* **Clearance Margin**: 1-2 cm grossly clear margin is sufficient (unlike 5cm in adenocarcinoma).
* **Minimal Handling**: Grasp normal tissue, NOT the tumor ("No-touch" technique).
* **En Bloc Resection**: If adherent to adjacent organs, must resect en bloc to avoid disrupting the tumor capsule.

### The "No Rupture" Mandate
* **Crucial Rule**: Absolutely avoid tumor rupture (腫瘤破裂).
* **Consequence**: Intraoperative rupture causes intraperitoneal dissemination → immediately upgrades tumor to metastatic equivalent → profoundly worsens prognosis.

### Lymphadenectomy Requirement
* **NO Routine Lymph Node Dissection**.
* GIST spreads hematogenously (liver) or via peritoneum. Lymphatic spread is extremely rare (<2%).

### Specific Operative Approaches
* **Gastric GIST**: Laparoscopic/Open Wedge Resection (楔形切除術). Preserve cardia and pylorus if possible.
* **Small Bowel GIST**: Segmental Small Bowel Resection (小腸切除術) with primary anastomosis.
* **Rectal GIST**: TME, LAR, or APR depending on height. High morbidity, hence neoadjuvant Imatinib is heavily favored to shrink tumor first.

### Informed Consent Details
* **General Risks**: Bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding structures.
* **Specific Risks**: Anastomotic leak (吻合口漏), tumor rupture during manipulation.
* **Long-term**: Permanent stoma (for low rectal GIST), possibility of requiring 3 years to lifelong targeted therapy (Imatinib).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care (ERAS Protocol)
* **Analgesia**: Multi-modal, opiate-sparing.
* **Diet**: Early oral feeding (早期進食) once nausea subsides.
* **Mobility**: Early mobilization (早期下床活動) to prevent DVT/chest infection.
* **Drains**: Remove as early as possible if output is minimal and non-bilious/non-feculent.

### Long-Term Surveillance Strategy
* High-risk patients: Contrast CT Abdomen & Pelvis every 3-6 months for 3 years, then every 6-12 months up to 10 years.
* Low-risk patients: CT every 6-12 months for 5 years.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications (<30 days)
* **Hemorrhage (出血)**: Intraluminal or intraperitoneal.
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口漏)**: Presents with fever, tachycardia, localized peritonitis, or purulent drain output.
* **Paralytic Ileus (腸麻痺)**.
* **Wound Infection / Dehiscence**.

### Late Surgical Complications
* **Adhesional Intestinal Obstruction (粘連性腸梗阻)**.
* **Incisional Hernia**.

### TKI (Imatinib) Specific Complications
* **Fluid Retention**: Classically periorbital edema.
* **GI Upset**: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.
* **Dermatological**: Skin rash.
* **Hepatotoxicity**: Requires monitoring of LFTs.

### Prognostic Risk Stratification System
* **Miettinen Criteria (AFIP Criteria)**: Defines recurrence risk (Very Low, Low, Intermediate, High).
* **Three Key Prognostic Factors**:
  1. **Tumor Size** (<2cm, 2-5cm, 5-10cm, >10cm).
  2. **Mitotic Count** (≤5 vs >5 per 50 HPF).
  3. **Tumor Location** (Gastric = better prognosis; Small Bowel/Rectal = worse prognosis).

| Size | Mitotic Count (/50 HPF) | Gastric GIST Risk | Small Bowel GIST Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ≤2 cm | ≤5 | Very Low | Low |
| 2-5 cm | ≤5 | Low | Low |
| >5 cm | ≤5 | Low | High |
| <2 cm | >5 | None expected | High |
| 2-5 cm | >5 | Moderate | High |
| >5 cm | >5 | High | High |

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the gold standard imaging for staging a newly diagnosed GIST? Answer: Contrast-enhanced CT of the abdomen and pelvis.
* Viva Question: Why do we not perform routine lymphadenectomy in GIST resection? Answer: GIST spreads primarily via the hematogenous route to the liver and by intraperitoneal seeding. Lymphatic spread is exceptionally rare (<2%).
* Trap: Assuming all incidental small GI nodules should be biopsied percutaneously for diagnosis. Pearl: Percutaneous biopsy of a resectable GIST is contraindicated due to the high risk of tumor rupture and needle-tract seeding.
* Pitfall: Resecting a 1.5cm incidental gastric GIST aggressively in an asymptomatic patient. Active endoscopic surveillance is the HA standard for asymptomatic gastric GISTs <2cm without high-risk EUS features.
* Trap: Giving Imatinib blindly to all GIST patients. Pearl: Always verify the mutation status. PDGFRA D842V mutated GISTs are highly resistant to Imatinib.
* Viva Question: During surgical mobilization, the tumor capsule tears and contents spill. What is the immediate consequence for patient management? Answer: Tumor rupture automatically upgrades the disease to stage IV / highest risk category, mandating lifelong adjuvant Imatinib therapy.
* Viva Question: What determines the duration of adjuvant Imatinib therapy for a high-risk GIST patient? Answer: Based on clinical trials, high-risk patients receive 3 years of adjuvant Imatinib, which shows significant survival benefit over 1 year.
* Pitfall: Planning a 5cm oncological resection margin for a suspected GIST. A 1-2cm macroscopically clear margin is entirely sufficient; wide margins do not improve survival in GIST.
