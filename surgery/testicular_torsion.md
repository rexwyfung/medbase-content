# Testicular Torsion (睪丸扭轉)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Testicular Torsion
* **Definition**: Acute twisting of the **Spermatic Cord (精索)** resulting in vascular compromise, ischaemia, and eventual infarction of the **Testicle (睪丸)**.
* **Classification**:
* **Intravaginal Torsion**: Occurs within the **Tunica Vaginalis (睪丸鞘膜)**; classic adolescent presentation.
* **Extravaginal Torsion**: Entire cord and tunica vaginalis twist together; classic neonatal presentation.

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Demographics
* **Peak Age**: Bimodal distribution.
* **Neonates**: First year of life (extravaginal).
* **Adolescents**: Ages 12-18 years (intravaginal).
* **HA Context**: Universally triaged as a Category I/II surgical emergency in Hong Kong A&E.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Anatomical Basis of Torsion
* **Bell-Clapper Deformity (鐘擺畸形)**: Abnormally high attachment of the **Tunica Vaginalis (睪丸鞘膜)** to the spermatic cord.
* **Consequence**: Testis hangs freely within the scrotal sac like a clapper in a bell, predisposing to rotation.
* **Bilaterality**: The defect is typically bilateral, mandating contralateral surgical fixation.

### Pathophysiological Cascade
* **Twisting**: The spermatic cord rotates, immediately altering vascular hemodynamics.
* **Venous Occlusion**: Thin-walled veins compress first → venous congestion → oedema.
* **Compartment Syndrome**: Oedema within the non-distensible tunica albuginea ↑ interstitial pressure.
* **Arterial Compromise**: Pressure exceeds arterial perfusion pressure → acute ischaemia.
* **Reperfusion Injury**: Re-establishing blood flow releases **Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) (活性氧)** causing further tissue damage.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Cardinal Symptoms
* **Onset**: Sudden, severe, unilateral **Scrotal Pain (陰囊痛)**.
* **Sleep Disruption**: Frequently awakens the patient from sleep.
* **Autonomic Symptoms**: Nausea and vomiting (due to visceral pain radiation).
* **Trauma History**: Can occasionally follow minor trauma or physical activity (e.g., cycling).

### Key Clinical Signs
* **Inspection**: Erythematous, oedematous, and asymmetrical scrotum.
* **Testicular Lie**: High-riding and horizontally oriented testis.
* **Palpation**: Exquisitely tender and hardened testis.
* **Cremasteric Reflex (提睪肌反射)**: Strictly absent.
* **Prehn's Sign**: Pain is NOT relieved (or worsens) upon manual elevation of the testis.

### TWIST Scoring System (Risk Stratification)
| Symptom/Sign | Points |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Testicular Swelling** | 2 |
| **Hard Testis** | 2 |
| **Absent Cremasteric Reflex** | 1 |
| **Nausea / Vomiting** | 1 |
| **High-Riding Testis** | 1 |
* **Low Risk**: 0-2 (Consider US to rule out).
* **Intermediate Risk**: 3-4 (US warranted).
* **High Risk**: 5-7 (Direct to Operating Theatre; skip US).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Discriminatory Differentials for Acute Scrotum
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Acute Epididymo-Orchitis (急性附睪睪丸炎)** | Gradual onset, +ve Prehn's sign, +ve Cremasteric reflex, Pyuria/Dysuria. |
| **Torsion of Hydatid of Morgagni (睪丸附件扭轉)** | "Blue Dot Sign" (藍點徵) visible through skin, localized upper pole tenderness. |
| **Incarcerated Inguinal Hernia (嵌頓性腹股溝疝)** | Bowel sounds in scrotum, palpable mass extending to deep inguinal ring. |
| **Idiopathic Scrotal Oedema** | Bilateral erythema, minimal pain, normal testicular palpation (common in boys <10). |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Primary Bedside Workup
* **Urinalysis (尿液分析)**: To rule out UTI (leukocyte esterase/nitrites). Normal in torsion.
* **Pre-op Bloods**: CBC, LRFT, Clotting profile, Group & Save (routine pre-op for emergency theatre).

### Role of Imaging (Ultrasound)
* **Rule**: Ultrasound MUST NOT delay definitive surgical exploration if clinical suspicion is high.
* **Modality**: **Colour Doppler Ultrasound (彩色多普勒超聲波)**.
* **Torsion Findings**:
* Decreased or totally absent intratesticular blood flow.
* **"Whirlpool Sign" (漩渦徵)**: Spiral twist of the spermatic cord at the external inguinal ring.
* Enlarged, heterogeneous testis (late ischaemic change).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Pre-Hospital & Emergency Department Algorithm
* **Triage**: Category I/II triage.
* **Analgesia**: Fast-acting IV opioid analgesia.
* **NBM Status**: Nil by mouth immediately for emergency surgery.
* **Manual Detorsion**:
* Can be attempted in A&E ("Open Book" method: medial to lateral rotation).
* Pain relief is immediate if successful.
* **Mandatory Caveat**: Does NOT replace surgery. Patient still requires emergency exploration.

### Surgical Indications
* **High Clinical Suspicion**: TWIST score 5-7 → Immediate **Emergency Scrotal Exploration (緊急陰囊探查術)**.
* **Equivocal Cases**: TWIST score 3-4 with negative/inconclusive Doppler US, but pain persists → Explore.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Pre-operative Consent Elements
* **Orchidectomy (睪丸切除術)**: Must warn patient/parents if the testis is gangrenous/unsalvageable.
* **Bilateral Orchidopexy (雙側睪丸固定術)**: Fixing both testes to prevent recurrence.
* **Infertility (不育)**: Risk of subfertility even if salvaged.
* **Atrophy**: Salvaged testis may still shrink over time.

### Surgical Steps: Scrotal Exploration
* **Incision**: Midline raphe or transverse scrotal incision.
* **Delivery**: Open the tunica vaginalis and deliver the affected testis.
* **Detorsion**: Untwist the spermatic cord.
* **Viability Check**: Wrap in warm saline-soaked gauze and administer 100% O2 for 5-10 minutes. Check for return of pink colour and arterial pulsation.

### Surgical Steps: Definitive Fixation
* **Viable Testis**:
* Perform **Bilateral Orchidopexy (雙側睪丸固定術)**.
* Use 3-point fixation with non-absorbable sutures (e.g., 3-0 Prolene).
* Suture tunica albuginea to dartos pouch, carefully avoiding the epididymis.
* **Non-Viable Testis**:
* Perform **Orchidectomy (睪丸切除術)** on the affected side.
* Perform prophylactic orchidopexy on the contralateral healthy side.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative Care
* **Scrotal Support**: Wear tight underwear or scrotal suspensory support to reduce oedema.
* **Analgesia**: Paracetamol + NSAIDs (if no contraindications).
* **Wound Care**: Keep dry for 48 hours; absorbable skin sutures usually fall out in 1-2 weeks.

### Rehabilitation & Discharge Advice
* **Activity Restriction**: Avoid strenuous exercise, heavy lifting, or cycling for 4-6 weeks to allow fixation to heal.
* **Follow-up**: Arrange outpatient clinic (SOPD) review in 6-8 weeks to check wound and assess testicular volume.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Prognostic Timeline (Time is Testicle)
* **< 6 hours**: > 90% salvage rate.
* **12 hours**: ~ 50% salvage rate.
* **> 24 hours**: < 10% salvage rate (near universal necrosis).

### Early & Intermediate Complications
* **Scrotal Haematoma (陰囊血腫)**: From inadequate hemostasis.
* **Surgical Site Infection (手術部位感染)**: Requires oral/IV antibiotics; rarely requires drainage.
* **Reactive Hydrocele (反應性陰囊積水)**.

### Late Complications
* **Testicular Atrophy (睪丸萎縮)**: Even after successful detorsion, reperfusion injury can cause volume loss.
* **Subfertility (次不育)**: Breakdown of the blood-testis barrier during ischaemia leads to anti-sperm antibody formation, affecting the healthy contralateral testis.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the most critical factor determining the prognosis of a torsed testicle? Answer: Time to surgical exploration (the "Time is Testicle" rule, specifically within 6 hours).
* Viva Question: Why do we always perform a bilateral orchidopexy if only one testicle is torsed? Answer: The underlying anatomical defect (bell-clapper deformity) is usually bilateral, so the contralateral side is at high risk of future torsion.
* Trap: Delaying surgery to obtain a formal ultrasound in a highly suspected case. Pearl: If clinical suspicion is high, proceed directly to emergency scrotal exploration; time is testicle.
* Pitfall: Inadequately consenting the patient before scrotal exploration. Always explicitly document consent for possible orchidectomy and contralateral fixation prior to induction.
