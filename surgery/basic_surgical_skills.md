# Basic Surgical Skills (基本外科技巧)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Definition of Basic Surgical Skills
* Foundational operative techniques required for tissue handling, hemostasis, wound closure, and infection control.
* Core competency for all medical graduates (interns/housemen) prior to independent clinical practice.

### Local Epidemiology and HA Requirements
* **Target group**: Interns in surgical/A&E rotations within Hospital Authority (HA) hospitals.
* **Common indications**: Traumatic lacerations (A&E), surgical incisions, central line/chest drain securing, excisional biopsies.
* **Requirement**: Mandatory completion of Basic Surgical Skills Course (BSSC) or equivalent local credentialing.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Skin and Fascial Anatomy for Suturing
* **Epidermis (表皮)**: Avascular, regenerative layer.
* **Dermis (真皮)**: Contains collagen, blood vessels, nerve endings → target for dermal/subcuticular sutures to relieve tension.
* **Subcutaneous tissue (皮下組織)**: Fat layer; holds sutures poorly; potential space for hematoma.
* **Fascia (筋膜)**: High tensile strength; critical layer for deep wound closure to prevent **Dehiscence (傷口裂開)**.
* **Langer’s Lines (朗格氏線)**: Natural skin tension lines. Incisions parallel to lines → ↓ tension, optimal cosmesis.

### Pathophysiology of Wound Healing (傷口癒合)
1. **Hemostasis (止血)** (Seconds-Hours): Vasoconstriction, platelet plug, coagulation cascade (fibrin matrix).
2. **Inflammation (發炎)** (Days 1-4): Vasodilation, neutrophil & macrophage infiltration (phagocytosis of debris/bacteria).
3. **Proliferation (增生)** (Days 4-21): Fibroblast migration (Type III collagen synthesis), angiogenesis, epithelialization.
4. **Remodeling (重塑)** (Days 21-1 Year): Type III collagen replaced by Type I collagen → ↑ tensile strength (max 80% of original skin).

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Signs of a Healthy Healing Wound
* Wound edges well-apposed (no tension/inversion).
* Minimal erythema (<1-2mm from incision line).
* No discharge or minimal clear serous exudate.
* Granulation tissue (肉芽組織) (+ve) in secondary intention wounds: beefy red, moist, granular.

### Signs of Surgical Site Infection (SSI) (外科手術部位感染)
* **Local signs**:
  * ↑ Erythema (spreading cellulitis).
  * ↑ Warmth, induration, and severe localized tenderness.
  * Purulent discharge (膿性分泌物).
  * Malodour.
* **Systemic signs**:
  * Pyrexia (>38°C), tachycardia, chills.

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Pre-operative Wound Assessment Protocol
* **Mechanism**: Crush vs Sharp cut vs Bite (Human/Animal) vs Contaminated (soil/feces).
* **Neurovascular status (神經血管狀態)**: Distal pulses, capillary refill, sensation (2-point discrimination), motor function BEFORE infiltration of **Local Anaesthetic (LA) (局部麻醉藥)**.
* **Foreign bodies**: X-ray to rule out radiopaque glass/metal if suspected.
* **Depth**: Assess involvement of tendon, capsule, bone, or fascia.

### Local Anaesthetic (LA) (局部麻醉藥) Workup and Dosing Protocol
* **Mechanism**: Blocks voltage-gated Na+ channels → prevents action potential propagation.
* **Lidocaine (利多卡因) 1%**: Contains 10mg/mL. Fast onset, short duration (1-2 hours).
* **Maximum Safe Doses (HA Standard)**:
  * Lidocaine *without* Adrenaline: **3 mg/kg** (Max ~200mg).
  * Lidocaine *with* Adrenaline: **7 mg/kg** (Max ~500mg).
  * Bupivacaine (Marcaine) *without* Adrenaline: **2 mg/kg**.
* **Contraindications for LA + Adrenaline**: End arteries (Digits, Nose, Penis, Ears) → risk of ischemic necrosis.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Wound Closure Clinical Algorithm
* **Primary Intention (第一期癒合)**: Immediate closure (sutures/staples/glue). Indication: Clean, fresh wounds (<6-8 hours, or <24h on face) with apposable edges.
* **Secondary Intention (第二期癒合)**: Left open to granulate from base upwards. Indication: Infected, heavily contaminated, or tissue loss.
* **Delayed Primary Closure (Tertiary Intention)**: Cleaned, packed, left open for 3-5 days, then sutured if no infection. Indication: Contaminated wounds requiring delayed assessment (e.g., bites, severe crush).

### Hong Kong HA Tetanus Prophylaxis Protocol
| Immunization History | Clean, Minor Wound | Tetanus-Prone Wound (Contaminated, Crush, Burns) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Completed course + Booster < 5 yrs ago** | None required | None required |

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Principles of Aseptic Non-Touch Technique (ANTT) (無菌技術)
* Scrubbing, gowning, gloving (closed technique).
* Cleaning skin: Chlorhexidine 2% in 70% isopropyl alcohol (or Povidone-Iodine for mucous membranes). Paint from centre → outwards. Do not re-cross cleaned area.
* Proper draping to isolate the sterile field.

### Tissue Handling and Suture Placement Principles
* **Forceps Handling**: Hold forceps (e.g., Toothed Adson) like a pen. Do not crush skin edges; use forceps to gently elevate dermis.
* **Needle Entry**: Enter skin at **90 degrees** to ensure eversion of wound edges. Eversion prevents depressed scars.
* **Rule of Halves**: Place first suture in the middle of the wound to distribute tension evenly.
* **Instrument Tie**: Use needle holder. Flat square knots; extra throws for synthetic monofilaments (e.g., Nylon needs 4-5 throws minimum).
* **Scalpel Grip**: Pencil grip for precise, short cuts; overhand (palmar) grip for long, firm incisions.

### Informed Consent for Minor Surgical Procedures
* Explain indication (wound closure, ↓ infection, promote healing).
* Explain procedure (LA injection, cleaning, suturing).
* Highlight specific risks: Pain, bleeding, SSI, **Scarring (疤痕)**, potential for nerve/vessel damage, need for suture removal.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Post-operative Wound Dressing Principles
* Apply sterile non-adherent dressing (e.g., Melolin) and secure with tape/film.
* Keep dry for 24-48 hours. After 48 hours, wound is generally sealed by epithelialization; gentle washing permitted (avoid soaking).
* Advise patient to elevate limb (if applicable) to ↓ edema and pain.

### Suture Removal Timing by Anatomical Site Protocol
| Anatomical Site | Timing for Removal | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Face / Neck** | 3 - 5 days | Highly vascular, heals rapidly. Prevents unsightly suture track marks. |
| **Scalp** | 7 days | High vascularity, strong skin layer. |
| **Trunk / Abdomen / Upper Extremity** | 7 - 10 days | Moderate tension zones. |
| **Lower Extremity / Back** | 10 - 14 days | High tension zones, thicker stratum corneum. |
| **Over Joints (e.g., Knee, Elbow)** | 14 days | Extreme tension with movement; requires maximum tensile strength before removal. |

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Surgical Complications (<24-48 hours)
* **Bleeding / Hematoma (血腫)**: Due to inadequate hemostasis or slipping of ligatures. ↑ pressure on wound → pain, necrosis.
* **LA Toxicity (LAST - Local Anaesthetic Systemic Toxicity)**: Tinnitus, perioral numbness, metallic taste, seizures, arrhythmias.

### Intermediate Surgical Complications (3-30 days)
* **SSI / Cellulitis**: Requires wound swab, empirical oral Abx (e.g., Augmentin/Flucloxacillin), and potentially opening the wound for drainage.
* **Wound Dehiscence (傷口裂開)**: Suture failure, knot slippage, or excessive tension leading to reopening.

### Late Surgical Complications (Months to Years)
* **Hypertrophic Scar (肥厚性疤痕)**: Thickened scar strictly confined to the boundaries of the original incision.
* **Keloid (蟹足腫)**: Exuberant scar tissue extending *beyond* the boundaries of the original wound (excess Type I/III collagen). ↑ prevalence in darker pigmented skin and presternal/shoulder areas. Treatment: Intralesional steroid (Triamcinolone) injections, silicone gel.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: How do you calculate the maximum volume of 1% Lidocaine (without adrenaline) for a 70kg patient? Answer: Max dose is 3mg/kg. 70kg x 3mg = 210mg max. 1% Lidocaine contains 10mg/mL. Therefore, max volume = 210mg / 10mg/mL = 21mL.
* Viva Question: When should you explicitly avoid using local anaesthetic containing adrenaline? Answer: When infiltrating tissues supplied by end-arteries (fingers, toes, tip of nose, penis, pinna of ear) to avoid irreversible ischemic necrosis.
* Trap: Assuming bite wounds (dog, cat, human) can be closed primarily if they present early. Pearl: Mammalian bites are highly contaminated (e.g., Pasteurella in cats, Eikenella in humans); they should generally be thoroughly irrigated, debrided, and left for secondary or delayed primary closure to prevent severe trapped infections.
* Trap: Taking a large "bite" of epidermis but a small "bite" of dermis when suturing. Pearl: This leads to wound inversion. You must take a wider bite in the deeper dermis to ensure the wound edges evert (pout outwards) for optimal cosmetic healing.
* Pitfall: Tying surgical knots too tightly across a wound. Tissue oedema will inevitably ↑ over the first 48 hours post-op, causing the excessively tight suture to cut into the skin and result in permanent "railroad track" scarring (蜈蚣疤) or localized tissue necrosis.
* Pitfall: Injecting local anaesthetic directly into the inflamed/infected tissue of an abscess. The acidic environment of infected tissue neutralizes the alkaline LA, rendering it ineffective. Perform a regional nerve block instead.
