# Stroke Management (中風處理)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Stroke (中風) and Transient Ischaemic Attack (短暫性腦缺血發作)
* **Stroke (中風):** Rapidly developing clinical signs of focal (or global) disturbance of cerebral function, lasting >24 hours or leading to death, with no apparent cause other than vascular origin.
* **Transient Ischaemic Attack / TIA (短暫性腦缺血發作):** Transient episode of neurological dysfunction caused by focal brain, spinal cord, or retinal ischaemia, without acute infarction.
* **Ischaemic Stroke (缺血性中風):** ~80% of cases. Thrombosis, embolism, or systemic hypoperfusion.
* **Haemorrhagic Stroke (出血性中風):** ~20% of cases. Intracerebral haemorrhage (ICH) or Subarachnoid haemorrhage (SAH).

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong
* **Leading Cause of Morbidity:** 4th leading cause of death in Hong Kong; major burden on HA acute and rehabilitation beds.
* **Ageing Population Impact:** Incidence strictly correlates with ↑ age in the HK demographic.
* **Risk Factors (HA Population):** High prevalence of poorly controlled hypertension (高血壓), diabetes mellitus (糖尿病), smoking, and atrial fibrillation (心房顫動).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of Cerebral Blood Supply
* **Anterior Circulation (前循環):** Internal Carotid Artery (ICA) → Middle Cerebral Artery (MCA) & Anterior Cerebral Artery (ACA). Supplies frontal, parietal, temporal lobes, basal ganglia.
* **Posterior Circulation (後循環):** Vertebral Arteries → Basilar Artery → Posterior Cerebral Arteries (PCA). Supplies brainstem, cerebellum, occipital lobe.
* **Circle of Willis (威利氏環):** Anastomotic polygon at skull base providing collateral flow.

### Pathophysiology of Ischaemic Stroke & The Penumbra (半暗帶)
* **Core Infarct (核心壞死區):** Region of severe ischaemia (CBF <10mL/100g/min) → irreversible cell death / necrosis.
* **Ischaemic Penumbra (缺血半暗帶):** Surrounding viable tissue (CBF 10-20mL/100g/min) functionally impaired but structurally intact → target of acute revascularization (Thrombolysis/Thrombectomy).
* **Cytotoxic Oedema:** Failure of ATP-dependent Na+/K+ pumps → intracellular fluid accumulation → ↑ Intracranial Pressure (ICP) (顱內壓).

### Pathophysiology of Haemorrhagic Stroke
* **Hypertensive Angiopathy:** Charcot-Bouchard microaneurysms in deep penetrating arteries (lenticulostriate) → rupture into basal ganglia/thalamus.
* **Cerebral Amyloid Angiopathy (CAA):** Amyloid-beta deposition in cortical/leptomeningeal vessels → lobar haemorrhages in elderly.
* **Vascular Malformations:** Arteriovenous Malformations (AVM) (腦動靜脈畸形) or aneurysms → typically younger patients.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Anterior Circulation Stroke Signs (前循環中風徵狀)
* **Hemiparesis/Hemiplegia (偏癱):** Contralateral face/arm/leg weakness.
* **Hemisensory Loss:** Contralateral numbness/loss of sensation.
* **Aphasia/Dysphasia (失語症):** If dominant hemisphere (usually left) is affected (Broca's/Wernicke's).
* **Hemianopia (偏盲):** Contralateral homonymous visual field defect.
* **Gaze Palsy:** Eyes deviate towards the side of the lesion (looking at the infarct).

### Posterior Circulation Stroke Signs (後循環中風徵狀)
* **Cranial Nerve Palsies:** Ipsilateral to lesion.
* **Motor/Sensory Deficits:** Contralateral to lesion (crossed signs).
* **Cerebellar Signs:** Ataxia (共濟失調), nystagmus, intention tremor.
* **Decreased GCS:** Coma or altered consciousness due to Reticular Activating System (RAS) involvement.
* **Cortical Blindness:** Bilateral occipital lobe involvement.

### Signs of Elevated Intracranial Pressure (顱內壓增高)
* **Cushing's Triad (庫欣氏三聯徵):** ↑ Systolic BP (widening pulse pressure), ↓ Heart Rate (bradycardia), Irregular respirations.
* **Pupillary Changes:** Fixed, dilated pupil (uncal herniation).
* **Papilloedema (視乳頭水腫):** Swelling of the optic disc (late sign).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Stroke Mimics (中風模仿症狀)
| Differential Diagnosis | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hypoglycaemia (低血糖)** | History of DM + insulin/sulfonylurea, rapidly reversible with IV dextrose. |
| **Todd's Paresis (托德氏麻痺)** | Post-ictal localized weakness following a focal seizure, resolves <48h. |
| **Hemiplegic Migraine** | Gradual onset "aura", visual scintillations, severe headache, younger patient. |
| **Space-Occupying Lesion** | Brain tumour/abscess → insidious progressive onset, papilloedema, +ve mass effect on CT. |
| **Bell's Palsy (貝爾氏麻痺)** | LMN facial nerve palsy (forehead involvement). Stroke is UMN (forehead spared). |

### Bamford / Oxfordshire Clinical Classification
| Stroke Syndrome | Clinical Criteria (Must meet specific combinations) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **TACS (Total Anterior)** | ALL 3: Higher dysfunction (Aphasia/Neglect) + Homonymous hemianopia + Contralateral hemiparesis/sensory loss. |
| **PACS (Partial Anterior)** | 2 of 3 TACS criteria, OR isolated higher dysfunction, OR isolated partial motor/sensory deficit. |
| **LACS (Lacunar)** | Pure motor, Pure sensory, Sensori-motor, or Ataxic hemiparesis. NO higher cortical dysfunction/hemianopia. |
| **POCS (Posterior)** | Cerebellar/Brainstem signs, crossed deficits, isolated hemianopia, or conjugate eye movement disorder. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Immediate Tier 1 Imaging Workup (Code Stroke)
* **Non-Contrast CT Brain (NCCT):** Gold standard 1st-line test to rule out Haemorrhage.
* **Ischaemic Signs on NCCT:** Loss of insular ribbon, obscurement of lentiform nucleus, hyperdense MCA sign (acute thrombus).

### Advanced Tier 2 Imaging Workup (Neurovascular)
* **CT Angiogram (CTA) Brain & Neck:** Identifies Large Vessel Occlusion (LVO) (大血管阻塞) for Endovascular Thrombectomy (EVT) and carotid stenosis.
* **CT Perfusion (CTP):** Differentiates dead core from salvageable penumbra (mismatch ratio). Crucial for extended window EVT (6-24 hours).
* **MRI Brain (DWI/ADC sequences):** Most sensitive for early ischaemic changes; identifies micro-bleeds.

### Essential Pre-operative Laboratory Workup
* **Capillary Blood Glucose (CBG):** Stat rule out hypoglycaemia.
* **Clotting Profile (PT/APTT/INR):** Crucial before thrombolysis/surgery, especially if on NOACs/Warfarin.
* **Complete Blood Picture (CBP):** Rule out thrombocytopenia (platelets <100k contraindicated for thrombolysis).
* **Renal Function Tests (RFT):** Baseline eGFR prior to IV contrast for CTA.

### Cardiac Staging & Embolic Workup
* **12-Lead ECG:** Rule out Atrial Fibrillation (AF) or acute MI.
* **24/48-Hour Holter Monitor:** Detect paroxysmal AF.
* **Echocardiogram (TTE/TOE):** Assess LVEF, valvular heart disease, intramural thrombus, or Patent Foramen Ovale (PFO).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### HA Code Stroke & Acute Medical Management
* **IV Thrombolysis (rTPA / Alteplase):** Indicated within **4.5 hours** of symptom onset.
* **Thrombolysis Contraindications:** BP >185/110 mmHg, recent surgery/trauma <14 days, active bleeding, INR >1.7, PLT <100, severe prior ICH.
* **Blood Pressure Targets (Ischaemic):** Permissive hypertension to maintain cerebral perfusion. Do not lower unless >220/120 mmHg (or >185/110 if receiving tPA).
* **Blood Pressure Targets (Haemorrhagic):** Strict control <140 mmHg systolic to prevent haematoma expansion.

### Endovascular Thrombectomy (EVT) Indications (血管內取血栓術)
* **Anterior Circulation LVO:** ICA or proximal MCA (M1) occlusion.
* **Time Window:** Within 6 hours (standard), or up to 24 hours if favourable core-penumbra mismatch on CTP (DAWN/DEFUSE-3 criteria).
* **Pre-morbid Status:** mRS 0-1 (independent).

### Surgical Indications for Ischaemic Stroke
* **Decompressive Hemicraniectomy (減壓性顱骨切除術):** Indicated for "Malignant MCA syndrome" (massive cerebral oedema causing midline shift/herniation).
* **Timing for Craniectomy:** Ideally within 48 hours for patients <60 years old with profound deficits to reduce mortality (though high risk of severe disability remains).

### Surgical Indications for Haemorrhagic Stroke (ICH)
* **External Ventricular Drain (EVD) (體外腦室引流):** Indicated for acute hydrocephalus due to intraventricular haemorrhage extension.
* **Surgical Evacuation (Craniotomy):** Indicated for superficial lobar clots (>30cm3) causing neurological deterioration. Deep basal ganglia clots generally managed conservatively.
* **Posterior Fossa Decompression:** Urgent suboccipital craniectomy for cerebellar haemorrhage >3cm causing brainstem compression or 4th ventricle effacement.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Decompressive Hemicraniectomy Principles
* **Large Trauma Flap:** Large question-mark shaped incision.
* **Bone Flap:** Must be large (≥12cm diameter) extending to the middle cranial fossa floor to adequately relieve uncal herniation.
* **Dural Expansion:** Crucial step; dura is opened and expanded with an artificial dural patch or pericranium.
* **Bone Storage:** Bone flap stored in anterior abdominal wall subcutaneous pocket or sterile bone bank for later cranioplasty (顱骨修補術).

### Carotid Endarterectomy (CEA) Principles (頸動脈內膜切除術)
* **Indication:** Symptomatic carotid stenosis (70-99%) to prevent secondary stroke. Should be done within 14 days of TIA/minor stroke.
* **Incision:** Anterior to sternocleidomastoid (SCM).
* **Vessel Control:** Control Common Carotid (CCA), Internal Carotid (ICA), and External Carotid (ECA).
* **Plaque Excision:** Arteriotomy, dissect plaque from arterial wall, patch angioplasty closure to prevent re-stenosis.

### Key Consent Issues for Stroke Surgery
* **Decompressive Hemicraniectomy Consent:** Explicit warning of "Survival with severe disability" (mRS 4-5) or Persistent Vegetative State. Risk of infection, epilepsy, and needing future cranioplasty.
* **CEA Consent:** Risk of peri-operative stroke/death (1-2%). Cranial nerve injury (Hypoglossal → tongue deviation; Vagus/RLN → hoarseness; Marginal mandibular → facial asymmetry).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Neuro-ICU Care and Monitoring
* **Neurowatch:** Hourly GCS and pupil checks.
* **ICP Monitoring:** Maintain ICP <20 mmHg and Cerebral Perfusion Pressure (CPP) 60-70 mmHg.
* **Head Elevation:** Bed at 30 degrees to promote venous drainage.
* **Glucose Control:** Strict normoglycaemia (4-10 mmol/L); avoid hyperglycaemia which worsens ischaemic injury.

### Secondary Prevention Strategy
* **Non-Cardioembolic Stroke:** Antiplatelets (Aspirin + Clopidogrel for 21 days, then monotherapy).
* **Cardioembolic Stroke (AF):** Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) or Warfarin. Delay initiation based on infarct size (1-3-6-12 day rule) to avoid haemorrhagic transformation.
* **Lipid Management:** High-intensity statin (Atorvastatin 80mg) aiming LDL <1.4 mmol/L.

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Rehabilitation
* **Physiotherapy (PT):** Early mobilization, spasticity management, gait training.
* **Occupational Therapy (OT):** ADL retraining, home modifications, cognitive assessment.
* **Speech Therapy (ST):** Bedside swallowing assessment (prevent aspiration pneumonia), aphasia rehabilitation.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<7 Days)
* **Haemorrhagic Transformation:** Reperfusion injury causing bleeding into an ischaemic infarct.
* **Cerebral Oedema:** Peaks at 3-5 days; risk of fatal herniation.
* **Peri-operative MI/Stroke:** Especially post-CEA or EVT.
* **Cranial Nerve Palsy:** Specific to neck explorations (CEA).

### Intermediate & Systemic Complications (1-4 Weeks)
* **Aspiration Pneumonia:** Leading cause of mortality in stroke survivors.
* **Venous Thromboembolism (DVT/PE):** High risk due to hemiplegia; require IPCs (Intermittent Pneumatic Compression) and LMWH once bleeding ruled out.
* **Urinary Tract Infection:** Due to neurogenic bladder/catheterization.
* **Pressure Injuries:** Due to immobility and sensory loss.

### Late Complications & Prognostic Scoring
* **Post-Stroke Epilepsy:** Occurs in ~10% of cases, often cortical lesions.
* **Spasticity & Contractures:** UMN lesions leading to severe hypertonia (requires Botox/Baclofen).
* **Post-Stroke Depression:** Under-diagnosed, requires SSRIs and psychological support.
* **Prognosis Scoring:** Modified Rankin Scale (mRS) used to assess functional outcomes (0 = asymptomatic, 6 = death; ≤2 is functional independence).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the defining CT head feature of a hyperacute MCA occlusion? Answer: The hyperdense MCA sign (representing the acute intraluminal thrombus).
* Viva Question: Which vascular territory is affected if a patient presents with pure motor hemiparesis but intact language and visual fields? Answer: Lacunar stroke (LACS) involving the internal capsule or basal ganglia, supplied by lenticulostriate arteries.
* Trap: Lowering blood pressure rapidly in an acute ischaemic stroke. Pearl: Permissive hypertension is necessary to perfuse the ischaemic penumbra. Do not give antihypertensives unless BP >220/120 mmHg (or >185/110 mmHg if planning IV thrombolysis).
* Trap: Assuming a patient with slurred speech and facial droop must have a stroke. Pearl: Always do a stat Capillary Blood Glucose (CBG) first. Hypoglycaemia is a common and easily reversible stroke mimic.
* Pitfall: Reversing Warfarin in acute ischaemic stroke before giving tPA. Administering Vitamin K/PCC makes the patient hypercoagulable and worsens the stroke. If INR is >1.7, tPA is strictly contraindicated; proceed directly to Endovascular Thrombectomy (EVT) workup.
* Pitfall: Performing a small craniectomy for malignant MCA syndrome. A small bone flap acts as a guillotine on the swelling brain, exacerbating cortical necrosis. The flap must be large (≥12cm) to allow adequate expansion.
