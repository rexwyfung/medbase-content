# Post-thyroidectomy Care (甲狀腺切除術後護理)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Definition of Post-thyroidectomy Care
* **Scope**: Immediate & short-term management following **Total Thyroidectomy (全甲狀腺切除術)**, **Hemithyroidectomy (半甲狀腺切除術)**, or **Subtotal Thyroidectomy (次全甲狀腺切除術)**.
* **Core Focus**: Airway protection, hemorrhage detection, calcium homeostasis, and vocal cord function.

### Local Epidemiology (Hong Kong HA Data)
* **Transient Hypocalcemia (短暫性低血鈣)**: ~20-30% (Resolves within 6 months).
* **Permanent Hypoparathyroidism (永久性副甲狀腺功能減退)**: <2%.
* **Transient RLN Palsy (短暫性喉返神經麻痺)**: ~5-8%.
* **Permanent RLN Palsy (永久性喉返神經麻痺)**: <1%.
* **Post-operative Bleeding/Hematoma (術後出血/血腫)**: ~1-2% (Highest risk within first 6-24 hrs).

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Relevant Surgical Anatomy
* **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) (喉返神經)**: Travels in tracheoesophageal groove. Crosses **Inferior Thyroid Artery (ITA) (甲狀腺下動脈)**.
* **External Branch of Superior Laryngeal Nerve (EBSLN) (喉上神經外支)**: Travels near **Superior Thyroid Artery (STA) (甲狀腺上動脈)**.
* **Parathyroid Glands (副甲狀腺)**: 4 distinct glands (superior & inferior pairs). Primarily supplied by **ITA**. 

### Pathophysiology of Post-thyroidectomy Hypocalcemia (術後低血鈣)
* **Mechanism**: Direct mechanical trauma → vasospasm → temporary ischemia.
* **Alternative Mechanism**: Inadvertent total excision of all parathyroid tissue.
* **Result**: ↓ **Parathyroid Hormone (PTH) (副甲狀腺素)** → ↓ Osteoclastic bone resorption + ↓ Renal Ca2+ reabsorption → **Hypocalcemia**.
* **Hungry Bone Syndrome (飢餓骨症候群)**: ↑ Ca2+ uptake into bones post-op in patients with pre-existing thyrotoxicosis or hyperparathyroidism.

### Pathophysiology of Post-operative Hematoma (頸部血腫)
* **Mechanism**: Slipped ligature (STA or ITA) or bleeding from raw muscle edges.
* **Consequence**: Rapid blood accumulation in tight pretracheal fascial space → impairs venous/lymphatic return → **Laryngeal edema (喉水腫)** → Airway obstruction. 

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Symptoms of Post-operative Hypocalcemia
* **Perioral Paresthesia (口周麻木)**
* **Fingertip/Toes Paresthesia (指尖感覺異常)**
* **Muscle Cramping/Spasms (肌肉抽筋)**
* **Palpitations (心悸)** (Secondary to arrhythmias)

### Clinical Signs of Post-operative Hypocalcemia
* **Chvostek's Sign (查沃斯克氏徵)**: Tapping facial nerve anterior to tragus → ipsilateral facial muscle twitching.
* **Trousseau's Sign (特魯索氏徵)**: Inflating BP cuff > systolic pressure for 3 mins → **Carpopedal spasm (腕足痙攣)**.
* **Hyperreflexia (反射亢進)**
* **Prolonged QTc Interval (QTc延長)** on ECG.

### Symptoms and Signs of Post-operative Hematoma
* **Rapid Neck Swelling (頸部迅速腫脹)**
* **Stridor (喘鳴)**
* **Respiratory Distress (呼吸窘迫)**
* **Excessive Drain Output (引流量過多)** (>100ml/hr of fresh blood)
* **Dysphagia (吞嚥困難)**

### Symptoms and Signs of Laryngeal Nerve Injury
* **Unilateral RLN Palsy**: **Hoarseness (聲音沙啞)**, weak "bovine" cough.
* **Bilateral RLN Palsy**: **Acute Airway Obstruction (急性呼吸道阻塞)**, **Stridor (喘鳴)** immediately post-extubation.
* **EBSLN Palsy**: Inability to hit high-pitched notes, voice fatigue (especially in singers/teachers).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Post-operative Airway Distress
| Condition | Timing | Key Features | Neck Appearance | Vocal Cord Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tension Hematoma (張力性血腫)** | 0-24 hrs | Acute stridor, firm neck | **Tense, swollen, ecchymosis** | Normal/Edematous |
| **Bilateral RLN Palsy (雙側喉返神經麻痺)** | Immediate (Extubation) | Acute stridor, aphonia | Normal | **Paramedian/Fixed** |
| **Laryngeal Edema (喉水腫)** | 24-48 hrs | Gradual stridor, hx of difficult intubation | Normal | Edematous/Swollen |
| **Tracheomalacia (氣管軟化症)** | Immediate (Extubation) | Hx of huge long-standing goiter, expiratory stridor | Normal | Normal |

### Differential Diagnosis of Post-operative Neck Swelling
| Condition | Timing | Characteristics | Management |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hematoma (血腫)** | <24 hrs | Tense, expanding, compressive | **Surgical Emergency** |
| **Seroma (血清腫)** | Days-Weeks | Soft, fluctuant, non-compressive | Aspiration/Conservative |
| **Wound Infection (傷口感染)**| >48 hrs | Erythema, warm, purulent discharge | Antibiotics +/- Drainage |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### Post-Operative Biochemical Workup (HA Protocol)
* **Serum Intact PTH (副甲狀腺素)**: Drawn at 4 hours post-op. Best early predictor of hypocalcemia.
* **Serum Adjusted Calcium (校正鈣)**: Drawn daily (e.g., 6 AM) until stable.
* **Serum Phosphate (血清磷)**: ↑ in hypoparathyroidism.
* **Serum Magnesium (血清鎂)**: Must check! ↓ Mg2+ causes refractory hypocalcemia.

### Laryngeal Assessment Workup
* **Flexible Nasoendoscopy (柔性鼻內視鏡)**: 
* **Indication**: Dysphonia, breathy voice, or stridor.
* **Finding in Unilateral RLN palsy**: Ipsilateral cord fixed in paramedian position.
* **Finding in Bilateral RLN palsy**: Both cords fixed with narrow airway chink.

### Emergency Workup for Post-operative Hematoma
* **Clinical Diagnosis (臨床診斷)**: DO NOT wait for imaging. DO NOT send patient to CT if stridor is present.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Post-operative Hypocalcemia Management Protocol
| Severity/PTH Level | Clinical Status | Medical Management (HA Formulary) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Risk** (PTH >1.5 pmol/L) | Asymptomatic, Ca >2.0 | No supplements needed. Observe. |
| **Moderate Risk** (PTH <1.5 pmol/L) | Asymptomatic, Ca 1.9-2.0 | **Oral Calcium Carbonate (碳酸鈣)** (e.g., 1g TDS) + **Alfacalcidol (阿法骨化醇)** (e.g., 0.5-1mcg BD). |
| **Severe/Symptomatic** | Paresthesia, Chvostek +ve, or Ca <1.9 | **IV 10% Calcium Gluconate (葡萄糖酸鈣)** (10ml in 50ml NS over 10-20 mins) via large vein (cardiac monitoring) → Oral stepping up. |

### Emergency Management of Post-operative Hematoma (SCOOP AND RUN)
* **Step 1 (Ward Bedside)**: Call for senior help immediately. Administer high-flow O2.
* **Step 2 (Impending Airway Loss)**: Remove wound dressing → **Cut skin & platysma sutures at bedside (床邊拆線)** → Evacuate clots with gloved finger to relieve pressure.
* **Step 3 (Definitive Care)**: Transfer immediately to Operating Theatre (OT) for neck exploration, washout, and definitive hemostasis.

### Management Algorithm for RLN Palsy
* **Immediate (Bilateral + Distress)**: Emergency Re-intubation → **Tracheostomy (氣管造口術)**.
* **Short-Term (Unilateral)**: **Speech Therapy (言語治療)** (Voice exercises).
* **Long-Term (>6-12 months unresolved)**: **Injection Laryngoplasty (聲帶注射成形術)** (e.g., Hyaluronic acid/fat) or **Medialization Thyroplasty (甲狀軟骨成形術)**.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Operative Principles to Minimize Post-operative Complications
* **Meticulous Hemostasis**: Tie STA/ITA securely. Use energy devices (e.g., Harmonic/Ligasure) carefully away from nerves.
* **Capsular Dissection (被膜解剖)**: Dissect directly on the true capsule of the thyroid to sweep parathyroid glands and their blood supply laterally.
* **Ligature of ITA**: Ligate terminal branches of ITA on the thyroid capsule, NOT the main trunk, to preserve parathyroid blood supply.
* **Visual Identification**: Routine identification of RLN prior to any division in the tracheoesophageal groove.
* **Parathyroid Auto-transplantation (副甲狀腺自體移植)**: If parathyroid is devascularized or inadvertently removed, mince into 1mm fragments and implant into **Sternocleidomastoid (SCM) muscle (胸鎖乳突肌)**.

### Pre-operative Consent Issues (術前同意書)
* **General Risks**: Bleeding, Infection, Scar/Keloid (疤痕增生).
* **Specific Risks**: 
* **Hoarseness/Voice Change (聲音沙啞)** (Temporary vs Permanent).
* **Hypocalcemia (低血鈣)** requiring lifelong supplements.
* **Lifelong Thyroxine Replacement (終生甲狀腺素補充)** (For total thyroidectomy).

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Ward Post-operative Care
* **Positioning**: **Head elevated 30° (抬高頭部)** to ↓ venous engorgement & tissue edema.
* **Airway Monitoring**: Keep Tracheostomy set/Suture removal kit + intubation equipment at bedside.
* **Observation**: Q1H vital signs & drain output monitoring for first 4-6 hours. 

### Surgical Drain Management (HA Practices)
* **Type**: Closed active suction drain (e.g., Radivac).
* **Indication**: Used to monitor bleeding, does NOT prevent tension hematoma. Many HA centers are adopting "drainless" thyroidectomies for uncomplicated cases.
* **Removal Criteria**: Output **<30ml / 24 hours**.

### Discharge Criteria
* **Airway**: Stable, no stridor, no hematoma.
* **Biochemical**: Calcium levels stable (>2.0 mmol/L) on current oral regimen for >24 hrs.
* **Wound**: Clean, dry, no signs of infection.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<24 hours)
* **Tension Hematoma (張力性血腫)**: Highest mortality risk.
* **Thyroid Storm (甲狀腺風暴)**: Extremely rare with modern pre-operative anti-thyroid prep (Carbimazole/Lugol's iodine). Presents with severe tachycardia, hyperthermia, agitation.
* **Tracheomalacia (氣管軟化症)**: Collapse of weak tracheal rings upon extubation.

### Intermediate Complications (Days to Weeks)
* **Hypocalcemia (低血鈣)**: Usually peaks around 24-48 hours post-op.
* **Seroma (血清腫)**: Fluid accumulation beneath skin flap.
* **Wound Infection (傷口感染)**: Cellulitis or abscess.

### Late Complications (Months to Years)
* **Permanent Hypoparathyroidism**: Requires lifelong Ca2+ and Vitamin D.
* **Permanent RLN Palsy**: Persistent hoarseness or aspiration risk.
* **Hypertrophic Scar/Keloid (疤痕增生)**: Common in Asian populations.
* **Hypothyroidism (甲狀腺功能低下)**: Expected outcome of Total Thyroidectomy; treated with daily Levothyroxine (左旋甲狀腺素).

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: How do you clinically differentiate between a unilateral and bilateral recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy post-operatively? Answer: Unilateral presents with a hoarse, breathy voice but adequate airway. Bilateral presents with severe stridor and acute respiratory distress immediately after extubation due to vocal cords adopting a paramedian fixed position.
* Viva Question: What is the most sensitive early biochemical predictor of post-operative hypocalcemia? Answer: Intact PTH level drawn at 4 hours post-operation.
* Viva Question: During a total thyroidectomy, a parathyroid gland is found to be dusky and devascularized. What is the next step? Answer: Remove the gland, confirm it is parathyroid tissue, mince it into 1mm fragments, and auto-transplant it into a muscle pouch in the ipsilateral sternocleidomastoid muscle, marked with a non-absorbable suture.
* Trap: Relying solely on a daily morning calcium level to rule out hypocalcemia in the first 24 hours. Pearl: Calcium levels lag behind parathyroid function; a patient can develop severe tetany despite an initially normal post-op calcium. Always monitor clinical signs (Chvostek/Trousseau) and rely on 4-hour post-op PTH.
* Trap: Assuming post-operative respiratory distress is solely due to laryngeal edema and simply prescribing IV dexamethasone. Pearl: You must actively rule out an expanding compressive neck hematoma. Always examine the neck under the dressing immediately.
* Pitfall: Delaying the opening of a post-operative neck wound in a patient with stridor to wait for the senior surgeon or operating theatre. Cut the skin and platysma sutures at the bedside immediately; this life-saving maneuver relieves pressure on the airway instantly.
* Pitfall: Vigorously pushing IV Calcium Gluconate via a small peripheral vein. It causes severe tissue necrosis and thrombophlebitis if extravasated. Always dilute it (e.g., in 50ml NS), administer slowly over 10-20 minutes, preferably via a large vein, with continuous cardiac monitoring.
