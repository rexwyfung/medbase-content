# Laryngitis & Vocal Cord Lesions (喉炎與聲帶病變)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology

### Clinical Definition of Laryngitis (喉炎)
* **Acute Laryngitis**: Inflammation of **laryngeal mucosa (喉黏膜)** <3 weeks duration. Primarily viral etiology.
* **Chronic Laryngitis**: Persistent inflammation >3 weeks. → Driven by **Laryngopharyngeal Reflux (LPR) (咽喉反流)**, smoking, allergies, or chronic irritant exposure.

### Clinical Definition of Benign Vocal Cord Lesions (良性聲帶病變)
* **Vocal Cord Nodules (聲帶結節)**: Bilateral, symmetrical, callous-like epithelial thickening at the junction of the anterior 1/3 and posterior 2/3 of the true vocal folds.
* **Vocal Cord Polyps (聲帶息肉)**: Typically unilateral, fluid-filled pedunculated or sessile lesion in Reinke's space, often with a reactive contralateral nodule.
* **Reinke's Edema (Reinke氏水腫)**: Bilateral, diffuse polypoid degeneration and fluid accumulation across the entire length of the vocal cords.

### Local Epidemiology in Hong Kong (香港流行病學)
* ↑ prevalence of vocal nodules in teachers and local service industries (frequent HA occupational health referrals) due to **Cantonese (廣東話)** being a tonal language, which demands ↑ vocal cord tension and rapid pitch changes.
* High incidence of **Reinke's Edema** linked to chronic smoking demographics in older populations.
* Endemic rates of **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) (鼻咽癌)** in HK → Unexplained RLN/vagal palsy causing hoarseness MUST prompt immediate exclusion of skull base extension of NPC.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology

### Surgical Anatomy of the Larynx (喉部解剖)
* **Cartilaginous Framework**: Thyroid, Cricoid (only complete cartilaginous ring), paired Arytenoids, Epiglottis.
* **Nerve Supply (神經支配)** via Vagus Nerve (CN X):
 * **Superior Laryngeal Nerve (SLN) (喉上神經)**: Internal branch (sensory supraglottis), External branch (motor to **cricothyroid muscle (環甲肌)** → tensors for high pitch).
 * **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) (喉返神經)**: Motor to ALL intrinsic laryngeal muscles (except cricothyroid). Sensory to subglottis.
* **Microanatomy of Vocal Fold**: Epithelium → Superficial Lamina Propria (SLP) = **Reinke's Space (Reinke氏間隙)** → Intermediate/Deep Lamina Propria (Vocal Ligament) → Thyroarytenoid Muscle (Vocalis).

### Pathophysiology of Vocal Cord Nodules and Polyps
* **Phonotrauma (Vocal Abuse)**: Overuse → ↑ shearing and aerodynamic forces at the mid-membranous vocal fold (point of maximum vibratory amplitude).
* **Microvascular Trauma**: Leads to capillary rupture.
 * Exudation and local edema → **Polyp (息肉)** formation.
 * Chronic mechanical stress → Basement membrane thickening, fibrosis, and epithelial hyperplasia → **Nodule (結節)** formation.

### Pathophysiology of Reinke's Edema
* Chronic exposure to smoking irritants + LPR/voice abuse → Chronic generalized inflammation.
* Poor lymphatic drainage in Reinke's space → Massive accumulation of gelatinous, myxoid matrix in the Superficial Lamina Propria.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs

### Key Symptoms of Laryngeal Disease (喉部疾病症狀)
* **Hoarseness/Dysphonia (聲音沙啞)**: Rough, raspy, or breathy voice. **>3 weeks MUST warrant urgent HA ENT referral (2-week rule for suspected malignancy)**.
* **Vocal Fatigue (聲帶疲勞)**: Voice worsens throughout the day with prolonged use (classic presentation of nodules).
* **Globus Pharyngeus (喉球症)**: Sensation of a lump in the throat, frequent throat clearing (classic in LPR).
* **Stridor (喘鳴)**: 
 * Inspiratory stridor → Supraglottic/glottic level obstruction.
 * Biphasic stridor → Subglottic/tracheal level obstruction.
 * **Red flag**: Indicates impending airway compromise!

### Key Clinical Signs on Examination (理學檢查體徵)
* **Breathiness**: Audible air escape due to incomplete glottic closure (seen in vocal palsy, large polyps).
* **Harshness/Roughness**: Aperiodic vibration from irregular vocal fold edges.
* **Neck Mass/Lymphadenopathy (頸部淋巴結腫大)**: Hard, fixed cervical nodes are a grave red flag for laryngeal/pharyngeal Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features

### Differential Diagnosis of Hoarseness (聲音沙啞鑑別診斷)

| Category | Condition | Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inflammatory** | **Acute Laryngitis (急性喉炎)** | Short duration (<3 weeks), preceding URTI, transient, odynophagia. |
| **Inflammatory** | **Laryngopharyngeal Reflux (LPR)** | Morning hoarseness, globus sensation, chronic non-productive cough, heartburn history. |
| **Benign Neoplastic** | **Vocal Cord Nodules (聲帶結節)** | Bilateral, occupational history (teachers/singers), vocal fatigue, breathy/raspy voice. |
| **Benign Neoplastic** | **Vocal Cord Polyps (聲帶息肉)** | Unilateral, acute onset often following a single severe screaming/coughing event. |
| **Benign Neoplastic** | **Reinke's Edema** | Bilateral diffuse swelling, heavy smoker history, unusually deep/low-pitched "masculine" voice in females. |
| **Malignant** | **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) (鱗狀細胞癌)** | Age >50, heavy smoker/drinker, progressive unremitting hoarseness, ear pain (referred), hemoptysis, weight loss. |
| **Neurological** | **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve Palsy** | Sudden breathy voice, choking on thin liquids, history of recent thyroid surgery, or left-sided apical lung mass (Pancoast). |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)

### 1st Line Bedside Investigations
* **Flexible Nasoendoscopy (FNE) (內視鏡檢查)**: Gold standard primary outpatient assessment. Evaluates vocal fold mobility (palsy), identifies mucosal lesions, and detects signs of LPR (posterior commissure hypertrophy, erythema, pachydermia).
* **Videostroboscopy (喉閃頻錄影檢查)**: Uses a strobe light synchronized with fundamental frequency to evaluate mucosal wave dynamics. Key to differentiate cysts (absent wave over lesion) from nodules/polyps (preserved wave). Rigid, scarred fold highly suspicious for early malignancy.

### Diagnostic Imaging for Laryngeal Lesions
* **CT Neck/Thorax with Contrast (電腦斷層掃描)**: Mandatory if FNE shows isolated vocal cord palsy (to trace RLN pathway from skull base down to aortic arch) OR if there is high clinical suspicion of malignancy (staging).
* **CXR (胸部X光)**: Baseline screening to rule out bronchogenic carcinoma/apical lung tumor compressing the left RLN.

### Pre-operative Objective Measures & Staging Workup
* **Voice Handicap Index (VHI-10)**: Validated patient-reported outcome measure to quantify the psychosocial and functional impact of dysphonia (justifies surgical intervention).
* **Acoustic Analysis**: Measures fundamental frequency, jitter (frequency perturbation), shimmer (amplitude perturbation), and Maximum Phonation Time (MPT).

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)

### Conservative Management for Benign Vocal Lesions
* **Vocal Hygiene & Voice Rest (聲帶休息)**: Absolute voice rest for 3-5 days indicated for acute vocal fold hemorrhage or acute traumatic laryngitis.
* **Speech Therapy (言語治療)**: **Absolute 1st line management for Vocal Cord Nodules**. Teaches optimal pitch, reduces laryngeal muscular tension, and corrects maladaptive compensatory phonation.

### Medical Management in Hong Kong HA Protocols
* **Acute Viral Laryngitis**: Supportive care. Hydration, humidification, simple analgesia. **Antibiotics are contraindicated** unless overt bacterial superinfection exists.
* **Laryngopharyngeal Reflux (LPR)**: High-dose Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPI) (e.g., **Omeprazole 20mg BD or Pantoprazole 40mg BD** for minimum 2-3 months) + lifestyle modification (elevate head of bed, avoid late meals/caffeine).

### Surgical Indications for Vocal Cord Lesions
* Refractory symptoms despite maximal speech therapy and strict medical management (PPIs).
* Need for histologic diagnosis (suspicion of malignancy, leukoplakia).
* Large **Vocal Cord Polyps/Cysts** causing symptomatic airway obstruction, recurrent hemorrhage, or intractable dysphonia.
* Progressive **Reinke's Edema** causing stridor, sleep apnea, or when patient requests voice improvement (only after smoking cessation).

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues

### Key Operative Principles of Microlaryngoscopy (顯微喉鏡手術原則)
* **Procedure**: **Suspension Microlaryngoscopy (SML) (懸吊式顯微喉鏡)**.
* **Anesthesia**: General Anesthesia (GA) with a **small endotracheal tube (size 5.0-6.0 Microlaryngeal tube) (氣管內管)** positioned posteriorly to maximize anterior surgical field.
* **Microflap Technique**: Make a micro-incision laterally, dissect and preserve the superficial lamina propria (SLP) to maintain the mucosal wave. 
* **Preservation of Vocal Ligament**: DO NOT resect deep to the SLP. Injury to the vocal ligament causes irreversible scarring.
* **Instrumentation**: **Cold Steel Instruments (冷器械)** are preferred over CO2 laser for benign lesions to avoid collateral thermal injury and subsequent fibrosis.

### Informed Consent Issues for Laryngeal Surgery (喉部手術知情同意)
* Risk of **no voice improvement** or **paradoxical worsening of hoarseness** (due to scarring/fibrosis).
* **Dental/Oral trauma**: High risk of lip laceration, tooth chipping, or luxation from the rigid metal suspension laryngoscope pressing on upper incisors (require gum guards).
* **Altered taste/tongue numbness**: Due to compression of the lingual nerve/chorda tympani by the laryngoscope blade.
* Requirement for strict post-operative voice rest.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation

### Immediate Post-operative Care for Airway Protection
* Close airway monitoring in PACU. High risk for **laryngospasm** or acute laryngeal edema upon extubation.
* Intravenous **Dexamethasone (地塞米松)** (e.g., 4-8mg) often administered intra-operatively or immediately post-op to ↓ reactive airway edema.
* Cold mist humidification.

### Vocal Rehabilitation and Speech Therapy (言語治療)
* **Strict Voice Rest (絕對禁聲)**: Mandatory for 3-7 days post-op to allow re-epithelialization. **Whispering is strictly forbidden**.
* **Post-op Speech Therapy**: Essential to prevent recurrence of functional lesions (nodules/polyps). Reinforces correct phonation mechanics during the healing phase.
* **Reflux Management**: Continuous PPI therapy to optimize the acidic mucosal environment for optimal healing.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis

### Early Surgical Complications
* **Airway Compromise**: Acute glottic edema or severe laryngospasm.
* **Hemorrhage**: Post-operative bleeding into the airway, risking rapid asphyxiation.
* **Oropharyngeal Trauma**: Mucosal lacerations, dental fractures, TMJ dislocation.

### Intermediate to Late Surgical Complications
* **Vocal Cord Scarring/Synechiae (聲帶粘連)**: Secondary to over-resection, thermal injury, or operating on the anterior commissure bilaterally at the same time. Leads to an **Anterior Glottic Web** and permanent dysphonia.
* **Recurrence of Lesion**: High recurrence rate if underlying phonotrauma, LPR, or smoking habits are not corrected post-operatively.

### Prognosis of Benign Vocal Cord Lesions
* **Nodules**: Excellent prognosis with dedicated speech therapy alone. Surgery rarely needed.
* **Polyps/Cysts**: Good structural prognosis with surgery, but functional voice outcomes depend on post-op rehabilitation compliance.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)

* Viva Question: What is the rule of thumb for hoarseness duration before urgent referral to ENT in Hong Kong? Answer: Any hoarseness lasting >3 weeks requires urgent ENT referral and flexible nasoendoscopy to definitively rule out laryngeal malignancy.
* Trap: Prescribing empirical antibiotics for acute, new-onset hoarseness or laryngitis. Pearl: Acute laryngitis is overwhelmingly viral in etiology. Management is entirely supportive (hydration, voice rest). Antibiotics are contraindicated unless there are overt signs of bacterial sepsis or purulence.
* Pitfall: Surgically resecting benign lesions from the anterior commissure on both vocal folds simultaneously during the same operation. This creates opposing raw mucosal surfaces, leading to the formation of an anterior glottic web (synechiae). Always perform staged procedures if bilateral anterior lesions must be excised.
* Viva Question: Which recurrent laryngeal nerve (RLN) is more prone to insidious injury and why? Answer: The left RLN. It has a significantly longer course, looping under the arch of the aorta in the mediastinum, making it highly susceptible to mediastinal pathology (e.g., aortic arch aneurysms, hilar lymphadenopathy, apical lung tumors).
* Trap: Instructing a post-operative laryngeal surgery patient to "whisper" instead of talking loudly to protect their healing vocal cords. Pearl: Whispering is a hyperfunctional phonatory state that actually increases muscular tension and strain on the vocal folds, impairing healing. Absolute silence (strict voice rest) is mandatory.
