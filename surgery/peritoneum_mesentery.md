# Peritonitis & Mesenteric Ischaemia (腹膜炎與腸繫膜缺血)

## 1. Clinical Definition & Local Epidemiology
### Clinical Definition of Peritonitis
* **Peritonitis (腹膜炎)**: Inflammation of the serosal membrane lining the abdominal cavity and its organs.
* **Primary (1°)**: Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP) without GI tract breach.
* **Secondary (2°)**: Due to GI perforation, infection, or ischaemia spilling contents into peritoneal cavity.
* **Tertiary (3°)**: Persistent/recurrent peritonitis >48h despite adequate surgical source control.

### Clinical Definition of Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Mesenteric Ischaemia (腸繫膜缺血)**: Inadequate blood flow to the GI tract → bowel infarction and necrosis.
* **Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia (AMI)**: Sudden onset (arterial embolus/thrombus, venous thrombosis, or non-occlusive).
* **Chronic Mesenteric Ischaemia (CMI)**: Episodic hypoperfusion (intestinal angina).

### Hong Kong Epidemiology & Local Context
* **Secondary Peritonitis**: One of the most common surgical emergencies in Hong Kong Hospital Authority (HA) hospitals (e.g., perforated peptic ulcer [PPU], acute appendicitis).
* **Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia**: ↑ incidence in HK due to an ageing population and ↑ Atrial Fibrillation (AF) prevalence. High mortality (>60%) primarily due to delayed presentation.

## 2. Surgical Anatomy & Pathophysiology
### Peritoneal Anatomy & Fluid Shifts
* **Parietal Peritoneum (壁層腹膜)**: Somatic innervation (intercostal/phrenic nerves) → well-localised, sharp pain.
* **Visceral Peritoneum (臟層腹膜)**: Autonomic innervation → poorly localised, dull, referred pain.
* **Pathophysiology of Peritonitis**: Irritant (pus/faeces/gastric acid) → hyperaemia → massive fluid shift into peritoneal cavity (3rd spacing) → **Hypovolaemic Shock (低血容量性休克)** & Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS).

### Mesenteric Vascular Anatomy
* **Coeliac Trunk (腹腔動脈幹)**: Supplies foregut.
* **Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA) (腸繫膜上動脈)**: Supplies midgut. *Most susceptible to emboli* due to its oblique takeoff angle from the aorta.
* **Inferior Mesenteric Artery (IMA) (腸繫膜下動脈)**: Supplies hindgut.
* **Watershed Areas (分水嶺區)**: Splenic flexure (**Griffith's point**) & rectosigmoid junction (**Sudeck's point**) → collateral circulation is weakest, highest risk for ischaemic colitis.

### Pathogenesis of Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Arterial Embolism (動脈栓塞)** (50%): Cardiac source (AF, infective endocarditis) → typically lodges distal to middle colic artery origin.
* **Arterial Thrombosis (動脈血栓)** (25%): Rupture of atherosclerotic plaque right at SMA origin → more extensive bowel ischaemia.
* **Non-Occlusive Mesenteric Ischaemia (NOMI)** (20%): Splanchnic vasoconstriction 2° to low cardiac output (shock, heart failure).
* **Mesenteric Venous Thrombosis (MVT) (腸繫膜靜脈血栓)** (5%): Hypercoagulable states, portal HTN, intra-abdominal sepsis.

## 3. Key Symptoms & Signs
### Clinical Presentation of Peritonitis
* **Severe Abdominal Pain (劇烈腹痛)**: Sudden onset (suggests perforation) or gradual (suggests progressive inflammation).
* **Rebound Tenderness (反跳痛)**: Blumberg's sign +ve.
* **Guarding & Rigidity (腹肌僵硬)**: Involuntary reflex spasm of abdominal musculature (board-like rigidity in PPU).
* **Systemic Signs**: Tachycardia, fever, hypotension, oliguria → imminent **Septic Shock (敗血性休克)**.

### Clinical Presentation of Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Pain Out of Proportion to Signs (疼痛與體徵不符)**: Hallmark of early AMI. Severe, unremitting visceral pain with a soft, non-tender abdomen initially.
* **Gut Emptying Symptoms**: Nausea, vomiting, sudden forceful bowel movement (often bloody).
* **Late Signs**: Peritonitis & haemodynamic collapse (indicates transmural infarction/perforation).

### Clinical Presentation of Chronic Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Intestinal Angina (腸絞痛)**: Postprandial abdominal pain 30-60 mins after eating.
* **Weight Loss (體重減輕)**: Due to food fear (sitophobia).

## 4. Differential Diagnosis & Discriminatory Features
### Differential Diagnosis of Acute Abdomen (急腹症)
| Condition | Key Discriminatory Features |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Perforated Peptic Ulcer (穿孔性消化性潰瘍)** | Sudden epigastric pain, NSAIDs/H. pylori history, board-like abdomen, pneumoperitoneum on CXR. |
| **Acute Pancreatitis (急性胰臟炎)** | Epigastric pain radiating to back, ↑ Amylase/Lipase (>3x limit), gallstones/alcohol history. |
| **Acute Appendicitis (急性闌尾炎)** | Migratory pain (periumbilical → RIF), localized peritonitis in RIF. |
| **Ruptured AAA (腹主動脈瘤破裂)** | Pulsatile abdominal mass, back pain, profound hypotension/shock. |
| **Bowel Obstruction (腸阻塞)** | Colicky pain, distension, absolute constipation, vomiting, dilated loops on AXR. |

## 5. Tiered Investigations & Pre-operative Workup (Labs, Imaging, Staging)
### Laboratory Investigations for Acute Abdomen
* **CBC**: ↑ WCC (leukocytosis) with left shift, ↓ Hb (if concurrent GI bleed).
* **Renal Function (U&E)**: ↑ Urea, ↑ Creatinine (pre-renal AKI due to 3rd spacing).
* **Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)**: Metabolic acidosis (代謝性酸中毒) with ↑ **Lactate (乳酸)** (late marker of bowel ischaemia).
* **Group & Save / Crossmatch**: Mandatory pre-operative prep.
* **Amylase/Lipase**: Rule out acute pancreatitis.

### Radiological Imaging for Peritonitis
* **Erect CXR (直立位胸部X光)**: Detects pneumoperitoneum (氣腹) (air under right hemidiaphragm) indicating perforated viscus.
* **CT Abdomen & Pelvis with IV Contrast**: Gold standard for delineating source of secondary peritonitis (abscess, mass, inflammation) if diagnosis is equivocal.

### Radiological Imaging for Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **CT Mesenteric Angiography (CTMA) (電腦斷層腸繫膜血管造影)**: Gold standard. Requires biphasic scan (arterial & venous).
* **CTMA Findings in AMI**: SMA filling defect, bowel wall thickening, **Pneumatosis Intestinalis (腸壁積氣)**, portal venous gas (highly fatal late signs).

### Pre-operative Preparation Protocol
* **Sepsis Six Protocol (HA Standard)**: High-flow O2, empirical broad-spectrum IV antibiotics, IV fluid resuscitation (crystalloids).
* **Gastric Decompression**: Insert Nasogastric (NG) tube (Ryle's tube).
* **Catheterization**: Insert Foley catheter → strict hourly urine output monitoring.

## 6. Management Algorithm & Surgical Indications (Conservative, Medical, HA Protocols)
### Medical Management of Primary Peritonitis
* **Indication**: Ascites in liver cirrhosis, peritoneal dialysis (PD) patients.
* **Treatment**: IV Ceftriaxone (HA protocol) after ascitic tap for culture. Usually requires NO surgical intervention.

### Resuscitation & Medical Management for Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Aggressive IV Fluids**: Correct profound hypovolaemia.
* **Anticoagulation**: IV Heparin (肝素) for mesenteric venous thrombosis (MVT) or to prevent clot propagation in arterial AMI.
* **Vasodilators**: Intra-arterial Papaverine (if NOMI is diagnosed on angiography).
* **Optimization**: Correct arrhythmias (AF), strictly avoid vasopressors if possible (exacerbates splanchnic ischaemia).

### Surgical Indications for Peritonitis
* **Absolute**: Secondary peritonitis with perforated viscus, strangulated bowel, ruptured appendicitis.
* **Goal**: Source control (縫合、切除或造口) + exhaustive peritoneal lavage.

### Surgical Indications for Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Absolute**: Peritonitis, transmural bowel infarction, failed medical therapy.
* **Goal**: Revascularization + resection of frankly necrotic bowel.

## 7. Key Operative Principles & Consent Issues
### Operative Principles for Secondary Peritonitis
* **Incision**: Midline Laparotomy (中線剖腹探查術) for maximum exposure.
* **Source Control**: Repair perforation (e.g., Graham patch for PPU), remove inflamed organ (appendicectomy), or bowel resection.
* **Peritoneal Lavage (腹腔沖洗)**: Copious warm saline washout (often >5L) to remove pus/faeces/fibrin.
* **Damage Control Surgery (損傷控制外科)**: If patient is physiologically exhausted (acidosis, hypothermia, coagulopathy triad) → leave abdomen open (Laparostomy / VAC dressing), plan for 2nd look.

### Operative Principles for Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia
* **Assessment**: Check bowel viability (colour, peristalsis, palpable mesenteric pulsations; use Wood's lamp + IV fluorescein if available).
* **Revascularization (Arterial Embolus)**: Transverse arteriotomy → SMA embolectomy (Fogarty catheter) *before* bowel resection (may reverse marginal ischaemia).
* **Resection**: Resect frankly necrotic bowel. *Do NOT construct primary anastomosis* if bowel viability is doubtful or patient in septic shock.
* **Stoma Formation**: Bring out viable bowel ends as end stoma / mucous fistula.
* **Second-Look Laparotomy**: Mandatory in 24-48 hours to reassess marginal bowel viability.

### Informed Consent (手術同意書)
* **General Risks**: Bleeding, infection, damage to adjacent organs, DVT/PE, MI/stroke, mortality.
* **Specific Risks**: Need for stoma (造口), high risk of **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸症候群)** requiring lifelong TPN if massive resection needed, open abdomen/ICU care post-op, extremely high mortality in AMI.

## 8. Post-operative Care (ERAS) & Rehabilitation
### Immediate Post-operative ICU/HDU Care
* **Haemodynamic Support**: Inotropes/vasopressors if needed, strict fluid balance.
* **Ventilation**: Elective intubation and ventilation for damage-control laparotomies.
* **HA Antibiotic Protocol**: Continue broad-spectrum IV antibiotics (e.g., IV Amoxicillin/Clavulanate + Metronidazole OR IV Cefuroxime + Metronidazole) guided by intra-op peritoneal cultures.

### Post-operative Nutrition & Gastrointestinal Care
* **Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN) (全靜脈營養)**: Initiated early if prolonged ileus or massive bowel resection (Short Bowel Syndrome) is anticipated.
* **Enteral Feeding**: Slowly introduced via NG/NJ tube once bowel function returns (stoma output, flatus) to maintain mucosal integrity.

### ERAS Ward Care & Rehabilitation Protocols
* **DVT Prophylaxis**: LMWH (Enoxaparin) + TED stockings once post-op bleeding risk subsides.
* **Mobilization**: Early mobilization and incentive spirometry/chest physiotherapy to prevent atelectasis.

## 9. Surgical Complications (Early, Intermediate, Late) & Prognosis
### Early Complications (<1 week)
* **Sepsis/Septic Shock (敗血性休克)**: Refractory hypotension, Multi-Organ Failure (MOF).
* **Wound Dehiscence/Burst Abdomen**: 2° to wound infection or high intra-abdominal pressure.
* **Anastomotic Leak (吻合口瘻)**: High risk if anastomosis performed in ischaemic/inflamed tissue or hypotensive patient.

### Intermediate Complications (1-3 weeks)
* **Intra-abdominal Abscess (腹腔膿腫)**: Presents with swinging pyrexia and localized pain. Requires CT-guided percutaneous drainage.
* **Prolonged Paralytic Ileus**: Bowel fails to regain motility, requiring prolonged NG decompression.

### Late Complications (>1 month)
* **Short Bowel Syndrome (短腸症候群)**: <200cm of functional small bowel remaining → severe malabsorption, requires lifelong TPN.
* **Incisional Hernia (切口疝)**: High risk following emergency laparotomy/laparostomy.
* **Adhesive Small Bowel Obstruction (腸粘連)**: Due to severe intra-abdominal inflammation and fibrinous bands.

### Prognosis & Mortality
* **Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia**: Overall mortality >60%. Prognosis heavily depends on time to revascularization.
* **Secondary Peritonitis**: Scoring systems (**APACHE II** or **Mannheim Peritonitis Index**) correlate directly with mortality risk.

## 10. Examiner's Pearls (High-yield viva questions and common pitfalls)
* Viva Question: What is the classic clinical presentation of Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia? Answer: Severe abdominal pain completely out of proportion to physical signs in a patient with a history of atrial fibrillation.
* Viva Question: Which artery is most commonly occluded in embolic mesenteric ischaemia and why? Answer: Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA) due to its acute, oblique takeoff angle from the aorta.
* Trap: Normal serum lactate rules out mesenteric ischaemia. Pearl: Serum lactate is a late marker of transmural bowel necrosis; a normal lactate level in early ischaemia does NOT rule out the diagnosis.
* Trap: Constructing a primary anastomosis after resecting ischaemic bowel in a haemodynamically unstable patient. Pearl: Always perform damage control surgery with an end stoma; never anastomose compromised bowel in a shocked patient.
* Pitfall: Forgetting to schedule a second-look laparotomy in mesenteric ischaemia. Always plan a mandatory second-look operation at 24-48 hours to assess marginal bowel viability.
* Pitfall: Delaying emergency surgery to obtain a CT scan in a patient with generalized peritonitis and haemodynamic instability. Resuscitate and proceed directly to the operating theatre for life-saving source control.
